Key requirements

  • Experience in enterprise architecture and/or solution or domain architecture, with the ability to connect strategy, governance and delivery
  • Strong capability to lead architecture discussions, influence stakeholders and drive alignment across teams without direct authority
  • Practical experience with enterprise architecture repositories and tools such as Ardoq or comparable solutions
  • Solid modelling skills, for example using ArchiMate, and the ability to create clear, decision-ready architecture diagrams
  • Strong knowledge and proven experience with Azure and the Microsoft technology stack, ideally supported by advanced-level certifications such as Azure Solution Architect
  • Hands-on experience in technical areas including cloud, infrastructure, networking, identity, APIs and integration, and security patterns
  • Strong analytical and problem-solving skills, with the ability to structure complex topics and communicate them clearly
  • A university degree in business informatics, computer science or a related field, or equivalent professional experience
  • Excellent communication skills with proficiency in English; German proficiency is an advantage

Application process

  • This role contributes to shaping and evolving the firm's enterprise-wide architecture across business, data, application, integration, technology and security domains.
  • The team connects strategy with execution by translating architectural direction into clear standards, reference architectures, patterns and roadmaps that delivery teams can apply in practice.
  • Maintain and evolve transparency of the IT landscape across applications, platforms, integrations and key data flows, including inventories, capability maps and dependency views
  • Ensure the IT landscape is accurately captured and usable in enterprise architecture repositories and tools, applying data quality practices and creating stakeholder-friendly views
  • Support architecture governance and compliance by helping teams align designs with agreed standards and guardrails, and by documenting exceptions and trade-offs clearly
  • Act as a trusted advisor to delivery teams and stakeholders, ensuring non-functional requirements such as security, resilience, performance, maintainability and cost are addressed early and consistently
  • Translate architectural direction into consumable assets, including minimum viable patterns, reusable building blocks, templates and reference implementations, to accelerate delivery and reduce rework
  • Evaluate technologies and standards, recommend fit-for-purpose solutions, and support standard configurations to improve resilience, scalability and cost-effectiveness
