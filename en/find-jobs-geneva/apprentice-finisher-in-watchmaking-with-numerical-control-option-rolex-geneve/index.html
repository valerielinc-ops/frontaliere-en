Original description

Motivated by an apprenticeship as a Finisher - Numerical Control option? Ready to become a reference in your trade thanks to high-quality training in a leading company? Join us! The watchmaking finisher receives the various parts of a watch once they have been machined. He/she subjects them to a series of finishing operations (polishing, satin-finishing, highlighting, etc.) designed to give their surface the final aesthetic appearance desired by the customer. This profession allows you to tackle precision mechanics as well as aesthetic and dimensional control. After initial manual training, the Numerical Control (NC) orientation allows you to specialize in carrying out operations using automated machines on large volumes. This apprenticeship is suitable for young people who are comfortable in technological and mechanical environments, with a keen eye for detail. • Profile - Visual acuity and manual dexterity - Concentration and patience - Interest in mechanics, metalworking, and technical drawing - Autonomy - Good spatial representation • What we offer - You will be guided by experienced trainers - You will be able to evolve in an exceptional working environment - This training will ensure you have excellent prospects - Rolex is committed to offering you more than just an apprenticeship: a real opportunity for personal and professional development Contact - If you are motivated to take on the challenge, do not hesitate to apply - Your application must include: - a curriculum vitae - a letter of motivation - your school reports for the last three years - your EVATech test results - Thank you for registering through the following link: https://eva-evatech.ch/
