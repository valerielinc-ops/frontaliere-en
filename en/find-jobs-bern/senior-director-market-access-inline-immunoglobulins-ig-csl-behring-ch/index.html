Role overview

Role Overview: The Senior Director of Market Access for Inline Assets will be responsible for shaping and implementing market access strategies for CSL Behring inline assets.

This role is accountable for maximizing the asset total life cycle value throughout product life cycle.

It requires strong expertise in obtaining, sustaining, and optimizing product pricing & reimbursement, and a comprehensive understanding of the payer environment in key international markets.

Main responsibilities

  • Key Responsibilities:
  • Lead the Market Access inline team in one Therapeutic Areas (TAs).
  • Build, mentor, and lead a high-performing global market access inline team, fostering a culture of collaboration, innovation, inclusion, and accountability.
  • Serve as a key member of the global Market Access Leadership Team (MALT), contributing to the development and execution of functional strategy, operations, and capability building.
  • Accountable for Market Access deliverables during the inline phase, continuously refining and improving standards for these deliverables.
  • Collaborate with Medical Affairs, Marketing, and other partner functions to influence brand strategy and execution, ensuring proper integration of market access considerations.
  • Keep abreast with payer policies and regulations, economic data within the assigned TA in key international markets.
  • Identify market access opportunities and risks, capture opportunities, mitigate risks through proactive planning, scenario analysis, and stakeholder engagement.
  • Deliver and periodically update the Global Value Dossier as a foundational document for affiliate Pricing & Reimbursement (P&R) and Health Technology Assessment (HTA) submissions and resubmissions.
  • Provide guidance and support to country teams in P&R and HTA submissions, negotiations, and authority responses, ensuring alignment with global strategies.

  • Extensive knowledge of global healthcare systems, payer landscapes, and the interplay between market access and international markets.
