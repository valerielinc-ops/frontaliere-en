L'Agefi shakes up leadership and seeks new partners (cross-border guide)

Frédéric Lelièvre leaves L'Agefi for Le Temps; Jonas Follonier becomes editor-in-chief and Olivier Bloch director

Contesto

In brief - Frédéric Lelièvre leaves L'Agefi for Le Temps - Jonas Follonier becomes editor-in-chief of L'Agefi - Olivier Bloch takes over the direction of L'Agefi ## Key facts - What: Change in L'Agefi's management - When: September 2023 - Where: L'Agefi, French-speaking Switzerland - Who: Frédéric Lelièvre, Jonas Follonier, Olivier Bloch - Amount: Not specified After six years at the helm of L'Agefi, Frédéric Lelièvre has decided to leave his roles as editor-in-chief and CEO to join Le Temps, where he will lead a new project dedicated to finance and economics. This change in management involves a double appointment within L'Agefi: Jonas Follonier, currently correspondent at the Federal Palace in Bern, becomes editor-in-chief, while Olivier Bloch takes over the direction. L'Agefi, a Swiss French-language economic and financial daily newspaper, is primarily present in French-speaking Switzerland, with readers also in Geneva, Lausanne, and Neuchâtel. The publication is known for its in-depth coverage of financial news and regulations, such as the sustainable finance law that came into force in 2022, which introduced transparency obligations for companies with more than 500 employees and a turnover of over 40 million francs. > "L'Agefi will continue to be a reference point for financial news in French-speaking Switzerland," declared Olivier Bloch. L'Agefi will now face the challenge of attracting new members to support its editorial activities in an increasingly competitive market.

Dettagli operativi

New Challenges for L'Agefi The transition at the top of L'Agefi opens a phase of reflection to strengthen the development of the publication and its positioning in the market. The priorities are to ensure the stability and quality of the newspaper, which has an online site and comes out twice a week in print. Antoine Hubert, owner of L'Agefi, has announced that he is looking for new support for the newspaper, considering options such as foundations, associations, or new investors. > "The search for new partners is motivated by the desire not to carry the project forward alone and to bring together other people who believe an independent economic and liberal voice is necessary in Romandy," Hubert emphasized. Hubert intends to strengthen L'Agefi's presence in German-speaking Switzerland, where it already has a certain audience, particularly in Zurich, and to diversify the newspaper's activities, exploring opportunities in events, such as participation in economic fairs in Basel and Geneva, in business services, and in specialized content. Romandy represents an important market for L'Agefi, with cantons such as Geneva and Vaud contributing significantly to the national GDP. The publication could also benefit from the provisions of the Swiss federal law on media subsidies (LSoMe), which came into force on March 1, 2023, which provides financial aid for independent media up to a maximum of 50 million francs per year. L'Agefi could also take advantage of initiatives to support local and regional media promoted by various Swiss foundations. Future prospects L'Agefi is not just a newspaper, but aims to become a platform that brings together economic, political, and entrepreneurial actors. Antoine Hubert confirmed that he has conducted discussions with Le Temps to build something together, but they did not lead to anything due to different visions. The challenge for L'Agefi will be to maintain its liberal and independent editorial line while opening up to new partners and growth opportunities. For example, the recent revision of the Cartel Act (LCart) that came into force on December 1, 2021, introduced lower control thresholds for business concentrations, with a combined turnover of CHF 100 million. - Opening up to new partners could happen through collaborations with financial institutions such as the Swiss National Bank in Zurich or the Zürcher Kantonalbank. > 'Our editorial independence is fundamental, but it's equally important to be open to opportunities that can strengthen our position in the Swiss market,' Antoine Hubert said.
