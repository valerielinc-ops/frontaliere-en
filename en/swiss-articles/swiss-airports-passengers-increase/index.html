In Brief The three main Swiss airports have handled more passengers and cargo in the winter semester 2025/26. The number of flights has decreased slightly. The growth engine was Zurich Airport, which handled 14.8 million passengers (+6.7%). Geneva welcomed 8.7 million people (+1.8%), while EuroAirport showed a decline to 3.8 million (-2.6%). ## The Passenger Boom at Zurich Airport Zurich Airport, located in the canton of Zurich, was the main growth engine of air traffic in Switzerland in the winter semester 2025/26. According to official data, the airport recorded a 6.7% increase in passengers, bringing the total to 14.8 million travelers. This result is largely due to the increase in traffic to and from Western Europe, particularly to French and German destinations. ## Geneva's Growth Geneva Airport, located in the canton of Geneva, has also shown an increase in traffic, albeit more modest than Zurich. Official data indicate a 1.8% increase in passengers, bringing the total to 8.7 million travelers. This result is due in part to the increase in traffic to and from Western Europe, as well as the increase in traffic to and from African and Asian destinations. ## EuroAirport's Decline EuroAirport, located at Basel-Mulhouse-Freiburg, has instead shown a decline in traffic, with a total of 3.8 million passengers (-2.6%).
