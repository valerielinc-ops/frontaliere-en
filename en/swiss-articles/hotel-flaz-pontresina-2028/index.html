Dettagli operativi

Economic and social implications In a national perspective, the beginning of construction for the Hotel Flaz in Pontresina is an important signal for tourism and for the updating of infrastructure in the alpine cantons. This development fits into a broader strategy of increasing economic investments in mountainous areas, as demonstrated by several projects financed by the federal direct tax and local VAT, which help strengthen the competitiveness of goods and services. The modernization of tourist facilities now moves in parallel with renewals in the labor market sectors and changes in living costs, with particular reference to factors related to the AVS/ALCV reforms and updates to the LPP/BVG. These factors inevitably affect the economic stability of Swiss residents in tourist areas. Within the cantonal context, the project opens scenarios for comparison between the current situation and the pre-intervention state: the shift from limited areas to a broader and more modern offering has the potential to increase the volume of tourist arrivals, also benefiting the distribution of revenues through efficient management of restaurants and wellness services. The new leisure area, planned for the Rosegplatz, will contribute to the national visibility of Pontresina as a cultural and sports hub, attracting a mix of visitors, from business clients to alpine tourists, for whom inelastic values such as the minimum wage and the cost of living are decisive in spending choices. Frequently Asked Questions
What is the duration of the winter season for which the Hotel Flaz is designed?
The Hotel Flaz will open its doors during the winter season 2028/2029, as announced in the press releases. The precise operational duration will be defined based on the seasonality and meteorological conditions, in line with regional standards that also take into account economic variables related to the cost of living and tourism policies.
What judicial proceedings preceded the start of the project?
The project has passed through several instances: after the territorial planning was approved in 2014, a committee proposed a modification, which was then deemed invalid by the municipality. Subsequently, the case was discussed before the cantonal administrative court and, finally, rejected by the federal court, allowing the definitive start of the works.
What impact is expected on local tourism?
The integration of new leisure spaces, including new business buildings and public sports facilities, aims to strengthen the tourist offer of Pontresina, encouraging a greater attraction of guests even beyond the winter season, with benefits at a large scale thanks to the creation of new jobs and the development of local partnerships.
