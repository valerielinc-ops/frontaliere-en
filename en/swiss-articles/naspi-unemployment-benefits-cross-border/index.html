Unemployment Benefits for Cross-Border Workers: Rules and Procedures (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 6 June 2026

Discover how the unemployment benefit works for those returning to Italy after working in Switzerland.

Contesto

In brief The NASpI is the unemployment benefit for those returning to Italy. The right is subject to the involuntary termination of the employment contract. It is necessary to pay contributions in Switzerland for insurance. ## Key facts - What: NASpI unemployment benefit - Where: Switzerland and Italy - Who: Cross-border workers - Requirement: Involuntary termination of the employment contract The NASpI, New Social Insurance for Employment, represents the cornerstone for workers who, after working in Swiss territory, find themselves unemployed and return to their country of residence, Italy. The current regulations stipulate that, in case of employment termination in Switzerland, the cross-border worker may access the protections provided by the Italian system, provided they meet specific criteria set by the competent authorities. The application process must be handled carefully, as the Swiss and Italian systems operate on separate tracks but are coordinated by international treaties in the field of social security. ### Access to insurance protection The right to the benefit is not automatic but depends strictly on the nature of the employment termination. If the contract is terminated by the employer, the worker acquires the right to request economic support. It is crucial to emphasize that voluntary resignations, except in specific cases of just cause, do not allow access to NASpI. The offices in charge verify the regularity of contributions paid during the working period. The entire mechanism is based on the principle of insurance continuity between the two states. Punti chiave

To initiate the procedure for applying for the NASpI, the worker must act promptly after receiving the termination letter or the end of the fixed-term contract. The first step is to register on the Italian pension authority's portal using the officially recognized national credentials. The documentation to be submitted includes the signed employment contract, the latest pay slips, and the end-of-contract letter issued by the Swiss employer. It is advisable to verify your contribution status before proceeding, ensuring there are no gaps that could jeopardize the outcome of the application. ### Operational steps for the application The procedure requires completing a specific online form where details about the employment in Switzerland will be entered. It is crucial to correctly indicate the start and end dates of the contract, as well as the gross salary received. Once the application is submitted, the system will generate a protocol that allows you to track the progress of the application. Although the procedure may seem linear, any error in filling out the form can cause significant delays in the disbursement of the amounts due. It is always advisable to keep track of all communications received from the authority. Frequently Asked Questions
Who is entitled to NASpI after working in Switzerland?
Cross-border workers residing in Italy who experience an involuntary termination of their employment in Switzerland, such as dismissal, are entitled to NASpI. It is necessary that the pension contributions have been regularly paid and that the terms set by Italian regulations for submitting the claim for compensation to INPS are respected.
What documents are needed for the application?
To submit the application, the employment contract, pay slips received during the employment period, the termination certificate issued by the Swiss employer, and the credentials for accessing the Italian pension authority's telematic services for online application submission are required.
Do voluntary resignations entitle one to NASpI?
Normally, voluntary resignations do not entitle one to the NASpI unemployment benefit. There are exceptions in cases of resignations for just cause, which must be appropriately documented and verified by the pension authority during the processing of the application.
