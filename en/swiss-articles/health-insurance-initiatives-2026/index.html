However, the implementation of these initiatives could be blocked by the State Council unless specific funding is available. This is a significant increase that could have a major impact on the taxation of the Canton of Ticino. The president of the commission, Fiorenzo Dadò, argues that adopting the Pmr would grant aid even to those who do not need it, such as a couple with 160,000 francs of disposable income who could access subsidies. This could create significant distortions in the distribution of subsidies. The socialist group leader, Ivo Durisch, opposes the adoption of the Pme, arguing that it penalises the elderly and chronically ill with minimum deductibles.
