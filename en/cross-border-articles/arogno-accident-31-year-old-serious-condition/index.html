Frequently Asked Questions
What are the most common causes of accidents in impervious natural areas in Switzerland?
The most common causes of accidents in impervious natural areas in Switzerland are poor lighting, distraction and sudden malori.
What is the average budget allocated by Canton Ticino to improve safety in smaller municipalities such as Arogno?
The budget may vary, but in 2025 the Canton Ticino has allocated CHF 2.5 million for security in public spaces, with smaller municipalities such as Arogno that could require targeted interventions with specific funds, as seen in Mendrisio with CHF 300,000 per signage and barriers.
What do I do to get immediate medical assistance if I stop at a party in the mountainous area of Canton Ticino, being resident in Italy?
In case of injury, you immediately compose 112; the operator will send an ambulance of the Mendrisiotto Ambulance Service (SAM) and, if necessary, the Fire Department. The average time of arrival is 12-18 minutes in the mountain areas. The wound is stabilized on site and transported to the Lugano Civic Hospital, where LAA covers up to CHF 100,000 for urgent care.
What are the Swiss regulations that oblige event organisers to install temporary lighting in high-risk areas, and what penalties do they impose for those who do not comply with them?
The cantonal regulation on safety in public spaces (updated in 2023) requires those who organize events with more than 300 participants to provide temporary lighting in areas at risk of falling. Failure to comply can result in fines of 5000 to 20000 CHF and the suspension of the authorization for the event, as well as possible civil liability.
How can I report a dangerous route or area to the municipal authorities of Arogno before an event, and what is the average response time?
You can send a report to the Accident Prevention Office (UPI) of the Municipality of Arogno by e-mail at upi@arogno.ch or fill out the online form available on the municipal website. The authorities are obliged to respond within 10 working days and, if necessary, send an inspection team to assess the risk and set up any barriers or signage.
