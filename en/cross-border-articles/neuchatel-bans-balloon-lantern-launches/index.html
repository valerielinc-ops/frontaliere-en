Neuchâtel bans balloons and lanterns: first in Switzerland

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 28 April 2026

The Grand Council of the Canton of Neuchâtel has approved the ban on releasing balloons and sky lanterns, with 55 votes in favor.

Contesto

In brief - Neuchâtel bans balloons and sky lanterns - 55 votes in favor, 36 against, 9 abstentions - First in Switzerland for balloons ## Key facts - What: Ban on releasing balloons and sky lanterns - When: Approved Tuesday, August 13, 2024 - Where: Canton of Neuchâtel, Switzerland - Who: Grand Council of Neuchâtel - Votes: 55 in favor, 36 against, 9 abstentions The Grand Council of Neuchâtel approved the ban on releasing balloons and sky lanterns on Tuesday. The measure, proposed by the VertPOP group, passed with 55 votes in favor, 36 against, and 9 abstentions. For balloons, this is a first in Switzerland. Deputy Cloé Dutoit (Greens), the first signatory of the proposal, stated that releasing balloons is equivalent to dispersing plastic waste into the environment, with negative consequences for wild and domestic animals. Sky lanterns, she specified, pose a non-negligible risk of fire, especially in summer. The release of sky lanterns is already banned in several regions of Switzerland, near airports, as well as in the cantons of Basel-Stadt, Basel-Landschaft, Valais, Geneva, and Fribourg. The right opposed the ban, defining the practice as 'very marginal.' The State Council, however, was in favor of the status quo, preferring greater awareness. UDC Damien Schär criticized the decision, arguing that releasing balloons and lanterns is a marginal practice. Analysis of Environmental Implications The ban on releasing balloons and sky lanterns in the Canton of Neuchâtel has a significant impact on the environment. Once released, balloons can end up in the natural environment, where they pose a danger to wildlife and domestic animals. Animals can ingest the balloons or get entangled in their strings, causing serious harm or even death. Floating lanterns, on the other hand, pose a fire hazard, especially during the summer months when weather conditions are more favorable for the spread of fire. The ban aims to prevent fires that could cause property damage and endanger people's lives. ### Comparison with Other Regions The ban on releasing floating lanterns is already in effect in various regions of Switzerland, near airports, as well as in the cantons of Basel-Stadt, Basel-Landschaft, Valais, Geneva, and Fribourg.
