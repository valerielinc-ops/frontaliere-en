In brief - Three new hearings scheduled next week in Crans-Montana - Two political figures among the suspects - 41 dead and 115 injured in the New Year's Eve fire ## Key facts - What: Hearings for the New Year's Eve fire - When: Next week - Where: Crans-Montana - Who: Three new suspects, including two political figures - Impact: 41 dead and 115 injured The investigation into the New Year's Eve fire in Crans-Montana is expanding with the interrogation of three new suspects, including two political figures. They will have to answer to charges of manslaughter and negligent arson. The current municipal official responsible for security in the municipality of Crans-Montana, Patrick Clivaz, has been summoned for Monday, May 11. Clivaz did not hold the position at the time of the fire safety checks carried out at the bar Le Constellation in 2018 and 2019. The current deputy head of the public security service of the municipality will instead be heard the following day at the Energypolis campus in Sion. The former mayor of Chermignon, who was in office between 2009 and 2016, will have to answer questions from the group of prosecutors and lawyers on May 13. All 13 suspects must answer to the same charges, namely negligent homicide, negligent bodily harm, and negligent arson in relation to the blaze that on New Year's Eve caused 41 deaths and 115 injuries. ### Implications for cross-border workers The New Year's Eve fire in Crans-Montana has had a significant impact on the local community, including cross-border workers who work and live in the region.
