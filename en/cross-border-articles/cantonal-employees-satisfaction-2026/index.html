Implications for Cantonal Employees The ISMAT 2025 report highlighted widespread dissatisfaction among cantonal employees, with significant implications for public administration. 59% of employees feel overworked, a figure that could lead to increased turnover and decreased productivity. For example, in the municipality of Lugano, the turnover rate in 2024 was 12%, higher than the cantonal average of 8%. Additionally, 55% of employees feel unrecognized, a factor that could negatively impact staff morale and motivation. In the municipality of Bellinzona, 60% of employees participated in an internal survey, highlighting a lack of recognition for the extraordinary work done during the pandemic. ### Professional Growth Opportunities Only 44% of employees see opportunities for professional growth, a concerning figure that could lead to a lack of incentives for employees to improve their skills. The ErreDiPi Committee has requested a more in-depth analysis of the data to identify areas for improvement. For example, in the municipality of Locarno, only 30% of employees participated in training courses in 2024, compared to a cantonal average of 45%. Frequently Asked Questions
What did the ISMAT 2025 report highlight?
The ISMAT 2025 report highlighted that 59% of cantonal employees feel overburdened, 44% see opportunities for professional growth, 43% are not satisfied with their remuneration, and 55% feel unrecognized. Additionally, 300 people reported episodes of discrimination, isolation, or personal attacks.
What did the ErreDiPi Committee request?
The ErreDiPi Committee requested a more in-depth analysis of the ISMAT 2025 survey data, systematically introducing cross-analyses by gender, age, seniority, employment percentage, function, and service. Furthermore, they requested the raw data to be made available in anonymized form to ensure transparency and allow for further independent evaluations.
What can the cantonal administration do to improve job satisfaction?
The cantonal administration can conduct a more in-depth analysis of the ISMAT 2025 survey data, plan workshops or focus groups with staff, evaluate the possibility of increasing remuneration and improving staff recognition, develop strategies to prevent discrimination and isolation in the workplace, and ensure transparency and clearly communicate with staff about the survey results and the measures adopted.
