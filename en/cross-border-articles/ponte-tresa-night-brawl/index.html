Dettagli operativi

The carabinieri from the Nucleo Operativo Radiomobile of Luino intervened promptly and started investigations to reconstruct the exact dynamics of the events related to the brawl that occurred in Ponte Tresa, a small town located on the border between the province of Varese and the Canton of Ticino, particularly near the border with the Ticino municipalities of Lugano and Locarno. One of the two injured individuals was taken to the hospital in Varese with a yellow code, with a prognosis of ten days, while the other was accompanied to the hospital in Luino with a green code, with less worrying conditions. The episode is still being examined by investigators, who are reviewing the images from the surveillance cameras in the area and collecting testimony from potential eyewitnesses. The brawl, which involved at least 5 people, broke out around 22:30 on Saturday evening and also involved some cross-border workers who were in the area for work or to spend the evening. According to initial estimates, the damage to private and public property amounts to around 10,000 euros, of which 5,000 euros are for the repair of a shop window and 3,000 euros for the replacement of a restaurant door. Additionally, some damage to cars parked in the area has been reported, with a total of 2,000 euros in damages. The current regulations on public order and security, such as the Legislative Decree of March 31, 1998, n. 112, provide that local authorities adopt adequate measures to prevent and suppress episodes of violence and vandalism. In particular, Article 13 of the decree provides that municipalities can adopt ordinances to regulate access and stay in certain public or private areas.
