Swiss hotel revenues up 3.9% in 2025

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 30 April 2026

Switzerland's hotel sector sees a 3.9% increase in 2025 revenue, with summer peaks and differences between categories and tourist areas.

Contesto

In brief - Swiss hotel turnover: +3.9% in 2025 - Revenue per night: +0.6% to 139.80 CHF - Summer peaks: July +5.8%, August +6.8% ## Key facts - What: Increase in hotel turnover - When: 2025 - Where: Switzerland - Who: Federal Statistical Office (FSO) - Amount: 6.2 billion francs The Swiss hotel sector recorded a 3.9% increase in turnover in 2025, reaching 6.2 billion francs. According to data released by the Federal Statistical Office (FSO), the growth was driven mainly by higher demand, with revenues per single night increasing by 0.6%, averaging 139.80 francs. Seasonal differences were significant, with the summer months recording the highest turnover, peaking at 680 million francs in July (+5.8% compared to the previous year) and 670 million in August (+6.8%). ### Seasonal and category differences The highest rates were observed in winter: in January the average was slightly above 162 francs (+1.0%), while in August it stopped at almost 133 francs (+3.1%) and in October at around 123 francs (-1.1%). The differences between hotel categories were clear: in 5-star hotels, the average revenue per night was around 410 francs in 2025, with a 6.3% annual increase; in 3-star establishments, however, the nightly price remained almost unchanged at 108 francs. ### Differences by tourist areas Tourist areas showed significant differences in prices. The highest prices were recorded in city centers, with almost 152 francs per night. In mountain resorts, the average was just under 145 francs, while in urban areas it was around 132 francs and in rural areas just over 106 francs. Mountain regions showed much more pronounced seasonal fluctuations compared to cities, considering that in winter the average price per night was 166 francs, compared to 125 francs in the summer season....
