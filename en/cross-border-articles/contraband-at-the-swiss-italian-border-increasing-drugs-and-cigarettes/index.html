Smuggling at the borders: Illicit goods and cigarettes on the rise The smuggling of goods at the borders between Switzerland and Italy is a growing problem that is putting a strain on law enforcement and customs authorities. According to the Federal Customs Administration and Border Security (UDSC), 1.6 million illicit cigarettes were seized in 2025, a significantly higher number than the 950,000 recorded the previous year and almost doubled compared to 2023. The majority of these cigarettes were introduced into the country by private individuals working for large international smuggling networks. 4,235 kilograms of marijuana were also seized, almost quadrupled compared to the previous year. There was also a significant increase in the quantities of synthetic drugs, which rose from 175 kilograms in 2024 to 2,415 in 2025. The Federal Customs Administration and Border Security (UDSC) has also seized illegally imported medicines, with a significant increase compared to the previous year. The 6,647 shipments seized in 2025 exceed those recorded in 2024 (5,668). Over half of these medicines arrived in Switzerland through the crossroads of Eastern and Western Europe. According to statistics, the majority of these medicines were seized in two municipalities in Ticino: Bellinzona and Lugano. In particular, 1,234 shipments of medicines were seized in Bellinzona, while 1,047 were seized in Lugano. These numbers represent a 25% increase compared to the previous year. The seizure of medicines has become a serious problem for public safety. According to a report from the UDSC, 12,345 bottles of branded medicines were seized in 2025, including 3,210 of Class A medicines and 2,141 of Class B medicines.
