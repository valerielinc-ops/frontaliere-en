Confsal unveils work manifesto: dignity, wages and rights at its core

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 1 May 2026

On May 1st, Confsal presented its work manifesto in Naples, highlighting the need for dignity, fair wages and rights for workers.

Contesto

In brief - Confsal launches the Manifesto of Work on May 1st in Naples - Dignity, wages, and rights at the heart of the agenda - Critical issues remain open on the May 1st Decree ## Key facts - What: Confsal's Manifesto of Work - When: May 1, 2026 - Where: Naples - Who: Confsal - Amount: Not yet specified On May 1, 2026, Confsal launched its Manifesto of Work during a demonstration in Naples. The manifesto, which represents the union's strategic vision, focuses on the dignity, wages, and rights of workers, both public and private. The choice of Naples as a symbolic location for the launch of the manifesto underscores the union's commitment to restoring economic and social dignity to workers in a period marked by deep inequalities and transformations. ### Critical Issues of the May 1st Decree During the speech, the need to intervene on the May 1st Decree was strongly emphasized, highlighting both the correctives already obtained and the critical issues still open. The General Secretary of Confsal, Angelo Raffaele Margiotta, underlined that the principle of equivalence has been accepted and the value of contracts that guarantee improved economic treatments, such as those signed by Confsal, has been recognized. This result has been described as important to protect contractual pluralism and enhance 'ultra-equivalent' contracts. Margiotta also reiterated that the concept of fair remuneration should not be limited to economic aspects but should include the set of normative protections that must be recognized for all workers, regardless of their job or sector. ### Work Safety and Collective Bargaining On the issue of work safety, the centrality of the safety supervisor, a key figure in prevention processes, was reiterated.
