Healthcare costs for cross-border workers: UFAS clarifies the procedure

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 25 April 2026

The Federal Social Insurance Office explains how medical expenses for Italian cross-border workers injured in Switzerland are handled.

Contesto

TL;DR - Italian cross-border workers' non-work medical expenses covered in Switzerland. - Expenses provisionally reimbursed to Swiss providers, then billed to foreign insurer. - Procedure applies to EU/EFTA insured individuals, ensuring no direct payment by patients. - UFAS clarification addresses concerns post-Crans-Montana tragedy. ## Key facts - Who covers expenses: Swiss insurance initially covers, then foreign insurer reimburses. - When applicable: For non-occupational accidents in Switzerland by EU/EFTA insured individuals. - Where applicable: Switzerland, for EU/EFTA insured individuals. - Who is involved: Swiss Federal Social Insurance Office (FSIO), foreign health insurers. - Regulation basis: Regulation (EC) No. 883/2004 under the Agreement on Free Movement of Persons. - Patient obligation: Patients receive invoice for verification but are not obliged to pay. - Work-related accidents: Covered by Swiss Accident Insurance (AAI), not this procedure. - UFAS role: Provides clarification and assistance for cross-border workers' medical expenses. ## Who covers the medical expenses for Italian cross-border workers? The Swiss Federal Social Insurance Office (FSIO) has clarified the procedure for the medical expenses of Italian patients who suffer a non-occupational accident in Switzerland. According to the FSIO, the expenses are settled in Switzerland and provisionally reimbursed to the service provider, such as a hospital. Subsequently, the invoices are sent to the competent foreign health insurer, not to the person concerned, who only receives an invoice for verification. ### The procedure in detail 1. Practical Implications for Cross-Border Workers The UFAS clarification has important implications for Italian cross-border workers in Switzerland. Firstly, it's crucial to know that in case of a non-occupational accident, medical expenses will be covered by the Swiss insurance and then reimbursed to the competent foreign insurer. This means that cross-border workers won't have to worry about paying medical bills directly. ### Comparison with the Previous Situation Before this clarification, there was much uncertainty regarding the management of medical expenses for Italian cross-border workers injured in Switzerland. Italian statements of not wanting to pay the bills had caused concern among cross-border workers. Now, thanks to the UFAS clarification, the situation is clearer and more transparent. ### Concrete Scenarios 1. Work-related accident: If an Italian cross-border worker is injured at work in Switzerland, the procedure will be different because work-related accidents are covered by accident insurance (AAI). In this case, the Swiss AAI will manage the medical expenses. 2. Non-occupational accident: If the accident occurs outside of work, the procedure described by the UFAS will apply. The expenses will be covered by the Swiss health insurance and then reimbursed to the foreign insurer. 3. Medical care abroad: If a Swiss cross-border worker needs medical care in Italy, the procedure will be similar. The expenses will be covered by the Swiss insurance and then reimbursed to the Italian insurer. ## What to Do in Case of an Accident? Frequently Asked Questions
Who pays for medical expenses for Italian cross-border workers injured in Switzerland?
Medical expenses for Italian cross-border workers injured in Switzerland are handled in Switzerland and provisionally reimbursed to the service provider. Subsequently, the bills are sent to the competent foreign health insurer, not to the person concerned, who only receives a bill for verification.
What happens if a Swiss insured person is injured abroad?
The same procedure applies in the opposite case: if a person insured in Switzerland suffers an accident or receives treatment in a hospital in an EU or EEA country, the expenses are handled similarly. This mutual assistance system is fundamental to ensure the continuity of care and the protection of patients' rights in both countries.
What to do in case of a non-occupational accident in Switzerland?
In case of a non-occupational accident in Switzerland, it is important to follow these steps: go to the emergency room, report the accident to your employer and foreign health insurance, keep the documentation, and contact the UFAS if you have doubts or problems.
