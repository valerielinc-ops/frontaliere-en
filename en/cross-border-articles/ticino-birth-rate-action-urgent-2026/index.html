Falling birth rates in Ticino: Deputy Isabella demands action

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 30 April 2026

Claudio Isabella of the Centre criticises the lack of effective measures against the decline in births in the Canton of Ticino

Contesto

In brief - Deputy Claudio Isabella criticizes parliament's action - Only one proposal approved out of several presented - Lack of foresight in policies ## Key facts - What: Criticism of inaction against declining birth rates - When: 30 April 2026 - Where: Canton Ticino - Who: Deputy Claudio Isabella (Centre) - Amount: Not specified Deputy Claudio Isabella of the Centre has criticized the Ticino parliament after it approved only one of the proposals aimed at combating the decline in births. Isabella strongly criticized the lack of foresight in Canton Ticino's policies regarding low birth rates. According to the deputy, it is not enough to recognize the problem; concrete action with effective measures is necessary. ### The current situation Declining birth rates are an increasing problem in Ticino, with a constant drop in births over the past years. The parliament approved only one of the proposals presented to address this demographic crisis. Isabella expressed concern over the lack of concrete initiatives and called for greater commitment from the cantonal authorities. ### Isabella's criticisms Claudio Isabella emphasized that declining birth rates are not a problem that can be solved with generic statements. A concrete action plan with specific measures to encourage births and support families is necessary. The deputy also criticized the lack of coordination among the different proposals presented, which has led to a stalemate. ### Approved proposals Among the proposals approved by the parliament, one concerns economic support for families with children. However, Isabella stated that this measure is not sufficient and that broader, more structural interventions are needed. A declining population could lead to a reduction in the workforce, increased pressure on public services, and greater dependence on immigration. Additionally, there could be a negative impact on the real estate market and educational services. ### Implications for Cross-Border Workers Cross-border workers employed in Ticino could be affected by low birth rates in various ways. Punti chiave

Concrete actions to tackle low birth rates To address low birth rates in Ticino, a concrete action plan with specific measures is necessary. Here are some actions that could be considered: ### Tax incentives The Canton of Ticino could introduce tax incentives for families with children, such as tax reductions or tax deductions. These incentives could help support families economically and encourage births. ### Direct economic support Another measure that could be considered is direct economic support for families with children. This could include family allowances, contributions for education, and other forms of financial assistance. ### Work-family reconciliation policies To support families, the Canton of Ticino could introduce work-family reconciliation policies, such as extended parental leave, flexible working hours, and childcare services. These measures could help parents better balance work and family responsibilities. ### Awareness campaigns Finally, the Canton of Ticino could launch awareness campaigns on the importance of birth rates and the support measures available. These campaigns could help inform families about the options available and encourage births. ### Conclusion Addressing low birth rates in Ticino requires an integrated and coordinated approach. The measures described above could help support families and encourage births, contributing to a more prosperous future for the Canton of Ticino.
