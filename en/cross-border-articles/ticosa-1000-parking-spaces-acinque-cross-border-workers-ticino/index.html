Ticosa: 1000 parking spaces for cross-border workers, Acinque speeds up project (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 14 April 2026

Acinque confirms its intention to complete 1000 parking spaces in Ticosa shortly, featuring photovoltaic coverage and urban redevelopment, following the assessment of the 33-page document submitted by the City of Como.

Contesto

TL;DR - Municipality of Como sent 33-page document to Acinque for Ticosa area project. - Acinque proposed 1,000 parking spaces, photovoltaic coverage, and commercial spaces. - Project aims to improve mobility for cross-border workers between Como and Ticino. ## Key facts - Document Length: 33 pages - Parking Spaces: Around 1,000 - Project Location: Ticosa area, outskirts of Como - Photovoltaic Coverage: Included in the project proposal - Commercial Spaces: Limited commercial spaces included - Project Timeline: Potential relief for commuters as early as 2027 - Cross-Border Impact: Improved traffic management for cross-border workers - Original Proposal Date: January 2024 The Municipality of Como has sent Acinque a 33-page document outlining the future of the Ticosa area, a strategic location on the outskirts of the city. Mayor Alessandro Rapinese emphasized the need for a prompt response: 'Now we need an answer,' the mayor stated. The multi-utility company, which had already been involved in the joint presentation with the municipal administration in January 2024, had proposed a project including the construction of around 1,000 parking spaces, a photovoltaic-covered parking area, some commercial spaces, and a review of the surrounding road network. In an official statement, Acinque reaffirmed its commitment: ‘Acinque is determined to implement the intervention in agreement with the local authority and confirms its interest in the Ticosa project.’ The company has initiated a detailed examination of the document received from the Municipality 'to delve into the technical and legal aspects, also in relation to the original proposal submitted by the company.' Dettagli operativi

The redevelopment of the Ticosa area is not just about parking spaces—it’s an opportunity to redefine mobility between Como and Ticino, with direct implications for cross-border workers. Currently, daily commuters crossing the border face structural issues: insufficient parking, congested traffic, and a lack of integrated solutions between the two sides of the border. ### Before vs After: what changes for cross-border workers Before the Ticosa project: - Insufficient and often unauthorized parking in surrounding areas - Chaotic traffic, especially during morning and evening rush hours - Lack of sustainable mobility alternatives between Como and Ticino - Prolonged waiting times to find a parking spot After the project is completed: - Around 1,000 dedicated parking spaces with photovoltaic coverage for sustainable energy management - Revised road network around the area to improve traffic flow - Possible integration with local public transport services - Reduction in unauthorized parking in restricted areas ### Concrete scenarios: what could happen If the project proceeds according to the timeline announced by Acinque, cross-border workers could see partial relief from mobility issues as early as 2027. However, the timeline will depend on how quickly the Municipality and the company reach a final agreement. Delays could mean continued inconvenience for commuters, with potential knock-on effects on travel times. Another critical factor will be the project’s real integration with existing infrastructure. Frequently Asked Questions
When will the 1,000 parking spaces in Ticosa be completed?
The exact timeline has not yet been specified. Acinque has stated its intention to complete the project as soon as possible, but no precise deadlines have been provided. The schedule will depend on agreements with the Municipality of Como and the technical construction timelines.
Will the parking spaces be free or paid?
This has not yet been specified. The project includes commercial spaces, but it remains unclear whether parking will be free or subject to fees. The final decision will be made by Acinque and the Municipality of Como.
Will the Ticosa project affect cross-border workers from Ticino who work in Como?
Yes, indirectly. Improved traffic management in Ticosa could reduce waiting times at border crossings like Brogeda or Gaggiolo, enhancing commuting efficiency for cross-border workers.
Will there be incentives for using public transport or car-sharing services?
This has not yet been specified. The project involves traffic flow revisions and photovoltaic roofing, but no direct incentives for public transport or shared mobility services have been announced. However, this could present an opportunity for future integrations.
How can I stay updated on the project's progress?
Follow official communications from Acinque and the Municipality of Como. Additionally, websites like *Frontaliere Ticino* will publish updates as soon as they become available. Subscribing to local newsletters is also recommended for real-time news.
