In brief - KOF: employment indicator at 2.2 points in Q2 2026 - Improvement of 0.1 points compared to Q1 2026 - Multi-year average at 1.7 points ## Key facts - What: KOF employment indicator - When: Second quarter 2026 - Where: Switzerland - Who: KOF Institute, Swiss Federal Institute of Technology Zurich - Amount: 2.2 points The outlook for employment in Switzerland, and particularly in Ticino, is improving. According to the KOF employment indicator, the prospects for the Swiss labor market are looking up, continuing the recovery that began in recent months despite the global context. This content was published on May 5, 2026, at 11:45. Despite uncertainties due to the global context, prospects for the Swiss labor market are improving: this is indicated by the employment indicator calculated by the KOF Institute, the business cycle research center of the Swiss Federal Institute of Technology Zurich. The parameter stood at 2.2 points in the second quarter, up 0.1 points from the January-March period and following the +1.2 recorded in the last part of 2025, according to tables published today by KOF. The index — which is based on the responses of 4,200 companies — remains above the multi-year average, which is 1.7 points. After the peak in mid-2022, the indicator had steadily declined for about three years, slipping into negative territory in the third quarter of 2025 for the first time since the pandemic. The recovery that began in the last quarter of last year is therefore continuing. ### Impact on cross-border workers For cross-border workers in Ticino, this improvement in employment prospects represents a positive signal.
