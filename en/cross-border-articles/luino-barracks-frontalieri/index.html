No migrant center in Fornasette: fire station instead (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 9 May 2026

The former barracks of the carabinieri in Fornasette, Luino, will be assigned to the fire department, not to migrants. Here's what changes for cross-border workers

Contesto

In brief - The former Fornasette barracks will not host migrants - 16 beds for Luino's firefighters - The Prefecture and the Interior Ministry have given the green light ## Key facts - What: The former Fornasette barracks will be allocated to firefighters - When: The decision was announced on May 9, 2026 - Where: Luino, just a few meters from the Swiss border - Who: Prefecture, Provincial Command of Firefighters, Interior Ministry - Amount: 16 beds for firefighters After months of controversy and petition drives, the former Fornasette barracks in Luino will not host an Extraordinary Reception Center (Cas) for migrants. Councilor Davide Cataldo announced that the building will instead be allocated to Luino's firefighters, who were struggling to find adequate accommodation. The Prefecture, the Provincial Command of Firefighters, and the Interior Ministry's department have approved the new destination for the structure, which will host 16 beds for the Luino detachment of firefighters. The decision comes after Roberto Cortese, delegate of the Cisl secretariat of firefighters, had represented the accommodation difficulties of firefighters assigned to Luino. In October 2025, Councilor Cataldo had written to all competent authorities to propose the assignment of the former barracks to firefighters, obtaining great availability and professionalism from all interested parties. ### Implications for cross-border workers The decision to allocate the former barracks to firefighters rather than a center for migrants could have an indirect impact on cross-border workers who work in Switzerland and reside in the Luino area.
