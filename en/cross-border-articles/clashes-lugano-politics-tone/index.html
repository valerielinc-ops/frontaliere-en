Clashes in Lugano bus stop, politicians must tone down (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 5 May 2026

Karin Valenzano Rossi, head of security for Lugano, comments on the clashes that occurred in the bus stop, which resulted in two people being injured.

Contesto

In brief - Clashes in Lugano's bus stop area have resulted in two people being injured. - Karin Valenzano Rossi, Lugano's head of security, urges politicians to lower the tone. - The clashes were also commented on by Raide Bassi and Matteo Poretti. ## Key facts - What: Clashes in Lugano's bus stop area - When: Friday evening - Where: Bus stop area in Lugano - Who: Karin Valenzano Rossi, Raide Bassi, Matteo Poretti - Injured: Two people Karin Valenzano Rossi, Lugano's head of security, commented on 'Radar Lunedì' about the clashes that occurred on Friday evening in the bus stop area, which resulted in two people being lightly injured. In the studio, guests of the show hosted by Sacha Dalcol, were also Raide Bassi, UDC municipal councilor, and Matteo Poretti, coordinator of MpS Luganese. Valenzano Rossi defined these urban phenomena as increasingly worrying, noting an increase in aggressiveness, anger, and violence that explode in the squares. ### Analysis of the clashes Poretti spoke of social degradation and unease that exacerbates tempers. He distinguished between right-wing and left-wing extremists, emphasizing that fascist and neo-Nazi extremists go against the concept of a liberal democracy, while those on the left support the improvement of rights and social conditions. Bassi, on the other hand, argued that this is not a struggle for something, but an ideological clash between good and evil. Valenzano Rossi reiterated that the right to demonstrate must be guaranteed and respected, but a certain measure must be found. She invited the institutions to guarantee all parties a legitimate exercise within the respect of everything, and when this becomes excessive, good communication between authorities and demonstrators is needed.
