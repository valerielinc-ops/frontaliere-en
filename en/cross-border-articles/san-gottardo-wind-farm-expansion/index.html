The expansion of the San Gottardo wind farm is a hot topic for the Canton of Ticino. According to the plan, six additional turbines would be installed, able to generate electricity for thousands of households. What does this mean for our region? Here are the key facts. The San Gottardo wind farm, located in the municipalities of Acquarossa and Blenio, is one of Switzerland's largest wind-power sites. At present it counts 16 turbines that produce about 80 GWh of electricity per year, enough to cover the needs of more than 20,000 households. The expansion has been under discussion for months, aimed at boosting renewable output and cutting CO₂ emissions. Six new turbines would lift the plant's production to 140 GWh per year, covering the needs of more than 35,000 households. Under Swiss law, wind installations need a building permit and must comply with the federal Energy Act. The authorisation procedure for the San Gottardo expansion started in 2022 and is expected to finish by the end of 2024. > "The expansion is an important step towards cutting CO₂ emissions and promoting renewable energy in Ticino." —...

The San Gottardo wind-farm expansion is a big opportunity for Ticino: state-of-the-art turbines will generate electricity more efficiently and with a smaller environmental footprint. - Current farm: 16 turbines, 32 MW, in Acquarossa and Blenio - Planned addition: eight turbines, raising total capacity to 64 MW - Expected output: about 160 GWh per year, equivalent to the consumption of roughly 38,000 Ticinese households New infrastructure is also planned: a storage depot for spare parts and a control centre for turbine monitoring. ⚠️ This means new jobs for the region. Around 100 construction jobs are expected, plus about 15 permanent positions for operation and maintenance. Under current law, the expansion must undergo an environmental-impact assessment (EIA) by 30 June 2024. > "Environmental sustainability is a top priority.
