On May 20 in Istanbul, the Germans will face Aston Villa, who overwhelmed Nottingham Forest with a heavy 4-0. The second leg of the Europa League semifinals saw Freiburg overturn the 2-1 defeat suffered in the first leg in Braga. The turning point of the match at Europa-Park Stadion came after just six minutes, when Ivorian Mario Dorgeles was sent off for a last-man foul on Beste. With an extra man, Freiburg took control of the game and went ahead at the 17th minute thanks to a lucky ricochet by Kübler. However, it was our Manzambi who made the stadium explode again, drawing a surgical right-footed shot from outside the area for the 2-0 at the 41st minute. However, Braga risked opening everything up again: three minutes later, Victor Gómez jumped over the goalkeeper but hit the post with an empty net. In the second half, Freiburg continued to push, but found a monumental Hornicek in their way. The Czech goalkeeper of Braga saved everything multiple times, but at the 72nd minute he had to surrender again: a telecomanded free kick by Grifo and a winning header by an unstoppable Kübler, author of a stunning double. When qualification seemed already secured, Pau Víctor reignited Portuguese hopes with a 3-1 header on a perfect assist by Victor Gómez.
