Bellinzona promotes work-life balance for employees

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 29 April 2026

Regulation amendment to foster equilibrium between private and professional life. Priority for well-being and attractiveness.

Contesto

In brief - Bellinzona proposes work-life balance in the employees' regulation - Amend articles 3 and 4 to insert new principle - Goal: personal well-being and attractiveness as an employer ## Key facts - What: Amendment to employees' regulation - When: April 29, 2026 - Where: Bellinzona - Who: Municipality of Bellinzona - Amount: Not specified Bellinzona could soon become a model for work-life balance for its employees. A motion presented by Martina Minoletti and Martina Malacrida Nembrini, municipal councilors of the Left Unit, proposes to include this principle in the Employees' Regulation of the City of Bellinzona. The amendment would concern articles 3 and 4 of the regulation, with the aim of promoting the well-being of the staff and strengthening the attractiveness of the entity as an employer. ### Proposed amendments The motion suggests two main amendments. A new point d) would be added to article 3 (Objectives and tools): 'The City of Bellinzona promotes and commits to work-life balance, adopting organizational measures that allow employees to harmonize professional commitments with personal needs.' Article 4 (Principles) would instead see the insertion of point i): 'The City of Bellinzona promotes and commits to the work-life balance of all employees.' ### Objectives of the amendment According to the proponents, formally adopting this principle would mean recognizing the well-being of the staff as a strategic element for the quality of public services. Furthermore, promoting an organizational culture capable of setting an example for the local economic fabric. Implications for Employees The introduction of the work-life balance principle could significantly impact the lives of municipal employees. Concrete measures could include flexible working hours, remote work options, and support for managing family responsibilities. These initiatives could improve employees' quality of life and boost their productivity. ### Comparisons with Other Administrations Bellinzona would not be the first administration to introduce such measures. Other Swiss cities have already adopted similar regulations with positive results. This could represent an opportunity for Bellinzona to position itself as a modern and inclusive employer, capable of attracting and retaining qualified staff. ### Examples of Concrete Measures 1. Flexible Hours: The possibility to adjust working hours to personal needs, such as managing children or other family responsibilities. 2. Remote Work: The opportunity to work from home for a few days a week, reducing commuting time and improving the balance between private and professional life. 3. Parenting Support: Measures such as extended parental leave, company-provided childcare facilities, and support for managing family responsibilities. 4. Training and Development: Training programs to enhance employees' skills and offer internal career opportunities. 5. Workplace Well-being: Initiatives to promote employees' physical and mental well-being, such as fitness programs, psychological counseling, and team-building activities. ### Benefits for the Administration The adoption of these measures could bring a series of benefits to the municipal administration. Frequently Asked Questions
What changes are proposed to the Employees' Regulation?
The proposed changes involve the inclusion of the principle of work-life balance in articles 3 and 4 of the regulation. A new point d) would be added to article 3 to promote the balance between private and professional life, while point i) would be inserted in article 4 with the same objective.
What are the expected benefits of adopting these measures?
The expected benefits include an improvement in staff well-being, a reduction in gender inequalities, greater attractiveness of the entity as an employer, and the promotion of an organizational culture capable of setting an example for the local economic fabric.
What happens if the motion is approved?
If the motion is approved, the Municipality will have to present an annual report to the Municipal Council with an account of the measures adopted in this area. This process may take a few months, but it represents an important step towards a more balanced and inclusive work environment.
