The Ticino Cantonal Police is reducing the number of radar controls on the roads of the Canton of Ticino

Contesto

According to the Director of the Department of Territory Claudio Zali, 'we have not yet quantified this reduction, but it has been decided to allocate more resources to other types of controls'. Excessive speed is not, in fact, the only cause of accidents or serious offenses. On the contrary, statistically it is not even the main one. The data from recent years show that, for example, in 2020, only 25% of road accidents in the Canton of Ticino were caused by excessive speed, while 40% were caused by human errors, such as distraction or fatigue. The radar controls will therefore be reduced and the controls on the psychological and physical state of drivers will be increased, in particular with regard to medications and psychotropic substances. Dettagli operativi

The decision to reduce radar controls was made after a careful evaluation of the results obtained in recent years. According to Zali, 'in recent years, a lot has been done in terms of prevention as well as repression. Given the results obtained, we asked ourselves if it was possible to ease the pressure'. The reduction of radar controls does not mean that road safety will no longer be a priority. On the contrary, the Ticino Cantonal Police intends to focus on other aspects of road safety, such as the prevention of accidents caused by drivers under the influence of psychotropic substances or medications. In 2020, for example, the Ticino Cantonal Police carried out 12,500 radar controls along the roads of the canton, with an average of 35 controls per day. Of these, 25% resulted in fines for excessive speed, with a total amount of 1.2 million Swiss francs. However, thanks to the reduction of radar controls, the Ticino Cantonal Police will be able to focus on other areas of intervention, such as the prevention of road accidents in the municipality of Lugano, where 150 road accidents occurred in 2022, with 20 injured and 2 dead. The reduction of radar controls will be implemented gradually, with a decrease of 20% of the controls by the end of 2024. This will mean that the Ticino Cantonal Police will be able to reduce the number of agents employed in radar controls and reallocate them to other areas of intervention. For example, they can be employed to control drivers under the influence of psychotropic substances or medications, as provided for by the federal law on road traffic of December 19, 1958, which provides for severe penalties for drivers who drive under the influence of psychotropic substances or medications. Drivers who cross the Canton of Ticino will therefore have to be aware of the new control measures. It is important to note that the reduction of radar controls does not mean that there will be no more controls. On the contrary, the Ticino Cantonal Police intends to increase controls in other areas, such as speed control in urban areas, in particular in Lugano, Locarno, and Bellinzona. For example, starting from January 1, 2022, the Ticino Cantonal Police has introduced a new speed control system based on fixed and mobile cameras. This system allows for real-time monitoring of vehicle speed and the issuance of fines to drivers who exceed the speed limits. According to the data published on the website of the Ticino Cantonal Police, in the first half of 2022, over 10,000 fines were issued for excessive speed, with a total amount of over 1 million Swiss francs.
