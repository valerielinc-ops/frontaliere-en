San Gottardo: first diaphragm falls on north side

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 29 April 2026

Important milestone in the construction of the second tube of the San Gottardo road tunnel. The first diaphragm has fallen on the north side.

Contesto

In brief - First diaphragm collapsed on the northern side of the Gotthard - Mechanical milling machine reached the cavern in the disturbance zone - Milestone celebrated by Uri authorities and USTRA ## Key facts - What: Collapse of the first diaphragm on the northern side of the Gotthard - When: Wednesday, April 29, 2026 - Where: Göschenen, Canton Uri - Who: Uri Canton authorities and Federal Roads Office (USTRA) - Advancement: 14 months of milling machine progress Today, Wednesday, April 29, 2026, a significant milestone was reached in the construction site for the second tube of the Gotthard road tunnel. Shortly before noon, the first diaphragm collapsed in the excavation on the northern side, on the Göschenen slope. The mechanical milling machine 'Alessandra,' after approximately 14 months of progress, reached the cavern of the so-called 'disturbance zone' north, which had previously been excavated using traditional methods and secured. The milestone was celebrated today by the Uri Canton authorities and the Federal Roads Office (USTRA), which also provided updates on the resumption of excavation in the south, scheduled for mid-May. ### Implications for cross-border workers The completion of the second tube of the Gotthard tunnel will have a significant impact on cross-border workers who daily cross the Italian-Swiss border.
