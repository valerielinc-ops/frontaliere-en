Contesto

In brief - Meeting on the island of Grímsey in Castronno on May 9th - Leonardo Piccione's account of the 'Icelandic longing' - Dialogue with Francesca Milano ## Key facts - What: Meeting on Grímsey and the 'Icelandic longing' - When: Saturday, May 9th at 17:30 - Where: Materia spazio libero, via Confalonieri 5, Castronno - Who: Leonardo Piccione and Francesca Milano - Cost: Free event On Saturday, May 9th at 17:30 in Castronno, at Materia spazio libero in via Confalonieri 5, a meeting dedicated to the Arctic Circle will take place. The event, titled 'Inside the Icelandic longing', will feature Leonardo Piccione, who will share his experience on the island of Grímsey, a small Icelandic outpost known for its isolation and unique community. Grímsey, suspended between isolation and a sense of community, is a place where fishermen, essential landscapes, and a dimension of time and space profoundly different from what we are used to coexist. It is here that the so-called 'Icelandic longing' takes shape, a particular nostalgia made of silences, light, and intense human relationships. During the meeting, Piccione will recount stories and encounters that bring to life the daily life 'at the edge of the world', offering an intimate look at a reality as remote as it is fascinating. Among the protagonists evoked is the figure of Daniel Willard Fiske, an eccentric bibliophile of the 19th century considered a sort of patron of the island, whose historical presence contributes to defining the cultural identity of Grímsey. Francesca Milano will dialogue with Leonardo Piccione in a discussion that promises to intertwine narration, reflection, and travel suggestions, bringing the audience a few steps from the Arctic Circle without leaving the room. ### A journey beyond usual boundari...
