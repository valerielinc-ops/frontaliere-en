Two Romanians arrested for church thefts in Ticino

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 28 April 2026

Two Romanian citizens were arrested for thefts in Ticino churches. Discover the details of the operation.

Contesto

In brief - Two Romanians stopped for thefts in Ticino churches - Money and objects stolen from at least two churches - Investigations ongoing for other incidents ## Key facts - What: Theft of money and objects - When: Sunday - Where: Claro and Dangio, Ticino - Who: Cantonal police - Amount: Not specified Two Romanian citizens, a 45-year-old and a 41-year-old, were stopped by law enforcement in Claro for a series of thefts in churches in Ticino. According to the Cantonal Police, the two would have stolen cash and various objects from at least two Ticino churches, one in Claro and one in Dangio. Investigations are still ongoing to verify their responsibility in other similar episodes, which may have also occurred in Ludiano and Olivone. The hypotheses of crime include repeated theft, repeated violation of domicile, and illegal entry. The investigation is coordinated by Public Prosecutor Veronica Lipari. The two detainees are residents abroad. ### Security implications This episode raises concerns about the security of churches and places of worship in the Canton of Ticino. The Cantonal Police have intensified checks to prevent further thefts and ensure the protection of religious goods. The faithful and church officials are invited to immediately report any suspicious activity to the competent authorities. ### Comparisons with other episodes This is not the first case of thefts in Ticino churches. In recent years, several similar episodes have occurred, leading the authorities to strengthen security measures. Churches are often targeted by thieves due to the presence of valuable objects and cash in the offerings. Analysis of Criminal Dynamics Thefts in churches represent a complex phenomenon that requires an in-depth analysis of criminal dynamics. Churches, often open to the public and with few advanced security systems, are an easy target for thieves. Moreover, the presence of cash and valuable objects, such as statues and sacred vestments, makes them even more attractive to criminals. ### Prevention Strategies To prevent similar thefts, it is essential to adopt adequate security measures. Some effective strategies include: - Installation of surveillance systems with cameras. - Use of safes to store cash. - Collaboration with law enforcement for regular patrols. - Raising awareness among the faithful about the importance of reporting suspicious activities. ### Impact on the Community Theft episodes in churches have a significant impact on the local community. Beyond the material damage, these crimes can generate fear and insecurity among the faithful and residents. It is important that the authorities and the community work together to ensure a safe and protected environment. ### International Comparisons The phenomenon of thefts in churches is not limited to Ticino. Similar episodes are also recorded in other regions of Europe and the world. However, the response of the authorities and collaboration with the community can make a difference in the prevention and resolution of these crimes. ### Online Reporting Procedures In addition to formal reporting at the police station, it is also possible to report thefts and suspicious activities online. The Cantonal Police has made a dedicated portal available for online reports, which allows providing information quickly and securely.
