Fonio slams UDC initiative at Pregassona party

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 1 May 2026

Giorgio Fonio criticizes the UDC's 'No to a Switzerland of 10 million' initiative over risks to jobs and wages. OCST celebrates with 400 people.

Contesto

In brief - OCST party in Pregassona with 400 people - Fonio: UDC initiative risks jobs and wages - OCST commitment to affordable housing ## Key facts - What: OCST Workers' Party - When: May 1, 2026 - Where: Pregassona Warehouse, Lugano - Who: OCST, Giorgio Fonio, Gianluca D'Ettorre - Attendance: 400 participants At the Pregassona Warehouse, the OCST organized the Workers' Party, attended by nearly 400 people, including workers and their families. The event, which started at 10:30 AM, offered conviviality with entertainment, music, aperitifs, and lunch, as well as reflections on work, training, affordable housing, and life after retirement. Gianluca D'Ettorre, president of OCST, emphasized the value of work that promotes personal growth and develops talents. He highlighted the role of OCST in supporting workers not only in negotiations but also in other aspects of daily life related to work quality. > 'Work is a fundamental pillar for personal and collective growth.' Giorgio Fonio, national councilor and regional secretary of Mendrisiotto, criticized the popular initiative 'No to a Switzerland of 10 million,' stating that if approved, it would jeopardize working conditions, wages, and create instability in sectors with labor shortages such as catering and nursing. He also criticized the Parliament for not improving working conditions for nursing staff, causing frustration in the sector. 📊 Current scenario vs. post-UDC initiative scenario: - Today: Average wages in the nursing sector in Ticino range between 6,000 and 8,000 CHF per month. - Post-initiative risk: 10-15% wage reduction, losing attractiveness for foreign workers. Dettagli operativi

The UDC initiative 'No to a Switzerland of 10 million' is at the center of Giorgio Fonio's criticisms, who fears negative impacts on work and wages. If approved, the initiative could lead to instability in already struggling sectors such as catering and nursing, where there is a shortage of personnel. Currently, Ticino has around 3,500 vacant positions in the healthcare sector, with an employment rate of 97.3% in 2023. Fonio also highlighted the frustration in the nursing sector due to the lack of improvement in working conditions by the Parliament, despite the promises made in 2021. 📊 Operational checklist for the nursing sector: - 2021: Promise of a 5% salary increase by 2023. - 2023: Actual increase of 2.5%, below expectations. - 2023: Shortage of 1,200 nurses in the Canton. The OCST, through its festival in Pregassona, wanted to remind the importance of work and the need to defend its value. Gianluca D'Ettorre emphasized how work should promote the person and develop their talents, also improving society. The organization is committed not only to negotiating with employers but also in other areas of daily life that influence the quality of work. 💡 Comparison of practical scenarios: - Current scenario: Average salary for a nurse in Ticino: CHF 7,500 per month. - Post-UDC initiative scenario: Possible 10% reduction in salary, directly impacting 15,000 workers in the healthcare sector. The commitment to affordable housing is another crucial point for the OCST. Alessandro Viri explained that finding sustainable housing is becoming increasingly difficult, especially for families, young people, the elderly, and vulnerable individuals. Frequently Asked Questions
What are the risks of the UDC initiative 'No to a Switzerland of 10 million'?
According to Giorgio Fonio, the initiative could jeopardize working conditions and wages, creating instability in sectors with personnel shortages such as the catering and nursing sectors. Furthermore, it could increase frustration in the nursing sector due to the lack of improvement in working conditions.
What does OCST do for affordable housing?
OCST is committed to managing residential properties with moderate rents, such as the Residenza Soabbia in Camorino, with 48 apartments, and other properties in Mendrisio, Biasca, and Magliaso. The goal is to ensure dignified and accessible housing for those at risk of being marginalized in the housing market.
How can I participate in OCST activities?
You can contact OCST for information on available housing and access criteria. Additionally, you can follow their initiatives and participate in public consultations to influence decisions regarding the world of work.
