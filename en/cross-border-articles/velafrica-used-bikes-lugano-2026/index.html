Velafrica returns to Lugano: second life for used bikes

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 27 April 2026

The Velafrica project returns to Lugano to give a second life to used bicycles. An initiative that involves the community and promotes sustainability

Contesto

In brief - Velafrica returns to Lugano with a new initiative for the recycling of used bicycles - The project promotes sustainability and involves the local community - The collected bicycles will be reused or dismantled for spare parts ## Key facts - What: Velafrica project for the recycling of used bicycles - When: Not yet specified - Where: Lugano and surroundings - Who: Local community and partner organizations - Amount: Not yet specified The Velafrica project, which aims primarily at recycling and reusing used bicycles, returns to Lugano with a new initiative involving the local community. This project not only promotes sustainability but also offers an opportunity to give a second life to bicycles that would otherwise end up as waste. The collected bicycles will be reused or dismantled for spare parts, thus contributing to reducing the environmental impact. The initiative is open to all residents of Lugano and its surroundings, who can bring their old bicycles to designated collection points. This project is an example of how the community can come together to promote sustainable practices and reduce waste. The collected bicycles will then be sent to Africa, where they will be used to improve local mobility and support development projects. ### Implications for the Community The Velafrica project not only offers an opportunity to recycle used bicycles but also involves the local community in a sustainability initiative. This type of project can have a significant impact on reducing waste and promoting ecological practices. Furthermore, the project can serve as an example for similar initiatives in other parts of the Canton of Ticino. ### Community Involvement Community participation is crucial for the success of the project. Procedure to Participate In order to participate in the Velafrica project, residents of Lugano and the surrounding areas can bring their old bicycles to the designated collection points. These points will be communicated through the project's official channels and will be easily accessible to everyone. It's important to ensure that the bicycles are in usable condition or, at least, that they can be disassembled for spare parts. ### Benefits for the Community The Velafrica project offers numerous benefits for the local community. Firstly, it promotes sustainability and reduces waste, contributing to a cleaner and healthier environment. Additionally, the project provides an opportunity to contribute to development projects in Africa, improving local mobility and supporting development initiatives. Lastly, the project involves the community in a common initiative, promoting a sense of belonging and shared responsibility. ### Comparison with the Previous Situation Before the launch of the Velafrica project, used bicycles in the Lugano area were often disposed of as waste, contributing to pollution and the saturation of landfills. Additionally, the project offers an opportunity to contribute to development projects in Africa, improving local mobility and supporting development initiatives. ### Future Scenarios The Velafrica project has the potential to expand to other parts of the Canton Ticino and the rest of Switzerland. Frequently Asked Questions
How can I participate in the Velafrica project?
You can participate by bringing your old bicycles to the designated collection points in the Lugano area. Visit the project's official website to find the nearest collection points and for more information.
What are the benefits of the Velafrica project for the community?
The Velafrica project promotes sustainability, reduces waste, and offers an opportunity to contribute to development projects in Africa. Additionally, it engages the community in a common initiative, fostering a sense of belonging and shared responsibility.
What happens to the collected bicycles?
The collected bicycles will be reused or dismantled for spare parts. Those in good condition will be sent to Africa to improve local mobility and support development projects.
