The Fischer case involves a cross-border worker who had their work permit revoked due to irregularities in the renewal procedures. The decision has raised concerns among cross-border workers, many of whom fear that the new procedures may make it more difficult to obtain and renew work permits. Brenn assured that the DFE is working to simplify the procedures and provide greater clarity on the regulations. ### Implications for cross-border workers The new procedures for cross-border work permits could have a significant impact on cross-border workers in Ticino. Analysis of the new procedures The new procedures for cross-border work permits introduced by the DFE involve a series of changes that could have a significant impact on cross-border workers. Among the main novelties, there is the introduction of a more rigorous verification system for renewal applications, which could increase processing times. This could cause delays in obtaining the permits, with consequent difficulties for workers who depend on these documents to carry out their activities. ### Practical support for cross-border workers To mitigate the impact of the new procedures, the DFE has announced a series of support initiatives for cross-border workers. Among these, the creation of a dedicated assistance center, which will provide practical support and detailed information on the new regulations. In addition, informative sessions will be organized to clarify doubts and provide assistance in filling out the renewal applications. ### Possible scenarios One of the possible scenarios is that the new procedures could lead to an increase in the processing times of the renewal applications for work permits. This could cause difficulties for cross-border workers who need to renew their permits in time to continue working. However, Brenn has assured that the DFE is working to minimize these impacts and to ensure that the procedures are as efficient as possible. ### Comparisons with other regions The new procedures for cross-border work permits in Ticino could be compared with those of other Swiss regions.
