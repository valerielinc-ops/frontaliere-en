Lavena Ponte Tresa, a small town on the border with Switzerland, has committed to cleaning and greening the area. In March, the municipality completed the planting of new greenery in Via Colombo Bassa and the new access to Piazza Gramsci. This initiative is part of a larger project to improve the appearance and air quality in the area. Mayor Massimo Mastromarino explained that the municipality asked local businesses to help maintain the greenery. 'We want businesses to feel involved in the care of our streets,' said the mayor. 'To do this, we decided to give away watering cans to help keep the area clean and green.' The system was created to encourage the care and decoration of the municipality's streets. Businesses received watering cans to help keep the area green and clean. The municipality encourages businesses to use the watering cans to keep the area green and clean.

Frequently Asked Questions
How does the watering can system donated by the Municipality of Lavena Ponte Tresa work?
The watering can system was created to encourage the care and decorum of the streets of the Municipality. Traders can use watering cans regularly to keep the area clean and green.
What is the number of main treatment areas identified by the Municipality of Lavena Ponte Tresa?
The Municipality has identified 5 main areas of care: Via Colombo bassa, new access to Piazza Gramsci, Piazza Gramsci, Via Colombo alta and Municipal parking.
Are the watering cans donated by the Municipality equipped with a rainwater collection system?
Yes, the watering cans distributed by the Municipality of Lavena Ponte Tresa were chosen for their ability to collect rainwater, thus reducing water waste and environmental impact.
Do merchants have to pay anything to receive watering cans?
No, the watering cans are donated by the Municipality to the businesses present. The mayor emphasized the goal of involving merchants in the care of the roads at no additional cost.
What are the criteria for choosing environmentally friendly products to be used for the care of essences?
Products must be free of harmful chemicals and comply with municipal guidelines for environmental protection. The Municipality provides specific indications to traders to ensure the sustainability of operations.
