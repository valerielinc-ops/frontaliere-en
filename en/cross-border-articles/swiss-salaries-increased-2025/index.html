Salaries in Switzerland increased in 2025: +1.8% on average

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 22 April 2026

The Federal Statistical Office announces an average salary increase of 1.8%, with significant differences between sectors.

Contesto

TL;DR - Swiss wages rose 1.8% in 2025, highest real increase since 2009. - Sector differences: public admin +3.3%, services +1.9%, industry +1.5%. - Inflation dropped to 0.2%, boosting purchasing power by 1.6%. - Cross-border workers saw salary increases, but tax rules apply. ## Key facts - Nominal wage increase: 1.8% in 2025, matching 2024 level. - Real wage increase: 1.6% in 2025, highest since 2009. - Inflation rate: 0.2% in 2025, down from 1.1% in 2024. - Public admin wage increase: 3.3% in 2025. - Services sector wage increase: 1.9% in 2025. - Industrial sector wage increase: 1.5% in 2025. - Cross-border worker minimum income: CHF 4,586 monthly as of January 1, 2025. - Gender pay gap: Women's salaries increased by 2.3%, men's by 1.5% in 2025. ## Nominal Wage Increase Wages in Switzerland saw an increase in 2025, thanks to a significant drop in inflation and higher nominal wages. According to a statement from the Federal Statistical Office (FSO), nominal wages increased by an average of 1.8%, matching the 2024 level and slightly surpassing the 1.7% of 2023. This increase led to a rise in purchasing power, with real wages growing by 1.6%, the highest value since 2009. ### Differences Between Sectors The evolution of real wages in 2025 shows notable differences depending on the various sectors. In the industrial sector, nominal wages grew by an average of 1.5%, while in the services sector, the increase was 1.9%. The most significant growth was observed in the public administration (+3.3%) and professional, scientific, and technical activities (+2.6%). In the Canton of Ticino, for example, average nominal wages increased by 1.7%, with a rise in purchasing power of 1.5%.
