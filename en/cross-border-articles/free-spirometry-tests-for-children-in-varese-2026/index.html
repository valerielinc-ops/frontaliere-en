Implications for Cross-Border Workers The initiative of free spirometry for children in Varese represents a concrete and relevant opportunity also for cross-border workers residing in Ticino, particularly in the localities of Lugano, Mendrisio, and Bellinzona, who work in Italy. The geographical proximity between the Canton of Ticino and Lombardy, with distances ranging from 20 to 50 km, makes Italian healthcare services easily accessible, offering a valid and less costly alternative compared to the healthcare costs of the Ticino healthcare system. For example, according to 2023 data, a spirometry exam in Italy can cost around 50-70 euros, while in Ticino the rates can exceed 150 francs, with longer waiting times. This difference can translate into an economic and timely advantage for families who want to monitor their children's respiratory health, especially in the presence of conditions such as asthma or other respiratory pathologies frequent among children. Furthermore, cross-border families often have to deal with bureaucratic complexities related to insurance and health coverage, which in Italy can be more streamlined and immediate, thanks to regulations such as Legislative Decree 175/2022, which has simplified access to prevention services for Italian citizens and foreigners residing abroad. ### Comparison with Healthcare Services in Ticino In Ticino, public healthcare services offer prevention and early diagnosis programs, including screenings for pediatric respiratory pathologies, but access can require more time and involve higher costs, especially for specialist visits or diagnostic tests. What to Do If Your Child Shows Symptoms of Asthma If your child exhibits symptoms such as difficulty breathing, wheezing, a feeling of chest tightness, or persistent dry cough, it is crucial to promptly consult your trusted pediatrician. Early diagnosis allows for better management of the condition and helps prevent more serious crises. Spirometry is a key tool: a simple, non-invasive test that provides valuable data to confirm asthma and monitor treatment effectiveness. In case of acute symptoms or severe breathing difficulty, it is essential to go immediately to the nearest Emergency Room, preferably at facilities like the Regional Hospital of Lugano or the Varese Polyclinic, which collaborate with pulmonology specialists. > Remember that childhood asthma can be effectively managed with an appropriate treatment plan, reducing the risk of complications. ### Use the Frontaliere Ticino Calculator For cross-border parents planning to have their children seen in Varese, careful trip planning is helpful. Who can participate in the free spirometry initiative?
