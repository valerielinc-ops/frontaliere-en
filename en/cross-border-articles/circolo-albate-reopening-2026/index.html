Albate's Circolo Reopens: A Covered Square for the Community

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 1 May 2026

The legendary bar of the Union of Cooperative Circles in Albate reopens on May 1, 2026, becoming a social and cultural gathering spot.

Contesto

In brief - Reopening of Circolo bar in Albate on May 1, 2026 - Inauguration of a painting course for children and teenagers - Management entrusted to a family of Turkish origin ## Key facts - What: Reopening of Circolo bar in Albate - When: May 1, 2026 - Where: Albate, a district of Como - Who: Union of Cooperative Circles - Amount: Not yet specified The Circolo bar in Albate, a historic gathering place for the district, reopened on May 1, 2026. This bar, which is much more than just a meeting place, represents the gateway to the world of cooperatives, a world made up of social cohesion, participation, and solidarity initiatives. The reopening was symbolically linked to Labor Day, underscoring the importance of work and community. The secretary of Circolo 1 (Albate) of the local Pd, soon to enter the board of the Union of Cooperative Circles, expressed his enthusiasm for the reopening. He emphasized how this bar is not just any place, but a true symbol of aggregation and solidarity. The reopening also coincides with the inauguration of a painting course for children and teenagers, led by Professor Mottin, which represents an additional value for the community. ### A symbol of social cohesion The Circolo bar in Albate is a place where the community gathers to discuss politics, culture, and daily life. In an era where everything moves fast and there is a risk of feeling more alone, a lively and active cooperative means demonstrating concretely that the cooperative model and neighborhood life have value. This value is made up of solidarity, security, and democratic values that must be preserved and safeguarded.
