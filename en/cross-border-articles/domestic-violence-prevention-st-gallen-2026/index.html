Domestic violence: prevention through dialogue in St. Gallen

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 27 April 2026

In St. Gallen, the police intercept potential domestic violence offenders and engage them in preventive talks with a 90% success rate.

Contesto

In brief - San Gallo police intercept potential domestic violence perpetrators - Preventive talks in public spaces with a 90% success rate - Interventions in 2,000 cases per year in the canton ## Key facts - What: Preventive interventions by San Gallo police - When: Currently ongoing - Where: Canton of San Gallo, Switzerland - Who: Threat management unit of San Gallo police - Amount: 2,000 interventions per year In Switzerland, the phenomenon of domestic violence is on the rise, with an 8% increase last year. In the Canton of San Gallo, law enforcement has adopted a unique preventive approach to combat this issue. Police officers intercept potential domestic violence perpetrators and invite them to preventive talks, often in public spaces such as cafes or restaurants. This method has shown a 90% success rate, with most of the contacted individuals accepting the help offered. The threat management unit of San Gallo police addresses the problem of domestic violence by intercepting potential perpetrators based on reports from victims or third parties. The officers, dressed in civilian clothes, meet the individuals in public spaces or, if preferred, at the police station. The goal is to prevent further episodes of domestic violence by offering psychological or specialized support to ease tensions and avoid more serious situations. ### The preventive approach The preventive approach of San Gallo police is based on dialogue and proactivity. The officers seek to understand family dynamics and offer solutions to prevent situations from worsening. This method is particularly effective during family crises, such as separations and divorces, when the risk of violence increases. Dettagli operativi

The preventive approach adopted by the police of St. Gallen represents an innovative model for managing domestic violence. This method not only reduces the number of serious incidents but also provides concrete support to people in need. Officers, dressed in civilian clothes, approach potential perpetrators of violence in public spaces, creating a less intimidating and more dialogue-friendly environment. ### The Role of the Community The community plays a crucial role in the success of this approach. Reports from victims or third parties are essential for identifying potential perpetrators of domestic violence. The police of St. Gallen work closely with local associations and institutions to ensure timely and effective intervention. ### Implications for Cross-Border Workers For cross-border workers living or working in Ticino, this preventive approach can offer a model of reference.
