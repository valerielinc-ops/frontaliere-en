Viganello and Gaza schools twin: cultural exchanges and exhibitions

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 29 April 2026

From 27 April to 7 May, Viganello middle school hosts an exhibition dedicated to the twinning with Al-Salam school in Gaza, with exchanges of letters and drawings.

Contesto

In brief - Twinning between Viganello school and Gaza school - Exchange of letters, drawings, and stories - Exhibition open to the public from April 27 to May 7 ## Key facts - What: School twinning - When: April 27 - May 7, 2026 - Where: Viganello middle school - Who: Viganello middle school and Al-Salam school of Gaza - Amount: Not yet specified LUGANO - From April 27 to May 7, the Viganello middle school will host an exhibition dedicated to the twinning project between the school and the Al-Salam tent school in Gaza, created and supported by the Future in Peace – Human Rights and International Justice association. Born from the collaboration between the association and Italian teacher Alessandro Fanciotti of Viganello middle school, the project has directly connected young people living in profoundly different realities. Through the exchange of letters, drawings, and personal stories, the students did not just communicate: they recognized each other, sharing dreams, fears, and hopes. 'For the boys of Gaza, the twinning represented much more than a school exchange,' reads the Future in Peace communiqué. 'It was a moment of respite, a fragment of normality in a life marked by deprivation and uncertainty. Speaking with peers from the other side of the world gave them back a feeling often denied: that of being seen, heard, considered. In a context where everything seems to collapse, even just knowing that someone thinks of them has reignited a spark of joy.' ### Impact on Viganello students For the students of Viganello, the impact was equally profound. 'Confronting such a harsh reality meant looking at their own lives with new eyes. Dettagli operativi

A Bridge Between Cultures The twinning between the Viganello middle school and the Al-Salam school in Gaza is a concrete example of how education can build bridges between different cultures. The students of Viganello have had the opportunity to confront a reality very different from theirs, developing greater awareness and gratitude for their situation. This project has shown that, despite distances and differences, it is possible to create significant connections through education and cultural exchange. ### The Importance of Education Education is a fundamental right that is often compromised in contexts of conflict. In Gaza, bombings have destroyed not only school buildings but also the continuity of young people's dreams. The Al-Salam school, supported by Future in Peace, offers a space where young people can still dream and learn. This twinning project not only allowed the students of Viganello to better understand the reality of Gaza but also offered the young people of Gaza a moment of normality and hope. ### The Exhibition and Its Impact The exhibition dedicated to the twinning project is an opportunity for the public of Viganello to see directly the impact of this cultural exchange. The drawings, letters, and testimonies of the students offer an authentic and surprising glimpse into this encounter between two worlds. The exhibition is open from Monday to Friday during school hours at the auditorium of the Viganello middle school and represents a concrete example of how human rights education can contribute to forming more attentive and aware citizens. ### Future Prospects This twinning project could pave the way for further cultural exchange initiatives between schools from different parts of the world.
