Webuild: CSC starts UN Geneva HQ renovation

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 30 April 2026

The consortium led by CSC Costruzioni has begun work on the renovation of Building E of the Palais des Nations in Geneva, with a 215 million euro contract.

Contesto

In brief - Start of works for the renovation of Building E of the Palais des Nations in Geneva - 215 million euro contract for CSC Costruzioni - Project part of the United Nations' Strategic Heritage Plan ## Key facts - What: Renovation of Building E of the Palais des Nations - When: April 30, 2026 - Where: Geneva, Switzerland - Who: Consortium led by CSC Costruzioni (Webuild Group) - Amount: 215 million euros MILAN (ITALPRESS) - Work has begun on the renovation of Building E of the Palais des Nations in Geneva, the historic European headquarters of the United Nations. CSC Costruzioni, part of the Webuild Group, leads a consortium responsible for this project, whose contract has reached a total value of approximately 215 million euros, thanks to the inclusion of a new phase of work. The Building E project represents the final construction phase of a renovation program launched in 2023 by the client Rènovation du Palais des Nations (RPN). The work will be carried out ensuring the full continuity of diplomatic activities across the entire Palais des Nations campus. The intervention on Building E, originally built in the 1970s, includes the demolition of eight levels of the current office structure, the refurbishment of the conference center podium spaces, and other adjustments. The interventions are part of the United Nations' Strategic Heritage Plan, aimed at adapting the complex to modern building regulations and contemporary safety and accessibility standards, while preserving the historical value of the original architecture. The project also includes the installation of new technological systems and the modification of conference support infrastructures, elements necessary for a diplomatic machine that hosts approximately 8,000 meetings and over 120,000 visitors...

The demolition of eight levels of the current structure and the redevelopment of the conference center's podium spaces represent a crucial step in adapting the complex to contemporary needs.
