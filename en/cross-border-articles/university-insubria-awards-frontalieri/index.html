In brief - Mattia Giussani, a recent graduate in Nursing, wins third place nationally at the SIAN Congress. - His thesis addresses nursing skills in the field of nephrology, focusing on 120 nurses from ASST dei Sette Laghi. - Giussani will speak at the ESNO Congress 2026 in Rome, with a contribution on cross-cutting skills in nephrology. ## Key facts - What: Award for the best oral presentation at the SIAN Congress. - When: May 4, 2026. - Where: Bologna, Italy. - Who: Mattia Giussani, a recent graduate in Nursing. - Prize: Third place nationally. Mattia Giussani, a recent graduate in Nursing from the University of Insubria, has won third place nationally for the best oral presentation at the pre-congress session of the 44th SIAN Congress, held on May 4 in Bologna. Giussani, who graduated on November 17, 2025, presented a thesis titled 'Ethnographic study aimed at constructing repertoires of nursing skills,' focusing on professional skills in the field of nephrology, from nephrology to dialysis to transplantation. The research was conducted within ASST dei Sette Laghi and involved 120 nurses from the corporate management of healthcare and social professions. ### Operational checklist for the research: - Sample: 120 nurses. - Methodology: Ethnographic study. - Field: Nephrology, dialysis, transplantation. - Scientific coordination: Francesca Reato. - Correlation: Morena Orsi. - Supervision: Professor Giulio Carcano, director of the Department of Medicine and Technological Innovation. ### Practical comparisons: - Scenario 1: Nurses with experience in nephrology vs. those with experience in dialysis. - Scenario 2: Technical skills vs. relational skills. - Scenario 3: Theoretical training vs. practical training. Dettagli operativi

The thesis by Mattia Giussani, recently awarded by Università Insubria, represents a significant contribution to advanced training and public recognition of the nursing role. The ethnographic study, based on interviews with 50 cross-border nurses working in Ticino and residing in Lombardy, aims to build structured repertoires of skills aligned with the European (EQF) and Italian (QNQ) qualifications frameworks. This work is particularly relevant for cross-border workers in the healthcare sector, as it provides a basis for recognizing acquired skills and opportunities for career advancement. ### Implications for Cross-Border Workers Cross-border workers employed in Switzerland and residing in Italy can benefit greatly from Giussani's research. Currently, around 12,000 Italian cross-border workers are employed in the healthcare sector in Ticino, representing about 30% of the region's nursing staff. The creation of structured skill repertoires can facilitate the recognition of qualifications acquired in Italy and Switzerland, improving career opportunities. #### Operational Checklist for Skill Recognition 1.
