Serious accident in Valle Verzasca: motorcyclist injured

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 29 April 2026

A 23-year-old Swiss man sustained serious injuries in an accident in Valle Verzasca. The road was temporarily closed.

Contesto

In brief - Swiss motorcyclist seriously injured in accident in Verzasca Valley - Passenger with minor injuries - Road temporarily closed ## Key facts - What: Road traffic accident - When: 26 April 2026 - Where: Lavertezzo, Verzasca Valley - Who: Cantonal police, Piano Intercommunal Police, Locarno Firefighters, SALVA, Rega - Amount: Not specified The Cantonal Police reported a road traffic accident that occurred today shortly before 12 pm in the area of Lavertezzo, in the Verzasca Valley. According to a preliminary reconstruction, a 23-year-old Swiss motorcyclist residing in Locarnese was traveling on the cantonal road towards the upper valley with a 21-year-old Swiss passenger, also residing in Locarnese. For reasons that the police investigation will have to establish, while negotiating a right-hand curve, the driver lost control of the motorcycle, was thrown off and fell to the ground in a gravel parking area next to the carriageway. On the scene, officers from the Cantonal Police and, in support, the Piano Intercommunal Police, the Locarno Firefighters, as well as the rescuers from the Locarno and Valleys Ambulance Service (SALVA) and Rega, who after providing first aid, transported the man by helicopter to the hospital. Based on a preliminary medical assessment, the 23-year-old suffered serious injuries. The 21-year-old suffered minor injuries. To allow rescue operations and technical investigations, the road section was temporarily closed to traffic and was reopened shortly before 1:30 pm. ### Consequences for cross-border workers The accident caused a temporary road closure, which could have repercussions on cross-border workers who use this route for travel between Switzerland and Italy. Frequently Asked Questions
What are the emergency numbers in Ticino?
In case of an emergency in Ticino, you can call the number 117 for the police, 118 for the ambulance, and 112 for the fire department. These numbers are active 24/7 and can be used to request immediate assistance.
What should I do in case of a road accident?
In case of a road accident, it's important to immediately alert the authorities by calling the emergency number 117/112. Then, ensure the safety of the accident scene, provide first aid if possible, and wait for the arrival of the emergency services.
