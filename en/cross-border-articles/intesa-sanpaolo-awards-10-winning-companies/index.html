Varese first in Lombardy in 2025 for export growth. Two companies awarded for excellence in plastics and fashion.

Contesto

In brief - Intesa Sanpaolo awards 10 winning companies, including Lati di Vedano and Pizval di Somma - Varese tops in Lombardy in 2025 for export growth - The two awarded companies excel in plastics and fashion - Swiss companies like F.lli Sutter (Mendrisio) and Fontana di Lugano (Lugano) show similar growth ## Key facts - What: Intesa Sanpaolo's Winning Companies Award - When: April 29, 2026 - Where: Bergamo - Who: Lati di Vedano and Pizval di Somma - Amount: Varese exports growth +21.7% - Regulation: Legislative Decree April 30, 2025, no. 50, article 3, paragraph 2 (tax incentives for exports) - Operational checklist: ESG assessment, international expansion plan, adoption of digital technologies Intesa Sanpaolo awarded 10 winning companies, including Lati di Vedano and Pizval di Somma. The ceremony took place in Bergamo and saw the participation of numerous Lombard companies. The province of Varese recorded the best evolution of exports in 2025, with a 21.7% value growth, driven by sectors such as mechanics (+10%), aerospace (+39%), and agro-food (+28%). In comparison, Ticino saw an 18.5% growth in exports, with companies like F.lli Sutter (Mendrisio) and Fontana di Lugano (Lugano) showing similar performance. The two awarded companies, Lati di Vedano and Pizval di Somma, operate respectively in the plastics and fashion sectors. Lati is one of the leading European producers of engineering-grade thermoplastics, with a turnover of 120 million euros in 2025 and a presence in 30 countries. Pizval, with a turnover of 85 million euros, specializes in the production and finishing of high-quality Leavers and Raschel laces, embroidery, and tulle, exporting 60% of its production to Europe and Asia. Thanks to the support of Intesa Sanpaolo and the Imprese Vincenti program, these companies were able to access the resources and skills necessary to expand into foreign markets. This has led to greater competitiveness and an increase in exports. For example, Lati di Vedano saw a 15% increase in exports in 2022, while Pizval di Somma recorded a 20% increase. ### Future scenarios With the increase in exports and the growth of local businesses, cross-border workers can expect a more dynamic and diversified job market. For example, Ticino companies such as Dufry and MSD have already started collaborating with Varese companies, creating new synergies. ### Comparison with other regions The province of Varese stands out for its export growth, surpassing other Lombard regions.
