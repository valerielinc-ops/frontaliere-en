Employed in logistics at the Cardiocentro Institute in Lugano and a student at the business process technician school, he is among the young talents who participated in SwissSkills 2025 in Bern. His daily life is divided between study and work in a dynamic environment like the hospital. 'I am responsible for supplying the departments with everything they need,' he explains. This activity keeps him constantly moving within the structure, bringing him into contact with different people. 'I enjoy walking around the hospital, making deliveries, and especially the interaction with colleagues in the departments. Even a simple hello makes the days lighter.' In addition to the relational aspect, there is also the more technical side of working in the warehouse. Picking, also known as order preparation, is another cornerstone of warehouse logistics. In short, it involves gathering all the items that make up the recipient's cart. 'I enjoy picking, preparing orders. It's a moment when I focus: for about forty minutes, I collect the items and think about how to organize everything. Arranging the objects precisely and neatly gives me satisfaction.' Attention to detail is fundamental in Alessandro's profession. Behind his journey, there is strong family support. 'For me, family is home, an important reference point in my life,' he explains.
