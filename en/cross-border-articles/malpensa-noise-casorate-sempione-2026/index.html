Noise peaks in Casorate Sempione near Malpensa. Mayor: temporary situation (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 6 May 2026

Environmentalists report noise peaks up to 90.5 dBA. Mayor Cassani confirms the discomfort but reassures: the situation is temporary.

Contesto

In brief - Noise peaks in Casorate Sempione - Values up to 90.5 dBA - Mayor: temporary situation ## Key facts - What: Noise peaks from Malpensa - When: 5 May 2026 - Where: Casorate Sempione - Who: Environmental associations, Mayor Cassani - Amount: 90.5 dBA Environmentalists report noise peaks from Malpensa, with values reaching 90.5 dBA. The situation was reported by the associations Dossier Malpensa, Sopravvivere a Malpensa, Amici della Terra, and WWF Insubria. According to their reports, Casorate Sempione experienced a particularly critical day with a continuous series of closely spaced departures starting at dawn. The associations point to significant figures: 26 flights were recorded during the night, with noise levels exceeding 70 dBA, including 20 between 2 and 6 am, most of which were cargo flights. These were added to 217 movements above 70 dBA during the day. The most relevant data concerns the overall airport noise level, which reached an LVA (Airport Noise Level) value of 66.9 dBA, close to the peak recorded on 10 April, when a value of 67.5 dBA was registered. The associations specify that the measurements are experimental, carried out with a non-certified station, and that they will need to be confirmed by official data. The maximum value recorded was reached by flight ADB3115, an Antonov Design Bureau, which reached 90.5 dBA. According to the associations, the increase in noise is due to the works on Malpensa's runway 35L, which have led to the diversion of traffic to runway 35R, resulting in an increase in overflights over inhabited areas. ### The mayor's position The mayor of Casorate Sempione, Dimitri Cassani, also commented on the issue, confirming the discomfort felt by the population. Dettagli operativi

The increase in noise in Casorate Sempione raises concerns among residents, many of whom are cross-border workers who commute to Switzerland and live in Italy. The temporary situation, linked to the works on runway 35L at Malpensa, has a significant impact on the quality of life of people living in the areas surrounding the airport. ### Impact on cross-border workers Cross-border workers who commute to Switzerland and reside in Italy are particularly affected by this situation. Nighttime noise, in particular, can negatively impact rest and sleep quality, with consequences for health and work productivity. Furthermore, the increase in nighttime air traffic, which is equivalent to 10 daily flights, can cause prolonged stress and discomfort. ### What to do For residents of Casorate Sempione and neighboring areas, it is important to monitor the situation and participate in the meetings of the Airport Commission. Mayor Cassani has assured that the work in the Commission continues with the aim of protecting the health of citizens. It is essential that residents express their concerns and request concrete solutions to reduce noise. ### Possible solutions Among the possible solutions are the adoption of technical measures to reduce aircraft noise, such as the use of alternative runways or the implementation of quieter technologies. Additionally, it is important that the airport operator provides official and transparent data on noise levels, so that residents can have a clear view of the situation and any solutions being implemented. ### Conclusions The situation in Casorate Sempione is temporary, but the impact on the well-being of residents is significant. It is essential that the competent authorities and the airport operator work together to find effective solutions...
