Brivio Bridge: 14 million for refurbishment, up to 40-minute delays

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 30 April 2026

The Brivio Bridge construction site, with a 14 million investment, will cause up to 40 minutes of delay for travelers.

Contesto

In brief - Brivio Bridge closed for 15 months - Up to 40 minutes delay for travel - Investment of 14 million euros ## Key facts - What: Structural upgrading of Brivio Bridge - When: Work begins on 4 May 2026 - Where: Province of Lecco, Lombardy - Who: Como-Lecco Chamber of Commerce, ANAS - Amount: 14 million euros - Duration: 15 months The Competitiveness and Development Table of the Province of Lecco, promoted by the Como-Lecco Chamber of Commerce, met to discuss the imminent start of the structural upgrading work on the Brivio Bridge. The intervention, which will involve closure to traffic from next Monday, 4 May, for a duration of approximately 15 months, was introduced by Vice President Mattia Micheli. Micheli highlighted the absolute necessity of the intervention to ensure the safety of travel, while fully aware of the inconveniences and increased travel times, estimated between 30 and 40 minutes. The provincial administration has explored all practicable alternatives to contain the inconveniences, initiating coordination with the local public transport agencies of the Como, Lecco, and Varese basins and of Bergamo, with the municipalities concerned, and with the operators providing public transport services crossing the bridge. The aim is to define alternative routes and services, with the commitment to minimize the inconveniences and close the site before the 2027-2028 school year. Eng. Gualandi, Head of the Network Management Area of the Lombardy Territorial Structure of ANAS, illustrated the technical details of the intervention, which aims to resolve the current states of degradation, excessive wear, and damage through cortical restorations and reinforcement of the existing pendini by installing tie rods. Dettagli operativi

Implications for cross-border workers The refurbishment works on the Brivio Bridge will have a significant impact on cross-border workers who use this infrastructure to travel between Lombardy and the Canton Ticino. The expected delays, up to 40 minutes, could greatly influence work schedules and daily activities. It is crucial that cross-border workers plan ahead, arranging alternative routes and using the substitute public transport services that will be provided. ### Alternative routes The Provincial Administration has started a coordination with the local public transport agencies to define routes and substitute services. Cross-border workers should inform themselves in advance about the new itineraries and the schedules of the transport means, to avoid inconveniences and delays. It is advisable to use updated navigation applications and consult the websites of the transport agencies to stay informed about any changes. ### Substitute services The local public transport agencies of the Como, Lecco and Varese and Bergamo areas are working to guarantee substitute services during the closure of the Brivio Bridge. Cross-border workers should verify with their respective transport companies the details of the available services and plan their movements accordingly. It is important to be flexible and prepared for any changes in schedules and routes. ## Useful tools to protect your net income To reduce FX leakage, compare CHF-EUR exchange options and banks for cross-border workers.

What to do for cross-border workers For cross-border workers using the Brivio Bridge, it is essential to plan your movements in advance. Here are some steps to follow: 1. Find out about alternative routes: Consult the websites of local public transport agencies to find out about available routes and substitute services. 2. Use updated navigation apps: Apps like Google Maps or Waze can provide real-time information on traffic and alternative routes. 3. Check public transport schedules: Contact transport companies to find out the updated schedules of buses and trains that might replace the service on the Brivio Bridge. 4. Plan alternative routes: Consider alternative routes such as the SP 62 of Valsassina or the SP 583 Lariana, which might offer alternative connections. 5. Stay updated: Subscribe to newsletters or local discussion groups to receive real-time updates on the works and transport services. ### Useful tools To help cross-border workers plan their movements, you can use the route calculator available on the Frontaliere Ticino website. This tool can help calculate travel times and find the most efficient alternative routes. Additionally, you can consult the transport map to view the routes and schedules of public transport. ### Conclusion The redevelopment works on the Brivio Bridge represent an important opportunity to improve the safety and efficiency of local infrastructure. However, the temporary inconveniences due to the closure of the bridge require careful planning by cross-border workers.
