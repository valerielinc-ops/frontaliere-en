Crans-Montana, hospital bills: not all sent to Italian patients

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 1 May 2026

Not all Swiss hospitals have sent bills to Italian patients injured in the Crans-Montana fire.

Contesto

In brief - Bills sent by some Swiss hospitals - Italy will not pay medical expenses - Diplomatic dispute ongoing ## Key facts - What: Bills sent to Italian patients - When: After the Crans-Montana fire - Where: Swiss hospitals - Who: Swiss hospitals and Italian patients - Amount: Not specified The practice contested by Italy seems limited to patients hospitalized in Sion. The copies, not to be paid, were not sent to patients hospitalized in other facilities. The bills - copies for information, not to be paid - sent by Valais to the families of some of the Italian citizens injured in the Crans-Montana fire continue to spark debate. The practice is formally correct, also provided for by a European regulation, but has fueled diplomatic tensions between Bern and Rome. Prime Minister Giorgia Meloni had spoken of 'inhumane bureaucracy'. According to what the Neue Zürcher Zeitung writes on Thursday, however, it seems that not all Swiss hospitals have followed the example of Sion. The Inselspital in Bern, for example, has 'suspended billing' for the time being, with the intention of proceeding 'in due course and in coordination with the authorities'. The University Hospital of Zurich has adopted a similar approach and the Kinderspital - also in Zurich - has stated that it has not yet transmitted anything to the insurance companies (and consequently in copy to the patients). The hospitals mentioned have not provided details, but they seem aware of the sensitivity of the issue, in the context of a drama that has caused suffering but also misunderstandings and anger.
