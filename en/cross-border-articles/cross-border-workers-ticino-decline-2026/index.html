Frequently Asked Questions
How many border workers work in Ticino in 2026?
According to the Federal Statistical Office, in the first quarter of 2026 there were 78,562 border crossers registered in Ticino. This figure represents a slight decrease of 0.2% compared to the previous quarter and 0.5% compared to the same period of the previous year, reversing the positive trend of 2025.
How will taxes for border workers change in Ticino from 2026?
From 2026, border workers working in Ticino will have to pay 40% of the taxes in Switzerland and the remaining 60% in their country of residence. This new tax legislation, together with the obligation to pay AVS contributions in the country of residence from 2025, aims to rebalance cross-border taxation.
What has been the general trend in the number of border workers in Switzerland in recent years?
At the national level, the number of border crossers with a G permit showed a significant increase. From 341,000 in the first quarter of 2021, it rose to about 413,000 at the end of March 2026, registering an overall increase of 21.3% in five years. This indicates a continuous demand for frontier workers at Swiss level.
What are the essential points to check for a border worker working in Switzerland?
It is essential to ensure that you have a valid G permit and that your main residence is abroad. It is also necessary to pay social security contributions in the country of residence and to fully understand the tax regulations of both the country of residence and Switzerland to avoid complications.
Can the decline in border crossers in Ticino affect cross-border services?
Yes, the decline in border crossers in Ticino, albeit modest, could affect the services offered to cross-border communities. For example, the Domodossola-Locarno railway line or cross-border health services such as' Health on the Move 'could be reduced or reorganised if the number of users decreases.
