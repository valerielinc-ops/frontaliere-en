This decision follows the arrest of four people linked to the Camorra and 'Ndrangheta, who were regularly residing in Roveredo. Graubünden's State Councillor Peter Peyer stated that, in the future, applicants will have to explicitly declare any criminal records or ongoing proceedings against them. This measure aims to prevent further criminal infiltration and strengthen controls on new residents. ### Implications for cross-border workers The new procedure could have a significant impact on cross-border workers who wish to settle in Graubünden. This could make it more difficult for individuals with a criminal past to obtain a residence permit. However, it is important to note that false declarations will result in the immediate withdrawal of the permit. ### Additional Documents Required Applicants may be required to provide additional documents, such as a certificate of good conduct, to verify the information provided. This could slow down the application process, but it is a necessary measure to ensure the security and integrity of the system. ### Collaboration Between Cantons Graubünden parliamentarian Samuele Censi highlighted the need to strengthen the information network and promote closer collaboration and systematic exchange of information between the various departments and with other cantons. This could lead to greater efficiency and better management of residence permits. ### Verification of Companies Authorities are working to verify the actual operation of companies with legal personality. This is a crucial step to prevent shell companies from entering into employment contracts solely to obtain residence permits for individuals linked to organized crime.
