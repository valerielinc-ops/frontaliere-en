Petrol exceeds 1.9 euros per litre: first time in two years (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 5 May 2026

Petrol price hits 1.926 euros per litre in self-service mode, while diesel slightly decreases.

Contesto

In brief - Petrol at 1.926 euros/litre self-service - Diesel at 2.044 euros/litre self-service - 6 cent increase for petrol ## Key facts - What: Average fuel prices - When: 5 May 2026 - Where: National road and motorway network - Who: Ministry of Enterprises and Made in Italy - Amount: 1.926 euros/litre for self-service petrol The price of petrol continues to rise. Today, 5 May 2026, for the first time in two years, it exceeds the 1.9 euro per litre mark. Diesel slightly decreases, remaining just over four cents above two euros, while LPG is at 82 cents per litre. The Ministry of Enterprises and Made in Italy reports that, based on the latest data collected by the Mimit Fuel Prices Observatory, today the average price of fuels in 'self-service' mode along the national road network is 1.926 euros/l for petrol and 2.044 euros/l for diesel. On the motorway network, however, the average self-service price is 1.981 euros/l for petrol and 2.114 euros/l for diesel. ### Details by sales method and brand According to the usual survey by Staffetta Quotidiana, IP has increased the recommended prices of petrol by 6 cents per litre and reduced those of diesel by four. For Q8, we record a one-cent decrease on petrol, and for Tamoil, a one-cent decrease on diesel.
