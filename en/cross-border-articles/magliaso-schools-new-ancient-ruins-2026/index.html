Roman ruins found in Magliaso during school construction

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 27 April 2026

Late Roman ruins emerged during work on new elementary schools. Archaeologists at work documenting the finds

Contesto

In brief - Roman ruins in Magliaso, 1500 years ago - Discovered during the construction of new schools - Archaeologists document for two weeks ## Key facts - What: Ruins from the late Roman period - When: Discovered during work on new elementary schools - Where: Magliaso, Canton Ticino - Who: Archaeologists - Duration: Two weeks for documentation In Magliaso, the future has brought the past to light. During the construction of new elementary schools, ruins dating back to the end of the Roman era, approximately 1500 years ago, have emerged. Archaeologists have been working at the site since February and will have two more weeks to complete the documentation of the discovery. Once this phase is concluded, the remains will be removed, and the area will be cleared to allow the construction of the new school building. ### The relationship between past and present Professor Lorenzo Cantoni, holder of the UNESCO chair at the University of Italian Switzerland, emphasized how each era leaves traces that can resurface during excavations and construction sites. In the face of these discoveries, a reflection always opens: to preserve, museum, cover, remove, or make way for new constructions. Cantoni explained that the central point is to find a balance between the protection of heritage and contemporary needs. On the one hand, there are those who prioritize the conservation and enhancement of the remains; on the other, those who believe that the territory must also be able to accommodate new functions and new works. Implications for the Territory and the Community The discovery of Roman ruins in Magliaso raises several questions for the local territory and community. The presence of such significant archaeological remains can influence not only the project for the new elementary schools but also the municipal master plan and future urban development policies. ### Conservation vs. Innovation One of the main dilemmas is that of conserving the remains versus the need to proceed with new constructions. The choice to remove the ruins to make way for the schools could be seen as a loss for the local historical heritage, but on the other hand, the realization of new school infrastructures is essential for the community. ### Community Involvement It is fundamental that the local community is involved in the decision-making process. The opinions of residents, historians, and conservation experts must be heard and considered. This can be done through public consultations, informative meetings, and open debates. ### Documentation and Enhancement Even if the ruins are not physically conserved on the site, their accurate documentation is a crucial step. This documentation can be used to create exhibitions, publications, and educational materials that enhance Magliaso's historical heritage. Furthermore, the data collected can be useful for future archaeological research and for understanding local history. ### Economic Impact The presence of archaeological remains can also have an economic impact on the territory. Tourist attractions related to Roman history can be developed, bringing a flow of visitors and contributing to the local economy.
