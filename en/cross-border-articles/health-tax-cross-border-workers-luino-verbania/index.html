The Pd denounces disparities between cross-border workers in Luino and Verbania, with potential union appeals and constitutional doubts.

Contesto

In brief - Health tax for cross-border workers creates disparity between Luino and Verbania - Pd accuses the center-right of normative confusion - Possible union appeals for constitutional doubts ## Key facts - What: Disparity in health tax for cross-border workers between Luino and Verbania - When: May 2026 - Where: Luino and Verbania - Who: Democratic Party, Mayor Enrico Bianchi, Senator Alessandro Alfieri - Amount: Not yet specified In the same factory, a worker from Luino might pay the health tax, while one from Verbania might not. This is the image chosen by regional councilor Samuele Astuti to describe the chaos that, according to the Democratic Party, is arising around the so-called health tax for cross-border workers. In front of Palazzo Verbania in Luino, the Pd gathered Senator Alessandro Alfieri, Astuti himself, and Mayor Enrico Bianchi for a press conference dedicated to cross-border workers, between health tax and returns to border municipalities. Astuti spoke of a measure 'born overnight' within the budget law, without consultation with territories, unions, and workers. 'When the new fiscal agreement on cross-border workers was created,' he recalled, 'there were assemblies, discussions, and a great deal of shared work. Here, instead, we found ourselves faced with a norm imposed from above.' For the regional councilor, the problem does not concern only cross-border workers but the entire balance of border areas: 'This tax risks weakening already fragile territories.' ### Normative confusion Today, according to Astuti, a paradoxical situation has been reached: 'Piedmont has decided not to apply it, while the Lombardy Region continues to push for its introduction as soon as possible.' Practical Implications for Cross-Border Workers The health tax for cross-border workers represents a significant change that could have a direct impact on workers who daily cross the border between Italy and Switzerland. In particular, the disparity between Luino and Verbania raises important questions about equal treatment and regulatory coherence. ### Comparison Between Luino and Verbania Workers from Luino and Verbania operating in the same company might find themselves in different situations. While cross-border workers from Luino might be subject to the health tax, those from Verbania might not be. This disparity not only creates confusion but could also lead to economic inequalities among workers. ### Constitutional Doubts The constitutional doubts raised by the Democratic Party and the unions could lead to legal challenges. If the regulation is declared unconstitutional, a review of the health tax might be necessary to ensure fair treatment for all cross-border workers. ### Impact on Border Towns The reduction of returns to border towns represents another critical aspect of the new regulation. Towns like Luino, which have historically benefited from these funds, might see a decrease in available resources. This could have a significant impact on local administrations and the services offered to residents. ### What Cross-Border Workers Should Do Cross-border workers residing in Luino or Verbania should closely monitor regulatory developments. It is advisable to consult a tax advisor to better understand the implications of the health tax and prepare for any changes.
