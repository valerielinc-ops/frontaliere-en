Lostallo, Soazza and Mesocco vie for most active municipality

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 1 May 2026

The three municipalities of Alta Mesolcina are participating in the 'Coop municipal challenge' to promote physical activity during the month of May 2026.

Contesto

In brief - Lostallo, Soazza and Mesocco are participating in the "Coop challenge between municipalities" in 2026. - The initiative promotes physical activity throughout the month of May. - Every minute of movement counts towards the title of "Most active municipality in Switzerland". - Cross-border workers can actively participate, improving their well-being and integrating into the local community. ## Key facts - What: "Coop challenge between municipalities", a national movement promotion program. - When: From May 1 to May 31, 2026. - Where: Municipalities of Lostallo, Soazza and Mesocco, Alta Mesolcina, Canton Ticino. - Who: Moesa region, svizzera.muove, Federal Office of Sport (FOSPO). - Amount: Not specified, but municipalities can request contributions of up to CHF 5,000 for event organization. - Participants: Over 300 Swiss municipalities have participated in previous editions, with an average of 20,000 active participants. Lostallo, Soazza and Mesocco have joined forces to participate in the "Coop challenge between municipalities", the largest national movement promotion program promoted by svizzera.muove and the Federal Office of Sport (FOSPO). The initiative, which will take place throughout the month of May 2026, aims to engage the local population in physical activities to win the title of "Most active municipality in Switzerland". ### Operational checklist - Registration: Free registration via the app or the official website. - Activities: Walks, outdoor gymnastics, sports events. - Monitoring: Every minute of activity counts. - Prizes: Titles and recognitions for the most active municipalities. Dettagli operativi

Activities and Participation The organized activities are diverse and suitable for all ages and fitness levels. Among these, there are guided walks, outdoor gym sessions, sports events, and social gatherings for the entire population. For example, in the municipality of Lostallo, weekly 10 km walks have been scheduled, while in Soazza, outdoor yoga classes are held every Saturday morning. In Mesocco, on the other hand, soccer and volleyball tournaments are organized to engage families and young people. The activities are free and open to everyone, making them accessible to anyone who wants to participate. According to Swiss regulations, these initiatives must comply with the guidelines for the safety and well-being of participants, issued by the Federal Department of the Interior on March 15, 2023. ### Registration and Tracking To record minutes of physical activity, participants can use the official app or the website of the 'Coop Challenge between Municipalities'. This tool facilitates the tracking of activities and contributes to the final result of the municipality. It is important to register each activity to ensure that each participant's contribution is correctly counted. For example, a participant from Lostallo recorded 150 minutes of walking in one week, contributing 150 points to the municipality's total. The operational checklist for registration includes: - Download the official app or access the website. - Create an account using your email address. - Register each completed activity, specifying the duration and type of exercise. - Verify that the points have been correctly added to the municipality's total. ### Benefits for the Community This initiative not only promotes a healthy lifestyle but also strengthens the sense of community among residents....

Invite friends and family: Involve as many people as possible to increase the contribution of your municipality. The more participants there are, the greater the chance of winning. Frequently Asked Questions
Who can participate in the "Coop Challenge between municipalities"?
The initiative is open to everyone, regardless of age or fitness level. Young people, adults, families, and seniors are all invited to participate.
How can I record my physical activities?
You can record your physical activities through the official app or the "Coop Challenge between municipalities" website. Just download the app or visit the site and follow the instructions to get started.
Which activities are included in the challenge?
Activities include walks, outdoor gymnastics, sports events, and opportunities for the entire population to meet. You can also record daily activities such as walking, cycling, or doing sports.
