In brief - Car thefts on the rise in Ticino - Land Rover, Alfa Romeo, and Porsche most targeted - AXA paid out 5 million francs in 2025 ## Key facts - What: Increase in car thefts - When: 2025 - Where: Ticino, Geneva, Vaud, Basel City - Who: AXA - Amount: 5 million francs Car thefts in Switzerland are increasing, and Ticino is no exception. The insurance company AXA recorded a higher number of claims in 2025 compared to previous years, with a peak in the cantons of Geneva, Vaud, and Basel City. Ticino also stands above the Swiss average. According to AXA, car thefts have nearly doubled compared to the pre-pandemic period, with a total of approximately 400 cases per year. Luxury cars are the most targeted, with an upward trend. Michael Villiger, head of motor vehicle claims at AXA, observed that 'on Swiss roads, vehicles are becoming increasingly modern and better equipped. Thieves seem to be targeting these models more often, and apparently, they are increasingly successful in stealing high-value vehicles.' ### Most affected models The most stolen cars are Land Rover SUVs, with a theft probability over four times higher than the average. They are followed by Alfa Romeo and Porsche. Also targeted are Audi, Jeep, Fiat, BMW, and Mercedes-Benz. Less exposed are Tesla, Suzuki, Subaru, Seat, Skoda, Mazda, Ford, Opel, Hyundai, Nissan, and Mitsubishi. ### Regional differences Regional differences may be linked to proximity to the border. Frequently Asked Questions
Which are the most stolen cars in Ticino?
