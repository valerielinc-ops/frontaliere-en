Lake Maggiore at +1.35m: Gobbi, balanced solution

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 1 May 2026

Ticino State Councillor Norman Gobbi comments on the rise of Lake Maggiore's maximum level to +1.35 metres.

Contesto

In brief - Lake Maggiore at +1.35 meters: a balanced solution - Italy caught off guard by rise to +1.40 meters - Gobbi: "Breach of agreements, UFAM intervention" ## Key facts - What: Maximum level rise of Lake Maggiore - When: Announced on May 1, 2026 - Where: Lake Maggiore, Canton Ticino - Who: Po River Basin Authority, Canton Ticino - Amount: +1.35 meters (ordinary), +1.40 meters (summer) Ticino State Councillor Norman Gobbi commented on the rise of the maximum level of Lake Maggiore to +1.35 meters above the zero hydrometric level of Sesto Calende, defining it as a "comprehensive balanced solution." The decision was made as part of the experimental program on the regulation of Lake Maggiore levels, conducted by the Po River Basin Authority. The agreed ordinary maximum level remains set at +1.35 meters, while the summer limit of +1.40 meters can only be considered in case of water severity. ### Reactions and Criticisms The Swiss authorities were not consulted and, consequently, were not aware of the decision to extend the summer maximum level to +1.40 meters. Canton Ticino immediately reported this breach of agreements to the Federal Office for the Environment (UFAM), asking it to intervene with the competent Italian authorities. Gobbi stated that the level of +1.35 meters is the result of a compromise matured through dialogue between Italian and Swiss institutions, within the framework of the bilateral body. ### Implications for Canton Ticino A possible rise in the lake level can lead to various critical issues in Ticino territory. From an environmental point of view, it can affect the balance of riverside ecosystems. Moreover, in case of flooding, the hydraulic safety margin is reduced, increasing the risk for riverside areas. Ticino authorities have emphasized the importance of maintaining an adequate regulation margin to contain potential floods, especially during the summer months. ### Use of Public Spaces Higher water levels in the lake can create difficulties in accessing the shores, affecting the accessibility of public spaces and recreational and tourist activities. This is particularly important as we approach the swimming season. Ticino authorities are evaluating the necessary measures to ensure the safety and usability of public spaces along the lake's shores. ### Dialogue Between Institutions Councilor of State Gobbi reiterated the importance of constant dialogue between Italian and Swiss institutions to ensure balanced and safe management of Lake Maggiore's water resources. Frequently Asked Questions
What is the ordinary maximum level of Lake Maggiore?
The ordinary maximum level of Lake Maggiore is set at +1.35 meters above the zero hydrometric level of Sesto Calende. This level was agreed upon between Italian and Swiss authorities within the framework of the Bilateral Body.
What happens in case of water severity?
In case of water severity, the summer maximum level of Lake Maggiore can be raised to +1.40 meters. However, this decision must be made in accordance with established procedures and with the involvement of Swiss authorities.
What are the implications for cross-border workers?
The rise in the level of Lake Maggiore can have implications for cross-border workers and residents along the lake shores. In particular, it can affect hydraulic safety and the use of public spaces. It is important to follow the instructions of the authorities and respect safety measures.
