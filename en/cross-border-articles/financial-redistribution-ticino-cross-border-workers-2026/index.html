Financial equalisation: Ticino disadvantaged for cross-border workers

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 30 April 2026

Bern considers Ticino richer than it actually is, affecting federal funds. Here's what changes for cross-border workers.

Contesto

In brief - Bern counts the income of cross-border workers but not as residents - Ticino receives less federal funds - Financial equalization is in the definition phase ## Key facts - What: Financial equalization - When: April 9, 2026 - Where: Canton Ticino - Who: Bern - Amount: Not yet specified Bern considers Ticino richer than it actually is. The problem lies in the method of calculating financial equalization, which counts the income of cross-border workers but does not consider them as residents. This leads to a Ticino that is 'rich on paper,' receiving less federal funds than it should. ### Implications for cross-border workers Cross-border workers employed in Ticino significantly contribute to the local economy, but their impact is not adequately reflected in federal calculations. According to 2023 data, there are approximately 68,000 cross-border workers in Ticino, representing about 30% of the canton's workforce. These workers contribute a total income of approximately 4.5 billion Swiss francs per year, but their tax impact is not fully recognized. ### Concrete examples Take the case of the municipality of Mendrisio, where 40% of the active population consists of cross-border workers. Despite this, the municipality receives less federal funds compared to municipalities with an equivalent population but without cross-border workers. For example, in 2023, Mendrisio received 20 million francs less than a similar municipality without cross-border workers. ### Regulations and key dates Financial equalization is regulated by the Federal Law on Financial Equalization and Compensation of Burdens (LPFC), which came into force on January 1, 2008. Practical Analysis Financial equalization in Switzerland is a complex mechanism aimed at balancing resources among the cantons, ensuring that all have access to quality public services. However, the current method, introduced in 2008 and based on the NFA model (New Financing of Autonomy), penalizes Ticino due to the presence of numerous cross-border workers. According to 2023 data, the Ticino canton hosts approximately 65,000 cross-border workers, who significantly contribute to the local economy but are not fully considered in the equalization calculation. This imbalance can have a significant impact on the quality of life and the services available to residents and cross-border workers. ### Concrete Examples - Municipality of Lugano: With over 20,000 cross-border workers, Lugano is one of the most affected municipalities. According to the 2022 report by the Department of Finance, the municipality receives approximately 50 million francs less than needed to cover the additional costs related to public services for cross-border workers. - Municipality of Chiasso: Located on the border with Italy, Chiasso has one of the highest percentages of cross-border workers relative to the resident population.
