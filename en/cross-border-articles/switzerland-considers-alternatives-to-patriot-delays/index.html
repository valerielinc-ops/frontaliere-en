Frequently Asked Questions
Why is the Swiss franc so strong against the euro?
The CHF is structurally strong for four main reasons: the constitutional debt brake (Schuldenbremse) that keeps Swiss public debt at around 27% of GDP, SNB gold reserves of over 1,000 tonnes, Swiss neutrality that attracts global capital in periods of tension, and historically low inflation. These factors combined mean that in periods of global uncertainty, capital flows into CHF, strengthening it further.
How much do I save using Wise instead of a bank transfer to convert my CHF salary to euros?
On a net salary of CHF 5,800 per month, the typical saving is EUR 50–100 per month compared with a classic bank transfer, which applies an exchange spread of 1.5–2.5% that is not always explicitly disclosed. Over a year the saving can reach EUR 600–1,200. Wise and Revolut use the mid-market rate with transparent fees in the range of 0.35–0.7%.
Do I need to declare my Swiss bank account to the Italian tax authorities?
Yes, if the balance exceeds EUR 5,000 at any point during the year. It must be declared in Schedule RW of the Italian personal income tax return (Redditi PF). This is not an additional tax on the balance but a tax monitoring obligation. Failure to declare is subject to penalties calculated as a percentage of the undeclared value. Even modest accounts used only for daily Swiss work expenses must be declared.
