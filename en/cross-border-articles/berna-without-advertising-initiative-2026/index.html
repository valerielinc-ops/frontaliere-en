Berna bans ads: initiative to prohibit commercial ads

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 1 May 2026

The 'Berna senza pubblicità' initiative aims to ban commercial ads without local references in public spaces.

Contesto

In brief - Initiative to ban commercial advertising without local references in Bern - Cultural posters and local signs not affected - Left-wing majority of the Municipal Council had approved a similar motion in 2024 ## Key facts - What: Initiative to ban commercial advertising without local references - When: Launched on May 10, 2026 - Where: Bern, Switzerland - Who: Left-wing parties and organizations critical of consumerism - Amount: Not specified The 'Bern without advertising' initiative was launched today, Friday, with the aim of reducing commercial influence in public spaces. Some left-wing parties and organizations critical of consumerism are calling for a ban on all commercial advertising without local references across the municipality. The initiative aims to establish a municipal regulation that prohibits this type of advertising on billboards and commercial screens. Cultural posters, public notice boards, and local signs would not be affected. In 2024, the left-wing majority of the Municipal Council had already approved a motion for a ban on advertising, but during the discussion on the 2026 budget, socialists and young socialists had backed down. This is because giving up the millions from advertising would not have been sustainable. The initiative could have significant implications for cross-border workers who work in Bern, particularly those involved in the advertising sector or who use public spaces to promote their services.
