KOF Barometer: Swiss economy slightly improves but outlook modest (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 4 May 2026

The KOF barometer stands at 97.9 points in April, but the Swiss economy remains below the long-term average of 100.

Contesto

In brief - KOF Barometer at 97.9 points in April - Increase of 2.3 points compared to March - Modest economic prospects for Switzerland ## Key facts - What: KOF Barometer - When: April 2026 - Where: Switzerland - Who: KOF Institute of the Federal Institute of Technology Zurich - Amount: 97.9 points The KOF barometer, the business cycle indicator of the KOF Institute of the Federal Institute of Technology Zurich, recorded a slight improvement in April, standing at 97.9 points. This value represents an increase of 2.3 points compared to the March data, revised from 96.1 to 95.6, which was the lowest since mid-2025. Despite this improvement, the index remains below the multi-year average of 100 points, indicating modest economic prospects for Switzerland. Experts at KOF commented that the prospects for the Swiss economy remain modest. The increase in the index was driven mainly by indicators from the manufacturing industry, services, and consumer demand. However, negative signals come from the hotel and restaurant sector, while indices related to foreign demand, financial and insurance services, and construction are virtually unchanged. ### Implications for cross-border workers For cross-border workers employed in Switzerland, these modest economic prospects could mean greater caution in the job market. In particular, the manufacturing and services sectors, which contributed positively to the index increase, could offer more job opportunities. However, the struggling hotel and restaurant sector could see a reduction in hiring or even layoffs. ### Comparison with the previous situation Compared to March 2026, when the KOF barometer recorded the lowest value since mid-2025, April shows an improvement. Dettagli operativi

Analysis of Economic Trends The Swiss economy shows tentative signs of recovery, but it is not yet at full speed. This is the message emerging from the KOF Institute's barometer. The improvement in the index in April is a positive signal, but not robust enough to indicate a full recovery. ### Growing Sectors The sectors that have positively contributed to the increase in the index are manufacturing, services, and consumer demand. These sectors could offer more job opportunities for cross-border workers, especially for those with specific skills in these areas. ### Struggling Sectors On the other hand, the hotel and restaurant sector continues to show negative signs. This could mean fewer job opportunities for cross-border workers operating in these sectors. Furthermore, the indices relating to foreign demand, financial and insurance services, as well as construction are almost unchanged, indicating a stable but not growing situation. ### Comparison with Other Indicators The KOF barometer is one of the most reliable indicators for anticipating the evolution of the Swiss economy. However, it is important to consider other economic indicators to have a complete picture of the situation. For example, GDP and the unemployment rate can provide further information on the health of the Swiss economy. ### Implications for Cross-Border Workers For cross-border workers, it is crucial to closely monitor the evolution of the Swiss economy. In a context of modest prospects, it is important to be ready to seize new job opportunities in growing sectors, while being aware of potential difficulties in declining sectors.
