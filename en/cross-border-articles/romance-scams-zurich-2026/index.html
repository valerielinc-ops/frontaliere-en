Romance scams in Switzerland: 10 arrests for millions in francs

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 29 April 2026

Large-scale operation in 6 Swiss cantons dismantles network of romance scams linked to the Nigerian criminal group Black Axe.

Contesto

In brief - 10 arrests for romantic scams in Switzerland - Operation in 6 cantons against Black Axe - Damages amounting to millions of francs ## Key facts - What: Romantic scams and money laundering - When: April 28, 2026 - Where: Six Swiss cantons - Who: Public Ministry II and Zurich cantonal police - Amount: Millions of Swiss francs A large-scale police operation conducted in six Swiss cantons has led to the arrest of ten men linked to the Nigerian criminal group Black Axe, suspected of cyber fraud and money laundering amounting to millions of francs. The Zurich prosecutors proceeded with the arrest of ten men adhering to or close to a Nigerian group suspected of aggravated money laundering, romantic scams, and other cybercrimes that have caused damages amounting to millions of francs. During a large-scale operation conducted in six cantons, numerous house searches were also carried out on Tuesday. The defendants are aged between 32 and 54, according to a joint statement from the Public Ministry II and the Zurich cantonal and municipal police. ### The investigations The searches, of homes and warehouses, outside of Zurich were carried out in collaboration with the respective cantonal police. With the exception of one case, the suspects are members of a Nigerian confraternity and are originally from Nigeria or people who support this group, the statement reads. More explicit is the EU agency for cooperation in law enforcement activities (Europol) which in a note, also issued today, designates the criminal group as Black Axe (Black Axe). ### The arrest of a high-ranking official According to Europol, a particularly significant result of the operation was the arrest of the organization's head for the entire Southern European region. Romantic Scams One of the main frauds is the romantic scam, often referred to with the English term romance scam: scammers create fake profiles on social networks or online dating sites. With great psychological skill, they pretend to maintain a romantic relationship with the victims for weeks or months, in order to create a strong emotional dependence. Once they have gained their trust, the scammers ask the victims for large sums of money under the pretext of financial difficulties or alleged investment opportunities. ### Implications for Cross-Border Workers Romantic scams can have a significant impact on cross-border workers who work in Switzerland and reside in Italy. These individuals, often already exposed to financial and bureaucratic challenges, can become easy targets for scammers. It is essential that cross-border workers are aware of the risks and adopt preventive measures to protect their savings and financial stability. ### What to Do to Protect Yourself To protect yourself from romantic scams, it is important to be skeptical of new online acquaintances and always verify the identity of the people you come into contact with. Additionally, it is advisable to avoid sharing personal or financial information with strangers and to be wary of requests for money, especially if they come from people who claim to be in difficulty. ### Legal Consequences In their statement, Zurich investigators warn of the risks of this scam, which often take on such proportions as to jeopardize the existence of the people involved. Since the investigations are still ongoing, the investigators cannot provide further information. Frequently Asked Questions
What are romantic scams?
Romantic scams, or romance scams, are frauds where scammers create fake profiles on social networks or dating sites to deceive victims and obtain money. Scammers pretend to have a romantic relationship with the victims for weeks or months, creating a strong emotional dependence, and then ask for large sums of money with various pretexts.
