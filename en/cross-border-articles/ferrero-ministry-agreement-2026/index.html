Ferrero and Ministry for sports in schools

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 28 April 2026

Ministry of Education and Merit and Ferrero spa sign agreement for educational and training activities in primary and lower secondary schools.

Contesto

In brief - Memorandum of Understanding between the Ministry of Education and Ferrero spa - Promotion of recreational and motor activities in schools - Goal: to spread the culture of physical activity ## Key facts - What: Memorandum of Understanding for educational and training activities - When: April 28, 2026 - Where: Headquarters of the Ministry of Education and Merit - Who: Ministry of Education and Merit and Ferrero spa - Amount: Not yet specified The Minister of Education and Merit Giuseppe Valditara and the President of Ferrero spa Massimo Micieli signed, at the headquarters of the MIM, the Memorandum of Understanding to promote educational and training activities, of a recreational, motor and sports orientation nature, in primary and lower secondary schools. The aim is to spread the culture of motor and sports activity as a lever for the training of students, as well as inclusion, guidance and combating school dropout. This Memorandum reflects the relevance and commitment in promoting physical activity as an educational and training tool that the Ministry of Education and Merit has wanted to interpret also through structural investments, including those destined for the improvement of school gyms, to support interventions of enhancement, safety and requalification of spaces dedicated to sport. ### Implications for cross-border workers The Memorandum of Understanding between the Ministry of Education and Merit and Ferrero spa represents a significant opportunity for cross-border workers who work in Ticino and whose children attend Italian schools. Dettagli operativi

Analysis of Playful-Motor Activities The playful-motor activities promoted by the agreement between the Ministry of Education and Merit and Ferrero spa are based on the scientifically validated Joy of Moving methodology. This methodology is based on movement and play, prioritizing the relational and participatory aspects. The goal is to contribute to the physical-motor, cognitive, and socio-emotional development of students, making the playful-motor activity an inclusive, participatory, and joyful experience. ### Benefits for Students Playful-motor activities can develop cognitive, relational, and emotional skills in students. Through sport, young people learn to collaborate, respect rules, manage emotions, and face commitment. Moreover, motor activity, especially when shared and outdoors, represents a natural antidote to the risk of isolation and digital dependence, fostering authentic relationships and balanced personal development. ### Teacher Training The agreement provides for the training of teachers through accredited courses, making available simple and easily replicable educational tools, but scientifically validated. This can improve the quality of teaching and the effectiveness of playful-motor activities in schools. For cross-border workers, this means that their children will benefit from quality education, with better-prepared teachers and more comprehensive school programs. ### Impact on School Dropout One of the goals of the agreement is to combat school dropout. Playful-motor activities can help keep students motivated and involved in school life, reducing the risk of abandonment. Procedures for implementing the protocol To implement the protocol, Italian schools will need to follow specific procedures. First, teachers will need to be trained through accredited courses, providing scientifically validated training tools. Subsequently, schools can introduce new recreational and motor activities into their curricula, using the teaching materials provided by Ferrero spa. ### Deadlines and timelines The timelines for implementing the protocol have not yet been specified, but it is expected that activities will begin by the end of the 2026 school year. Interested schools will be able to participate in training courses and receive the necessary teaching materials to start the new activities. ### Tools and resources available The Ministry of Education and Merit and Ferrero spa will provide schools with a series of tools and resources to implement the protocol. These include teaching materials, teacher training courses, and technical support for creating spaces dedicated to physical activity. Frequently Asked Questions
What are the benefits of recreational and motor activities for students?
Recreational and motor activities promote the physical, cognitive, and socio-emotional development of students. Through sports, young people learn to collaborate, respect rules, manage emotions, and face commitment. Moreover, physical activity fosters authentic relationships and balanced personal development.
How can cross-border workers benefit from the agreement?
Cross-border workers can benefit from the agreement through an expanded educational offer for their children, positively impacting their well-being and personal growth. Additionally, teacher training and the provision of scientifically validated educational tools can improve the quality of education offered.
What are the procedures for implementing the agreement?
To implement the agreement, Italian schools will need to train teachers through accredited courses and introduce new recreational and motor activities into their curricula. The timelines for implementation are not yet specified, but it is expected that the activities will begin by the end of the 2026 school year.
