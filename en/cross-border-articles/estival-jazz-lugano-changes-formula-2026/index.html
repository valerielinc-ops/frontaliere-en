Estival Jazz Lugano changes format: two paid evenings

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 28 April 2026

From 2026, Estival Jazz offers two paid evenings with Ernia and Antonello Venditti. The era of free concerts is over.

Contesto

In brief - Estival Jazz changes format: two paid evenings - Ernia and Antonello Venditti are the first announced artists - End of the era of free concerts ## Key facts - What: Change of format for Estival Jazz - When: From 2026 - Where: Lugano - Who: City of Lugano, Roberto Badaracco, Jacky Marti - Amount: Tickets from 57.75 CHF to 65 CHF LUGANO - Times change and so do institutions; the powers that be can only adapt. This is the case for Estival, which for its 46th edition has decided, as already anticipated at the end of March, to change its formula: no longer (only) free concerts. But two paid dates with highly attractive names. The Lugano festival, known for its tradition of absolute quality, has announced two paid evenings for 2026. The first artist, the trapper-singer Ernia, will perform on Saturday, July 11th in Piazza Riforma. The second, Antonello Venditti, will be the star of the evening on Friday, July 10th. The decision was announced at a press conference with a sad and particularly subdued tone, dominated by long silences. Present were the Head of Department Roberto Badaracco, the Director of the Events and Congresses Division Claudio Chiapparino, and what is now the former artistic director of Estival, Jacky Marti, who confirmed his step back. The reason for the change, as anticipated by the deputy mayor himself, is linked to costs and the need to 'find a new balance capable of reconciling artistic excellence and economic sustainability'. ### A golden age that will not return 'Both under the direction of Marti-Wyden and with the participation of the City of Lugano, Estival Jazz can boast a tradition of absolute quality, great visibility on a global level, and of which we are extremely proud,' adds Badaracco, '45 years that represent a golden age tha...

Frequently Asked Questions
Why has Estival Jazz decided to switch to a paid model?
The decision was made due to rising costs and the need to find a new balance between artistic excellence and economic sustainability. Sponsors have become less generous, and artists demand higher fees, making the free concert model unsustainable.
Which artists have been announced for the first paid evenings?
The first artists announced are Ernia, who will perform on Saturday, July 11, and Antonello Venditti, who will be the star of the evening on Friday, July 10.
