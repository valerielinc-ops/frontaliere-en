May 1st in Varese: work and rights in the AI era

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 1 May 2026

CGIL, CISL and UIL take to the streets to demand more rights, security and fair wages in the era of artificial intelligence. Ezio Cigna: work is the foundation of dignity.

Contesto

In brief - CGIL, CISL and UIL in Varese for May Day 2026 - Focus on rights, safety and fair wages in the era of artificial intelligence - Ezio Cigna: work is the foundation of dignity and social cohesion - Parade started from Piazzale Trieste, concluded in Piazza Monte Grappa ## Key facts - What: Union demonstration for May Day - When: May 1st, 2026 - Where: Varese, Italy - Who: CGIL, CISL, UIL - Amount: Not yet specified May Day in Varese was not just a celebration, but a proposal and a mobilization. CGIL, CISL and UIL joined forces to put work at the center of a phase of profound transformation, marked by digitization, ecological transition and artificial intelligence. The parade, which started from Piazzale Trieste, reached Piazza Monte Grappa, where the interventions of the union delegates took place. The title chosen for the event was "Dignified work: collective bargaining, new protections and new rights for Italy that is changing in the era of artificial intelligence." ### Interventions and conclusions On stage, accompanied by the music of the Jefferson Cleaners, the representatives of the three union organizations took turns: Giulia Co’ (CGIL) – FICCTEM, Flavio Cervellino (CISL) – Filca CISL dei Laghi (construction), Mariastella Sella (UIL) – UIL Public Function. The conclusions were entrusted to Ezio Cigna of the national CGIL, who underlined how work is not just one variable among others, but the foundation of people's dignity and social cohesion. ### Safety and quality of work Among the central themes of the demonstration, workplace safety. The numbers of work-related deaths continue to represent an open wound, a structural problem that requires more controls, more prevention and a shared responsibility throughout the production chain. Implications for cross-border workers The May Day demonstration in Varese highlighted issues of great relevance for cross-border workers who work in Italy and reside in Switzerland. The issue of low-wage work and inequalities is particularly felt among cross-border workers, who often have to face low wages and precarious working conditions. The demand for fair wages and greater job security is crucial to ensuring a dignified life for those who cross the border daily. ### Comparison with the previous situation Compared to previous years, this year's demonstration has paid particular attention to technological transformations and the impact of artificial intelligence on the world of work. This is a topic that directly concerns cross-border workers, who must adapt to a rapidly evolving job market. The demand for new protections and strong collective bargaining to govern innovation is a priority for all workers, including cross-border workers. ### Future scenarios In a context of increasing digitalization, it is essential that cross-border workers are protected by fair employment contracts and adequate safeguards. The demonstration underscored the importance of redistributing time and wealth so that the benefits of technological innovation can be shared by all. This also means guaranteeing access to fundamental social rights, such as public health, which is a particularly relevant issue for cross-border workers who work in Italy. ### Concrete procedures For cross-border workers who wish to participate in similar initiatives or who want to delve deeper into the issues discussed, it is important to inform themselves about the activities of local and national unions.
