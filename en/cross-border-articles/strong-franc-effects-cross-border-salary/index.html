Strong franc: how the CHF-EUR exchange rate affects cross-border salaries (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 17 February 2026

The Swiss franc keeps strengthening against the euro. For cross-border workers spending in Italy, the exchange rate can mean hundreds of euros more or less each month.

Contesto

TL;DR - EUR/CHF at 0.913 in Feb 2026, franc up 9% in 3 years - Cross-border workers earn more due to strong franc - Strong franc increases labour costs for Ticino companies - Exchange rate fluctuations impact taxes and savings ## Key facts - Exchange rate: EUR/CHF at 0.913 in February 2026 - Franc appreciation: 9% in three years, 18% in five years - Cross-border workers: Nearly 80,000 employed in Ticino, living in Italy - Monthly salary: CHF 5,000 net per month, EUR 5,435 at 0.92 rate - Impact on salary: EUR 430 more than five years ago at same franc salary - Labour costs: Higher for Ticino companies with cross-border employees - Tax implications: Swiss withholding tax in francs, Italian taxes in euros - Mortgage repayments: Cheaper in Italy for those earning in francs In February 2026, the EUR/CHF exchange rate hit 0.913: the Swiss franc has appreciated by 9% in three years and over 18% in five years. For the nearly 80,000 cross-border workers employed in Ticino and living in Italy, this trend has direct, tangible consequences on take-home pay. A cross-border worker earning CHF 5,000 net per month, converting at a rate of 0.92, takes home approximately EUR 5,435 — a full EUR 430 more than five years ago at the same franc salary. This effect acts as an automatic raise for anyone paid in francs and spending in euros, though it can quickly reverse if the franc weakens.

Dettagli operativi

But a strong franc does not only bring advantages. For Ticino companies, the strengthening Swiss currency means relatively higher labour costs for cross-border employees, making resident workers more competitive. On the tax front, the implications are complex: Swiss withholding tax is in francs, but Italian taxes (IRPEF, surcharges) are calculated on the euro conversion, with the risk of significant year-on-year fluctuations. Additionally, mortgage repayments or rent in Italy become cheaper for those earning in francs, but savings held in euros lose purchasing power against the franc.

Punti chiave

To manage this variable effectively, it is essential to monitor the exchange rate constantly and plan conversions strategically. Some cross-border workers keep a franc account and a euro account, converting only when the rate is favourable. Others use fintech services with interbank rates and low fees. In any case, knowing the exact impact of the exchange rate on your salary is crucial. Frequently Asked Questions
How does my salary change if I earn 5,000 CHF net per month in Switzerland?
If the exchange rate is 0.92, your salary in euros would be about 5,435 EUR, about 430 EUR more than five years ago at the same salary in francs.
How does the EUR/CHF exchange rate affect Italian taxes (IRPEF, additional) for a border crossing?
Italian taxes are calculated on the conversion of your salary into euros. An appreciation of the Swiss franc can increase your tax base in Italy, potentially raising taxes. For example, with a salary of 5,000 CHF and an exchange rate of 0.92, the taxable amount in euros increases, affecting the IRPEF and the additional ones.
What happens if the Swiss franc suddenly weakens for a frontier?
A sudden weakening of the Swiss franc can significantly reduce a frontier worker's real income. It is estimated that this drop can even reach 300-400 EUR per month, affecting purchasing power and personal financial planning.
What strategies do frontier workers adopt to manage EUR/CHF exchange rate volatility?
Many border workers adopt different strategies. Some keep separate accounts in francs and euros, converting only at favorable times. Others rely on fintech services that offer interbank exchange rates and reduced fees to optimize conversions.
How does the EUR/CHF exchange rate affect border crossers' life decisions?
The 'change factor' influences the life choices of border workers. A strong franc makes working in Switzerland more attractive, affecting the decision to move or stay in Italy. It can also affect the planning of important purchases, such as real estate or vehicles.
