Low wages and poverty risk in Ticino: PLR questions the State Council The PLR's inquiry aims to understand what investments in economic competitiveness can contribute to increasing wages and reducing social exclusion. The political party emphasizes the importance of developing strategies that favor innovation and attractiveness in the Canton of Ticino, to improve the economic conditions of workers and reduce the poverty risk. According to data from the Federal Statistical Office (FSO), the poverty rate in Ticino is 12.6%, higher than the national average of 9.2%. This phenomenon particularly affects population groups with low wages, such as workers in the services, construction, and agriculture sectors. Among the questions presented by the PLR are those related to the measures that the State Council intends to adopt to incentivize employment and improve the quality of life of residents. In particular, the political party asks for information on strategies to: - increase minimum wages in Ticino, currently set at 3,443 Swiss francs gross per month for adult workers; - improve access to vocational and continuing education, with particular attention to the most vulnerable categories; - incentivize the hiring of long-term unemployed workers, through measures such as the reduction of social contributions for companies that hire these workers. The State Council will have to provide detailed answers to these inquiries, providing a clear vision of future strategies to address the challenge of poverty risk in Ticino. ## Concrete examples In Locarno, for example, the municipality has implemented a series of measures to reduce poverty, such as the establishment of a support service for people in difficulty and the creation of a fund for affordable housing.
