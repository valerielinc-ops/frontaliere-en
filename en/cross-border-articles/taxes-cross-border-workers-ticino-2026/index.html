Frequently Asked Questions
What changes for 'old' cross-border workers in Ticino?
For 'old' cross-border workers in Ticino, there will be no changes in the tax regime. This means that withholding tax and refunds will remain unchanged. It's important to monitor any changes in Italian and Swiss regulations that could affect refunds and withholding tax.
How does withholding tax work for cross-border workers?
Withholding tax is a system where taxes are paid directly by the employer. This means that the cross-border worker does not need to worry about filing a tax return in Switzerland, greatly simplifying tax management. However, it's important to remember that cross-border workers must still declare their income in Italy, where they may benefit from certain deductions and benefits.
What are refunds and how can I obtain them?
Refunds are tax refunds that cross-border workers can obtain. These refunds allow you to recover part of the taxes paid, thus reducing the overall tax burden. To obtain refunds, you need to file a tax return in Italy and follow the procedures provided for by Italian law.
