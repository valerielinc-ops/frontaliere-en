Switzerland-Serbia 2026-29 cooperation program

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 29 April 2026

President Guy Parmelin presents a new cooperation program with Serbia, focusing on economy and innovation.

Contesto

In brief - Switzerland and Serbia launch 2026-29 cooperation program - Focus on economy and innovation - Bilateral agreement for innovation ## Key facts - What: New 2026-29 cooperation program - When: April 29, 2026 - Where: Serbia - Who: President Guy Parmelin and his Serbian counterpart Aleksandar Vučić - Amount: Trade exchanges worth approximately one billion francs On the occasion of the 110th anniversary of bilateral relations, Swiss Confederation President Guy Parmelin presented a new cooperation program for the period 2026-29 in Serbia. The program, focused on democratic good governance, economic development, strengthening basic vocational training, climate change, and sustainable urban development, supports Serbia in the European integration process. This is what emerges from a statement released Tuesday by the Swiss Federal Department of Economic Affairs, Education and Research (DEFR). During the visit, Parmelin met with Serbian President Aleksandar Vučić and Prime Minister Đuro Macut. The meeting also saw the signing of an agreement between the Swiss State Secretariat for Economic Affairs (SECO) and the Serbian Ministry of Science, Technology and Innovation (NITRA) for a bilateral innovation program. This agreement aims to accelerate market access for new products and services, strengthen collaboration between the private sector and research, and initiate regular dialogue on human rights. ### Implications for cross-border workers The new cooperation program could have positive implications for cross-border workers who work in Switzerland and reside in Serbia. In particular, the strengthening of basic vocational training and support for economic development could create new job opportunities and improve living conditions for cross-border workers.
