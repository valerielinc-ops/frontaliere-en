Frequently Asked Questions
What are the main economic exchanges between Switzerland and North Macedonia?
In 2023, economic trade between Switzerland and North Macedonia reached about 185 million francs. Swiss exports include machinery (35 million), pharmaceuticals (28 million) and watches (12 million). Imports from North Macedonia concern fruits and vegetables (42 million), metals (30 million) and textiles (25 million).
How many Swiss companies operate in North Macedonia and which sectors are most active?
About 100 Swiss companies operate in North Macedonia, an increase of 15% since 2020. Of these, 15 are Ticino. The main sectors of interest include engineering, energy and agriculture, with significant direct investments, for example CHF 50 million in the energy sector.
How does international cooperation between Switzerland and North Macedonia work?
Switzerland has an international cooperation programme with North Macedonia active until 2028, with a budget of CHF 45 million. This programme supports reforms in democratic governance, economic development, water management and environmental protection.
What are the tax and labour regulations for Swiss companies investing in North Macedonia?
Swiss companies must check local foreign investment laws (Foreign Investment Act, 2019). North Macedonia has a VAT rate of 18% and a corporate tax rate of 10%. It is also necessary to comply with the 2021 Labour Law and applicable environmental regulations.
How can the strengthening of Swiss-Macedonian economic relations affect border workers in Ticino?
Strengthening economic relations can create new job opportunities for border workers in Ticino, especially in the areas of democratic governance and economic development. Collaborations between Swiss and Macedonian companies are expected to increase, with a positive impact on cross-border employment.
