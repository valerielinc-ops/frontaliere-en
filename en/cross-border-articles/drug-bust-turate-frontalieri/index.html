Drug raid in the woods: three arrested in Turate (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 10 May 2026

Como police arrested three irregular Moroccans for drug dealing in the territory of Turate. Drugs and a machete were seized.

Contesto

In brief - Three Moroccans arrested for drug dealing in Turate - Narcotics and a machete seized - All three were irregulars and homeless ## Key facts - What: Arrests for drug trafficking - When: May 9, 2026 - Where: Turate area, Como - Who: Como State Police - Amount: 22 grams of cocaine, 15 grams of heroin, 19 grams of hashish, 370 euros in cash The Como State Police arrested three Moroccans, aged 29, 38, and 30, all irregulars and homeless, for drug trafficking. The raid took place last Friday in the Turate area, near Como, by the fourth section of the Mobile Squad of Como. The operation aimed to combat drug trafficking in wooded areas. Each of the three arrested had a well-defined role: the 29-year-old managed calls with clients and prepared the doses; the 30-year-old handled street deliveries, and the 38-year-old acted as a lookout. The trio also had a machete ready for use. Upon the arrival of the officers, the trio attempted to flee but were arrested, and the machete was seized. ### Operation details During a personal inspection, the 29-year-old Moroccan was found in possession of 22 grams of cocaine, 15 grams of heroin, 19 grams of hashish, approximately 370 euros in cash, believed to be proceeds from the illicit activity, as well as materials for weighing the substances and various phone lines. The 38-year-old had prior criminal and police records for theft, robbery, and threats. The other two were found to have no prior convictions. ### Implications for cross-border workers This type of police operation can significantly impact security and the perception of safety in border areas, especially for cross-border workers who commute daily between Italy and Switzerland.
