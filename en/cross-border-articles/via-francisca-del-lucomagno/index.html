The Via Francisca del Lucomagno is a hiking route that connects the Swiss border to Pavia, passing through various Italian and Swiss regions, including the Canton of Ticino. Recently, the first stage of the hike started from Lavena Ponte Tresa to celebrate the 10th anniversary of the itinerary. The route is open to everyone and will last seven days, from March 27 to April 2. Four pilgrims, accompanied by Neven Adzaip from Spiritual Tour, will cover the entire route, but the hike is also open to those who want to participate in single stages. The Via Francisca del Lucomagno route stretches for about 320 kilometers, passing through 18 Ticino municipalities, including Lugano, Mendrisio, and Bellinzona. The inaugural stage, which started from Lavena Ponte Tresa, saw the participation of over 50 people, who covered the first 20 kilometers of the hike. Dettagli operativi

The Via Francisca del Lucomagno trail offers a spectacular view of Lake Lugano and the surrounding mountains. Participants will have the opportunity to discover the history and culture of the region, as well as enjoy unspoiled nature. Guide Neven Adzaip will accompany pilgrims along the route, providing information on the history and geography of the area. The stages of the trail have been planned to offer a good combination of natural and cultural landscapes. The 35-kilometer route stretches from Lavena Ponte Tresa to Bodio, passing through Ticino municipalities such as Ponte Tresa, Caslano, and Magliaso. Along the way, participants can admire the beauty of Lake Lugano and its surroundings, with the possibility of visiting historical sites such as Caslano Castle and the Church of San Pietro in Ponte Tresa. Guide Neven Adzaip, with his many years of experience, will provide detailed information on the history and culture of the region, including anecdotes and curiosities about the places visited. For example, during the Caslano stage, participants can discover the history of the castle, which dates back to the 12th century, and admire its medieval architecture. The stages of the trail have been planned to offer a good combination of natural and cultural landscapes. Each stage has an average length of 10-12 kilometers and requires about 4-5 hours of walking. The route is considered to be of medium difficulty, with a difference in altitude of 500-700 meters. To participate in the trail, it is necessary to be in good physical health and have adequate preparation. It is recommended to wear comfortable shoes and bring water, snacks, and an umbrella. Frequently Asked Questions
What is the total length of the Via Francisca del Lucomagno and which regions does it cross?
The Via Francisca del Lucomagno stretches for about 320 kilometers, crossing Italian and Swiss regions, including the Canton of Ticino, to connect the Swiss border to Pavia.
What is the approximate cost of participating in a single stage of the Via Francisca del Lucomagno?
The cost varies from 50 to 100 Swiss francs per day, depending on the length of the stage and the services requested.
What are the public transportation options to get to the Via Francisca del Lucomagno starting point from Milan?
From Milan you can reach Lavena Ponte Tresa by train to Varese, then take the C23 bus line to Ponte Tresa (about 1 hour and 15 minutes total). Alternatively, there is a direct train from Milan Central Station to Lugano (1 hour), from which you can continue by bus or regional train to Ponte Tresa.
Is it possible to participate in the Via Francisca del Lucomagno with children or elderly people?
Yes, the course is open to everyone, but it is recommended to evaluate physical preparation. The first stages (20-30 km) are more accessible, while the later stages (40-50 km) require good condition. It is mandatory to follow the marked trails and respect your limits.
What customs regulations must be observed when crossing the Swiss-Italian border on the way?
There are no customs checks along the way, but you must have a valid ID (ID card or passport) with you. If you are carrying photographic equipment or electronic devices, declare them to customs if they exceed the value limits for tax exemption.
