Dettagli operativi

The new directives from DECS (Department of Education, Culture and Sports) represent a significant response to the growing concern over technology addiction among youth. The ban on mobile phone use applies to all compulsory schools in Ticino, also covering surrounding areas such as courtyards and school meeting places. This initiative, which came into effect on September 1, 2023, aims to improve the quality of education and the well-being of students. The penalties for violating the new regulations have not been specified, but DECS indicated that the approach will be primarily educational. Families of cross-border workers, especially those from municipalities like Mendrisio and Ponte Tresa, will need to adapt to these new rules, which could alter their daily routines. For instance, parents may need to plan how to communicate with their children during school hours, considering that mobile phones will not be usable. Moreover, DECS has planned training interventions for teachers and parents, with sessions scheduled from October 2023 to December 2024, focusing on the importance of proper education regarding the use of electronic devices. The training will include an operational checklist for parents and teachers, providing practical guidelines on how to manage the absence of devices in schools. Data collected from recent research indicate that 60% of compulsory school students reported feeling distracted during lessons due to mobile phone use. With the expansion of the ban, an improvement in classroom concentration is expected, with a projected 30% reduction in conflicts among students related to mobile phone use, as highlighted by industry experts. Frequently Asked Questions
When does the ban on mobile phones in Ticino schools come into force?
The ban on the use of mobile phones and mobile devices in Ticino's compulsory schools comes into force on 30 March 2026. The measure involves all school areas, including courtyards and outdoor spaces.
What are the reasons for the ban on mobile phones in schools?
The DECS banned mobile phones to reduce technological dependence and improve concentration. A 2022 study finds that 62% of middle school students use their cell phones during class, causing distractions and conflict.
How will violations of the mobile phone ban be handled?
The penalties have not been specified, but the DECS will adopt an educational approach. Schools will receive an operational checklist for alternative activities, teacher training and technological risk awareness.
Will the ban on mobile phones affect Ticino cross-border commuters?
Yes, cross-border commuters with children in Mendrisio or Ponte Tresa will have to adapt to the regulations. Mobile phones will not be usable during school hours, requiring new communication strategies from parents.
Which European regions have measures similar to Ticino?
Ticino aligns itself with regions such as France, Spain and Swiss Vaud, which have already banned mobile phones in schools. These initiatives aim to promote social interactions and improve academic productivity.
