Frequently Asked Questions
What are the main causes of young people fleeing Ticino?
The main causes of the flight of young people from Ticino are the more competitive job opportunities, the higher level of training and the more attractive living conditions compared to those offered in the Canton. These factors push young people to move mainly to Lombardy and the neighboring Italian regions.
What are the tax incentives offered by the Municipality of Lugano for young people?
The Municipality of Lugano offers a reduced cantonal income tax rate of 50% for young people between 20 and 35 years old who return or move to the territory, until 2026, with the possibility of extension.
How do I apply for the reduced tax rate for young people in Lugano?
To apply for the reduced rate of 50% in Lugano, the young person must access the cantonal portal, fill out the 'Request for tax relief' form by 31 December 2026, attach an employment contract or internship certificate carried out outside Ticino and a copy of the diploma. The application is evaluated within 30 days; If approved, the reduction applies to taxable income for the next three tax years.
What is the budget and internship opportunities for engineering graduates in Bellinzona?
The paid internship project launched by Bellinzona in 2024 has a budget of CHF 500,000. It is intended for engineering and technology graduates and provides for six-month contracts with a monthly salary equal to 80% of the average cantonal salary. The goal is to place at least 60% of the participants in local companies within six months of the end of the internship.
What tax incentives does a Ticino company receive when it hires an employee under 30?
Ticino companies that hire employees under 30 benefit from a tax credit of up to 50,000 francs per year, according to the 2022 cantonal law. The government has allocated a total of 10 million francs for this program, with an automatic refund within 90 days of presenting the payslip. The credit can be combined with deductions for young workers.
