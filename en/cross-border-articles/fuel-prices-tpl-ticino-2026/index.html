Fuel prices threaten public transport in Ticino

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 27 April 2026

High fuel prices are threatening local public transport, with additional costs estimated at 0.54 million euros per day for diesel.

Contesto

In brief - Rising fuel costs threaten local public transport - Additional costs estimated at 0.54 million euros per day for diesel - Risk of service cuts or stops ## Key facts - What: Rising fuel costs - When: April 27, 2026 - Where: Italy - Who: Agens, Municipalities - Amount: 0.54 million euros per day for diesel The rising fuel costs risk grounding local public transport. Agens, the Association representing the interests of the transport and services sector within the Confindustria system, has noted that the current crisis has generated significant economic burdens for public transport. The estimated additional daily costs are 0.54 million euros for diesel alone. The prospective annual extra cost is over 200 million euros, and the overall extra cost for the sector is over 400 million euros, including commercial transport operated with the remaining 47% of the national fleet. This scenario is causing distress for companies in the sector, which risk having to cut services or worse, stop operations. For this reason, they are asking the Government to extend the tax credit to vehicles used for passenger transport in order to compensate for the extra energy costs. ### Implications for Ticino The rising fuel costs could have a significant impact on public transport in the Canton of Ticino, where many cross-border workers depend on public transport to travel between Italy and Switzerland. Local transport companies may have to face increased operating costs, with possible repercussions on the services offered. The call from Agens is joined by municipalities from north to south. Frequently Asked Questions
What are the main concerns of local public transport companies?
The main concerns of local public transport companies are the increases in fuel costs, which could lead to service cuts or ticket price hikes. This could significantly impact cross-border workers who rely on public transport to travel between Italy and Switzerland.
What can cross-border workers do to cope with high fuel prices?
Cross-border workers can monitor any changes in services offered by local transport companies and consider alternative transport options, such as using private vehicles or carpooling. Additionally, they can use tools like the transport cost calculator and ticket comparator available on the Frontaliere Ticino website to find the most convenient solution for their needs.
What are the possible solutions to cope with high fuel prices?
Possible solutions to cope with high fuel prices include government and regional intervention with support funds for local transport companies. Furthermore, local transport companies could further diversify their vehicle fleet by introducing methane, hybrid, and electric vehicles to reduce the impact of high fuel prices on operating costs.
