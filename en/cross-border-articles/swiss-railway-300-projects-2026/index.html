Railway: progress in infrastructure expansion

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 30 April 2026

300 projects underway to expand the Swiss railway network by 2035, with work ongoing in Lausanne and Fribourg.

Contesto

In brief - 300 projects underway for railway expansion - Work in progress in Lausanne and Fribourg - Priorities redefined for financial reasons ## Key facts - What: Expansion of the Swiss railway network - When: Projects underway for the 2025 and 2035 phases - Where: Switzerland, with a focus on Lausanne, Fribourg, Basel, and Eastern Switzerland - Who: Federal Office of Transport (FOT) and SBB - Amount: Not yet specified Last year, Swiss companies achieved important milestones in the expansion of the railway network. This is what emerges from the latest report on the state of the works published today by the Federal Office of Transport (FOT). Currently, there are around 300 projects in the design or implementation phase as part of the 2025 and 2035 expansion phases and the future infrastructure development program. In a statement, the FOT lists a series of construction sites. The largest project in the expansion program concerns the Lausanne station, where SBB started work in 2025. At Fribourg station, the platforms have been completed. SBB has put into service on schedule the first major infrastructure measure of the 2035 expansion phase, which allows for an intensified offer in the Laufental (BL). ### Impact on cross-border commuters The expansion of the Swiss railway network will have a significant impact on cross-border commuters who cross the Italian-Swiss border daily to go to work. With the intensification of railway services, travel times between major Swiss cities and Italian border areas could be reduced, improving the quality of life of cross-border workers. Dettagli operativi

Practical analysis: implications for cross-border workers The expansion of the Swiss railway network will have a significant impact on cross-border workers who daily cross the Italian-Swiss border to go to work. With the intensification of rail services, travel times between major Swiss cities and Italian border areas could be reduced, improving the quality of life for cross-border workers. ### Comparison with the previous situation Before the start of these projects, cross-border workers often had to rely on private transport or less frequent rail services, with longer and less predictable travel times. The expansion of the railway network and the intensification of services represent a tangible improvement over the previous situation, offering cross-border workers more efficient and sustainable travel options. ### What to do to take advantage of the new services To take advantage of the new rail services, cross-border workers can start planning their trips taking into account the new frequencies and updated schedules. It is advisable to regularly consult the websites of the FFS and local operators to stay updated on any changes or improvements in services. In addition, cross-border workers can consider purchasing rail passes that offer discounted rates for frequent trips. These passes can represent a convenient investment for those who regularly use the train to commute to work, reducing transportation costs in the long run. ### Next steps The Federal Council intends to gradually develop the railway network and, for financial reasons, the priorities of the projects must be redefined. What are the main projects currently underway for the expansion of the Swiss railway network?
