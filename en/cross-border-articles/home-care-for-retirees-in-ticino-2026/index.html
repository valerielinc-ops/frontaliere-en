Luca Frasa, Municipal Councilor of Quinto, emphasizes how these care services are essential to allow elderly people to stay in their own homes, reducing hospitalizations and easing the healthcare system. However, the current system could penalize those who have saved a lifetime, forcing them to progressively consume their own assets to cover needs that are not a choice but a necessity. ### The paradox of home care Home care is not a luxury but an essential service. It allows elderly or frail people to stay in their own home, in their own environment, close to their loved ones. As also recalled in the cantonal political debate, they reduce hospitalizations and ease the healthcare system as a whole. However, the issue cannot be treated solely as a financial matter. It is true that the financial sustainability of the healthcare system is a real problem, but the central point is how the cost-sharing is applied. One thing is to ask for a reasonable contribution, another is to create a system that risks hitting those who have always done their duty: the middle class, retirees who are not poor enough to receive full aid, but not wealthy enough to easily afford recurring expenses for assistance. Dettagli operativi

Practical implications for retirees Retirees in Ticino who need home care are facing a complex situation. On one hand, home care is essential to maintain their dignity and quality of life. On the other hand, the costs of these services can be prohibitive, especially for those who do not have access to full subsidies. This creates a paradox: those who have saved their entire lives find themselves having to dip into their savings to cover expenses that are not a choice but a necessity. ### The political debate The cantonal political debate has highlighted the need to find a balance between financial sustainability and equity. It is important that institutions take responsibility for managing public resources well, but it is equally crucial to avoid measures that penalize those who have contributed to the community. Home care must be accessible to everyone, regardless of their economic situation. ### Concrete scenarios Imagine a retiree who has worked for decades and saved prudently. Upon retiring, they discover they need home care. If the cost-sharing system is not fair, they might find themselves gradually depleting their savings, putting their own future and that of their family at risk. This scenario is unacceptable and must be avoided. ### Comparisons with the previous situation Before the current cost-sharing system, retirees could access home care without the risk of seeing their savings eroded. However, with rising costs and the need for financial sustainability, it is necessary to find a balance that ensures access to care without penalizing those who have worked and saved their entire lives. ## Recommended tools For an updated estimate, use the net salary calculator and the CHF-EUR exchange comparator.
