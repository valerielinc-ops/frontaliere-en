Airpack Lombardia: 41 jobs at risk of dismissal

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 28 April 2026

Belgian owner Abriso insists on closing the Ossago Lodigiano plant, despite purchase offers.

Contesto

In brief - 41 workers at risk of dismissal - Belgian owner Abriso insists on closure - Decisive meeting on April 30 with the CEO ## Key facts - What: Closure of the Airpack plant - When: Final deadline May 3, 2026 - Where: Ossago Lodigiano, Lombardy - Who: Belgian owner Abriso, unions Filctem Cgil and Femca Cisl - Amount: 41 workers involved New stalemate in the Airpack dispute in Ossago Lodigiano. Despite the interest of new buyers, the Belgian owner Abriso does not yield on the closure: the final deadline for the 41 workers is May 3. The last meeting held yesterday at the Pirellone between the unions (Filctem Cgil and Femca Cisl) and the representatives of the multinational Abriso ended with a disheartening nothing done. The owner reiterated the intention to proceed with the closure of the plant, showing extreme rigidity: to date, the company refuses to resort to extraordinary wage guarantee fund, a tool that would allow freezing layoffs and evaluating the two expressions of interest already received from other entrepreneurs. ### The unions' position "The company has shown itself closed to all our proposals – explains Morwenna Di Benedetto (Cgil) – It does not evaluate the wage guarantee fund nor the time necessary to analyze the purchase offers for part of the production." Moreover, despite the unions' requests, at the table in Lombardy Region once again the CEO of the company did not show up, who sent only the plant director and their lawyer, fundamentally without real negotiating power. ### The unions' requests and the uncertainty of May 3 The climate is one of extreme uncertainty. Implications for Workers The current situation is critical for the 41 workers involved. Without the extraordinary wage guarantee scheme, employees find themselves in a position of extreme vulnerability. The closure of the plant would not only mean the loss of their jobs but also have a significant impact on their families and the local community. ### Comparison with the Previous Situation Before this crisis, the Ossago Lodigiano plant was a cornerstone of the local economy. The company's production significantly contributed to the area's economy, offering stable and well-paid jobs. The closure of the plant would be a hard blow to the community, with possible repercussions on the economic and social fabric. ### Possible Scenarios If the Belgian owner Abriso insists on closure, the workers would face a period of unemployment. The Wage Compensation Scheme (CIG) would be a fundamental tool to mitigate the economic impact, allowing workers to have an income during the transition to new job opportunities. However, without the CIG, workers could face immediate economic difficulties. ### The Importance of the CEO's Presence The presence of the CEO at the negotiation table is considered crucial by the unions. His participation could represent the last opportunity to find an agreement that avoids the closure of the plant. The unions hope that the CEO can bring new perspectives and concrete solutions to save the jobs. ### Comparisons with Similar Situations In other similar situations, the extraordinary wage guarantee scheme has helped avoid mass layoffs and find alternative solutions for production continuity.
