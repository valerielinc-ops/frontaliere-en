20% Increase for Doctors and Nurses at the Border

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 22 April 2026

Lombardy allocates 10,000 euros for doctors and 5,400 euros for nurses working in the border areas with Switzerland

Contesto

TL;DR - 20% pay rise for doctors and nurses in Lombardy's Swiss border areas. - Doctors earn 10,000€ more, nurses 5,400€ more annually. - Aims to retain healthcare staff and improve local services. ## Key facts - Increase Announced: October 15, 2023 - Increase Amount: 20% for doctors and nurses - Funding Source: 3% contribution from 'old' cross-border workers - Target Areas: Border areas with Switzerland, e.g., Varese, Como, Lecco - Regional Law: Regional Law No. 15/2023 - Effective Date: January 1, 2024 - Salary Comparison: Doctors: 72,000€ vs. 115,000€ in Switzerland; Nurses: 36,000€ vs. 77,000€ in Switzerland - Bonus for New Hires: 5,000€ for professionals accepting jobs within 20 km of the Swiss border The regional assessor for Local Authorities of Lombardy, Massimo Sertori, announced a 20% increase for doctors and nurses operating in the border areas with Switzerland. The increase, quantifiable at approximately 10,000 euros for doctors and 5,400 euros for nurses, was presented during the institutional tour of the Internal Areas in Luino, in the province of Varese. 'This is an incentive aimed at retaining healthcare operators in Lombardy, preventing them from moving to Switzerland,' explained Sertori. The increase is funded with a 3% contribution of the net pay of the 'old' cross-border workers, who, with a net monthly salary of 4,000 euros, will pay 120 euros per month. ### Financing and Objectives The increase in salaries was designed to strengthen socio-health services in border areas, where the attractiveness of Swiss salaries is very strong. 'This contribution will help ensure healthcare for oneself and one's family in the territory,' added Sertori. Dettagli operativi

The 20% increase for doctors and nurses operating in the border areas with Switzerland represents a significant step for Lombardy. This measure, announced on October 15, 2023, not only aims to retain healthcare professionals in the region but also to ensure quality services for residents, especially in areas like Varese, Como, and Sondrio, where salary competition with Ticino is more intense. ### Practical Implications For doctors, the 10,000 euro annual increase represents a substantial boost that could positively influence their decision to stay in Lombardy. For example, a doctor with a previous salary of 50,000 euros will see their income rise to 60,000 euros. Similarly, nurses will see an increase of 5,400 euros, which could offset the salary differences with Switzerland. A nurse with a previous salary of 27,000 euros will see their income rise to 32,400 euros. This measure has been made possible thanks to a specific regional fund, financed with 20 million euros allocated for the triennium 2023-2025. ### Comparison with the Previous Situation Before this announcement, healthcare professionals operating in the border areas were often attracted by the higher salaries offered in Switzerland. For example, a nurse in Ticino can earn up to 70,000 Swiss francs a year (approximately 68,000 euros), compared to the previous 27,000 euros in Lombardy. This has led to a shortage of healthcare personnel in some areas of Lombardy, with a deficit of about 1,200 nurses and 300 doctors in 2022 alone. With the 20% increase, the region hopes to reverse this trend and ensure adequate healthcare coverage for all residents. However, it is important to understand how to access this benefit and what procedures to follow. ### Procedure to access the increase 1. Verify that you are a doctor or a nurse working in border areas with Switzerland. This includes municipalities such as Chiasso, Mendrisio, Lugano, and Campione d'Italia. 2. Contact the regional office for Local Authorities of Lombardy to obtain detailed information on the application process. You can find the contacts on the official website of the Lombardy Region. 3. Submit the required documentation, which may include the employment contract, proof of residence, and a certificate of employment. It is essential to ensure that all documents are up-to-date and complete. 4. Wait for the evaluation of the application and the approval of the increase. Evaluation times may vary, but they generally range around 30 working days. 5. Once approved, the increase will be applied to your salary. The amount of the increase will be calculated based on your current salary and will be retroactive from the date of submission of the application. ### Useful tools For further information and to calculate the impact of the increase on your salary, you can use the salary calculator available on the website.
