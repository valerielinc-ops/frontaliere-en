Just days before the municipal elections on March 8, 2026, significant participation has already been recorded: 20% of eligible voters have cast their ballots in Lugano and Locarno, 18% in Mendrisio, and 14% in Bellinzona. These figures are consistent with previous elections, such as the one on health insurance funds, which saw over 51% voter turnout. According to Luca Tanner, head of the Bellinzona office of the chancellery, mail-in voting has become predominant, accounting for 90-95% of total votes. With the merging of municipalities, the number of polling stations has been gradually reduced: from 13 to 9, and starting in 2026, there will only be 4. This shift is also reflected in the increasing convenience provided by mailboxes distributed across various neighborhoods, making it easier for voters to submit their completed ballots.

Dettagli operativi

Impacts and Changes for Cross-Border Workers The widespread adoption of voting by mail is not just a local phenomenon but also an indication of how civic participation methods are adapting to modern times. For cross-border workers, this method represents an opportunity: the ability to vote without needing to return to Italy during workdays, thus reducing logistical challenges and potential income loss. According to current regulations, cross-border workers who maintain residency in Italy and retain voting rights in their municipality of origin can take advantage of mail-in voting to participate in Italian elections. However, it is essential to check with your municipality to obtain the electoral ballots in a timely manner and to follow the instructions for correctly completing the documents. Frequently Asked Questions
Can I vote by mail in the Italian elections if I am a cross-border commuter with residence in Italy and work in Switzerland?
Yes, you can use postal voting for Italian elections while maintaining your residency in Italy. Inquire at the municipality of origin to obtain the cards and follow the instructions for correct validation.
How do you verify the identity of voters for postal voting in Italy for cross-border commuters?
Identity verification is done by handwriting on the voting form and including a copy of the valid identity card or passport in the electoral envelope, according to the indications of the municipality of residence in Italy.
What are the deadlines for sending the ballot paper by mail in the Italian elections if I live in Ticino and work in Switzerland?
The deadlines vary by Italian municipality, but generally the ballot must be sent at least 5-7 days before the election date. Check the exact date on your municipality's website or contact them directly to avoid postal delays.
Can I also use postal voting for the Ticino elections if I am resident in Italy but work in Switzerland?
No, postal voting in Ticino is reserved for Swiss citizens residing in the canton. If you are resident in Italy, you can only vote for Italian elections by mail, not for Ticino elections.
What happens if I don't receive my postal ballot paper in good time before the elections?
In case of non-receipt, you can request a duplicate from your Italian municipality of residence. However, it is essential to act promptly: contact the electoral office at least 10 days before the deadline to avoid losing your right to vote.
