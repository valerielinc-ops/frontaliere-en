Controversy over Swiss flag on On shoes

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 1 May 2026

The Federal Institute of Intellectual Property has granted On the right to use the Swiss flag on its shoes, despite production taking place abroad. The decision has sparked controversy.

The Federal Institute of Intellectual Property (IPI) has recently granted Swiss company On permission to use the Swiss flag on its shoes, despite the fact that production is entirely abroad. This decision has sparked lively controversy in the Swiss business world. The company, known for its running shoes, has received permission to apply the red cross to its products, even though they are manufactured in Asian countries. The IPI justified the decision by stating that this measure is necessary to maintain development and research activities in Switzerland. However, competitors fear that this decision could undermine the credibility of Swiss brands. Urs Furrer, director of the Swiss Trade and Craft Federation, expressed strong irritation at this decision, calling it incomprehensible. ### Rules on trademark protection Previously, the rules on trademark protection stipulated that to use 'Swiss made', 60% of production costs had to be generated in Switzerland, including an essential step in manufacturing. Frequently Asked Questions
What is the reason for the IPI granting On permission to use the Swiss flag?
The Swiss Federal Institute of Intellectual Property (IPI) justified the decision stating that this measure is necessary to maintain development and research activities in Switzerland. According to the IPI, easing the rules strengthens innovation and the competitiveness of Swiss companies in the global market.
What are the concerns of competitors?
Competitors fear that this decision could undermine the credibility of Swiss brands. In particular, Roberto Martullo, owner of Küzli SwissSchuh, believes that the cross emblem should remain a reward for those who export after producing in Switzerland, where costs and wages are higher.
