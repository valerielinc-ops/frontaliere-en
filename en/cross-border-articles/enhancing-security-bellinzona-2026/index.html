Safety in Bellinzona: motion to improve quality of life (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 8 May 2026

Karim Spinelli (PLR) presents motion to strengthen security and institutional presence in sensitive areas of Bellinzona

Contesto

In brief - Motion to strengthen security in Bellinzona - Institutional presence in sensitive areas - Pilot projects to improve livability ## Key facts - What: Motion to improve security and quality of life - When: 8 May 2026 - Where: Bellinzona, Ticino - Who: Karim Spinelli (Plr), Municipal Council - Amount: Not specified The motion presented by Karim Spinelli, on behalf of the Plr group in the Municipal Council of Bellinzona, aims to strengthen security and quality of life in the city. The proposal focuses on a balance between institutional presence, care of the territory, and security, benefiting all neighborhoods. In particular, there is a perceived less regular presence of proximity services in various neighborhoods, while in central areas such as Bellinzona Station and viale Stazione, specific situations of discomfort and a decrease in the livability of public spaces are reported. ### Proposed measures The motion invites the executive to evaluate and adopt measures in the most sensitive areas, particularly in the Station area and along viale Stazione. These measures should be aimed at an adequate strengthening of territorial presence and the quality of interventions compared to the current situation. Furthermore, it is proposed to involve social operators or mediators if appropriate. In addition to these measures, the Municipality is invited to evaluate one or more pilot projects in the same areas that integrate in a coordinated manner institutional presence, interventions in public spaces, and social support measures. ### Involvement of local realities Another crucial aspect of the motion is the maintenance and strengthening of constant dialogue between municipal authorities and local realities. Implications for cross-border workers The motion presented by Karim Spinelli could have a significant impact on the quality of life for cross-border workers in Bellinzona. Improving the safety and livability of public spaces can make the city more attractive to transnational workers, who often seek a safe and well-organized environment to live and work in. Furthermore, strengthening the dialogue between municipal authorities and local entities could facilitate the integration of cross-border workers into the community, promoting a sense of belonging and engagement. ### Comparison with the previous situation Before the presentation of this motion, the perception of insecurity in some areas of Bellinzona could have been an obstacle for cross-border workers considering the city as a place of residence. With the adoption of the proposed measures, there could be an improvement in the perception of safety, making Bellinzona a more attractive choice for transnational workers. Additionally, the introduction of pilot projects could offer new opportunities for participation and engagement for citizens, including cross-border workers. ### Future scenarios If the motion is accepted, a series of initiatives aimed at improving the safety and livability of public spaces could be implemented. For example, mediation and social support services could be introduced to address specific situations of discomfort. Participating in periodic consultations and neighborhood commission meetings can offer the opportunity to express their needs and concerns, actively contributing to improving the quality of life in the city. Additionally, cross-border workers can report situations of discomfort or suggest improvements through the communication channels made available by the Municipality. ### Step-by-step procedure 1.
