Programming and Themes of the Festival Under the leadership of Filippo Armati, the 2026 edition of Afro-Pfingsten is shaping up to be one of the most ambitious. The musical lineup will feature a dialogue between icons of African music and new talents from the contemporary urban scene, with a particular focus on cross-over projects. Confirmed artists include Fela Kuti and Youssou N'Dour, legends of African music, alongside emerging groups like Burna Boy and Wizkid. Alongside concerts, the program expands with high-level workshops, including a music production course led by Don Letts, producer of Fela Kuti, and an urban graphic session guided by Banksy, who has previously collaborated with Afro-Pfingsten. A dedicated section for visual arts will be designed to interact directly with the city's urban spaces, featuring exhibitions in historic galleries like the Kunsthalle in Winterthur and public installations in areas such as the Peace Quarter. 📊 Key figures: - Afro-Pfingsten has seen a 25% increase in attendees over the past three years, reaching 15,000 visitors in 2025. - The budget for the 2026 edition is estimated at 2.5 million CHF, with public and private funding. Social sustainability remains a central theme, a cornerstone of the festival. The program will include debates and meetings with activists and intellectuals, aiming to delve into issues of equity and cooperation. Confirmed guests include Chimamanda Ngozi Adichie, Nigerian writer, and Wangari Maathai, Kenyan Nobel Peace Prize winner.
