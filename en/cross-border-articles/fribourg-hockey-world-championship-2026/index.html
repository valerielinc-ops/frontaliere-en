In brief - Fribourg to host the 2026 hockey World Championship at BCF Arena - 80,000 people for Gottéron's title celebration - 200,000-250,000 expected attendees during the tournament ## Key facts - What: 2026 hockey World Championship - When: 2026 - Where: BCF Arena, Fribourg - Who: Gottéron, John Gobbi (CEO) - Amount: 200,000-250,000 expected attendees Fribourg is preparing for an unforgettable year. After the historic title of Gottéron, the city will host the 2026 hockey World Championship at the BCF Arena. The privately-owned venue will host Group B of the world championship, with teams like Canada, Sweden, Czechia, and Slovakia. John Gobbi, CEO of Gottéron, described an unprecedented enthusiasm: '80,000 people gathered in the city for the title celebration, with over 30,000 in front of the rink.' ### Economic and sporting impact The new arena played a crucial role in the club's success. 'None of this would have been possible without the new rink, neither in terms of atmosphere nor economically,' said Gobbi. The structure allowed reinvestment in the company, improving the first team, youth sector, and women's movement. Ticket sales are expected to bring between 200,000 and 250,000 attendees to the canton during the tournament, a significant economic boost for the region. ### The Swiss National Team Gobbi also commented on the situation of the Swiss National Team, which approached the World Championship in a problematic way. 'I don't think it will leave any traces,' he said, emphasizing the solidity of the group and the very high level of the Swiss championship.
