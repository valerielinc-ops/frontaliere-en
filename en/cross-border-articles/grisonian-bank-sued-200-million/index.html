Di Valerie Linc · Published on 4 May 2026

A multi-million lawsuit has been filed against the Grisons Cantonal Bank and its affiliate BZ Bank for alleged breach of contractual obligations.

According to available information, the case is related to a relationship with a client and an investment product managed by BZ Bank. The plaintiff accuses the banks of failing to fulfill their obligations, while the latter state that they consider the accusations unfounded and reject any request. The complaint was filed as a conciliation request with the mediation office of the Höfe district (canton of Schwyz). The former owner of BZ Bank, Martin Ebner, sold the institution to the Grisons Cantonal Bank in 2022, which became the sole owner at the beginning of 2025. Ebner had created the bank in 1985, making it the focus of his investment activities. In particular, he specialized in advising private, professional, and institutional clients in the sectors of stock investments and participations. ### Implications for cross-border workers This complaint could have significant repercussions for cross-border workers who use the services of the Grisons Cantonal Bank and BZ Bank.
