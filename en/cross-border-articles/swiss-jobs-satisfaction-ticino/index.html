Most satisfying jobs in Switzerland: Ticino data (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 5 May 2026

A study reveals that the most satisfying jobs in Switzerland are not the highest-paying or least stressful. Here are the details for Ticino cross-border workers.

Contesto

In brief - Healthcare at the top of satisfaction - Construction artisans very satisfied - Salary is not the main factor ## Key facts - What: Study on job satisfaction in Switzerland - When: May 2026 - Where: Switzerland, with a focus on Ticino - Who: Research Institute Sotomo - Amount: 4,300 survey participants It's not just about the money. This is what emerges from a study on the satisfaction of Swiss people regarding their profession. At the top of the survey conducted by the Sotomo Research Institute are healthcare professions, with 3.9 points out of 5. In craftsmanship, construction, and agriculture, the level of satisfaction (3.7 points) is also particularly high. Following are the consulting sector (3.5) and education/training (3.5). At the opposite extreme are jobs in administration, organization, and accounting (2.1). Low satisfaction also for those working in factory production (2.2) and in the sales field (2.5). ### Healthcare professions in the spotlight More in detail, from the profile of pride in one's work, healthcare profiles reach the highest values. And also in the perception of job security, the medical and care sector is at the top, followed by social professions, construction, and public administration. ### Salary is not everything In the survey, the 4,300 participants indicated that the most important aspect of all is the balance between work and private life, followed by varied and stimulating tasks and the possibility of doing something positive. Salary comes only in fourth place. ### The situation in Ticino For cross-border workers working in Ticino, these data are particularly relevant. Dettagli operativi

Analysis of the implications for cross-border workers ### Sectors with greater satisfaction The Sotomo study shows that the sectors with the highest satisfaction are healthcare, craftsmanship, construction, and agriculture. These sectors not only offer greater job satisfaction but also a higher perception of job security. For Ticino cross-border workers, working in these sectors could mean greater stability and personal satisfaction. ### Work-life balance One of the most important factors for job satisfaction is the balance between work and private life. Thanks to its proximity to Italy, Ticino offers cross-border workers the possibility to return home every day, reducing the stress related to long commutes. This factor is particularly relevant for those working in high-stress sectors, such as healthcare. ### Training opportunities For those who wish to change sectors or improve their skills, Ticino offers numerous training opportunities. The Confederation and the Cantons are investing around one billion francs to train new professionals, including support for salaries during the training period. This could be an interesting opportunity for cross-border workers who wish to enter sectors with a high level of satisfaction. ### Comparison with other Swiss regions Compared to other Swiss regions, Ticino offers a unique context for cross-border workers. The proximity to Italy and the possibility to work in sectors with a high level of satisfaction make the region an attractive option for those seeking a balance between work and private life.
