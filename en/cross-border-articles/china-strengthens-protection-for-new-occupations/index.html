China strengthens protection for new job workers

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 27 April 2026

New guidelines to protect digital platform workers, focusing on compensation and algorithms.

Contesto

In brief - China publishes guidelines to protect workers in new occupations. - Fair compensation and timely payments for platform workers. - Strengthening algorithm governance and transparency. ## Key facts - What: Guidelines for protecting workers in new occupations. - When: Published on April 26, 2026. - Where: China. - Who: General offices of the Central Committee of the Communist Party of China and the State Council. - Amount: 84 million workers employed in new forms of work. China has intensified efforts to protect the rights and interests of workers employed in new forms of occupation. The guidelines, published by the general offices of the Central Committee of the Communist Party of China and the State Council, urge Internet platforms and express delivery companies to set reasonable compensation for workers, taking into account the tasks performed and the intensity of the work. Additionally, the guidelines urge ensuring full and timely payments. ### Algorithm governance The guidelines emphasize the need to strengthen algorithm governance on Internet platforms. Interested companies are invited to fulfill their social responsibilities, enhance algorithm regulation, and improve transparency. Workers must have the right to be informed, participate, and decide on rules related to algorithms. ### Supervision and prevention The guidelines call for the introduction of comprehensive measures to curb excessive competition and strictly prevent violations of the legitimate rights and interests of workers. Furthermore, the need to improve information communication to operators and guide workers towards reasonable expectations regarding income and benefits is emphasized. Dettagli operativi

The new guidelines published by China represent a significant step towards protecting workers employed in new forms of employment. These occupations, which include delivery riders, ride-hailing drivers, and online marketing operators, have emerged with the development of the Internet and the digital economy. Currently, 84 million people in China are employed in these forms of work, playing a crucial role in ensuring essential services and livelihoods for the population. ### Implications for Workers The new guidelines aim to ensure that digital platform workers receive reasonable compensation and timely payments. This is an important step to improve working conditions and ensure that workers are adequately remunerated for their efforts. Additionally, the guidelines urge companies to strengthen algorithm governance, improving transparency and ensuring that workers have the right to be informed and to participate in decisions related to algorithms. ### Comparison with the Previous Situation Before the introduction of these guidelines, workers in new occupations in China often faced precarious working conditions and lack of transparency in payments. The new measures aim to bridge these gaps, offering greater protection and security to workers. This represents a significant improvement over the previous situation, where workers were often left without adequate protection. ### Future Scenarios With the introduction of these guidelines, it is expected that working conditions for workers in new occupations will significantly improve. Companies will be required to define reasonable compensation and ensure timely payments, thus improving the quality of life for workers.
