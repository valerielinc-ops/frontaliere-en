Domestic violence roadmap: Jans reports a positive balance

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 27 April 2026

The roadmap against domestic and sexual violence has proven effective, but further measures are needed to combat femicides.

Contesto

In brief - Effective domestic violence roadmap after 5 years - Further measures needed against femicides - Number 142 operational from 1st May ## Key facts - What: Roadmap against domestic and sexual violence - When: Launched 5 years ago, progress report on 27 April 2026 - Where: Switzerland - Who: Confederation and Cantons - Amount: 22,066 offences in 2025 (+4.4% compared to 2024) Launched five years ago, the roadmap against domestic and sexual violence has proven its effectiveness. This was stated by Federal Councillor Beat Jans, who presented a progress report. However, he emphasized the need for further tools to address the phenomenon of femicides. According to the Federal Department of Justice and Police (DFJP), the implementation of various measures at federal and cantonal levels has led to significant progress. ### Progress and tools Among the progress made, the Basel representative highlighted the importance of electronic monitoring, which has significantly improved the protection of victims. Cantonal pilot projects have demonstrated the effectiveness of this tool, which can be adapted according to the situation. Furthermore, the Cantons have defined uniform quality standards for managing threats, which have been positively received by all parties involved. These standards help to identify, assess, and contain the risks of violence promptly. The Federal Council has proposed amendments to the law on assistance to victims of crime, facilitating access to specialized care and providing for the free provision of medico-legal documentation, which can be used as evidence. Dettagli operativi

Implications for cross-border workers The roadmap against domestic and sexual violence has a significant impact on cross-border workers living and working in Ticino. Electronic monitoring and uniform quality standards for managing threats offer greater protection for victims, regardless of their nationality or residence status. ### Concrete procedures To access assistance and protection services, cross-border workers can contact the 142 number, operational 24/7. This national service provides immediate support and information on how to proceed in case of domestic violence. Additionally, the Cantons are expanding the offer of emergency accommodation, ensuring a safe refuge for victims. ### Practical scenarios Imagine a cross-border worker residing in Ticino and working in Italy. In case of domestic violence, they can turn to the 142 number to obtain immediate assistance. The service will provide information on how to access specialized care and free medico-legal documentation, usable as evidence in case of a report. Furthermore, the cross-border worker can be directed towards emergency accommodation if necessary. ### Comparison with the previous situation Before the implementation of the roadmap, victims of domestic violence in Ticino had to face more complex and less coordinated procedures. The lack of uniform standards and dedicated assistance services made it more difficult to obtain protection and support. With the new measures, victims can rely on a more efficient and accessible system, regardless of their nationality. ## Recommended tools For an updated estimate, use the net salary calculator and the CHF-EUR exchange comparator.

Frequently Asked Questions
What are the main advancements in the roadmap against domestic violence?
Among the main advancements are the introduction of electronic monitoring, which has improved the protection of victims, and the definition of uniform quality standards for threat management. Additionally, the Federal Council has proposed amendments to the law on assistance to victims of crime, facilitating access to specialized care and providing for the free provision of medico-legal documentation.
What does the number 142 provide?
The number 142 is a national service for victims of domestic violence that will offer 24/7 assistance. This service provides immediate support and information on how to proceed in case of domestic violence, including access to specialized care and emergency housing.
What are the new measures planned to combat femicides?
Among the new measures planned are the introduction of a national law on protection against violence, which will standardize the regulations in this area, and a package of measures to improve protection from domestic violence in civil and civil procedural law. Additionally, a revision of the weapons law and the extension of the 'Equality Prevents Violence' campaign are planned.
