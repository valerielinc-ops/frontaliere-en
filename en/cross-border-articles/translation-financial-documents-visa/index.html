Identify the required documents: Check which financial documents are required for the visa application. Usually, the last three years of tax returns, bank statements, and salary certificates are needed. For example, a cross-border worker in Lugano might need tax returns for 2021, 2022, and 2023, along with bank statements from the last 12 months. 2. Contact an authorized translator: Find a sworn translator authorized by the Swiss consular offices in Italy or on the websites of local chambers of commerce. In Ticino, for instance, you can find sworn translators at the Chamber of Commerce of the Canton of Ticino. Ensure the translator is recognized by both Swiss and Italian authorities to avoid issues with the validity of the documents. 3. Submit the documents: Hand over the original documents to the translator, who will handle the translation and sworn certification. The cost of the translation can vary depending on the complexity and length of the documents. Frequently Asked Questions
How much does a sworn translation for financial documents cost for Switzerland?
The cost of a sworn translation for financial documents varies depending on the type and length of the text, but on average it is between 50 and 150 CHF per document. For example, a tax return can cost around CHF 70, while a bank statement can cost up to CHF 120.
What financial documents are needed for the border visa in Ticino?
Generally, the last three years of tax returns, bank statements and salary certificates are required. Depending on the municipality of residence in Ticino, additional documents, such as those relating to real estate, may also be required.
Where can I find an authorised sworn translator for Swiss documents?
You can find a list of licensed sworn translators at Swiss consular offices in Italy or on the websites of local chambers of commerce. It is advisable to choose a translator with specific experience in Swiss regulations, especially those of the Canton of Ticino.
What are the deadlines for submitting translated visa documents?
Deadlines for submitting translated documents vary depending on the municipality of residence in Ticino. Usually, documents must be submitted within 30 days of the visa application, but for some municipalities such as Lugano, the deadline may be shorter, for example 15 days.
How long does it take to get a sworn translation of financial documents?
The turnaround time for a sworn translation can range from a few days to several weeks, depending on the complexity of the document and the translator's workload. A tax return can take about a week, while a more complex bank statement can take up to two weeks.
