Yes to the G Permit for Cross-Border Apprentices in Ticino Practically speaking, the G permit for cross-border apprentices is a fundamental solution for young people aiming to combine vocational training with work in Ticino. This authorization allows residence and work in the canton for the entire traineeship duration, without time limits tied to temporary contracts or unstable work conditions. The regulation applies to those starting apprenticeships with certified companies in the canton, involving key sectors such as commerce, industry, crafts, and services. For example, a young person from Chiasso or Mendrisio beginning an apprenticeship at a medium-sized company based in one of these towns can benefit from the G permit without time restrictions, provided the traineeship lasts up to three years, as per Swiss law. Applications are submitted to the Ticino Department of Finance and Economy (DFE) offices, with documentation including proof of enrollment in a recognized training institute, the training contract, and proof of temporary residence in Switzerland. > Operational checklist: > - Enrollment in a recognized Swiss training institute > - Valid training contract with a certified company > - Identity documents and proof of temporary residence > - Any health insurance attestations The permit issuance process is generally quick, with waiting times around 30 days. A notable innovation is the automatic renewal of the permit, which occurs without requiring a new application, provided the training continues uninterrupted. This measure helps ensure greater stability for young workers and facilitates their integration into the territory, positively impacting the local socio-economic fabric. Yes to the G Permit for Cross-Border Apprentices in Ticino For cross-border apprentices from Italy, especially from the Trentino-Alto Adige municipalities like Bolzano, Merano, or Brunico, obtaining the G permit is a significant breakthrough, easing access to the Ticino job market and improving living and integration conditions. This measure, officially introduced through the regulatory revision of January 1, 2022, allows young people aged 15 to 25, enrolled in recognized training institutes, to work in Ticino with greater simplicity and security. To qualify for the G permit, apprentices must meet key requirements, including enrollment in an official training institution such as the Bellinzona Technical School or Lugano Vocational Training Center, and a valid traineeship contract with at least 20 hours per week. The application must be submitted at least 30 days before the traineeship begins via the official portal of the Ticino Department of Territory, supported by documents such as the training contract, a copy of identity documents, and proof of residence in Italy. > Concrete example: a young person from Bolzano enrolled in an IT technician course who plans a 6-month traineeship in Lugano can apply for the G permit at a cost of about 150 Swiss francs.
