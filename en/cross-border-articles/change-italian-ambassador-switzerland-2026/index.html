Italian Ambassador Gian Lorenzo Cornado will leave the Italian diplomatic mission in Bern, Switzerland, on June 29 next year, as he reaches the retirement age. This was announced by the Italian news agency ANSA. The diplomat, who was present in Crans-Montana just a few hours after the Constellation fire, has managed in recent months the delicate phase of supporting the Italian families involved in the New Year's tragedy and the difficult relations between Italy and Switzerland, which have deteriorated after the release of Jacques Moretti, the owner of the burnt-down venue, and then following the dispute over the payment of medical expenses incurred in Switzerland by the injured Italians. As a sign of protest against the progress of the investigation by the Sion prosecution, on January 24 the Italian Government recalled him to Rome where he remained for over two months, before returning to Bern. ### Cornado's diplomatic career Ambassador of Italy to Switzerland and Liechtenstein since September 2023, Cornado was Italy's Permanent Representative to the United Nations and other international organizations in Geneva between 2018 and 2023.
