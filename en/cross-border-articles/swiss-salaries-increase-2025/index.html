Swiss salaries rise: +1.6% in 2025

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 28 April 2026

Real wages in Switzerland increased by 1.6% in 2025, the most significant gain since 2009

Contesto

In brief - Real wages +1.6% in 2025 - Inflation at 0.2% - Chemical-pharmaceutical and public administration sectors growing ## Key facts - What: Increase in real wages - When: 2025 - Where: Switzerland - Who: Federal Statistical Office (FSO) - Amount: +1.6% The data from the Federal Statistical Office (FSO) certifies that the purchasing power of Swiss employees is growing again at the most sustained pace in the last sixteen years. In 2025, real wages in Switzerland increased by 1.6% compared to the previous year. The nominal wage index recorded an average increase of 1.8%, reaching 106.1 points (base 2020 = 100). The strong slowdown in inflation made the difference, with consumer prices rising by an average of only 0.2%. ### Growing sectors The tertiary sector drove the overall dynamics with a +1.9% nominal increase, while the secondary sector stood at +1.5%. Within industry, the most marked growth came from the coke and refining, chemical and pharmaceutical industry (+3.1%), a central sector also in the economy of the Canton Ticino and the Lake Geneva region. Above the sector average are also the manufacture of metal products (+2.4%) and the computer, electronics, optics, watchmaking and electrical equipment sector (+2.3%). ### Public administration in the lead In the services sector, the top spot goes to public administration, with a +3.3% nominal increase, followed by professional, scientific and technical activities (+2.6%) and the publishing, audiovisual and telecommunications sector (+2.5%). Among the sectors where remuneration has remained almost unchanged are health and social care (+0.4%) and administrative and support service activities (+0.7%). Dettagli operativi

The increase in real wages in Switzerland represents a positive signal for workers, particularly for cross-border workers operating in the Canton of Ticino. The 1.6% growth in 2025 is the most significant gain since 2009, when the increase was 2.6%. This increment is particularly relevant for cross-border workers, who often face high living costs both in Switzerland and Italy. ### Implications for Cross-Border Workers Cross-border workers who work in Switzerland and reside in Italy can benefit from this increase in real wages. However, it is important to consider that withholding tax and AVS and LPP contributions can significantly influence the net salary. Cross-border workers should therefore carefully plan the management of their income to maximize the benefits of this salary increase. ### Comparison with Previous Years Over the past twenty years, a similar growth rate was only recorded in 2015 and 2020 (+1.5% in both cases). The strong slowdown in inflation, with consumer prices rising by an average of only 0.2%, significantly contributed to this increase in purchasing power. This comparison highlights how 2025 is a particularly positive year for Swiss workers. ### Growing Sectors and Opportunities The sectors that recorded the most marked growth, such as coke manufacturing and refining, chemical and pharmaceutical industries (+3.1%), offer interesting opportunities for cross-border workers. These sectors are central to the economy of the Canton of Ticino and the Lake Geneva region, making the area particularly attractive for cross-border workers.
