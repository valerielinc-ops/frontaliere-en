Contesto

In brief - Thousands of Americans renounce their US passport - The procedure costs up to 10,000 dollars - Bern is the reference point for renunciations ## Key facts - What: Renunciation of US citizenship - When: Significant increase in recent years - Where: US Embassy in Bern - Who: American citizens residing in Europe - Amount: Up to 10,000 dollars for the procedure Saying goodbye to the US passport goes through Bern. More and more American expats are choosing to renounce their citizenship. According to the Aargauer Zeitung, requests are piling up at US embassies in Europe, also because completing the procedure requires a lot of patience and considerable financial resources. In Bern, thousands of US citizens voluntarily renounce their passport each year. A renunciation can cost up to 10,000 dollars. Renouncing US citizenship is anything but simple. The official tax has recently been reduced from 2,350 to 450 dollars, but with legal fees, the total cost can quickly reach between 7,000 and 10,000 dollars. Moreover, the procedure can take more than a year, according to the Moody's consulting agency. This is in addition to significant risks: those classified as 'covered expatriates' can suffer long-term tax disadvantages. Even entering the United States can become more complicated, with the risk, in some cases, of no longer being able to visit family in the country. ### Why Bern? Since waiting times at US representations in Europe now exceed twelve months, more and more Americans are turning to the Bern embassy. Here, the number of renunciations has increased from a few hundred cases a year in the early 2000s to several thousand today. ### Fiscal and political reasons Once fiscal reasons, today mainly political. Implications for cross-border workers The renunciation of U.S. citizenship has significant implications for cross-border workers living in Ticino and working in Switzerland. Here's what you need to know: ### Practical difficulties Opening a bank account in Switzerland can become complicated for U.S. citizens. The need to report financial movements to U.S. authorities adds an extra layer of complexity. This can affect the daily management of finances and tax planning. ### Tax risks Those classified as 'covered expatriates' may face long-term tax disadvantages. This includes higher taxes and complications in filing tax returns. It is crucial to consult a tax expert to fully understand the implications. ### Access to the United States Entry into the United States can become more complicated for those who renounce their U.S. passport. In some cases, a special visa may be required to visit family or friends. This can limit freedom of movement and affect quality of life. ### Concrete scenarios If you are considering renouncing your U.S. passport, it is important to carefully weigh the pros and cons.
