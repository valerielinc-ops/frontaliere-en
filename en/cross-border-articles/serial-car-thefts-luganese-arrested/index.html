Series of car thefts: 40-year-old from Lugano area arrested (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 6 May 2026

A 40-year-old from the Lugano area has been arrested for car thefts. Suspicious items and thousands of francs in cash were found.

Contesto

In brief - 40-year-old arrested for car thefts - Items of dubious origin found - Thousands of francs seized ## Key facts - What: Car thefts - When: Not yet specified - Where: Luganese area - Who: Cantonal police and Public Prosecutor's Office - Amount: Several thousand francs A 40-year-old Swiss citizen residing in the Luganese area, with numerous specific prior convictions, was arrested by the Cantonal Police and the Public Prosecutor's Office. The man is suspected of systematically committing thefts from cars, stealing cash, valuable items, and credit cards, which were then used to abuse data processing systems. The search of the room used by the 40-year-old led to the discovery of several items of dubious origin, bank cards, documents, and several thousand francs in cash. The suspected crimes include aggravated theft, repeated theft of movable property, repeated abuse of a data processing system, repeated violation of domicile, and breach of the Federal Law on the Transport of Passengers. The restrictive measure on liberty was confirmed by the Judge of Coercive Measures (GPC). The investigation is coordinated by Public Prosecutor Veronica Lipari. ### Security tips The Cantonal Police emphasized the importance of not leaving valuable items such as electronic devices, bags, wallets, cash, and other items visible inside your vehicle. Additionally, it is crucial to always lock your vehicle, even if you are away for short periods. For cross-border workers who work in Ticino and reside in Italy, it is essential to pay particular attention to the security of their vehicles, especially if parked in public areas or near border crossings such as Brogeda or Gaggiolo. Frequently Asked Questions
What are the most effective preventive measures against car theft?
The most effective preventive measures include installing alarm systems, using steering wheel lock devices, parking in well-lit areas, and avoiding leaving valuable items in plain sight inside the vehicle.
What to do in case of theft?
In case of theft, it is essential to immediately contact the competent authorities and file a report. This step is crucial to start investigations and increase the chances of recovering stolen goods.
Which border crossings are most at risk for car theft?
Border crossings like Brogeda and Gaggiolo are frequent transit points for cross-border workers and can represent an easy target for thieves. It is important to park in safe areas and adopt preventive measures.
