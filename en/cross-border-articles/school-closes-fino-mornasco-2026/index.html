School closing in Fino Mornasco: birth rate drop and protests (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 4 May 2026

Mayor Fornasiero confirms the closure of Rodari School in Socco, a hamlet of Fino Mornasco, due to the decline in births.

Contesto

In brief - Rodari School in Socco closes due to declining birth rates - 830 signatures collected against the closure - Mayor: "Irreversible decision" ## Key facts - What: Closure of Rodari primary school in Socco - When: Starting next school year - Where: Socco, a hamlet of Fino Mornasco - Who: Mayor Roberto Fornasiero - Amount: 830 signatures collected against the closure The mayor of Fino Mornasco, Roberto Fornasiero, responded to citizens' protests regarding the closure of Rodari primary school in Socco, a hamlet of the town. The decision was made due to the drastic decline in birth rates in recent years. "The decision made was carefully considered and analyzed from every angle. It is final and there's no going back," declared the mayor. The petition against the school's closure collected 830 signatures, but the mayor reiterated the need to acknowledge the decline in birth rates. In Fino Mornasco, in the last two years, slightly more than 60 children were born annually, while in the previous two-year period, slightly more than 70. The last large cohort, in 2017, counted 121 children. ### Situation analysis This year, 83 residents of Fino Mornasco were called to enroll in first grade, but only 44 families chose the town's schools. This led to a reduction in first-grade classes from 4 to 3 for the next school year. The school in Socco, developed over two floors with the last expansion dating back over 50 years, is no longer economically sustainable. The mayor also responded to criticisms regarding the use of PNRR funds for school safety compliance.
