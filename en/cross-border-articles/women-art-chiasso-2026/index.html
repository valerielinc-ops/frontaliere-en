The Importance of Inclusive Art Inclusive art is a growing field that recognizes the value of artistic expression by individuals with diverse abilities and backgrounds. The 'Women in Art' exhibition in Chiasso, which featured seven artists, is a concrete example of how art can be used to promote social inclusion and mutual understanding. The exhibited works not only reflect the creativity of the artists but also their personal experiences and the challenges they have faced. According to data from the OSC Day Center, 60% of the exhibited works were created using mixed techniques, combining painting, collage, and recycled materials. ### The Role of the OSC Day Center The OSC Day Center in Chiasso is a place of support and community for people with special needs. Founded in 2010, the center offers daytime services to over 50 users, with an annual cost per capita of approximately 30,000 CHF, partially funded by the Canton of Ticino and private donations. Through initiatives such as the 'Women in Art' exhibition, the center provides an opportunity for users to express themselves and connect with the broader community.
