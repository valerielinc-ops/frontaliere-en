Frequently Asked Questions
When do the rules for the pension of border workers Switzerland-Italy change?
The new rules for the coordination of AVS/INPS pensions for border workers working in Switzerland and residing in Italy will come into force from 2026. These changes are introduced by the New Frontier Agreement signed on 23 December 2020, which aims to change pension management.
Who are the border workers affected by the new pension rules from 2026?
The new pension rules, in force since 2026, will particularly affect border workers who work in the Canton of Ticino and reside in Italy at a distance of more than 20 km from the Swiss border. For them, the New Frontier Agreement introduces significant changes in the management of AVS/INPS pensions.
What are time windows and how do they affect the pension of border workers over 20 km?
'Time windows' are mechanisms introduced by the New Frontier Agreement to coordinate AVS and INPS contributions. For border workers residing more than 20 km from the border, these windows can affect the start date and duration of pension contributions, affecting the final amount of the pension.
What are the key steps for a frontier to coordinate AVS/INPS pension contributions?
To coordinate AVS/INPS contributions, a border worker should verify the distance of the residence from the Swiss border, understand the specific time windows for those residing over 20 km, actively coordinate AVS and INPS contributions, and carefully plan the final pension to maximize benefits.
What is the New Frontier Agreement and when was it signed?
The New Frontier Agreement is an agreement between Switzerland and Italy, signed on 23 December 2020, which introduces important changes in the coordination of AVS/INPS pensions for cross-border workers. Its main provisions will come into force from 2026, with a focus on resident border crossers over 20 km from the border.
