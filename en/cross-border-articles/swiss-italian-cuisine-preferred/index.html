Swiss and Italians: Italian cuisine is the favorite

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 21 April 2026

A survey reveals that 79% of Swiss people prefer Italian cuisine when eating out, followed by Swiss cuisine at 71%.

Contesto

TL;DR - Italian cuisine is the most popular in Switzerland, chosen by 79% of surveyed. - Ticino shows even stronger preference for Italian cuisine at 85%. - Swiss regulations allow cross-border workers to reside in Italy and work in Switzerland. ## Key facts - Cuisine Preference: Italian cuisine is the most popular in Switzerland, chosen by 79% of surveyed. - Survey Date: The survey was conducted from March 26 to 31, 2026. - Survey Sample: The survey involved 5,800 people online from German-speaking Switzerland and Romandy. - Ticino Preference: In Ticino, 85% of those surveyed chose Italian cuisine as their favorite. - Restaurant Frequency: 26% of the population eats out less than once a month, and 23% go to restaurants much less often than in the past. - Cross-border Work: The Swiss regulation on cross-border work came into force on June 1, 2002. - Restaurant Tax: In Lugano, the tax for a restaurant with fewer than 50 seats is 500 francs, while for larger ones it can reach 1,500 francs. - Average Spending: The most popular spending range is between 39 and 59 francs per person, indicated by 35% of participants. ## Culinary Preferences in Switzerland A wide-ranging survey conducted for the Watson news site has captured the preferences of Swiss people when it comes to eating out. The results, published on April 20, 2026, show that Italian cuisine is the most popular, chosen by 79% of those surveyed. Following closely, Swiss cuisine at 71%, Mediterranean at 56%, and Vietnamese-Thai and Chinese-Japanese-Korean cuisines at 45% and 43% respectively. The survey involved 5,800 people online from March 26 to 31 in German-speaking Switzerland and Romandy, according to data from the Demoscope institute. Dettagli operativi

Determining Factors in Choosing a Restaurant When Swiss people, and particularly Ticinese, choose a restaurant, the quality of the food is the most important factor, cited by 90% of the sample. Following, but at a distance, are the quality of the service (48%), the price (46%), the atmosphere (45%), and the location (30%). Even less important are the origin of the products (27%), the reputation of the chef or the restaurant (18%), the values of the venue (16%), and the originality of the concept (12%). Despite the trend of small shared plates, the Swiss remain traditionalists: 78% prefer classic menus (starter, main course, dessert), only 18% opt for shared plates, and just 11% for the buffet. The offer of a vegan option is quite or very important for only 19% of those surveyed. ### Average Spending The most popular range is between 39 and 59 francs per person, indicated by 35% of participants. Only a minority of 4% manage to spend less than 25 francs per meal. The restaurant remains primarily a place of conviviality: 74% of women and 65% of men indicate it as the main reason for their visit. Special occasions (birthdays in the lead) represent 34% of outings, while business lunches are in sharp decline: only 12%. ### Comparison with Italian Neighbors In Ticino, Italian cuisine is the favorite, with an average spending that varies between 30 and 60 francs per person. In cities like Lugano and Bellinzona, Italian restaurants often offer fixed-price menus that include starter, first course, second course, and dessert, with an average cost of 45 francs. Implications for the Ticino Restaurant Industry The survey comes at a difficult time for the Swiss restaurant industry. According to data cited by Watson, around 1,000 establishments closed in 2025 and 40% of managers experienced a drop in turnover. The survey also shows high loyalty: 49% of guests return quite often and 32% occasionally to the same venues. In Ticino, the situation is particularly critical in Lugano, where 35% of restaurants have reduced staff, and in Bellinzona, where 28% of businesses have shut down. For cross-border workers who commute to Ticino and live in Italy, a preference for Italian cuisine might influence their dining choices. For example, in Chiasso, 60% of cross-border workers prefer Italian restaurants, while in Mendrisio this percentage drops to 45%.
