Acquarossa invests 250,000 francs in healthcare hub and Dagro trolleybus line (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 21 March 2026

The Municipality of Acquarossa approves two credits totaling 250,000 francs: trolleybus maintenance and design of new 47-million franc social-healthcare center.

Contesto

TL;DR - Acquarossa investe - Filovia 2026 - Hub sanitario ## Key facts - Municipio: Acquarossa - Data: 17 marzo 2026 - Importo: 100000 e 150000 franchi svizzeri - Progetto: Héritage, hub sanitario - Costo: 47 milioni di franchi - Scadenza: maggio 2026 - Ente: La Quercia Foundation The Municipality of Acquarossa, located in the heart of the Blenio Valley, has recently approved two significant infrastructure investments with a dual purpose: to enhance the territory and ensure essential services for both local residents and tourists. On Tuesday, March 17, 2026, the Municipal Council unanimously approved two expenditure credits of 100,000 and 150,000 Swiss francs respectively. The first credit supports extraordinary maintenance of the Malvaglia-Dagro trolleybus line, a historic infrastructure connecting villages and valleys, involving an upgrade of the control system, which dates back 30 years. The intervention, with a total cost of about one million francs, is already underway and will be completed by May 2026. According to the Board of Directors of the company that owns the trolleybus, the renewal is crucial to guarantee continuity, safety, and reliability—key elements for maintaining the area's tourism appeal and sustainable mobility. The second credit of 150,000 francs represents a municipal contribution to the La Quercia Foundation, which manages the Acquarossa nursing home and is designing a new social-healthcare hub valued at 47 million francs. The project, called 'Héritage' and designed by architect Simone Tocchetti, foresees construction on land owned by the foundation, integrating and expanding the existing facility for the elderly in their third and fourth age. Dettagli operativi

The intervention on the Malvaglia-Dagro trolleybus line is a significant example of public-private investment in a local transport infrastructure strategic for Ticino and the Blenio Valley. The system, dating back over thirty years, requires a complete update of the control system, which will be replaced with modern technologies capable of enhancing safety and operational management. The estimated total cost for this extraordinary maintenance is one million francs, of which 100,000 have been allocated by the Municipality. The remaining amount is covered by the trolleybus company and other public contributions, not specified in the source. This intervention is particularly important for cross-border and tourist mobility, as the trolleybus serves border areas with daily flows of cross-border commuters traveling between Italy and Switzerland, especially through border crossings like Brogeda and Gaggiolo. Ensuring a reliable transport system promotes not only passenger safety but also environmental sustainability by encouraging the use of electric public transport. At the same time, the 'Héritage' social-healthcare hub embodies a large-scale project for Canton Ticino, aligned with cantonal and federal policies aimed at modernizing healthcare and social facilities. The total value of the structure is 47 million francs, a significant sum that reflects the complexity and importance of the work. The La Quercia Foundation, beneficiary of the municipal credit of 150,000 francs, has been active for years in elderly care and with this project aims to integrate healthcare, social, and residential services into a single entity.
