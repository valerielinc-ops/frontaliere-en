Bellinzona Town Hall and Collegiate Church to become more accessible: CHF 1.385 million investment

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 20 April 2026

The Municipality of Bellinzona has allocated CHF 1.385 million to eliminate architectural barriers at the Town Hall and Collegiate Church. Elevators, platform lifts, and accessible restrooms will be upgraded. Work is set to begin in 2026.

Contesto

TL;DR - Bellinzona to spend 1.385M francs on accessibility improvements by 2026. - Palazzo Civico to get elevator refurbishment and lifting platform. - Collegiate Church to receive barrier-free access solutions. - Works aim to comply with cantonal and federal accessibility regulations. ## Key facts - Total Cost: 1.385 million francs - Palazzo Civico Cost: 1.285 million francs - Collegiate Church Cost: 100,000 francs - Completion Date: 2026 - Initial Budget (2020): 260,000 francs for Palazzo Civico - Accessibility Features: Elevator refurbishment, lifting platform, restroom updates - Impact on Cross-Border Workers: Minimal, but planning recommended - Funding Source: Municipal and cantonal resources The Municipality of Bellinzona has submitted a credit request for 1.285 million francs to eliminate architectural barriers at Palazzo Civico, while an additional 100,000 francs has been allocated for the Collegiate Church of San Pietro. The works have been approved by the Municipal Council and will be completed by 2026, with a total funding exceeding five times the amount initially envisaged solely for the Town Hall in 2020. ### The project for Palazzo Civico: elevator refurbishment and lifting platform The solution initially approved in 2020 involved installing a new elevator inside the shaft between the main staircase and the office corridors. However, this option was discarded due to the fragility of the historic glass panels on the main staircase. The revised plan now includes refurbishing the existing elevator shaft, ensuring the same transport capacity but with a less invasive intervention. For cross-border workers employed in Bellinzona or visiting the city for professional reasons, the ongoing accessibility works represent a tangible improvement in urban livability. However, during the construction phase, there may be temporary inconveniences related to pedestrian routes and access to municipal offices. Here’s what changes compared to the current situation and what is planned for the future. ### Before vs. after: practical comparison Before the works: - Civic Palace: access to the second floor was restricted to those without mobility issues due to an outdated elevator and the absence of platform lifts. Ground-floor restrooms did not fully comply with accessibility standards. - Collegiate Church: the forecourt and nave were only accessible via stairs, effectively excluding people with disabilities. After the works: - Civic Palace: full access to all floors via a modern elevator and a platform lift for the portico. Ground-floor restrooms will meet accessibility standards. - Collegiate Church: barrier-free access to the forecourt and nave, thanks to platform lifts and a translifter. ### Timelines and procedures during construction Work is set to begin in 2026 and is expected to last several months. During this period, the Municipality has announced that: - Alternative routes will be provided for access to municipal offices. - Informational signs will be installed to indicate accessible paths. - The Collegiate Church will remain open to the public, with controlled access to the construction site. ### Impact on cross-border workers For transborder commuters who frequent Bellinzona for work, the construction works will have minimal impact on daily routines, provided they plan their trips in advance. Frequently Asked Questions
When will the works at Palazzo Civico and Collegiata officially start?
The works are scheduled to begin in 2026, though the exact start date has not yet been announced by the Municipality. The City Council has confirmed they will commence within the year, with an estimated duration of several months.
Will the public restrooms at Palazzo Civico remain accessible during the works?
During the construction period, the ground-floor restrooms at Palazzo Civico will be upgraded to meet accessibility standards. However, some areas may be temporarily closed for renovation. It is advisable to check with the Municipality before visiting the office.
Can I request a special permit to access areas not affected by the construction sites?
Yes, the Municipality of Bellinzona allows special permits for accessing zones unaffected by the works. Requests can be submitted via email to accessibilita@bellinzona.ch or by phone at +41 91 821 21 11.
Are there tax benefits for cross-border workers in Bellinzona who require accessibility solutions?
Currently, no specific tax benefits are planned for cross-border workers in relation to accessibility works. However, the Canton of Ticino offers subsidies for adapting homes to accessibility standards. For details, consult the Ticino Cantonal Chancellery website under the 'Accessibility and Disability' section.
How can I report any inconveniences during the works at Palazzo Civico or Collegiata?
You can report issues or suggestions through the online form on the Bellinzona Municipality website ([bellinzona.ch/segnalazioni](nav:traffic-history)), via the municipal app (if available), or in person at the municipal office counter.
