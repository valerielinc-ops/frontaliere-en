The energy crisis has had a significant impact on fuel demand in Switzerland. According to data from the Federal Statistical Office, fuel demand has increased by over 10% in recent months. This has led to a rise in fuel prices, which have surged by over 20% compared to previous levels. For example, the price of 95-octane gasoline has risen from 1.83 CHF per liter to 2.22 CHF per liter, while diesel has risen from 1.93 CHF per liter to 2.35 CHF per liter. According to the Federal Statistical Office, Switzerland is one of the regions most dependent on oil and gas imports, making it particularly vulnerable to market shocks. The Federal Statistical Office expects fuel prices to continue rising throughout 2026. This increase in fuel prices has a significant impact on Ticinese and frontier residents, who often travel to Italy to fill up. For instance, in Chiasso, a Ticinese border town, fuel prices are typically 10-15% lower than in Switzerland.
