Quadroni trial: former police chief disputes charges

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 1 May 2026

The former head of the Scuol police station disputes the charges of abuse of authority, unlawful confinement, and violation of domicile.

In brief - The former police chief of Scuol contests the charges of abuse of authority. - The Public Prosecutor's Office requests a suspended prison sentence of two years. - The verdict will likely be issued next week. ## Key facts - What: Contesting charges of abuse of authority, kidnapping, and home invasion. - When: April 30, 2026. - Where: Regional Court Lower Engadine. - Who: Former police chief of Scuol, Adam Quadroni. - Amount: Requested prison sentence: 2 years suspended. The former police chief of Scuol contested today, Thursday, April 30, 2026, before the Regional Court Lower Engadine the charges brought against him. Among these are abuse of authority, kidnapping, and home invasion. The Public Prosecutor's Office requests a suspended prison sentence of two years. A fine instead for Adam Quadroni, accused of threatening the police. The court reviewed this morning the main facts at the origin of the charges: the searches, the threats, and the intervention with the special unit of the police, which arrested using strong measures the former construction entrepreneur Adam Quadroni in June 2017. The acts are contained in 18 binders. To respond, the former police chief of Scuol, who was heard for over an hour. The 69-year-old retiree said that it was not his intention to give a lesson to Quadroni, a person he has always treated with respect and with whom he never had discussions either in a professional or private context. At that time, added the former police officer, Quadroni aroused his pity: he had family conflicts, the financial situation was precarious, and so was his work situation.
