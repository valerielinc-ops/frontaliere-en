Sunrise must integrate SSR's HBB offer

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 28 April 2026

The Federal Administrative Court ruled that Sunrise must include the SSR's HBB offer, with features for sensory-impaired users.

Contesto

In brief - Sunrise must integrate SSR's HBB - Federal Administrative Court decision - Features for sensory-disabled users ## Key facts - What: Integration of SSR's HBB offering into Sunrise - When: Judgment published on 28.04.2026 - Where: Switzerland - Who: Federal Administrative Court, Swiss Federation of the Deaf, Swiss Broadcasting Corporation - Amount: Not specified The Federal Administrative Court has ruled that Sunrise must integrate the SSR's HBB (Hybrid Broadcast Broadband) offering into its television product. This interactive feature, accessible via the red button on the remote control, allows traditional TV programs to be combined with supplementary services. Among these are functions designed for people with sensory disabilities, such as subtitles and other accessibility tools. The decision was made following an appeal filed by the Swiss Federation of the Deaf and the Swiss Broadcasting Corporation, which saw their request granted. Previously, the Federal Office of Communications (OFCOM) had rejected the thesis of the obligation to broadcast for Sunrise. The HBB component is an international standard that enriches the television experience with interactive content and additional services, making the television offering more inclusive and accessible to a wider audience. ### Implications for cross-border workers For cross-border workers residing in Ticino and working in Switzerland, this decision could mean an improvement in accessibility to television content.
