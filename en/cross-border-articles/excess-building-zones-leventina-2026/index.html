In an interview with laRegione.ch, Nastasi emphasized the need for concrete guidance from the Canton of Ticino on how to proceed with potential dezoning and compensation. In Giornico, for example, only a few plots remain undeveloped, making a review of building zones urgent. The current situation has led to an excess of areas designated for construction, creating a mismatch between supply and real demand. Nastasi stressed the importance of having a clear plan to avoid further problems and ensure sustainable urban development. ### Implications for cross-border workers The overdevelopment of building zones could also have repercussions for cross-border workers employed in Ticino. With an increase in building zones, there could be a greater supply of real estate, influencing the housing market and, consequently, the cost of living for cross-border workers. Furthermore, any dezoning could lead to changes in local infrastructure, affecting quality of life and mobility. ### Procedure for compensation Regarding compensation, Nastasi has requested clarification on how it will be managed and who will be eligible. The Canton will need to establish clear criteria to avoid disputes and ensure a transparent process.
