Rooftop fire in Chiasso, 30 evacuated (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 4 May 2026

A fire broke out on the roof of a building in Piazza Indipendenza, Chiasso, leading to the evacuation of around thirty people.

Contesto

In brief - Roof fire in Chiasso, thirty people evacuated - Two workers slightly injured during roof work - Flames contained outside the building ## Key facts - What: Roof fire in a residential building - When: Monday, May 4, 2026, just before 13:30 - Where: Piazza Indipendenza, Chiasso - Who: Four workers, Cantonal Police, Chiasso Municipal Police, Mendrisiotto Emergency Medical Service (SAM), Mendrisiotto Cantonal Fire and Rescue Service (CSCPM) - Injuries: Two workers slightly injured A fire broke out on the roof of a residential building in Piazza Indipendenza, Chiasso, in the early afternoon of Monday. According to initial reports, the flames developed while four workers were carrying out roof repairs using a gas torch. The Cantonal Police have launched an investigation to clarify the cause of the fire. As a precaution, the temporary evacuation of about thirty people present in the building was ordered. On the scene, Cantonal Police officers, supported by Chiasso Municipal Police, Mendrisiotto Emergency Medical Service (SAM) rescuers, and Mendrisiotto Cantonal Fire and Rescue Service (CSCPM) firefighters intervened. The flames remained confined to the outside of the building. According to an initial medical assessment, one worker suffered minor burns to his hands, while another experienced mild symptoms of intoxication. Both were taken to the hospital by ambulance for further examination. To allow extinguishing operations, Via Livio and Corso San Gottardo were temporarily closed to traffic. The situation is under control, but investigations are still ongoing to determine the origin of the fire. ### Implications for cross-border workers The fire and the evacuation of Piazza Indipendenza in Chiasso had a significant impact on the daily lives of cross-border work...

Analysis of Causes and Consequences The fire on the roof of Piazza Indipendenza in Chiasso has raised several questions about the causes and the safety measures adopted during the work. According to initial reconstructions, the flames would have developed while four workers were using a gas torch. This type of incident underscores the importance of strictly following safety procedures during work involving the use of gas equipment. The investigations by the Cantonal Police are still ongoing to determine the exact causes of the fire. It is crucial that the authorities clarify whether there were violations of safety regulations or if the accident was caused by a malfunction of the equipment. The conclusions of this investigation could lead to changes in workplace safety regulations, especially for work involving the use of gas. ### Impact on the Local Community The fire and the evacuation have had a significant impact on the local community, particularly on cross-border workers who work or reside in the Chiasso area. The temporary evacuation of about thirty people has caused inconveniences, especially for those who had to go to work or return home. The roads closed to traffic, Via Livio and Corso San Gottardo, have further complicated movements, forcing many to seek alternative routes. For cross-border workers who use public transport, the incident has caused delays and route changes. It is important that cross-border workers are always prepared for possible emergencies and disruptions, informing themselves in real-time about traffic conditions and possible road closures. Frequently Asked Questions
What should I do in case of a fire at the workplace?
In case of a fire at the workplace, it is crucial to stay calm and follow the evacuation procedures indicated by the authorities. Use the marked escape routes, do not go back to retrieve personal items, and reach the designated meeting point. Immediately inform the emergency services and provide all necessary information for a prompt response.
What safety measures should be adopted during work involving the use of gas?
During work involving the use of gas, it is essential to strictly follow safety procedures. Companies must ensure that workers are adequately trained and that equipment is regularly checked and maintained. Construction sites should be equipped with fire extinguishers and other safety devices to promptly intervene in case of emergency.
How can I prepare for potential emergencies?
To prepare for potential emergencies, it is important to know the evacuation protocols and the nearest escape routes to your workplace or residence. Stay informed in real-time about traffic conditions and any road closures. Having a backup plan for travel is fundamental, especially if you need to cross the Italian-Swiss border to get to work.
