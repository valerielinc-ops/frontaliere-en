Cyber threats in Switzerland: Swisscom raises alarm

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 27 April 2026

Swisscom reports a significant increase in cyber threats in Switzerland, with growing risks for businesses due to AI and geopolitical tensions.

In brief - Swisscom detects increase in cyber threats in Switzerland - AI and geopolitical tensions raise risks - Software and data must be actively managed ## Key facts - What: Increase in cyber threats in Switzerland - When: 2026 - Where: Switzerland - Who: Swisscom - Amount: Not yet specified The context of cyber threats in Switzerland has significantly worsened, according to an analysis by telecommunications company Swisscom. In particular, artificial intelligence (AI), geopolitical tensions, and growing digital dependencies are increasing risks for businesses. This content was published on April 27, 2026. In a new report, the Swiss telecommunications operator Swisscom has identified a significant intensification of cyber threats in the past year. According to the "Cybersecurity Threat Radar 2026," published on Monday, AI, geopolitical tensions, and global dependencies have made attacks more complex and difficult to control. The group cites state-sponsored cyberattacks, hybrid warfare, disinformation, and attacks on software supply chains as main risks. Digital transformation involves an increasing dependence of companies on cloud platforms, external codes, AI models, and networked industrial facilities. "Those who do not know how software is created, where data is processed, or under which legal frameworks suppliers are subject, risk losing control with potentially serious consequences for the entire company," the report states. ### Implications for businesses AI, in particular, acts as an accelerator. While it can increase productivity and innovation, it also amplifies existing risks in the absence of control. What are the main cyber risks for businesses in Switzerland?
