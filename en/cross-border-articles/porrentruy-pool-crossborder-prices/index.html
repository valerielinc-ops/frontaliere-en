Porrentruy: double pool tickets for cross-border workers (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 27 May 2026

In Porrentruy, pool tickets for cross-border workers have doubled and season passes are no longer available: adults pay 15 CHF, youths 10 CHF, tickets only sold online.

Contesto

In brief - Doubled entry fees for non-residents and cross-border workers - Adult ticket: 15 CHF, youth: 10 CHF - Season passes banned for non-residents - Online access only, valid ID required - What: Restrictions and price increases at the municipal pool - When: Summer 2024, pool reopening - Where: Porrentruy, Switzerland - Who: Municipal Council, Municipality of Porrentruy - Amount: Adults 15 CHF, youth 10 CHF (non-residents) The reopening of Porrentruy's municipal pools this summer has brought new rules directly affecting cross-border workers and those living along the Italian-Swiss border. As reported by ComoZero, the Porrentruy Municipal Council approved a legal change that allows the Municipality to introduce restrictive measures for access to the public pool. Anyone who neither resides nor works within the Swiss Confederation—including Italian cross-border commuters—now faces a ticket price that has literally doubled: adults pay 15 francs (compared to 7.50 CHF for residents), while youth pay 10 francs instead of the usual 5 CHF. Non-residents are also barred from buying season passes. The only way to purchase a ticket is online, and a valid identity document must be shown at the entrance. According to the Municipality, the main reason for these changes is to ensure safety, peace, and public health, following incidents of rudeness and disorder in previous years. Dettagli operativi

Practical implications for cross-border workers The new rates and restrictions imposed by the Porrentruy municipal swimming pool directly affect Italian cross-border workers employed in Canton Ticino, especially those who, during the summer, seek accessible facilities across the border. In the past, the possibility of purchasing season tickets or accessing the facility at standard rates was a convenient solution for those who daily cross border points such as Brogeda, Chiasso, or Gaggiolo. Now, the doubling of ticket prices and the discontinuation of season tickets represent a significant economic obstacle. While the cost for a cross-border family was previously manageable through season tickets and resident rates, today each single entry involves an expense of 15 CHF per adult and 10 CHF per youth, available only via online purchase and with the requirement to present a valid identity document. The decision to exclude non-residents from season tickets has a concrete impact on cross-border workers who, despite working in Switzerland with a G permit, do not reside within Swiss territory. ## Before/after comparison and potential scenarios Before the change, access was simpler and less expensive: standard rates, available season tickets, and no marked distinction between residents and non-residents. Now, the imposition of double costs and restrictions on purchasing methods makes the pool less accessible to cross-border commuters. For those who work in Ticino and reside in Italy, the situation also complicates family budget management, considering that these extra expenses add to those for AVS, LPP, LAMal, withholding tax, and tax rebates, which are already high for cross-border workers. Frequently Asked Questions
How much does a ticket for a cross-border worker cost at the Porrentruy swimming pool?
The entrance ticket for non-resident adults or cross-border workers at the Porrentruy communal swimming pool costs 15 Swiss francs, while for young people the fee is 10 francs. These figures are doubled compared to the standard tariffs reserved for Swiss residents, who pay 7.50 CHF for adults and 5 CHF for young people.
Is it possible to subscribe to a seasonal subscription as a cross-border worker?
No, non-residents in Switzerland, including cross-border workers, are prohibited from subscribing to seasonal subscriptions for the Porrentruy communal swimming pool. Access is only allowed through the purchase of single tickets online and presenting a valid identity document.
How is the ticket purchased if one is not a resident of Switzerland?
Non-residents can only purchase tickets online. At the Porrentruy swimming pool cash desk, they must show a valid identity document to be able to enter. Seasonal subscriptions are not available for this category of users.
