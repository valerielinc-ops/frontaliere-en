SBB in Ticino: management announces changes, Chiasso depot remains open (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 15 May 2026

SBB management visits Ticino to announce new cuts, but Chiasso depot will not close

Contesto

In brief - SBB announces new cuts in Ticino - Chiasso depot will not close - Press conference in Bellinzona on May 19 - New production model from 2026 - Public assembly in Chiasso ## Key facts - What: Announcement of new cuts and reorganization of SBB Cargo - When: May 19, 2024 - Where: Bellinzona and Chiasso - Who: SBB management and Committee "No to the dismantling of SBB Cargo in Ticino" - Amount: 40 jobs lost in Ticino in 2023 The management of the Swiss Federal Railways (SBB) will travel to Ticino on Tuesday, May 19, to announce new cuts and changes in the management of freight transport. The announcement was anticipated by the Committee "No to the dismantling of SBB Cargo in Ticino", which spoke of a probable closure of the Chiasso depot, with the loss of other tens of jobs. However, Roberta Cattaneo, director of SBB South Region, reassured that "the Chiasso depot will not close", referring the details to the press conference scheduled in Bellinzona. The reorganization of SBB, announced last year, has already led to the closure of eight terminals in Switzerland, including those in Cadenazzo and Lugano, with the consequent loss of about forty full-time jobs in Ticino. The new production model, which will come into force with the timetable change on December 13, 2026, aims to make the traffic of single wagons more efficient, increasing the level of train occupancy and reducing costs. ### Public assembly in Chiasso The Committee "No to the dismantling of SBB Cargo in Ticino" has invited the population to participate in a public assembly on Tuesday, May 19 at 20:30 at the Cinema Teatro di Chiasso. Dettagli operativi

Analysis of the implications for cross-border workers The changes announced by the FFS could have a significant impact on cross-border workers employed in the freight transport sector in Ticino. The closure of terminals and the reorganization of services could lead to a reduction in jobs and changes in working conditions. It is important that affected workers inform themselves about the details of the reorganization and any professional retraining opportunities offered by the FFS. ### Comparison with the previous situation Before the reorganization, the freight transport sector in Ticino offered numerous job opportunities for cross-border workers, thanks to the presence of several terminals and proximity to Italy. The closure of some terminals and the reorganization of services could reduce these opportunities, making it more difficult for cross-border workers to find jobs in the sector. ### Future scenarios If the FFS reorganization leads to a significant reduction in jobs, affected cross-border workers may need to consider moving to other regions of Switzerland or changing sectors. It is important that workers inform themselves about the professional retraining opportunities offered by the FFS and the requirements for working in other sectors. ### Comparison with other cantons The situation in Ticino is not isolated. Other Swiss cantons, such as Zurich, are also undergoing reorganization processes in the freight transport sector. However, Ticino is particularly affected due to its geographical position and dependence on cross-border traffic. Cross-border workers employed in the freight transport sector in Ticino may face greater challenges compared to their colleagues in other regions of Switzerland.
