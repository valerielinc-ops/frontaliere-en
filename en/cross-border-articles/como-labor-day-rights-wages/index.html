Como, Labour Day: dignified wages and rights

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 1 May 2026

Hundreds of people gathered in Piazza Perretta for Labour Day, organized by the Cgil, Cisl and Uil unions. Key demands: dignified wages, safety and stability.

Contesto

In brief - Hundreds gather in Perretta Square for Labor Day - Unions Cgil, Cisl, and Uil organize the event - Demands: dignified wages, safety, stability ## Key facts - What: Labor Day demonstration - When: May 1, 2026 - Where: Perretta Square, Como - Who: Unions Cgil, Cisl, and Uil - Amount: Not specified Hundreds of people gathered this morning, Friday, May 1, 2026, in Perretta Square for Labor Day. The event was organized by the unions Cgil, Cisl, and Uil. As per tradition, amidst the waving of flags, speeches from the stage alternated with musical interludes. At the heart of the debate was the need to reaffirm the value of dignified work in a constantly changing global scenario. The reflections proposed by the unions focused on the centrality of collective bargaining, the protection of acquired rights, and the imminent transformations of the job market induced by artificial intelligence, and naturally, the tragedy of workplace deaths. ### Union leaders' speeches Among others, Daniele Magon (Cisl dei Laghi), Dario Esposito (Uil Lario), and Sandro Estelli, secretary of Cgil Como, were present. 'From the delegates, from the workers in healthcare, schools, services, offices, and factories, a clear request has come: more rights, dignified wages, safety, stability, regular contracts, and a more equitable distribution of wealth. Talking about dignified work also means saying enough to workplace deaths, truly investing in prevention, training, and inspections, recognizing the fundamental role of RLS and RLST. It means addressing the gender pay gap, defending public services, governing artificial intelligence without letting multinational corporations decide the future of work alone,' said Estelli (Cgil). ### Concrete demands 'And it means talking about our city: the cos...
