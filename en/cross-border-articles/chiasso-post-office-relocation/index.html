The Chiasso Post Office is moving to Via Bossi: here's what's changing (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 12 March 2026

The Yellow Giant has decided to relocate the Chiasso Post Office to Via Bossi. The Municipality has already given its agreement. Let's discover the details and the consequences for cross-border workers.

The news was announced by laRegione: the Chiasso Post Office is preparing to leave its headquarters in Piazza Indipendenza to move to Via Bossi. This decision was made by the Yellow Giant, i.e. Swiss Post, as part of a broader strategy. The Chiasso Municipality has been informed and has already given its agreement. The relocation of the Chiasso Post Office is just one of the many changes affecting the border town. In recent years, Chiasso has seen a series of transformations, from the new shopping center to the renewal of the historic center. However, this move could have a significant impact on the daily lives of cross-border workers who live or work in the area. The Chiasso Post Office is a reference point for many cross-border workers, who use it to send and receive packages, pay bills, and perform other postal operations. With the move to Via Bossi, cross-border workers will have to adapt to a new location, which could be more or less convenient depending on their place of residence...

Dettagli operativi

The relocation of the Chiasso Post Office is part of a broader strategy by Swiss Post, which aims to streamline and optimize its network of post offices across the country. This rationalization process was initiated a few years ago and has already led to several changes in other Swiss cities. In particular, Swiss Post is seeking to concentrate its services in larger and more modern offices, capable of offering a wider range of services and handling a larger volume of operations. This approach allows for improved efficiency and reduced operating costs. The new Chiasso Post Office in Via Bossi will be equipped with state-of-the-art technology and a larger space, able to accommodate more customers. In addition, it will have automatic teller machines for cash withdrawals and deposits, which will reduce waiting times and improve the customer experience. Regarding the timing, the move is scheduled for the end of 2026 or the beginning of 2027. Swiss Post is working closely with local authorities and cross-border worker representatives to ensure a smooth transition without service interruptions. During the transition period, the Chiasso Post Office in Piazza Indipendenza will remain open and operational. Swiss Post has assured that there will be no service interruptions and that customers will be able to continue using postal services without any problems. As for the consequences for cross-border workers, the move to Via Bossi could result in some changes in daily habits. For example, cross-border workers who regularly use the post office to send packages or pay bills may need to adjust their schedules or choose to use other nearby post offices. Frequently Asked Questions
When will the Chiasso post office move and where will the new headquarters be?
The crossing is scheduled for the end of 2026 or early 2027 in via Bossi, in Chiasso, in a central area easily reachable.
What will be the impact of the move on the efficiency of postal services for frontier workers?
The new office in via Bossi, equipped with state-of-the-art technologies and automatic doors, will improve efficiency, reducing waiting times and increasing the ability to manage a greater volume of operations.
How can I check the updated hours of the new Chiasso post office in via Bossi after the move?
The times of the new post office of Chiasso in via Bossi will be published on the official website of the Swiss Post Office (poste.ch) and on the information screens of the office itself. You should check the page dedicated to moving or contact customer service at 0800 187 187 for confirmation.
What banking services will be offered in the new post office of Chiasso in via Bossi?
The new post office in Via Bossi will offer basic banking services such as withdrawals, payments and payment of bills through PostFinance. It will not be an advanced financial advisory point, but it will ensure essential operations for frontier workers.
Will there be changes in shipping services for packages to Italy after the move of the post office?
No, shipment services for packages to Italy will remain unchanged. They can be made both at the new post office and through the online service of the Swiss Post Office. Delivery times and rates will not change.
