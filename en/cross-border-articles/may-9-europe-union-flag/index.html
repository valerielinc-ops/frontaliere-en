9 May: Display the European Union Flag (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 7 May 2026

The European Federalist Movement invites you to display the European flag on 9 May, Europe Day.

Contesto

In brief - The 9th of May marks Europe Day. - The date commemorates the 1950 Schumann Declaration. - 2026 is the 40th anniversary of Altiero Spinelli's death. ## Key facts - What: Display of the European flag. - When: Every year on the 9th of May. - Where: Gallarate and other locations. - Who: European Federalist Movement. - Cost: Not specified. The European Federalist Movement of Gallarate has called on citizens, political forces, associations, and public administrators to display the European flag on the 9th of May, Europe Day. This date commemorates the 1950 Schumann Declaration, which initiated the process of European unification with the creation of the European Coal and Steel Community. This year also marks the 40th anniversary of the death of Altiero Spinelli, a pioneer of the European project with the 1941 Ventotene Manifesto. Displaying the European flag is a symbolic gesture representing unity among European peoples and a message of resistance against divisions imposed by imperialisms. The European Federalist Movement invites everyone to display the flag in their windows and on local institutions to continue promoting the project of a united Europe. ### Historical significance The 9th of May is a significant date for Europe, as it commemorates the Schumann Declaration, which laid the foundations for the creation of the European Coal and Steel Community. This community was conceived as the first concrete core of a European Federation, indispensable for maintaining peace. The Schumann Declaration was a fundamental step in the process of European integration, leading to the creation of the European Union. ### The role of Altiero Spinelli Altiero Spinelli was a key figure in the European federalist movement. Implications for cross-border workers The invitation to display the European flag on May 9th also has implications for cross-border workers who live and work between Italy and Switzerland. This symbolic gesture can represent a way to strengthen the sense of belonging to the European community and to promote the values of unity and solidarity. For cross-border workers, who often find themselves navigating between two different legal and cultural systems, European unity can offer opportunities for collaboration and mutual exchange. ### Comparison with other initiatives The invitation from the European Federalist Movement of Gallarate fits into a broader context of initiatives aimed at promoting European unity. Across the continent, there are numerous organizations and movements working to strengthen the bonds between European peoples and to promote the values of peace and cooperation. Displaying the European flag is a simple but effective way to participate in this movement and to show support for European unity. ### Practical scenarios Imagine a scenario where citizens of a border town, such as Chiasso or Mendrisio, decide to display the European flag on May 9th. This gesture could inspire a local debate on the importance of European unity and the role of cross-border workers in building a cross-border community. It could also lead to concrete initiatives to promote collaboration between local communities and to strengthen ties between Italy and Switzerland. ### Comparison before and after Before the invitation from the European Federalist Movement, many citizens might not be aware of the significance of May 9th or the importance of the European flag. Frequently Asked Questions
Why is it important to display the European flag on May 9th?
Displaying the European flag on May 9th is a symbolic gesture that represents unity among European peoples and a message of resistance against divisions imposed by imperialisms. This act can raise public awareness about the importance of European unity and promote values of peace, solidarity, and cooperation.
Who can participate in the European Federalist Movement's invitation?
All citizens, political forces, associations, and public administrators are invited to participate in the European Federalist Movement's invitation. Those who do not own a European flag can contact the European Federalist Movement of Gallarate for more information on how to obtain one.
What are the practical implications for cross-border workers?
For cross-border workers, displaying the European flag can be a way to strengthen their sense of belonging to the European community and to promote values of unity and solidarity. This gesture can also inspire local debate on the importance of European unity and the role of cross-border workers in building a cross-border community.
