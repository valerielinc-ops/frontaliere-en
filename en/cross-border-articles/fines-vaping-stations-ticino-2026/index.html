Up to 500 francs fine for vaping in train stations

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 27 April 2026

The Swiss Association for Tobacco Prevention calls for fines for those who violate the ban on smoking and vaping on the tracks of Swiss train stations

Contesto

In brief - The Swiss Association for Tobacco Prevention proposes fines of up to 500 francs for vaping in stations - SBB receives between three and four reports per week for smoking on the tracks - Director Luciano Ruggia asks for improved signage for vaping areas ## Key facts - What: Proposal for fines for violating the smoking and vaping ban - When: April 27, 2026 - Where: Swiss railway stations - Who: Swiss Association for Tobacco Prevention - Amount: Up to 500 francs The Swiss Association for Tobacco Prevention has proposed introducing fines of up to 500 francs for those who violate the smoking and vaping ban on the tracks of Swiss stations. Currently, smoking is allowed within a radius of two meters from the designated ashtrays, but the use of electronic cigarettes is not always respected. Many vapers release vapor into the crowd, causing discontent among other users. The director of the association, Luciano Ruggia, stated that a fine of 500 francs would be reasonable and justified. He also emphasized the need to improve signage, which currently does not include devices for vaping. Ruggia proposed introducing ground markings in areas where smoking is allowed to make the ban clearer. Swiss Federal Railways (SBB) explained that currently no fines are issued for smoking and vaping on the tracks, but failure to comply with the regulations can result in consequences such as removal, compensation requests, or legal proceedings. Additionally, administrative costs of at least 25 francs can be charged. Analysis of Practical Implications The proposal to introduce fines for smoking and vaping on the platforms of Swiss stations could have several practical implications for travelers, including cross-border workers who regularly use railway stations. First, it is important that travelers are aware of the rules and possible sanctions. Fines of up to 500 francs could represent a significant deterrent for those who do not comply with the smoking and vaping ban. Furthermore, the proposal to improve signage could make it clearer where smoking and vaping are allowed. Currently, the signs only depict traditional cigarettes and not vaping devices. The introduction of ground markings in areas where smoking is allowed could help reduce violations of the ban. ### Comparisons with the Current Situation Currently, the FFS do not issue fines for smoking and vaping on the platforms, but failure to comply with the regulations can result in consequences such as removal, requests for compensation, or legal proceedings. Additionally, administrative costs of at least 25 francs can be charged. The proposal to introduce fines of up to 500 francs would represent a significant change from the current situation. ### Future Scenarios If the proposal is approved, travelers who smoke or vape on the platforms of Swiss stations could be subject to fines of up to 500 francs. This could lead to greater compliance with the rules and a more smoke-free and vapor-free environment in the stations. What to do if you are fined If you are fined for smoking or vaping on the tracks of Swiss stations, it is important to follow the procedures indicated by the SBB. First of all, you need to pay the fine within the set deadline. If you believe the fine is unjust, you can file an appeal. The SBB provides detailed information on how to file an appeal and what to do in case of dispute. ### Procedure to file an appeal 1. Fine receipt: Keep the fine receipt, which contains important information such as the reference number and the payment deadline. 2. Appeal form: Fill out the appeal form available on the SBB website or at railway stations. 3. Documentation: Attach all relevant documentation to the appeal application, such as photographs, testimonies, or other evidence that may support your position. 4. Submission of the appeal: Send the completed appeal form and the attached documentation to the address indicated by the SBB. 5. Waiting for the response: Wait for the SBB's response, which may request further information or documentation. ### Useful tools For cross-border workers who want to know more about the rules and possible penalties for smoking and vaping in Swiss stations, you can use the fine calculator available on the Frontaliere Ticino website.
