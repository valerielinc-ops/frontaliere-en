Luxury homes in Switzerland: prices still high but trend slowing (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 6 May 2026

Prices of luxury real estate in Switzerland rose by 3% in 2025, but the market shows signs of weakening. Here's what changes for cross-border workers.

Contesto

In brief - Luxury property prices +3% in 2025 - Market slowdown expected for 2026 - St. Moritz the most expensive location ## Key facts - What: Increase in luxury property prices - When: 2025 - Where: Switzerland - Who: UBS - Amount: +3% national average In 2025, luxury properties in Switzerland became more expensive, but the growth trend is progressively slowing down. According to a study published by UBS, the prices of high-standard properties increased by an average of 3% in a year. This increase is lower than the dynamics recorded in the overall Swiss real estate market. The Alpine regions drove demand, with average price increases of 6%, driven by wealthy foreign buyers. St. Moritz remains the most expensive location in the country, with an average price of 52,000 francs per square meter, followed by Gstaad and Verbier with around 45,000 francs. Outside the Alps, Cologny on Lake Geneva and Küsnacht on Lake Zurich are the most expensive locations. ### Implications for cross-border workers For a 150 square meter apartment in a privileged position, in the Swiss luxury municipalities, you need to spend between 4 and 5 million francs. Single-family homes with a plot of over 1,000 square meters often exceed 10 million. Despite an average annual increase of 5% in the last five years, UBS observes signs of market weakening, especially on Lake Geneva and Lake Zurich. For 2026, the bank predicts weaker development, also penalized by the weak economic situation. ### Situation in Ticino In Ticino, last year prices recorded stagnation. The local luxury real estate market is characterized by rather long sales times. Those who wish to sell a luxury property urgently must expect a price reduction.
