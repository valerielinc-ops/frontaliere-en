China braces for record cross-border travel for May Day

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 28 April 2026

China is preparing for a record increase in cross-border travel during the May Day holidays, with peaks of over 2.4 million passengers per day.

Contesto

In brief - Peak of 2.4 million cross-border movements in China for Labour Day - Airports and ports at the border with Hong Kong and Macao overwhelmed - Extraordinary measures to manage traffic ## Key facts - What: Record increase in cross-border travel - When: Labour Day holidays 2026 - Where: China, international airports and border ports - Who: National Immigration Administration (NIA) - Amount: 2.4 million passengers per day China is preparing for a record increase in cross-border travel during the upcoming Labour Day holidays. According to the National Immigration Administration (NIA), daily passenger flows in and out of the country should average 2.25 million, with a peak of over 2.4 million in a single day. The five-day holidays, starting on May 1st, represent one of the busiest periods of the year for travel, driven by strong demand for tourism and family visits. Major international airports are expected to see significant increases in passenger traffic. According to the NIA, daily cross-border flows should reach approximately 102,000 passengers at Shanghai's Pudong International Airport and 55,000 at Guangzhou's Baiyun International Airport. Land ports at the border with Hong Kong and Macao, in southern China, are also expected to experience heavy traffic. Daily passenger volumes should reach approximately 230,000 at the Luohu Port in Shenzhen. The Gongbei Port in Zhuhai and the Hong Kong-Zhuhai-Macao Bridge Port should handle approximately 396,000 and 129,000 passengers per day, respectively.
