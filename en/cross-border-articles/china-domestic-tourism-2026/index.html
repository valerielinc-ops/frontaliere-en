In brief - 1.9 billion domestic trips in China - Tourism spending at 271 billion dollars - 6% growth in travel ## Key facts - What: Growth in domestic tourism - When: First quarter 2026 - Where: China - Who: Ministry of Culture and Tourism - Amount: 1.9 billion trips, 271 billion dollars In the first quarter of 2026, China's domestic tourism market saw stable growth in both the number of trips and tourism spending. According to data from a sample survey of domestic travelers conducted by the Ministry of Culture and Tourism, over 1.9 billion domestic trips were made, a 6% increase compared to the previous year. Urban residents made 1.4 billion trips, with a 6.5% increase, while rural residents made 498 million trips, up 4.6%. Domestic tourism spending increased by 2.9% year-on-year, reaching 1.86 trillion yuan (approximately 271 billion dollars). Of this total, urban residents spent 1.53 trillion yuan, a 3.5% increase, while rural residents' spending amounted to 320 billion yuan, with a slight increase of 0.4%. ### Concrete examples and comparisons To make a comparison, Ticino recorded about 2.5 million tourists in 2025, with an average spending of 1,200 Swiss francs per person. In absolute numbers, China far surpasses Ticino, but the per capita spending is significantly lower.
