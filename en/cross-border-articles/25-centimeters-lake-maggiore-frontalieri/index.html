25 cm more for Lake Maggiore: what changes for cross-border workers

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 1 May 2026

From next summer, the maximum level of Lake Maggiore can be raised by 25 centimeters. Here's what changes for cross-border workers

Contesto

In brief - Lake Maggiore: maximum level +25 cm - Concerns about flooding - Basin Authority decides ## Key facts - What: Increase in maximum lake level - When: From summer 2022 - Where: Lake Maggiore - Who: Po River Basin Authority - Amount: +25 cm From next summer, the maximum level of Lake Maggiore can be raised by 25 centimeters. The goal is to have a larger water reservoir in case of need. But there are those who fear flooding. Concern has also been expressed by the Ticino authorities. ### The parties involved On one side, there is the agricultural world of the Po Valley, which asks for more water to combat summer drought and avoid the risk of losing the harvest. On the other side, the lakeside municipalities of Lake Maggiore fear flooding, i.e., ending up flooded, with consequences for the safety of the inhabitants, the protection of natural ecosystems, and the activities of tourism entrepreneurs. ### The decision The new maximum level is set by the Po River Basin Authority, a public Italian body based in Parma. The Basin Authority announced that from next summer the maximum level of the lake can be raised to 1.5 meters above zero hydrometric, i.e., 25 centimeters more than the period 2015-2021. ### The concerns Not everyone agrees with this decision. The lakeside municipalities fear flooding, damage to the lakeside resorts, the reduction of the surfaces of the beaches and the areas of the lake normally usable, and hence the decline in tourist attractiveness. ### The management of the lake The level of Lake Maggiore is physically regulated by the Ticino Consortium, which manages the Miorina Dam in Golasecca, in the province of Varese. Dettagli operativi

Implications for cross-border workers The rise in the level of Lake Maggiore could have several implications for cross-border workers living and working in the area. Firstly, there could be an impact on tourism, a sector in which many cross-border workers are employed. If the beaches and lakeside areas normally accessible were to decrease, there could be a reduction in tourist attractiveness, with consequent repercussions on employment. ### Environmental concerns Another aspect to consider is the environmental impact. The rise in the lake's level could indeed have negative consequences on natural ecosystems, such as the Bolle di Magadino and the reed beds in the area between Verbania and Dormelletto. This could also have repercussions on agriculture and water quality, important aspects for cross-border workers living and working in the area. ### Safety measures The Basin Authority has announced that the rise in the lake's level will be carried out in full compliance with hydraulic and environmental safety regulations for all areas involved in the measure. However, concerns remain, especially regarding the safety of the inhabitants and the protection of natural ecosystems. ### Possible solutions To mitigate the negative effects of the rise in the lake's level, various measures could be taken. For example, drainage infrastructure could be improved and measures could be taken to protect the most vulnerable areas. Furthermore, monitoring and research activities could be promoted to better understand the effects of the rise in the lake's level. ### Reactions of the Ticino authorities The Ticino authorities have expressed concern about the rise in the lake's level.
