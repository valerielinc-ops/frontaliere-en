Berna approves burial with pets: new rules for 2026

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 27 April 2026

From 2026, in Berna it will be possible to be buried with your pet in dedicated areas. Here's what changes and how it works

Contesto

In brief - New cemetery regulation approved in Bern - Burial with pets in dedicated areas - Pets interred in urns ## Key facts - What: New cemetery regulation - When: April 24, 2026 - Where: Bern - Who: Bern municipal council - Amount: Not specified The Bern municipal council approved on Thursday the new cemetery regulation that allows being buried with one's pet. Dedicated areas, called 'Person with pet', will be created specifically for this new burial method. Pets must be interred in urns. This decision is part of a complete revision of funeral regulations that modernizes cemetery provisions. Among other novelties, people will be able to be buried in sections dedicated to minors up to the age of 18, whereas the previous limit was 14 years. All the planned changes have received cross-party consensus, except for a proposal by the faction composed of Alternative Left, Labour Party, and Alternative Forum, which asked to ban the deliberate killing of animals to be buried with a person. The Council deemed that the cemetery regulation was not the appropriate venue to enact such a provision. The Municipality was outvoted regarding competencies: in the future, it will be the Municipal Council that decides on major renovations and the suppression of cemeteries, a prerogative that the executive would have preferred to maintain. The Municipal Council unanimously approved the completely revised regulation, waiving a second examination of the matter. ### Complete revision of funeral regulations The new regulation stems from a postulate by the Green Liberals that the Municipal Council had accepted in March 2021.
