In brief - Cottarelli meets students of the Liceo Manzoni in Varese - Focus on geopolitical tensions and global scenarios - Analysis based on the book 'Senza giri di parole' ## Key facts - What: Meeting with students of the Liceo Economico Sociale - When: Thursday, May 7, 2026, from 9:15 AM to 10:45 AM - Where: Aula Magna of Liceo Manzoni, via Morselli, Varese - Who: Carlo Cottarelli, economist and professor at the Catholic University of Milan - Cost: Not specified Economist Carlo Cottarelli returns to the classrooms of Varese for a meeting with the students of the Liceo Economico Sociale 'A. On Thursday, May 7, 2026, from 9:15 AM to 10:45 AM, Cottarelli will meet with the fifth-grade classes in the Aula Magna of via Morselli, as part of the PESES project promoted by the Catholic University. The meeting, organized by Professor Stefania Barile, will focus on the first chapter of Cottarelli's book 'Senza giri di parole', dedicated to global economic, political, and military tensions. The guiding theme will be the complexity of global balances, from the rivalry between the United States and China to the Thucydides Trap, to the Doomsday Clock and the daily repercussions of geopolitical and commercial tensions. School principal Francesco Maieron stated: 'We want our students to not be passive spectators of change. The PESES project and the availability of Professor Cottarelli offer a unique opportunity to transform economics from a subject of study into a tool for interpreting the world.' ### Context and implications The meeting with Cottarelli at the Liceo Manzoni takes place in a context of growing interest in global geopolitical and economic issues. • ## Analysis of Geopolitical Tensions The meeting with Carlo Cottarelli at the Liceo Manzoni in Varese offers an in-depth analysis of current geopolitical tensions. Cottarelli, a renowned economist and professor at the Catholic University of Milan, will focus on the first chapter of his book 'Senza giri di parole', dedicated to global economic, political, and military tensions. Students will have the opportunity to understand the power dynamics among major powers, such as the rivalry between the United States and China, and the global economic challenges. ### The Thucydides Trap One of the central themes of the meeting will be the Thucydides Trap, a concept that describes the risk of conflict related to the rise of a new power. Cottarelli will analyze how this dynamic can influence global balances and the daily impact on national economies. Students will better understand the implications of these tensions and how to address them in a global economic context. ### The Doomsday Clock Another crucial theme will be the Doomsday Clock, a symbol of geopolitical and commercial tensions. Cottarelli will explain how this indicator reflects global threats and the challenges that nations must face. Students will delve into the daily impact of these tensions and how they influence national and global economies. ### Tools to Interpret the Present The meeting will not be limited to a catalog of pre-packaged answers but will provide students with tools to learn how to read the present.
