Baume-Schneider: healthcare, AVS and immigration in 1st May speech

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 2 May 2026

Federal Councillor Elisabeth Baume-Schneider spoke about healthcare, AVS and relations with Europe in her 1st May speech in Liestal.

Contesto

In brief - Baume-Schneider criticizes the National Council over the nursing care initiative - Warns of the risks of the SVP's 'No to a Switzerland of 10 million!' initiative for relations with the EU - Stresses the need to fund the 13th AVS pension without eroding reserves ## Key facts - What: Elisabeth Baume-Schneider's May 1st speech - When: May 1st, 2026 - Where: Liestal (BL) - Who: Federal Councillor Elisabeth Baume-Schneider (SP) - Amount: Eight women victims of domestic violence in Switzerland in 2026 Federal Councillor Elisabeth Baume-Schneider (SP) used her Labour Day speech in Liestal (BL) to address crucial topics such as healthcare, AVS, and relations with Europe. The Jurassian launched an appeal to defend purchasing power and social progress, stating that these must be 'conquered and sometimes defended with all our strength'. Baume-Schneider sharply criticized the National Council for 'watering down' the moderate proposals of the Federal Council on the nursing care initiative. 'The already moderate proposals of the Federal Council have been considerably watered down', she declared, adding that healthcare personnel might perceive these decisions as an affront. The minister called for mobilization to achieve improvements in the sector. Another central point of the speech was the SVP's initiative 'No to a Switzerland of 10 million!', up for vote on June 14th. Baume-Schneider described the initiative as 'dangerous', emphasizing that it would put bilateral relations with Brussels and the free movement of persons at risk. She warned that limiting immigration would worsen the shortage of qualified personnel, especially in the healthcare sector, which is heavily dependent on foreign workers. Implications for cross-border workers Baume-Schneider's speech has significant implications for cross-border workers employed in Switzerland, particularly in the healthcare sector. The criticism of the UDC initiative 'No to a Switzerland of 10 million!' is relevant for cross-border workers, as a restriction on immigration could directly impact their ability to work in Switzerland. ### Healthcare sector The Swiss healthcare sector is highly dependent on foreign personnel, including cross-border workers. A reduction in immigration could exacerbate the shortage of qualified staff, with negative consequences for the quality of care. Baume-Schneider emphasized that 'less immigration does not mean a lesser need for care. It means less personnel,' a warning that should be seriously considered by cross-border workers in this sector. ### AHV and pensions The funding of the thirteenth AHV pension is a crucial issue for cross-border workers who contribute to the Swiss pension system. Baume-Schneider's concern about the weakening of the AHV and the possible increase in retirement age is relevant to all workers, including cross-border workers. It is important to monitor developments in this area to understand how they might affect their pension rights. ### Equality and fundamental rights Baume-Schneider's emphasis on equality as a fundamental right is a call to ensure equal opportunities and respect for all workers, regardless of their nationality.
