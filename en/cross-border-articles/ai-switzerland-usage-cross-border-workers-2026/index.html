AI in Switzerland: over a third of adults use it (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 7 May 2026

In Switzerland, 37.8% of adults use AI tools, surpassing the global average of 17.8%.

Contesto

In brief - Over a third of Swiss adults use AI - Switzerland ranks 14th in the world - AI is used for work, learning, and creativity ## Key facts - What: Use of artificial intelligence tools - When: First quarter of 2026 - Where: Switzerland - Who: Adults aged 15 to 64 - Amount: 37.8% in Switzerland vs 17.8% global average In Switzerland, the use of generative artificial intelligence (AI) is on the rise. According to the 'Global AI Diffusion' report published by Microsoft, in the first quarter of 2026, over one in three adults (37.8%) uses AI-related tools. This places Switzerland in 14th position in the world ranking, far exceeding the global average of 17.8%. The United Arab Emirates leads the ranking with 70.1%, followed by Singapore (63.4%) and Norway (48.6%). The adoption of AI in Switzerland is steadily increasing, from 34.8% at the end of 2025 to 37.8% in the first quarter of 2026. This trend reflects the increasingly widespread integration of AI in daily life, both for work and creative purposes. For example, the use of AI tools for data analysis can help cross-border workers make more informed decisions and improve their work performance. ### Challenges for Cross-Border Workers Despite the opportunities offered by AI, cross-border workers may face some challenges.
