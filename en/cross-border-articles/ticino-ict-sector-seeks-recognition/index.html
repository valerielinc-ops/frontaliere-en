ICT Ticino: 18,000 workers seek recognition

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 30 April 2026

Ticino's ICT sector, with 18,000 employees, asks for representation in the Tripartite Commission. The State Council acknowledges its strategic importance.

Contesto

In brief - ICT sector with 18,000 workers in Ticino - Request to join the Tripartite Commission - State Council recognizes strategic importance ## Key facts - What: Request for representation in the Tripartite Commission - When: April 29, 2026 - Where: Bellinzona - Who: ATED, TECH, ICT and Digital Ticino umbrella association - Amount: 14,000-18,000 employees in the ICT sector The ICT and digital sector in Ticino, increasingly central to economic development, remains underrepresented in institutional mechanisms related to the labor market. ATED, the TECH, ICT and Digital Ticino umbrella association, has raised this issue and initiated direct discussions with the State Council. In its official response, the State Council recognized the strategic role of digital transformation and ICT technologies for the development of the canton, emphasizing their impact on competitiveness, innovation, and service quality. Regarding ATED's request to join the Tripartite Commission, the State Council noted that nominations 'follow the current legal framework and come from employer representative organizations.' Nevertheless, the association was invited to the commission's meeting on April 29, 2026, where it presented the Ticino ICT sector. President Luca Mauriello and Director Cristina Boggia Giotto reiterated a key point: 'ICT is now a cross-cutting infrastructure of the economy, but it is not adequately represented in the places where working conditions are defined.' ### The complexity of the ICT sector The ICT sector in Ticino comprises over 200 distinct roles, often reduced to a simplified view. Dettagli operativi

Analysis of Implications The request by ATED to be represented in the Tripartite Commission is a crucial step for the ICT sector in Ticino. The Tripartite Commission is a body that brings together representatives of employers, workers, and the government, playing a key role in defining working conditions and economic policies. ATED's presence could lead to better representation of the specific needs of the ICT sector, which is growing rapidly and increasingly central to the Ticino economy. ### Comparison with the Previous Situation Before ATED's request, the ICT sector in Ticino had no formal representation in the Tripartite Commission. This led to several challenges, including the lack of adequate regulatory tools and difficulty in attracting and retaining qualified talent. ATED's presence could help overcome these challenges, ensuring the sector's voices are heard and policies are aligned with the market's real needs. ### Future Scenarios If ATED's request is accepted, several positive changes for the ICT sector in Ticino could be observed. These include the revision of the Normal Work Contract (CNL) for informatics, the introduction of more favorable policies for attracting talent, and the creation of a more competitive work environment. However, the process of reviewing regulations and policies could be long and complex, requiring the involvement of all stakeholders. ### Comparative Table: Current vs. Future Situation | Aspect | Current Situation | Future Situation (if request is accepted) | |---------|------------------|-----------------------------------------------------| | Representation in the Tripartite Commission | Absent | Present | | Alignment of CNL with the sector's real value | Inadequate | Adequate | | Attraction of Talent | Difficult | Facilitated |...

Frequently Asked Questions
What is the importance of ICT sector representation in the Tripartite Commission?
Representation of the ICT sector in the Tripartite Commission is crucial to ensure that the sector's voices are heard and that policies are aligned with the real needs of the market. This can lead to better definition of working conditions and more favorable policies for attracting talent.
What are the critical issues highlighted by the ICT sector in Ticino?
Among the critical issues highlighted by the ICT sector in Ticino are the complexity of the sector, which includes over 200 distinct roles, often reduced to a simplified view, and the current Standard Work Contract (CNL) for IT, considered not aligned with the real value of the sector.
What are the potential implications for cross-border workers in the ICT sector in Ticino?
Cross-border workers in the ICT sector in Ticino could benefit from better representation and more favorable working conditions. The revision of the CNL could lead to more adequate minimum wages and greater competitiveness of the sector, improving career opportunities and quality of life for workers.
