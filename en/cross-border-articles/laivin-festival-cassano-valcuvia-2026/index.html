LaivIn 2026: 300 students for theatre festival (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 25 May 2026

From May 26 to 28, the LaivIn Plus Festival will bring 300 young people to Cassano Valcuvia for 12 shows and scattered theatre workshops.

Contesto

In brief - 300 students in 3 days for 12 theater performances and workshops - Festival organized by Teatro Periferico with Alchemilla - Participation of high schools and unaccompanied foreign minors ## Key facts - What: LaivIn Plus theater and workshop festival - When: From May 26 to 28, 2026 - Where: Cassano Valcuvia and Mantua - Who: Teatro Periferico, Alchemilla, Fondazione Cariplo - Number: 300 participating students - Where: Cassano Valcuvia, a town of 650 inhabitants on the border with Switzerland The well-being of the young people and their growth, along with that of the entire community, are the goal and at the same time the added value of the LaivIn Plus Festival, scheduled for May 26 to 28 in Mantua and Cassano Valcuvia. Paola Manfredi, artistic director of Teatro Periferico, which organizes the Valcuvia edition of the Festival with Alchemilla, tells us about the vitality and strengths of the event, which will bring 300 young people to the village over three days for 12 theater performances and workshops. Teatro Periferico has been entrusted with the task of organizing this Festival, which takes place simultaneously in Mantua with Teatro Magro, directly by Fondazione Cariplo and the project leader Alchemilla. Cassano Valcuvia, a town of 650 inhabitants on the border with Switzerland, is not perhaps a marginal place, but certainly a peripheral one, in an area also poorly served by public transport. However, the choice of the promoters of LaivIn is the same as that of Teatro Periferico, i.e., to support cultural centers in peripheral areas because they are important in ensuring community growth, social cohesion, and participation. The participation of 300 students from various high schools in Lombardy and Piedmont, along with unaccompanied minors and young people with disabilities, creates an inclusive and stimulating environment. For cross-border workers living and working in Ticino, such initiatives can offer opportunities for integration and cultural exchange.
