Frequently Asked Questions
How can I protect my garage from car theft?
Garages must be equipped with a security system that includes at least one electronic door locking system, a video surveillance camera system, and an alarm system that is activated in the event of an intrusion. Additionally, it is important to monitor the access keys to ensure that only authorized individuals can access the garage.
What is the basic regulation for garage safety in Switzerland?
The Swiss Federal Act on Garage Safety (GSA) of 22 April 2015.
What are the penalties for those who do not comply with the safety regulations of garages in Ticino?
In Ticino, failure to install mandatory security systems (cameras, alarm, electronic locking) can result in administrative penalties of up to 5,000 Swiss francs for the garage owner. In the event of subsequent theft, the insurance company may refuse compensation if cantonal regulations are not complied with.
How does key monitoring work in Swiss garages to prevent theft?
Swiss garages must adopt key tracking systems (e.g. electronic registers or name badges) that record who picks up or returns the keys. This reduces the risk of unauthorised access: in the event of theft, the authorities can quickly trace the person responsible using the access data.
What changes for cross-border commuters who work in Switzerland but park their car in Italy? What rules do they have to comply with?
Cross-border commuters who park in Italy must comply with local regulations (e.g. locking with a padlock or approved anti-theft system). In Switzerland, if the garage is owned by a company, federal rules apply; In the event of theft, the responsibility falls on the garage owner or the insurance company, as the case may be.
