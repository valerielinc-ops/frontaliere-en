Social farming in Ticino: strategies and inclusion (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 7 May 2026

The Curare Cura project concludes its focus groups with strategies for social farming in Ticino, involving operators and beneficiaries.

Contesto

In brief - Focus group concluded on the Curare Cura project - Strategies for social agriculture in Ticino - Involvement of operators and beneficiaries ## Key facts - What: Conclusion of the third Focus Group of the Curare Cura project - When: 6 May 2026 - Where: Chiasso, Canton Ticino - Who: Social operators and beneficiaries of social agriculture - Amount: Not yet specified The third Focus Group of the Curare Cura project, part of the Interreg cross-border cooperation program, has come to an end. The meeting, held in Chiasso, saw the participation of social operators and beneficiaries of social agriculture in Canton Ticino. During the day, experiences and viewpoints were shared on the inclusion paths activated in the territory. At the heart of the debate was the impact of socio-occupational activities on the quality of life of people in vulnerable situations, with particular attention to the changes observed in relational, emotional, and work aspects. ### Experiences and benefits Among the topics addressed were the categories of beneficiaries showing the greatest potential for growth through inclusion measures, including people with disabilities, at-risk youth, the unemployed, and asylum seekers. Ample space was devoted to the tools used daily by operators and institutions to support inclusion paths. The focus then shifted to the future, questioning the role of social agriculture in preventing discomfort, as well as responding to already emerging needs. ### Future prospects Among the open questions is the possibility of developing new intervention models that integrate social agriculture and welfare in the Ticino context, also identifying any correctives to the current setup. Practical Analysis The Curare Cura project represents a significant step towards social and work integration in the Canton of Ticino. Social farming, as a tool for inclusion, offers numerous opportunities for cross-border workers in the agricultural sector. Socio-occupational activities can improve the quality of life of people in vulnerable situations, offering them an inclusive and supportive work environment. ### Comparison with the Previous Situation Before the launch of the Curare Cura project, opportunities for social and work integration for cross-border workers in the agricultural sector were limited. With the introduction of this project, a new scenario has opened up that promotes integration and social cohesion. The experiences shared during the focus groups highlighted the importance of an integrated approach between social farming and welfare, offering new perspectives for the future. ### Concrete Scenarios A concrete scenario could be the integration of new intervention models that combine social farming and welfare. This could include the creation of specific training programs for cross-border workers, providing them with the necessary skills to work efficiently and inclusively. Furthermore, the integration of psychological and social support tools could further improve the quality of life of cross-border workers. ### Concrete Procedures For cross-border workers interested in participating in social farming programs, it is important to follow some concrete procedures.
