Artificial Intelligence at the University of Varese: a morning of studies and comparisons

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 27 April 2026

The University of Insubria is organizing an event on artificial intelligence on May 6, 2026, with expert interventions and new guidelines for the University.

Contesto

In brief - IA@Insubria event on May 6, 2026 in Varese - Free participation upon registration - Expert talks and new guidelines for the University ## Key facts - What: Event on artificial intelligence - When: May 6, 2026, from 9:30 AM to 1:30 PM - Where: Aula Magna, via Ravasi, Varese - Who: University of Insubria, experts and institutional representatives - Cost: Free, upon registration The University of Insubria has announced an event dedicated to artificial intelligence, which will take place on May 6, 2026, at the Aula Magna in via Ravasi, Varese. The initiative, titled IA@Insubria, is aimed not only at faculty and PhD students but also at citizens interested in understanding the impact of new technologies. The event, which will run from 9:30 AM to 1:30 PM, aims to illustrate how artificial intelligence is transforming research, teaching, and knowledge transfer within the various departments of the University. ### Program and guests The proceedings will open with institutional greetings from Rector Maria Pierro and Vice Rector Umberto Piarulli. Among the guests are also Varese Mayor Davide Galimberti and Councilor Stefano Malerba, testifying to the link between the university and local administration. Of particular relevance will be the contribution of Francesca Paola Carli, representative of the AI Office of the European Union, who will intervene remotely to frame the topic in a broader normative and strategic context. ### Thematic sessions The core of the morning will be divided into two technical sessions featuring researchers from different departments. Implications for the territory The IA@Insubria event represents a unique opportunity for the Insubrian territory and beyond. Artificial intelligence is rapidly becoming a key element in various sectors, from the economy to medicine, and the University of Insubria is positioning itself as a fundamental player in promoting these technologies. The participation of local institutional representatives, such as the mayor of Varese Davide Galimberti and councilor Stefano Malerba, underscores the importance of creating synergies between the academic world and institutions to foster technological and economic development in the territory. ### International comparison The presence of Francesca Paola Carli, representative of the AI Office of the European Union, adds an additional level of relevance to the event. Her intervention will allow local initiatives to be framed within the European regulatory and strategic context, offering a broader and more global vision of the challenges and opportunities related to artificial intelligence. This international comparison is crucial to ensure that the guidelines and projects of the University of Insubria are aligned with the best practices and European regulations. ### Multidisciplinarity and innovation One of the most interesting aspects of the event is the multidisciplinary nature of the interventions. The participation of researchers from different departments, from the hard sciences to the humanities and legal disciplines, demonstrates how artificial intelligence is transforming every field of knowledge. Frequently Asked Questions
Who can participate in the IA@Insubria event?
The event is open to everyone, including faculty, PhD students, undergraduate students, and interested members of the public. Participation is free upon registration on the Eventbrite platform.
What are the new guidelines presented during the event?
The new university guidelines on artificial intelligence will be presented by Davide Tosi. These guidelines aim to guide the use of artificial intelligence technologies in university life, ensuring an innovative and responsible approach.
What topics are covered during the technical sessions?
The technical sessions cover a wide range of topics, from biotechnology to medicine, from economics to human sciences. Each department of the University of Insubria will contribute with specific interventions, offering a comprehensive overview of the applications of artificial intelligence.
