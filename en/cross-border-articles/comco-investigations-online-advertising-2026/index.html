COMCO investigates illegal online ad deals

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 30 April 2026

The Swiss Competition Commission opens two investigations into search engines for alleged illegal agreements between travel companies and online casinos.

Contesto

In brief - COMCO opens two investigations into illicit agreements in online advertising - Sectors involved: travel and online casinos - Possible harmful effects for consumers ## Key facts - What: Investigations into advertising via search engines - When: April 30, 2026 - Where: Switzerland - Who: Competition Commission (COMCO) - Amount: Not specified The Competition Commission (COMCO) has opened two investigations into advertising through search engines. The first concerns the travel sector and the second the online casino sector. According to information received through self-disclosures, several companies in the travel sector and several online casinos may have agreed not to compete on major search engines, refraining from bidding on keywords related to competing brands. This approach could constitute an illicit agreement and have harmful effects for consumers, indicates COMCO. Indeed, search engine results may have been influenced to the point of making it more difficult to compare competitors. ### Travel sector The first investigation concerns three companies that offer travel packages in Switzerland. The companies involved may have limited competition through illicit agreements, negatively affecting consumers. COMCO is investigating to determine the extent of these agreements and any potential sanctions to be applied. ### Online casino sector The second investigation involves almost all online casinos in the Confederation. In this case as well, companies may have agreed not to compete on certain keywords, limiting transparency and competition in the sector. Frequently Asked Questions
What are illicit agreements in online advertising?
Illicit agreements in online advertising are agreements between competing companies to limit competition on specific keywords in search engines. This can negatively affect the search results for users, making it harder to compare offers.
What are the implications for consumers?
The implications for consumers include a reduction in transparency and competition in the market, making it harder to compare offers and find the best conditions. This can lead to higher prices and a reduction in the quality of services offered.
What can consumers do to contribute to investigations?
Consumers can report suspicious behavior to the COMCO, compare offers from different companies, and stay informed about the latest news in the field of online advertising. This can help ensure a fairer and more transparent market.
