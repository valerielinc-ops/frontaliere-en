New laws and national strategy against domestic violence

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 27 April 2026

Confederation and Cantons announce progress but more effective tools are needed. New laws coming in 2027.

In brief - New national strategy against domestic violence for spring 2027 - Legislative package to strengthen victim protection in 2027 - Extension of prevention campaign 'Equality prevents violence' ## Key facts - What: New national strategy against domestic violence - When: Spring 2027 - Where: Switzerland - Who: Confederation and Cantons - Amount: Not yet specified The 'roadmap' against domestic violence launched five years ago has brought concrete progress, but the numbers remain alarming. In Bern, during a press conference, representatives of the Confederation and the Cantons reviewed the situation. As of May 1st, the short number 142 will be active, a national helpline for victims of domestic and sexual violence. Uniform standards for managing threats have been introduced, and electronic monitoring of potential aggressors has been enhanced. Federal Councillor Beat Jans announced that at the beginning of 2027, a legislative package will be submitted to the Federal Council to strengthen victim protection in civil and procedural law. Also in the works is a revision of the weapons law and a new national strategy against domestic, sexualized, and gender-based violence, scheduled for spring 2027. Dettagli operativi

Analysis of the new measures The new measures announced by the Confederation and the Cantons represent a significant step forward in the fight against domestic violence. The 142 helpline, active since May 1st, offers immediate support to victims of domestic and sexual violence. This service is available 24/7 and offers assistance in multiple languages, making it accessible to a wider audience. The uniform standards for managing threats and electronic monitoring of potential aggressors are additional tools aimed at protecting victims and preventing new episodes of violence. These tools have been introduced to ensure a more effective and coordinated response to domestic violence situations. ### Practical implications for cross-border workers For cross-border workers residing in Ticino and working in Italy, these new measures represent an opportunity to access more effective and coordinated assistance services. The 142 helpline will be an important point of reference for those in need of immediate support. Furthermore, the new national strategy and legislative revisions aim to create a safer and more protected environment for all victims of domestic violence, regardless of their nationality or residence status. ### Comparison with the previous situation Before the introduction of these new measures, victims of domestic violence in Switzerland had access to less coordinated and less effective assistance services. The new 142 helpline and uniform standards for managing threats represent a significant improvement over the past. The line is available 24 hours a day and offers assistance in various languages. For the electronic monitoring of potential aggressors, the competent authorities will follow standardized procedures to ensure the safety of the victims. ### Useful tools For further information and support, cross-border workers can use the salary calculator to plan their finances and access useful resources for their safety and well-being. What is the phone number for the domestic violence helpline?
The phone number for the domestic violence helpline is 142. This line is available 24/7 and offers immediate support in multiple languages.
What are the new measures announced by the Confederation and the Cantons?
The new measures announced include the activation of the short number 142 for the helpline, uniform standards for managing threats, electronic monitoring of potential aggressors, a legislative package to strengthen the protection of victims in civil and criminal law, a revision of the weapons law, and a new national strategy against domestic, sexualized, and gender-based violence.

The new national strategy against domestic, sexualized, and gender-based violence will be available in spring 2027.
