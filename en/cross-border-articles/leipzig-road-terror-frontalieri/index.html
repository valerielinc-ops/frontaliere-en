The Leipzig hit-and-run driver had already foreshadowed his act in April. According to the Zeit, the 33-year-old German had written in a chat message to his wife that he had 'not driven a car among the crowd for a long time'. At the newspaper's request, a spokesperson for the Leipzig police confirmed that the message was in the possession of the authorities, but 'only on the evening of the day of the incident'. It would have emerged during the subsequent investigations into the wild ride. Before the incident, the man and his wife were already living separately. According to the Zeit's research, she had apparently already turned to social services in March. In April, she also filed several complaints against her husband, for threats, defamation, and insult. At least once, the man would have threatened to kill other family members. As several people close to the investigation told the Zeit, the man would be suffering from psychosis and would have himself spoken of hearing delusional voices. After sending the chat message, on April 17, the 33-year-old voluntarily went to a psychiatric hospital. On April 29, he left the clinic again. The man, a resident of Leipzig, on Monday afternoon in the city center sped through a busy pedestrian area with a car. He ran over several people: a 63-year-old woman and a 77-year-old man died.
