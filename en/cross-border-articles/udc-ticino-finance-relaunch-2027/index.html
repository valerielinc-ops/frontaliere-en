UDC Ticino: 'Ready to Revitalize Finance and Economy' (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 30 May 2026

The UDC expresses its intention to lead the Department of Finance and Economy to restore public accounts and revitalize Ticino.

Contesto

In brief - The UDC Ticino declares itself ready to lead the DFE starting from April 2027. - Goal: Restore public finances and relaunch the Cantonal economy. - Criticism of Christian Vitta’s political record, who is stepping down after 12 years. - Need for greater vision and courage for reforms. ## Key facts - What: UDC Ticino’s announcement to lead the DFE. - When: April 2027. - Where: Canton Ticino. - Who: UDC Ticino. - Amount: Not specified. Christian Vitta’s announcement not to run again for the Council of State marks a new phase for the Canton Ticino. The UDC acknowledges Vitta’s institutional correctness and mediation skills but emphasizes the need for greater vision and courage to tackle the necessary reforms for Ticino. According to the UDC, Vitta’s political record cannot be merely celebratory. After 12 years leading the Department of Finance and Economy (DFE), the party believes it is time for a change of pace. In April 2027, the UDC states it is ready to take charge of the DFE to contribute to the relaunch of the Canton. ### Criticisms and proposals The UDC asserts that this is not the time for party calculations or half-measures. Courage, responsibility, and clear choices are needed. The party criticizes the governing parties for too often dismissing, delaying, or watering down democentric proposals, which have been deemed valid and partly endorsed by the people. > 'This is not the time for party calculations or half-measures. Courage, responsibility, and clear choices are needed.' The UDC wants to move from words to action, restoring order to public finances and relaunching the Ticino economy. The goal is to make Ticino attractive again for companies and investors.

Dettagli operativi

Why is it important for cross-border workers? The economic stability of the Canton of Ticino is crucial for cross-border workers who commute to Switzerland while residing in Italy. A financially sound canton ensures greater job opportunities, investments, and services that directly impact the daily lives of commuters. ### What changes for cross-border workers? A boost to public finances could lead to improvements in infrastructure, such as roads, public transport, and essential services. This translates into greater efficiency in travel times between Italy and Switzerland, reducing the time lost in daily commutes. ### Possible scenarios - Scenario 1: If the Swiss People's Party (SVP) succeeds in balancing public accounts, cross-border workers could benefit from a reduction in tax pressure, with positive impacts on net pay. - Scenario 2: A revitalisation of the Ticino economy could attract new companies, creating job opportunities for both residents and cross-border workers. ### Comparison with the current situation Currently, Ticino's public debt is rising, which limits the canton's room for manoeuvre in terms of investments and reforms. The SVP proposes a change of pace to reverse this trend, with the goal of making Ticino more competitive at national and international level. ## Recommended tools For an updated estimate, use the net salary calculator and the CHF-EUR exchange comparator.
