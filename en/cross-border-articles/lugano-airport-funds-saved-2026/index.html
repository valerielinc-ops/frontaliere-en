Operational details

The issue of federal funding for smaller airports is regulated by Swiss legislation, which provides contributions to ensure high standards of safety and operation, even for airports with traffic volumes that cannot be compared to international hubs like Zurich or Geneva. Specifically, the federal contribution for Lugano Airport currently amounts to about 5 million francs per year, a sum primarily intended to cover security costs, airport infrastructure maintenance, and air traffic control. The savings plan, presented by the Federal Council in an attempt to reduce public expenditure, would have significantly cut these funds, putting at risk not only the operational safety of the airport but also its ability to attract flights and investments. The practical effects of such a cut would have included staff reductions, decreased investment in technology, and difficulties in maintaining runways and facilities. The 2026 legislation on airport financing requires that, to maintain operational status, every airport must meet safety standards certified by the Federal Office of Civil Aviation (FOCA). In Ticino, the Department of Territory and airport authorities work closely to meet these requirements, involving institutions such as SUPSI and USI for technical studies and innovations in the aeronautical field. The National Council’s vote thus confirmed the strategic importance of Lugano Airport, recognizing that an indiscriminate cut would have had repercussions also economically and in terms of employment. Canton Ticino benefits from a significant economic spin-off linked to airport activities, involving not only direct employees but also transport, hotel, and commercial services.
