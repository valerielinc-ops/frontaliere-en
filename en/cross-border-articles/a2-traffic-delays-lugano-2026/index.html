Di Valerie Linc · Published on 30 April 2026

Nightmare morning on the A2 northbound with delays up to one hour and forty minutes. Accidents and police checks complicate traffic

In brief - Delays up to 1h40 on the A2 northbound - Lane reduction before San Nicolao - Accidents at Vedeggio-Cassarate and police checks ## Key facts - What: Lane reduction and accidents - When: Morning of April 30, 2026 - Where: Between Chiasso and Lugano Nord - Who: Police and road assistance - Impact: Delays up to 1h40 The morning of April 30, 2026, was a nightmare for drivers heading north on the A2. The queue began as early as 6:00 AM, right after Mendrisio, due to a lane reduction before the San Nicolao tunnel. The TCS confirmed disruptions between Chiasso and Lugano Nord, with delays of up to one hour and forty minutes. The primary cause of the significant delay was the reduction of lanes from two to one. Before the Melide dam bridge, a police patrol checked the road surface for possible debris or objects on the carriageway. A scooter was towed away by road assistance, and absorbent material for polluting liquids was visible on the asphalt. The queue towards Lugano, which started earlier than usual, extended from Chiasso with the onset of rush hour. Traffic was also congested on the cantonal road, starting from Chiasso heading north, in a morning made even more complicated by unfavorable weather. An accident also occurred at the Vedeggio-Cassarate roundabout, with a scooter on the ground and a police patrol managing the traffic.
