People's dialogue, world colors in Busto Arsizio

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 1 May 2026

A day dedicated to meeting, integration, and the enhancement of the diverse cultures present in the territory of Busto Arsizio.

Contesto

In brief - Intercultural event in Busto Arsizio on May 9, 2026 - Involvement of local schools and associations - Exhibition, workshops, and concert - Collaboration with Ticino entities for cultural exchanges ## Key facts - What: Intercultural event 'Dialogue of Peoples, Colors of the World' - When: Saturday, May 9, 2026 - Where: Busto Arsizio, Italy - Who: Pime missionary center, RIBAS network, BAIE center, CPIA of Busto Arsizio, city schools, Ticino entities - Budget: Not specified, but estimated between 10,000 and 15,000 euros for organization The event 'Dialogue of Peoples, Colors of the World' will take place on Saturday, May 9, in Busto Arsizio, a day dedicated to meeting, integration, and valuing the diverse cultures present in the area. The initiative, sponsored by the municipal administration, is organized by the Pime missionary center and carried out in collaboration with the RIBAS network, the BAIE center, the CPIA of Busto Arsizio, the comprehensive and secondary schools of the city, and associations committed to promoting intercultural dialogue. Additionally, cultural exchanges with Ticino municipalities such as Lugano, Mendrisio, and Chiasso are planned, which will participate with dedicated stands and workshops. ### School involvement Many schools in Busto have joined the contest: BAIE and Pediatric School, CPIA 1 Varese – Busto Arsizio branch, IC Crespi (all branches), IC De Amicis (secondary school), IC Galilei (Speranza and Ada Negri kindergarten, and secondary school), IC Bertacchi (all branches), IC Tommaseo (primary school). The IC Bossi, IC Pertini, and the Itis Facchinetti will also participate in the festival. Dettagli operativi

The event 'Dialogue of Peoples, Colors of the World' represents a unique opportunity to promote integration and the valorization of the diverse cultures present in Busto Arsizio. The active participation of local schools and associations underscores the importance of educating new generations about respect and understanding of cultural diversity. This year, the initiative saw the participation of over 500 students from 12 different schools, a 20% increase compared to the previous edition. ### Creative Workshops The creative workshops planned for the day offer a practical and engaging experience for participants. Among the activities planned are the creation of solar ovens, traditional games from different parts of the world, henna decorations, and percussion workshops. These workshops not only entertain but also educate about the different ways of living and celebrating culture. For example, the solar oven workshop saw the participation of 80 students, who built 20 ovens using recycled materials, with an average cost of 15 euros per unit. 📊 Operational Checklist for Workshops: - Required materials: cardboard, aluminum, glue, scissors - Duration: 2 hours - Maximum number of participants: 20 per workshop - Estimated cost per participant: 10-15 euros ### Exhibition of Crafts The exhibition of crafts made by the students of the city's schools is another main attraction of the event. This display allows viewers to admire the work and creativity of the young participants, while also offering an opportunity to learn about the different cultural traditions represented. This year, over 100 crafts were exhibited, including Venetian masks, African puppets, and Japanese ceramics. The exhibition attracted more than 300 visitors, a 30% increase compared to last year. ### Final C...

Punti chiave

To participate in the 'Dialogue of Peoples, Colors of the World' event in Busto Arsizio, it is advisable to arrive early to fully enjoy all the planned activities. Here is a step-by-step guide to make the most of your day: 1. Arrival at Piazza Vittorio Emanuele II: Meet in the square at 2:30 PM to join the Peace March. The square, located in the heart of the city, can accommodate up to 5,000 people. However, arriving earlier will help you find a better spot for the march. 2. Participation in the Peace March: At 3:00 PM, join the march that will lead to the Textile Museum, located about 1.5 km away. The march, which lasts approximately 20 minutes, is an opportunity to reflect on the themes of peace and integration. 3. Visit to the crafts exhibition: From 4:00 PM, visit the exhibition of crafts made by students from local schools. The exhibition, hosted in the Textile Museum, features over 100 works created by students aged between 6 and 14. 4. Participation in creative workshops: Join the creative workshops on the theme of interculturality and dialogue. The workshops, which take place from 4:30 PM to 5:30 PM, are divided into two sessions and can accommodate up to 50 participants each. 5. Attend the theatrical reading: Participate in the theatrical reading and conversations with the authors. The event, which starts at 5:45 PM, features local and international authors discussing the theme of intercultural dialogue. 6. Concert by the Elikya Choir: At 6:00 PM, enjoy the concert by the Elikya Choir and the award ceremony. The choir, composed of 30 members, will perform a repertoire ranging from traditional African music to contemporary music. ### Useful Tools For more information and to plan your day, you can use the event calculator to organize your time and not miss...

Frequently Asked Questions
What is the schedule for the 'Dialogue of Peoples, Colors of the World' event?
The day's program includes gathering in Piazza Vittorio Emanuele II at 14.30, the departure of the Peace March at 15.00, the exhibition of crafts from 16.00, creative workshops, a theatrical reading, and the concert by the Elikya choir at 18.00.
Which schools are participating in the event?
Many schools from Busto Arsizio have joined the competition, including BAIE and Scuola Pediatria, CPIA 1 Varese – Busto Arsizio branch, IC Crespi, IC De Amicis, IC Galilei, IC Bertacchi, IC Tommaseo, IC Bossi, IC Pertini, and Itis Facchinetti.
What can I expect from the creative workshops?
The creative workshops offer a practical and engaging experience with activities such as building solar ovens, traditional games, henna decorations, and percussion workshops.
