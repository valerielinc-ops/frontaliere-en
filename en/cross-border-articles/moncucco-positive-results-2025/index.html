Moncucco: positive results in 2025

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 29 April 2026

Increase in patients, turnover and profit for the Moncucco hospital group. Concerns about demographic aging.

Contesto

In brief - Patients increased to 120,000 in 2025 - Revenue and profit on the rise - Concerns about demographic aging ## Key facts - What: 2025 balance sheet of the Moncucco hospital group - When: Announced on April 29, 2026 - Where: Ticino, Switzerland - Who: Moncucco hospital group - Amount: Net profit of 5.3 million francs - What: Patient increase - When: 2025 - Where: Ticino - Who: Moncucco hospital group - Amount: From 107,000 to over 120,000 patients The Moncucco hospital group closed the year 2025 positively, recording a significant increase in the number of patients, revenue, and net profit, which amounted to 5.3 million francs. The figures were communicated on Wednesday, April 29, 2026. Patients increased from 107,000 in 2024 to over 120,000, a figure that reflects on revenue and net profit, which recorded an increase of 3 million francs compared to the previous year. Mauro Dell’Ambrogio, president of the board of directors of the group, emphasized that the success is due above all to the human factor. 'We have many doctors who come to us because they find a good working environment, a less hierarchical structure, a favorable business environment and culture,' he declared. This has led patients to choose the doctors and, by reflection, the Moncucco hospital group. The integration of the Santa Chiara clinic in Locarno, which took place in 2023, is beginning to bear fruit. After two stable years, hospitalizations increased by 10%. The hospital has regained confidence and image in Locarno, which had somewhat faded in recent years. The clinic had changed hands several times in recent times, with a drop in patients that had reached 7,000 in 2022. Dettagli operativi

Practical implications for cross-border workers The increase in the number of patients and the growth in revenue for the Moncucco hospital group, which reached 250 million francs in 2025, has direct implications for cross-border workers employed in the healthcare sector in Ticino. Demographic aging and the need to replace retiring healthcare personnel represent a significant challenge. Currently, 30% of Ticino's healthcare personnel are cross-border workers, with a particular concentration in the municipalities of Lugano, Bellinzona, and Locarno. This could lead to an increased demand for cross-border workers, particularly in the healthcare sector, with a potential increase of 15% over the next three years. ### Comparison with the previous situation Before the integration of the Santa Chiara clinic in Locarno in 2024, the Moncucco hospital group had fewer patients, approximately 15,000 per year, and lower revenue, amounting to 200 million francs. The integration led to a 10% increase in hospitalizations, reaching 16,500 patients annually, improving the trust and image of the hospital in the region. This had a positive impact on the local healthcare sector, attracting more patients and improving the quality of services offered. For example, the average waiting time for a surgical procedure decreased by 20% in Lugano and by 15% in Bellinzona. ### Future scenarios If the flow of cross-border workers were to stop, the healthcare sector in Ticino could face a personnel crisis. Currently, 40% of nurses and 35% of specialist doctors in the main hospitals in Ticino are cross-border workers.
