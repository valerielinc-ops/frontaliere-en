Geneva hosts the Italian Research Day in the World, with a focus on Italo-Swiss scientific cooperation

Contesto

In brief - Italian researchers in Switzerland gather in Geneva - Focus on life sciences and Italo-Swiss collaboration - Event organized at Bracco in Plan-les-Ouates ## Key facts - What: Italian Research Day in the World - When: April 29, 2026 - Where: Plan-les-Ouates, Geneva - Who: Italian researchers in Switzerland - Attendance: Around 60 participants Geneva hosted on Tuesday, April 29, 2026, the ninth edition of the Italian Research Day in the World (GRIM). The event, which takes place annually around April 22, the anniversary of the birth of Rita Levi-Montalcini, Nobel Prize winner in medicine, brought together around 60 Italian researchers active in Switzerland. The initiative, organized at the Bracco site in Plan-les-Ouates, in the canton of Geneva, focused on life sciences and opportunities for collaboration between the Italian and Swiss scientific and health systems. ### Italo-Swiss scientific cooperation The GRIM highlights the contribution of the Italian scientific community abroad and the dialogue with the host countries. During the event, examples of applied research in the medical field were presented, with particular attention to the transfer of scientific results to clinical practice and patient care. The importance of stable transnational networks emerged, which allow addressing complex challenges in life sciences. ### The role of Italian training In her opening speech, the Italian Consul General in Geneva, Nicoletta Piccirillo, emphasized how the presence of a numerous and qualified community of Italian scientists significantly contributes to the Swiss research ecosystem. Frequently Asked Questions
What is the purpose of the Italian Research Day in the world?
The Italian Research Day in the world (GRIM) highlights the contribution of the Italian scientific community abroad and promotes dialogue with the host countries. The event is held annually around April 22, the anniversary of the birth of Rita Levi-Montalcini, Nobel Prize winner in medicine.
What are the opportunities for collaboration between Italy and Switzerland in the research sector?
Opportunities for collaboration between Italy and Switzerland in the research sector include the exchange of knowledge and skills between researchers from both countries, participation in stable transnational networks, and access to joint funding programs.
How can cross-border workers benefit from Italian-Swiss scientific cooperation?
Cross-border workers can benefit from Italian-Swiss scientific cooperation by participating in networking events, seeking joint funding, and collaborating with Swiss institutions. It is important to stay informed about the regulations related to cross-border work and funding opportunities for research.
