Valsolda: Record Investments for Safety and Tourism on Lake Ceresio (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 10 March 2026

Over 4 million euros invested to revitalize the shores and ensure safety and sustainable tourism development in Valsolda.

Contesto

Between 2021 and 2023, several targeted projects were completed, such as the redevelopment of the Albogasio parking lot and the restoration of the banks at San Mamete, benefiting both residents and tourists. The overall plan foresees investments exceeding 4 million euros, with strong focus on hydrogeological safety and the enhancement of tourism, key elements for an area that also looks toward the Ticino cross-border market. The Lombardy Region has provided decisive support for this plan, which aims to connect the two shores of the lake by creating a modern, integrated and sustainable tourist route linking historic villages, natural areas, and quality infrastructure. This is a strategic investment that will not only improve the tourist experience but also help prevent hydraulic and landslide risks, which are particularly relevant in a mountainous area like Ceresio. Dettagli operativi

From a technical perspective, the Ceresio Basin Authority’s plan is based on a comprehensive strategy that combines routine maintenance with extraordinary interventions and major infrastructure projects. With a total budget exceeding 4 million euros, the works have been planned to ensure a balance between environmental protection, safety, and tourism promotion. The reference regulations are the Lombardy regional laws on hydrogeological risk management, which require five-year programming of mitigation interventions and continuous monitoring. The redevelopment of the banks at San Mamete, for example, includes bank consolidation through naturalistic engineering works that respect local biodiversity, minimizing environmental impact. Additionally, the Albogasio parking lot upgrade was designed to facilitate visitor access, with sustainable solutions such as rainwater management and dedicated spaces for soft mobility. The tenders launched for the 2024-2025 period involve interventions worth several hundred thousand euros, with particular attention to transparent procedures and the involvement of local companies, also to promote employment and economic development in the area. The source does not specify whether these works will have direct impacts on cross-border regulations regarding safety or tourism, but it is clear that the project fits within a context of growing collaboration between Lombardy and Canton Ticino, especially in border areas like Valsolda and Mendrisio. From an infrastructural standpoint, the plan also includes the enhancement of pedestrian and cycling paths along the lake, creating safer and more functional connections between historic villages and natural areas—essential elements to attract tourism focused on quality and environmental sustainability....

Furthermore, the tourism redevelopment of Valsolda offers new economic development prospects for hospitality, restaurant, and commercial activities, which can benefit from increased visitor flows coming from Canton Ticino and across the border. The sustainability and modernization of infrastructure encourage quality tourism that is more environmentally respectful and capable of showcasing local excellence. This is a key factor to attract investments and create new jobs, a crucial topic for the Ceresio area, increasingly integrated with the Ticino economy. Frequently Asked Questions
What is the investment plan for Valsolda and how could it affect job opportunities?
The investment plan includes the redevelopment of the Albogasio car park, the arrangement of the banks in San Mamete and the creation of a modern and sustainable tourist route. These improvements could increase job opportunities for Ticino cross-border workers and cross-border workers, especially in the tourism and hospitality sectors.
What is the expected impact of the investment plan on Valsolda for Swiss cross-border commuters working in Italy?
The plan will improve the quality of life and could create new job opportunities, especially in proximity tourism and accommodation activities, thanks to tourism enhancement and greater connectivity.
What are the tax breaks for cross-border commuters who work in Italy but live in Switzerland after the redevelopment of Valsolda?
The tax benefits for cross-border commuters remain unchanged: taxation in Italy applies only to income produced on Italian territory, while in Switzerland taxes are paid on total income. The redevelopment does not introduce new specific benefits, but it could improve the attractiveness of some commercial activities in the neighboring areas.
Will the new cycle and pedestrian paths between Valsolda and the Canton of Ticino also be accessible to cross-border workers who travel daily?
Yes, the new pedestrian and cycle paths along the lake are designed to be integrated with the existing infrastructure, facilitating travel even for those moving between Italy and Switzerland. However, there are no routes dedicated exclusively to cross-border commuters, but they improve general connectivity.
How can I check if the works in Valsolda will affect the crossing times of border crossings such as Brogeda or Gaggiolo?
The redevelopment works do not directly involve interventions on border crossings, but improve local traffic and road safety in the neighboring areas. To check for any impact on crossing times, it is advisable to consult the updates of the Swiss and Italian customs authorities or the websites of the motorway companies involved.
