Bicycle Theft in Giubiasco: Arrested for Unpaid Fuel

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 27 April 2026

Three electric bicycles stolen in Giubiasco, two arrested for not paying for fuel. Discover the details of the operation.

Contesto

In brief - Three e-bikes stolen in Giubiasco - Estimated value between 2'000 and 5'000 CHF each - Two arrested for fuel theft ## Key facts - What: E-bike theft - When: Friday afternoon - Where: Giubiasco train station - Who: Two Romanian citizens - Amount: Value of the bikes between 2'000 and 5'000 CHF Three e-bikes were stolen on Friday afternoon at the Giubiasco train station. Each bike had an estimated value between 2'000 and 5'000 Swiss francs. The theft was reported the following morning by one of the victims. The stolen bikes were found disassembled inside a car with Romanian license plates. The two alleged thieves, Romanian citizens aged 24 and 21, are currently in detention. ### The police operation The Mendrisiotto cantonal police stopped the two suspects on Saturday afternoon, around 1:30 PM, as they attempted to reach Italy with the stolen goods. The operation was triggered by a report of fuel theft at a service station in Coldrerio. During the search, burglary tools were also found inside the vehicle. ### Lack of surveillance cameras The theft was committed by cutting the bike locks. A witness expressed disappointment at the lack of surveillance cameras in the area, making the thieves' operation easier. 'I find it sad that there is no video surveillance,' said the man who filed the report. ### Possible criminal organization According to the investigation, the two arrested individuals may be part of an organization specialized in various types of theft. Further investigations will determine their possible responsibility in connection with recent e-bike and bicycle thefts in Ticino over the past few weeks. The possible charges include theft, damage, receiving stolen property, and driving without authorization. ### Implications for cross-border workers...

This episode not only highlights the vulnerability of public infrastructure but also the need for stricter measures to protect the property of citizens and cross-border workers. ### Security of public infrastructure The absence of surveillance cameras in the Giubiasco train station area facilitated the thieves' operation. This episode underscores the importance of investing in advanced surveillance systems to prevent future thefts. Local authorities should consider installing cameras in strategic areas to ensure greater security. ### Impact on cross-border workers Cross-border workers who use means of transport such as bicycles for their daily commute are particularly vulnerable to such thefts. The lack of security may deter cross-border workers from using alternative means of transport, increasing traffic and pollution. It is crucial that local authorities take measures to improve security and prevent future thefts. ### Comparison with similar situations This episode is not isolated.
