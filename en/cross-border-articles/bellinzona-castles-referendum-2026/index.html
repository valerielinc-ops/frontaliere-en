Bellinzona Castles referendum: the people decide (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 11 May 2026

Over 4,600 signatures collected against the credit for the Fortress project. The referendum succeeded, now it's up to the citizens.

Contesto

In brief - Successful referendum with over 4,600 signatures - Credit of 18.565 million francs under discussion - Three reasons to say no to the project ## Key facts - What: Referendum against the credit for the Fortezza project - When: 11 May 2026 - Where: Bellinzona, Canton Ticino - Who: Referendum promoters and citizens of Bellinzona - Amount: 18.565 million francs for phase 1A, 496,000 francs for phase 2 The Castles of Bellinzona, a UNESCO World Heritage site, are at the center of a lively city debate. On Monday, 11 May, at 11:00 AM, the promoters of the referendum will deliver the collected signatures: over 4,600 in total, of which at least 3,600 have already been verified as belonging to citizens with the right to vote in the municipality of Bellinzona. The quorum has therefore been reached, and the word now passes to the citizens. The referendum challenges the credit of 18.565 million francs approved by the Municipal Council for the so-called phase 1A of the Fortezza project, plus another 496,000 francs to design phase 2. A substantial investment, decided without sufficiently listening to the voice of the people. ### Three reasons to say NO The first reason is a matter of identity. Calling the Castles of Bellinzona Fortezza is a choice that erases centuries of history. The Castles of Bellinzona have always been called that, and they must continue to be called that. The second reason is a matter of rights. The project involves charging a ticket to access the inner courtyard of Castelgrande and the Murata gallery, spaces that today belong to everyone, free of charge. An economic barrier on a common heritage, already widely funded by the Ticino taxpayer, is unacceptable. The third reason is a matter of democracy. Dettagli operativi

Practical Implications for Cross-Border Workers The referendum on the Bellinzona Castles has practical implications for cross-border workers who live and work in the Canton of Ticino. The final decision will have a direct impact on the use of one of the region's main tourist sites, which attracts thousands of visitors every year, including many cross-border workers. ### Access to the Castles If the referendum approves the Fortezza project, access to the inner courtyard of Castelgrande and the Murata gallery could become paid. This would mean a significant change from the current situation, where these spaces are free. For cross-border workers who enjoy visiting the Bellinzona Castles, this could mean increased costs to enjoy one of Ticino's main cultural heritage sites. ### Site Management Another crucial aspect concerns the management of the site. If the project is approved, the management of the Castles will be entrusted to a foundation. This could lead to changes in maintenance, tourist promotion, and the management of cultural events. For cross-border workers in the tourism or cultural sector, this could mean new opportunities or challenges, depending on the foundation's choices. ### Cultural Identity The name change from Bellinzona Castles to Fortezza is another point of discussion. For many, the Castles represent a symbol of cultural and historical identity. A name change could impact the perception of the site, both locally and internationally. For cross-border workers living in Ticino, the Bellinzona Castles are a cultural and historical reference point, and a potential name change could influence their sense of belonging. ### Conclusion The referendum on the Bellinzona Castles is a crucial moment for the local community and for cross-border workers liv...

Frequently Asked Questions
What is the amount of the credit contested by the referendum?
The referendum contests a credit of 18,565 million francs for phase 1A of the Fortezza project, plus another 496,000 francs for phase 2.
What does the Fortezza project involve?
The Fortezza project involves the restoration and management of the Castles of Bellinzona, with the introduction of an entrance ticket for some areas and the management entrusted to a foundation.
What are the main criticisms of the Fortezza project?
The main criticisms concern the change of name of the Castles, the introduction of an entrance ticket for areas that are currently free, and the management of the site by a foundation, which could limit democratic control by citizens.
