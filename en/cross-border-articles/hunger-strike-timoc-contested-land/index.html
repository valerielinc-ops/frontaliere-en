Hunger strike in Varese: Timoc's fight for contested land

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 30 April 2026

Timoc, 70, protests outside the Carabinieri barracks to access documents blocked in his home.

Contesto

In brief - Timoc protests to access blocked documents - Legal dispute over a plot of land for six years - Precautionary measure prevents access to the house ## Key facts - What: Hunger strike to access legal documents - When: For several months - Where: Via Saffi, Varese - Who: Timoc, Romanian farmer and beekeeper - Amount: Not specified Timoc, 70 years old, has started a hunger strike in front of the Carabinieri barracks in Varese. The man, of Romanian origin and in Italy since 2000, is a farmer and beekeeper. His protest concerns access to documents kept in his home, blocked due to a precautionary measure. Timoc is subject to a precautionary measure and wears an electronic bracelet on his ankle following a two-year sentence for mistreating his ex-wife and three children. The presence of his ex-spouse in the house prevents him from re-entering to retrieve the papers, which he claims are fundamental to defending his rights over a disputed plot of land on Via Monte Generoso. The legal dispute concerns a plot of land and has lasted six years. ### The protest routine Timoc patrols the area in front of the barracks during the day, but in the evening he is forced to return to his field, where he maintains his link with the agricultural life that has always characterized his years in Italy. His presence on Via Saffi is not continuous for 24 hours, but his determination is evident. He has already tried other avenues, even going to Rome to plead his case. His protest in front of the command on Via Saffi continues, an act of solitude but also of stubbornly demanding attention to a case that, for him, has become the focus of an entire existence. ### Practical implications Timoc's situation highlights a difficult short circuit between the needs of justice and private urgencies...
