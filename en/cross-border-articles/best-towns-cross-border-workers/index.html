The 10 best municipalities for cross-border workers (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 4 December 2025 · Updated on 29 April 2026

Ranking of Italian border municipalities: rents, municipal IRPEF, distance from the border.

Contesto

TL;DR - Cross-border workers must consider rent, taxes, and commute times. - Municipalities near the border offer lower rents but fewer services. - Cities like Como and Varese provide better quality of life but higher costs. - Personal priorities dictate the best choice between affordability and amenities. ## Key facts - Rent variation: Up to 40% between neighboring towns. - Municipal IRPEF: Ranges from 0.3% to 0.9%. - Fiscal refunds: Municipalities within 20 km of the border receive fiscal refunds from Switzerland. - Saltrio rent: €450-600/month for a one-bedroom apartment. - Saltrio distance: 2 km from the border with a 15-25 minute commute. - Como rent: €700-1,000/month for a one-bedroom apartment. - Como distance: 5 km from the border with a 30-60 minute commute. - Municipal surcharge: Difference of €160/year on a €40,000 income between 0.4% and 0.8%. ## How to choose the ideal municipality for a cross-border worker Choosing where to live is one of the most important decisions for a cross-border worker. Key factors are: rent costs (which can vary by 40% between neighbouring towns), municipal IRPEF surcharge (from 0.3% to 0.9%), distance to the nearest border crossing, average queue times at the border during rush hour, availability of schools and family services, and quality of public transport to the border. Municipalities within 20 km of the border also receive fiscal refunds from Switzerland, which translate into better local services.

The cheapest towns for rent are Saltrio, Clivio and Cantello (province of Varese), with one-bedroom apartments at €400-600/month. They are also the closest to the Gaggiolo/Stabio border crossing, with commute times of 15-25 minutes to Mendrisio or Chiasso (Source: Immobiliare.it data, 2026). Como or Varese: which is better for a cross-border worker? Como offers better rail connections to Chiasso (train every 30 min, 10-minute ride) but higher rents and heavy traffic on the A9 motorway. Varese has more affordable rents and good road links to Stabio/Mendrisio, but longer rush-hour commutes (40-70 min). For those working in Lugano, Varese is often preferable via Ponte Tresa. How does the municipal surcharge affect net salary? The municipal IRPEF surcharge ranges from 0.3% to 0.9% of taxable income. On an income of €40,000, the difference between a municipality at 0.4% and one at 0.8% is about €160/year — a modest saving compared to the rent difference between towns (Source: Agenzia delle Entrate, municipal surcharge rates 2026). Frequently Asked Questions
Which municipality has the lowest rent near the border?

Which one is the best value for money for schools?
Clivio (VA) offers good school quality with primary and secondary schools of the first degree, at low prices (average rent €400-550/month). The proximity to Mendrisio (5 km) facilitates access to Swiss schools for those who want it.
