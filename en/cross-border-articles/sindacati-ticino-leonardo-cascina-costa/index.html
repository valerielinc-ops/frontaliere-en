Frequently Asked Questions
What does Article 42 of the national Logistics, Freight Transport and Shipping contract say about the change of contract?
Article 42 establishes that in the event of a change of contract, the staff must be transferred to the new company with the same rights and prerogatives.
What is the role of the Prefecture of Varese in the Leonardo Cascina Costa dispute?
The Prefecture of Varese has agreed to receive representatives of the trade unions and the successor company to discuss the dispute and find a solution that satisfies all parties involved.
What are the rights of cross-border workers in the event of a change of contract between Switzerland and Italy according to Article 42?
Article 42 of the national contract for Logistics, Freight Transport and Shipping guarantees that, in the event of a change of contract, the staff will be transferred to the new company with identical rights, duties, remuneration and social security prerogatives. This also applies to frontier workers, who must maintain the same contractual conditions.
How can I check if my employer complies with the protections provided for in Article 42 in the event of a change of contract?
You can compare your current contract with the terms offered by the new successor company, making sure that your duties, hours, salary and social security entitlements remain the same. If in doubt, consult a trade union or a labour consultant specialising in cross-border commuters.
What happens if the new company does not comply with Article 42? What are the protections for workers?
If the new company does not apply Article 42, the unions may initiate a dispute at the Prefecture or at the competent authorities. Workers have the right to maintain their acquired rights and, in the event of non-compliance, they can resort to legal procedures for the recognition of protections.
