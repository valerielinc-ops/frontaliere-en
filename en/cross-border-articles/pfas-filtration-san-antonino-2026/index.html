PFAS in drinking water: San Antonino installs advanced filtration (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 7 May 2026

The Municipality of San Antonino has installed an activated carbon filtration system to remove PFAS from drinking water, with an investment of 1.8 million francs.

Contesto

In brief - Advanced system to remove PFAS from drinking water - Investment of 1.8 million francs - Checks confirm effectiveness ## Key facts - What: Activated carbon filtration for PFAS - When: 2026 - Where: San Antonino, Ticino - Who: Municipality of San Antonino - Amount: 1.8 million francs The Municipality of San Antonino has put into operation an activated carbon filtration system to remove PFAS (chemical compounds resistant to degradation) from drinking water. This intervention was carried out to address an environmental criticality with an operation defined as cutting-edge. The checks carried out after the plant's start-up confirm the effectiveness of the intervention. The concentration of PFAS has been reduced to values below 2 ng/l, falling within the desired parameters. The work, carried out near the school center in the Boschetti area, stood out for its technical complexity. Despite the difficulties, the municipality completed the project, placing itself among the first in Ticino and among the pioneers in Switzerland in the adoption of solutions of this type. The total investment amounts to approximately 1.8 million francs and has been fully recognized by the Confederation, which attributed the contamination of the aquifer to the works related to the Monte Ceneri base tunnel. ### Implications for cross-border workers The intervention represents an example of timely and effective management of an environmental criticality, with the aim of protecting public health and the quality of the territory's resources. For cross-border workers who live or work in the San Antonino area, this means having access to safer and higher-quality drinking water. Dettagli operativi

Analysis of Practical Implications The installation of the activated carbon filtration system in San Antonino has significant implications for the quality of drinking water in the region. PFAS, known for their resistance to degradation, can have negative effects on human health if present in high concentrations. The removal of these chemical compounds is therefore a crucial step to ensure the safety of drinking water. ### Comparison with the Previous Situation Before the installation of the filtration system, PFAS levels in San Antonino's drinking water were above safety parameters. This necessitated timely intervention to ensure public health. With the installation of the new system, PFAS levels have been reduced to values below 2 ng/l, meeting the established safety parameters. ### Future Scenarios The intervention in San Antonino could serve as a model for other communities in Ticino and Switzerland facing similar water contamination issues. The timely and effective management of an environmental criticality like this is an example of how local authorities can work to protect public health and the quality of the territory's resources. ### Implications for Cross-Border Workers For cross-border workers residing or working in the San Antonino area, the installation of the filtration system represents a significant improvement in drinking water quality. This intervention ensures that the water they consume is safe and of high quality, reducing health risks associated with the presence of PFAS. ### Procedures and Controls The activated carbon filtration system has been installed and tested to ensure maximum effectiveness. Frequently Asked Questions
Why is the removal of PFAS from drinking water important?
Removing PFAS from drinking water is crucial to ensure public health. These persistent chemicals can have negative effects on human health if present in high concentrations. The installation of the activated carbon filtration system in San Antonino has reduced PFAS levels to below 2 ng/l, meeting the established safety parameters.
How does the activated carbon filtration system work?
The activated carbon filtration system uses activated carbon to absorb and remove PFAS from drinking water. This method is effective because activated carbon has a large surface area that can absorb a wide range of chemical compounds, ensuring high-quality filtration. The system has been installed and tested to guarantee maximum effectiveness.
What are the implications for cross-border workers residing or working in San Antonino?
For cross-border workers residing or working in San Antonino, the installation of the filtration system represents a significant improvement in drinking water quality. This intervention ensures that the water they consume is safe and of high quality, reducing health risks associated with the presence of PFAS. It is important to be aware of the measures taken and to participate in any public meetings or consultations organized by the Municipality.
