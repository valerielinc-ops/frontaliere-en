Frequently Asked Questions
Who were the Angels of Ponte Chiasso?
The Angels of Ponte Chiasso were Giuseppina Panzìca, Giovanni Gavino Tolis, and Paolo Boetti, three heroes who saved hundreds of Jews at the Italian-Swiss border during World War II. They were awarded the Gold Medal for Civil Merit for their courage and humanity.
What is the meaning of the commemorative plaque?
The commemorative plaque represents a recognition of the courage and humanity demonstrated by the Angels of Ponte Chiasso. It honors their memory and underscores the importance of remembering the atrocities of war and the heroic actions that saved human lives.
