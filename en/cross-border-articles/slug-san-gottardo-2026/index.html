Frequently Asked Questions
Why is there so much traffic on the Gotthard during the Easter holidays?
Due to the 20% increase in vehicles compared to the previous year, with peaks of over 3.5 hours of waiting and queues of up to 21 km.
What is the best alternative route to Italy during traffic peaks at the Gotthard?
During traffic peaks, an alternative can be the Gotthard Base Tunnel (A2 motorway), if open, or the secondary route through the San Bernardino Pass (B9), if weather conditions permit. Always check the road conditions before you set off.
How can I check in real time if the Gotthard Tunnel is closed for maintenance or accidents before I leave?
You can check the status of the tunnel on the official website of the Swiss Federal Roads Office (FEDRO) or via the 'Safe Way' app. In addition, the Uri Cantonal Police publishes updates on Twitter/X (@PoliziaUri) and on the cantonal website. These channels provide detailed information about closures, queues, and alternatives.
What happens if I arrive at the Gotthard tunnel and find a traffic jam without having updated my navigation system? Can I be fined?
Yes, according to Swiss law, if you do not follow the instructions of the updated navigation systems or the police reports, you risk a fine of up to 300 francs. The police advise you to always check travel times and follow road signs to avoid penalties.
What are the less busy alternatives to the Gotthard to reach Italy by car during long weekends?
An alternative is the Great St. Bernard tunnel, which connects Martigny (CH) to Aosta (IT), but is subject to winter closures. Another option is the Lukmanier Pass (CH) or the Maloja Pass (CH), both with less traffic but longer and more winding routes.
