In Switzerland, the speed limit in residential areas and built-up zones is 50 km/h, but on motorways and main roads it can rise to 80 or 120 km/h, depending on signage. Swiss law imposes severe sanctions for exceeding the limit by more than 40 km/h, including hefty fines, point deductions, and, in the most serious cases, license revocation. Italy’s regulations set limits at 50 km/h within urban centers and 90 km/h on main rural roads, with similar penalties for speeding violations. Among the most notable incidents intercepted at Gaggiolo and Brogeda, a 45-year-old Italian driver was stopped in January while traveling at 121 km/h in Chiasso, and another in Mendrisio in February was caught going approximately 70 km/h over the limit. These episodes not only endanger everyone’s lives but also lead to practical consequences such as immediate license withdrawal and fines that can exceed 350 CHF, depending on the severity. The cantonal police have announced that they will step up checks at border crossings and along major routes, paying particular attention to dangerous behaviors by some Italian drivers. Swiss legislation also bans circulation in Switzerland for those with multiple serious violations and can impose temporary or permanent bans to discourage misconduct and ensure safety. Joint efforts between authorities in Lugano, Bellinzona, and border command units from Italy are vital to reducing this phenomenon. In case of fines or stops, Swiss authorities do not make exceptions: immediate license withdrawal can be ordered, with fines exceeding 350 CHF for serious offenses, along with possible temporary or permanent driving bans.
