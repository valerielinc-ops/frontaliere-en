Dettagli operativi

Easter tourism promises pleasant surprises in Ticino. Bookings for Easter have increased by 16.7% compared to last year, according to data from HotellerieSuisse Ticino. This positive trend is reflected throughout the region, with a greater demand for accommodation in municipalities such as Lugano, Bellinzona, and Locarno. The data shows that 75% of bookings were recorded from Friday to Sunday, while 66% from Friday to Monday. Hotel operators are enthusiastic about the provisional booking figures, which exceed 60%. The Ticino tourism season is off to a great start, with a decline in demand of only 4.2% compared to last year. This positive trend is also reflected in the demand for transport services, with an increase of 12.5% compared to last year. The president of HotellerieSuisse Ticino, Sonja Frey, stated: 'The figures promise well for the entire Easter period. We are enthusiastic to see an increase in demand for accommodation and transport services.' However, the president also noted that the 4 or 5-star market feels that tourists from the Middle East are not arriving. According to HotellerieSuisse Ticino's data, 70% of bookings were made by families with minor children, while 30% were made by couples or singles. Frequently Asked Questions
What is the percentage of employment planned for Easter in Ticino?
The percentage of Easter employment in Ticino is 66% from Friday to Monday and 75% from Friday to Sunday.
What is the impact of the strong Swiss franc on international tourism in Ticino during Easter?
The strong Swiss franc makes Ticino cheaper for international guests, increasing bookings.
How does the tax return for a frontier worker working in Ticino work but resides in Italy?
A frontier worker resident in Italy who works in Ticino must declare income both in Switzerland (where he pays taxes to the source) and in Italy, but with the credit for the taxes already paid. Since 2021, Switzerland has applied a tax to the source of 8-13% for frontiers, while Italy recognises a credit of up to 100% of Swiss taxes paid, avoiding double taxation.
What are tax benefits for seasonal workers in Ticino tourism in 2024?
Seasonal workers in Ticino may benefit from a reduced tax scheme: the tax on the source is reduced to 7.5% for contracts under 90 days. In addition, a bonus of CHF 500 has been introduced since 2024 for those who work at least 30 consecutive days in the hotel and catering sector.
Does an Italian citizen employed in Ticino have the right to Italian health care during the working period?
Yes, but only for urgent services. The frontal must sign up for the Italian National Health Service (SSN) and present the European Health Insurance Card (TEAM) for treatment in Switzerland. The costs of non-emergency benefits in Ticino are borne by Italy only if authorized in advance.
