Zurich Airport: 5% air traffic growth in April (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 4 May 2026

Pre-pandemic levels exceeded with 23,300 flights, first annual increase since 2019. AWP data on IFR.

In brief - Air traffic Zurich +5.2% in April 2026 vs 2024 - Pre-pandemic levels surpassed for the first time - Awp data on IFR rules: scheduled flights, cargo, business ## Key facts - What: Air movements at Zurich Airport - When: April 2026 - Where: Zurich Airport - Who: Awp Agency - Amount: +5.2% vs April 2024, 23,300 movements - What: Comparison with 2019 - When: January-April 2026 - Where: Zurich Airport - Who: Awp Agency - Amount: -3.3% vs same period 2019 Zurich Airport recorded a 5.2% increase in air traffic in April 2026 compared to the same month in 2024, surpassing pre-pandemic levels for the first time. According to data processed by the Awp agency, air movements totaled 23,300, a significant recovery compared to previous years. This increase is part of a steady growth trajectory, with a 3.8% rise in the January-April 2026 period compared to the same period the previous year. However, when compared to the same months in 2019, the last pre-pandemic year, the number of movements is still 3.3% lower. The data concerns aircraft operating under IFR rules, i.e., instrument flight rules, and includes scheduled flights, charter flights, cargo, business jets, and private aircraft. It is important to note that months are not always perfectly comparable due to the different number of bank holidays and weekdays.
