Rhaetian Railway signs new deal with Taiwan (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 1 June 2026

The Rhaetian Railway has signed a collaboration agreement with Taiwan's Alishan Forest Railway.

Contesto

In brief - The Rhaetian Railway has signed a collaboration agreement with Taiwan's Alishan Forest Railway. - The agreement aims to promote mutual marketing initiatives and exchange technical and operational expertise between the two railway operators. ## Key Facts - What: Collaboration agreement between Rhaetian Railway and Alishan Forest Railway. - When: The contract was signed today. - Where: The partnership involves Switzerland and Taiwan. - Who: The Rhaetian Railway and Alishan Forest Railway are the key players in this agreement. The Rhaetian Railway, renowned for its UNESCO-listed Red Train, has signed a collaboration agreement today with Taiwan's Alishan Forest Railway, a railway that traverses the country's mountainous forests. This marks the third international partnership for the Red Train, which has previously established similar agreements with Japan's Hakone Tozan Railway and Canada's Rocky Mountaineer. 📊 Objectives of the Agreement The partnership focuses on two main aspects: mutual promotion and knowledge exchange. Mutual promotion will include joint marketing initiatives, such as tourist packages that combine railway experiences in both destinations. The Alishan Forest Railway, which spans 86 km and reaches an altitude of over 2,216 meters, shares a strong connection with international tourism and sustainable transport, much like the Rhaetian Railway. From a technical standpoint, the focus will be on operational optimization. The Rhaetian Railway will share its expertise in managing high-altitude railway routes and technologies to reduce environmental impact, while the Alishan Forest Railway will contribute solutions for operating on steep mountain lines and in tropical climate conditions. 💡 Tourism Implications for Ticino Thanks to the new...

Action To take advantage of this new agreement, cross-border workers traveling between Switzerland and Italy can explore the transportation options offered by the Rhaetian Railway. This operator, which has recently expanded its network thanks to the agreement with Taiwan, provides strategic and advantageous connections for those working in Ticino but residing in Italy. For example, the railway connection between Tirano and St. Moritz, which crosses the Bernina Pass, is a particularly appreciated option among cross-border commuters coming from Valtellina. The monthly cost of a commuter pass for this route can range between 150 and 200 CHF, depending on applicable discounts. Another example involves workers residing in Como who commute to Mendrisiotto. Thanks to new integrations with local railway services, it is possible to access transportation combinations that reduce travel times by up to 20%. For regular train users, the cost of the Arcobaleno cross-border subscription, which includes routes between Canton Ticino and Lombardy, starts at approximately 130 CHF per month. #### Operational Checklist - Visit the official Rhaetian Railway website to check the updated schedules and routes included in the agreement. - Compare the costs of railway subscriptions with other modes of transport, such as cars, taking into account tolls and fuel. - If you reside in Italian municipalities close to the border, such as Chiasso, Como, or Luino, check for potential tax benefits for cross-border workers. #### CTA To learn more about transportation services between Switzerland and Italy, visit our transport calculator.
