Bear spotted in Valposchiavo: return after years

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 1 May 2026

A camera trap captured a bear on April 29 in the woods between Le Prese and Miralago, reopening the debate on coexistence with wildlife

In brief - Bear spotted in Valposchiavo on April 29, 2026 - First confirmed presence since 2020 - Camera trap between Brusio and Poschiavo ## Key facts - What: Presence of a bear in Valposchiavo - When: April 29, 2026 - Where: Between Le Prese and Miralago - Who: Grisons Canton Hunting and Fishing Office - Amount: Not specified A camera trap captured a bear on April 29 in the woods between Le Prese and Miralago, in Valposchiavo. It has been years since a bear was last sighted in this area. Following recent reports of a bear in Lower Engadine, between Ftan and the Forno Pass, an individual has now been spotted further south, in Valposchiavo. While no one has seen the animal with their own eyes, its presence was confirmed on April 29 by a private camera trap between Brusio and Poschiavo. The information is reported on the website of the Grisons Canton Hunting and Fishing Office, which features a map showing sightings. It has been several years since a bear was last recorded south of the Bernina. The last sighting dates back to spring 2020, when an individual was captured on a mobile phone in broad daylight in a hunting area near the Splüga Pass, close to the Bernina Pass. Between 2012 and 2013, the presence of the M13 bear caused several issues in Valposchiavo. The bear had lost its shyness towards humans and approached alpine pastures and houses in search of food. On February 19, 2013, it was shot in the Miralago area. ### Implications for wildlife The return of the bear to Valposchiavo reopens the debate on coexistence with wildlife. The presence of a bear in this area requires careful and coordinated management between local authorities and wildlife experts. The presence of bear M13 between 2012 and 2013 had raised several problems, but since then no other bear sightings had been reported in this area. The return of the bear may require an adaptation of the habits and activities of the local inhabitants and tourists. ### Coexistence scenarios Coexistence between humans and bears is possible, but it requires a high level of vigilance and respect for wildlife. It is important to adopt preventive measures to avoid conflicts and promote peaceful coexistence. Local authorities and wildlife experts are working to ensure effective management of the bear's presence and to inform local inhabitants and tourists about the safety measures to be adopted. ### Safety procedures In case of a bear sighting, it is important to remain calm and follow the instructions of the local authorities. It is advisable to avoid approaching the bear and leaving food outdoors. In case of an attack, it is important to defend yourself and try to drive the bear away without provoking it.
