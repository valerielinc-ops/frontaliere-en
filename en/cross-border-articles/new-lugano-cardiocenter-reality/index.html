New Lugano Cardiocentre: three additional floors inaugurated (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 4 May 2026

Three additional floors inaugurated at the Lugano Cardiocentre, with improvements for patients and operators

Contesto

In brief - Cardiocentro Lugano expanded with three floors - Better patient care - Goal: contain healthcare costs ## Key facts - What: Expansion of Cardiocentro Lugano - When: Official inauguration today - Where: Lugano - Who: EOC, DSS, Federal Councillor Ignazio Cassis - Amount: Not specified Today, the expansion of the Cardiocentro in Lugano was officially inaugurated, adding three floors that will house cardiac, thoracic, vascular surgery, and angiography. Until now, these specialties were distributed across various sites of the Ente Ospedaliero Cantonale (EOC). The new structure will allow for better patient care, increased safety and quality of treatment, as well as improved workflows for the hospital entity. The General Director of EOC, Glauco Martinetti, emphasized that 'this expansion means, above all, better patient care, better safety, better quality, and obviously also an improvement in workflows for the hospital entity.' Also present at the inauguration was the Director of DSS Raffaele De Rosa, who highlighted how 'this investment has several objectives. The first is to confirm the presence of the Cardiocentro Ticino Institute in favor of the entire population. Secondly, it further promotes the concentration of complex cases and allows for a higher quality of care, more safety for patients, more expertise, with the goal of also containing the growth of healthcare costs and health insurance premiums.' ### Impact on cross-border workers The expansion of the Cardiocentro in Lugano represents a significant step for cross-border workers who work and reside in Ticino. The concentration of medical specialties under one roof simplifies access to care for residents and cross-border workers, reducing waiting times and improving the quality of healthcare services....

Dettagli operativi

The expansion of the Cardiocentro in Lugano represents a significant step forward for the Ticino healthcare system, with important implications for cross-border workers who live and work in Canton Ticino. Concentrating medical specializations under one roof not only improves the quality of care but also simplifies access to healthcare services for residents and cross-border workers. ### Advantages for cross-border workers For cross-border workers, the expansion of the Cardiocentro in Lugano offers several concrete advantages. Firstly, the centralization of medical specializations reduces waiting times for appointments and procedures, allowing for faster and more coordinated care. This is particularly important for those who work in Switzerland and live in Italy, where access to healthcare services can be more complex. Moreover, the increased operational efficiency of the Cardiocentro could translate into reduced healthcare costs, with a potential positive impact on health insurance premiums. This is a crucial aspect for cross-border workers, who often face high healthcare costs in both Italy and Switzerland. ### Comparison with the previous situation Before the expansion, medical specializations were distributed across various locations of the EOC, which resulted in longer waiting times and less efficient care management. With the expansion, patients will benefit from faster and more coordinated care, significantly improving the quality of care. ### Future scenarios The expansion of the Cardiocentro in Lugano is just the first step in a broader project to reorganize the Ticino healthcare system. What to do for cross-border workers For cross-border workers who wish to take advantage of the new services offered by the Cardiocentro in Lugano, it is important to be informed about the necessary steps to access healthcare. Here is a practical guide to navigating the new healthcare system: 1. Check your insurance coverage: Make sure you have valid health insurance coverage both in Italy and Switzerland. Cross-border workers must have Swiss health insurance in accordance with the LAMal. 2. Access to care: To access care at the Cardiocentro in Lugano, you need a prescription from a general practitioner. Cross-border workers can consult general practitioners both in Italy and Switzerland. 3. Required documentation: Ensure you have all the necessary documentation, including identity documents, work and residence permits, and your health card. 4. Scheduling appointments: Appointments and procedures can be scheduled through the Cardiocentro's online booking system or by contacting the facility directly. 5. Costs and reimbursements: Find out about the costs of healthcare services and the reimbursements offered by your health insurance. Cross-border workers can benefit from partial or full reimbursements, depending on their insurance coverage. ### Useful Tools For further information and to plan your care, you can use the following tools: - Salary calculator: To calculate your net salary and better understand healthcare costs. - Health insurance comparator: To compare different health insurance options and find the coverage best suited to your needs. - Guide to work permits: For information on work and residence permits for cross-border workers. ### Conclusion The expansion of the Cardiocentro in Lugano represents an important opportunity for cross-border workers who...
