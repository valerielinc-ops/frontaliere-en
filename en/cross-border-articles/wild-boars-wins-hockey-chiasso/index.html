Dettagli operativi

The Chiasso Hockey Tournament is a major event for the Ticino community, attracting not only local residents but also hockey fans from towns like Mendrisio, Balerna, and Vacallo. The 2024 edition, held at the Chiasso Palaghiaccio from April 12-14, featured eight amateur teams and more than 120 registered athletes. The Wild Boars, already champions in 2022, were warmly celebrated for their victory: they secured the title in the final against the Sharks Mendrisio with a 5-3 score, confirming their technical and tactical leadership. 📊 During the group stage, the Wild Boars scored 22 goals in four matches, conceding only 7: numbers that reflect their athletic preparation, well above the tournament average. Noteworthy performances included goalie Marco Rossi, who posted a 92% save percentage, and striker Luca Bernasconi, top scorer with 11 points (7 goals, 4 assists). Federation rules applied to the tournament follow the Swiss Ice Hockey Federation regulations, which since 2020 require all amateur tournaments to provide accident insurance with a minimum coverage of CHF 50,000 per athlete. Frequently Asked Questions
How many players were enrolled in the Hockey Chiasso 2024 amateur tournament?
Over 150 players were registered, with a total of 10 teams.
What is the total cost for a team enrolling in the Hockey Chiasso 2024 amateur tournament, considering registration and accident protection?
The total cost for a team is CHF 550, which includes the registration fee (CHF 400) and the contribution for the accident protection fund (CHF 150).
How much does accommodation and catering cost on average for an athlete in Chiasso?
During the event, athletes spent an average of CHF 60 per accommodation and catering; for a team of 15 players the total budget is about CHF 900. The municipality of Chiasso has allocated CHF 3'000 for sports promotion, but does not directly cover individual expenses.
What is the mandatory minimum insurance cover for players and how does it integrate with the tournament injury protection fund?
Federal legislation requires a minimum coverage of CHF 50'000 per athlete; the tournament adds an injury protection fund of CHF 150 per team, so the total protection for each player is at least CHF 50'150. LAA insurance is mandatory and must be submitted by 25 May.
How do you manage the taxation of the CHF 550 fee for Italian cross-border commuters registered for the tournament?
For Italian cross-border commuters, the total fee of CHF 550 (registration fee 400 + protection 150) is subject to withholding tax in Switzerland; However, thanks to the double taxation treaty, the amount can be deducted in the Italian tax return, avoiding double taxation.
