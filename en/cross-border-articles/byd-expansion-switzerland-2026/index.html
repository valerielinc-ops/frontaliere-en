BYD expands in Ticino: 50 dealerships by 2026 (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 21 March 2026

The Chinese manufacturer BYD aims for 50 dealerships in Switzerland by 2026, with two already operational in Ticino.

Dettagli operativi

BYD's expansion in Switzerland is part of an ambitious strategy aimed at responding to the growing demand for electric vehicles and promoting more sustainable mobility. Switzerland represents a crucial market for eco-friendly cars: according to the Federal Statistical Office, in 2022, electric vehicle sales recorded a 39% increase, with over 20,000 vehicles registered, thanks in part to government incentives offering up to 5,000 Swiss francs for the purchase of electric cars. BYD dealerships, which will focus on strategic municipalities such as Lugano, Bellinzona, and Mendrisio, will be designed to offer innovative and personalized shopping experiences. The new sales points will not only showcase the latest models from the brand, such as the BYD Atto 3 and the BYD Dolphin, but will also provide specialized maintenance and assistance services, contributing to a robust support network. These facilities will be equipped with cutting-edge technologies for diagnostics and maintenance, thus ensuring high-quality service, in line with BYD's commitment to customer satisfaction. Interestingly, BYD has been a pioneer in the electric vehicle sector, halting the production of fossil fuel vehicles in 2003 and maintaining a leadership position in new energy vehicle sales for over a decade in China. With its expansion strategy in Switzerland, BYD aims not only to sell cars but also to educate consumers about the benefits of electric mobility.
