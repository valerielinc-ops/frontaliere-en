Frequently Asked Questions
Which are the 10 municipalities in the Province of Como that will go to the polls?
The 10 municipalities in the Province of Como that will go to the polls are Turate, Domaso, Cerano d'Intelvi, Valsolda, Plesio, Guanzate, Lipomo, Montorfano, Casnate con Bernate, and Campione d'Italia.
When will the vote take place?
Voting will take place on Sunday, May 24, and the morning of Monday, May 25, 2026, with the possible runoff round on June 7-8, 2026.
Who are the main candidates?
Among the main candidates are Roberta Clerici, Leonardo Carioni, Cristiano Banfi, and Leonardo Calzeroni from the PD in Turate, Pietro Angelo Leggeri in Domaso, Maurizio Carlo Prada in Cerano d'Intelvi, Giovanni Somaini, Daniela Gini, Alberto Sampietro, and Monica Colacicco in Guanzate, Giuliano Capuano in Montorfano, Anna Seregni and Filippo Ballatore in Casnate con Bernate, and Luca Frigerio, Barbara Marchesini, and Sergio Aureli in Campione d'Italia.
What are the implications for cross-border workers?
The municipal elections could have a significant impact on cross-border workers living and working in these areas. The new mayors and municipal councils could introduce new policies and services that could directly affect cross-border workers, such as mobility and public transport policies, services for integration and social inclusion, and tax incentives.
What can cross-border workers do to actively participate in the elections?
Cross-border workers can inform themselves about the candidates' programs, participate in the elections, contact the candidates, attend debates and public meetings, and monitor the election results. Additionally, they can use useful tools such as the municipal tax calculator and the municipal policy comparator to better understand the implications of the elections.
