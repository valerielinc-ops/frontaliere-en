Founded in 1999, the service was born from the merger of various local Croce Verde organizations, including those in Mendrisio, Balerna, Chiasso, Morcote, and Riva San Vitale. Today, director Carlo Realini, who has been at the helm of the S.A.M. since 2019, recounts how the service has evolved over time, from 5 vehicles and 30 volunteers to a fleet of 12 ambulances and 120 operators, 80 of whom are full-time professionals. ### Origins and evolution The S.A.M. was created to respond to medical emergencies in Mendrisiotto, a region with a steadily growing population, which has increased from 45,000 inhabitants in 1999 to over 60,000 today. The merger of the various Croce Verde organizations allowed for the optimization of services and more effective coverage. In 2000, the S.A.M. handled 1,200 interventions, while in 2023, the number of interventions exceeded 6,000, with an average response time of 8 minutes. ### Current challenges Today, the S.A.M. faces new challenges. The increase in population and the complexity of medical emergencies require greater preparation and resources. Analysis of Healthcare Emergencies The evolution of Mendrisiotto's Medical-Urgent Assistance Service (S.A.M.) reflects the social and demographic transformations of the region. With a 15% increase in the resident population between 2010 and 2023, and a 20% growth in complex healthcare emergencies, the service has had to adapt to ensure effective assistance. For example, in 2022, the S.A.M. managed over 5,000 interventions, with an average response time of 8 minutes, compared to 12 minutes recorded in 2015. ### Comparison with Previous Situation Before the merger of the Red Cross in 2018, healthcare emergencies were managed by various local organizations, such as the Red Cross of Mendrisio, Balerna, and Morbio Inferiore. The merger allowed for the centralization of resources and the optimization of intervention times. Today, the S.A.M. is able to respond more quickly and efficiently to emergencies. For example, in the municipality of Mendrisio, the average intervention time has decreased from 15 minutes in 2017 to 10 minutes in 2023. Additionally, the merger has allowed for a 15% reduction in operational costs, thanks to better management of resources and equipment. ### Future Scenarios With the increase in population and the growing complexity of healthcare emergencies, the S.A.M. will continue to invest in personnel training and equipment updates. The goal is to maintain a high-quality service, ensuring safety and assistance to all citizens. For 2024, the S.A.M. has planned an investment of 1 million Swiss francs for the purchase of new ambulances and advanced medical equipment. What to do in case of emergency In case of a medical emergency, it is essential to know the phone numbers and procedures to follow. The Mendrisiotto Ambulance Service (S.A.M.) is available 24/7 to intervene in critical situations, covering an area that includes municipalities such as Mendrisio, Balerna, Morbio Inferiore, Novazzano, and Stabio. In 2022, the S.A.M. responded to over 3,000 emergency calls, demonstrating the importance of an efficient and well-organized service. ### Procedure to follow 1. Call the emergency number: The emergency number for the S.A.M. is 144. This number is valid throughout Switzerland and ensures an immediate response. 2. Provide precise information: It is essential to provide your name, exact address, and a detailed description of the emergency. For example, if you are calling from Lugano, specify whether you are in the Lugano Centro district or a peripheral area such as Breganzona. 3. Wait for the arrival of the personnel: Stay on the line until the S.A.M. personnel arrive. The average waiting time for an intervention in Mendrisiotto is about 8 minutes, but it can vary depending on the location and the severity of the emergency. 4. Follow the instructions: Follow the instructions provided by the S.A.M. personnel during the call. For example, in case of cardiac arrest, the personnel may guide you through the resuscitation maneuvers. ### Useful tools For more information and to better understand the service, you can visit the S.A.M.
