Sustainable fashion and stories of rebirth in Varese

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 30 April 2026

Students and oncological women united in a sustainable fashion show in Varese, with recycled clothes and messages of resilience

Contesto

In brief - Sustainable fashion show in Varese with students and oncological women - Recycled clothes and messages of resilience - Event as part of EcoRun 2026 ## Key facts - What: Sustainable fashion show - When: April 25, 2026 - Where: Varese, Piazza Monte Grappa - Who: ISIS Newton students and women from the 'Le 0048' group - Amount: Not yet specified At EcoRun 2026 in Varese, a sustainable fashion show brought together students and oncological women. The event, titled 'Fashion and Sustainability,' took place on Saturday, April 25, in Piazza Monte Grappa, with an exhibition at the Torre Civica. The protagonists of the initiative were the students of the evening courses in Clothing Operator and Made in Italy at ISIS Newton, alongside the women of the group 'Le 0048 – The strength of oncological women.' The fashion show represented the culmination of a journey built around three symbolic actions: destroy, assemble, and recreate. The outfits presented on the runway are the result of a recovery and regeneration process of existing clothes, transformed into new creations through recycling and enhancing materials. A clear message: it is possible to give new life to what seemed destined for discard. ### A message of resilience Sustainability, as emerged from the speeches and the introductory text read during the event, is not only environmental. It is also – and above all – human. The women of Le 0048 brought an intense testimony, made of personal experiences marked by oncological disease, but also of strength, resilience, and ability to rebirth. 'We are not here just to see clothes, but to testify to a change,' was emphasized. A change that also passes through daily choices and awareness of the value of life. Dettagli operativi

The 'Fashion and Sustainability' initiative in Varese was a concrete example of how fashion can become a vehicle for important messages. The students of ISIS Newton worked hard to transform existing clothes into new creations, demonstrating that recycling can be both creative and sustainable. This project not only showcased the students' skills but also gave them the opportunity to reflect on themes such as resilience and the value of life. The women of the group 'Le 0048' brought a powerful testimony, showing how oncological disease can be faced with courage and determination. Their stories deeply touched the audience, inviting everyone to reflect on the meaning of life and the importance of living every moment with awareness. The fashion show was a moment of sharing and mutual growth, where fragility was transformed into strength and creativity into a message of hope. ### An educational and social project The 'Fashion and Sustainability' project was born with the aim of creating an authentic and deep dialogue between different generations and experiences. The students had the opportunity to work closely with the women of the group 'Le 0048', learning not only recycling and regeneration techniques but also fundamental values such as resilience and solidarity. This exchange allowed the young people to understand the importance of supporting those who face difficulties, and the women to find a new purpose and energy through sharing their experiences. The event also had a significant impact on the local community. The presence of institutional representatives, including the mayor of Varese Davide Galimberti, underscored the importance of initiatives that promote sustainability and solidarity.
