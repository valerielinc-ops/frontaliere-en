Pro Velo Ticino: Sabbadini replaces Vitali

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 27 April 2026

Claudio Sabbadini has been elected the new president of Pro Velo Ticino, replacing Marco Vitali. Discussion on projects to improve cycling mobility in the Ticino valleys.

Contesto

In brief - Claudio Sabbadini is the new president of Pro Velo Ticino - Marco Vitali remains on the executive committee - Discussion on projects for cycling mobility in the Ticino valleys ## Key facts - What: Change of presidency at Pro Velo Ticino - When: Assembly held last Saturday - Where: Biasca - Who: Claudio Sabbadini, Marco Vitali - Amount: Not specified During the event held in Biasca, Pro Velo Ticino saw a change at the top. The assembly took note of the resignation of the committee president Marco Vitali, who stepped down due to his move to Alsace. Vitali, however, will remain a member of the executive committee. To replace him, Claudio Sabbadini, the outgoing vice-president and interim secretary of the association, was elected. In his speech of thanks, Sabbadini praised Vitali's great commitment to cycling mobility in Ticino and expressed recognition for being able to continue to count on his expertise and experience. During the assembly, members approved a modification of the statutes and appointed three new members to the committee. ### Projects for cycling mobility At the end of the assembly's work, the public was able to follow three interventions concerning cycling mobility in the Riviera and the Valleys. Juri Clericetti, president of the Bellinzona and Alto Ticino Regional Tourist Organization, outlined the activities of his organization and the sporting and leisure events in which the OTR is involved. Rémy Martinoli, president of Blenio Bike, emphasized the importance of having obtained recognition from Svizzera Mobile for 6 routes in Val Blenio. Sara Widmer, municipal councilor of Biasca, highlighted several gaps in the cycling network and hoped for greater commitment from the municipality in this area. ### Implications for cross-border workers...
