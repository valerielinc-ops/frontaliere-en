Di Valerie Linc · Published on 1 May 2026

A 63-year-old faces trial for providing materials to a Russian weapons of mass destruction program

Contesto

In brief - A 63-year-old tried in Bellinzona for supplies to Russian weapons program - Goods worth 75,000 francs without export license - Prosecution seeks 16-month suspended sentence ## Key facts - What: Supply of goods to Russian weapons program - When: Between 2020 and 2024 - Where: Federal Criminal Court of Bellinzona - Who: A 63-year-old commercial employee - Amount: 75,000 francs worth of goods supplied, offers for another 934,000 francs A 63-year-old will appear before the Federal Criminal Court (TPF) of Bellinzona for allegedly supplying materials presumed to be destined for a weapons of mass destruction program. According to the indictment presented by the Federal Public Prosecutor's Office, between 2020 and 2024, the man, a commercial employee at a company that supplies businesses with laboratory materials and medical and pharmaceutical products, delivered goods worth 75,000 francs and made offers for another 934,000 francs, without ever requesting an export license. The defendant is accused of aggravated violation of the Federal Law on the Control of Dual-Use Goods. This is an expedited procedure, possible when the defendant admits the facts. The prosecution is seeking a 16-month suspended sentence, compensation of 60,000 francs to the Confederation, and the payment of procedural costs of approximately 9,000 francs. It will be up to a single judge of the criminal court to determine whether the expedited procedure is appropriate and whether the requested penalty is adequate. ### Implications for cross-border workers This case raises important questions for cross-border workers in the export and supply of sensitive materials sectors.
