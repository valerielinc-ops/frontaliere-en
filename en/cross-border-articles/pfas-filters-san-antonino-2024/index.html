San Antonino: new PFAS filters in operation (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 7 May 2026

The municipality of San Antonino has activated an innovative activated carbon filtration system to remove PFAS substances from drinking water.

Contesto

In brief - New PFAS filters active in San Antonino - Investment of 1.8 million francs - Contamination linked to the Monte Ceneri tunnel ## Key facts - What: New activated carbon filtration system - When: Recently activated - Where: San Antonino, Ticino - Who: Municipality of San Antonino - Amount: 1.8 million francs The Municipality of San Antonino has recently put into operation an innovative activated carbon filtration system to remove PFAS from drinking water. This intervention was carried out near the school complex in the Boschetti area and represents a significant step forward for water quality in the region. The checks carried out have confirmed the effectiveness of the system, with the concentration of PFAS reduced to values below 2 ng/l, thus meeting the desired parameters. Despite the technical complexity of the project, the Municipality of San Antonino has succeeded in completing a cutting-edge work, being among the first in Ticino and among the pioneers in Switzerland to adopt a solution of this type. The total investment of approximately 1.8 million francs has been fully recognized by the Confederation, which has attributed the contamination of the groundwater to the works related to the Monte Ceneri base tunnel. This recognition underscores the importance of the intervention and the need to ensure safe drinking water for citizens. ### Impact on the community The activation of the new PFAS filters in San Antonino represents a significant step forward for public health and the quality of life of residents. PFAS, or perfluoroalkyl substances, are chemical compounds that can have negative effects on human health and the environment.
