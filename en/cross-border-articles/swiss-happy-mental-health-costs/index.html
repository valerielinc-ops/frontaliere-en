Switzerland: mental health costs keep rising (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 6 May 2026

Increasing expenses for mental health in Switzerland, with rising costs and more young people seeking treatment.

Contesto

In brief - Mental health costs +6% in 2024 - Total expenditure 3.2 billion CHF - Young people and women most affected ## Key facts - What: Increase in mental health spending - When: 2024 - Where: Switzerland - Who: Swiss Health Observatory (Obsan) - Amount: 3.2 billion CHF Switzerland is a country where the majority of the population declares themselves generally happy, but costs in the outpatient sector for boys and girls are increasing. This is what emerges from the new bulletin on mental health from the Swiss Health Observatory (Obsan), drafted on behalf of the Federal Office of Public Health (FOPH). The report was based on data collected in 2024, indicating that women and young people report feeling more often discouraged and depressive states. In 2024, the expenditure for mental health covered by compulsory insurance increased by approximately 6%, reaching around 3.2 billion francs, equivalent to 7.5% of total healthcare costs. Outpatient care recorded an average expenditure of 244 francs per insured person. Obsan signals that costs related to mental health have been constantly increasing since 2006. In parallel, an increase in the use of outpatient care and therapies has been observed, with higher costs for girls and women compared to boys and men (197 francs against 156 francs per each insured person in 2024). ### Reversal of trend among minors Among minors in 2024, a reversal of trend was recorded: for the first time, the percentage increase in costs was almost double for boys (+8.9%) compared to girls (+4.9%). ### Stable psychological well-being In 2024, levels of psychological well-being in Switzerland remained quite stable compared to the previous year: approximately 72% of the population declared feeling 'often or always happy'. Impact on cross-border workers The increase in mental health costs in Switzerland also has a significant impact on cross-border workers in Ticino. With the rise in outpatient care expenses, cross-border workers may face higher costs for psychological therapies and mental health treatments. This could affect their economic well-being and quality of life. ### Comparison with the previous situation Before 2024, mental health costs had been steadily increasing since 2006, but the 6% increase recorded in 2024 represents a significant jump. This increase is particularly evident among young people and women, who report feeling more often discouraged and depressed. For cross-border workers, this means it is important to be aware of the rising costs and plan accordingly. ### Concrete scenarios If a cross-border worker works in Switzerland and needs mental health care, it is important to know that costs may be higher than in the past. It is crucial to inform oneself about the services offered and the associated costs, in order to plan healthcare expenses adequately. Frequently Asked Questions
What are the average costs for outpatient mental health care in Switzerland?
In 2024, the average expenditure for outpatient mental health care was 244 francs per insured person, with higher costs for girls and women (197 francs) compared to boys and men (156 francs).
What are the main challenges for mental health in Switzerland?
The main challenges for mental health in Switzerland include the increase in indirect costs, such as loss of productivity and quality of life, and the need to strengthen access to care, prevention, and service coordination, particularly for children and young people.
