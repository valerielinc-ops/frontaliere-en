G7 in Evian: stricter border checks with France (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 6 May 2026

From June 10 to 19, enhanced checks at crossings with France for the G7 summit in Evian-les-Bains

Contesto

In brief - Enhanced checks from June 10 to 19 - G7 Summit in Evian-les-Bains from June 15 to 17 - Measures to ensure security in Switzerland ## Key facts - What: Internal border checks - When: From June 10 to 19, 2026 - Where: Border with France - Who: Swiss Federal Council - Amount: Up to 5000 military personnel for security The Swiss Federal Council has decided to strengthen internal border checks along the border with France on the occasion of the G7 summit in Evian-les-Bains. The measure, requested by the Canton of Geneva, will be in effect from June 10 to 19, 2026. The summit will take place from June 15 to 17 and will see the participation of the heads of government of the major industrialized nations and several partner states. The proximity to Switzerland exposes the cities of Geneva and Lausanne, as well as the entire Lake Geneva region, to security risks. In the past, during previous G7 summits, violent clashes, acts of vandalism, and sabotage have occurred. Furthermore, the high number of people protected by international law in the region and the tense geopolitical situation pose a series of challenges. During the indicated period, authorities will be able to carry out checks on people even in the absence of suspicions or close individual border crossings depending on the situation. The Federal Customs and Border Security Office (FCBSO) will strengthen its device based on the situation, as already happened on the occasion of the major sporting events of the summer of 2024. The Federal Council intends to strengthen its support for the cantons affected by the summit: Geneva, Vaud, and Valais. Practical Analysis The enhanced security measures for the G7 summit in Evian-les-Bains will have a significant impact on cross-border workers who daily cross the border between Switzerland and France. Stricter controls could cause delays and inconveniences, especially for those using the busiest border crossings. ### Scenario A: Regular Crossing If you are a cross-border worker who regularly crosses the border, it is important to plan your trips in advance. Check the border crossings you intend to use and verify any closures or restrictions. It might be useful to consider alternative, less crowded crossings to avoid delays. ### Scenario B: Business Travel If you need to cross the border for work reasons, make sure you have all the necessary documents with you. Checks might be more thorough, so it is advisable to have your work permit, passport, and any other required documents at hand. Additionally, check with your employer for any specific arrangements during the summit period. ### Scenario C: Personal Travel If you have personal plans that involve crossing the border during the summit period, plan ahead. It might be useful to contact local authorities for updated information on checks and any restrictions. Additionally, consider using alternative means of transport if the main border crossings are closed or particularly crowded. ### Comparison with the Previous Situation Before the introduction of these measures, border controls between Switzerland and France were less strict. Cross-border workers could cross the border more easily and without significant delays. However, during international events like the G7 summit, it is necessary to enhance security measures to ensure the protection of all those involved.
