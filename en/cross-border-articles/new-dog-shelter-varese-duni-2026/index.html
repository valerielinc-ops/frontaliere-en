New Varese ai Duni dog shelter: project approved, phased construction

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 1 May 2026

The Environment Commission has approved the project for the new Varese ai Duni dog shelter, but increased costs require a two-phase construction.

Contesto

In brief - Project approved for the new dog shelter in Varese ai Duni - Implementation in two phases due to increased costs - Priority to the refuge and wildlife ## Key facts - What: New municipal dog shelter - When: Project approved in 2026 - Where: Duni area, Varese - Who: Varese Environment Commission - Amount: 550,000 euros initially planned The path for the new municipal dog shelter in Varese takes a decisive step forward, albeit with a change of strategy dictated by the budget. The Environment Commission has approved the technical and economic feasibility project for the structure that will rise in the Duni area, but the work will not be completed in one go. The increase in costs has indeed pushed the administration to opt for a 'step-by-step' implementation. The Environment Councillor, Nicoletta San Martino, announced that the Municipality will intervene with new resources in the next budget variation, but to speed up the process, work will proceed in functional lots. The first phase involves the construction of the dog refuge, the cattery, and the area dedicated to wildlife. The second phase will concern the construction of the sanitary dog shelter as a separate building. ### Technical details The municipal architect Gianluca Gardelli illustrated in the Environment Commission the details of a structure calibrated to the real needs of the territory. The structure will have a capacity of about 40 boxes, designed to accommodate medium to large-sized dogs. A central body will house veterinary clinics, offices, food preparation rooms, and technical rooms. Spaces with prefabricated single-storey structures for the first aid of injured wild animals are also planned. The Duni area is known to be a transit point for local wildlife. Dettagli operativi

The realization of the new dog shelter in Varese ai Duni represents a significant step for animal welfare and the protection of wildlife in the region. The decision to proceed with the project in two phases is dictated not only by economic needs but also by a strategy for more efficient and sustainable management. ### Implications for the Community The first phase of the project, which includes the construction of the dog shelter, the cat shelter, and the area dedicated to wildlife, will have an immediate impact on the local community. These spaces will be fundamental to adequately accommodate dogs and cats, ensuring they have a safe and comfortable environment. Additionally, the area dedicated to wildlife will allow for immediate assistance to injured animals, contributing to the conservation of local biodiversity. The second phase, which involves the construction of the veterinary shelter as a standalone building, will be crucial to ensure advanced veterinary services and an optimal working environment for the staff. Until the completion of the second phase, veterinary functions will be integrated within the shelter structure or will continue to rely on the current headquarters at via Ottorino Rossi. ### Sustainability and Environmental Protection The project has paid great attention to sustainability and environmental protection. The buffer zone of approximately 10 meters between the structures and the 'ecological corridor' is an example of how the project has been designed to minimize the impact on local wildlife. Frequently Asked Questions
What are the phases of the new dog shelter in Varese ai Duni?
The project will be carried out in two phases. The first phase includes the construction of the dog shelter, the cattery, and the area dedicated to wildlife. The second phase involves the construction of the veterinary clinic as a separate building.
Why was the project divided into two phases?
The division into two phases is due to the increase in costs compared to the initial estimates. This approach allows for faster implementation and more efficient management of available resources.
What measures have been taken to protect the environment and wildlife?
The project includes a buffer zone of about 10 meters between the structures and the 'wildlife corridor' to avoid obstructing the passage of wild animals. Sound-absorbing barriers have been installed to limit noise pollution, and the fences have been repositioned to move the dog shelter activities away from wildlife paths.
