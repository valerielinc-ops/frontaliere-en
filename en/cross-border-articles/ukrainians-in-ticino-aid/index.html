Frequently Asked Questions
How many Ukrainians have received the S Permit in Switzerland and how many have been welcomed in Ticino?
Around 70,000 people have received the S Permit in Switzerland, with around 3,000 Ukrainian refugees welcomed in Ticino, including over 1,200 in Lugano and over 500 in Bellinzona.
How long does the S Permit last for Ukrainian refugees in Switzerland?
The S Permit is valid for one year and can be renewed.
How are Ukrainian unaccompanied minors protected in Ticino?
Specific protection mechanisms are activated, including the appointment of a legal representative, and the canton has created a team of experts for their protection.
How much funds has Ticino allocated to support Ukrainian refugees and how are they distributed?
Ticino has allocated 10 million francs, of which 5 million for social assistance and 3 million for economic assistance.
Why have some Ukrainian refugees had their asylum applications rejected in Switzerland?
Because they come from regions of western Ukraine considered 'relatively safe' by the federal parliament, such as Volhynia, Rivne, Lviv, Ternopil, Transcarpathia, Ivano-Frankivsk and Chernivtsi.
