Strong Franc: The Dual Impact for Cross-Border Commuters in Ticino (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 2 March 2026

The Swiss franc reaches historic highs against the euro and dollar. An analysis of its impact on Ticino's economy and the purchasing power of cross-border commuters.

Contesto

TL;DR - Swiss franc at historic highs against major currencies. - Strong franc benefits cross-border workers' purchasing power. - Ticino's economy faces challenges due to currency strength. ## Key facts - Currency Strength: Swiss franc at historic highs against euro and dollar. - Date of Analysis: February 25, 2026. - Safe Haven Asset: Franc perceived as safe haven during global uncertainty. - Real Exchange Rate: Franc relatively stable since 2015, slight recent appreciation. - Impact on Workers: Cross-border workers gain more euros from Swiss salaries. - Economic Pressure: Ticino's industry faces competitiveness challenges. - Sector Impact: Tourism sector in Ticino becomes more expensive for foreign tourists. - Currency Overvaluation: Franc overvalued by 4-5% against the euro, affecting businesses. The Swiss franc continues to be a hot topic, reaching exchange rates against major currencies not seen in a long time. On "Frontaliere Ticino," a platform followed daily by thousands of cross-border workers, it's a constant subject of discussion: what does such a strong franc concretely mean for those who earn in Switzerland and spend in euros in Italy? The source tvsvizzera.it, in an analysis from February 25, 2026, highlights how the nominal strength of the Swiss currency is almost historic, especially against the euro, and close to 2011 peaks against the dollar. This robustness is fueled by a dynamic well-known in financial markets: in times of global uncertainty, the franc is perceived as a safe haven asset. If in 2011 it was the American financial crisis and European debts that pushed investors towards Swiss security, today other geopolitical and macroeconomic tensions are guiding capital flows, transforming the franc into a true crisis currency. Dettagli operativi

The impact of the strong franc manifests with different nuances depending on the sector and perspective. For cross-border commuters, the most immediate advantage is an increase in purchasing power in Italy. With a franc strengthening against the euro, the salary earned in Switzerland translates into a larger amount of Italian currency, making daily expenses, purchases, and even mortgages in Italy more affordable. This results in a tangible benefit for families who cross the Brogeda, Gaggiolo, or Ponte Tresa passes every day, improving their quality of life and household budget management. However, the flip side affects Ticino's economy, particularly sectors most exposed to international markets. Jean-Philippe Kohl of Swissmem, the Swiss technology industry association, highlights that "compared to the euro, the franc is overvalued by 4-5%. This is a significant figure and, for many companies, exceeds what they can bear to remain competitive." Businesses, especially manufacturing and precision companies in Luganese and Sottoceneri, struggle to pass on increased costs to final prices without losing customers. They can compensate for an overvaluation of 3-4% with greater productivity or lower margins, but beyond this threshold, business stability is at risk. Nick Hayek, CEO of Swatch Group, complained that a full 308 million francs of his group's revenue decline was due to the currency effect, a figure that accumulates to 1.4 billion francs lost over twelve years due to "currency erosion." Ticino's tourism sector also feels the effects of the situation. While it's more affordable for Swiss residents to travel abroad or shop across the border, for tourists arriving from Eurozone countries or the United States, Switzerland becomes even more expensive. Punti chiave

In this strong franc scenario, cross-border commuters face opportunities and challenges that require attention and careful financial management. The main opportunity is, without a doubt, currency exchange optimization. Carefully monitoring exchange rates and converting euros into francs at the right time can make a substantial difference to the monthly budget. Many banks and exchange services offer different conditions, and a careful comparison can maximize the value of your salary in euros. For Ticino's companies, however, the challenge is ongoing. The demand for greater productivity and innovation become imperatives to absorb additional costs. This translates into constant pressure on employees, including cross-border commuters, to maintain high standards of efficiency and quality. The stability of the Ticino labor market, which saw a decrease in cross-border commuters in 2025 (source: frontaliereticino.ch, "frontalieri-ticino-calo-2025"), could be influenced by these currency dynamics, although Q4 2025 data show signs of a counter-trend in some sectors. It is essential for workers to stay updated on the economic conditions of their sector and, if possible, diversify their skills. 💡 Practical tips for cross-border commuters: - Monitor the exchange rate: Use reliable tools to track the CHF/EUR trend. - Plan conversions: If you have significant expenses in euros, consider converting francs when the rate is more favorable. - Smart budgeting: Take advantage of increased purchasing power to save or invest, without neglecting expenses in Switzerland. - Stay informed about your sector: Understanding your company's challenges can help you anticipate any impacts on your employment. Frequently Asked Questions
How is the Swiss franc performing against the euro at the moment?
The Swiss franc is almost historic in its nominal strength against the euro, reaching levels not seen for some time.
What is the impact of the strong franc on the Ticino tourism sector?
The strong franc makes Switzerland more expensive for tourists from the eurozone and the United States, potentially reducing the number of visitors and influencing employment in the hotel and catering sector.
What does the strong franc mean for exporting Ticino companies?
A strong franc makes Ticino products and services more expensive for foreign buyers, reducing competitiveness in international markets. Companies must increase productivity or reduce margins to compensate, with possible risks to employment stability if overvaluation exceeds 3-4%.
How can I optimize my frontier salary with the strong Swiss franc?
Carefully monitor CHF-EUR exchange rates. Converting earned francs into euros at the most appropriate time can maximize your purchasing power in Italy. Compare offers from different banks and currency exchange services to get the best terms.
What is the difference between nominal and real exchange rate for frontier workers?
The nominal rate is the direct value of the franc against the euro, which increases purchasing power in Italy. The real rate, adjusted for inflation, is more relevant to economic competitiveness. Although the nominal franc is strong, the real franc has been more stable, mitigating the impact on the local economy.
