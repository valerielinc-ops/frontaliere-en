The Chiasso Customs Office is preparing to become a modern technological hub. The Chiasso customs office is a strategic point for the traffic of goods and people between Switzerland and Italy. Every year, thousands of people and tonnes of goods cross the border. With the introduction of advanced technologies, such as automatic detection systems and digital management of documents, the Chiasso customs office aims to improve the efficiency and security of controls. Athos Capoferri, director of the Chiasso customs office, stated: 'Our priority is to leverage the most advanced technologies to improve the management of goods and people flows, while ensuring security and regulatory compliance.' Increased efficiency in checks and reduced waiting times could promote greater traffic fluidity and reduce costs for businesses. Frequently Asked Questions
When does the Chiasso customs digitization project begin?
The project began in 2023 and should be completed by 2026.
How many vehicles and people have transited through Chiasso customs in 2022?
In 2022, they passed through the customs of Chiasso more than 1.5 million vehicles and more than 2 million people.
What are the main technologies introduced to Chiasso customs to improve efficiency?
Chiasso customs introduced the automatic vehicle detection system and a digital document management platform. The automatic detection system helps to quickly identify vehicles, reducing waiting times by more than 30%. The digital management platform allows you to quickly examine and validate transport and freight documents, improving the overall efficiency of customs controls.
How much was invested in modernising the infrastructure and technologies of Chiasso customs in 2022?
In 2022, 2.5 million Swiss francs were invested in the modernization of the infrastructure and technologies of Chiasso customs. This investment is part of the plan to improve the ability to manage goods flows and people through the introduction of advanced technologies.
How does Chiasso Customs ensure compliance with the regulations for freight carriers?
Chiasso customs guarantees compliance with regulations by requiring freight carriers to use automatic detection systems and to expose transport and goods documents clearly and easily accessible. A new legislation has also been introduced which provides for the creation of a register of freight carriers that do not comply with regulations, to improve safety and compliance.
