Investigation opened into response system after Le Constellation bar fire. Victims' lawyers report shortcomings

Contesto

In brief - Investigation into Vaud Canton Rescue Organization - Complaint for alleged shortcomings in the intervention device - Fire at Le Constellation bar: 41 dead and 115 injured ## Key facts - What: Alleged gaps in rescue management - When: May 1, 2026 - Where: Crans-Montana, Canton of Valais - Who: Vaud Canton Rescue Organization - Amount: 41 dead and 115 injured The Vaud Canton Rescue Organization has come under scrutiny from the judiciary for alleged gaps in rescue management following the Crans-Montana fire. The complaint was filed by lawyers representing some of the victims of the fire at Le Constellation bar, which occurred on New Year's Eve. The Valais public prosecutor's office has confirmed the opening of a parallel investigation to the main case, which includes thirteen defendants. Italian lawyer Fabrizio Ventimiglia argued that the care provided to the injured in the immediate aftermath of the fire was insufficient. According to his statements, there were no stretchers, thermal blankets, or oxygen tanks, the latter being fundamental to allow the injured to breathe immediately. The Vaud Canton Rescue Organization defends itself in a statement, asserting that it is surprised by the accusations against its intervention device. According to the organization, the teams engaged on the ground are always composed of qualified professionals specifically trained to manage emergency situations with multiple victims. ### Implications for cross-border workers The ongoing investigation could have significant repercussions for cross-border workers who work or reside in the Canton of Valais. Analysis of the allegations The allegations against the Vaud rescue organization primarily concern the management of resources and the timeliness of intervention. According to the victims' lawyers, the shortcomings in the intervention system have compromised the organization's ability to respond immediately. ### Lack of resources One of the critical points highlighted is the lack of stretchers, thermal blankets, and oxygen bottles. These tools are essential to provide immediate assistance to the injured, especially in emergency situations with a high number of victims. The lack of these resources may have compromised the organization's response capacity, delaying assistance to the injured and worsening their conditions. ### Staff training The Vaud rescue organization has stated that the teams engaged on the ground are composed of qualified professionals specifically trained to handle emergency situations. However, the allegations from the victims' lawyers suggest that the training may not be sufficient to handle complex situations with a high number of victims. ### Intervention procedure Another critical aspect concerns the intervention procedure. According to the victims' lawyers, the management of resources and the coordination between the different rescue teams could be improved. A more efficient intervention procedure could ensure a more timely and effective response in case of emergency. ### Implications for cross-border workers The allegations against the Vaud rescue organization could have significant repercussions for cross-border workers who work or reside in the Canton of Vaud.
