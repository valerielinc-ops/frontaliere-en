TikTok has become a sort of showcase for recruitment, with videos showing the thefts and inviting young people to join. The main target is to steal luxury cars from Swiss garages. For over a year, Swiss dealerships have been targeted by increasingly audacious thieves. According to the Federal Office of Police (Fedpol), since 2025 there have been approximately 360 thefts and attempted break-ins at garages. The modus operandi is consolidated: the criminals break in with violent methods and, in a few minutes, steal high-end cars such as Lamborghinis, Ferraris, Porsches, and Mercedes. The latest theft occurred in March in the cantons of Thurgau and St. Gallen, where luxury vehicles and license plates worth over 1.5 million francs were stolen. Arrested were a 31-year-old woman and two men aged 24 and 43, all French. ### Implications for cross-border workers Theft of luxury cars not only affects dealerships but can also have repercussions for cross-border workers who work in Switzerland. Here are some recommendations: 1. Park in safe places: Use guarded parking lots or monitored areas to reduce the risk of theft. 2. Use anti-theft devices: Install advanced anti-theft devices such as alarms, steering wheel locks, and GPS tracking systems. 3. Do not leave valuable items in sight: Avoid leaving valuable items in the vehicle, especially when parked in public areas. 4. Report any suspicions immediately: In case of suspicions or unusual activities, contact the competent authorities immediately. ### Comparison with the Previous Situation The current situation represents a deterioration compared to previous years.
