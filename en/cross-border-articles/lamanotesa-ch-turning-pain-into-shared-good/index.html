Dettagli operativi

Practical Analysis: Restorative Justice and Family Support Restorative justice is an approach aimed at repairing the harm caused by a crime, rather than punishing the offender. This method is particularly effective for the families of victims, offering them an opportunity to express their emotions and find a sense of closure. The Lamanotesa.ch Foundation has adopted this approach to help the families of the victims of the 2011 Marrakech attack, which also affected several families from Ticino. According to the Foundation's data, over 50 families from the Canton of Ticino have participated in restorative justice meetings, with a satisfaction rate of 92%. ### Implications for Cross-Border Workers For cross-border workers living in Ticino, restorative justice can offer a model for dealing with pain and loss. Participating in meetings like those organized by the Lamanotesa.ch Foundation can help create a sense of community and mutual support, fostering emotional healing. For example, in the municipality of Lugano, 30 cross-border workers participated in a support program in 2022, with positive results in terms of reducing post-traumatic stress. ### Concrete Procedures If you are a cross-border worker interested in participating in a restorative justice meeting, you can contact the Lamanotesa.ch Foundation for information on upcoming events. Frequently Asked Questions
What is restorative justice?
Restorative justice is an approach that aims to repair the harm caused by a crime, rather than punish the offender. This approach can be particularly useful for victims' families, offering them an opportunity to express their emotions and find a sense of closure.
How can I participate in a restorative justice meeting?
You can contact the Lamanotesa.ch Foundation for information on upcoming events and how to participate. Make sure to bring documents that prove your participation and follow the guidelines provided by the Foundation.
What are the benefits of restorative justice for cross-border workers?
Restorative justice can offer cross-border workers a model for dealing with pain and loss. Participating in meetings like those organized by the Lamanotesa.ch Foundation can help create a sense of community and mutual support, promoting emotional healing.
