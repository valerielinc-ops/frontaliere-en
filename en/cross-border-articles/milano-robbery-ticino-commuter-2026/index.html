Robbery in Milan: Ticino cross-border worker robbed of a 35,000 CHF watch

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 21 April 2026

Three arrests for the robbery of a Swiss citizen residing in the Canton of Lucerne, who was followed and robbed in Milan after a train journey from Lugano.

Contesto

TL;DR - Robbery of Swiss citizen in Milan, 2026 - Victim robbed of 35,000 francs watch - Three suspects arrested, one still at large ## Key facts - Victim: 34-year-old Swiss citizen from Lucerne - Date: Sunday, January 25, 2026 - Location: Via Vittor Pisani, near Milan Central Station - Stolen Item: Watch worth around 35,000 francs - Suspects: Three Egyptians and one Moroccan, aged 19-21 - Arrests: Three suspects arrested, one still at large - Crime: Aggravated robbery in concert - Train: Tilo train running as RE-80 ## The robbery facts On the evening of Sunday, January 25, 2026, a 34-year-old Swiss citizen residing in the Canton of Lucerne but traveling from Lugano, was the victim of a robbery in Milan. The man, who arrived in the Lombard metropolis on a Tilo train running as RE-80, was surrounded and attacked in the area of via Vittor Pisani, near the Central Station. The unfortunate victim, already identified during the train journey, was followed for a few hundred meters outside the station and robbed of a watch estimated to be worth around 35,000 francs. The man also suffered physical injuries. ### The arrests According to the State Police, three detention orders have been issued against two North Africans already detained in San Vittore prison for similar offenses, and a third individual has been arrested. A fourth accomplice is currently being sought. The criminals, three Egyptians and a Moroccan aged between 19 and 21, are accused of aggravated robbery in concert. ## Implications for cross-border workers This episode raises concerns about the safety of cross-border workers traveling between Ticino and Italy. Frequently Asked Questions
What should I do if I am a victim of a robbery?
If you are a victim of a robbery, call the authorities immediately (State Police at 113 or Local Police at 112), do not pursue the criminals, gather information about the robbers, file a report at the nearest police station, and contact your insurance to report the incident and request assistance.
How can I protect myself from robberies during travel?
To protect yourself from robberies during travel, avoid displaying valuable items, be aware of your surroundings, inform yourself about high-risk areas before moving, consulting reliable sources or local authorities, and use safe transportation services, preferring less crowded travel times.
What documents are necessary to file a report?
To file a report, make sure you have with you an identity document (passport or ID card), details of the robbery (time, place, and description of the criminals), a list of stolen items with estimated value, and emergency contacts (phone numbers of the authorities and insurance).
