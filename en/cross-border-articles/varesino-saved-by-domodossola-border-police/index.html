Varese man rescued by Domodossola Border Police (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 16 May 2026

A 57-year-old man, resident in the province of Varese, was rescued by the Domodossola Border Police after expressing the intention to take his own life.

Contesto

In brief - A 57-year-old man saved by Domodossola Border Police - Rescued at Brig railway station, in Switzerland - Crucial intervention to prevent the worst ## Key facts - What: Rescue of a man in crisis - When: Wednesday evening - Where: Brig railway station, in Switzerland - Who: Domodossola Border Police - Amount: Not specified A man aged between 50 and 60, residing in the province of Varese, was saved by the Domodossola Border Police after expressing the intention to take his own life. The intervention concluded with the man's return home with his family. The incident occurred on Wednesday evening at the Brig railway station in Switzerland, where officers from the Domodossola Border Police Sector were conducting a routine cross-border control service. The man, heading abroad without a specific destination, drew the attention of the police officers due to his high state of agitation during the checks. During the conversation with the officers, he mentioned his intention to commit an extreme act. Immediately understanding the delicacy of the situation, the officers managed, albeit with difficulty, to convince him to follow them back to Italy, accompanying him back to Domodossola, from where he had departed autonomously. Subsequent investigations revealed that his family had reported his disappearance the day before to the Carabinieri of Angera. The man's brother and daughter, both residing in the Varesotto area, were then contacted and immediately set off for Domodossola. Given the man's psychophysical condition, upon arrival at the offices in via Bonomelli, he was entrusted to the care of the medical staff of a medicalized ambulance requested by the police officers themselves.
