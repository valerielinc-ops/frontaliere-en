Dettagli operativi

To work in Switzerland as a cross-border worker, obtaining the G permit is fundamental. This document is issued by the competent immigration office for the place of work and allows cross-border workers to legally work in Switzerland while maintaining their residence in their country of origin. ### Requirements for the G permit The G permit is specific to citizens of European Union countries and the European Free Trade Association (EFTA). To obtain it, you need to present a series of documents, including: - Employment contract: indicating the duration and type of work. - Proof of residence: a document attesting to your residence in the country of origin. - Health insurance: it is mandatory to demonstrate that you have valid health insurance in Switzerland. ### Application process The application process for the G permit takes place through the competent office of the municipality of work. For example, if a cross-border worker works in Lugano, they must go to the Lugano Population Office. It is important to note that the G permit is only valid for a period of six months and must be renewed to continue working in Switzerland. Renewal requires the presentation of new documents, such as an update of the employment contract. ### Associated costs The costs for the G permit application can vary. Generally, there are costs of around 150-200 CHF for the procedure. This is in addition to any translation costs for official documents, if necessary. ### Operational checklist Here is an operational checklist to ensure you have everything you need: - [ ] Signed employment contract - [ ] Valid identification document - [ ] Proof of residence in the country of origin - [ ] Valid health insurance - [ ] Completed application form ### Practical examples Consider a concrete example: an...

Additionally, to receive your salary in Switzerland, it is necessary to open a bank account in Switzerland. There are many banks in Switzerland that offer accounts for cross-border workers, such as UBS, Credit Suisse, and Raiffeisen. It is essential to compare the conditions offered, such as management fees and charges for withdrawals abroad. For example, some banks may charge up to 10 CHF for a withdrawal in foreign currency, while others offer free withdrawals. In the Ticino region, for instance, those working in Lugano might also consider local banks like BancaStato, which offers specific accounts for cross-border workers with advantageous conditions. It is important to conduct thorough research to find the bank that best suits your needs, taking into account monthly expenses and the services offered. Finally, registration with the AVS (Old Age and Survivors Insurance) is mandatory for all cross-border workers employed in Switzerland. The AVS is the Swiss social security system and provides coverage for illness, disability, and old age. Every worker must contribute to this system, with a current rate of 8.7% on gross salary, equally divided between employer and employee. For example, if a cross-border worker earns 4,000 CHF per month, their contribution to the AVS will be approximately 174 CHF. ## Document checklist for working in Switzerland - Work permit: Ensure you have a valid work permit, which can be a G permit for cross-border workers. - Bank account: Open an account with a Swiss bank that offers advantageous conditions for cross-border workers. - AVS registration: Register with the AVS and start paying the necessary contributions. - Health insurance: Choose a health insurance plan that meets Swiss regulations, as it is mandatory.
