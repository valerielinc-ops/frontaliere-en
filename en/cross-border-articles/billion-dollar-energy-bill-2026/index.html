In brief - Diesel over 2 francs per litre in Switzerland - Additional energy costs: 4.9 billion francs - Italy could spend three times as much ## Key facts - What: Increase in fuel and fossil fuel prices - When: After the attack on Iran in 2026 - Where: Switzerland and Italy - Who: Families and businesses - Amount: 4.9 billion francs in Switzerland The military escalation in the Persian Gulf is driving up the prices of fuels and fossil fuels. The energy crisis is having serious repercussions for families and the economy in Switzerland. Those who travel by car or heat their homes with fossil energies feel the direct effects of the war in the Middle East: diesel has exceeded 2 francs per litre — this has not happened since 2022, after the Russian invasion of Ukraine — and petrol has increased by over 20 cents since the beginning of March. The most significant increases are seen in kerosene, whose price has more than doubled since the start of military operations, and heating oil, which has gone from 100 to 150 francs for 100 litres. Switzerland does not directly import oil from the Persian Gulf, but it suffers the consequences of rising prices in energy markets. If the prices of petrol, diesel, heating oil, kerosene and natural gas remained at the levels of 23 March for a year, the additional energy costs for consumers in Switzerland would amount to 4.9 billion francs, writes Cyril Brunner, a researcher at the Swiss Federal Institute of Technology in Zurich (ETH), on his LinkedIn profile. He arrived at this figure by taking into account the price increases and extrapolating the costs over annual consumption, he explained to the Tages-Anzeiger. Frequently Asked Questions
What are the main causes of the increase in fossil fuel prices?
The main causes of the increase in fossil fuel prices are linked to the conflict in the Middle East, which has disrupted the global transport of oil and gas. This has led to an increase in demand and a reduction in supply, with consequent price hikes.
How can I reduce fuel consumption for daily commutes?
To reduce fuel consumption, you can adopt various strategies, such as carpooling with other cross-border workers who travel the same route, using public transportation for daily commutes, or considering the purchase of an electric or hybrid car.
What are the alternatives to heating with heating oil?
Alternatives to heating with heating oil include installing more efficient heating systems, such as heat pumps, or switching to renewable energy sources, such as solar panels for hot water production. Additionally, you can consider installing thermal insulation systems to reduce heat loss.
