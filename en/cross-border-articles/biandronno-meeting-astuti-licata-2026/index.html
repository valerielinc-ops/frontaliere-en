Biandronno: public meeting with Astuti and Licata on healthcare and transport

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 2 May 2026

Biandronno Più organizes a public meeting with regional councilors Astuti and Licata to discuss healthcare and transport in the Varesotto area.

Contesto

In brief - Public meeting in Biandronno with Astuti and Licata on May 6, 2026 - Focus on healthcare and transportation in the Varesotto area - Free entry and open to all ## Key facts - What: Public meeting on healthcare and transportation - When: Wednesday, May 6, 2026, at 8:45 PM - Where: Fallaci Hall, Piazza Cavour, Biandronno - Who: Regional councilors Samuele Astuti (PD) and Giuseppe Licata (FI) - Cost: Free entry Biandronno Più has organized a public meeting with regional councilors Samuele Astuti of the Democratic Party and Giuseppe Licata of Forza Italia. The event will take place on Wednesday, May 6, 2026, at 8:45 PM in the Fallaci Hall of Piazza Cavour in Biandronno. The meeting aims to create a direct dialogue between citizens and institutions to address crucial issues of the territory, such as healthcare and transportation. The initiative was born with the objective of keeping the relationship between elected officials and the community alive even outside of election campaigns, emphasizing the importance of decisions that impact daily life. ### Healthcare and transportation at the center of the debate The two main topics of the meeting will be healthcare and transportation. Regarding healthcare, the focus will be on the organization of services, waiting lists, general medicine, and the relationship between the Region and local facilities. The second topic will be dedicated to transportation and traffic, with attention to infrastructure, connections between the municipalities of Lake Varese, and public transportation. Ample space will also be reserved for the interventions and questions of the citizens, with the aim of clarifying the functioning of the institutions and the competencies of the different administrative levels. ### Participation and objectiv...

Implications for cross-border workers and residents The public meeting in Biandronno represents an important opportunity for cross-border workers and residents of the Varesotto area to discuss the challenges and opportunities related to healthcare and transportation. For cross-border workers who commute to Switzerland and reside in Italy, the issue of transportation is particularly relevant, as it directly affects their quality of life and their ability to move easily between the two countries. ### Comparison between local and Swiss realities A comparison between local realities and those in Switzerland could highlight the differences and similarities in healthcare services and transportation. For example, cross-border workers might ask for information on how to access healthcare services in Switzerland and what the procedures are for using healthcare services in Italy. Additionally, opportunities for improving transportation infrastructure to facilitate movement between the two countries could be discussed. ### Future scenarios and cross-border collaborations The meeting could also pave the way for cross-border collaborations to improve healthcare services and transportation. For example, the possibility of creating agreements between local Italian and Swiss authorities to improve the quality of healthcare services and make transportation more efficient could be explored. This could include the creation of preferential routes for cross-border workers and the promotion of joint initiatives to improve the accessibility of services. ### Active participation and community involvement The active participation of citizens is fundamental to the success of the meeting. How to participate in the meeting To participate in the public meeting with regional councilors Astuti and Licata, simply show up at the Sala Fallaci in Piazza Cavour, Biandronno on May 6, 2026, at 20:45. Admission is free and open to everyone, so no reservation is required. It is advisable to arrive a few minutes early to find a seat and prepare for the debate. ### What to bring to the meeting During the meeting, participants can bring documents or notes related to specific issues they wish to discuss. For example, they might bring information about waiting lists for medical visits, public transport issues, or proposals to improve local infrastructure. This can help make the debate more productive and focused on concrete solutions. ### After the meeting: next steps After the meeting, it is important to follow up on the initiatives and projects that may arise from the debate. Participants can stay in touch with the organizers of Biandronno Più and the regional councilors for updates and further discussions. Additionally, they can join working groups or local commissions to actively contribute to the improvement of healthcare and transport services in the area. ### Useful tools for cross-border workers For cross-border workers who want to learn more about healthcare and transport services, the salary calculator can provide an idea of the costs and benefits related to cross-border work.
