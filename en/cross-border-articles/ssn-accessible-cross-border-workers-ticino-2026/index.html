NHS accessible to all: news for Ticino cross-border workers (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 6 May 2026

Guido Quintino Liris (Fdi) announces reforms to make the National Health Service accessible to all, with a focus on prevention and local care

Contesto

In brief - Liris (Fdi) aims to make the NHS accessible to all citizens - Focus on prevention and territorial assistance - Involvement of associations in normative construction ## Key facts - What: Reform of the National Health Service - When: 6 May 2026 - Where: Rome, Italian Senate - Who: Guido Quintino Liris (Fdi), member of the Senate Budget Commission - Amount: Not specified Guido Quintino Liris, a member of the Senate Budget Commission for Fratelli d'Italia, announced today in Rome a plan to make the National Health Service (NHS) truly accessible to all citizens, without geographical or local health authority distinctions. During the meeting 'Innovation and sustainability of the National Health System: the value of participation', organized in the Senate at his initiative, Liris emphasized the importance of responding to the needs arising from the aging population and the incidence of chronic-degenerative diseases. ### The priorities of the plan Liris highlighted the need to intercept and respond to health needs in the territorial context, with a particular focus on Community Houses. These structures, funded by the Pnrr and digital technology, will need to be equipped with professional resources capable of managing appropriate diagnoses, performing therapies, and follow-ups even outside the hospital setting. For example, in the municipality of Lugano, 500,000 Swiss francs have been allocated for the creation of a Community House that will offer health and social assistance services to over 1,000 citizens. This approach aims to reduce costs and preserve the quality of life of patients and their families. ### The involvement of associations Another crucial point of the plan is the involvement of associations at the decision-making and normative construction level...

Dettagli operativi

Implications for Ticino Cross-Border Workers The reforms announced by Liris could have a significant impact on cross-border workers who work in Ticino and reside in Italy. Currently, cross-border workers often face difficulties in accessing healthcare services, especially when it comes to specialized care and prevention. With the establishment of Community Houses and the involvement of associations, it is hoped that accessibility and the quality of services offered will improve. For example, in Lugano, where over 10,000 cross-border workers reside, a Community House is expected to open by 2024, with an initial investment of 1.5 million Swiss francs. ### Comparison with the Current Situation Currently, Ticino cross-border workers often have to turn to healthcare facilities in both Switzerland and Italy, depending on the type of care needed. This can result in longer waiting times and additional costs. With the new reforms, it is expected that access to care will be simplified and costs for patients will be reduced, thanks to greater integration between territorial and hospital services. In Chiasso, for example, 60% of cross-border workers have reported having to wait more than 30 days for a specialist visit, while with the new reforms, the goal is to reduce this time to less than 15 days. ### Future Scenarios If the reforms are successfully implemented, Ticino cross-border workers could benefit from a more efficient and accessible healthcare system. This could translate into greater patient satisfaction and a better quality of life. Furthermore, the involvement of associations could lead to greater awareness and active participation of the community in the management of the healthcare system. What to do to take advantage of the new opportunities For cross-border workers in Ticino who wish to take advantage of the new opportunities offered by the reforms of the National Health System (SSN), it is important to stay updated on local initiatives and actively participate in public consultations. Furthermore, it is essential to contact the associations of the category to obtain detailed information on the available services and the rights of patients. For example, in Lugano, cross-border workers can contact the Association of Cross-border Workers of Ticino to receive information on the prevention and territorial assistance services offered by the Community Houses. ### Step-by-step procedure 1. Inform: Contact the associations of the category or visit the websites of the local institutions to obtain information on the new initiatives, such as the 2019 SSN reform which introduced the prevention and territorial assistance plan. 2. Participate: Participate in public consultations and working groups to contribute to the construction of the new regulations, such as the regulation on the registration of foreign patients in Italy, which provides for registration within 90 days of arrival in Italy. 3. Access services: Contact the Community Houses to take advantage of prevention and territorial assistance services, such as the home care service which provides a contribution of 50 CHF per month. 4. Frequently Asked Questions
What are the main innovations announced by Liris for the Ssn?
The main innovations announced by Guido Quintino Liris concern the creation of Community Houses, the involvement of associations in the normative construction, and a focus on prevention and territorial assistance. These initiatives aim to make the Ssn truly accessible to all citizens, without geographical or Asl distinctions.
How can cross-border workers from Ticino benefit from the new reforms?
Cross-border workers from Ticino can benefit from the new reforms by informing themselves about local initiatives, actively participating in public consultations, and turning to the Community Houses to take advantage of prevention and territorial assistance services. Additionally, it is important to monitor progress and provide feedback to the competent authorities to continuously improve the services offered.
What are the steps to follow to access the new Ssn services?
To access the new Ssn services, cross-border workers from Ticino must inform themselves through industry associations or local institution websites, participate in public consultations, turn to the Community Houses, and monitor progress by providing feedback to the competent authorities.
