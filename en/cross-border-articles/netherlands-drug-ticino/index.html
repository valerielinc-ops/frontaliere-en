A Dutch citizen was stopped with four kilos of cocaine at the Kreuzlingen border, Switzerland.

Contesto

TL;DR - Dutch man arrested with 4kg cocaine in Ticino - Drugs hidden in car at Swiss-Italian border - Severe penalties for drug trafficking in Switzerland - Collaboration between Swiss and Italian authorities ## Key facts - Cosa: 4 kilograms of cocaine - Quando: Last weekend - Dove: Kreuzlingen border, canton of St. Gallen - Chi: 25-year-old Dutch citizen - Importo: 10 years in prison for over 100 grams of cocaine - Scadenza: June 27, 2018 (law modification) A 25-year-old Dutch citizen was stopped by Swiss authorities at the Kreuzlingen border in the canton of St. Gallen with four kilograms of cocaine. The event occurred last weekend. The drugs were hidden in his car. The young man was arrested and the cocaine was confiscated. The police have opened an investigation to determine the origin of the drugs and the young man's intentions.

The Drug and the Border A young Dutch man was arrested at the border between Switzerland and Italy with four kilograms of cocaine hidden in his vehicle. The arrest was made possible thanks to a tip from the Italian police and the collaboration between the authorities of the two countries. The border between Switzerland and Italy, particularly in the Ticino region, is a critical point for drug trafficking. - The Ticinese authorities have intensified controls in recent years, thanks also to the Swiss legislation on drug trafficking, which provides for severe penalties for those caught with over 10 grams of cocaine. - The law of June 27, 2018, which modifies the federal law on narcotics, has toughened penalties for drug trafficking, providing for up to 10 years in prison for those who sell over 100 grams of cocaine. > "The fight against drug trafficking is a priority for the Ticinese authorities, who work closely with Italian law enforcement to stop this type of illicit activity," said a spokesperson for the Ticino cantonal police. ## Operational Checklist - Regular checks at border crossings - Collaboration between Swiss and Italian authorities - Use of advanced technologies to identify narcotics - Continuous training for law enforcement ## Practical Scenarios Comparison In 2020, the Ticino police seized 15 kilograms of cocaine at the border with Italy, while in 2022, 20 individuals involved in drug trafficking were arrested.
