In brief - A 20-year-old Italian arrested in Varese with 40 blocks of hashish - Drugs hidden in the woods to evade the police - Arrested in the act - Cross-border workers at risk: Swiss borders and strict regulations ## Key facts - What: Arrested dealer with 40 blocks of hashish - When: Monday, May 4, 2026 - Where: Varese - Who: State Police - Amount: 40 blocks of hashish (approximately 4 kg) - Regulation: Law 309/1990, art. 73 (possession with intent to supply) A 20-year-old Italian was arrested in Varese on Monday, May 4, 2026, in the act of possessing narcotic substances with intent to supply. The State Police officers, coordinated by the Varese Commissariat, identified the young man thanks to evident traces that led to the discovery of a storage room containing cash amounting to approximately 5,000 euros, materials for weighing and packaging the drugs, and 40 blocks of hashish. The young man, originally from Varese, was referred to the judicial authority and is now detained at 'Miogni' in Varese, awaiting trial. ### The desperate escape The young man, surrounded by law enforcement, tried to get rid of the drugs by throwing a bag with 40 blocks of hashish into a wooded area situated between Varese and Gazzada Schianno. A desperate move that did not help him evade the police, who nevertheless found the drugs hidden in the woods. According to the estimates of the State Police, the value of the seized drugs is around 20,000 euros. ### Implications for cross-border workers This arrest raises some important issues for cross-border workers who work in Switzerland and reside in Italy.
