Fuel tax cut extended: diesel 24.4c, petrol 6c discount

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 30 April 2026

The Italian government has extended the reduction of fuel excise duties for three weeks, with a differentiated discount for petrol and diesel.

Contesto

In brief - Three-week extension for the reduction of fuel excise duties - Diesel discount: 24.4 cents per litre - Petrol discount: 6 cents per litre ## Key facts - What: Extension of fuel excise duty reduction - When: From 1st May 2026 for three weeks - Where: Italy - Who: Italian government - Amount: Diesel 24.4 cents/litre, petrol 6 cents/litre The Italian government has extended the reduction of fuel excise duties for another three weeks, which was set to end on Friday, 1st May. The measure, introduced in April, aims to contain the rise in prices caused by the war in the Middle East. The discount for diesel remains at 24.4 cents per litre, while for petrol it drops to 6 cents per litre. Prime Minister Giorgia Meloni explained that the discount on diesel has been kept higher because the increase in market price has been heavier for this fuel. ### Impact on cross-border workers The extension of the reduction of fuel excise duties will have a significant impact on cross-border workers who work in Ticino and reside in Italy. With the cost of fuel representing an important part of monthly expenses, this measure can alleviate the economic burden for those who travel daily between the two countries. However, it is important to note that the reduction of the discount on petrol could influence mobility choices, pushing some to consider alternative means of transport. ### Comparison with other European measures While Italy extends the reduction of excise duties, other European countries are introducing measures to contain fuel consumption. This differentiated approach raises questions about long-term strategies to address the energy crisis. The decision to maintain a higher discount for diesel compared to petrol reflects market dynamics and the specific needs of different sectors. However, it is essential to consider the long-term implications of this measure. ### Market effects The reduction in excise duties can influence the fuel market in various ways. On the one hand, it can stimulate demand, especially for diesel, which benefits from a more substantial discount. On the other hand, it could also encourage the use of more fuel-efficient vehicles, such as diesel ones, which are generally more economical to manage. ### Environmental considerations While the reduction in excise duties can provide immediate economic relief, it is important to consider the environmental implications. Encouraging the use of fossil fuels could contradict sustainability and emission reduction goals. In a context of growing environmental awareness, this measure could be seen as a step back from energy transition policies. ### Comparison with other European policies In contrast to the Italian approach, other European countries are adopting measures to reduce fuel consumption. For example, some states are introducing incentives for the purchase of electric or hybrid vehicles, or implementing low-emission zones in cities. These policies aim to reduce dependence on fossil fuels and promote more sustainable mobility. ### Future scenarios It is possible that, in the future, Italy will follow the example of other European countries and introduce measures to contain fuel consumption.
