Fuel duty cut extended: full diesel discount, reduced petrol

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 2 May 2026

The government extends the fuel duty cut, but with a distinction between diesel and petrol. Find out what changes and how it affects you.

Contesto

In brief - Excise duty cut extended for 21 days - Full discount on diesel, reduced on petrol - Measure to contain price hikes linked to international crisis ## Key facts - What: Excise duty cut on fuels - When: From Saturday, May 2, 2026 - Where: Italy - Who: Italian government - Amount: 20 cents per litre for diesel, 5 cents for petrol The Italian government has decided to extend the excise duty cut on fuels for another 21 days, but with a significant change: the discount remains substantial on diesel while it is reduced on petrol. The measure, effective from Saturday, May 2, comes in response to the price increase linked to tensions in the Middle East. The reduction remains at 20 cents per litre for diesel, while for petrol it drops to 5 cents. A choice that marks a shift from previous months, when cuts were more uniform and amounted to 25-30 cents in total between excise duties and VAT. ### Why this distinction? The government aims to particularly support diesel, considered strategic for goods transport and various productive sectors. In this context, additional tax credits are also planned for road transport operators. The difference between the two fuels is not accidental, but responds to a targeted strategy to support the economy in a moment of strong international instability. ### Effects on prices With the confirmed cut, the price of diesel could remain below the 2 euro per litre threshold, while the impact on petrol will be more contained compared to previous weeks. The measure remains temporary and does not introduce a price cap, but aims to contain the immediate effects of energy price hikes. Frequently Asked Questions
How long will the fuel excise duty cut last?
The fuel excise duty cut has been extended for another 21 days, starting from Saturday, May 2, 2026. This measure is temporary and valid until the end of May, pending the evolution of energy markets.
What is the difference between the excise duty cut on diesel and petrol?
The reduction remains at 20 cents per litre for diesel, while for petrol it drops to 5 cents. This distinction was introduced to particularly support diesel, considered strategic for freight transport and various productive sectors.
How will the fuel excise duty cut be financed?
The measure will be financed through antitrust sanctions and additional VAT revenue. This means the government has found alternative resources to support this measure without further burdening public finances.
