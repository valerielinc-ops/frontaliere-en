Valposchiavo: Growing Tourism in 2025 (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 24 February 2026

Poschiavo sees a significant increase in overnight stays and cultural visits, thanks to the Wakker Prize and local initiatives.

Contesto

TL;DR - Poschiavo sees 81,000 overnight stays in 2025, up from 74,000. - Valposchiavo Museums attract 8,000 visitors, with 6,700 for 'Pastry Chefs' exhibition. - Wakker Prize 2025 boosts Poschiavo's visibility and tourism. ## Key facts - Overnight stays: 81,000 in 2025, up from 74,000 in the previous year. - Museum visitors: 8,000 visitors to Valposchiavo Museums in 2025. - Exhibition visitors: 6,700 visitors to 'Pastry Chefs - Valposchiavo Adventures in Europe' in 2024. - Wakker Prize: 2025, highlighting Poschiavo's historical and cultural heritage. - Tourism initiatives: Thematic guided tours and collaborations with UNESCO Albula-Bernina Heritage Association. - Flyer distribution: 'Poschiavo - Excellent' flyer reprinted due to growing demand. - Cross-border workers: Growing tourism may create new job opportunities in the region. - Transportation: Rhaetian Railway offers efficient service for commuters and tourists. ## Poschiavo's Tourism Boom 📊 81,000 overnight stays in 2025: Poschiavo, located in the Canton of Grisons, recorded a notable increase compared to 74,000 in the previous year. This positive trend was accompanied by a record 8,000 visitors to the Valposchiavo Museums, with a steady rise in recent years. The exhibition 'Pastry Chefs - Valposchiavo Adventures in Europe', for instance, attracted 6,700 visitors in 2024 alone. > According to Thomas Fries, director of Valposchiavo Tourism, the valley's increased visibility is also tied to the prestigious Wakker Prize 2025, which highlighted the region's historical and cultural heritage. However, Fries advises caution when analyzing the data: 'It is difficult to assess the exact reasons for travel and stays.' Frequently Asked Questions
What has been the impact of the Wakker Prize 2025 on tourism in Poschiavo?
The Wakker Prize 2025 has enhanced the local historical and cultural heritage, increasing the visibility of Poschiavo and contributing to the increase in overnight stays (81,000 in 2025) and visitors to museums (8,000).
What is the impact of mass tourism in Poschiavo on the cost of living for cross-border commuters?
Mass tourism in Poschiavo could increase the cost of living, especially when it comes to rent and property prices. However, hard data for 2025 indicate a 5% increase in rents compared to 2024, with no significant changes in basic food prices.
What are the language requirements to work in tourism in Poschiavo if I am an Italian cross-border commuter?
To work in tourism in Poschiavo, it is essential to know at least two languages: Italian, German and English. German is often required for Swiss and German clientele, while English is indispensable for international tourists. Some establishments require specific certifications, such as the Goethe-Zertifikat for German.
How can I check if an employer in Poschiavo offers permanent contracts to Italian cross-border commuters?
In Switzerland, permanent contracts are common in the tourism and culture sectors. To check, you can consult the offers on platforms such as JobUp.ch or contact the accommodation facilities in Poschiavo directly. Please note: Contracts may include 3-6 month probationary clauses.
What are the tax breaks for Italian cross-border commuters working in tourism in Poschiavo?
Italian cross-border commuters working in Switzerland can benefit from the double taxation treaty, paying taxes in Italy but at reduced rates in Switzerland. In addition, some companies offer meal or accommodation vouchers, tax-free up to 600 CHF/month. Always check with a tax advisor.
