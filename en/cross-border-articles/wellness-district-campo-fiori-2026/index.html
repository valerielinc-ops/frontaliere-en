Wellness district in the shadow of Campo dei Fiori

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 29 April 2026

Discover how 2% of taxpayers hold 15% of the income in these hillside communes of Varese.

Contesto

In brief - 2% of taxpayers hold 15% of income - Average income of 34,746 euros in the area - Luvinate leads with an average income of 37,654 euros ## Key facts - What: Declared average income - When: 2024 declarations - Where: Municipalities of the Varese hills - Who: Taxpayers of the municipalities of Luvinate, Casciago, Barasso, Comerio, Galliate Lombardo, Ranco, Bodio Lomnago - Amount: Weighted average income of 34,746 euros In the 'well-being district' in the shadow of Campo dei Fiori, the municipalities of the Varese hills show a concentration of wealth above the provincial average. The municipalities of Luvinate, Casciago, Barasso, Comerio, Galliate Lombardo, Ranco, and Bodio Lomnago, with a total of 13,900 inhabitants and 10,548 taxpayers, present a fiscal identity that clearly emerges from the 2024 declarations published by the Ministry of Economy. Luvinate tops the ranking with an average declared income of 37,654 euros per taxpayer, closely followed by Galliate Lombardo with 37,585 euros. Comerio, Ranco, Casciago, Bodio Lomnago, and Barasso follow with average incomes of 34,551, 34,518, 34,039, 34,029, and 33,672 euros respectively. The weighted average income of the entire area is 34,746 euros, 24% higher than the provincial average. The most significant data is the concentration of the upper part of the income distribution. Out of the 10,548 taxpayers in the area, 237 declare over 120,000 euros in total income, equal to 2.33% of the total, compared to 0.97% of the provincial average. In some municipalities, such as Galliate Lombardo, Casciago, and Barasso, the percentage of taxpayers with incomes over 120,000 euros rises to 3.55%, 3.35%, and 3.20% respectively. Implications for cross-border workers The data on average income and wealth concentration in the Varese hills municipalities can have various implications for cross-border workers who commute to Switzerland and reside in Italy. Firstly, the presence of a population segment with high incomes can influence the local real estate market, making the purchase or rental of properties more expensive. This can pose a challenge for cross-border workers seeking to settle in these areas, especially if their income is not high enough to compete with local residents. ### Comparison with the previous situation Before the publication of this data, the perception of wealth in the area was based on anecdotal observations and not on official data. Now, with the 2024 declarations, it is possible to have a clearer picture of income distribution and local economic dynamics. This can help cross-border workers make more informed decisions about choosing a place of residence and better evaluate the economic opportunities offered by the area. ### Future scenarios With the increasing concentration of wealth, it is possible that the municipalities of the Varese hills will attract more and more professionals and affluent families. This could lead to a further increase in the prices of real estate and services, making the area even more exclusive. However, it could also lead to an improvement in public services and infrastructure, thanks to the greater availability of financial resources.
