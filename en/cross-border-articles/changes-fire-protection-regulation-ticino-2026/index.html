Analysis of Changes The amendments to the fire protection regulation, which came into force on January 1, 2024, introduce a series of new provisions that could have a significant impact on Ticino municipalities. Among the main novelties are the introduction of new training obligations for municipal staff and the obligation to equip themselves with more advanced fire protection equipment. For example, municipalities will now have to train at least two members of staff each year on advanced fire extinguishing techniques, with an estimated cost of around 5,000 CHF per course. Furthermore, it will be mandatory to equip themselves with state-of-the-art fire extinguishers, with an average investment of 2,000 CHF per municipal building. These changes could entail additional costs for the municipalities, which already have to cope with tight budgets. ### Comparisons with the Previous Situation Before the amendments, Ticino municipalities were required to comply with less stringent regulations in terms of fire protection. The new provisions introduce higher standards, which could require significant investments in terms of human and technical resources. For example, the municipality of Lugano has estimated a 30% increase in operating costs for fire protection management. Furthermore, the lack of specific clarifications on the implementation of the new regulations has created further uncertainties among local administrators.
