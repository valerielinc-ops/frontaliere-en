This concerning data emerges from a Nomisma survey dedicated to the proximity retail sector. The balance between opened and closed businesses in the province is negative by 12%. However, there are also positive news: employment has increased by 18.3%, for a total of 5,328 units. Furthermore, the sale prices of shops have decreased by 6.9%, while the rental price has increased by 23%. Nationwide in Italy, over 86,000 neighborhood shops have disappeared in the last 10 years, with a negative balance exceeding 106,000 units compared to the peak of 2018. In Lombardy, the situation is not better, with a negative balance of 12,978 units lost.
