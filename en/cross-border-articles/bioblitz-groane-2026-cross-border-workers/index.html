BioBlitz Lombardy 2026: three days of nature at Parco delle Groane (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 8 May 2026

From May 15 to 17, 2026, Parco delle Groane hosts BioBlitz Lombardy, a citizen science event to discover local biodiversity.

Contesto

In brief - BioBlitz Lombardia 2026 from 15 to 17 May at Parco delle Groane - Activities for families and nature enthusiasts - Scientific monitoring with the iNaturalist app ## Key facts - What: BioBlitz Lombardia 2026 - When: 15-17 May 2026 - Where: Parco delle Groane and other natural areas - Who: Regione Lombardia, AREA Parchi, Parco Regionale dell’Oglio Sud, Riserva Naturale Le Bine - Cost: Free event, registrations open From 15 to 17 May 2026, Parco delle Groane e della Brughiera Briantea will transform into a large open-air laboratory thanks to BioBlitz Lombardia 2026. This citizen science event, promoted by Regione Lombardia, will involve numerous natural areas in the territory, offering a rich calendar of appointments open to citizens, families, enthusiasts, and curious individuals. The initiative, now in its eleventh edition, represents one of the most important events dedicated to citizen science in Lombardia. ### Activities and biodiversity discovery Among the planned activities, there will be guided excursions, naturalistic monitoring, activities for children and families, and immersive experiences dedicated to the fauna and flora of the territory. The stars of this edition will be butterflies, but there will also be space for bats, fireflies, nocturnal birds, pollinating insects, amphibians, reptiles, and soil macroinvertebrates. Participants, accompanied by expert naturalists, will be able to actively contribute to the collection of scientific data through the iNaturalist app, becoming an integral part of a project to monitor regional biodiversity. ### Detailed program The program will kick off on Friday, 15 May with a series of evening appointments dedicated to nature after sunset. Dettagli operativi

Implications for cross-border workers The BioBlitz Lombardia 2026 offers a unique opportunity for cross-border workers who live and work between Italy and Switzerland. Participating in this event not only allows them to discover the natural wealth of the territory but also to actively contribute to scientific research. For cross-border workers residing in Lombardy and working in Ticino, this type of initiative is a way to connect with the local community and promote ecosystem conservation. ### Activities for families and enthusiasts The activities planned during the BioBlitz are suitable for everyone, from children to adults. Guided excursions and naturalistic monitoring offer an educational and engaging experience, allowing participants to learn more about local biodiversity. For families, there are dedicated workshops for children, who can discover the world of insects and animals in an interactive and fun way. ### Contribution to scientific research The data collected during the BioBlitz will be used to monitor regional biodiversity and promote ecosystem conservation. Participants, using the iNaturalist app, can actively contribute to the collection of scientific data, becoming an integral part of a citizen science project. This type of initiative is fundamental for scientific research, as it allows the collection of valuable information about natural ecosystems. ### Environmental awareness The BioBlitz Lombardia 2026 is also an opportunity to raise public awareness about the importance of environmental protection. Through the planned activities, participants will better understand the importance of ecosystem conservation and the role each person can play in protecting nature. How to Participate To participate in BioBlitz Lombardia 2026, you need to register online. Registrations are already open and can be made through the official website of Parco delle Groane or Riserva Fontana del Guercio. It is advisable to book in advance, as some activities may have a limited number of participants. ### What to Bring During the activities, it is advisable to bring comfortable and suitable clothing for the hike, hiking shoes, a flashlight for night activities, and, if available, binoculars for wildlife observation. It is also useful to download the iNaturalist app on your smartphone to actively contribute to the collection of scientific data. ### Where to Stay For those coming from outside the region, there are numerous accommodation options available near Parco delle Groane. You can find hotels, bed & breakfasts, and agritourisms that offer advantageous rates for BioBlitz participants. It is advisable to book in advance to ensure availability. ### Conclusion BioBlitz Lombardia 2026 is a must-attend event for all nature and science enthusiasts. Participating in this event not only offers the opportunity to discover local biodiversity but also to actively contribute to scientific research and ecosystem conservation. For cross-border workers living and working between Italy and Switzerland, this type of initiative is a way to connect with the local community and promote environmental protection. Frequently Asked Questions
What is BioBlitz Lombardia 2026?
BioBlitz Lombardia 2026 is a citizen science event promoted by Regione Lombardia that will take place from May 15 to 17, 2026, at Parco delle Groane and other natural areas. The event offers a rich calendar of appointments dedicated to the discovery of local biodiversity, with guided excursions, naturalistic monitoring, and activities for families and enthusiasts.
How can I participate in BioBlitz Lombardia 2026?
To participate in BioBlitz Lombardia 2026, you need to register online through the official website of Parco delle Groane or Riserva Fontana del Guercio. Registrations are already open and can be made in advance to ensure availability.
What activities are planned during BioBlitz Lombardia 2026?
During BioBlitz Lombardia 2026, various activities are planned, including guided excursions, naturalistic monitoring, activities for children and families, and immersive experiences dedicated to the fauna and flora of the territory. Among the main activities are the discovery of butterflies, the observation of bats, the listening to the sounds of the night, and the observation of fireflies and nocturnal birds.
