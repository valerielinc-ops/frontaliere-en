The Accident-Prone Road Between Vezia and Cureglia (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 24 February 2026

Too many accidents on the Vezia-Cureglia stretch: Vezia's municipal council requests action from the Canton to improve road safety.

Contesto

TL;DR - High accident rate on Vezia-Cureglia road - Latest collision on Feb 18, 2026, injured 4 - Municipal council urges Canton for safety measures ## Key facts - Cosa: Cantonal road connecting Vezia to Cureglia - Quando: Latest accident on February 18, 2026 - Dove: Vezia to Cureglia, Ticino - Chi: Municipal council of Vezia - Importo: 4 people injured in the latest accident - Scadenza: Municipal meeting held on February 23, 2026 The cantonal road connecting Vezia to Cureglia has become notorious for its high accident rate, with numerous incidents continuing to occur. The latest, on February 18, 2026, involved a head-on collision between two vehicles, resulting in four injuries. This stretch of road, characterized by sharp curves and heavy traffic, has a long history of documented accidents over the years, including serious incidents dating back to 2024, 2013, and even 2010. Vezia's municipal council decided to address the issue during an extraordinary meeting held on February 23. As confirmed by municipal secretary Laura Bariffi, the matter of the “accident-prone road” was at the center of the discussion, aiming to raise awareness within the Department of Territory about the urgency of improving safety. Since the road falls under cantonal jurisdiction, the municipality's ability to act is limited. Nevertheless, the council has resolved to urge the Canton to implement decisive measures to prevent further tragedies.

Dettagli operativi

Implications for Cross-Border Workers This road serves as a critical passage for many cross-border workers who commute daily across the border to work in Ticino. Its hazardous nature affects not only local residents but also those traveling to and from border points like Ponte Tresa or Brogeda. Frequent accidents mean not just risks to personal safety but also traffic delays, directly impacting daily work routines. Structural interventions such as the addition of safety barriers, improved street lighting, or the creation of wider lanes could significantly reduce risks. Recent regulations in Ticino have already encouraged improvement projects for cantonal roads, but it is essential for the Department of Territory to prioritize this stretch. For cross-border workers, enhanced road safety translates to better quality of life and more reliable travel times.

Frequently Asked Questions
What is the most dangerous road for cross-border commuters in Ticino and why?
The cantonal road between Vezia and Cureglia is known for its hazardousness due to sharp curves and heavy traffic, with numerous documented accidents, including four injured in a head-on collision in 2026.
What impact do accidents on the Vezia-Cureglia road have on the daily life of cross-border commuters?
Accidents increase personal safety risks and cause traffic delays, impacting the daily work of cross-border commuters with possible delays and additional stress.
What are the safest alternatives for border crossing Ticino from Ponte Tresa or Brogeda to Vezia?
The safest alternatives include the A2 (roadway) to the Lugano Sud exit or the cantonal road 394 to Agno, which offers a more linear and less curved route. However, these options can extend the travel time of 10-15 minutes.
How can I report a dangerous road route like that between Vezia and Cureglia to push the authorities to intervene?
You can report directly to the Department of Canton Ticino through the official portal [www.ti.ch/transport](https://www.ti.ch/transport) or by contacting the road service at 0800 144 144. Reports are taken to assess structural measures.
Are there applications or services that warn in real time of the critical points of frontier traffic between Switzerland and Italy?
Yes, you can use applications such as 'Waze' or 'Google Maps' that report real-time accidents and intense traffic. The website [www.tio.ch](https://www.tio.ch) also offers a service dedicated to frontier workers with constant updates on the most dangerous traits.
