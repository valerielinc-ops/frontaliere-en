In brief - An investigation reveals 70 avoidable deaths in cardiac surgery in Zurich - 11 deaths classified as avoidable between 2016 and 2020 - Lack of planning and communication ## Key facts - What: Investigation into mortality in cardiac surgery - When: Between 2016 and 2020 - Where: University Hospital of Zurich - Who: Investigative commission chaired by Niklaus Oberholzer - Amount: 70 patients could have survived elsewhere The University Hospital of Zurich is at the center of a scandal that has shaken the Swiss medical community. An administrative investigation, commissioned by the cardiac surgery clinic, has revealed serious shortcomings in the management of the department during the years of direction of Dr. Francesco Maisano. Between 2016 and 2020, more than 300 deaths were analyzed, of which 11 were classified as avoidable. The investigative commission, chaired by former federal judge Niklaus Oberholzer, found a mortality rate much higher than the norm. According to the data, 70 patients, a quarter of the total, could have survived if operated on in another hospital. Oberholzer specified that interpreting the mortality rate is complex, as it also takes into account patients saved who would not have survived elsewhere. However, he emphasized that there were cases in which the patient's death could be directly attributed to the prosthesis developed by Maisano. The hospital admitted its responsibilities, with the chairman of the board of directors, André Zemp, describing the results of the investigation as 'shocking'. ### The shortcomings found The investigation highlighted several shortcomings in the management of the cardiac surgery department.
