Dettagli operativi

Implications for cross-border workers and businesses The concerns expressed by the Ticino Chamber of Commerce have significant implications for cross-border workers and businesses operating in the Canton. The increase in stamp duties and the lack of structural reforms could lead to higher costs for companies and property owners. This could influence the decision to invest or stay in the Canton, especially for businesses operating across borders. ### Comparison with the previous situation Before the recent concerns, Ticino was considered a Canton with a favorable tax environment for businesses and cross-border workers. However, the increase in taxes and the lack of structural reforms could change this perception. Businesses may seek to relocate their activities to other Cantons or countries with a more favorable tax environment. This could have a negative impact on Ticino's economy and job availability for cross-border workers. ### Future scenarios If Ticino's public finances continue to deteriorate, we may see an increase in relocations and a reduction in investments in the Canton. This could lead to a decrease in jobs and an increase in unemployment. Additionally, the increase in taxes could reduce the purchasing power of cross-border workers and businesses, negatively affecting the local economy. ### Comparison with other Cantons While 21 out of 26 Cantons present a 2025 budget in the black, Ticino closes with a deficit of 32.5 million francs. This comparison highlights the need for structural reforms and a virtuous path of savings even in public administration. Frequently Asked Questions
What are the main concerns of the Ticino Canton's Order of Chartered Accountants?
The main concerns of the Ticino Canton's Order of Chartered Accountants revolve around the Canton's public finances, the risk of delocalization, and the transfer of businesses and taxpayers due to increasing burdens and subsidies. Additionally, criticism has been expressed regarding the message on the first stage of implementing health insurance initiatives and the expected increase in stamp duties.
What are the implications for cross-border workers and businesses?
The implications for cross-border workers and businesses include a possible increase in costs due to stamp duties and the lack of structural reforms. This could influence the decision to invest or stay in the Canton, leading to an increase in delocalization and a reduction in investments in Ticino.
What can cross-border workers and businesses do to manage the fiscal situation?
Cross-border workers and businesses can closely monitor the decisions of the State Council and the proposals for tax reform, participate in public debates and consultations, and consider tax planning options.
