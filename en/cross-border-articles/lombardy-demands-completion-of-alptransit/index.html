The Lombardy Regional Council unanimously approves the motion to enhance access lines to the Ceneri, Gotthard, and Lötschberg base tunnels.

In brief - Lombardy approves motion for completion of AlpTransit - Upgrade of access lines to Ceneri, Gotthard, Lötschberg tunnels - Political-institutional support for the Alliance South of the Alps ## Key facts - What: Motion for upgrading access lines to AlpTransit tunnels - When: May 5, 2025 - Where: Lombardy Regional Council - Who: Lombardy Regional Council, ProGottardo - Amount: Not specified - Context: Alliance South of the Alps with Ticino, Piedmont, Liguria The Lombardy Regional Council unanimously approved on May 5 the motion committing the executive, president, and regional government to ensure the upgrading of access lines to the Ceneri, Gotthard, and Lötschberg base tunnels. This political-institutional support for the completion of AlpTransit is historic, as emphasized in the press release issued by ProGottardo. Lombardy's political choice follows the Alliance South of the Alps, which in 2025 was joined by the Ticino legislature, Lombardy, Piedmont, and Liguria. This position complements the similar stance taken by the Ticino Grand Council in 2023, accentuating international interest and pressure as the Federal Council and Parliament prepare to debate the future of railway infrastructure. ### Impact on cross-border workers The completion of AlpTransit could have a significant impact on cross-border workers who daily cross the Italian-Swiss border. Analysis of Practical Implications The completion of AlpTransit could have several practical implications for cross-border workers who commute to Switzerland from Italy. One of the main advantages would be the reduction in travel times, thanks to more efficient and better-integrated railway infrastructures. This could translate into greater punctuality and a reduction in traffic-related unforeseen events, making cross-border commuting more reliable and less stressful. ### Comparison with the Current Situation Currently, cross-border workers using the Ceneri and Gotthard base tunnels have to deal with infrastructures that, while advanced, could benefit from further improvements. Enhancing the access lines could make the journey smoother and more predictable, reducing waiting times and improving train punctuality. This would be particularly advantageous for workers who need to adhere to strict schedules and often face traffic-related or railway delay issues. ### Future Scenarios If the completion of AlpTransit proceeds as planned, cross-border workers could benefit from increased train frequency, more direct connections, and better integration between public transport services in Italy and Switzerland. This could make cross-border commuting more attractive and sustainable, encouraging more people to choose the train as their primary mode of transport. Additionally, a more efficient railway system could help reduce the environmental impact of cross-border traffic, aligning with sustainability and CO2 emission reduction goals. ### Procedure and Timelines The motion approved by the Lombardy Regional Council commits the regional authorities to actively promote the enhancement of access lines to the base tunnels.
