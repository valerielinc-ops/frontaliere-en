SBB: new summer routes for cross-border workers to Italy and Europe (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 18 April 2026

Direct trains from Zurich to Rimini will run from 30 May 2026. TGV services to Avignon and Marseille will depart from Lausanne and Geneva from 16 April. Nightjet overnight trains will connect to Austria and Germany.

Let’s examine in detail the practical implications of these changes. ### Benefits for cross-border workers: time and cost savings For workers who cross the border between Ticino and Lombardy daily, the new routes offer tangible savings in both time and money. The direct Zurich-Rimini connection, for example, eliminates the need to navigate Milan’s motorway traffic and the subsequent transfer at Milano Centrale. Those who opt for driving must contend with tolls, fuel costs, and the risk of border queues, especially during the summer months. Connections to France also present an interesting alternative. Avignon and Marseille are increasingly popular destinations for Ticino cross-border workers, both for work and leisure. With the new TGV services from Lausanne and Geneva, these cities can now be reached in under four hours without detouring through Paris or facing delays due to transfers. Nightjet services are ideal for those traveling to Austria or Germany for work or holidays. The ability to depart in the evening and arrive at the destination the next morning is a major advantage, particularly for those with rigid work schedules. Frequently Asked Questions
What are the exact departure dates for the new FFS summer routes?
The new routes depart on 16 April 2026 for connections to France (Avignon, Aix-en-Provence, Marseille) and on 30 May 2026 for the direct Zurich-Rimini train. The Nightjet services to Austria and Germany will depart on a date yet to be announced.
How much do tickets cost for the new FFS routes?
The FFS have not yet announced the ticket prices. You can monitor official updates on the Swiss railways’ website or via their app for real-time information.
Are Nightjet trains reliable in terms of punctuality?
Nightjet services historically experience delays more frequently than daytime trains. In 2025, FFS punctuality stood at 94.1%, but this figure includes all trains, not just night trains. For Nightjet journeys, it is always advisable to check the schedule before departure.
What happens if my train is replaced by a bus due to construction work?
If your train is replaced by a substitute bus service, the FFS guarantee that the service will operate, though travel times may increase. It is recommended to depart at least one hour earlier than the original train’s scheduled time.
Can I take my car on the train with the new routes?
The new FFS routes do not include car transport. To travel abroad with your own vehicle, you must use the car-on-train service or rent a vehicle at your destination.
Are there discounts for Ticino cross-border workers on the new routes?
The FFS have not yet announced any specific discounts for Ticino cross-border workers. However, periodic promotions or advantageous commuter subscriptions may be introduced. It is advisable to check the official website or contact customer service for the latest information.
