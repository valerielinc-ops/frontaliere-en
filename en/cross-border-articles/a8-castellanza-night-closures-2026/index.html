A8 Milan-Varese: Night Closures at Castellanza Junction in March 2026 (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 21 March 2026

Three nights of night closures at the Castellanza junction for noise barrier renovation works, impacting traffic towards Milan and cross-border commuters.

On the A8 Milan-Varese motorway stretch, crucial for commuter and commercial traffic between Lombardy and Canton Ticino, significant night closures are planned at the Castellanza junction heading towards Milan. The decision was announced by local authorities to allow renovation works on the noise barriers, an intervention aimed at improving the quality of life for residents along the motorway, but which will inevitably affect traffic, especially for Ticino cross-border commuters who daily travel towards Milan or the Lombard hinterland. The closures are scheduled for the nights of Tuesday, March 24, Wednesday, March 25, and Thursday, March 26, 2026, from 9:00 pm until 5:00 am the following morning. During these time slots, access to the A8 motorway towards Milan from the Castellanza junction will not be possible. This junction is strategic for many cross-border commuters who use the A8 to reach Ticino via the Gaggiolo or Brogeda border crossings and then continue towards the economic hubs of Lugano, Mendrisio, or Bellinzona. Dettagli operativi

The noise barrier renovation project along the A8 motorway is a highly anticipated technical intervention, especially for residents living near the Castellanza junction. Noise barriers are essential elements to reduce noise pollution caused by heavy traffic, particularly during nighttime hours, a factor that affects the quality of sleep and well-being of thousands of residents. From an operational standpoint, the construction site requires a total closure of the junction towards Milan for three consecutive nights, from 9:00 pm to 5:00 am. This allows the works to be carried out safely without completely interrupting traffic during the day. Operating during nighttime hours is a well-established practice in major motorway projects, aimed at limiting disruptions and keeping traffic flowing during critical daytime hours. Current regulations in Lombardy require that such infrastructure works be communicated in advance and accompanied by clear instructions for motorists to ensure safety and traffic fluidity. Temporary signage and directions for the alternative Legnano junction are an integral part of the traffic management plan during the works. For cross-border commuters who travel daily from Canton Ticino to Lombardy, it is advisable to monitor official communications from the Ticino Department of Finance and Economy (DFE), as well as information channels from both Canton Ticino and Lombard authorities. Cross-border cooperation is essential to minimize negative impacts on commuters and economic activities. No details have been provided regarding possible additional costs resulting from detours or delays, nor specific information on any further phases of work after March 2026. Punti chiave

Ahead of the night closures at the Castellanza junction, Ticino cross-border commuters and motorists heading towards Milan must carefully organize their journeys. The first recommendation is to avoid using the A8 from Castellanza on the specified nights, opting instead for the alternative entry at Legnano, which remains open and offers a safe route to the Lombard capital. For those working or residing in Canton Ticino who use the Gaggiolo or Brogeda border crossings daily, it is useful to plan departure times in advance to avoid being stuck or facing long waits. Detours may add at least 10-15 minutes to travel time, depending on traffic. In addition to the alternative route, a practical suggestion is to regularly check traffic information and official communications from both Italian and Ticino authorities. Tools like the Frontaliere Ticino website provide constant updates and can help choose the best time and route. Those commuting for work can also check if it is possible to shift their start or end times to avoid the closure periods. Another option is to consider using public transport for some segments, if logistics allow. Although temporary, these works highlight the importance of careful and timely planning for cross-border commuters, whose work and personal lives are closely tied to smooth traffic flow between Ticino and Lombardy. Infrastructure maintenance is necessary, but coordination between regions and timely information are key to minimizing inconvenience. Frequently Asked Questions
When are the night closures expected at the Castellanza junction on the A8 Milan-Varese?
Closures are scheduled for the nights of Tuesday 24, Wednesday 25 and Thursday 26 March 2026, from 21:00 until 5:00 the following morning.
What are the alternatives for Ticino cross-border commuters during the night closures of the Castellanza junction?
During the closures, it is advisable to use the alternative junction of Legnano to reach Milan, with a lengthening of the journey of about 10-15 minutes depending on traffic.
What online tools can I use to check the traffic status on the A8 during night closures?
You can consult the Frontaliere Ticino website (nav:traffic-history) which provides real-time updates on accidents and diversions. The Lombardy Region portal also publishes live maps and alerts via app. Both show the closures scheduled from 24 to 26 March 2026 and suggest alternative routes.
How long does the route from Castellanza to Milan take on average using the Legnano junction during closures?
The detour via the Legnano junction adds about 10-15 minutes to the journey, depending on the night vehicular flow. In normal traffic conditions, the journey goes from 12 minutes (direct) to 22-27 minutes, still allowing you to reach Milan within working hours.
Is it possible to use public transport to avoid night closures of the A8, and what are the most practical options for cross-border commuters?
Yes, commuters can take the Varese-Milan regional train, which runs every 30 minutes and lasts about 45 minutes, then connecting with the Ticino railway network. Alternatively, interregional buses leave Varese for Milan every hour. Both solutions reduce exposure to closures and limit delays.
