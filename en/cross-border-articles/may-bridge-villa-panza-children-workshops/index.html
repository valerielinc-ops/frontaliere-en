May Day Bridge at Villa Panza: guided tours and children's workshops

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 1 May 2026

From May 1 to 3, 2026, Villa Panza in Varese hosts guided tours and creative workshops for children inspired by artist Josef Albers.

Contesto

In brief - Ponte del Primo Maggio at Villa Panza from May 1 to 3, 2026 - Interactive guided tours and creative workshops for children - Exhibition dedicated to Josef Albers ## Key facts - What: Guided tours and creative workshops - When: From May 1 to 3, 2026 - Where: Villa Panza in Varese - Who: FAI - Fondo per l'Ambiente Italiano ETS - Cost: Not yet specified From May 1 to 3, 2026, Villa Panza in Varese will host a program of special guided tours and creative workshops for children. The initiative is linked to the exhibition 'Meditations' dedicated to the artist Josef Albers. On Saturday, May 2, 'Special Josef Albers. Exercises to learn how to see' is scheduled, an interactive guided tour that explores the central themes of the artist's research. The tour focuses on the phenomenology of perception, offering a non-conventional experience where observation is combined with active experimentation. Visitors will be guided through a series of eye exercises aimed at understanding the changing and relative nature of color. The tours will take place at 11:30 AM and 3:30 PM. ### Creative workshops On Friday, May 1, and Sunday, May 3, creative workshops for children and families are organized. These workshops, part of the Panza Kids program, start with an interactive visit to the exhibition and villa environments. Children will explore the principles at the heart of Albers' research, such as the interaction between colors and their relative and changing nature. Each workshop is followed by creative activities inspired by the 'exercises to learn how to see' developed by Albers in his teaching experience. ### Detailed program The program includes two main appointments: 'Omaggio d'Autore' for children aged 4-6 and 'Tra un tono e l'altro' for children aged 7-9. Implications for Families The initiative at Villa Panza in Varese represents a unique opportunity for families to spend the May Day long weekend in a cultural and creative context. The guided tours and workshops are designed to actively engage children, offering them an educational and fun experience. This type of activity is particularly useful for stimulating the creativity and curiosity of the youngest, as well as promoting an interactive approach to art. ### Comparison with Other Cultural Initiatives Compared to other similar cultural initiatives, the Villa Panza program stands out for its focus on active experimentation and the phenomenology of perception. While many exhibitions limit themselves to displaying works of art, this initiative integrates creative workshops that allow children to put into practice what they have observed. This approach makes the experience more engaging and memorable. ### Practical Scenarios Families wishing to participate in the workshops can plan their visit based on the guided tour schedules. It is advisable to book in advance, especially for the children's workshops, which may have a limited number of places available. Furthermore, families can take advantage of the opportunity to visit other exhibitions and permanent collections at Villa Panza, further enriching their cultural experience. ### Implications for Cross-Border Workers For cross-border workers who work in Ticino and reside in Lombardy, this initiative represents an opportunity to spend a cultural weekend in a nearby context. The geographical proximity and ease of access make Villa Panza an ideal destination for a day trip. Moreover, the activities proposed are suitable for families with children, offering a fun and educational alternative to traditional tourist outings...
