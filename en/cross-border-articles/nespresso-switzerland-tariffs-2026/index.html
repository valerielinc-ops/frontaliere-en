Nespresso stays in Switzerland despite US tariffs (cross-border guide)

Nestlé confirms its loyalty to Switzerland for Nespresso production, despite US tariffs. 16,000 jobs to be cut by 2027.

In brief - Nespresso stays in Switzerland despite US tariffs - 16,000 jobs to be cut by 2027 - Investments in coffee, pet food, and nutrition ## Key facts - What: Confirmation of Nespresso production in Switzerland - When: Announced on May 6, 2026 - Where: Switzerland - Who: Nestlé, CEO Philipp Navratil - Amount: 16,000 jobs to be cut by 2027 Nestlé has confirmed that the production of Nespresso capsules will remain in Switzerland, despite the tariffs imposed by the United States. CEO Philipp Navratil has ruled out any transfer of the plants abroad, emphasizing the group's loyalty to Switzerland. In an interview with Aargauer Zeitung, Navratil explained that the group is considering whether the tariffs will be reimbursed by the American government, but has never considered moving production. 'We have not considered the idea of moving Nespresso production out of Switzerland for this reason,' he stated. Despite the customs duties, Nestlé continues to invest and focus on market trends. 'From a political point of view, one can have an opinion on tariffs, but we focus on how to manage them best,' Navratil declared. 'We continue to invest, despite the tariffs. We think long-term and focus on market trends.' ### Impact on cross-border workers For cross-border workers employed in the food sector in Switzerland, this decision represents job stability. The confirmation of production in Switzerland means that local jobs remain secure, at least for the moment. However, Nestlé's global savings program, which includes cutting 16,000 jobs by the end of 2027, could have repercussions on the Swiss job market.
