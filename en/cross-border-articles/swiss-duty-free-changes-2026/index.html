Swiss to end in-flight duty-free sales

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 29 April 2026

From late September 2026, Swiss will stop selling duty-free products on board. The offer will move online.

Contesto

In brief - Swiss stops duty-free sales on board aircraft - Offer moved online to Worldshop - Decision due to drop in demand and changing habits ## Key facts - What: Stopping duty-free sales on board - When: From late September 2026 - Where: On all Swiss flights - Who: Swiss International Air Lines - Amount: Not specified Swiss has announced that it will stop selling duty-free products on board its aircraft starting from late September 2026. The decision is motivated by the drop in demand and the changing habits of passengers. Fewer and fewer people buy on board, preferring to compare prices online and buy before departure. The offer will be transferred to the digital store Worldshop of Miles & More, which will offer a wider range of products, including exclusive items made with materials from decommissioned Swiss aircraft. ### Implications for passengers The decision by Swiss will have a significant impact on passengers who were used to shopping on board. Those who wish to purchase duty-free products will have to do so through the online store, which offers a wider selection and the possibility to plan purchases in advance. This change reflects a broader trend in the retail sector, where more and more consumers prefer to shop online rather than in-store. The airline explained that spontaneous purchases from the duty-free trolley on board have become the exception rather than the rule. This shift towards digital is in line with Swiss's strategy to modernize its offer and meet the needs of passengers.
