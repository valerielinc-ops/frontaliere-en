Copernicus: 95% of Europe with above-average temperatures in 2025

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 29 April 2026

Record heatwaves in Scandinavia, shrinking glaciers, and extensive forest fires. Here are the key data points from the 2025 Copernicus report.

Contesto

In brief - 95% of Europe with above-average temperatures in 2025 - Record heatwave in Scandinavia - 1 million hectares of forests burned ## Key facts - What: "European State of the Climate 2025" report - When: April 29, 2026 - Where: Europe - Who: Copernicus and World Meteorological Organization (WMO) - Amount: 95% of Europe with above-average temperatures At least 95% of Europe recorded annual temperatures above average in 2025. This is revealed by the "European State of the Climate 2025" report by the EU's weather service, Copernicus, and the World Meteorological Organization (WMO). A record three-week heatwave hit the Scandinavian Peninsula countries, with temperatures near and within the Arctic Circle exceeding 30 degrees Celsius. Glaciers in all European regions recorded a net loss of mass, with Iceland experiencing the second-largest glacial loss ever. Snow cover was 31% below average. The Greenland ice sheet lost 139 gigatonnes (139 billion tonnes) of ice. The average annual sea surface temperature for the European region was the highest ever recorded, and 86% of the region experienced at least "strong" marine heatwaves. Wildfires burned approximately 1,034,550 hectares, the largest area recorded. River flows were below average for eleven months of the year across Europe. Storms and floods affected thousands of people across Europe, although extreme precipitation was less widespread than in previous years. Dettagli operativi

Analysis of the implications The Copernicus 2025 report highlights how climate change is having a significant impact on Europe and, in particular, on the Canton of Ticino. Higher temperatures and reduced snowfall can have negative consequences for the local economy, particularly for the tourism sector and agriculture. ### Impact on tourism Tourism is one of the most important sectors for the economy of the Canton of Ticino. Higher temperatures and reduced snowfall can negatively affect the winter season, reducing the number of tourists visiting the ski resorts. This could have a significant impact on employment and revenue in the tourism sector. ### Impact on agriculture Agriculture is another crucial sector for the economy of the Canton of Ticino. Higher temperatures and reduced precipitation can negatively affect agricultural production, particularly the cultivation of fruit and vegetables. This could lead to an increase in the prices of agricultural products and a reduction in the availability of local food. ### Impact on public health Heatwaves and forest fires can pose a risk to public health. Higher temperatures can increase the risk of heat-related illnesses, such as heatstroke and dehydration. Additionally, forest fires can cause respiratory problems and other diseases related to air quality. ### Adaptation measures To address these changes, the Canton of Ticino is implementing various adaptation measures. These include the promotion of renewable energies, sustainable management of water resources, and the protection of forested areas. Frequently Asked Questions
What are the main consequences of climate change in Europe according to the Copernicus 2025 report?
The main consequences of climate change in Europe in 2025 include annual temperatures above average in 95% of the continent, record heatwaves in Scandinavia, net loss of glacial mass, a 31% reduction in snow cover, a loss of 139 gigatonnes of ice from the Greenland ice sheet, the highest-ever recorded average annual sea surface temperature, and wildfires that have burned approximately 1,034,550 hectares of forest.
How is the Canton of Ticino addressing climate change?
The Canton of Ticino is implementing various adaptation measures to address climate change, including the promotion of renewable energies, sustainable management of water resources, protection of forested areas, and awareness programs to inform the population about the risks associated with climate change and preventive measures.
What concrete actions can cross-border workers take to adapt to climate change?
Cross-border workers can monitor weather conditions, use renewable energies, manage water resources sustainably, and participate in awareness programs promoted by local authorities. These actions can help mitigate the effects of climate change and protect the environment and public health.
