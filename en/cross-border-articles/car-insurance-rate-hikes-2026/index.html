Dettagli operativi

According to the survey, only a minority of insured individuals change insurance companies despite premium increases. This could be due to a lack of information or difficulty in finding a better offer. However, insured individuals can save money by changing insurance companies or negotiating with their current insurance company. A concrete example: if an insured individual from Lugano currently pays 800 francs per year for car insurance, they could save up to 200 francs per year by changing insurance companies. This may seem like a modest amount, but over the course of 5 years, the savings would be 1,000 francs. To save money, insured individuals can also consider increasing the deductible or choosing an insurance policy with a lower premium. However, it is essential to carefully consider the options before making a decision, as an insurance policy with a lower premium may not offer the same coverage as a policy with a higher premium. In Ticino, the law requires insurance companies to offer a minimum coverage of 100,000 francs for material damage and 5 million francs for bodily injury. However, some insurance companies offer higher coverage, up to 10 million francs for bodily injury. Frequently Asked Questions
How much will car insurance premiums increase in 2026 for cross-border commuters?
In 2026, almost one in two people will pay more for car insurance. The Comparis survey of 1,026 policyholders shows that in French-speaking Switzerland, more than a third saw increases exceeding 200 francs, with examples from Lugano (+150 CHF) and Bellinzona (+250 CHF).
How to check and compare the new car premium against the previous year's?
Car insurance policies in Switzerland renew annually; the company sends a letter with the new premium. It’s advisable to review the proposed premium, compare it with the previous year’s, and check for any changes. If the increase exceeds 100‑150 CHF, consider alternative offers or negotiate with your current insurer.
How much can you save by switching car insurance companies?
Switching companies can save 200‑500 CHF per year. A policyholder in Locarno saved 300 CHF by switching to another policy; a 30-year-old driver with a 20,000 CHF car can save around 200 CHF, while a 50-year-old with a 50,000 CHF vehicle can reduce their premium by 500 CHF. The cumulative savings over 5 years exceed 1,000 CHF.
What are the consequences of increasing the deductible to lower car insurance premiums?
Increasing the deductible is a quick way to reduce premiums: a higher deductible can lower costs by 100‑150 CHF per year. However, in the event of a claim, the policyholder will have to cover a larger share of expenses. Assess your financial capacity and required coverage level before adjusting the deductible.
