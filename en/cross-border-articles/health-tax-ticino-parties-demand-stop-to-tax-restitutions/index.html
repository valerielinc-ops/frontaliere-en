Health Tax: Ticino Parties Demand Stop to Restitutions (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 19 February 2026 · Updated on 20 February 2026

Motion by FDP, The Centre, SVP, and Lega to the Council of State: suspend tax restitutions to Italy for violating the tax agreement with the new health tax.

Contesto

TL;DR - Ticino parties unite against Italy's 'health tax' on cross-border workers. - Joint motion to suspend tax restitutions to Italy over alleged agreement breach. - Tax affects 'old' cross-border workers, hired before July 2023. ## Key facts - Cosa: Joint motion to suspend tax restitutions to Italy. - Quando: Motion submitted in February 2024. - Dove: Ticino, Switzerland. - Chi: FDP, The Centre, SVP, and Lega parties. - Importo: Italy aims to collect 100-150 million euros annually from the tax. - Scadenza: Tax applies to income earned in Switzerland by cross-border workers. - Accordo: Violation of Swiss-Italian tax agreement, Article 9. - Convenzione: Vienna Convention on the Law of Treaties invoked. The political front in Ticino is uniting against the controversial "health tax" introduced by Rome. In a decisive move, the canton's main parties – FDP (PLR), The Centre (Il Centro), SVP (UDC), and Lega – have submitted a joint motion to the Council of State. The demand is clear: to suspend, either fully or partially, the transfer of tax restitutions (ristorni) from cross-border workers' source taxes to Italy. The signatories, Alessandro Speziali (FDP), Fiorenzo Dadò (The Centre), Alain Buehler (SVP), and Daniele Piccaluga (Lega), are not mincing words. They define the new Italian tax as a genuine additional levy affecting the so-called "old cross-border workers," meaning those who were already working in Switzerland before July 2023. The heart of the issue lies in an alleged violation of the bilateral tax agreement. According to Article 9 of the accord, these workers must be taxed exclusively in Switzerland. Dettagli operativi

The Legal Basis and Agreement Implications The motion submitted in Bellinzona is not merely a political outburst but rests on a specific legal foundation. The signatories explicitly invoke the Vienna Convention on the Law of Treaties. This fundamental text of international law allows a state to suspend the application of a treaty in the event of a "material breach" by the other party. This is precisely the point: the "health tax" is interpreted as a serious and manifest violation of the Swiss-Italian tax agreement. > The tax agreement stipulates that cross-border workers hired before July 17, 2023, must pay taxes only in the country where they work, i.e., Switzerland. The new Italian tax, being a mandatory levy based on income, is seen as a disguised form of double taxation. ⚠️ What is the health tax? Introduced by the 2024 Budget Law, it is a contribution to the Italian National Health Service for fiscally resident cross-border workers in Italy who work in Switzerland (and are not subject to taxation in Italy under the agreement). The rate is variable and applies to the net income earned in Switzerland. The stated goal is to finance the healthcare system of border regions like Lombardy, which is estimated to collect between 100 and 150 million euros annually. The suspension of the "ristorni"—the 40% share of source taxes paid by cross-border workers that Switzerland returns to Italian border municipalities—would represent a significant economic retaliation.

What It Means for Cross-Border Workers and Future Scenarios The escalating tension between Ticino and Rome creates a climate of significant uncertainty for tens of thousands of workers. While the political move in Ticino aims to protect cross-border workers from what is perceived as double taxation, it also opens up scenarios of diplomatic confrontation with unpredictable outcomes. Frequently Asked Questions
What is the 'health tax' for cross-border commuters and how does it work?
The 'health tax' is a contribution to the Italian National Health Service, introduced with the 2024 Budget Law, to be paid by cross-border workers residing in Italy and workers in Switzerland, not subject to taxation in Italy due to the bilateral agreement. The rate is variable and applies to net income received in Switzerland.
What is the impact of the 'health tax' on the calculation of my net salary as a cross-border commuter?
The Italian 'health tax', calculated on Swiss income, can reduce your net salary. Use an updated net salary calculator to consider this new deduction, along with withholding taxes and currency exchange.
How can I check if I am considered an 'old cross-border commuter' according to the Italian-Swiss agreement?
You are an 'old cross-border commuter' if you started working in Switzerland before 17 July 2023. Check the start date of your contract or consult your Swiss employer to confirm your status, as only this category is exempt from the Italian health tax according to the bilateral tax agreement.
What do I risk if I don't pay the Italian health tax as a cross-border commuter?
Risks of administrative sanctions or debt collection procedures by the Italian Revenue Agency. The tax is mandatory for cross-border commuters residing in Italy and not subject to taxation there. However, the Ticino motion could block the application for old cross-border commuters, but until final decisions, it is advisable to monitor official updates.
Can Switzerland really suspend tax rebates to Italian municipalities in protest?
Yes, Ticino has presented a motion to partially or totally suspend rebates (40% of cross-border workers' withholding taxes) in retaliation against the Italian health tax. This measure, if approved, would have a direct impact on the budgets of border municipalities, but requires the approval of the cantonal and federal governments.
