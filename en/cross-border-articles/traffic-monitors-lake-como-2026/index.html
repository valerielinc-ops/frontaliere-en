Traffic monitors active on Saturdays too on the Statale Regina

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 27 April 2026

From 2 May 2026, traffic monitors will also be operational on Saturdays to reduce queues on the Statale Regina between Colonno and Ossuccio.

Contesto

In brief - Traffic observers also active on Saturdays from May 2, 2026 - Extended service to reduce queues on the Statale Regina - Critical issues emerged due to increased tourist traffic ## Key facts - What: Extension of traffic observers service - When: From May 2, 2026 - Where: Section between Colonno and Ossuccio on the Statale Regina - Who: Province of Como, Anas and the cooperative in charge - Amount: Service active from Monday to Saturday, 7:00–19:00 Starting next Saturday, May 2, traffic observers will be operational along the Statale Regina in the section between Colonno and Ossuccio also on Saturdays. This measure extends a service previously guaranteed from Monday to Friday during the 7:00–19:00 time slot. The decision was made in response to the critical issues that have emerged in recent weeks, with episodes of heavy congestion recorded even in the absence of heavy vehicles. The structural increase in traffic flows, particularly of a tourist nature, has increasingly affected weekends as well. The expansion of the service aims to improve traffic flow, reduce travel times, and increase safety levels for all road users along the narrow stretches of Tremezzina. The result is the outcome of coordination between the Province of Como, Anas, and the cooperative in charge of managing the observers, within the framework of a shared commitment to address the critical issues of the Regina road in a structural manner. ### Implications for cross-border workers The extension of the traffic observers service will have a significant impact on cross-border workers who use the Statale Regina for their daily commutes. Dettagli operativi

• ## Analysis of Critical Issues The critical issues that have emerged along the Statale Regina have been caused by a structural increase in traffic flows, particularly of a tourist nature. This increase has increasingly affected weekends, making it necessary to expand the traffic observer service. The presence of observers on Saturdays will help improve traffic flow and reduce travel times, making movements easier for all road users. ### Comparison with Other Solutions In addition to expanding the traffic observer service, other solutions have been considered to address the critical issues of the Statale Regina. These include the introduction of advanced monitoring systems and the optimization of traffic lights along the route. However, the extension of the traffic observer service has been considered the most immediate and effective solution to reduce queues and improve road safety. ### Implications for Cross-Border Workers The extension of the traffic observer service will have a significant impact on cross-border workers who use the Statale Regina for their daily commutes. With the increase in tourist traffic, especially on weekends, the presence of observers on Saturdays will help reduce queues and improve road safety. This is particularly important for those who work in Switzerland and reside in Italy, as the Statale Regina is one of the main links between the two countries. ### Future Scenarios If the initiative proves effective, the extension of the service to Sundays could be considered. This would allow for a more structural approach to traffic critical issues, especially during peak tourist seasons.
