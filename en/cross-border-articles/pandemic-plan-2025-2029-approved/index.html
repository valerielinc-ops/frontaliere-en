2025-2029 Pandemic Plan Approved: What Changes

The State-Regions Conference has approved the new 2025-2029 Pandemic Plan with an investment of 1.1 billion euros

Contesto

In brief - 2025-2029 Pandemic Plan approved by the State-Regions Conference - 1.1 billion euro investment to strengthen the healthcare system - New strategies for managing respiratory pandemics ## Key facts - What: Approval of the 2025-2029 Pandemic Plan - When: April 30, 2026 - Where: Italy - Who: State-Regions Conference - Amount: 1.1 billion euros The State-Regions Conference has given the green light to the new 2025-2029 Pandemic Plan. 'The approval of the new Plan represents an important political, technical, and institutional result and a crucial step for the strengthening and growth of our healthcare and institutional system,' declares Health Minister Orazio Schillaci. The Plan approved today, compared to the previous one from 2021-2023, has a five-year duration and defines a methodological approach that can be applied to pandemics with different epidemiological characteristics in terms of transmissibility, pathogenicity, and impact on health and healthcare services. The Plan presents a programmatic innovation both in approach and in the definition of actions: it is the first unitary strategy for preparing and responding to potential respiratory transmission pandemics, and it is not only aimed at individual pathogens. The document was elaborated with the contribution of national institutions, regions, and experts. ### Innovations and Investments The Plan is based on a clear principle: to guarantee security, transparency, and coordination among all institutional levels. The continuous dialogue between the Government and the Regions has been based on a common objective: the protection of citizens and public health. Frequently Asked Questions
What are the main innovations of the new Pandemic Plan 2025-2029?
The main innovations of the new Pandemic Plan 2025-2029 include the digitalization of epidemiological monitoring systems, the advanced use of data for prevention, the strengthening of surveillance platforms, and the development of flexible and adaptive protocols. Additionally, the Plan introduces phased operational planning with defined actions and clear responsibilities.
How can cross-border workers stay updated on new health procedures?
Cross-border workers can stay updated on new health procedures by monitoring official communications from the Italian government and Swiss health authorities. Additionally, they can use the available online services to access health information and services.
What are the next steps for the implementation of the new Pandemic Plan?
The next steps for the implementation of the new Pandemic Plan include the implementation of new strategies and protocols, as well as continuous monitoring of the effectiveness of the measures adopted. Furthermore, the Italian government will continue to invest in prevention, laboratory capacity, and strategic stockpiles to ensure the safety and health of citizens.
