MASI: The art that explains Ticino to border workers (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 18 February 2026

At LAC Lugano, the 'Feeling and Observation' exhibition unveils the Canton's soul until 2026. A journey into Ticino's identity, between Italian culture and the Swiss state.

Contesto

TL;DR - Exhibition on Ticino's art from 1848 to WWII - Explores Ticino's Italian-Swiss cultural duality - Shows how artists shaped Ticino's identity - Open until July 2026 at MASI Lugano ## Key facts - Cosa: Exhibition titled 'Feeling and Observation' - Quando: From 1848 to the end of World War II - Dove: MASI Lugano, LAC venue, Lugano - Chi: Curated by Tobia Bezzola - Importo: Admission ticket is 20 CHF - Scadenza: Exhibition open until July 26, 2026 - Orario: Tuesday to Sunday, from 11:00 AM - Indirizzo: Piazza Bernardino Luini 6, Lugano It's not just a matter of francs and work permits. Working in Ticino means immersing yourself daily in a unique cultural reality, a crossroads that blends Swiss efficiency with a profoundly Italian soul. A new, important exhibition at MASI Lugano, located at the LAC, offers a precious key to deciphering this DNA. It is titled 'Feeling and Observation' and will remain open until July 2026, providing an in-depth look at Ticino's art from 1848, the year the federal state was founded, to the end of World War II. The exhibition, curated by Tobia Bezzola, is not just a list of works. It's a narrative that explores what the official presentation calls 'the characteristic ambivalence of the Canton, which on one hand has an Italian cultural identity and on the other politically belongs to the Swiss federal state'. For every cross-border worker who crosses the Brogeda or Ponte Tresa border, this duality is a daily reality: they speak Italian, experience a culture similar to Lombardy's, but operate within a Swiss administrative, legal, and economic system. The exhibition stages this creative tension, showing how local artists, as well as collectors and intellectuals who immigrated from the North and South, interpreted and shaped the identity...

Dettagli operativi

The exhibition focuses on a fundamental historical period. Starting from 1848, Ticino constantly had to negotiate its position within the new Confederation. Art thus becomes a mirror of social and economic dynamics. The exhibition highlights how 'influences from the South and the North' were not just stylistic currents, but represented real flows of people, ideas, and capital. German artists seeking Mediterranean light and landscapes, Italian intellectuals fleeing complex political situations: they all found an adoptive home in Ticino, helping to make it a vibrant and cosmopolitan cultural laboratory. > The collections of MASI reflect the evolution of the recent history of visual arts in Ticino. This is not only shaped by the regional artistic community, but also by the artists, collectors, dealers, and scholars who chose Ticino as their adoptive homeland. For the cross-border worker, this historical perspective is enlightening. The flows of people that characterize the region's economy today have deep roots. Understanding how the Canton has historically attracted and integrated talent from abroad helps to contextualize one's own work experience. This is not a recent phenomenon, but a constant that has defined Ticino's identity, making it permeable and dynamic. The exhibition, enriched by important loans, therefore offers a unique opportunity to look beyond the office or construction site and understand the cultural foundations of one's workplace.

Punti chiave

Visiting 'Feeling and Observation' is more than just a cultural outing. It is an investment in your understanding of the region. Understanding the cultural nuances of Ticino, its history of hospitality, and its hybrid identity can improve professional relationships and enrich the daily experience across the border. The exhibition is an opportunity to see the Canton not just as an employer, but as a complex and fascinating cultural system. ## Tips for your visit - When: The exhibition is open until July 26, 2026, from Tuesday to Sunday, from 11:00 AM. - Where: MASI Lugano, LAC venue (Lugano Arte e Cultura), Piazza Bernardino Luini 6, Lugano. - Price: The admission ticket is 20 CHF. 💡 Experiencing Ticino is not just about earning an income, but also about seizing the opportunities for personal growth it offers. Frequently Asked Questions
When can you visit the exhibition 'Sentiment and observation' at MASI in Lugano?
The exhibition can be visited until 26 July 2026, from Tuesday to Sunday, from 11.00 am.
How to manage the Swiss-Italian cultural duality in the daily life of a cross-border commuter in Ticino?
Recognizing and appreciating the cultural ambivalence of Ticino, where Swiss efficiency blends with the Italian soul, can improve professional relationships and enrich the work experience.
What is the ticket price for the exhibition 'Sentiment and observation' at MASI in Lugano?
The entrance ticket to the exhibition 'Feeling and Observation' costs CHF 20 per person. There are 50% discounts for university students with a valid card, for over 65s and for registered unemployed. Children under 16 enter for free, while groups of ten or more people can request an additional reduction.
How to calculate the net salary in euros for a cross-border commuter working in Ticino?
To estimate the net in euros of a Swiss salary, simply convert the gross into CHF with the current exchange rate (e.g. 1 CHF ≈ 0.92 EUR) and subtract Swiss taxes (about 12-15% for medium-low incomes) and social security withholdings (5.125%). Many cross-border commuters use the online calculator on our website to get the accurate result for the current day.
What are the most convenient means of transport to get to the MASI in Lugano from the Italian border?
The MASI in Lugano can be easily reached from the Italian border by various means: the S10 regional train leaves from Chiasso and arrives in Lugano in 12 minutes; bus line 1 connects Lugano to Mendrisio and Como in about 30 minutes; by car, the A2 motorway allows direct access, with paid public parking near the LAC (fee 2.50 CHF/hour).
