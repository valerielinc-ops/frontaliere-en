In brief - One third of Swiss people refrain from traveling abroad due to insecurity - Political instability and wars influence travel habits - Spain and Portugal are the most appreciated destinations - Ticino cross-border workers prefer nearby destinations like Italy and France ## Key facts - What: Feeling of insecurity for traveling abroad - When: 2026 Barometer conducted in February/March - Where: Switzerland - Who: Swiss Touring Club (TCS) - Amount: 33% of Swiss people consider foreign vacations insecure The war is deterring travel abroad. According to the TCS, one third of Swiss people do not feel safe leaving the country for vacations. The sense of security among Swiss people when traveling abroad is worsening. According to the tenth barometer of the Touring Club (TCS), one third consider vacations outside national borders as rather insecure, compared to 19% a year ago. This is a rate that had not been reached since the pandemic. According to the survey results, published today, political instability and wars are the main factors influencing travel habits (66%). These are followed by terrorist threats (44%), crime (34%), and health risks (33%). ### Implications for cross-border workers The desire to travel remains high. The percentage of those who never do it has decreased, from 19% in 2025 to 9% this year. About 10% travel more often than before, 40% less, while for 38% the frequency has remained unchanged. 'This demonstrates a stabilization of the desire to travel, after the recovery effect observed in the first years following Covid,' explains the TCS in a note. European destinations are gaining popularity, with Spain and Portugal particularly appreciated, while North America has lost some of its appeal. Travel Trend Analysis The travel trends of Swiss citizens are changing significantly, directly impacting cross-border workers in Ticino. According to the recent travel barometer, political instability and armed conflicts are the main factors influencing travel decisions. This has led to greater caution among Swiss travelers, with an increase in preference for closer and safer European destinations. > "Safety has become the top priority for many travelers," says an industry expert. ### Comparison with Previous Editions Compared to previous editions of the barometer, there has also been a change in the choice of transportation. Two years ago, 67% of respondents preferred private cars, while today this preference has dropped to 51%, the same level as air and train travel. This change could be due to greater awareness of the risks associated with international travel and a greater availability of alternative transportation options. 📊 Concrete Data: - Private Car: 51% - Airplane: 24% - Train: 25% ### Future Scenarios If the geopolitical situation were to worsen further, it is likely that the number of Swiss people giving up foreign travel will increase. However, the desire to travel remains high, and many people may seek safer and closer alternatives.
