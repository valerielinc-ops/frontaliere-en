Grisons send Italy bill for Olympic traffic costs (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 8 May 2026

The canton of Grisons has sent a bill for 2.6 million francs to Italy to cover the costs of the transport plan during the Milan-Cortina 2026 Olympics.

Contesto

In brief - Grisons sends Italy a 2.6 million bill for Olympic costs - Transport plan cost 3.5 million, less than expected - Italy has no written commitment to pay ## Key facts - What: Bill for transport costs for the Milano-Cortina 2026 Winter Olympics - When: 7 May 2026 - Where: Grisons Canton, Switzerland - Who: Rhaetian government - Amount: 2.6 million francs The canton of Grisons has sent a bill for 2.6 million francs to Italy to cover the costs of the transport plan during the Milano-Cortina 2026 Winter Olympics. The plan, which successfully avoided traffic chaos, cost less than expected thanks to flexible adjustments during the event. The Rhaetian government has taken note of the final accounts, which amount to 3.5 million francs, reduced by over 35% from the 5.5 million approved in October. The measures were planned based on the indications of the Italian organizers regarding the expected attendance. The canton is willing to cover its own services amounting to approximately 0.9 million francs, while the remaining 2.6 million should be assumed by Italy, pursuant to the principle of causality. ### The transport plan and costs The transport plan set up in Grisons during the Milano-Cortina Winter Olympics prevented traffic chaos. The canton is willing to cover its own services amounting to approximately 0.9 million francs. The costs due to the event, amounting to 2.6 million francs, should however be assumed by Italy, pursuant to the principle of causality. State Councillor Carmelia Maissen, director of the Department of Infrastructure, Energy and Mobility, stated that the canton will inform the Lombardy region about the actual costs incurred and invite it to cover them. ### The Italian response It is not certain that Italy will pay the bill.
