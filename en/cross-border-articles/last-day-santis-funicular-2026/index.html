Last day to ride the Säntis cable car

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 30 April 2026

From May 1st, the Säntis cable car will be closed for renovations. The new cabins will be able to transport up to 85 passengers.

In brief - Last day to ride the Säntis cable car: April 30, 2026 - Total renovation of the cable car with a cost of 30 million francs - New cabins with a capacity of 85 passengers ## Key facts - What: Total renovation of the Säntis cable car - When: From May 1, 2026 - Where: Säntis, Eastern Switzerland - Who: Säntis cable car authorities - Amount: 30 million francs The Säntis cable car, one of the most frequented ski lifts in Switzerland, is making its last passenger transport today, April 30. From tomorrow, May 1, and until late autumn, the facility will be completely renovated. The new cabins will be able to transport up to 85 passengers, maintaining a journey time of approximately eight minutes. The total cost of the renovation is expected to be 30 million francs. During the construction period, the Säntis will only be reachable on foot and the restaurant at the top will remain closed. The new cabins will offer better comfort and greater stability in case of strong gusts of wind. The actual opening date of the new cable car will depend on the weather conditions of the coming months. ### Implications for visitors Visitors planning to go up to the Säntis will have to consider the absence of the cable car for several months. Those who had booked events at the top will not be able to use the facilities until the end of the year. The mountain, which is 2,502 meters high, is a popular destination for hikers and tourists, but during the renovation work, access will be limited. ### History of the cable car Put into operation in the summer of 1935, the Säntis cable car has a long history of service.
