COMCO investigates illegal keyword bidding agreements

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 30 April 2026

Two investigations opened by COMCO in the travel and online casino sector for alleged illegal keyword bidding agreements.

Contesto

In brief - COMCO opens two investigations into keyword bidding - Travel and online casino sectors involved - Illegal agreements to avoid competition ## Key facts - What: Investigations into illegal agreements in keyword bidding - When: April 30, 2026 - Where: Switzerland - Who: COMCO - Amount: Not specified The Competition Commission (COMCO) has opened two investigations into advertising through search engines. The first concerns the travel sector and the second the online casino sector, as stated in a notice issued on Thursday. To stand out from their competitors, companies can bid on search engines for specific keywords, known as 'keyword bidding'. This way, they enhance their visibility, influencing the results that users will see first during their internet searches, explains COMCO. According to information received through self-disclosures, several companies in the travel sector and several online casinos would have agreed not to compete on the main search engines, refraining from bidding on keywords related to competing brands. This approach could constitute an illegal agreement and have harmful effects for consumers, indicates COMCO. Indeed, search engine results could have been influenced to the point of making it more difficult for users to compare competitors. ### Implications for consumers COMCO's investigations could have a significant impact on consumers. If confirmed, the illegal agreements could have altered the visibility of search results, making it more difficult for consumers to compare offers from different operators. This could influence purchasing decisions and limit competition in the market. ### Sectors involved The first investigation concerns three companies offering travel packages in Switzerland.
