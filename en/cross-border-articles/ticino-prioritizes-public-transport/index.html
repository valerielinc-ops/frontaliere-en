Dettagli operativi

Implications for cross-border workers The increase in traffic and the need to invest in public transport have direct implications for cross-border workers commuting to Ticino. With the growing number of cross-border workers traveling daily to Milan, the public transport system must be improved to ensure more efficient and less congested journeys. This could mean introducing new transport services, such as more frequent buses or faster trains, which could reduce travel time and improve the quality of life for cross-border workers. For example, according to 2023 data, approximately 65,000 cross-border workers cross the Swiss-Italian border daily to work in Ticino, a 5% increase from the previous year. Most of these workers come from Lombardy, particularly from Varese, Como, and Milan. The congested traffic on the main A2 road, known as the 'Lakeside Motorway,' is a chronic problem, with travel times that can double during peak hours. > "The situation is unsustainable. Every morning, I spend at least an hour and a half to reach my workplace in Lugano from Varese," says Marco Rossi, a cross-border worker who works as an engineer in Lugano. ## Comparison with other cities Other cities, such as Zurich and Geneva, have already significantly invested in public transport. Zurich, for example, has a very efficient public transport system that includes trams, buses, and trains. This has made the city one of the most livable in the world. Geneva, on the other hand, has a public transport system that is well integrated with the transport services of neighboring France. Ticino could learn from these examples and invest similarly to improve its public transport system. What to do concretely To tackle the traffic challenge, Ticino must take concrete measures. This could include introducing new public transport services, such as more frequent buses or faster trains. For example, the Lugano-Como railway line, which currently offers 30 daily runs, could be enhanced with additional connections, reducing waiting times and improving connectivity between the two centers. Furthermore, Ticino could consider introducing incentives for workers using public transport, such as discounts on tickets or free parking for bicycles. These incentives could encourage more people to use public transport and reduce the number of private vehicles on the roads. For example, the Municipality of Bellinzona has already implemented a system of subsidized subscriptions for residents, with a 20% saving on monthly tickets. ### Operational Checklist - Evaluate the introduction of new bus and train lines - Analyze the possibility of increasing the frequency of existing services - Study incentives for using public transport, such as ticket discounts - Implement free parking for bicycles at main stations - Collaborate with municipalities to promote subsidized subscriptions ## Useful Tools For cross-border workers seeking to navigate the Ticino public transport system, several useful tools are available. What are the main traffic challenges in Ticino?
The main traffic challenges in Ticino include the growing number of cross-border workers traveling daily to Milan and the need to invest in public transport to address this issue. Congested traffic can cause delays and have a negative impact on the Ticino economy.

Ticino can introduce new public transport services, such as more frequent buses or faster trains. Additionally, it might consider introducing incentives for workers who use public transport, such as discounts on tickets or free parking for bicycles.
