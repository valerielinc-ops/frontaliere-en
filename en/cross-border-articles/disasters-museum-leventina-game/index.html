The exhibition, open to the public from May 4, 2026, transforms historical disasters into a board game, making learning an engaging and educational experience. ### A journey through time The exhibition is structured as a large board game, where each square represents a significant historical event. Visitors can move along the path, discovering how local communities have reacted to landslides, epidemics, and fires over the centuries. Each square is accompanied by explanatory panels, archive images, and historical reconstructions that illustrate the events and the strategies adopted to address them. ### Practical implications for cross-border workers This exhibition offers a unique opportunity for cross-border workers to better understand the history and culture of Ticino.
