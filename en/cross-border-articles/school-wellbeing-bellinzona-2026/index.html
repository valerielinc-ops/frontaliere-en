Bellinzona: new school wellbeing programs

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 27 April 2026

New educational programs for Bellinzona primary schools starting in 2026

Contesto

In brief - New educational programs for Bellinzona elementary schools - Collaboration with the ASPI Foundation - Launch scheduled for September 2026 ## Key facts - What: Educational programs for school well-being - When: Launch September 2026 - Where: Elementary schools in Bellinzona - Who: Bellinzona Municipal School Institute and ASPI Foundation - Amount: Not yet specified The Management of the Bellinzona Municipal School Institute has developed, in collaboration with the Foundation of the Italian Switzerland for Aid, Support, and Protection of Childhood (ASPI), a structured device aimed at enhancing and making the preventive educational offer more consistent, in favor of the well-being and integrity of students. This project has been shared and endorsed by the Head of the Education, Culture, Youth, and Sociality Department, Renato Bison, and submitted and approved by the Municipality. The concrete content of the tool provides that in all fourth-grade classes of the Municipal School Institute, the program 'Dillo forte', already known and offered in some locations of the city for over 15 years, will be proposed. In the fifth-grade classes, the programs 'E-www@i!' and '#Join4Respect' will be offered, with the aim of strengthening relational skills, awareness in the use of digital tools, and the promotion of a culture of respect. The operational launch is scheduled to begin with the start of the next school year. Meanwhile, the Management has started the planning work in close collaboration with the ASPI Foundation representatives, in order to ensure a coherent and effective implementation throughout the territory. Dettagli operativi

Practical Analysis The introduction of new educational programs in Bellinzona represents a significant step towards a school system more focused on students' well-being. These programs aim to enhance relational skills, awareness in the use of digital tools, and the promotion of a culture of respect. But what does this concretely mean for students, parents, and teachers? ### For Students Students in the fourth and fifth grades will benefit from a more comprehensive and coordinated educational approach. The 'Dillo forte' program will help students develop relational skills and prevent bullying. The 'E-www@i!' and '#Join4Respect' programs will provide tools for safe use of social media and promote a respectful school environment. ### For Parents Parents can expect a significant improvement in the consistency and effectiveness of educational initiatives. These programs will offer additional tools to support the well-being and harmonious development of their children. Furthermore, parents will be informed about progress and school activities through regular communications. ### For Teachers Teachers will have access to additional tools to support students in their personal and social development. The training and support provided by the ASPI Foundation will ensure that teachers are adequately prepared to manage the new initiatives. This project aims to enhance the educational and preventive work that teachers carry out routinely. ### Comparison with Other Schools Bellinzona is not the only city to introduce similar programs. Other schools in Switzerland are adopting similar approaches to address modern educational challenges. Frequently Asked Questions
What are the educational programs introduced in Bellinzona?
The programs introduced are 'Dillo forte' for fourth-grade classes, and 'E-www@i!' and '#Join4Respect' for fifth-grade classes. These programs aim to strengthen relational skills, awareness in the use of digital tools, and the promotion of a culture of respect.
When will these new programs start?
The operational start of the new programs is scheduled for the beginning of the next school year, in September 2026.
Who is involved in the implementation of these programs?
The Directorate of the Municipal School Institute of Bellinzona, in collaboration with the Foundation of the Italian Switzerland for the Aid, Support, and Protection of Childhood (ASPI), is involved in the implementation of these programs.
