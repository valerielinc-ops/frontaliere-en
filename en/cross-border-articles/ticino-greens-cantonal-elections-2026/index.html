Green Ticino: strategy for the 2026 cantonal elections

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 27 April 2026

The Green Party of Ticino is exploring alliances for the 2026 cantonal elections, aiming for a broader progressive front.

Contesto

In brief - Greens Ticino explore alliances for 2026 cantonal elections - Goal: progressive front with MPS and PS - Greta Gysin might run for the State Council ## Key facts - What: Strategy for the 2026 cantonal elections - When: Assembly held on April 26, 2026 - Where: Locarno, Ticino - Who: Greens of Ticino - Amount: Not yet specified The Greens of Ticino are working to define their strategy ahead of the 2026 cantonal elections. During an assembly held in Locarno on April 26, 2026, the party tasked its coordination with exploring possible alliances to present a broader progressive front. 'We have been given the mandate to explore the alliance certainly with the MPS and the PS, with whom we are already in contact,' explained Samantha Bourgoin, co-coordinator of the party, to the microphones of SEIDISERA. Moreover, the Greens intend to deepen contacts with other formations, such as Più Donne and the PC. Analyses following the 2023 elections had highlighted that part of the Greens' electorate identifies with the party's ecological policies but not with its social policies. Furthermore, the alliance with the PS had resulted in a performance below expectations. However, Bourgoin emphasized that the Greens are convinced that their work in Parliament can make a difference. 'Because as Greens, we are certainly the most credible and the most aggressive in advancing our themes,' she stated. Regarding government action, Bourgoin deemed 'dangerous' a right that gains ground by blaming the left and the Greens for the deficits. 'Together with other forces, with other movements, we would be stronger,' she added. For the State Council, many await a 'yes' for the Government race from the current national councilor Greta Gysin. Analysis of Implications The strategy of the Ticino Greens to explore alliances with other progressive political forces could have a significant impact on the 2026 cantonal elections. This approach could attract a broader electorate, including those sensitive to ecological issues but not necessarily to the social policies promoted by the Greens. Furthermore, a united progressive front could more effectively counter the narrative of the right, which often blames economic deficits on the left and the Greens. ### Comparison with Previous Elections In the 2023 elections, the alliance between the Greens and the PS did not meet expectations, partly due to the divergence between ecological and social policies. This time, the Greens intend to present a broader front that can also include other political groups. This could help overcome internal divisions and present a more united and coherent image to the electorate. ### Possible Scenarios If the alliance with MPS and PS materializes, the Greens could achieve better results compared to previous elections. Additionally, the possibility of collaborating with other political forces, such as Più Donne and the PC, could further expand the electoral base. However, it is important that the party maintains its credibility on ecological issues, which represent one of its strengths. ### Practical Implications for Cross-Border Workers For cross-border workers residing in Ticino, the 2026 cantonal elections could have a significant impact on local policies, particularly those related to the environment and sustainability. A united progressive front could promote more ambitious policies in these sectors, with possible benefits for the quality of life and the environment.
