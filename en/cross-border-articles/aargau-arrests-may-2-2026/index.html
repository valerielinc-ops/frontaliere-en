Two arrests in one night in Aargau: a Tunisian and a Moroccan (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 4 May 2026

The Aargau cantonal police arrested two men in two different municipalities in the night between Friday and Saturday.

In brief - Two arrests in one night in Aargau - A Tunisian and a Moroccan involved - Both asylum seekers, one in Switzerland and one in Germany ## Key facts - What: Two arrests - When: Night between Friday and Saturday, May 2, 2026 - Where: Schinznach Dorf and Döttingen, Aargau - Who: Aargau Cantonal Police - Amount: Not specified In the night between Friday and Saturday, the Aargau Cantonal Police made two arrests in two different municipalities. In Schinznach Dorf, a surveillance camera recorded a man entering a private property at 4:00 AM. Residents immediately alerted the police, who found a 38-year-old Tunisian asylum seeker in possession of a moped. The man was arrested on suspicion of attempted theft. A few hours later, in Döttingen, a resident found a man sleeping in a shed. The police intervened and arrested a 37-year-old Moroccan, registered as an asylum seeker in Germany. The man claimed to have ingested chemical products for pest control and was taken to the hospital for examination. In the end, he was handed over to the prosecution for trespassing.
