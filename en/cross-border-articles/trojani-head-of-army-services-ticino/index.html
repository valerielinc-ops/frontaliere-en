Dettagli operativi

A Ticino native to head two important military services. Stefano Trojani, 59, is a native of Ticino and has extensive experience in the defense sector. Previously, he worked with the cantonal administration, particularly in 2021, as project manager for mass vaccinations and contact tracing during the COVID-19 pandemic. His experience and knowledge of the territory make him an ideal candidate for this role. The Military Intelligence Service and the Preventive Protection Service of the army are two important services that work closely with civilian and military authorities to ensure national security. The first service deals with the collection and analysis of strategic and operational information, while the second service focuses on preventive protection of critical infrastructure and strategic resources. According to the National Security Law of April 18, 2017, the Military Intelligence Service is responsible for collecting and analyzing information related to threats to national security. In 2020, the service received funding of 13.4 million Swiss francs to carry out its activities. Concrete examples of the Military Intelligence Service's activities include border surveillance and collection of information on terrorist groups active in the region. For example, in 2019, the service contributed to dismantling a human trafficking network operating between Ticino and Lombardy. The Preventive Protection Service of the army, on the other hand, deals with the protection of critical infrastructure, such as power plants and transportation networks. Punti chiave

The appointment of Trojani has been welcomed by Ticino authorities, who have emphasized the importance of Ticino's presence in the Swiss army. 'The appointment of Colonel SMG Stefano Trojani as head of two important military services is a recognition of his work and experience,' said a representative of the Department of Institutions of the Canton of Ticino. Colonel Trojani, a native of Bellinzona, will be responsible for managing two strategic services of the Swiss army: the Intelligence Service and the Operational Support Service. His appointment was made possible thanks to his extensive experience in the field of national security and defense. According to the Federal Law on the Swiss Army of March 21, 2013, Ticino is one of the most important cantons for national defense, with a presence of over 2,500 soldiers and 15 infantry units. The presence of Ticino in the Swiss army is fundamental to ensuring the security of the region and the country. What are the main tax advantages for frontier workers in Ticino but reside in Italy?
The Ticino border guards resident in Italy pay taxes in Switzerland, but may benefit from a 30% deduction on taxable income in Italy, according to the Convention against double taxation between the two countries.
What is the agreement on social security between Switzerland and Italy for frontier workers?
Frontier workers are subject to Swiss social insurance legislation, but retain the rights acquired in both countries. Italy pays social security contributions in Switzerland, ensuring health and pension coverage in both countries.
