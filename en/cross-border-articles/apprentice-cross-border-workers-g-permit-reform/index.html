Dettagli operativi

The reform of the G permit for cross-border apprentices is a significant step forward in the landscape of cross-border work in Switzerland. This measure, approved by the National Council, simplifies bureaucratic procedures and offers greater clarity and stability to apprentices and companies involved. The current legislation, provided for by the Federal Law on Foreigners and their Integration (LStrI), stipulated that EU/EFTA cross-border workers with open-ended employment contracts or those lasting more than a year could obtain a G permit of five years. For shorter contracts, the validity of the permit coincided with the duration of the employment relationship. However, cross-border apprentices were subject to annual renewals, a procedure that created uncertainty and administrative complications. With the new reform, the G permit will be issued for the entire duration of the apprenticeship, which can vary from two to four years. This means that cross-border apprentices will no longer have to go through the annual renewal process, saving time and resources for both themselves and companies. The reform is particularly relevant for Canton Ticino, where cross-border apprentices represent a significant component of vocational training. Ticino, with its proximity to Italy, has always had a significant flow of cross-border workers, and this reform will contribute to simplifying procedures for apprentices who choose to train in Switzerland. The decision of the National Council was taken after a long parliamentary process, which saw the motion approved by the Council of States in September 2025.
