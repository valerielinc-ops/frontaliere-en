In brief - The management commission of the Canton of Ticino discusses the division of the initiatives on health insurance funds. - The Lega proposal costs around 50 million CHF to the Canton. - The socialists criticize the division as an attempt to favor the Lega initiative. ## Key facts - What: Discussion on the division of initiatives on health insurance funds. - When: 8 hours ago (at the time of publication). - Where: Management Commission of the Canton of Ticino. - Who: Lega group leader Boris Bignasca and socialist group leader Ivo Durisch. - Amount: Around 50 million CHF for the Lega initiative. The Management Commission of the Canton of Ticino is at the center of a heated debate regarding the initiatives on health insurance funds. The Lega proposal, which envisages a cost of around 50 million CHF for the Canton, seems to have the favor of the majority. The president of the Centro Fiorenzo Dadò proposed to divide the application message of the two proposals, a move that finds the favor of the Lega group leader Boris Bignasca. 'We are at a standstill: the initiatives are indeed approved, but the State Council holds them 'hostage', preventing them from being implemented, as this formal hurdle of promulgation through publication is missing,' explained Bignasca. The Lega proposal, which already has two rapporteurs, seems to have a relatively easy solution. 'My colleague Natalia Ferrara and I act as rapporteurs for the Lega initiative,' explained Dadò. Regarding the socialist initiative, Dadò asked the PS members in Management to present their report, opening the possibility for other parties to act as rapporteurs. ### Implications for cross-border workers Cross-border workers working in Ticino could be directly affected by the changes in health insurance funds.
