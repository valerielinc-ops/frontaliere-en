Di Valerie Linc · Published on 2 May 2026

The 23-year-old from Orino secures his first professional victory in the sixth stage of the Tour of Turkey

Contesto

In brief - Christian Bagatin wins the queen stage of the Tour of Turkey - First professional victory for the 23-year-old from Orino - Solo success at the finish line in Feslikan ## Key facts - What: Victory in the sixth stage of the Tour of Turkey - When: May 1, 2026 - Where: Feslikan, Turkey - Who: Christian Bagatin, MHB Bank CSB Telecom Fort - Margin: 1'01'' ahead of the second-placed rider Christian Bagatin, a 23-year-old from Orino, secured his first professional victory in the sixth stage of the Tour of Turkey. The young cyclist from the Hungarian team MHB Bank CSB Telecom Fort, with Italian roots, crossed the finish line solo in Feslikan after a breakaway that started 70 kilometers from the finish. Bagatin took advantage of the long final climb to distance his breakaway companions and finish 1'01'' ahead of Australian Sebastian Berwick, who claimed the leader's jersey. ### A well-deserved success Bagatin had a perfect day from a competitive standpoint. Initially, he joined the breakaway to support the work of Alessandro Fancellu, the team's classification rider. However, as the final climb began, Bagatin accelerated and continued alone, maintaining a good lead to the finish line. 'I can't believe it, it's incredible,' Bagatin said after the victory. 'It was a difficult race to interpret: I joined the breakaway to support Fancellu's work, but then I realized they weren't catching me anymore. On the final climb, I heard my DS Orlando Maini cheering me on; I thought about the sacrifices made, the support of my girlfriend and teammates. It was amazing.' ### A historic moment for Varesotto cycling Bagatin's victory is the first professional win of the season for Varesotto cycling, which is showing promising signs with podium finishes from the Bessega twins and soli...
