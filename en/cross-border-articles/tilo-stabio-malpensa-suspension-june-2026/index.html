TILO Stabio-Malpensa: no trains for a month (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 30 May 2026

From June 7 to July 5, 2026, TILO services between Stabio and Malpensa will be suspended for RFI works. Replacement buses and longer travel times are planned.

Contesto

In brief - TILO S40 and S50 connections suspended between Stabio and Malpensa from June 7, 2026. - The stop will last four weeks until July 5 for RFI work in Italy. - Replacement buses every 30 minutes towards Gallarate and every hour direct. ## Key facts - What: Suspension of TILO S40 and S50 trains - When: From June 7 to July 5, 2026 - Where: Stabio - Varese - Malpensa Airport route - Who: TILO and Rete Ferroviaria Italiana (RFI) - Service: Replacement buses every 30 and 60 minutes From June 7 to July 5, 2026, rail traffic between Canton Ticino and Malpensa Airport will experience a significant interruption. TILO has announced that the S40 and S50 line connections will be suspended on the section between Stabio and the Milanese hub. This decision, officially communicated through a note released on Friday, is linked to necessary infrastructure enhancement works on Italian territory, managed directly by Rete Ferroviaria Italiana (RFI). For the cross-border commuter who uses these routes daily to travel between the province of Varese and Mendrisiotto, a period of significant logistical changes is on the horizon, coinciding with the start of the summer season. ### Impact on S40 and S50 lines The suspension affects the entire sector connecting the Swiss station of Stabio with Varese and Malpensa Airport terminal. During these four weeks, TILO trains will terminate their journey at Stabio, making it necessary to transfer to alternative means for anyone who needs to continue to Italy. This situation requires careful planning for those who need to reach their workplace or customs checkpoints, constantly monitoring waiting times at borders to evaluate road alternatives. Dettagli operativi

The alternative mobility plan drawn up to cope with the closure of the railway line provides for two main road solutions. The first option consists of a replacement bus service that will run every 30 minutes. These vehicles will connect Stabio to Gallarate, making all the intermediate stops normally planned for lines S40 and S50. This service is mainly designed for those who need to reach the intermediate locations between the border and the Gallarate hub, providing a similar capillary service to the railway, although with reduced speeds. ### Direct connections to the airport For those whose final destination is Malpensa airport, TILO has planned a second service: a direct bus that will run every hour between Stabio and the airport, without making intermediate stops. This solution aims to reduce travel times for passengers heading to flights, but TILO explicitly warns that the availability of seats on these buses is limited. It is not possible to guarantee a seat for all passengers, especially during peak hours or on long weekends with high tourist traffic. Using public transport in Ticino will therefore require greater flexibility than the usual rail service. The railway company has emphasized that travel times will inevitably be longer than the train journey. Road traffic between Stabio, Varese and Gallarate can heavily impact the punctuality of replacement buses, a factor that every cross-border worker or traveler will need to consider when planning their departure time. Punti chiave

Management of terminals and online schedules A crucial technical detail for those traveling to Malpensa concerns movements within the airport area. Replacement buses may not serve both terminals with the same frequency or manner as trains. TILO specifies that, between the two terminals of the Milan airport, travelers will have to use the existing internal airport services. This adds an additional logistical step for those who need to reach Terminal 1 or Terminal 2, potentially increasing travel stress if not planned well in advance. For workers operating under the new 2026 tax agreement and who hold a G permit, the regularity of travel remains a key point in their daily lives. Even if the train suspension is temporary, the impact on time management is real. It is advisable to check if your LAMal insurance or pension coverage includes specific protections in case of serious delays that prevent you from working, although the responsibility in this case falls on the transport management. TILO's transparency in communicating the measure well in advance, however, allows for more conscious organization of shifts and travel. In conclusion, from June 7 to July 5, 2026, the watchword for those traveling between Stabio and Malpensa will be planning. The limitation of seats on buses and the lengthening of travel times are variables that cannot be ignored. It is strongly recommended to use official apps and reference sites to monitor real-time rail and road traffic status. Frequently Asked Questions
What are the exact dates of the TILO train suspension?
TILO connections between Stabio and Malpensa (lines S40 and S50) will be suspended for four weeks, starting from June 7 until July 5, 2026. The suspension is due to infrastructure enhancement works carried out by Rete Ferroviaria Italiana (RFI) on Italian territory.
How do the replacement buses between Stabio and Gallarate work?
A replacement bus service with a frequency of every 30 minutes is planned. These buses connect Stabio to Gallarate and make all intermediate stops normally served by lines S40 and S50. Travel times will be longer compared to the train, and seating is limited.
Is there a direct connection to Malpensa Airport?
Yes, in addition to the bus to Gallarate, there will be a direct bus every hour between Stabio and Malpensa Airport without intermediate stops. However, to move between the two airport terminals (Terminal 1 and Terminal 2), passengers will need to use the airport's internal shuttle services.
