Contesto

In brief - Conference on the mafia and Italy-Switzerland cooperation on May 11, 2026 in Milan - Fiorenza Bergomi, judge of the Federal Criminal Court, will participate - Topic: legislation, investigations, and cross-border cooperation ## Key facts - What: Conference on the mafia and Italy-Switzerland cooperation - When: May 11, 2026 - Where: University of Milan - Who: Fiorenza Bergomi, Nando Dalla Chiesa, Annamaria Astrologo, Pasquale Addesso, Giuseppe Furciniti - Cost: Not yet specified The conference 'The mafia beyond borders: legislation, investigations, and cooperation between Italy and Switzerland' will be held on Monday, May 11, 2026 at the University of Milan. Among the speakers will be Fiorenza Bergomi, judge of the Criminal Court of the Federal Criminal Court based in Bellinzona. Bergomi has also worked as a prosecutor at the Cantonal Ministry for several years. In addition to Bergomi, Nando Dalla Chiesa, Annamaria Astrologo, Pasquale Addesso, and Giuseppe Furciniti will speak. The conference will be moderated by Fabio Basile. ### Speakers and topics The conference will feature experts from both countries. Nando Dalla Chiesa will speak about 'Organized crime and education in legality: institutions and territory'. Annamaria Astrologo, scientific coordinator of the Ticino Observatory on organized crime, will share her experience and knowledge of the phenomenon. Pasquale Addesso, deputy prosecutor of the District Anti-Mafia Directorate of Milan, and Giuseppe Furciniti, head of the Anti-Mafia Investigative Directorate Milan, will discuss investigations and strategies to combat organized crime. ### Importance of cross-border cooperation Cooperation between Italy and Switzerland is fundamental to combat organized crime that operates across borders. Analysis of Cross-Border Challenges The fight against organized crime across borders requires close collaboration between the authorities of the two countries. The Milan conference represents an opportunity to discuss the challenges and opportunities of this cooperation. Fiorenza Bergomi, with her experience in the Swiss judicial system, can offer a unique perspective on the difficulties and possible solutions. ### Experiences and Best Practices Annamaria Astrologo, scientific coordinator of the Ticino Observatory on Organized Crime, will bring her knowledge and experiences gained in Ticino. Her participation is crucial to understanding how organized crime operates in a region with a strong presence of cross-border workers. ### Investigative Strategies Pasquale Addesso and Giuseppe Furciniti, with their experience in anti-mafia investigations, will discuss the most effective investigative strategies. Their presentations will be useful for understanding how Italian authorities tackle organized crime and how they can collaborate with Swiss authorities. ### Education in Legality Nando Dalla Chiesa, delegate of the Rector of the University of Milan, will speak about the importance of education in legality. This topic is particularly relevant for young people and those living in areas with a strong presence of organized crime. ### Impact on Cross-Border Workers Cross-border workers who work in Switzerland and reside in Italy, or vice versa, may be particularly interested in the topics discussed at the conference.
