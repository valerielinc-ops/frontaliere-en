Switzerland and civil aviation: 61% of Swiss in favor (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 4 May 2026

According to a study commissioned by Aviationsuisse, the majority of Swiss recognize the strategic and economic importance of airports.

Contesto

In brief - 61% of Swiss people have a positive attitude towards air travel - 80% consider civil aviation important for the country - 71% find an air ticket tax not very useful ## Key facts - What: Study on public opinion regarding civil aviation - When: Conducted between January 22 and February 5, 2026 - Where: German-speaking and French-speaking Switzerland - Who: Commissioned by Aviationsuisse - Amount: 3,700 adults surveyed - Where: One third live near Zurich, Basel, and Geneva airports In recent years, Swiss airports and civil aviation have faced numerous challenges, including lawsuits related to noise, initiatives against night flights, and proposals to increase ticket prices. Despite these pressures, a new study conducted by Sotomo on behalf of Aviationsuisse reveals a more nuanced picture. The majority of the Swiss population does not seem to be asking for new restrictions and recognizes the central role of airports, both economically and strategically. The survey, conducted between January 22 and February 5, 2026, involved approximately 3,700 adults in German-speaking and French-speaking Switzerland. Roughly one third of the participants live near the airports of Zurich, Basel, and Geneva. This context is marked by local initiatives, such as one in Zurich that seeks to reduce night flights between 11 PM and 11:30 PM, and a lawsuit in Geneva against new taxes on takeoffs after 10 PM. According to the study, 61% of respondents have a positive attitude towards air travel, which is mainly used for vacations and work. More than half of the sample is proud of Swiss aviation, and for about two-thirds of respondents, the sector represents an opportunity rather than a burden. Dettagli operativi

• ### Analysis of population preferences The study reveals that 61% of respondents have a positive attitude towards air travel, mainly used for holidays and work. This data is significant as it shows that, despite criticism, a substantial part of the population appreciates the speed and convenience of air travel. Moreover, more than half of the sample is proud of Swiss aviation, indicating a sense of national pride towards this sector. ### Economic and strategic impact For about two-thirds of respondents, civil aviation represents an opportunity rather than a burden. This data underscores the economic importance of the sector, which significantly contributes to the Swiss economy. 80% of the population considers civil aviation important for the country, a figure that reflects awareness of its strategic relevance for international connectivity and trade. ### Preferences for international solutions Environmental concerns remain present, but the study shows that the population would prefer internationally coordinated solutions rather than purely national measures. This approach could be more effective in reducing CO₂ emissions without compromising the competitiveness of the aviation sector. 71% consider a ticket tax, such as that proposed by an environmental initiative, to be of little use, but there is a willingness to pay more, provided that the funds raised are actually used to reduce CO₂ emissions. ### Comparison with other initiatives In Zurich, an initiative is underway to reduce night flights between 11 pm and 11.30 pm. In Geneva, on the other hand, there is an appeal pending against the new taxes on take-offs after 10 pm.
