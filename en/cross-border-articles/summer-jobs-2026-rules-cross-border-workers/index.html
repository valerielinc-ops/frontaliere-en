Summer jobs, here are the rules to know (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 9 June 2026

Salaries, insurance and age limits for young cross-border workers in Ticino.

Contesto

In brief - The minimum wage law does not apply to young people between 13 and 18 years old. - The minimum wage is approximately 20 francs per hour for adults. - AVS contributions start being due from the age of 17. ## Key facts - What: Minimum wages, insurance, and age limits for young cross-border workers. - When: During the summer of 2026. - Where: Canton Ticino. - Who: Young cross-border workers. - Amount: Approximately 20 francs per hour for adults. The school year is about to end, and for many young people, the summer job season is also opening up. Some are looking for a bit of independence, others want to save some money, and others simply want to gain their first work experience. But even a temporary job, maybe just a few weeks, is not without rules: before saying “yes” and signing a contract, it’s essential to know about wages, age limits, insurance, and rights. ### Concrete examples For a 16-year-old cross-border worker who works 20 hours a week in a bar in Lugano, the minimum wage would be approximately 400 francs per week (20 hours x 20 francs per hour). However, if the same young person works only 10 hours a week, the minimum wage would be approximately 200 francs per week (10 hours x 20 francs per hour). ### References to specific Ticino municipalities In Bellinzona, for example, young cross-border workers can find jobs in shops and restaurants in the historic center. Practical Implications - Young people aged 13-18 can work without minimum wage requirements. - Adults must receive a minimum wage of approximately 20 CHF per hour. - AVS contributions become mandatory from age 17. ### Age Limits - Under 16s cannot work in bars. - The Labour Law dedicates a specific section to minors' protection, with regulations designed to prevent excessively demanding or dangerous activities. ### Insurance - AVS contributions become mandatory from age 17. ## Additional Useful Information - Employers must ensure a safe and suitable working environment for minors. - Young workers must receive a written employment contract specifying working conditions and salary. - AVS contributions are calculated as 10.6% of gross salary. - Example: A 17-year-old worker earning 20 CHF/hour for 40 working hours will pay approximately 85 CHF in AVS contributions. ## Real-life Examples - A Lugano restaurant hires a 16-year-old boy to assist during dinner service. The boy works 20 hours/week for 4 weeks at 18 CHF/hour. The employer must ensure a safe environment suitable for minors and provide a written employment contract. - A Bellinzona family hires a 14-year-old girl for household chores. The girl works 10 hours/week for 8 weeks at 15 CHF/hour. In this case, no AVS contributions are required. ## Operational Checklist - Verify the worker's age and ensure compliance with minors' protection regulations. - Provide a written employment contract specifying working conditions and salary. - Calculate AVS contributions and ensure proper payment. - Guarantee a safe and suitable working environment for minors. ## Practical Scenario Comparisons - A 17-year-old worker earning 20 CHF/hour for 40 working hours will pay ~85 CHF in AVS contributions. Action - Check the minimum wage for your age: in Switzerland, the minimum wage varies based on age and type of work. For example, a minor (under 18 years old) can earn a minimum wage of CHF 2'350 per month, while an adult (18 years or older) can earn a minimum wage of CHF 3'000 per month (2022 data). - Check the age limits for your job: for example, minors under 15 years old cannot work, while minors under 18 years old can only work for a limited number of hours per week (max 9 hours/day, 42 hours/week, for light work). - Make sure to pay AVS contributions if you are 17 years or older: AVS contributions (Old-Age and Survivors' Insurance) are mandatory for all Swiss workers from the age of 17. ### Concrete Examples - A 17-year-old working as a waiter in Lugano can earn CHF 2'500 per month, but must pay 10% AVS contributions on their salary (CHF 250). - A 20-year-old working as a sales assistant in Chiasso can earn CHF 3'200 per month, but must pay 10% AVS contributions on their salary (CHF 320). ### Operational Checklist - Check the minimum wage for your age and type of work. - Check the age limits for your job and make sure you don't exceed them. - Make sure to pay AVS contributions if you are 17 years or older. ### Comparison of Practical Scenarios - A summer worker who earns CHF 2'500 per month for 3 months can earn a total of CHF 7'500, but must pay CHF 750 in AVS contributions (10% of salary). - A summer worker who earns CHF 3'200 per month for 3 months can earn a total of CHF 9'600, but must pay CHF 960 in AVS contributions (10% of salary). ### CTA - Use our calculator to check your net salary.
