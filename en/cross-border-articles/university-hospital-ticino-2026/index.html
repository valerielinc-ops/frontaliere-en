In brief - New Cardiocentro plans inaugurated - Distributed university hospital project under evaluation - Sanvido: 'We hope to receive the green light soon' ## Key facts - What: Distributed university hospital project - When: May 4, 2026 - Where: Ticino - Who: Ente Ospedaliero Cantonale (EOC) - Amount: Not yet specified Federal Councillor Ignazio Cassis retraced the history of the Cardiocentro in Lugano, highlighting how its birth was accompanied by resistance and controversy, typical of Ticino parochialism. Cassis, who was the cantonal doctor at the time of the project, recalled how the best cure for Ticino patients was, years ago, a three-hour train journey to Zurich. Today, thanks to the Cardiocentro, those treatments are also available in Ticino. The president of the EOC Board of Directors, Paolo Sanvido, announced that the Cardiocentro will continue to grow and that another major project is on the horizon: a cantonal university hospital. Sanvido explained that the concept is new compared to what we are used to in Switzerland, with the idea of a distributed university hospital. The project is currently on the table of the State Council, awaiting a green light to further explore this possibility. ### Cardiocentro growth The director of the Cardiocentro, Massimo Manserra, spoke of growth across the board. In addition to the three new floors added to the hospital, the Cardiocentro is implementing the 'Heart, vessels and lungs' project, which will expand the possibility of taking care of the center. Since April 1st, all thoracic surgery services of the EOC have been integrated into the Cardiocentro. Practical Analysis The project of a distributed university hospital in Ticino represents a significant opportunity to improve access to healthcare for residents and cross-border workers in the canton. Currently, many Ticinese and cross-border workers have to travel to Zurich or other Swiss cities to access specialized care. A distributed university hospital could reduce this need, offering high-quality services closer to home. ### Benefits for cross-border workers Cross-border workers in Ticino could benefit from improved local healthcare facilities. This could reduce waiting times for specialized care and improve the quality of life for those living and working in Ticino. Furthermore, a distributed university hospital could offer training and research opportunities, improving the quality of healthcare services available in the region. ### Challenges and considerations However, there are also challenges to consider. The project will require significant investment and must be managed to ensure a balance between supply and demand. Additionally, it will be important to ensure that costs do not increase excessively, reflecting on health insurance premiums. The director of the Department of Health and Social Affairs, Raffaele De Rosa, emphasized the importance of finding this balance to ensure a sustainable healthcare system. ### Future scenarios If the project is approved, Ticino could become a model for other Swiss cantons. A distributed university hospital could attract high-level healthcare professionals and offer collaboration opportunities with universities and research centers.
