Swiss Competition Commission probes search engine ads: travel and online casinos

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 30 April 2026

The Swiss Competition Commission opens two investigations into illegal agreements between travel sector companies and online casinos

Contesto

In brief - COMCO investigates illicit agreements between travel sector companies and online casinos - Companies refrain from competing on keywords in search engines - Possible negative effects for consumers ## Key facts - What: Investigations into search engine advertising - When: April 30, 2026 - Where: Switzerland - Who: Competition Commission (COMCO) - Amount: Not specified The Competition Commission (COMCO) has opened two investigations into search engine advertising. The first concerns three companies offering tourist packages in Switzerland, while the second involves almost all online casinos. Companies can bid on search engines for certain keywords, thus influencing the visibility of their offers. However, COMCO has received information that several companies in the travel sector and numerous online casinos have agreed not to compete on major search engines, refraining from bidding on certain keywords related to competing brands. This approach could constitute an illicit agreement and have harmful effects on competition and consumers. According to COMCO, comparing offers from competitors could become more difficult for consumers. COMCO received this information as part of self-disclosures, suggesting that some companies themselves have recognized the possible illegality of their agreements. ### Implications for consumers COMCO's investigations could have important implications for Swiss consumers, particularly those looking for tourist packages or online casino services. If companies agree not to compete, consumers may not see all available options, which could limit their ability to find the best offers.
