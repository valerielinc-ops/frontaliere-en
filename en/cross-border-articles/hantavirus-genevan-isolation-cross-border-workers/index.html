In brief - Geneva resident in isolation after flight with hantavirus patient - FOPH: low risk for the population - Passenger was not on the MV Hondius cruise ship ## Key facts - What: Isolation of a Geneva resident after contact with hantavirus patient - When: May 13, 2026 - Where: Flight from St. Helena to Johannesburg - Who: Federal Office of Public Health (FOPH) - Risk: Low risk for the population A Geneva resident is in home isolation after traveling on the same flight as a person seriously ill with hantavirus. The Federal Office of Public Health (FOPH) stated that the individual is asymptomatic and that the risk to the population is low. The Geneva resident was on the flight from St. Helena to Johannesburg, where a seriously ill passenger was also present. However, they were not on board the MV Hondius cruise ship, where the virus caused the death of three passengers. Cantonal authorities are in close contact with the person and monitoring their health. The Swiss patient who contracted hantavirus on the 'Hondius' cruise ship is still in the University Hospital of Zurich. His wife is in home isolation. For passengers who were on the plane with the infected Swiss, there is no danger, according to the FOPH. This is because the patient only developed a fever three days after arrival. ### Implications for cross-border workers For cross-border workers who travel frequently, it is important to be aware of the risks associated with diseases like hantavirus.
