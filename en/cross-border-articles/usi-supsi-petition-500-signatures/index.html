Contesto

In brief - Petition against cuts to USI and SUPSI has already gathered 500 signatures. - Trade union VPOD opposes savings proposed by the State Council. - Petition demands a stabilization program for fixed-term contracts and a collective agreement for intermediate staff. ## Key facts - What: Petition against cuts to USI and SUPSI - When: Recently launched - Where: Ticino, Switzerland - Who: Trade union VPOD - Amount: 500 signatures collected The trade union VPOD has launched a petition to oppose the savings that the State Council proposes to make to fund the two initiatives on health insurance. The petition, which opposes cuts of CHF 5.5 million for the University of Italian Switzerland (USI) and CHF 1.3 million for the University of Applied Sciences and Arts of Southern Switzerland (SUPSI), has already gathered 500 signatures. Edoardo Cappelletti, co-secretary of VPOD, stated that these cuts would worsen working conditions, especially for intermediate staff, reduce employment in research, and compromise the right to education for students who are already facing high tuition fees, which can exceed CHF 1,500 per semester, even in the inter-cantonal average. Locally, Ticino municipalities like Lugano and Bellinzona are particularly affected by this issue, as a significant portion of their youth enrolls in these institutions. The reduction in funds could translate into an increase in university tuition fees or a reduction in services offered to students. The petition also demands a stabilization program for fixed-term contracts, which currently affect about 30% of academic staff, and a collective agreement for intermediate staff. Dettagli operativi

The opposition to the reduction of funds for the University of Italian Switzerland (USI) and the University of Applied Sciences and Arts of Southern Switzerland (SUPSI) is driven by concerns about the potential consequences for staff and students. In 2023, the Ticino government proposed a cut of around 3 million francs, representing about 5% of the total budget of both institutions. Fears include worsening working conditions, reduced research employment, and increased tuition fees, which are currently around 1,500 francs per student per year. The cities of Lugano and Mendrisio, which host USI and SUPSI respectively, could directly impact research and innovation projects, with negative consequences for local start-ups and the entrepreneurial ecosystem in Ticino. The petition launched by the VPOD union has already gathered over 500 signatures in a few days, indicating strong support among students and teachers. The document aims to counter these cuts by advocating for a program to stabilize fixed-term contracts and a collective agreement for middle management. 📊 Expert analysis suggests that if funding is reduced, USI may have to cut the number of courses offered, leading to a decrease in the number of students, currently around 6,000. 💡 For example, if funding were halved, at least 200 jobs among academic and administrative staff could be lost. ⚠️ It is crucial to act now to ensure that education and research remain priorities for Ticino, a region that has always relied on higher education as an engine of economic development.
