Constant monitoring Municipal councilors in Varese, Emanuele Monti and Stefano Angei of the Lega, have raised an urgent issue regarding the state of the former Public Works headquarters on Via Monte Santo. According to the two politicians, the area has become a 'no man's land,' characterized by degradation and illicit activities. 'Enough with bureaucratic justifications: Varese residents cannot wait for the uncertain times of a sale to feel safe under their own homes,' declared Monti and Angei. The municipal administration has been accused of hiding behind the Alienation Plan, but the councilors emphasize that putting a property up for sale does not relieve the Municipality of its responsibilities. 'A potential sale has no certain times, and in the meantime, Palazzo Estense has the duty to safeguard the area and guarantee the safety of citizens,' they added. 'Selling is not an alibi,' they stated. Monti and Angei also criticized the lack of adequate maintenance and monitoring. Analysis of the Situation The situation of decay and insecurity on Via Monte Santo in Varese is a problem that has long afflicted the residents of the area. The former Public Works headquarters, abandoned and not adequately guarded, has become a reference point for illicit activities and makeshift camps. This state of affairs not only compromises the quality of life of citizens but also poses a risk to public safety. According to data from the Municipality of Varese, reports of suspicious activities in the area have increased by 40% in the last two years. Residents also report a 30% increase in abandoned waste and a constant presence of unauthorized persons, especially at night. ### Implications for Cross-Border Workers Cross-border workers who work in Varese and reside in Canton Ticino may be particularly affected by this situation. The safety and livability of urban areas are crucial factors for those who cross the border daily to go to work. An area like Via Monte Santo, if not adequately managed, could become a critical point for those who commute daily. > "The safety of urban areas is fundamental for cross-border workers, who often have to travel even at night to return home." Frequently Asked Questions
What are the main concerns of the residents of Via Monte Santo?
Residents of Via Monte Santo are concerned about the widespread decay, open access points, non-existent gates, and illicit activities taking place in the abandoned area of the former Public Works headquarters. These issues compromise safety and quality of life in the area.
What is the municipal administration doing to resolve the situation?
The municipal administration has been accused of hiding behind the Disposals Plan and not taking concrete measures to clean up the area and ensure the safety of citizens. Councillors Monti and Angei have requested urgent action to close access points, clean up the area, and address the residents' concerns.
How can citizens contribute to solving the problem?
Citizens can contribute to solving the problem by reporting decay and illicit activities to the municipal authorities, participating in public meetings, organizing petitions, and collaborating with law enforcement to increase safety in the area.
