Equans lays off 19 employees in Monteceneri: the details

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 27 April 2026

Equans has announced 19 layoffs at its Monteceneri site, affecting the optical fiber and facility management sectors.

Contesto

In brief - 19 employees laid off by Equans in Monteceneri - Sectors involved: fiber optics and facility management - Decline in turnover for the Ticino branch ## Key facts - What: Layoffs - When: Recently announced - Where: Monteceneri, Ticino - Who: Equans - Amount: 19 employees Nineteen employees of Equans, a branch based in Monteceneri of the group formed in 2018 from the merger with Alpiq, have been laid off. The decision, anticipated by the Corriere del Ticino, was announced to the OCST union by the company's management in the past few hours. The affected employees, as always reported by the Corriere del Ticino, are free to leave immediately and can leave the company before the notice period if they find another job. The number of jobs cut is slightly lower than that announced in March, when 20-22 positions were mentioned. The sectors affected by the layoffs are three, particularly fiber optics, heating system management, and the administrative sector related to these two activities. ### Implications for cross-border workers The layoffs at Equans in Monteceneri could have significant repercussions for cross-border workers employed in Ticino. With the decline in turnover of the Ticino branch, where around 220 people currently work, the situation could become more complex for those seeking job opportunities in the region. Cross-border workers who depend on sectors such as heating system management and fiber optics may have to face greater uncertainty in the job market.
