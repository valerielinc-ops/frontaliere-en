Overtourism in Switzerland: Lucerne and Grindelwald collapsing

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 29 April 2026

Lucerne surpasses Venice in tourist overnight stays. Initiative to block new hotels and Airbnb restrictions.

Contesto

In brief - Lucerne surpasses Venice with 1.5 million overnight stays in 2024 - Initiative to block new hotels in Lucerne - Restrictions on Airbnb in Ticino, Zurich, Lucerne, and Geneva ## Key facts - What: Initiative to block new hotels in Lucerne - When: From March 2026 - Where: Lucerne - Who: Red-green circles - Amount: 6,300 current hotel beds In Switzerland, the debate on tourist accommodation has reached a breaking point. While a lack of visitors was once a concern, it is now the excess that is causing dissatisfaction. From Lucerne to Grindelwald, to the small Iseltwald, managing flows on peak days has become an unsustainable challenge. In Lucerne, tension is palpable. With 1.5 million overnight stays in 2024, the city has set a new historic record, surpassing pre-pandemic volumes. The NZZ newspaper highlighted a symbolic figure: the ratio of tourists to inhabitants in Lucerne is now higher than that of Venice. The political response: an initiative to block new hotels. Since the end of March 2026, a signature collection has been underway in Lucerne for a popular initiative promoted by red-green circles. The goal is to freeze hotel beds, currently at around 6,300. The proposal provides for an iron rule: the opening of a new facility will only be granted in exchange for the closure of another of the same category. This is not the first time the city has reacted: in 2017, citizens voted to eliminate a bus parking lot in the center, a clear signal against the invasion of tourist buses that had already then raised the issue of overtourism. ### Impact on the real estate market One of the most critical issues is the impact of tourism on the real estate market. Dettagli operativi

Overtourism is having a significant impact on the quality of life for residents in Switzerland, particularly in the most popular tourist areas such as Lucerne and Grindelwald. The rise in property prices and short-term rentals is making it difficult for locals to find affordable housing. Additionally, overcrowding on public transport and roads is causing congestion and daily discomfort. ### Restrictions on Airbnb Restrictions on Airbnb and other short-term rental platforms are a direct response to the increase in property prices and the scarcity of housing for residents. In Ticino, the rental limit set at 90 days per year is a measure aimed at reducing the impact of tourism on the local real estate market. Zurich, Lucerne, and Geneva have followed suit, implementing similar regulations to limit the use of these platforms. ### Environmental Impact The increase in tourism also has a significant impact on the environment. Overcrowding on public transport and roads is causing an increase in pollution and congestion. Furthermore, the rise in the number of tourists is putting a strain on the natural resources of tourist areas, such as water and energy. ### Local Community Reactions The reactions of the local community to overtourism are varied. Some residents are concerned about the impact on their lifestyle and quality of life. Others see tourism as an economic opportunity and are in favor of measures to better manage tourist flows. However, there is a growing consensus that a balance needs to be found between tourism and the quality of life for residents. ## Recommended tools For an updated estimate, use the net salary calculator and the CHF-EUR exchange comparator.

Frequently Asked Questions
What are the restrictions on Airbnb in Ticino?
In Ticino, the limit for short-term rentals is set at a maximum of 90 days per year. This measure was introduced to reduce the impact of tourism on the local housing market and ensure that there are enough homes available for residents.
How can I better plan my movements in Switzerland to avoid overcrowding?
To avoid overcrowding, try to plan your movements in advance and use alternative means of transport such as bicycles or less crowded public transport. You can also use tools like the cost of living calculator and rental comparator to find practical solutions for living better in Switzerland.
What measures has Lucerne taken to manage overtourism?
Lucerne has introduced an initiative to block new hotels and limit current hotel beds to around 6,300. Additionally, the city has implemented regulations to limit the use of platforms like Airbnb and has removed a bus parking lot in the center to reduce the invasion of tourist buses.
