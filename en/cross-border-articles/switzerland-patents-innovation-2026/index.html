In brief - Roche files 681 patents, more than Novartis - 10% of patents on electronic cigarettes - TE Connectivity among the patent giants ## Key facts - What: Patent filings in Switzerland - When: 2025 - Where: Switzerland - Who: Roche, Novartis, Philip Morris, Japan Tobacco, TE Connectivity - Amount: 9,914 patents in total Switzerland confirms its position as the most innovative country in Europe in terms of patents, according to statistics from the European Patent Office (EPO) in Munich. Last year, Swiss companies, researchers, and developers filed 9,914 patents, recording the highest per capita number ever. Below are the five most interesting aspects emerging from the new statistics. ### Roche overtakes Novartis Roche is the Swiss company that, last year, filed the highest number of patents with the European Patent Office, totaling 681 inventions and developments. By comparison, Novartis registered 94 filed patents. The apparent gap in terms of innovation between Roche and its competitor is explained by the fact that Roche does not limit itself to the development of drugs but also designs machinery. Specifically, these are laboratory equipment intended for diagnostics, i.e., the identification of diseases, which analyze, among other things, blood samples. Novartis, on the other hand, does not have a comparable diagnostics division. ### One patent out of ten concerns electronic cigarettes A surprising figure concerns the two cigarette manufacturers, Philip Morris International and Japan Tobacco International: last year, the two companies filed a total of over 1,000 patents from Switzerland, representing 10% of the total. Which are the main Swiss companies that file patents?
