In brief - Guide for separations in Ticino with multidisciplinary support - Focus on legal, economic, and psychological needs - Section dedicated to children called 'Little sailors' ## Key facts - What: Guide for separations in Ticino - When: Presented in 2026 - Where: Canton Ticino - Who: FaMo+ - Amount: Not yet specified Bellinzona - In Ticino, 'The affectionate guide' has been presented, a tool designed to support families during the delicate process of separation. The guide was developed by FaMo+ and offers comprehensive support, accompanying parents through legal, economic, psychological, and relational aspects. Valentina Testoni, family mediator for FaMo+, explains that the guide is the result of a multidisciplinary intervention. 'It is the result of a multidisciplinary intervention that responds to the need of parents to have clear and unambiguous answers,' says Testoni. The guide includes a section dedicated to children called 'Little sailors,' which addresses their needs and how to communicate with them. ### Structure of the guide The guide is structured like a map, allowing each person to start with the most urgent need, whether economic, legal, or related to social assistance. This flexible approach responds to the need to personalize support based on the individual needs of families. ### Focus on children A significant part of the guide is dedicated to children, with a specific section called 'Little sailors.' This section addresses the needs of children during separation, providing tools to communicate with them and ensure that their rights are respected. The guide emphasizes the importance of listening, as provided by law in procedures involving minors. ### Situation in Ticino In Ticino, separation is not always conflictual, explains the mediator. What are the main aspects covered in the guide "La guida affettuosa"?
The guide addresses legal, economic, psychological, and relational aspects of separation, with a specific focus on the needs of children. It includes a section dedicated to children called "Piccoli marinai", which provides tools to communicate with them and ensure that their rights are respected.
Who developed the guide "La guida affettuosa"?
The guide was developed by FaMo+, an organization that offers support to families in Ticino. The guide is the result of a multidisciplinary intervention involving various professional figures.
What support services are available for single-parent families?
