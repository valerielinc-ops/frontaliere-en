Novartis: 13% drop in net profit in Q1 2026

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 29 April 2026

Pharmaceutical group Novartis reports a 13% drop in net profit, with Entresto sales significantly down.

Contesto

In brief - Novartis net profit -13% in Q1 2026 - Entresto sales plummeted by 42% - Revenue at 13.1 billion dollars ## Key facts - What: Novartis net profit decline - When: First quarter 2026 - Where: Switzerland - Who: Novartis - Amount: 3.2 billion dollars (2.5 billion francs) The pharmaceutical group Novartis recorded a 13% drop in net profit in the first quarter of 2026, amounting to approximately 3.2 billion dollars (2.5 billion francs). This decline was primarily attributed to the competition from generic drugs against Entresto, a medication for heart failure. Entresto sales plummeted by 42%, according to a statement released by the pharmaceutical giant. The group's revenue decreased by 1% compared to the first quarter of 2025, reaching 13.1 billion dollars. At constant exchange rates, the turnover decreased by 5%. In the fourth quarter of 2025, revenues had still increased by 1% and by 8% in the third quarter. Analysts had predicted revenue of 13.4 billion dollars. ### Analysis of the decline The decline in operating profit was 9%, at 4.2 billion dollars. Despite this, CEO Vas Narasimhan expressed optimism about the medium-term prospects. "From the third quarter, we will be able to review our current forecasts in more detail, as that is when we expect the data relating to the main candidate drugs in our pipeline," he stated. Regarding Entresto, the CEO and CFO Mulkul Mehta argued that "everything is proceeding as planned." "It has always been clear that this first quarter would be particularly marked," Mehta recalled. Narasimhan insisted on new treatments and the launch of new products, arguing that the group is "on the right track" to achieve its annual goals. ### Future prospects This year, Novartis still aims for "low single-digit sales growth." Dettagli operativi

Analysis of the implications The decline in Novartis' net profit has raised concerns among investors and pharmaceutical sector analysts. The competition from generic drugs, particularly for Entresto, has had a significant impact on the group's sales and profit. This decline could influence Novartis' future strategies, which will have to face a wave of patent expirations related to fundamental therapies. ### Growth strategies Novartis is betting on new drugs like Kesimpta and Pluvicto to support its growth. Kesimpta is a drug for the treatment of multiple sclerosis, while Pluvicto is used in the treatment of prostate cancer. These new products could offset the decline in Entresto sales and ensure long-term growth. ### Impact on the stock market The decline in Novartis' stock has had a significant impact on the SMI index, which lost 0.57%. This decline could influence investor confidence and lead to greater volatility in the pharmaceutical sector. However, market observers highlight the excellent performance of Novartis' shares over the last two years, which could mitigate the negative short-term impact. ### Regulation and pricing Price setting in the United States and Europe is a crucial issue for Novartis. CEO Vas Narasimhan has emphasized the need for solutions in the area of reimbursement that ensure adequate valuation of innovations. This is particularly important for new drugs like Kesimpta and Pluvicto, which could have a significant impact on the market. ### Acquisitions and development Novartis has recently acquired Avidity Biosciences, a California-based company specializing in genetic neuromuscular diseases.
