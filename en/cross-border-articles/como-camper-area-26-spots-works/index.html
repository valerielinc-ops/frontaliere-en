Como: work starts on 26-spot camper area

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 28 April 2026

Construction has begun on the expansion of the camper area in Tavernola. Here's when the work will be finished and the project details.

Contesto

In brief - Work begins on the expansion of Tavernola's camper area - From 10 to 26 spots thanks to 16 new stalls - Completion expected by August 11, 2026 - Total project cost: 307,000 euros - Bid discount: 4.15% - Contracting company: firm from the province of Palermo ## Key facts - What: Expansion of Tavernola's camper area - When: Start of work not specified, completion expected by August 11, 2026 - Where: Tavernola, Como - Who: Construction contracted to a firm from the province of Palermo - Amount: 307,000 euros with a 4.15% discount on the base bid The construction for the expansion of Tavernola's camper area, managed by Csu, has finally begun. The area, built in 2015, will go from the current 10 to 26 spots thanks to the creation of 16 new stalls separated by flower beds in the adjacent green area. Picnic tables will also be added, a project that responds to the growing tourist demand, often exceeding supply and causing discontent in the neighborhood. The project was approved by the municipal council last February and then received the green light from the executive the following month. Completion of the work is expected by August 11, 2026. The contract was awarded to a firm from the province of Palermo for 307,000 euros with a 4.15% discount on the base bid. ### Implications for cross-border workers For cross-border workers using Tavernola's camper area, the expansion represents an opportunity to enjoy more services and space. With the increase in available spots, queues and discomfort related to overcrowding will be reduced, improving the stay experience. ### Comparison with the previous situation Before the expansion, Tavernola's camper area could only accommodate 10 camper vans, an insufficient number to meet tourist demand, especially during the good sea...
