Frequently Asked Questions
How much does gasoline cost in Ticino compared to last year?
In 2026 the average gasoline price in Switzerland is 2.20 CHF per litre, with peaks of 2.30 CHF in the busiest areas of Ticino. In 2025 the average was about 1.80 CHF, so the increase is 22% in one year. The data include stations in Lugano, Mendrisio and Bellinzona.
Why do stations raise gasoline prices faster than they lower them?
Laurent Horvath notes that Ticino service stations adjust prices upward with the market within three days, but take about ten days to lower them when crude prices fall. This delay creates a profit advantage, because consumers pay higher prices for longer. The effect is called opportunistic behavior.
How much can a commuter's monthly expense increase who drives 50 km a day?
A commuter who travels 50 km a day, five days a week, spends about 250 CHF per month with gasoline at 1.80 CHF per litre. With the current average price of 2.20 CHF per litre, the expense rises to about 300 CHF, an increase of over 20%. The rise mainly affects those with low-to-middle income.
What strategies can I use to save on gasoline in Ticino?
To keep costs down, compare prices across stations using apps like Tanken or Benzinpreis, buy fuel when you find deals, plan routes to cut unnecessary kilometres, organize car-pooling with colleagues and consider public transport or an electric vehicle for the long term. Weekly promotions can save up to 10%.
