GEV Insubria Olona: protecting Ticino's environment

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 29 April 2026

The Insubria Olona Volunteer Ecological Guards educate about respecting the environment and protect flora and fauna in Ticino

Contesto

In brief - The GEV Insubria Olona protect the environment in Ticino - They educate people to respect nature - They carry out scientific dissemination activities ## Key facts - What: Environmental protection and scientific dissemination - When: Daily activities - Where: Territory under the jurisdiction of the GEV Insubria Olona - Who: Insubria Olona Volunteer Ecological Guards - Amount: 12 active volunteers The Volunteer Ecological Guards (GEV) Insubria Olona are a group of twelve volunteers passionate about nature and the environment, who every day commit to protecting the territory and educating new generations to respect the environment. You don't need to be a hero to contribute to environmental protection; small choices in our daily lives can transform our little piece of garden into a place that does so much good for nature. This was explained by Giorgio Trespioli, coordinator of the GEV Insubria Olona, interviewed by Santina Buscemi in 'La Materia del Giorno'. When on duty, the GEV are public officials in every respect. To go on duty, the process is well defined: registration is not enough, but you must follow a course and pass an exam to be prepared in the various areas of competence. Once you have obtained the badge, the Guards move around the vast territory under their responsibility, protecting flora and fauna and also dealing with scientific dissemination. ### GEV Activities The GEV Insubria Olona carry out a wide range of activities, including the surveillance of rivers, parks and local fauna, the reporting of environmental damage and scientific dissemination in collaboration with the municipal administrations and schools. Dettagli operativi

Implications for Cross-Border Workers The activities of GEV Insubria Olona not only protect the environment but also offer educational and volunteering opportunities for cross-border workers living and working in Ticino. Participating in these initiatives can be a way to better integrate into the local community and actively contribute to the protection of the territory. ### Outreach Activities GEV organizes daytime and nighttime excursions that often sell out. These activities are loved by participants and offer the opportunity to learn how joyful and respectful observation of the animals living in our territory can be. The excursions are a way to get closer to nature and understand the importance of environmental protection. ### Volunteering Opportunities For those interested in joining GEV, there are two options: becoming an Ecological Volunteer Guard or joining as a Civic Volunteer. Both options require a commitment and a passion for the environment but offer the possibility to actively contribute to the protection of the territory and scientific outreach. ### Education of New Generations GEV also works with schools to educate new generations about respect for the environment. They organize hands-on activities such as building bug hotels for insects, which are not only fun for children but also teach the importance of biodiversity and ecosystem protection. ### Collaboration with Municipal Administrations GEV collaborates with municipal administrations to organize events and scientific outreach activities. This collaboration is fundamental to reaching a wider audience and raising awareness in the community about the importance of environmental protection. ### Protection of Flora and Fauna GEV monitors and protects local flora and fauna. How to Join the GEV If you're interested in joining GEV Insubria Olona, there are two options: becoming an Ecological Volunteer Guard or joining as a Civic Volunteer. To become an Ecological Volunteer Guard, you need to attend a course and pass an exam. This process ensures that new volunteers are prepared in the various relevant areas. ### Becoming an Ecological Volunteer Guard 1. Registration: Contact Parco Pineta for information about the training course. 2. Training Course: Attend the training course to become an Ecological Volunteer Guard. 3. Exam: Pass the final exam to obtain the Ecological Volunteer Guard badge. 4. Activities: Participate in environmental protection and scientific dissemination activities. ### Becoming a Civic Volunteer 1. Contact: Contact GEV Insubria Olona for information on how to become a Civic Volunteer. 2. Commitment: Dedicate a few hours a week to participate in volunteer activities. 3. Activities: Participate in excursions, educational activities, and other initiatives organized by the GEV. ### Useful Contacts For more information, you can visit the GEV Insubria Olona Instagram page or contact the group directly. The GEV are always looking for new volunteers and collaborators to expand their activities and reach a wider audience. ### Conclusion GEV Insubria Olona is an example of how small daily actions can make a difference in environmental protection. Participating in their activities not only helps to protect the territory but also offers the opportunity to learn and grow together with the community. If you're interested in joining them, don't hesitate to contact them and discover how you can contribute to environmental protection in Ticino. Frequently Asked Questions
What do the Insubria Olona Voluntary Ecological Guards do?
The Insubria Olona Voluntary Ecological Guards (GEV) are responsible for protecting the territory, monitoring rivers, parks, and local wildlife, reporting environmental damage, and promoting scientific education in collaboration with local municipalities and schools.
How can I join the Insubria Olona Voluntary Ecological Guards?
To join the Insubria Olona Voluntary Ecological Guards, you need to complete a training course and pass an exam to become a Voluntary Ecological Guard. Alternatively, you can join as a Civic Volunteer and participate in volunteer activities.
What activities are organized by the Insubria Olona Voluntary Ecological Guards?
The Insubria Olona Voluntary Ecological Guards organize daytime and nighttime excursions, educational activities in schools, scientific dissemination events, and hands-on activities such as building bug hotels for insects.
