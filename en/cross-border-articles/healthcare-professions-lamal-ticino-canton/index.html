The Canton of Ticino has recently made important decisions regarding access to mandatory health insurance (LaMal) for healthcare professionals working in the territory. This decision has a significant impact on cross-border workers who reside in Italy and work in the healthcare sector in Switzerland. The new rules aim to clarify the requirements and procedures for accessing LaMal, thus ensuring greater clarity and security for transborder workers. ### Implications for Cross-Border Workers The new provisions of the Canton of Ticino mainly concern healthcare professionals working in Ticino but residing in Italy. These workers will now have to follow specific procedures to access LaMal, which may include the submission of additional documents or proof of a real link with the Swiss territory. The cantonal authorities have emphasized the importance of these measures to ensure a fair and sustainable healthcare system. ### Operational Checklist 1. Procedures and Requirements The new rules for cross-border healthcare professionals' access to LaMal, which came into effect on January 1, 2023, include a series of specific requirements. Among these, demonstrating a genuine link to Swiss territory, which can be proven through tax residence, withholding tax payments, or participation in Swiss social assistance programs. Additionally, workers will need to present additional documents attesting to their professional activity in Ticino, such as employment contracts, certificates of employment, and income tax returns. 📊 Operational Checklist: - Tax residence in Switzerland (e.g., certificate of domicile) - Withholding tax payments (e.g., certificate from the AFC) - Participation in Swiss social assistance programs (e.g., accident insurance) - Employment contract in Ticino - Certificate of employment - Swiss income tax return ### Comparison with the Previous Situation Before these new provisions, cross-border healthcare professionals could access LaMal through less stringent procedures. For example, a simple declaration of residence in a border municipality such as Mendrisio or Chiasso was sufficient. The new rules represent a significant change, especially for those who do not have a direct link to Swiss territory.
