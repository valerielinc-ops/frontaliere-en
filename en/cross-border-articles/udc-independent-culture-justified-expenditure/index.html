UDC interpellation on cantonal funding for independent cultural spaces. Doubts about effectiveness and financial sustainability

Contesto

In brief - UDC questions State Council on independent culture spending - Grant for co-financing cultural spaces - Doubts about effectiveness and financial sustainability ## Key facts - What: UDC interpellation on independent culture funding - When: May 4, 2026 - Where: Bellinzona, Canton Ticino - Who: UDC, State Council, DECS - Amount: Up to 30,000 francs per year per project A new intervention in support of independent culture comes under the scrutiny of cantonal politics. With an interpellation, the UDC group, led by Lara Filippini, raises a series of questions about the sustainability and opportunity of projects promoted by the Department of Education, Culture, and Sport (DECS). At the center of the discussion is the proposal, communicated to the municipalities of Ticino on April 23, 2026, to introduce a competition grant to encourage the provision of spaces dedicated to independent culture. The model provides for cantonal co-financing equal to 50% of the rental costs borne by the municipalities, up to a maximum of 30,000 francs per year per project. The initiative is part of the Cantonal Cultural Policy Guidelines 2024-2027 and is based on a study by the Cultural Observatory of the Canton of Ticino, which highlights a growing demand for accessible and shared spaces among operators in the sector. However, according to the signatories of the interpellation, the current financial context requires a more cautious reflection. The Canton is indeed facing a structural deficit and the need to contain public spending. ### Doubts about effectiveness and sustainability In this scenario, the deputies ask whether it is justified to allocate resources - albeit limited for each project - to initiatives whose effectiveness is not yet clearly demonstrated. Dettagli operativi

Implications for cross-border workers and cultural operators The UDC's interpellation raises relevant issues for cross-border workers operating in the cultural sector in Ticino. The possibility of accessing co-financed cultural spaces could offer new opportunities for artists and cultural operators, but the financial sustainability of the project is a critical point. Cross-border workers in the cultural sector could benefit from more accessible and shared spaces, but it is essential that the criteria for accessing funds are clear and transparent. ### Comparison with the previous situation Before the introduction of this call, the municipalities of Ticino did not have access to cantonal co-financing for the provision of cultural spaces. The new initiative represents a significant step forward, but it is essential that the Government demonstrates the effectiveness of the project to justify the investment. Cross-border workers operating in the cultural sector should closely monitor the development of this initiative to understand how they might benefit from the new spaces and available funds. ### Future scenarios If the project is approved and successfully implemented, cross-border workers could have access to more modern and functional cultural spaces, fostering the growth of the sector. However, if doubts about effectiveness and financial sustainability prevail, the project could be revised or even canceled. It is therefore crucial that the Government provides detailed clarifications and demonstrates that the investment is justified. ## Recommended tools For an updated estimate, use the net salary calculator and the CHF-EUR exchange comparator.

Frequently Asked Questions
What is the objective of the competition for independent culture?
The aim of the competition is to encourage the provision of spaces dedicated to independent culture, with a cantonal co-financing of 50% of the rental costs borne by the municipalities, up to a maximum of 30,000 francs per year per project.
What are the main doubts raised by the UDC?
The main doubts raised by the UDC concern the effectiveness and financial sustainability of the project, the risk of dispersing funds in fragmented and limited-utility interventions, and the need for rigorous verification criteria to avoid inefficiencies.
What can interested cross-border workers do?
Interested cross-border workers can monitor official communications, participate in public consultations, assess financial sustainability, and consult industry associations for support and additional information.
