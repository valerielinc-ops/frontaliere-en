Ticino speed checks: radar locations 4-10 May

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 1 May 2026

The cantonal police and local police announce the locations of mobile and semi-stationary speed checks.

Contesto

In brief - Speed checks from May 4 to 10 - Specific locations across the Canton of Ticino - Goal: preventing road accidents ## Key facts - What: Speed checks - When: From May 4 to 10, 2024 - Where: Various districts in the Canton of Ticino - Who: Cantonal police and municipal police - Amount: Not specified The cantonal police and municipal police of the Canton of Ticino have announced the locations where mobile and semi-stationary speed checks will be carried out from May 4 to 10, 2024. The main objective of these checks is to prevent road accidents, a topic of particular relevance for cross-border workers who daily cross the Italian-Swiss border to go to work. ### Mobile speed checks Mobile speed checks will be carried out in various locations across the different districts of the Canton of Ticino. Here are the specific locations: Bellinzona District - Bellinzona - Giubiasco - Cadenazzo - Sant’Antonino - Sementina - Gnosca - Monte Carasso Locarno District - Orselina - Locarno - Muralto - Ascona - Golino - Losone Vallemaggia District - Avegno Lugano District - Lugano - Massagno - Origlio - Viganello - Davesco-Soragno - Sonvico - Montagnola - Grancia - Paradiso - Maroggia - Cassarate - Breganzona - Manno - Gravesano - Agno Mendrisio District - Balerna - Stabio - San Pietro - Besazio ### Semi-stationary speed checks Semi-stationary speed checks will be carried out in two specific locations: - Sant’Antonino - Magadino These checks are an integral part of the road safety prevention activities carried out by the Ticino authorities. Cross-border workers who daily use the roads of the Canton of Ticino are invited to respect the speed limits to avoid fines and, above all, to ensure their own safety and that of other road users.

What to do in case of a check In case of a speed check, cross-border workers must follow specific procedures to avoid problems and ensure a quick resolution of the situation: 1. Stop immediately: If you are signaled by a police car or a radar, it is important to stop immediately and safely. Do not attempt to flee or avoid the check, as this could result in more severe penalties. 2. Show your documents: At the time of the check, you must show your driver's license, the vehicle registration document, and, if requested, your G permit. Make sure all documents are valid and up-to-date. 3. Follow the police instructions: Follow the instructions of the police officers and respond in a polite and cooperative manner. Avoid discussions or aggressive behavior, as this could worsen the situation. 4. Check the fine: In case of speed limit violation, you may receive a fine. Carefully check the fine and, if necessary, ask the police officers for clarification. It is important to pay the fine within the indicated terms to avoid additional penalties. 5. Contact your insurance: If the vehicle is involved in an accident during the check, it is important to contact your insurance immediately to report the accident and get assistance. The insurance can provide legal and financial support in case of disputes. ### Useful tools To plan your trips best and avoid speed checks, cross-border workers can use various tools and resources: - Navigation apps: Use updated navigation apps like Google Maps or Waze to receive real-time information about road checks and plan alternative routes. - Official websites: Consult the websites of the Ticino authorities to get updated information about speed checks and local regulations. - Route calculator: Use the route calculator to estimate travel times...
