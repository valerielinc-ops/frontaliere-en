LUVE secures 100m deal with global hyperscaler

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 30 April 2026

The Varese-based company LUVE wins a multi-year contract to cool next-generation data centers.

Contesto

In brief - LUVE signs over 100 million deal with global hyperscaler - Supply of high-efficiency data center solutions - Focus on artificial intelligence and sustainability ## Key facts - What: Multi-year framework agreement - When: Not specified - Where: Uboldo, Italy - Who: LUVE and global hyperscaler - Amount: Over 100 million euros in the first two years LUVE, the Uboldo-based group specializing in industrial cooling systems, has signed a multi-year framework agreement with a leading global hyperscaler. The potential value of the agreement exceeds 100 million euros in the first two years alone, depending on project execution and customer requests. Hyperscalers, such as Amazon Web Services, Microsoft Azure, and Google Cloud, manage thousands of servers distributed across dozens of countries. The demand for cooling systems is growing exponentially, driven by the explosion of artificial intelligence, which requires increasingly powerful hardware and thus more energy-intensive and hot. The supply concerns high-efficiency solutions designed for data centers characterized by high computational density. 'This agreement is part of our strategy towards medium- and long-term goals and confirms the solidity of our positioning in the sector of critical cooling applications, as well as our ability to support customers in the transition towards more sustainable and energy-efficient data center infrastructures,' said Matteo Liberali, president and CEO of LUVE. ### Market implications The agreement represents a significant step for LUVE in the digital gold rush, a rapidly expanding sector.
