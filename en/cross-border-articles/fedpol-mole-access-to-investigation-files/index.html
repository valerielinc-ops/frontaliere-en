In brief - Fedpol officer arrested for selling information to a criminal organization - Access to investigation files, including international ones - Immediate measures taken by Fedpol to reduce the risk of leaks ## Key facts - What: Arrest of a Fedpol officer for selling confidential information - When: April 28, 2026 - Where: Operation in three cantons (Basel-Landschaft, Basel-Stadt, and Aargau) with judicial assistance from France and Germany - Who: Fedpol officer, aged between 40 and 50 - Amount: Not specified - Details: The officer had access to investigation information, including international ones The Fedpol officer arrested on April 28 on charges of acting as a mole for a criminal organization had access to investigation information, including international ones. This was revealed by SRF, along with other details about his functions within the Federal Office of Police. It was previously known that he was the man employed at the Federal Security Service, which deals with the protection of sensitive properties and high-ranking officials of the Confederation, and that he would have passed on information - for a fee - to a criminal organization active in drug trafficking. It is now known that the man arrested 10 days ago had previously worked, until a couple of years ago, always for Fedpol at the International Police Cooperation, a coordination center that manages information exchanges and investigations. It is not known in which position, whether the past one or the current one, he committed the crimes of which he is accused and for which the presumption of innocence applies.
