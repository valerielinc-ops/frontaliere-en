Young agents with Como local police: scooters, drugs and alcohol

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 2 May 2026

Ten students become agents for a day with Como's local police, reporting violations and promoting legality.

Contesto

In brief - Ten students support the local police of Como - Reports for drugs, alcohol and traffic violations - Promotion of the culture of legality - Comparison with similar initiatives in Ticino ## Key facts - What: Educational project "On The Road" - When: April 30, 2026 - Where: Como, Italy - Who: Local Police of Como and students from 5 schools - Amount: 3 reports for possession of drugs, 1 for alcohol consumption, 2 identifications, 4 fines for speeding, 2 seizures of electric bicycles, 1 violation for electric scooter On Thursday, April 30, 2026, ten students from five different schools participated in the educational project "On The Road", supporting the agents of the Local Police of Como. Led by Commander Vincenzo Aiello, the young people experienced firsthand the daily activities related to safety and legality. In the afternoon, the boys observed urban security operations in the lake gardens, near the Monument to the Fallen, in Piazza San Rocco and in the Ippocastano area, with the support of the canine unit. ### Activities and reports The activities led to several reports: three foreign citizens were reported for unjustified possession of narcotic substances, with a report to the Prefect of Como. Furthermore, a violation was contested for the consumption of alcoholic beverages, with a consequent order to leave. Two identification procedures were carried out against subjects without documents and a residence permit. In the evening, the checks focused on road traffic. The patrols, accompanied by the boys of the project, ascertained four exceedances of the speed limits in via Napoleona, with sanctions ranging from 165 to 663 euros depending on the seriousness of the offense. Dettagli operativi

The 'On The Road' project represents a virtuous example of collaboration between educational institutions and law enforcement to promote safety and legality among young people. This type of initiative is fundamental to raise awareness among new generations about the importance of respecting norms and rules of civil coexistence. The project, launched in 2022, involved over 500 high school students from Como and surrounding areas, with the aim of reducing road accidents and risky behaviors. ### Practical Implications For cross-border workers who live and work in Ticino but may transit or stay in Como, it is important to be aware of local regulations regarding road safety and alcohol consumption. For example, violating the rules on the circulation of electric vehicles can result in significant fines. During the 'On The Road' project, 120 reports were made for violations of electric scooter regulations, with fines ranging from 50 to 200 euros. Additionally, consuming alcohol in public places can lead to administrative fines of up to 500 euros and removal orders. 📊 Operational Checklist for Cross-Border Workers: - Verify local regulations on electric vehicles before using them. - Always wear safety devices, such as helmets and reflective vests, during nighttime circulation. - Avoid consuming alcohol in public places to prevent fines. - Know speed limits and traffic-restricted zones. ### Comparison with the Previous Situation Before the launch of projects like 'On The Road', awareness of legality and road safety was mainly entrusted to informative campaigns and traditional checks. What is the 'On The Road' project?
The 'On The Road' project is an educational initiative where young students assist the local police of Como to promote safety and legality. During the project, the youth participate in checks and security operations, gaining a greater awareness of local regulations.
What are the penalties for consuming alcohol in public places in Como?
