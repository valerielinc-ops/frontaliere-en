Contesto

In brief - Four players leave FC Lugano - Contracts expire on June 30, 2026 - Belhadj Mahmoud won the Swiss Cup ## Key facts - What: End of employment relationship - When: June 30, 2026 - Where: Lugano, Canton Ticino - Who: FC Lugano - Amount: Not specified FC Lugano has announced the end of the employment relationship with Mohamed Belhadj Mahmoud, Hicham Mahou, Zachary Brault-Guillard, and Serif Berbic. The contracts of these four players expire on June 30, 2026, and will not be renewed. Mohamed Belhadj Mahmoud, who arrived in Lugano on August 25, 2021, leaves the club after five seasons, during which he contributed to winning the Swiss Cup. As part of the Tunisian national team, he has distinguished himself for his grit and combativeness. Hicham Mahou, a left-sided player with great explosiveness, has been with the bianconeri since July 2022, collecting 107 appearances and 6 goals. Zachary Brault-Guillard, who arrived from CF Montréal in July 2024, has totaled 47 appearances in two seasons. Serif Berbic, a goalkeeper who joined Cornaredo in August 2022, has mainly played with the bianconeri Under 21 and had a loan spell in the Challenge League with AC Bellinzona in the 2024/25 season. The club thanks each of these players for their contribution and wishes them all the best for the future, both sportingly and personally. ### Implications for fans and cross-border workers The departure of these players represents a significant change for FC Lugano.
