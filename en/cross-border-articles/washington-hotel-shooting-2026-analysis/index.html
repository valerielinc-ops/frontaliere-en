In brief - Cole Allen, a 31-year-old Californian, accused of assaulting a federal agent and using a firearm - Allen intended to target administration officials, according to interim Justice Minister Todd Blanche - Controversy over the security setup at the Washington Hilton Hotel ## Key facts - What: Court appearance of Cole Allen, attacker of Donald Trump - When: Monday, April 28, 2026 - Where: Washington, USA - Who: Cole Allen, a 31-year-old Californian teacher - Amount: Not specified The 31-year-old Californian teacher Cole Allen, who on Saturday fired shots inside the Washington Hilton Hotel, just meters away from the room where Donald Trump, his vice JD Vance, and White House accredited correspondents were having dinner, is to appear before a judge for the first time on Monday. According to interim Justice Minister Todd Blanche, Allen intended to target administration officials and is currently accused of assaulting a federal agent and using a firearm to commit a violent crime. The man, who was not injured during his arrest, had sent a message to his family before taking action, a sort of manifesto against the president in which he described himself as 'a friendly federal assassin'. ### Controversy over the security setup Despite some 'veterans' of the social event claiming that security measures were stricter than in previous years, the controversy in the United States focuses precisely on the gaps in the setup, as reported by the Wall Street Journal. A setup 'designed to stop an army', and in particular the protesters outside, according to a former FBI official, but evaded by Allen simply by booking a room in the hotel and checking in the day before, after traveling by train from Los Angeles to Washington via Chicago.
