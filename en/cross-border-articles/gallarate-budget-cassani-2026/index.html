Gallarate: Cassani's 2026 budget approved, debt dispute

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 30 April 2026

The tenth budget of the Cassani era in Gallarate was approved, with 92 million in revenue and a 16 million cash surplus.

Contesto

In brief - 2026 budget approved in Gallarate with 14 favorable votes - Total revenues of approximately 92 million euros - Final cash balance exceeding 16 million ## Key facts - What: 2026 municipal budget - When: 2026 - Where: Gallarate - Who: Cassani Administration - Amount: 92 million euros in revenues - Amount: 16 million cash balance - Amount: 1.8 million administration result - Amount: 11 thousand euros available surplus - Amount: 12 million investments in the territory - Amount: 24% social spending The tenth budget of the Cassani era in Gallarate was approved with 14 favorable votes and 7 against. The report, presented by the Budget Councillor Corrado Canziani, highlighted total revenues of approximately 92 million euros and a final cash balance exceeding 16 million. The administration result stands at 1.8 million, but the effectively available surplus reduces to approximately 11 thousand euros after the provisions required by law. The majority, composed of Lega, Fratelli d'Italia, Lista Civica Cassani, and Centro Popolare, defended the report, describing it as in continuity with previous years and coherent with the administrative program. Among the points claimed, the weight of social spending, which exceeds 24% including the local plan, and investments in the territory, amounting to over 12 million euros. It was also emphasized the respect of legal parameters regarding personnel and indebtedness. Mayor Andrea Cassani claimed the administration's ability to intercept external resources: 'We have brought approximately 50 million euros in funds from tenders to Gallarate, certainly requiring co-participation. Dettagli operativi

• ### Implications for citizens and services The increase in debt is a significant concern for the opposition, which fears a negative impact on municipal services. According to the minorities, the increase in debt could lead to a reduction in current spending, compromising the quality of services offered to citizens. In particular, the lack of transparency regarding the costs of the swimming pool has raised criticism, with the opposition calling for greater clarity on how such expenses have been managed and financed. - ### Comparison with previous years The 2026 budget represents a point of continuity with previous years, as emphasized by the majority. However, the increase in debt has raised concerns among opposition councilors, who see this choice as a risk to the financial stability of the municipality. The majority, on the other hand, defends the choices made, highlighting investments in energy efficiency and infrastructure that, according to them, will bring long-term benefits. - ### Future scenarios While the majority sees the 2026 budget as an opportunity to improve the city's infrastructure and services, the opposition fears that the increase in debt could lead to an unsustainable financial situation. The administration's ability to intercept external resources, such as the 50 million euros from grants, represents a strength, but also a challenge, as such funds require financial participation from the municipality. - ### Detailed analysis of investments Investments in the territory, amounting to over 12 million euros, include energy efficiency and public infrastructure projects.
