Practical Analysis The wage gap between northern and southern European countries is a well-known phenomenon, but recent data confirms that the situation has not improved. Switzerland, Austria, and Germany continue to offer the highest salaries to recent graduates, while Italy struggles to compete. ### Comparison with Switzerland In Switzerland, recent graduates can expect salaries that are almost double those offered in Italy. This gap is even more evident when considering the cost of living, which is higher in Switzerland but offset by higher salaries. For cross-border workers employed in Switzerland, it's important to also consider withholding taxes and pension contributions. The Swiss tax system can be complex, but with good planning, it's possible to optimize your tax and pension situation. ### Comparison with Austria and Germany Austria and Germany offer salaries that are slightly lower than those in Switzerland but still significantly higher than in Italy. These countries represent an interesting alternative for young Italians seeking job opportunities abroad. However, in these countries as well, it's important to consider taxes and pension contributions. For example, in Austria, the pension system is similar to Switzerland's, with mandatory contributions to the AHV and the BVG. ### Comparison with Poland and Spain Poland and Spain offer salaries that are closer to those in Italy but still lower than in Switzerland, Austria, and Germany.
