Luino: Artoni seeks abolition of health tax for cross-border workers (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 13 May 2026

Mayoral candidate Furio Artoni denounces double taxation on cross-border workers and calls for the abolition of the health tax.

According to Artoni, this tax is the result of a policy that thinks in terms of abstract national categories and does not take into account the real needs of border territories. Switzerland, through the president of the Ticino State Council Claudio Zali, has declared that relations with Italy are at an all-time low and that additional levies on cross-border workers must be compensated by deducting them from the returns. This means less money for border towns like Luino, with direct consequences on services and infrastructure. The health tax, described as double taxation, is calculated on the cross-border work income already taxed at source in Switzerland. It is not parameterized to individual services actually provided, but has a general character. Dettagli operativi

The health tax for cross-border workers is a complex issue that requires an in-depth analysis of the tax implications and possible solutions. Double taxation represents a significant burden for cross-border workers, who already face challenges related to commuting and managing finances in two different countries. ### Comparison with the previous situation Before the introduction of the health tax, cross-border workers benefited from a simpler and more transparent tax system. The health tax has further complicated the situation, making an additional payment necessary that is not directly related to the healthcare services received. This has led to widespread discontent among cross-border workers and border communities. ### Future scenarios If the health tax is not abolished, cross-border workers will face an additional tax burden, which could negatively impact their quality of life and their ability to save. The threat of blocking the returns by the Canton of Ticino could also lead to a reduction in municipal services, further worsening the situation. On the other hand, the abolition of the health tax could lead to a significant improvement in the economic conditions of cross-border workers and border communities. However, it is essential that the Italian and Swiss authorities find a fair and sustainable solution to avoid further tensions and conflicts. ### Implications for border municipalities Border municipalities like Luino significantly depend on the returns to finance municipal services. Frequently Asked Questions
What is the health tax for cross-border workers?
The health tax for cross-border workers is a levy calculated on the income from cross-border work already taxed at source in Switzerland. It is not based on individual services actually provided, but has a general character, typical of tax levies.
What are the implications of the health tax for cross-border workers?
The health tax represents a double taxation for cross-border workers, who already face challenges related to commuting and managing finances in two different countries. It can have a significant impact on the net income and quality of life of cross-border workers.
