Massive Atlantic raid: 30 tons of cocaine seized (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 8 May 2026

The Spanish Guardia Civil intercepted 30.2 tons of cocaine aboard the Arconian merchant ship, the largest seizure in Spain in a single operation.

In brief - Major raid in the Atlantic: 30 tons of cocaine seized - International operation against drug trafficking - 23 people arrested ## Key facts - What: Seizure of 30.2 tons of cocaine - When: May 1, 2026 - Where: Off the coast of Western Sahara - Who: Spanish Civil Guard - Amount: 812 million euros The Spanish Civil Guard carried out the largest drug seizure in a single operation, intercepting 30.2 tons of cocaine aboard the merchant vessel Arconian, flying the flag of the Comoros. The operation, coordinated by Judge Ismael Moreno of the Audiencia Nacional, led to the arrest of 23 people. The cargo, consisting of 1,279 packages of cocaine, was valued at over 812 million euros. The cargo ship was intercepted on May 1 off the coast of Western Sahara while sailing from Sierra Leone to Libya. During the boarding, agents arrested 17 crew members and six other people hidden on the ship, armed with three rifles, a pistol, and ammunition. The drugs were hidden in a long metal cavity inside the vessel. The operation was conducted with the support of the U.S. DEA and the intelligence unit of the Dutch police. ### Impact on cross-border workers The drug seizure operation in the Atlantic, although not directly linked to the Canton of Ticino, has significant implications for the security and stability of the region.
