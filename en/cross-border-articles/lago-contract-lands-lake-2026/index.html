Lago Contract: Lands Lake invites mayoral candidates to a discussion

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 27 April 2026

The Lands Lake association is organizing public meetings with the mayoral candidates of Baveno, Stresa, Luino, and Laveno Mombello to discuss the future of Lake Maggiore as a unified system.

These meetings, scheduled for the first decade of May 2026, aim to discuss the future of Lake Maggiore as a unified system. The association, founded in 2025 to celebrate the bicentennial of scheduled navigation on the lake, has developed a programmatic document addressing crucial issues such as integrated mobility, local economy, and tourist flow management. The four selected municipalities were not chosen randomly. Baveno and Stresa are key hubs for the international image of Verbano and the hospitality economy. Luino and Laveno Mombello, on the other hand, can play an increasing role in reorganizing lake connections and services. The goal is to create an area governance capable of treating the lake as territorial infrastructure, not just as a postcard....

Analysis of Implications The meetings organized by Lands Lake represent an important step towards more integrated and sustainable management of Lake Maggiore. Integrated mobility is one of the central themes of the Lake Contract, and improvements in this sector could have a significant impact on cross-border workers who cross the border every day to work in Ticino. ### Integrated Mobility Integrated mobility, which includes navigation, railway, cycling, and common ticketing, is fundamental to improving the quality of life for residents and cross-border workers. A more efficient transport system could reduce travel times and improve accessibility to the various locations around the lake. This is particularly important for cross-border workers who need to commute daily between their workplace and residence. ### Local Economy The local economy is another crucial aspect of the Lake Contract. Promoting short supply chains, covered markets, and activities related to fishing could create new job opportunities and improve the economic sustainability of the region. For cross-border workers, this could mean an increase in job opportunities and greater economic stability. ### Management of Tourist Flows Managing tourist flows is another important topic. A shared approach among the lakeside municipalities could improve the distribution of tourists and reduce the environmental impact. This could lead to a more livable environment for residents and cross-border workers, with less congestion and a better balance between tourism and daily life. ### Shared Governance Shared governance is a key element of the Lake Contract. Frequently Asked Questions
What are the main themes of the Lake Contract?
The main themes of the Lake Contract include integrated mobility, local economy, tourist flow management, and shared governance among the lakeside municipalities. These themes aim to create a unified system for the management of Lake Maggiore.
How can I participate in the public meetings organized by Lands Lake?
Public meetings will be broadcast live on the Lands Lake association's YouTube channel. You can actively participate through questions and comments, following the instructions provided by the association to stay updated on developments.
What are the implications for cross-border workers in Ticino?
Implications for cross-border workers include improvements in integrated mobility, new job opportunities, and a better quality of life. Shared governance of the lake could facilitate daily transportation and improve accessibility to the various locations around the lake.
