Chiasso 2025: reduced deficit and exceptional surplus (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 6 May 2026

Chiasso's 2025 accounts show a reduced ordinary deficit of 2.8 million and an exceptional surplus of 23.1 million thanks to revaluations

Contesto

In brief - Ordinary deficit reduced to 2.8 million - Exceptional surplus of 23.1 million - Revaluations for 25.9 million ## Key facts - What: 2025 Financial Statements of Chiasso - When: 2025 - Where: Chiasso, Canton Ticino - Who: Municipality of Chiasso - Amount: Ordinary deficit 2.8 million, exceptional surplus 23.1 million The year 2025 was less gloomy than expected for Chiasso. The border town managed to contain the ordinary management deficit to around 2.8 million francs – an improvement compared to the Budget. But two revaluations led to an 'exceptional' surplus. Chiasso's financial statements show a deficit for 2025 in ordinary management. The red figure was reduced to around 2.8 million francs compared to the 5.8 million budgeted. A result achieved thanks to higher revenues. According to Finance Department Head Luca Bacciarini, there has been an increase in legal entities and withholding taxes, which 'demonstrates a strong attractiveness of Chiasso as a place to do business'. But there were also lower expenditures. In particular, savings were made on personnel amounting to 400,000 francs, but - explains Bacciarini - 'without laying off staff'. ### Extraordinary revaluations The municipal authorities then highlighted two extraordinary revaluations: the Municipality's participation in AGE and the 'La Perfetta' property, whose book values were increased by a total of 25.9 million. An operation that brought the total annual result into positive territory with an exceptional surplus of 23.1 million. But this exercise had no impact on liquidity and debt reduction.
