Victims' lawyers sue the Vaud Cantonese Organization for insufficient intervention equipment after the Le Constellation bar fire

Contesto

In brief - The Vaud cantonal organization reported for shortcomings in rescue operations - Stretchers, thermal blankets, and oxygen tanks were missing - A parallel investigation opened by the Vaud public ministry ## Key facts - What: Complaint for shortcomings in rescue operations - When: May 1, 2026 - Where: Crans-Montana, Canton Valais - Who: Lawyers for the victims, Vaud public ministry - Amount: 41 victims and 115 injured The fire at the Le Constellation bar in Crans-Montana (VS) on New Year's Eve has opened a new line of investigation. This time, the Vaud public ministry has targeted the Vaud cantonal rescue organization due to alleged gaps in the intervention system. The complaint was filed by some lawyers for the victims, explained the prosecution to Keystone-ATS today, confirming an advance by Swiss and Italian media, including the Telegiornale of RSI on Wednesday. Interviewed yesterday by the RTS public service broadcaster, Italian lawyer Fabrizio Ventimiglia argued that the care of the injured in the immediate aftermath of the fire was insufficient. 'There were no stretchers, but above all no thermal blankets and oxygen tanks. The latter are fundamental to allow the injured to breathe immediately,' he argued. According to RTS information, the public ministry has therefore opened a parallel investigation to the main line, in which thirteen defendants are involved. For its part, the Vaud cantonal rescue organization defends itself in a statement, in which it says it is surprised by the accusations against its intervention system.
