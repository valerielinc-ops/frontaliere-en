Reimbursable Healthcare Expenses for Cross-Border Workers (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 26 February 2026

A comprehensive guide to reimbursable healthcare expenses in Italy for cross-border workers employed in Switzerland, including regulations and procedures for 2026.

Contesto

While the Swiss and Italian healthcare systems are fundamentally different, there are specific regulations that allow cross-border workers to request reimbursement for medical expenses incurred for treatments received in Switzerland. But which expenses are actually reimbursable, and what steps need to be followed? According to the directives established by European Regulation 883/2004, cross-border workers are entitled to medical assistance both in their country of residence (Italy) and in their country of employment (Switzerland). However, it is essential that the expenses are thoroughly documented and comply with the reimbursement criteria set by the Italian National Health Service (SSN). Dettagli operativi

The process to obtain reimbursement involves several steps and requires attention to documentation. To begin, it is necessary to keep all receipts for medical expenses incurred in Switzerland, including detailed invoices and medical certificates. These documents must be submitted to the competent ASL (Local Health Authority) in Italy. Once the ASL receives the documentation, it verifies whether the service is recognized by the Italian SSN and calculates the reimbursable amount. For example, a specialist visit costing 150 CHF in Switzerland might only be reimbursed up to the maximum limit set in Italy, which for a similar visit could be around €80. 💡 To simplify the process, many cross-border workers turn to consultants who specialize in cross-border healthcare matters or use the services of specialized CAF centers. It is also possible to consult INPS and ASL information centers to clarify specific doubts. Keep in mind that reimbursement times may vary from region to region, averaging between 60 and 90 days.

Punti chiave

In addition to direct reimbursements, it is useful to know that certain healthcare expenses can be deducted from the Italian income tax return. This applies to expenses exceeding the annual threshold of €129.11. For example, if your medical expenses amount to €500, you can deduct 19% of the amount exceeding the threshold, resulting in a tax saving. ⚠️ It is essential to present the original tax documents and translated documentation in Italian, where required. Furthermore, those who have chosen LAMal insurance in Switzerland must check with their insurer to see if part of the expenses can be directly covered. Frequently Asked Questions
What is the process for obtaining reimbursement of health expenses incurred in Switzerland as a cross-border commuter residing in Italy?
Keep all receipts, present them to the competent Italian ASL, which verifies the recognizability of the service and calculates the refundable amount according to the Italian rates.
Can I deduct healthcare expenses incurred in Switzerland from my Italian tax return?
Yes, you can deduct health expenses that exceed the deductible of €129.11 per year. The tax saving will be 19% on the excess amount.
How does the reimbursement of dental treatment costs incurred in Switzerland as an Italian cross-border commuter work?
Reimbursements for dental treatment in Switzerland are only possible if the service is recognised by the Italian NHS. Detailed invoices and medical certificates must be presented to the competent ASL, which reimburses according to Italian rates. Often the difference between the Swiss cost and the Italian reimbursement remains the responsibility of the cross-border commuter.
Can I obtain reimbursement of the costs of medicines purchased in Switzerland as a cross-border commuter?
Yes, but only if the drugs are prescribed by a doctor and are included in the Essential Levels of Assistance (LEA) of the Italian NHS. Present the prescription and the receipt to the ASL for reimbursement, calculated on the Italian rates.
What documents do I need to request reimbursement of Swiss medical expenses from the Italian ASL?
Detailed receipts/invoices, medical certificates, prescription (if drugs) and documentation translated into Italian if not already in the language are required. All documents must be presented to the ASL of residence, which verifies coverage according to European and Italian standards.
