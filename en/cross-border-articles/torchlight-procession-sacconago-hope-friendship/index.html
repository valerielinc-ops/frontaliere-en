Frequently Asked Questions
When did the Sacconago Torch arrive in Madonna in Campagna in 2026?
The Sacconago Torch arrived in Madonna in Campagna on Sunday, May 3, 2026, at 10:52 pm. The event was attended by over 70 people, including children and the elderly, bearing a symbol of faith and hope.
How old is the tradition of the Sacconago Torch and who participates in it?
The tradition of the Sacconago Torch has lasted for over 70 years, having begun in 1953. Every year, about 200 people participate, from Sacconago and neighboring municipalities such as Agno, Magliaso and Caslano, uniting families and friends.
How much does the crystal torch cost and who made it?
The crystal torch, made by local craftsman Franco Mello, has an estimated value of CHF 5,000. This represents an increase over previous wooden torches, which cost around CHF 3,000, but adds a touch of elegance to the event.
How is communication and sharing managed during the Sacconago Torchlight?
A dedicated group chat allows the real-time sharing of images and videos, also allowing those who cannot physically participate to follow the event. Over 100 photos and 50 videos were shared during the evening.
What are the costs of organizing the Sacconago Torch and how are they covered?
The total expenditure for the organization of the Sacconago Torch was CHF 7,000. These costs are mainly covered by local donations and sponsors, demonstrating community support for the annual event.
