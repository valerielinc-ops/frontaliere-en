Mosque in Cantù: victory for Assalam after 12 years (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 5 May 2026

The State Council has recognized Assalam's right to build a mosque in Cantù, ordering the Municipality to pay the costs.

Contesto

In brief - The State Council has recognized Assalam's right to build a mosque in Cantù. - The Municipality of Cantù has been ordered to pay the court costs. - The decision was filed on May 5, 2026. ## Key facts - What: Recognition of the right to build a mosque. - When: May 5, 2026. - Where: Cantù, Italy. - Who: State Council, Assalam Association. - Amount: 6,000 euros in court costs. After 12 years of litigation, the State Council has finally put an end to the legal battle between the Municipality of Cantù and the Assalam Association. The decision, filed on Tuesday, May 5, 2026, recognized the validity of the association's claims, declaring null and void the municipal measures following rulings no. 3877/2025 and 3880/2025. The State Council ordered the appointment of a Commissioner ad acta within 30 days to issue the building permit. ### The State Council's decision The State Council recognized that the Municipality of Cantù's behavior was not in line with previous rulings. The Municipality had attempted to change the intended use of the building by reopening the investigation, making it difficult and costly to obtain the building permit. Ruling no. 07908/2025 established that the Municipality had evaded the compliance obligations arising from previous rulings, imposing unnecessary requirements on the Assalam Association. ### Conclusions and costs The Municipality of Cantù has been ordered to pay the court costs, amounting to 6,000 euros plus legal accessories. Lawyers Vincenzo Latorraca and Michela Luraghi emphasized that the Association's reasons for obtaining a place for collective prayer and the right to worship have been fully recognized.
