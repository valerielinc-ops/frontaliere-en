Drug arrest in Capolago: 54 grams of cocaine seized (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 7 May 2026

The Cantonal Police arrested an Albanian citizen with 54 grams of cocaine on the A2 highway. Here's what happened.

Contesto

In brief - Arrest of a 24-year-old Albanian for drugs - 54 grams of cocaine seized - Operation coordinated with local police ## Key facts - What: Aggravated violation of the Federal Narcotics Act - When: 29.04.2026 - Where: Capolago (Segoma area), A2 motorway - Who: Cantonal police, Lugano city police, Mendrisio city police, Chiasso municipal police - Amount: 54 grams of cocaine The Public Prosecutor's Office and the Cantonal Police announced the arrest of a 24-year-old Albanian citizen residing in Albania. The man was stopped on April 29, 2026, in the territory of Capolago, in the Segoma area, aboard a vehicle heading north on the A2 motorway. The operation was coordinated by the Cantonal Police with the collaboration of the anti-drug services of the Lugano City Police, the Mendrisio City Police, and the Chiasso Municipal Police. During the search of the car, carried out by the collaborators of the Federal Customs and Border Security Office (UDSC), approximately 54 grams of cocaine were found. The offenses include aggravated violation of the Federal Narcotics Act and violation of the Federal Aliens Act. ### Implications for cross-border workers The arrest in Capolago raises some important issues for cross-border workers who daily cross the border between Italy and Switzerland.
