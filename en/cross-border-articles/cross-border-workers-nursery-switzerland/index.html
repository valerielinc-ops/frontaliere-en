Nurseries in Ticino for cross-border workers: costs and subsidies (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 26 May 2026

Practical guide for cross-border workers with children: how to enroll kids in Ticino nurseries, costs, waiting times, and available subsidies.

Contesto

In brief - Nurseries in Ticino: costs and waiting lists - Available subsidies for cross-border workers - Alternatives in Italy for those working in Switzerland ## Key facts - What: Guide to nurseries for cross-border workers - Where: Canton Ticino - Who: Cross-border workers with children - Costs: Variable, subsidies available - Waiting lists: Variable times Nurseries in Ticino are a fundamental resource for cross-border workers employed in Switzerland with young children. The costs of nurseries in Ticino can vary significantly depending on the facility and the municipality, but there are subsidies and benefits available for the families of cross-border workers. Waiting lists can be long, so it is important to register in advance. ### Costs and subsidies The costs of nurseries in Ticino can range from a few hundred to over a thousand francs per month. However, cross-border workers can benefit from subsidies and financial aid that significantly reduce expenses. It is important to inquire at the municipality of residence in Switzerland and at INPS in Italy to learn about the available benefits. ### Waiting lists Waiting lists for nurseries in Ticino can be long, especially in larger cities like Lugano, Bellinzona, and Mendrisio. It is advisable to register as soon as possible to increase the chances of securing a spot. Some private nurseries may have shorter waiting lists, but the costs are generally higher.

Dettagli operativi

Alternatives in Italy For cross-border workers who are unable to find a place in a nursery in Ticino, there are alternatives in Italy. Many Italian municipalities along the border with Switzerland offer nursery services at more affordable costs. However, it's important to consider travel times and logistics, especially for those who work fixed hours. ### Cost Comparison The costs of nurseries in Italy are generally lower than those in Ticino. However, it's also important to consider the quality of service and distance from home. In some cases, it may be more cost-effective to pay slightly more for a nursery in Ticino, particularly if you work just a few kilometers from the border. ### Logistics and Timing Logistics is a crucial factor when choosing a nursery. For cross-border workers employed in Ticino, it may be more convenient to have a nursery near the workplace, even if this means dealing with longer waiting lists. Punti chiave

Enrollment Procedure To enroll their children in a nursery in Ticino, cross-border workers must follow a specific procedure. First, it is necessary to contact the Swiss municipality of residence to obtain information on available facilities and waiting lists. Subsequently, it is possible to visit the nurseries and submit the enrollment application. ### Required Documents The documents required for enrollment may vary depending on the municipality and the facility. Generally, the residence permit, employment contract, residence certificate, and the child's birth certificate are required. It is important to inquire at the municipality of residence for a complete list of the required documents. ### Deadlines and Times The deadlines for enrolling in nurseries in Ticino may vary depending on the municipality and the facility. It is advisable to inquire well in advance and submit the application as soon as possible to increase the chances of securing a place. In some cases, it may be useful to contact private nurseries as well, which may have shorter waiting lists.
