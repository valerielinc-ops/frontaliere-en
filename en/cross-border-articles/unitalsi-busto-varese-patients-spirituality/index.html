Unitalsi Busto and Varese: spiritual relief and miracles for the sick

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 2 May 2026

Discover how Unitalsi of Busto Arsizio and Varese supports the sick through pilgrimages and spiritual assistance.

Contesto

In brief - The Unitalsi was founded in 1903 from a personal experience of Gianbattista Tommasi. - Volunteers assist the sick during pilgrimages to Lourdes. - The association also manages accommodation facilities and solidarity projects. ## Key facts - What: Assistance to the sick and pilgrimages to Lourdes - When: Founded in 1903 - Where: Busto Arsizio and Varese - Who: Unitalsi volunteers - Amount: Not specified The Unitalsi, told from the Varesotto territory, is dedicated to assisting the sick and pilgrimages to Lourdes. The new episode of the podcast 'Soci all Time' gives voice to local associative realities, with Nicola Ruggero, referent of the Busto Arsizio sub-section, and Giuseppina Brusa, for Varese. Together, they retrace the history, activities, and meaning of a commitment that unites faith and solidarity. The roots of the Unitalsi date back to 1903, when Gianbattista Tommasi, a Roman nobleman affected by a severe form of deforming arthritis, left for Lourdes in search of healing. After days without improvement, Tommasi found himself in a state of deep despair. At the Grotto of Massabielle, Tommasi interpreted a divine intervention as a sign of hope. From that experience, the idea of making the pilgrimage accessible even to the most fragile was born. Pope Pius X welcomed the proposal, officially giving life to the association. The heart of the Unitalsi are the volunteers, distinguished as bearers and ladies. The bearers take care of transporting the sick, helping them with movements using stretchers or wheelchairs and in daily care. The ladies, recognizable by their white uniform, assist women, supporting them in the most delicate moments of the day. Alongside practical assistance, there is a fundamental relational dimension. Dettagli operativi

The Unitalsi of Busto Arsizio and Varese is a concrete example of how faith and solidarity can come together to support the sick. The association not only organizes pilgrimages to Lourdes but also manages accommodation facilities and solidarity projects, such as the Casa Fabrizio Frizzi in Milan. This structure offers free accommodation to families with children suffering from cancer, demonstrating Unitalsi's commitment to supporting people in need. The volunteers of Unitalsi play a fundamental role in assisting the sick. The stretcher-bearers take care of transportation and daily care, while the ladies assist women in the most delicate moments. This relational approach creates deep bonds between volunteers and the sick, making the pilgrimage experience not only spiritual but also emotionally significant. Unitalsi's commitment is not limited to pilgrimages. The sub-sections of Busto Arsizio and Varese are active in organizing summer stays and moments of aggregation, offering continuous support to pilgrims. These events not only provide a moment of leisure but also strengthen the bonds between volunteers and the sick, creating a community of support and sharing. Frequently Asked Questions
How can I become a volunteer for Unitalsi?
To become a volunteer for Unitalsi, you can contact the local sub-sections in Busto Arsizio or Varese. Volunteers are always looking for new people willing to offer their support to the sick during pilgrimages and activities organized by the association.
What are the main activities of Unitalsi?
The main activities of Unitalsi include accompanying people to religious services and processions, serving at the sanctuary pools, managing the logistics of trips and stays, and providing continuous emotional support to pilgrims. The association also manages accommodation facilities and solidarity projects, such as the Casa Fabrizio Frizzi in Milan.
What is the significance of the pilgrimage to Lourdes for the sick?
The pilgrimage to Lourdes represents a moment of spiritual relief and miracles for the sick. During the journey, volunteers offer emotional and practical support, creating deep bonds with the pilgrims. This experience unites faith and solidarity, offering a moment of hope and sharing.
