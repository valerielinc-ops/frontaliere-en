715k euros from NRRP for disability autonomy

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 30 April 2026

The NRRP-funded project for the autonomy of people with disabilities in Medio Olona includes equipped apartments and work internships.

Contesto

In brief - 715,000 euros from the PNRR for the autonomy of people with disabilities - Nine individualized paths and two equipped apartments - Five internships started and two work contracts signed ## Key facts - What: Project for the autonomy of people with disabilities - When: Presented at the end of April 2026 - Where: Medio Olona, particularly Olgiate Olona and Gorla Minore - Who: Castellanza District, Medio Olona Special Consortium Company Services to the Person and local cooperatives - Amount: 715,000 euros - Goal: Transform spaces into a step towards independence - Method: Individualized paths and work training - Results: Two work contracts already signed From assistance to autonomy: 715,000 euros from the PNRR for people with disabilities in Medio Olona. Equipped apartments, internships, and individual projects: the Castellanza District's plan to guide people towards an independent life. The project, funded with 715,000 euros from the PNRR, was presented in recent days by the Municipality of Olgiate Olona, which is part of the District. No longer just assistance, but paths built around the abilities and aspirations of each person. Each participant is followed by a multidisciplinary team that develops an individual plan, with specific objectives and continuous support over time. ### The house as a life laboratory The heart of the project is two dedicated apartments: one is already active in Gorla Minore, the second is about to start in Olgiate Olona. These are not assisted structures in the traditional sense. Dettagli operativi

Implications for cross-border workers The PNRR-funded project for the autonomy of people with disabilities in Medio Olona represents an innovative model that could have implications for cross-border workers who work in Ticino and reside in Lombardy. Promoting autonomy and workplace inclusion are values that can be extended to all communities, regardless of borders. ### Comparison with traditional services Compared to traditional assistance services, this project introduces a more modern and personalized approach. While traditional services often limit themselves to providing assistance, this project aims to build individualized paths that take into account the abilities and aspirations of each person. This approach could be adopted in other contexts, improving the quality of life for cross-border workers with disabilities. ### Work and training opportunities The project offers not only training and work opportunities but also continuous support that can be extended to cross-border workers. The possibility of obtaining a work contract after an internship is an example of how workplace inclusion can be effectively promoted. This model could be replicated in other areas, offering more job opportunities for cross-border workers. ### Support network and social inclusion The support network that includes volunteering, neighborhood networks, and local realities is a key element of the project. This model of social inclusion could be extended to cross-border workers, creating a more inclusive and supportive community. Punti chiave

What to do to participate If you are interested in participating in the project or learning more, you can contact the Castellanza District or the Medio Olona Special Consortium Company Services to Persons. It is important to follow the indicated steps and submit the necessary documentation to be evaluated for individualized paths. ### Steps for registration 1. Contact the Castellanza District or the Medio Olona Special Consortium Company Services to Persons. 2. Submit the required documentation, which includes medical certificates and other relevant documents. 3. Participate in an initial assessment to determine the most suitable individualized path. 4. Start the training and continuous support path. 5. Participate in internships and, if possible, obtain a work contract. ### Useful tools For further information and support, you can use the salary calculator to compare job opportunities in Ticino and Lombardy. Additionally, the permit comparator can help you better understand the requirements for working and living in both regions. ### Conclusion The PNRR-funded project for the autonomy of people with disabilities in Medio Olona represents an important step towards inclusion and autonomy. This model could be extended to cross-border workers, offering more job and social inclusion opportunities. Frequently Asked Questions
Who can participate in the project?
The project is open to all people with disabilities residing in the Medio Olona area. It is necessary to contact the Castellanza District or the Medio Olona Special Consortium Company Services to the Person for further information and to submit the required documentation.
What are the requirements to obtain a work contract?
To obtain a work contract, it is necessary to complete an internship and demonstrate your skills through a training and continuous support path. The project offers a reference tutor and continuous support even after the start of the internship.
How can I support the project?
You can support the project by participating as a volunteer, joining neighborhood networks, or collaborating with local realities in the area. The active participation of the community is fundamental to ensure that people with disabilities can live autonomously and integrated.
