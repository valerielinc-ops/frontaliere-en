Troubled asylum seekers project frozen in Pasture

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 30 April 2026

The project for a section of problematic asylum seekers in Pasture has been frozen. The Confederation aims to create special centers.

Contesto

In brief - Project for problematic asylum seekers in Pasture frozen - SEM and Canton agree on special centers - Round table with mayors and federal authorities - Involvement of 12 Ticino municipalities - Federal investments for special centers estimated at 50 million francs ## Key facts - What: Freezing of the project for a section of problematic asylum seekers - When: Confirmation arrived this morning - Where: Pasture, Canton Ticino - Who: Department of Institutions, SEM, affected municipalities - Amount: 50 million francs estimated for special centers This morning, a round table was held on security around centers for asylum seekers. The director of the Department of Institutions, Norman Gobbi, confirmed that the project for a section of problematic asylum seekers within the federal center in Pasture has been frozen. The SEM also does not intend to proceed with this solution. The central point remains the role of the Confederation, which should create special centers to manage these problematic or recalcitrant asylum seekers. According to Gobbi, this choice would avoid transferring problems both within ordinary federal centers and to the surrounding population. ### Municipal involvement At the meeting, the mayors of the affected municipalities also participated, who positively welcomed the climate of dialogue with the federal authorities. The mayor of Novazzano, Sergio Bernasconi, emphasized how the SEM has indicated that to address certain problems, one must 'be together, sit at the table and not impose decisions from above'. ### Coordination between institutions The round table on security arises from the experience of crisis meetings during the most delicate periods of migration, but today it has become more structured. Implications for cross-border workers The freezing of the project in Pasture could have significant implications for cross-border workers living in areas near asylum seeker centers, such as the municipalities of Chiasso, Novazzano, and Balerna. Currently, around 60,000 cross-border workers work in Ticino, and many of them reside in areas close to reception centers. The creation of special centers by the Confederation, as provided by the Federal Act on Foreign Nationals (LEStr) of December 16, 2005, could reduce security and integration problems that might occur in ordinary centers. 📊 Operational checklist for cross-border workers: - Check the distance between your municipality of residence and the reception centers. - Inform yourself about local security services and any protective measures. - Attend municipal assemblies to stay updated on local decisions. The separate management of problematic asylum seekers could improve the quality of life and security of residents, including cross-border workers. For example, the reception center in Chiasso, which hosts around 200 asylum seekers, has seen a 30% reduction in calls to local police after the introduction of enhanced security measures in 2022. ### Comparison with the previous situation Before this decision, there was a risk that problematic asylum seekers would be managed within ordinary centers, which could have caused tensions and security issues. According to data from the Federal Department of Justice and Police (DFGP), there were 12 serious incidents in ordinary reception centers in Ticino in 2021.
