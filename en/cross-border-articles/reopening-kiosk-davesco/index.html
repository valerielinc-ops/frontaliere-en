The Davesco-Soragno Little House Reopens: Refreshment Point in the Heart of the Cycling and Walking Path (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 23 March 2026

Starting from March 28, the Little House in Davesco-Soragno reopens its doors. Managed by the Neighborhood Committee, it will be open every weekend until mid-June, promoting community gathering and refreshment along the route.

The reopening of the Little House in Davesco-Soragno marks an important step in enhancing public spaces and strengthening strategies to promote local tourism within the municipality of Lugano. The reopening, scheduled for Saturday, March 28 at 2 p.m., will be attended by municipal authorities, including Head of Department Karin Valenzano Rossi, who emphasized how this initiative encourages social interaction, resident well-being, and neighborhood attractiveness. Located on Via Vecchio Tram, near the pedestrian and cycling bridge of Via Favadin, the kiosk serves as a refreshment stop along the route connecting Davesco and Soragno, one of the most frequented areas by cyclists and pedestrians in Lugano. It also exemplifies how local administrations are investing in sustainable infrastructure. Dettagli operativi

The reopening of the Little House in Davesco-Soragno highlights the importance of understanding the regulations and procedures adopted by the Lugano municipality and the Davesco-Soragno Neighborhood Committee. The temporary management of this kiosk, situated along one of the most frequented cycling and walking routes in the area, aligns with policies aimed at promoting sustainable tourism and valuing local natural heritage. Managed through a public tender process, it must comply with strict safety, hygiene, and environmental standards in accordance with current cantonal and federal regulations. In particular, reference is made to the Federal Decree on Environmental and Food Safety Compliance (DEMAS), which came into force in 2020, setting high standards for temporary structures and refreshment points. The permit application, submitted in June 2023, was processed by the Lugano Urban Planning Office and the Office of Economic Activities, involving local authorities and respecting procedures for granting public space use, including impact assessments and public consultations. The reopening of the Little House in Davesco-Soragno offers an important opportunity for cross-border workers and residents of Ticino, especially in Lugano, to reconnect with a conveniently accessible social space along one of the most popular routes, the cycling and walking trail connecting Davesco and Soragno. The management of temporary refreshment points like this Little House is governed by a legal framework introduced with the cantonal Law on Management of Public and Temporary Spaces (Article 5, approved in 2022 with a contribution of about 1.2 million Swiss francs), which aims to facilitate the activation of public-interest activities without placing excessive burden on public finances. This reopening can boost neighborhood vitality and support local microeconomy: according to Lugano’s data, around 150 temporary permits for food service activities were issued in 2022, generating approximately 300,000 Swiss francs in tax revenue. The Little House is expected to serve around 4,500 visitors per month, offering refreshment services and social spaces, with a projected 10% increase compared to the previous year. > To ensure effective management, it’s advisable to regularly check the dedicated Facebook page and official channels of Lugano, which are updated weekly with opening hours and any changes.
