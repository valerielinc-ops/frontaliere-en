SUVA: fatal workplace accidents in Switzerland down 80% since the '80s

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 27 April 2026

SUVA data: fatal workplace accidents reduced by 80% from 1986-1990 to 2020-2024, despite increase in employed workers. Innovation and training key.

Contesto

In brief - Fatal workplace accidents -80% from 1986-1990 - From 207 to 64 annual victims - Risk of fatal injury from 6.7 to 1.8 per 100'000 employed ## Key facts - What: Reduction in fatal workplace accidents - When: 1986-1990 vs 2020-2024 - Where: Switzerland - Who: SUVA - Amount: -80% fatal injuries Workplace safety in Switzerland has made enormous progress, with the risk of fatal injuries decreasing by nearly 80% since the 1980s thanks to constant efforts in prevention and training. This is the encouraging data published on Monday by the Swiss National Accident Insurance Fund SUVA on the occasion of World Day for Safety and Health at Work, which takes place on Tuesday, April 28, 2026. In the period 1986-1990 in Switzerland, an average of 207 people per year lost their lives at work, a figure that dropped to 64 between 2020 and 2024, despite an increase in the number of active workers. The risk of fatal injury has decreased from 6.7 to 1.8 per 100'000 full-time employees. ### Innovation and training SUVA focuses on innovation: for example, through the use of virtual reality glasses, employees experience dangerous situations in a very realistic way and learn to avoid them. Another fundamental point is the education of staff, with the transmission and implementation of rules considered vital. Employees must know the safety rules and apply them on a daily basis. ### Implications for cross-border workers The progress in workplace safety is the result of the interaction of various factors. Prevention measures provide an important contribution: the decisive factor is to prioritize safety in daily work activities and consistently implement protective measures. SUVA provides specific training programs to ensure that workers are adequately prepared. ### Application of Safety Regulations The rigorous application of safety regulations is essential to ensure a safe working environment.
