Frequently Asked Questions
How does health spending work in Italy and Europe?
Health expenditure in Italy and Europe is very high, covering almost 80% of the assistance for chronic patients. Drug spending is particularly high due to the growing population.
What is the demographic impact on health spending in Italy?
The longer-term population in Italy increases health spending, with almost 80% of the assistance for chronic patients, of which 6 out of 10 elders take at least 5 medications per day.
What are the specific rules for access to drugs for Swiss frontier workers in Italy?
Swiss frontierers in Italy can access drugs through the National Health System (SSN) if registered as resident in Italy. They must have an Italian tax code and a health card number. Some drugs may require a specific prescription or may only be covered partially, so it is advisable to check with your physician or local ASL.
How does European regulation affect the pharmaceutical industry on competitiveness in Switzerland and Italy?
The European regulation on the pharmaceutical industry aims to ensure the safety, effectiveness and quality of the drugs. These rules can influence competitiveness in Switzerland and Italy, as companies must comply with strict standards to market their products. In Italy, joining these European regulations helps maintain the competitiveness of the pharmaceutical sector, although it can increase compliance costs.
What are the tax implications for frontier workers working in Italy and living in Switzerland?
Frontiers working in Italy and living in Switzerland are generally taxed in Italy. They can benefit from a reduced tax regime, thanks to a bilateral agreement between the two countries. However, they must declare their income in Italy and may benefit from a tax credit in Switzerland to avoid double taxation. It is advisable to consult a tax advisor specializing in cross-border issues to optimize their tax situation.
