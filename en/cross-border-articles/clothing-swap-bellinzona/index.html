In Bellinzona, the exchange of clothes unites sociality and sustainability (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 6 April 2026

The initiative of the association Meteora promotes conviviality, solidarity, and environmental awareness.

Contesto

TL;DR - Clothing Exchange in Bellinzona celebrates 10 years - Event promotes sustainability and social interaction - Participants take more clothes than they bring now ## Key facts - Cosa: Scambio di abiti senza denaro - Quando: Ogni aprile e ottobre - Dove: Bellinzona, presso il municipio - Chi: Associazione Meteora - Fondazione: 2019 - Obiettivo: Ridurre sprechi e promuovere solidarietà - Partecipanti: Aumento di giovani e uomini In Bellinzona, at the municipality's court, the Meteora association's initiative has reached its tenth anniversary with the 'Clothing Exchange'. The event, held every April and October, combines social interaction and sustainability. For six hours, participants can bring clothes they no longer use and take what they need, without any exchange of money. The initiative, founded in 2019, aims to create a meeting point between different generations and promote waste reduction.

Dettagli operativi

The Clothing Exchange event in Bellinzona has seen a significant increase in the number of participants. Initially, people were more inclined to bring clothes than to take them, but today the trend has reversed. Less is brought and more is taken. This change reflects a greater awareness of excessive consumption and the environmental impact of low-quality clothing. Participation has become more mixed, with an increase in young people and men. Frequently Asked Questions
How does the 'Clothes Exchange' initiative work in Bellinzona?
The 'Clothes Exchange' initiative in Bellinzona is a free event held every April and October, where participants can bring used clothes and take what they need without exchanging money. Clothes are checked and cleaned to ensure safety and hygiene, and then distributed to participants according to their needs and preferences.
What is the environmental impact of the 'Clothes Exchange' in Bellinzona in terms of waste reduction?
The 'Clothes Exchange' in Bellinzona collected over 500 clothes and accessories in 2022, significantly reducing waste and promoting sustainability in line with the Ticino Law 2018/15.
What documents do I need to participate in the Dress Exchange in Bellinzona as an Italian cross-border commuter?
No specific document is required to participate. Just show up with clothes in good condition to exchange. However, if you are bringing more than 10 garments, it is recommended to register online in advance through the Meteora association website to avoid long waits.
Is the Bellinzona Clothes Exchange also open to those who do not live in Ticino?
Yes, the event is open to everyone, including Italian cross-border commuters and residents of other regions. There are no residence restrictions, but it is advisable to check the opening hours (usually 10:00-16:00) and the availability of free parking in the vicinity of the Bellinzona Town Hall.
How are the clothes not taken during the Clothes Exchange in Bellinzona handled?
The untraded garments are donated to local charitable organizations or sent for textile recycling, in accordance with the Ticino Law 2018/15. Only a small part, in non-reusable conditions, is disposed of as waste.
