Impact on Traffic and Transportation The weather change could have a significant impact on traffic and transportation in Ticino. Intense rains could cause delays and disruptions, especially on main roads such as the A2 highway, which connects the Canton of Ticino with the rest of Switzerland and Italy. According to data from the Department of Territory of the Canton of Ticino, in 2025 over 150 incidents related to adverse weather conditions were recorded, with a peak during the autumn months. It is important to check traffic conditions before setting off, especially for cross-border workers who commute daily between Italy and Switzerland. For cross-border workers crossing the border, it is crucial to be aware of the current regulations. For example, as of January 1, 2026, the Canton of Ticino has introduced new measures for managing traffic in case of bad weather, providing fines up to CHF 200 for those who do not comply with danger signals. Additionally, the municipalities of Lugano, Bellinzona, and Chiasso have implemented real-time monitoring systems of road conditions, accessible via dedicated apps. #### Operational Checklist for Cross-Border Workers: - Check weather forecasts through official apps like MeteoSvizzera or Ticino Traffic. - Monitor the conditions of main roads, such as the A2 and cantonal roads, through the websites of the municipalities or local authorities. - Plan alternative routes in case of closures or delays, for example, using secondary roads such as the SS340 between Lugano and Mendrisio. - Ensure you have an emergency kit in your car, including a flashlight, blankets, and first aid supplies. ### Outdoor Activities For those who love outdoor activities, the weekend was ideal. What to do in case of bad weather In case of bad weather, it is essential to take precautions to ensure your safety and that of others. Here is a practical guide: 1. Check the weather forecast before you set off. In Ticino, weather conditions can change rapidly, especially in spring. For example, after the May 1st 2026 long weekend, heavy rainfall is expected with an accumulation of about 50-70 mm in 24 hours, especially in the areas of Lugano and Bellinzona. 2. Appropriate clothing: prepare with waterproof jackets and anti-slip shoes. Temperatures can drop to 10°C, especially in mountainous areas such as Acquarossa and Blenio. 3. Plan alternative routes: in case of traffic disruptions, such as flooding or landslides, it is useful to know secondary roads. For example, the cantonal road connecting Lugano to Mendrisio might be closed due to landslides, forcing the use of the A2 as an alternative. 4. Check road conditions: in case of heavy rain, mountain roads such as those of the San Gottardo can become dangerous.
