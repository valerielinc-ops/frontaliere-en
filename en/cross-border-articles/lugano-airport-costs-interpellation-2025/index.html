Lugano Airport: rising costs, 25 questions to the Municipality (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 3 May 2026

Councillors Sara Beretta-Piccoli and Carola Barchi ask for clarifications on the costs and management of Lugano-Agno Airport

Contesto

In brief - 4 million francs in public contracts for 2025 - 25 questions to the Municipality on PSIA and federal concession - Strategic priorities: new PSIA card and concession renewal ## Key facts - What: Interpellation to the Municipality of Lugano on airport costs - When: Recently presented, concerns 2025 - Where: Lugano, Canton Ticino - Who: Municipal councilors Sara Beretta-Piccoli and Carola Barchi (PLR) - Amount: Over 4 million francs in public contracts Municipal councilors Sara Beretta-Piccoli and Carola Barchi of the PLR have presented an interpellation to the Municipality of Lugano titled 'More than the flights, the costs of the airport are taking off.' The interpellation, consisting of 25 questions, concerns the expected expenditure for 2025 of over 4 million francs in public contracts for the management of Lugano-Agno airport. These contracts are not subject to the approval of the Municipal Council, raising questions about transparency and the management of funds. ### Strategic priorities The Municipality of Lugano has reiterated that the airport remains a strategic asset for the city. Among the priorities indicated are the new PSIA card (Sectoral Plan of Aeronautical Infrastructure), the new satellite approach system, and the renewal of the federal concession, which expires in 2026. The PSIA card is a fundamental document that outlines the future development of the airport and on which many of the questions in the interpellation are focused. ### The councilors' criticisms Beretta-Piccoli and Barchi criticize the approach of the Municipality, arguing that 'a political direction must precede, not follow, the planning tools.' Dettagli operativi

Analysis of the implications The motion by the municipal councilors raises important questions about the financial and strategic management of Lugano-Agno Airport. The planned expenditure of over 4 million francs for 2025 in public contracts, which do not require the approval of the Municipal Council, is a critical point. This raises doubts about transparency and accountability in the management of public funds. ### Management of contracts The fragmentation of spending commitments into individual contracts of relatively small size could constitute a circumvention of the will expressed by the Municipal Council. This approach could limit democratic control over financial decisions, a crucial aspect for the trust of citizens and cross-border workers who use the airport. ### The role of public-private partnership Defining a public-private partnership for investments in the airport is another key issue. This type of collaboration could lead to more efficient management and a greater contribution of resources, but it requires careful planning and a clear definition of objectives. The councilors ask the Municipality to provide more details on this point, including the timelines for its implementation. ### The PSIA sheet and the federal concession The PSIA sheet is a fundamental document for the future development of the airport. Its definition is essential to outline the guidelines for investments and operations. The renewal of the federal concession, which expires in 2026, is another critical aspect. The lack of clarity on these points raises concerns about the Municipality's ability to effectively manage the airport. ### Future scenarios If the Municipality fails to provide clear and transparent answers to the questions raised, it could increase dissatisfaction among cit...

What cross-border workers should do Cross-border workers using Lugano-Agno Airport should closely monitor the developments of this interpellation. Transparency and efficient management of the airport are crucial to ensure quality services and competitive rates. ### Monitor the Municipality's communications It is important to follow the official communications of the Municipality of Lugano regarding the interpellation and the responses provided. This can help better understand the Municipality's priorities and strategies for the airport. ### Participate in public consultations Cross-border workers can participate in public consultations and meetings of the Municipal Council to express their concerns and suggestions. This can positively influence future decisions regarding the airport. ### Use the airport's services Cross-border workers should continue to use the airport's services, monitoring any changes in fares and services offered. In case of significant increases or reductions in services, it is important to make their voices heard through official channels. ### Use the cost calculator To get an idea of the costs associated with using the airport, cross-border workers can use the cost calculator available on the site. This tool can help better plan trips and manage the budget. ### Conclusion The interpellation of the municipal councilors is an opportunity to improve the transparency and management of Lugano-Agno Airport. Cross-border workers must stay informed and actively participate in the decision-making process to ensure that their needs are taken into account.
