Frequently Asked Questions
When does the A9 motorway between Chiasso and Como close and what impact does it have on cross-border commuters?
The A9 between Chiasso and Como will be closed on some nights between the end of March and the beginning of April 2026 for extraordinary maintenance. This will affect the approximately 65,000 Ticino cross-border commuters who travel to Lombardy every day, especially during the evening return time. Significant delays and the need for alternative routes are expected.
What are the exact dates and times of the closures on the A9 for cross-border commuters?
The main closures will take place on the night between March 31 and April 1, 2026, from 22:00 to 5:00. The Como Centro-Chiasso section in the direction of Switzerland and Lake Como-A59 towards Lainate will be closed. Another closure is scheduled for the night between 30 and 31 March 2026, from 21:00 to 5:00, at the entrance to Lomazzo Nord.
What are the recommended alternative routes for cross-border commuters during the closures of the A9?
Those returning to Switzerland from Milan will have to exit at Como Centro and follow the SP 233 (Via per Cernobbio), with an estimated extension of 25-40 minutes. For those going towards Lainate from Chiasso, we recommend the entrance to Lomazzo Sud. It is crucial to check for real-time updates via navigation apps.
How much longer will it take cross-border commuters to get to and from work due to the A9 closures?
Travel times will increase considerably. For example, a commuter from Mendrisio to Saronno might take 1 hour and 15 minutes instead of the usual 50. Those who work in the healthcare sector in Varese should provide a buffer of at least 30 minutes for night shifts, with detours that can add up to 40 minutes.
What can cross-border commuters do to prepare for night closures of the A9?
It is advisable to check the exact timetables on the Autostrade per l'Italia website, download the 'Ticino Traffic' app to monitor customs traffic and assess the use of the train (S11/S40 Trenord lines). It is helpful to plan your commute at least 48 hours in advance and consider teleworking if possible.
