Luino: Massarenti accuses Lombardy of healthcare data manipulation (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 6 May 2026

Marco Massarenti, candidate for 'La nostra Luino', criticizes data presented by the Lombardy Region on healthcare, highlighting disparities and structural problems.

Contesto

In brief - Massarenti denounces the trick of percentages in Lombardy's healthcare - Criticizes the management of healthcare services and access to services - Highlights the issue of accredited private sector and the SISS IT system ## Key facts - What: Criticism of public healthcare management in Lombardy - When: May 2026 - Where: Luino, Lombardy - Who: Marco Massarenti, candidate of 'La nostra Luino' - Amount: 21% increase in urgent services Marco Massarenti, candidate of the civic movement 'La nostra Luino', sent a note criticizing the data presented by the Lombardy Region regarding public healthcare. According to Massarenti, the numbers presented by the regional authorities do not reflect the reality experienced by citizens. 'Looking at the data that the regional authorities presented yesterday, it seems almost like reading the bulletin of another region. It's a numerical sleight of hand that never meets the gaze of citizens who every morning, phone in hand, try to book a visit at the hospitals in Varesotto', writes Massarenti. The candidate highlights that, although urgent services have increased by 21%, healthcare is like a blanket that's too short: 'if you pull hard on one side to cover emergencies and make a good impression in the reports, you inevitably leave the rest out in the cold'. This means that while emergencies are managed, the rest of the healthcare services suffer from this priority. Massarenti also criticizes the single CUP system, which should manage more than half of the services. According to him, this system is a hollow shell because large private groups continue to keep their schedules closed, offering spots only to those who pay out of pocket. Dettagli operativi

Massarenti's criticism of public healthcare in Lombardy raises important questions about how healthcare services are managed and accessible to citizens. In particular, the issue of accredited private providers is crucial. According to Massarenti, large private groups continue to manage their schedules in a way that favors those who can pay out of pocket, leaving out those who depend on the public system. This creates a significant disparity in access to care, especially for those who cannot afford to pay for private services. The situation is even more critical in a border area like Luino, where proximity to Switzerland offers different and often better conditions. This healthcare desertification could have a significant impact on the quality of life of residents. ### The problem of the SISS computer system The SISS computer system, used by general practitioners, is another critical point. According to Massarenti, this system is outdated and causes continuous problems for doctors, turning a simple prescription into a bureaucratic odyssey. This not only slows down the work of doctors but also has a direct impact on the health of patients, who have to face long waits and complicated procedures. The lack of an efficient and up-to-date computer system is a problem that affects many Italian regions, but in a border context like Luino, where competition with the Swiss healthcare system is strong, it becomes even more evident. The Lombardy Region must address these structural criticalities to guarantee a quality healthcare service to all citizens. ### Implications for cross-border workers For cross-border workers who work in Lombardy and reside in Switzerland, the situation of public healthcare is particularly relevant. Punti chiave

If you are a cross-border worker employed in Lombardy and residing in Switzerland, it's crucial to know your rights and the procedures to access healthcare services. Here are some concrete steps you can follow to ensure fair access to care: 1. Inform yourself about available services: Contact your municipality of residence in Switzerland to learn about the healthcare services you're entitled to and how to access them. 2. Book appointments in advance: Use the single CUP system to schedule medical visits ahead of time and make sure you have all the necessary documents. 3. Consider private options: If the waiting lists for the public system are too long, look into private options, but be sure to understand the costs and insurance coverage. 4. Use the SISS computer system: If you're a general practitioner, report any issues with the SISS system to the relevant authorities to improve service efficiency. 5. Participate in public consultations: Join public consultations and local assemblies to make your voice heard and help improve the healthcare system.
