Switzerland: 2025 Salaries Rise Up to 2% for Specialists (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 29 May 2026

According to Adecco, average salaries in Switzerland will increase by up to 2% in 2025, with higher raises for specialists in AI and ESG sustainability.

Contesto

In brief - Average salaries in Switzerland set to grow between 1.5% and 2% in 2025 - Larger increases for specialists in artificial intelligence and ESG sustainability - Companies favor targeted recruitment over broad staff expansions ## Key facts - What: average salary increase in Switzerland - When: year 2025 - Where: Switzerland, including Canton Ticino - Who: Swiss companies and specialists - Amount: increase between 1.5% and 2%; up to CHF 10,000 more per year According to the Adecco survey, based on over 15,000 hiring interviews, average salaries in Switzerland will rise between 1.5% and 2% throughout 2025, following inflation trends. This increase is also confirmed for Canton Ticino, an area heavily frequented by Italian cross-border workers. Swiss companies are adopting a targeted recruitment strategy, prioritizing the hiring of niche professional profiles rather than indiscriminately expanding staff. ### Specialist profiles and wage disparities The most significant increases concern specialist profiles, particularly those with skills related to digital transformation and sustainability. Specialists in artificial intelligence (AI) and ESG (Environmental, Social, and Governance) criteria are in higher demand and see their salaries rise markedly, even up to CHF 10,000 more per year. For standard roles, salary adjustments remain more modest and aligned with national averages. Adecco emphasizes that salary progression is increasingly linked to each professional's concrete contribution to company projects and results achieved, rather than hierarchical position. This change also affects cross-border workers with a G permit working in Ticino, who could benefit from such increases if involved in specialist roles. Dettagli operativi

The wage increase expected in Switzerland and the Canton of Ticino, ranging from 1.5% to 2% in 2025, has significant implications for Italian cross-border workers employed in the region. The situation is not uniform: while standard roles see more modest increases, specialized profiles in strategic fields such as artificial intelligence and environmental sustainability can secure substantial salary hikes, up to 10,000 francs more per year. ### Implications for cross-border workers in Ticino For a cross-border worker with a G permit, an improvement in remuneration can translate into greater purchasing power, even after accounting for withholding tax and double taxation regulated by the new 2026 agreement between Italy and Switzerland. However, to benefit from these increases, cross-border workers must maintain and develop skills that align with market demands, particularly in AI and ESG sectors. Swiss companies’ targeted recruitment prioritizes professionals with specialized profiles capable of actively contributing to corporate projects. This approach results in a wage disparity that rewards those with the most in-demand skills. Additionally, as salaries rise in line with inflation, cross-border workers must closely monitor AVS (old-age and survivors' insurance) and LPP (occupational pension plan) contributions, as well as LAMal health insurance coverage, to assess the overall impact on their net income and pension benefits. The situation at border crossings like Brogeda and Gaggiolo remains critical for cross-border workers, who daily face commute times and the challenges of cross-border employment. Punti chiave

For cross-border workers employed in Ticino, understanding and leveraging the salary increases planned for 2025 requires a strategic and well-informed approach. Here are some concrete steps to take in order to maximize the benefits from rising wages in Switzerland: ### Procedure for Cross-Border Workers 1. Professional development: investing in training is essential, especially in areas like artificial intelligence and ESG criteria, sectors that show the highest salary growth. 2. Work permit check: ensure that the G permit is up to date and compliant with regulations to avoid issues at the border and with tax authorities. 3. Payslip review: use tools such as the salary calculator to assess the impact of increases on net income, taking into account withholding tax as well as AVS and LPP contributions. 4. Tax management: stay informed about the updates in the new 2026 tax agreement between Italy and Switzerland regarding double taxation, to optimize tax returns and benefit from the expected refunds. 5. Health insurance: verify your LAMal coverage and consider any additional options, since salary increases can also affect insurance premiums. 6. Commuting time monitoring: plan daily travel considering traffic at the Brogeda and Gaggiolo border crossings, especially if there are changes in the Ticino labor market. These actions help cross-border workers concretely benefit from salary increases in Switzerland, improving their purchasing power and quality of life.
