Signed on the sidelines of the 53rd International Fleet Gathering in Romanshorn, the agreement was announced by the Swiss company Schweizerische Bodensee-Schifffahrt (SBS). This rapprochement with the German Bodensee-Schiffsbetriebe (BSB) represents a significant turning point for passengers using navigation services on the lake. As of April 26, 2026, tickets issued by the two companies are valid reciprocally. This means that passengers with a BSB ticket can board SBS ships and vice versa. Implications for cross-border workers The agreement between Swiss and German shipping companies on Lake Constance has significant implications for cross-border workers living and working in the region. The mutual validity of tickets greatly simplifies cross-border travel, eliminating the need to purchase separate tickets for each leg. This not only saves time but also money, making travel more accessible. ### Comparisons before and after the agreement Before the agreement, cross-border workers had to purchase separate tickets for each leg, which could be costly and complicated. With the introduction of mutual ticket validity, passengers can now travel freely between the two companies without having to worry about purchasing additional tickets. This represents a significant improvement in the quality of service offered. ### Practical scenarios Imagine being a cross-border worker living in Switzerland and working in Germany. Before the agreement, you had to buy a ticket for the Swiss leg and another for the German leg. With the agreement, you can buy a single ticket valid for both legs, saving time and money. Additionally, the resumption of stops in Constance from 2027 allows you to explore the German city and enjoy its attractions without having to change transportation. ### Advantages of the single day ticket The introduction of the single day ticket represents an additional advantage for cross-border workers. With a single ticket, you can travel freely around the entire Lake Constance during the day, exploring different parts of the lake and enjoying its attractions. Frequently Asked Questions
What are the main advantages of the agreement for cross-border workers?
The main advantages of the agreement for cross-border workers include the mutual validity of tickets between Swiss and German companies, the resumption of stops in Constance starting from 2027, and the introduction of a single daily ticket for the entire Lake Constance. These advantages simplify cross-border travel, saving time and money.
When will the single daily ticket come into effect?
The single daily ticket is in the definition phase and should be available by the end of May 2026. This project will be implemented in collaboration with a working group of the International Lake Constance Conference (IBK).
What does the resumption of joint management of the Romanshorn shipyard mean?
The resumption of joint management of the Romanshorn shipyard represents an important step towards closer collaboration between the two nations. This project could lead to significant improvements in infrastructure and services offered, benefiting both passengers and shipping companies.
