Dettagli operativi

From a legal and contractual standpoint, the situation opens up several scenarios for the affected workers. Although the dismissal of eight people does not technically fall under the definition of collective redundancy according to the Swiss Code of Obligations (which usually applies to at least 10 layoffs in companies of a certain size), the methods and economic reasons must be clear and transparent. Laid-off workers are entitled to a notice period, which varies based on years of service, and a detailed work certificate. ⚠️ Check Your Rights For every worker, whether a cross-border commuter or resident, it is crucial to know their rights in case of termination of employment: - Notice period: Check what is stipulated in the individual contract or, in its absence, by the Code of Obligations (Art. 335c CO). - Unemployment benefits (RAV): Cross-border workers who lose their job in Switzerland are entitled, under certain conditions, to unemployment benefits paid by INPS in Italy, calculated based on contributions paid in Switzerland (U1 form). - Work certificate: The company is obliged to provide a certificate describing the nature and duration of the employment relationship, as well as an assessment of performance and conduct. This reorganization in the Locarno area could be a precursor to other similar moves in the canton. The pressure to reduce healthcare costs is high, and private clinics are trying to position themselves strategically, often at the expense of job stability. It is a warning bell that cannot be ignored.

Frequently Asked Questions
What happens to the clinic Varini of Orselina and the Hildebrand Center of Brissago?
The clinic Varini of Orselina and the Hildebrand Center of Brissago announced a new collaboration, but eight physiotherapy staff were fired.
What is the impact of collective (or similar) dismissal on the unemployment procedure for Swiss-Italian frontier workers?
Dismissed frontier workers may apply for unemployment benefit in Italy through INPS, based on Swiss contributions (model U1). The period of notice varies according to the years of service, as determined by the contract or by the Swiss Code of Obligations (Art. 335c CO).
What are the criteria for obtaining the Italian unemployment allowance (RAV) after a dismissal in Switzerland?
In order to have the right to unemployment benefit (RAV) provided by the INPS you must have paid contributions in Switzerland at least 12 months in the last 24, be unemployed unintentionally, be enrolled in the Swiss placement and submit the model U1 within 7 days of the dismissal. The amount is calculated on the average Swiss salary of the last 12 months, with a maximum of 1,300 euros per month.
How to calculate legal notice according to Article 335c CO for a front desk with 5 years of service?
Article 335c of the Code of Obligations provides for a notice that varies with service age. For a frontier with 5 years of age the minimum period is 2 months, increased one month for each year of additional service up to 12 months. The notice may be reduced for individual agreements, but the company must comply with the legal minimum.
What professional training opportunities are available for healthcare providers in Ticino?
Ticino institutions offer refresher courses for healthcare personnel, including advanced physiotherapy certifications, administrative management and German language. The Canton Ticino Frontier Training program finances up to 70% of the costs for recognised courses, up to CHF 2,500 per participant per year. Registration is open to those with at least 6 months of employment in Switzerland.
