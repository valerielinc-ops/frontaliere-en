This mechanism emerged clearly from a conversation intercepted in Italy, where two entrepreneurs discuss an Italian company affected by an anti-mafia injunction. The proposed solution? Transfer the company to Switzerland, 'with the clear aim of bypassing the injunction that had affected it' and 'to operate in a country where it would be sheltered from any judicial actions'. ### Operation Blizzard-Folgore On March 25, 2025, the Anti-Mafia Directorate (DDA) of Trento, in collaboration with the Catanzaro Prosecution, launched Operation Blizzard-Folgore, which resulted in 17 arrests across seven Italian provinces and preventive seizures worth approximately 50 million euros in Italy and Switzerland. 'The ’Ndrangheta today does finance,' declared the then chief prosecutor of Trento, Sandro Raimondi, at the time. On the same day, upon order of the Federal Department of Justice (MPC), several companies in Lugano were searched. Frequently Asked Questions
What are the anti-mafia injunctions?
Anti-mafia injunctions are measures adopted by Italian authorities to exclude companies with links to organized crime from public tenders and contracts. These measures are provided for by Italian law to prevent mafia infiltration in the public tender sector.
What are the consequences for companies involved in illegal activities?
Companies involved in illegal activities may face preventive seizures, asset confiscations, and administrative sanctions. Additionally, their executives can be arrested and prosecuted for crimes such as money laundering, tax fraud, and mafia-type criminal association.
