A free 100% plant-based meal to discover in Lugano (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 5 May 2026

Greenpeace offers a free plant-based meal in Lugano to promote sustainable eating. Discover the details of the initiative.

Contesto

In brief - Greenpeace offers a free vegetarian meal in Lugano on May 6. - The event takes place on the square between the Cassarate river and the EST USI-SUPSI Campus. - The menu includes bowls with Swiss ingredients such as peas, broad beans, and soy. ## Key facts - What: A free 100% vegetarian meal - When: Wednesday, May 6, 2026, from 11 am to 2 pm - Where: Square between the Cassarate river and the EST USI-SUPSI Campus, Lugano - Who: Greenpeace Switzerland in collaboration with local schools - Cost: Free, with online registration required Greenpeace Switzerland, in collaboration with several schools in the Lugano-Viganello district, invites the public to discover the benefits of a more plant-based diet. The event is scheduled for Wednesday, May 6, from 11 am to 2 pm, on the square between the Cassarate river and the EST USI-SUPSI Campus. On this occasion, a fully plant-based meal made with Swiss products will be offered for free, subject to online registration until all available spots are filled. The initiative aims to highlight the advantages of a diet richer in plant-based proteins, which contributes to the protection of the climate, the environment, and health. The lunch is organized together with the Liceo cantonale di Lugano 3, the Scuola media di Viganello, and the Centro professionale tecnico del settore tessile di Lugano-Viganello, which includes the Scuola d'arti e mestieri della sartoria and the Scuola specializzata superiore di abbigliamento e design della moda. The menu offers various bowl options based on ingredients such as peas, broad beans, and soy grown in Switzerland.
