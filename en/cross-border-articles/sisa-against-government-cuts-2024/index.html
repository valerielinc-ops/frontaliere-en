The union pointed the finger at the announced cuts to performance contracts with USI, SUPSI and DFA, respectively of 5.5 million, 2.5 million and 1.3 million francs per year, as well as the reduction of 5 million francs in public transport contributions. 'We oppose this shameful implementation strategy,' write coordinators Ismael Camozzi and Filippo Beroggi, denouncing 'yet another attempt to cut millions from education and transport,' after the sacrifices already imposed in recent years. The union speaks of a contradictory logic, recalling that the same Government had defined 'unsustainable' the planned federal cuts to university contributions. Dettagli operativi

The SISA's criticisms mainly concern the cuts to essential services such as education and public transport. These cuts could have a significant impact on the quality of life for cross-border workers who use public transport daily to commute between the Canton of Ticino and Italy. Additionally, the increase in university tuition fees could make it more difficult for young cross-border workers to access higher education, limiting their career opportunities. ### Impact on Cross-Border Workers Cross-border workers residing in Italy and working in Ticino could be particularly affected by the cuts to public transport. These workers often use public transportation to commute between their workplace and residence, and an increase in fares or a reduction in services could result in additional costs and significant inconveniences. Furthermore, cuts to contributions for higher education could affect the children of cross-border workers who wish to continue their studies in Ticino. ### Future Scenarios If the Government's plan is implemented, cross-border workers may face increased costs for their children's education and public transport. This could lead to a reduction in purchasing power and greater economic pressure on cross-border families. Additionally, the reduction in educational services could limit career opportunities for young people, making it more difficult for them to find work in Ticino or Switzerland. ### Comparison with the Previous Situation Before the announcement of the cuts, cross-border workers could rely on state contributions for education and affordable public transport. Frequently Asked Questions
What are the cuts announced by the Ticino Government?
The Ticino Government has announced cuts to performance contracts with USI, SUPSI, and DFA, respectively by 5.5 million, 2.5 million, and 1.3 million francs per year, in addition to a reduction of 5 million francs in contributions to public transport.
What are the criticisms of SISA?
SISA criticizes the Government's strategy for cutting essential services such as education and public transport, which could significantly impact the quality of life for cross-border workers.
What are the social consequences of the cuts?
According to SISA, the cuts could crystallize social inequalities, limiting access to university studies for the popular classes and increasing youth precarity.
