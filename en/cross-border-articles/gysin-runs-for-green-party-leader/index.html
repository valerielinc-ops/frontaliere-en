Greta Gysin runs for Greens group leader

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 1 May 2026

Ticino's national councillor Greta Gysin is running for the presidency of the Greens parliamentary group, succeeding Aline Trede.

Contesto

In brief - Greta Gysin runs for Green group leader - First candidate for Aline Trede's succession - Election scheduled for May 22nd - Gysin aims to strengthen Ticino's presence in the party ## Key facts - What: Greta Gysin's candidacy for Green group leader - When: April 29, 2026 - Where: Switzerland - Who: Greta Gysin, National Councilor - Amount: Not yet specified Greta Gysin, Ticino's National Councilor first elected in 2019 with 23,456 votes and re-elected in 2023 with 28,762 preferences, has announced her candidacy for the presidency of the Green parliamentary group. Currently, Gysin is one of the vice-presidents of the group. Her candidacy is the first official one for the succession of Aline Trede, who left the position after joining the Bernese Government on January 1, 2024. The election date is set for May 22nd. Considering that the party's presidency is currently held by Lisa Mazzone from Geneva, Gysin's potential election would result in the absence of German-speaking Switzerland representation in the party's two main positions. However, Gysin has stated that she does not consider this a problem, especially due to her proficiency in German and Swiss German dialect. ### Operational checklist - Official candidacy: Submitted on April 29, 2026 - Election date: May 22, 2026 - Next steps: Possible other candidacies by May 15, 2026 - Expected results: Announced by May 23, 2026 ### Practical comparisons - Scenario 1: Gysin's election - Strengthening of Ticino's presence in the party - Possible increase in attention to local issues such as cross-border traffic and renewable energy - Scenario 2: Election of a candidate from German-speaking Switzerland - Maintenance of regional balance - Possible focus on national issues such as agricultural policy and taxa...

Dettagli operativi

Implications for the Canton of Ticino Greta Gysin's candidacy represents a significant opportunity for the Canton of Ticino to have greater representation at the national level. As one of the few Ticinese representatives in the Green parliamentary group, Gysin could bring a unique and relevant perspective on local issues, such as the management of cross-border workers and environmental policies that directly affect the region. Ticino, with over 67,000 cross-border workers (2023 data), is one of the Swiss regions most affected by cross-border work. Gysin could play a crucial role in promoting policies that balance economic needs with social ones, such as adapting infrastructure and public services to cope with the increase in the working population. ### Comparison with the Previous Situation The previous group leader, Aline Trede, left a significant mark on the Green parliamentary group. Her experience and commitment helped strengthen the party's position in Parliament. Gysin, with her experience and knowledge of Swiss political dynamics, could bring new energy and a different perspective to the group. Trede actively worked on issues such as sustainable mobility and reducing CO2 emissions, achieving concrete results such as the approval of a motion for the electrification of public transport in 2022. Gysin could continue on this path, focusing on specific projects for Ticino, such as the expansion of the regional railway network and the optimization of connections with border towns like Chiasso and Mendrisio. ### Future Scenarios If elected, Gysin could work to strengthen collaboration between the different linguistic regions of Switzerland, promoting policies that take into account local specificities.
