Ticketless train: Switzerland experiments with the future of mobility. What changes for cross-border workers

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 27 April 2026

Switzerland introduces the Bibo system for ticketless travel. National test starting April 27 with 3000 users, including cross-border workers from Ticino.

This system, developed by Axon Vibe in collaboration with the Swiss Federal Railways (SBB) and the public transport sector, under the direction of Alliance SwissPass, will be tested on a large scale from April 27 to June 30, 2026. The test will involve around 3000 users, including several dozen from Ticino. The principle behind Bibo is simple: just have Bluetooth active on your smartphone. The app automatically detects boarding and alighting, calculates the route, and charges at the end of the day only the cost of the trips actually taken. There is no need to check in or out, thus eliminating the risk of forgetting to activate or deactivate the journey. Dettagli operativi

How Bibo Works The Bibo system operates using the Bluetooth technology integrated into smartphones. When a user boards public transport, the app automatically detects the location and activates a virtual ticket. Similarly, when the user alights, the app records the exit and calculates the cost of the journey. This system eliminates the need to manually purchase tickets or remember to activate and deactivate the trip. ### Advantages for Cross-Border Workers For cross-border workers, Bibo offers several advantages. Firstly, it reduces the risk of forgetting to buy a ticket or manually activating the app. This can avoid fines and incorrect charges, making the journey smoother and worry-free. Additionally, the system is designed to be as inclusive as possible, ensuring that traditional alternatives remain available for those who do not wish to use Bluetooth technology or prefer other payment methods. ### Possible Challenges Despite the advantages, there are some challenges to consider. For example, the system requires users to have a compatible smartphone and to keep Bluetooth always on. This could be a barrier for some people, especially those who are not used to using complex apps or prefer more traditional payment methods. Furthermore, the system will continue to detect movements outside of public transport, although Alliance SwissPass ensures that this data will not be used for tariff purposes. ### Conclusion In conclusion, the Bibo system represents a significant step forward in the future of mobility, offering a simpler and more automated way to travel on public transport. What to do to participate in the test To participate in the Bibo system test, interested users can register through the FFS app. It is necessary to have a smartphone compatible with Bluetooth technology and ensure that Bluetooth is always active during travel. Once registered, users can start using the system immediately, benefiting from the convenience of an automatic ticket. ### Step-by-step procedure 1. Download the FFS app on your smartphone. 2. Frequently Asked Questions
What happens if I forget to turn on Bluetooth?
If you forget to turn on Bluetooth, the Bibo system won't be able to detect your presence on public transport and won't charge any costs. However, it's important to ensure that Bluetooth is always on to avoid fines or incorrect charges.
Can I still use traditional tickets?
Yes, the FFS have confirmed that EasyRide and the classic ticket office will continue to operate in parallel. This ensures that traditional alternatives remain available for those who do not wish to use Bluetooth technology or prefer other payment methods.
Will my data be used for other purposes?
No, Alliance SwissPass guarantees that the data collected by the Bibo system will not be used for tariff purposes or transmitted to third parties. The system will continue to detect movements outside public transport, but this data will not be used for commercial purposes.
