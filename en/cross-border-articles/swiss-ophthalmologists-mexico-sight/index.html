Ticino ophthalmologists in Mexico: free surgeries to restore sight

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 27 April 2026

A team of Swiss doctors, mostly from Ticino, are performing free cataract surgeries for patients in Quintana Roo, Mexico.

Contesto

In brief - A Swiss team operates on Mexican patients to treat cataracts. - The initiative is by the Swiss Foundation against Blindness. - The operations take place in a public hospital in Cancún. ## Key facts - What: Free operations to treat cataracts. - When: Since November 2001, with the last mission in 2026. - Where: Public hospital in Cancún, Quintana Roo, Mexico. - Who: Swiss Foundation against Blindness, with a team of 17 doctors, opticians, and assistants. - Amount: Up to 60 patients operated per day, for a total of nearly 500 operations in two weeks. In Mexico, millions of people have lost their sight due to cataracts, a disease amplified by tropical UV rays, diabetes, and poor nutrition. However, the decisive factor remains economic: the operation costs too much for the majority of patients, who wait up to ten years before regaining their sight. The Swiss Foundation against Blindness has been trying to change this for 26 years. It all began in November 2001 when the ophthalmologist from Olten, Alex Heuberger, went to Acapulco without speaking a word of Spanish. “We operated on 50 people, and they were all happy, as was I. We said to ourselves: we want to continue,” Heuberger told Falò. More than two decades later, a team of 17 doctors, opticians, and assistants—mostly from Ticino—returned to Quintana Roo for two weeks of operations. The goal is to operate on nearly 500 patients, up to 60 per day, in a public hospital in Cancún provided by the state. By 7:30 AM, the line in front of the facility is already long. They come from the poorest rural areas of the region. “We try to bring them light again,” explains Theo Singer, president of the foundation, in his sixth year in Mexico, to RSI. ### Operational difficulties The difficulties are not lacking. Dettagli operativi

The initiative by the Swiss Foundation against Blindness represents an example of international solidarity that has a significant impact on the lives of people in Mexico. Swiss ophthalmologists, particularly those from Ticino, bring not only advanced medical skills but also a sense of humanity and dedication that transcends national borders. ### Implications for Mexican Patients For Mexican patients, the opportunity to undergo free surgery means a turning point in their quality of life. Cataracts are a debilitating condition that limits the ability to work, study, and interact with the surrounding world. Restoring sight to these individuals not only improves their physical health but also their psychological and social well-being. For example, Gonzalo can now return to teaching, contributing to the local community, while Mauro, the adolescent with disabilities, can finally see the world around him. ### Challenges for Swiss Ophthalmologists Swiss ophthalmologists face numerous challenges during their missions in Mexico. Operating conditions are often difficult, with sudden blackouts, heat, and humidity that can compromise the effectiveness of the interventions. Additionally, the lack of pre-operative knowledge among patients requires great adaptability and competence from the doctors. Despite these difficulties, the Swiss team continues to operate with dedication, demonstrating a constant commitment to the foundation's mission. ### The Importance of International Collaboration Collaboration between the Swiss Foundation against Blindness and Mexican authorities is crucial for the success of the missions.
