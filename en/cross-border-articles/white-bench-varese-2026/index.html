Varese, first white bench against child abuse

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 2 May 2026

The first white bench dedicated to the number 114 for the protection of minors was inaugurated in Varese, on the occasion of the National Day against pedophilia and child pornography.

Contesto

In brief - The first white bench dedicated to the number 114 inaugurated in Varese - The project is promoted by the VareseSi-Cura Committee with the patronage of the Municipality - The number 114 is active 24/7 and managed by Telefono Azzurro ## Key facts - What: Inauguration of the first white bench dedicated to the number 114 - When: May 5, 2026, at 10 AM - Where: Giardini Estensi di Varese - Who: VareseSi-Cura Committee, Municipality of Varese, Telefono Azzurro - Amount: Not yet specified The first “White Bench 114 – Child and Adolescent Emergency” has been inaugurated at the Giardini Estensi in Varese, an initiative aimed at breaking the silence on child abuse and making known concrete tools to ask for help. The inauguration, promoted by the VareseSi-Cura Committee with the patronage of the Municipality of Varese, took place on Tuesday, May 5, at 10 AM, on the occasion of the National Day against Paedophilia and Child Pornography. This project represents a national first and was born with the aim of shedding light on a growing and often hidden phenomenon. At the heart of the project is the public utility number 114, active 24 hours a day and managed by Telefono Azzurro, which offers immediate support to minors who are victims of violence as well as to adults who want to report situations of risk. According to the most recent data, over 113,000 minors are followed by social services for abuse, with an increase of 58% compared to 2018. In 87% of cases, violence occurs within the family and 40% of victims suffer multiple forms of abuse. The white bench represents only the first step of a broader project entitled “Safe Minors Become Safe Adults”. Dettagli operativi

Implications for the Community The inauguration of the white bench in Varese represents a significant step in the fight against child abuse. The presence of a visible and permanent symbol in the heart of the city invites citizens to reflect on a problem often underestimated and to become aware of the importance of reporting risky situations. The white bench, in addition to being a reference point for those in need of help, also serves as a reminder to the community of the existence of the number 114 and the services offered by Telefono Azzurro. ### Comparison with the Previous Situation Before the inauguration of the white bench, awareness of child abuse in Varese was limited to occasional campaigns and local initiatives. The new bench represents a tangible and permanent change, which we hope can have a lasting impact on collective awareness. The collaboration between the Municipality, the VareseSi-Cura Committee, and other local realities has made it possible to create an integrated project that goes beyond simple reporting, promoting a structured support network. ### Future Scenarios The project 'Safe Minors Become Safe Adults' could be replicated in other cities, creating a national network of white benches dedicated to the number 114. This approach could help reduce the number of unreported abuse cases and improve the quality of life for minors at risk. Furthermore, the establishment of new protocols of understanding between institutions, law enforcement, schools, and hospitals could lead to more effective and coordinated management of reports and interventions. ## Recommended tools For an updated estimate, use the net salary calculator and the CHF-EUR exchange comparator.

What to do in case of need If you are aware of a case of abuse or if you need support, you can contact the number 114, active 24 hours a day. The number is managed by Telefono Azzurro and offers immediate assistance both to minors who are victims of violence and to adults who want to report risky situations. The white bench in the Giardini Estensi of Varese is a visible and permanent reference point, reminding you of the availability of this service. ### Reporting procedure 1. Contact the number 114: You can call the number 114 at any time, 24 hours a day, to report a case of abuse or to ask for support. 2. Provide detailed information: During the call, try to provide as many details as possible about the risky situation, so that the operators can intervene promptly. 3. Stay on the line: The operators of Telefono Azzurro will guide you through the necessary procedures and provide you with the support you need. 4. Follow the instructions: Follow the instructions given by the operators to ensure the safety of the minor involved and to facilitate the intervention of the competent authorities. ### Useful tools For further information and support, you can visit the Telefono Azzurro website or contact the Municipality of Varese. Frequently Asked Questions
What does the white bench in Varese represent?
The white bench in Varese is a visible and permanent symbol dedicated to the number 114, which provides immediate support to minors who are victims of violence and to adults who want to report situations of risk. It is an invitation not to turn a blind eye and to know the available tools for help.
How can I report a case of abuse?
You can contact the number 114, which is active 24 hours a day and managed by Telefono Azzurro. Provide detailed information about the risk situation and follow the instructions of the operators to ensure the safety of the minor involved.
What is the goal of the project 'Minori al sicuro diventano adulti sicuri'?
The goal of the project is to promote a network among institutions, law enforcement, schools, and hospitals through new agreements, to shift from an emergency-based approach to a preventive and structural one, avoiding that the consequences of abuse drag on over time.
