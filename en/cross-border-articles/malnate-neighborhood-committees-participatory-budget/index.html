Malnate: neighborhood committees start work

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 29 April 2026

Malnate's new Neighborhood Committees begin their term with participatory budgeting and targeted projects.

Contesto

In brief - New neighborhood committee representatives elected in Malnate - Participatory budget presented for local projects - First meeting on April 20 in the Council Chamber ## Key facts - What: Start of the work of the Neighborhood Committees - When: April 20, 2026 - Where: Council Chamber of Malnate - Who: Municipal administration and new representatives - Amount: Municipal funds for projects proposed by the committees After the elections on April 12, the new Neighborhood Committees of Malnate officially began their term with a first introductory meeting held on Monday, April 20, in the Council Chamber. The event marked the operational debut of the elected representatives for the areas of Centro, Folla, Baraggia, Rovera, Rogoredo, and Gurone. Citizens chose their representatives for three macro-areas. For Centro, Folla, and Baraggia, Greta Carla Achini (spokesperson), Mauro Carretta, Michele Gargano, Carlo Cencin, and Maria Ranzi were elected. In Rovera and Rogoredo, the team consists of spokesperson Sara Innocenti with Jennifer Meessen, Paola Baron, and Cristina Enrica Giuseppina Filippelli. Gurone has Maria Paola Ferioli as spokesperson, accompanied by Fabrizio Pignatti, Francesca Teresa Santoni, Anna Linda Fantoni, and Manuela Aletti. The San Salvatore area remains uncovered: as no candidacies were received within the deadline, the regulations provide for new elections to be held within a year for this neighborhood only. One of the central points of the meeting was the presentation of the participatory budget, a tool introduced with the amendment of the regulations in December 2025. Dettagli operativi

Analysis of the New Introductions The introduction of participatory budgeting represents one of the main innovations in the new regulation of Malnate's Neighborhood Committees. This tool allows citizens to propose specific projects for the improvement of their neighborhood, with the guarantee that municipal funds will be used to realize these initiatives. The Neighborhood Committees will play a crucial role in gathering citizens' needs and presenting concrete projects to the municipal administration. ### Practical Implications for Cross-Border Workers For cross-border workers residing in Malnate, the introduction of participatory budgeting and Neighborhood Committees represents an important opportunity to actively participate in community life. Cross-border workers can propose projects that address the specific needs of their residential areas, thus contributing to the improvement of living conditions for all residents. Additionally, the possibility of participating in public meetings and directly dialoguing with the municipal administration offers a unique opportunity to make their voices heard and influence decisions regarding their neighborhood. ### Comparisons with Other Local Initiatives The initiative of Malnate's Neighborhood Committees fits into a broader context of civic participation and local development. In fact, similar tools have been introduced in many Italian cities to involve citizens in the management of the territory and the improvement of infrastructure. However, the introduction of participatory budgeting represents a significant novelty, as it allows citizens to have direct control over the funds allocated to local projects. Punti chiave

How to Participate To actively participate in the work of Malnate's Neighborhood Committees, citizens can follow these simple steps: 1. Inform: Contact the municipal administration to obtain information about ongoing projects and upcoming committee meetings. 2. Participate: Attend public meetings organized by the Neighborhood Committees to express your needs and suggestions. 3. Propose: Present specific projects for the improvement of your neighborhood, using the participatory budget. 4. Collaborate: Work together with committee members and the municipal administration to realize the approved projects. ### Useful Tools To facilitate citizen participation, the municipal administration has made several useful tools available: - Town Website: A section dedicated to Neighborhood Committees with updated information on ongoing projects and activities. - Official App: A mobile application to stay updated on meetings and participate in online surveys. - Project Calculator: An online tool to estimate the costs and benefits of projects proposed by citizens. ### Conclusion The start of the work of Malnate's Neighborhood Committees represents a unique opportunity for citizens to actively participate in community life and contribute to improving living conditions in their neighborhoods. With the support of the participatory budget and the commitment of the new representatives, Malnate is heading towards a future of collaboration and local development. For more information on the projects and activities of the Neighborhood Committees, citizens can contact the municipal administration or participate in the public meetings organized by the committees themselves. Frequently Asked Questions
How can I propose a project for my neighborhood?
To propose a project, you can participate in the public meetings organized by the Neighborhood Committees or contact the municipal administration directly. The participatory budget allows you to present specific projects for the improvement of your neighborhood, with the guarantee that municipal funds will be used for the realization of these initiatives.
What are the next steps for the Neighborhood Committees?
The next steps for the Neighborhood Committees include presenting the projects at the city festival on May 16th and collecting further suggestions from the citizens. The municipal administration has expressed its support for these projects and reiterated the importance of collaboration between citizens and institutions for the well-being of the community.
How can I stay updated on the activities of the Neighborhood Committees?
To stay updated on the activities of the Neighborhood Committees, you can visit the website of the Municipality of Malnate or download the official app. These tools will allow you to stay informed about the meetings, ongoing projects, and participation opportunities.
