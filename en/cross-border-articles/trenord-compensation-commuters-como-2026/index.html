Trenord: 17 lines eligible for compensation, Como worst

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 30 April 2026

Alarm data for commuters: 17 out of 42 lines qualify for compensation, with the Asso-Seveso-Milan line being the worst overall.

Contesto

In brief - 17 out of 42 Trenord lines eligible for compensation in February 2026 - Asso-Seveso-Milano line worst with 9 compensations - Angelo Orsenigo (Pd): "Alarm bells, we need clarity" ## Key facts - What: Compensation for delays over 15 minutes - When: January-February 2026 - Where: Lombardy, Trenord lines - Who: Commuters on Trenord lines - Amount: Not specified As many as 17 out of 42 Trenord lines in February 2026 will entitle subscribers to compensation. There were 25 in January, two figures much higher than throughout 2025. The worst line overall is in the province of Como, the Asso-Seveso-Milano (9 times it entitled its commuters to compensation from January 2025 to February 2026), followed by Mariano/Camnago-Seveso-Milano (6 times), Lecco-Molteno-Como (4), Como-Saronno-Milano (3), Chiasso-Como-Monza-Milano (3). "These are alarming numbers, considering that compensation, unlike the bonus, canceled by the Region at the beginning of 2024, takes into account delays over 15 minutes, while the reference standard is 5 minutes. In the official communication, Trenord cites strikes as the main cause; an explanation that does not satisfy us at all," comments Angelo Orsenigo, regional councilor of the Pd. ### Implications for cross-border workers The data processed by the Pd regional group, based on Trenord's official communications, reveal that between January 2025 and February 2026, in the span of 14 months, there were 113 critical lines, which entitled to compensation. The right kicks in when more than 10% of the trains on a line, over the course of a month, are canceled or arrive at the destination with a delay of more than 15 minutes. Dettagli operativi

Analysis of the causes of delays The causes of delays and cancellations are manifold and complex. According to Trenord, strikes are one of the main causes, but Orsenigo disagrees. 'Strikes have been resolved in one or two days, while the problem is persistent,' he states. This suggests that there are other underlying causes to the service issues. ### Infrastructure problems One of the possible causes of delays is the maintenance and upgrading of infrastructure. Railway lines in Lombardy are often subject to maintenance and upgrading work, which can cause delays and cancellations. However, these works should be planned in such a way as to minimize the impact on commuters. ### Traffic management Another possible cause is traffic management. Railway lines in Lombardy are among the busiest in Italy, and traffic management can be complicated. Trenord has to manage a large number of trains every day, and any problem can cause a chain reaction of delays. ### Weather conditions Weather conditions can also play a role. In winter, railway lines can be subject to snow and ice, which can cause delays and cancellations. However, these conditions should be forecast and managed adequately. ### Implications for cross-border workers For cross-border workers who use these lines, delays and cancellations represent a significant problem. Many cross-border workers have to reach their workplace in Switzerland every day, and delays can cause punctuality issues and stress. Moreover, the additional costs for alternative transport can be a significant burden. ### Comparison with other regions Compared to other Italian regions, Lombardy has a below-average punctuality rate. Punti chiave

What to do to obtain compensation To obtain compensation, commuters must follow a specific procedure. Here are the steps to follow: 1. Collect evidence: Keep tickets and travel documents that prove delays or cancellations. 2. Apply for compensation: Fill out the compensation request form available on the Trenord website. 3. Submit the request: Send the completed request along with the collected evidence to Trenord. 4. Wait for a response: Trenord will evaluate the request and communicate the outcome within a specified time period. ### What to do in case of frequent delays If delays are frequent, commuters may consider using alternative means of transport. However, it is important to keep in mind that additional costs can be a significant burden. Furthermore, commuters can contact Trenord to ask for information on service improvements and possible solutions. ### Contacting Trenord For further information and assistance, commuters can contact Trenord through their website or customer service. It is important to provide all necessary information to obtain a timely and accurate response. ### Using the compensation calculator To calculate the amount of compensation you are entitled to, commuters can use the compensation calculator available on the Frontaliere Ticino website. This tool can help determine the amount of compensation based on the delays and cancellations suffered. ### Conclusion The alarming data on delays and cancellations of Trenord lines in Lombardy require greater transparency and concrete solutions. Commuters, particularly cross-border workers, must be informed and supported to address these issues. Trenord must work to improve traffic management, infrastructure, and communication with commuters to ensure a quality service. Source: comozero.it
