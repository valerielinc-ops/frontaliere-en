Dettagli operativi

Implications for the Community The news of a tuberculosis case in a primary school in Saint-Maurice has raised concerns among parents and the local community. The health authorities of the Canton of Ticino have assured that all necessary measures are being taken to contain the situation. However, it is crucial that everyone is aware of the symptoms of tuberculosis and knows how to protect themselves. Tuberculosis can be transmitted through the air when an infected person coughs, sneezes, or talks. Common symptoms include a persistent cough for more than three weeks, persistent fever, night sweats, unexplained weight loss, and fatigue. According to data from the Federal Office of Public Health (FOPH), 433 cases of tuberculosis were reported in Switzerland in 2022, with an incidence rate of 5.1 cases per 100,000 inhabitants. In the Canton of Ticino, there were 12 cases, with a rate of 5.7 per 100,000 inhabitants. If you suspect you have been exposed to tuberculosis, it is important to consult a doctor as soon as possible. The health authorities are also working with the school to ensure that all hygiene measures are in place to prevent further cases. This includes regular cleaning of surfaces, promoting good hygiene practices among students and staff, and adequate ventilation of the premises. ### Operational Checklist for Schools 1. What are the symptoms of tuberculosis?

How is tuberculosis transmitted?
Tuberculosis can be transmitted through the air when an infected person coughs, sneezes, or talks. It is important to practice good hygiene habits to prevent the spread of the disease.
