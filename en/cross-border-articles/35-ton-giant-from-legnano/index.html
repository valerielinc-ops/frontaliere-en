35-tonne giant born in Legnano

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 1 May 2026

Camu Srl of Legnano presents T-Mill, a 35-tonne CNC machine tool for high-precision machining.

Contesto

In brief - 35-ton CNC machine developed in Legnano - Designed for machining up to 10 tons - Made with Lombard components ## Key facts - What: T-Mill, CNC machining center - When: Project lasted one and a half years - Where: Legnano, Lombardy - Who: Camu Srl, family-run business - Amount: 35 tons, weight of the machine A colossus weighing 35 tons, four times the weight of a T-Rex, has been born in a small company in Legnano. This is the T-Mill, an automated machine tool designed for high-precision milling and drilling operations. Camu Srl, a company specializing in the production of vertical lathes and turning centers, has developed this prototype almost entirely with components supplied by Lombard companies. The T-Mill is capable of machining parts up to 10 tons, placing it among the most relevant solutions in the national landscape. ### An innovative project The T-Mill is a 'T' CNC machining center designed for high-precision milling and drilling operations. The company, led by the Cantoreggi family, celebrates its first 25 years of activity with this product. 'We wanted to add a different machine,' explained Caterina Cantoreggi, 'and the need of a client in the oil & gas sector pushed us to develop this project.' ### Applications and comments The machine is designed for complex machining on five simultaneous axes, equipped with automatic pallet changer and capable of operating on materials such as alloy steels, duplex and super duplex. Its main applications concern the oil & gas, petrochemical, naval and energy sectors. Mario Gualco, president of CNA Meccanica di Produzione Lombardia Nord-Ovest, commented: 'This is an impressive plant in terms of size and performance, capable of producing valves for refineries and machining large parts with complex geometries.' Implications for the Manufacturing Sector The T-Mill represents a significant step forward for the manufacturing sector, particularly for companies operating in the oil & gas, petrochemical, maritime, and energy sectors. The ability to work on pieces up to 10 tonnes with high precision opens new possibilities for the production of large and complex components. ### Comparison with Traditional Solutions Compared to traditional machine tools, the T-Mill offers notable advantages in terms of precision and machining capability. Its 'T' structure and the ability to operate on five axes simultaneously make it ideal for complex machining that requires high precision. Additionally, the automatic pallet changer allows optimizing production times, reducing downtime. ### Usage Scenarios The T-Mill can be used in various industrial scenarios. For example, in the oil & gas sector, it can be employed for the production of valves and other critical components for refineries. In the maritime sector, it can be used for machining large and complex pieces, such as hull parts or engine components. In the energy sector, it can contribute to the production of components for turbines and other equipment. ### Market Impact The introduction of the T-Mill on the market could have a significant impact, especially for companies seeking advanced solutions for machining large pieces. The ability to operate on materials such as alloy steels, duplex, and super duplex makes it a versatile solution suitable for a wide range of industrial applications. ### Final Considerations The T-Mill is an example of how small businesses can innovate and compete in the global market.
