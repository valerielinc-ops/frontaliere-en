3,000 officials protest austerity measures in Geneva (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 8 May 2026

Between 2,500 and 3,000 people protested in Geneva against the austerity measures planned by the Canton, including wage freezes and indexation freezes.

Contesto

In brief - 3,000 officials demonstrate in Geneva against austerity measures - Wage increases frozen for 4 years and indexation frozen - Cuts of 533 million francs without union consultation ## Key facts - What: Protest against austerity measures - When: May 7, 2026 - Where: Geneva, starting from Parc des Cropettes - Who: Inter-union committee of state employees and subsidized sector - Amount: Cuts of 533 million francs Between 2,500 and 3,000 people demonstrated today in Geneva, initiated by the Inter-union committee of state employees and the subsidized sector, to protest against the austerity measures planned by the Canton. Starting from Parc des Cropettes, the procession crossed the Monte Bianco bridge before reaching the old town, where the Grand Council is located. 'Salaries under attack, services at risk,' chanted the protesters, who have taken to the streets for the fifth time since November. 'Our work is essential, our salary is existential,' read the signs. The procession followed a strike. The unions and state employees remain mobilized against the austerity measures planned in the 2026 bis budget project. They particularly denounce the freeze on wage increases for 2026 and the three following years, as well as the freezing of indexation. The Inter-union committee estimates wage losses of up to 5.1%. ### Implications for cross-border workers The indignation of public employees reached a new level with the presentation this morning of a catalog of additional austerity measures by a group of experts commissioned by the State Council. The document, which lists cuts of 533 million francs, was made public without any prior consultation with the personnel organizations. Dettagli operativi

Analysis of Savings Measures The savings measures announced by the Canton of Geneva will have a significant impact on public sector workers and, indirectly, on cross-border workers employed in Switzerland. The freeze on salary increments for four years and the suspension of indexation represent an estimated wage loss of up to 5.1%, according to the Interunion Committee. This means that workers will see a decrease in their purchasing power, especially in a context of inflation. ### Comparison with the Previous Situation Before the austerity measures, public sector workers benefited from annual salary increases and indexation that took inflation into account. With the new budget project, not only are the increases frozen, but indexation is also suspended, meaning that salaries will not keep up with rising prices. This could lead to a decrease in the standard of living for many workers, especially those with lower salaries. ### Scenarios for Cross-Border Workers For cross-border workers employed in the public sector in Geneva, these measures could mean a reduction in disposable income. With the freeze on salaries and the increase in the cost of living, many may need to review their budget and consider alternative options. Some might decide to seek employment in other Swiss cantons or in Italy, where economic conditions might be more favorable. However, it is important to note that the savings measures are not yet final and may be subject to changes following negotiations with the unions. ### Impact on Public Services The savings measures planned by the Canton of Geneva also include cuts to public services, such as the reduction of social aid, the increase in the number of students per class, and the higher occupancy of beds in nursing homes. Frequently Asked Questions
What are the savings measures planned by the Canton of Geneva?
The savings measures planned by the Canton of Geneva include freezing salary increases for 2026 and the three following years, freezing indexation, reducing social aid, increasing the number of students per class, raising the occupancy rate of beds in nursing homes, and ending the free public transport in the canton. These measures are planned in the 2026 bis budget project and in the catalog of additional savings measures presented by a group of experts commissioned by the State Council.
