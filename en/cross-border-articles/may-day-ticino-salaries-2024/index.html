May Day in Ticino: wages and rights at protest heart

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 1 May 2026

Around two thousand people marched in Lugano demanding fair wages and denouncing wage dumping

Contesto

In brief - Around two thousand people demonstrated in Lugano for workers' rights - Wages and unemployment are the main concerns - The UDC initiative 'No to a Switzerland of 10 million!' was criticized ## Key facts - What: May Day demonstration in Ticino - When: 1 May 2024 - Where: Lugano and Pregassona - Who: Swiss Trade Union Federation and OCST - Amount: Around two thousand participants in Lugano, five hundred in Pregassona May Day was celebrated in Ticino with demonstrations that saw the participation of around two thousand people in Lugano and five hundred in Pregassona. The protests focused on demands for wages and working conditions. The regional secretary of UNIA Ticino, Giangiorgio Gargantini, expressed concern about the situation of the Ticino labor market, emphasizing the need to guarantee dignified wages and to combat wage dumping. Gargantini also criticized the UDC initiative 'No to a Switzerland of 10 million!', calling it racist and divisive, and urged to vote 'no' on 14 June. ### The unions' demands The unions denounced the phenomenon of wage dumping, which affects not only the lowest wages but also medium and high ones. The fight against unemployment and the guarantee of wages that allow a decent living in the canton were the main demands. The agreement on minimum wages was seen as a first step, but the unions are asking for solutions at all levels of the wage scale. ### The UDC initiative and its consequences The UDC initiative 'No to a Switzerland of 10 million!' was at the center of the criticism. According to Gargantini, this initiative would have devastating consequences for Ticino, increasing the number of cross-border workers and attacking the rights of migrant workers. Dettagli operativi

Analysis of the implications The May Day demonstrations in Ticino highlighted the concerns of workers and unions regarding the labor market situation. The demands mainly concern wages and working conditions, with particular attention to the phenomenon of wage dumping. ### Wage dumping Wage dumping is a phenomenon that affects not only the lowest wages but also medium and high ones. This occurs when employers offer wages below market rates to reduce labor costs. This can lead to a reduction in the quality of work and greater precarity for workers. ### The consequences of the UDC initiative The UDC initiative 'No to a Switzerland of 10 million!' could have significant consequences for Ticino. According to Gargantini, this initiative could increase the number of cross-border commuters and attack the rights of migrant workers. The end of family reunification, for example, could have a negative impact on the lives of cross-border commuters and their families. ### The demands of the unions Unions are calling for solutions at all levels of the wage scale to combat wage dumping. The agreement on minimum wages has been seen as a first step, but unions are calling for further measures to guarantee dignified wages and better working conditions. ### The May Day demonstrations The May Day demonstrations in Ticino saw the participation of around two thousand people in Lugano and five hundred in Pregassona. These demonstrations highlighted the concerns of workers and unions about the labor market situation and put wages and working conditions at the center of the demands. ## Recommended tools For an updated estimate, use the net salary calculator and the CHF-EUR exchange comparator.
