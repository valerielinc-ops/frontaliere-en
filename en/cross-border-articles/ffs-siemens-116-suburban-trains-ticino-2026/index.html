SBB and Siemens: 116 new trains for Ticino

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 30 April 2026

Two billion francs for 116 double-decker trains for suburban traffic. They will enter service from 2031.

Contesto

In brief - 116 new trains for suburban traffic - 2 billion francs contract - Service start from 2031 ## Key facts - What: 116 new double-decker trains - When: Service start from 2031 - Where: Ticino and Romandy - Who: SBB and Siemens - Amount: 2 billion francs SBB and Siemens signed on Thursday the contract for 116 new double-decker trains destined for suburban traffic. The total value of the agreement is two billion francs. The Thurgau-based competitor Stadler Rail had filed an appeal against the tender, but subsequently withdrew it. The agreement provides for the supply of 116 six-car trains of the Desiro DoSto type, with an option for another 84. Project management, testing and commissioning will take place in Switzerland, while the carriages will be built in Germany. Siemens Mobility, the industrial giant's division active in the railway sector, has around 6,000 employees in Switzerland and is investing 110 million francs in a new site in Wallisellen, in the canton of Zurich. The new trains, which will enter service from 2031, will be primarily destined for the Zurich S-Bahn network (95 trains) and Romandy (21 trains). With around 540 seats, they offer a significantly higher capacity compared to the first-generation models they will replace. Passengers will have 10% more seats and 30% more standing room. ### Impact on suburban traffic The introduction of these new trains represents a significant step for improving suburban traffic in Ticino and Romandy. The greater capacity of the new trains will allow a larger number of passengers to be accommodated, thus reducing overcrowding and improving travel comfort. Analysis of Practical Implications The introduction of the new Desiro DoSto trains will have a significant impact on suburban traffic in Ticino and Romandy. With greater capacity and advanced technologies, these trains will offer an improved travel experience for passengers. ### Greater Capacity and Comfort The new trains offer approximately 540 seats, a 10% increase compared to first-generation models. Additionally, 30% more standing room will allow for more passengers to be accommodated during peak hours. This capacity improvement will reduce overcrowding and enhance travel comfort, making public transport more attractive for commuters. ### Advanced Technologies and Safety The new trains are equipped with advanced technologies that ensure a safer and more pleasant travel experience. Cutting-edge safety systems, such as automatic brakes and obstacle detection systems, will reduce the risk of accidents and improve overall railway safety. Furthermore, the use of lightweight materials and advanced energy technologies will contribute to reducing energy consumption and CO2 emissions. ### Economic and Employment Impact Siemens Mobility's investment in a new site in Wallisellen represents a further step towards the development of railway infrastructure in Switzerland. This investment will create new job opportunities and support the local economy, contributing to the economic growth of the canton of Zurich and Switzerland as a whole.
