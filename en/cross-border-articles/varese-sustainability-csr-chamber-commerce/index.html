Sustainability returns to the center of the economic debate in Varese with the arrival of the CSR and Social Innovation Fair. The event, which has become a national reference point for dialogue between companies and social actors, lands in the Varese area with a specific focus: “Circular ecosystem: environment, solidarity and collaboration”. The initiative is promoted by a prestigious network that includes Bocconi University, Sustainability Makers, Global Compact Network Italy, ASviS, Sodalitas Foundation, Unioncamere and Koinètica. The goal of the Varese leg is to provide concrete tools to adopt circular economy solutions. It will not only be about theory, but about how collaboration along the supply chain and synergy with the third sector can translate into real competitive advantages for companies. Dettagli operativi

Implications for Varesine Companies The event represents a unique opportunity for Varesine companies to acquire concrete skills on the circular economy. Collaboration along the supply chain and synergy with the third sector can translate into significant competitive advantages. For example, companies in the textile sector can adopt recycling and reuse practices for materials, thus reducing production costs and improving their brand image. According to a study by the Politecnico di Milano, companies that adopt circular economy practices can reduce operating costs by up to 20%. ### Practical Examples During the conference, replicable examples from key sectors of the local and national production fabric will be presented. Companies in the plastics sector can adopt innovative technologies for recycling materials, such as the 'Plastic Free' project in Lugano, which allowed the recycling of over 500 tonnes of plastic in 2022. Those in the cosmetics sector can develop sustainable and biodegradable products, following the example of Bellinzona, where 30% of the cosmetics produced are already biodegradable. Construction can adopt green building practices, using eco-compatible materials and reducing environmental impact, as required by Swiss regulations that mandate 20% recycled materials in new constructions from 2025. 📊 Operational Checklist for Adopting Sustainable Practices: - Analysis of costs and benefits of adopting circular economy practices - Training of personnel on sustainability issues - Collaboration with local entities and the third sector for pilot projects - Monitoring and evaluation of results obtained ### Comparison with the Previous Situation Before the arrival of the CSR Fair in Varese, local companies had few opportunities to discuss sustainability iss...
