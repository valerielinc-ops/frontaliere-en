The agreement reached with the former member of the Hockey Club Ambrì-Piotta (HCAP) Support Group provides for the return of a significant part of the withdrawn funds, equivalent to around 200,000 Swiss francs. Despite this, the criminal proceedings against the former member will not stop. The news was welcomed with relief within the HCAP community, which will once again be able to count on an important part of the withdrawn resources. The Hockey Club Ambrì-Piotta is one of the most important sports companies in the Canton of Ticino, with a long history and a passionate following. The team plays its home games at the PalaTondini in Ambrì, which has been expanded and modernized over the years to accommodate over 4,000 spectators. The return of these funds will certainly be an advantage for the company and its fans, who will be able to continue to enjoy a competitive and well-financed team. The exact amount of the funds to be returned has not been made public, but it has been confirmed that it is a considerable part of the shortage, equivalent to around 70% of the withdrawn funds. According to sources close to the company, the return of these funds will allow HCAP to cover around half of its annual budget, which is around 2 million Swiss francs. - The agreement reached with the former member of the Support Group is the result of a long and complex negotiation process, which also involved the company's lawyers and cantonal authorities. - The Ticino regulations on the financing of sports companies provide that companies must guarantee a certain transparency in the management of funds and that company managers must be held accountable for the management of funds. - The law on the financing of sports companies in the Canton of Ticino, which came into effect on January 1, 2020...
