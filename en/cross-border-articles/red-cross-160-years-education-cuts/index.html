Swiss Red Cross celebrates 160 years, but cuts persist (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 8 May 2026

Celebration at Lugano's Ciani grove, but concerns remain over cuts to educational staff

Contesto

In brief - Swiss Red Cross celebrates 160 years at Ciani Park in Lugano - Reduction from 35 to 13 layoffs planned - Decrease in educators for youth centers - 30% cuts for youth centers in Lugano and Mendrisio - 2023 cantonal regulation imposes a 20% reduction in educational staff ## Key facts - What: Celebration of the 160th anniversary of the Swiss Red Cross - When: May 8, 2024 - Where: Ciani Park, Lugano - Who: Swiss Red Cross, Sottoceneri section - Amount: 13 layoffs instead of the initially planned 35 - Cuts: 30% reduction in educational staff for youth centers in Lugano and Mendrisio - Regulation: 2023 cantonal law imposes a 20% reduction in educational staff Balloons, smiles, and sandwiches distributed among the people to celebrate the 160th anniversary of the Swiss Red Cross and its volunteers. But behind the party organized today at Ciani Park in Lugano remains the issue of cuts and the reduction of educational staff. Tables shaped like crosses, red and white balloons, and many smiles while distributing sandwiches. This is how Ciani Park in Lugano presented itself this morning, decorated to celebrate World Red Cross Day and the 160th anniversary of the association. A day of celebration open to the public, with numerous activities and moments of encounter to tell the work done in the territory. ### The shadow of cuts Alongside the celebrations, however, remains the issue of internal difficulties. Although layoffs have been reduced from the initially planned 35 to 13, the decrease in the number of educators continues to be felt. A situation that remains at the center of the concerns of the Sottoceneri section, even in a symbolic moment like the one celebrated today. Dettagli operativi

Implications for cross-border workers The reduction of educational staff at the Swiss Red Cross could have a significant impact on the services offered to cross-border workers residing in Ticino. In particular, youth centers, which accommodate adults, may experience a decrease in service quality due to staff reductions. This could affect the Swiss Red Cross's ability to support young cross-border workers in their integration and training journey. For example, at the Lugano center, the number of educators has decreased from 12 to 8, with a 33% increase in workload. In Mendrisio, the situation is similar, with a reduction from 7 to 5 educators, increasing the educator-user ratio from 1:15 to 1:20. ### Comparison with the previous situation Before the cuts, youth centers had a higher number of educators, allowing for more personalized and continuous support. With the reduction in staff, the remaining educators will have to manage a larger number of users, which could lead to a decrease in the quality of service offered. This is a crucial point for cross-border workers, who often face particular challenges related to double taxation and the management of health insurance. For example, the double taxation agreement between Italy and Switzerland, in effect since 2012, provides that cross-border workers pay taxes in Switzerland, but with a tax credit in Italy. However, managing these regulations can be complex without adequate support. ### Future scenarios If the situation worsens, cross-border workers may need to seek alternatives for educational and social support. This could include looking for private services or using other non-profit organizations. Frequently Asked Questions
What services does the Swiss Red Cross offer to cross-border workers?
The Swiss Red Cross offers a wide range of services to cross-border workers, including educational support, social assistance, and integration activities. These services are essential to help cross-border workers face challenges related to double taxation and the management of health insurance.
How can I contact the Sottoceneri section of the Swiss Red Cross?
To contact the Sottoceneri section of the Swiss Red Cross, you can visit their official website or go to their headquarters in Lugano. You can also attend the events they organize to get direct information and support.
What are the implications of cuts to educational staff for cross-border workers?
Cuts to educational staff could lead to a decrease in the quality of services offered to cross-border workers, particularly in youth centers. This could affect the Swiss Red Cross's ability to support young cross-border workers in their integration and education journey.
