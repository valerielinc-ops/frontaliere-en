Tunisian cross-border worker arrested in Como for resisting police

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 1 May 2026

A 19-year-old Tunisian man, legally in Italy but homeless, was arrested for violence and resisting a public official.

Contesto

In brief - Arrest of a 19-year-old Tunisian in Como - Attempt to hide narcotic substance - Aggressive behavior at the police station ## Key facts - What: Arrest for violence and resistance to public official - When: Around midnight on April 29, 2026 - Where: Via Cadorna, Como - Who: State Police of Como - Amount: 21 grams of hashish The State Police of Como arrested a 19-year-old Tunisian citizen in the night between April 29 and 30, 2026. The young man, who is legally in Italy but has no fixed abode, was stopped during a routine check in Via Cadorna. He appeared particularly agitated, prompting the police to conduct a more thorough inspection. With a quick gesture, the young man threw a brown package containing a narcotic substance under the police car, which was later identified as hashish weighing approximately 21 grams. The young man was taken to the police station to verify the contents of the package and his identity, which he initially refused to provide. Even inside the police car, the detained individual began to behave in a disruptive manner, insulting and threatening the officers. The aggressive behavior continued at the police station, where the young man attempted to physically assault the officers, who then immobilized him. Investigations revealed that the young man had prior offenses for unlawful possession of objects intended to cause harm, aggravated theft, and robbery, in addition to a verbal warning from the Chief of Police of Como. The 19-year-old was arrested for violence, threats, and resistance to public officials and was reported for possession and dealing of narcotic substances.
