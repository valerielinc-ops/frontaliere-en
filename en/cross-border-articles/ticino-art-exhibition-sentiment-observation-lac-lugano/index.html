The Soul of Ticino on Display: Understand Where You Work (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 17 February 2026

At the LAC in Lugano, the 'Sentiment and Observation' exhibition reveals Ticino's identity between Italy and Switzerland. A unique opportunity for cross-border workers.

TL;DR - Exhibition explores Ticino's cultural identity from 1848 to WWII - Art reflects Ticino's dual Italian-Swiss identity - Exhibition open from April 18, 2025, to July 26, 2026 - Cross-border workers contribute to Ticino's cultural landscape ## Key facts - Title: Sentiment and Observation - Location: LAC (Lugano Arte e Cultura), Lugano - Dates: April 18, 2025, to July 26, 2026 - Curator: Tobia Bezzola - Key Period: 1848 to the end of World War II - Price: 20 CHF - Cover Image: Schildkröte mit Kerbel (1923) by Richard Seewald - Source: Ticinonline, 18.02.2026 Every day, thousands of cross-border workers ('frontalieri') cross the border posts of Brogeda, Gaggiolo, or Ponte Tresa. The routine is marked by work, traffic, and the franc-euro exchange rate. But how many of us truly know the history and soul of the Canton that hosts us? An unmissable opportunity to deepen this connection comes directly from the cultural heart of Lugano, the LAC (Lugano Arte e Cultura). The Museo d'arte della Svizzera italiana (MASI) presents a new, impressive exhibition titled "Sentiment and Observation", which will be open from April 18, 2025, to July 26, 2026. This is not just an art exhibition, but a true journey into Ticino's identity, an exploration of how this border region has evolved culturally and artistically. ## A Bridge Between North and South The exhibition, curated by Tobia Bezzola, focuses on a crucial period: from the founding of the Swiss federal state in 1848 to the end of World War II. The exhibition highlights what the source calls the "canton's characteristic ambivalence." On one hand, a deeply Italian cultural identity; on the other, political and structural belonging to the Swiss Confederation. Dettagli operativi

Going into detail, "Sentiment and Observation" offers a clear vision of how Italian and Germanic artistic currents met and clashed right here in Ticino. The selection of works, enriched by important loans, allows for tracing a path that goes beyond simple chronology. One can observe how the landscape, society, and political tensions influenced the artists. The MASI's permanent collection, which includes prestigious donations like that of the Richard and Uli Seewald Foundation, is the backbone of this narrative. An example is the exhibition's cover image, 'Schildkröte mit Kerbel' (1923) by Richard Seewald, a German artist who found a second home in Ticino. ## What does this mean for a cross-border worker? Visiting this exhibition means equipping oneself with a new key to understanding the territory. Understanding the cultural roots of the place where you work is not a minor detail, but an element that enriches the daily experience. The exhibition shows how Ticino has always been a crossroads, a place of meeting and synthesis. The same role that, in a sense, the cross-border worker plays today: a figure connecting two economic and cultural systems. Understanding this history helps one feel less like a guest and more like an integral part of a complex and fascinating reality. 📊 Event Details: - Title: Sentiment and Observation - Location: LAC (Lugano Arte e Cultura), Lugano - Dates: From Friday, April 18, 2025, to Sunday, July 26, 2026 - Hours: Every day except Monday, from 11:00 AM - Price: 20 CHF
