In brief - Swiss glaciers with low winter snow cover - Average deficit of 25% compared to the 2010-2020 period - Similar situation to record years 2022 and 2023 ## Key facts - What: Low snow cover on Swiss glaciers - When: Winter 2025-2026 - Where: Upper Valais, Ticino, Grisons - Who: Swiss glacier monitoring network (GLAMOS) - Amount: Average deficit of 25% compared to the 2010-2020 period Swiss glaciers are at risk after a dry winter. The low winter snow cover makes them vulnerable to intense melting during the summer. This phenomenon is particularly evident for the glaciers of Upper Valais, Ticino, and Grisons. The warm and dry month of April contributed to this situation, as highlighted by the Swiss glacier monitoring network (GLAMOS) in a report published on Friday. GLAMOS measured the thickness of the snow accumulated during the winter on 25 Swiss glaciers, comparing them with previous years. The average deficit of the winter 2025-2026 reaches 25% compared to the layers measured in the 2010-2020 period. 'The starting situation for the summer is therefore unfavorable,' glaciologist Matthias Huss told the Keystone-ATS agency. ### Implications for cross-border workers Snow acts as a protective layer, preserving the underlying ice from direct solar radiation. If this layer melts early, the glacier is exposed to melting earlier and for longer. This is compounded by the so-called albedo effect: dark ice absorbs more solar energy than light snow and therefore heats up more quickly. This further accelerates glacier melting.
