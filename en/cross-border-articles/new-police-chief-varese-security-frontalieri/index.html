In brief - Paolo Iodice is the new police chief of Varese. - Goal: security and continuity. - Focus on predatory crimes and scams targeting the elderly. ## Key facts - What: Appointment of the new police chief of Varese. - When: May 4, 2026. - Where: Varese, Italy. - Who: Paolo Iodice. - Amount: Not specified. Paolo Iodice is the new police chief of Varese. The official presentation took place on Monday morning in the offices of Piazza Libertà. Iodice outlined the guidelines for his term, emphasizing the importance of continuity and commitment to the security of the area. 'We will uphold continuity, and ensure security for Varese and its province with the utmost effort and dedication,' Iodice declared during the meeting with local press. The new police chief highlighted his familiarity with the area and its challenges. 'I am fortunate to have learned to appreciate the respect for rules, the sense of generosity, and the hard work that bring honor to this community,' he stated. Iodice thanked his predecessors, Michele Morelli and Carlo Ambrogio Mazza, and expressed his intention to continue their work. A significant focus was placed on security, particularly regarding the most widespread criminal phenomena in certain areas of the province. 'The territory has experienced and continues to experience various challenges, particularly predatory crimes,' Iodice explained. 'Such as the unbearable scams targeting the elderly, which I consider utterly despicable, as well as pickpocketing, robberies, drug dealing in the woods, and crimes against individuals in the most populated areas.' ### Goals of the new police chief The declared goal is to strengthen the organization of the police forces and systemize skills and experiences. Implications for cross-border workers The appointment of Paolo Iodice as the new police chief of Varese represents an important opportunity for cross-border workers who work in Switzerland and reside in Italy. Territorial security is an absolute priority, which translates into a safer environment for everyone, including cross-border workers who cross the border daily. ### Focus on predatory crimes One of the main goals of the new police chief is to combat predatory crimes, such as scams targeting the elderly, pickpocketing, robberies, and drug dealing in the woods. These crimes can have a significant impact on the quality of life of cross-border workers, who often use public transportation and move through urban and rural areas. The fight against these criminal phenomena will contribute to creating a safer environment for everyone. ### Collaboration with institutions Iodice has emphasized the importance of collaboration with institutions and the judiciary. This integrated approach is fundamental to ensuring effective and coordinated security. For cross-border workers, this means that law enforcement will be better equipped to respond to their needs and protect them from potential threats. ### Prevention and repression The new police chief has reiterated that prevention is an absolute priority. This proactive approach is essential to ensure that cross-border workers can live and work in a safe environment. Repression, although necessary, is seen as a last resort. This means that law enforcement will focus on preventive measures to avoid crimes from happening in the first place. ### Experience and competence Iodice's long operational experience is an added value for the community of Varese and for cross-border workers. What to do for cross-border workers For cross-border workers who live and work between Varese and the Canton Ticino, it is important to be aware of the new safety initiatives promoted by the new chief of police. Here are some concrete steps that can be useful: 1. Stay informed about safety initiatives: Keep up to date with the new safety measures implemented by Chief of Police Iodice. This can include attending community meetings or reading official communications. 2. Report any crimes: In case of predatory crimes or other forms of criminality, it is essential to report the situations to the competent authorities immediately. This helps to prevent further crimes and to improve overall safety. 3. Collaborate with law enforcement: Participating in collaboration programs with the local police can be an effective way to contribute to the safety of the territory. This can include neighborhood watch programs or awareness initiatives. 4. Use safety tools: Adopting personal safety measures, such as using bicycle locks or protecting personal property, can help prevent predatory crimes. 5. Participate in community events: Participating in events organized by the community can help strengthen social bonds and improve overall safety. This can include cultural, sports, or educational events. ### Useful Tools For further information and to stay updated on the latest news and safety initiatives, cross-border workers can use the salary calculator and other resources available on the Frontaliere Ticino website. Frequently Asked Questions
What are the main priorities of the new Varese police chief?
The main priorities of the new police chief Paolo Iodice are the security of the territory, the continuity of the work already carried out by his predecessors, and the fight against predatory crimes, such as fraud against the elderly, pickpocketing, robberies, and drug dealing in the woods.
How can cross-border workers contribute to the security of the territory?
Cross-border workers can contribute to the security of the territory by informing themselves about security initiatives, reporting any crimes, collaborating with law enforcement, using personal security tools, and participating in community events.
What is the new police chief's approach to crime prevention?
The new police chief Paolo Iodice has emphasized that prevention is an absolute priority. This proactive approach aims to prevent crimes from happening in the first place, using preventive measures and collaborating with institutions and the judiciary.
