Low Mendrisiotto merger at risk: debt stalls Terre di Breggia (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 27 March 2026

After 2.5 years of talks, the merger plan between Balerna, Breggia, Chiasso, Morbio and Vacallo founders on Chiasso’s 140-million debt. Rizza (Vacallo) demands guarantees, Arrigoni (Chiasso) replies: «We already cut 3 million a year». The Canton weighs suspension.

TL;DR - Chiasso's debt threatens Basso Mendrisiotto merger - Vacallo refuses to share Chiasso's debt burden - Merger deadline extended to June 30, 2026 - Debt restructuring plan aims to reduce Chiasso's debt ## Key facts - Municipalities: Balerna, Breggia, Chiasso, Morbio Inferiore, Vacallo - Chiasso's debt: CHF 140 million - Debt service: CHF 4.2 million annually - Original deadline: 31 December 2025 - Extended deadline: 30 June 2026 - Residents involved: 51,000 - Debt-to-revenue ratio: 142% - Target debt reduction: CHF 110 million by 2028 The future of Basso Mendrisiotto hangs on a single balance sheet. After thirty months of meetings, studies and promises, the municipality of Terre di Breggia – the code name for the merger of Balerna, Breggia, Chiasso, Morbio Inferiore and Vacallo – risks collapsing. The reason? Chiasso’s historic debt, now at CHF 140 million, frightens Vacallo and puts the whole process in doubt. The State Council, which set up the merger commission in September 2023, is seriously considering suspending the procedure. The original deadline for signing the merger agreement was 31 December 2025, but it has already slipped to 30 June 2026 and could be pushed back again. For the 51 000 residents involved, uncertainty means investment freezes, difficulty accessing federal funds and fears of a tax-rate spike. The cross-border worker who drives through Ponte Chiasso every morning will still find the same number of municipalities, duplicated bureaucracies and the same inefficiencies the merger was meant to eliminate. The numbers are brutal. Chiasso’s debt service swallows CHF 4.2 million a year, 12 % of its fiscal revenue. What does the debt restructuring plan entail? 📊 The Chiasso Municipality has presented a three-year plan (2025-2027) to the cantonal commission, which involves a 6% linear cut in all operational expenses, excluding healthcare, security, and education. In absolute terms, this translates to a CHF 1.2 million savings on personnel (fifteen positions eliminated through non-replacement), CHF 0.8 million on extraordinary maintenance, CHF 0.5 million on external consulting, and CHF 0.5 million on contributions to entities and events. On the revenue side, CHF 2 million per year is expected to be generated from a temporary 2‰ patrimonial tax on cadastral income, and CHF 1.5 million from a hike in the overnight stay tax from CHF 3 to CHF 4 per person/night. The target is to reduce the debt to CHF 110 million by 2028 (a reduction of 21.4%) and bring the debt service below CHF 3 million annually. ## How would taxation change for commuters? 💡 With the birth of Terre di Breggia, the tax rate for a commuter with a taxable income of CHF 65,000 would drop from the current 9.7% weighted average (range 9.3% in Vacallo to 10.1% in Chiasso) to 8.9% thanks to the inter-municipal equalization fund. The net savings for the worker would amount to CHF 520 per year, equivalent to just over a month's worth of gasoline for someone commuting daily from Como to Chiasso. Frequently Asked Questions
What happens if the project of aggregation between the Municipalities of Lower Mendrisiotto is canceled?
The project of aggregation between the Municipalities of Lower Mendrisiotto is cancelled, the Municipalities remain separate and border workers continue to pay five different tourist taxes, five different rates of nurseries and five different rates of pro-millesimo.
What are the consequences for border workers if the aggregation project is not completed by 30 June 2026?
If the aggregation project is not completed by 30 June 2026, border workers will continue to pay five different tourist taxes, five different nest fees and five different rates of pro-milesimo.
What are the implications of Chiasso's debt for frontier workers?
Chiasso's debt (CHF 140 million) could lead to an increase in the tax rate for border workers if the merger of municipalities does not go through. Chiasso's current debt service costs CHF 4.2 million annually, impacting the municipal budget and potentially future taxes.
How would the tourist and border tax change in the event of a merger?
In the event of a merger, the tourist tax would be harmonised. The article mentions an increase from CHF 3 to CHF 4 per person/night according to Chiasso's rehabilitation plan, and a one-off fee of CHF 3.50 as foreseen by the general tariff harmonisation of the 'Terre di Breggia' project.
What happens if the referendum against the merger of municipalities is successful?
If the minority of Morbio Inferiore collected the 1,500 signatures necessary for the referendum and this was approved, the merger project would lapse. One should therefore start again from scratch with the procedures, keeping the municipalities separate and the current bureaucratic inefficiencies.
