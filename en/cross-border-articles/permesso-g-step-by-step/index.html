Frequently Asked Questions
What documents are required for the G permit application?
Passport, Certificate of Residence and Employment Contract
What is the competent office for applying for the G permit?
The Immigration and Labour Office of the Canton of Ticino
What are the average times and costs for applying for the G permit in Ticino?
The times for obtaining the G permit generally vary from 1 to 3 months. However, with the assistance of a consultant, a response is possible within 6 weeks. Costs range from CHF 100 to CHF 500, depending on the specific case and the complexity of the request. For example, a request handled in 6 weeks can cost CHF 200.
By when do I need to apply for the G permit after the start of work?
According to the current legislation, specifically the law of 25 October 2018, frontier workers must apply for the G permit within 30 days from the date of commencement of work in Switzerland. It is essential to respect this deadline to avoid delays or complications in the regularization process.
Does my employment contract have to meet specific requirements for the G permit in Ticino?
Yes, it is crucial that the employment contract complies with the Ticino legislation on cross-border work. This is established by Article 34 of the Decree of the Council of State of 13 December 2013. Compliance verification is an important step in ensuring that the G permit application proceeds smoothly and is valid.
