Hupac grows and strengthens Busto: positive profit and new connections

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 29 April 2026

Hupac closes 2025 with a profit of 3.5 million francs and increasing volumes, despite the criticalities of the railway network.

Contesto

In brief - Hupac closes 2025 with a profit of 3.5 million francs - Transport volumes increased by 4.3% - New strategic connections from Busto Arsizio - 4.5% increase in transalpine traffic through Switzerland - New weekly connections: Köln Nord-Busto Arsizio (25 pairs) and Ludwigshafen-Busto Arsizio (30 pairs) ## Key facts - What: Hupac Annual Report 2025 - When: 2025 - Where: Busto Arsizio, Switzerland, Belgium-Italy - Who: Hupac Group - Amount: Profit of CHF 3.5 million, volume increase of 4.3% Hupac closes 2025 with a profit of 3.5 million francs and transport volumes increased by 4.3%. Despite the criticalities of the railway network, the group recorded significant growth, especially in the core business of transalpine traffic through Switzerland, with an increase of 4.5%. This result was achieved also thanks to the acquisition of new traffic on the Belgium-Italy axis. Transalpine traffic via France and Austria increased by 26.9%, while in non-transalpine traffic volumes grew by 2.9%. ### New strategic connections Hupac has launched new strategic connections from Busto Arsizio, including Köln Nord-Busto Arsizio and Ludwigshafen-Busto Arsizio, with weekly frequencies of 25-30 train pairs each. These new connections aim to improve the resilience of the offer for customers and to compensate for the delays caused by construction sites on the railway network. Hupac's RadicalShift2Rail concept concentrates traffic flows on a few high-capacity terminals, transported by high-frequency shuttle trains. ### Impact on combined traffic Despite the ongoing construction work on the railway network, which has compromised the capacity of the infrastructure, Hupac has managed to maintain a growing turnover of 3.1%, reaching CHF 646 million. Analysis of implications for cross-border workers The growth of Hupac and the strengthening of strategic connections from Busto Arsizio have important implications for cross-border workers who work in Ticino and reside in Italy. With the increase in transport volumes, which went from 1.2 million units in 2020 to 1.5 million in 2023, and the introduction of new connections, it is likely that combined transport will become more efficient and reliable. This could translate into shorter travel times and greater punctuality for commuters using the transport services offered by Hupac. ### Comparison with the previous situation Before the introduction of new connections, cross-border workers could face significant delays due to work on the railway network. For example, in 2022, travel times between Busto Arsizio and Lugano could vary between 1h45m and 2h30m due to interruptions and delays. With the implementation of the RadicalShift2Rail concept, Hupac has sought to mitigate these problems by concentrating traffic flows on high-capacity terminals, such as that of Busto Arsizio, and using high-frequency shuttle trains. Frequently Asked Questions
What are the new rail links introduced by Hupac in 2025?
Hupac introduced new strategic links from Busto Arsizio, including Köln Nord-Busto Arsizio with 25 pairs per week and Ludwigshafen-Busto Arsizio with 30 pairs per week. These connections aim to improve supply resilience and compensate for delays caused by construction sites on the railway network.
How did the increase in transalpine traffic affect Hupac's results in 2025?
The increase in transalpine traffic through Switzerland of 4.5% contributed significantly to Hupac's positive results in 2025. This enabled the group to post a profit of CHF 3.5 million and a 4.3% increase in transport volumes.
What are the main infrastructure challenges faced by Hupac in 2025?
Hupac faced challenges related to the continuous construction work on the railway network, which compromised the capacity of the infrastructure. Despite this, the group implemented performance safeguarding measures and optimised high-capacity terminals to maintain a 3.1% growth in turnover.
How do Hupac's new rail connections affect border workers working in Ticino?
Hupac's new rail connections improve the efficiency and reliability of combined traffic, reducing travel times and increasing punctuality. This results in a more attractive and convenient transport for border workers working in Ticino and residing in Italy.
What are the future implications for border workers as a result of improvements in Hupac services?
If Hupac continues to invest in innovation and infrastructure, journey times could improve further and transportation costs could decrease. This could have a positive impact on the cost of living for border crossers, making combined transport a cheaper choice.
