Frequently Asked Questions
What is cross-border work in Ticino and how was promoted by Antonio Cannavale?
Cross-border work in Ticino is an area for transactions between Ticino and neighbouring municipalities. Antonio Cannavale has worked to facilitate these transactions, for example by collaborating with the municipality of Mendrisio for the creation of a system of exchange of goods and services that led to a reduction of 20% of illegal transactions.
What impact has the renovation project of the town of Lavena Ponte Tresa promoted by Antonio Cannavale had?
The project, funded with 500,000 francs, has significantly improved the quality of local life with new infrastructure and public services.
What are the tax breaks for cross-border workers between Italy and Ticino in 2024?
Cross-border workers between Italy and Ticino can take advantage of a reduced taxation of 50% on income produced in Switzerland, thanks to the Italian-Swiss agreement. This benefit applies if you reside in Italy and work in Ticino for at least 60 days a year.
How does public transport work for cross-border commuters between Como and Lugano?
Cross-border commuters can use the 'Ticino Trasporti' bus service and the SBB/SNCF railways to travel between Como and Lugano. An integrated 'Frontalieri Ticino-Lombardia' tariff is active, which simplifies travel with monthly passes starting from CHF 80.
What documents do I need to work in Ticino as an Italian citizen in 2024?
To work in Ticino as an Italian citizen, in addition to a valid passport or identity card, you must apply for a work permit G (for cross-border commuters) from the Ticino Cantonal Labour Office. The permit is valid for 5 years and requires proof of an employment contract in Ticino.
