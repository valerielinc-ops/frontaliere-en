Less Pressure at the Border: Irregular Entries Down (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 18 February 2026

Data for January 2026 from the FOCBS show a sharp decline in irregular entries in Ticino, down to 392 cases. A sign of normalization for border crossings.

TL;DR - Irregular entries in Switzerland dropped to 795 in January 2026 - Ticino saw 392 irregular entries, down from 439 in December 2025 - Afghan migrants are the most represented nationality in January - Decreased pressure may lead to smoother border crossings ## Key facts - What: Significant drop in irregular stays recorded in Switzerland - When: January 2026 - Ticino entries: 392 cases detected at the southern border - National entries: 795 cases across the Confederation - Main nationality: Afghan - Data source: Federal Office for Customs and Border Security (FOCBS) - Report date: February 17, 2026 A sign of easing pressure on the southern border. Data published by the Federal Office for Customs and Border Security (FOCBS) for January 2026 indicate a significant drop in irregular stays recorded in Switzerland, with Ticino, while remaining the main entry point, fully following this trend. In January, cases detected at the southern border were 392, a decrease from 439 in December 2025. This decline becomes even more pronounced when looking at the overall national picture. Irregular entries across the Confederation have fallen from 3,387 in January 2024 to 1,166 in January 2025, and now to 795 this year. This is a significant downturn that directly affects the border crossings in the Mendrisiotto region, such as Chiasso and Gaggiolo, which are crossed daily by thousands of cross-border workers. The most represented nationality among migrants who entered irregularly in January is Afghan. The decrease concerns not only entries but also related activities: cases of suspected smuggling activity are also lower than the previous month, although slightly higher than the same period last year. Dettagli operativi

The Regulatory Framework and Trends The analysis of FOCBS data reveals a consolidated downward trend, a fact that should be interpreted in the context of Swiss and European migration policies. The management of these flows is primarily the responsibility of the Cantons, as established by the Federal Act on Foreign Nationals and Integration (FNIA). However, federal-level coordination, carried out by the FOCBS in collaboration with cantonal police forces, plays a crucial role. The final national data, which will also include findings from police forces, will be published in March by the Federal Statistical Office, but the FOCBS's preliminary figures are already a solid indicator. 📊 Key Data (January 2026 vs Previous): - Irregular entries in Ticino: 392 (vs 439 in Dec '25) - Irregular entries in Switzerland: 795 (vs 1,166 in Jan '25 and 3,387 in Jan '24) - Hand-overs to foreign authorities: Down compared to the previous month and January 2025. This contraction in entries has direct implications for border management. Less pressure allows border guards to focus resources on more targeted checks, potentially making crossings smoother for cross-border commuters while maintaining a high level of security. The phenomenon of smugglers, although slightly increasing on an annual basis, appears to be under close observation, indicating a continuous effort to dismantle the criminal networks that exploit the desperation of migrants. The current situation, therefore, is not a matter of chance but the result of coordinated action between various authorities at multiple levels.

What Does This Mean for Cross-Border Workers? For those who cross the border every day, a drop in irregular migratory pressure is positive news. Although security checks remain a constant, a more manageable situation at the crossings can translate into fewer inconveniences and a more serene general atmosphere. The decrease in irregular entries helps to reinforce the perception of a secure and well-guarded border, a fundamental element for the economic and social stability of the Insubric region. Fewer emergencies to manage mean more resources can be dedicated to the ordinary flow of people and goods, of which cross-border workers are an essential component. This context of renewed normality allows for a calmer focus on the practical and financial aspects of working in Switzerland. 💡 Practical Tips: - Monitor traffic: Even with less pressure, rush hours remain critical. Using traffic monitoring apps is always a good habit. - Documents in order: Always carry your G permit, ID card, and vehicle documents to facilitate any checks. - Financial planning: Stability at the border should not overshadow the importance of careful financial management, influenced by exchange rates and tax regulations. Precisely because stability allows for better future planning, it is essential to have a clear picture of your income. Understanding the impact of deductions and the exchange rate on your salary is the first step towards effective financial management. Frequently Asked Questions
What is the current situation of irregular entrances in Switzerland?
According to data from the Federal Office for Customs and Security of the borders (UDSC), irregular entrances in Switzerland declined significantly, from 3'387 in January 2024 to 795 in January 2026.
What impact does the decrease in irregular traffic inputs for frontier workers?
The decrease in irregular inputs can lead to a smoother flow of people and goods to the valics, reducing the discomforts for the fronts during peak hours, although it is advisable to continue monitoring traffic with specific apps.
What are the main measures taken by Switzerland to counter irregular entrances from the Italy border?
Switzerland has strengthened border controls, increased patrols and improved cooperation with the Italian authorities, helping to reduce irregular entrances. These measures led to a significant decline in recorded cases, as evidenced by January 2026 data.
