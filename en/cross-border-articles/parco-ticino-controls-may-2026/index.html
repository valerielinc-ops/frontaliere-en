Ticino Park: intensified checks for May Day weekend (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 5 May 2026

Thanks to checks and awareness, uncivil behaviors have decreased in Ticino Park during the May Day weekend.

Contesto

In brief - Intensified controls in Ticino Park for the May Day weekend - Dozens of fines issued for access to restricted areas - Zero abandoned waste in the controlled zones ## Key facts - What: Controls and awareness campaign in Ticino Park - When: May Day weekend 2026 - Where: Cava, Mezzanino, Ponte della Becca, Linarolo, Baia del Re, Area Vul, Ciottele in Pavia, Sayonara, Ronchi, Castagnolo, Torricella, Bosco Ghisolfa - Who: Park rangers, Gev of Parasacco and Pavia, Gev of Pavia Province, Vigevano volunteers, Civil Protection - Amount: Dozens of fines issued The long weekend of May Day and the almost summer-like temperatures attracted hundreds of tourists to the beaches and forests along the Ticino River. This year, thanks to the thorough control and prevention measures initiated weeks ago by the order of the Park President, Ismaele Rognoni, the situation has significantly improved, at least regarding abandoned waste, a major issue in some areas. The goal of this task force by the Park Authority, which required a considerable effort from the surveillance and volunteer staff, is clear: to ensure respectful use of the natural areas and combat degradation phenomena. On Friday, May 1st, the joint service involved park rangers, Gev of Parasacco and Pavia, and two Gev of Pavia Province in the areas of Cava and Mezzanino, Ponte della Becca, Linarolo, Baia del Re, Area Vul, and Ciottele in Pavia. In total, dozens of fines were issued for accessing restricted areas. As for abandoned waste, the information campaign resulted in a notable improvement in behavior, demonstrating that awareness efforts and surveillance are discouraging uncivil behavior. Dettagli operativi

Analysis of Results The results of the checks over the May Day weekend show a significant improvement compared to previous years. The reduction in abandoned waste and the limited number of fines issued indicate that awareness and surveillance activities are having a positive impact. This is an encouraging sign for everyone who frequents the park, including cross-border workers who often use these areas for leisure. ### Comparison with Previous Years In the past, Ticino Park has faced significant problems related to abandoned waste and uncivil behavior. Thanks to the commitment of President Rognoni and the park staff, the situation has improved considerably. This comparison highlights the importance of surveillance and awareness to keep natural areas clean and safe. ### Future Scenarios If the checks continue with the same intensity, it is likely that the park will remain a clean and safe place for all visitors. Cross-border workers can expect a more pleasant and environmentally respectful visiting experience. It is fundamental to continue following the rules and collaborating with the park staff to maintain these positive results. ## Recommended tools For an updated estimate, use the net salary calculator and the CHF-EUR exchange comparator.

Punti chiave

What to do for cross-border workers For cross-border workers who wish to visit Ticino Park, it is important to follow some guidelines to avoid penalties and contribute to the preservation of the park: 1. Respect restricted areas: Ensure you do not enter prohibited zones to protect the environment and wildlife. 2. Bring bags for waste: Use garbage bags to collect your waste and dispose of it properly. 3. Collaborate with park staff: Follow the instructions of the staff and volunteers to ensure a respectful and safe visit. ### Useful tools For further information and to plan your visit to Ticino Park, cross-border workers can use the visit calculator available on the website. This tool helps plan your trip and learn about the park's rules. ### Conclusion The intensified checks in Ticino Park over the long weekend of May 1st have demonstrated the effectiveness of vigilance and awareness. Cross-border workers can enjoy a cleaner and safer environment by respecting the rules and collaborating with the park staff. Continuing on this path is fundamental to maintaining the park as a place of leisure and relaxation for everyone. Source: varesenews.it

Frequently Asked Questions
Which areas of Parco del Ticino are monitored during the May Day weekend?
The monitored areas include Cava, Mezzanino, Ponte della Becca, Linarolo, Baia del Re, Area Vul, Ciottele in Pavia, Sayonara, Ronchi, Castagnolo, Torricella, and Bosco Ghisolfa.
How many fines were issued during the weekend?
Around twenty fines were issued for accessing restricted areas on Friday, May 1st, and one fine on Sunday, May 3rd.
How can I help keep Parco del Ticino clean?
You can help by respecting restricted areas, bringing bags for trash, and collaborating with park staff.
