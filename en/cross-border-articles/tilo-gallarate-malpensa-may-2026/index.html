TILO: route changes for Malpensa on May 30-31 (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 28 May 2026

TILO S50 trains will have Gallarate as their terminus next weekend due to work on the Italian network.

Contesto

In brief - TILO S50 trains will not reach Malpensa on May 30-31 - Temporary terminus at Gallarate due to infrastructure works - Passengers must continue with Trenord's RE51 line ## Key facts - What: Change in route for TILO S50 trains - When: Saturday 30 and Sunday 31 May 2026 - Where: TILO S50 line, terminus Gallarate - Who: Passengers heading to Malpensa - Amount: Not specified During the upcoming weekend, TILO S50 connections will have Gallarate as their terminus due to infrastructure enhancement works on the Italian network. This change, effective from 00:01 on Saturday, May 30 until the end of service on Sunday, May 31, will require passengers heading towards Malpensa terminals to continue their journey on Trenord's RE51 line. ### Impact on passengers Passengers should expect longer travel times than usual. The online timetable is currently only partially updated, so it is advisable to check the schedules before departure. This change may cause inconvenience for cross-border commuters using the TILO S50 line to reach Malpensa, especially for those with flights departing or arriving during the weekend. ### Assistance for passengers with disabilities In Ticino, passengers with disabilities who require assistance can contact the toll-free number 0800 007 102 (from Switzerland) or the number +41 800 007 102 (from abroad), available from 5:00 to 24:00. In Italy, passengers with disabilities are invited to communicate their needs at least 48 hours before departure to the dedicated number 800.210.955 (from Italy, active every day from 8:15 to 19:45).

Frequently Asked Questions
Why won't the TILO S50 trains reach Malpensa next weekend?
The TILO S50 trains won't reach Malpensa due to infrastructure enhancement works on the Italian network. The temporary terminus will be Gallarate, and travelers will need to continue with the Trenord RE51 line.
What should travelers heading to Malpensa do?
Travelers heading to Malpensa should get off at Gallarate and continue with the Trenord RE51 line. It is advisable to check the updated schedules and plan the trip with more advance notice.
How can travelers with disabilities get assistance?
In Ticino, travelers with disabilities can contact the toll-free number 0800 007 102 (from Switzerland) or the number +41 800 007 102 (from abroad) available from 5:00 AM to midnight. In Italy, they can communicate their needs to the dedicated number 800.210.955.
