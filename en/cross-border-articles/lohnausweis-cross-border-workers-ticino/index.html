This certificate, issued by Swiss authorities, contains detailed information about the salary, tax deductions, and pension contributions. For Ticino cross-border workers, the Lohnausweis is mandatory and must be presented annually for the Italian tax return. The document includes data such as gross salary, tax withholdings, AVS and LPP contributions, and other relevant information for the tax declaration. ### What the Lohnausweis contains The Lohnausweis is a comprehensive document that provides a detailed overview of a cross-border worker's income and tax deductions. The information contained in the certificate includes: - Annual gross salary - Withholding taxes at source - AVS (Old-Age and Survivors' Insurance) contributions - LPP (Professional Pension) contributions - Deductions for health insurance (LAMal) - Other mandatory deductions and contributions This information is essential for correctly completing the Italian tax return and for avoiding errors that could lead to penalties or paying more taxes than due. ### How to obtain the Lohnausweis To obtain the Lohnausweis, Ticino cross-border workers must follow a specific procedure. Generally, the Swiss employer is responsible for issuing the certificate, which must be delivered to the worker by the end of the fiscal year. Concrete actions for cross-border workers For Ticino cross-border workers, it is important to follow some concrete actions to correctly manage the Lohnausweis. First of all, it is essential to keep the certificate in a safe and easily accessible place. Additionally, it is advisable to make a copy of the document and keep it separately, in case the original is lost or damaged. ### Verification of information Once the Lohnausweis is received, it is important to carefully verify all the information contained in the document. In particular, it is necessary to check that the gross salary, tax withholdings, and pension contributions are correct. If discrepancies are noticed, it is important to immediately contact the employer to request a correction. ### Presentation to the accountant The Lohnausweis must be presented to your accountant or CAF for the preparation of the tax return. It is important to verify with your accountant the deadlines and methods of presenting the document, in order to avoid delays or errors. Furthermore, it is advisable to provide the accountant with all the necessary information for a correct preparation of the declaration, such as any deductible expenses or additional contributions. ### Keeping documents It is important to keep the Lohnausweis together with other tax documents for any future checks. In particular, it is advisable to keep the certificate for at least five years, which is the prescription period for income declarations in Italy.
