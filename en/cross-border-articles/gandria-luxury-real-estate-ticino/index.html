Christian Constantin lands in Gandria: 10.6 million for luxury (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 1 March 2026

Valais entrepreneur Christian Constantin invests 10.6 million francs in a new luxury residential complex in Gandria, Luganese, featuring 14 apartments and 30 parking spaces.

Contesto

TL;DR - Christian Constantin invests 10.6M CHF in luxury Gandria complex - 14 high-end apartments with 30 parking spaces in Gandria - Project under review, subject to appeals per Ticino law ## Key facts - Investment: 10.6 million Swiss francs for luxury residential complex - Location: Gandria, Ticino, below Via Raccord - Plot Size: Approximately 2,500 square meters - Apartments: 14 mid-to-high-end apartments - Parking Spaces: 30 parking spaces available - Architecture: Three distinct but integrated buildings with rooftop gardens - Process: Neighborhood Plan submitted, subject to appeals per Ticino law - Impact: Potential increase in property prices and rental costs in the region The real estate landscape of Luganese is enriched by a new, significant chapter thanks to the initiative of Christian Constantin, a well-known Valais entrepreneur and president of FC Sion. After focusing on an ambitious hotel project between Paradiso and Melide, with the relaunch of Capo San Martino, Constantin is now turning his attention to Gandria, one of the jewels on the shores of Lake Ceresio. The operation, as reported by Corriere del Ticino, involves an estimated investment of 10.6 million francs for the construction of a luxury residential complex. This clearly demonstrates a desire to consolidate his presence in the Ticinese real estate sector, an important signal for the cantonal economy. The project, which has recently seen the submission of the Neighborhood Plan, will extend over a plot of approximately 2,500 square meters, strategically located below Via Raccord, the artery connecting the historic core of the village with the road to Porlezza. Dettagli operativi

The submission of the Neighborhood Plan represents only the 'first procedural step' for Constantin's initiative, as emphasized by the source. This means that the project is still subject to appeals or observations, as provided by applicable law in Ticino. The Law on Territorial Development (LST) and its Implementation Regulation (RLST) establish a clear process for new constructions, including phases of public consultation and the possibility for residents or associations to raise objections. This process is essential to ensure urban planning and land protection, aspects particularly felt in a canton with demographic and construction pressure like Ticino, especially in prestigious areas such as Luganese. For cross-border workers, the evolution of projects of this magnitude has indirect but significant implications. An offering of luxury apartments of this scale, while not directly accessible to most cross-border workers, contributes to pushing up the average prices of properties and rents throughout the region. The demand for housing in Luganese remains high, and the introduction of new high-end units may exacerbate the shortage of more affordable housing, making it more challenging for cross-border workers to find convenient housing solutions near the border or key work hubs like Lugano. This scenario fits into a context where the cost of living in Ticino, particularly rent, already represents a significant expense for those working here. Cantonal and municipal authorities are called to balance economic development and investment attractiveness with the need to ensure a balanced and accessible real estate market for all income brackets.
