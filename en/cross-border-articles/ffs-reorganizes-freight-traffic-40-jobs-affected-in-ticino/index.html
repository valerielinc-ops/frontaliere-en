SBB is reorganizing single-car traffic, affecting 40 jobs in Ticino

Contesto

In brief - Swiss Federal Railways (SBB) reorganize single wagon traffic - 40 employees in Ticino will be involved - Changes also for Chiasso depot Key facts - What: Reorganization of SBB's single wagon traffic - When: By December 2026 - Where: Ticino and throughout Switzerland - Who: Swiss Federal Railways (SBB) - Amount: 40 employees in Ticino SBB announced last Friday that they will strengthen single wagon traffic (SWT) by December 2026, a key move in the reshaping of freight traffic. The reorganization of SWT, currently struggling with losses, is a crucial step in the overhaul of SBB's freight traffic. From the timetable change on December 13, 2026, SWT will become significantly more efficient. The goal is to have a self-financing freight traffic by 2033, as set by the Confederation, which owns SBB. Nationally, about 50 service points with too low demand will no longer be used for SWT but will continue to be served on request by complete trains. SBB will ensure almost unchanged volumes (98%) and much more profitable thanks to higher train occupancy. The reorganization of SWT will affect about 200 employees throughout Switzerland, including about 40 in Ticino. SBB offers a solution to all affected persons: another workplace at SBB Cargo Switzerland, a different role within SBB, in an affiliated company or partner railway, or a professional retraining within SBB. In this context, SBB strictly adheres to the collective labor agreement (CLA). Dismissals will be an exception. The upcoming changes will mainly involve relocations of operational staff's workplace. Currently, SBB has too many specialists concentrated in some regions and too few in others. The company must deploy its workforce where there are goods to transport.
