Implications for cross-border workers The data presented does not include the income of cross-border workers, who represent a significant part of the workforce in the province of Varese. This excludes an important part of the local economic landscape, especially in border areas. Cross-border workers, who live in the province of Varese but work mainly in Switzerland, follow a different tax regime and do not declare their income in the usual way for Italian taxpayers. This different tax regime can significantly influence local economic data, especially in terms of average income and income distribution. ### Comparison with previous data Last year, the gap between Luvinate and Galliate Lombardo was over 600 euros, while this year it's a photo finish. Frequently Asked Questions
What is the average gross income in the province of Varese?
The average gross income in the province of Varese is 28,109 euros. This data is based on last year's tax declarations for the 2024 tax year.
Which are the municipalities with the highest average incomes in the province of Varese?
The municipalities with the highest average incomes in the province of Varese are Luvinate with 37,654 euros, Galliate Lombardo with 37,585 euros, Comerio with 34,551 euros, Ranco with 34,518 euros, and Casciago with 34,039 euros.
Why don't the income data include cross-border workers?
Cross-border workers follow a different tax regime and do not declare their income in the usual way for Italian taxpayers. This different tax regime excludes their income from local tax data.
