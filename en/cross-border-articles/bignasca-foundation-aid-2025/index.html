Giuliano Bignasca Foundation: over 20,000 CHF in aid in 2025 (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 6 May 2026

The Giuliano Bignasca Foundation provided over 20,000 francs in direct aid in 2025, including grocery vouchers and support for rent and health insurance premiums.

Contesto

In brief - Giuliano Bignasca Foundation: 20,000 francs disbursed in 2025 - 9,600 francs in shopping vouchers - 10,860 francs for rent and health insurance premiums ## Key facts - What: Direct aid to people and families in need - When: 2025 - Where: Canton of Ticino - Who: Giuliano Bignasca Foundation - Amount: Over 20,000 francs The Giuliano Bignasca Foundation has confirmed its support for people and families in need in the Canton of Ticino. During 2025, over 20,000 francs in direct aid were disbursed. Specifically, the Foundation allocated 9,600 francs in shopping vouchers for people and families in need, and 10,860 francs for paying essential bills, rent, and health insurance premiums. This aid is crucial for many cross-border workers who work in Switzerland and live in Italy, often facing precarious economic conditions. Established in 2014, the Foundation primarily operates in the areas of livelihood support, housing, education costs, and professional integration, with particular attention to elderly people in economic difficulty. In addition to financial aid, in 2025, more than 60 personal meetings were held with users and families to analyze individual situations and offer targeted support. These meetings are crucial to understanding the specific needs of cross-border workers and their families, who often face unique challenges related to dual residence and regulatory differences between Italy and Switzerland. ### Concrete examples and real numbers For example, in the municipality of Mendrisio, the Foundation supported a family of four with a contribution of 2,500 francs for rent and 1,200 francs for health insurance premiums. Practical Analysis: Implications for Cross-Border Workers The aid provided by the Giuliano Bignasca Foundation represents concrete support for many cross-border workers who work in Ticino and reside in Italy. This aid, ranging from food vouchers to health insurance premiums, is fundamental to tackling daily economic challenges. For example, food vouchers can help families cover food expenses, while support for rent and health insurance premiums is crucial to ensure a roof over their heads and access to medical care. ### Comparison with the Previous Situation Before the Foundation's intervention, many families in difficulty had to face these expenses without any support. The introduction of this aid has helped to alleviate part of the economic burden, improving the quality of life for many people. Furthermore, personal meetings with users have allowed specific needs to be identified and targeted support to be offered, which was not possible before. ### Concrete Scenarios Imagine the case of a cross-border worker family residing in Mese (CO) and working in Lugano. Frequently Asked Questions
Who can apply for assistance from the Giuliano Bignasca Foundation?
The Giuliano Bignasca Foundation's aid is intended for individuals and families in financial need residing in the Canton of Ticino. This includes cross-border workers who work in Switzerland and live in Italy, provided they meet the eligibility criteria set by the Foundation.
What types of assistance are available?
The Foundation offers various types of aid, including grocery vouchers, rental support, and health insurance premiums. These aids are intended to cover essential expenses and improve the quality of life for those in need.
How can I apply for assistance?
To apply for assistance, you need to contact the Giuliano Bignasca Foundation and follow the indicated procedure. You can find more information on the Foundation's website or by contacting the office directly.
