Chinese repression in Switzerland: NGOs criticize Bern

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 30 April 2026

NGOs denounce the lack of concrete measures to protect Tibetans and Uyghurs in Switzerland despite recognizing transnational repression

Contesto

In brief - NGOs disappointed by lack of concrete actions against Chinese repression - Report by the Federal Council of February 12, 2025 - 1,375 documented cases of physical repression since 2014 ## Key facts - What: Transnational repression against Tibetans and Uyghurs - When: For years, with official recognition in 2025 - Where: Switzerland, with documented cases internationally - Who: NGO Voices, Uyghur Association of Switzerland, Young Tibetans in Europe Association - Amount: 1,375 documented physical cases since 2014 The Swiss Federal Council recognized in 2025 the surveillance and pressure activities carried out by the Chinese government on Tibetan and Uyghur communities in Switzerland. However, NGOs criticize the lack of concrete measures to protect these communities. Selina Morell, head of the China program at Voices, states: 'We are very disappointed that, concretely, nothing has changed.' Forms of repression include physical and virtual intimidation, direct or indirect through family members, with the aim of silencing dissenting voices abroad. There are no precise figures on repression in Switzerland, but observers believe the phenomenon is increasing. Internationally, the NGO Freedom House has documented 1,375 cases since 2014, but these are only the most serious physical episodes. ### Measures announced but not yet implemented In the report published on February 12, 2025, the Federal Council outlined several measures, including bilateral dialogue with Beijing, raising awareness among actors potentially confronted with the phenomenon, exchanging with the affected diasporas, and creating a consultation service for victims. Dettagli operativi

Implications for cross-border workers The transnational repression does not only concern the Tibetan and Uyghur communities, but it has broader implications for all cross-border workers living and working in Switzerland. The lack of concrete measures to protect the victims of this repression can create a climate of insecurity and fear, influencing the daily life of many. ### Comparison with the previous situation Before the official recognition of transnational repression by the Federal Council, the Tibetan and Uyghur communities in Switzerland had been denouncing for years the pressures and intimidations they suffered. However, without concrete measures, the situation remains critical. The creation of a strategic steering group is a step forward, but NGOs believe that it is necessary to act with greater urgency. ### Possible scenarios If the Swiss government does not take concrete measures to protect the victims of transnational repression, the phenomenon could continue to grow, with negative consequences for security and freedom of expression in Switzerland. On the other hand, the implementation of effective measures could contribute to creating a safer environment for all communities, including cross-border workers. ### International comparisons Transnational repression is not an isolated phenomenon. Other countries, such as Canada and the United States, have adopted measures to protect communities victimized by pressures from foreign governments. Frequently Asked Questions
What forms of transnational repression have been documented in Switzerland?
Forms of transnational repression include physical and virtual intimidation, direct or indirect through family members, aimed at silencing dissenting voices abroad. These acts can include kidnappings, arbitrary detentions, assaults, illegal expulsions, surveillance, and intimidation.
What has the Swiss Federal Council done to address transnational repression?
The Swiss Federal Council has recognized the surveillance and pressure activities carried out by the Chinese government on Tibetan and Uyghur communities in Switzerland. It has outlined several measures, including bilateral dialogue with Beijing, raising awareness among potentially affected actors, exchanging information with the interested diasporas, and creating a counseling service for victims.
What are the priorities of NGOs to address transnational repression?
NGOs, such as Voices and diaspora associations, believe that the priority should be the creation of a contact point for victims. This service would provide support and protection to those involved and witnesses, contributing to creating a safer environment for all communities in Switzerland.
