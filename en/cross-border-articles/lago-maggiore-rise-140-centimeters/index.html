Lake Maggiore to rise up to 1.40 meters

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 29 April 2026

Decisive agreement to increase the lake's water capacity by 20-30 million cubic meters

Contesto

In brief - Lake Maggiore can rise up to 1.40 meters - +20-30 million cubic meters of water available - Agreement between Italy, Switzerland and Basin Authorities ## Key facts - What: Maximum rise of Lake Maggiore - When: Decision made on March 15, 2023 - Where: Lake Maggiore (Italy-Switzerland border) - Who: Po River Basin Authority, Piedmont and Lombardy Regions, Ticino Consortium, Italy-Switzerland Bilateral Body - Amount: 20-30 million cubic meters more The Permanent Institutional Conference has given the green light to the rise of Lake Maggiore's waters up to 1.40 meters. This decision, coordinated by the Po River Basin Authority, is the result of an agreement between Piedmont and Lombardy Regions, Ticino Consortium, Italy-Switzerland Bilateral Body, and various stakeholders. The rise will allow an increase of 20-30 million cubic meters of available water, without the need for new works or infrastructure. Alessandro Delpiano, Secretary General of the Basin Authority, commented: "Raising the level of Lake Maggiore by up to 15 centimeters is an extraordinary example of how we can and must adapt to climate change. We have managed to have up to 30 million cubic meters more water available without new works or infrastructure." ### Collaboration between institutions The collaboration between the Ministry of the Environment, Lombardy Region, Piedmont Region, and Ticino Consortium has made this result possible. Delpiano expressed satisfaction with the agreement, emphasizing the importance of having more water for agriculture, environmental mitigation, and the life of the Po up to its mouth, in full respect of hydraulic safety conditions and environmental protection. Dettagli operativi

The raising of Lake Maggiore by up to 1.40 meters, approved by the resolution of the Ticino State Council on March 15, 2023, represents a significant step in adapting to climate change. This increase in water level, which will provide an additional 20-30 million cubic meters of water, will be crucial for agriculture and environmental mitigation. ### Impact on Agriculture The additional water will greatly benefit agriculture, especially in the context of climate change. In Ticino, where agriculture is a vital component of the local economy, the rise in lake level will allow for greater irrigation of fields, improving agricultural production and ensuring greater food security. For example, the municipalities of Locarno and Ascona, which heavily depend on agriculture, will benefit from greater water availability for irrigating vineyards and olive groves. According to data from the Department of Public Economy of the Canton of Ticino, the rise in lake level could lead to a 15-20% increase in local agricultural production. ### Environmental Mitigation Raising the lake level will also contribute to environmental mitigation. The additional water will help maintain the ecosystem of the lake and the Po River, improving water quality and supporting biodiversity. This is particularly important in a period of climate change, where water resource management has become a priority. For example, Lake Maggiore is a crucial habitat for various species of fish and migratory birds. The increase in water level will help maintain aquatic habitats and support biodiversity.
