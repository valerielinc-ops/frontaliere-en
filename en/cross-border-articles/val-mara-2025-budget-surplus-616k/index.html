Val Mara: 2025 surplus of 616k francs

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 28 April 2026

The municipality of Val Mara closes 2025 with a surplus of 616,000 francs, exceeding negative forecasts. Targeted investments and stable tax pressure at 80%.

Contesto

In brief - 2025 Budget of Val Mara: surplus of 616 thousand francs - Negative forecast exceeded by 1.4 million - Tax pressure maintained at 80% ## Key facts - What: 2025 Financial Statement of the Municipality of Val Mara - When: 2025 - Where: Val Mara, Ticino - Who: Municipality of Val Mara - Amount: 616,285 francs surplus The Municipality of Val Mara closed 2025 with a surplus of 616,285 francs, exceeding the negative forecast of 782,330 francs by over 1.4 million. This positive result represents the third financial statement since the merger of the municipalities of Maroggia, Melano, and Rovio in 2022. 'The positive result confirms careful and responsible financial management but must be interpreted with the necessary caution. It is indeed influenced by partly temporary factors and a still limited historical series of financial data, typical of the first years following the merger,' reads a note issued by the Municipality. ### Economic context and Municipality's choices The general economic situation remains characterized by uncertainties, particularly linked to the conflict in the Middle East and customs duties that continue to slow down markets. This is added to important prospects of impact on the accounts of local authorities, such as the implementation, starting from 2027, of the cantonal and federal initiatives on health insurance premiums and the abolition of the imputed rental value, as well as the increase in costs for public transport and the Arcobaleno Tariff Community. Despite this scenario, the Municipality has decided to maintain the tax pressure at 80%, considering it an indispensable element of good attractiveness for the Municipality. Implications for Residents and Cross-Border Workers The positive balance of the Municipality of Val Mara represents a sign of stability and prudent management of public resources. For residents and cross-border workers who work in the municipal area, this result translates into greater security regarding the quality of the services offered and the sustainability of future investments. The decision to maintain the tax burden at 80% is a positive element, as it helps to avoid tax increases that could affect the purchasing power of families. ### Comparison with the Previous Situation Before the aggregation of the municipalities of Maroggia, Melano, and Rovio in 2022, the financial situation of the individual municipalities was more fragmented and less stable. The aggregation has allowed for the optimization of resources and the planning of more targeted and sustainable investments. For example, the new heating plant of the Municipal House of Maroggia and the expansion of the Melano School Center are projects that improve the quality of life and infrastructure for all residents. ### Future Scenarios With the update of the financial plan scheduled for the current year, the Municipality of Val Mara is preparing to face future challenges with greater security. The net investments planned for approximately 13.8 million francs represent an indicative estimate, but they demonstrate the willingness to continue investing in projects that improve the quality of life and infrastructure. Prudence and sustainability remain the pillars of the Municipality's financial management, ensuring balanced and lasting development. ### Comparison with Other Municipalities in Ticino Val Mara is not the only municipality in Ticino to record a positive balance.
