Isolino Virginia reopens: a journey through time among stilt houses and nature

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 30 April 2026

After three years of closure, Isolino Virginia reopens to the public with a new museum setup and dining services.

Dettagli operativi

The reopening of Isolino Virginia, located in Lake Varese, represents a unique opportunity to rediscover a cultural heritage of inestimable value. The new setup of the Ponti Museum offers an immersive experience that allows visitors to dive into the daily life of the Neolithic period, thanks to the faithful reconstruction of a hut from that era and the presence of archaeological finds displayed as they are uncovered during excavations. The section dedicated to fishing and weaving is particularly interesting, as it helps to better understand the daily activities of the island's inhabitants. The presence of a reconstructed loom and hunting tools provides a complete picture of prehistoric life, making the museum experience even more engaging. For example, the reconstructed loom was made following traditional techniques and uses authentic materials, such as linen and wool, to offer a tangible experience of the past. The return of catering services is another important aspect of the reopening. The Giulietta al Lago restaurant offers a high-quality gastronomic stop, allowing visitors to fully enjoy the tranquility of the island. This service is particularly appreciated by visitors, who can thus combine their museum visit with a moment of relaxation and tasting. The restaurant offers seasonal menus with local products, such as fish from Lake Varese and Ticino cheeses, providing an authentic culinary experience. For those who wish to organize a group visit or a school trip, it is possible to book personalized guided tours and workshops. This service is particularly useful for schools, which can thus deepen their knowledge of prehistory in a real and engaging context. Punti chiave

To visit Isolino Virginia, it is necessary to follow some simple but important procedures. Firstly, it is advisable to book in advance, especially for groups and schools, to ensure the availability of guided tours and workshops. Bookings can be made by writing to museivarese@cooperativasullarte.it. For groups of more than 10 people, a notice of at least 15 days is required. Guided tours cost 5 euros per person, while educational workshops for schools are offered at 7 euros per student. The connection to the island is guaranteed by the Biandronno dock, located in via Marconi, on the cycle-pedestrian path. The boat will make numerous trips from 10:30 am to 6:00 pm, every Saturday and Sunday starting from the month of May. It is advisable to arrive with some advance to avoid prolonged waits. The ticket for the boat costs 3 euros per person, while children under 6 years travel for free. 💡 Operational Checklist: - Advance booking for groups and schools - Check boat schedules and restaurant table reservation - Check weather conditions - Respect safety rules during the visit For those who wish to book a table at the Giulietta al Lago restaurant, it is possible to do so through the site www.giuliettaallago.it. It is advisable to book in advance, especially on weekends, to ensure the availability of a table. The restaurant offers menus starting from 25 euros per person, with vegetarian and vegan options available. During the visit, it is important to respect the safety rules and the indications provided by the museum staff. It is forbidden to touch the exhibited finds and always follow the indications of the guides during the guided tours.
