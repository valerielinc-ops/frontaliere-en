Trenord: 25 lines with compensation, disastrous start to 2026

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 30 April 2026

Alarm bells for Trenord: 25 lines with compensation in January 2026, 17 in February. The Pd demands clarity.

Contesto

In brief - 25 lines with compensation in January 2026 - 17 lines in February 2026 - Data worsened compared to 2025 ## Key facts - What: Compensation for delays over 15 minutes - When: January and February 2026 - Where: Lombardy and Canton Ticino - Who: Trenord and Lombardy Region - Amount: 25 lines in January, 17 in February Data for the regional Trenord railway service has significantly worsened. The Democratic Party, with regional councilor Samuele Astuti, has criticized the situation, describing the start of 2026 as 'disastrous'. According to data processed by the regional Pd group, based on official communications from Trenord, between January 2025 and February 2026, 113 critical lines were compensated. More than a third of the months were compensated, with 5 entries in the list of problematic lines. ### Data analysis The right to compensation is triggered when more than 10% of trains on a line, over the course of a month, are canceled or arrive at their destination with a delay of more than 15 minutes. In January 2026, 25 lines had performance below an acceptable level, while in February there were 17. These numbers are much higher compared to the entire 2025, when only three lines had been reported as problematic. ### Political reactions Regional councilor of the Pd Samuele Astuti stated: 'These are alarming data that mark a disastrous start to 2026 for commuters and Trenord users. Evidently, something is not working, and it is not enough to blame the strikes, which, however, have been resolved in one or two days. The problem is persistent and Assessor Lucente must explain what is happening.' Dettagli operativi

Analysis of the causes The causes of Trenord's service disruptions have been primarily attributed to strikes, as indicated in the company's official communication. However, Pd regional councilor Samuele Astuti has expressed doubts about this explanation, emphasizing that the problem is persistent and cannot be attributed solely to a few days of strikes. ### Impact on commuters Trenord's service disruptions significantly impact commuters who daily use trains to travel between Canton Ticino and Lombardy. Train delays and cancellations can cause punctuality issues at work and increase travel times, negatively affecting the quality of life for cross-border workers. ### Comparison with other regions Compared to other Italian regions, the railway service offered by Trenord in Lombardy and Canton Ticino is considered one of the most problematic. This is highlighted by data on compensation, which show a high number of critical lines and a significant deterioration compared to the previous year. ### Future prospects To improve the situation, it is necessary for Trenord and the Lombardy Region to adopt concrete measures to reduce disruptions and guarantee a more reliable service to commuters and cross-border workers. This could include investments in railway line maintenance, the purchase of new trains, and the strengthening of personnel. ### Implications for cross-border workers Cross-border workers who use Trenord trains to travel daily between Canton Ticino and Lombardy can check if they are entitled to compensation by consulting Trenord's official website. Frequently Asked Questions
How can I check if I am entitled to compensation for Trenord service disruptions?
You can check if you are entitled to compensation by visiting Trenord's official website and checking if the line you use has been reported as problematic. If more than 10% of the trains on that line are cancelled or arrive with a delay of more than 15 minutes within a month, you are entitled to compensation.
What can I do if I have experienced service disruptions with Trenord?
You can report the disruptions directly to the company through their official website or by contacting customer service. Additionally, you can request compensation if you are entitled to it, following the instructions on Trenord's website.
