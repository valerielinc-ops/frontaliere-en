May Day: unions rally to defend wages and jobs

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 1 May 2026

Around fifty Swiss towns host demonstrations for International Workers' Day.

Contesto

In brief - Around fifty Swiss localities host events for May Day - The USS warns: the UDC initiative threatens jobs and wages - Numerous speeches scheduled with Socialist Party representatives ## Key facts - What: Events for International Workers' Day - When: May 1, 2026 - Where: Across the country - Who: Swiss Trade Union Federation (USS) - Amount: Not yet specified Around fifty Swiss localities are hosting events today, Friday, May 1st, for International Workers' Day. The unions' slogan is 'Defend wages and jobs. No to isolationism.' According to the Swiss Trade Union Federation (USS), the UDC initiative 'No to a Switzerland of 10 million' threatens jobs and wages. The end of free movement of people would mean the end of wage protection, the organization states in a press release. 'Weakening controls would stimulate wage dumping,' warns the USS, which calls for the rejection of the UDC proposal. Numerous speeches are scheduled. The president of the USS and Council of States member Pierre-Yves Maillard will speak in Lausanne. The co-presidents of the Socialist Party, Cédric Wermuth and Mattea Meyer, will visit Baden (AG) and then Langenthal (BE), and Unterseen (BE), respectively. The two socialist federal councillors will also give speeches: Beat Jans in Biel (BE) and Solothurn, Elisabeth Baume-Schneider in Liestal (BL). ### Implications for cross-border workers The May Day events not only represent a moment of union mobilization but also have a significant impact on cross-border workers employed in Switzerland. Defending wages and jobs is crucial for those who cross the Italian-Swiss border daily, especially in a context of economic and political uncertainty.
