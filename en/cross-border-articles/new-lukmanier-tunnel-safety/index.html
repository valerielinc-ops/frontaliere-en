Contesto

In brief - The new Lukmanier tunnel will improve road safety in the Canton of Ticino. - The tunnel construction is planned in the Canton of Ticino, between the municipalities of Acquarossa and Faido. - The goal is to reduce road accidents and improve traffic flow. - The project will be realized with cantonal and federal funds, for an estimated amount of 120 million Swiss francs. ## Key facts - What: Construction of a new tunnel at Lukmanier. - When: construction start planned for 2025, completion by 2030. - Where: Canton of Ticino, Switzerland, between the municipalities of Acquarossa and Faido. - Who: Canton of Ticino and Swiss Confederation. - Amount: 120 million Swiss francs. The Canton of Ticino and the Swiss Confederation are working together to improve road safety in the Lukmanier region. The construction of a new tunnel is planned to reduce the number of road accidents and improve traffic flow. The project will be financed with cantonal and federal funds, as part of the 2020-2030 road investment plan. Road safety is a priority for the Ticino and Swiss authorities. The new Lukmanier tunnel will be equipped with advanced safety technologies, such as traffic monitoring systems and LED lighting. The construction of the tunnel will be carried out to minimize the impact on the environment and traffic. ### Project details The new Lukmanier tunnel will be an important intervention to improve road safety in the region. The project involves the construction of a tunnel approximately 2 kilometers long and a service road for local traffic. New Lukmanier Tunnel for Enhanced Safety The analysis of road safety data in the Lukmanier region highlighted the need for interventions to reduce the number of accidents. The new tunnel will feature advanced safety technologies, including traffic monitoring systems and LED lighting. Construction will be carried out to minimize environmental and traffic impact. ### Benefits for Cross-Border Workers The new Lukmanier tunnel will be a significant improvement for road safety in the region. Cross-border workers using the Lukmanier pass to commute to work in Switzerland will benefit from a safer and faster route. Reduced road accidents and improved traffic flow will be key benefits for commuters. For instance, residents in Bellinzona and Locarno will find it easier to reach workplaces in northern Switzerland. The tunnel construction will also be an opportunity to enhance the region’s infrastructure and boost the economic competitiveness of the Ticino Canton. The new tunnel will serve as a crucial link between Switzerland and Italy, contributing to strengthened economic and social ties between the two regions.
