In brief - The asparagus of Cantello grows only in the municipality of Cantello - The variety is French, called 'Precoce d'Argenteuil' - The white color is obtained with the technique of blanching ## Key facts - What: White asparagus of Cantello - When: Cultivated between March and June - Where: Exclusively in the municipality of Cantello - Who: Local producers with De.C.O. brand - Amount: Maximum production of 8-9 tonnes per hectare The asparagus of Cantello is a unique product, protected by the Communal Denomination of Origin (De.C.O.). This brand guarantees that the asparagus is cultivated exclusively in the territory of the municipality of Cantello, respecting a precise specification that defines its characteristics. But what makes this vegetable so special? Here are five things to know. ### Origin and cultivation The asparagus of Cantello must be cultivated exclusively in the territory of the municipality of Cantello. This geographical requirement is fundamental to obtain the De.C.O. and does not allow exceptions. The link with that specific soil is an integral part of the product's identity. The variety used is the 'Precoce d'Argenteuil', a French cultivar that found ideal conditions in the Varese area to take root. ### Cultivation techniques The characteristic color of the asparagus of Cantello, white with the tip turning pink, is not accidental. It is the result of blanching, a technique that involves covering the shoots with a mound of earth to prevent exposure to light. This process prevents the synthesis of chlorophyll, maintaining the white color. Harvesting occurs when the shoot has reached a maximum of 22 centimeters, still buried or just emerged. Frequently Asked Questions
What is the De.C.O. of Cantello asparagus?
The De.C.O. (Denominazione Comunale di Origine) is a brand that guarantees that Cantello asparagus is cultivated exclusively in the territory of the municipality of Cantello, respecting a precise regulation that defines its characteristics.
Why is Cantello asparagus white?
The white color of Cantello asparagus is obtained through the technique of etiolamento, which involves covering the shoots with a mound of earth to prevent exposure to light and thus the synthesis of chlorophyll.
How is Cantello asparagus harvested?
The harvesting of Cantello asparagus is strictly manual and takes place between March and June using a sgorbia, a knife with a narrow and pointed blade that allows the shoot to be cut below the surface of the ground without damaging the plant's roots.
