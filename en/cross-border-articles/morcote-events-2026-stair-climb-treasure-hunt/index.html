Morcote kicks off the 2026 events season: staircase race and treasure hunt

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 27 April 2026

Two unmissable events: the Morcote Scal on May 2nd and the Treasure Hunt on May 9th in the most beautiful village of Canton Ticino.

Contesto

In brief - Morcote Scal on May 2 with 420 steps to conquer - Treasure Hunt on May 9 with riddles and point challenges - Mandatory registrations by April 30 ## Key facts - What: Morcote Scal and Treasure Hunt - When: May 2 and 9, 2026 - Where: Morcote, Canton Ticino - Who: Organized by the municipality of Morcote - Fee: 20 francs per team for the Treasure Hunt Morcote, one of the most beautiful villages in Canton Ticino, kicks off the 2026 event season with two unmissable appointments. On May 2, the Morcote Scal returns, an uphill race that turns the monumental staircases of the village into an open-air sports arena. The route winds through the historic streets of Morcote up to the Church of Santa Maria del Sasso, overcoming 420 steps. The format is direct elimination and not timed: participants compete in heats of five runners, with the top two advancing to the next round and the others admitted to a recovery heat. The novelty of this year is the KidScal, a category reserved for children aged 9 to 12. For each registration, 1 Swiss franc will be donated to the Amilcare Foundation, which operates in Ticino to support adolescents in difficulty. The race takes place from 13:30 to 18:00. Registrations are limited and mandatory and can be made on the site morcotescal.ch. Registrations on the day of the race will not be accepted. On May 9, following the success of last year, the Treasure Hunt returns with a renewed format through the alleys of Morcote. The historic core becomes a stage route where teams face riddles, puzzles, and point challenges. The ranking can change until the last station, making the race unpredictable and engaging for all ages. The meeting point is set by 9:00 AM at the Garavello parking garage, seventh floor. Practical Analysis: What to Expect from the Events The Morcote Scal represents a physical and mental challenge, with a course that tests endurance and the ability to overcome obstacles. The knockout formula ensures a high level of competition and engagement. The presence of the KidScal also offers younger participants the opportunity to join in and have fun in a safe and controlled environment. The Treasure Hunt, on the other hand, is an event that requires logical skills and teamwork. The point-based challenges and riddles make the race unpredictable, with the possibility of overturning the standings until the last station. This event is ideal for families and groups of friends who want to spend a day filled with fun and discovery. ### Comparison with the Previous Situation Compared to previous years, the Morcote Scal introduces the novelty of the KidScal, offering an additional opportunity for young participants. The Treasure Hunt, on the other hand, features a renewed format with new challenges and riddles, making the event even more engaging and challenging. Both events, however, maintain their essence of celebrating the culture and history of Morcote, offering participants a unique and unforgettable experience. ### Concrete Scenarios Imagine you are a cross-border worker living in Morcote and want to participate in the Morcote Scal. If you do not qualify, you can still participate in the recovery heat. At the end of the race, you can enjoy the breathtaking views of Morcote and contribute to a good cause. For the Treasure Hunt, bring a notepad and a pen to note down the answers to the riddles. 5. Participation: Show up at the meeting point indicated in the confirmation email. For the Morcote Scal, the meeting point is at the Church of Santa Maria del Sasso. For the Treasure Hunt, the meeting point is at the Garavello parking garage, seventh floor. ### Deadlines Registrations for both events are open until April 30, 2026.
