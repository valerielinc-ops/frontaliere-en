Two-year-old drowns in Morcote

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 30 April 2026

Tragedy in Vico Morcote: a two-year-old child drowned in a private pool. The alarm was raised in the early afternoon of Thursday, April 30th.

Contesto

In brief - Tragedy in Vico Morcote: 2-year-old child drowns - Alarm at 14 on Thursday, April 30 - Intervention by police and rescuers ## Key facts - What: Drowning of a two-year-old child - When: April 30, 2026, 14:00 - Where: Vico Morcote, Canton Ticino - Who: Common Alarm Center (Cecal), Cantonal Police, Ceresio Sud Police, Croce Verde di Lugano - Amount: Not specified In the early afternoon of Thursday, April 30, just before 14:00, the Common Alarm Center (Cecal) received a report regarding a two-year-old child found in a pool of a private residence in Vico Morcote. On the scene, officers from the Cantonal Police and, in support, the Ceresio Sud Police, as well as rescuers from the Croce Verde di Lugano, intervened. After performing resuscitation maneuvers, the child was transported by ambulance to the hospital where he died. To offer psychological support, the intervention of the Care Team was requested. ### The circumstances of the incident The exact circumstances of the incident have not yet been made public. However, the timely intervention of law enforcement and rescuers underscores the importance of readiness in emergency situations. The community of Vico Morcote is in mourning for the loss of such a young member. ### The importance of pool safety This tragic event once again raises the issue of pool safety, especially for young children. Frequently Asked Questions
What preventive measures can be taken to avoid accidents in the pool?
Adopting preventive measures such as fences, covers, and alarm systems can make a significant difference in preventing accidents. Fences around pools, for example, can prevent unauthorized access, especially by children. Pool covers, on the other hand, can prevent drowning when the pool is not in use.
What to do in case of an emergency in the pool?
In case of an emergency, it is crucial to act quickly and effectively. Immediately contact emergency services, perform resuscitation maneuvers if possible, provide accurate information to rescuers, and collaborate with authorities during investigations.
How can I support the affected family and promote pool safety?
