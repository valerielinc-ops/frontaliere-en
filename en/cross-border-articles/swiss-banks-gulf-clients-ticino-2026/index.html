Capital transfers and potential residence moves to Switzerland due to the Iran conflict

Contesto

In brief - Fund transfers to Switzerland underway - Stricter regulatory constraints for banks - Doubts about the stability of the Emirates ## Key facts - What: Capital transfers and potential residency transfers - When: From March 2026 - Where: Switzerland, Emirates - Who: Wealthy clients from the Gulf, Swiss banks - Amount: Almost a quarter of the assets managed in Switzerland come from the Gulf The war in Iran is having unexpected consequences, such as renewed interest in the Swiss financial hub. Some clients are already transferring funds, or even considering moving, as the conflict affects a region that aimed to promote economic and financial stability. It's not yet an exodus, but the signs are becoming clearer. ### Transfers underway 'Fund transfers are underway' confirms Patrick Akiki, head of the financial services market for the consulting firm PwC Switzerland. But it takes time, he continues, because 'regulatory constraints have changed. We are much stricter, and Swiss banks are doing what is necessary to ensure the compliance of cases before proceeding with the transfers.' He knows this from having had these discussions with numerous banks. He is not alone: other consultants make the same observation, even if they prefer not to be quoted. ### Banks' discretion Officially, most banks do not comment, prefer to remain discreet, perhaps to avoid giving the impression of taking advantage of a delicate situation. They also do not comment on another emerging trend, that of large fortunes that in recent years have moved to Dubai or to the other Emirates and now doubt their choice. They not only want to secure their money, but also to secure themselves. ### Doubts about the stability of the Emirates As for the Emirates, their model is faltering with the current...

Implications for cross-border workers The renewed interest in Switzerland could also have implications for cross-border workers in Ticino. According to Deloitte consultants' calculations, nearly a quarter of the wealth managed in Switzerland comes from this region. However, the current situation is leading to a reassessment of residence and investment choices. ### Future scenarios If the conflict were to persist, it is likely that more affluent individuals would consider Switzerland as a safe destination. This could lead to an increase in demand for luxury real estate, private schools, and specialized financial services. Furthermore, Switzerland could become an increasingly attractive destination for financial sector professionals. ### Concrete procedures For those considering a move, it is important to understand the procedures for obtaining a residence permit, opening a bank account, and managing tax issues. Swiss banks are ready to assist new clients, but it is crucial to respect regulatory constraints and ensure compliance. ### Comparison with the Emirates The Emirates are seeking to reassure their citizens and residents. Dubai has even relaxed the rules regarding forfait taxation, particularly regarding the number of days of residence required. What to do to move to Switzerland If you are considering moving to Switzerland, there are several important steps to follow. First, you need to obtain a residence permit. This can be done through the Swiss consulate in your country of origin or through the local authorities once you arrive in Switzerland. ### Opening a bank account Once you have obtained the residence permit, the next step is to open a bank account. Swiss banks are ready to assist you, but it is important to provide all the necessary documentation to comply with regulatory constraints. ### Tax management Tax management is another crucial aspect. Switzerland has double taxation agreements with many countries, which can help avoid double taxation. It is advisable to consult a tax expert to understand the specific implications for your situation. ### Schools and real estate If you have children, it is important to consider school options. Switzerland offers a wide range of private and public schools.
