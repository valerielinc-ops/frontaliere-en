Frequently Asked Questions
What is the current state of trade negotiations between Switzerland and the United States following the US Supreme Court ruling?
Negotiations continue beyond the March 31 deadline, with two meetings already held and two more planned, to reach a mutually beneficial agreement.
How does taxation change for Ticino companies that import fabrics from the United States after the Supreme Court ruling?
After the ruling, textiles imported from the United States are subject to an additional 10% tax, replacing the previous specific duties (e.g., 20%). For example, an order of 10,000 francs could have an increase of 1,000 francs.
How does the new 10% tax affect Ticino importers of industrial machinery from the USA?
The additional 10% tax now applies to all products imported from the USA, including industrial machinery. For example, an order of 50,000 francs will be increased by 5,000 francs, reducing the competitiveness of Ticino companies that depend on these products.
What are the alternatives for a Ticino company that wants to avoid the 10% tax on US products?
The only current alternative is to negotiate with US suppliers to reduce prices or look for suppliers in other countries. However, the current Swiss-US trade agreement could offer long-term solutions, but they are not yet operational.
Which Ticino products could benefit from a trade agreement with the USA according to the Swiss government's forecasts?
The Swiss government aims to export typical Ticino products such as cheese, beer and slaughtered meat. These sectors could see a reduction in trade barriers and an increase in exports to the US.
