Ticino ski resorts: funding review

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 29 April 2026

Parliamentary inquiry to revise funding for Ticino ski resorts, with 5.6 million for maintenance until 2028.

Contesto

In brief - Contribution of 5.6 million francs for Ticino ski resorts - Structural issues emerged since the '90s - Parliamentary question to review funding ## Key facts - What: Contribution for ski lift maintenance - When: Winter seasons until 2028 - Where: Ticino (Airolo, Bosco Gurin, Campo Blenio, Carì, Nara) - Who: Council of State of the Canton of Ticino - Amount: 5.6 million francs The last credit approved by the Canton of Ticino for the ski resorts is 5.6 million francs, destined for the maintenance of the ski lifts in the localities of Airolo, Bosco Gurin, Campo Blenio, Carì, and Nara. This contribution will cover the activities of the winter seasons until 2028. In the same parliamentary session, the credit for smaller ski resorts such as Alpe di Neggia, Bedea-Novaggio, Cioss-Prato, Mogno, AiroloLüina, Dalpe, and Prato Leventina was also increased. The approval of the contributions was accompanied by critical reflections from some deputies, leading to the presentation of a parliamentary question signed by Andrea Rigamonti (PLR) and co-signed by Boris Bignasca (Lega) and Evaristo Roncelli of Avanti con Ticino&Lavoro. The question aims to review the funding method of the ski resorts, highlighting the structural issues affecting these localities. ### Structural issues Over the past decades, the Canton of Ticino has consistently supported the ski lifts as a tool for regional and tourist policy. However, structural difficulties related to the economic sustainability of the ski resorts have emerged since the '90s. Several studies have highlighted recurring issues: low altitude of the facilities, strong dependence on natural snowfall, limited revenues, and constant need for investments to renew the infrastructure. ### Climate change and tourist preferences In recent...

Analysis of Implications The parliamentary question represents an important step in reviewing the financing system of Ticino's ski resorts. The highlighted criticalities, such as the low altitude of the facilities and the dependence on natural snowfall, require a more targeted and sustainable approach. ### Destagionalization and Sustainability The Canton of Ticino has already begun to orient its actions towards destagionalization and support for current management. This approach is fundamental to address climate change and the evolution of tourist preferences. However, it is necessary to ensure that public contributions are used efficiently and in a targeted manner, in order to maximize the territorial impact. ### Monitoring and Information Gathering The parliamentary question underlines the importance of monitoring and collecting information already during the first winter season after the credit is granted. This will allow the necessary corrections to be made in view of the next framework credit for the 2029/2030 season. Accurate monitoring is essential to ensure that public contributions are used effectively and that structural criticalities are addressed adequately. ### Infrastructure and Services The highlighted criticalities also concern the lack of adequate accommodation infrastructure and the difficulty of ensuring the opening of the facilities during the winter season. It is fundamental to invest in modern and sustainable infrastructure, as well as in quality services, to attract tourists and ensure the economic sustainability of the ski resorts. ### Comparisons with Other Regions It is useful to compare the situation of Ticino's ski resorts with that of other Alpine regions.
