Anna Zambon of the Gallarate PD asks the new president Agostini for guarantees for families and staff. Criticism of the administration for past management.

In brief - Nursery schools crisis in Gallarate, plan by June - New president Agostini, criticism of past management - Risk of school closures ## Key facts - What: Crisis at Gallarate Nursery Schools Foundation - When: Recovery plan expected by June 2026 - Where: Gallarate, Lombardy - Who: Anna Zambon (Pd Gallarate), Alessandro Agostini (new president) - Amount: Not specified The crisis at Gallarate's nursery schools is not inevitable. This is the strong message from Anna Zambon, a member of Gallarate's Pd, following the appointment of Alessandro Agostini as the new president of the Nursery Schools Foundation. The situation is delicate and concerns families, staff, and the entire city. The recovery plan, expected by June, must be respected to provide clarity on the future of the schools, classes, services, and staff. Zambon does not hesitate to criticize past management, calling it inadequate, and urges the administration to take political responsibility. The crisis did not start today and cannot be blamed on the new president, nor can it be presented as an inevitability. The challenge now is to avoid a setback in services with the possible closure of a school and many classes. ### Anna Zambon's Criticisms Anna Zambon, in her statement, wishes the new president Agostini good luck but emphasizes that his appointment cannot become an excuse to delay the expected answers. The recovery plan is due by June, and that deadline must be met. Families and educators need to know clearly what will happen next year. Zambon criticizes past management, calling it unwise, and asks the administration to clarify who chose, supported, and allowed them to operate. Dettagli operativi

The crisis of the kindergartens in Gallarate is an issue that concerns not only the city, but also the cross-border workers living in Ticino and working in Lombardy. The possible closure of school buildings and classes could have a significant impact on families who choose Gallarate for its proximity to the Swiss border. The situation requires special attention from local and regional authorities to ensure that educational services remain accessible and of high quality. Cross-border families, in particular, may face additional difficulties if adequate measures are not taken to prevent the closure of fundamental educational facilities. ### The implications for cross-border workers Cross-border workers living in Ticino and working in Lombardy could find themselves in a difficult situation if the kindergartens in Gallarate were to close. The proximity to the Swiss border makes Gallarate a popular choice for many families seeking a balance between work and family life. The closure of school buildings and classes could force families to look for alternatives in other cities, significantly impacting their lifestyle and daily routines. It is crucial that local and regional authorities work together to find solutions that ensure the continuity of educational services for all families, including those of cross-border workers. ### Possible solutions To prevent the closure of school buildings and classes, a concrete and detailed relaunch plan is necessary. Local authorities must collaborate with families, staff, and communities to find solutions that ensure the sustainability of Gallarate's kindergartens.
