Nature on the plate: Greenpeace's veggie cuisine in Lugano (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 7 May 2026

Greenpeace Switzerland promotes a veggie cuisine initiative in Lugano with a free meal for 300 people. Find out how and why.

Contesto

In brief - 300 people at a vegetarian event in Lugano - Free meals based on soy, beans, and peas - Greenpeace promotes plant-based proteins ## Key facts - What: Natur'a tavola initiative - When: 6 hours ago - Where: USI/SUPSI East Campus, Lugano - Who: Greenpeace Switzerland and local schools - Amount: 300 participants A healthy, quality, and sustainable diet. This is the theme of the initiative promoted today by Greenpeace Switzerland in collaboration with the schools in the neighborhood. A meal offered to the curious based on soy, beans, and peas to raise awareness about vegetarian cuisine. More than 300 people present at the East Campus of the city of Lugano. A completely vegetarian meal offered in Lugano. This is Natur'a tavola, the initiative promoted by Greenpeace Switzerland in collaboration with the educational institutions of the neighborhood that took place today at the USI/SUPSI East Campus. "For us, it was very important to say that proteins—which in public opinion are nutrients of animal origin—can also be of plant origin," says Abraham Dali, a collaborator of Greenpeace and organizer of Natur'a tavola. "Today, here in Lugano, we have proof because we offer a free meal to all the boys and girls and to the curious who want to try this cuisine. We really want to demonstrate that it is possible for every person to receive their protein needs solely from plant-based proteins." "We are serving crispy tofu like nuggets and a plant-based protein with barbecue sauce," says a chef. Another collaborator, who is preparing a plant-based mayonnaise, tells us that "this cuisine is very important. The idea was to show people that it is something very simple. Frequently Asked Questions
What is Natur'a tavola?
Natur'a tavola is an initiative promoted by Greenpeace Switzerland in collaboration with local schools to raise awareness about plant-based cooking. The event offers free meals based on plant proteins to demonstrate that it is possible to obtain all the necessary nutrients from plant sources.
What are the benefits of a plant-based diet?
A plant-based diet can offer numerous benefits, including a reduced risk of heart disease and diabetes, better digestion, and a lower environmental impact. Additionally, plant proteins are often more affordable and easier to prepare than animal proteins.
How can I start following a plant-based diet?
You can start by informing yourself about plant proteins, trying new recipes, planning your meals, buying quality ingredients, involving your family, and monitoring your progress. Use the [savings calculator](nav:cost-of-living) to see how a plant-based diet can affect your budget.
