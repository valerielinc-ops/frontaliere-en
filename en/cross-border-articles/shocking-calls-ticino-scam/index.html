Shock call scams in Chiasso and Biasca, two arrests The cantonal police of Ticino have recently arrested two people in Chiasso and Biasca for scams linked to so-called 'shock calls'. These scams consist of a phone contact with the victim, often under the pretext of being a relative in difficulty or a bank official, in order to obtain money or sensitive information. ⚠️ The police recommend being always prudent and vigilant when receiving suspicious calls. It is essential not to hand over money or assets to strangers and to report suspicious calls to the Common Alarm Center (CECAL) immediately. ## Concrete examples of scams - A victim from Chiasso received a call from an individual who claimed to be her son, stating that he had been involved in a road accident and needed urgent money to pay bail. Frequently Asked Questions
How to recognize and prevent 'so-called shock' scams in Switzerland, especially for the elderly?
Verify the identity of the caller, do not hand over money or personal information, and contact the authorities immediately if you have any concerns. Inform the elderly about the modus operandi of scammers.
What is the most effective way to verify the identity of a caller posing as a doctor or professional in Switzerland?
Ask for the phone number of the institution where the caller belongs and contact the institution directly using a reference phone number found in a trusted source (e.g., the hospital's official website).
What are the signs that a call from a Swiss number could be a 'shock call' scam?
Signs include urgent requests for money for family emergencies, excited voices pressing for an immediate response, and calls from unregistered numbers or with foreign area codes. In Ticino, 30% of reported scams are 'shock calls', often with numbers that simulate Switzerland (e.g. +41 91...).
How can I check if a Swiss doctor or public office is really calling me?
Do not trust the displayed number – scammers spoof it. Contact the institution or hospital directly via the official number (e.g. Lugano hospital: +41 91 811 11 11) or through your family doctor. The Ticino cantonal police always recommend verifying the call with an independent source.
What should I do if I have already handed over money to a scammer due to a 'shock call' in Ticino or Lombardy?
Report the incident immediately to the Ticino cantonal police (CECAL: +41 91 814 30 00) or to the carabinieri in Italy. In Ticino, in 2022, 70% of victims recovered part of the sum thanks to timely reports. Keep all evidence (receipts, records).
