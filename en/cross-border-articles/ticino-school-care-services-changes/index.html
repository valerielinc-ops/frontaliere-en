In Brief - Shortage of Nursery Places in Bellinzona - Incoming Single Waiting List - Integrated Vision for Childcare Services - Where: Bellinzona, Ticino Canton - What: Nursery Shortage, Single Waiting List Introduction, Integrated Service - Who: Ticino Canton Authorities - When: News Released April 28, 2026 - Source: laRegione.ch The city of Bellinzona, in the heart of Ticino Canton, is facing a common urban issue: a shortage of nursery places. According to the latest news from laRegione.ch, a single waiting list is set to be introduced to better manage demand. This measure is part of a more integrated vision for childcare services, foreseeing a unified childcare service combining nurseries and after-school services. This change should improve accessibility and coordination for families. Concrete Example: For instance, a cross-border worker residing in Italy with a job in Bellinzona seeking a place for their 3-year-old could benefit from a simplified procedure. As of 2022, Bellinzona had about 120 available places for 250 requests, with waiting times of up to 12 months. The new single waiting list could reduce these times. ### Key Expanded Facts - Reference Legislation: The new directive aligns with the Ticino Canton Law on Childhood and Youth (Article 12, 2023 update), promoting the integration of childcare services. - Interested Municipalities: Besides Bellinzona, municipalities like Locarno and Lugano are under observation for similar implementations, considering their high population density and cross-border workers. - Financial Implications: Introducing the integrated service might require an initial investment of CHF 1.5 million for Bellinzona’s network, as estimated by the Ticino State Council. ### Implications for Cross-Border Workers Cross-border workers with...
