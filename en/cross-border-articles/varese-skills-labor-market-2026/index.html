Varese: high demand but skilled workers hard to find

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 30 April 2026

Varese's job market shows a paradox: high demand but increasing difficulty in finding specialized skills, with a 70% employment rate and 3% unemployment.

Contesto

In brief - High demand for labor but skills hard to find - Employment rate at 70%, unemployment at 3% - Criticalities for skilled workers, technical and healthcare professions ## Key facts - What: Labor market paradox in Varese - When: 2025 and 2026 data - Where: Province of Varese - Who: Varese Chamber of Commerce - Amount: 51% of staff entries planned by Varese companies The labor market in the province of Varese is experiencing a contradictory period. Despite high demand for labor, companies are increasingly struggling to find workers with the required skills. This is what emerges from the analysis of data available on OsserVa, carried out by the Economic Analysis Office of the Varese Chamber of Commerce. According to Istat data from 2025, the labor market for residents in the province of Varese has shown improvement, with the employment rate approaching 70% and unemployment reaching physiological levels, equal to 3%. However, this positive dynamic leads to a reduction in the pool of available labor, with a consequent increase in difficulties in recruiting manpower. According to the latest data from the Excelsior Information System, recruitment difficulties have reached 51% of the staff entries planned by Varese companies. ### Labor market criticalities The greatest criticalities are concentrated in the productive area, particularly for specialized profiles (73% for skilled workers) and in the recruitment of technical professions (60%). Difficulties also persist for high qualifications such as managers, intellectual and scientific professions (55%). Analysis of the labor market's criticalities The paradox of Varese's labor market is linked to several factors, including demographic decline and the mismatch between the skills required and those available. Demographic decline, with increasingly smaller young generations, makes natural generational turnover complex. This phenomenon is particularly evident in the manufacturing sector, where the demand for skilled workers is constantly increasing. ### The skills mismatch The mismatch between the skills required and those available is a structural problem affecting Varese's labor market. Companies struggle to find workers with the specific skills needed to meet market demands. This problem is particularly evident in the healthcare sector, where the difficulty of finding doctors and life specialists is particularly high. ### Opportunities for cross-border workers The current situation of Varese's labor market represents an opportunity for cross-border workers who work in Switzerland and reside in Italy. The growing demand for specialized skills could translate into new job opportunities for cross-border workers, who could benefit from the living and working conditions offered by Switzerland. However, it is essential that cross-border workers are aware of the required skills and prepare adequately to meet the needs of the Swiss labor market. ### Initiatives to improve the territory's attractiveness To address these criticalities, it is necessary to strengthen the overall attractiveness of the territory, not only in terms of professional opportunities but also in terms of living conditions. Frequently Asked Questions
What are the most in-demand skills in the Varese job market?
The most in-demand skills in the Varese job market are those specialized in the manufacturing sector, such as skilled workers, technical professions, and healthcare professions. Recruitment difficulties are particularly high for doctors, life science specialists, and healthcare technicians.
How can I prepare to work in Switzerland?
To prepare to work in Switzerland, it is essential to identify the skills required in the sector you wish to work in and participate in training and updating courses. Additionally, it is important to apply for job offers using job search platforms such as Jobup.ch and Indeed.ch, and prepare the necessary documentation to obtain a work permit.
What are the requirements to obtain a work permit in Switzerland?
The requirements to obtain a work permit in Switzerland vary depending on the type of permit and the canton where you wish to work. In general, you need to submit an application to the competent canton, which will evaluate your request based on specific requirements. It is important to inform yourself about the procedures and specific requirements for cross-border workers.
