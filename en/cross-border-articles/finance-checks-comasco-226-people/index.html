Financial Guards' Checks: 226 People Identified in Como

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 27 April 2026

Operations by the Financial Guards in the province of Como: 226 people checked, 3 illegal workers discovered, and narcotic substances seized

Contesto

In brief - 226 people identified in Financial Police checks - 3 undocumented workers discovered - 14 grams of narcotic substances seized ## Key facts - What: Financial Police checks - When: April 2026 long weekend - Where: Province of Como, Italy - Who: Financial Police - Amount: 226 people identified, 3 undocumented workers, 14 grams of narcotic substances The Financial Police conducted a large-scale control operation in the province of Como over the long weekend. The operations involved various areas of Como, with 36 patrols and 77 officers engaged. In total, 150 vehicles were checked and 226 people identified. Among the most significant results, there was the seizure of over 14 grams of narcotic substances and the identification of three undocumented workers. In Alto Lario, between Menaggio and Oria, the officers carried out targeted interventions against drug trafficking. Two people were reported to the Prefecture and over 2 grams of hashish were seized. In Olgiatese, an individual was stopped with two joints. In Erba, a previous check led to the reporting of a person to the judicial authorities for possession of 12.60 grams of hashish and 997 euros in cash. In Como, the local Group's officers checked several commercial establishments, identifying irregular workers. In Erba, similar checks led to the discovery of three undocumented workers in the catering sector, with a request for activity suspension to the Labour Inspectorate. During the checks, an irregular worker was also identified and two fines were issued for violations related to electronic receipts. ### Implications for cross-border workers The Financial Police checks not only aim to combat undocumented work and drug trafficking but also have a significant impact on cross-border workers employed in Ita...
