Five facts about Swiss patents

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 1 May 2026

Switzerland is Europe's most innovative country when it comes to patents. Here are the most interesting figures from 2025.

Contesto

In brief - Switzerland leads in patents in Europe - Roche surpasses Novartis with 681 patents - 10% of patents concern electronic cigarettes ## Key facts - What: Patent filings in Switzerland - When: 2025 - Where: Switzerland - Who: Swiss companies, researchers, and developers - Amount: 9,914 patents filed Switzerland remains the most innovative country in Europe in terms of patents, according to statistics from the European Patent Office (EPO) in Munich. Last year, Swiss companies, researchers, and developers filed 9,914 patents, recording the highest per capita number ever. Roche is the Swiss company that, last year, filed the most patents with the European Patent Office, totaling 681 inventions and developments. By comparison, Novartis registered 94 filed patents. The apparent gap in innovation between Roche and its competitor is explained by the fact that Roche does not limit itself to the development of drugs but also designs machinery. Specifically, these are laboratory equipment intended for diagnostics, i.e., the identification of diseases, which analyze, among other things, blood samples. Novartis, on the other hand, does not have a comparable diagnostics division. A surprising figure concerns the two cigarette manufacturers, Philip Morris International and Japan Tobacco International: last year, the two companies filed a total of over 1,000 patents from Switzerland, representing 10% of the total. 'Since 2008, we have invested more than $14 billion in the development, production, and marketing of smoke-free products,' said Luisa Biegmann, spokesperson for Philip Morris, in response to our request. Dettagli operativi

Switzerland stands out not only for the absolute number of patents filed but also for the diversity of sectors involved. This indicates a solid and well-distributed innovation ecosystem. For every million inhabitants, Switzerland registers nearly 1,100 patent applications, followed by Finland with approximately 600 and Sweden with 450. In absolute terms, Switzerland ranks seventh. This data is significant for cross-border workers in Ticino, as it demonstrates that the region is a hub of innovation and technological development. Swiss companies are heavily investing in research and development, which can translate into new job opportunities and professional growth for cross-border workers. ### Implications for cross-border workers For cross-border workers in Ticino, the strong presence of innovative companies can mean greater career opportunities and access to cutting-edge technologies. Companies like Roche, Philip Morris, and TE Connectivity offer not only jobs but also a dynamic and stimulating work environment. Additionally, the presence of global research centers in Switzerland can attract international talent, creating a diverse and highly qualified community of professionals. ### Comparison with other countries Switzerland ranks seventh in absolute terms for the number of patents filed, surpassed by countries like the United States, China, Japan, South Korea, Germany, and France. However, in per capita terms, Switzerland is at the top, with nearly 1,100 patent applications for every million inhabitants.
