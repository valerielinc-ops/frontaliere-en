Swiss Post moves to Castel San Pietro grocery store (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 6 May 2026

Starting June 8th, postal services will be available at the grocery store in Largo Bernasconi. Extended hours and home delivery services.

Contesto

In brief - Postal services moved to the grocery store in Castel San Pietro - Extended hours from Monday to Saturday - Home delivery service for deposits and withdrawals ## Key facts - What: Postal services transfer - When: Starting June 8, 2026 - Where: Grocery store in Largo Bernasconi, Castel San Pietro - Who: Swiss Post - Hours: Monday to Friday 8:00-12:00 and 15:00-18:30, Saturday 8:00-12:00 and 15:00-17:30 Starting June 8, 2026, the postal services in Castel San Pietro will be hosted at the grocery store in Largo Bernasconi. This decision follows the decrease in demand at the counter over the past years. Swiss Post has collaborated with the municipality and local businesses to find a suitable solution for the population. On the afternoon of Wednesday, June 3, Swiss Post employees will be present at the local branch to inform the population about the change, answer questions, and offer support for the installation and use of the Post-App on their smartphones. Additionally, all households in the area will receive an informative leaflet with details about the new offer. Customers will benefit from extended opening hours compared to the past. In the future, postal services will be accessible from Monday to Friday from 8:00 to 12:00 and from 15:00 to 18:30, and on Saturdays from 8:00 to 12:00 and from 15:00 to 17:30. Additionally, with the 'home cash deposits and withdrawals' service, the population can perform these operations directly at their doorstep. ### Implications for cross-border workers The transfer of postal services to the grocery store represents an opportunity for cross-border workers residing in Castel San Pietro. Practical Analysis: Implications for Cross-Border Workers The transfer of postal services to the grocery store in Castel San Pietro has several practical implications for cross-border workers living and working in the area. Firstly, the extended hours offer greater flexibility for those with demanding work schedules. Being able to access postal services until 18:30 on weekdays and until 17:30 on Saturdays can make a big difference for those with little time during the week. Furthermore, the home delivery service for deposits and withdrawals is a novelty that can simplify the lives of cross-border workers. This service allows postal operations to be carried out without having to physically go to the store, saving time and effort. It is particularly useful for those who have difficulty moving around or who prefer to manage their finances comfortably from home. ### Comparison with the Previous Situation Before the transfer, postal services were offered in a dedicated branch with more limited hours. The new grocery store offers extended hours, making postal services more accessible. Additionally, the home delivery service represents an innovation that can simplify the lives of residents, including cross-border workers. The transfer of postal services to the grocery store is an example of how Swiss Post is adapting its network of branches to meet the needs of the population. This change could be a model for other locations, especially in areas with a significant presence of cross-border workers. ### Concrete Scenarios Imagine a cross-border worker who works in Lugano and resides in Castel San Pietro.
