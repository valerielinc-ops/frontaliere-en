Retail trade in Ticino: +2.7% in Q1 2026

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 30 April 2026

Retail trade in Ticino reports a 2.7% increase in revenue in the first quarter of 2026, with the food segment growing by 3.2%.

Contesto

In brief - Retail trade up 2.7% in Q1 2026 - Food segment +3.2%, non-food +1.8% - NielsenIQ data based on 40 major retailers ## Key facts - What: Increase in retail trade revenues - When: Q1 2026 - Where: Canton Ticino, Switzerland - Who: NielsenIQ (Niq) - Amount: +2.7% (total), +3.2% (food), +1.8% (non-food) Retail trade in Ticino recorded a significant increase in revenues in the first quarter of 2026. According to data collected by market research firm NielsenIQ (Niq), revenues rose by 2.7% year-on-year. The food segment saw an increase of 3.2%, while the non-food segment grew by 1.8%. "The market for fast-moving consumer goods is showing solid progress, with clear growth impulses in the food and fresh produce sectors," says Silke Volejnik, manager at Niq. "At the same time, consumer behavior remains differentiated, as price and volume effects influence trends differently depending on the product categories." The data published by Niq as part of its Markt Monitor Schweiz is based on collaboration with over 40 major Swiss retailers, representing approximately 40-50% of the sector's total turnover. Specialized trade is not included, and Markt Monitor Schweiz "does not claim to be representative of the entire market," Niq specifies. ### Implications for cross-border workers For cross-border workers employed in the retail trade sector in Ticino, this growth represents an opportunity.
