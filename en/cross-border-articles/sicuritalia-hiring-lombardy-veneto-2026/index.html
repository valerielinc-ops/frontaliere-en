Sicuritalia hires 150 security guards in Lombardy and Veneto (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 6 May 2026

The Italian security leader opens 200 positions, with 100 hires in Lombardy and 50 in Veneto. Permanent contracts.

Contesto

In brief - Sicuritalia hires 150 security guards in Lombardy and Veneto - 100 positions open in Lombardy and 50 in Veneto - Permanent contracts with onboarding for juniors ## Key facts - What: Hiring for security guards - When: 2026 - Where: Lombardy and Veneto - Who: Sicuritalia - Amount: 800 million euros in revenue and 18,000 employees Sicuritalia, a leading group in the security sector in Italy with over 150,000 clients, 800 million euros in revenue, and 18,000 employees, has announced the opening of 200 new positions for security guards. The selections are open throughout the national territory, with a significant strengthening of the workforce in the regions of Lombardy (100 positions) and Veneto (50 positions). The job opportunities concern various strategic sectors: Transport, Healthcare, and Industry. In the regions of Lombardy and Veneto, the hires will focus on surveillance services at hospitals, airports, train stations, and international tech companies. The requirements for candidates include being of legal age, EU citizenship, psycho-attitudinal requirements in accordance with the law, and possession or willingness to hold a weapon. The company offers a permanent contract, and for junior profiles, an onboarding process and support for obtaining the weapon permit and the prefectural decree are provided. Additionally, positions are open for Security Officers (unarmed security) for Organized Large Distribution (GDO). ### Sectors and locations The job opportunities concern various strategic sectors: Transport, Healthcare, and Industry. Implications for cross-border workers The new hires at Sicuritalia represent a significant opportunity for cross-border workers in Lombardy and Veneto. With 100 positions open in Lombardy and 50 in Veneto, cross-border workers can benefit from permanent contracts and an onboarding process for junior profiles. This is particularly relevant for those residing in Ticino and working in Italy, offering new career opportunities in the security sector. The open positions cover various strategic sectors, including Transport, Healthcare, and Industry, meaning cross-border workers can find jobs in different sectors and adapt to their skills and interests. Additionally, positions for Security Officers (unarmed security) for Organized Large-Scale Distribution (GDO) offer an alternative for those not interested in working with weapons. ### Comparison with the previous situation Before this initiative, job opportunities for cross-border workers in the security sector were limited and often temporary. Sicuritalia's new hires represent a significant change, offering permanent contracts and an onboarding process for junior profiles. This not only provides job stability but also a path for professional growth. Furthermore, the diversity of strategic sectors covered by the new positions offers cross-border workers greater flexibility in choosing the sector in which to work. ### Practical scenarios Imagine you are a cross-border worker residing in Ticino and working in Lombardy.
