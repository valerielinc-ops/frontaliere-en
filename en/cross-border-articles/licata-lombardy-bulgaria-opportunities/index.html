Licata: Schengen and euro open new opportunities for Lombardy and Bulgaria

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 29 April 2026

Giuseppe Licata, a regional councillor from Forza Italia, underlines the importance of strengthening the Lombardy-Bulgaria axis after the country's entry into Schengen and the Euro.

Contesto

In brief - Schengen and the euro open new opportunities for Lombardy and Bulgaria - Increase in tourist flows between Italy and Bulgaria - Giro d'Italia in Bulgaria from May 8 to 10 ## Key facts - What: Conference 'Bulgaria – the new sustainable tourist destination for the Italian market' - When: April 24, 2026 - Where: Sofia, Bulgaria - Who: Giuseppe Licata, Regional Councilor of Forza Italia - Amount: Not yet specified Giuseppe Licata, Regional Councilor of Forza Italia and member of the Productive Activities Commission, participated in Sofia at the conference 'Bulgaria – the new sustainable tourist destination for the Italian market: strategic partnership between the public and private sector'. The meeting, held on April 24, highlighted the importance of strengthening tourist and economic relations between Italy, particularly Lombardy, and Bulgaria, which recently joined the Schengen area and will enter the euro area in 2026. Licata stated: 'Adhesion to the Schengen area facilitates the circulation of people, professionals, and ideas. Entry into the euro strengthens economic stability, simplifies commercial exchanges, encourages investments, and fosters the development of stronger economic and social relations.' ### Cooperation and development Italy and Bulgaria have solid and growing economic relations, with Italy among the top three economic and commercial partners of the country. Lombardy, as the 'economic engine' of Italy, plays a central role in this relationship, both for its tourist vocation and industrial capacity and for the propensity of its companies to internationalize. Dettagli operativi

Practical implications for cross-border workers The entry of Bulgaria into the Schengen area and the euro opens new opportunities for cross-border workers who work in Lombardy and reside in Ticino. The simplification of commercial exchanges and economic stability can foster new collaborations and investments, creating a more stable and favorable economic environment. ### New job opportunities Strengthened cooperation between Lombardy and Bulgaria can lead to new job opportunities for cross-border workers. Lombardy companies might seek collaborations with Bulgarian firms, creating jobs both in Italy and Bulgaria. This could be particularly advantageous for professionals in the tourism and industrial sectors. ### Travel facilitation With Bulgaria's accession to the Schengen area, cross-border workers will benefit from easier and less costly travel between the two countries. The circulation of people will be facilitated, making it simpler for cross-border workers to move for work or tourism. ### Impact on tourism The increase in tourist flows between Italy and Bulgaria represents a great opportunity for cross-border workers in the tourism sector. The 'Grande Partenza' of the Giro d'Italia in Bulgaria, which will take place from May 8 to 10, is an event that could attract many Italian tourists, creating new job opportunities for cross-border workers. ### Comparisons before and after entry into the euro zone Before Bulgaria's entry into the euro zone, cross-border workers had to deal with currency exchanges and variable exchange rates, which could affect their earnings and expenses. With the adoption of the euro, cross-border workers will benefit from greater economic stability and simplification of commercial exchanges, making it easier to manage their finances. #...
