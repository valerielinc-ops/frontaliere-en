Spring bank holidays: heavy traffic on highways and border crossings (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 4 May 2026

The Federal Roads Office expects disruptions on A2, A13 and border crossings during the Ascension, Pentecost and Corpus Christi long weekends.

Contesto

In brief - Ascension, Pentecost, and Corpus Christi bank holidays - Heavy traffic on A2 and A13 - Queues also at Brogeda and Gaggiolo border crossings ## Key facts - What: Predictions of heavy traffic - When: 14-17 May, 22-25 May, 4-7 June 2026 - Where: A2, A13 motorways and border crossings - Who: Federal Roads Office (FEDRO) - Amount: Not specified The Federal Roads Office (FEDRO) has predicted a significant increase in traffic during the upcoming spring bank holidays, particularly during the Ascension (14-17 May), Pentecost (22-25 May) and Corpus Christi (4-7 June) holidays. These periods will see an increase in holiday-related travel and festive days, with consequent disruptions along the main road routes in the Canton of Ticino. ### Critical motorways and border crossings The main arteries affected will be the A2 motorway, which crosses the Gotthard, and the A13 motorway, which passes through the San Bernardino. FEDRO signals that the formation of queues on the main arteries often induces drivers to exit the motorway early, spilling onto the ordinary road network. This behavior causes disruptions in neighboring towns and can worsen the situation to the point of compromising local traffic functioning. The most critical sections include the A2 between the Belchen tunnel and Härkingen, in the Lucerne region (A2/A14) and between Erstfeld and Biasca, corresponding to the Gotthard tunnel. Analysis of Criticalities The increase in traffic during the spring bank holidays does not only concern the main highways, but also border crossings such as Brogeda and Gaggiolo. These crossings are critical points for cross-border workers who move daily between Italy and Switzerland. The expected queues could cause significant delays, affecting punctuality at work and the quality of life for cross-border commuters. ### Impact on Pedestrian and Cyclist Mobility In addition to automotive traffic, USTRA reports that pedestrian and cyclist mobility could also be affected. Cantonal and municipal networks might see an increase in pedestrian and cyclist traffic, especially in urban areas. This could cause inconvenience for those who use these means of transport to move daily. ### Tips for Cross-Border Workers For cross-border workers who need to cross the border during the spring bank holidays, it is advisable to plan trips in advance. USTRA suggests checking real-time traffic conditions and considering the use of public transport or alternative routes. Additionally, it is important to respect the expected travel times and avoid detours on the secondary network to avoid worsening the situation. ### Comparisons with Previous Years Compared to previous years, traffic forecasts for the 2026 spring bank holidays indicate a significant increase in queues on the main highways. This could be due to several factors, including the increase in the number of cross-border workers moving daily between Italy and Switzerland and the increase in tourism in the region. Frequently Asked Questions
What are the dates of the spring bank holidays in 2026?
The spring bank holidays in 2026 fall during the Ascension Day (14-17 May), Pentecost (22-25 May), and Corpus Christi (4-7 June) holidays.
Which are the most critical highways during the spring bank holidays?
The most critical highways during the spring bank holidays are the A2 and A13, which cross the Gotthard and San Bernardino passes. Additionally, border crossings like Brogeda and Gaggiolo may see an increase in traffic.
What are the alternatives to highway traffic?
Alternatives to highway traffic include the Great St. Bernard Pass, the Simplon Pass, and shuttle trains through Lötschberg and Simplon. Moreover, the Gotthard Pass will be reopened to traffic, offering another option for drivers.
