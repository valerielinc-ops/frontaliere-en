Youth Debate: 500 students compete in Bellinzona

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 27 April 2026

500 students compete in the final of 'Youth Debate' in Bellinzona, defending theses on current topics in front of a jury.

Contesto

In brief - 500 students compete in Bellinzona - Topics: 'No to a Switzerland of 10 million!' and 'Smartphones: no at school!' - Winners: Camignolo middle school and Liceo 2 of Lugano ## Key facts - What: Final of 'La gioventù dibatte' - When: April 27, 2026 - Where: Bellinzona - Who: 500 students from middle schools and high schools - Edition: 15th The final of 'La gioventù dibatte' took place in Bellinzona, an event that saw 500 students from middle schools and high schools compete in debates on current topics. The students had to defend an assigned thesis in front of a jury within set time limits. The final debate topics were 'No to a Switzerland of 10 million!' for high schools and 'Smartphones: no at school!' for middle schools. ### The challenges and the winners The winners of this 15th edition were the students from Camignolo middle school and the pair from Liceo 2 of Lugano. Annika Skandella from Camignolo middle schools highlighted how defending a thesis you don't fully agree with opens your eyes and allows you to see all points of view. Riccardo Ferrara from Liceo Lugano 3 stated that this experience made him grow and change his way of presenting himself and talking to others. ### The importance of debate Debate is a fundamental exercise to learn how to argue, understand the pros and cons of a topic, form a conscious opinion, and express it. In an era dominated by social media bubbles, this exercise is more necessary than ever. Sofia Carboni from Liceo Lugano 2 stated that discussing current topics is very useful to inform oneself about important and current issues.
