Sustainable cycling commuting in Insubria

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 28 April 2026

A teacher shares his experience of cycling between Milan and Varese, highlighting the challenges and potential of a more sustainable system.

Contesto

In brief - A professor from Insubria uses bike and train to reach the university - Critical route between Varese Nord and the Bizzozero campus - Lack of bike lanes and dangers for cyclists ## Key facts - What: Experience of cycling commuting - When: Currently - Where: Between Milan and Varese - Who: Alberto Castelli, professor at the University of Insubria - Amount: Not specified A professor at the University of Insubria, Alberto Castelli, has decided to share his experience of sustainable commuting between Milan and Varese. Every day, Castelli leaves his home by bicycle, reaches the Cadorna square station in Milan, where he takes the train with his bicycle, and gets off at Varese Nord. From there, he cycles to the Bizzozero campus, where he teaches. This method, which seems ideal for a northern European country, clashes with the infrastructural difficulties of Varese. The route from the Varese Nord railway station to the Bizzozero campus is not easy for cyclists. Castelli has to face two dangerous intersections, where it is advisable to get off the bike and cross on foot on the pedestrian crossings to avoid accidents. One of the most critical points is Largo Ennio Flaiano, where drivers come from two directions and are impatient to enter the highway. After these intersections, Castelli takes viale Borri, where neither bicycles nor pedestrians are allowed in some stretches. ### The cyclist's revenge Once arrived in front of the university campus, Castelli has his small revenge. While cars are congested in the parking lot and in line on Dunant street, he can tie his bike a few meters from the entrance of the building where he teaches. Castelli's experience highlights the potential of a more sustainable transport system, but also the challenges that still persist. The lack of adequate infrastructure for cyclists in Varese is a significant obstacle. A bike path connecting the center of Varese to the Bizzozero campus could make a big difference. ### Implications for cross-border commuters For cross-border workers who commute to Lombardy and live in Ticino, Castelli's experience offers an example of how sustainable commuting can be achieved. However, the infrastructural difficulties highlighted in his story show that there is still much to be done to make cycling a safe and accessible choice for everyone. ### Comparison with other cities In many cities in Northern Europe, such as Copenhagen and Amsterdam, cycling infrastructure is well developed and safe. This has led to a significant increase in the number of people choosing the bicycle as their main means of transport. In Varese, however, the situation is still far from these standards. ### Future scenarios If Varese decided to invest in cycling infrastructure, it could become a model for other Italian cities. What are the main obstacles for cycling commuting in Varese?
The main obstacles for cycling commuting in Varese are the lack of adequate infrastructure and the presence of critical points such as Largo Ennio Flaiano, where drivers come from two directions and are impatient to enter the highway. Additionally, Viale Borri is not designed for bicycles and pedestrians in some sections.
