Bellinzona 2025: 11 million leap forward

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 30 April 2026

Bellinzona's 2025 accounts close with a deficit of 2.7 million, improving by 10.7 million compared to the budget

Contesto

In brief - Bellinzona 2025 accounts: CHF 2.7 million deficit - Improvement of CHF 10.7 million compared to budget - Net investments of CHF 25 million ## Key facts - What: Bellinzona Municipality 2025 financial statement - When: 30 April 2026 - Where: Bellinzona, Canton Ticino - Who: Bellinzona Municipality - Amount: CHF 2.7 million deficit, CHF 10.7 million improvement The 2025 accounts for Bellinzona close with a CHF 2.7 million deficit, a significant improvement compared to the forecasted CHF 13.4 million deficit. This nearly CHF 11 million leap forward was made possible through cost containment and revenues exceeding expectations, including tax revenue. Despite this improvement, the Municipality fears financial uncertainties arising from the implementation of initiatives on health insurance premiums, which could negatively impact municipal finances. The message on the 2025 financial statement was approved by the Municipality and transmitted to the Municipal Council for examination. The accounts indicate a CHF 2.7 million deficit, worse by CHF 800,000 compared to 2024. The CHF 10.7 million improvement was made possible through cost containment and revenues exceeding expectations. The City's own capital remains above the CHF 60 million threshold. ### Investments and development Despite an overall still difficult situation, Bellinzona continued with investments, amounting to CHF 25 million net. The city surpassed 47,000 residents in 2025 and reached a cruising speed with the consolidation of structures. Personnel expenses were contained, with increases mainly linked to nursing homes and the Somen center. ### Impact of cantonal policies The Municipality emphasizes that this positive evolution clashes with cantonal policies that drain part of the resources.
