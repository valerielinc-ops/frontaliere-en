In brief - Varese celebrates San Vittore with the Girometta d’oro to Andrea Chiodi - Mera & Longhi and the 'super grandma' aged 102 also awarded - The mayor announces the new city theater ## Key facts - What: Girometta d’oro award ceremony - When: May 3, 2026 - Where: Varese - Who: Bosina Family, Regiù Luca Broggini - Amount: Not specified Varese woke up under the sign of the Bosina tradition. A Sunday, May 3, that started at dawn, when at six o’clock the torch left the Sacro Monte to descend towards the heart of the city, crossing the districts and bringing with it the sense of community that officially opens the celebrations for the patron saint, San Vittore. The solemn moment took place in the Salone Estense, where the Bosina Family, led by Regiù Luca Broggini, awarded the Girometta d’oro 2026 to director Andrea Chiodi. A well-deserved recognition for his ability to promote Varese through art. Chiodi accepted the award with gratitude: 'I am very moved because in this hall, when I was in high school, I started doing theater. Being a director in a city that does not have a drama theater is a curious challenge, a bit like a lifeguard who works among swimming pools, but this award is for all the people who with me have built this path.' During the award ceremony, the mayor Davide Galimberti wanted to underline the identity value of the day: 'It has emerged very well how the city should be proud of its fellow citizens and the ability to form and consolidate different realities that invest in themselves, from economic activities to figures like Andrea Chiodi.' Galimberti then reflected on the post-pandemic period: 'After Covid, it seemed that culture was dead, that there was no more perspective. The event continued with a well-attended procession escorted by the Flag Throwers of Ferno to the Basilica of San Vittore. Here, the traditional Faro ritual took place: the cotton ball was set on fire by Monsignor Gabriele Gioia, the city's provost, marking the official start of the patronal festivities. The ceremony highlighted the importance of community and tradition, fundamental elements for the city of Varese. The Girometta d'oro, a symbol of excellence and commitment, was awarded not only to recognize individual merits but also to celebrate each recipient's contribution to the growth and well-being of the Varese community. ### Implications for the Community The celebration of San Vittore and the awarding of the Girometta d'oro represent a moment of reflection and unity for the city of Varese. The presence of figures such as Andrea Chiodi, Mera & Longhi, and Felicita Maria Mortarino underscores the importance of valuing local traditions and promoting culture and entrepreneurship. The announcement of the new city theater by Mayor Galimberti is a positive sign for the future of the city, which aims to strengthen its cultural offerings and create new opportunities for citizens and visitors. ### Future Scenarios With the start of construction for the new city theater, Varese is preparing to become a cultural reference point for the region. This project, along with the valorization of local businesses and the celebration of traditions, will contribute to strengthening the city's identity and promoting tourism.
