La Palma Hotel in Muralto renews and targets year-round business

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 29 April 2026

The La Palma hotel in Muralto has completed a 12.7 million franc renovation and is changing strategy, staying open all year round.

Contesto

In brief - La Palma in Muralto renovated with a 12.7 million investment - Ticino365 strategy: open all year round - 73 rooms available, including three in the nearby Casa Palma ## Key facts - What: Renovation and new strategy of Hotel La Palma - When: Recent completion of works - Where: Muralto, Canton Ticino - Who: Hotel owners (Pajko Redzepagic and daughters Azra and Ema) - Amount: 12.7 million francs Hotel La Palma in Muralto, which has been present for over fifty years on the shores of Lake Maggiore, has completed a major renovation costing 12.7 million francs. The property, owned by Slovenian magnate Pajko Redzepagic and managed with the second generation of the family, daughters Azra and Ema, has decided to change strategy and remain open all year round, following the Ticino365 strategy. 'On the one hand, it was necessary, the structure has its age. On the other hand, it was important to be attractive again in terms of the market,' confirms director Philippe Schaerer. The renovation involved sanitary facilities, fire systems, air conditioners, and other infrastructure, for a total of 73 rooms available, including three in the nearby Casa Palma. 'We discussed last year how to do it. We made the decision to remain open this year, as a test to see how it goes. We are very positive about destagionalization: we are convinced that even in all periods it is possible to work,' explains Ema Redzepagic. ### Implications for cross-border workers The new strategy of Hotel La Palma could have a significant impact on cross-border workers working in Canton Ticino. With the year-round opening, the hotel could attract more tourists and visitors, offering new job opportunities and services for the local community.
