Drug trafficking halted at Brogeda: two arrested and two kilos of heroin seized (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 16 April 2026

Carabinieri seize 1.1 kg of heroin and 800 g of cocaine from a rental car at the Como Brogeda border crossing. Two Nigerian men, aged 30 and 33, have been arrested.

Contesto

TL;DR - Carabinieri seized 1.1 kg heroin, 800 g cocaine at Como Brogeda border. - Two Nigerian men arrested, 500 euros and phones seized. - Operation part of routine cross-border control between Italy and Switzerland. - Drug trafficking threat highlighted, strategic hub for narcotics trade. ## Key facts - Cosa: 1.1 kg heroin, 800 g cocaine, 500 euros, 3 phones seized. - Quando: April 15, 2026, around 10:00 PM. - Dove: Como Brogeda motorway border crossing, Italy-Switzerland. - Chi: Two Nigerian men, ages 30 and 33. - Importo: 1.1 kg heroin, 800 g cocaine, 500 euros in cash. - Scadenza: Standard legal procedures initiated for the suspects. - Operazione: Part of routine cross-border control services. - Strategic Hub: Como Brogeda area is a key transit point for drug trafficking. On Wednesday, April 15, 2026, around 10:00 PM, the Carabinieri of the Operational and Mobile Unit in Como carried out an on-the-spot arrest at the Como Brogeda motorway border crossing, which lies between Italy and Switzerland. The officers stopped two Nigerian men, aged 30 and 33, as they were returning to Italy in a rented car. The individuals’ visible nervousness during the routine check prompted the agents to conduct a thorough search of the vehicle. During the inspection, 1.1 kg of heroin was discovered — divided into 83 pellets and additional packages — along with 800 grams of cocaine, split into 13 pellets and fragments. In addition to the drugs, 500 euros in cash, suspected proceeds from illegal activities, were seized, along with three mobile phones that will be analyzed to reconstruct drug trafficking routes and contact networks. Frequently Asked Questions
What happened at the Como Brogeda crossing recently?
On 15 April 2026, the Carabinieri arrested two Nigerian men at the Como Brogeda crossing, seizing 1.1 kg of heroin, 800 grams of cocaine and 500 euros in cash. The operation, which took place around 22:00, is part of cross-border controls against drug trafficking, and could involve delays and more in-depth controls for those who cross the border.
Will the anti-drug operation in Brogeda affect waiting times for border guards?
Yes, the operation of 15 April 2026 at the Como Brogeda crossing could cause an increase in controls and, consequently, longer waiting times for border crossers. This is particularly likely in the evening or at night, with possible repercussions on the punctuality and scheduling of daily trips between Italy and Switzerland.
Why is the Como Brogeda crossing so relevant for drug trafficking?
Como Brogeda is a strategic point for cross-border drug trafficking thanks to its geographical position between the Canton of Ticino and the province of Como. It offers rapid communication routes between Italy and Switzerland, making it a logistical hub for the passage of drugs to European markets. The authorities maintain high levels of control precisely for this reason.
What are the penalties for drug trafficking in Switzerland and Italy?
In Switzerland, drug trafficking is regulated by the Federal Law on Narcotic Drugs (LStup) and provides for severe penalties. In Italy, Article 73 of the Criminal Code punishes imprisonment and drug dealing with penalties of up to 20 years' imprisonment. Both countries criminally prosecute the possession of drugs for the purpose of drug dealing.
Has there been an increase in drug seizures at the Como-Brogeda border in recent years?
Yes, the data shows a growing trend. In 2025, Como authorities seized over 500 kg of drugs along the Como-Brogeda route, registering a 15% increase compared to 2024. This increase underlines the growing threat of cross-border drug trafficking and the need to step up controls.
