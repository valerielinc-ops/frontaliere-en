Lake Maggiore: 20-30 million cubic meters more water

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 29 April 2026

The decision will increase water availability by 20-30 million cubic meters. Agreement between Italy and Switzerland for a 15 cm rise.

Contesto

In brief - Lake Maggiore: rise of 15 cm - Water availability +20-30 million cubic meters - Italy-Switzerland agreement for climate change adaptation ## Key facts - What: Rise in the level of Lake Maggiore - When: Recently decided - Where: Lake Maggiore, Italy-Switzerland border - Who: Po River Basin Authority, Piedmont and Lombardy Regions, Ticino Consortium, Italy-Switzerland Bilateral Body - Amount: 20-30 million cubic meters of additional water The decision to raise the level of Lake Maggiore by 15 centimeters, up to 1.40 meters, was made at the end of the Permanent Institutional Conference. This increase will lead to an increase in water availability between 20 and 30 million cubic meters. The agreement is the result of coordination led by the Po River Basin Authority, together with the Piedmont and Lombardy regions, the Ticino Consortium, the Italy-Switzerland Bilateral Body, and other stakeholders. Alessandro Delpiano, Secretary General of the Basin Authority, commented: 'Raising the level of Lake Maggiore by 15 centimeters is an extraordinary example of how we can and must adapt to climate change.' Delpiano continued by emphasizing that this decision will bring 'more water for agriculture, environmental mitigation, and the life of the Po up to its mouth, in full respect of hydraulic safety conditions and environmental protection.' ### Implications for cross-border workers The rise in the level of Lake Maggiore will have a significant impact on water resource management in the region. Practical Analysis The rise of Lake Maggiore represents an important step towards more sustainable management of water resources in the region. For cross-border workers living and working in the surrounding areas, this could mean greater water availability for agriculture and improved environmental conditions. However, it is important to monitor any changes in water levels that could affect daily activities and hydraulic safety. ### Impact on Agriculture The increased water availability will be particularly beneficial for agriculture in the region. Cross-border workers in the agricultural sector could see improved cultivation conditions and greater stability of water resources. This could lead to increased productivity and a reduction in concerns related to drought. ### Environmental Mitigation The rise of Lake Maggiore will also contribute to environmental mitigation. The greater water availability will help maintain aquatic ecosystems and improve water quality. This is particularly important for animal and plant species that depend on Lake Maggiore for their survival. ### Hydraulic Safety Despite the benefits, it is essential that authorities continue to closely monitor water levels to ensure hydraulic safety. Cross-border workers should be aware of potential risks related to the rise of the lake and take necessary precautions to protect their properties and activities. ### Concrete Procedures For cross-border workers living in the areas surrounding Lake Maggiore, it is important to follow the instructions of local authorities in case of changes in water levels. Frequently Asked Questions
What are the main benefits of raising Lake Maggiore?
The main benefits include greater water availability for agriculture, improved environmental conditions, and greater stability of water resources. This will contribute to environmental mitigation and hydraulic safety in the region.
How can I protect my property from potential risks related to the lake's rise?
To protect your property, it's important to follow the instructions of local authorities, install protective barriers, and regularly monitor the lake's conditions. Make sure you have an emergency plan in case of flooding or other water-related events.
What are the concrete procedures that cross-border workers should follow to adapt to the changes?
Cross-border workers should monitor water levels, optimize crop irrigation, participate in authority consultations, and adopt sustainable practices for water resource management. It's also important to respect regulations and guidelines related to water resource management.
