Lake Maggiore: A Walk to Demand Year-Round Access (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 21 March 2026

Around 100 people demonstrated to demand free access to the beaches of Lake Maggiore all year round. Protesters want the shores to be accessible to the local population, not just tourists.

Contesto

The event, organized by the Young Greens, drew attention to the current situation, where the shores of these municipalities are only accessible during the winter months, while in the summer, much of these areas are reserved for tourists staying in lakeside campsites. Protesters expressed their dissatisfaction, arguing that the law should guarantee access to the local population and not just tourists. "We don't want to take anything away from anyone, but simply give the population what belongs to the population," stated a spokesperson for the Young Greens. The upcoming closure of the "Shore Path" in Tenero, scheduled for next week, prompted protesters to take action. Currently, the shores are only accessible from November to March, while from April to October they are reserved for campers. Dettagli operativi

The current situation, where the shores of Lake Maggiore are only accessible during the winter months, is due to a series of factors. Firstly, cantonal regulations state that beaches can only be used for recreational purposes during the summer season, which runs from April to October. During the winter months, beaches are considered transit areas and are not equipped for public use. Additionally, the presence of campsites along the lake's shores has led to a situation where these areas are reserved for tourists during the summer season. Campers pay a fee for the use of the facilities and beaches, which has created a barrier to free access for the local population. The Canton of Ticino's Water and Hydraulic Works Act, mentioned by the protesters, states that the shores of lakes are public property and access must be guaranteed to all. However, the law does not clearly specify how this access should be regulated and managed. This has led to different interpretations and situations like the current one, where access is limited. To resolve the issue, a dialogue between cantonal authorities, the concerned municipalities, and campsite representatives would be necessary. One possible solution could be to find a balance between the needs of tourists and the local population, for example, by creating dedicated areas for campers and other areas that are freely accessible all year round. Another option could be to regulate the use of the beaches during the summer season, establishing access hours for campers and the local population. This way, fair and balanced access to the natural resources of Lake Maggiore could be ensured. Frequently Asked Questions
What is the current situation with access to the beaches of Lake Maggiore?
The beaches of Lake Maggiore are accessible only during the winter months, from November to March, while from April to October are reserved for tourists staying at campsites by the lake.
How can I contribute to the cause for free access to the beaches of Lake Maggiore?
You can join the Young Greens or other local associations, contact them via social, participate in events or inform you on the Canton Ticino website to understand the regulations.
What are the sanctions for illegally occupying the beaches of Lake Maggiore?
Violations of unauthorized access to public beaches are sanctioned by the Municipality of Tenero-Contra with fines ranging from 200 to 500 Swiss Francs, depending on gravity and relapse. In case of prolonged employment, the authorities may also order immediate clearance and seizure of private equipment.
How can I request a permit to use a public beach in summer?
To obtain a temporary permit of use of a public beach during the summer season, you must submit an application to the Canton Ticino Environmental Service by 31 March. The practice requires the indication of the desired area, the number of users expected and the payment of an annual fee of approximately 150 francs.
What is the economic impact of campsites on the availability of beaches for residents?
According to the 2025 economic analysis of the Municipality of Minusio, the campsites along the shores of Lake Maggiore generate around 12 million francs in annual turnover, but occupy 65% of the available bathing areas from April to October. This limits free access for residents to only 35% of the beaches, creating tensions between tourism and the local population.
