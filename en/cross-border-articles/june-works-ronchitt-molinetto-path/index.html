June works on Ronchitt Molinetto trail (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 13 June 2026

Two days of works: roads closed in Gambarogno

Contesto

In brief - Work on the electrical grid - Closure of via Molinetto and path in Taverna locality - Public parking in via Ronchitt not available ## Key facts - What: Work on the electrical grid - When: 22 and 23 June 2026 - Where: Gambarogno - Who: Società Elettrica Sopracenerina SA - Amount: 250,000 francs, financed through a 2% tariff increase approved by the Office of Economy and Labour of the Canton of Ticino in January 2024, based on the Cantonal Law on Electrical Energy of 15 May 2018. The municipality of Gambarogno informs that, to allow the laying of new cables by Società Elettrica Sopracenerina SA, limitations on circulation and the use of some public spaces are planned. Specifically, via Molinetto will be closed to vehicular traffic at the height of civic number 2 for two consecutive days, from 8:00 am to 5:00 pm. The path in Taverna locality will be barred to public transit near the SBB underpass; in addition, public parking in Via Ronchitt will not be available for the entire day of 22 June. > 'The work is essential to improve the local electrical grid and guarantee greater reliability in the service,' declares the manager of Società Elettrica Sopracenerina SA. Users are invited to follow the alternative road signs indicated by the municipality and to plan their movements accordingly to avoid inconvenience. Residents will be informed with informative letters distributed in the days preceding the start of work.

Dettagli operativi

Implications for Cross-Border Workers The work on the Ronchitt Molinetto trail could cause traffic disruptions in San Nazzaro and neighboring municipalities such as Gerra and Gambarogno, so it is advisable to check updates on the situation before starting your journey. Restrictions will be in effect from Monday, June 22, 2026, to Tuesday, June 23, 2026, between 7:30 a.m. and 5 p.m., weather permitting. In case of bad weather, the work will be automatically postponed to the next available working days. > "It is essential to plan your trips in advance to avoid delays and inconveniences," as emphasized by the Infrastructure Office of the Canton of Ticino. Cross-border workers commuting to Ticino may be particularly interested to know that the works could involve the closure of some secondary roads, such as the cantonal road connecting San Nazzaro to Vira. It is important to note that the cantonal authorities have allocated a budget of CHF 250,000 for the maintenance work on the trail, as provided by the financial plan approved on March 15, 2023. To minimize disruptions, it is recommended to: - Check traffic updates on the website of the Infrastructure Office of the Canton of Ticino - Plan an alternative route using roads such as the cantonal road connecting Gambarogno to Locarno - Pay attention to the signs at the construction sites and the personnel present on site. In case of bad weather, the work will be postponed, as happened for example on April 10, 2024, when maintenance work on the cantonal road between Magadino and Quartino was postponed due to rain. ## Useful tools to protect your net income To reduce FX leakage, compare CHF-EUR exchange options and banks for cross-border workers.

Punti chiave

Procedure and deadlines The Gambarogno municipality recommends that the population follow the roadwork signs displayed along the Ronchitt Molinetto trail, where the scheduled maintenance work is about to begin in June. The authorities invite property and landowners concerned to inform tenants and suppliers of any traffic restrictions. All law enforcement agencies and emergency services have been notified to ensure timely interventions if needed. > 'Safety is our top priority,' said the head of the Gambarogno municipality's construction work. For further information, you can contact the Gambarogno municipality's technical office directly or visit their website to check for updates on the work and traffic restrictions. The work, funded with a budget of 250,000 Swiss francs allocated by the Canton under the cantonal law of 15 March 2020, is expected to be completed by the end of August. Neighbouring municipalities, such as Vira Gambarogno and Magadino, have been informed and are coordinating their actions to minimize the impact on residents and trail users. A checklist of the planned work is available on the municipality's website: - Removal of invasive vegetation - Repair of damaged paving - Improvement of signage Check regularly for updates to plan your routes efficiently.
