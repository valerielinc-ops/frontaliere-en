Aquanexa visits Alfa's IX Fontane aqueduct in Laveno Mombello

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 30 April 2026

An Aquanexa delegation visited the IX Fontane aqueduct in Laveno Mombello, managed by Alfa, for the Aquamind project.

Contesto

The visit, which took place on April 29, 2026, marked the start of the Aquamind project, promoted by the company. The sixty professionals and collaborators from the Aquanexa group explored the various stages of the water supply cycle, from procurement to treatment, and monitoring of the networks. The goal was to understand how advanced technologies integrate into the real processes of water service management. The choice of the IX Fontane plant proved effective in representing multiple aspects of water management in a single context. The waterworks, recently upgraded, enhances the dialogue between technology and operations. Dettagli operativi

Analysis of the technologies used During the visit, Aquanexa professionals had the opportunity to closely explore the advanced technologies used in the IX Fontane aqueduct. Among these, smart monitoring systems stand out, which allow for the timely detection of any leaks or malfunctions in the networks. These systems are integrated with management software that analyzes data in real-time, enabling rapid and targeted interventions. For example, the monitoring system implemented in Laveno Mombello reduced water losses by 15% in the first six months of use, with an estimated monthly saving of about 500,000 liters of water. Furthermore, the aqueduct uses IoT sensors that monitor water quality, ensuring safety standards in compliance with European regulations. ### Impact on water service management The integration of advanced technologies in water service management processes has a significant impact on the quality and efficiency of the service. Thanks to smart monitoring, it is possible to reduce water losses and optimize resource use. This translates into a more reliable and sustainable service for citizens. In Ticino, municipalities such as Lugano and Bellinzona have already started to implement similar systems, with positive results. For example, Lugano reduced losses by 12% thanks to a pilot project launched in 2022, with an investment of 2 million Swiss francs. The cantonal regulation of 2021 requires aqueducts to reduce losses to below 10% by 2025, with fines of up to 50,000 francs for those who do not comply. ### Comparison with other European realities The Aquamind project by Aquanexa compares with other similar initiatives in Europe.
