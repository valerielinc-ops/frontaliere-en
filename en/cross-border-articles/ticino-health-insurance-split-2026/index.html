Ticino health insurers: initiatives may split

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 28 April 2026

The Ticino State Council presents a partial plan for health insurance initiatives, but parties propose to separate them

Contesto

In brief - Two health insurance initiatives approved in Ticino last autumn - Government plan of 60 million for partial application - Proposal to split to avoid stalemate ## Key facts - What: Splitting of health insurance initiatives - When: Plan presented 2 weeks ago, discussion in Parliament - Where: Canton of Ticino - Who: State Council, Grand Council, political parties - Amount: 60 million francs The Ticino State Council has presented a plan to partially implement, starting from January 2027 and definitively from 2029, the health insurance initiatives promoted by the Lega and the Socialist Party, approved by the Ticino citizens last autumn. However, the proposed package, which provides for an investment of 60 million francs, has not convinced the main parties. In Parliament, in fact, the idea of treating the two proposals separately is emerging. ### Proposal to split During a meeting between the Government and Parliament, the day's president Fiorenzo Dadò, a Grand Councilor of the Centro, proposed to split the two initiatives. According to Dadò, the two proposals, although concerning health insurance, are substantially different and trying to finance them together could lead to a stalemate. 'We risk ending up next year without having achieved anything and therefore given nothing to the population, who instead expect help,' Dadò declared. ### Two different approaches The Government's package provides for the implementation of the Socialist Party's initiative, which proposes to increase the beneficiaries of subsidies, and the Lega's initiative, which proposes to completely exempt the premiums paid. According to Dadò, it would be appropriate to decide separately on these two issues, eventually leaving the Government to search for the necessary funds. ### Reactions...

Dettagli operativi

Analysis of the initiatives The two healthcare initiatives approved by Ticino citizens represent different approaches to managing public health. The Socialist Party's initiative aims to increase the number of subsidy beneficiaries, offering broader support to low-income families and workers. This could have a positive impact on cross-border workers in Ticino, many of whom could benefit from greater access to healthcare subsidies. On the other hand, the Lega dei Ticinesi's initiative proposes to completely exempt the premiums paid for healthcare. This approach could reduce the financial burden on taxpayers, including cross-border workers, who often face high healthcare costs. However, the complete exemption of premiums could have a significant impact on the Canton Ticino's budget, requiring additional funding or cuts in other areas. ### Comparison with the previous situation Before the approval of these initiatives, the healthcare system in Ticino was already under pressure due to rising healthcare costs and population growth. The two initiatives represent an attempt to address these challenges, but their practical and financial implications remain a subject of debate. The proposal to split the initiatives could offer a more flexible solution, allowing the Parliament to separately evaluate the merits of each initiative. ### Possible scenarios If the Gran Consiglio decided to accept only one of the two initiatives, the consequences could be significant. For example, the approval of the Socialist Party's initiative without the premium exemption could lead to an increase in public spending, with possible repercussions on the cantonal finances. What are the main differences between the two health insurance initiatives?
