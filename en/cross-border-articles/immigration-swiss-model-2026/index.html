Contesto

In brief - Switzerland is a demographic model thanks to immigration - Philippe Wanner: population will not reach 11 million - Economy more important than migration policies ## Key facts - What: Analysis of the Swiss demographic model - When: 4 May 2026 - Where: Switzerland, with a focus on Ticino - Who: Professor Philippe Wanner - Amount: Swiss population will not reach 11 million Professor Philippe Wanner explained why Switzerland is considered a demographic model. In an interview with laRegione.ch, the professor emphasized the crucial role of immigration in maintaining the country's demographic balance. Switzerland, thanks to its migration policy, has managed to compensate for the decline in birth rates and maintain an active and dynamic population. ### Impact on Ticino For the canton of Ticino, this situation is particularly relevant. The region, which borders Italy, is one of the most densely populated in Switzerland and greatly benefits from immigration. Cross-border workers, who work in Switzerland but reside in Italy, represent a significant part of the Ticino workforce. According to Wanner, the economy counts more than migration policies, and this is evident in how Ticino manages immigration. ### Comparison with other regions Unlike other Swiss regions, Ticino has a younger and more dynamic population thanks to the constant influx of foreign workers. This has a direct impact on social security, such as AHV and LPP, which benefit from a larger number of contributors. Additionally, the presence of cross-border workers significantly contributes to the local economy, with a positive impact on sectors such as trade, services, and industry. ### Future scenario Wanner stated that Switzerland will never reach the threshold of 11 million inhabitants.
