TiLo S40 disruptions: staff scheduling error

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 28 April 2026

Trenord explains the issues on April 25th: problem with staff shift scheduling. Here's what happened.

Giovanni, between Mendrisio and Varese - Who: Trenord - Amount: Not specified On Saturday, April 25th, the TiLo S40 line experienced disruptions due to limited rail traffic between Mendrisio and Como S. Giovanni, as well as between Mendrisio and Varese. Trenord explained that the issue was caused by a misalignment in staff shift scheduling, limited to Saturday only. Trenord's press office apologized to passengers for the inconvenience and specified that connections between Varese and Como and between these cities and Ticino were ensured respectively by the S50 Biasca-Varese-Malpensa line and the RE80 Milano-Como-Locarno and S10 lines. ### What happened On April 25th, disruptions on the TiLo S40 line were caused by limited rail traffic between Mendrisio and Como S. Giovanni, as well as between Mendrisio and Varese. The Swiss Federal Railways (FFS) had indicated a generic 'event in Italy' as the cause of the disruptions, explaining that the problem arose from a lack of staff in Italy. Today, Trenord has taken a position on the matter, clarifying that the service was not provided due to a misalignment in staff shift scheduling, limited to Saturday only. ### Implications for cross-border commuters Disruptions on the TiLo S40 line have had a significant impact on cross-border commuters who use this line for daily trips between Ticino and the Italian cities of Varese and Como. Analysis of the causes The error in staff scheduling is a problem that can have various causes, including lack of coordination between different operational units, shortage of qualified personnel, or ineffective management of human resources. In this case, Trenord has clarified that the misalignment was limited to Saturday only, which suggests that it might be an isolated error rather than a structural problem. ### Impact on cross-border commuters Cross-border commuters using the TiLo S40 line to travel daily between Ticino and the Italian cities of Varese and Como have been particularly affected by the disruptions. The lack of regular service has forced many to seek alternative transportation, such as buses or private cars, with additional costs and longer travel times. Moreover, the lack of timely information has made it difficult to plan movements, causing further inconveniences. ### Comparison with other lines The S50, RE80, and S10 lines have ensured connections between the affected cities, but they do not offer the same frequency and convenience as the TiLo S40 line. This has made it necessary for passengers to adapt, modifying their usual schedules and routes. Furthermore, alternative lines may not be as efficient in managing passenger flow, especially during peak hours. ### Future scenarios In the future, it is crucial that Trenord and the competent authorities work to prevent similar disruptions, ensuring accurate staff scheduling and better coordination between the different railway lines. Additionally, it is important to provide timely information to passengers so that they can adequately plan their movements.
