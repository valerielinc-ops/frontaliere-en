Ticino nurses: no reduction in working hours

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 28 April 2026

The National Council has decided to keep the working week for nurses at a maximum of 50 hours, rejecting the proposal to reduce it to 45 hours.

Contesto

In brief - Nurses' working week remains at 50 hours - Proposal to reduce it to 45 hours rejected - 14,000 vacancies in the sector ## Key facts - What: Decision on the duration of nurses' working week - When: 28 April 2026 - Where: Bern, Switzerland - Who: Swiss National Council - Amount: 50 hours (maximum duration of the working week) The Swiss National Council has decided to maintain the maximum duration of the working week for nurses at 50 hours, rejecting the proposal to reduce it to 45 hours. The decision was made during a special session in Bern, where the implementation of the popular initiative 'For strong nursing care,' approved by the people in 2021, was discussed. The majority rejected the idea of shortening the working week, opting for a normal duration of 42 hours, against the 40-42 hours requested by the Government and the 38 hours demanded by the left. The decision came after the submission of an urgent appeal signed by 190,000 people calling for the full and immediate application of the popular initiative. The debate is still ongoing, but the majority has already clearly expressed its position. Federal Councillor Elisabeth Baume-Schneider recalled that there are currently 14,000 vacancies in the nursing care sector, emphasizing the importance of improving working conditions to attract new professionals. ### Implications for cross-border workers For cross-border workers employed in the nursing care sector in Ticino, the decision of the National Council means that the situation remains unchanged. The maximum duration of the working week will remain at 50 hours, while the normal duration will be set at 42 hours.
