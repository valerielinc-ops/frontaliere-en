End to health insurers' millions for sports

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 29 April 2026

The National Council approves Lorenzo Quadri's motion to limit health insurers' sponsorships to major sports clubs.

Contesto

In brief - The National Council approves Lorenzo Quadri's motion - Health insurers will no longer be able to sponsor major sports clubs - The motion now goes to the Council of States ## Key facts - What: Amendment to the health insurance law - When: 29 April 2026 - Where: Bern, Switzerland - Who: National Council, Lorenzo Quadri (Lega/TI) - Vote: 117 in favor, 71 against, 4 abstentions The National Council approved with 117 votes in favor, 71 against, and 4 abstentions the motion by Lorenzo Quadri (Lega/TI) calling for an amendment to the health insurance law. The motion aims to limit health insurers' sponsorship of major sports clubs, allowing only support for small and local initiatives that promote movement. The Council of States will now examine the proposal. Quadri explained that the fact that health insurers, which offer basic insurance, sponsor major sports clubs while premiums increase, is increasingly criticized. It is not understood why premium money is used for clubs with million-dollar budgets. However, the Federal Council rejects the proposal, arguing that funds destined for sponsorships are part of the administrative costs of the insurers and that it is up to them to decide how much to invest in this form of advertising. ### Implications for cross-border workers Quadri's motion could have a significant impact on cross-border workers residing in Ticino and working in Italy. Many Swiss sports clubs, particularly those at the highest level, receive sponsorships from health insurers. If the motion is definitively approved, these clubs may have to seek other sources of financing, which could influence their ability to offer services and sports activities at affordable prices.
