Dettagli operativi

For Ticino’s cross-border workers, the ongoing negotiations between Switzerland and the United States represent a scenario that requires close monitoring, particularly in key sectors such as trade and logistics. While Switzerland seeks a stable and predictable trade agreement, the United States maintains a more rigid stance, as evidenced by the investigation launched in March 2026. This context creates a dual risk for cross-border workers: on one hand, a potential simplification of customs procedures for Ticino-based companies; on the other, a tightening of checks on products and services exported to the U.S. ### Possible scenarios for cross-border workers #### Scenario 1: Trade agreement with reduced tariffs If negotiations were to conclude successfully, Ticino-based companies could benefit from reduced or eliminated tariffs on certain products exported to the United States. This would streamline logistics operations for cross-border workers employed in sectors such as fashion, mechanical engineering, or dairy products. However, the direct impact on employees may not be immediate, as the benefits would primarily apply to businesses. #### Scenario 2: U.S. investigation and stricter inspections The investigation launched by the United States could lead to more stringent checks on Ticino goods entering the U.S. For cross-border workers operating in transport or logistics, this would mean longer wait times at border crossings such as Brogeda or Gaggiolo. Frequently Asked Questions
What is the main goal of the Switzerland-USA negotiations?
The objective is to establish a clear and stable trade framework that both parties can rely on in the future. According to Guy Parmelin, the aim is to create a lasting agreement, not to rush negotiations for quick results.
What does the US investigation against Switzerland involve?
The United States has launched an investigation into alleged unfair or discriminatory trade practices. The Federal Council has rejected the accusations, submitting a written defense within the set deadlines. This process is separate from the trade agreement negotiations.
How could life change for Ticino’s cross-border workers?
If the trade agreement is successfully concluded, Ticino-based companies could benefit from reduced or eliminated tariffs on certain products exported to the USA. However, the US investigation could lead to stricter controls and delays at border crossings.
Which sectors are most exposed to these changes?
The most exposed sectors include retail and wholesale trade, logistics and transport, and financial services, as these are directly involved in exporting to the United States.
Where can I find official updates on the negotiations?
You can follow official communications from the Federal Department of Economic Affairs (SECO), the State Secretariat for Economic Affairs (SECO), and the Ticino Cantonal Office for Foreign Trade for timely updates.
What should I do if my job is linked to exports to the USA?
Contact your employer to determine whether your company is involved in exporting to the USA and ask about any new tariffs, updated customs procedures, or potential transport delays.
