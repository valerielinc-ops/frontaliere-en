Frequently Asked Questions
How high is the price of gasoline in Switzerland compared to those in Italy?
According to data from the Federal Office of Energy, the average price of gasoline in Switzerland is about CHF 1.80 per litre, while in Italy it is about EUR 1.50 per litre, or about CHF 1.65 per litre.
How many litres of petrol can I import into Switzerland without incurring penalties?
Swiss legislation does not specify a quantitative limit for occasional refuelling abroad, but from 1 January 2022, a limit of 200 litres per person for the import of petrol has been introduced.
What are the travel costs for petrol in Como da Chiasso?
The cost of travel for petrol in Como da Chiasso is relatively low. The distance is about 15 km and the travel time is minimal. You have to consider the cost of fuel for the round trip, but the savings on the price of gasoline (up to 0.15 CHF/litre) often largely covers this expense, making the operation convenient.
Can I use my Swiss credit card to pay for gas in Como?
Generally yes, many petrol stations in Como accept international credit cards and ATMs, including Swiss ones. However, it is always advisable to check in advance if the chosen station accepts payments with Swiss cards to avoid inconveniences when refuelling.
Are there any additional taxes to be paid if I bring gasoline from Italy to Switzerland?
Swiss law provides for a limit on the import of petrol per person. As of 1 January 2022, this limit is set at 200 litres. Exceeding this amount could result in the payment of customs duties and VAT, so it is essential to comply with this limit to avoid penalties.
