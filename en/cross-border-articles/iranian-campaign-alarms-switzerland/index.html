"Sacrifice your life": Iran's campaign alarms Switzerland (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 3 May 2026

The Iranian embassy in Bern spreads an appeal urging Iranian citizens to sacrifice themselves for the homeland, causing concern in Switzerland.

Contesto

In brief - The Iranian embassy in Bern issues a call for life sacrifice - The Janfada campaign raises concern in Switzerland and other Western countries - The FDFA has discussed with the Iranian ambassador in Bern ## Key facts - What: Call for life sacrifice - When: May 3, 2026 - Where: Switzerland, Germany, United Kingdom - Who: Iranian embassy in Bern - Amount: Approximately 26 million people registered (unverified data) The Iranian embassy in Bern has issued a call urging Iranian citizens, both in the country and abroad, to 'sacrifice themselves for the homeland.' The message is part of the international campaign by the Tehran regime called Janfada, a term meaning 'I sacrifice my life' or 'self-sacrifice,' which is causing significant unease in several Western states. The campaign's symbol, reported by Nzz am Sonntag, is an image shared on the embassy's official channels: a soldier in combat uniform holding an Iranian flag. Above the image is the word 'Janfada' and an invitation to participate in the initiative. The stated goal is to show that the Iranian people would be ready to play an active role in the 'confrontation with the American-Zionist enemy' and in the 'defense of the Islamic Republic.' According to Iranian authorities, approximately 26 million people have already registered for the campaign, a figure that cannot be verified. However, according to various international reports, the message has been widely disseminated through SMS in Iran and via embassies in various countries around the world, including Switzerland, Germany, and the United Kingdom. High-level political figures have also publicly supported the initiative: President Masud Pezeshkian has stated that he is ready, like millions of Iranians, to 'sacrifice his life for the country.' ### Inter...

Dettagli operativi

Implications for Switzerland In Switzerland, the Federal Department of Foreign Affairs (FDFA) has confirmed awareness of the campaign and discussed it with the Iranian ambassador in Bern during regular diplomatic contacts. The FDFA reminds that foreign representations are obliged to respect international law and the laws of the host state. The Iranian embassy in Switzerland, which also shared the message on Telegram and the platform X, did not respond to media requests for clarification. In other countries, however, Iranian diplomatic representatives have stated that the campaign is exclusively 'symbolic' in nature and represents a political response to tensions with the United States and Israel, without any violent implications. An explanation that, however, appears to be in contrast with the rhetoric used in the official materials, where the theme of personal sacrifice and willingness to 'give one's life for the country' is central. ### Analysis of the Situation The concrete meaning of the campaign remains unclear and subject to divergent interpretations. In Germany, authorities speak of a 'mysterious recruitment campaign' by the Islamist regime, inserted into a broader strategy of mobilization against the 'American-Zionist enemy.' The domestic intelligence service hypothesizes that the context is related to Iran's fear of a possible U.S. military offensive. Even more decisive is the reaction of the United Kingdom, where the government has summoned the Iranian ambassador in London following content deemed 'unacceptable and inciting.'
