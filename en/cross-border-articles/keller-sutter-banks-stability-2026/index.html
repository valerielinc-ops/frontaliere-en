Keller-Sutter warns banks: 'All must contribute to stability' (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 25 May 2026

The Swiss finance minister warns the banking sector: Switzerland's stability is not guaranteed and requires responsibility from major banks as well.

Contesto

In brief - Swiss stability requires contribution from everyone, including banks. - UBS opposes the tightening of capital requirements. - Switzerland's AAA rating remained intact despite the rescue of Credit Suisse. ## Key facts - What: Finance Minister Karin Keller-Sutter warned banks about the need to contribute to stability. - When: 23 April 2026 - Where: Bern, during a private banking symposium. - Who: Karin Keller-Sutter, Swiss Federal Councillor and Finance Minister. - Amount: Over 100 billion francs for the rescue of Credit Suisse in March 2023. Swiss Finance Minister Karin Keller-Sutter has issued a warning to the banking sector, stating that the country's stability is not guaranteed and requires responsibility from major banks as well. In a global context marked by geopolitical tensions and economic uncertainties, Switzerland offers businesses something precious: stability. However, businesses also have a role to play. This was stated by the Federal Councillor during a symposium dedicated to private banking, organized by the Swiss Private Bankers Association (ASBP) and the Swiss Association of Asset Management and Institutional Banks (ABB). ### Warning to banks Keller-Sutter stated that 'Stability must also be contributed by those who benefit from it today but could put it at risk tomorrow.' The reference is clearly to UBS, which in recent weeks has opposed the government's projects to tighten capital requirements for systemically important institutions. 'The Federal Council appreciates that Switzerland has a major bank, but we must limit the risk to taxpayers,' added the 62-year-old. The stability of the Swiss banking system is crucial for the financial security of everyone, including transnational workers. However, the Credit Suisse case has shown that even the most solid systems can be vulnerable. The new regulations proposed by the Swiss government aim to further strengthen the stability of the banking system, reducing risks for taxpayers and ensuring greater security for everyone, including cross-border workers. ### Future scenarios If the new regulations are implemented, cross-border workers could benefit from an even more stable and secure banking system.
