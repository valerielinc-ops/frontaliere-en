School dental services: government tackles conflicts of interest (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 8 May 2026

The Ticino League questions the State Council on transparency and conflicts of interest in the awarding of school dental services

Contesto

In brief - The Lega dei Ticinesi questions the State Council about conflicts of interest in school dental services - A professional from the commission would have obtained the mandate - Logistical and territorial criteria for assignment are requested ## Key facts - What: Assignment of school dental service to a professional from the commission - When: Interrogation presented today - Where: Mendrisiotto municipality, Canton Ticino - Who: Lega dei Ticinesi, State Council - Amount: Not specified Following the assignment of the school dental service for a municipality in Mendrisiotto, the Lega dei Ticinesi has submitted an interrogation to the State Council. Coordinator Daniele Piccaluga and Boris Bignasca raise doubts about the transparency and organization of the procedures adopted by the Canton. According to the information gathered, the mandate would have been assigned to a professional who is part of the commission involved in the evaluation or assignment process. Meanwhile, a dentist active in the municipal area for several years and already operational in the local school context would not have obtained the mandate. ### Implications for cross-border workers Cross-border workers residing in Italy and working in Ticino could be affected by these procedures, especially if their children attend schools in the Canton. The issue of transparency and impartiality in the assignment of school services is crucial to ensure that services are provided fairly and without favoritism. Furthermore, the valorization of local professionals could reduce travel times for students, an important aspect for cross-border families. Dettagli operativi

• ## Analysis of the implications The situation that has emerged in the municipality of Mendrisiotto raises important issues not only for transparency, but also for the efficiency of school dental services. The possibility that a member of the commission could obtain a mandate within the same procedure raises doubts about the correctness of the process. Furthermore, the assignment of the service to a studio located outside the municipality entails additional travel for the students, with consequent loss of time and indirect costs that could be avoided by valuing professionals present in the municipal area. ### Comparison with the previous situation Before this interrogation, there were no doubts about the transparency of the procedures for assigning school dental services. However, the current situation demonstrates that a review of the methods of awarding mandates is necessary. Valuing local professionals could not only improve the efficiency of the services, but also reduce costs for families and schools. ### Future scenarios If the State Council decided to modify the legislation to prevent conflicts of interest, the procedures for assigning school dental services could become more transparent and impartial. This could lead to greater trust from families and a better quality of services offered. Furthermore, the valorization of local professionals could reduce travel times for students, improving their school experience. ### Impact on cross-border workers For cross-border workers who reside in Italy and work in Ticino, transparency and impartiality in the assignment of school services are fundamental. Frequently Asked Questions
What are the procedures for appointing and operating the commission responsible for assigning school dental services?
The procedures for appointing and operating the commission responsible for assigning school dental services are not yet specified. The Lega dei Ticinesi has submitted an interrogation to the State Council to request clarifications on this point.
Does the current legislation allow members of the commission to participate and obtain mandates within the procedures they are involved in?
The current legislation is not clear on this point. The Lega dei Ticinesi has asked the State Council to clarify whether the legislation allows members of the commission to participate and obtain mandates within the procedures they are involved in.
Are there specific provisions to prevent conflicts of interest or situations of incompatibility?
There are no specific provisions to prevent conflicts of interest or situations of incompatibility. The Lega dei Ticinesi has asked the State Council to consider introducing specific provisions to prevent such situations.
