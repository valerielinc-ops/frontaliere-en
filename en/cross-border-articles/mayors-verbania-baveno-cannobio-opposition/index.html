Italian mayors oppose Lake Maggiore rise

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 29 April 2026

The mayors of Verbania, Baveno, and Cannobio criticize the decision to raise Lake Maggiore's maximum level to 1.40 meters.

Contesto

In brief - Mayors of Verbania, Baveno, and Cannobio oppose the rise of Lake Maggiore - Maximum level raised to 1.40 meters above zero hydrometric - Criticism for damage to tourism and local economy ## Key facts - What: Raising the maximum level of Lake Maggiore - When: Recent decision, not yet specified - Where: Lake Maggiore, border between Italy and Switzerland - Who: Mayors of Verbania, Baveno, and Cannobio - Amount: 1.40 meters above zero hydrometric The mayors of Verbania, Giandomenico Albertella, Baveno, Alessandro Monti, and Cannobio, Gianmaria Minazzi, have expressed strong opposition to the decision to raise the maximum level of Lake Maggiore to 1.40 meters above the zero hydrometric level of Sesto Calende. This choice represents an increase of 15 centimeters compared to the previous threshold. The mayors define the decision as 'wrong and not shared with the territory,' arguing that 'one hundred and forty centimeters above zero hydrometric is a wrong choice' compared to the historical threshold of 100 centimeters managed for decades. According to the mayors, this increase will cause significant damage to tourism and the local economy, reducing the beaches, one of the main attractions of the lake. ### Criticism of communication methods The mayors also protest the way the decision was communicated. 'A process conducted predominantly at a technical level by the Po River Basin Authority, with the support of the irrigation needs of the Po Valley, without adequate protection of the interests of the riverside territories and without the effective involvement of the local administrations most directly affected,' they state. Analysis of Economic Implications The rise in the level of Lake Maggiore could have a significant impact on the local economy, particularly on the tourism sector. The beaches, which are one of the main attractions of the lake, could be reduced, decreasing the number of visitors and, consequently, the economic income for local communities. This could have a cascading effect on commercial activities such as restaurants, shops, and rental services, which depend on the flow of tourists for their survival. ### Environmental Impact In addition to the economic implications, the rise in the level of the lake could have an environmental impact. Coastal areas could suffer from erosion, and local flora and fauna could be affected. Furthermore, the rise in the level of the lake could increase the risk of flooding in some areas, requiring protective and adaptive measures. ### Comparison with Other Regions To compare the situation of Lake Maggiore with other regions, it is useful to consider how other coastal areas manage the levels of their lakes and rivers. For example, Lake Como has a similar management, with levels regulated to balance the needs of irrigation and protection of coastal areas. However, each lake has unique characteristics that require specific approaches. ### Future Scenarios If the decision to raise the level of Lake Maggiore is maintained, local communities will have to adapt to the new conditions. Frequently Asked Questions
What are the main concerns of the mayors regarding the rise of Lake Maggiore?
The mayors of Verbania, Baveno, and Cannobio are concerned about the damage to tourism and the local economy caused by the rise in the lake's water level. They argue that the beaches, one of the lake's main attractions, will be reduced, negatively impacting local economic activities. Additionally, they criticize the lack of involvement of local administrations in the decision-making process.
What can cross-border workers do to oppose the decision?

What are the environmental implications of the rise of Lake Maggiore?
The rise in the lake's water level could cause erosion of coastal areas, affect local flora and fauna, and increase the risk of flooding in some areas. These impacts require protective and adaptive measures to mitigate environmental damage.
