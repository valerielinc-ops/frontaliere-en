Lombardy invests 12.3 million in fashion: opportunities for Ticino cross-border workers (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 18 April 2026

Lombardy allocates over 12 million euros for the Next Fashion call for projects, involving 73 companies with a focus on SMEs and sustainability. How to involve Ticino-based businesses as well

Contesto

TL;DR - Lombardy allocates €12.3M for Next Fashion call. - 73 fashion businesses supported, focusing on innovation and sustainability. - Ticino companies can join if collaborating with Lombardy businesses. - Funding covers up to 60% of eligible expenses for micro and small businesses. ## Key facts - Investimento totale: €22.8 milioni - Fondo regionale: €12.3 milioni - Aziende finanziate: 73 - Provincia con più beneficiari: Milano (28 aziende) - Durata progetto: 24 mesi, estendibile a 30 - Finanziamento max: 1 milione di euro per partnership - Requisiti: Innovazione, sostenibilità, collaborazione tra 3-5 aziende - Scadenza domanda: Non ancora specificata Lombardy Region has allocated €12.3 million for the Next Fashion call for proposals, funded under the 2021–2027 Regional Fesr Programme. The initiative, promoted by the Departments of Tourism, Territorial Marketing and Fashion, and University and Research, supports 73 businesses in the fashion sector, with a particular focus on small and medium-sized enterprises and collaboration among entities of varying sizes. The funded projects must promote innovation, sustainability and the enhancement of high-quality craftsmanship, integrating digital technologies throughout the entire production chain. The call for proposals encouraged partnerships of 3 to 5 members, fostering technology transfer and the pooling of expertise. The total eligible investment amounts to €22.8 million, with regional funding covering up to 60% for micro and small businesses, 50% for medium-sized enterprises and 40% for large companies. The geographical distribution of beneficiaries shows a strong concentration in the province of Milan (28 businesses), followed by Bergamo (12) and Brescia (7). Dettagli operativi

For cross-border workers from Ticino, this call for proposals represents a concrete opportunity to engage with a rapidly evolving sector, marked by a growing focus on sustainability and innovation. Ticinese companies operating in the fashion industry — particularly those already collaborating with Lombardy — could apply as partners in funded partnerships, benefiting from a non-repayable grant covering up to 60% of eligible expenses. A key aspect is the project duration: funded activities must be completed within 24 months, with the possibility of an extension of up to 6 months. This means that participating Ticinese companies will need to meet strict deadlines, especially in areas such as process digitalization and environmental sustainability. ### Comparison with the current situation: what changes for cross-border workers? Before this call for proposals, collaboration opportunities between Ticino and Lombardy in the fashion sector were limited to isolated initiatives or smaller-scale projects. With "Next Fashion," however, Regione Lombardia introduces a structured mechanism that explicitly encourages cross-border collaboration, provided that participating companies meet sustainability and innovation requirements. Frequently Asked Questions
Which fashion sectors are eligible for the Next Fashion call for proposals?
The call for proposals funds projects in the fashion, textile and accessories sectors that promote innovation, sustainability and digitalisation. Activities that are purely commercial without a development plan are excluded.
Can a Ticino-based company apply on its own, or must it necessarily have Lombard partners?
The call for proposals encourages collaboration between companies, but it does not explicitly prohibit Ticino-based companies from applying as single entities. However, partnerships consisting of 3 to 5 companies are awarded a higher score during the evaluation process.
What is the maximum amount of the grant for a partnership?
The regional grant can reach up to **1 million euros per partnership**, regardless of the number of partners involved. The actual amount depends on the size of the companies and the financial plan submitted.
Must Ticino-based companies comply with Swiss regulations for projects funded by Regione Lombardia?
Yes, Ticino-based companies must ensure compliance with cantonal and federal Swiss regulations regarding state aid and grants. It is advisable to consult a tax advisor to avoid overlaps with any local restrictions.
What are the deadlines for submitting applications?
The deadline for submitting applications has not yet been specified in the source. It is essential to constantly monitor the official call for proposals website [Bandir Lombardia](https://bandir.lombardia.it) for any updates.
How can I find Lombard partners to participate in the call for proposals?
You can contact **Camerale Ticino**, **SUPSI**, **USI** or industry associations such as **Atic Ticino** and **Moda Ticino** to receive support in finding partners. Additionally, attending sector-specific trade fairs in Lombardy (e.g. **Milano Moda**) can help establish contacts.
