Canton Ticino, like the rest of Switzerland, is facing a significant challenge in the healthcare sector: a drug shortage. This problem, which has plagued the country for years, has prompted the Federal Council to take action. Recently, the Swiss government has presented a recipe, a set of concrete measures to tackle this shortage and ensure an adequate supply of medicines to the population. The drug shortage in Switzerland is a complex issue with various causes. One of the main reasons is the transfer of production to Asia, particularly China and India. This has led to supply disruptions, especially for affordable, everyday essential medicines such as antibiotics, and for those suffering from chronic illnesses, such as Parkinson's disease, epilepsy, or psychiatric disorders. Dettagli operativi

The measures proposed by the Federal Council have been well-received by the Ticinese population, who hope they will lead to a concrete and lasting solution to the problem. The Swiss government, aware of the seriousness of the situation, has decided to take decisive action, proposing a multi-level approach. Short-term measures, such as replacing information leaflets with QR codes, are already being implemented and should yield tangible results in the short term. Medium and long-term measures, however, require broader collaboration and a long-term commitment from all involved stakeholders. The Swiss government is working closely with healthcare actors, such as pharmacies, doctors, and pharmaceutical companies, to ensure these measures are effective and sustainable over time. One of the main challenges is reducing dependence on Asian production, particularly from China and India. The Swiss government is exploring various options to incentivize local and regional production, thus ensuring greater security in the supply of medicines. This process requires time and investment, but it is fundamental to reducing Switzerland's vulnerability in this sector. Another proposed measure is the categorization of medicines. This will allow for the quick identification of medicines most at risk of shortage and focus efforts on ensuring their availability. Additionally, better monitoring of supply difficulties will enable timely intervention and the finding of alternative solutions, such as importing medicines from other European countries. The counter-proposal to the popular initiative "Yes to Medical Supply Security" is another important step towards a lasting solution. Cheap drugs in Switzerland, what's new?
The Swiss government presented a recipe to counter the shortage of drugs, with short, medium and long term measures. Among these, the replacement of the illustrative leaflets with QR codes to reduce the plasters in the supply of drugs.
How will the new measures of the Swiss Federal Council affect access to drugs for Italian frontier workers in the Canton Ticino?
The new measures, such as the replacement of illustrative leaves with QR codes and the abolition of specific packaging for the Swiss market for approved drugs also in the EU, should facilitate the supply and reduce deficiencies, thus improving access to medicines also for Italian frontiers.
