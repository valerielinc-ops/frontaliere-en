Sesto Calende: fines for roads damaged by fiber optic sites

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 1 May 2026

The local police of Sesto Calende have started checks and fines for roads damaged by fiber optic construction sites, funded by the PNRR.

Contesto

In brief - Checks and fines for roads damaged by construction sites in Sesto Calende - PNRR-funded interventions for fiber optics - Local police issued fines to the executing company ## Key facts - What: Checks and fines for roads damaged by construction sites - When: April 22, 2026 - Where: Sesto Calende, Italy - Who: Local police and Technical Office of Sesto Calende - Amount: Not specified Citizens' reports and delays in restoring road surfacing prompted the administration of Sesto Calende to carry out rigorous controls through the local police and technical office. On Wednesday, April 22, work finally began to repair the road surface in areas where fiber optic laying had left open excavations or inadequate temporary coverings. As explained by Mayor Elisabetta Giordani's council, 'the interventions are not under the direct control of the municipal offices, but fall under national-level projects funded through the PNRR, aimed at covering the so-called “grey areas” with high-speed network infrastructure. Moreover, companies operating in the telecommunications sector act within the scope of the relevant national legislation, the so-called Telecommunications Decree, in force since 2003, which recognizes public utility and primary importance to these interventions, consequently limiting the margins of direct intervention by the Municipalities, even in terms of requesting guarantees or bonds.' The Administration defined the management of the past weeks as 'unacceptable,' emphasizing how the lack of timely restorations created potential damage and hazards for circulation and users.
