Piccaluga: 'Open dialogue with UDC, Norman and Claudio different souls' (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 4 May 2026

The coordinator of the Lega dei Ticinesi, Daniele Piccaluga, talks about the growing movement and the differences between Norman Gobbi and Claudio Zali.

Contesto

In brief - Piccaluga: 'I feel the base bubbling' - Two souls in the Lega: Zali 'free spirit', Gobbi 'team player' - Open dialogue with the UDC for the 2027 elections ## Key facts - What: Interview with Daniele Piccaluga, coordinator of the Lega dei Ticinesi - When: 12 hours ago - Where: La domenica del Corriere on Teleticino - Who: Daniele Piccaluga, Norman Gobbi, Claudio Zali - Amount: Not specified Daniele Piccaluga, coordinator of the Lega dei Ticinesi, has openly discussed the movement and its future prospects. In an interview with Gianni Righinetti for La domenica del Corriere on Teleticino, Piccaluga described a growing movement with a base that is 'bubbling'. He talked about the two souls of the Lega: Claudio Zali, described as a 'free spirit who says what he thinks', and Norman Gobbi, defined as a 'team player'. Piccaluga also confirmed that the mutual jabs between the two simply reflect their different characters. ### 2027 Elections On the political front, Piccaluga discussed the 2027 elections, hypothesizing a possible Lega derby if both candidates, Zali and Gobbi, were to run. He emphasized that there are open discussions with the UDC, but there are no vetoes or conditions. Relations with Piero Marchesi are described as 'good, frank, frequent'. Piccaluga expressed his personal preference for a joint run with the two Lega candidates. ### Candidacy for the State Council Regarding his potential candidacy for the State Council, Piccaluga did not close the door: 'If the base asked me, I wouldn't say no'. He also confirmed being summoned by the Hospita-Lega parliamentary inquiry commission and having responded present.
