Dettagli operativi

The housing shortage in Zurich is a problem that is deterring candidates from Zurich-based companies. According to a survey of businesses, housing is the top concern for companies. A 2023 survey by the London Chamber of Commerce found that 50% of companies said high house and rent prices negatively impact their business. The situation is particularly serious in Zurich, where the housing shortage is a serious problem. - In Zurich, the average price of an apartment is about 450,000 Swiss francs, up 10% from 2022. Rents, on the other hand, have reached 2,500 Swiss francs per month for a two-room apartment. - The Zurich Chamber of Commerce estimated that the city needs at least 10,000 new homes per year to meet demand. However, in 2022 only 4,500 homes were built. > The situation is also critical in the neighbouring Ticino municipalities, such as Winterthur and Stettfurt, where many Zurich workers are forced to look for more affordable housing. 📊 According to data from the Federal Statistical Office (UST), in 2022 Ticino recorded a 15% increase in housing demand, while supply increased by only 5%. 💡 The housing shortage is having a direct impact on Zurich companies, which are struggling to find candidates willing to move to the city due to high housing costs. - 60% of the companies interviewed said they had to give up qualified candidates because of the housing situation. - One of Zurich's largest companies, UBS, said it had to offer its employees a bonus of 10,000 Swiss francs to help them cover rent costs. ⚠️ The situation could worsen with the entry into force of the new housing regulation, scheduled for 2025, which provides for an increase in the quota of affordable housing from 30% to 50% of the total. For example, the Federal Housing Act (LHab) of 21 June 2019 provides that at least 20% of the housing built must be intended for social use. Frequently Asked Questions
What is the current situation of accommodation prices in Zurich?
The average price of an apartment in Zurich is around 450,000 Swiss francs, an increase of 10% compared to 2022.
What is the impact of the housing shortage on companies in Ticino and Zurich?
70% of companies in Ticino and 50% of those in Zurich consider the housing shortage to be an obstacle to economic development, with 60% of Zurich companies forced to forego qualified candidates.
How can I access the 2022 'Affordable Accommodation' program in Ticino?
The 'Affordable Housing' programme envisages the construction of 10,000 units by 2025. To access it, applicants must have been resident in Ticino for at least 2 years, have an annual income of less than CHF 80,000 and submit an application to the Cantonal Building Office by 30 June 2024. Applications are evaluated based on family priorities and job availability.
What are the average rental costs for a two-bedroom apartment in Zurich and Lugano?
In Zurich, the average monthly rent for a two-bedroom apartment is around 2,500 Swiss francs, according to 2023 data. In Lugano, the same type costs an average of CHF 1,800 per month, a difference of 28% compared to Zurich. Both cities show an annual increase of 5-7% in the last three years.
What tax incentives does the canton of Ticino offer to companies that finance employee housing?
The canton of Ticino allows companies to tax deduct up to 30% of the expenses incurred for employee rent support, with a maximum ceiling of CHF 12,000 per employee per year. In addition, companies participating in co-housing projects can benefit from an additional 5% tax credit on the value of the real estate investment.
