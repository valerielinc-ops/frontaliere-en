Practical implications of the new tax agreement The new tax agreement has introduced significant changes for cross-border workers, particularly for 'new cross-border workers'. The latter must now consider concurrent taxation between Italy and Switzerland, which requires more careful tax planning. It is important to consult an accountant experienced in cross-border taxation to optimize the tax return and avoid double payments. ### Comparison between old and new cross-border workers 'Old cross-border workers', hired before July 17, 2023, continue to benefit from the regime of exclusive taxation in Switzerland. This means they pay taxes only in Switzerland and do not have to worry about double taxation. For 'new cross-border workers', however, the situation is more complex and requires more accurate management of tax declarations. ### Savings strategies To maximize savings, cross-border workers can adopt various strategies. For example, doing weekly shopping in Italy for basic products and only buying specific products in Switzerland. Additionally, it is possible to save on fuel by choosing the cheapest gas stations, both in Italy and Switzerland. Another effective strategy is to use online price comparison services to find the best deals on goods and services. ### Impact of the CHF/EUR exchange rate The exchange rate between the Swiss franc and the euro can have a significant impact on the purchasing power of cross-border workers. It is important to constantly monitor the exchange rate and adjust spending accordingly. For example, if the euro weakens against the Swiss franc, it may be advantageous to make more purchases in Switzerland. Frequently Asked Questions
What are the advantages of living in Italy and working in Switzerland?
Living in Italy and working in Switzerland offers numerous advantages, including lower rents and utility bills, more accessible services, and generally more sustainable family management. Additionally, the purchasing power of Swiss francs in Italy is usually higher compared to the euro.
How can I optimize my expenses between Italy and Switzerland?
To optimize expenses between Italy and Switzerland, it's important to take inventory of fixed and variable expenses, compare the prices of basic products between the two countries, and use online price comparison tools. Additionally, it's useful to create a monthly budget and monitor expenses to stay within the planned limits.
What are the implications of the new tax agreement for cross-border workers?
The new tax agreement introduces a concurrent taxation between Italy and Switzerland for 'new cross-border workers', i.e., those who started working in Switzerland after July 17, 2023. This requires more careful tax planning and consultation with an accountant experienced in cross-border taxation.
