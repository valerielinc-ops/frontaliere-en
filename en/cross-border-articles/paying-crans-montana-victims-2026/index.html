Switzerland is considering covering healthcare costs for Italian victims of the Crans-Montana fire on January 1, 2026. During his meetings with Giorgia Meloni and Sergio Mattarella, Swiss Confederation President Guy Parmelin mentioned that Switzerland could cover hospitalization costs for Italian patients. The European regulation on the distribution of healthcare costs indeed provides an exception in this regard. "The regulation has so far been applied to all European countries and not just Italy," Parmelin stated. "However, there is a possibility of an exception, and we want to evaluate the consequences if the Federal Council wants to use it. Consequences not only from a financial point of view but also regulatory. We will conduct this analysis." ### Analysis of implications The idea of covering healthcare costs for Italian victims has sparked heated debate in Bern. While the Swiss government seems open to the possibility of using the exception provided by European regulations, the Swiss Parliament is divided. National Councilor of the Liberal Radical Party (PLR) Cyril Aellen criticized the proposal, arguing that the issue should be addressed with ordinary law.
