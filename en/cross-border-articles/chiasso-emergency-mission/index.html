Mission Emergency: Chiasso Becomes 'Capital' of Rescue and Safety Chiasso has transformed into a reality acceleration for a day, where law enforcement and rescue services have come together to present their activities and services to citizens. Among the participants are the Chiasso Municipal Police, the Cantonal Police, the TPO Transport Police, the Federal Customs Administration and Border Security (UDSC), and the Canton P Rescue Centre. These entities have set up stands throughout the city, where citizens can inform themselves and get a clearer idea of how law enforcement works and how it interacts with citizens. "Security is a fundamental public good, and as law enforcement, it is our duty to protect it," says the head of the Chiasso Municipal Police. "This event is a unique opportunity to make our services known and to listen to the concerns of the community." One concrete example of how law enforcement works together is the 'Border Security' project, launched in 2020 with a CHF 500,000.00 grant. This project has allowed for increased police presence along the Italian border and improved cooperation between law enforcement agencies. According to data provided by the authorities, in 2022, 12,500 border controls were carried out, with a 20% increase compared to 2021. The Cantonal Police presented its 'Road Safety' project, which involves the installation of 20 new cameras in critical points of the Ticino road network. This project was funded with CHF 1,200,000.00 from the Ticino Road Safety Fund. ⚠️ Citizens can also inform themselves about the 'Medical Emergency' project, launched in 2019 with a CHF 750,000.00 grant.
