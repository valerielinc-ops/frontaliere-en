What is the legal procedure for foreign minors arrested in Switzerland for crimes such as theft and vandalism?
Foreign minors arrested in Switzerland for offences such as theft and vandalism are subject to the Swiss juvenile legal system. This includes an investigation, a possible trial in a juvenile court, and penalties that can range from guardianship measures to prison sentences of up to 3 years, depending on the severity of the offense and the age of the minor.
What are the rights of Swiss cross-border commuters working in Italy in the event of customs checks?
Swiss cross-border commuters have the right to be informed of the reasons for the checks and to request consular assistance. During the checks, they must comply with border procedures and present the required documents, such as a passport or ID card and work documentation. Italian and Swiss regulations guarantee the protection of their fundamental rights.
