Free energy advice for Verbano Valley residents (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 6 May 2026

Bill savings, incentives, and renewables: researchers from the University of Insubria are available to help make your home more efficient

Contesto

In brief - Free energy advisory service for the residents of Verbano Valleys - Personalized support for energy efficiency - Available economic incentives at national and regional levels ## Key facts - What: Free energy advisory service - When: Active from 2026 - Where: Verbano Valleys - Who: Università degli Studi dell’Insubria - Cost: No cost for the citizen Within the Bosco Clima project, led by the Comunità Montana Valli del Verbano, a free advisory and information service on energy efficiency is also active. This service is aimed at all citizens who wish to reduce their energy consumption, carry out energy retrofitting interventions, evaluate the installation of a photovoltaic system, or approach the world of energy communities. The service is managed by researchers from Università degli Studi dell’Insubria and offers personalized support at no cost to the citizen. Improving the energy efficiency of one's home indeed brings concrete and measurable benefits: it reduces energy bills, increases the value of the property, improves living comfort, and contributes to reducing environmental impact. To all this are added the economic incentives currently available at national and regional levels, which make the choice of sustainable solutions and the use of renewable energies more accessible. ### How the advisory service works Anyone who turns to the Bosco Clima energy advisory service can receive personalized information on how to reduce their energy consumption, learn in detail about the incentives currently available, and obtain support for specific assessments, clarifying any doubts before making a decision. Dettagli operativi

Implications for cross-border workers For cross-border workers living in the Verbano Valleys and working in Switzerland, the energy desk represents a unique opportunity to improve the energy efficiency of their homes. With rising energy costs, reducing consumption can mean significant savings on bills, which can be reinvested in other expenses or saved. Moreover, the incentives available at national and regional levels make sustainable solutions, such as the installation of photovoltaic systems or the replacement of gas boilers with biomass boilers or heat pumps, more accessible. ### Before and after comparison Before the launch of the Bosco Clima project, citizens of the Verbano Valleys had to search for information and incentives to improve the energy efficiency of their homes on their own. This often involved long times and additional costs for consulting. With the free energy desk, citizens can now access personalized and qualified support, reducing the time and costs of the energy transition. ### Concrete scenarios Imagine a cross-border worker living in a house with an outdated gas boiler. Thanks to the energy desk, they can receive detailed information about the available incentives for replacing it with a biomass boiler or a heat pump. Additionally, they can evaluate the installation of a photovoltaic system to further reduce energy consumption and bills. With the desk's support, they can also participate in local energy communities, sharing the benefits of renewable energies with other citizens. ### Comparison with other regions Compared to other Italian regions, the Verbano Valleys offer a unique and free energy support service. Punti chiave

Concrete actions for cross-border workers To take advantage of the service offered by the Bosco Clima energy office, cross-border workers can follow these steps: 1. Inform yourself: Visit the site boscoclimaenergia.super.site to learn about the available interventions and incentives. 2. Download the guide: Download the 2026 Energy Saving Guide to have a first point of reference. 3. Contact the office: Write to boscoclimaenergia@vallidelverbano.va.it to schedule an appointment and receive personalized support. 4. Evaluate the options: With the help of the office, evaluate the different intervention options to improve the energy efficiency of your home. 5. Request incentives: Submit the application for the available national and regional incentives. 6. Carry out the interventions: Proceed with energy upgrading interventions, such as the installation of photovoltaic systems or the replacement of gas boilers. 7. Join energy communities: Consider the possibility of joining local energy communities to share the benefits of renewable energy. ### Useful tools To further explore, cross-border workers can use the energy savings calculator available on the Frontaliere Ticino website. This tool allows you to estimate the savings achievable with different energy efficiency interventions, helping to make informed decisions. ### Final CTA To schedule an appointment with the Bosco Clima energy office, write to boscoclimaenergia@vallidelverbano.va.it or visit the site boscoclimaenergia.super.site.
