The Angelo Vidoletti Movement denounces the state of disrepair of pedestrian crossings in Varese, asking for a structured maintenance plan

Contesto

In brief - Pedestrian crossings in Varese are poorly visible or erased - MAV urges the municipal administration to take urgent action - Highway Code requires maintenance for safety ## Key facts - What: Deterioration of pedestrian crossings in Varese - When: Reported on May 5, 2026 - Where: Municipality of Varese - Who: Movimento Angelo Vidoletti (MAV) - Amount: Not specified The Movimento Angelo Vidoletti (MAV) has raised a critical issue regarding the state of pedestrian crossings in the municipality of Varese. According to the movement, many pedestrian crossings are now poorly visible or completely erased, posing a serious risk to the safety of pedestrians, the elderly, and children. The leader of MAV, Stefania Bardelli, stated that the municipal administration has a legal and moral duty to intervene to ensure traffic safety, as required by the Highway Code. MAV has requested a structured maintenance plan across the entire municipal area to address this widespread problem. ### Implications for road safety The deterioration of pedestrian crossings not only represents an aesthetic problem but, above all, a concrete danger to road safety. The lack of visibility of the crossings can increase the risk of accidents, especially in areas with high pedestrian traffic. MAV has emphasized that the maintenance of pedestrian crossings is a legal responsibility of the entities owning the roads, as established by the Highway Code. The request for a structured maintenance plan is therefore a necessary step to ensure the safety of all citizens. ### The role of the Highway Code The Italian Highway Code requires road-owning entities to ensure the maintenance and safety of traffic.
