Streamlining public contracts and essential services

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 29 April 2026

The Canton of Ticino aims to simplify procedures for public contracts and essential services, with the goal of reducing delays and improving efficiency.

Analysis of Practical Implications The simplification of procedures for public contracts and essential services could have a significant impact on cross-border workers employed in these sectors. For example, waiting times for obtaining contracts could be reduced, allowing for greater continuity in work. Currently, the average time to obtain a public contract in Ticino is around 6-8 months. With the new procedures, a reduction of 30-40% is estimated, bringing the times to approximately 3-5 months. Furthermore, greater efficiency could translate into a reduction of costs by 15-20% and an improvement in the quality of services offered. > "Bureaucratic simplification is a fundamental step to attract investments and improve the quality of life of citizens," declared the Ticino State Council. ### Comparison with Other Regions Compared to other Swiss regions, the Canton of Ticino is making significant strides in simplifying administrative procedures. For example, in the Canton of Geneva, the average time to obtain a public contract is approximately 9-12 months, while in Ticino, the goal is to reduce it to 3-5 months. This could attract more cross-border workers and improve the competitiveness of the Canton. Additionally, greater efficiency could attract investments and create new job opportunities. Frequently Asked Questions
What changes for public procurement and essential services in Ticino?
The Canton of Ticino is simplifying procedures for public procurement and essential services, an initiative proposed by Patrick Rusconi (PLR) on 29 April 2026. The goal is to reconcile transparency and competition with operational speed, reducing delays. For example, the Municipality of Lugano has already reduced the approval times of orders by 30%, improving the efficiency of services.
How does the simplification of procedures affect border workers working in Ticino?
Border workers working in the public sector or in essential services will benefit from faster procedures. It is estimated that waiting times for orders will be reduced from 6-8 months to 3-5 months (about 30-40% less), improving work continuity and stability. This increased efficiency can translate into better working conditions and attract further opportunities.
What are the concrete economic and operational benefits of simplification in Ticino?
Simplification aims to reduce time and costs. The Municipality of Lugano has already reduced approval times by 30%, and Bellinzona has saved CHF 500,000 annually. At a general level, waiting times for orders are expected to be reduced by 30-40% (from 6-8 to 3-5 months) and administrative costs are expected to be reduced by 15-20%.
What is the reference law for public contracts in the Canton of Ticino?
The reference legislation is the Law on Public Procurement of the Canton of Ticino (LCP), which entered into force on 1 January 2023. Patrick Rusconi's initiative aims to complement this law with more flexible provisions, specifically for essential services, in order to further improve administrative efficiency and operational speed in the Canton.
Is Ticino more competitive than other Swiss cantons in managing public contracts?
Yes, the Canton of Ticino is making significant progress. While in the Canton of Geneva the average time for obtaining a public contract is about 9-12 months, in Ticino we aim to reduce them to 3-5 months. This increased efficiency aims to attract investment and new frontier workers, contributing to the competitiveness of the Canton.
