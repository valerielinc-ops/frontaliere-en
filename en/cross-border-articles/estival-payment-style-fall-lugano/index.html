Estival Jazz now paid: Lugano controversy

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 28 April 2026

Councillor Omar Wicht criticizes the paid evenings of the musical event, asking the Executive for clarifications

Contesto

In brief - Estival Jazz introduces paid evenings - Criticism from municipal councilor Omar Wicht - Citizen contribution of 1.5 million francs - Comparison with similar events in Switzerland ## Key facts - What: Introduction of paid evenings at Estival Jazz - When: 2026 - Where: Lugano - Who: Municipal councilor Omar Wicht - Amount: 1.5 million francs - Context: Citizen contribution for the 'Lugano capital of culture' project Estival Jazz, the historic musical event in Lugano, has sparked a political debate with the introduction of paid evenings. Municipal councilor of the Lega Omar Wicht presented an interrogation asking for clarifications on the amount of the citizen contribution for 2026 and the decision to make some evenings paid. Wicht criticizes this choice, calling it a 'loss of style,' and reminds that Estival Jazz has always been a free event, a heritage of the city. 'Estival Jazz was born in 1979 as a summer city event and over the years has become a reference point for generations of Luganesi,' says Wicht. 'Why turn it into another business opportunity?' The criticism also extends to the allocation of 1.5 million francs for the 'Lugano capital of culture' project, which Wicht did not support and now sees compromised by this decision. ### Implications for citizens The introduction of paid evenings could significantly change the accessibility of the event for citizens and tourists. Estival Jazz has always represented a free cultural opportunity, and the new policy could influence participation and appreciation of the event. Furthermore, the decision raises questions about the role of public contribution and the management of the city's cultural resources. 📊 Comparison with other cultural events - Montreux Jazz Festival: Introduction of paid tickets in 1995...

Dettagli operativi

The introduction of paid evenings at Estival Jazz raises several practical implications for citizens and the organization of the event. Firstly, free access has always been a distinctive feature of Estival Jazz, allowing a wide and diverse audience to participate. The new policy could limit accessibility, especially for families and young people with limited budgets. For example, in 2019, the festival recorded over 50,000 attendees, with an estimated economic impact of 3 million francs for the city of Lugano. Monetization could reduce these numbers, influencing both tourism and the local economy. ### Comparison with other cities Unlike other music festivals in Switzerland and Europe, Estival Jazz has always maintained free access, distinguishing itself for its inclusivity. The introduction of paid evenings could align the event with more commercial models, losing part of its unique appeal. For example, the Montreux Jazz Festival, which introduced paid tickets for some evenings, saw a 15% reduction in free attendance in 2022. Furthermore, the decision could influence the perception of the event as a cultural opportunity or as a paid entertainment product. In Ticino, municipalities like Bellinzona and Locarno have maintained free events, such as the Blues to Bop Festival and Moon & Stars, attracting respectively 20,000 and 30,000 visitors annually. ### Future scenarios If the trend towards monetization continues, Estival Jazz could see a reduction in the audience and a greater emphasis on commercial aspects. However, if the event manages to balance paid evenings with free ones, it could maintain its inclusive character. Frequently Asked Questions
Why is Estival Jazz introducing paid nights?
Estival Jazz introduces paid nights to improve the quality of the event and increase revenues. However, this decision has raised criticism, as the event has always been free and represents a cultural heritage for the city of Lugano.
What is the amount of the citizen contribution for Estival Jazz in 2026?
The city's contribution for Estival Jazz in 2026 is CHF 1.5 million. This funding is part of the 'Lugano capital of culture' project and aims to promote culture and art in the city.
What are the criticisms of City Councillor Omar Wicht?
City Councillor Omar Wicht criticises the introduction of paid nights at Estival Jazz, calling it a 'fall in style'. Wicht argues that the event has always been free and that this decision could turn it into a business opportunity, compromising its cultural value.
How does Estival Jazz compare to other cultural events in Switzerland?
Estival Jazz stands out for having always been free, unlike other events such as the Montreux Jazz Festival and the Locarno Film Festival, which introduced paid tickets. This difference has made Estival Jazz an inclusive and accessible event for everyone.
What are the risks and opportunities of introducing paid evenings?
Risks include a reduction in participation and a loss of appeal to the public. The opportunities, on the other hand, concern an increase in revenues and an improvement in the quality of the artists. The final decision will be up to the local authorities, who will have to balance these considerations.
