Ancient Greek certified in Lombardy: first in Italy

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 30 April 2026

From May 6 to 7, 2026, students from Lombardy's classical high schools will be able to obtain an official certification in Ancient Greek.

Contesto

In brief - First official certification in Ancient Greek in Lombardy - Tests from 6 to 7 May 2026 - Levels A1, A2, and B1 ## Key facts - What: Official certification in Ancient Greek - When: 6 and 7 May 2026 - Where: Participating schools and Catholic University of Milan - Who: Regional School Office for Lombardy, Lombard classical high schools, Catholic University of the Sacred Heart, University of Milan, University of Pavia - Cost: Not specified Lombardy is introducing the first official certification in Ancient Greek. A historic initiative promoted by the Regional School Office for Lombardy, marking a fundamental step in the renewal of classical language teaching. Director General Luciana Volta emphasized the collaboration between schools and universities, born from a memorandum of understanding involving three major universities: Catholic University of the Sacred Heart, University of Milan, and University of Pavia, along with the network of Lombard classical high schools. The tests will take place in two distinct moments: on 6 May 2026 for levels A1 and A2 in the computer labs of participating schools, and on 7 May 2026 for level B1 in person at the Catholic University of Milan, in Largo Gemelli. The certification system includes progressive levels – A1, A2, and B1 – accompanied by syllabi and essential vocabularies already published on the USR website, within the section dedicated to educational innovation. ### A model inspired by modern languages The goal is to make Ancient Greek a measurable, transparent, and recognizable skill, following the model of contemporary language certifications. Dettagli operativi

Implications for cross-border students The new certification in Ancient Greek represents a significant opportunity for cross-border students attending classical high schools in Lombardy. This initiative not only enhances linguistic and cultural skills but also provides formal recognition that can be included in academic and university curricula. ### Comparisons with the previous situation Before this certification, proficiency in Ancient Greek was primarily assessed through internal exams at individual schools. The new certification, however, introduces a standardized and regionally recognized system that allows for a transparent and comparable evaluation of students' skills. ### Practical scenarios Students who obtain the certification in Ancient Greek can benefit from a competitive advantage in their university path, especially in humanities faculties such as Literature, Philosophy, and History. Furthermore, the certification can be an added value in the curriculum for those intending to pursue careers in translation, teaching, or academic research. ### Concrete procedures To participate in the certification exams, students must register through their schools. The exams will take place in two distinct moments: May 6, 2026, for levels A1 and A2, and May 7, 2026, for level B1. Students must present an identity document and follow the instructions provided by the participating schools. ### Guidance value The certification in Ancient Greek also offers guidance value for students, allowing them to compare themselves with shared standards and make more informed future choices. Punti chiave

What to do to participate To participate in the ancient Greek certification, students must follow the procedures indicated by their schools. It is important to register by the established deadlines and prepare adequately for the scheduled tests. Participating schools will provide all the necessary information for registration and exam preparation. ### Deadlines and required documents The tests will take place on May 6th and 7th, 2026. Students must present a valid ID and follow the instructions provided by the schools. It is advisable to prepare using the syllabi and essential lexicons published on the USR website. ### Useful tools For further information and to prepare for the tests, students can consult the website of the Regional School Office for Lombardy and use the teaching materials provided by the schools. Additionally, they can use the language skills calculator to assess their level of preparation and plan their study path effectively. ### Conclusion The ancient Greek certification represents an important innovation in the field of classical language teaching. It offers students the opportunity to obtain formal recognition of their skills and to enhance their school curriculum. For cross-border workers attending classical high schools in Lombardy, this initiative represents a concrete opportunity to enrich their educational path and make future choices with greater awareness.
