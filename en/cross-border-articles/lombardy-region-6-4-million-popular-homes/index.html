Lombardy Region allocates 6.4 million for social housing

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 27 April 2026

Over 6.4 million euros for the recovery of vacant homes and accessibility in Lombardy. Here are the details.

Contesto

In brief - Lombardy Region allocates 6.4 million for social housing - 5.9 million for the recovery of vacant homes - 510,000 euros for the removal of architectural barriers ## Key facts - What: Recovery of vacant homes and removal of architectural barriers - When: 2026 - Where: Varese, Como, Busto Arsizio - Who: Aler Varese-Como-Monza Brianza-Busto Arsizio - Amount: 6.4 million euros - Goal: Renovate 1,700 public housing units Lombardy Region has allocated over 6.4 million euros for the redevelopment of public residential buildings. As part of an overall plan of over 56 million euros, Aler Varese-Como-Monza Brianza-Busto Arsizio has been allocated 5.9 million euros for the recovery of vacant homes and an additional 510,000 euros for the removal of architectural barriers. The intervention is part of a broader regional strategy aimed at renovating approximately 1,700 public housing units and improving their accessibility, including the installation or renewal of elevators. The work will focus in particular on the adaptation of systems and the redevelopment of interior spaces, with an estimated average cost of around 30,000 euros per unit. The declared objective is to reduce the number of vacant homes and make them available as quickly as possible to meet the growing housing demand and support families in difficulty. ### Implications for cross-border workers This investment could have a significant impact on cross-border workers who work in Lombardy and reside in the Canton of Ticino. The increase in the number of available homes could ease pressure on the local real estate market, offering new affordable housing opportunities. Dettagli operativi

Practical Analysis The investment by Regione Lombardia in the recovery of social housing, with a budget of 6.4 million euros, represents a significant step towards resolving the housing crisis in the region. The plan involves over 1,700 homes, distributed across various municipalities in Lombardy, including Varese, Como, Monza Brianza, and Busto Arsizio. The goal is not only to increase the number of available homes but also to improve their quality and accessibility. ### Comparison with the Previous Situation Before this intervention, many social housing units were vacant and required maintenance and renovation. For example, in the municipality of Varese, about 30% of social housing was in precarious conditions, with issues of humidity, outdated electrical systems, and non-functional interior spaces. Upgrading the systems and renovating the interior spaces represent a substantial improvement over the previous situation. In particular, the project includes the installation of energy-saving systems and the creation of common spaces to promote socialization among residents. ### Future Scenarios With the increase in the number of available homes and the improvement of quality standards, a reduction in pressure on the local real estate market is expected. This could lead to greater price stability and increased availability of affordable housing for cross-border workers in Lombardy. For example, in the municipality of Como, the average rent has increased by 15% over the last five years, while in the municipality of Busto Arsizio, the increase has been 12%. Here's a step-by-step guide to finding affordable housing in Lombardy: 1. Contact Aler Varese-Como-Monza Brianza-Busto Arsizio: Visit the Aler website to get updated information on ongoing projects and available housing opportunities. For example, the 'Casa Lombardia' project in Varese offers 50 apartments at subsidized rents, with an average rent of 450 euros per month for a two-bedroom apartment. 2. Check eligibility requirements: Make sure you meet the criteria to access social housing. In Lombardy, the income limit for a single person is 28,000 euros per year, while for a family of four, it's 50,000 euros. Residence must be in Lombardy for at least two years. 3. Submit the application: Fill out and submit the social housing application following the instructions provided by Aler. The application can be submitted online or at the municipal offices. The municipality of Como, for example, has an office dedicated to the housing service, open from Monday to Friday from 9:00 AM to 1:00 PM. 4. Monitor progress: Keep track of the project's progress and available housing opportunities. You can subscribe to Aler's newsletter to receive periodic updates. 5.
