Varese: New 2026 Chamber of Commerce Grants for the Integration of Foreign Citizens The investment of 150,000 euros in these two grants represents a strategic step aimed at enhancing the skills of foreign citizens and overcoming the logistical and training barriers that often hinder their integration into the local job market. In a context like that of Ticino, where approximately 20% of the workforce comes from neighboring countries or abroad, such measures play a fundamental role. Before this initiative, integration policies focused mainly on the social aspect, leaving less room for practical and concrete solutions such as vocational training and labor mobility. With the introduction of these grants, the reference framework changes, focusing on interventions that can produce tangible results in the short term. For example, the possibility of funding mobility projects improves the quality of life for foreign workers, reducing travel times and facilitating their presence in local businesses. In Ticino, municipalities like Mendrisio and Lugano have already launched internal mobility pilot projects, with results that have reduced travel times by 15% for some groups of workers, increasing their active participation. Furthermore, the focus on training meets a growing demand for specific skills, such as those required in the construction, hospitality, and logistics sectors. Vocational training, with update courses funded up to 10,000 euros per project, aims to bridge the gap between demand and supply, promoting more stable and lasting job integration. For example, in the municipality of Bellinzona, the training project allowed 120 foreign workers to obtain recognized qualifications, increasing the chances of stable employment by 25%.
