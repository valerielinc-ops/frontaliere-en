Sustainable architecture in Ticino: USI's new IAST

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 28 April 2026

The new Institute for Sustainable Architecture and Technology at USI promotes the reuse and conservation of existing buildings.

Contesto

In brief - New Institute for Sustainable Architecture and Technology (IAST) at USI - Building reuse as a priority - Digital technologies for preliminary analysis ## Key facts - What: Creation of the new Institute for Sustainable Architecture and Technology (IAST) - When: Officially inaugurated on April 16, 2026 - Where: Academy of Architecture, USI, Ticino - Who: Professor Ena Lloret-Fritschi and Professor Muck Petzet - Amount: Not specified Architecture is an act of transformation that shapes space to meet human needs. However, every building intervention involves a loss: something is altered, removed, or destroyed. Sustainability, on the other hand, aims to minimize environmental impact. This tension between the drive to build and the imperative to preserve is at the heart of today's debate. The new Institute for Sustainable Architecture and Technology (IAST) of the Academy of Architecture at USI, directed by Professor Ena Lloret-Fritschi and Professor Muck Petzet, was officially inaugurated on April 16, 2026. The institute brings together expertise in areas such as heritage, construction, reuse, structural engineering, and digital technologies, with the aim of approaching sustainability in an integrated and multidisciplinary way. ### The importance of reuse According to Professor Muck Petzet, building new structures requires considerable resources and energy and has a significant environmental impact. Quoting Luigi Snozzi, 'Every intervention involves destruction; destroy intelligently.' Architects have the responsibility to question the necessity of the act itself. We must ask ourselves whether an intervention, and in particular a new construction, is really necessary, or whether we can work with what already exists. Practical Implications for Cross-Border Workers The new IAST directives could have a significant impact on cross-border workers living and working in Ticino. Promoting the reuse and preservation of existing buildings could lead to a greater availability of affordable housing, reducing pressure on the real estate market. Additionally, the use of digital technologies for preliminary building analysis could accelerate renovation and upgrading processes, improving the quality of life for residents. ### Comparison with the Previous Situation Before the establishment of IAST, sustainability in architecture was often seen as a secondary goal, subordinate to the need to build new structures. With the advent of the new institute, there is a paradigm shift where sustainability becomes the guiding principle of every project. This integrated and multidisciplinary approach could lead to greater awareness and responsibility among architects and builders, with tangible benefits for the environment and the community. ### Future Scenarios If IAST successfully promotes the reuse and preservation of existing buildings, we could see a significant reduction in CO2 emissions and natural resource consumption. Furthermore, the use of digital technologies could make design and construction processes more efficient and transparent, improving building quality and user satisfaction. However, to achieve these goals, a change of mindset will be necessary not only among architects and builders but also among politicians and citizens. Frequently Asked Questions
What is IAST and what are its goals?
IAST is the Institute for Sustainable Architecture and Technology of the Academy of Architecture at USI. Its goals are to promote the reuse and conservation of existing buildings, using digital technologies and AI to reduce environmental impact and improve the quality of construction interventions.
How can I participate in IAST initiatives?
You can participate in IAST initiatives by registering for the workshops and seminars organized by the institute. Additionally, you can propose research projects or initiatives that promote sustainable architecture and collaborate with IAST to improve the quality of life and the environment in Ticino.
What are the benefits of sustainable architecture for cross-border workers?
Sustainable architecture can lead to a greater availability of affordable housing, reducing pressure on the real estate market. Furthermore, it can improve the quality of life for residents, thanks to the use of digital technologies for preliminary building analysis and the definition of targeted intervention strategies.
