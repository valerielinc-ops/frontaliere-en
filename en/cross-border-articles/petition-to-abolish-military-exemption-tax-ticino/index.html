Petition to abolish the military service exemption tax

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 26 April 2026

The Communist Youth launches a petition to abolish the military service exemption tax, defined as unfair and discriminatory.

The Communist Youth has launched a petition addressed to the Federal Council and the Federal Assembly with the aim of abolishing the military service exemption tax. During a press conference held in Bellinzona, the young communists presented the petition entitled 'Let's take back our money: abolish the military service exemption tax!', claiming the abolition of a tax that imposes on citizens not incorporated into military service an annual payment of 11 years equal to 3% of the taxable income, with a minimum annual amount set at around 400 francs. The tax has been criticized as a financial punishment for young people excluded from military service for health reasons, who are doubly disadvantaged compared to their peers in a context of youth precariousness and decreasing purchasing power. The European Court of Human Rights has on several occasions condemned the criteria on which this tax is based, defining them as discriminatory. Dettagli operativi

The petition launched by the Communist Youth to abolish the military service exemption tax raises important issues of equity and tax justice. The current tax imposes on citizens not incorporated into military service an annual payment of 11 years equal to 3% of the taxable income, with a minimum annual amount set at approximately 400 francs. This represents a significant financial burden for young people who are already penalized for physical or mental reasons. ### Comparison with the Previous Situation Before the introduction of this tax, citizens exempt from military service were not subject to additional payments. The introduction of the tax has created a disparity between young people who can perform military service and those who, for health reasons, cannot. The European Court of Human Rights has on several occasions condemned the criteria on which this tax is based, deeming them discriminatory. The rulings date back to 2009 and, more recently, to 2021. ### Future Scenarios If the petition were successful, the abolition of the military service exemption tax could bring significant financial relief to many young people. Furthermore, the full refund of taxes paid for the years following April 30, 2009, could represent an additional benefit for those who have paid this tax in violation of an international obligation already established and binding for Switzerland. This refund could help offset the financial difficulties that many young people face in a context of youth precarity and decreasing purchasing power. The campaign to abolish this tax is part of a broader battle against the tax pressure that rearmament and militarization policies are imposing on Swiss citizens.
