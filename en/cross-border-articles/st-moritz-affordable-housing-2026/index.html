St. Moritz builds homes with income-based pricing

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 27 April 2026

The municipality of St. Moritz is starting the construction of 19 affordable apartments with income-based pricing, with other projects for 150-200 housing units in the works.

By the summer of next year, 19 apartments will be ready. The income-based pricing system ensures that those who earn more pay more, and those who earn less pay less. 'We want a system where those who earn more pay more, and those who earn less pay less,' explains Vice Mayor Reto Matossi. 'We are thinking of a system to rent at an affordable price for those who do not earn 10-15,000 francs a month, such as those working in construction or in the hotel industry.' Frequently Asked Questions
How can I apply for affordable housing in St. Moritz?
To apply, you need to visit the website of the Municipality of St. Moritz or contact the relevant office for information on the application process and required criteria. It's important to gather all necessary documents, such as income certificates, employment contracts, and documentation of your current housing situation, and submit the application to the Municipality of St. Moritz.
What are the criteria for accessing affordable housing in St. Moritz?
The criteria for accessing affordable housing in St. Moritz are based on income. The Municipality will evaluate applications based on the applicant's income and current housing situation. It's important to provide all requested information and attach the necessary documentation to ensure the application is evaluated correctly.
How many homes will be built in St. Moritz?
Currently, 19 apartments are under construction in the Signal area, with other projects in the pipeline that foresee the construction of 150-200 new residential units. The Municipality of St. Moritz is committed to promoting the realization of public utility housing to address the shortage of primary housing in the area.
