Russotto on Bellinzona: 'A choice I might not repeat' (cross-border guide)

Andrea Russotto recalls his years in Ticino, marked by family sacrifices and the pressure of being labeled as the new Del Piero.

In brief - Andrea Russotto arrived in Bellinzona at 16 years old - Collected 9 appearances and 1 goal in Challenge League - Today plays in Sicilian Eccellenza ## Key facts - What: Andrea Russotto's career in Ticino - When: 2004/05 season - Where: Bellinzona, Switzerland - Who: Andrea Russotto, AC Bellinzona - Amount: 9 appearances in Challenge League, 8 in Super League, 5 in Swiss Cup Andrea Russotto, born in 1988, is a name that made many football fans dream. Growing up in Lazio's youth academy, he was hailed as the 'new Del Piero' and arrived in Bellinzona, under the shadow of the Castles, at just 16 years old. The media pressure was overwhelming, but the choice to move to Ticino was courageous. With AC Bellinzona, between loans and returns, he collected 9 appearances and 1 goal in Challenge League, 8 appearances in Super League with 1 assist, and 5 matches in the Swiss Cup adorned with 3 goals. ### A heavy comparison The comparison with Del Piero was enormous, perhaps too big for a boy who was still searching for himself. Russotto chose Bellinzona also to distance himself from overwhelming media pressure. 'Well, oh, maybe the comparison was a bit too big. Surely, though, at that time I was at a crucial moment in my growth and had important qualities. Comparisons in football come quickly, but being associated with sacred monsters like Del Piero was perhaps premature. I was still too young.' ### The impact of leaving home At first, it was almost traumatic. I grew up in a small town near Rome, between the parish football pitch and the friends from football school. Finding myself so far from home at such a young age was not easy. My family made enormous sacrifices: my mom and my sister left practically everything to follow me to Switzerland, while my father went back...
