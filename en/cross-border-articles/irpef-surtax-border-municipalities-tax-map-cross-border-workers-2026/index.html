IRPEF Surtax: The Tax Map of Border Municipalities (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 22 February 2026

Where is it best to live? A comparison of 2026 rates in Como, Varese, and Verbano. The impact on a cross-border worker's net salary can exceed 400 CHF per year.

Contesto

TL;DR - Cross-border workers' net pay affected by municipal surtax on income. - New tax agreement impacts 'new cross-border workers' in Italy. - Municipal surtax rates vary, affecting net income significantly. ## Key facts - Tax Agreement Date: July 17, 2023 - Tax-Free Allowance: €10,000 - Maximum Surtax Rate: 0.8% - Example Income: €62,400 (65,000 CHF) - Taxable Base: €52,400 (after €10,000 allowance) - Surtax Payment: Two installments: down payment and final balance - Payment Deadlines: Follow tax return deadlines for new cross-border workers For a cross-border worker, choosing where to live is never just a matter of kilometers from the Brogeda or Gaggiolo border crossings. It's a strategic decision with a direct impact on the net paycheck. At the heart of this calculation is an often-underestimated tax: the municipal surtax on personal income (addizionale comunale all'IRPEF). This tax, with rates set independently by each Italian municipality, creates a veritable fiscal map along the border, with affordable havens and significantly more expensive areas. For 'new cross-border workers' – those who started working in Ticino after July 17, 2023 – this variable has become even more crucial. Under the new tax agreement, Italy taxes their Swiss income (with a €10,000 tax-free allowance), and the municipal surtax applies to this taxable base. A rate of 0.8% (the maximum allowed) versus one of 0.4% can mean a difference of hundreds of euros at the end of the year. This disparity has triggered a kind of 'fiscal competition' among border municipalities, which seek to attract high-income residents like cross-border workers. Dettagli operativi

How the Surtax Affects Your Salary To understand the real impact, let's take the example of a 'new cross-border worker' with a gross annual income of 65,000 CHF. Under the new agreement, Switzerland withholds 80% of the tax at source, while Italy taxes the income according to its own rules. Assuming a EUR/CHF exchange rate of 0.96, the income in euros is approximately €62,400. Applying the €10,000 allowance, the taxable base for Italian IRPEF is €52,400. The municipal surtax is calculated on this amount. The differences immediately become apparent. 📊 Map of 2026 Rates (estimates based on current data): - Como (city): 0.8% - Varese (city): 0.8% - Lavena Ponte Tresa (VA): 0.8% - Luino (VA): 0.8% - Maslianico (CO): 0.75% - Cernobbio (CO): 0.6% - Campione d'Italia: Specific regime, historically zero or very low taxation on this front. In our example, a resident of Como or Varese would pay €419 in surtax (€52,400 * 0.8%). A resident of Cernobbio, however, would pay €314. A difference of over 100 euros, which at the current exchange rate is about 105 CHF. If the rate were even lower, the savings would increase accordingly. For higher incomes, the difference grows proportionally. ⚠️ Payment Warning: The surtax is paid in two installments: a down payment (usually 30% of the previous year's tax) and a final balance. For new cross-border workers in their first year of taxation in Italy, the payment methods follow the deadlines of the tax return. It is essential not to miss these deadlines to avoid penalties.

Punti chiave

Residency Strategies: More Than Just Taxes Choosing where to live based solely on the municipal surtax rate would be a mistake. The decision must take a broader picture into account. A lower rate might be offset by higher rents, a greater distance from the workplace in Ticino (resulting in higher transport costs and time lost at the Ponte Tresa or Chiasso border), or a lower quality of municipal services (daycare, schools, healthcare). 💡 Residency Choice Checklist: - IRPEF Surtax Rate: Check the current rate and trends from recent years on the Ministry of Economy and Finance website. - Real Estate Costs: Compare rental and purchase prices in different areas. - Transportation: Calculate the costs and travel times to Ticino, including tolls and vehicle wear and tear. - Local Services: Evaluate the quality and availability of schools, healthcare, and other essential family services. - Quality of Life: Consider subjective factors such as proximity to family, green spaces, and cultural offerings. The optimal choice is a compromise among all these elements. Frequently Asked Questions
What is the impact of IRPF's add-on on my salary?
The additional municipal can increase your net salary of hundreds of euros, depending on the rate applied. For example, a resident in Como or Varese would pay €419 additional on a gross income of CHF 65'000, while a resident in Cernobbio would pay €314.
How does the new tax agreement affect the Swiss income of new frontier workers?
The new tax agreement allows Italy to tax the Swiss income of the new frontiers (with a deductible of 10'000 euros), applying the additional municipal on this taxable basis.
How does the IRPF add-on affect my savings as a front door?
The additional municipal tax can increase the total tax up to 0.8% on the taxable basis, reducing the net income of some hundred euros per year, depending on the municipality of residence and the declared income.
