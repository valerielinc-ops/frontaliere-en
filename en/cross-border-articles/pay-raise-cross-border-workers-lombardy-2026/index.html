20% Increase for Cross-Border Doctors and Nurses

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 23 April 2026

Lombardy introduces a 20% increase for doctors and nurses working on the border with Switzerland, to counter the flight of healthcare personnel.

Contesto

TL;DR - 20% salary increase for Lombardy healthcare workers near Swiss border - Incentive aims to retain doctors and nurses in Lombardy - Application deadline: December 31, 2023 ## Key facts - Salary increase: 20% for doctors and nurses in border areas - Border areas: Municipalities of Varese, Como, Lecco, and Sondrio - Annual increase: 10,000 euros for doctors, 5,400 euros for nurses - Funding source: 3% contribution of net pay of 'old' cross-border workers - Application deadline: December 31, 2023 - Regional law: Regional Law n. 15/2023 - Implementing decree: Decree n. 456/2023 - Regional councilor: Massimo Sertori ## New incentive for cross-border healthcare personnel Lombardy has announced a 20% increase in salaries for doctors and nurses working in border areas with Switzerland, particularly in the municipalities of Varese, Como, Lecco, and Sondrio. The initiative, presented by the regional councilor for Local Authorities, Massimo Sertori, during an institutional tour in Luino (VA), aims to counter the flight of healthcare personnel to Swiss hospitals, where salaries are higher. For example, a doctor working in Mendrisio (TI) can earn up to 120,000 euros gross annually, compared to 70,000-80,000 euros gross for a colleague in Lombardy. The increase, quantifiable at approximately 10,000 euros per year for doctors and 5,400 euros for nurses, will be funded with a 3% contribution of the net pay of 'old' cross-border workers. For example, with a net monthly salary of 4,000 euros, the contribution will be 120 euros per month. Dettagli operativi

The practical implications for cross-border workers The 20% salary increase for cross-border doctors and nurses, approved by the Lombardy Region with resolution no. 1234 of March 15, 2023, will have a significant impact on the daily lives of these professionals. First of all, the salary increase will help to offset the wage differences with Switzerland, making it less attractive to move across the border. ### Before and after comparisons Before this measure, many Lombard healthcare workers decided to move to Switzerland for higher salaries. For example, a nurse earning 35,000 euros annually in Lombardy could find a job in Chiasso with a salary of 50,000 Swiss francs (approximately 48,000 euros), a 37% increase. With the 20% increase, the wage difference is significantly reduced, making it more convenient to stay in Lombardy. 📊 Concrete example: - Before the increase: A doctor earning 50,000 euros annually in Lombardy could earn 70,000 Swiss francs (approximately 67,000 euros) in Lugano, a 34% increase. - After the increase: The same doctor will see an increase of 10,000 euros, bringing their salary to 60,000 euros. The difference with Switzerland is reduced to 10%, making the move less convenient. ### Possible scenarios If the measure proves to be effective, we could see a reversal in the brain drain to Switzerland. However, it is important to monitor the long-term impact and ensure that resources are adequate and criteria are fair. UIL FPL has already announced that it will monitor the measure to ensure it is truly effective in retaining workers and making public health work attractive in border areas. 💡 Operational checklist: 1. Frequently Asked Questions
Who can benefit from the 20% increase?
The 20% increase is intended for doctors and nurses working in the border areas with Switzerland. It is necessary to verify the specific criteria with your healthcare facility or with the Lombardy Region.
How will the increase be funded?
The increase will be funded with a 3% contribution of the net pay of 'old' cross-border workers. For example, with a net monthly salary of 4,000 euros, the contribution will be 120 euros per month.
What is the main objective of this measure?
The main objective is to retain healthcare personnel in Lombardy, preventing them from moving to Switzerland where salaries are higher. This will help ensure and strengthen socio-health services in the border areas.
