Frequently Asked Questions
What changes for cross-border workers with the new agreement?
The new agreement between the Italian Revenue Agency, the Guardia di Finanza, and the Prosecutor's Office of Cremona provides for greater collaboration between Italian and Swiss authorities. This could lead to more frequent and in-depth checks for cross-border workers, who will need to pay particular attention to the correct declaration of income and the management of withholding taxes.
Which documents should I keep to be compliant?
Cross-border workers should keep documents such as employment contracts, payment receipts, tax declarations, documents related to withholding taxes, and documents related to refunds. These documents are essential to demonstrate tax compliance in case of checks.
How can I ensure I am compliant with the new regulations?
To ensure compliance with the new regulations, cross-border workers should verify the accuracy of their tax declarations, keep all documentation related to income and taxes, consult an accountant or tax expert, and stay updated on new procedures and regulations.
