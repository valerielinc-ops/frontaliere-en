Minimum wage in Ticino: changes for cross-border workers from 2029 (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 25 May 2026

From 2029, the minimum wage in Ticino will rise to 21.75-22.25 francs per hour. Here's what changes for cross-border workers and residents.

Contesto

In brief - Minimum wage in Ticino will rise to 21.75-22.25 francs per hour from 2029 - Beneficiaries: 23,000 workers, including 15,000 cross-border commuters - Increase of over 300 francs per month ## Key facts - What: Increase in minimum wage in Ticino - When: From 2029 - Where: Canton Ticino, Switzerland - Who: Ticino Cantonal Parliament - Amount: 21.75-22.25 francs per hour (around 4,000 francs gross per month) The Ticino Cantonal Parliament approved on Monday the compromise that from 2029 will bring the minimum wage to a range between 21.75 and 22.25 francs per hour. Around 23,000 workers will benefit, 15,000 of whom are cross-border commuters. The increase in the minimum wage in Ticino, currently between 20 and 20.50 francs per hour (21.80-22.35 euros), is a done deal. The Grand Council indeed accepted on Monday the compromise reached in recent weeks by the main parties. The agreement, which emerged as a counter-proposal to the initiative of the Socialist Party (PS) entitled 'For a social minimum wage', provides for a progressive increase in the minimum wage. From 2027 it will rise to 20.50-21 francs per hour, from 2028 to 21-21.50 francs and from 2029 to 21.75-22.25 francs. From 2030 the salary will also be indexed to inflation. Compared to the current situation, the minimum wage will thus increase by over 300 francs per month and reach around 4,000 francs gross (4,360 euros). ### Cross-border commuters and residents on the same level 'This will benefit over 23,000 people, 8,000 of whom are residents', illustrated in the chamber the co-president of the PS Fabrizio Sirica, underlining how the latter will benefit from a measure that will allow them to have a sufficient salary to live on and to depend less on state assistance. Dettagli operativi

News on the primacy of the minimum wage over collective labor agreements (CCL) But the increase in the minimum wage is not the only novelty. There is another important aspect to highlight: the compromise adopted by the Cantonal Parliament provides for the primacy of the minimum wage over collective labor agreements (CCL), contrary to what is stipulated by the law currently in force. In other words, the remuneration provided for in the CCLs will have to be adapted and cannot be lower than the minimum wage. This must happen by December 31, 2029. And this was precisely one of the controversial points for which the left had launched its new initiative: after the Ticino electorate had accepted the principle of introducing a minimum wage in 2015, part of the business sector had tried to circumvent the relevant law by creating a pseudo-union (called TiSin) to sign CCLs that provided for hourly wages much lower than those envisaged by the minimum wage. This attempt, however, ended in vain: in 2022, the Labor Inspectorate Office had defined the CCLs concluded by this association as lacking legal basis. However, a sword of Damocles hangs over the issue of the primacy of the minimum wage over CCLs. At the federal level, in fact, the Parliament is discussing a motion presented by Council of States member Erich Ettlin, which stipulates that the minimum wages provided for in CCLs of general obligatory nature must prevail over those at the cantonal level. The Council of States has already approved the proposal and the National Council is likely to do the same, after its Economic and Taxation Commission also said yes to the Ettlin motion last week.
