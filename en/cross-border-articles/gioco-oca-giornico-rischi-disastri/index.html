A Giornico, a board game to reflect on risks and disasters

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 1 May 2026

At the Leventina Museum in Giornico, an interactive board game tells the story of disasters from the Middle Ages to Covid.

Contesto

In brief - Interactive snakes and ladders game at the Leventina Museum - 63 squares with historical events and disasters - Jolly cards to manage unexpected events ## Key facts - What: Interactive snakes and ladders game on disasters - When: Not specified - Where: Leventina Museum, Giornico - Who: Leventina Museum - Amount: 63 squares At the Leventina Museum in Giornico, the classic snakes and ladders game is transformed into an original tool to tell the story of disasters and catastrophes that have marked local and global history. Thus, the 'risk game' is born, an interactive exhibition that guides visitors on a chronological journey from the 12th century to the present day, through 63 squares that allow you to travel through the various epochs. Each stop represents an event, not only related to Leventina but also to international contexts that have had repercussions on the territory. ### History and disasters "They represent a history linked to events that have occurred in Leventina, but not only", explains Diana Tenconi, director of the museum. "There are also facts that have happened elsewhere and have influenced disaster or catastrophe situations linked to the region." Central to the experience are the jolly cards, which allow players to prepare for the unexpected. "The visitor chooses three cards that they consider useful for facing dangers", explains Alessandra Ferrini, responsible for the ethnographic collection of the State. "Then, as they advance in the game, they discover whether their choices were adequate or not to face dangers." Frequently Asked Questions
What is the Risk Game at the Leventina Museum?
The Risk Game is an interactive exhibit at the Leventina Museum in Giornico that tells the story of disasters and catastrophes through a chronological snakes and ladders game from the 12th century to the present day. Visitors can choose joker cards to prepare for the unexpected and reflect on risk management.
How can I participate in the Risk Game?
To participate in the Risk Game, you can visit the Leventina Museum in Giornico. No reservation is required, but it is advisable to check the opening hours and any restrictions. The game is suitable for all ages and offers an educational and engaging experience.
What are the specific risks of the Ticino territory?
The specific risks of the Ticino territory include natural disasters such as landslides, floods, and earthquakes, as well as health emergencies such as pandemics. It is important to be prepared and have a family emergency plan to deal with these situations.
