Third anti-food waste fridge inaugurated in Tenero

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 29 April 2026

On April 28, 2026, a third anti-waste public fridge was inaugurated in Tenero, Ticino, promoted by the Madame Frigo association.

Contesto

In brief - Third anti-waste fridge inaugurated in Tenero - Initiative promoted by Madame Frigo - Goal: combat food waste ## Key facts - What: Inauguration of a public anti-waste fridge - When: April 28, 2026 - Where: Tenero, Ticino - Who: Madame Frigo Association - Amount: Third fridge in Ticino On April 28, 2026, the third public anti-food waste fridge promoted by the Madame Frigo association was inaugurated in Tenero, Ticino. This new fridge joins those already present in Locarno and Pregassona, bringing the total number of active stations in the canton to three. The initiative, born with the aim of combating food waste, allows those who have too much food to leave it and those who need it to take it. The fridge in Tenero was inaugurated on Via Verbano and represents another step towards creating a culture of sharing and sustainability. The Madame Frigo project, coordinated by the Lucerne association of the same name, is already widely spread in inland Switzerland and is now expanding into Ticino. In Locarno, the public fridge has been placed outside the hangar space of the Area cooperative, along with other local associations such as Okkio and Basta poco. Alissa Hernandez Pita, one of the promoters of the project in Locarno, explained that the yellow fridge is used daily, with vegetables and bread being the most frequently deposited and taken food items. ### How the project works The operation of the public fridge is simple: those who have excess food can leave it in the fridge, while those who need it can take it. However, there are some rules to follow. In Tenero, for example, a QR code will allow joining a dedicated WhatsApp group, facilitating communication among participants. The inauguration of the third anti-food waste public fridge in Tenero represents an important opportunity for cross-border workers who work in Ticino. This project not only promotes sustainability but also offers concrete support to those who might be facing economic difficulties. Cross-border workers, who often have to manage high costs, can benefit from this initiative to access fresh and quality food without incurring additional expenses. ### Implications for Cross-Border Workers For cross-border workers in Ticino, the Madame Frigo project is a valuable resource. Many of them might find themselves in situations where the food budget is limited, and access to a public fridge can make a difference. Furthermore, the initiative promotes a culture of sharing and solidarity, values that can be particularly appreciated by those living and working in a cross-border context. ### Comparisons with the Previous Situation Before the introduction of public fridges, cross-border workers facing economic difficulties had to rely on other forms of assistance, such as food banks or private donations. With the arrival of Madame Frigo, they now have a more immediate and accessible solution. This change represents a significant step forward towards greater inclusion and support for the cross-border worker community. ### Concrete Scenarios Imagine a cross-border worker who works in Locarno and, due to unforeseen economic circumstances, finds themselves in difficulty at the end of the month. Frequently Asked Questions
How can I participate in the Madame Frigo project?

How can I contribute to reducing food waste?
