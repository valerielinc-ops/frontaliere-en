Petition against military exemption tax: 'The government privileges the army'

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 26 April 2026

The Communist Youth launches a petition to abolish the military service exemption tax in Ticino, accusing the government of prioritizing the army over youth precarity.

Contesto

TL;DR - Communist Youth of Ticino petitions to abolish military service exemption tax. - Tax seen as unfair financial punishment for young people in precarious job market. - Government defends tax as essential for national defense but acknowledges youth precariousness. ## Key facts - Petition by: Communist Youth of the Canton of Ticino. - Tax purpose: Compensate state expenses for military training and management. - Petition location: Presented in front of the Government Palace in Bellinzona. - Government stance: Tax is necessary for national defense, no plans to abolish it shortly. - Petition impact: Could lead to greater investment in youth policies if successful. - Signing methods: Online via Communist Youth's website or at protests in Bellinzona. - Petition deadline: No specific deadline, continues until government decision. ### The petition of the Communist Youth The Communist Youth of the Canton of Ticino has launched a petition to abolish the military service exemption tax. According to the political group, this tax represents a 'financial punishment' for young people, who already face a precarious job market. The petition was presented following a series of protests organized in front of the Government Palace in Bellinzona. The military service exemption tax is an amount that young Swiss citizens must pay to avoid military service. This tax was introduced to compensate for the expenses incurred by the State for the training and management of the armed forces. However, the Communist Youth argues that this tax is unfair, especially in a context of youth precariousness. ### The accusations of the Communist Youth According to the Communist Youth, the government of the Canton of Ticino is prioritizing the needs of the army over those of the young people. The practical implications of the petition The petition by the Communist Youth could have several practical implications for young people in the Canton of Ticino. If the exemption tax from military service were abolished, young people would save a significant amount of money each year. This money could be used to cover daily expenses or invest in their education. ### Comparison with the previous situation Before the introduction of the exemption tax, young Swiss people were not required to pay any amount to avoid military service. The introduction of the tax represented a significant change, especially for young people who already face economic difficulties. The Communist Youth's petition seeks to return to the previous situation, abolishing the tax and improving the living conditions of young people. ### Future scenarios If the petition is successful, the government of the Canton of Ticino might be forced to review its youth policies. This could lead to greater investment in youth policies, improving the living conditions of young people. However, it is still too early to say whether the petition will have a concrete impact. ### Procedures for signing the petition To sign the petition, young people in the Canton of Ticino can visit the Communist Youth's website or participate in the protests organized in front of the Government Palace in Bellinzona. What is the military service exemption tax?
The military service exemption tax is an amount that young Swiss citizens must pay to avoid military service. This tax was introduced to compensate the state for the costs of training and managing the armed forces.
