Chaos initiative: Ticino committee opposes (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 5 May 2026

The united Ticino committee against the 'No to a 10 million Switzerland' initiative has gathered to counter the project

Contesto

In brief - Ticino committee against the chaos initiative - Voting on June 14 - Risk to wages and jobs ## Key facts - What: Federal popular initiative 'No to a Switzerland of 10 million' - When: Voting on June 14, 2026 - Where: Canton of Ticino - Who: United Ticino committee - Amount: Not specified The United Ticino committee against the federal popular initiative 'No to a Switzerland of 10 million' met today in a press conference in Bellinzona. The initiative, defined as 'chaotic', was at the center of the committee's arguments, which expressed concern about the potential social and economic impacts. According to the committee, the initiative would jeopardize wages, jobs, and the quality of public services. Furthermore, the committee emphasized that the initiative is deeply divisive and discriminatory, fueling fears and social tensions instead of addressing real problems. Among the members of the committee are the Swiss Trade Union Federation (SGB) Ticino and Moesa, Unia Ticino, VPOD Ticino, SSM Svizzera italiana, syndicom Ticino and SEV Ticino, the Socialist Party of Ticino, the Greens of Ticino, the Movement for Socialism, the Swiss Communist Party, the Workers' and People's Party, Alternative Forum, Scintilla Collective, the Independent Union of Students and Apprentices SISA, Mendrisiotto Open Region and Swiss Workers' Relief SOS Ticino. The committee stated that the promoters of the initiative are against environmental protection, complacent with real estate speculation, against reducing road traffic, and indifferent to the overcrowding of public transport. They have raised real problems but want to deceive by blaming foreigners, a strategy that solves nothing and only serves to divide those who work. ### Impact on cross-border workers The initiative could have...

Dettagli operativi

The committee has expressed concern about the possible impact of the initiative on pensions. Limiting the number of workers who finance the pension system would deprive it of the necessary funds for its sustainability. This would inevitably lead to new proposals for deterioration, especially at a time when the so-called 'baby boomers' are reaching retirement age. ### Impact on cross-border workers The initiative could have a significant impact on cross-border workers who work in Ticino. The accompanying measures to free movement have not sufficiently protected the Ticino labor market, and the initiative could further worsen working conditions. The committee has expressed concern about wage dumping and replacement dismissals, which impoverish the territory and legitimately worry those who work. ### Defense of fundamental rights The committee emphasized that the initiative is against the Swiss tradition of hospitality and would put the right to asylum and other fundamental rights at risk. Foreigners risk being expelled or having family reunification denied, in violation of fundamental human rights. ### Committee's strategy The committee stated that the promoters of the initiative are against environmental protection, complacent with real estate speculation, against reducing road traffic, and indifferent to the overcrowding of public transport. They have raised real problems but want to deceive by solving them with the hunt for foreigners, a strategy that solves nothing and only serves to divide workers. ### Concrete actions The committee has invited citizens to vote 'no' on the initiative to defend the rights of all and to counter any racist drift. Frequently Asked Questions
What is the goal of the Ticino unitary committee?
The Ticino unitary committee opposes the federal popular initiative 'No to a Switzerland of 10 million' to defend the rights of everyone and counter any racist tendencies. The committee is concerned about the potential social and economic impacts of the initiative, which would jeopardize wages, jobs, and the quality of public services.
What are the risks for cross-border workers?
The initiative could further worsen the working conditions of cross-border workers in Ticino. The accompanying measures to free movement have not sufficiently protected the Ticino labor market, and the initiative could lead to wage dumping and replacement layoffs, impoverishing the territory and legitimately worrying those who work.
How can I actively participate in the debate?
You can actively participate in the debate by informing yourself about the implications of the initiative, attending press conferences and public debates, and voting 'no' on the initiative on June 14. You can also support organizations that defend the rights of workers and foreigners.
