Varese: temporary medical clinics for over 5,000 citizens

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 29 April 2026

From 1st May 2026, four temporary medical clinics in Varese serve over 5,000 citizens without a family doctor.

Contesto

In brief - Four temporary medical clinics in Varese starting May 1, 2026 - Over 5000 citizens without a family doctor - Extended hours and additional services ## Key facts - What: Temporary medical clinics - When: Starting May 1, 2026 - Where: Gazzada, Malnate, Laveno Mombello, Mesenzana - Who: ASST Sette Laghi - Number of people served: Over 5000 With the closure of the temporary clinic in Daverio on May 1, 2026, four points remain dedicated to patients without a family doctor in the ASST Sette Laghi area. The temporary medical clinics have been activated to ensure continuity of care for citizens who have been left without a general practitioner. The changes concern the cessation and readjustment of hours, with the aim of ensuring continuity of care. ### Gazzada Schianno Patients who were followed in Daverio will move to Gazzada, where one of the doctors who retired in February has decided to return, making some hours available. Here, the 'orphan patients' from the area of Buguggiate to Daverio, and from Mornago to Gazzada, are referred. In total, there are 2500 citizens who can turn to the clinic, which is open 25 hours a week. ### Laveno Mombello The temporary and extraordinary AMT continues in May in Laveno Mombello at the continuity of care center (via Ceretti 8), with free access. The clinic was activated for the patients of the doctor from Cocquio Treviso who retired. However, the situation is improving: from the initial 1500 citizens without a doctor, the number has decreased to 800 who need to turn to the temporary service. ### Malnate The temporary offer in Malnate on via Kennedy continues in May. Dettagli operativi

Practical Implications The activation of temporary medical clinics represents a temporary solution for citizens who find themselves without a family doctor. This service is fundamental to ensure continuity of care, especially during a transitional period like the current one. ### Gazzada Schianno For residents of Buguggiate, Daverio, Mornago, and Gazzada, the Gazzada Schianno clinic offers an immediate solution. With 25 hours of opening per week, citizens can access the necessary medical services without having to travel too far. This is particularly important for the elderly or those with reduced mobility who might have difficulty reaching other medical centers. ### Laveno Mombello In Laveno Mombello, the temporary clinic has seen a reduction in the number of citizens without a family doctor, from 1500 to 800. This improvement is due to the availability of new doctors and the readjustment of opening hours. Free access and continuity of care ensure that no one is left without medical assistance. ### Malnate In Malnate, the expansion of opening hours thanks to the availability of an additional doctor is an important step to ensure a more efficient service. With around 1500 citizens affected, the Malnate clinic is a crucial resource for the local community. ### Mesenzana In Mesenzana, the clinic activated for the nearly 1000 citizens without a family doctor is open 6 hours a week. Although limited in opening hours, this service is essential to ensure access to basic medical care. ## Additional Services Since April 13th, the email service for prescription requests has been active, with a response time of approximately 3 days. What to do If you are a citizen without a family doctor, it's important to know that you can turn to the temporary medical clinics activated in your municipality. Here's what to do: 1. Identify the nearest clinic: Check if your municipality is covered by one of the temporary clinics in Gazzada, Malnate, Laveno Mombello, or Mesenzana. 2. Check the hours: Each clinic has specific hours. Make sure you know the opening hours and plan your visit accordingly. 3. Use the email service for prescriptions: If you need a prescription, you can send a request to the address amt.varese@asst-settelaghi.it. The service has been active since April 13th, and prescriptions are processed within approximately 3 days. 4. Contact the continuity of care service: In case of emergency or need for medical assistance outside the clinic's opening hours, you can contact the continuity of care service at the number 116117. ### Next steps If you are a citizen without a family doctor, it's important to stay updated on the latest news regarding temporary medical clinics. Frequently Asked Questions
How can I request a prescription via email?
As of April 13, 2026, you can send a prescription request to the email address amt.varese@asst-settelaghi.it. Prescriptions will be processed within approximately 3 days. This service is particularly useful for simple prescriptions that do not require an in-person visit.
What are the opening hours of the temporary medical clinics?
The opening hours vary depending on the clinic. In Gazzada Schianno, the clinic is open 25 hours a week. In Laveno Mombello, the clinic is open with free access. In Malnate, the opening hours have been extended thanks to the availability of an additional doctor. In Mesenzana, the clinic is open 6 hours a week.
What should I do if I need medical assistance outside the opening hours of the clinics?
You can contact the continuity of care service (formerly known as emergency medical service) at the number 116117. This service is available from 19.00 to 8.00 every day and from 8.00 to 19.00 on weekends and bank holidays.
