Spring dinner for Lebanon: AVSI organizes event in Castiglione Olona

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 23 April 2026

On Saturday, May 9th, in Castiglione Olona, the AVSI Foundation is promoting a charity dinner for the Lebanese communities affected by the war.

Contesto

TL;DR - AVSI event - Lebanon aid - May 9 2026 ## Key facts - Data: 9 maggio 2026 - Luogo: Castiglione Olona - Organizzatore: AVSI Foundation - Donazione: 100 euro a persona - Obiettivo: Sostegno alle comunita libanesi - Collaborazione: Fondazione Mediolanum - Registrazione: Entro il 5 maggio 2026 On Saturday, May 9, 2026, at the Tenuta Allevamento Razza Ticino in Castiglione Olona, the first edition of the evening 'Under the Same Sky for Lebanon' will be held, organized by the AVSI Foundation. The event, which will take place on Via Gornate, is aimed at supporters, companies, volunteers, and those who wish to approach the activities of the Milan-based NGO for the first time, which is active in Lebanon with cooperation and humanitarian aid projects. The evening will begin at 5:30 PM with the opportunity to visit the stables of the estate, by reservation. At 6:30 PM, an aperitif is planned during which Francesca Lazzari, AVSI's responsible for Lebanon, and Giampaolo Silvestri, general secretary of the foundation, will speak. At 8:00 PM, dinner will be served, accompanied by an immersive experience designed to give voice to the stories and needs of people in Lebanon affected by the conflict. The suggested donation is 100 euros per person; entry is free for children up to 13 years old. To participate, registration on AVSI's website is required. Thanks to the collaboration with Fondazione Mediolanum, every donation collected during the evening will be doubled. ### Context and Impact AVSI is actively engaged in Lebanon with projects aimed at supporting local communities, particularly the most vulnerable. Dettagli operativi

Spring Dinner for Lebanon: AVSI Organizes Event in Castiglione Olona The AVSI Foundation, an Italian non-governmental organization founded in 1972, is recognized by the Ministry of Foreign Affairs and registered in the list of Civil Society Organizations of the AICS. It operates in about forty countries with development cooperation and humanitarian aid projects in the sectors of education, health, work, and emergencies. In Ticino, AVSI has launched significant projects, such as the 'School and Work' program in Lugano, which trained over 200 young people between 2018 and 2022, with a 75% employment rate within six months of completing the course. Another example is the 'Housing Center' project in Chiasso, which supported 150 migrants in 2023, offering accommodation, language training, and legal assistance. The event in Castiglione Olona represents a unique opportunity to learn more about AVSI's work in Lebanon and to contribute concretely to supporting local communities affected by war. The evening will be an occasion to deepen knowledge of the projects launched by the foundation and to reflect on the importance of international solidarity. ### 📊 AVSI Projects in Lebanon AVSI has been present in Lebanon since 2014, with a focus on education and emergencies. In 2023, it launched: - 12 schools in rural areas, with 3,000 student beneficiaries. - 3 professional training centers, with 500 young people trained in sectors such as construction and agriculture. - 2 mobile clinics, which carried out 10,000 medical visits. ### 💡 Operational Checklist for Participants - Confirmation of participation: By May 15, 2024, via email to rete.sostenitori@avsi.org or by phone at 340 4890393. - Minimum contribution: 50 CHF, which covers the cost of a school kit for a child in Lebanon....

Spring Dinner for Lebanon: AVSI Organizes Event in Castiglione Olona To participate in the charity dinner 'Under the Same Sky for Lebanon,' you need to book through AVSI's website. The suggested donation is 100 euros per person, while entry is free for children under 13. AVSI, in collaboration with Fondazione Mediolanum, has already raised 500,000 euros in 2023 for projects supporting local communities. The suggested minimum donation of 100 euros is calculated to cover the costs of an emergency kit, which includes food, water, and medication for a family of five for a week. ### 💡 Operational Checklist 1.
