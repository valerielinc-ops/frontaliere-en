Fuel tax cut extended to May 22

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 2 May 2026

The Council of Ministers has extended the fuel tax cut until May 22, 2026. Here's what changes for cross-border workers

Contesto

In brief - Excise duty cut extended until May 22 - Two separate decrees for procedural reasons - Resources from additional VAT revenue on fuels ## Key facts - What: Extension of the excise duty cut on fuels - When: Until May 22, 2026 - Where: Italy - Who: Council of Ministers - Amount: 21 days of extension The excise duty cut on fuels, extended by the Council of Ministers for three weeks, is valid until May 22. The first decree was adopted on Friday, while the second intervention will arrive in the coming days. The extension covers a total of 21 days, as specified in the explanatory report to the decree law. The resources from the additional VAT revenue on fuels, expected within about ten days, will be quantified to extend the reduction of excise duties. This measure has important implications for cross-border workers who travel daily between Italy and the Canton of Ticino, where fuel costs significantly influence the family budget. ### Implications for cross-border workers For cross-border workers who work in Ticino and reside in Italy, the extension of the excise duty cut represents temporary relief. With the cost of living continuously rising, every saving on fuel is welcome. However, it is important to monitor the evolution of the situation to be prepared for any future changes. The current extension until May 22 offers a window of time during which cross-border workers can plan their movements with greater peace of mind. Cross-border workers who use their car daily to reach their workplace in Ticino will benefit from this extension. For example, a cross-border worker who travels 50 km a day between Varese and Lugano, with an average consumption of 6 liters per 100 km, will save approximately 30 euros per month with the excise duty cut. Analysis of Practical Implications The extension of the excise duty cut on fuels until May 22, 2026, represents a temporary measure that provides economic relief to cross-border workers. However, it is important to consider the long-term implications. The reduction in excise duties, currently set at 30 cents per liter of gasoline and 24 cents per liter of diesel, could influence fuel prices not only in Italy but also in Switzerland, where cross-border workers often refuel. For example, a cross-border worker traveling daily from Lugano to Como could save approximately 150 euros per year with the current cut. ### Comparison with the Previous Situation Before the extension, the excise duty cut had been introduced for a limited period. The new extension until May 22, 2026, offers greater stability, allowing cross-border workers to better plan their movements. However, it is important to monitor the evolution of the situation to be prepared for any future changes. The current extension offers a window of time during which cross-border workers can benefit from savings on fuels, but it is crucial to stay updated on any regulatory changes that could influence the duration and extent of the excise duty cut. ### Future Scenarios One possible scenario is that the excise duty cut will be further extended beyond May 22, based on the evolution of the economic situation. Another scenario is that the cut will be gradually reduced or eliminated, depending on the available resources and the fiscal policies of the government.
