Intervention at Gandria customs: four goats rescued

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 20 April 2026

According to the Federal Customs Administration and Border Security (FCABS), four goats attracted the attention of the border crossing staff. The goats, on two occasions, risked causing an accident, endangering traffic circulation. The FCABS published a post on their social media channels, announcing the intervention. The customs teams intervened to secure the animals in a pen located under the customs parking area. After some checks, it was possible to trace the owner, who a few hours later arrived at the scene to retrieve his animals, safe and sound. ### Implications for Road Safety This episode raises some questions about road safety near border crossings. The presence of domestic or wild animals can pose a risk to traffic, especially in high-traffic areas near borders. The timely intervention of the customs authorities prevented potential accidents, but it is important that pet owners are aware of the risks and take preventive measures to avoid similar situations. ### Intervention Procedures The FCABS followed a standard procedure for managing such situations. Frequently Asked Questions
What should I do if I encounter a domestic or wild animal on the road?
If you encounter domestic or wild animals on the road, it's important to stay calm and respect road signs. If the animal poses a danger, you should immediately contact the relevant authorities, such as the local police or customs authorities, for assistance.
What are the standard procedures for managing dangerous situations caused by animals?
Customs authorities and local law enforcement follow standard procedures for managing dangerous situations caused by animals. First, they identify the danger and take necessary measures to secure the animals. Then, they conduct checks to trace the owner and ensure the animals are returned to their rightful owner.
How can I help prevent dangerous situations caused by domestic animals?
To prevent dangerous situations caused by domestic animals, it's important to take preventive measures such as keeping animals under control and respecting current regulations. Additionally, it's helpful to report any risky situations to the relevant authorities and actively collaborate with the local community to ensure everyone's safety.
