Vaud Parliament demands Valérie Dittli's resignation

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 28 April 2026

The Vaud Council of State has approved a symbolic resolution calling for the resignation of State Councillor Valérie Dittli.

Contesto

In brief - The Vaud Cantonal Parliament demands the resignation of Valérie Dittli - Symbolic resolution approved with 72 votes in favor - Dittli refuses to resign ## Key facts - What: Resolution for the resignation of Valérie Dittli - When: 20 April 2026 - Where: Canton of Vaud - Who: Vaud Cantonal Parliament - Amount: 10,000 francs of public money The Vaud Cantonal Parliament has approved today, Tuesday, a resolution formally requesting that State Councilor Valérie Dittli resign. Although the text has symbolic value, it aims to be 'a strong message' for the centrist representative to make this decision on her own initiative. The text was presented by the ecologist deputy Kilian Duggan, leader of the Greens, following the publication on Friday of a new compromising report. It was supported by all left-wing parties and the Green Liberals. The right-wing (PLR and UDC) largely abstained. The resolution, which expresses a wish and is not binding, was approved with 72 votes in favor, 5 against, and 58 abstentions. The State Council has three months to respond, knowing that it has no legal prerogative to suspend and even less to 'fire' one of its members. Dittli, for her part, has already ruled out resigning last Friday. In a personal statement, she defended herself against some accusations and reiterated her willingness to continue acting for the Canton. The State Councilor concluded an agreement for a criminal complaint against her to be withdrawn. This written agreement provided, in particular, for the allocation of 10,000 francs of public money to the complainant. ### Implications for cross-border workers The political situation in the Canton of Vaud could have repercussions for cross-border workers employed in this region.
