After Christian Vitta and Norman Gobbi, Claudio Zali also advocated the idea in the columns of the Mattino della Domenica. The State Council has until the end of June to decide whether to withhold the 120 million francs of withholding tax from cross-border workers. Just like fifteen years ago, the infamous blocking of the refunds is once again making headlines and sparking public debate. This time, the breaking point - in an already tense climate - is the health tax, a contribution that Lombardy intends to request from the old cross-border workers, those still benefiting from the refund regime. ### Parties' opinions The issue of reciprocity The problem had already been raised by Christian Vitta a few weeks ago. It's about reciprocity towards Italy, explains the PLR president Alessandro Speziali. 'It's also about maintaining a certain active pressure on Bern, because we know - and this is not whining - that our Canton faces difficulties as a border Canton. I think this is a correct pressure tool to then restore a respectful political environment for everyone.' Frequently Asked Questions
What are the ristorni?
Ristorni are the tax refunds that cross-border workers receive for the withholding tax paid in Switzerland. These refunds are calculated based on an agreement between Switzerland and Italy.
What are the implications of the ristorni block for cross-border workers?
The block of ristorni could lead to a reduction in the purchasing power of cross-border workers and an increase in the tax burden. It is important that cross-border workers inform themselves about the possible implications and prepare for any changes.
What can cross-border workers do to mitigate the impact of the ristorni block?
Cross-border workers can consider other tax options, such as tax residency in Switzerland, and consult a tax advisor to better understand the implications of the ristorni block and plan accordingly.
