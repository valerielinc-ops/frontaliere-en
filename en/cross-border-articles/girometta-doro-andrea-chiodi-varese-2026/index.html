Andrea Chiodi wins 2026 Girometta d'oro

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 2 May 2026

The Varese director receives the city's highest honor for bringing Varese's name to the forefront of contemporary culture and theater.

Contesto

In brief - Andrea Chiodi wins the 2026 Girometta d'oro - Ceremony on May 3 in Varese - Award for his bond with the territory ## Key facts - What: 2026 Girometta d'oro Award Ceremony - When: Sunday, May 3, 2026 - Where: Salone Estense, Varese - Who: Bosina Family - Amount: Not yet specified Andrea Chiodi, a director from Varese, is the winner of the 2026 Girometta d'oro. The ceremony will take place on Sunday, May 3, at the Salone Estense in Varese. Chiodi was chosen for his contribution to contemporary culture and theater, bringing the name of Varese to the forefront. Born in 1979, Chiodi is known for his ability to reinterpret classics and his deep connection to the city. Among his most famous works are the direction of 'The Taming of the Shrew' and the 'Tra Sacro e Sacro Monte' festival. The ceremony will begin at 6:00 AM with a torchlight procession from the Sacro Monte, which will pass through all the city's districts. At 11:00 AM, the procession will move to the Basilica for the Rito del Faro. During the Solemn Mass, 'Mother of the Year,' centenarian Felicita Maria Mortarino, will also be celebrated. The morning will conclude in Canonica Square with an aperitif offered by the Bosina Family. In addition to Chiodi, Mera & Longhi will be awarded for their 130 years of activity, and Tarèl, a Lombard tavern on Via Vetera opened just one month ago, will also receive an award. A moment will also be dedicated to the historic Aermacchi motorcycles, born in the Varese workshops. 'The reasons for awarding Andrea Chiodi and other surprises will be revealed on Sunday,' anticipates Luca Broggini, 'but awarding a talent so tied to our land is for us a source of immense pride.' ### The bond with the territory Andrea Chiodi is not only a successful director but also a cultur...
