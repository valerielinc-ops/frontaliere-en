Lombardy Region funds territorial marketing: Varese obtains 35,000 euros to promote its cultural and tourist excellences.

Contesto

TL;DR - Varese gets 35,000 euros for territorial marketing. - Funds aim to boost tourism and cultural growth. - Project targets 15% increase in foreign tourists by 2027. ## Key facts - Investment: 35,000 euros allocated to Varese for territorial marketing. - Objective: Promote economic, cultural, and tourist growth in Lombardy. - Target: 15% increase in foreign tourists by 2027. - Project Focus: Enhancing Varese's natural heritage, particularly the Sacro Monte. - Funding Source: Lombardy Region's '2026 Territorial Promotion Line'. - Regional Goal: Build a recognizable and coherent image of Lombardy by 2026. - Comparison: Lugano invested 50,000 Swiss francs in 2025 for similar projects. - Regulations: Maximum funding of 50,000 euros per project in Lombardy. The Lombardy Region has allocated a total investment of 319,813 euros to support eight territorial marketing projects presented by the provincial capital municipalities. Among the beneficiaries is Varese, which will receive 35,000 euros to enhance its unique features. These funds, made available by the '2026 Territorial Promotion Line', have fully financed the municipalities' proposals, with the aim of promoting the economic, cultural, and tourist growth of the Lombard territory. Territorial marketing has been identified as a fundamental lever to strengthen the attractiveness and visibility of the territories, especially in an increasingly competitive context. The regional tourism assessor, Debora Massari, emphasized the importance of using this tool to authentically and distinctively tell the identity of the places, networking cultural heritage, local traditions, craftsmanship, and gastronomic excellence. ### The financing of these projects also fits into the framework of the new 'tourist brand identity' of Lomba...

Dettagli operativi

Varese, with a grant of 35,000 euros, will have the opportunity to develop initiatives that promote its uniqueness and excellence. The city and its territory are rich in tourist, cultural, and historical potential, and regional support will further strengthen its presence on the national and international scene. The funded activities will contribute to enhancing Varese's image, promoting events, thematic reviews, and integrated campaigns that highlight its local identity and make it an increasingly attractive destination for tourists. In addition to Varese, other provincial capitals of Lombardy have received significant contributions for their territorial marketing projects: Bergamo: 20,000 euros, Brescia: 48,983 euros, Cremona: 47,580 euros, Lodi: 43,000 euros, Mantova: 42,000 euros, Pavia: 41,250 euros, Sondrio: 42,000 euros. These funds are destined for projects that, like Varese's, aim to create attractive experiences for visitors, strengthening the valorization of local identities and generating lasting value for the communities. ### The Commitment of Regione Lombardia With this measure, Regione Lombardia demonstrates a strong belief in the strength of the territories and their ability to network. Debora Massari has clarified: 'With this measure, we invest in projects that put local identities at the center and transform them into attractive experiences, capable of generating lasting value. Fully financing all the presented projects means recognizing this quality and accompanying it on a structured growth path.'
