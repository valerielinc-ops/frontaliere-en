The Republic and Canton of Ticino officially welcomed the new aspiring officers of the Police Academy of the Fifth Examination District (SCP 2026), inaugurated on March 2, 2026, at 8:00 AM in Giubiasco. This event marks a pivotal step in strengthening cantonal security, essential for a border region like Ticino. The opening ceremony featured prominent institutional figures, including the Director of the Department of Territory, Claudio Zali, the Head of the Training Section, Captain Christophe Cerinotti, and the Director of the Police Training Center (CFP), Andrea Pronzini. They addressed the new cadets with official speeches, emphasizing the critical role they will play in serving the community. Dettagli operativi

The training program awaiting these recruits is structured over two intensive years, culminating in the Professional Examination for the Federal Professional Certificate of Police Officer. The first year takes place entirely at the Police Academy of the Fifth Examination District (SCP) in Giubiasco, where cadets will acquire the theoretical and practical foundations necessary for the profession. This initial phase is critical for instilling ethical principles, legal knowledge, and operational techniques essential for tackling the complex realities of police work. Subsequently, during the second year, recruits will transition to the 'officer in training' phase within their respective forces, applying their acquired knowledge in real-world scenarios and confronting the specificities of their future roles. The training aims to prepare professionals capable of operating in a dynamic cantonal context, characterized by proximity to Italy and the intense flow of people and goods through border crossings like Brogeda and Gaggiolo. Ticino's security is a strategic priority, not only for residents but also for the approximately 70,000 cross-border commuters who enter daily for work. Ticino's law enforcement agencies must manage a wide range of situations, from preventing common and cross-border crimes such as theft and drug trafficking, often with international ramifications, to controlling increasingly heavy traffic on major routes like the A2 motorway. Collaboration with Italian authorities, such as through the Police and Customs Cooperation Center in Chiasso, is a vital aspect of their mission. Punti chiave

Ticino's commitment to strengthening its police force is not merely a matter of numbers but an investment in the region's quality of life and economic security. A well-trained and adequately staffed police force is a cornerstone of social stability, essential for attracting investments and ensuring a safe environment for businesses and workers, including the many professionals who cross the border daily, contributing to the local economic fabric. The presence of qualified officers acts as an effective deterrent against crime and provides a constant safeguard for legality, key elements for building trust among citizens and economic operators. Alongside the crucial activities of recruitment and training, the Cantonal Police continues to raise public awareness about current risks, such as online fraud. Recently, cases of phishing scams have been reported, where emails falsely use the names of police officials to accuse recipients of serious crimes, imposing time pressures and urging responses to unofficial email addresses (e.g., domains like post.com). The Cantonal Police advises maximum caution: always verify the source of such communications and never provide personal or banking information. Official communications from the Cantonal Police are sent via recognized channels and official domains (e.g., @polca.ti.ch or @ti.ch) and never include threatening tones or immediate payment requests via email. In case of doubt, it is always advisable to contact the police directly through official numbers 117/112 for emergencies or 0848 25 55 55 for the central office, avoiding responses to suspicious messages.
