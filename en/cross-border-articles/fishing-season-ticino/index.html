Opening of Fishing Season in Ticino (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 13 March 2026

Fishing in Ticino's rivers and alpine lakes will resume on March 15, 2026, for holders of annual permit D1 and on April 1, 2026, for holders of tourist permit T1.

According to information published on the website of the Department of the Environment, Energy, and Mobility of the Canton of Ticino, fishing in rivers and alpine lakes will be allowed again from March 15, 2026, for holders of annual permit D1 and from April 1, 2026, for holders of tourist permit T1. This opening concerns all alpine lakes and hydroelectric basins located below 1200 meters of altitude, except for the tributaries of alpine lakes and hydroelectric basins located above 1200 meters of altitude. Fishermen can thus return to practicing their passion in one of the most beautiful and biodiverse natural environments in Switzerland. The Canton of Ticino, with its mountains, lakes, and rivers, offers a wide range of possibilities for fishing, both for beginners and experts. Frequently Asked Questions
When does fishing reopen in Ticino in 2026?
Fishing reopens on March 15, 2026 for D1 license holders and on April 1, 2026 for the T1 tourist license. This applies to lakes and waterways below 1200 meters, except for tributaries above that altitude.
How much does an annual fishing license cost in Ticino?
The annual D1 license costs 60 CHF and allows fishing in all waters of Canton Ticino. The T1 tourist license (7 days) costs 20 CHF, with geographical and temporal restrictions.
What are the daily catch limits in Ticino?
The limit is 5 fish per day per person. For trout, the quota drops to 3 specimens, with a minimum size of 30 cm. Net fishing has a daily limit of 2 fish.
Where can you fish in Lake Lugano?
Lake Lugano (271m above sea level) is one of the most popular spots, with fishing allowed in designated areas. Closed from February 15 to March 31 to protect spawning. Night fishing is only permitted in specific zones.
How to apply for a fishing license in Ticino?
The license can be requested at the Canton Ticino Fisheries Office. You need an ID and payment of the fee (60 CHF for annual D1 or 20 CHF for tourist T1). Specific rules are available on the Department of Environment's website.
