Chiasso-Brogeda: 5 kg of heroin seized in car (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 28 February 2026

Turkish-Moroccan couple stopped with 5 kg of heroin at the Chiasso-Brogeda border crossing. Arrested for international drug trafficking.

Contesto

TL;DR - Couple arrested at Chiasso-Brogeda border with 5kg heroin - Drugs hidden in car seat double-bottom compartment - Both individuals charged with international drug trafficking - Border crossings like Chiasso-Brogeda are strategic for drug trafficking ## Key facts - Cosa: 5 kilograms of heroin - Quando: Yesterday - Dove: Chiasso-Brogeda highway crossing - Chi: 51-year-old Moroccan man and 28-year-old Turkish woman - Residenza: Genk, Belgium - Carica: International drug trafficking - Aumento sequestri: 15% negli ultimi anni - Accordo: Nuovi accordi bilaterali tra Italia e Svizzera An alarming discovery at the border between Switzerland and Italy: yesterday, at the Chiasso-Brogeda highway crossing, authorities arrested a couple consisting of a 51-year-old Moroccan man and a 28-year-old Turkish woman. Both, residents of Genk, Belgium, were stopped by the Guardia di Finanza, with the support of the Federal Office for Customs and Border Security, while transporting a load of 5 kilograms of heroin. The drugs were cleverly hidden in a double-bottom compartment under one of the car seats. The suspicious behavior of the driver, combined with vague descriptions of their final destination, raised suspicions among customs officers. It was their attention to detail, such as noticing plastic ties protruding from the seat, that triggered further inspections, leading to the discovery of the narcotics. Both individuals were transferred to the Bassone prison in Como, charged with international drug trafficking. This seizure once again highlights how border crossings like Chiasso-Brogeda are strategic points for combating drug trafficking and other illegal activities. Frequently Asked Questions
What are the strategic points for contrasting drug trafficking and other illegal activities on the Swiss-Italy border?
The border crossings, such as Chiasso-Brogeda, are strategic points for contrasting drug trafficking and other illegal activities. The authorities continue to monitor cross-border flows carefully to ensure the security of the region and limit the infiltration of criminal activities.
What is the impact of customs controls intensified on transit times for frontiers to the Chiasso-Brogeda Pass?
Increased controls can cause slowdowns, especially during peak periods. However, the authorities try to minimize the impacts on frontiers, ensuring efficient controls. It is recommended to schedule the passage during less congested hours.
What documents must the border crossing the border between Switzerland and Italy?
Frontiers must have a valid work permit, a passport or identity card, and a driving licence. It is also advisable to have a customs declaration if you carry valuable goods or significant amounts of money. Make sure all documents are up-to-date and in order to avoid delays to controls.
How do new bilateral agreements between Switzerland and Italy affect border security?
Bilateral agreements strengthen cooperation between the Italian and Swiss authorities, allowing a faster and more direct exchange of operational information. This improves the effectiveness of controls and contributes to greater security at the border, reducing the risk of illicit traffic and ensuring a smoother management of cross-border flows.
What are the consequences of drug trafficking?
Frontiers charged with drug trafficking can face severe penalties, including prolonged detention. In addition, such an accusation may lead to the loss of work permit in Switzerland, with significant impacts on personal and professional life. It is essential to comply with all customs regulations to avoid such consequences.
