Dettagli operativi

Impact on Cross-Border Workers: Stability and CHF-EUR Exchange Rate Such low inflation is generally good news for cross-border workers employed in Ticino. With stable prices, salaries in Swiss francs retain their value without significant erosion. However, the real impact for those living in Italy and working across the border hinges on the CHF-EUR exchange rate. - Strong Franc: The combination of low inflation and a strong currency ensures high purchasing power for those converting their salaries into euros. This difference represents the so-called 'border advantage.' - Italian Inflation: With higher inflation in Italy compared to Switzerland, cross-border workers may face rising costs when converting their income into euros. - Exchange Rate Balance: If the euro strengthens, the advantage could diminish. Therefore, it's crucial to monitor the situation and choose the right moment to make conversions. ⚠️ On the real estate front, the greater statistical transparency promised by the FSO for 2026 (new indices on real estate transactions) could reveal further details about purchase and rental costs in Ticino, a crucial aspect for those considering moving closer to their workplace.

Frequently Asked Questions
What is inflation in Switzerland now?
Inflation in Switzerland is currently at 99.9 points, down 0.1% from the previous month and a minimal increase of 0.1% from the previous year.
What impact does price stability in Switzerland have on vehicle insurance for cross-border commuters?
The increase in costs for vehicle insurance in Switzerland (as indicated in the new CPI 2026 basket) could increase the monthly costs of cross-border commuters. However, thanks to the general stability of prices, the impact is limited, making it still advantageous to compare with Italian tariffs, considering the purchasing power maintained thanks to the solid Swiss franc.
How much does it cost to ensure a car in Switzerland compared to Italy for a frontier?
In Switzerland the average premium for a basic cover of civil liability is approximately CHF 600 per year, while in Italy a similar policy costs approximately EUR 400. With the current exchange rate of CHF 0,96/EUR, the Swiss premium is approximately EUR 625, i.e. approximately 56% more. Frontiers can save by choosing cross-border insurance or higher franchise policies.
What is the trend of the franc-euro exchange rate over the past 12 months?
In January-2025-January-2026, the CHF/EUR exchange ranges from 0,93 to 0,98, with an average of 0,95. The peak of 0,98 was recorded in March 2025, while the minimum of 0,93 was in September 2025. These variations have influenced the purchasing power of frontier workers, making it more advantageous to convert wages in the highest rate months.
How does the new IPC 2026 basket affect the rent of an apartment in Ticino for frontiers?
The IPC 2026 shows an annual increase of 0.1% for the accommodation, pushing the average rent in Lugano to about 1.800 CHF per month, amounting to approximately 1.880 EUR per change of 0.96. Compared to Italy, where the average rent is 700 EUR, the cost remains higher, but the limited growth of the ICP limits the incidence on the budget of the frontier workers.
