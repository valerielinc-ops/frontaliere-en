Pfas in water: Sant'Antonino activates carbon filtration (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 7 May 2026

The new system reduces Pfas to values below 2 ng/l, fully funded by the Confederation. Here's what changes for cross-border workers.

Contesto

In brief - New activated carbon system for PFAS in Sant'Antonino - Cost 1.8 million, funded by the Confederation - PFAS concentration now below 2 ng/l ## Key facts - What: Activated carbon filtration for PFAS - When: 7 May 2026 - Where: Sant'Antonino, Ticino - Who: Municipality of Sant'Antonino - Amount: 1.8 million francs The new activated carbon filtration system for removing PFAS from drinking water has been put into operation in Sant'Antonino, Ticino. This project, strongly desired by local authorities, represents a significant step for water quality in the Canton of Ticino. The system, costing 1.8 million francs and entirely funded by the Confederation, was realized near the school center in the Boschetti area. The checks carried out have confirmed the effectiveness of the intervention, with the concentration of PFAS reduced to values below 2 ng/l, thus falling within the desired parameters. The Municipality of Sant'Antonino has managed to complete a cutting-edge project, ranking among the first in Ticino and among the pioneers in Switzerland to adopt a solution of this kind. The Confederation recognized the investment after lengthy checks, attributing the groundwater contamination to the works related to the Monte Ceneri base tunnel. This intervention represents a concrete example of timely and effective management of an environmental criticality, protecting public health and the quality of the territory's resources. ### Implications for cross-border workers For cross-border workers residing or working in the Sant'Antonino area, this intervention means a significant improvement in drinking water quality. Practical Analysis: What Changes for Cross-Border Workers The installation of the new activated carbon filtration system in Sant'Antonino has a direct impact on the quality of life for cross-border workers residing or working in the area. The reduction of PFAS to levels below 2 ng/l represents a significant improvement compared to the previous situation, where contamination levels were above safety limits. ### Health Implications PFAS, or perfluoroalkyl substances, are chemical compounds that can have negative effects on human health. Their presence in drinking water can increase the risk of diseases and disorders, making their removal fundamental. With the new filtration system, cross-border workers can be certain that the water they consume is safe and meets quality standards. ### Comparison with Other Solutions The activated carbon filtration system is one of the most effective solutions for removing PFAS from drinking water. Unlike other methods, such as chlorination or ozonation, activated carbon is capable of removing a wide range of contaminants, offering a more comprehensive and durable solution. This type of filtration is particularly suitable for areas with high levels of contamination, such as Sant'Antonino, where works related to the Monte Ceneri base tunnel have contributed to groundwater contamination. ### Procedures for Cross-Border Workers For cross-border workers who wish to obtain more information about the new filtration system, the Municipality of Sant'Antonino has made an assistance and information service available.
