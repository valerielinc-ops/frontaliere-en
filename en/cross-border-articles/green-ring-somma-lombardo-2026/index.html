Somma Lombardo's Great Green Ring: 25 km of nature and history (cross-border guide)

A 25 km trail through forests, marshes, and historic monuments, inaugurated on May 16, 2026.

Contesto

In brief - The Great Green Ring of Somma Lombardo inaugurated - 25 km route between nature and history - Official inauguration on May 16, 2026 ## Key facts - What: Great Green Ring of Somma Lombardo - When: Inauguration on May 16, 2026 - Where: Somma Lombardo, Italy - Who: CAI Somma Lombardo, Municipal Administration - Amount: 40 signposts installed The Great Green Ring of Somma Lombardo, a 25 km route through forests, marshes, streams, and historical monuments, was officially inaugurated on May 16, 2026. The project, designed by architect Andrea Zanardi and developed by the CAI Somma section, was realized with the support of Parco del Ticino and the municipal administration. The route, composed of existing trails and dirt roads, offers a unique experience between nature and history, from Prehistory to the 20th century. The signage, composed of arrows and boards with QR codes, allows the ring to be traversed in a clockwise or counterclockwise direction. The route is divided into sections: to the north, the wet areas; to the south, the heathland; to the west, the testimonies of aviation activity and the war period; to the east, the Ticino River and its canals. The official inauguration took place on May 16, 2026, with an inaugural walk starting from the sanctuary Madonna della Ghianda. The project presentation took place on May 8 at the Fondazione Casolo headquarters. ### A decade-long project The genesis of the project was almost decade-long, with the inclusion in the trail cadastre and the installation of signs by about fifteen CAI volunteers. Zanardi explained that mainly existing posts were used to minimize the impact on the territory. Dettagli operativi

A path for everyone The Grande Anello Verde of Somma Lombardo has been designed to be accessible to everyone, with sections suitable for different levels of difficulty. For cross-border workers looking for a relaxing activity on the weekend, the path offers a unique opportunity to explore the local nature and history. The short 4 km route, for example, is suitable for families and beginners, while the longer sections offer a challenge for more experienced hikers. ### Comparisons with other paths The Grande Anello Verde stands out for its combination of nature and history. Unlike other paths in the area, such as the Sentiero del Viandante or the Sentiero del Ponte Ticino, the Grande Anello Verde offers a variety of unique experiences. The wetlands to the north, the heathland to the south, the testimonies of aviation activity to the west, and the Ticino River to the east create a diversified and surprising route. ### Practical scenarios For cross-border workers who work in Somma Lombardo or nearby, the Grande Anello Verde can become an ideal destination for Sunday excursions. The path can be walked in part or in full, depending on the available time and the desired level of difficulty. Additionally, the path offers the opportunity to discover hidden corners of the territory, such as the Villoresi canal and the industrial canal, which become the Grande Naviglio. ### Concrete procedures For those who wish to walk the Grande Anello Verde, it is advisable to start with the short 4 km route to familiarize themselves with the area. For the more experienced, it is possible to plan a full-day hike, bringing water, snacks, and appropriate clothing. What to do to participate To participate in the inauguration or plan a hike on the Grande Anello Verde, you can follow these steps: 1. Inform yourself: Visit the CAI Somma Lombardo website for more information on the route and the tables with QR codes. 2. Plan: Choose the most suitable route based on the level of difficulty and the time available. 3. Prepare: Bring water, snacks, and appropriate clothing. 4. Start: Begin the route from the Madonna della Ghianda sanctuary or another access point along the route. 5. Explore: Follow the arrows and the tables with QR codes to discover the sites of interest along the route. ### Useful tools To plan your hike as best as possible, you can use the route calculator available on the Frontaliere Ticino website.
