A new space for migrants opens in Chiasso: ‘Dagatrà’ (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 12 March 2026

‘Dagatrà’ provides a refuge and a socialization space for migrants in Chiasso, with activities for all ages.

Contesto

TL;DR - Dagatrà is a new center in Chiasso for migrant integration. - 30 volunteers support migrants with social and job opportunities. - The center offers Italian language lessons and financial education. ## Key facts - Location: Chiasso, Switzerland - Initiative: Mendrisiotto Regione Aperta association - Volunteers: Approximately 30 - Migrants in Ticino: Around 40,000 - Initial Investment: 120,000 Swiss francs - Future Funding: 2 million Swiss francs in 2026 - Unemployment Rate: 3.5% in Ticino (2022) - Average Salary: CHF 4,500 to CHF 7,500 per month In the heart of Chiasso, the new space ‘Dagatrà’ marks a significant step towards the integration of migrants into the local community. Opened in the premises of a former bar, this center has become a meeting place where migrants can feel welcomed and supported. The Mendrisiotto Regione Aperta association has promoted this initiative, leveraging an environment of freedom and serenity for users. Approximately 30 volunteers, who are the backbone of the initiative, are dedicated to ensuring that everyone feels welcome, creating an atmosphere of conviviality and support. ‘Dagatrà’ is not just a place for socializing, but also a center for useful information for migrants regarding services, rights, and job opportunities in Ticino. It is estimated that Ticino hosts around 40,000 migrants, and this center aims to provide concrete resources to facilitate their integration. Daily interactions, enriched by children's games and moments of cultural exchange, make this space a fundamental reference point for the community. Dettagli operativi

From a regulatory perspective, ‘Dagatrà’ fits into a context of increasing attention to migration policies in Switzerland and particularly in the Canton of Ticino. The humanitarian emergency related to migration has led institutions to reflect on new ways of welcoming and integrating migrants. The Ticino municipalities, including Chiasso, Lugano, and Bellinzona, have initiated various initiatives to ensure adequate support for migrants, focusing on orientation services, legal assistance, and job opportunities. In 2026, the cantonal government has planned specific funding for projects like ‘Dagatrà’, with an estimated amount of 2 million Swiss francs, recognizing the importance of such structures for social well-being. Collaboration with charities, volunteer associations, and social services, such as Caritas Ticino and the Red Cross, is proving crucial for the success of such initiatives. For example, in 2025, the municipality of Chiasso already recorded a 30% increase in the demand for support services for migrants, highlighting the need for a coordinated approach. Furthermore, an increase in awareness-raising activities aimed at the citizenry is planned, to enhance awareness of the importance of integration and respect for human rights. Efforts to combat xenophobia and promote a culture of inclusion represent key objectives of local policies. According to a 2022 report from the Department of Institutions of the Canton of Ticino, 65% of citizens support integration initiatives, but there is still a 20% of the population that expresses concerns regarding local employment. In this context, community support is essential to ensure the success of projects like ‘Dagatrà’, which aims to become a model to follow not only in Ticino but throughout Switzerland. Punti chiave

For migrants and cross-border workers who wish to settle and work in Ticino, ‘Dagatrà’ represents a fundamental resource. Participating in the activities organized at the center not only promotes socialization but also provides practical information on local regulations and job opportunities. It is highly advisable to visit the center for guidance on how to navigate the bureaucratic processes for residency and obtaining employment. Ticino stands out for its dynamic job market, particularly in the municipalities of Lugano, Mendrisio, and Bellinzona, where the logistics and services sectors are experiencing rapid growth. For instance, in 2022, the unemployment rate in Ticino was around 3.5%, compared to the Swiss average of 4.5%, highlighting the robustness of the local economy. Dagatrà provides specific information on job offers, with opportunities ranging from positions in IT to roles in tourism, with average salaries varying from CHF 4,500 to CHF 7,500 per month, depending on experience and sector. For job seekers, it is useful to explore job opportunities in Ticino, which boasts a dynamic and growing labor market. Frequently Asked Questions
What are the requirements to register with the municipality of Chiasso as a migrant?
According to the operational checklist of ‘Dagatrà’, migrants must register at the municipality of Chiasso, obtain information on the social rights to which they are entitled and enroll in training courses available.
How many migrants are estimated in Ticino and what are the objectives of 'Dagatrà' for their integration?
Approximately 40,000 migrants are estimated in Ticino. 'Dagatrà' aims to offer concrete resources to facilitate their integration, through useful information, language courses, job opportunities and a mentoring program with 15 local professionals.
What recreational activities and courses offers the 'Dagatrà' centre for migrants?
The 'Dagatrà' centre offers a variety of recreational activities and courses for migrants, including Italian language lessons, financial education courses and children's games. Every month, it hosts thematic events such as the 'Working Orientation Day', where experts provide information about job opportunities and regulations. In addition, a mentoring program with 15 local professionals provides direct support to migrants seeking work.
What is the role of volunteers in the 'Dagatrà' centre?
Volunteers are the core of the 'Dagatrà' initiative, with about 30 people committed to ensuring an environment of freedom and serenity for users. Their role is crucial to create an atmosphere of conviviality and support, helping migrants feel welcomed and actively participate in the activities and services offered by the centre.
How do you finance the Dagatrà project and what are the future prospects?
The Dagatrà project is funded with an initial investment of 120,000 Swiss francs, supported by public funds and private donations. In 2026, the cantonal government provided specific funding for projects such as 'Dagatrà', with an estimated amount of CHF2 million. This recognizes the importance of social welfare structures and aims to make 'Dagatrà' a model of integration not only in Ticino but throughout Switzerland.
