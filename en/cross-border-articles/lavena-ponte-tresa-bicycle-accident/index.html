The accident occurred on Via Campagna, a narrow and sloped road section connecting the inhabited centers of Lavena Ponte Tresa and Ponte Tresa. The child was seated on the rear saddle of a bicycle ridden by an adult when they were suddenly ejected due to a sudden braking maneuver. The child was immediately assisted by the air ambulance of the Ticino Canton's Medical Air Rescue Service, called to the scene by the 118 emergency number. The air ambulance transported the child to the 'Sant'Anna' Hospital in San Fermo della Battaglia (Como), Italy, to receive necessary medical care. Frequently Asked Questions
What do rescuers do in the event of a road accident in Switzerland?
Swiss emergency responders are trained to respond quickly and effectively in the event of a traffic accident. The air ambulance of the Air Medical Rescue Service of the Canton of Ticino was called to the rescue by 118 and took the child to the 'Sant'Anna' hospital in San Fermo della Battaglia (Como) within 10 minutes of the accident.
What is the Swiss regulation for minors on bicycles?
In Switzerland, the Road Safety Act (LSV) requires bicycle drivers, including minors, to wear a protective helmet. However, there is no specific age regulation for accompanying cyclists, unlike in Italy.
How long does it take for the Canton of Ticino air ambulance to reach Lavena Ponte Tresa in case of emergency?
The air ambulance of the Air Medical Rescue Service of the Canton of Ticino covers an area of about 100 km². On average, it intervenes within 8-10 minutes from the time of the call to 118, as evidenced by the accident in Lavena Ponte Tresa on 1 April 2024, where rescue took place in less than 10 minutes.
What are the methods of reimbursement for hospital expenses incurred in Italy by a Swiss cross-border commuter in the event of an emergency?
Swiss cross-border commuters are also covered by compulsory health insurance (KVG) for hospital treatment in Italy, provided that the hospital has an agreement and is located within 30 km of the border. Just present the reimbursement form with the invoice and medical documentation; Payment takes 30-45 days and expenses are 100% reimbursed for emergencies.
Is the use of a helmet mandatory for children under 12 years of age cycling in Switzerland?
In Switzerland, the Road Safety Act (LSV) obliges all cyclists, including children, to wear a protective helmet. Since 2019, the legislation has been strengthened and provides for a penalty of up to CHF 200 for those who do not comply with the obligation, with regular checks by the local police.
