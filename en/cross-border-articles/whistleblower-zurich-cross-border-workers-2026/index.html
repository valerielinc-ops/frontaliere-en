USZ director thanks dismissed whistleblower

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 10 May 2026

The director of Zurich University Hospital expresses gratitude to the dismissed whistleblower who reported irregularities in the cardiothoracic clinic

Contesto

In brief - USZ director thanks dismissed whistleblower - Investigation reveals above-average mortality rate - 68-74 additional deaths between 2016 and 2020 ## Key facts - What: Investigation into irregularities at the USZ cardiothoracic surgery clinic - When: Period 2016-2020 - Where: University Hospital Zurich (USZ) - Who: Director Monika Jänicke, former cardiothoracic surgeon whistleblower - Amount: 68-74 additional deaths compared to the statistical average The director of the University Hospital Zurich (USZ), Monika Jänicke, has expressed her gratitude to the whistleblower who reported irregularities in the cardiothoracic surgery clinic. In an interview with the 'SonntagsZeitung', Jänicke stated: 'He deserves all our respect'. The whistleblower, a former cardiothoracic surgeon from the same clinic, had publicly reported the irregularities and raised the issue several times. Jänicke emphasized the importance of his contribution, although she did not comment on the whistleblower's dismissal, calling it a matter of the past. The administrative investigation revealed serious shortcomings in the cardiothoracic surgery clinic, with an above-average mortality rate. Between 2016 and 2020, out of approximately 4,500 surgical procedures, there were 68 to 74 additional deaths compared to what would have been statistically expected. At the center of the scandal is the 'Cardioband', a cardiac valve prosthesis developed by a company in which the Italian professor Francesco Maisano, former director of the clinic, had a stake. Maisano was relieved of his duties in 2020 and is now head of the department at the San Raffaele Hospital in Milan.
