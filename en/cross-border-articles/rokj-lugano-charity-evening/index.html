ROKJ Ticino: charity evening with Sebalter on May 6th in Lugano

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 30 April 2026

ROKJ Ticino is hosting a charity evening on May 6th featuring Sebalter to support children and young people in financial need.

Contesto

In brief - ROKJ Ticino charity evening on May 6th in Lugano - Sebalter will be present - Fundraising for children in need ## Key facts - What: ROKJ Ticino charity evening - When: May 6th, 2026, 6:00 PM - Where: Asilo Ciani, Lugano - Who: ROKJ Ticino, Sebalter - Amount: Not specified The ROKJ Ticino association will officially present itself to the territory with a charity evening scheduled for Tuesday, May 6th at 6:00 PM at Asilo Ciani in Lugano. The initiative, which will feature the participation of singer Sebalter, aims to raise awareness and collect funds for children and young people living in families with economic difficulties. The goal is to promote equal opportunities, talent development, and social integration. Active since 2020 and formally established in 2025 by the Rotary and Rotaract clubs in Ticino, ROKJ Ticino is inspired by the Swiss project ROKJ – Rotary for children and young people. The association aims to remove economic obstacles that limit access to fundamental experiences for growth, such as sports, music, education, and educational activities. Through targeted contributions for young people up to 18 years old, ROKJ Ticino supports those who demonstrate talent and motivation but do not have the necessary resources. ### Objectives and supporters The evening of May 6th will also be an opportunity to meet with authorities and entities active in social work and will include an auction of activities and initiatives. 'We want to ensure that no young person has to give up their talent for economic reasons,' states President Giorgio Tognola. The association is supported by several Rotary Clubs in Ticino and Rotaract, as well as by local donors and partners. ### Implications for cross-border workers For cross-border workers living in Ticino, this ini...

Dettagli operativi

Analysis of Implications The solidarity evening organized by ROKJ Ticino on May 6 in Lugano not only represents an opportunity to raise funds but also to raise awareness in the community about the economic difficulties that many young people face. This type of initiative can have a significant impact on the lives of many children and adolescents, offering them access to resources and opportunities they would otherwise not have. For example, in 2022, ROKJ Ticino supported 150 young people in difficulty, providing them with meals, school materials, and recreational activities for a total value of 50,000 CHF. ### Comparisons with Other Initiatives Compared to similar initiatives, ROKJ Ticino stands out for its targeted approach and collaboration with local entities and authorities. The presence of Sebalter, a prominent artist, adds extra value to the event, attracting the attention of a wider audience and potentially increasing fundraising. For example, last year a similar concert in Bellinzona raised 30,000 CHF, thanks to the participation of a local artist. The Lugano evening could surpass this result thanks to the greater visibility of Sebalter. ### Future Scenarios If the initiative is successful, it could be replicated in other cities in the Canton of Ticino, involving an increasing number of people and increasing the positive impact on the community. For example, Locarno and Chiasso could be the next stops. Buy Tickets: Tickets are available at 50 CHF for adults and 25 CHF for young people under 18. Make a Donation: If you can't attend in person, consider making a donation. You can make a bank transfer to the postal account 12-156-8 with the reference 'Donation ROKJ Ticino' or use crowdfunding platforms like RaiseNow. Participate in the Auction: During the evening, there will be a charity auction with items and services donated by local businesses. Who can participate in the ROKJ Ticino solidarity evening?
Everyone can participate in the ROKJ Ticino solidarity evening. The event is open to the public and represents an opportunity to support young people in difficulty and promote social integration.
