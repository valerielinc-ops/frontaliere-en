Lombardy increases healthcare salaries at the border: +20% for doctors and nurses

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 26 April 2026

New state law funds the increase with a levy from 3% to 6% on the salaries of cross-border workers

Contesto

TL;DR - Lombardy raises salaries for border healthcare workers by 20% to retain talent. - Increase funded by 3% deduction from cross-border workers' salaries. - Affects 7,000 workers in Varese, Como, Lecco, and Varese. - Doctors gain €10,000/year, nurses €5,400/year. ## Key facts - Cosa: Aumento del 20% per i lavoratori sanitari nelle aree di confine. - Quando: Implementazione entro settembre 2024, retroattivo dal 1° gennaio 2024. - Dove: Comuni entro 20 km dal confine svizzero, tra cui Varese, Como, Lecco. - Chi: 7.000 lavoratori sanitari, tra cui medici e infermieri. - Importo: Investimento di 45 milioni di euro, con un aumento di €10.000 lordi/anno per i medici e €5.400 lordi/anno per gli infermieri. - Scadenza: Contributo del 3% sui salari dei lavoratori frontalieri a partire da ottobre 2024. - Fonte: Legge Regionale 213/2023 e decreto attuativo. Lombardy will introduce a salary increase for doctors and nurses in the border areas with Switzerland by September. The aim is to counter the flight of healthcare personnel to the Canton of Ticino, where salaries are traditionally higher. The measure is provided for by State Law 213 of 2023, which establishes a 20% increase for healthcare workers. ## Who is involved The increase will affect approximately 7,000 healthcare workers, with an investment of around 45 million euros. Doctors will see an increase of approximately 10,000 euros gross per year, while nurses will receive around 5,400 euros gross more per year. The municipalities involved are those located less than 20 km from the Swiss border, including Varese, Como, Lecco, and Varese. ### Concrete examples - Varese: A doctor with a gross salary of 60,000 euros will see an increase of 10,000 euros, bringing their salary to 70,000 euros gross per year. - Como: A nurs...

Dettagli operativi

The increase in salaries for healthcare workers at the Swiss border represents a significant step forward for Lombardy. The measure, funded through a contribution on the salaries of cross-border workers, aims to retain healthcare personnel in Lombardy, preventing the exodus to the Canton of Ticino, where working conditions and salaries are often more advantageous. ## Implications for cross-border workers Cross-border workers who work in Lombardy and reside in Ticino will see a reduction in their net salary due to the 3% contribution. However, this sacrifice is justified by the improvement in working conditions for local healthcare workers, who in turn guarantee quality healthcare services for the entire community, including cross-border workers. ### Comparison with the previous situation Before this law, healthcare workers in Lombardy near the Swiss border were often disadvantaged compared to their Swiss colleagues. The 20% increase aims to bridge this gap, making working conditions more competitive. This could also attract new healthcare professionals to the border areas, further improving the quality of services offered. ### Concrete examples and real numbers For example, a nurse working in a hospital in Varese with an annual gross salary of 36,000 euros will see an increase to 43,200 euros. A specialist doctor who earned 72,000 euros will move to 86,400 euros. These increases are funded by a 3% contribution on the salaries of cross-border workers who live in Ticino but work in Lombardy. ### Regulations and dates The Lombardy regional law no. 12/2023, which came into force on January 1, 2024, introduces the 3% contribution on the salaries of cross-border workers.
