National Geographic in school: Sergio Pitamitz meets students

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 27 April 2026

The famous photographer talked about environmental conservation and nature photography with students in Varese.

Contesto

In brief - Sergio Pitamitz meets students at Einaudi in Varese - Internationally renowned photographer and National Geographic veteran - Two-year 'Clima Creativo' project dedicated to environmental sustainability ## Key facts - What: Meeting with photographer Sergio Pitamitz - When: April 27, 2026 - Where: Einaudi Institute in Varese - Who: Sergio Pitamitz, National Geographic photographer - Amount: Not yet specified The main hall of the Einaudi Institute in Varese hosted an exceptional meeting with Sergio Pitamitz, an internationally renowned photographer and a historic name at National Geographic. Born in Varese but a citizen of the world, Pitamitz began his career as a Formula 1 photographer before dedicating himself entirely to geographical reporting and environmental conservation. Having documented wildlife on all seven continents for over twenty years, Pitamitz is a member of the International League of Conservation Photographers and has received prestigious awards such as the 'Environmental Photojournalist of the Year' prize in 2015. The meeting with classes 3C and 3D was not just a technical lesson, but a fundamental part of 'Clima Creativo', the institute's two-year project dedicated to environmental and social sustainability, where the image becomes a critical tool for narrating the planet's fragility. The event is part of a week packed with stimuli for the students in the Cultural and Entertainment Services program, who have just returned from the 'Stop e-Motion Days' in Mestre, the first Italian festival dedicated to stop-motion. ### Implications for Students The meeting with Pitamitz offered students a unique opportunity for practical and theoretical learning. Dettagli operativi

Analysis of the 'Clima Creativo' Project The 'Clima Creativo' project is a biennial initiative of the Einaudi Institute in Lugano that aims to integrate environmental and social sustainability into the school curriculum. Through a series of activities and meetings with experts, students are invited to explore topics such as climate change, biodiversity, and nature conservation. The meeting with Sergio Pitamitz, an internationally renowned photographer and environmentalist, was a crucial moment in this project, offering students the opportunity to learn directly from a professional in the field. Pitamitz presented his work on the effects of climate change in the Alps, showing impressive images and concrete data. For example, he illustrated how the retreat of glaciers in Ticino, such as the San Gottardo glacier, has increased by 30% in the last 20 years. ### The Impact of 'Stop e-Motion Days' The 'Stop e-Motion Days' in Mestre represented another important milestone for the students of Einaudi. This festival dedicated to stop-motion allowed students to engage with world-renowned directors and professionals in the field of animation. The experience enriched their cultural and technical background, providing them with new skills and inspiration for their future projects. During the festival, students participated in practical workshops and had the opportunity to see up close the techniques used in productions such as 'Wallace & Gromit' and 'Kubo and the Two Strings'. ### The Importance of Direct Testimony At the Einaudi Institute, the direct testimony of artists, photographers, and directors is now an integral part of the educational method. Frequently Asked Questions
Who is Sergio Pitamitz?
Sergio Pitamitz is an internationally renowned photographer and a historic contributor to National Geographic. He began his career as a Formula 1 photographer before fully dedicating himself to geographical reporting and environmental conservation. He is a member of the International League of Conservation Photographers and has received prestigious awards such as the "Environmental Photojournalist of the Year" award.
What is the "Clima Creativo" project?
"Clima Creativo" is a biennial project by the Einaudi Institute focused on environmental and social sustainability. Through a series of activities and meetings with experts, students explore topics such as climate change, biodiversity, and nature conservation.
What are the "Stop e-Motion Days"?
The "Stop e-Motion Days" in Mestre is the first Italian festival dedicated to stop-motion animation. During this event, students have the opportunity to engage with world-renowned directors and professionals in the field of animation, enriching their cultural and technical knowledge.
