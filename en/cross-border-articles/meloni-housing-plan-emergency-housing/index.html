Meloni's Housing Plan: green light for emergency decree

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 30 April 2026

The Council of Ministers approves the Housing Plan with 10.5 billion for public and private construction. Meloni: priority for affordable housing

Contesto

In brief - Cdm approves Housing Plan with 10.5 billion - Meloni: priority for affordable housing - 60,000 public housing units to be recovered ## Key facts - What: Housing Plan decree-law - When: April 30, 2026 - Where: Italy - Who: Council of Ministers - Amount: 10.5 billion euros The Council of Ministers has approved the decree-law with the 'urgent provisions for the implementation of the Housing Plan'. The measure, presented by Prime Minister Giorgia Meloni, addresses the housing emergency in Italy with a plan articulated in three pillars. 'The problem of the housing emergency exists everywhere, it does not only concern large cities like Milan, Rome, Florence, Naples', explained Meloni. 'We believe that the State should strengthen the response of public residential construction, but also respond to the needs of a different segment of the population'. ### Three pillars of the Housing Plan The first pillar provides for extraordinary measures for the recovery and maintenance of the existing stock of public residential construction. The goal is to make available 60,000 homes that today cannot be assigned, with an investment of 1.7 billion euros. Furthermore, 4.8 billion euros are allocated for urban regeneration. The second pillar concentrates and simplifies all national and European resources destined for the housing emergency in a single instrument, for a total of 3.6 billion euros. The third pillar represents an innovation, creating the conditions to involve private investments. The State ensures the private investor bureaucratic simplifications and fast procedures.
