Next week, on Wednesday, April 1, a moment of remembrance will be held at the Italian Embassy in Bern in memory of the 41 victims, an event that could represent a step towards diplomatic thaw. Cornado's presence at the ceremony underscores the importance of a return to normalcy in relations between the two countries. Dettagli operativi

The current diplomatic situation is part of a broader framework of dialogue between Italy and Switzerland, aimed at strengthening judicial cooperation and overcoming the differences that emerged during the investigation into the Crans-Montana disaster. The Italian request, formulated by the government of Giorgia Meloni, to establish a joint investigative team, has been met with a positive response from Swiss authorities, albeit with some reservations. Judicial cooperation is taking shape through meetings between investigators from the two countries, which took place in Valais and Bern in recent days. The handling of the investigation has raised many criticisms, both in Italy and in Switzerland, regarding transparency and the speed with which responsibilities were addressed. The release of Moretti, which sparked outrage in Italy, was a breaking point, but now efforts are being made to overcome tensions through a more structured dialogue. Cooperation is also taking place through the exchange of information and meetings between police authorities and magistrates. A concrete example is the meeting between representatives of the cantonal police and their Italian colleagues on Wednesday, aimed at establishing shared protocols for future investigations. The presence of Ambassador Cornado at the Crans-Montana commemoration fits into this strategy of détente. The will of both parties is to strengthen mutual trust and accelerate investigative procedures, so that full light can be shed on the causes of the fire. Judicial cooperation between the two countries is based on well-defined regulations, including the 2004 cooperation agreement, which allows for rapid exchange of information and investigative support.
