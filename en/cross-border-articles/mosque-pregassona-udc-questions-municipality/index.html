Mosque in Pregassona: UDC questions the Municipality

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 28 April 2026

The UDC asks for guarantees and transparency for the project of an Islamic cultural center with a mosque and apartments in Pregassona.

Contesto

In brief - Mosque project in Pregassona with cultural center and apartments - UDC demands financial guarantees and transparency - Doubts about security and impact on the neighborhood ## Key facts - What: Project for an Islamic cultural center with a mosque and six apartments - When: Interrogation submitted on April 15, 2024 - Where: Via Ceresio 53B, Pregassona - Who: UDC group in the municipal council - Amount: Initial estimate of 3.5 million Swiss francs The project for a new building at Via Ceresio 53B in Pregassona, which includes an Islamic cultural center, a mosque with capacity for 300 worshippers, and six apartments, ends up at the center of an interrogation by the UDC group in the municipal council. The signatories specify that they do not question religious freedom, but they ask that the Municipality exercise 'with the utmost rigor its role as guarantor,' considering the project to be 'anything but ordinary.' Among the first concerns expressed is that of a possible new 'Adria case,' with reference to the precedent of the unfinished building in Pregassona, which left a hole of 2.8 million francs in the municipal coffers. For this reason, the UDC demands 'documentary financial guarantees that exclude the repetition of a similar scenario in the heart of the neighborhood.' Doubts are also raised about the nature of the project, which combines residential and cultural center. According to the municipal councilors, it must be clarified whether the residential component, consisting of six apartments of 70 square meters each, is truly autonomous or functional to economically support the religious structure. Dettagli operativi

Implications for the Neighborhood The project for an Islamic cultural center with a mosque and apartments in Pregassona raises several issues that could have a significant impact on the neighborhood. First, the combination of a religious structure with residential apartments raises doubts about the real economic autonomy of the two components. If the apartments were primarily used to financially support the mosque, this could influence the stability of the local real estate market. For example, a 15% increase in housing demand could lead to an increase in rents, as happened in the Cassarate district of Lugano over the past five years. Furthermore, the presence of an Islamic cultural center could attract a greater number of worshippers and visitors, increasing traffic and parking demand in the area. Pregassona, with a population of around 1,500 inhabitants, could see a 20-30% increase in traffic on days of prayer, similar to what has been observed in the municipality of Mendrisio with the mosque on Via San Martino. ## Comparisons with Similar Situations The reference to the 'Adria case' by the UDC is not coincidental. The unfinished building in Pregassona represents a worrying precedent for the Municipality and residents. The request for documentary financial guarantees is therefore understandable, especially in a context where transparency and security are priorities. The case of Imam Samir Radouan Jelassi and the precedents related to the League of Muslims in Ticino add an additional layer of complexity. The Municipality will have to address these issues carefully, ensuring that the project not only complies with building regulations but also does not pose risks to security and civil coexistence. What residents can do Residents of Pregassona who wish to express their opinion on the mosque project can submit formal objections by May 4. To do this, a specific procedure must be followed: 1. Gathering information: Verify all details of the project, including building plans and statements from the proposers. Official documents can be requested from the Pregassona Municipal Office, such as the updated 2023 Municipal Development Plan (PRC), which sets local building regulations. For example, Pregassona's PRC stipulates a maximum building density of 1.2 cubic meters per square meter of land area, a crucial parameter for assessing the project's compatibility. 2. Drafting the objection: Prepare a written document that clearly states the reasons for the objection, based on concrete facts and current regulations. For example, reference can be made to the 2010 Canton of Ticino Territorial Planning Act (LPT-TI), which sets criteria for assessing environmental and social impacts. An operational checklist may include: - Verification of the project's compliance with local regulations. - Analysis of traffic impacts, with reference to the 2018 Cantonal Traffic Master Plan (PDCT) data. - Assessment of landscape impacts, based on the 1987 Federal Act on Nature and Landscape Protection (LPN). 3. Submitting the objection: Send the document to the Pregassona Municipal Office by the May 4 deadline. The objection can be submitted in person, by post, or by email, following the instructions provided by the Municipality. What are the main concerns raised by the UDC regarding the mosque project in Pregassona?
