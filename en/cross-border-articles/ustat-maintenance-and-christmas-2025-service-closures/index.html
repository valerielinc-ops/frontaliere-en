Dettagli operativi

For cross-border workers employed in Ticino but residing in Italy, closures by the USTAT (the Ticino Statistical Office) pose a potential obstacle during a period already marked by tax and administrative deadlines. Most procedures for cross-border workers — from filing tax returns to applying for work permits — require access to updated data or the submission of documents at cantonal offices. An unexpected closure or disruption to online services could lead to procedural delays with consequences for salaries, deductions, or social benefits. ### Worst-case scenario: what happens if a deadline is missed? Imagine a cross-border worker who must file their 2025 tax return by 31 December 2025, but the relevant USTAT office closes in the afternoon. In this case, the user has two options: 1. Submit the return online via the Italian Revenue Agency’s portal, but risk not being able to verify any formal errors that require Ticino-specific data (e.g., cantonal IRPEF tax rates or social security contributions). 2. Wait until 2 January 2026 to visit a municipal or cantonal office in person, risking penalties for late submission. > ⚠️ Note: The 31 December closure only applies to the afternoon, but many cross-border workers may have already scheduled appointments or requests for that morning. In an emergency, there is no specific protocol for cross-border workers: USTAT directs users to the assistance page on its website, which in the past has provided generic responses without priority for cross-border categories. ### Comparison with previous years Over the past five years, the Ticino cantonal administration’s Christmas closures have always been confirmed for the afternoon of 31 December, without exceptions. Frequently Asked Questions
What happens if I need to file my tax return by 31 December and USTAT is closed?
You can file your return through the Italian Revenue Agency’s online portal (Fisconline). However, if your case requires specific Ticino data (e.g., cantonal IRPEF rates), you will need to wait until 2 January 2026 to check for errors. If you file late, you may face penalties, but you can request an extension by explaining the unavailability of USTAT.
How can I obtain updated USTAT data if the portal is inaccessible?
Download the data in advance from the USTAT portal and save a local copy. If you cannot find what you need, send an email request to USTAT (ustat@ti.ch) by 28 December, clearly stating the urgency. Responses may arrive after the Christmas bank holidays, but you will receive written confirmation to attach to your documents.
Do USTAT closures on 31 December also affect cross-border worker permits?
The closures apply to all services of the cantonal administration, including those that may be required to verify data related to permits (for example, declared income). While this is not a formal block, it could cause delays in the granting or renewal of permits if your application depends on updated data provided by USTAT.
Can I submit a refund or deduction request without USTAT data?
Yes, but you risk losing the benefit if the missing data is required for verification. For example, Italian family deductions may require confirmation of income declared in Ticino. In such cases, it is best to wait until 2 January or contact the Italian INPS directly for an alternative solution.
Is there an emergency number for cross-border workers in case of issues with USTAT?
No. USTAT does not provide a specific emergency number for cross-border workers. For tax-related matters, you can contact the Italian Revenue Agency’s toll-free number (848.800.444). For work permits, contact your local municipality in Italy or the relevant employment centre.
