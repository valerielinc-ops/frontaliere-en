Varese: 'The Sea' film and live Gaza debate

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 30 April 2026

Varese's Cineclub Filmstudio 90 screens 'The Sea' with a debate and live link to the Global Sumud Flotilla to Gaza.

Contesto

In brief - Screening of the film 'The Sea' in Varese on May 6, 2026 - Live link with the Global Sumud Flotilla heading to Gaza - Participation of Francesca Albanese and Maddalena Oliva ## Key facts - What: Screening of the film 'The Sea' and debate - When: May 6, 2026, 9:15 PM - Where: Filmstudio 90, Varese - Who: Cineclub Filmstudio 90, Francesca Albanese, Maddalena Oliva - Cost: Ticket €7.00 (reserved for ARCI members) The Cineclub Filmstudio 90 in Varese hosts a special evening dedicated to the film 'The Sea', scheduled for May 6, 2026 at 9:15 PM. The event includes the screening of the film followed by a live streaming connection with the Global Sumud Flotilla, currently sailing towards Gaza. A unique opportunity for in-depth analysis and discussion, enriched by the participation of exceptional guests, including Francesca Albanese, who will dialogue with the journalist from Il Fatto Quotidiano, Maddalena Oliva. ### Plot and meaning The plot of the film tells the story of a 12-year-old Palestinian boy who tries to reach the sea for the first time. After being turned away at a checkpoint, he escapes and crosses Israel clandestinely; his father pursues him, risking arrest and work. An essential road movie that intertwines tension and tenderness, with a powerful idea: the right of a child to dream. Official candidate for the Oscars 2026, 'The Sea' has sparked strong reactions at the institutional level for its ability to challenge the dominant narrative on Israel. The film offers a direct and unrhetorical look at the daily reality marked by permits, controls, and borders, providing an intense and provocative testimony. ### Participation and debate The evening of cinema and reflection is open to debate and participation. Practical Analysis The evening at Filmstudio 90 in Varese not only offers a cultural opportunity but also a moment of collective reflection on highly topical issues. The film 'The Sea' addresses themes such as freedom, human rights, and the daily difficulties faced by those living in conflict zones. For cross-border workers who live and work between Italy and Switzerland, this event can be an opportunity to engage with different stories and realities, enriching their perspective. ### Comparison with the Previous Situation In the past, events of this kind were less frequent and often limited to academic or institutional contexts. The choice to organize a public screening followed by a debate with a live connection to Gaza represents a significant step towards greater awareness and civic participation. This type of initiative can help create a more open and inclusive dialogue, engaging a wider and more diverse audience. ### Concrete Scenarios Cross-border workers attending this event can find points of reflection on how the themes addressed in the film relate to their daily lives. For example, the issue of permits and checks can be a relevant topic of discussion for those who cross the borders between Italy and Switzerland daily. Furthermore, the debate can offer the opportunity to engage with experts and activists, gaining a greater awareness of the political and social dynamics that influence their lives. ### Concrete Procedures To participate in the event, cross-border workers must ensure they are members of ARCI. If they are not already, they can join by following the instructions available on the Filmstudio 90 website. Once registered, they can purchase a ticket for €7.00 and attend the evening.
