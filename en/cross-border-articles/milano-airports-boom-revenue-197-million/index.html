Milan Airports: Record Revenue and 197M Dividends

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 28 April 2026

Malpensa and Linate close 2025 with 876.8M revenue and 8% passenger traffic growth. Record dividends for shareholders.

Contesto

In brief - Malpensa and Linate close 2025 with 42.3 million passengers (+8%) - Revenues at 876.8 million (+6.5%), net profit at 190.6 million (+11.7%) - Dividends of 197.8 million, 0.7912 euros per share ## Key facts - What: 2025 Balance Sheet of Milan Airports - When: April 28, 2026 - Where: Milan Malpensa and Linate - Who: Sea spa - Amount: 876.8 million euros (revenues), 190.6 million (net profit) The year 2025 ends on a positive note for Sea spa, the company managing Milan Malpensa and Milan Linate airports. The shareholders' meeting, held on April 28 at Linate's headquarters, approved the annual balance sheet confirming the group's expansive trend. Throughout the year, Milan's airports handled 42.3 million passengers, an 8% increase compared to 2024. Even more significant was the growth in cargo traffic, reaching 759.4 thousand tonnes, approximately a 4.3% increase compared to the 728 thousand tonnes of the previous year. Operational data reflects on the economic accounts: the group's management revenues rise to 876.8 million euros, a 6.5% increase compared to the 823.1 million of 2024. Ebitda grows by 15%, from 350.5 to 403.3 million euros, while net profit stands at 190.6 million euros, with an approximately 11.7% increase compared to the 170.6 million of the previous year. Net financial indebtedness also increased, reaching 325.7 million euros, approximately a 28% increase compared to the 254.4 million of 2024. ### Investments and dividends Among the main investments in recent years are the refurbishment of Terminal 1 before the Olympics and the new T2-Gallarate railway, activated at the beginning of 2026.
