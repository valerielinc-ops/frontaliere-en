Di Valerie Linc · Published on 30 April 2026

The ECB keeps interest rates unchanged, with implications for cross-border workers and the Ticino economy.

Contesto

In brief - The ECB keeps interest rates unchanged. - Deposit rate at 2.00%, refinancing at 2.15%, marginal at 2.40%. - Inflation and growth under observation. ## Key facts - What: ECB decision on interest rates. - When: 30 April 2026. - Where: Frankfurt, Germany. - Who: Governing Council of the European Central Bank. - Amount: Deposit rate at 2.00%, refinancing at 2.15%, marginal at 2.40%. The European Central Bank has decided to keep the three key interest rates unchanged. In detail, the interest rates on deposits with the central bank, on main refinancing operations and on marginal lending operations will remain unchanged at 2.00%, 2.15% and 2.40%, respectively. The decision was taken in light of new information that is broadly in line with the previous assessment of inflation prospects. However, the upside risks to inflation and the downside risks to growth have intensified. The conflict in the Middle East has caused a sharp increase in energy prices, pushing up inflation and weighing on the confidence climate. The implications of the war for medium-term inflation and economic activity will depend on the intensity and duration of the energy price shock as well as the extent of its indirect and second-round effects. The longer the war continues and the longer energy prices remain high, the greater the likely impact on broader measures of inflation and the economy. ### Impact on Canton Ticino For cross-border workers who work in Switzerland and reside in Italy, the ECB's decision to keep rates unchanged could have significant implications. Analysis of the implications for cross-border workers The ECB's decision to keep interest rates unchanged has several implications for cross-border workers who work in Switzerland and reside in Italy. Firstly, high inflation could erode purchasing power, making it harder for cross-border workers to manage daily expenses. Additionally, high energy prices could affect transportation and heating costs, crucial elements for those who commute daily between the two countries. ### Comparison with the previous situation Before the ECB's decision, interest rates had already been increased to combat inflation. However, the current situation is more complex due to the conflict in the Middle East, which has led to a sharp rise in energy prices. This scenario requires a more cautious approach from the ECB, which must balance the need to control inflation with that of supporting economic growth. ### Possible scenarios If inflation continues to rise, the ECB may be forced to review its monetary policy and raise interest rates. This could have a negative impact on credit costs and investment returns, affecting cross-border workers who have loans or investments in euros.
