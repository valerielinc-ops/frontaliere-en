Proposte 2026 in Cernobbio: textile market worth 1.3 billion euros (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 4 May 2026

The 33rd edition of Proposte, the home textile fair, opens at Villa Erba with 87 international exhibitors and a focus on sustainability and innovation.

Contesto

In brief - Proposte 2026 opens at Villa Erba with 87 international exhibitors - Home textiles market worth 1.3 billion euros - New Interni.Proposte Award for innovation in the sector ## Key facts - What: 33rd edition of Proposte, home textiles fair - When: May 5, 2026 - Where: Villa Erba, Cernobbio - Who: 87 international exhibitors, including 37 Italian ones - Amount: Home textiles market worth 1.3 billion euros Everything is ready for the ribbon-cutting of the 33rd edition of Proposte, the world preview of home textiles and drapery, which will open its doors tomorrow, May 5, at Villa Erba. With a lineup of 87 international exhibitors (+11% compared to 2025), the event confirms itself as the strategic hub to discover trends and innovations in the sector of textiles, trimmings, and wall coverings. The concept chosen for 2026, 'Heritage Forward,' outlines the fair's strategy: to merge the invaluable manufacturing heritage with the push towards digitalization, sustainability, and internationalization. An approach that transforms Proposte into a contemporary experience, enriched by renewed spaces and high-profile collaborations with the world of author design. ### Cristina Celestino's Inspirational Interiors At the heart of the experiential restyling of this edition is the collaboration with architect Cristina Celestino. Called upon to reinterpret the common areas of the congress center (a work by Mario Bellini), Celestino has translated the Heritage Forward theme into a path that transforms transit spaces into relational environments. The focal point of the intervention concerns the enhancement of the Regina Wing, connected to the new Esedra Lounge through a corridor set up with fabrics provided by the exhibiting companies.
