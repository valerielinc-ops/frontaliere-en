USI Startup Centre ranks among the best in Europe (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 24 February 2026

The USI center is ranked 69th in Europe and 5th among Swiss hubs according to the Financial Times.

TL;DR - USI Startup Centre ranks 69th in Europe, 5th in Switzerland. - Only two Swiss university incubators made the list. - USI fosters entrepreneurial culture among researchers, students. - Center offers training, incubation, and networking opportunities. ## Key facts - Ranking: 69th in Europe, 5th in Switzerland. - Date: Published on February 19, 2026. - Countries: 25 countries analyzed. - Incubators: 180 incubators and accelerators evaluated. - Criteria: Effectiveness, network, investment attraction. - Top Swiss: Venture Kick in Schlieren (ZH). - Services: Training, incubation, community building. - Location: Lugano, Ticino, Switzerland. The Università della Svizzera italiana (USI) once again stands out in the field of innovation and entrepreneurship support. Its Startup Centre, making its debut in the prestigious 'Europe’s Leading Start-Up Hubs' ranking published by the Financial Times in collaboration with Statista and Sifted, has secured the 69th position in Europe and 5th among the 14 Swiss hubs selected. This important ranking, published on February 19, 2026, analyzes 180 incubators and accelerators from 25 countries and is based on a combination of startup feedback, expert recommendations, and long-term results. This achievement is even more significant considering that the USI Startup Centre is one of only two Swiss university incubators included in the list. This recognition highlights USI's commitment to fostering an entrepreneurial culture among researchers, students, and entrepreneurs. According to the university, the center helps transform innovative ideas and scientific results into high-impact solutions, solidifying USI's role as an attractive hub for education and technology transfer. Dettagli operativi

Opportunities for cross-border workers and startups in Ticino The success of the USI Startup Centre represents an opportunity for those living and working in the Canton of Ticino. This incubator focuses on high-intensity scientific entrepreneurial initiatives, offering concrete tools and high-level training. Cross-border workers who wish to start their own business or collaborate with innovative startups can find a fertile and stimulating environment at USI. Key services offered by the center include: - Entrepreneurial training programs designed for students and professionals seeking to acquire specific skills. - Dedicated incubation, providing physical spaces, mentorship, and access to a network of strategic contacts. - Community building activities, fostering networking among entrepreneurs, researchers, and investors. Additionally, thanks to Lugano's strategic location, Ticino can attract talent and capital from both Italy and the rest of Switzerland. The proximity to key border crossings, such as Brogeda and Ponte Tresa, makes this ecosystem particularly accessible for those residing across the border.

Punti chiave

Practical tips for those looking to innovate If you're a cross-border worker with a business idea or want to collaborate with startups in Ticino, the USI Startup Centre can provide you with a launchpad. To access the programs, it's important to prepare a solid business plan and understand local regulations, such as those related to work permits (G or B). Additionally, given the importance of networking, participating in events organized by the center can open significant doors. 💡 Remember: the Ticino environment is highly competitive but offers great opportunities for those who are determined and well-prepared. Frequently Asked Questions
What position does the USI Startup Centre occupy in the Financial Times' 'Europe's Leading Start-Up Hubs' ranking?
The USI Startup Centre ranks 69th in Europe and 5th among the 14 Swiss hubs selected in the Financial Times' 'Europe's Leading Start-Up Hubs' ranking.
What are the main services offered by the USI Startup Centre for cross-border commuters?
The USI Startup Centre offers entrepreneurial training programs, dedicated incubation with spaces, mentorship and networking, and community building activities to foster collaboration between entrepreneurs, researchers and investors.
What are the requirements to participate in the incubation programs of the USI Startup Centre as an Italian cross-border commuter?
To access the USI Startup Centre's incubation programs, Italian cross-border commuters must have a valid Swiss work permit (G or B) and submit a detailed business plan. It is advisable to attend networking events organized by the center to increase the chances of selection.
Does the USI Startup Centre offer support in finding funding for startups led by cross-border commuters?
Yes, the center provides access to a network of investors and mentors, as well as specific programs to attract capital. However, the availability of funding depends on the sector and the maturity of the project. Entrepreneurs can also benefit from Ticino tax breaks.
How can I assess whether Ticino is economically viable to start a startup as a cross-border commuter?
Use the living cost comparator provided by USI to compare fixed expenses (rent, transport, taxes) between Ticino and your area of residence in Italy. It also considers the local market potential and opportunities for collaboration with Swiss companies.
