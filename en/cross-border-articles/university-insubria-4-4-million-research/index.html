Insubria University: €4.4m for Como and Varese research

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 28 April 2026

Lombardy Region funds IMPACTT and SustAA projects to boost research and sustainable innovation infrastructure.

Contesto

In brief - Università Insubria receives 4.4 million from Regione Lombardia - Funding for IMPACTT and SustAA projects - Infrastructure enhancement in Como, Varese, and Busto Arsizio ## Key facts - What: Funding for research infrastructure - When: April 28, 2026 - Where: Como, Varese, Busto Arsizio - Who: Università Insubria, Regione Lombardia - Amount: 4,400,000 euros The Università dell'Insubria in Como and Varese has received a 4.4 million euro funding from Regione Lombardia to enhance research infrastructure. The funding, awarded through a regional tender, will be used to develop new technological platforms and strengthen existing ones at the Como, Varese, and Busto Arsizio campuses. The funding is part of the overall 50 million euro allocation of the Regional Fesr Program 2021-2027, exclusively destined for public and private universities in Lombardy for the creation and modernization of research infrastructure. ### Funded projects The funding will be managed by Criett, the Research and Technological Transfer Center of Università dell'Insubria, founded in 2024. Criett has secured resources for two strategic projects: IMPACTT and SustAA. The first project, IMPACTT, receives nearly 3 million euros (2,999,860.77 euros) and aims to enhance the Material Analysis and Characterization Platform in Como and establish a new biomedical research platform in Busto Arsizio. The goal is to valorize research and technological transfer in the sectors of advanced materials and diagnostics for aging, within the context of the Silver Economy. The second project, SustAA, receives approximately 1.4 million euros (1,414,454.40 euros) and involves the creation in Varese of an infrastructure dedicated to sustainable innovation in the sector of amino acids and their derivatives. Dettagli operativi

• ## Implications for the territory The 4.4 million euro funding represents a significant opportunity for the Como, Varese, and Busto Arsizio area. Enhancing research infrastructures will not only improve the quality of university education but also contribute to creating new collaborations between the academic world and local businesses. This dialogue is fundamental to transforming research into concrete innovation, which can lead to new products, services, and technologies. ### Collaboration with businesses One of the main objectives of the funding is to strengthen the link between universities and businesses, particularly small and medium-sized enterprises. This type of collaboration is essential to transfer the knowledge gained through research into practical solutions that can be implemented in the market. For example, the IMPACTT project aims to create a direct connection between local businesses and international research centers, facilitating the exchange of ideas and technologies. ### Economic impact Investing in research infrastructures can have a significant economic impact on the territory. By attracting high-profile projects and talents, the University of Insubria can contribute to creating new jobs and stimulating local economic growth. Furthermore, enhancing research infrastructures can attract external investments, further strengthening the region's economy. ### Sustainable innovation The SustAA project, dedicated to sustainable innovation in the amino acids and their derivatives sector, is an example of how research can contribute to developing sustainable solutions for key sectors such as nutraceuticals, pharmaceuticals, cosmetics, and food. • ## Next Steps Now that the funding has been allocated, the University of Insubria is working to define the next operational phases of the IMPACTT and SustAA projects. This includes detailed planning of research activities, purchasing new equipment, and creating new collaborations with local businesses. Professor Flavia Marinelli and her team are organizing a meeting with the university's scientific community to discuss the use of the new funds and ensure they are used effectively. ### What Businesses Can Do Small and medium-sized enterprises interested in collaborating with the University of Insubria can contact Criett to explore collaboration opportunities. This can include participation in joint research projects, access to new technologies, and the use of research infrastructures to develop new products and services. Businesses can also benefit from technology transfer, which can help them stay competitive in a constantly evolving market. ### Opportunities for Researchers Researchers interested in participating in the IMPACTT and SustAA projects can contact Criett for information on how to join the research team. This is an excellent opportunity for researchers to work on innovative projects and collaborate with top-level experts.
