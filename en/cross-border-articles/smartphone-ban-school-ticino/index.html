Smartphone Ban in Ticino Schools: New Measures (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 21 March 2026

Starting March 30, Ticino extends the smartphone ban to all compulsory schools to protect the health of young people.

Starting March 30, 2024, students will not be allowed to use smartphones and other electronic devices during class hours and in all school areas. This initiative was presented by DECS director Marina Carobbio-Guscetti as a necessary response to protect the public health of young people and ensure a healthier educational environment. The excessive use of electronic devices and social media is an increasing concern for parents and educators. According to a survey conducted in 2023 in middle schools in municipalities like Lugano and Locarno, 68% of students reported using their smartphones during school hours, despite the existing ban. This has prompted the DECS to strengthen measures, especially in common areas like cafeterias and playgrounds, where monitoring is more challenging. Dettagli operativi

The new directives establish that, both inside and outside schools, phones must be turned off and not visible throughout the entire school day. This measure will apply to all municipal schools in Ticino, including those in Lugano and Bellinzona, and will also encompass teacher training and active family involvement. The DECS's intervention is part of a broader context of prevention and awareness regarding the risks associated with technology. Schools, such as the Comprehensive Institute of Mendrisio, will be called upon to organize meetings and workshops to discuss the dangers of excessive social media use. According to research conducted in 2022, 70% of Ticino adolescents spend more than 3 hours a day on social media, marking a 25% increase compared to 2020. This phenomenon has led to growing concerns about attention span and mental health issues among adolescents, as evidenced by a 15% rise in anxiety cases among young people. To educate young people on the conscious and responsible use of technology, schools will implement a digital education program that will take place throughout the school year. However, current regulations regarding digital education do not impose penalties for violators, raising questions about their effectiveness. Punti chiave

For parents, it is essential to stay informed about the new directives regarding the smartphone ban in Ticino schools and to actively participate in this educational process. Collaboration between institutions and families will be fundamental for the success of these measures. Schools are not alone in this task; parents need to be involved in raising awareness about the potential risks associated with technology. For example, the Municipality of Lugano has recently launched a training program for parents, with monthly meetings addressing the risks linked to excessive smartphone use, highlighting that 30% of middle school students reported concentration problems. To support families, useful tools are available on our website, such as the salary calculator that can help better plan expenses related to education and technology. This new approach to the smartphone ban in schools will significantly impact school life and the mental well-being of students. According to a study conducted by SUPSI, 20% of students reported feeling less stressed when they do not use their phones at school. The DECS will continue to monitor the evolution of the situation and implement necessary measures to ensure a healthy learning environment. The new regulations will come into effect on January 1, 2024, and will include penalties for violations, such as fines of up to 500 CHF for families that do not comply with the rules. Specific details about the new regulations and their implementation will be communicated to schools and families before the new rules take effect. Frequently Asked Questions
What is the date of the ban on using smartphones in Ticino schools?
The ban on smartphone usage in Ticino schools begins on March 30, 2024.
What are the forecasts of reducing the use of electronic devices in Ticino schools after the introduction of the ban?
It is expected to reduce the use of at least 40% by the end of the school year 2024.
How will teachers be trained to manage the ban on smartphones in Ticino schools?
The DECS of Ticino will provide specific training to school staff on the risks associated with excessive use of electronic devices. This includes digital education sessions and strategies to monitor and manage the use of devices in the school, as part of the operational checklist provided to schools.
What initiatives are planned to involve parents in the ban on smartphones in Ticino schools?
The DECS will launch an awareness program for parents, with information meetings in various Ticino municipalities, such as Mendrisio and Bellinzona. These meetings aim to educate parents about the risks related to excessive use of devices and to build an educational alliance to support student welfare.
What are the expectations of reducing the use of social media among Ticino students after the implementation of new regulations?
With the implementation of new regulations, it is hoped to reduce the use of electronic devices in Ticino schools by at least 40% by the end of the school year 2024. This approach includes greater vigilance and education on the conscious use of technology, aiming for a healthier and responsible relationship with digital devices.
