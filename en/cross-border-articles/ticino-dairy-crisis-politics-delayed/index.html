Di Valerie Linc · Published on 26 April 2026

The Communist Party questions the government about the price of milk in Ticino, at historic lows. A political response to the dairy crisis is urgently needed.

Practical implications for farmers The crisis of Ticino milk has significant practical implications for local farmers. With the price of milk at historic lows, many agricultural businesses risk closure, with economic and social consequences for mountain communities. The loss of these businesses would not only mean the disappearance of an important economy but also the loss of a centuries-old cultural and technical heritage. ### Comparison with the previous situation Before the closure of Lati and the opening of the Blenio Valley dairy, the situation was different. The processing of Ticino milk was more stable, and farmers could count on a stronger domestic market. Now, the situation has changed, and farmers find themselves having to face an unprecedented crisis. ## Possible scenarios If the situation does not improve, several scenarios are possible. Firstly, there could be an increase in the importation of dairy products from abroad, with negative consequences for the local economy. Furthermore, the closure of many agricultural businesses would result in job losses and a negative impact on mountain communities. ### What farmers can do Farmers can try to diversify their production, for example by producing high-quality cheeses or other dairy products. Frequently Asked Questions
What are the main questions posed by the Communist Party to the State Council?
The main questions posed by the Communist Party to the State Council concern the volume of non-Ticino milk processed in Ticino in recent years, how and by whom it is processed, and whether it is possible to replace this milk with locally produced milk even during the winter period. Additionally, they have asked how the State Council intends to maintain the processing of Ticino milk in Ticino and avoid irregularities.
What are the practical implications for farmers?
The practical implications for farmers include the risk of closure of agricultural businesses, job losses, and a negative impact on mountain communities. Furthermore, the crisis of Ticino milk could lead to an increase in the import of dairy products from abroad, with negative consequences for the local economy.
What can farmers do to address the crisis?
Farmers can organize into cooperatives or associations to increase their bargaining power and create a stronger supply chain. Additionally, they can diversify their production, collaborate with other agricultural businesses, and lobby authorities to obtain political and economic support.
