Farewell to Csoa Il Molino: debris removal begins (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 4 May 2026

The City of Lugano starts the redevelopment work of the former slaughterhouse area. Estimated duration between one and a half and two months.

Contesto

In brief - Demolition work begins at Csoa Il Molino - Estimated duration between one and a half and two months - Ongoing investigation into criminal liability ## Key facts - What: Demolition and requalification of the former slaughterhouse area - When: Starting today, estimated duration between one and a half and two months - Where: Former slaughterhouse area, Lugano - Who: City of Lugano - Amount: 35,000 francs in compensation The City of Lugano has started today the requalification work of the former slaughterhouse area, marking the end of the symbol of Ticino self-management. The first step consists in the removal and disposal of the rubble, a preliminary operation indispensable for the realization of the architectural project Campus Matrix. The overall duration of the operation is estimated between one and a half and two months. Csoa Il Molino has always divided public opinion, but today, less than five years after its eviction and partial demolition, the final chapter is definitively written. The decision to start the work came after a long process that involved the city authorities and the self-managers. ### Timeline of events Before the 'night of the bulldozers', the city executive had given an ultimatum to the occupants with formal termination of the convention of 18 December 2002 signed between City, Canton and Alba Association. If the spaces were not freed within the deadline, the procedure would have followed its course as provided by law. To date, no request for mediation has been received by the Executive. From that ultimatum, little more than a month passed, characterized by weeks of tension between self-managers and authorities. Dettagli operativi

Impact on the community and future prospects The removal of the debris from the Csoa Il Molino represents a significant moment for the city of Lugano, not only from an urban planning perspective, but also socially. Once the redevelopment is complete, the area of the former slaughterhouse will be destined to host the architectural project Campus Matrix, an initiative aimed at transforming the area into a cultural and educational hub. For cross-border workers living and working in Ticino, the redevelopment of this area could offer new opportunities for integration and participation in the city's cultural life. However, the question of criminal responsibilities and the decisions made during the eviction and demolition remains open and could have repercussions on the local social and political climate. ### Removal and redevelopment procedure The procedure for removing the debris has been carefully planned to ensure the safety and efficiency of the operation. The estimated duration of between one and a half months and two months indicates a complex undertaking, which requires the collaboration of various authorities and professionals in the field. For cross-border workers interested in following the progress of the work, it is possible to consult the official websites of the City of Lugano and the Canton of Ticino for updates and detailed information. Transparency and open communication are fundamental to maintaining the community's trust and ensuring that the process is carried out in accordance with the laws and regulations in force. ### Comparison with the previous situation Before the eviction and demolition, the Csoa Il Molino was a symbol of self-management and cultural resistance.
