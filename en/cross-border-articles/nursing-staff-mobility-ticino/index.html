Nursing staff are increasingly in demand beyond the border. According to a report by the Italian Nursing Up union, around 7,000 people who have trained in Italy choose to move to work in another country every year. The national president of Nursing Up, Antonio De Palma, does not mince words and defines this phenomenon as 'expropriation of the future'. The training of a nurse costs around 30,000 euros, so it can be calculated that every year Italy loses over 200 million euros due to this hemorrhage of qualified personnel. Dettagli operativi

Switzerland is one of the countries most affected by this phenomenon, thanks to its high standard of living and the job opportunities offered by the healthcare sector. Companies specializing in the 'export' of nurses, such as Germitalia, offer permanent contracts and language courses to help new hires integrate into the Swiss job market. For example, in Lugano, in the canton of Ticino, the demand for qualified nurses is constantly increasing, with over 200 positions requested in 2022 alone. However, the Nursing Up union highlights the significant salary gaps between Italy and Switzerland, which represent one of the main reasons why young nurses choose to emigrate. According to data from the Federal Statistical Office (FSO), in 2020, the average salary of a nurse in Switzerland was around 6,300 francs per month, while in Italy it was about 2,500 euros per month. This wage gap is a strong incentive for young Italian nurses to seek work in Switzerland. Additionally, Switzerland offers a range of extra benefits, such as the opportunity to work in a multicultural environment and gain professional experience in an international context. To help new hires integrate into the Swiss job market, specialized companies like Germitalia provide language courses and ongoing training. For instance, in Locarno, the nursing school in the canton of Ticino offers training courses in Italian and German for foreign nurses. Moreover, the 2008 Labor Act (LPL) stipulates that employers must offer their employees the opportunity to participate in continuous training to improve their language and professional skills. Frequently Asked Questions
How much does it cost to get a work permit in Switzerland for an Italian nurse?
The work permit costs around 1,000 Swiss francs and must be renewed every 2 years.
What are the main requirements to work as a nurse in Switzerland?
Italian nurses must obtain a work permit, pass a language and professional competence exam, and obtain recognition of professional qualifications, which can cost up to 5,000 Swiss francs.
How long is a work permit for Italian nurses valid in Switzerland?
The work permit for Italian nurses in Switzerland is valid for two years, i.e. it must be renewed every 2 years. This guarantees constant monitoring of the work situation and compliance with the regulations in force for foreign professionals in the Swiss health sector.
What are the costs associated with the recognition of qualifications for Italian nurses in Switzerland?
The process of recognition of professional qualifications for Italian nurses in Switzerland can involve costs of up to CHF 5,000. It is a necessary investment to be able to legally practice the profession and access job opportunities in the Swiss health system.
What are the average salary differences between nurses in Italy and Switzerland?
The average salary of a nurse in Switzerland is around 6,300 Swiss francs per month, while in Italy it is around 2,500 euros per month. This significant wage gap is one of the main factors driving many Italian nurses to seek employment across borders.
