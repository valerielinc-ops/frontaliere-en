Biumo Park opening: Saturday, May 9th (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 4 May 2026

New equipped space with basketball court, ping pong and teqball. Ribbon cutting at 3 PM with tournaments and music

Contesto

In brief - New park in Biumo with basketball court, ping-pong and teqball - Inauguration Saturday May 9, 2026 at 3 PM - Informal tournaments, music with jam sessions and DJ sets - Collaboration with local associations for inclusive spaces ## Key facts - What: Inauguration of new park in Biumo - When: Saturday May 9, 2026 - Where: Via Arconati, Biumo, Varese - Who: Municipality of Varese, COVO Association, COOPUF Cooperative - Amount: 250,000 € (preliminary estimate, subject to change) - Accessibility: 10 minutes from Ponte Tresa and 15 minutes from Mendrisio The new park in Biumo, located on Via Arconati, will be inaugurated on Saturday, May 9, 2026. This equipped space was created as part of the works for the new student housing in Varese, with an estimated investment of 250,000 €. The park offers a regulation basketball court (20x10 meters), ping-pong tables (4 in total), a teqball court, benches, and green areas for socializing. The initiative is organized in collaboration with the youth association COVO and the cooperative COOPUF, two active entities in the neighborhood and the city. The park gates will open at 1 PM for those who want to start with a picnic. At 3 PM, the official ribbon-cutting ceremony with the authorities is scheduled, followed by informal basketball tournaments, ping-pong challenges, and space for music with jam sessions and DJ sets. ### Implications for cross-border workers For cross-border workers residing in Italy and working in Ticino, this new park represents an additional opportunity to spend moments of relaxation and socializing during their free time. Dettagli operativi

Practical Analysis The inauguration of the new Biumo park, scheduled for Saturday, May 9th, represents an important addition to the range of recreational activities available to cross-border workers in Ticino. Located just a few minutes from the Italian-Swiss border, the park covers an area of approximately 10,000 square meters and offers equipped spaces for sports such as soccer, basketball, and tennis, as well as areas for picnics and relaxation. According to data from the Municipality of Varese, around 12,000 cross-border workers reside in the province of Varese and work in Ticino, and the Biumo park could accommodate up to 500 people per day during weekends. The proximity to the border and the presence of equipped spaces make this park an ideal place to spend leisure time. Furthermore, the initiative promoted by local associations, such as the Associazione Giovani di Biumo, underscores the importance of collaboration between public entities and youth organizations to create shared and inclusive spaces. The total cost of the project was approximately 500,000 Swiss francs, partly financed by the Municipality of Varese and partly by local sponsors. ### Comparison with the Previous Situation Before the realization of this park, cross-border workers residing in Italy and working in Ticino had few options to spend their leisure time actively and socially. The lack of equipped spaces and local initiatives limited opportunities for aggregation and well-being. For example, a survey conducted in 2022 by the Associazione Frontalieri del Ticino revealed that 65% of respondents desired more recreational opportunities in the border area. Frequently Asked Questions
What activities are available in the new Biumo park?
The new Biumo park offers a basketball court, ping pong and teqball tables, benches, and spaces for socializing. Additionally, informal basketball tournaments, ping pong challenges, and music space with jam sessions and DJ sets are planned.
