Mobile and internet costs rise from 2026

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 30 April 2026

Sunrise increases mobile and internet rates starting August 2026. Here's what changes for cross-border workers in Ticino.

Contesto

In brief - Sunrise raises tariffs from August 2026 - Increases up to 2 francs per contract - Yallo and Lebara also affected ## Key facts - What: Sunrise, Yallo, Lebara tariff increases - When: From August 2026 - Where: Switzerland, Canton Ticino - Who: Sunrise, Yallo, Lebara - Amount: +1.50 CHF for main contract, +0.75 CHF for multiple subscriptions, +0.50-2 CHF for Yallo and Lebara Sunrise has announced that starting from August 2026, it will increase the prices of all mobile telephony and Internet subscriptions. The planned increases are 1.50 francs for each main contract of private and business customers and 75 cents for multiple or combined subscriptions. For Yallo and Lebara brand subscriptions, the increases will range from 50 cents to 2 francs per month, depending on the type of contract. The price adjustments will apply to both existing subscriptions and those of new customers. The increases do not affect specific offers for commercial customers and custom B2B solutions, as well as offers from secondary brands Swype and CHmobile, Sunrise Prepaid and Home Security, additional services (options), and installment payments for devices (payment plans). ### Communication to users Private and business customers affected will be informed in writing, via the bill, starting from June 1, 2026. Sunrise is not the only one taking this path: Swisscom raised prices on April 1, 2026, while Salt will do the same with the prices of some mobile telephony subscriptions starting from June 1, 2026. ### Impact on cross-border workers For cross-border workers residing in Ticino and working in Italy, these increases could affect the monthly budget allocated to communications.
