A new agreement between Clinica Varini and Clinica Hildebrand leads to the shutdown of the physiotherapy team in Orselina. Eight people will lose their jobs.

TL;DR - Clinica Varini Orselina and Clinica Hildebrand collaborate, ending physiotherapy team's activities. - Eight employees will lose jobs starting August 1, 2026. - Management assures compliance with legal and contractual provisions. ## Key facts - Collaboration Start: July 1, 2026 - Service End: August 1, 2026 - Employees Affected: Eight physiotherapy team members - Clinics Involved: Clinica Fondazione Varini in Orselina and Clinica Hildebrand in Brissago - Sector Impacted: Healthcare sector in Ticino, especially cross-border workers - Legal Compliance: Management assures compliance with legal and contractual provisions News that is shaking the healthcare sector in the Locarno region and alarming workers, including cross-border ones. The Clinica Fondazione Varini in Orselina and the Clinica Hildebrand in Brissago have announced a new collaboration agreement. The most immediate and painful consequence of this synergy is the cessation, starting from August 1, 2026, of the entire physiotherapy team's activities at the Orselina institute. This involves eight people who, according to reports from La Regione, will lose their jobs. The management of the hillside clinic, when contacted for clarification, tried to downplay the situation, specifying that it is not about giving up the department, but rather a reorganization. The director's words were precise: > The collaboration "will entail an organizational change in the management of therapeutic services, which will be entrusted to Clinica Hildebrand from next July 1, 2026." This strategic move thus involves a transfer of management, not a suppression of the service itself. However, for the eight employees involved, the substance changes little. Implications for the Healthcare Job Market The healthcare sector in Ticino is one of the main employment pools for cross-border workers, particularly for specialized roles like nurses, healthcare assistants, and, indeed, physiotherapists. This incident, though small in number, is a warning bell that reflects a broader trend: the search for efficiency and the concentration of services in the private healthcare sector. Clinics, under pressure from costs and competition, are seeking synergies to optimize resources, and this can translate into restructuring and staff cuts. What does the guarantee of compliance with "legal and contractual provisions" actually mean? In Switzerland, this implies respecting the notice periods stipulated in the individual contract or the relevant Collective Labour Agreement (CLA), if applicable. For mass dismissals (which apply to 10 or more layoffs, so not in this case), more stringent consultation procedures are required. For these eight workers, the outcome will depend on the notice periods and any support offered in finding new employment. Professional unions, such as OCST and Unia, always monitor these situations closely to ensure the protection of workers' rights, including those of cross-border workers. ⚠️ Watch the trends: Cost rationalization is a phenomenon that is set to continue. For those working in the sector, it is crucial to understand these dynamics and not take job stability for granted, even in a field as seemingly crisis-proof as healthcare.

How does the guarantee of compliance with the 'legal and contractual provisions' work in the event of dismissal?
In Switzerland, this means complying with the notice periods set out in the relevant individual contract or collective bargaining agreement (CEA), if applicable. Trade unions, such as OCST and Unia, monitor these situations to ensure the protection of workers' rights, including cross-border workers.
What are the implications for Italian cross-border commuters in the Ticino healthcare sector as a result of corporate reorganisations?
Italian cross-border workers in the Ticino health sector could face job chases and increased competition. It is advisable to invest in continuous training, maintain active networking and monitor the market to anticipate changes.
What are the minimum notice periods for a cross-border physiotherapist dismissed in Ticino?
The notice period for a cross-border physiotherapist in Ticino is established by the Collective Labour Agreement or by the individual contract. It is typically one month for each year of seniority, with a minimum of one month and a maximum of three months. If the contract provides for more favorable conditions, these prevail.
How can I access Swiss unemployment benefits if I work as a cross-border commuter and lose my job?
To qualify for unemployment benefits in Switzerland, the cross-border commuter must have paid contributions for at least 12 months in the last two years and submit the application within seven days of losing their job. The benefit corresponds to 70% of the insured salary, up to CHF 148,200 per year, and is managed by the AHV/IV.
What outplacement opportunities do Ticino clinics offer to dismissed physiotherapists?
Ticino clinics that close a ward often offer outplacement services: refresher courses funded up to CHF 5,000, assistance in writing CVs and contacts with employment agencies. In addition, the OCST and Unia unions provide support in finding new positions in hospitals or private facilities in the region.
