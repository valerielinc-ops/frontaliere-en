Frontaliers Sabotage arrives in Varese: the film that conquered Ticino (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 27 February 2026

The film 'Frontaliers Sabotage', a phenomenon in Ticino, lands in Varese on March 3 with two special screenings and the presence of the cast.

Contesto

TL;DR - 'Frontaliers Sabotage' arrives in Varese after Swiss success. - Screenings on March 3 at MIV cinema with main cast. - Film explores cross-border worker dynamics humorously. ## Key facts - Film Title: 'Frontaliers Sabotage' - Release Date: January 1, 2026 in Ticino - Screening Date: March 3, 2026 in Varese - Screening Times: 6 PM and 9 PM - Main Cast: Roberto Bussenghi, Loris J. Bernasconi, Amelie - Director: Alberto Meroni - Cinema Location: MIV cinema in Varese - Film Genre: Comedy with cross-border themes The film 'Frontaliers Sabotage', already a sensation in Ticino cinemas with over 15,000 viewers, is finally coming to Varese. This cinematic work, which even held its own against the blockbuster 'Avatar' during the Christmas holidays in Switzerland, will be screened for the first time in Italy on Tuesday, March 3, at the MIV cinema in Varese. Two screenings are scheduled: one at 6 PM and another at 9 PM. The evening will be made even more special by the presence of the main cast, including Roberto Bussenghi, who plays the Italian cross-border worker, and Loris J. Bernasconi, the famous Swiss customs officer. Also attending will be Amelie, who plays Bernasconi's wife, and the director Alberto Meroni, ready to meet the audience and share behind-the-scenes stories about the film. Launched in Ticino on January 1, 2026, the feature film has marked a milestone in the local cinematic landscape, proving that stories deeply rooted in cross-border realities can compete with international blockbusters. This second chapter of the 'Frontaliers' saga, which began in 2007 as a radio series broadcast by RSI, humorously explores the daily dynamics between Italian and Swiss cross-border workers, focusing on cultural and linguistic misunderstandings. Dettagli operativi

The arrival of 'Frontaliers Sabotage' on the Italian side of the border is no coincidence. Varese, a city symbolic of cross-border commuting, represents a natural meeting point for those who live the complex reality of working and living between two countries. The film itself includes scenes shot right in the border area, a choice that underlines the centrality of this region in the dynamics portrayed in the movie. The 'Frontaliers' saga is an ironic yet profound representation of the challenges faced by those who work in Switzerland but live in Italy. The themes range from cultural prejudices to bureaucracy, and even linguistic aspects like the famous 'Itagliano', a curious fusion of Italian and Swiss-German. This story not only entertains but also invites reflection on the complexity of cross-border realities, in a context where regulations, taxes, and policies often create paradoxical situations. With the film wrapping up its run in Ticino cinemas to embark on a new journey along the border strip, the implications are clear: there is a growing interest in stories that, while localized, resonate universally.
