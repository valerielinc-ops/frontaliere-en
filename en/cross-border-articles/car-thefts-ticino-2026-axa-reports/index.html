AXA reports a rise in car thefts in Switzerland, particularly in border cantons like Ticino. Here's what you need to know and how to protect yourself.

Contesto

In brief - AXA reports increase in car thefts in Switzerland - Ticino above Swiss average - Land Rover, Alfa Romeo and Porsche most stolen ## Key facts - What: Increase in car thefts in Switzerland - When: 2025 - Where: Border cantons, incl. Ticino - Who: AXA - Amount: 5 million CHF paid out in 2025 Car thefts in Switzerland are on the rise, particularly in border cantons. AXA, the country's largest motor vehicle insurer, has reported a significant increase in claims related to car thefts. In 2025, the company paid out approximately 5 million francs in compensation, nearly double the pre-pandemic period, when the figure was between 2.5 and 3 million. Almost 400 cases are reported each year, with an upward trend for luxury cars. "Swiss roads are seeing more modern and better-equipped vehicles. Thieves seem to be targeting these models more often and, it appears, are increasingly successful in stealing high-value vehicles," observes Michael Villiger, head of motor vehicle claims at AXA. ### Most affected cantons Regionally, the most affected cantons are Geneva, Vaud and Basel City. However, Ticino also stands above the Swiss average. Thefts are rarer in the canton of Obwalden and in Glarus. "These differences could be linked, among other things, to the proximity to the border," says Villiger. "In these regions, organized gangs seem to operate more frequently, transporting vehicles abroad," explains the expert. ### Most stolen models The analysis finally highlights which models are particularly prone to theft. The probability that a Land Rover SUV will be stolen is more than four times higher than the average. But Alfa Romeos and Porsches are also particularly targeted. Frequently Asked Questions
Which are the most stolen car models in Ticino?
The most stolen car models in Ticino include Land Rover, Alfa Romeo, and Porsche. These are followed by Audi, Jeep, Fiat, BMW, and Mercedes-Benz. These vehicles are particularly targeted by organized gangs for resale.
What to do in case of car theft in Switzerland?
In case of car theft in Switzerland, it is important to immediately report the theft to the local police, contact your insurance company, and provide all details of the vehicle and the circumstances of the theft. Collaborating with the authorities can increase the chances of recovering the vehicle.
How to protect your vehicle from theft in Ticino?
To protect your vehicle from theft in Ticino, it is advisable to park in safe places, install anti-theft devices, not leave valuable items in sight, and use tracking systems. Check your insurance policy to ensure you are covered in case of theft.
