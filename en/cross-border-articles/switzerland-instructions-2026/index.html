From immigration to housing, here are the hot topics that are changing the country

In brief - On June 14, vote on the initiative 'No to a Switzerland of 10 million!' - Ticino disadvantaged in financial equalization - Increase in luxury car thefts ## Key facts - What: Popular initiative 'No to a Switzerland of 10 million!' - When: Vote on June 14, 2026 - Where: Switzerland - Who: Swiss population - Amount: Not yet specified Switzerland continues to be a crossroads of challenges and opportunities. From the debate on immigration to housing difficulties, the country faces complex issues that directly involve cross-border workers and the local population. One of the hottest topics at the moment is the popular initiative 'No to a Switzerland of 10 million!', which will be put to the vote on June 14. This initiative calls for limiting immigration from abroad, a topic that has divided Swiss public opinion for decades. Another critical aspect concerns financial equalization, which sees the Canton of Ticino at a disadvantage. Bern considers Ticino richer than it actually is because, in calculating equalization, it counts the incomes of cross-border workers but does not count them as residents. This results in a Canton that is 'rich on paper' and receives less federal funds. ### Housing challenges Finding a home in Switzerland is becoming increasingly difficult. In recent years, those who have looked for accommodation know this well: dozens of applications, queues for viewings, rising prices. The situation is particularly critical in large cities, where demand far exceeds supply. ### Security A worrying phenomenon is the increase in luxury car thefts. Some of these heists are organized by criminal networks and carried out by young people recruited on social media. Dettagli operativi

The challenges that Switzerland is facing have a direct impact on the daily lives of cross-border workers and residents. Housing difficulties, for example, affect not only the quality of life but also the ability to attract and retain qualified workers. The situation is particularly critical for cross-border workers, who often have to travel long distances to reach their workplace. ### Implications for cross-border workers Cross-border workers represent a significant portion of the active population in Switzerland, especially in the Canton of Ticino. The financially disadvantageous equalization for Ticino has a direct impact on services and infrastructure, which could benefit from greater federal funds. This could improve the quality of life and working conditions for cross-border workers. ### Opportunities Despite the challenges, Switzerland also offers numerous opportunities. Civil service, for example, is a valid alternative to military service and offers young people the opportunity to engage in socially useful activities. This can be an opportunity for young cross-border workers to better integrate into Swiss society. ### Social policies Social policies must adapt to new demographic challenges. The aging population requires strengthening of healthcare structures and social services. This can create new job opportunities for cross-border workers, who can help fill gaps in these sectors. ### Security The increase in luxury car thefts poses a challenge for law enforcement. Authorities must adopt more effective measures to combat this phenomenon, which can have a negative impact on security and quality of life. To tackle these challenges, it is essential to be informed and prepared. Here are some concrete actions that cross-border workers and residents can take: ### Inform yourselves about popular initiatives The vote on June 14th on the initiative 'No to a Switzerland of 10 million!' is a crucial moment for the country's future. It is important to inform yourselves about the implications of this initiative and to participate in the vote to express your opinion. ### Look for a home in advance Finding a home in Switzerland can be difficult, but with adequate planning it is possible. It is advisable to start the search early, explore different options, and be ready to present the necessary documentation to speed up the process. ### Protect your belongings To counter the increase in luxury car thefts, it is important to take preventive measures. Installing alarm systems, parking in safe places, and using locking devices can reduce the risk of theft. ### Participate in civil service Civil service represents an opportunity for young people to contribute to Swiss society. It is important to inform yourselves about the access methods and the activities planned, to make a conscious choice. ### Use the tools on the site To tackle these challenges as best as possible, it is useful to use the tools available on the site.
