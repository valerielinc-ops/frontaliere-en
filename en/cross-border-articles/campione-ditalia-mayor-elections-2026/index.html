Campione d'Italia: three-way race for mayor

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 27 April 2026

Three candidates are running for the position of mayor in Campione d'Italia. Find out who they are and what they propose.

Contesto

In brief - Three candidates for the mayor's seat in Campione d'Italia - Elections on 24 and 25 May 2026 - 28 candidates for the Municipal Council ## Key facts - What: Elections for the mayor of Campione d'Italia - When: 24 and 25 May 2026 - Where: Campione d'Italia - Who: Sergio Aureli, Luca Frigerio, Barbara Marchesini - Number: 28 candidates for the Municipal Council Campione d'Italia is ready for a three-way race for the mayor's seat. The elections will take place on 24 and 25 May 2026, and the candidacies have been officially announced with the deposit at the Town Hall of three civic lists. The candidates are Sergio Aureli, Luca Frigerio, and Barbara Marchesini. In total, there are 28 candidates for the Municipal Council. There are no shortages of surprises, with the absence of the outgoing mayor Roberto Canesi, who has decided not to run again after five complex years marked by the crisis of the enclave. Sergio Aureli, 51, is an outgoing minority municipal councilor, a safety and civil protection engineer, and national president of the volunteer firefighters. His list, 'Development and solidarity for Campione,' also includes Thomas Boni, an engineer at the Ticino Electricity Company, and Giacomo Lombardi, a delegate of Doctors Without Borders for Italian Switzerland. Luca Frigerio, 56, former manager of the Casino and the gaming house of Saint-Vincent, leads the list 'Campione rilancia.' Among the candidates is also the outgoing deputy mayor Tanina Padula. Frigerio takes over from the outgoing mayor Canesi. Barbara Marchesini, 50, a lawyer, is at the helm of the list 'Marchesini for Campione d'Italia.'
