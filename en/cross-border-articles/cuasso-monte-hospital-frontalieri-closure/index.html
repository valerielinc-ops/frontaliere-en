Cuasso al Monte: definitive closure of the cross-border workers' hospital

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 21 April 2026

After twenty years of debate, the cross-border workers' hospital in Cuasso al Monte is closing permanently. Services have been moved to the municipal building.

Contesto

TL;DR - Lombardy Region closes cross-border workers' hospital in Cuasso al Monte after 20 years of debates. - Services transferred to municipal premises, affecting 1,200 cross-border workers. - Security issues remain unresolved; economic impact estimated at 500,000 euros annually. ## Key facts - Decision: Definitive closure of the cross-border workers' hospital in Cuasso al Monte. - Date: Decision formalized last week in an institutional meeting. - Location: Cuasso al Monte, Lombardy Region, Italy. - Participants: Guido Bertolaso, Mauro Moreno, Loredana Bonora. - Cost: Annual cost of maintaining the facility amounted to approximately 500,000 euros. - Services: Blood draws and major specialist visits proposed on a weekly basis in municipal premises. - Impact: 40% of cross-border workers will need to move to healthcare facilities in Switzerland. - Security: Request for a meeting with the Prefect and the Varese Police to evaluate solutions. ## Final Decision After 20 years of debates and unfulfilled projects, the Lombardy Region has decided to definitively close the cross-border workers' hospital in Cuasso al Monte. The decision was formalized last week in an institutional meeting attended by Guido Bertolaso, the regional Welfare assessor, Mauro Moreno, the general director of ASST Sette Laghi, and Loredana Bonora, the mayor of Cuasso al Monte. According to the joint statement by Asst Sette Laghi and the Municipality, “there are no conditions for reactivating the facility with socio-health functions within the site of Cuasso al Monte.” The definitive closure of the cross-border workers' hospital marks the end of a long period of uncertainty and projects that never came to fruition. ### Service Transfer To ensure a basic healthcare presence in the area, the activation...

Frequently Asked Questions
Where will the basic healthcare services be relocated?
Basic healthcare services will be moved to the town hall of Cuasso al Monte. This includes blood draws and main specialist visits that will be conducted in municipal-owned premises.
What does the “Bus & Bici” project involve?
The “Bus & Bici” project involves connections between Cuasso al Monte, Porto Ceresio station, and the medical center of Besano. The goal is to improve accessibility to healthcare services and other essential services for cross-border workers.
What is the Lombardy Region doing to improve security?
Regional Welfare Councillor Guido Bertolaso announced a request for a meeting with the Prefecture and Police Headquarters of Varese to evaluate the most adequate solutions to improve security in the area.
