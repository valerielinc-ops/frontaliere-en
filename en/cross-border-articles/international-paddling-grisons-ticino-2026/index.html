International canoe races between Grisons and Ticino: what you need to know (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 8 May 2026

On Saturday 9 and Sunday 10 May 2026, canoe races on the Moesa and Ticino rivers. Over 110 athletes from 7 nations, including Australia.

Contesto

In brief - International canoe races between Grisons and Ticino on 9 and 10 May 2026 - Over 110 athletes from 7 nations, including Australia - Races valid as European Cup trials and Swiss absolute championship 2026 ## Key facts - What: International canoe races - When: Saturday 9 and Sunday 10 May 2026 - Where: Moesa and Ticino rivers - Who: Ticino Canoe Group - Number: Over 110 athletes from 7 nations Canoeing will take center stage between Grisons and Ticino for a weekend of international races on the Moesa and Ticino rivers. On Saturday 9 and Sunday 10 May 2026, the seventh edition of TicinoMoesa will be held, organized by the Ticino Canoe Group. This year, the races will also count as trials for the European Cup and as the Swiss absolute championship 2026. ### Races and participants The races will see the participation of more than 110 athletes representing seven nations, including distant Australia. However, the conditions of the Moesa and Ticino rivers could influence the race routes. After the 2024 edition, where the river conditions were optimal, this year the low-snow winter and the dry month of April have resulted in very low flows for early spring. Despite the recent rains, it is not yet certain that the Moesa will be navigable. ### Race schedule On Saturday at 11 a.m., the classic race will take place. If the flow of the Moesa is sufficient, the route will wind between Roveredo and Lumino. If it is necessary to move to the Ticino, paddling will take place between Castione and Bellinzona with arrival in the Torretta area. The race will also count as the Swiss absolute championship 2026. Implications for cross-border workers The international canoe races between Grisons and Ticino represent a unique opportunity for cross-border workers who live and work in the region. Not only as spectators, but also as potential participants or volunteers. The event, in fact, requires the collaboration of many people to ensure the success of the races. ### How to participate If you are a canoe enthusiast and want to participate in the races, you need to register through the official website www.ticinomoesa.ch. Registrations are open to everyone, but it is important to respect the deadlines and specific requirements for each race category. ### Volunteering If you want to contribute as a volunteer, you can contact the Ticino Canoeists Group. Volunteering is an excellent way to integrate into the local community and experience the excitement of the races up close. Tasks can range from logistics management to safety, to supporting participants. ### Where to stay For cross-border workers who wish to participate in or attend the races, it is important to plan accommodation in advance. Bellinzona and the surrounding areas offer numerous options, from luxury hotels to budget apartments. It is advisable to book in advance, especially considering the influx of athletes and spectators from all over the world. ### Transportation The region is well connected thanks to the Swiss railway and road network. If you arrive by train, Bellinzona station is well connected to the main urban centers of Ticino and Switzerland. If you prefer to travel by car, there are numerous parking spaces available near the race areas. ### What to bring If you decide to attend as a spectator, it is important to bring comfortable clothing suitable for the weather conditions.
