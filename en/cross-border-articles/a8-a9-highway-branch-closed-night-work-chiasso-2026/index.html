A8-A9 closed at night towards Chiasso: alternative routes for cross-border workers

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 19 April 2026

From Wednesday 22 to Thursday 23 April 2026, the A8-A9 branch towards Chiasso will be closed at night for maintenance work. Cross-border commuters heading to Ticino should use alternative routes via Lainate Arese, Origgio and Saronno.

Frequently Asked Questions
Can I still use the A8-A9 branch towards Chiasso after 11:00 PM on April 22?
No. The restriction is absolute: from 11:00 PM to 5:00 AM the following day, the A8-A9 branch towards Chiasso will be closed for maintenance work. Anyone on this stretch will be required to follow alternative routes or stop until the reopening.
What are the alternatives if I pass the Lainate Arese exit after 11:00 PM?
If you pass the Lainate Arese exit after 11:00 PM, you must continue on the A8 towards Varese. From there, you can exit at Legnano and follow alternative route 2: viale Marco Polo Ovest, SP527 Bustese towards Uboldo/Saronno, entering the A9 at the Saronno exit.
How much extra time will the alternative routes take?
The estimated additional time is 15-20 minutes for route 1 (via Origgio) and 20-25 minutes for route 2 (via Saronno), compared to the direct A8-A9 route. These times may increase in case of congestion on provincial roads.
Are there alternative public transport services during the closure?
During the overnight closure, public transport services may be subject to changes. It is advisable to check the updated schedules for regional trains (TILO) or line services from Milan to Chiasso/Mendrisio. Information is available on the SBB/CFF or local operator websites.
Who should I contact for real-time traffic updates?
For real-time updates, you can call the traffic hotline at 803.111 (available 24/7). Alternatively, check the Autostrade per l'Italia app, the autostrade.it website, or the variable message signs along the highways.
