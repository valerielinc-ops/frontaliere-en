Busto Arsizio prison: Cecilia Strada's EU Parliament complaint

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 2 May 2026

Overcrowding exceeding 200%, inhuman conditions, and suicides: the critical situation of Busto Arsizio prison reported in Europe.

Contesto

In brief - Overcrowding over 200% in Busto Arsizio prison - 437 inmates for 240 available places - 15 suicides in Italian prisons since the beginning of 2025 ## Key facts - What: Cecilia Strada's complaint to the European Parliament - When: April 26, 2026 - Where: European Parliament, Strasbourg - Who: Cecilia Strada, PD MEP - Amount: 437 inmates for 240 available places The prison of Busto Arsizio is at the center of a serious complaint presented to the European Parliament by MEP Cecilia Strada. During a debate on the rule of law, Strada described a dramatic situation within the prison facility, with an overcrowding rate of over 200%. According to the updated official data on the prison's website, there are 437 people detained, while the available places are only 240. The detention rooms are 177, but 18 are not available. The prison police should have 190 officers, but 21 are missing. ### Inhumane conditions Strada described a reality marked by serious critical issues: 'I was in the Italian prison of Busto Arsizio, where the overcrowding rate is over 200%: it's a hell.' She spoke of three-tier bunk beds, with inmates sleeping a hand's breadth from the ceiling, and three square meters of space per inmate that are a mirage. 'Exposed electrical cables everywhere,' she added, emphasizing how 'in cell, dignity dies and prisoners die.' ### Suicides in prison The reference is also to the most recent events. 'A few days after our visit to Busto Arsizio, an inmate hanged himself,' Strada recalled, speaking of 15 suicides since the beginning of the year in Italian prisons. A figure that, according to the MEP, highlights an increasingly critical picture. Dettagli operativi

Implications for the Penitentiary System The report by Cecilia Strada raises important questions about the Italian penitentiary system and its critical issues. Overcrowding in prisons not only violates the fundamental rights of inmates but also prevents any path of social reintegration. This problem has direct consequences for the entire community, as inmates, once released, may have greater difficulties in reintegrating into society. ### Comparison with Other Realities The situation in the prison of Busto Arsizio is not an isolated case. According to the dossier 'Dying in Prison' by Ristretti Orizzonti, in 2025 there would be 238 people who died within Penitentiary Institutions in Italy, of which 79 would have committed suicide. These numbers highlight a system that, instead of improving, becomes more problematic year by year. The lack of staff, insufficient health and psychological assistance, and degrading living conditions contribute to an increasingly critical situation. ### Future Scenarios If urgent action is not taken, the situation could worsen further. Overcrowding and inhuman conditions in Italian prisons could lead to an increase in suicides and a deterioration in the living conditions of inmates. This would have a negative impact not only on the inmates but also on the entire society, as an ineffective and inhuman penitentiary system does not contribute to the social reintegration of inmates. ### Comparison with Canton Ticino While the Italian penitentiary system faces serious critical issues, Canton Ticino has adopted measures to improve the conditions of its prison facilities. What to do Faced with this critical situation, it is essential that Italian authorities take urgent measures to improve prison conditions and guarantee the fundamental rights of inmates. Here are some concrete steps that can be taken: 1. Increase the number of available places: It is necessary to build new prison structures or expand existing ones to reduce overcrowding. 2. Hire more staff: The shortage of prison staff must be addressed by hiring new agents and providing adequate training. 3. Improve living conditions: It is essential to ensure dignified living conditions for inmates, with adequate spaces and health and psychological services. 4. Promote social reintegration: Social reintegration programs must be implemented to help inmates reintegrate into society once released. ### Useful tools For those who wish to delve deeper into the situation of Italian prisons and possible solutions, the following tools may be useful: - Salary calculator: to understand the economic implications of prison work. - Guide to social reintegration: for information on how to access social reintegration programs. - Legal assistance: for those in need of legal support in penitentiary matters. ### Conclusion Cecilia Strada's denunciation to the European Parliament has highlighted a critical situation in Italian prisons, particularly in the prison of Busto Arsizio. It is fundamental that Italian authorities take urgent measures to improve prison conditions and guarantee the fundamental rights of inmates.
