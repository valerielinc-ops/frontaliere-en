Managers at Insubria: Rasizza and Battioni on May 4th

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 30 April 2026

Rosario Rasizza and Stefano Battioni meet with Insubria University students to discuss business management and technological changes.

Contesto

In brief - Rosario Rasizza and Stefano Battioni in the spotlight on May 4th - Dialogues with Professor Alberto Onetti - Shared entrepreneurial and managerial experiences ## Key facts - What: Meeting with managers and students - When: May 4, 2026 - Where: Università degli Studi dell’Insubria, Varese - Who: Rosario Rasizza, Stefano Battioni, Professor Alberto Onetti - Fee: Not yet specified Students at Università degli Studi dell’Insubria will have the opportunity to meet two prominent managers, Rosario Rasizza and Stefano Battioni, on May 4th. The event, organized by the Department of Economics, is part of a series of meetings in the Management course that brings business leaders into the classroom for a direct exchange with students on business management topics. Following journalist Riccardo Luna and numerous professionals, with the presentation of corporate cases including Amadori and Maserati, it is now the turn of Rosario Rasizza, co-founder and CEO of Openjobmetis SpA, and Stefano Battioni, former CEO of Illva Saronno, with previous experience at Barilla and Colgate-Palmolive. The event will take place in Room 8 at via Monte Generoso 71 in Varese. At 14:15, there will be a dialogue with Stefano Battioni, while at 16:30, Rosario Rasizza will take the stage. Both dialogues will be moderated by Professor Alberto Onetti. The afternoon is open to the interested public. ### The importance of blending theory and practice Professor Alberto Onetti emphasizes the importance of bringing real cases into the classroom: 'Theory alone is not enough. The business world cannot be studied only from books. Dettagli operativi

Implications for Students and the Territory The meeting on May 4th represents a unique opportunity for students to engage with concrete experiences from the business world. The chance to interact directly with high-level managers allows students to better understand corporate dynamics and acquire practical skills that will be fundamental for their professional future. Professor Alberto Onetti emphasizes how openness to the local community is a key element of this series of meetings: 'We decided to open this meeting to the territory, in a logic of third mission. This means that not only students, but also local professionals and entrepreneurs can benefit from these experiences and knowledge exchanges.' ### The Role of International Experiences Stefano Battioni, with his extensive international experience, brings a unique perspective to the classroom. His experience in the United States during the launch of Barilla pasta and his role as CEO of Illva Saronno offer students a global perspective on the challenges and opportunities of the international market. Rosario Rasizza, with his experience in the labor and employment sector, provides a valuable contribution on topics such as human resource management and the social role of the company. His presence as President of Assosomm and his inclusion in the 'Staffing 100 Europe' list testify to his commitment and competence in these sectors. ### The Importance of Corporate Sustainability Another relevant aspect of the meeting is the discussion on corporate sustainability. Stefano Battioni, during his tenure as CEO of Illva Saronno, led the group's international development and corporate sustainability plan.
