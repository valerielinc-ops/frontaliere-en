May Day united in Marghera

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 1 May 2026

Cgil, Cisl and Uil together to celebrate Labour Day with a focus on dignified work.

Contesto

In brief - Cgil, Cisl and Uil celebrate May Day together in Marghera - Focus on dignified work and divergences on the Labour Decree - Landini criticizes the decree, Bombardieri and Fumarola appreciate it ## Key facts - What: Unitary celebration of May Day - When: 1st May 2026 - Where: Marghera - Who: Cgil, Cisl, Uil - Amount: Not specified The May Day 2026 was celebrated under the sign of the rediscovered union unity for Cgil, Cisl and Uil. Maurizio Landini, Pierpaolo Bombardieri and Daniela Fumarola were together on the stage in Marghera, after the 'tear' of last year, when the three leaders had chosen three different cities to celebrate, separately, the Workers' Day. This year, however, they are back together, putting at the center of the demonstration the theme of dignified work. Meanwhile, after the launch of the Labour Decree in the Cdm, the three leaders have different ideas: praised by Bombardieri and Fumarola, rejected by Landini. 'Being in the square in a unitary manner does not mean thinking in the same way,' the words of the Cgil leader during the press conference presenting the Rome Concertone. Landini said about the decree that he found 'singular that for the day of the workers' festival, all the money inside the decree is for companies. For workers there is not a single euro. Experience should teach that continuing to give incentives to companies for hiring, as is known, does not serve to increase them.' For Landini 'the underlying issue is called 'salary' and therefore tax.' Bombardieri, on the other hand, said he was 'very satisfied' highlighting that 'for the first time there is a legislative intervention that identifies the right salary and the dignified salary with the contracts of Cgil, Cisl and Uil.' Frequently Asked Questions
What are the main differences between the Dl Lavoro and previous regulations?
The Dl Lavoro introduces the identification of a dignified salary through union contracts, conditioning tax relief on the use of these contracts. This is an important step that could have a significant impact on the working conditions of cross-border workers.
How can I participate in union assemblies if I work in Italy and live in Ticino?
You can participate in union assemblies by contacting your local or national union section. Many union organizations also offer the possibility to participate in online events, making it easier for cross-border workers to get involved.
What are the next steps for cross-border workers in terms of union negotiations?
The next steps include continuing to monitor union negotiations, participating in assemblies, and consulting experts to better understand the implications of the new regulations. It is important to make your voice heard and contribute to the decisions that affect working conditions.
