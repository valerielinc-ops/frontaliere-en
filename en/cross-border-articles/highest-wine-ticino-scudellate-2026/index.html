Ticino's highest wine produced in Scudellate

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 2 May 2026

Discover the '909' wine, produced at 909 meters above sea level in Muggio Valley, and its unique characteristics

Contesto

In brief - Wine '909' produced at 909 meters in Valle di Muggio - Official presentation on May 3, 2026 - Limited production of around 1,200 bottles per year ## Key facts - What: Organic wine '909' - When: Presentation on May 3, 2026 - Where: Scudellate, Valle di Muggio, Ticino - Who: Monte Generoso diffused hotel foundation - Amount: Around 1,200 bottles per year The highest wine in Ticino is produced in Scudellate, in Valle di Muggio. Here, the Monte Generoso diffused hotel foundation has revived an ancient tradition, bringing back to life the vine rows at an altitude of 909 meters. The result is wine '909', an organic product that promises to attract the attention of tourists and oenology enthusiasts. Claudio Zanini, president of the foundation, explained that the name '909' comes from the altitude at which the vineyards are located. 'We couldn't choose a different name', said Zanini, emphasizing the importance of maintaining a strong link with the territory. The wine will be officially presented on May 3, 2026 with an invitation-only celebratory event. The exceptional godmother, whose name has not yet been revealed, will add a touch of glamour to the event. Zanini recalled that the area was once characterized by dry stone walls and agricultural activities, and that the collaboration with Agroscope has allowed the restoration of this tradition. ### Production and characteristics The wine '909' is produced from white grapes, a cross between Bronner and Gamaret. The rows are limited and the production is equally limited, with around 1,200 bottles per year. The vineyards are located under the Casa dei Gelsi and the Osteria Manciana, both structures of the diffused hotel, and count around 850 vine feet.
