Free Museum Entry in Swimwear at Beyeler Foundation

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 2 May 2026

The Beyeler Foundation in Riehen offers free entry in swimwear for a day dedicated to Paul Cézanne and his paintings of bathers.

Contesto

In brief - Free museum entry in swimwear - Initiative inspired by the Paul Cézanne exhibition - High attendance expected in the coming weekends ## Key facts - What: Swimmers' Day with free entry - When: May 1, 2026 - Where: Fondation Beyeler, Riehen (BS) - Who: Fondation Beyeler - Cost: Free entry The Fondation Beyeler in Riehen, located in the canton of Basel-Stadt, has launched an initiative as unusual as it is fascinating. On May 1, 2026, the museum will open its doors for free to anyone arriving in swimwear. Named "Swimmers' Day," this unique proposal draws inspiration from the exhibition dedicated to the renowned French artist Paul Cézanne (1839–1906), celebrated for his works depicting swimmers. ### A cultural experience in swimwear The aim of the event is clear: to bring the public closer to art in a new way by breaking down traditional barriers between visitors and artworks. "We want people to feel like they are part of the art, to experience the museum in a more relaxed and accessible context," stated a spokesperson for the Fondation Beyeler. The Cézanne exhibition, which explores the theme of swimmers as a central motif, offers a unique opportunity to dive—both literally and figuratively—into the painter's universe. Cézanne's masterpieces, such as "Les Grandes Baigneuses" (1906), are iconic works that have influenced generations of artists and embody the fusion of nature, the human body, and introspection. ### Expectations and organization The Fondation anticipates a large turnout of visitors, particularly over the weekends. The event is strategically timed to coincide with the reopening of swimming pools and bathing areas along the Rhine, which attract thousands of people each year. Practical Analysis The initiative by the Beyeler Foundation represents an extraordinary opportunity for cross-border workers and residents of the Canton of Ticino to approach art in a decidedly unusual context: free entry to the museum for those who show up in swimwear. This unique concept not only aims to make art more accessible but also to break down traditional barriers that often make museums seem elitist or overly formal places. ### Implications for Cross-Border Workers For the approximately 70,000 cross-border workers who commute to Switzerland daily for work (according to data updated in 2022), the initiative represents an opportunity for leisure and cultural enrichment without straining the family budget. Considering that the average cost of entry to museums in Switzerland ranges between 15 and 25 CHF, the possibility of free access to the Beyeler Foundation allows for savings while living a unique experience. For cross-border workers residing, for example, in Como or Varese, reaching the museum's location in Riehen, near Basel, could be an excellent opportunity for a day trip. From Lugano, one of the main centers of Ticino, the train journey takes approximately 3 hours and 30 minutes, with a cost slightly over 100 CHF for a round-trip second-class ticket. The event could therefore attract not only local residents but also those living in neighboring areas. ### Comparison with Other Cultural Initiatives The idea of offering free or discounted entries to promote museums is not new, but the method proposed by the Beyeler Foundation is particularly innovative. What is the goal of the 'Bathers' Day' at the Beyeler Foundation?
The goal of the 'Bathers' Day' is to reduce the distance between the public and the artworks, creating a more engaging and informal experience. The initiative is inspired by the exhibition dedicated to French painter Paul Cézanne and his famous paintings of bathers.
Who can participate in the 'Bathers' Day'?
