Como and Cantù join forces for Lake Como Creativity Week

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 2 May 2026

The municipalities of Como and Cantù are collaborating for the Lake Como Creativity Week, with a three-year agreement signed on May 7, 2026

Contesto

In brief - The municipalities of Como and Cantù collaborate for the Lake Como Creativity Week - Three-year agreement signed on May 7, 2026 - Autumn event in coordination with Cantù's Wood Festival - Participation of Ticino municipalities such as Lugano and Mendrisio - Estimated budget of €500,000 for the three-year period ## Key facts - What: Collaboration between the municipalities of Como and Cantù for the Lake Como Creativity Week - When: Agreement signed on May 7, 2026 - Where: Como-Lecco Chamber of Commerce - Who: Municipality of Como, City of Cantù, Como-Lecco Chamber of Commerce, Ticino municipalities - Amount: €500,000 for the 2026-2028 triennium The bond between the Municipality of Como and the City of Cantù strengthens in the name of creativity and know-how, following the virtuous experience of the previous year. The two administrations have defined the lines of a strategic collaboration for the 2026-2028 triennium, formalized in a council deliberation. The agreement will be officially signed on May 7 at the Como-Lecco Chamber of Commerce. The signing will take place during the Como Creative City UNESCO territorial plenary, the annual meeting that brings together institutions, bodies, and stakeholders of the Larian creative district. The first concrete result of this agreement will concern the autumn of 2026. The Lake Como Creativity Week will indeed take place in close coordination with the event 'Cantù Città del Mobile – Festival del Legno'. Dettagli operativi

The collaboration between Como and Cantù for the Lake Como Creativity Week represents a significant step for the Larian creative district. This three-year agreement, signed on May 7, 2026, not only strengthens the ties between the two cities but also promotes the culture of making and local craftsmanship, key elements for the international competitiveness of the territory. The event, which will be held annually, involves an initial investment of 500,000 euros, equally divided between the two municipalities, with the aim of attracting at least 10,000 visitors per year. ### Implications for the Territory The integration of craftsmanship and creativity, supported by the UNESCO designation of Como as a Creative City for Crafts and Folk Art, offers new opportunities for local artisans and creatives. The Lake Como Creativity Week, in coordination with the Cantù Wood Festival, will be a major event that will attract visitors and professionals in the sector, contributing to the enhancement of the cultural and craft heritage of the territory. Furthermore, the proximity to Ticino, particularly to municipalities such as Lugano and Mendrisio, could favor cross-border exchanges, as provided for in the 2024 bilateral agreement between Italy and Switzerland for the promotion of cultural tourism, which includes tax incentives for cross-border cultural events. ### Future Scenarios With the agreement signed on May 7, 2026, new prospects open up for the Larian creative district. The collaboration between Como and Cantù could extend to other sectors, further promoting the culture of making and innovation.
