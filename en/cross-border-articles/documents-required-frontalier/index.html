Action To start working in Switzerland as a cross-border worker in the Canton of Ticino, you need to follow the procedure for applying for the G permit. This permit allows residents of neighboring countries to work in Switzerland without having to transfer their residence. It is essential to have all the necessary documents and follow the procedure correctly to avoid delays or rejections. ### Required Documents The documents required for the G permit application include: - Valid passport or identity card - Work contract signed by a Swiss employer - Certificate of residence issued by the municipality of residence in Italy (e.g., Lugano, Mendrisio, Chiasso) - Criminal record certificate issued by Italian authorities - Completed and signed application form ### Procedure The procedure to obtain the G permit may vary slightly depending on the municipality of residence. For example, in Lugano, the process may take about 4-6 weeks, while in Chiasso it might be faster, with processing times of 2-4 weeks. It is important to verify the specific requirements of your municipality of residence. ### Operational Checklist Here is an operational checklist to facilitate the process: 1.
