RSI on display: the history of Ticino that every cross-border worker lives (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 17 February 2026

From February 16th to 22nd in Airolo, RSI exhibits archive photos from the 60s to the 2000s. A free opportunity to understand the Canton's economic and social evolution.

TL;DR - RSI opens archives to show Ticino's history - Exhibition 'Una Storia' showcases 40 years of transformations - Traveling exhibition stops in Leventina, Airolo - Free admission, open from Feb 16th to 22nd, 2026 ## Key facts - Evento: Una Storia - Italian-speaking Switzerland in RSI archive photographs - Luogo: Caseificio del Gottardo, Via Fontana 3, 6780, Airolo - Data inizio: Monday, February 16th, 2026 - Data fine: Sunday, February 22nd, 2026 - Orari: Every day from 09:00 to 20:00 - Costo: Completely free - Vernissage: Friday, February 19th at 18:30 - Periodo storico: 1960s to 2000 The Italian-speaking Swiss Radio and Television (RSI) is opening its archives to showcase the Canton's collective memory. Dettagli operativi

Looking at these photographs is not just an exercise in nostalgia. For a cross-border worker, it means observing the roots of Ticino's current economic fabric. The images from the '60s and '70s document the construction and industrial boom, a period when Italian labor was crucial for the Canton's growth. One can almost see the foundations of the companies and infrastructures that today employ tens of thousands of people from Lombardy and Piedmont. ## Beyond Photography: Economic Evolution The shots from the '80s and '90s tell a different story: the transition to the advanced service sector, the growth of Lugano's financial center, and the development of the service industry. This shift profoundly changed the skills required and the type of work available, leading to the emergence of new professions and opportunities. > The exhibition offers a historical perspective on the evolution of the Ticino labor market, an essential context for anyone working in this Canton today. Understanding the transition from a production-based economy to a service-based one helps interpret current dynamics and future challenges. 📊 Ticino in Transformation (1960-2000): - 1960s-70s: Strong industrial and infrastructural growth. Increased demand for skilled and unskilled labor. - 1980s: Opening of the Gotthard road tunnel (1980), consolidation of the banking and fiduciary sector. - 1990s-2000s: Service sector boom, development of technology hubs, and establishment of Ticino as a hub for fashion and pharmaceuticals.

Frequently Asked Questions
When will the exhibition 'A History - Italian Switzerland in the archive photos RSI' in Leventina?
The exhibition will be held from Monday 16 February to Sunday 22 February 2026.
What is the historical path of the Ticino economy as shown in the exhibition 'A History'?
The exhibition highlights the transition from the strong industrial growth of the 60s and 70s, to the opening of the Gotthard tunnel and consolidation of the banking sector in the 80s, up to the boom of the tertiary sector and development of technological poles in the 90s and 2000s.
How to get to the Caseificio del Gottardo di Airolo by public transport from Milan?
For those departing from Milan, the fastest route is the EuroCity Milan-Zürich train (about 3 h) with a change in Bellinzona for the RegioExpress to Airolo (15 min). Alternatively, the FlixBus Milan-Bellinzona bus (2 h 45 min) arrives at Bellinzona station, from where the S10 line leaves every 30 min for Airolo. The dairy is a 5-minute walk from the station.
Is it necessary to book for the vernissage on February 19 and how do I do it?
Reservations are recommended for the vernissage on February 19 at 6:30 pm. Simply log in to rsi.ch/eventi, enter your name, surname and cross-border badge number, then confirm by 15 February. Registration is free of charge and guarantees a seat in the room, where you can meet curators and other cross-border workers.
What are the networking opportunities for cross-border commuters during the exhibition in Leventina?
During the exhibition in Leventina, two networking moments are organized: the vernissage on 19 February, with 30-40 seats reserved for cross-border workers, and the afternoon of 21 February, when the dairy hosts a discussion table on job opportunities in the tertiary sector. By participating, you can exchange contacts with local companies and discover specific job offers for the Italian workforce.
