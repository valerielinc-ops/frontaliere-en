Social media and minors: bans aren't enough

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 2 May 2026

Redesigning platforms to prevent addiction is the solution, not limiting access

Contesto

In brief - Bans on accessing social media do not solve the problem of addiction - Platforms need to be redesigned to be less toxic - Minors must be protected without being excluded ## Key facts - What: Analysis on the impact of social media on minors - When: 2 May 2026 - Where: Ticino, Switzerland - Who: Simonetta Caratti, journalist - Amount: Not specified Minors growing up with smartphones in their pockets are exposed to a myriad of digital stimuli, including social media. The latter, often designed to create addiction, represent a significant challenge for parents and educators. According to Simonetta Caratti, journalist at laRegione.ch, access bans are not the solution. The most effective response would be to redesign the platforms to make them less toxic and safer for young users. ### The social media trap Social media are designed to capture users' attention through mechanisms of immediate reward and constant feedback. These elements, combined with algorithms that personalize content based on users' preferences, can create a digital environment that fosters addiction. Minors, in particular, are more vulnerable to these mechanisms due to their cognitive and emotional immaturity. ### The limits of bans Imposing age limits for accessing social media may seem like a simple solution, but it does not address the root of the problem. Bans can be easily circumvented and, in some cases, may even increase curiosity and the desire to access these digital environments. Moreover, excluding minors from social media does not protect them from negative influences that may come from other online sources. ### Redesigning the platforms The solution proposed by Caratti is to redesign social platforms to make them safer and less addictive. Implications for cross-border workers Cross-border workers living in Ticino and working in Italy face unique challenges when it comes to protecting their children from the risks of social media. The geographical and cultural proximity to Italy can influence the digital habits of young people, making it even more important to adopt effective strategies to promote safe and responsible use of social media. ### Before/After Comparisons Before the advent of social media, minors were exposed to a limited number of external influences. Today, with unlimited access to a wide range of online content, young people must navigate a complex and often dangerous digital environment. Redesigning social platforms could offer a safer environment, reducing exposure to harmful content and promoting positive interactions. ### Concrete Scenarios Imagine a scenario where social platforms are redesigned to be less addictive. Algorithms could be modified to reduce exposure to toxic content, while feedback mechanisms could be made more transparent. Additionally, monitoring and support tools could be introduced to help young users manage their online time more effectively. These changes could have a significant impact on the mental health and well-being of minors, making the digital environment a safer and more welcoming place. ### Digital Education Digital education is fundamental to preparing young people to navigate the online world safely and responsibly. School programs that teach digital skills and promote conscious use of technology can make a difference. What to Do Concretely To address the problem of social media addiction, it's important to adopt an integrated approach that combines the redesign of platforms with digital education and awareness. Here are some concrete steps that can be taken: 1. Redesign social platforms: Collaborate with social platform developers to modify algorithms and implement more transparent feedback mechanisms. This can include reducing exposure to toxic content and promoting positive interactions. 2. Digital education: Introduce school programs that teach digital skills and promote responsible technology use. These programs can include lessons on how to recognize and manage the risks associated with social media. 3. Open dialogue: Create an open dialogue between parents and children about social media use. This can help young people express their concerns and receive guidance and support. 4. Monitoring tools: Use parental control applications and other monitoring tools to help parents manage their children's online time more effectively. 5. Institutional support: Support initiatives and organizations that promote digital education and aim to make the digital environment safer for young people. ### Deadlines and Procedures There are no specific deadlines associated with this initiative, but it's important to act promptly to address the problem of social media addiction. Frequently Asked Questions
What are the main risks associated with minors' use of social media?
The main risks include exposure to harmful content, addiction, cyberbullying, and privacy violations. These risks can significantly impact the mental health and well-being of young users.
How can parents protect their children from the risks of social media?
Parents can protect their children by using parental control applications, educating young people about responsible technology use, and fostering open dialogue about social media. Additionally, it's important to monitor children's online activity and provide guidance and support when needed.
What solutions are proposed to make social media safer for minors?
Proposed solutions include redesigning social platforms to be less addictive, digital education through school programs, and institutional support to promote a safer digital environment.
