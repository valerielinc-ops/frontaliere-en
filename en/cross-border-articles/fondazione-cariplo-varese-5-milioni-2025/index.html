Cariplo Foundation: 5 million for 48 Varese projects in 2025 (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 4 May 2026

The Cariplo Foundation has approved the 2025 budget, allocating nearly 5 million euros to 48 projects in the province of Varese.

Contesto

In brief - Fondazione Cariplo approves 2025 budget with 187.2 million euros - 48 projects funded in Varese for over 4.9 million euros - Contributions in culture, social, research, and environment ## Key facts - What: Approval of the 2025 budget - When: 2025 - Where: Province of Varese - Who: Fondazione Cariplo - Amount: 4.9 million euros - Projects: 48 In 2025, Fondazione Cariplo distributed over 187.2 million euros to support 1,265 projects, an increase from the 164.5 million euros in 2024. On the financial side, the year ended with a surplus of 353.9 million euros and a market value of 13.049 billion euros as of December 31, 2025. The province of Varese was awarded 48 grants totaling over 4.9 million euros, distributed among art and culture (20 grants, 1.1 million), personal services (14 grants, 1.4 million), scientific research (2 grants, 0.2 million), and environment (3 grants, 0.1 million). These are added to the so-called cross-cutting challenges with 9 grants for approximately 2.1 million euros. Carlo Massironi, a member of the Central Charity Commission for the province of Varese, emphasizes how Fondazione Cariplo's intervention in Varese stands out for the breadth and continuity of support in the cultural and social sectors. The numbers indicate widespread project development, rooted in the territory. An investment that consolidates mature experiences and accompanies their future evolution. Sarah Maestri, also a member of the Commission, adds that the value of the action in the Varese area lies in the ability to intercept emerging needs and support active and competent local networks. Analysis of Funded Sectors The contributions from Fondazione Cariplo in Varese have been distributed across various sectors, each with a specific impact on the territory and the community. In the cultural sector, the 20 grants totaling 1.1 million euros aim to enhance the artistic and cultural heritage of the province, offering new opportunities for growth and development. The 14 grants totaling 1.4 million euros in the personal services sector are intended to improve the quality of life and support local communities. The 2 grants totaling 0.2 million euros in scientific research support innovative projects that can have a significant impact at both the local and national levels. Finally, the 3 grants totaling 0.1 million euros in the environmental sector aim to promote sustainability and the protection of the territory. ### Cross-cutting Challenges Cross-cutting challenges, with 9 grants totaling approximately 2.1 million euros, include projects that span multiple sectors and have a broader impact on the community. These projects include initiatives to strengthen local communities, contributions to Community Foundations, and emblematic projects that can have a lasting positive effect on the territory. Cross-cutting challenges are a key element of Fondazione Cariplo's strategy, which aims to create a positive and sustainable impact across all sectors. ### Impact on Cross-border Workers For cross-border workers living and working in Varese, the projects funded by Fondazione Cariplo can offer new opportunities for personal and professional growth.
