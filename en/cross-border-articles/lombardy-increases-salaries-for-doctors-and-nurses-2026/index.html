Lombardy increases doctors' and nurses' salaries to stop the exodus to Switzerland

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 23 April 2026

As of September 2026, doctors and nurses in border areas with Switzerland will see increases of up to 10,000 euros gross per year. Lombardy is investing 45 million to retain healthcare personnel.

TL;DR - Lombardy raises salaries for doctors and nurses near Swiss border - 20% pay increase to retain healthcare workers - Funded by 3% contribution from cross-border workers' salaries ## Key facts - Cosa: Aumento del 20% degli stipendi per medici e infermieri - Quando: Dal settembre 2026 - Dove: Aree di confine con la Svizzera in Lombardia - Chi: 7.000 operatori sanitari, tra medici e infermieri - Importo: Investimento totale di circa 45 milioni di euro - Fonte di finanziamento: Contributo del 3% sul salario netto dei lavoratori frontalieri - Aumento annuo lordo: Circa 10.000 euro per medici e 5.400 euro per infermieri - Legge di riferimento: Legge statale 213 del 2023 ## Significant pay rise for doctors and nurses on the border Lombardy has announced a significant increase in salaries for doctors and nurses working in the border areas with Switzerland. The aim is to counter the flight of healthcare personnel to the Swiss healthcare system, where salaries are traditionally higher. The increase, provided for by state law 213 of 2023, will come into effect from September 2026. Massimo Sertori, the regional councillor for Local Authorities and Mountain Areas, confirmed during a hearing before the VIII Commission of the Regional Council that the aim is to implement the legislation by September. 'This is an important measure to retain our healthcare workers and prevent them from moving to Switzerland', Sertori declared. ### Who benefits from the increase The pay rise will affect around 7,000 healthcare workers, including doctors and nurses, working in healthcare facilities near the Swiss border. How is the salary increase funded?
The salary increase is funded through a 3% contribution on the net salary paid by 'old' cross-border workers, who work in Lombardy but reside in Switzerland.
