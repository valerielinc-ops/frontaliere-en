Contesto

In brief - The Green Party presents five parliamentary acts for mobility - Focus on fuels, public transport and slow mobility - Goal: reduce traffic during peak hours ## Key facts - What: Five parliamentary acts for mobility - When: Not yet specified - Where: Canton Ticino - Who: The Green Party - Goal: Improve mobility and reduce traffic The Green Party of Canton Ticino has presented five parliamentary acts aimed at improving mobility and reducing traffic during peak hours. These acts focus on various aspects of mobility, including the high prices of fuels, investments in public transport, shared mobility, and strengthening slow mobility. ### Investments in public transport One of the parliamentary acts proposed by the Green Party concerns investments in public transport. The goal is to make public transport more efficient and accessible, thereby reducing the number of cars on the roads during peak hours. This could have a significant impact on cross-border workers who cross the border daily to go to work in Ticino. ### Shared mobility Another parliamentary act focuses on shared mobility. The Green Party proposes to incentivize the use of shared cars and other forms of shared mobility to reduce the number of vehicles on the roads. This could be particularly useful for cross-border workers who could share their journey with colleagues or other commuters. ### Strengthening slow mobility Finally, the Green Party proposes a parliamentary act to strengthen slow mobility, i.e., mobility on foot and by bicycle. This could include the creation of new cycle paths and the promotion of initiatives to encourage the use of bicycles for daily travel.
