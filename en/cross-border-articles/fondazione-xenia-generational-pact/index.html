Fondazione Xenia: Generational Pact for Disabled Children

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 28 April 2026

An innovative project to ensure a peaceful future for disabled children, inspired by the tradition of the court house.

Contesto

In brief - Generational Pact project for long-term care - Host families in exchange for mutual assistance - Model inspired by the traditional court house ## Key facts - What: Generational Pact for assistance to disabled children - When: Project currently active - Where: Fondazione Xenia, Orino - Who: Fondazione Xenia, participating families - Amount: Free accommodation for host family In the new episode of Soci All Time, the podcast by Radio Materia produced in collaboration with Csv Insubria, an truly innovative project that is making the whole community talk was discussed: the “Generational Pact” of the Fondazione Xenia in Orino. This initiative, born from an idea of two parents from Milan, aims to guarantee a serene and inclusive future for people with intellectual disabilities, creating a model of solidarity among families that could forever change the way we approach the issue of long-term care. During the episode, Orlando Mastrillo interviewed Luca Grasso, father of two children with autism and one of the key figures of the Foundation. Grasso told the story of the project, its meaning and how it is solving one of the most serious problems for families with disabled people: what happens when the parents are no longer there. ### A Pact of Solidarity between Generations The “Generational Pact” is an agreement between two family units that, within a large property of the Fondazione Xenia, commit to providing each other with mutual assistance. A younger family, which shares the values of the Foundation, is hosted for free in an apartment of the structure. In exchange, this family commits to assisting the disabled children of the founding family when, due to age or health reasons, it will no longer be possible to do so. Dettagli operativi

The Requirements to Participate in the Pact For families interested in participating in the project, specific requirements are foreseen, including the sharing of inclusion values and the commitment to care for young people with disabilities. Furthermore, a legal process is envisaged, culminating in the signing of a contract and a notarial deed, which guarantees the solidity of the agreement and the respect of the commitments made. In exchange for this commitment, the host family has the opportunity to live for free in an apartment of the structure in Orino, thus solving any housing problem and creating at the same time a support network for their children and for the families of the community. ### A Dignified and Inclusive Future With the aim of giving dignity and independence to young people with disabilities, the Generational Pact represents an innovative model of long-term care. This project not only guarantees a serene future for disabled children, but also creates a supportive and inclusive community, where families can count on mutual support. The Xenia Foundation, with its commitment and vision, is demonstrating that it is possible to address the challenges of caring for people with disabilities in an innovative and supportive way, inspired by the tradition of the court house. ### Practical Implications for Cross-Border Workers For cross-border workers residing in Ticino and working in Italy, this project could represent a model of long-term care for their disabled children. The possibility of living for free in an apartment of the structure in Orino, in exchange for mutual assistance, could be an ideal solution for many families. Punti chiave

What to Do to Participate To participate in the Generational Pact, interested families must contact the Xenia Foundation in Orino and follow the legal process. It's important to share the values of inclusion and solidarity and commit to caring for young people with disabilities. The hosting family will have the opportunity to live for free in an apartment within the facility, thus solving any housing issues and creating a support network for their children and for the families in the community. ### Deadlines and Procedures The legal process for signing the contract and the notarial agreement is essential to ensure the solidity of the agreement and the respect of the commitments made. Interested families must carefully follow the procedures indicated by the Xenia Foundation and commit to respecting the requirements. The deadline for submitting the application has not yet been specified, but it is important to contact the Foundation as soon as possible to obtain all the necessary information. ### Useful Tools For further information and to contact the Xenia Foundation, you can visit the official website or contact the facility directly. Additionally, you can use the salary calculator to evaluate the financial implications of participating in the project.
