Magliaso lifts advisory on sparing water use (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 27 February 2026

Announcement from the Municipality: the pipeline has been repaired. The request to limit potable water use is no longer necessary.

The announcement was made on Friday, February 27, after technicians from the Municipal Technical Office successfully completed the necessary repairs. The situation caused some inconvenience for residents and local businesses, particularly for those commuting between Italy and Switzerland who rely on the municipality's resources for their daily needs. However, the swift intervention and prompt communication from municipal authorities helped minimize complications. > 'The situation has returned to normal, and we thank citizens for their cooperation,' said a spokesperson for the Municipality, emphasizing the importance of responsible water resource management even under ordinary conditions.

Dettagli operativi

The pipeline rupture, which occurred on Tuesday, February 24, disrupted the municipality's water supply. In similar cases, local authorities are required to continuously monitor potable water availability to ensure service continuity, especially during periods of high consumption or extraordinary events. Emergency situations like the one experienced in Magliaso highlight the significance of infrastructure in managing water resources. According to data provided by the Swiss Association of Water Supply Companies (SVGW), over 1 billion Swiss francs have been invested in recent years to modernize cantonal water networks. However, unpredictable events such as mechanical failures or adverse weather conditions can still jeopardize supply. ⚠️ Cross-border workers in sectors like construction or food service in Ticino, both of which heavily depend on water usage, should always monitor local municipal communications and plan accordingly.
