The Swiss economy showed signs of recovery in the fourth quarter of 2025, registering a 0.2% increase in gross domestic product (GDP) compared to the previous three months. This figure, provided by the State Secretariat for Economic Affairs (SECO), is particularly significant considering the 0.4% contraction recorded in the third quarter, which was mainly due to challenges in the chemical and pharmaceutical sectors, exacerbated by the imposition of new customs duties on exports to key markets such as the European Union. The pharmaceutical industry, which accounts for approximately 38% of Swiss exports, experienced a 3.7% decline in sales volumes between July and September 2025. Overall, the Swiss economy closed the year with a growth rate of 1.4%, exceeding the 1.2% in 2024 and 1.3% in 2023. Dettagli operativi

The current economic context offers various points of reflection for cross-border workers employed in Ticino. SECO has indicated that a potential slowdown in economic growth is expected for 2026, primarily due to geopolitical uncertainty and global trade policies. This scenario could have repercussions on the cross-border labor market and the tax conditions of Italian workers employed in Switzerland. Under current regulations, the law on cross-border taxation stipulates that income earned in Switzerland is subject to withholding tax, with rates ranging from 5% to 10%, depending on income levels. Additionally, income earned in Italy must be regularly declared in the Italian income tax return, using the 'Modello Unico'. For example, a cross-border worker residing in Como who earns CHF 50,000 annually in Ticino might need to pay approximately CHF 2,500 in withholding tax, in addition to declaring the amount in Italy to avoid double taxation. To optimize their tax situation, it is highly recommended to consult a tax expert who can provide guidance on taking advantage of deductions for medical expenses, mortgages, or dependents. 📊 Operational Checklist for Cross-Border Workers: - Verify the applicable withholding tax rates based on income level. - Gather all necessary documentation for the Italian income tax declaration (CU form, foreign income certification). - Check for any available tax credits to avoid double taxation. - Monitor tax deadlines: in Italy, the deadline for the 'Modello Unico' is generally November 30; in Switzerland, tax refunds are calculated annually and can affect net income. ⚠️ A significant aspect to consider is tax refunds. Frequently Asked Questions
How does the Swiss economy behave in the fourth quarter of 2025?
The Swiss economy showed signs of recovery in the fourth quarter of 2025, recording an increase in gross domestic product (GDP) by 0.2% compared to the previous three months.
What is the impact of the revision of the cantonal minimum wage on the purchasing power of frontier workers in Ticino?
The revision fixed the minimum wage at 20.50 CHF (+2.5% compared to 2024), strengthening the purchasing power and contributing to the growth of private consumption.
What sectors in Ticino have benefited more from economic growth in 2025?
In 2025, the construction industry in Ticino benefited more from economic growth, an increase of 3.2% in public and private contracts. Municipalities such as Mendrisio and Chiasso have seen an increase of 4% in the granting of building permits, thanks to infrastructure projects such as the requalification of the railway node and the expansion of the road network.
What are the main challenges for Ticino companies in 2025?
Ticinese companies in 2025 face challenges such as increasing international competition and increasing commodity costs, which for some categories have increased by up to 12% compared to 2024. It is essential to monitor the balance between domestic demand and foreign trade and implement support measures for the most vulnerable sectors.
How can frontier workers optimize their tax situation?
Frontiers can optimize their tax situation by consulting an expert to exploit deductions for medical, mortgage or family expenses. It is important to check the rates applicable to the tax at source, collect the necessary documentation for the tax return in Italy and monitor tax deadlines to avoid double taxation.
