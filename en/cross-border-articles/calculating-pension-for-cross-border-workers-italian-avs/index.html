Practical Implications for Cross-Border Workers For cross-border workers, it is crucial to understand how Swiss AVS and Italian INPS contributions combine to determine the final pension. The totalization of contributions is a complex process that requires attention and planning. It is important to verify that all contributions have been correctly recorded and that there are no missing contribution periods. ### Totalization Procedure The procedure for totalizing AVS and INPS contributions involves several steps. First, it is necessary to gather all documents related to the contributions paid in Switzerland and Italy. Subsequently, it is possible to submit a totalization application to the competent authorities. The authorities will evaluate the documentation and calculate the amount of the final pension. ### Deadlines and Required Documents The deadlines for submitting the totalization application vary depending on the individual situation. It is important to check the specific deadlines and ensure that the application is submitted in a timely manner. The required documents include AVS and INPS contribution certificates, identity documents, and any other documents requested by the competent authorities. ### Comparison of Scenarios A vs B Suppose a cross-border worker has worked in Switzerland for 10 years and in Italy for 15 years.
