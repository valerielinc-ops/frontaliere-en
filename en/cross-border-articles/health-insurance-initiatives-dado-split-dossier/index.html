Dadò's plan for health insurers emerges

Each initiative on health insurers follows its own path with separate funding measures. Fiorenzo Dadò's proposal.

Contesto

In brief - Each initiative on health insurance continues separately - Different financing measures for each initiative - The risk that only the Lega's initiative will come into force ## Key facts - What: Proposal by Fiorenzo Dadò to treat the initiatives on health insurance separately - When: After the meeting with the State Council in the Management Commission - Where: Canton Ticino - Who: Fiorenzo Dadò, State Council, political parties - Amount: Not specified The proposal by Fiorenzo Dadò to treat the initiatives on health insurance separately has caught everyone off guard. According to the Centro president, the Parliament should be able to propose two reports with accompanying financing measures. 'If someone is not in agreement, they can always launch a referendum and call on the people to express themselves,' Dadò declared. The proposal came after the meeting with the State Council in the Management Commission and could disrupt the cards on the table. Each initiative continues on its own path with its own parliamentary process and separate financing measures, unlike the Government's proposal which envisaged a single package with staggered entry into force and common financing measures. Daniele Piccaluga, Lega coordinator, has stated that he has taken note of Dadò's proposal and that the movement will evaluate it before expressing itself. The important thing, explains Piccaluga, is that the Lega's initiative be applied immediately in its entirety. The Socialist party, on the other hand, has the sole objective that both initiatives be implemented as soon as possible, packaged or not. 'The initiatives must both be implemented as soon as possible, packaged or not. They must not be treated differently,' declared the group leader Ivo Durisch. ### Risks and challenges...
