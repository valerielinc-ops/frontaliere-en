Lake Geneva cleanup: 2.5 tons of waste collected in two days (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 3 May 2026

Over 1,000 volunteers, including 300 divers, took part in the 13th edition of Net'Léman, collecting 2,592 kg of waste

Contesto

In brief - 2,592 kg of waste collected in two days - 1,000 volunteers, 300 divers - 424 kg of plastic, 1,088 cotton swabs ## Key facts - What: Lake Geneva cleanup operation - When: May 2-3, 2026 - Where: Ten locations around the basin, including Cologny, Nyon, Morges, Rolle, Evian - Who: Association for the Protection of Lake Geneva (ASL) - Amount: 2,592 kg of total waste, 424 kg of plastic, 1,088 cotton swabs During the weekend of May 2-3, 2026, over 1,000 volunteers participated in the 13th edition of Net'Léman, the Lake Geneva cleanup operation. Among the participants, around 300 divers worked both on the surface and underwater to collect waste. The initiative took place simultaneously in ten Swiss and French locations, including Cologny, Nyon, Morges, Rolle, and Evian. Although the total volume of waste collected has decreased compared to previous editions, the Association for the Protection of Lake Geneva (ASL) highlighted the persistent presence of plastic and everyday objects, which are particularly harmful to the lake's ecosystem. ### Types of waste collected Among the waste collected over the weekend were 424 kilograms of plastic, including 41 kg of PET. The high number of cotton swabs is also concerning: a total of 1,088. This figure indicates a clear trend in the improper disposal of cotton buds through toilets. Other waste collected includes 88 e-cigarettes, both disposable and rechargeable, as well as approximately 36,600 traditional cigarette butts. The tally also includes 150 lighters, 44 tires, 10 nitrous oxide canisters, 7 scooters, and larger items such as a drone, a computer, a ladder, a car battery, a door, and an anchor. ### Impact on the ecosystem The ASL emphasized that, despite the decrease in the total volume of waste collected, the presence...

The quality of the water and the health of the lake ecosystem are fundamental to the daily life and well-being of local communities.
