Action: What to do if you want to become a security agent in Ticino If you are a cross-border worker interested in becoming a security agent in Ticino, there are some steps you need to follow: 1. Check if you are eligible to become a security agent. To do this, you will need to pass an entrance exam for the CPSICUR training. 2. If you pass the entrance exam, you will be able to attend the CPSICUR training at the Institute of Continuing Education (IFC). The training consists of 40 hours of lessons divided into 5 topics. 3. At the end of the training, you will need to take an exam to obtain the security agent authorization. 4. Once you have obtained the authorization, you will need to request the extract of the Swiss criminal record (and foreign if the applicant resides or has resided abroad in the 5 years prior to the application) issued no more than three months ago. 5. Finally, you can submit your application for the security agent authorization to the Ticino Cantonal Police. If you follow these steps and meet the requirements, you will be able to become a security agent in Ticino and contribute to the security of public events. Frequently Asked Questions
What are the requirements to become a security agent in Ticino?
To become a security agent in Ticino, you need to pass an entrance test for the CPSICUR training, attend the training at the Institute of Continuing Education (IFC), pass an exam to obtain the security agent authorization, request the Swiss criminal record extract, and submit your application to the Ticino Cantonal Police.
What is the mandatory training for security agents in Ticino?
The mandatory training for security agents in Ticino is the CPSICUR training, which consists of 40 hours of lessons divided into 5 topics. Both basic and continuous training must be carried out by the IFC, which works closely with the Cantonal Police.
What checks does the Cantonal Police carry out on the employees of private security agencies?
The Ticino Cantonal Police carries out checks on the employees of private security agencies to ensure that agents and security agencies have the specific authorization issued by the Cantonal Police.
