Dettagli operativi

Practical implications for cross-border workers The event organized by Confindustria Como offers cross-border workers who commute to Ticino and reside in Italy a concrete opportunity to deepen the link between art, culture, and health. According to a 2022 study conducted by the University of Italian Switzerland (USI), 68% of the cross-border workers interviewed stated that they had improved their psycho-physical well-being by participating in cultural events. For example, residents of Como who work in Lugano can benefit from discounts on tickets for exhibitions and concerts thanks to agreements between the Municipality of Como and cultural institutions in Ticino. - Operational checklist for cross-border workers: - Verify the active agreements between your municipality of residence and the cultural institutions in Ticino. - Plan participation in cultural events during weekends or days off. - Take advantage of initiatives such as 'Culture in Ticino', which offers tourist packages at reduced prices for cross-border workers. ### Comparison with the previous situation In the past, the link between art, culture, and health was a less explored theme. Today, thanks to initiatives like that of Confindustria Como, the importance of cultural participation as a pillar of a new welfare is increasingly recognized. For example, the 2019 Ticino cantonal regulation on health promotion introduced contributions of up to 5,000 CHF for projects that integrate art and well-being. Frequently Asked Questions
What is the main objective of the event organized by Confindustria Como?
The main objective of the event is to explore the link between art, culture, and health, promoting cultural participation as a pillar of a new welfare system. The meeting on May 13, 2026, will be dedicated to discussing how art and culture can contribute to well-being and longevity.
Who are the speakers at the event?
The event's speakers include Gaetana Mariani, Coordinator of the Territorial Health Table of Confindustria Como, Enzo Grossi, Scientific Director of Villa Santa Maria, Sergio Gaddi, Art Historian, Gianluigi Spata, President of the Order of Physicians of Como, and Claudio Zanon, Scientific Director of Motore Sanità. The meeting will be moderated by Stefano Rudilosso, Head of Communication and Press Office of Confindustria Como.
How can I register for the event?
To register for the event, visit the Confindustria Como website and look for the section dedicated to the event. Fill out the online registration form and you will receive a confirmation email. Participation is free, but registration is mandatory.
