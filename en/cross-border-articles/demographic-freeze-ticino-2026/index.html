Demographic decline alarms Ticino's future

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 30 April 2026

Low birth rate and aging population concern the Canton of Ticino. Parliamentary question for a cantonal strategy.

Contesto

In brief - Ticino at risk of demographic glaciation - Birth rate below replacement level - Elderly population on the rise ## Key facts - What: Possible demographic glaciation - When: April 28, 2026 - Where: Canton of Ticino - Who: Claudio Isabella, Alessandro Corti, Il Centro Group - Amount: Not specified The Canton of Ticino is facing a potential demographic emergency. The alarm was raised by Claudio Isabella and Alessandro Corti, along with the Il Centro Group and the young members of Il Centro, through an interrogation presented on April 28, 2026. The phenomenon, described as a possible 'demographic glaciation,' is characterized by a birth rate consistently below the replacement level and a progressive increase in the elderly population in need of care. Public data indicates a scenario that, in the medium term, risks putting pressure on the sustainability of the Canton's social, pension, and economic systems. According to the promoters, ignoring the problem would mean intervening too late, when the effects will already be consolidated. In recent months, the Grand Council has discussed the so-called 'poker on birth rates,' only partially accepting it. The proposals put forward were not considered adequate by the State Council. However, concerns are growing from various sectors: economic associations, trade unions, schools, and research bodies describe the situation as critical or even dramatic. According to the signatories, the issue cannot be addressed in a fragmented way. 'A clear political management is needed, with a defined departmental responsibility and a cantonal strategy capable of integrating family policies, work, training, taxation, and territorial attractiveness.' What are the main concerns related to demographic aging in Ticino?
The main concerns involve the low birth rate and the increase in the elderly population requiring care. These factors could put pressure on the sustainability of the Canton's social, pension, and economic systems.
What does the parliamentary interpellation submitted by Claudio Isabella and Alessandro Corti ask for?
The interpellation asks the State Council to clarify which Department will be responsible for the demographic dossier, with what timelines and objectives, and whether monitoring measures and new statistical tools are planned.
