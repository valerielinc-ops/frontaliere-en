Ticino is preparing to transform one of its most exploited yet valuable areas into a large public green space. The official signing that marks the beginning of this initiative was carried out by five municipalities: Agno, Bioggio, Manno, Muzzano, and Vezia, who signed a letter of intent for the creation of Vedeggio Park. Announced officially on April 27, 2024, this step represents a crucial move in a project aimed at balancing accessibility, environmental protection, and sustainable development. The area, already included in the cantonal master plan R9, has undergone preliminary studies, but now enters a concrete phase of implementation. Dettagli operativi

This initiative fits within a broader sustainable land-use planning strategy adopted by Canton Ticino, which seeks to enhance green areas and improve residents’ quality of life in municipalities. The signing today, held at the Agno town hall, solidifies the commitment of all involved parties to collaborate towards a shared goal. The park project will unfold in several phases: initially, the study and design stage will focus on defining priorities and effective solutions, also considering local residents’ requests. Subsequently, implementation will begin, with works ranging from simple interventions—such as improving picnic zones and fountains—to more complex tasks like trail construction and access requalification. The park’s management will be entrusted to a dedicated body, involving local associations and volunteers, to ensure continuous monitoring and optimal usability. An essential aspect will be integrating the park with the mobility system, aiming to facilitate pedestrian and bicycle access, reduce vehicle traffic, and improve air quality. The strategy also emphasizes raising awareness among the community about the importance of preserving this space, promoting educational activities and civic participation. From a regulatory perspective, the project adheres to the guidelines set by the cantonal master plan R9, which defines criteria for protecting and developing green areas, along with regional regulations governing natural resource management and urban planning. Collaboration between municipalities and the canton allows access to public funds and European grants to cover planning and implementation costs. Punti chiave

For residents and cross-border workers in Mendrisio or Lugano, the new Vedeggio Park offers a tangible opportunity to enhance daily well-being. The chance to enjoy green spaces, relaxation areas, and pedestrian routes benefits those living in densely populated zones or near busy traffic arteries. It naturally raises questions about how to actively contribute to the development and maintenance of this public space. Staying informed through official channels—such as the websites of the municipalities and the Department of Territory—and participating in public consultations is highly recommended. Those planning outings or walks can utilize helpful tools available on Frontaliere Ticino, including the salary calculator and mobility maps for sustainable travel options. Remember, this project not only aims to improve quality of life but also strengthens community spirit and civic responsibility—key elements for maintaining a vibrant, inclusive territory. The collaboration between citizens, local authorities, and associations will drive this transformation, with the goal of creating a park model that could serve as an example regionally and nationally. Construction is scheduled to begin in 2025, with the first results expected by 2026. Stay updated on deadlines and participation opportunities by regularly visiting the Frontaliere Ticino website and the official portals of the involved municipalities.

Frequently Asked Questions
What is the purpose of the Park of Wisdom?
The Park of Vedeggio aims to combine usability, environmental protection and sustainable development, creating a green lung that can be beneficial to the population and the environment.
What is the path of realization of the Parco del Vedeggio in terms of phases and timing?
The project includes phases of study and design (concluded by 2024), construction (starting works in 2025) and management. The first achievements are planned for 2026.
What are the main recreational activities in the Park of Vedeggio?
The park will include walking and cycling trails, picnic areas, relaxation areas and spaces dedicated to cultural and social events, promoting outdoor activities all year round.
