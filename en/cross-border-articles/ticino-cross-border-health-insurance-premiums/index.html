Dettagli operativi

The causes of the decrease in wages The decrease in wages of cross-border workers is due to various factors, including the economic crisis and competition in the labor market. The Movement for Socialism wants to combat cash sickness benefits, which are considered a monstrosity. Cash sickness benefits are an additional cost for cross-border workers, who already pay taxes and contributions in Switzerland. For example, a cross-border worker who works in Lugano and earns 60,000 francs per year, has to pay around 1,200 francs in cash sickness benefits every year, in addition to 10,000 francs in taxes and contributions. This means that their net income is around 48,800 francs per year. ### The consequences of the decrease in wages The decrease in wages of cross-border workers has negative consequences on their quality of life. Many of them are forced to leave Ticino to look for work elsewhere. The Movement for Socialism wants to ensure that cross-border workers have access to dignified working conditions and adequate wages. For example, in Chiasso, the percentage of cross-border workers who have left the city to look for work elsewhere has increased by 15% in the last two years. Furthermore, the 2019 cross-border work regulation provides that Swiss employers must pay a contribution of 2,500 francs per year for each cross-border worker employed.
