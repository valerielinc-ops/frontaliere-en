How to protect your TWINT account Prevention is always better than cure. Here are some practical tips to protect your TWINT account and avoid becoming a victim of fraud. ### Enable two-factor authentication Two-factor authentication (2FA) adds an extra layer of security to your account. When you enable this feature, in addition to your password, you will be asked for a verification code sent to your phone number or generated by a dedicated app. This makes it much harder for criminals to access your account even if they manage to steal your password. ### Use complex and unique passwords Create complex passwords that include uppercase and lowercase letters, numbers, and symbols. Avoid using the same password for multiple accounts and change your credentials regularly. You can use a password manager to store and generate secure passwords. ### Be careful with suspicious messages Do not click on links or open attachments in emails or SMS that appear to come from TWINT or your bank. Always verify the authenticity of the sender and contact the relevant institution directly if you have any doubts. ### Keep your device up to date Keep your smartphone and apps always up to date to protect yourself from the latest security vulnerabilities. Updates often include security patches that fix known issues. ### Monitor your account activity Regularly check the transactions and activities of your TWINT account. If you notice anything suspicious, act immediately to block access and contact support. ## Compare with the previous situation Before these attacks, cross-border workers could use TWINT without worrying too much about security.
