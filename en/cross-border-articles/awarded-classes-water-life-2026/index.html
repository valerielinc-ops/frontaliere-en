Winning classes of 'Water is Life' contest awarded (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 9 May 2026

Classes 5B of Bellinzona Nord, 2B of Gambarogno and 3/4 of Brissago win the creative contest promoted by the Ticino Plumbers Association

Contesto

In brief - The 5B class of Bellinzona Nord wins the 'Water is Life' contest - Participation of elementary schools in the Canton of Ticino - Prizes for the 2B class of Gambarogno and the 3/4 class of Brissago ## Key facts - What: Creative contest 'Water is Life' - When: 2026 - Where: Canton of Ticino - Who: Association of Ticino Water Engineers - Amount: Not specified The creative contest 'Water is Life', promoted by the Association of Ticino Water Engineers (AFT) on the occasion of World Water Day 2026 and addressed to the elementary schools of the Canton of Ticino, has concluded with participation and involvement. An initiative born to invite the youngest to reflect on the value of water through creativity, observation and shared work. The projects received have shown sensitivity, commitment and an excellent ability to transform a fundamental theme into concrete, cared for and meaningful works. Each class has interpreted the contest with its own gaze, telling water as a precious resource, common good and essential part of daily life. ### Final ranking At the end of the evaluation, the final ranking was as follows: the 5B class of Bellinzona Nord Elementary Schools won first place, the 2B class of Gambarogno School, Quartino site, won second place, and the 3 and 4 classes of Brissago Elementary Schools won third place. 'We are really happy with the participation that this contest has been able to generate', comments Matteo Negri, President of the Association of Ticino Water Engineers. 'The works received have shown us creativity, attention and awareness. It was nice to see how the children have been able to give shape, with simplicity and authenticity, to such an important theme'. ### Educational importance Beyond the ranking, the contest has represented above all a...

Practical Analysis The 'Water is Life' competition involved numerous elementary schools in the Canton of Ticino, offering children the opportunity to reflect on the value of water through creative projects. This type of initiative not only promotes environmental awareness but also stimulates critical thinking and collaboration among young students. ### The Importance of Awareness Raising awareness about the importance of water is crucial, especially in a context like that of the Canton of Ticino, where the management of water resources is fundamental. For cross-border workers who live in Italy and work in Switzerland, participating in educational projects like this can be a way to involve their children in activities that promote important values, such as respect for the environment and the community. ### Comparison with the Previous Situation Before this initiative, the schools in the Canton of Ticino did not have a specific competition dedicated to water. The introduction of 'Water is Life' represented a significant step towards promoting a more aware water culture. This type of project can be replicated in other regions, also involving Italian schools on the border with Switzerland. ### Future Scenarios In the future, the Associazione Fontanieri Ticinesi could organize other similar competitions, perhaps expanding participation at the regional or national level. This could offer further opportunities for cross-border workers to involve their children in educational and creative activities, strengthening the bond between Italian and Swiss communities. ### Implications for Cross-Border Workers For cross-border workers, participating in initiatives like this can be a way to integrate their children into the local community, promoting shared values and fostering coll...
