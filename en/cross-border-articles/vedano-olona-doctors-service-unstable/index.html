Vedano Olona mayor denounces instability of doctors

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 30 April 2026

The mayor of Vedano Olona, Sergio Mina, denounces the instability of the general medicine service and asks for urgent interventions.

Contesto

In brief - Mayor of Vedano Olona denounces instability of medical service - ASST Sette Laghi called to ensure continuity - Priority for the elderly and families with minors - Municipal investments for facilities: €250,000 over three years - Comparison with Ticino scenario: Lugano and Mendrisio with 92% coverage rates ## Key facts - What: Instability of basic medical service - When: Official communication April 2026 - Where: Vedano Olona, outpatient clinic via Sirtori 1 - Who: Mayor Sergio Mina, ASST Sette Laghi - Amount: Municipal investments for facilities: €250,000 over three years - Comparison: Medical coverage rate in Ticino (Lugano and Mendrisio: 92%) The mayor of Vedano Olona, Sergio Mina, has sent a new official communication to the Socio-Sanitary Directorate of ASST Sette Laghi to request the strengthening and continuity of the service at the outpatient clinic in vicolo Sirtori. In the letter, the mayor denounced the instability of the general medical service, asking for immediate interventions to ensure assistance to citizens. The missive follows previous reports sent in October 2025 and January 2026, emphasizing how the situation is becoming unsustainable for the local community. ### Municipal investments and lack of doctors In the letter, the mayor highlights the economic and logistical effort made by the municipal administration to make Vedano Olona an attractive place for healthcare professionals. Targeted interventions have been implemented to reduce the management costs borne by General Practitioners, with the aim of stabilizing the healthcare facility in via Sirtori 1. Dettagli operativi

Implications for the citizens of Vedano Olona The instability of the general medicine service in Vedano Olona has serious implications for citizens, particularly for the most vulnerable segments of the population. The elderly and families with children are the ones most affected by the discontinuity of the service, having to face difficult journeys to access necessary care. For example, many residents must travel to Varese or Cantù, respectively 10 and 15 kilometers away, to receive basic medical assistance. The lack of a stable and accessible healthcare facility puts public health protection at risk, making an intervention by ASST Sette Laghi urgent. ### Comparison with the previous situation Before the municipal interventions, the situation was already critical, but the administration's efforts to reduce management costs and make the facility more attractive to doctors did not yield the expected results. The discontinuous presence of doctors has made further intervention by the mayor necessary, who decided to write directly to the Socio-Sanitary Directorate of ASST Sette Laghi to request guarantees and concrete solutions. > "The situation is unsustainable and requires immediate intervention to ensure that the citizens of Vedano Olona have access to basic care." ### Future Scenarios If immediate measures are not taken, the situation could worsen further, with an increase in difficulties for citizens in finding timely healthcare assistance. It is essential that ASST Sette Laghi promptly responds to the mayor's request, ensuring a stable and accessible healthcare facility for all residents of Vedano Olona.
