New Bodio-Giornico bike path: 2.1 km of safety and connection

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 28 April 2026

The new bike path connecting Bodio and Giornico along the Ticino River has been inaugurated, with an investment of 3.7 million francs.

Contesto

In brief - New Bodio-Giornico bike and pedestrian path, 2.1 km long - Total investment of 3.7 million francs - Eliminated conflicts between cycling and industrial traffic ## Key facts - What: New bike and pedestrian path - When: April 28, 2026 - Where: Along the Ticino River between Bodio and Giornico - Who: Department of Territory (DT), Federal Roads Office (FEDRO), Municipality of Giornico - Amount: 3.7 million francs The new bike and pedestrian path connecting the districts of Bodio and Giornico along the Ticino River is now fully accessible. The Department of Territory (DT), the Federal Roads Office (FEDRO), and the Municipality of Giornico have announced the completion of the works. The new infrastructure extends for 2.1 kilometers and is part of the national route N3 North-South of the Swiss Mobile network. It offers cyclists and pedestrians a dedicated and safer connection, protected from vehicle traffic. The realization of the new path has allowed the elimination of conflicts between cycling traffic and the heavy traffic of the industrial area of Bodio. Furthermore, the new route dedicated to slow mobility completes the works carried out in recent years by FEDRO for the bypass of the industrial area. Its realization took place in coordination with the construction and commissioning of the Giornico Heavy Vehicle Control Center (HVCC). The opening of the route coincides with the first anniversary of the birth of the new Municipality of Giornico, born from the merger of Giornico and Bodio. The new infrastructure also symbolically represents a physical union between the two districts. ### Costs and funding The first section, realized and entirely financed by FEDRO, cost approximately 2.2 million francs. Dettagli operativi

The new Bodio-Giornico cycle and pedestrian path represents a significant step forward for sustainable mobility and citizen safety. The 2.1-kilometer path offers a dedicated and safer connection for cyclists and pedestrians, away from vehicular traffic. This project is an integral part of the national N3 North-South route of the Swiss Mobile network, which promotes slow and sustainable mobility throughout Switzerland. The realization of the path has eliminated conflicts between cycling traffic and the heavy traffic of the Bodio industrial area. This is a significant advantage for the safety of cyclists and pedestrians, who can now use the path without the risk of accidents with industrial vehicles. Furthermore, the new route dedicated to slow mobility complements the work carried out in recent years by USTRA for the bypass of the industrial area, further improving safety and traffic flow. The path was realized in coordination with the construction and commissioning of the Giornico Heavy Vehicle Control Center (CCVP). This coordination allowed for the optimization of project times and costs, ensuring a high-quality result. The opening of the route coincides with the first anniversary of the birth of the new municipality of Giornico, born from the merger of Giornico and Bodio. The new infrastructure also symbolically represents a physical union between the two neighborhoods, strengthening the sense of community and belonging. ### Implications for cross-border workers The new cycle and pedestrian path offers new opportunities for cross-border workers who work in Ticino and reside in Italy. Frequently Asked Questions
How long is the new Bodio-Giornico bike and pedestrian path?
The new Bodio-Giornico bike and pedestrian path is 2.1 kilometers long and connects the neighborhoods of Bodio and Giornico along the Ticino River.
Who funded the creation of the new path?
The first section was fully funded by USTRA for approximately 2.2 million francs. The second section, costing 1.5 million francs, was funded 79% by the Canton and 21% by the municipalities of the Regional Transport Commission Tre Valli (CRT3V).
What are the benefits of the new path for cross-border workers?
The new path offers a dedicated and safer connection for cyclists and pedestrians, away from vehicular traffic. Cross-border workers can use the path for daily commutes or recreational activities, improving their quality of life and reducing environmental impact.
