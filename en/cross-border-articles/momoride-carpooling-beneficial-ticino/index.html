MomòRide: Ticino's charity carpooling challenge

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 28 April 2026

The first collective carpooling challenge for charity starts on May 1st in Ticino, aiming to collect points to donate 1,000 francs.

Contesto

In brief - MomòRide starts on May 1st in Ticino - Goal: 40,000 points to donate 1,000 CHF - Participants from Balerna, Chiasso, and Novazzano ## Key facts - What: Collective carpooling challenge - When: May 1st for a month - Where: Balerna, Chiasso, and Novazzano - Who: Cross-border commuters and local businesses - Amount: 1,000 CHF donation The MomòRide challenge, the first Ticino initiative for charitable carpooling, starts on May 1st and will last for the entire month. The goal is to collect 40,000 points through car sharing among commuters and businesses in Balerna, Chiasso, and Novazzano. The 1,000 CHF donation, sponsored by Banca dello Stato del Canton Ticino, will go to the Fondazione Provvida Madre in Balerna. Participants will need to track their commutes with the Mobalt app, earning points for each trip. These points will add up with those of other carpoolers in the area. Once the common target is reached, the donation will be made. The initiative is promoted by ERS-MB, PSE Pian Faloppia, lastminute foundation, and Mobitrends. The collective challenge aims to create a virtuous circle, promoting carpooling for mobility and the environment, with positive social impacts as well. Further challenges will be launched in autumn, with other public utility entities in the area as beneficiaries. Organizations interested in sponsoring collective challenges can contact [email protected] for more information. The number of participants is continuously growing, with new businesses launching the project among their employees in conjunction with the first collective challenge. In addition to businesses, all workers in the three municipalities are invited to participate. By actively participating, not only do they reduce the environmental impact of their commutes, but they also contribute to a public utility project. This type of initiative promotes greater awareness of the importance of sustainable mobility and resource sharing. ### Comparison with Other Initiatives Compared to other carpooling initiatives, MomòRide stands out for its collective and beneficial nature. This approach not only encourages participation but also solidarity and a sense of community. ### Concrete Scenarios Imagine a scenario where all commuters in the Chiasso, Balerna, and Novazzano area actively participate in the MomòRide challenge. Action: how to participate in the MomòRide challenge To participate in the MomòRide challenge, follow these steps: 1. Download the Mobalt app: The app is available for iOS and Android and can be downloaded for free. 2. Register as a user: Create a personal account and enter your details. 3. Track your movements: Every time you share your car for a commute, track the route with the app. 4. Accumulate points: Every tracked journey contributes to the accumulation of points. 5. Reach the goal: Once you reach 40,000 points, a donation of one thousand francs will be made. ### Deadlines and prizes The MomòRide challenge will last throughout the month of May. Participants can earn individual prizes each month, in addition to contributing to a public utility project. Companies that promote carpooling among their employees can also benefit from collective prizes. ### Useful tools For more information and to register, visit the official MomòRide website and download the Mobalt app. You can also contact the organization for further details and to find out how to participate in future collective challenges. ### Final CTA If you are a cross-border worker who works in the area of Chiasso, Balerna and Novazzano, don't miss the opportunity to participate in the MomòRide challenge. Frequently Asked Questions
How can I participate in the MomòRide challenge?
To participate in the MomòRide challenge, you need to download the Mobalt app, register as a user, and start tracking your commute by carpooling. Each tracked trip contributes to the accumulation of points, which add up to those of other participants.
What is the goal of the MomòRide challenge?
The goal of the MomòRide challenge is to collect 40,000 points through carpooling among commuters and companies in Balerna, Chiasso, and Novazzano. Upon reaching this goal, a donation of one thousand francs will be made to the Provvida Madre Foundation of Balerna.
Who can participate in the MomòRide challenge?
The MomòRide challenge is open to all commuters and workers in the municipalities of Balerna, Chiasso, and Novazzano. Companies can also promote carpooling among their employees and participate in the collective challenge.
