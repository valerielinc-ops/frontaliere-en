From S status to B permit in Ticino Article 74 paragraph 2 of the Asylum Law is the core of the strategy for the integration and regularization of Ukrainian refugees who arrived in Switzerland with S status. This article establishes that if provisional protection is not revoked, beneficiaries automatically qualify for a B permit after five years of legal residence in Switzerland, through a transition mechanism aimed at promoting social and work integration. Practically, this means that Ukrainian citizens arriving with S status in Ticino between 2022 and 2024 will see their status formalized without additional bureaucratic procedures after 2027, helping to stabilize their legal and social position within the Canton. For cross-border workers and local businesses, this development can bring benefits and challenges. On one side, greater stability allows professionals and companies to plan more securely for the future, also facilitating access to social benefits and participation in integration policies. On the other, an increase in B permit holders could exert more pressure on public services such as healthcare, schools, and transportation, as well as on the local labor market, already under stress in areas like Lugano, Mendrisio, and Bellinzona. For instance, in 2023, the number of residents with a B permit in Ticino was around 55,000, of whom about 8,000 are Ukrainian nationals—a figure that could rise with this transition. However, the law does not specify in detail how residence and integration checks should be carried out, leaving room for interpretation and potential disputes.
