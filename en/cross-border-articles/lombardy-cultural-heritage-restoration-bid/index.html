21.5 million for restoring Lombardy's cultural heritage (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 4 May 2026

Lombardy Region and Fondazione Cariplo fund projects to transform public properties into cultural hubs. Applications due by June 24, 2026.

Contesto

In brief - 21.5 million euros to restore cultural assets in Lombardy - Funding up to 1 million euros per project - Applications by June 24, 2026 ## Key facts - What: Restoration and refurbishment of public buildings - When: Call open from May 4, 2026, applications by June 24, 2026 - Where: Lombardy - Who: Lombardy Region and Fondazione Cariplo - Amount: 21.5 million euros in total, with contributions up to 1 million euros per project Over 21 million euros to revive Lombardy's cultural heritage. Today, the call for 'Integrated Culture Projects 2026-2029' was presented at Palazzo Lombardia, a measure aimed at restoring and refurbishing the region's public cultural heritage. The initiative stems from the collaboration between Lombardy Region, which is investing 18 million euros, and Fondazione Cariplo, with a budget of 3.5 million. The goal is to transform old buildings into new centers of aggregation and beauty, creating small and large cultural hubs that blend with communities, from mountain villages to the outskirts of big cities. The idea behind the call is that culture is the first antidote against social and youth discomfort, as well as a fundamental driver for tourist attractiveness and local economic development. The call is open to public entities in Lombardy, which can also participate in partnership with private entities. Lombardy Region will fund up to 50% of the project costs, with amounts ranging from a minimum of 500,000 euros to a maximum of 1 million euros. The first installment will be disbursed in the last months of 2026, and all approved projects must be completed by no later than 2029. ### How to participate Interested entities have until June 24, 2026, to submit their applications. Practical Analysis: Implications for Cross-Border Workers The call for the restoration of Lombard cultural assets represents a significant opportunity for cross-border workers employed in Lombardy. The creation of new cultural hubs can have a positive impact on the local economy, offering new job opportunities and improving the quality of life in local communities. Furthermore, the enhancement of cultural heritage can attract more tourists, increasing business opportunities for cross-border workers operating in the tourism and catering sectors. ### Before/After Comparison Before the start of this project, many public properties in Lombardy were underutilized or abandoned, representing a cost for local communities. With the restoration and refurbishment of these properties, new spaces for cultural events, exhibitions, and community activities are created, which can attract tourists and residents, stimulating the local economy. This can lead to an increase in job opportunities for cross-border workers, especially in sectors such as tourism, catering, and cultural services. ### Concrete Scenarios Imagine a cross-border worker employed in the catering sector in a Lombard city. Action: what to do concretely If you are a Lombard public entity interested in participating in the cultural heritage restoration tender, follow these steps: 1. Check the requirements: Ensure you are a Lombard public entity and have a project that fits the tender criteria. 2. Prepare the documentation: Gather all the necessary documents for the application, including detailed projects and cost estimates. 3. Submit the application: Complete the online procedure through the "Bands and Services" platform of Regione Lombardia by June 24, 2026. 4. Wait for evaluation: After submitting the application, wait for the evaluation by the tender commissioners. 5. Start the project: If your project is approved, begin the restoration and refurbishment work on the public property. 6. Use the support service: Take advantage of the support service offered by Fondazione Cariplo to define and implement the enhancement plans. ### Useful tools For further information and to submit the application, visit the "Bands and Services" platform of Regione Lombardia. Frequently Asked Questions
What is the maximum amount of funding per project?
The maximum amount of funding per project is 1 million euros. Lombardy Region will fund up to 50% of the project costs, with amounts ranging from a minimum of 500,000 euros to a maximum of 1 million euros.
What is the deadline for submitting applications?
The deadline for submitting applications is June 24, 2026. The procedure is exclusively online and must be completed through the Lombardy Region's “Bandi e Servizi” platform.
Who can participate in the call?
Lombard public entities can participate in the call, and they can submit their application in partnership with private entities. The procedure is exclusively online and must be completed by June 24, 2026.
