Pizza and drink prices soaring: Italy's 30 most expensive cities

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 30 April 2026

Osservatorio Prezzi e Tariffe data shows a significant rise in pizza and drink costs across Italy, with Bolzano topping the list.

Contesto

In brief - Bolzano is the most expensive city for pizza and drink - Palermo and Sassari follow with average prices above 14 euros - Reggio Calabria is the most economical with less than 10 euros ## Key facts - What: Average price of pizza and drink - When: Data updated to 2026 - Where: Italy - Who: Price and Tariff Observatory of the Ministry of Enterprises and Made in Italy - Amount: From 9 to 28 euros depending on the city and the venue The invitation to eat a pizza is becoming increasingly costly. Once a symbol of conviviality and accessibility, today the ritual of pizza and drink is beginning to crack, especially for families. The updated data from the Price and Tariff Observatory of the Ministry of Enterprises and Made in Italy, according to Altroconsumo, precisely captures this change, showing not only widespread increases but also a deeply unequal Italy, where in the same city a pizza with a drink can cost up to three times as much depending on the venue chosen. Among the thirty cities that Altroconsumo has selected for their representativeness and the completeness of the data, allowing for comparisons over the years, the most expensive are neither Milan nor Venice. At the top, we find Bolzano (on average 15 euros for pizza and drink), Palermo and Sassari (both just over 14.50 euros). Trento and Florence confirm their place in the high range. While Rome (11.45 euros) remains a gastronomic capital that is relatively accessible. ### Price variability The average cost, however, hides what is perhaps more interesting, i.e., the price variability between one pizzeria and another in the same city.
