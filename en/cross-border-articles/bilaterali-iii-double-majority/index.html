In brief - Bilaterals III may require double majority - States Commission proposes constitutional amendment - Contradiction between free movement and autonomous immigration management ## Key facts - What: Incorporation of Bilaterals III into the Federal Constitution - When: Parliamentary initiative deposited on Tuesday - Where: Switzerland - Who: Commission of Political Institutions of the States - Amount: Not specified The Commission of Political Institutions of the States has presented a parliamentary initiative requesting the incorporation of the Bilaterals III package into the Swiss Federal Constitution. This step would imply the need to obtain a double majority, both of the people and the Cantons, for the approval of the agreements. The proposal aims to eliminate uncertainties regarding the constitutionality of the amendment to the agreement on the free movement of persons. The commission highlighted a contradiction between the expansion of the free movement of persons and Article 121a of the Federal Constitution, which concerns the autonomous management of immigration. The new transitional provision would authorize the approval of the package despite the constitutional norms on the limitation of immigration. Furthermore, the commission noted that this amendment would put an end to the discussion on the need for a double majority, following the opinion of a constitutional law professor heard at the end of March. ### Implications for cross-border workers The incorporation of the Bilaterals III package into the Federal Constitution could have important implications for cross-border workers who work in Switzerland.
