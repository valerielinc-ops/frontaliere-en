Gambarogno Trail: New Interreg Project in Varese (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 31 May 2026

The study of feasibility for the Interreg project to create a cross-border trail connecting Gambarogno to Lombardy has been presented in Varese.

Contesto

In brief The feasibility study for the Maggiore Lake Trail project was presented in Varese. The project, part of the Interreg program, aims to create a structured connection that unites the shores and interior of Lake Maggiore, involving Gambarogno in a path that transcends geographical and administrative barriers between Switzerland and Italy. The mayor of Gambarogno, who participated in the event, emphasized the historical unity of the territory, regardless of national borders. The presentation in Varese underscores the commitment of local authorities to promote transnational collaboration that values the landscape and tourist attractions of the area. ### Focus on transnational collaboration The integration between the Canton Ticino and the Italian border regions is the driving force behind this proposal. It is not just about infrastructure, but about a strategic vision that aims to strengthen the bond between local communities through shared infrastructure. Participation in an Interreg project highlights the importance of European and Swiss funds for cooperation projects that facilitate joint development. For cross-border workers and residents, the development of walking paths and slow mobility routes represents an opportunity to experience the territory in a new way, reinforcing that common identity that the mayor of Della Santa emphasized during the event. The focus is now on the implementation phase, where cooperation between Gambarogno institutions and Italian partners will be crucial for the success of the trail.

Dettagli operativi

Implications for local mobility The realization of the 'Maggiore Lake Trail' entails significant reflections for those who cross the border daily. Although the project focuses on enhancing paths, the positive impact on the livability of the Gambarogno-Varese area is evident. For cross-border commuters working in the Canton of Ticino, the creation of safe and integrated routes can become a resource for soft mobility, reducing dependence on major border crossings like Brogeda or Ponte Tresa for non-work-related travel. Enhancing the territory through borderless paths also meets a growing demand for quality of life from those residing in border areas. Compared to the past, when routes were often fragmented, the new feasibility study proposes a unified vision that promotes the fluidity of cross-border travel. Territorial cooperation is not just an abstract concept but translates into more efficient management of the landscape resources that link Gambarogno and the neighboring areas in Italy. ### Comparison of development scenarios Before this initiative, territorial planning tended to stop at the customs border. With the advent of more structured Interreg projects, a paradigm shift is being observed: the territory is becoming a single development entity. This vision allows for the coordinated planning of services, maintenance, and tourism promotion, avoiding waste and maximizing the attractiveness of the entire Lake Maggiore region. For the worker who lives in Italy and works in Switzerland, this means having access to a better-maintained environment, with services that overcome administrative fragmentation. Frequently Asked Questions
What does the feasibility study for the Maggiore Lake Trail entail?
The study, presented in Varese, outlines the guidelines for creating a transnational trail that connects Gambarogno to Lombardy. The goal is to promote territorial integration and mobility without borders, valorizing the common landscape resources between Switzerland and Italy through the Interreg program.
What role does Gambarogno play in the project?
Gambarogno is a key player in the project. The mayor of Gambarogno, Della Santa, emphasized the importance of this territorial connection, highlighting that the territory unites communities beyond any administrative border, making the Ticinese municipality a fundamental pillar for the success of the transnational trail.
How can I monitor the project's progress?
It is recommended to follow the official channels of the Gambarogno authorities and the Interreg partners. The operational phases will require coordination between the technical offices of Switzerland and Italy; participating in public consultations is the main way to stay informed and have your voice heard.
