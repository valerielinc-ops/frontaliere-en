Revitalization of the Ticino River: Projects between Airolo and Rodi (cross-border guide)

Present the works of revitalization and hydraulic system improvement of the Ticino River in Alta Leventina, with an investment of 8 million CHF.

The event was attended by the State Councillor Norman Gobbi, the president of CMAL Valerio Jelmini, the director of Ritom SA Lucas Dürr, and the head of the Watercourses Office of the DT Laurent Filippini, along with numerous guests and citizens. ### The project and its objectives The project was initiated in 2015, in parallel with the renewal of the concession for the use of water at Ritom, which included the adoption of compensatory measures for the environment. In this context, the DT proposed to extend the works of river systemization over the entire stretch between Airolo and Rodi, assigning the responsibility and coordination to the CMAL, which also integrated protective measures against floods. The works, divided into the sections Airolo–Stalvedro, Piotta, and Audan–Varenzo–Rodi, were aimed at two main objectives: on one hand, the maintenance and adaptation of the banks to increase the safety of the habitable areas, and on the other hand, the restoration of the river's original space, with particular attention to the naturalistic and landscape...

What to Expect in the Next Years The works began in 2023 and by the end of 2025, the civil engineering interventions were tested. Currently, the final forestry and finishing works are ongoing, with completion expected by the end of 2026. Over the next five years, further management of the green areas will be necessary, particularly for the maintenance of the plantations and the control of invasive species. ### Future Maintenance and Management The total cost of the project is approximately CHF 8 million, funded by the Canton, Confederation, CMAL, and Ritom SA according to a pre-established cost-sharing key. The future management of the project will be entrusted to CMAL, which will oversee regular and extraordinary maintenance, ensuring the sustainability of the implemented interventions. ## Useful Tools for Cross-Border Workers For cross-border workers living or working in the area affected by the works, it is important to stay informed about the changes and opportunities offered by the project. The thematic trail 'Lungo il fiume' offers an opportunity to discover the beauty of the landscape and the interventions carried out. Furthermore, the river's hydraulic system contributes to protecting the habitable areas, increasing the safety for residents and local commercial activities. Frequently Asked Questions
What is the main goal of the Ticino river revitalization works?
The main goals of the Ticino river revitalization works are to maintain and adapt the riverbanks to increase the safety of the areas under construction, and to restore part of the river's original space, with a particular focus on naturalistic and landscape aspects, and to improve the aquatic habitat.
Who has funded the Ticino river revitalization project?
The Ticino river revitalization project has been funded by the Canton, Confederation, CMAL, and Ritom SA according to a pre-established sharing key, with a total cost of 8 million francs.
What is the role of the thematic trail 'Lungo il fiume'?
The thematic trail 'Lungo il fiume' was created to promote knowledge of the project and to raise awareness among the population. The route is enriched by eleven informative posts that illustrate the interventions carried out and their significance in relation to the natural development of the river and its interactions with the forest and aquatic fauna.
