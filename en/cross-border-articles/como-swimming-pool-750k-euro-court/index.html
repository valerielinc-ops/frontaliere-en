Como Swimming, 750k euros in court: minority report (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 5 May 2026

Minority councilors of the Como municipality have filed a report with the Prosecutor's Office for the management of 750,000 euros for the Geno Avenue swimming pool.

Contesto

Briefly - 750,000 euro allocation for Como Nuoto - Minority exposes to the Prosecutor's Office - Dispute over maintenance and funding ## Key Facts - What: Municipal allocation for swimming pool renovation - When: May 2021 (delivery), December 2022 (resolution) - Where: Como, Italy - Who: Como Municipality, ASD Como Nuoto - Amount: 750,000 euros (250,000 regional, 500,000 municipal) The political battle over the swimming pool in viale Geno in Como has reached the courts. Minority councilors of the Municipality have filed a detailed expose with the Prosecutor's Office, asking for clarifications on the management of the concession to ASD Como Nuoto. At the center of the report is a public funding of 750,000 euros for renovation work, which the signatories believe the Municipality should not have allocated. The amount is divided into 250,000 euros of regional contributions and 500,000 euros of municipal funds. ### The signing councilors The councilors involved are Lorenzo Cantaluppi (FdI), Alessandro Falanga (FI, then resigned), Stefano Fanetti (Pd), Eleonora Galli (Pd), Stefano Legnani (Pd), Patrizia Lissi (Pd), Barbara Minghetti (Svolta Civica), Giordano Molteni (Fi), Elena Negretti (Lega), Vittorio Nessi (Svolta Civica), Paola Tocchetti (Fi), Antonio Tufano (FdI), Luca Vozella (Svolta Civica). The signatures of the current councilors Davide Gervasoni (Fi) and Paola Ceriello, who joined Forza Italia after the drafting of the expose, do not appear. ### The story It all began in May 2021, when the pool was handed over to Como Nuoto in the state in which it was found, with the clause 'as is'.
