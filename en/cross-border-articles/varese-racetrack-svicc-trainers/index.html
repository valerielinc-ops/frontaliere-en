Svicc evicts trainers, closes Varese stables

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 2 May 2026

Svicc evicts trainers from Varese stables within 60 days. Demolition and renovation project underway.

Contesto

In brief - Svicc evicts trainers within 60 days - Contracts valid until 2033 according to trainers - Demolition and renovation project ## Key facts - What: Eviction of stable trainers - When: Communication of April 16, 2026 - Where: Stables on via Galdino, Varese - Who: Società Varesina Incremento Corse Cavalli (Svicc) - Amount: Between 150 and 200 thoroughbreds in the stables Svicc has notified the trainers of the stables on via Galdino in Varese of the termination of the lease contracts and the obligation to leave the boxes within 60 days. The communication of April 16 came after years of tension between the company and the trainers. Svicc has contested alleged contractual breaches, including the failure to deliver insurance policies and documentation on the horses. The company also communicated the refusal to renew the contracts expiring in 2027, justifying it with a demolition and renovation project of the entire via Galdino complex. According to Svicc, this plan makes the presence of the trainers in the stables incompatible. ### Trainers' response The trainers, through Grizzetti Galoppo, have fully contested Svicc's reconstruction. According to the defense, the contract would still be valid and already renewed until 2033. The alleged violations have been defined as 'generic and unproven', while the cessation of the hippodrome's concession would not justify the termination of the relationship. The defense has invited the company to refrain from initiatives that could compromise the activity. Dettagli operativi

Implications for Varesina Horse Racing The conflict between Svicc and the trainers of the stables on via Galdino has significant implications for Varesina horse racing. Without the facilities of the stables, the training activity risks coming to a halt, with direct consequences on the well-being of the horses and the economic activities linked to the racecourse. Over the past fifteen years, the stables on via Galdino have been the scene of a recurring clash, often conducted with controversial methods. The cutting off of utilities, water, light, and gas, has been repeatedly evoked as a tool of pressure by the owners to prompt the payment of overdue box fees. The trainers have long denounced the conditions of a structure now deemed dilapidated and in need of extraordinary maintenance interventions. ### Future Scenarios If Svicc were to prevail in the legal dispute, the project to demolish and restructure the entire via Galdino complex could lead to an overall redevelopment of the area. However, this scenario would entail the temporary suspension of training activities and could have a negative impact on the local horse racing sector. On the other hand, if the trainers manage to defend the validity of their contracts, the situation could stabilize, allowing the continuation of training activities and the reopening of the racecourse. However, the issue of the condition of the stables and the need for extraordinary maintenance interventions would remain open. ### The Role of the Municipality and the New Management The Municipality of Varese, owner of the Bettole racecourse, has entrusted the new management of the facility to Varese Turf & Sport.
