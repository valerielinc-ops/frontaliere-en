Swiss fuel prices hit annual high: +13% in 3 months

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 29 April 2026

Fuel prices in Switzerland have reached an annual high of 1.90 CHF per litre. Diesel is at 2.17 CHF per litre.

Contesto

In brief - Petrol at 1.90 CHF/litre, annual record - Diesel at 2.17 CHF/litre, +20% in 3 months - Brent oil at 112 USD/barrel ## Key facts - What: Petrol and diesel prices - When: 29 April 2026 - Where: Switzerland - Who: Touring Club Svizzero (TCS) - Amount: 1.90 CHF/litre (petrol), 2.17 CHF/litre (diesel) The price of petrol in Switzerland has reached the annual record of 1.90 francs per litre, according to data from the Touring Club Svizzero (TCS). This increase is mainly due to the war in Iran and the consequent rise in oil prices. TCS spokesman Marco Wölfli specified that at the beginning of the week, fuel prices increased by about 5 centimes. The average price for a litre of unleaded 95 petrol is now 1.90 francs, that of unleaded 98 is 2.01 francs and that of diesel is 2.17 francs. Before the outbreak of the war in Iran, at the end of February, prices were still at 1.67 francs for unleaded 95, 1.78 francs for unleaded 98 and 1.79 francs for diesel. This represents an increase of 13% for petrol and 20% for diesel. The price of a barrel of Brent oil was around 112 dollars, while five days ago it cost 9 dollars less. Following the conflict and the consequent blockade of the Strait of Hormuz, the price rose from around 70 dollars to temporarily reach 120 dollars. ### Implications for cross-border workers The increase in fuel prices has a significant impact on cross-border workers who cross the border every day to go to work. Practical Analysis The increase in fuel prices in Switzerland has a significant impact on cross-border workers who cross the border every day to go to work. For many, the cost of fuel represents an important expense, especially for those who travel long distances. The increase in the price of diesel, in particular, could influence transport costs for goods and passengers, with possible repercussions on the prices of consumer goods. ### Comparison with Previous Prices A comparison with the prices at the end of February shows a significant increase. Unleaded gasoline 95 has gone from 1.67 francs to 1.90 francs, an increase of 13.8%. Unleaded gasoline 98 has increased from 1.78 francs to 2.01 francs, an increase of 12.9%. Diesel has recorded the most substantial increase, going from 1.79 francs to 2.17 francs, an increase of 20.7%. These increases are due mainly to the geopolitical situation and the consequent rise in the price of oil. ### Future Forecasts The TCS publishes at irregular intervals estimates of the current prices of gasoline and diesel in Switzerland based on information from various sources and random samples. However, there may be variations depending on the region and the service station. The greater increase in the price of diesel is explained by the greater demand from industry. If the geopolitical situation were to worsen further, it is possible that prices will continue to rise, with further repercussions on consumers and the economy. ### Tips for Cross-Border Workers For cross-border workers who use the car to go to work, it is advisable to monitor fuel prices and plan any additional expenses.
