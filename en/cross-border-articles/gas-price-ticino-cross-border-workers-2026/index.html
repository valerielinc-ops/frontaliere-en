Why cross-border workers pay more for fuel in Ticino (cross-border guide)

Fuel prices in Ticino remain high, with differences of over 20 cents per litre between different areas. Here's what changes for cross-border workers

Contesto

In brief - Fuel prices in Ticino range from 1.80 to 2.20 CHF/litre - Price differences of over 20 cents between areas - High prices since the start of 2024 - Competition among service stations ## Key facts - What: Fuel prices in Ticino - When: Since the start of 2024 - Where: Ticino, Switzerland - Who: TCS, motorists, service stations - Amount: 1.80-2.20 CHF/litre Fuel prices in Ticino remain high, despite some fluctuations. According to TCS, it is unlikely that fuel costs will change in the short term. The situation is particularly evident in the area between Magliaso and the border, where the lowest fuel and diesel prices are recorded. At some unstaffed stations, 95-octane petrol costs 1.80 CHF per litre, while diesel is at 2.08 CHF. These prices encourage many motorists to fill up in these areas. In the rest of the canton, prices range between 1.80 and 1.87 CHF, still high compared to average prices before the start of the war in Iran. ### Price differences between areas Price differences are significant even within the canton. For example, in Rivera, prices exceed 2 CHF for 95-octane petrol and 2.20 CHF for diesel. According to Laurent Pignot, TCS spokesperson, these differences are due to various factors, including the location of the service station, the presence of a shop, available staff, and nearby competition. To help motorists find the best prices, TCS has launched a fuel price radar, which allows savings of several cents per litre with little distance. However, TCS emphasizes that a detour is only worthwhile for a fuel fill-up of 50 litres or more. ### Impact on cross-border workers Cross-border workers who commute to Ticino and live in Italy are particularly affected by these fuel price increases. Analysis of Implications The rise in gasoline prices in Ticino has a significant impact on cross-border workers who commute to Switzerland and reside in Italy. These workers face additional transportation costs, which can significantly affect their family budget. Furthermore, cross-border workers already face high costs for health insurance and other expenses related to cross-border work. The increase in gasoline prices can further exacerbate the financial situation of these workers. ### Comparison with the Previous Situation Before the start of the war in Iran, gasoline prices in Switzerland were significantly lower. At the beginning of the year, gasoline 95 cost an average of 1.65 CHF per liter. However, with the start of the fighting, the price quickly rose, reaching 1.79 CHF in March and 1.90 CHF by the end of April. A similar trend was recorded for diesel. Despite a slight drop last week, prices remain high. ### Future Scenarios It is likely that gasoline prices will remain high for some time. This can have a significant impact on cross-border workers who face additional transportation costs. However, the Federal Council has assessed the risk of a general macroeconomic recession as low. This means that, despite the high cost of fuel, the Swiss economy should remain stable. ### Adaptation Strategies For cross-border workers, it is important to adopt adaptation strategies to cope with the increase in fuel costs. Filling up at stations with the lowest prices, using the TCS price radar, and planning trips efficiently can help reduce expenses. By using this tool, cross-border workers can save several cents per liter. For example, cross-border workers can plan their trips to fill up at stations with the lowest prices. Additionally, they can try to reduce the number of unnecessary trips and use alternative means of transport when possible. ### Monitor price forecasts Monitoring price forecasts can help cross-border workers predict fuel costs and plan accordingly. The TCS provides regular updates on gasoline and diesel prices, which can be useful for cross-border workers. ### Use alternative means of transport In some cases, cross-border workers can consider using alternative means of transport to reduce fuel costs. This can help reduce fuel costs and improve quality of life. ### Request tax benefits Cross-border workers can also consider requesting tax benefits for fuel costs. Frequently Asked Questions
Why are gasoline prices in Ticino so high?
Gasoline prices in Ticino are high due to various factors, including the current geopolitical situation and competition among service stations. Additionally, prices vary significantly across different areas of the canton, with differences of over 20 cents per liter.
How can I find the service stations with the lowest prices?
The TCS has launched a fuel price radar that allows you to find the service stations with the lowest prices. By using this tool, you can save several cents per liter. However, it's important to also consider the additional time and distance that might be required to reach these stations.
What can I do to reduce fuel costs?
To reduce fuel costs, you can adopt various strategies, such as filling up at stations with the lowest prices, using the TCS price radar, planning trips efficiently, and monitoring price forecasts.
