Family Bonus 2026: Benefits for Cross-Border Workers (cross-border guide)

Discover all the bonuses and benefits for cross-border worker families in 2026: family allowances, tax deductions, and new measures.

Contesto

TL;DR - Family benefits for cross-border workers in Ticino in 2026 - Daycare bonus covers up to 40% of expenses - Tax deductions for dependent children: €950 per child ## Key facts - Family Allowances: 3,600-4,200 CHF per child annually, depending on age and employment situation - Daycare Bonus: Up to 40% of expenses for accredited daycare facilities - Tax Deductions: €950 per child for cross-border workers in 2026 - Application Deadline: March 31, 2026 for family allowances - Tax Return Submission: Between May and June 2026 in Italy - Daycare Requirements: Family residence in Italy and child enrolled in accredited daycare in Ticino or Lombardy - Regulatory Application: Valid for cross-border workers with a G permit In 2026, cross-border workers employed in Ticino will be able to take advantage of a range of family benefits designed to support those living across the border but actively contributing to the Swiss economy. Among the main measures in place are cantonal family allowances, daycare bonuses, and tax deductions related to children. ## Family Allowances Family allowances in Ticino include annual amounts ranging from 3,600 CHF to 4,200 CHF per child, depending on the child's age and the parent's employment situation. This benefit is also accessible to cross-border workers holding a G permit, provided the family resides in Italy and is certified by the Cantonal Compensation Office. ## Daycare Bonus A significant new measure for 2026 is the introduction of a contribution to daycare costs for cross-border worker families. According to the Department of Finance and Economy (DFE), the bonus will cover up to 40% of expenses for accredited daycare facilities. Dettagli operativi

The fiscal implications of these bonuses are significant for cross-border workers, especially considering the additional IRPEF tax in border municipalities. In 2026, the new tax agreement between Italy and Switzerland will provide greater transparency in managing deductions for dependent children, with a system of direct tax credits applicable to Italian income tax returns. ## Tax Deductions for Dependent Children For cross-border workers with children, the tax deduction for 2026 is set at €950 per child, an increase compared to previous years. This benefit can be claimed during the income tax declaration process by using the CU certificate provided by the Swiss employer. ⚠️ Important Deadlines: - Family allowance application: by March 31, 2026 - Submission of income tax returns in Italy: between May and June 2026 ## Daycare Bonus Requirements - Family residence in Italy - Child enrolled in accredited daycare facilities in Ticino or Lombardy - Certified annual expense documentation 💡 Practical Tip: Make sure to gather all necessary documents in time to avoid delays. Frequently Asked Questions
What are the family benefits for frontier workers in Ticino in 2026?
Family allowances in Ticino include annual amounts ranging from 3,600 CHF to 4,200 CHF per child depending on age and working situation. In addition, a contribution to the costs of nurseries and tax deductions related to children has been increased to €950 per child.
What is the procedure to request the nest bonus for frontiers in Ticino in 2026?
To request the nest bonus, the frontiers must present the documentation of the annual expenses certified, the inscription of the child at kindergartens accredited in Ticino or Lombardy, and the confirmation of family residence in Italy, within the deadline indicated by the cantonal counter.
What are the procedures for obtaining tax deductions for children in Italy as a frontier in 2026?
To obtain tax deductions, frontier workers must attach the CU issued by the Swiss employer in the tax return in Italy and comply with the deadlines for submission, usually between May and June 2026.
