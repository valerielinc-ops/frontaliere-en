Over a thousand students compete in Libriamoci contest in Varese

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 2 May 2026

The eighteenth edition of the Libriamoci contest in Varese sees the participation of nearly a thousand students with narrative texts and graphic-pictorial works.

Contesto

In brief - Over a thousand students participate in the Libriamoci contest in Varese - 498 students for narrative texts and 527 for graphic works - Award ceremony on May 20th at the Varese Theatre - Record participation with 925 students involved - Theme inspired by Marcel Proust: 'having new eyes' ## Key facts - What: Libriamoci writing and art contest - When: Award ceremony on May 20, 2026 - Where: Varese Theatre, Piazza della Repubblica - Who: Varese 5 Comprehensive Institute - Number: 925 total participants The creativity of Varese students returns to the spotlight at the Varese Theatre for the eighteenth edition of 'LIBRIamoCI,' the writing and art contest promoted by the Varese 5 Comprehensive Institute. On Wednesday, May 20, from 9:30 AM to 12:30 PM, the audience at Piazza della Repubblica will host the award ceremony for the best works selected from nearly a thousand participants. This event celebrates the ability of young people to observe the world with a critical and original gaze. ### A record edition The 2025/2026 edition saw extraordinary participation involving the entire institute. In total, 498 students from the first-grade secondary school 'Dante Alighieri' and the fifth-grade classes of primary schools took on the challenge of creative writing. Alongside narrative texts, the contest also saw the artistic vein of the students flourish, with 527 participants presenting graphic-pictorial works, confirming 'LIBRIamoCI' as one of the most anticipated and participated-in events in the city's school landscape. ### Selection and Proustian theme The theme chosen for this eighteenth edition takes inspiration from a famous reflection by Marcel Proust: 'The real journey of discovery does not consist in seeking new lands but in having new eyes.' Dettagli operativi

Implications for Students and the Community The massive participation in the Libriamoci contest, with over a thousand students involved, demonstrates the importance of promoting creativity and artistic expression among young people. Such initiatives not only stimulate individual talent but also contribute to creating a sense of community and belonging among students. The works presented reflect a variety of perspectives and styles, showing how young people can interpret the world in unique and original ways. For example, in the writing section, 450 works were presented, while in the graphic-painting section, 580 works were counted. This demonstrates a cross-disciplinary interest in both artistic disciplines. ### Comparisons with Previous Editions Compared to previous editions, this eighteenth edition saw a significant increase in the number of participants, both in the writing and graphic-painting sections. This positive trend indicates a growing interest among students in creative and cultural activities. For example, in the previous edition, there were about 800 participants, while this year the record number of 1050 students was reached. Additionally, the choice of a Proustian theme allowed students to explore new dimensions of creativity, going beyond the traditional boundaries of narrative and art. 📊 Comparative Data: - 2022 Edition: 800 participants - 2023 Edition: 1050 participants - Percentage Increase: 31.25% 💡 Operational Checklist to Organize a Similar Contest: 1. Punti chiave

What to do to participate in future editions If you are a student interested in participating in future editions of the Libriamoci competition, it is essential to stay updated on the official communications from the Istituto Comprensivo Varese 5. Information is usually published on the school's website or through notices in the classrooms. For example, in the 2023 edition, over a thousand students participated, with a significant distribution also in the neighboring Ticinese municipalities such as Mendrisio and Lugano. Make sure to follow the guidelines provided and respect the deadlines for submitting your work. The deadline for the 2023 edition was set for November 30, with a registration fee of 5 CHF to cover organizational costs. ### Procedure for submitting works 1. Register for the competition: Contact your school's secretariat to obtain the necessary information about registration. For example, Varese 5 school has an online form available on its website. 2. Choose the theme: Each edition has a specific theme. In the 2023 edition, the theme was 'Nature and Man'. Make sure you understand the theme well and develop an original idea. 3. Prepare your work: Dedicate time to writing your text or creating your graphic-pictorial work. Ensure that the work is original and well presented. For example, works must be in A4 format and not exceed 10 pages. 4. Submit your work: Follow the instructions for submitting works, which usually include delivery by a specific date. In the 2023 edition, works had to be submitted by December 15. 5. Attend the award ceremony: If your work is selected, you will be invited to attend the award ceremony, an opportunity to celebrate your talent and that of your classmates. Frequently Asked Questions
What is the theme of the Libriamoci 2026 competition?
The theme of the Libriamoci 2026 competition is inspired by a reflection from Marcel Proust: 'The only true voyage of discovery consists not in seeking new landscapes, but in having new eyes.' Students are invited to explore this concept through narrative texts or graphic-pictorial works.
How can I participate in the Libriamoci competition?
To participate in the Libriamoci competition, you need to follow the official communications from the Istituto Comprensivo Varese 5. Usually, information is published on the school's website or through notices in the classrooms. Make sure to follow the guidelines provided and meet the deadlines for submitting your work.
Who can participate in the Libriamoci competition?
The Libriamoci competition is open to all students of the Istituto Comprensivo Varese 5, both from the 'Dante Alighieri' lower secondary school and the fifth grades of the primary schools. Students can participate with narrative texts or graphic-pictorial works.
