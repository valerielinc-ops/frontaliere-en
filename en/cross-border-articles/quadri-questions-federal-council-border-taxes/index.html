Quadri challenges federal council on borders and truck taxes

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 28 April 2026

Lorenzo Quadri (Lega dei Ticinesi) presents an interpellation on the new NMTS system for heavy traffic tax.

Contesto

In brief - Quadri criticizes the new NMTS system for the heavy traffic tax. - Drivers' self-declarations without effective immediate checks. - Doubts about border security and possible tax losses. ## Key facts - What: Interpellation on the NMTS system for the heavy traffic tax. - When: April 28, 2026. - Where: Bern. - Who: Lorenzo Quadri (Lega dei Ticinesi). - Amount: Not specified. National Councilor Lorenzo Quadri (Lega dei Ticinesi) has submitted an interpellation to the Federal Council, questioning the effectiveness of the new NMTS system for managing the performance-related heavy traffic tax (TTPCP). The system, introduced in recent years, has replaced the previous model based on customs records and cross-checking of vehicle data. According to Quadri, the new system has significant shortcomings, including reliance on self-declarations by drivers or transport companies, often made through digital applications. This method, according to the interpellation, is not accompanied by sufficiently effective immediate control tools. ### Criticities of the new system Quadri raises the concrete risk of inaccurate or fraudulent declarations, with possible repercussions on tax revenue and the fairness of the system. The new system also allows data to be modified even after exiting Swiss territory, making the verification work more complex for the authorities. Among the points raised is also the reduction of the operational competencies of the customs authorities. The councilor asks to know the reasons for this choice and whether the Government intends to review it, in light of the reported shortcomings. Practical Analysis: Implications for Cross-Border Workers The new NMTS system could have significant repercussions for cross-border workers in the heavy transport sector. The lack of immediate checks and the possibility of modifying data after leaving Swiss territory could lead to inaccuracies in declarations, with consequent tax impacts. Furthermore, the reduction of operational competencies of customs authorities could affect border security, a crucial aspect for cross-border workers who cross the borders daily. ### Concrete Scenarios If the Federal Council does not intervene to improve the NMTS system, tax losses and an increase in fraudulent declarations could occur. This could lead to ineffective management of the heavy traffic tax, with negative repercussions for both public finances and the equity of the system. Additionally, the lack of adequate checks could compromise border security, increasing the risk of illicit trafficking such as drugs or illegal immigration. ### Comparison with the Previous System The previous system, based on customs registrations and cross-checking of vehicle data, was considered more reliable and less prone to fraud. The transition to the new digital system has introduced new criticalities, including dependence on self-declarations and the lack of immediate checks. This comparison highlights the importance of an effective control system to ensure the reliability and equity of the heavy traffic tax. ### Concrete Procedures For cross-border workers in the heavy transport sector, it is important to be aware of the criticalities of the new NMTS system.
