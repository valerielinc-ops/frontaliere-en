Crans-Montana: aid services handling 700 cases

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 30 April 2026

Four months after the tragedy, victim assistance services manage nearly 700 cases, with 400 in Valais alone and 20 in Ticino.

Contesto

In brief - 700 cases handled by counseling services after the Crans-Montana tragedy - 400 cases in Valais, 20 in Ticino - Immediate aid already disbursed since January 9th ## Key facts - What: Management of cases for victims and families - When: From January 2026 - Where: Valais and Ticino - Who: Victim assistance services (LAV) - Amount: 700 total cases, 400 in Valais, 20 in Ticino Four months after the Crans-Montana tragedy, victim assistance services, operating under the federal law on victim assistance (LAV), continue to be in high demand. Currently, these centers, which offer free counseling on rights, legal and financial aid, are managing nearly 700 cases. Of these, 400 are concentrated in Valais alone, while about twenty cases have been handled in Ticino. Internationally, several Australian, Belgian, Italian, and French families have inquired about constituting themselves as civil parties and understanding how proceedings work in Switzerland. Sonia Golay, head of these counseling services in Valais, reported that many questions also concern transportation and accommodation costs. Roland Favre, head of the Valais Canton's Social Services Coordination Office, specified that these counseling services, already at the limit of their capacity, have received additional resources from the Valais government. Staff has increased from 10 to 15.4 full-time equivalents, allowing the hiring of more counselors, administrative staff, and a full-time legal expert. Collaborators have agreed to increase their working hours. The first disbursements of immediate aid to victims were made as of January 9th, as they were urgent for part of the victims. • ## Practical Implications Analysis The management of nearly 700 cases by the Crans-Montana counseling services highlights the significant impact of the tragedy and the need for ongoing support for victims and their families. The counseling services, operating under the federal law on victim assistance (LAV), offer free support in various areas, including rights, legal and financial aid. ### International Support The requests for information from foreign families, including Australians, Belgians, Italians, and French, underscore the importance of cross-border support. These families are seeking to constitute themselves as civil parties and understand the legal proceedings in Switzerland. This aspect is particularly relevant for cross-border workers who might find themselves in similar situations and require legal and financial assistance. ### Additional Resources and Assistance The counseling services, already at the limit of their capacity, have received additional resources from the Vaud Government. This has allowed for an increase in personnel, including counselors, administrative staff, and a full-time legal expert. The collaborators have agreed to increase their working hours to cope with the situation. ### Immediate Aid and Long-Term Assistance The first payments of immediate aid to victims were made starting January 9th, as they were urgent for part of the victims. The law provides that this aid is granted regardless of the financial capabilities of the beneficiaries. The immediate aid scheme concluded at the end of March and was replaced by longer-term assistance, except for victims still hospitalized.
