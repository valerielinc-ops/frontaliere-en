Swiss flight evacuated: passengers refuse to leave carry-on

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 28 April 2026

An Airbus A330 of Swiss was evacuated in New Delhi after an aborted takeoff. Many passengers did not follow safety instructions.

Contesto

In brief - Swiss flight evacuated in New Delhi - Passengers did not leave carry-on baggage - EASA: dangerous behavior ## Key facts - What: Evacuation of a Swiss Airbus A330 - When: Saturday evening, April 26, 2026 - Where: New Delhi Airport, India - Who: 228 passengers, 13 crew members - Amount: 4 injured An Airbus A330 operated by Swiss, carrying 228 passengers and 13 crew members, had to abort its takeoff due to an engine malfunction. The aircraft, traveling at approximately 190 km/h, was evacuated as a precaution. Images of the evacuation have caught the attention of aviation safety specialists, as many passengers left the aircraft with their carry-on baggage, despite safety instructions. ### Compromised air safety According to the Tages-Anzeiger, the time lost retrieving personal belongings from compartments ranges between 8 and 15 seconds, a critical duration as international regulations require a complete evacuation in less than 90 seconds. Bags and suitcases can obstruct other passengers, block access points, slow down evacuation via inflatable slides, or even damage them. The European Aviation Safety Agency (EASA) emphasized that while a person's life takes precedence over their personal effects, some passengers' desire to carry their bags increases the danger for everyone. In a statement released on Monday, Swiss noted that 'there is still a need for information' on this topic and that they are evaluating how to better raise public awareness. ### Crew behavior The company's spokesperson also justified the behavior of the cabin crew, who in some videos are seen shouting at passengers. 'They give very clear and firm instructions. This may seem unusual, as they are generally known for being calm and welcoming,' they stated. ### Consequences of the evacuation...

Frequently Asked Questions
What to do in case of an aircraft evacuation?
In case of evacuation, it is crucial to carefully listen to the crew's instructions, immediately leave your carry-on baggage, follow the directions to the nearest exit, and use the emergency slides only if necessary. Every second counts and delay can have serious consequences.
Why is it important to leave carry-on baggage during an evacuation?
Leaving carry-on baggage during an evacuation is essential for a quick and safe evacuation. Bags and suitcases can obstruct other passengers, block access points, slow down evacuation through inflatable slides, or even damage them. Every second counts and delay can have serious consequences.
What can airlines do to improve safety during evacuations?
Airlines can introduce more aggressive awareness campaigns to educate passengers about the importance of leaving carry-on baggage in case of emergency. Additionally, new technologies could be implemented to facilitate rapid evacuation, such as more effective alarm systems or improved emergency slides.
