Education Day 2026: schools and businesses meet

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 30 April 2026

The third edition of Confindustria Varese's Education Day will be held on May 5, 2026, at Ville Ponti with hundreds of students.

Contesto

In brief - Education Day 2026 on May 5th in Varese - Hundreds of students involved - Projects for all age groups - Collaborations with Ticino schools and local businesses ## Key facts - What: Third edition of Education Day - When: Tuesday, May 5th, 2026 - Where: Ville Ponti, Varese - Who: Confindustria Varese, local schools, and Ticino partners - Participants: Hundreds of students - Budget: Approximately 50,000 euros, funded by sponsors and public contributions The third edition of Confindustria Varese's Education Day will take place on Tuesday, May 5th, 2026, at the Ville Ponti Conference Center. These projects aim to guide students towards practical 'know-how' and to accompany them in choosing their higher education path, bringing them into close contact with local productive realities. Dettagli operativi

The Education Day by Confindustria Varese represents a unique opportunity for students to come into direct contact with the working world. Through projects like 'Eureka! It Works!' and 'Pmi Day', the younger generation can acquire practical skills and better orient themselves in the delicate transition from high school to university or the working world. In 2025, over 5,000 students participated in these events, a 20% increase compared to the previous year. ### Implications for cross-border commuters For cross-border workers who commute to Ticino and reside in Italy, events like Education Day can offer interesting insights for their children's education. Collaboration between schools and businesses can indeed open new learning and professional growth opportunities, even for young people living in border areas. For example, students residing in Mendrisio or Lugano can participate in training projects in collaboration with Varese-based companies, benefiting from internships and apprenticeships that offer concrete experiences. 📊 Operational checklist for cross-border commuters: - Verify the requirements for participation in cross-border projects. - Contact local schools for information on collaborations with Ticino-based companies. - Explore internship and apprenticeship opportunities in Swiss companies. - Use current regulations to facilitate labor mobility. ### Comparison with the previous situation Compared to previous editions, the 2026 Education Day stands out for the increased participation of students of different age groups and the variety of projects presented. This demonstrates a growing interest from schools and businesses in promoting a business culture and innovation among young people. For example, a school in Lugano presented an electronic waste recycling project, securing a 10,000 CHF grant from a local company. Another project, developed by students in Mendrisio, focused on optimizing logistics processes, achieving an estimated 15% reduction in operational costs. ### Regulations and Dates Current regulations in Ticino allow schools to benefit from contributions of up to 5,000 CHF for projects in collaboration with local businesses. The deadline for project submissions is set for September 30, 2026. Frequently Asked Questions
What projects are presented during the Education Day?
During the Education Day, projects such as 'Eureka! It Works!', 'Pmi Day', 'Generazione d’Industria', and 'Latuaideadimpresa' will be presented. These projects involve students of different ages and aim to promote business culture and innovation.
How can I enroll my school in the Education Day?
To enroll your school in the Education Day, you need to contact Confindustria Varese directly to receive information about the projects and the enrollment procedures. The deadline for enrollment and specific dates may vary, so it is advisable to inquire promptly.
What are the benefits for students who participate in the Education Day?
Students who participate in the Education Day have the opportunity to acquire practical skills, get in touch with the working world, and receive feedback from industry experts. Additionally, they can present their own projects and receive recognition for their efforts.
