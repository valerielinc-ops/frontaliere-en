This initiative, promoted by a dialogue laboratory established in 2022, aims to assess whether territorial merging is the best path to meet the needs of the local population, which remains fragmented and lacks cohesion. The region, historically divided by cultural and administrative barriers, faces a fundamental question: join forces to ensure more effective public services, economic development, and sustainability, or preserve municipal autonomy, which guarantees decisions closer to citizens. The debate involves political representatives, interest groups, and residents, with a focus on specific targets: youth, seniors, businesses, and seasonal visitors.

Operational details

Six municipalities in the Locarno District are examining their future: merger or autonomy? To better understand the implications of this complex discussion, it’s useful to analyze two main merger scenarios: one urban, potentially involving Locarno and Minusio, and one peri-urban, which would also include municipalities like Tenero-Contra, Orselina, and San Nazzaro. The key difference between these models lies in scope and management of public services. Urban consolidation could lead to more coherent planning of infrastructure, transportation, and commercial development, boosting the district’s attractiveness. Already in 2021, Locarno invested around 20 million Swiss francs in public mobility projects, aiming to improve bus networks and cycling paths. Key points

Currently, seven municipalities in the Locarno area are engaged in a pivotal debate that could redefine their future: merge or maintain autonomy? This strategic decision affects residents of Domodossola, Ascona, Losone, Centovalli, Terre di Pedemonte, Ronco sopra Ascona, and the valleys of Verbano, who must carefully consider the implications of each option. For Locarno’s citizens, influence over decisions is already within reach: it’s recommended to closely follow developments through public meetings, online consultations, and official communications. For those seeking more insights, tools like salary calculators, tax comparison charts, and transportation maps are valuable resources to evaluate how choices could impact daily life. For example, comparing the municipal taxation rates of Ascona (average 75‰) and Losone (average 80‰) can help understand potential savings or increased costs under different scenarios.
