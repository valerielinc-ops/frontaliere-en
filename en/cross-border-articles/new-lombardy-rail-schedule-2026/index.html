Impact on Cross-Border Routes: What to Know Before June ### ### The Most Used Routes by Ticino Cross-Border Workers For cross-border workers living in Lombardy and working in Ticino, the most strategic rail routes connect Gallarate, Saronno, and Milan with Lugano, Bellinzona, and Locarno. These lines are operated by Trenord and Trenitalia in collaboration with the Swiss Federal Railways (SBB), but timetable programming is the responsibility of the Lombardy region. The adjustment on June 14, 2026, could therefore modify departure and arrival times, with possible repercussions on the punctuality of connections. ### ### Planned Disruptions and Infrastructure Works During the discussion table, Councillor Lucente highlighted that the coming months will be characterized by «numerous infrastructure interventions across the territory». These works could cause temporary inconveniences, such as partial route closures, reduced services, or replacement with substitute buses. For commuters crossing the border, it is essential to monitor official announcements from the Lombardy Region and Trenord to avoid last-minute surprises. ### ### Traffic Data: A Positive Trend The numbers provided by Trenord speak for themselves: between January and April 2026, 274,000 regional trains were registered in Lombardy with 75 million passengers on board. Compared to the same period in 2025, there was an increase of 9%, with peaks of 20% on public holidays. This positive trend suggests that more and more people are choosing the train as an alternative to private transport, also for cross-border journeys. Frequently Asked Questions
Which are the main railway stations in Lombardy for commuters traveling to Ticino?
The stations most frequently used by Ticino commuters are Gallarate, Saronno, and Milano Cadorna. Direct trains to Lugano, Bellinzona, and Locarno depart from these stations, operated by Trenord in collaboration with the Swiss Federal Railways (FFS).
How can I check if my usual route is affected by the new timetable?
Starting from mid-May 2026, consult the official Trenord website ([www.trenord.it](https://www.trenord.it)) or download the mobile app to view updated timetables and any route changes. You can also subscribe to Regione Lombardia’s newsletters to receive direct alerts.
What happens if there are scheduled service disruptions due to construction work during the new timetable?
Regione Lombardia has announced that the coming months will feature infrastructure works that may cause temporary disruptions. In such cases, Trenord will provide replacement bus services. Monitor official announcements on the Trenord and Regione Lombardia websites for advance notice of changes.
Can cross-border workers benefit from tax relief on public transport?
Yes. Cross-border workers residing in Italy and working in Switzerland can take advantage of refunds provided under the Italo-Swiss agreement, which include benefits for public transport. Check with your employer or the Italian National Institute for Social Security (INPS) to learn how to claim these benefits.
How can I report an issue with the new railway timetable?
You can report disruptions or service issues via the Trenord app, the official website [www.trenord.it](https://www.trenord.it), or by contacting commuter associations such as the Gallarate-Milano Commuter Committee or the Saronno TPL Node Travelers’ Committee. These groups are important advocates for commuter needs.
What alternatives exist if train travel is no longer convenient after June 14?
If the new timetable doesn’t meet your needs, consider alternative options such as carpooling with BlaBlaCar, combining train travel with replacement buses, or adjusting your working hours with your Swiss employer.
