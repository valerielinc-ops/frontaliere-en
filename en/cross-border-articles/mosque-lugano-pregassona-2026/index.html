New mosque in Lugano: questions and doubts

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 28 April 2026

Seven UDC city councilors submit an interrogation to the Lugano Municipality regarding the mosque project in Pregassona

Contesto

In brief - New mosque proposed in Pregassona, Lugano - Seven UDC councilors submit interrogation - Deadline for objections: May 4, 2026 ## Key facts - What: Project for a new mosque in Pregassona - When: Construction request published, deadline for objections May 4, 2026 - Where: Pregassona, Lugano - Who: Seven UDC municipal councilors, Lugano Municipality - Amount: Not yet specified A new mosque could be built in Pregassona, a district of Lugano. Seven UDC municipal councilors, led by Alain Bühler, have submitted an interrogation to the Municipality expressing various doubts and concerns. The project, signed by Samir Gomaa, president of the Muslim League in Ticino, includes a cultural center, a mosque, and six apartments on the upper floors. The construction request has already been published, and the deadline for any formal objections expires on May 4, 2026. The UDC councilors do not intend to question the freedom of religion, guaranteed by the Federal Constitution. However, they ask the Municipality to rigorously exercise its role as a guarantor towards the residents of the district and the community. Among the concerns raised is the risk of urban decay, already evident with an unfinished building in the district, a symbol of entrepreneurial failure. ### Doubts about the project The project is presented as a residential building with a cultural component.
