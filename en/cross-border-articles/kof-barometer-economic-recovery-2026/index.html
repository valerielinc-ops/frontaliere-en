Swiss economy recovering, but modest outlook

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 30 April 2026

The KOF barometer stands at 97.9 points in April, with mixed signals from various economic sectors.

Contesto

In brief - Swiss economy: slight recovery at 97.9 points - Manufacturing industry and services: improving - Catering and hospitality: negative signs ## Key facts - What: KOF Economic Barometer - When: April 2026 - Where: Switzerland - Who: KOF Institute of ETH Zurich - Amount: 97.9 points The Swiss economy shows timid signs of recovery, but it does not yet appear to be in full swing: this is the indication that emerges from the barometer of the KOF Institute, the business cycle research center of the Federal Polytechnic of Zurich. In April, the indicator stood at 97.9 points, 2.3 points more than the data - revised from 96.1 to 95.6 - of March, which had been the lowest since mid-2025, reports the KOF in a statement today. Although improving, the parameter remains at a level below the multi-year average, which is 100. 'The prospects for the Swiss economy remain modest,' comment the KOF experts. The increase in the index was contributed to in particular by the indicators of the manufacturing industry, services and consumer demand; negative signs, on the other hand, come from the hotel and catering sector, while the indices relating to foreign demand, financial and insurance services, as well as construction are almost unchanged. ### Barometer analysis The KOF presents its monthly barometer - published since the 1970s - as an indicator that anticipates the evolution of the economy. It consists of several data: currently it includes hundreds of sub-indicators. Implications for cross-border workers The slight economic recovery signaled by the KOF barometer could have several implications for cross-border workers in Switzerland, particularly in the canton of Ticino. With the increase in indicators for the manufacturing industry and services, there could be more job opportunities in these sectors. However, the hotel and catering sector, which has been particularly affected, may continue to struggle, with possible impacts on cross-border workers in these areas. ### Comparison with the previous situation Before the pandemic, the Swiss economy was in a phase of stable growth, with a KOF barometer consistently above the long-term average of 100 points. The current situation, with an index at 97.9 points, represents an improvement compared to the lows of 2025, but is still far from pre-pandemic levels. This means that while there is a recovery, the Swiss economy has not yet fully recovered. ### Possible scenarios If the recovery continues, we could see an increase in job opportunities in sectors such as manufacturing and services. However, if the situation in the hotel and catering sector does not improve, we could see an increase in unemployment in these areas. Cross-border workers should closely monitor the evolution of the situation and consider any changes in their sector of work. ### Comparison with other economic indicators The KOF barometer is just one of the economic indicators that monitor the health of the Swiss economy. Other indicators, such as the consumer price index and the unemployment rate, can provide further information on the economic situation.
