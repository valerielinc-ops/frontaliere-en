Mandatory dental insurance: double tax for Ticino residents (cross-border guide)

The No to the dental care initiative committee explains why the proposal is costly and disproportionate

Contesto

In brief - Initiative for mandatory dental insurance in Ticino - No Committee: double tax for citizens and businesses - Annual cost up to 150 million francs ## Key facts - What: Initiative for mandatory dental insurance - When: Presented at a press conference on May 5, 2026 - Where: Lugano, Canton Ticino - Who: No Committee on the dental care initiative - Amount: Annual cost up to 150 million francs The No Committee on the dental care initiative, composed of representatives from PLR, UDC, Lega, and Centro, as well as representatives from the economic world and dental associations, presented today at a press conference the reasons for their opposition to the introduction of mandatory dental insurance. According to the committee, the proposal is costly, disproportionate, and would result in double taxation for citizens. At the heart of their position is the belief that the problem of access to dental care exists but is limited to a minority of the population and is already addressed with targeted tools. Maurizio Augustoni, a member of the Gran Consiglio from Il Centro, stated: 'We don't want to downplay the problem, but the data clearly shows that it is limited and already addressed today with targeted tools.' In this context, the introduction of a generalized system is considered an excessive response. Also, as highlighted by Matteo Quadranti, a member of the Gran Consiglio from PLRT, the dental sector is one of the few healthcare areas where costs have remained under control over time: 'Dental care is one of the few areas of healthcare where costs have remained under control over the past twenty years.' ### Impact on citizens Among the main concerns is the direct impact on citizens. Practical analysis: implications for cross-border workers The introduction of mandatory dental insurance in Ticino could have a significant impact on cross-border workers employed in the canton. Currently, cross-border workers benefit from a Swiss healthcare system that, although costly, offers comprehensive coverage for medical care. However, the addition of mandatory dental insurance could impose an additional financial burden on transborder workers. ### Comparison with the current situation Currently, cross-border workers in Ticino already pay contributions for mandatory health insurance (LAMal) and professional pension schemes (LPP). The introduction of mandatory dental insurance would represent an additional cost, reducing the net salary available. This could have a significant impact on purchasing power, especially for workers with medium to low incomes. ### Possible scenarios If the initiative is approved, cross-border workers would face an increase in insurance contributions. This could lead to a reduction in available salary, affecting savings capacity and quality of life. Furthermore, the increase in labor costs could make Ticino less competitive as a place of work, influencing the decision of many cross-border workers to continue working in the canton. ### Impact on businesses Ticino businesses, already facing international competition, could suffer an additional competitive disadvantage. The increase in labor costs could make it more difficult to attract and retain talent, negatively affecting the canton's economic growth.
