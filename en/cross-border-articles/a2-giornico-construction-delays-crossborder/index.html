Frequently Asked Questions
What are the expected timelines for the A2 Giornico interchange construction and how will it impact cross-border commuters?
The construction site will last from October 2024 to March 2026, with work divided into 3 phases. Travel time increases of up to 70% are expected during peak hours, with a Como-Bellinzona journey potentially taking up to 1h17m. Mandatory detours and increased traffic on secondary roads are anticipated.
What alternative transportation methods are recommended for cross-border commuters during the A2 Giornico construction?
For commuters in the Val Leventina, the Swiss Federal Railways (SBB) will increase regional train services. Park-and-ride facilities in Giornico and Bodio will be expanded. For those coming from Italy, the SS2 is not recommended due to other construction sites. The recommended detour is the SP 64.
What do truck drivers need to know about the A2 Giornico construction site?
Truck drivers require mandatory reservations for nighttime transit (10:00 PM - 5:00 AM) via the UST portal. A 30% highway surcharge is expected, and alternative routes via San Bernardino involve additional 15% tolls. Estimated losses for the export sector are significant.
Are there any aids or reimbursements available for Ticino businesses affected by the disruptions from the A2 Giornico construction?
The Chamber of Commerce estimates monthly losses of 4.7 million CHF for exports. An Economic Compensation Plan for Ticino businesses exists, with a maximum of 15,000 CHF/year. Businesses must complete an online form within 30 days of the event to request reimbursements.
What are the route options for cross-border commuters from Lombardy and Piedmont during the A2 Giornico construction?
Options include a northern route via the A13 and the San Gottardo Pass (with possible winter nighttime closures), a western alternative via the SS336 and Monte Ceneri (with a risk of congestion in Rivera), or using the Luino-Locarno ferry service, which has been enhanced.
