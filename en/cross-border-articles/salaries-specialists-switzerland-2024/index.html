Swiss Salaries to Rise 1.5-2% in 2024, Favoring Specialists (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 29 May 2026

Swiss salaries are expected to increase by 1.5-2% in 2024, with specialists in corporate transformation and artificial intelligence benefiting the most. 💡 The growth is moderate, but experts warn that the Swiss labor market is facing significant challenges, including a shortage of skilled workers. 📊 The country's economic growth is expected to slow down in 2024, with the GDP forecast to increase by 1.5%. ⚠️ The Swiss government has introduced measures to attract foreign workers, including the introduction of a new cross-border worker permit. The permit allows cross-border workers to work in Switzerland without having to obtain a residence permit. 📊 However, the permit is only available to workers who meet certain requirements, including having a job offer and sufficient financial resources. 💡 The Swiss labor market is also facing challenges related to the aging population and the need for workers to adapt to new technologies. 📊 The country's education system is expected to play a key role in addressing these challenges, with a focus on promoting lifelong learning and upskilling. 💡

Contesto

In brief - Average salary increase in Switzerland: +1.5-2% in 2024 - Specialists in corporate transformation and AI the most advantaged - Regional disparities: Zurich pays 5,000-10,000 CHF more per year ## Key facts - What: Average salary increase in Switzerland - When: 2024 - Where: Switzerland, with regional disparities - Who: Specialists in corporate transformation and AI - Amount: Increase between 1.5% and 2% Average salaries in Switzerland are set to increase between 1.5% and 2% in 2024, in line with inflation. However, the main beneficiaries of these increases will be specialists in corporate transformation, particularly in the fields of sustainability and artificial intelligence. This is according to a survey published by Adecco, based on over 15,000 job interviews. ### Salary disparities The situation in the Swiss labor market shows a tendency for companies to make targeted recruitments, rather than a generalized expansion of staff. This results in disparities in the evolution of remuneration: for most roles, the increase remains moderate, while for specialist profiles, the growth is much more marked. ### The 'Zurich premium' There are also significant regional disparities. The so-called 'Zurich premium' continues to be a reality: employees working in or around the economic capital of Switzerland are paid between 5,000 and 10,000 francs more per year than the national average.

Dettagli operativi

Practical analysis: what does this mean for cross-border workers? For cross-border workers employed in Switzerland, this salary increase could represent an opportunity, but with some important considerations. First and foremost, it is crucial to understand that not all sectors will benefit equally from this rise. Cross-border workers in specialized fields such as business transformation, sustainability, and artificial intelligence may see a more significant increase in their wages. ### Comparison with the previous situation Before this increase, salaries in Switzerland were already among the highest in Europe, but the disparity between different sectors and regions was evident. With this new rise, the gap appears set to widen further, particularly between skilled and unskilled workers. For cross-border workers, this means that the choice of sector could have an even greater impact on their salary. ### Impact on the cost of living It is also important to consider the impact of this increase on the cost of living. With a moderate salary rise for most workers, purchasing power may not increase significantly, especially in regions like Zurich where the cost of living is already very high. For cross-border workers living in Italy and working in Switzerland, this could mean a greater disparity between their salary and the cost of living in their place of residence. ## Useful tools to protect your net income To reduce FX leakage, compare CHF-EUR exchange options and banks for cross-border workers.
