May Day 2026 in Switzerland: peaceful protests and capitalism criticism

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 1 May 2026

Thousands of protesters took part in May Day marches across Switzerland, with vandalism in Zurich and criticism of the G7 and capitalism.

Contesto

In brief - Thousands of protesters in Switzerland for May Day - Vandalism episodes in Zurich with 8 arrests - Criticism of the G7 and capitalism ## Key facts - What: May Day marches in Switzerland - When: May 1, 2026 - Where: Zurich, Basel, Geneva, Bern, Biel - Who: Protesters, cantonal police, new socialist National Councillor Michèle Dünki-Bättig - Amount: 15,000 people in Zurich, 2,500 in Basel, 3,000 in Geneva, 1,000 in Bern, 400 in Biel Swiss squares on May Day saw peaceful marches in much of the country, with episodes of vandalism in Zurich. Thousands of protesters took to the streets as per tradition today across Switzerland for May Day. The main event took place in Zurich, with an estimated 15,000 people according to the organizers. It was here that episodes of violence occurred, with damage to shop windows and buildings housing bank branches. At the closing rally, newly sworn-in socialist National Councillor Michèle Dünki-Bättig spoke, fresh from her oath in the House of Representatives. 'This country cannot function without us workers,' said the Zurich native, urging solidarity and not to let themselves be divided. ### Criticism of the UDC initiative Like several other politicians throughout the day, the deputy in her speech criticized the UDC initiative 'No to a Switzerland of 10 million!', which will be voted on in a month and a half. May Day was also celebrated in numerous other Swiss cities (around fifty), without any major incidents. In Basel, where participants reached 2,500 according to figures provided by the cantonal police, some confusion was created when the head of the march took an unauthorized detour.
