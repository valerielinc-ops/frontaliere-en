Gottardo Pass reopens early on May 8th (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 5 May 2026

Thanks to favorable weather conditions, the Gottardo Pass reopens as early as Friday, May 8th, easing traffic ahead of the long weekend

Contesto

In brief - Gotthard Pass reopens on Friday, May 8th - Early reopening thanks to favorable weather - Traffic eased for Ascension and Pentecost ## Key facts - What: Reopening of the Gotthard Pass - When: Friday, May 8th, 2026 at 11:00 AM - Where: Gotthard Pass, Canton Ticino - Who: Federal Office for Roads (FEDRO) - Cost: Not specified The Gotthard Pass reopens early thanks to favorable weather conditions. The Federal Office for Roads (FEDRO) has announced that the Gotthard Pass road will reopen to traffic this Friday, May 8th, at 11:00 AM. This early reopening compared to the scheduled date is due to less snowfall than last year and favorable weather conditions that allowed for faster completion of snow clearance and road restoration work. The reopening of the Gotthard Pass is important news for cross-border commuters who use this road for travel between Italy and Switzerland. The Gotthard Pass road will be available during the busy traffic periods of the Ascension and Pentecost holidays, easing the north-south A2 corridor. This means less congestion and shorter travel times for those crossing the border. ### Impact on cross-border commuters For cross-border workers who commute to Switzerland and live in Italy, the reopening of the Gotthard Pass is a significant advantage. The Gotthard Pass road offers an alternative to the A2, reducing travel times and improving daily quality of life. Practical Analysis The early reopening of the Gotthard Pass has several practical implications for cross-border workers. Firstly, it reduces travel times and congestion, especially in view of the Ascension and Pentecost holidays. This is particularly useful for those who need to cross the border daily for work. ### Comparison with the Previous Situation Before the reopening, cross-border workers were forced to use the A2, which is often congested, especially during holidays. The reopening of the Gotthard Pass offers a more direct and less busy alternative, significantly improving the quality of daily life. Moreover, the seasonal extension of the A2 exit in Göschenen will protect the municipality from parasitic traffic, allowing travelers to reach the road to the San Gottardo Pass without having to bypass the column at the north portal of the San Gottardo road tunnel. ### Concrete Scenarios Imagine a cross-border worker who works in Lugano and lives in Como.
