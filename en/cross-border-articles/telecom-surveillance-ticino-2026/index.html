Telecom surveillance: +40% in Switzerland, but real-time slows

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 28 April 2026

In 2025, antenna searches increased by 69%, while real-time surveillance grew by only 3%.

Contesto

In brief - Telecommunications surveillance +40% in 2025 - Slowdown in real-time surveillance growth - Antenna-based searches +69% ## Key facts - What: Increase in telecommunications surveillance - When: 2025 - Where: Switzerland - Who: SCPT Service - Amount: +40% total, +69% antenna searches BERN - In 2025, the number of telecommunications surveillance measures in Switzerland increased by approximately 40%. The main reason was a massive increase in searches for antenna coverage areas. According to a statement released today by the Postal and Telecommunications Surveillance Service (SCPT), antenna-based searches in 2025 increased by approximately 69% compared to the previous year. This increase is the determining factor that drove up the overall total of surveillance measures. For other types of interventions, the growth dynamic slowed down compared to 2024. The number of real-time surveillance measures rose by 3%, reaching 1,878 cases. Retroactive surveillance measures increased by 6% (6,531 cases), while emergency searches grew by 5% (1,287 cases). On the other hand, searches for convicted individuals more than halved, dropping from 35 cases the previous year to 17 in 2025. ### Implications for cross-border workers Cross-border workers living and working in Ticino could be affected by these measures, especially if involved in investigations or surveillance procedures. It is important to be aware of the current regulations and surveillance procedures to avoid legal problems. In particular, the growth in antenna-based searches could impact the privacy and security of communications, making it necessary to exercise greater control over personal devices. ### Comparison with 2024 Compared to the previous year, 2025 saw a significant change in surveillance dynamics. Trend Analysis In 2025, the SCPT Service recorded a significant increase in information requests, with a 32% rise compared to the previous year. Complex information, such as copies of identity documents, increased by 39% to 44,836, while simple information, such as IP addresses, was processed in 650,034 cases, compared to 495,119 in 2024. ### Distribution by Type of Crime Real-time and retrospective surveillance primarily concerned crimes against property, with a 45% increase. Measures for crimes against life and personal integrity accounted for approximately 24% of the total, marking a 75% growth. The most significant percentage increase was recorded in the area of crimes against sexual integrity, with a +124%, although this category represents only 2% of the total measures. Measures for crimes against personal freedom also increased significantly (+50% approximately), while those related to crimes against public tranquility decreased by 28%. ### Role of the Confederation's Information Activities Service (SIC) The SIC ordered 334 surveillances in 2025, compared to 106 the previous year. The number of information requests rose from 12,789 to 39,130. However, the SCPT Service clarified that the SIC's counting methods differ from their own, making the data not directly comparable. ### Implications for Cross-Border Workers Cross-border workers living and working in Ticino must be aware of the new trends in telecommunications surveillance. The growth in antenna-based searches and information requests could influence their daily lives, making it necessary to exercise greater control over communications and personal devices.
