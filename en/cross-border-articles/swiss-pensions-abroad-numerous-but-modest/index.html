Frequently Asked Questions
How to apply for a Swiss pension if I live in Italy?
To apply for Swiss pension if you live in Italy, you need to contact the Federal Office of Social Insurance (OFAS) or your Frontier Ticino clearing office. They will provide you with the information you need to apply.
What if I don't have the necessary documents to apply for a Swiss pension?
If you do not have the necessary documents to apply for Swiss pension, you may not receive payments. It is important to check with the Federal Office of Social Insurance (OFAS) or your Frontier Ticino clearing office to understand what to do.
How is the Swiss pension taxed for a border worker residing in Italy?
Swiss pensions received by border workers residing in Italy are subject to Italian taxation, with a rate of 23%. This contrasts with the 11% applied in Switzerland. In addition, frontier workers in Italy do not benefit from Swiss tax breaks, such as single-tax deductions or direct federal tax reductions.
What is the average Swiss pension amount for frontier workers living in Italy?
In 2020, the average Swiss pension paid to border workers residing in Italy was around 25,000 Swiss francs per year. This figure is lower than the average of about 40,000 Swiss francs per year received by pensioners residing in Switzerland, affecting the standard of living.
Is the number of Swiss pensions paid abroad increasing?
Yes, the number of Swiss pensions paid abroad is constantly increasing. In 2021, around 60,000 pensions were granted to people residing outside Switzerland, compared to 50,000 in 2016. This increase reflects the increase in pensioners and increasing international mobility.
