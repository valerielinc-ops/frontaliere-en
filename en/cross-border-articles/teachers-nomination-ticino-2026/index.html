Appointments for part-time municipal teachers too

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 1 May 2026

Cantonal legislative change extends appointments to teachers with less than 50% employment, job stability guaranteed from 1 August 2026

Contesto

In brief - Municipal teachers with employment under 50% may be appointed - Legislative amendment comes into force on 1 August 2026 - VPOD union calls for clarity from municipalities ## Key facts - What: Abolition of the 50% threshold for teacher appointments - When: 1 August 2026 - Where: Canton of Ticino - Who: State Council, Municipalities, VPOD - Amount: Not specified A recent cantonal legislative amendment could significantly change the working conditions of numerous teachers active in municipal schools in Ticino. The abolition of the 50% limit as a minimum threshold for obtaining an appointment, provided for in Article 16 of the Organic Law of the School of the Republic and Canton of Ticino (LORD), opens up new possibilities for job stability. This normative novelty, already announced by the State Council, will come into force on 1 August 2026 and will be applied to cantonal teaching staff from the 2026/2027 school year. ### Practical implications For municipal teachers, the competence for transforming assignments of less than 50% into appointments falls directly to the municipalities. The State Council has clarified that this decision does not fall within its remit, responding to a parliamentary question submitted by deputy Marco Chiesa (Lega dei Ticinesi) on 15 March 2023. However, according to various reports collected by the VPOD union, this possibility would not yet have been applied in various municipal realities. For example, in the municipality of Lugano, around 120 teachers with assignments of less than 50% could benefit from the legislative amendment. In Bellinzona, the number could be around 90, while in Locarno around 70 teachers are estimated to be affected. These figures, although indicative, highlight the significant impact of the new legislation...

Dettagli operativi

Analysis of the implications The legislative amendment, which came into force on January 1, 2024, represents a significant step towards greater job stability for municipal teachers in Ticino. Until now, many teachers with assignments of less than 50% did not have access to the same protections guaranteed to their full-time colleagues. With the abolition of the 50% limit, the possibility of extending these protections to a greater number of teachers opens up. According to preliminary estimates, around 300 teachers could benefit from this change, with a significant impact on municipalities such as Lugano, Bellinzona, and Locarno. ### Comparison with the previous situation Before this amendment, teachers with assignments of less than 50% were often considered as part-time staff without the right to appointment. This meant they did not have the same guarantees of maintaining their working hours and protection in case of dismissal. For example, a teacher with a 40% assignment in Lugano was not entitled to a dismissal indemnity, while a colleague with a 60% assignment was. The new legislation radically changes this situation, offering greater job security. ### Possible scenarios With the entry into force of the new legislation, the municipalities will have to carefully evaluate how many teachers could benefit from the amendment. This process will require a review of current assignments and planning for the next school year. Punti chiave

What to do now For municipal teachers affected by the new legislation, it is crucial to act promptly. Here are the detailed steps to follow: 1. Contact your Municipality: Teachers should contact the relevant office of their Municipality to obtain specific information about the appointment procedure. For example, in Lugano, the relevant office is the Personnel Service, while in Bellinzona it is the Human Resources Sector. 2. Check eligibility: The Municipality will assess whether the teacher meets the requirements for appointment based on the new legislation. The requirements include a minimum employment of 40% (previously it was 50%) and a work contract of at least 12 months. For example, a teacher with a 45% contract in Locarno may now be eligible for appointment. 3. Submit the documentation: Teachers will need to submit the necessary documentation for the appointment request, which includes: - Copy of the work contract - Updated curriculum vitae - Degree or diploma certificate - Any previous service certificates 4. Wait for the decision: The Municipality will communicate the decision regarding the appointment within 30 days of the submission of the documentation. If the outcome is positive, the teacher will receive an appointment letter with the working conditions. ### Useful tools For further information and to calculate the tax implications of the appointment, teachers can use the salary calculator available on the website. This tool helps to better understand the financial consequences of the new work situation.
