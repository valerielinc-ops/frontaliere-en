Implications for cross-border workers and businesses The use of artificial intelligence (AI) by the Competition Commission (COMCO) could have significant implications for cross-border workers and businesses operating in Ticino. According to recent data, Ticino has over 67,000 cross-border workers, representing approximately 22% of the canton's workforce. AI could make the fight against cartels more efficient, ensuring greater competition and transparency in the market. For example, AI could analyze prices of goods and services in real-time in municipalities like Lugano, Mendrisio, and Bellinzona, identifying any illegal agreements between businesses. However, the lack of clear criteria could lead to uncertainties and risks. Without precise guidelines, businesses could face arbitrary decisions, with potential fines of up to 10 million Swiss francs, as provided by Article 49a of the Cartel Law (LCart). Additionally, cross-border workers could experience payment delays or other bureaucratic complications if the AI systems are not well calibrated. ### Comparison with the previous situation Before the introduction of AI, COMCO had to rely on traditional data analysis methods, which were slower and less effective. For example, a traditional investigation could take up to 18 months to complete, as in the case of the 2018 inquiry into cartels in the construction sector. AI could significantly improve the efficiency and effectiveness of investigations, reducing the time to a few months. However, it is essential that clear criteria are defined to ensure the security and reliability of the system.
