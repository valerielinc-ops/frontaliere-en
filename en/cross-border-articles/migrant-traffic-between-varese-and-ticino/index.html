Frequently Asked Questions
What is migrant trafficking?
Migrant trafficking is an illegal activity that involves transporting people across national borders without the consent of the competent authorities. It often involves the use of illegal means of transport and the payment of sums of money to traffickers.
What are the consequences of migrant trafficking for cross-border workers?
Migrant trafficking can pose a threat to the security and public order of border areas, with consequences for the daily life of cross-border workers who work and live in these areas. Furthermore, the phenomenon can have an impact on the local economy and on job safety.
What can I do as a cross-border worker to protect myself from migrant trafficking?
As a cross-border worker, it is important to be aware of the risks associated with migrant trafficking and to follow local news to be informed about the measures taken by the authorities to combat this phenomenon. In case of problems or concerns, you can contact the cross-border worker assistance service offered by the local authorities.
