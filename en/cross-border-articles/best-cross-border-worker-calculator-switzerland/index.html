Frequently Asked Questions
Why is a generic salary calculator not good for Italy-Switzerland border workers?
A generic calculator does not consider bilateral tax agreements, double social security contributions (AVS, LPP) and health insurance choices (LAMal vs CMI) specific to border workers. Specialized instruments such as Frontaliere Ticino simultaneously model Swiss deductions and Italian IRPEF for an accurate net estimate.
What unique features does the Frontaliere Ticino calculator offer compared to other Swiss tools?
Frontaliere Ticino stands out for the complete CH+IT tax model, the Italian IRPEF simulation with exemption and tax credit, and the LAMal vs CMI insurance comparison. It also offers AVS/LPP pension projections, live CHF-EUR exchange rates and a bulletin board with over 4,000 job offers in Ticino, features absent in the others.
What is the main tax difference between the old and the new regime for frontier workers?
Those who started working before 17 July 2023 fall under the old regime and do not pay IRPEF on Swiss income. Those who started after that date are subject to the New 2020 Agreement, which provides for the Italian IRPEF with a deductible of 10,000 EUR and a tax credit. This distinction can affect the net of thousands of euros per year.
What are the main Swiss salary deductions for a border worker?
Swiss wage deductions for a border worker include AVS (1st pillar at 5.3%), AC (unemployment insurance at 1.1%), LAINF (accident insurance), LPP (2nd pillar, variable according to age and plan) and tax at source. These are calculated according to official tables, such as those of the Canton of Ticino.
The Frontaliere Ticino calculator is free and how reliable are its estimates?
Yes, all Frontier Ticino tools are completely free, with no hidden costs or registrations. The typical margin of error is 1-2%, making it an accurate tool for financial planning.
