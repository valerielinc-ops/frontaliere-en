Swissminiatur 2026: The Sessa Gold Mine Unveiled (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 15 March 2026

Melide's Swissminiatura park inaugurates its 2026 season with a groundbreaking new attraction: the Sessa Gold Mine, a life-sized immersive experience dedicated to the history of Malcantone's gold mining.

Melide - Swissminiatura has opened its gates for the 2026 season with a revolutionary new experience that breaks away from the park's decade-long tradition of miniature replicas. For the first time, the central tunnel has been transformed into the Sessa Gold Mine, an immersive tribute to the region's rich gold mining history that will captivate the expected 200,000 visitors this year. The inauguration took place today with the presence of local authorities and dozens of families from Varese, Como, and the Ticino Canton. The idea was born from the desire to share a lesser-known story: between 1850 and 1940, the Malcantone mountains produced over 300 kg of gold, making the region a significant Swiss mining hub. "For the first time in 65 years of history, we've decided to abandon the 1:25 scale to tell an authentic story," explains the park's director. Dettagli operativi

The Sessa Gold Mine project represents a CHF 2.5 million investment, funded 60% by private sponsors and 40% by cantonal contributions. The technical installation includes motion sensors, holographic projections, and a CHF 50,000 audio system that recreates the underground environment. The works required 18 months and the approval of the Cantonal Commission for the Protection of Nature and Landscape. From a regulatory standpoint, the attraction falls under the 'thematic museum' category, with a construction permit issued by the Lugano Municipality in December 2024. The electrical installation was carried out in accordance with SIA 118 standards and includes a 72-hour autonomous emergency system. The RC insurance coverage has been increased to CHF 10 million to cover the specific risks associated with the underground environment. For border residents, the attraction offers a unique opportunity: visit a piece of Ticino's history with a 15% discount on presentation of a valid G permit or border resident card. The offer is valid until October 31, 2026, and includes a 10% discount on the self-service restaurant and souvenir shop. Frequently Asked Questions
How much is the ticket for the Sessa Gold Mine at Swissminiatura for a front panel?
The ticket for the Sessa Gold Mine at Swissminiatura, included in the access to the park, costs 19 francs for frontiers with valid G permit, with 15% discount on the normal price of adults (22 francs). Children up to 15 years pay CHF 14.
What is the expected economic impact for the Ticino region thanks to the increase of visitors to Swissminiatura in 2026?
The expected economic impact exceeds 7 million francs, considering that each visitor spends on average 35 extra francs in local services.
What are the opening hours of the Sessa Gold Mine at Swissminiatura in 2026?
The Sessa Gold Mine is open every day from 9 to 18 until 1 November. During July and August the hours are extended until 19:30. The park recommends arriving by 9:30 or after 16:00 in Italian bridges to avoid long waits.
Does the Sessa Gold Mine offer accessible services for people with disabilities?
Yes, the route in the mine is accessible with a guide for people with reduced mobility. You can request the tour in Italian Sign Language (LIS) by reservation. The park also has picnic areas and equipped toilets.
How can I reach Swissminiatura from Como or Varese by public transport?
From Como or Varese, take the TILO train line S10 towards Lugano to Melide station (10 minutes walk from the park). Alternatively, you can use an Italian tourist bus that stops in the park's reserved area, with 50 seats available.
