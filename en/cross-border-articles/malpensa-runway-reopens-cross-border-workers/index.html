Malpensa: runway 35L reopens, what changes for cross-border workers (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 10 May 2026

The twenty-year works on Malpensa's runway 35L have ended. Reopening is scheduled for Sunday, May 10th. Here's what changes for cross-border workers

Contesto

In brief - Runway 35L at Malpensa completed - Reopening scheduled for Sunday, May 10 - Checks underway with the Piaggio P-180 ## Key facts - What: Reopening of Runway 35L at Malpensa - When: Sunday, May 10, 2026 - Where: Malpensa Airport - Who: Enav with the Piaggio P-180 - Amount: 3 kilometers of runway reconstructed The works on Runway 35L at Malpensa, known as the twenty-year works, have finally been completed in the night between Friday, May 8 and Saturday, May 9. The left runway, looking towards the north, has been completely reconstructed for almost four kilometers, with the renewal of the light signals for pilots and the creation of new drainage channels to cope with extreme weather phenomena. These works, mainly financed with European funds, were started in mid-March and involved an important restructuring operation. On Saturday morning, the flights of Enav's Piaggio P-180 began, the specialized aircraft for radio measurements. The aircraft was at work from 11 am in the southern area of the airport, between Novara and Milan. Once the radio measurements checks are completed, the runway will be officially reopened to civil traffic on the morning of May 10. This event is of particular interest to cross-border workers who use Malpensa Airport for their travels, as the reopening of Runway 35L could influence waiting times and air traffic management. ### Impact on cross-border workers The reopening of Runway 35L at Malpensa represents an important novelty for cross-border workers who use the airport daily. With the runway back in operation, there may be changes in waiting times and boarding and disembarking procedures.
