Venice: a sound experience inspired by Hildegard of Bingen (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 10 May 2026

A unique experience in Venice, inspired by Saint Hildegard of Bingen, offers a sonic prayer for visitors.

Contesto

In brief - Sound experience inspired by Hildegard of Bingen in Venice - 21 artists involved, including Brian Eno and Jim Jarmusch - Until November 22, 280 visitors per day ## Key facts - What: Sound experience "The ear is the eye of the soul" - When: From May 10 to November 22, 2026 - Where: Mystic Garden of the Discalced Carmelites and Complex of Santa Maria Ausiliatrice, Venice - Who: Curators Hans Ulrich Obrist and Ben Vickers - Amount: 21 artists involved In Venice, the Pavilion of the Holy See at the 61st Venice Biennale presents "The ear is the eye of the soul," a sound prayer inspired by Saint Hildegard of Bingen. This project, curated by Swiss Hans Ulrich Obrist and Ben Vickers, offers a unique experience that combines art, spirituality, and music. The pavilion is located in two places: the Mystic Garden of the Discalced Carmelites in Cannaregio and the Complex of Santa Maria Ausiliatrice in Castello. ### The sound experience At the entrance of the Mystic Garden, visitors can equip themselves with headphones to live a moment of reflection by listening to the "sound prayer." Until November 22, a maximum of 280 people per day will be able to experience this. The "sound prayer" is the result of the work of 21 artists, including well-known names such as musician Brian Eno and director Jim Jarmusch. The fluidity with which the different pieces overlap creates a single piece that evolves from instrumental to moments of silence, passing through the spoken word. ### The context of the Biennale The sound experience echoes the theme of this year's Biennale Arte, "In Minor Keys," curated by the Swiss-Cameroonian Koyo Kouoh. Frequently Asked Questions
How can I book my visit to the sound experience?
You can book your visit through the official website of the Venice Art Biennale. Make sure to book in advance to guarantee access, given the limited number of daily visitors.
What documents are required to participate in the experience?
Make sure you have a valid ID with you and, if necessary, your cross-border worker permit. Always check the specific requirements for travel between Italy and Switzerland.
Can I participate in the experience with my family?
Yes, the experience is open to everyone. However, keep in mind that the number of visitors is limited to 280 per day, so it's advisable to book in advance to guarantee access for the whole family.
