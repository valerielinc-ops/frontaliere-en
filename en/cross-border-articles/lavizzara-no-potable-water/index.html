Frequently Asked Questions
What are the causes of a problem with the water network that has affected the municipality of Lavizzara?
According to the municipality, the cause of the problem is to be attributed to a technical failure in the water distribution network.
What is the emergency plan activated by the managing body to provide drinking water during the problem in Lavizzara?
The managing body has put in place an emergency plan with the supply of drinking water through mobile vehicles and the distribution of bottled water.
How can I request bottled water from the managing body if I live in Lavizzara?
To request bottled water, you must go to the headquarters of the managing body in Bellinzona, present an identity document and fill out the request form. The service is free for affected families and provides for the delivery of 20 liters per day for a period of 48 hours, renewable upon authorization.
What are the rights of Italian cross-border commuters working in Lavizzara in the event of a drinking water outage?
Italian cross-border commuters are entitled to a minimum supply of drinking water for domestic needs, as stipulated in the Federal Water Supply Act. If the interruption exceeds 24 hours, they can claim compensation of CHF 30 per day per person, which must be submitted to the managing body within 15 days with a copy of the employment contract.
What preventive measures is the managing body taking to avoid future failures in the Lavizzara water network?
The managing body has launched a structural overhaul program that involves the replacement of 12 km of old pipes with corrosion-resistant HDPE material by December 2024. In addition, it installed pressure sensors at 15 critical points to detect faults in real time and increased the annual maintenance budget by 20% to CHF 1.8 million.
