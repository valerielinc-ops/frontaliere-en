Initiative to cancel F-35 purchase

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 28 April 2026

A civic association launches an initiative to cancel the F-35 contract, deemed too costly and unsuitable.

Contesto

In brief - Initiative to cancel the purchase of F-35s - Signature collection until October 28, 2027 - Bern may adjust the army's budget ## Key facts - What: Initiative to cancel the F-35 contract - When: Launched today, signature collection until October 28, 2027 - Where: Switzerland - Who: Civic association 'No to the F-35s' - Amount: Not yet specified The civic association 'No to the F-35s' has launched a popular initiative to cancel the contract for the purchase of American F-35 fighter jets. According to the sponsoring committee, these aircraft are 'wholly unsuitable' and cost 'definitely too much'. The Federal Chancellery has published the text of the initiative in the Federal Gazette, which provides that Bern should renounce the purchase of the F-35s and adjust the army's budget accordingly. The initiative is supported by over 220 personalities who declared themselves in favor after the withdrawal of the 'Stop-F-35' initiative in 2022. In August 2022, the Left Alliance decided to withdraw its text after the signing of the contract for the purchase of 36 fighter jets. The Association has questioned the Confederation regarding the amount, the deadline, and the resolution of the contract for the purchase of the American aircraft. ### Implications for cross-border workers The initiative could have significant implications for cross-border workers employed in the defense sector or related fields. If the initiative is successful, there may be changes in the army's budget and, consequently, in the number of available jobs. Dettagli operativi

Practical Analysis The initiative to cancel the purchase of the F-35s could have a significant impact on the Swiss army's budget and the companies that provide services and products for defense. If the initiative is successful, the Swiss Confederation would have to abandon the purchase of the F-35s and adjust the army's budget accordingly. This could lead to a reduction in the number of fighter jets purchased or a reallocation of resources to other defense projects. ### Comparison with the Previous Situation Before the launch of this initiative, the Swiss army had already signed a contract for the purchase of 36 F-35 fighter jets. The 'Stop-F-35' initiative was withdrawn in 2022 after the contract was signed. Now, with the launch of a new initiative, the debate on the opportunity to purchase these aircraft is reopened. ### Implications for Cross-Border Workers Cross-border workers who work in the defense sector or related sectors could be affected by any changes in contracts and orders. If the initiative is successful, there may be fewer job opportunities related to the F-35s. However, if the initiative is rejected, cross-border workers could continue to benefit from orders related to the F-35s. ### Possible Scenarios If the initiative is accepted, the Swiss Confederation would have to abandon the purchase of the F-35s and adjust the army's budget accordingly. This could lead to a reduction in the number of fighter jets purchased or a reallocation of resources to other defense projects. Additionally, there could be a public debate on the opportunity to invest in other defense systems or to reform the structure of the Swiss army. • ## Action For cross-border workers who wish to support or oppose the initiative, it's important to stay informed and actively participate in the public debate. You can sign the initiative or take part in public discussions on the topic. Additionally, it's crucial to monitor the decisions of the Federal Council and any potential changes to the army budget. ### Signature collection procedure The collection of signatures for the initiative has begun and will continue until October 28, 2027. The promoters of the initiative are trying to gather as many signatures as possible to present the request to the Federal Council. Once the necessary signatures are collected, the Federal Council will have to examine the initiative and decide whether to accept or reject it. ### Deadlines and required documents Deadlines and required documents for participating in the signature collection have not yet been specified.
