Dettagli operativi

The AVS compensation fund is a fundamental institution for the Swiss social security system. It manages the contributions and benefits of the Old Age and Survivors' Insurance (AVS), which provides a pension to Swiss citizens and cross-border workers who have reached retirement age or who are in need. AVS is a mandatory social insurance, and the compensation fund is responsible for collecting contributions from employers and employees, as well as paying the benefits provided. AVS benefits AVS benefits include: - Old-age pension: Paid to Swiss citizens and cross-border workers who have reached retirement age (65 years for men and 64 years for women). The pension is calculated based on the contributions made during the working life. - Disability pension: Paid to people who, due to a disability, are no longer able to perform gainful employment. The amount of the pension depends on the degree of disability. - Survivors' pension: Paid to the family members of a deceased person who was entitled to an AVS pension. The amount of the pension depends on the number of dependent family members. - Supplementary benefits: Paid to AVS pension beneficiaries who have insufficient income to cover their basic needs. Supplementary benefits are paid by the cantons and are subject to certain conditions. AVS contributions AVS contributions are paid by employers and employees. Employers contribute a percentage of their employees' gross salary, while employees contribute a percentage of their gross salary. Contributions are calculated based on income brackets and are subject to an annual maximum. Frequently Asked Questions
How does the AVS compensation box work?
The AVS compensation fund is an institution that manages the contributions and benefits of Old and Superstite Insurance (AVS) in Switzerland. It collects contributions from employers and workers and provides the benefits provided for by AVS, such as old age pension and earnings loss allowances (IPG) Covid.
What is the route to report a suspect of fraud to the AVS Compensation Fund?
To report a suspect of fraud, contact the AVS Compensation Fund directly at the official phone number or via email, providing precise details. Alternatively, please contact the Public Ministry responsible for legal action.
Who pays for loss of earnings Covid if they are a frontier?
The earnings loss allowances (IPG) Covid, delivered during the pandemic, were managed by the Swiss AVS compensation fund. Frontiers could also have the right if they have the requirements of Swiss law. The article mentions that these allowances were intended to support workers who suffered a significant reduction in income due to the health crisis.
What are the consequences if I am caught unduly receiving AHV benefits?
It is a criminal offence to receive undue AHV benefits, as in the case of the CHF 2.7 million fraud mentioned above. The consequences can include reimbursement of amounts received unlawfully, criminal penalties and, potentially, a ban on access to future social benefits. Swiss law provides for severe penalties for those who commit social insurance fraud.
How can I check whether my AHV compensation office is secure?
To protect yourself, it is essential to check the authenticity of any communication you receive from the AHV compensation office. If you have any doubts, contact the institution directly through official channels (website, telephone numbers indicated on official documents). Always keep your contribution and benefit records in order for checks.
