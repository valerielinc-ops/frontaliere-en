The alert for heavy snowfall has been raised to level 4 in several alpine regions of Ticino.

In Ticino, heavy snowfall is becoming a growing concern. The Federal Office of Meteorology and Climate MeteoSvizzera announced today an alert for heavy snowfall in the mountains in large parts of the Alps. In the western Ticino, the level of risk has been raised to grade 4 (strong). A strong southerly current has begun to bring moist air from high altitudes towards the alpine region.

Dettagli operativi

The situation is particularly critical in the San Gottardo Pass, where heavy snowfall has already caused significant inconveniences. In 2021, the San Gottardo Pass was closed for more than 24 hours due to heavy snowfall, forcing hundreds of vehicles to stop. Punti chiave

The economic impact of heavy snowfall is significant. According to an analysis by the Ticinese Economic Committee, economic losses are estimated in millions of francs. Tourism activities, in particular, have suffered a significant impact, with a 30% reduction in tourist visits during the snowfall period. Local businesses had to implement emergency measures, such as suspending some activities and reducing operating hours. Frequently Asked Questions
What are the most affected areas in Ticino?
The most affected areas in Ticino are Passo del San Gottardo, Lugano, Bellinzona and the mountain areas of the region.
What is the impact of snowfall on the flow of cross-border workers between Ticino and Switzerland?
Nevicates can cause delays or interruptions in transport, increasing transport costs and housing for workers. For example, in Locarno, workers can be forced to stay in the city for days during intense snowfall.
How do I equip my vehicle to face snow when I travel to Switzerland?
To travel to Switzerland in snow conditions it is mandatory to mount chains or winter tyres approved. The Municipality of Lugano requires control of brakes, lights and antifreeze liquid, and advises to verify in real time the state of the roads on the site of the Federal Office of meteorology. Keep at hand a blanket, emergency food and a load cell phone.
What public transport means are available if the roads of Lugano and Bellinzona are closed for snow?
When the main roads of Lugano or Bellinzona are closed for snow, the authorities activate alternative transport services. The suspended railway lines are replaced by buses on rubber equipped with winter tires, and are made available to the front of the “snow‐coach” buses that connect the Alpine municipalities to the border. The information is updated on the site of the Canton in real time.
How much does transport and accommodation increase for snow-locked fronts in the Locarno area?
In the area of Locarno, snow closures forced the frontals to stop in the city for several days. According to the Ticinese Institute for Economic Development, the average additional cost is approximately CHF 200 per day for extra transport, food and accommodation, with an estimated total expenditure of CHF 3 million in 2022 for the 15,000 workers concerned.
