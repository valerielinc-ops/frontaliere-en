The workshop's theme is "Stories carved in stone, let's create an animated cartoon together," an initiative aimed at introducing young people to the secrets of the cup-and-ring marked stones, ancient stones with carvings that have not yet been fully deciphered. ### A unique experience The workshop will be led by Gianni Bertossa, a resident of Roveredo with a varied background in graphics, social work, and theater. Bertossa has studied with masters such as Peter Honegger, Peter Shub, and Bernhard Stöckli, bringing extensive experience in the field of educational theater. The initiative was designed to complement the activities of Museo Moesano, which is a reference point for local culture and history. ### What to expect During the workshop, participants will work together to create an animated cartoon based on the stories and mysteries of the cup-and-ring marked stones. Dettagli operativi

An Educational and Creative Opportunity The summer theater workshop at the Moesano Museum is an initiative of great educational value. Participants will have the opportunity to immerse themselves in local history through a practical and creative approach. Creating an animated cartoon based on the cup-and-ring stones allows young people to develop artistic and narrative skills, as well as stimulating their curiosity for archaeology and history. ### The Importance of the Cup-and-Ring Stones The cup-and-ring stones are ancient stones with engravings dating back to remote times. Their meaning and language are not yet fully deciphered, making them a fascinating subject for exploration and discovery. This workshop offers participants the opportunity to approach these mysteries in an interactive and engaging way. ### The Role of the Moesano Museum The Moesano Museum is a cultural reference point in the area. Its mission is to preserve and promote the local historical and cultural heritage. This summer workshop is an example of how the museum can actively involve the community, especially the younger generations, in the discovery and appreciation of history. ### An Experience for the Whole Family The workshop is not just an opportunity for children and young people, but also for families. The final show, open to the public, allows parents and friends to see the results of the participants' work. It is an occasion to celebrate together the success and fun of this educational experience. ### How to Prepare To participate in the workshop, it is important to register by the deadline of July 29, 2026. Punti chiave

What to do to participate To register for the summer theater workshop at the Moesano Museum, you need to follow a few simple steps. First, contact the museum via email at info@museomoesano.ch or by calling 091.8271666. It is important to provide all the requested information, such as the participant's name, age, and the contacts of parents or guardians. ### Documents and preparation No specific documents are required for registration, but it is useful to have updated contact information at hand. Participants should be ready to bring basic materials such as notebooks, pencils, and colors, although the museum will provide most of the necessary materials. ### Participation and payment Participation in the workshop is reasonably priced and includes materials, snacks, and the community lunch on Friday. Payment can be made at the time of registration or directly at the museum. It is advisable to confirm participation as soon as possible to secure a spot. ### What to bring Participants should bring a notepad, pencils, colors, and any other materials they deem useful for the workshop. The museum will provide the rest of the necessary materials, but it is always useful to be prepared. ### Contacts and additional information For further information and to register, you can contact the Moesano Museum via email at info@museomoesano.ch or by calling 091.8271666. The museum is open to the public and also offers guided tours and other educational activities. ### Conclusion The summer theater workshop at the Moesano Museum is a unique opportunity for children and young people to discover local history in an interactive and creative way.
