Frontalieri: Only Surplus Properties in Zurich (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 23 March 2026

One out of ten new immigrants chooses Zurich, but finds only dilapidated apartments or exorbitant rents. What changes for those working in Ticino.

In 2022, around 12,000 immigrants chose Zurich, accounting for 10% of arrivals in Switzerland. A figure that's enticing but clashes with everyday reality: those looking for a home in the Swiss economic capital find only 'surplus of the real estate market'. This is revealed by the analysis of the Zurich Cantonal Bank (ZKB) published on 23 March 2026, based on federal statistics. To give an idea, one person in twenty of the entire Swiss population lives in Zurich, but the market is so saturated that new arrivals end up in 'junk' apartments or with rents outside the market.

Dettagli operativi

The paradoxical situation also emerged in Bellinzona during the annual meeting of Italian-Swiss frontier associations. Frontaliere Ticino President Marco Bernasconi recounts: 'Many of our members work in Zurich but sleep in Lugano or even across the border because north of the Gotthard they don't find anything decent for less than 2,500 CHF per month for a three-room apartment'. A border-crosser who crosses the Brogeda pass every morning can save up to 800 CHF on rent, even counting the cost of transportation and time. ## Useful tools to protect your net income To reduce FX leakage, compare CHF-EUR exchange options and banks for cross-border workers.

Punti chiave

The UDC in Zurich has launched a cantonal initiative to give priority in housing to those already residing in Switzerland, but the proposal is divisive. According to the cantonal secretary for construction, it's not just a matter of rules but numbers: 'Zurich has less than 0.2% of vacant apartments, the lowest in Western Europe'. The result is that Italian frontiersmen, who already have to demonstrate to the Department of Institutions (DFE) a valid rental contract to obtain the G permit, find themselves signing contracts for damp or poorly insulated rooms. Frequently Asked Questions
How does the real estate market in Zurich work for Italian cross-border commuters?
The real estate market in Zurich is saturated and newcomers end up in 'junk' apartments or with out-of-market rents. Italian cross-border commuters must prove that they have a valid lease to obtain the G permit, but landlords can ask for difficult conditions such as three months' deposit and bank references.
How much can an Italian cross-border commuter who chooses to live in Lugano instead of Zurich save for a three-room apartment?
A cross-border commuter can save up to CHF 800 per month (considering rent and transport) by choosing Lugano instead of Zurico for a three-room apartment, with an annual saving of about CHF 9,600.
What are the minimum requirements for an apartment to obtain a G permit in Switzerland?
To obtain the G permit, the accommodation must meet minimum requirements: at least 20 square meters for one person, an additional 12 square meters for each roommate, an opening window and an independent heating system. Failure to comply with these standards may lead to the rejection of the application.
How can I get accommodation in Zurich if I am a cross-border commuter on a budget?
Consider housing cooperatives in Zurich, which offer rents that are 20-30% lower than the market. Access requires the subscription of a membership fee (minimum 2,000 CHF) and a waiting list of 3-5 years. Alternatively, search on less conventional platforms or dedicated Facebook groups.
What documents do I need to speed up the search for an apartment in Zurich as a cross-border commuter?
To conclude a rental contract quickly, prepare a complete package: employment contract, the last three pay slips, an extract from the criminal record and the accident insurance certificate. Having these documents ready can allow you to close the deal within 24 hours.
