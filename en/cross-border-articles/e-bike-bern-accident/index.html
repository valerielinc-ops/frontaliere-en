Italian Dies in E-bike Crash in Canton Bern

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 30 April 2026

A 60-year-old man, an Italian resident of Canton Bern, died after an e-bike accident in Bätterkinden, Canton Bern, on April 28, 2026.

The victim was traveling with a companion when, for reasons still to be clarified, he fell from his e-bike and lost consciousness. Passersby and emergency responders immediately intervened, beginning resuscitation efforts. The man was urgently transported to hospital, where he died shortly after. The cause of the incident is currently attributed to a possible medical emergency, and the Bern cantonal authorities have opened an investigation to reconstruct the exact sequence of events. Swiss traffic and safety regulations require the use of protective gear and adherence to traffic rules, but also attention to sudden medical issues, as might have been the case here. Frequently Asked Questions
What safety measures are recommended for cross-border commuters using e-bikes?
It is advisable to undergo a medical check-up before starting, verify your health status, wear helmets and safety devices, adhere to traffic rules, and plan your route using official tools. Collaborating with local authorities and knowing emergency procedures are also essential strategies.
What should I do if I experience health issues while riding an e-bike?
If you feel unwell, you should stop immediately, activate safety devices, and call the local emergency numbers. It’s also important to inform authorities and follow first aid procedures, remaining calm and cooperating with rescue personnel.
What tools can a cross-border worker use to improve safety in Switzerland?
