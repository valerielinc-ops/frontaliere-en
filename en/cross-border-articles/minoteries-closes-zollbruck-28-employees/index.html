Minoteries to close Zollbrück mill, 28 jobs affected (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 5 May 2026

The Minoteries Group will close the Steiner mill in Zollbrück in the first half of 2027, affecting 28 employees.

Contesto

In brief - Minoteries to close Zollbrück mill in 2027 - 28 employees affected - Reorganization for synergies and logistics ## Key facts - What: Closure of Steiner mill in Zollbrück - When: First half of 2027 - Where: Zollbrück (BE) - Who: Minoteries Group - Amount: 28 employees affected The Minoteries Group, a leader in the milling sector, has announced the closure of the Steiner mill in Zollbrück in the first half of 2027. The decision was made in light of regulatory and structural needs that would have required significant investments in the Zollbrück site. The group has decided to leverage existing capacities and highly modern resources at its sites in Granges-près-Marnand (VD) and Müllheim-Wigoltingen (TG). The organic flours produced in Zollbrück will be centralized in Granges-près-Marnand, where there is a mill for special flours. Products such as semolina, flakes, and muesli will in the future be manufactured at the site of Schweizerische Schälmühle E. Zwicky AG in Müllheim-Wigoltingen, integrated into the group in 2025. The reorganization will strengthen synergies between sites, simplify logistics, and improve the use of existing production capacities. The Steiner mill, which is now closing, has belonged to the Minoteries Group since 2011. The site has made a name for itself thanks to its expertise in processing cereals, legumes, and seeds with Bio and Demeter brands. A successor solution is being sought for the infrastructure, and the 28 employees affected will be presented with a social plan. ### Impact on Cross-Border Workers The closure of the mill in Zollbrück could have a significant impact on cross-border workers employed at the site.
