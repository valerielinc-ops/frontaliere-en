Lavena Ponte Tresa: drug dealing ring intercepted (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 13 March 2026

The local police of Lavena Ponte Tresa intercepted a drug trafficking ring with a raid at the home of a suspected dealer.

Contesto

TL;DR - Police uncovered drug network involving minors in Lavena Ponte Tresa. - Two young men arrested with drugs and cash near railway station. - Drug dealing among minors increased by 38% in first half of 2024. - Operation involved cross-border cooperation with Swiss authorities. ## Key facts - Operation Date: March 6, 2024 - Arrested Individuals: Two young men, ages 19 and 21 - Drugs Seized: 68 grams of hashish, 15 grams of marijuana - Cash Seized: 320 euros - Minors Involved: At least 11 minors aged 12 to 14 in past six months - Cross-Border Cases: 214 cases in 2023 between Lavena Ponte Tresa and Ticino municipalities - Legal Penalties Italy: 6 to 20 years prison, fines from 26,000 to 260,000 euros for supplying drugs to minors - Legal Penalties Switzerland: Fines up to 1,000 CHF for cannabis possession, harsher penalties for dealing The local police of Lavena Ponte Tresa carried out a targeted operation against drug trafficking, uncovering a network that also involved minors. The action, launched in the second week of June, was coordinated with the Arcisate local police command, allowing officers to monitor movements between the two municipalities and the border areas. The operation resulted in the arrest of two young men, a 19-year-old resident of Induno Olona and a 21-year-old living in Lavena Ponte Tresa, caught during a drug handover near the railway station parking lot. Officers seized 68 grams of hashish and 15 grams of marijuana, along with 320 euros in cash, presumably proceeds from dealing. During the operation, 'smoke' was also found hidden in the stove of one of the homes involved, likely in an attempt to destroy evidence. Dettagli operativi

The operation was conducted on the evening of March 6, involving several local police officers. The officers carried out a territory control service, intercepting two young men on Via della Stazione, near the Italian-Swiss border. The drug handover was documented thanks to mobile cameras and the assistance of drug-sniffing dogs, already used in similar operations in Chiasso and Mendrisio over the past six months. 📊 According to data from the Ticino Cantonal Police, in 2023 there were 214 cross-border drug dealing cases between Lavena Ponte Tresa and the Ticino municipalities of Lugano, Locarno, and Bellinzona. Most seizures involved cannabis (67%), followed by cocaine (22%) and other substances (11%). The March 6 operation led to the seizure of 37 grams of hashish, 12 doses of cocaine, and 1,800 euros in cash. The suspects, both residents of the Varese area, were referred to the Italian judicial authorities and also reported to the Swiss authorities due to the risk of repeat offenses on Swiss territory. ⚠️ Local police stated that 'this operation takes place in an increasingly worrying context,' noting that drug dealing is growing especially near border crossings. In 2022, cooperation between Italian and Ticino police was strengthened by the introduction of the Cross-Border Police Agreement (D.L. 18/12/2022, art. 14), which allows for joint operations and the exchange of operational information. Seized sums exceeding 1,000 euros are now automatically reported to the Federal Police Office in Bern, under Swiss anti-money laundering regulations (L. 1/1/2023, threshold CHF 1,000). 💡 Operational checklist for law enforcement: - Targeted surveillance near crossing points (stations, customs checkpoints, cross-border bus stops) - Use of mobile cameras and drug-sniffing dog...

Frequently Asked Questions
What if a minor is found with drugs in Italy?
In Italy, the possession of narcotic substances by minors may result in the reporting to the Court of Minors and the intervention of social services.
What is the difference between the Italian and Swiss regulations for drug dealing with minors?
In Italy, Law 49/2006 provides penalties from 6 to 20 years and fines from 26,000 to 260.000 euros. In Switzerland, the Law on Drugs (LStup) applies more severe penalties for child trafficking, with pecuniary sanctions of up to CHF 1,000 for cannabis possession, but harder punishment for the drug.
What are the sanctions for an adult who is stopped with a small amount of cannabis at the Italo-Switzerland border?
If the adult is surprised with less than 5 grams of cannabis, in Italy it can receive an administrative penalty between 500 and 2,000 euros and, in case of relapse, up to 3 years of imprisonment. In Switzerland, the law provides for a fine of up to CHF 1,000; for higher or recurring amounts, the penalty can reach 2 years in prison. 2024 figures show that 38% of seizures are less than 5 grams.
How does the 2022 Transfrontal Police Agreement work between Italy and Switzerland for drug trafficking investigations?
The agreement allows joint operations, immediate exchange of operational information and the possibility to refer suspects to the competent authority of the other country. In 2023 there were 12 joint incursions, with 68% of the seizure of narcotics reported simultaneously to both procures. Moreover, the sums seized over 1,000 euros are automatically transmitted to the Federal Office of Police in Bern for anti-money laundering control.
What social services are available for minors surprised with drugs at the Italo-Switzerland border?
In Italy, minors are addressed to the Court of Minors, where they can receive recovery programs, psychological advice and school inclusion; 70% of the cases of 2024 were entrusted to such paths. In Switzerland, Jugendhilfe offers similar assistance with family mediation and therapy. Both countries cooperate to monitor reintegration, avoiding relapses.
