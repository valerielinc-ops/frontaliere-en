Frequently Asked Questions
What are the main documents required to start working in Switzerland?
The main documents required to start working in Switzerland as a cross-border worker are the G permit, the work contract, health insurance, and registration with AHV. These documents are essential to ensure a smooth and legal transition into the Swiss job market.
How do you obtain the G permit?
The G permit can be requested at the competent office of the Canton of Ticino. It is important to submit all the required documents and follow the procedures indicated by the Swiss authorities. Once the G permit is obtained, it is possible to sign a work contract with a Swiss company.
What are the tax implications for cross-border workers in Switzerland?
Cross-border workers in Switzerland are subject to withholding tax, which is a form of direct taxation. It is important to know the tax rates and the deductions available to reduce the tax burden. Additionally, it is important to know the rules for filing tax returns and ensure that the deadlines are respected.
