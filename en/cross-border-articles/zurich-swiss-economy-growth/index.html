Zurich leads Swiss economy but grows below average

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 27 April 2026

Zurich generates 21% of Switzerland's GDP but grows only 1.3% annually, below the national average of 1.7%.

Contesto

In brief - Zurich generates 21% of Switzerland's GDP - Average annual growth of 1.3% (2008-2022) - Service sector accounts for 70% of local GDP ## Key facts - What: Zurich's share of Swiss GDP - When: Data from 2008 to 2022 - Where: Canton of Zurich - Who: Study by Zürcher Kantonalbank (ZKB) - Amount: 21% of Swiss GDP generated by Zurich Zurich, with 21% of Switzerland's GDP, dominates the national economy but records below-average growth. According to a study by Zürcher Kantonalbank (ZKB), the cantons of Zurich, Bern, Vaud, and Geneva generate nearly half of Switzerland's GDP. However, between 2008 and 2022, Zurich recorded an average growth of 1.3%, below the national average of 1.7%. ZKB attributes this to the predominance of the service sector, which represents 70% of Zurich's GDP. This sector, while labor-intensive, typically has lower growth rates compared to industry. Nevertheless, Zurich's productivity level is high, with added value per hour worked exceeding the national average by 5%. ### The FIFA effect A particular factor influencing Zurich's GDP is the FIFA effect. During the period of the soccer World Cup, the revenues of the association based in Zurich influence the local GDP by up to one percentage point, compared to 0.3% nationally. Although temporary, this effect significantly contributes to the local economy during World Cup years. The larger cantons, including Zurich, Bern, Vaud, and Geneva, are responsible for 80% of the national GDP, while the remaining 15 cantons contribute 20%. This economic disparity highlights the importance of the larger cantons in the Swiss economy.
