Cross-border workers: documents for starting in Switzerland (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 12 June 2026

A practical guide to essential documentation for cross-border commuters: from employment contracts to health insurance, here is what to prepare before crossing the border.

Contesto

In brief - Signed and valid employment contract - Valid identity document - AVS certificate for social security - LAMal health insurance documentation ## Key facts - What: Hiring documents checklist - When: Before the start of the employment relationship - Where: Canton Ticino and Swiss territory - Who: Employer and cantonal authorities - Obligation: Document submission for G permit Starting a career in Canton Ticino requires precise bureaucratic preparation, which is essential to regularize one's professional and tax status. The first step for any worker residing in Italy and employed in Switzerland is signing the employment contract. This document must clearly specify duties, gross salary, and contractual conditions, serving as the basis for all subsequent administrative procedures. ### Managing the G permit Among the main requirements is the application for the G permit, which authorizes work activities for residents of Italy. The Swiss employer usually handles the notification or application procedure for the permit with the competent cantonal authorities. It is necessary to provide a copy of a valid identity document and, in specific cases, certifications attesting to the professional qualifications declared during the interview. The accuracy of this data is essential to avoid delays in obtaining the authorization required to work legally within Ticino. Once obtained, the permit becomes the primary identification document during the validity period of the employment relationship, ensuring regular access at border crossings such as Brogeda or Gaggiolo, where it is vital to always keep one's status in good standing.

Dettagli operativi

The aspect of old age and disability represents a fundamental pillar in the life of the cross-border worker. Every employee must be registered in the AVS system, the Old Age and Survivors Insurance, which constitutes the first pillar of the Swiss pension system. The employer provides registration and payment of contributions, but it is the employee's responsibility to provide the pension number or insurance certificate if already in possession. Parallel to this, the management of LAMal is an obligatory requirement. The cross-border worker must prove health coverage, choosing between the Swiss system or, where possible, the exemption if they meet specific requirements. This choice must be documented correctly to avoid administrative sanctions. ### Bank and tax data In addition to insurance documents, the opening or communication of a bank account is required. Many employers require that the salary be credited to an institution that operates in Swiss francs. It is advisable to compare the available options via a currency converter to manage transfers to Italy as efficiently as possible. From a tax point of view, the salary will be subject to withholding tax, calculated according to the current rates in the Canton of Ticino. Ensure that the personal data communicated to the employer are always up to date, including marital status and the presence of dependents, as these elements directly influence the rate applied. Every variation must be communicated promptly to the source tax office to avoid discrepancies or complications in the annual tax return, in accordance with the applicable double taxation agreements. ## Useful planning tools To estimate your pension strategy, use the pension planner and the pillar 3 simulator.

Punti chiave

The onboarding process concludes with a systematic verification of the requirements set by the company. It is necessary to prepare a file containing, in addition to the documents already mentioned, bank details (IBAN) that support transactions in Swiss currency. Many Ticino-based companies also require the completion of internal forms for the management of the second pillar (LPP), the occupational pension scheme that complements the AVS to ensure a future retirement income. It is essential to carefully read every clause of the company regulations before the final signature, especially regarding the probationary period and notice clauses. ## Operational checklist for the first day To facilitate your integration, ensure you have the following at hand: - Original identity document and photocopy - Signed employment contract in duplicate - Certificate of domicile or self-declaration of residence - Recent passport photo for any company badges - Information on health insurance coverage Every worker must be aware that the documentation provided at the time of hiring constitutes the personal file that will accompany their entire career in Switzerland. In case of doubts regarding the correct completion of social security or tax forms, it is always advisable to consult your company's human resources department. To monitor the progress of your net salary and understand the impact of tax and social security deductions, you can use the salary calculator provided to correctly plan your monthly budget between Italy and Switzerland.

Frequently Asked Questions
Which identity documents are required to work in Ticino?
A valid identity card or a current passport is required. These documents are essential both for processing the G permit application and for identification purposes with your employer and cantonal authorities.
Is it mandatory to have a Swiss bank account?
Although it is not a federal law, most employers in Switzerland require a current account in Swiss francs for salary payments, as this simplifies transaction management and currency exchange.
Who handles the G permit application?
The procedure for the G permit is usually handled directly by the Swiss employer, who is responsible for notifying the relevant authorities in Canton Ticino of the hiring, once the worker has provided the necessary documentation.
