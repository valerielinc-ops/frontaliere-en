Clashes in Lugano Bus Stop: Valenzano Rossi, 'There's Much Concern'

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 2 May 2026

Two minor injuries, but the political background of the clashes worries Lugano authorities. Karin Valenzano Rossi: 'It has nothing to do with nightlife'

Contesto

In brief - Clashes in Pensilina, Lugano, two minor injuries - Karin Valenzano Rossi: 'Ideological matrix, not nightlife' - Concern over political polarization in the city ## Key facts - What: Clashes between two ideologically opposed groups - When: Friday evening, May 3, 2024 - Where: Pensilina, Lugano - Who: Karin Valenzano Rossi, Head of Security for the Lugano Municipality - Injuries: Two minor injuries The Head of Security for the Lugano Municipality, Karin Valenzano Rossi, has ruled out that the nightlife played a role in the clashes that occurred on Friday evening in Pensilina. Two people were slightly injured, but it is certain that this is not a consequence of the nightlife. Valenzano Rossi also confirmed this to the Corriere del Ticino: 'The investigations will tell us what happened.' However, one thing seems clear: at the root of the disorder is a political, indeed ideological, matrix. Yesterday in the city, the May Day march took place, a legitimate and peaceful demonstration. On the margins, however, two opposing groups faced each other. 'The clashes — explains the municipal official — would seem to be a consequence of these confrontations, of polarized positions.' History, in part, repeats itself. Last October, on the occasion of an unauthorized anti-fascist demonstration, some troublemakers had targeted the march, with far-right slogans and some pyrotechnic objects. Individuals linked to the ultra world, as in this case according to what was gathered by the Corriere del Ticino. For months, the topic has engaged Lugano's politics, up to the ban on the march 'for the Patria and remigration' organized by the Swiss National Front. The Municipality is concerned.
