Strike in Italy: Impacts on Tilo Trains (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 24 February 2026

From February 27 to 28, Tilo trains will experience disruptions due to a strike in Italy. Here’s what you need to know.

Contesto

TL;DR - TILO trains disrupted Feb 27-28, 2026 due to Italy strike - Cross-border services halted; Swiss services normal - Replacement bus for Malpensa Airport via S50 line ## Key facts - Cosa: TILO train services disrupted due to national strike in Italy - Quando: From February 27, 2026, 9 PM to February 28, 2026, 8:59 PM - Dove: Affects cross-border lines RE80, S10, S30, S40, S50 - Chi: Cross-border commuters and workers in Ticino - Servizio sostitutivo: Replacement bus between Stabio and Malpensa Airport - Orari garantiti: 6 AM to 9 AM and 6 PM to 9 PM Starting from Friday, February 27 at 9 PM until Saturday, February 28 at 8:59 PM, TILO train services will face significant disruptions due to a national strike in Italy. TILO trains on the RE80, S10, S30, S40, and S50 lines will not cross the border, directly affecting cross-border commuters. However, services within Swiss territory will continue to operate normally. For those using the S50 line to reach Malpensa Airport, a replacement bus service will be available between Stabio and the airport, with no intermediate stops. This is a crucial service for cross-border commuters traveling for work or leisure. > 'We urge travelers to pay attention to announcements and information at stations and on trains,' states a note from Trenord, emphasizing the need for proper planning to avoid inconveniences.

Dettagli operativi

The national strike in Italy not only affects commuters but also has implications for cross-border workers in Ticino. With the closure of TILO lines, cross-border commuters may need to consider alternatives in their daily routes. It is advisable to check the guaranteed time slots, which will be active from 6 AM to 9 AM and from 6 PM to 9 PM, during which connections listed in the 'Minimum Guaranteed Services' will operate. This measure has been introduced to mitigate disruptions during strike events, but it may not cover all travelers' needs. For those working in Lugano, Bellinzona, or other Ticino cities, it is essential to consider potential delays and plan your travel in advance. Local authorities and employers are encouraged to provide support and updated information to ensure that cross-border commuters can continue moving without excessive interruptions.

Punti chiave

In summary, it is crucial for cross-border commuters to actively stay informed about the developments of the strike and any changes in transport services. Regularly check the Trenord website for updates and consult the information provided by train stations. 💡 If you plan to use the replacement bus, schedule your times to avoid prolonged waits.
