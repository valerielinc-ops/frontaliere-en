Solaro closes the medical clinic at Villaggio Brollo

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 30 April 2026

The Temporary Medical Clinic at Villaggio Brollo in Solaro will close early on May 21.

Contesto

In brief - The Temporary Medical Outpatient Clinic in Villaggio Brollo in Solaro will close on May 21. - Services will be transferred to the Community House in Garbagnate Milanese. - The new facility offers advanced medical services and free access without an appointment. ## Key facts - What: Early closure of the Temporary Medical Outpatient Clinic in Villaggio Brollo. - When: May 21, 2026. - Where: Solaro, Villaggio Brollo. - Who: Asst Rhodense. - Amount: Not specified. The Temporary Medical Outpatient Clinic in Villaggio Brollo in Solaro will close early. Asst Rhodense has communicated to the municipal administration the decision to terminate the agreement for the use of the spaces in Casa Brollo in Piazza Grandi ahead of schedule. The service will end on May 21, more than three months earlier than the scheduled expiry date of August 31. The early closure of the outpatient clinic was notified with at least 30 days' notice. Until May 21, it will still be possible to continue using the service, booking visits at the number 331 2626495 on Mondays and Wednesdays from 10 am to 12 pm, and Fridays from 1.30 pm to 3.30 pm. The same number can be used to receive information about the alternative services available in the area. Asst Rhodense's choice is linked to the activation of the new Community House in Garbagnate Milanese, operational since March 26 at Via Per Cesate 62. Here, a Territorial Medical Outpatient Clinic is active with a doctor present five days a week, from 8 am to 8 pm, with free access without an appointment. For fragile or non-transportable patients, the Continuity of Care Unit service is also available, which can schedule home visits. Dettagli operativi

• ## Analysis of the implications The closure of the Villaggio Brollo clinic and the transfer of services to the Casa di Comunità in Garbagnate Milanese have several implications for the residents of Solaro. The new facility offers a wider range of medical services, but the distance could be an obstacle for some citizens, particularly the elderly or those with reduced mobility. The Casa di Comunità in Garbagnate Milanese is equipped with a Territorial Medical Outpatient Clinic open five days a week, from 8 AM to 8 PM, with free access without an appointment. This represents a significant improvement compared to the Villaggio Brollo clinic, which was only open during limited hours. However, the distance between Solaro and Garbagnate Milanese could be a problem for some residents, especially those who do not have their own means of transportation. The Continuity of Care Unit service, which can schedule home visits, could mitigate this problem, but it is not clear if it will be sufficient to cover all the needs of Solaro's citizens. Furthermore, the closure of the Villaggio Brollo clinic could increase the pressure on the existing healthcare services in the area, particularly on the basic medical clinics. The Municipality of Solaro has expressed its concern about the closure of the clinic and has asked Asst Rhodense to send new doctors to the area. The Health Policy Councillor Christian Caronno emphasized the Municipality's commitment to activating the service at Villaggio Brollo and thanked the professionals who operated in the clinic.
