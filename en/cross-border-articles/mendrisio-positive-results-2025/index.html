Mendrisio: 4 years of positive balances

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 29 April 2026

The 2025 municipal account closes with a surplus of 0.8 million francs, exceeding negative estimates

Contesto

In brief - Mendrisio closes 2025 with a surplus of 0.8 million francs - Initial forecasts indicated a loss of 2.3 million - Extraordinary revenues and cost containment ## Key facts - What: 2025 Municipal Financial Report - When: 2025 - Where: Mendrisio, Switzerland - Who: Municipality of Mendrisio - Amount: 0.8 million francs surplus - Initial forecast: Loss of 2.3 million francs Mendrisio closed 2025 with a positive result, marking the fourth consecutive year of a surplus. The municipal financial report, released on Wednesday, reveals a surplus of 0.8 million francs, a figure that contrasts with the initial forecasts that predicted a loss of 2.3 million. This result was made possible by controlling the costs related to municipal autonomy and extraordinary revenues, particularly those related to tax windfalls and withholding taxes. Tax revenues showed stability in the yield from individuals and a significant revaluation of that from legal entities. Currently, the tax multiplier is 77% for individuals and 82% for legal entities. ### Future prospects The good result of 2025 opens positive prospects for the coming years of the municipal legislature. However, the 2026 budget presents a deficit of 3.4 million francs, indicating that the financial situation remains fragile. The tax multiplier will remain unchanged at 77% for individuals and 82% for legal entities. ### Historical comparisons In recent years, Mendrisio has shown a positive trend in municipal accounts. 2024 closed with a surplus of nearly 5 million francs, while 2023 saw a surplus of 131,000 francs. Analysis of Revenues and Expenditures The improvement in Mendrisio's municipal accounts has been made possible by a combination of factors. On one hand, the containment of expenses directly related to municipal autonomy has played a crucial role. This includes efficient resource management and the reduction of operating costs. On the other hand, extraordinary revenues, particularly those related to tax windfalls and withholding taxes, have significantly contributed to the surplus. ### Fiscal Stability Fiscal revenues have shown stability in the yield from natural persons and a significant revaluation of that from legal entities. This indicates that companies operating in Mendrisio are contributing substantially to the municipal budget. The stability of the yield from natural persons suggests that the tax base has remained solid, despite global economic challenges. ### Outlook for 2026 The 2026 budget presents a deficit of 3.4 million francs, indicating that the municipality will face significant financial challenges. However, the fact that the tax multiplier remains unchanged at 77% for natural persons and 82% for legal entities suggests that there will be no immediate tax increases. This could offer some relief to local residents and businesses. ### Comparisons with Other Municipalities Mendrisio is not the only municipality in Ticino to show positive results. Other municipalities, such as Lugano and Bellinzona, have also closed their budgets with surpluses. However, the situation remains fragile, and municipalities will need to continue to carefully monitor their finances to ensure long-term stability. ### Impact on Public Services The positive results of the municipal accounts can have a direct impact on the public services offered in Mendrisio.
