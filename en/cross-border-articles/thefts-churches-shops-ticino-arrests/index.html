Arrests for thefts in churches and shops

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 1 May 2026

Two Romanians arrested for thefts in churches and shops in Ticino and Grisons. The police invite to report any other incidents.

Contesto

In brief - Two Romanians arrested for thefts in churches and shops in Ticino and Grisons. - Thefts committed between April 20 and 26, 2026. - The police invite to report any other incidents. ## Key facts - What: Thefts in churches and shops - When: Between April 20 and 26, 2026 - Where: Ticino and Grisons - Who: Two Romanians aged 41 and 45 - Amount: Cash, silverware and burglary tools The cantonal police of Ticino arrested two Romanians, aged 41 and 45, residing abroad, suspected of committing thefts in churches and shops in Ticino and Grisons. The arrests took place on Sunday, April 26, in Claro, thanks to a report received by the Common Alarm Centre of the Cantonal Police. According to the investigations, the two would be responsible for thefts in at least six churches and in a self-service grocery store. ### The affected churches Personal and vehicle searches on the two suspects led to the discovery of cash, silverware and various burglary tools. The hypotheses of crime are repeated theft, repeated violation of domicile and illegal entry. The investigation is coordinated by the public prosecutor Veronica Lipari. The checks will now have to establish any possible responsibility of both in other similar thefts committed in Ticino and Grisons, but also in the rest of the national territory. For this reason, the authorities invite ecclesiastical entities, parishes and citizens who believe they have suffered a similar theft and/or have found damage to the offering boxes in the period between April 20 and 26, 2026 to contact the Public Ministry, via Pretorio 16, 6900 Lugano or to fill in the form available at the web address https://www4.ti.ch/index.php?id=115265. ### The police appeal The cantonal police of Ticino have launched an appeal to the population to report...
