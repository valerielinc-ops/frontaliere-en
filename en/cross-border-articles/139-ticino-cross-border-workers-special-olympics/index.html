139 Ticino cross-border athletes at Special Olympics National Summer Games

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 28 April 2026

139 athletes with intellectual disabilities from Ticino will participate in the Special Olympics National Summer Games in Zug from May 28 to 31, 2026.

Contesto

In brief - 139 athletes from Ticino at the National Summer Games - 1600 total participants with intellectual disabilities - 16 sports on the program ## Key facts - What: National Summer Games of Special Olympics - When: 28-31 May 2026 - Where: Zug, Switzerland - Who: Athletes with intellectual disabilities, including 139 from Ticino - Amount: 1600 total participants From 28 to 31 May 2026, Zug becomes the heart of inclusive sports in Switzerland, hosting the National Summer Games of Special Olympics Switzerland. Athletes from Italian-speaking Switzerland, including 139 participants from Ticino, join over 1600 competitors for four days of sports, inclusion, and celebration. Competitions will take place in 16 disciplines, including athletics, basketball, bocce, golf, artistic gymnastics, pétanque, sailing, swimming, tennis, and table tennis. The event, held every two years alternating between summer and winter editions, is the largest of its kind in Switzerland for people with intellectual disabilities. In addition to the athletes, 600 coaches and delegations from 11 countries will be present, including Austria, France, Germany, Gibraltar, Italy, Liechtenstein, Monaco, Poland, San Marino, Ukraine, and Uzbekistan. The opening ceremony will take place on Thursday, 28 May, with a parade of athletes, the Special Olympics anthem, and the lighting of the 'flame of hope.' Among the expected guests is Federal Councillor and Minister of Sport Martin Pfister, while singer Marc Sway will present a song created for the occasion. The ceremony will be hosted by Christa Rigozzi and Nik Hartmann. ### The philosophy of Special Olympics At the heart of the event is the philosophy of Special Olympics, which emphasizes individual abilities and personal commitment. For cross-border workers residing in Ticino and working in Switzerland, this event can be an occasion to actively participate as volunteers or simply to support local athletes. ### How to Participate as a Volunteer If you are a cross-border worker interested in participating as a volunteer, you can contact the local organizing committee for information on how to join the team of volunteers. The event involves up to 1500 volunteers and offers a unique opportunity to contribute to an inclusive and meaningful sporting event. ### Side Activities and Olympic Village In addition to the sporting competitions, the Olympic Village set up at the OYM hall will offer interactive activities, stands, and refreshment points. The proximity between the competition venues favors the meeting between athletes, the public, and volunteers, contributing to creating a cohesive and participatory atmosphere. This is an excellent way for cross-border workers to experience the Games up close and support Ticino athletes. ### Support for Families The event also includes a Family Program designed for about 200 family members, with moments of exchange and support. This program offers a valuable opportunity for the families of athletes to actively participate and share the experience of the Games. ### Opportunities for Athletes For many athletes, participation in the National Games represents an important step towards the World Games in Santiago, Chile 2027. Frequently Asked Questions
How can I become a volunteer at the National Summer Games of Special Olympics?
To become a volunteer, contact the local organizing committee through the official website of Special Olympics Switzerland. All necessary information on how to join the volunteer team and contribute to the event will be provided.
Which sports disciplines are scheduled for the National Summer Games?
The scheduled sports disciplines include athletics, basketball, bocce, golf, artistic gymnastics, pétanque, sailing, swimming, tennis, and table tennis. In total, there will be 16 sports disciplines in which athletes will compete.
What is the main objective of the National Summer Games of Special Olympics?
The main objective of the National Summer Games is to promote inclusion and sports for people with intellectual disabilities. The event focuses on the individual abilities and personal commitment of the athletes, offering them the opportunity to compete in a balanced and inclusive environment.
