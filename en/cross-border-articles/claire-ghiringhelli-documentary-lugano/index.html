In brief - Documentary about Claire Ghiringhelli screening in Lugano - Story of personal and sports revival - Goal: Paris 2024 Paralympics ## Key facts - What: Documentary about Claire Ghiringhelli - When: May 8, 2026, 10:30 AM - Where: Cinema Iride, Lugano - Who: Director Edoardo Nerboni, Locarno Rowing Society - Achievement: Third place at the 2025 European Championships in Plovdiv On Sunday morning at the Iride in Lugano, the documentary 'Tenére, tenére, tenére fino alla fine' will be screened, telling the story of Claire Ghiringhelli, a paralympic rowing athlete affiliated with the Locarno Rowing Society. The film, directed by Edoardo Nerboni, follows Claire's human and sports journey, from the diagnosis of a tumor in the spine to her rebirth through sport. Claire, a mother and professional, has transformed a radical challenge into a new opportunity for life, demonstrating unshakable will and daily discipline. ### Claire's story The documentary takes the viewer along Claire's path, showing not only the athlete but also the person behind the challenge. Among the 2025 results, Claire achieved third place at the European Championships in Plovdiv, victory in the World Cup in Lucerne, and fourth place at the World Championships in Shanghai. These milestones represent stages of a bigger story, that of someone who chooses not to give up. 📊 Concrete data: - 2025 European Championships in Plovdiv: 3rd place with a time of 8:45.23 in the paralympic single scull. - 2025 World Cup in Lucerne: Victory with a time of 8:32.15. - 2025 World Championships in Shanghai: 4th place with a time of 8:40.56. ### The event in Lugano The documentary screening is scheduled for Sunday, May 8, 2026, at 10:30 AM, at Cinema Iride in Lugano.
