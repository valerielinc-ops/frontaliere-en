The city of Bellinzona, known for its medieval castles and strategic location on the border between Switzerland and Italy, is going through a difficult period. In recent months, many shops have closed or announced their closure. Just in March, four shops ceased operations or announced their closure: Benetton, Prinzess, and Al Pino Outdoor Apparel on Viale Stazione, and Boutique Particolari in the Municipio area. San Giovanni is also considering closing its doors. The situation has been described as 'sad' by some residents, who fear for the city's future. 'The future looks sad. Bellinzona is now a dormitory town,' said one resident. The city seems to have become a place where people move to sleep, but not to live and work. The commercial crisis in Bellinzona is not an isolated phenomenon. Many Swiss cities are facing similar challenges, related to online competition and changes in consumer behavior. The commercial crisis in Bellinzona: causes and solutions The commercial crisis in Bellinzona is a complex phenomenon with multiple causes. One of the main causes is online competition, which is radically changing the way people shop. Many traditional shops are unable to compete with the prices and convenience offered by e-commerce sites. For example, according to data from the Swiss Union of Merchants (USC), in 2022, online commerce in Switzerland reached a volume of 10.4 billion francs, with a 12% increase compared to the previous year. ## Changes in consumer behavior Another cause is the change in consumer behavior. People tend to spend less and choose more affordable products. This is leading to a reduction in demand for luxury and high-end goods, which were traditionally sold in Bellinzona's shops. According to a survey conducted by market research company GfK, 62% of Swiss consumers reduced their spending on clothing and accessories in 2022. ## Mobility and parking issues Finally, the city is also facing mobility and parking issues. The historic area of Bellinzona is often congested, and parking spaces are limited, which can discourage people from visiting the city. For example, according to data from the City of Bellinzona, in 2022, 3.5 million visitors were counted, with a 10% decrease compared to the previous year. ## Urban revitalization projects To address these challenges, the city of Bellinzona is working on urban revitalization projects and supporting local commercial activities. A fund of 2 million francs has been established to support small businesses, and projects have been launched to improve mobility and parking in the city.
