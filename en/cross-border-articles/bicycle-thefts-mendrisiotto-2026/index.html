Two arrests for bicycle thefts in Mendrisiotto

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 27 April 2026

Two Romanian citizens arrested with three dismantled bicycles and burglary tools. Police remind prevention tips to avoid thefts.

Contesto

In brief - Two Romanian citizens arrested for bicycle thefts in Mendrisiotto - Three dismantled bicycles and burglary tools found in the vehicle - Recent e-bike and bicycle thefts in Ticino ## Key facts - What: Arrests for bicycle thefts - When: Saturday, April 25, 2026 - Where: Chiasso, Mendrisiotto - Who: Public Prosecutor's Office and Cantonal Police - Amount: Three dismantled bicycles and burglary tools Two Romanian citizens, a 24-year-old and a 21-year-old residing in Romania, were arrested last Saturday, April 25, in Mendrisiotto. The operation was launched following a report of non-payment for fuel at a service station in Coldrerio by a vehicle with Romanian plates. The Cantonal Police, in collaboration with the Municipal Police of Chiasso and the Federal Customs and Border Security Office (UDSC), intercepted the vehicle and the two men near the Chiasso railway station. During the searches, three dismantled bicycles were found inside the vehicle, with wheels separated from the frames, some of which had already been reported stolen, as well as burglary tools. The investigations will now establish any possible responsibility of the two men in relation to recent thefts of e-bikes and bicycles that occurred in Ticino in the past few weeks. Analysis of the implications for cross-border workers The recent bicycle thefts in Mendrisiotto and other areas of Ticino have a significant impact on cross-border workers who use bicycles daily to commute between the Canton of Ticino and Lombardy. Bicycle security is a crucial aspect for those who work in Switzerland and reside in Italy, especially in areas like Chiasso and Mendrisio, where the border is very close and bicycle traffic is intense. ### Comparison with the previous situation In recent months, there has been an increase in bicycle and e-bike thefts in Ticino. This phenomenon has led the authorities to strengthen controls and raise awareness among the population about the importance of adopting preventive measures. The collaboration between the Cantonal Police, the Municipal Police of Chiasso, and the Federal Customs and Border Security Office (UDSC) represents an important step in combating this type of crime. ### Future scenarios With the increase in bicycle thefts, it is likely that Swiss and Italian authorities will intensify their collaboration to prevent and combat this phenomenon. Cross-border workers who use bicycles to commute between the two countries will need to be particularly attentive and adopt all necessary measures to protect their means of transport. ### Procedure in case of theft In case of theft, it is essential to act promptly.
