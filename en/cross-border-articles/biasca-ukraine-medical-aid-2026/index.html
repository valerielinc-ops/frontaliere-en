From Biasca to Ukraine: six volunteers on humanitarian mission (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 9 May 2026

Six Hunpa volunteers leave Biasca to provide medical aid in Ukraine, with two teams splitting to cover the northeast and south of the country.

Contesto

In brief - Six volunteers depart from Biasca for Ukraine with medical aid - Two teams will operate in the northeast and south of the country - Objective: Medical support and delivery of supplies ## Key facts - What: Humanitarian mission with medical support and delivery of supplies - When: From May 9 to 17, 2026 - Where: Ukraine, divided into northeast and south - Who: Six volunteers from Hunpa, three women and three men, with nursing skills - Amount: Not specified - Destination: Pediatric hospital in the Mykolaiv region - Material: Products for an infant with special needs A new mission to Eastern Europe for six volunteers from 'Hunpa,' an acronym for 'Humanitarian nursing & paramedical aid.' The operational base is Biasca, with activities ranging from Ticino to the world. The group, composed of three women and three men, includes volunteers specialized in nursing. Two vehicles are used: a support vehicle and an ambulance equipped for emergency interventions, also usable as a mobile laboratory. The destination is Ukraine, with a division into two teams once the border is reached. One team will proceed to the northeast of the country, while the other will head south. The main objectives are medical support in the areas most affected by the conflict and the delivery of medical supplies. In particular, a specific collection of products will be delivered for an infant with special needs, hospitalized in a pediatric hospital in the Mykolaiv region. ### The mission and objectives The mission, which will take place from May 9 to 17, 2026, represents another commitment by Hunpa to provide humanitarian support in conflict zones.
