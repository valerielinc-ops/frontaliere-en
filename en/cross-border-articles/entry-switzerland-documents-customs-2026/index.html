Implications for cross‑border workers Entering Switzerland as a cross‑border worker requires careful preparation, especially for those working in the canton of Ticino. Cross‑border workers must ensure they have all necessary documents and understand customs rules to avoid problems. For example, for people living in Chiasso or Como and working in Lugano, it is essential to know that the value limit for declaring goods is 200 CHF. ## Entry procedure The entry procedure into Switzerland is relatively straightforward, but being prepared is important. Cross‑border workers must: 1. Hold a valid work permit, such as a G permit, which can be obtained from the Federal Office for Migration (SEM). 2. Have a valid identity document, such as an ID card or passport. 3. Declare goods that exceed a certain value, such as jewellery or items worth more than 200 CHF. ## Operational checklist Here is an operational checklist for cross‑border workers: - Valid work permit - Valid identity document - Declaration of goods over 200 CHF - Valid health insurance in Switzerland ## Consequences of irregular entry Irregular entry into Switzerland can have serious consequences, such as fines and expulsion. For instance, under Swiss law, irregular entry can be punished with a fine of up to 10,000 CHF.
