Leading the way is Uniformes – Behind the Uniform, where director Romain Graf chooses to go beyond stereotypes, portraying the more human and social side of the police. An intense immersion into the daily life of a Geneva police station, filled with tough decisions, eloquent silences, and team spirit. Following a botched intervention, a police officer fights between life and death. The opening of an internal investigation, aimed at clarifying the incident, puts the involved colleagues under pressure: did they really act according to the rules? The interrogations leave their mark, forcing the officers to question themselves to the core of their private lives. From fiction to documentary, The Oligarch and the Art Dealer (L’oligarca e il mercante d’arte) by Andreas Dalsgaard brings to the screen the controversial Bouvier case, revealing the behind-the-scenes of an exclusive elite where even trust seems to have a price. When the Russian oligarch Dmitry Rybolovlev accuses his trusted Swiss art dealer Yves Bouvier of defrauding him for over a billion dollars, a feud is sparked that sweeps through the international art market: a murky world where masterpieces become currency, fortunes are hidden in plain sight, and power dictates every rule. ### America, Let’s Talk America, Let’s Talk (America, parliamone) by Fabrizio Bonolini and...
