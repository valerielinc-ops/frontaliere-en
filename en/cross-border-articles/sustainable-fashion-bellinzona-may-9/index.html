Sustainable fashion in Bellinzona: reuse and repair day

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 28 April 2026

On Saturday, May 9th in Bellinzona, workshops, upcycling, and sheep shearing to raise awareness against fast fashion

Contesto

In brief - Clothing Repair Day in Bellinzona - Upcycling workshops and sheep shearing - Free entry and open to all ## Key facts - What: Clothing Repair Day - When: Saturday, May 9, 2024 - Where: Department of Territory headquarters in Bellinzona - Who: Organized in collaboration with Sartoria sociale, SOS Ticino, RiCircolo, Curiolino, Pro Verzasca, and other partners - Cost: Free entry - Activities: Clothing repairs, upcycling workshops, creative embroidery workshops, textile industry ateliers, participatory installations, demonstrations on transforming raw wool On Saturday, May 9, the Department of Territory headquarters in Bellinzona will host Clothing Repair Day, an event dedicated to the circular economy and sustainable fashion. The initiative, born in collaboration with various local organizations such as Sartoria sociale, SOS Ticino, RiCircolo, Curiolino, Pro Verzasca, and other partners, aims to raise awareness about reuse and the environmental impact of the textile sector, one of the most polluting globally. Throughout the day, from 10 AM to 5 PM, the public can participate in a rich program of practical and interactive activities: on-demand clothing repairs, upcycling workshops, creative embroidery workshops (visible mending), ateliers dedicated to the textile industry, participatory installations, and demonstrations on transforming raw wool with natural dyes. Frequently Asked Questions
What is the Clothing Repair Day?
The Clothing Repair Day is an event dedicated to the circular economy and sustainable fashion, organized to raise awareness about reuse and the environmental impact of the textile sector. The event includes upcycling workshops, creative embroidery workshops, textile supply chain ateliers, and demonstrations on the transformation of raw wool.
Who organizes the event?
The event is organized in collaboration with various local organizations, including Sartoria sociale, SOS Ticino, RiCircolo, Curiolino, and Pro Verzasca. These organizations work together to promote sustainable practices and raise community awareness about the importance of reuse and repair.
How can I participate?
To participate, visit the website of the Department of Territory for detailed information about the event. Registration is not required for most activities, but it is advisable to check the website for any updates or changes.
