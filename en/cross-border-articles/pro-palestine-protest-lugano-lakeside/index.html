Pro-Palestine march takes over Lugano lakeside

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 2 May 2026

Around fifty protesters marched down Via Nassa and along the lakeside, blocking traffic and sparking mixed reactions.

Contesto

In brief - Around fifty pro-Palestine demonstrators marched in Lugano - The protest blocked traffic along the lakeside for about 30 minutes - Two officers on foot and a police motorcycle managed the situation - The demonstration was not authorized, violating art. 13 of the Cantonal Police Law (LCPol) ## Key facts - What: Unauthorized pro-Palestine march - When: April 30, 2026, 6:30 PM - Where: Via Nassa and lakeside of Lugano, between Parco Ciani and Lido - Who: Around fifty demonstrators, including some residents of Lugano and Bellinzona - Fine: Expected fine for unauthorized demonstration: CHF 500-1,000 A march in support of Palestine suddenly appeared in the center of Lugano this evening, surprising both drivers and residents. As shown in a video sent to us by one of our readers, around fifty demonstrators first crossed Via Nassa towards Lake Ceresio, shouting pro-Palestine slogans, and then turned back towards the center, occupying a lane of the lakeside road. The demonstration caused traffic disruptions between Parco Ciani and Lido, with queues up to 200 meters long. The account given to Tio by an eyewitness of the impromptu demonstration also mentions two officers on foot supporting a police motorcycle that was blocking traffic towards the city center. 'I find it absurd that, as usual, people do whatever they want - says the person who reported the incident - even blocking traffic. Doing this on a bank holiday in a city that already has so many problems doesn't seem very smart to me. But that's just my opinion' they conclude. ### Citizens' reactions The demonstration sparked mixed reactions among citizens.
