Frequently Asked Questions
When the Swiss franc is lowered against the euro, how does the purchasing power of border workers behave in the Canton of Ticino?
The positive exchange rate trend can positively influence the purchasing power of frontier workers, as they can buy more products and services with the same amount of money.
How many border workers work in the Canton of Ticino according to official data for 2022?
About 78,000 workers, equal to about 22% of the Ticino workforce.
What documents are needed to apply for a residence permit for border workers in Ticino?
To apply for a residence permit in Ticino, border workers must present a health insurance certificate. The cost of the permit is CHF 30. In the absence of such a certificate, it is possible to obtain a residence permit for a limited period, according to the provisions of the Information Service of the Canton of Ticino.
How much does a residence certificate for border workers in Ticino cost?
The cost to obtain a residence certificate in Ticino is CHF 20. To apply, border workers must present both a residence permit and a valid health insurance certificate, as specified by the Statistical Office of the Canton of Ticino.
What is the impact of the euro-franc exchange rate on the choice of the labour sector for frontier workers?
The variability of the euro-franc exchange rate can influence the choice of the employment sector. Border crossers may prefer sectors that are more resilient to currency fluctuations, such as finance, over others such as tourism, to mitigate personal economic risks.
