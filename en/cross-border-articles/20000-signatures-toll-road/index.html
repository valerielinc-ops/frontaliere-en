20,000 signatures against Lombardy toll road (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 5 May 2026

Angelo Orsenigo and Gigi Ponti, regional councilors of the Pd, have collected 20,000 signatures against the toll on the Milan-Meda Pedemontana

Contesto

In brief - 20,000 signatures against the toll on the Pedemontana Milano-Meda - Angelo Orsenigo and Gigi Ponti deliver the signatures to the president of the regional council - The toll risks diverting traffic onto the intermunicipal network ## Key facts - What: Petition against the toll on the Pedemontana Milano-Meda - When: May 5, 2026 - Where: Lombardy - Who: Angelo Orsenigo and Gigi Ponti, regional councilors of the Pd - Amount: 900 million euros Angelo Orsenigo and Gigi Ponti, regional councilors of the Pd, delivered this afternoon another 3,350 signatures to the president of the regional council, Federico Romani, and addressed to the president of the Regional Council Fontana to urge him to reconsider the toll on the Pedemontana in the stretch between Milano and Meda. Previously, about 10,000 had already been deposited, while in the last few days a petition, promoted by several committees, has reached almost 6,000 signatures, bringing the total number of adherents from the various initiatives to nearly 20,000. The goal is to obtain a clear answer from the Lombardy Region on the intention to introduce the toll on the B2 Milano-Meda stretch of Pedemontana. A choice of this Council that, in addition to heavily burdening citizens, commuters, and businesses in Brianza, will have significant consequences on local traffic and the quality of life of the communities involved. Frequently Asked Questions
What is the goal of the petition against the toll on the Pedemontana Milano-Meda?
The goal of the petition is to obtain a clear answer from the Lombardy Region regarding the intention to introduce a toll on the B2 Milano–Meda section of the Pedemontana. The promoters of the petition believe that the introduction of the toll will heavily burden citizens, commuters, and businesses in Brianza, with significant consequences for local traffic and the quality of life in the affected communities.
What are the implications for cross-border workers?
The introduction of the toll on the Pedemontana Milano-Meda could increase transportation costs for cross-border workers, forcing them to seek alternative routes that may be longer and more congested. This could have a negative impact on the quality of life and satisfaction of cross-border workers, who already face challenges related to living costs and tax differences between Italy and Switzerland.
What can cross-border workers do to oppose the toll?
Cross-border workers can join the petition promoted by civic committees and regional councilors of the Pd. Additionally, they can contact their political representatives to express their concerns and request a reconsideration of the decision. It is important that cross-border workers are informed and involved in the decision-making process to ensure that their needs are taken into account.
