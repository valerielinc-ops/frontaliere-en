Health insurance premiums: political agreement to implement initiatives (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 13 May 2026

The parliamentary commission decides to apply the initiatives on health insurance premiums without waiting for the definition of financial coverage

Contesto

In brief - Health insurance premium initiatives approved by the people on 28 September 2025 - Entry into force independent of funding definition - Discussion on financial coverage will continue separately - What: Health insurance premium initiatives - When: 28 September 2025 - Where: Canton of Ticino - Who: Parliamentary Commission ‘Management and Finance’ - Amount: Not yet specified The Parliamentary Commission ‘Management and Finance’ of the Canton of Ticino has reached an agreement on the delicate dossier of the health insurance premium initiatives approved by the people on 28 September. The socialist initiative aims to limit premiums to 10% of available income, while the league initiative proposes to make them fully deductible from taxes. According to La Regione, the shared principle is clear: both initiatives must enter into force independently of the definition of their funding. The discussion on financial coverage will therefore continue separately, without blocking the application of the popular vote. This was confirmed by the outgoing president of the commission, Fabrizio Sirica, according to whom the commissioners agree on the need to set a date for entry into force immediately. The issue should reach the Grand Council in June, with separate reports dedicated to both implementation and funding. For the socialist Ivo Durisch, this is ‘an important step forward,’ which guarantees respect for the popular will. The League is also in favor: the group leader Boris Bignasca emphasizes, however, that the league’s proposal would be applicable more quickly than that of the PS, considered more complex. ### Implications for cross-border workers The health insurance premium initiatives will have a significant impact on cross-border workers who work in Ticino.
