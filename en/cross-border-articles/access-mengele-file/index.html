Access to Mengele dossier: what changes for cross-border workers (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 6 May 2026

Swiss intelligence services now allow access to the Mengele dossier at the Federal Archives, in accordance with the 2001 decision.

Contesto

In brief Access to the Mengele dossier now possible at the Swiss Federal Archives The decision stems from a reassessment and a practice from 2001 The dossier was handed over in 2001 to the Swiss Federal Archives (SFA) by the Police Service The request for consultation is subject to evaluations by the Swiss Intelligence Service (SIC) The final decision will be communicated soon ## Key facts - What: Access to the Mengele dossier at the Swiss Federal Archives - When: Announcement on May 4, 2026 - Where: Switzerland, Federal Archives, Bellinzona - Who: Swiss Intelligence Service (SIC), Federal Administrative Court - Amount: Not specified The Swiss Confederation’s Intelligence Service (SIC) announced that, following a reassessment, the dossier on Nazi criminal Josef Mengele will be accessible at the Swiss Federal Archives (SFA). The decision originates from a previous ruling in 2001, which provided for access to archival files evaluated by the Bergier Commission. The dossier, compiled by the police service of the Federal Public Ministry, was handed over to the SFA in the same year. The possibility of consultation, previously rejected, can now be requested after a review involving the Federal Administrative Court (FAC). The final decision will be communicated shortly, with details on procedures and conditions still to be defined. The Mengele dossier, known as the 'Angel of Death,' contains documents related to the activities of the Nazi doctor, responsible for inhumane experiments at Auschwitz-Birkenau. After the war, Mengele managed to evade Nuremberg trials, adopting false identities and relocating first to Argentina, then to Brazil, where he died in 1979. Practical Analysis: Implications and Scenarios For cross-border workers residing in Italy and employed in Switzerland, this decision opens new historical knowledge and judicial investigation scenarios. The possibility of consulting the Mengele dossier at the Swiss Federal Archives allows access to documents previously considered confidential or protected, contributing to clarifying Mengele's contacts and activities in the Swiss context. Swiss law, in line with the 2001 legislation and the Bergier Commission's practice, establishes that archival funds are more accessible than in the past. For cross-border workers, this opening can also mean greater historical awareness and a strengthening of the sense of justice regarding events linked to Nazi crimes. The possibility of obtaining the documents, upon request and evaluation, could lead to new discoveries or confirmations of Mengele's possible contacts with Switzerland, such as his 1956 vacations or visits to the canton of Zurich. Comparing the past with the present, this opening marks an important step towards transparency. Previously, the dossier was subject to an extended protection period, and access requests had been denied, including the last one in February 2026. Now, however, a new process is envisaged that could lead to consultation, with methods and conditions yet to be defined. For border residents, this decision represents an example of how collaboration between authorities and courts can contribute to shedding light on dark pages of the past. Furthermore, the adoption of more open practices for archival funds can stimulate further access requests and in-depth analyses on historical and judicial topics related to World War II, double taxation, and Italian-Swiss relations. Practical Actions and Procedures for Cross-Border Workers For those wishing to consult the Mengele dossier, the first step is to submit a formal request to the SIC, which evaluates each application thoroughly, balancing research interests with the protection of privacy rights. The procedure involves filling out a specific form and attaching any relevant documentation that verifies the purpose of the consultation. It is advisable to first check the official SIC or Federal Archives website to verify the request procedures, eligibility criteria, and any deadlines. Requests can also be submitted by individuals involved in an ongoing legal case, such as a judicial proceeding initiated in February 2026. Once the application is submitted, the SIC will carry out the necessary verifications and inform about the outcome. The decision will include any access conditions, such as consultation hours, duration, and required documents. Following these instructions carefully is essential to avoid refusals or delays. To facilitate the process, it is recommended to use the online tools available on the SIC website, such as the salary calculator to verify potential taxes or reimbursements, and to prepare a list of identification documents and motivations. Frequently Asked Questions
How can I request access to the Mengele dossier?
To request access, you need to submit an application to the Swiss Intelligence Service (SIC), which will evaluate it according to Swiss law. The request requires filling out an official form and attaching any relevant documentation explaining the purpose. The final decision will be communicated through methods and conditions to be defined, and the process can be followed via the official SIC website.
What documents are needed for the application?
It is advisable to prepare an identity document, a detailed motivation, and any relevant documentation attesting to the purpose of the request. The exact procedures are available on the official SIC website, which also provides the online application form.
When will it be possible to consult the dossier?
The final decision will be communicated shortly. The request will be evaluated by the SIC, which may also establish specific access modalities and conditions. Consultation could be possible within the next few months, subject to court approval and the definition of operational procedures.
