Spring bank holidays: more traffic on road and rail (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 4 May 2026

SBB increases capacity with 58 special trains and 130,000 extra seats. USTRA advises alternatives to avoid congestion.

Contesto

In brief - 58 special trains and 130,000 additional seats - Lenzburg works affect schedules - USTRA advises highway alternatives ## Key facts - What: FFS service enhancement and USTRA advice - When: Spring bank holidays 2026 - Where: Switzerland and Ticino - Who: FFS and USTRA - Amount: 130,000 additional seats This year, in anticipation of increased travel for the Ascension and Pentecost long weekends, the FFS have decided to enhance services between German-speaking Switzerland and Ticino with 58 additional special trains and around 130,000 extra seats. This represents a 25% increase compared to 2025, as stated in today's press release. However, the railways note that certain trains may still experience high passenger numbers. The FFS therefore recommend planning your journey in advance and booking your seats. ### Extra carriages To increase the number of available seats, the FFS will also add some carriages to regular trains, including EuroCity services to Italy. Additionally, on Ascension Thursday and the Saturday before Pentecost, an extra TILO RE80 service will be offered from Locarno to Milano Porta Garibaldi. These services will not pass through Chiasso as usual, but via Varese, with intermediate stops at Gallarate and Rho Fiera Milano. ### Better to book As mentioned, the FFS recommend planning your journey in advance by checking the special train services in the online timetable (recognizable by the absence of a line number), booking your seats, and taking into account the mandatory bike reservation. Thanks to the graphic representation in the FFS Mobile app and on FFS.ch, you can select your desired seats on most national long-distance InterCity trains, to Italy, and in first class to France. Tips for motorists The weekends of Ascension (May 14-17), Pentecost (May 22-25), and Corpus Christi (June 4-7) are expected to be 'busy' on the roads as well. For this reason, the Federal Roads Office (FEDRO) has communicated some tips for motorists. Holiday and festive travel will cause traffic disruptions, notes FEDRO, particularly along the north-south axes of the Gotthard (A2) and San Bernardino (A13). Alternatives to the Gotthard motorway tunnel include the Great St. Bernard Pass, the Simplon Pass, and shuttle trains through Lötschberg and Simplon. The Gotthard Pass will open to traffic in the coming days and will serve as an alternative route. ### Avoid congesting city centers Intense traffic along the main axes, in the presence of queues, often leads to increased traffic exiting the motorway early towards ordinary roads, causing inconvenience to residents of neighboring localities and worsening traffic problems in the affected region. For this reason, FEDRO recommends continuing the journey on the motorway even in situations of congestion, planning sufficient time for travel, and avoiding diverting onto the secondary network. ### Construction sites on the Simplon and at Kerenzerberg On the A9 stretch near Casermetta al Simplon, traffic will be restored to two lanes on public holidays to avoid traffic restrictions due to the presence of the construction site. For more information: a9-simplon.ch.
