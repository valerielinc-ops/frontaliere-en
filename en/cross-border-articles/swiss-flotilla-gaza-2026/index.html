Swiss Gaza Flotilla: Eight Safe and Sound

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 30 April 2026

The eight Swiss who departed with the Global Sumud Flotilla are safe and sound after the Israeli navy's intervention.

Contesto

In brief - Eight Swiss citizens who departed for Gaza are safe and sound - The Israeli navy stopped the boats off the coast of Greece - Greenpeace Switzerland asks Federal Councillor Ignazio Cassis to intervene ## Key facts - What: Stop of the Global Sumud Flotilla - When: April 30, 2026 - Where: Greek territorial waters - Who: Israeli navy - Amount: 175 people involved The eight Swiss citizens who departed for Gaza with the Global Sumud Flotilla are safe. This is what an activist on one of the boats told Keystone-ATS after the Israeli navy stopped about twenty boats and 175 people off the coast of Greece last night. 'All the boats with Swiss citizens on board are safe and sound and are currently in Greek territorial waters,' said Anne Rochat from Vaud. Last autumn, a Swiss delegation had already participated in the Global Sumud Flotilla. In total, 19 Swiss citizens were among the more than 450 activists. Then, too, the Israeli armed forces had intercepted the boats and temporarily detained the passengers. In a statement released today, Greenpeace Switzerland says it has written to Federal Councillor and Foreign Minister Ignazio Cassis 'to ask him to take immediate measures to defend international law and guarantee the protection of the flotilla.'
