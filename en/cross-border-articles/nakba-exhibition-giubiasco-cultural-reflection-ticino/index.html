Far from the beaten path, the Galleria Job in Giubiasco presents the exhibition "Collettiva Nakba," starting from January 25, 2026. This event promises to be much more than a simple exhibition; it's a true invitation to reflect on one of the most complex geopolitical issues of our time. The exhibition, which will remain open until February 19, presents itself as a "poetic reaction to colonial fury," an exploration of the catastrophe—the meaning of the Arabic term 'Nakba'—through a deliberately heterogeneous collection of works. It's not just about images, but an assembly of perspectives, words, and books aimed at shaking the visitor's conscience. Dettagli operativi

The curatorial approach, led by Marino Cattaneo, is explicit in its intent: to provide "materials to listen, reflect, try to understand, rediscover a political conscience, imagine a different world." The exhibition doesn't limit itself to presenting photographs of a walled Jerusalem but aims to construct a more "tortuous" and complex reading path that escapes media oversimplification. The intellectual core of the exhibition is well represented by a quote from the Israeli historian Ilan Pappé, from his essay "The End of Israel": > "The centuries-old attempt by the West, led by the United Kingdom, to impose a Jewish state on an Arab country seems to have come to an end. [...] Their only hope for the future will be a willingness to live as citizens with equal rights in a liberated and de-colonized Palestine." This clear stance defines the critical perimeter of the event, positioning it as a cultural and political act. For visitors, it's an opportunity to delve into a perspective often marginalized in public debate. ## Practical Information - Where: Galleria Job, Via Borghetto 8, 6512 Giubiasco - When: From Sunday, January 25 to Thursday, February 19, 2026 - Hours: Wednesday and Thursday from 14:00 to 18:00, or by appointment by calling +41 79 621 37 38.

Punti chiave

For a cross-border worker, life in Ticino goes beyond the home-to-work commute. Events like the "Collettiva Nakba" represent a precious opportunity to integrate into the social and cultural fabric of the canton, transforming the time spent across the border into a richer and more complete experience. Dedicating a few hours to such an exhibition, easily reachable in Giubiasco, means allowing for a space of intellectual decompression, a way to process the world's complexity outside of daily work pressures.
