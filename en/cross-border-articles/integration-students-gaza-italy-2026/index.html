Gaza students in Italian schools: 1.5 million for integration (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 6 May 2026

The Ministry of Education allocates 1.5 million for the integration of students from Gaza in Italian schools.

Contesto

In brief - The Ministry allocates 1.5 million for the integration of students from Gaza. - Italian L2 labs and disciplinary paths planned. - Deadline for school participation: May 26, 2026. ## Key facts - What: Call for the school integration of students from Gaza. - When: Published on May 6, 2026. - Where: Italy. - Who: Ministry of Education and Merit. - Amount: 1.5 million euros. The Ministry of Education and Merit has published a call for proposals worth 1.5 million euros to support the reception and school integration of students from Gaza. The measure is aimed at state and non-commercial paritary schools and the Provincial Centers for Adult Education (CPIA). The goal is to ensure educational continuity and the right to study for boys fleeing the Palestinian territory. Schools can activate Italian L2 language labs and training paths in the main disciplinary areas, from the linguistic-artistic-expressive to the mathematical-scientific-technological. The call also provides for the active involvement of the territory, with partnerships and collaborations with administrations, associations, foundations, third sector entities, universities, research centers, and local networks. Minister Giuseppe Valditara commented on the measure, recalling a previous commitment: 'I have kept a promise made to the young Palestinian students we have welcomed among us so that they would not lose the chance of a worthy future.' Schools interested can submit an application for participation by 6:00 PM on May 26, 2026. ### Implications for cross-border workers For cross-border workers living in Ticino and working in Italy, this call represents an opportunity to contribute to the integration of students from Gaza. Practical Analysis The integration of students from Gaza into Italian schools represents a significant step towards inclusion and educational equality. Italian schools can activate Italian L2 language labs and training courses in the main disciplinary areas, offering concrete support to students in difficulty. The active involvement of the territory is a key element of the call. Educational institutions are called to build partnerships and collaborations with central and local administrations, associations, foundations, third-sector entities, universities, research centers, and networks already operational at the local level. This integrated approach could favor a more inclusive and supportive learning environment for students from Gaza. ### Concrete Scenarios 1. Italian L2 Labs: Schools could organize Italian language labs for foreigners, offering linguistic support to students from Gaza. These labs could be integrated into the school curriculum and could include conversation, reading, and writing activities. 2. Disciplinary Paths: Schools could develop training courses in the main disciplinary areas, such as the linguistic-artistic-expressive and mathematical-scientific-technological fields. These paths could be customized to meet the specific needs of students from Gaza. 3. Involvement of Families: Schools could organize meetings with the families of students to discuss their needs and the available resources.
