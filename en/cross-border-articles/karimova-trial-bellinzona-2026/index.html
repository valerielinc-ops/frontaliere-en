Karimova trial in Bellinzona: money laundering and corruption charges

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 29 April 2026

Gulnara Karimova, daughter of the former Uzbek president, is accused of financial crimes. The trial is held in Bellinzona.

Contesto

In brief - Gulnara Karimova accused of money laundering and corruption - Trial in Bellinzona, Lombard Odier bank involved - Funds in question amount to hundreds of millions of dollars ## Key facts - What: Trial for financial crimes - When: From April to May 2026 - Where: Federal Criminal Court of Bellinzona - Who: Gulnara Karimova, former wealth manager of Lombard Odier, Lombard Odier bank - Amount: Hundreds of millions of dollars and euros The trial of Gulnara Karimova, daughter of the former president of Uzbekistan, began on Monday morning at the Federal Criminal Court in Bellinzona. Karimova is accused of money laundering, breach of trust, corruption, and heading a criminal organization. The main defendant is not present in court: imprisoned in her country until 2028, she reportedly wanted to participate in the proceedings, but the Uzbek judicial authorities did not allow it. Also on the dock are a former wealth manager of Lombard Odier, as well as the Geneva private bank itself, both prosecuted for money laundering. The former right-hand man of Gulnara Karimova, prosecuted in particular for aggravated money laundering, corruption, and forgery of titles, completes the picture of the co-defendants. He is also not present. ### Detailed charges Gulnara Karimova, who was for a time ambassador of Uzbekistan to the UN in Geneva, is accused of having opened bank accounts at Swiss financial institutions through nominees. The amount of funds that would have been deposited there, and then transferred to obscure their criminal origin, is estimated at hundreds of millions of dollars and euros.
