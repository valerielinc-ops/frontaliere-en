De André in Locarno: Culture Beyond the Border (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 18 February 2026

A free event at the Palazzo dei Congressi in Muralto explores De André's final masterpiece. An unmissable opportunity for cross-border workers to experience Ticino's culture.

Contesto

TL;DR - Conference on Fabrizio De André's 'Anime salve' in Locarno. - Free event at Palazzo dei Congressi, Muralto. - Philological analysis by Jan Gaggetta, expert on De André. ## Key facts - Event: Conference on Fabrizio De André's 'Anime salve'. - Date: Thursday, February 19, 2026. - Time: 6:00 PM. - Location: Palazzo dei Congressi, Muralto. - Organizer: Società Dante Alighieri of Locarno. - Speaker: Jan Gaggetta, post-doctoral researcher at the University of Verona. - Admission: Free. - Album: 'Anime salve', released in 1996 in collaboration with Ivano Fossati. The cultural bond between Italy and Canton Ticino is strengthened by a high-profile event dedicated to one of the greatest Italian singer-songwriters. On Thursday, February 19, 2026, at 6:00 PM, the Palazzo dei Congressi in Muralto will host the conference "The 'anime salve' of Fabrizio De André. How a singer-songwriter works". The event, organized by the Società Dante Alighieri of Locarno, offers a unique opportunity, especially for the many cross-border workers who cross the border daily to work in the Locarno area and its surroundings. This is not just a concert or a tribute, but a deep dive into the creative process that gave birth to "Anime salve", De André's final testament album, released in 1996 in collaboration with Ivano Fossati. For those finishing their workday, perhaps leaving an office in Locarno or a factory in the Magadino Plain, this event represents a valuable chance to enrich their Ticino experience, going beyond the work routine and discovering the Canton's vibrant cultural offerings. Furthermore, the free admission makes it accessible to all.

Dettagli operativi

Inside a Poet's Workshop The heart of the conference will be a philological analysis of De André's work, a rare opportunity to peek behind the curtain of artistic creation. The speaker, Jan Gaggetta, is a leading figure in this field: a post-doctoral researcher at the University of Verona, he dedicated his doctoral thesis, defended between Fribourg and Siena, specifically to the album "Anime salve". Gaggetta will guide the audience through the documents of the Fabrizio De André Archive, showing how a song is born: from the first ideas jotted down in a notebook, often inspired by reading a book, to the final drafting of the lyrics. This approach allows for an appreciation of the depth and complexity of an album that explores themes of solitude, minorities, and the marginalized. The "anime salve", the solitary souls saved precisely because they are on the fringes, become a powerful metaphor that can resonate particularly with the experience of those who live astride a border, in a constant balance between two worlds. > The birth and development of some songs will be observed: from the initial ideas [...] to the actual drafting of the verses. The event therefore promises not to be a simple celebration, but a true cultural seminar that reveals the working method of an artist who marked the history of Italian music and poetry, a shared heritage that unites the two shores of Lake Maggiore.

Frequently Asked Questions
What are the dates and times of events dedicated to Fabrizio De André in Locarno?
The event 'Le 'anime Salve' by Fabrizio De André. As a songwriter works, it will be held on 19 February 2026 at 18:00 at the Palazzo dei Congressi in Muralto.
What is the path of cultural integration offered to the frontiers during the event on De André in Muralto?
The event offers an immersion in the creative process of De André, with physiological analysis and access to the archives, integrating the working life of the frontiers with the Ticino cultural fabric.
How can I reach the Palace of Congresses in Muralto if I work in Locarno?
The Muralto Congress Centre is easily accessible from Locarno. You can take the S25 railway line of FART (Ferrovie Autolinee Regionali Ticinesi) from Locarno to Muralto station. The trip takes about 10 minutes, making it a convenient option for those who work in Locarno and want to participate in the event.
What are the main themes explored on the album 'Anime Salve' by Fabrizio De André?
Fabrizio De André's 'Anime Salve' album explores deep themes such as loneliness, minorities and the latest in society. The songs address the stories of individuals on the margins of society, reflecting on their condition of 'saving souls', which, despite solitude, find a sort of salvation in their own marginality.
Who is Jan Gaggetta and what is his role in the event on Fabrizio De André?
Jan Gaggetta is a post-doc researcher at the University of Verona, specialized in the study of Fabrizio De André's work. He discussed his doctoral thesis on the album 'Anime Salve'. During the event at the Palazzo dei Congressi in Muralto, Gaggetta will lead the physiological analysis of the album, showing the creative process behind the birth of the songs, using material from the Archivio Fabrizio De André.
