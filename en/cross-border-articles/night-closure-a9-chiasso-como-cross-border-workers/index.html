A9 Chiasso-Como: Nights of Closures and Construction Sites (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 1 March 2026

The A9 motorway between Como and Chiasso will experience overnight closures for exceptional transports and the return of daytime construction sites, significantly impacting cross-border commuters.

Contesto

TL;DR - A9 motorway closures start March 2026 affecting cross-border commuters - Overnight closures from 10 PM to 5 AM between Como Centro and Chiasso - Daytime construction reduces lanes, peak hours have two lanes - Detours and delays expected, impacting travel times and costs ## Key facts - Motorway: A9 Lainate-Como-Chiasso - Closure Dates: Starting March 2026 - Overnight Closure: 10:00 PM to 5:00 AM, March 3-4 - Closure Stretch: Como Centro to Chiasso, northbound - Daytime Construction: Starting March 1, Fati viaduct - Peak Hours Lanes: Two lanes from 5-10 AM and 4-7 PM - Impacted Commuters: Cross-border workers to Ticino - Construction Focus: Pavement restoration and drainage system Cross-border mobility between Italy and the Canton of Ticino is gearing up for new challenges. The A9 Lainate-Como-Chiasso motorway, a vital artery for thousands of cross-border workers commuting daily to Ticino, will face interruptions and delays starting from the early days of March 2026. Autostrade per l’Italia has announced overnight closures and the resumption of significant maintenance work, which will inevitably affect travel times and trip planning. Specifically, to allow for the passage of exceptional transports, the stretch between Como Centro and Chiasso, heading north (towards Switzerland), will be closed from 10:00 PM on Tuesday, March 3, until 5:00 AM on Wednesday, March 4. This overnight interruption will require drivers, particularly cross-border workers returning to Ticino or preparing for early morning shifts, to exit at the Como Centro junction. From there, the recommended route involves continuing on local roads towards Chiasso until reaching the city border. Dettagli operativi

Not only will the nights be eventful: starting from Monday, March 1, daytime construction sites will also resume operations. The work will focus on the Fati viaduct, strategically located before the Monte Quarcino tunnel and the Lago di Como exit. These interventions, aimed at broader pavement restoration and the creation of a new platform water drainage system, will require a lane diversion with traffic reduced to one lane in each direction. Traffic management will be dynamic: Autostrade per l’Italia plans to adjust the construction site setup based on traffic flows and estimated travel times. During peak hours, from 5:00 AM to 10:00 AM heading towards Switzerland and from 4:00 PM to 7:00 PM heading towards Lainate/Milan, two lanes will be guaranteed. This is an attempt to mitigate the impact on commuting, though it will not entirely eliminate inconveniences. Punti chiave

The implications of these changes to the A9 motorway's traffic flow are numerous and directly impact the routines of cross-border commuters. While the overnight closures are temporary, they require careful planning for those working shifts or needing to travel during these hours. The alternative route on local roads, although a well-established option, often results in longer travel times due to passing through urban areas and local congestion, especially near the Chiasso border. This translates not only into valuable time lost but also increased fuel consumption and, consequently, higher daily commuting costs. The pavement restoration work and the creation of the new drainage system on the Fati viaduct are necessary interventions for the safety and durability of the infrastructure. However, managing traffic with a single lane in each direction, even with two lanes available during peak hours, represents a significant bottleneck. Cross-border workers traveling daily to Lugano, Mendrisio, or Bellinzona will need to anticipate delays and traffic jams, particularly on days with higher traffic volumes.
