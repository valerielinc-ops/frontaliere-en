Frequently Asked Questions
How does the Swiss franc exchange rate behave at the moment?
The exchange rate of the Swiss franc is at an all-time high, with a value of about 1,095 euros for one franc.
How does the Swiss franc at record values affect the real estate properties of border workers in Ticino?
The record value of the Swiss franc may increase the value of real estate in Ticino, with potential increases of 15-20% in property value between 2020 and 2023, depending on the market and location.
What does the Swiss National Bank do to control the value of the franc?
The Swiss National Bank (SNB) intervenes directly in the market by selling foreign currency to increase the supply of francs. This helps to contain its value, as interest rates are already at 0% and can no longer be effectively used for this purpose.
What are the concrete advantages of a strong franc for a Ticino municipality?
A strong franc makes imported products less expensive. For example, a municipality that buys 10,000 euros of food from abroad could save about 1,000 euros thanks to the favorable exchange rate, reducing costs for municipal coffers.
How can an announcement by the SNB affect border crossers' investments?
An announcement by the SNB about wanting to contain the franc, as happened recently, can create uncertainty. This could lead to a reassessment of the investment strategies of frontier workers, especially for those who own real estate or commercial assets that benefit from the strong franc.
