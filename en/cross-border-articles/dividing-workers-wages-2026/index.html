Dividing workers means lowering wages: the May Day message in Lugano

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 23 April 2026

May Day returns to Lugano with a focus on workers' rights, regardless of origin, contract, or economic sector.

Contesto

TL;DR - Demonstration in Lugano on May 1st to unite workers for fair wages. - Focus on solidarity among workers, especially cross-border commuters. - Organized by Swiss Union of Public Services and other sector associations. - Aims to counter labor market fragmentation and precarious working conditions. ## Key facts - Event: Demonstration for workers' rights and fair wages. - Date: May 1st. - Location: Lugano, Canton of Ticino. - Organizers: Swiss Union of Public Services (SSM), Syndicom, and other sector associations. - Main Objective: To unite workers and ensure fair wages and dignified working conditions. - Target Group: Workers from diverse origins and economic sectors, particularly cross-border commuters. - Participation: Open to all citizens, free of charge. - Tools: Net salary calculator and CHF-EUR exchange comparator available for participants. On May 1st, the event returns to Lugano with a clear message: dividing workers means lowering wages. This is the central theme of the demonstration that will take place in the Ticino city, an event that brings together workers from diverse origins and economic sectors. The demonstration is organized to reaffirm the importance of solidarity among workers, regardless of their employment contract or the sector in which they operate. ### The context of the demonstration The choice of Lugano as the venue for the demonstration is not random. The Canton of Ticino, with its strong presence of cross-border workers and transnational employees, is a symbolic place to discuss working conditions and workers' rights. Dettagli operativi

The May Day demonstration in Lugano is not just a symbolic event, but also has important practical implications for workers, particularly for cross-border commuters. ### Practical Implications for Workers One of the main implications of the demonstration is raising awareness about the importance of solidarity among workers. When workers are divided, it is easier for employers to impose precarious working conditions and lower wages. The demonstration aims to unite workers to ensure that everyone has fair rights and wages. ### Comparisons Before and After Before the demonstration, many workers, especially cross-border commuters, find themselves working under conditions of uncertainty and with lower wages compared to their Swiss colleagues. After the demonstration, it is hoped that there will be greater awareness and action to ensure fair working conditions for everyone. ### Concrete Scenarios One of the concrete scenarios that the demonstration aims to address is that of precarious employment contracts. Many workers, especially cross-border commuters, have fixed-term or part-time contracts, which often do not guarantee fair wages and stable working conditions. The demonstration aims to promote more stable employment contracts and ensure fair wages for all workers. ### Concrete Procedures To participate in the demonstration, citizens can follow these procedures: 1.
