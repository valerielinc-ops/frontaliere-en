Swiss media adopt ethical code on AI (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 7 May 2026

A new code of conduct for the responsible use of artificial intelligence in Swiss media, inspired by the Council of Europe, aims to strengthen public trust.

Contesto

In brief - Swiss media adopt a code of conduct on AI - The code is inspired by a Council of Europe convention - Goal: to strengthen public trust ## Key facts - What: Code of conduct on AI for Swiss media - When: Presented on May 7, 2026 - Where: On the sidelines of the Swiss Media Forum in Lucerne - Who: Association of German-speaking Swiss publishers (VSM), Médias Suisses, Stampa Svizzera, private radio and television, SSR, Keystone-ATS - Amount: Not specified The media sector in Switzerland has introduced a code of conduct for the responsible use of artificial intelligence (AI). This tool, inspired by a Council of Europe convention, aims to strengthen public trust in this technology. 'Trust is the most precious asset for the media,' states Andrea Masüger, president of the Association of German-speaking Swiss publishers Schweizer Medien (VSM). The text, presented today on the sidelines of the Swiss Media Forum in Lucerne, is also supported by the publishers of French-speaking Switzerland (Médias Suisses), Italian-speaking Switzerland (Stampa Svizzera), the organizations of private radio and television, the SSR, and Keystone-ATS. The Press Council, which also deals with ethical issues related to AI, welcomes this initiative. ### Implications for cross-border workers While some studies highlight public distrust of content generated with the help of AI, the code of conduct must act as a self-regulation tool. It refers to the Council of Europe's framework convention on artificial intelligence and human rights, signed at the end of March 2025 in Strasbourg by Federal Councillor Albert Rösti. What does the new code of conduct on AI for Swiss media provide?
The new code of conduct on AI for Swiss media provides that media companies and their employees remain responsible for the editorial content published, whether it has been produced by humans or with the support of AI systems. Media outlets commit to training their staff on AI and respecting copyright.
