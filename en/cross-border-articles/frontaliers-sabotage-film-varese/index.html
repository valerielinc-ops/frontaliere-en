Frontaliers Sabotage lands in Varese after its success in Ticino (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 28 February 2026

The second film in the Frontaliers saga, with over 15,000 viewers in Ticino, arrives in Varese on March 3 with two special screenings and the presence of the cast.

Bernasconi - Director Present: Alberto Meroni - Swiss Distribution: April 2024 The cultural phenomenon 'Frontaliers Sabotage,' the second chapter of the saga that has captured the hearts of the Ticino audience, is finally arriving in Varese. After achieving extraordinary success in the cinemas of Canton Ticino, surpassing 15,000 viewers and competing with giants like Avatar – only trailing the unbeatable Checco Zalone – the film dedicated to the stories of cross-border workers and Swiss border guards is now ready to conquer the Italian side. On Tuesday, March 3, the MIV in Varese will host two special screenings, at 6:00 PM and 9:00 PM, offering cross-border workers and the local community the chance to see themselves reflected in the hilarious dynamics of border life. This event is not just a movie screening but a genuine encounter with a piece of popular culture that humorously portrays the challenges and peculiarities of daily life for those who cross the border every day. The presence of actors Roberto Bussenghi (the cross-border worker) and Loris J. Bernasconi (the border guard), along with the border guard's wife Amelie and director Alberto Meroni, will enrich the evenings, allowing the audience to interact with the faces behind these now-iconic characters.

Dettagli operativi

The 'Frontaliers' are not a new phenomenon: they originated back in 2007 as radio sketches for RSI, the Swiss Italian broadcasting company, later evolving into a successful TV series between 2010 and 2014, before making their way to the big screen. Their strength lies in their ability to portray, with humor and sharp observation, the cultural misunderstandings and language barriers that mark daily life at the border. The famous 'Itagliano,' that peculiar linguistic blend of Italian and Swiss expressions, is just one of the many themes that deeply resonate with the experiences of thousands of cross-border workers. The success in Ticino, with over 15,000 attendees, demonstrates how these characters have entered the collective imagination, acting as a humorous bridge between two worlds that meet and clash every day. Some scenes from 'Sabotage' were filmed on the Italian side, further strengthening the connection to the homeland of many workers.
