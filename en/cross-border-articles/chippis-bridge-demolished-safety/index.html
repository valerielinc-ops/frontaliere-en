Chippis demolishes historic bridge for safety (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 10 May 2026

The municipality of Chippis has demolished an 1880 bridge to prevent risks related to the Fios fault. Safety works cost 1.8 million francs.

Contesto

In brief - Historic bridge demolished for safety in Chippis - Fios fault threatens debris buildup - Works cost 1.8 million francs ## Key facts - What: Demolition of historic bridge - When: 8 May 2026 - Where: Chippis, Canton Valais - Who: Municipality of Chippis - Amount: 1.8 million francs The historic bridge of Chippis, built in 1880, was demolished last Thursday for safety reasons. The decision was made by the Municipality of Chippis due to the extensive Fios geological fault, which threatens to cause debris buildup and the overflow of the Navizence River. The fault, 250 meters long and one meter wide, was discovered in October 2025 and continues to concern local authorities. "Although the bridge was classified as a protected structure, we chose to demolish it for safety reasons," explained Mayor Olivier Perruchoud during a public information event on 9 March last. The demolition of the 'Pont du cimetière' lasted about half an hour and avoided the risk of flooding in the residential area of Bord, where about fifty inhabitants live. By the end of the month, works to raise and widen the embankments will be carried out to limit the risk of flooding. The total cost of the operation is 1.8 million francs, of which 85% is borne by the Canton and the rest by the Municipality. Meanwhile, a temporary ramp has been installed on the right bank of the river to allow access to the Bord district for both vehicles and pedestrians. ### Impact on the community The demolition of the bridge has a significant impact on the local community. The temporary ramp installed will allow residents to access the Bord district, but the lack of a permanent bridge represents a challenge for mobility.
