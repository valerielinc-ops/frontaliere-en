Varese: jobs available but skilled workers are missing

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 30 April 2026

Employment is growing but there are difficulties in finding technical and scientific profiles. Here are the 2025 data

Contesto

In brief - Employment rate in Varese close to 70% - Unemployment at 3%, physiological levels - Difficulty in finding labor at 51% ## Key facts - What: Paradox of available work but lack of specialized workers - When: 2025 data - Where: Province of Varese - Who: Varese Chamber of Commerce - Amount: 51% difficulty in finding labor The productive system of the province of Varese is experiencing a paradoxical period: despite the continuous generation of labor demand, companies struggle to find workers with the required skills. This is what emerges from the elaboration of data available on OsserVa, carried out by the Economic Analysis Office of the Varese Chamber of Commerce. According to Istat data for 2025, the labor market for residents in the province of Varese has shown an improvement, with the employment rate approaching 70% and unemployment reaching physiological levels, equal to 3%. However, this positive scenario leads to a thinning of the available labor pool, with a consequent increase in the difficulty of finding labor, which, according to the latest data from the Excelsior Information System, has reached 51% of the personnel entries planned by Varese companies. ### The most difficult positions to find In addition to the general quantitative issue, there is also a mismatch regarding some professional figures required by the territory. In an area with a strong manufacturing vocation like that of the province of Varese, the major criticalities are concentrated precisely in the productive area, particularly for specialized profiles (73% for specialized workers) and in the recruitment of technical professions (60%). Dettagli operativi

The pressure of artificial intelligence An additional element to highlight is the difficulty in finding professionals connected to the digital and environmental transition: application design analysts and specialists (79%), network and database experts (73%), security technicians and environmental protection (69%). This happens because the race for technological innovation and the need to adapt to new environmental regulations require increasingly specialized skills that are difficult to find in the local market. ### Implications for cross-border workers This situation of paradox, where there is available work but a lack of specialized workers, has significant implications for cross-border workers employed in the province of Varese. On one hand, the growth in employment and the reduction in unemployment represent a positive signal for the local economy. On the other hand, the difficulty companies face in finding qualified personnel could lead to an increase in opportunities for cross-border workers, especially in sectors such as healthcare and technology, where skills are harder to come by. However, it is important to consider that the situation could also lead to increased competition between local workers and cross-border workers for available positions. Furthermore, the need for increasingly specialized skills could make it more difficult for cross-border workers to find jobs in sectors where the required qualifications are very specific. ### What to do if you are a cross-border worker If you are a cross-border worker looking for a job in the province of Varese, it is important to stay updated on the skills required by the local market.
