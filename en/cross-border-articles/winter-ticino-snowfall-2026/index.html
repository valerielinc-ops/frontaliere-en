Winter returns to Ticino: heavy snow risk According to MeteoSwiss, snow accumulations are expected to reach 50–80 cm in the high mountains, locally even 100 cm. Along the southern side of the Valais Alps, moist air will channel in cold air, causing heavy snowfall. Residents of the canton of Ticino must be prepared for a long, cold winter, with temperatures that could drop to the valley floor, between –5 and –10 °C, despite an altitude of just over 1,000 metres above sea level. In Bellinzona, at 230 metres above sea level, temperatures will range between 0 and 5 °C, with a chance of intermittent precipitation. The Regione Viva of the Municipality of Bellinzona has already drawn up an emergency plan to manage snowfall and extreme temperatures. Winter returns to Ticino: heavy snow risk The advice is to keep an eye on the weather forecast and be ready for possible traffic problems. If you are travelling, make sure you have up-to-date information on the weather situation. Check the MeteoSwiss bulletin for the latest information. If you are planning to go out, make sure you are equipped with the necessary tools to cope with the weather conditions. 📊 Weather forecast The weather situation in Ticino is currently characterised by a high risk of snowfall. According to MeteoSwiss forecasts, temperatures will drop from Thursday 17 March, with the possibility of snow down to 800 metres. The wind will be moderate to strong, with gusts that could reach 100 km/h. Traffic and road conditions In the event of snowfall, traffic could be severely slowed or even blocked. It is therefore important to be prepared for possible traffic problems. 🚗 - Mountain roads: the road to Bellinzona has already been closed several times this year due to snow and ice. - A2 motorway: the A2 is the main communication route between the Canton of Ticino and the rest of Switzerland. However, in the event of snowfall, traffic could be severely disrupted. - Nearby municipalities: the municipalities of Lugano, Mendrisio and Como could be affected by snow and ice. Regulations and prescriptions - Road Act (15 June 2018): the law states that roads must be cleared within 24 hours of the end of snow or the removal of earth or ice. - Travel document: if you are travelling, make sure you have your travel document with you and know the prescriptions for driving in snow and ice. - Insurance: make sure you have insurance that covers the costs of repairing or replacing the vehicle in the event of an accident. Frequently Asked Questions
Which areas of Ticino are most affected by snowfall?
The Locarno area, the Maggia Valley, the Verzasca Valley and the Leventina Valley are among the most affected areas.
What is the budget envisaged by the Municipality of Lugano for the winter emergency plan?
The Municipality of Lugano has set aside a budget of 500,000 Swiss francs for the mobilization of road maintenance teams, the deployment of snow machines and the provision of assistance services for residents.
How does snowfall affect cross-border traffic between Ticino and Italy?
Heavy snowfall can cause temporary road closures, especially on the A2 (San Gottardo) and on the SS334 towards Como, with possible delays for cross-border commuters. It is advisable to check real-time updates on MeteoSwiss and on the official accounts of the Ticino and Italian authorities.
What are the rights of cross-border workers in the event of a road block due to snow?
Cross-border commuters are entitled to pay even in the event of a road block that is not their fault, provided that they comply with the instructions of the authorities. It is advisable to promptly notify the employer of the impossibility of reaching the workplace by email or SMS.
What do cross-border commuters need to do to prepare for winter travel between Switzerland and Italy?
Check the weather conditions on MeteoSwiss, equip your car with winter tyres (mandatory in Ticino from 1 November to 30 April), check alternative routes and find out about any transit restrictions via the apps of the Ticino and Italian road authorities.
