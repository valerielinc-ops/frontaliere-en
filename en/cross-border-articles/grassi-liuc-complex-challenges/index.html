Grassi: 'Complex challenges, but Liuc is ready'

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 1 May 2026

Roberto Grassi, new president of Liuc-Università Cattaneo, tackles the challenges of the manufacturing world and innovation.

Contesto

In brief - Roberto Grassi is the new president of Liuc-Università Cattaneo in Castellanza - Liuc is a university born from businesses and for businesses - Main challenges include demographic winter and artificial intelligence ## Key facts - What: Appointment of Roberto Grassi as president of Liuc - When: Not yet specified - Where: Castellanza, Italy - Who: Roberto Grassi, entrepreneur and former president of Confindustria Varese - Amount: Not yet specified Roberto Grassi is the new president of Liuc-Università Cattaneo in Castellanza. An entrepreneur and former head of Confindustria Varese, he takes over during a transitional phase that he defines as 'in continuity and harmony.' The appointment comes after Riccardo Comerio's move to the presidency of ITS Incom, a decision made following recent internal changes in the system. 'It's a path of continuity and harmony. Varese has a unique climate, made up of personal as well as institutional relationships. My availability also stems from a phase of reorganization; for Riccardo Comerio, managing both roles would have been complex. I was asked to step in and I accepted. For us, Liuc is the most important investment made over the years as a confindustrial system. It's a great honor but also a responsibility, especially in a not-so-simple phase, which is also stimulating.' Liuc is often described as a unique institution. How is the relationship with businesses strengthened today, and what are the main challenges? 'It's a university born from businesses and for businesses; that's what makes it unique. Strengthening this bond is already underway and will continue. It's strategic because the manufacturing world needs new skills. Dettagli operativi

In recent times, there has been much talk about networking and creating a system. Is this really a concrete priority today? And what are the next steps in that direction? 'Today, creating a system is no longer a slogan but a necessity. Collaborations, shared projects, and innovation initiatives demonstrate this. The next step will be to update the strategic plan, in continuity with what has been done in recent years, to define shared growth and development objectives, both internationally and in terms of education.' How important is the Varese context in this journey? 'It's very important. It's a territory with a strong industrial culture and quality relationships. This makes the difference and allows us to tackle even the most complex challenges with a shared approach.' ### Continuous training and innovation Continuous training is another crucial topic for Liuc. 'Skills today age quickly, and companies struggle to find qualified people. We need to invest in the growth of our employees. Liuc is at the forefront in this field, offering customized paths to meet the needs of businesses.' Technological innovation, particularly artificial intelligence, represents a significant challenge. 'AI is changing the way we work and do business. Liuc is committed to training professionals capable of managing these new technologies, offering cutting-edge courses and laboratories.' ### Collaborations and shared projects Collaborations with other universities and research centers are fundamental. 'We have over 150 international connections in 40 countries. This allows us to exchange knowledge and best practices, enriching our educational and research offerings.' What are the main challenges that Liuc is facing?
