How does compensation work for a pedestrian run over in Switzerland if the driver is an Italian cross-border commuter?
In the event of an investment, the pedestrian is entitled to compensation from the driver's motor liability insurance, even if he is Italian. The procedure follows Swiss law: the pedestrian must submit a request to the insurance company within 2 years of the accident. If the driver is up to date with the insurance, the reimbursement covers medical expenses, physical and moral damage.
What are the penalties for a Swiss driver who causes a serious speeding accident in Bioggio?
Penalties for speeding in Bioggio include fines of up to 10,000 francs and the suspension of the license from 6 months to 2 years. If the accident causes serious injuries, as in the case of the pedestrian hit, the revocation of the license can be immediate and permanent in the event of a repeat offense.
Can I cross the border crossings between Italy and Switzerland on foot without danger even during the day?
