In brief - Ammonia leak at Chiasso ice rink - Cause: Faulty gasket in a compressor fitting - Three employees slightly intoxicated ## Key facts - What: Ammonia leak - When: Tuesday morning, discovered around 8:30 AM - Where: Chiasso ice rink - Who: Three municipal workers - Amount: Three employees intoxicated The ammonia leak that occurred on Tuesday morning at the Chiasso ice rink was caused by a technical fault. According to Corrado Tettamanti, commander of the Mendrisiotto Fire Department, the problem was due to a gasket inside a compressor fitting. The leak was discovered around 8:30 AM by three municipal workers, who immediately closed the door and called for help, thus preventing the spread of ammonia in the surrounding area. The three employees, including one woman, were slightly intoxicated. ### Safety implications The prompt action of the municipal workers prevented the situation from worsening, demonstrating the importance of a rapid response in similar emergencies. This incident underscores the need to keep refrigeration systems in perfect condition and to adequately train staff in emergency management. ### Safety procedure In similar cases, it is essential to follow a clear and well-defined procedure. What caused the ammonia leak in Chiasso?
