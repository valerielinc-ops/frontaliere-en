In brief - New direct trains from Zurich to Venice and La Spezia - Summer connections to Rimini - Night train for Ticino skiers ## Key facts What: Improvements to the FFS timetable 2027 When: From April 5, 2027 Where: Ticino and other Swiss regions Who: FFS (Swiss Federal Railways) Amount: Not specified Swiss Federal Railways (FFS) have announced significant improvements to the railway timetable from April 5, 2027, with a particular focus on Ticino cross-border workers. Here's a detailed analysis of the most important changes. ### New international connections From 2027, Ticino cross-border workers will benefit from new direct trains from major Swiss cities to international destinations. There will be two daily connections from Zurich to Venice and La Spezia, with travel times of approximately 4 hours and 3 hours and 15 minutes, respectively. Additionally, weekly connections from Zurich and Lugano to Rimini on the Italian Adriatic coast will be activated during the summer season. ### Night train for Ticino skiers One of the most anticipated changes for Ticino cross-border workers is the introduction of a night train for skiers, connecting the region with ski resorts in the Canton of Valais. The train will depart in the evening from Lugano and arrive early in the morning, allowing skiers to spend the entire day on the slopes. This service will be active during the winter season, from December 2027 to March 2028. ### Operational and regulatory checklist To ensure the success of these new connections, FFS are working in collaboration with local authorities and railway operators in the countries involved. Dettagli operativi

Analysis The improvements to the FFS 2027 schedule will bring benefits to commuters and tourists, but it is the cross-border workers of Ticino who will reap the most advantage from the new changes. The new direct connections from Zurich to Venice and La Spezia will facilitate travel to Italian cities, reducing travel times and connections. In addition, cross-border workers from Ticino will be able to comfortably reach the ski resorts of the region during winter weekends thanks to the S10 trains, which connect the Ticinese municipalities of Lugano, Bellinzona, and Locarno to the ski resorts of the Grisons canton. The new FFS 2027 schedules will come into effect on December 10, 2026, and will include an increase in the number of trains on the Zurich-Italy line, with a frequency of one train per hour to Venice and two trains per hour to La Spezia. In addition, the S10 trains will be enhanced with a frequency of one train every 30 minutes during peak hours, improving the connection between Ticino and neighboring ski resorts. For cross-border workers from Ticino, these improvements mean time and money savings. For example, a cross-border worker who works in Zurich and lives in Lugano currently takes around 1 hour and 45 minutes to reach the workplace. With the new FFS 2027 schedules, travel time will be reduced to around 1 hour and 30 minutes, with a departure frequency of one hour. In addition, the cost of a return ticket from Lugano to Zurich currently amounts to around 120 Swiss francs. With the new schedules, cross-border workers will be able to save on travel costs, thanks to the increased frequency of trains and the possibility of purchasing discounted subscriptions. Action To take advantage of the new connections, follow these steps: 1. Check the updated timetables on the SBB website. For example, if you live in Lugano and work in Mendrisio, you can check the timetable of the night train that leaves Lugano at 23:45 and arrives in Mendrisio at 00:05. 2. Buy tickets online or at the ticket offices. The price of the ticket may vary depending on the chosen comfort class and the frequency of trips. For example, a single second-class ticket may cost around 8.60 CHF, while a monthly pass may cost around 350 CHF. 3. Use the TILO Pigiama service for night trips. This service offers night connections between the main cities of Ticino, such as Lugano, Bellinzona, and Mendrisio, and allows cross-border workers to save time and money, as they do not have to go home every evening. For example, a cross-border worker who works in Mendrisio and lives in Lugano could use the TILO Pigiama service to go home in the evening and save on accommodation costs in Mendrisio. > "The TILO Pigiama service is an excellent solution for cross-border workers who want to save time and money," said an SBB spokesperson. ### Operational checklist Here is a checklist of things to do for Ticino cross-border workers who want to use the new SBB connections: Check the updated timetables on the SBB website Buy tickets online or at the ticket offices Use the TILO Pigiama service for night trips Check the tax and customs regulations to understand how to declare travel expenses Compare the costs of different means of transport to find the most economical option ### Practical scenario comparisons Ticino cross-border workers have several options to get to work, including car, bus, and train.
