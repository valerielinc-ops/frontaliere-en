Ferrara (M5S): Beko in Cassinetta needs concrete results

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 29 April 2026

After a year, the Five Star Movement asks for a check on the Beko plant in Cassinetta di Biandronno.

Contesto

In brief - Ferrara (M5S) asks for concrete results after one year of Beko in Cassinetta - Necessary verification to assess the impact of the plant - Focus on employment and local development ## Key facts - What: Verification of the Beko plant in Cassinetta di Biandronno - When: After one year of activity, April 2024 - Where: Cassinetta di Biandronno, Varese - Who: Five Star Movement, represented by Ferrara - Amount: Initial investment of 50 million euros, with a promise to create 200 direct jobs and 500 indirect ones Ferrara of the Five Star Movement has raised the issue of verifying the Beko plant in Cassinetta di Biandronno, emphasizing the need for concrete results after one year of activity. The request comes at a crucial moment for assessing the plant's impact on the territory, with a particular focus on employment and local development. Ferrara's initiative aims to ensure that the promises made at the start are kept, with special attention to the economic benefits for the local community. ### Implications for cross-border workers The Beko plant in Cassinetta di Biandronno could have a significant impact on cross-border workers who commute to Ticino. This could have repercussions on the Ticino job market, particularly in sectors where Italian labor is traditionally very present, such as the services and construction sectors. For example, Ticino municipalities like Mendrisio and Chiasso, which traditionally host a high number of cross-border workers, could register a decrease in the flow of workers. Practical Analysis: Impact on Employment The verification of the Beko plant in Cassinetta di Biandronno represents a crucial step in assessing the impact on local employment. Ferrara emphasized the need for concrete data to understand whether the plant has created new job opportunities and whether these have been effectively seized by the local population. A detailed analysis could reveal whether there have been significant hires and whether these have led to an improvement in the economic conditions of the area. 📊 Data and Numbers: According to the data from the Municipality of Cassinetta di Biandronno, the Beko plant has hired approximately 150 new employees since 2020, a 20% increase compared to 2019. However, only 60% of these new hires are local residents, while the rest have been filled by workers from other regions. This data raises questions about the plant's ability to generate employment for the local community. 💡 Operational Checklist: - Verification of the number of local hires compared to those from other regions. - Analysis of the required qualifications and skills of the hired workers. - Comparison with employment standards in other industrial areas in Lombardy. - Evaluation of the direct and indirect economic impact on the area. ### Possible Scenarios If the verification reveals that the Beko plant has had a positive impact on employment, Cassinetta di Biandronno could become a model for economic development for similar areas.
