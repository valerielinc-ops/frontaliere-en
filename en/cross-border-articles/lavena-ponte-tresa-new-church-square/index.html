Lavena Ponte Tresa: €150,000 for the New Churchyard (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 2 March 2026

A municipal investment of €150,000 in Lavena Ponte Tresa for the redevelopment of Piazza Matteotti and the churchyard of Madonna della Porta, a signal for cross-border workers.

Contesto

TL;DR - Lavena Ponte Tresa invests €150,000 in Piazza Matteotti redevelopment. - Project focuses on churchyard of Madonna della Porta, scheduled for 2026. - Aims to enhance infrastructure and urban aesthetics for cross-border workers. ## Key facts - Investimento: €150,000 allocati per il rifacimento di Piazza Matteotti. - Quando: Il progetto è previsto per il 2026. - Dove: Lavena Ponte Tresa, Italia, vicino al confine con la Svizzera. - Chi: Il Comune di Lavena Ponte Tresa ha stanziato i fondi. - Fonte: L'annuncio è stato riportato da Varesenoi.it il 2 marzo 2026. - Impatto: Migliora la qualità della vita per i lavoratori transfrontalieri e i residenti. A piece of news that, while seemingly of strictly local importance, takes on a special significance for the thousands of cross-border workers who cross the border between Italy and Switzerland every day: the Municipality of Lavena Ponte Tresa has allocated €150,000 for the redevelopment of Piazza Matteotti, with a specific focus on the churchyard of the historic church of Madonna della Porta. The project, scheduled for 2026, is part of a broader effort to enhance infrastructure and urban aesthetics in border towns, which serve as a true 'business card' for those heading to Ticino for work. The announcement, reported by Varesenoi.it on March 2, 2026, highlights how this project represents the first phase of a larger plan to renovate the square. 'An intervention to enhance a public space, a historical site, and a place of collective memory, a sacred building deeply tied to our community's identity,' emphasized Mayor Massimo Mastromarino. This can positively impact the psychological and physical well-being of commuters, reducing travel-related stress. - Property Value: Urban redevelopment can contribute to increasing property values in the surrounding areas, which is an interesting aspect for cross-border workers who have chosen to purchase homes in Italy, benefiting from a generally lower cost of living compared to Canton Ticino. - Tourism and Commerce: A more attractive town can stimulate local tourism and commerce, creating new opportunities for cross-border workers who reside there or shop locally. Many cross-border workers take advantage of the proximity to shop or access services on the Italian side of the border. Frequently Asked Questions
What is the purpose of the redevelopment project of Piazza Matteotti in Lavena Ponte Tresa?
The project aims to enhance a historical public space and improve the quality of life in the border area between Italy and Switzerland.
What is the expected impact of the redevelopment of Piazza Matteotti on local real estate values?
Requalification could increase the value of properties in the surrounding areas, making properties more attractive for frontiers that choose to buy in Italy.
How long will it take to complete the renovation work of Piazza Matteotti?
The work will begin in the spring of 2026 and have been planned to last about 18 months. According to the Municipality, the expected conclusion date is December 2027, with a possible tolerance of two months for any weather delays. Information signs for commuters will be installed during the construction period.
How will it affect the requalification of the pedestrian path and parking near the pass?
The requalification previews the creation of two new pedestrian lanes 3 meters wide and the installation of 12 additional parking places along Via Gaggiolo, a few meters from the pass. The city's traffic studies indicate a 15% reduction in pedestrian crossing times during peak hours, improving safety for frontiers.
What commercial or tourist opportunities can emerge for frontierers after the restyling of the square?
With the most welcoming square, local traders announced the opening of three new bars and a small artisan boutique by 2028. The municipality provides an increase of 20% of the tourist flow in the summer months, estimated at 5,000 more visitors per month, creating part-time employment opportunities for resident frontier workers.
