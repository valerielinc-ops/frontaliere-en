MomòRide launches carpooling challenge for Mendrisiotto commuters

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 28 April 2026

A collective carpooling challenge to collect points and donate 1000 CHF to the Provvida Madre Foundation of Balerna

Contesto

In brief - MomòRide launches carpooling challenge for Mendrisiotto commuters - Goal: collect 40,000 points in a month - Donation of 1,000 CHF to the Provvida Madre Foundation ## Key facts - What: Collective carpooling challenge - When: From May 1, 2026 - Where: Balerna, Chiasso, Novazzano - Who: MomòRide, Banca dello Stato del Canton Ticino - Amount: 1,000 CHF donated to the Provvida Madre Foundation A collective challenge has been launched for commuters in the Lower Mendrisiotto. It's the first of its kind by MomòRide, a two-year 'carpooling' project aimed at companies and workers in Balerna, Chiasso, and Novazzano. Starting this Friday, May 1st, anyone in the area who has decided to contribute to sustainable mobility and is ready to share their car is invited to join forces. The goal? To collect 40,000 points in a month. The result, it is announced, will translate into a donation of 1,000 francs to support the initiatives of the Provvida Madre Foundation in Balerna. In practice, participants will need to commit to tracking all their commuting trips shared by car using the Mobalt app, thus accumulating points for each trip, which will be added to those of all other 'carpoolers' in the area. Once the common target is reached, Banca dello Stato del Canton Ticino, sponsor of the challenge, will make the charitable donation. The declared intent is to create 'a virtuous circle, in which, in addition to the benefits of promoting carpooling for mobility and the environment, positive social impacts are also created.' The May challenge, in fact, will not be the only one. Other initiatives will be promoted in the autumn and will have, it is anticipated, other public utility bodies in the area as recipients. Practical Implications for Commuters The carpooling challenge launched by MomòRide represents a concrete opportunity for the commuters of Mendrisiotto to contribute to sustainable mobility and to benefit from a solidarity initiative. Participating in the challenge not only helps to reduce traffic and pollution but also allows you to accumulate points that translate into an economic contribution for a local charity. ### Comparison with the Previous Situation Before the start of the MomòRide project, the commuters of Mendrisiotto did not have a structured initiative that promoted carpooling collectively and with a solidarity goal. The new challenge represents a significant step forward, offering a concrete incentive for those who decide to share the car with colleagues or other commuters. Furthermore, the possibility of accumulating points and contributing to a charitable donation adds an added value to participation. ### Future Scenarios If the May challenge is successful, it is likely that MomòRide will continue to promote similar initiatives in the future. This could lead to greater awareness of the importance of carpooling and an increase in the number of commuters who adopt this mode of transport. Furthermore, collaboration with public utility entities could expand the positive impact of the initiatives, involving more and more local actors and creating a virtuous effect for the entire community. ### Comparison with Other Initiatives Compared to other carpooling initiatives, the MomòRide challenge stands out for its collective and solidarity approach. How to Participate in the Challenge To participate in the carpooling challenge, commuters must follow these steps: 1. Download the Mobalt app and register as users. 2. Start tracking their shared car journeys. 3. Accumulate points with each trip and contribute to reaching the common goal of 40,000 points. 4. Monitor progress and encourage other commuters to participate. ### Deadlines and Prizes The carpooling challenge will last one month, starting on May 1, 2026. Upon reaching 40,000 points, Banca dello Stato del Canton Ticino will donate 1,000 CHF to the Fondazione Provvida Madre di Balerna. Additionally, participants can benefit from a prize pool of 500 francs, provided by the promoting municipalities, destined for the crews of companies based in the three municipalities. ### Useful Tools For further information and to participate in the challenge, commuters can visit the official MomòRide website or contact the email address info@momoride.ch. Additionally, they can use the Mobalt app to track their movements and monitor the points accumulated. ### Conclusion The carpooling challenge launched by MomòRide represents a unique opportunity for the commuters of Mendrisiotto to contribute to sustainable mobility and support a solidarity initiative. Participating in the challenge not only helps to reduce traffic and pollution but also allows accumulating points that translate into an economic contribution for a local charitable entity. Frequently Asked Questions
How can I participate in the MomòRide carpooling challenge?
To participate in the challenge, you need to download the Mobalt app, register as a user, and start tracking your car-sharing trips. Each journey contributes to the accumulation of points, which will be added to those of other participants in the area.
What is the goal of the carpooling challenge?
The goal of the challenge is to collect 40,000 points in a month. Upon reaching this target, Banca dello Stato del Canton Ticino will donate 1,000 CHF to the Provvida Madre Foundation of Balerna.
What are the benefits for participants in the challenge?
In addition to the social contribution, participants can benefit from a prize pool of 500 francs, provided by the promoting municipalities, destined for the crews of companies based in the three municipalities. Furthermore, the MomòRide project is supported by lastminute foundation and Mobitrends, which offer resources and tools to promote carpooling.
