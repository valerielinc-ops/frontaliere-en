The modification, approved by the Province of Varese for safety reasons, involves a one-way loop that also affects the municipalities of Bardello with Malgesso and Bregano, Besozzo, Biandronno, Cadrezzate with Osmate, and Brebbia. The intervention, the first of its kind promoted by the Province of Varese on such a large scale, aims to achieve three main objectives: improve traffic flow and livability of the area, rationalize the routes of heavy vehicles avoiding the most critical points, and ensure a more efficient and punctual local public transport service. ### Details of the experimental traffic The proposed modification, which has the consent of the 6 municipalities, the TPL Agency, the public service manager, and Asea (Association of freight forwarders and road transport), provides for the temporary and experimental establishment of a transit ban for vehicles with a gross weight exceeding 3.5 tonnes in certain sections of the municipalities of Bardello with Malgesso and Bregano, Biandronno, and Travedona Monate. Frequently Asked Questions
Which municipalities are affected by the new experimental traffic regulations for trucks?
The municipalities affected by the new experimental traffic regulations for trucks are Bardello with Malgesso and Bregano, Besozzo, Travedona Monate, Biandronno, Cadrezzate with Osmate and Brebbia.
What are the restrictions for heavy vehicles?
The restrictions for heavy vehicles include the ban on transit for vehicles with a gross weight exceeding 3.5 tonnes in certain sections of the municipalities of Bardello with Malgesso and Bregano, Biandronno, and Travedona Monate. Additionally, further restrictions have been established for vehicles with a gross weight exceeding 3.5 tonnes on other roads in the affected municipalities.

The new experimental traffic regulations could have a significant impact on cross-border workers who use heavy vehicles for transporting goods between Italy and Switzerland.
