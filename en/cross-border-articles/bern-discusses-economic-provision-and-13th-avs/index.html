Bern discusses economic supply and 13th AVS payment The financing of the 13th AVS pension payment represents a crucial challenge for the future of Switzerland's social security system. The proposal put forward by the Social Security Commission suggests a combined increase in VAT and social contributions to ensure a stable flow of resources to support this additional payment. Currently, the AVS (Old-Age and Survivors Insurance) is one of the cornerstones of the Swiss pension system, and the introduction of the 13th payment aims to improve the quality of life for retirees and people with disabilities. However, the parliamentary debate is expected to be intense, given the need to balance the state's financial requirements with considerations of social equity. 📊 Funding proposal: The Commission has estimated that introducing the 13th payment would entail an annual cost of approximately 3.3 billion Swiss francs. To cover this expense, an increase in VAT by 0.3 percentage points has been proposed, raising it to 8.3% from the current 8.0%. Simultaneously, AVS salary contributions would be increased by 0.4%, directly impacting both workers' paychecks and employers' costs. For example, a cross-border worker with a gross monthly income of 5,000 Swiss francs would see an increase of about 20 francs in monthly contributions. 💡 Local implications: In Ticino municipalities like Lugano and Mendrisio, where a high percentage of the population consists of retirees, the debate over the 13th payment takes on particular importance. In 2022, Ticino had approximately 80,000 AVS benefit recipients, many of whom had incomes below 2,000 Swiss francs per month. The 13th AVS payment, officially introduced with the 2021 amendment to the Federal Law on Old-Age and Survivors Insurance, serves as a vital economic support for many cross-border workers. Currently, the average amount of the 13th AVS payment for a beneficiary is estimated at about 1,200 CHF annually, but this value can vary based on contributions made and years of service. ### Economic impacts in Ticino municipalities Municipalities like Chiasso, Lugano, and Mendrisio host a significant number of cross-border workers who, through their contributions, support key sectors of the local economy, including commerce, construction, and healthcare. For instance, according to 2022 data, over 70,000 cross-border workers are employed in Ticino, representing about 30% of the canton’s total workforce. The economic stability ensured by federal policies, such as the regular disbursement of the 13th AVS payment, is therefore essential not only for individual workers but also for the continuity of many strategic sectors. 📊 Practical example: A cross-border worker with an annual salary of 50,000 CHF and an annual AVS contribution of 8,500 CHF would receive a 13th AVS payment proportional to their years of contribution.
