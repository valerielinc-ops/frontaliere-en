A9, nighttime closures and fixed construction sites: chaos for cross-border workers (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 2 March 2026

The A9 motorway between Chiasso and Como will be closed at night for exceptional transports, while daytime construction sites resume: here's what changes for thousands of commuters starting March 2026.

Contesto

The A9 Lainate-Como-Chiasso motorway, a vital artery for commuting between Italy and the Canton of Ticino, will face nighttime closures and the resumption of significant daytime construction works, promising considerable disruptions for those traveling between the two sides of the border. Autostrade per l’Italia has announced a specific nighttime closure: from 10:00 PM on Tuesday, March 3, to 5:00 AM on Wednesday, March 4, the section between Como Centro and Chiasso heading north will be closed to traffic. This measure has been implemented to allow the passage of exceptional transports, an event that, although temporary, requires careful planning from travelers. Dettagli operativi

The 'endless construction site' between Switzerland and Italy, as it is often labeled by commuters, is unfortunately a reality cross-border workers must contend with. The main interventions, resuming in daytime mode from Monday, March 1, 2026, focus on the Fati viaduct. This crucial infrastructure is strategically located before the Monte Quarcino tunnel and the Lago di Como exit, critical points for traffic flow. The planned works include road surface rehabilitation and the implementation of a new platform water drainage system, essential interventions for the safety and durability of the motorway. To manage vehicle flow during these operations, Autostrade per l’Italia has arranged a lane diversion in both directions. This means that traffic will flow on a single lane in each direction for most of the time. However, to mitigate disruptions during peak hours, a flexible construction site schedule has been planned. Specifically, 'this setup will ensure two lanes of traffic during peak hours, namely between 5:00 AM and 10:00 AM towards Switzerland and from 4:00 PM to 7:00 PM towards Lainate/Milan.' This flexibility is crucial for cross-border workers, who make up the majority of traffic during these time slots. Frequently Asked Questions
What are the night closures planned for the A9 Lainate-Como-Chiasso motorway?
The night closure is scheduled from 3 March (22:00) to 4 March (05:00) on the Como Centro - Chiasso section northbound.
What is the expected impact of daytime work on the Fati viaduct for cross-border commuters during peak hours?
During peak hours (5:00-10:00 towards Switzerland and 16:00-19:00 towards Lainate/Milan), 2 lanes will be guaranteed, partially mitigating the inconvenience.
What are the consequences of the exceptional transport on 3 March on the A9 for those travelling to Switzerland?
On 3 March, from 22:00 to 5:00 on 4 March, the A9 Como-Chiasso will be closed north of Como Centro. Cross-border commuters will have to exit at Como Centro and continue on the ordinary road system to the Chiasso customs, with possible slowdowns.
What happens at the construction site on the Fati viaduct during peak hours for cross-border commuters?
To mitigate the inconvenience, the construction site on the Fati viaduct will guarantee 2 lanes to Switzerland between 5:00 and 10:00. This is to facilitate the transit of cross-border commuters during morning rush hour.
What works are planned on the Fati viaduct and when will they resume?
From 1 March 2026, daytime work on the Fati viaduct will resume, which includes the rehabilitation of the pavement and a new water collection system. These interventions focus on a crucial section before the Monte Quarcino tunnel.
