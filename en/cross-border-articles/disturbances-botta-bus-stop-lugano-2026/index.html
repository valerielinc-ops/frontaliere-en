In brief - Clashes at the Botta shelter in Lugano with two minor injuries - Possible political motives and rivalry between fan groups - Police and emergency services intervention with use of pepper spray ## Key facts - What: Disturbances and physical clashes - When: Friday evening, shortly after 10:30 PM - Where: Botta shelter, Lugano - Who: People from far-right and far-left groups with ties to Ticino fan groups - Injuries: Two minor injuries The situation escalated on Friday evening, shortly after 10:30 PM, at the Botta shelter in Lugano. According to reports from Cdt, the clashes occurred between individuals from far-right and far-left groups with ties to Ticino fan groups, particularly hockey fans. Some witnesses reported blood and issues even at Maghetti. According to an initial reconstruction provided by the authorities, the situation deteriorated for reasons that the investigation will need to determine, resulting in two minor injuries, who were promptly assisted. On the scene, agents from the Lugano City Police intervened, later supported by the Cantonal Police and the Ceresio Nord Police, as well as the rescuers from the Croce Verde di Lugano. During the intervention, some individuals obstructed the work of the officers, making the use of pepper spray necessary. The situation normalized shortly before 11:00 PM. Checks have been initiated to clarify the dynamics of the events, identify the perpetrators, and determine individual responsibilities. ### Implications for cross-border workers The disturbances at the Botta shelter raise questions about public safety and the management of sporting events in the Canton of Ticino.
