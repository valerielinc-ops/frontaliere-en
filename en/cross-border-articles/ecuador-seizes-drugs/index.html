The intervention is part of an offensive against drug trafficking in the country, which has become a crucial passage for most of the cocaine produced in South America. The law enforcement agencies intercepted three speedboats with approximately one ton of illegal cargo off the port of Manta, a strategic area for drug trafficking to Europe and other continents. Almost simultaneously, a large-scale operation near the Galapagos Islands led to the confiscation of another two tons and the arrest of five people. The Ministry of Defense confirmed the action, highlighting that the operations are part of the international cooperation agreements that Ecuador maintains with foreign countries to combat transnational crimes in aquatic spaces. These agreements include the sharing of intelligence, joint training, and collaboration in interception operations. Dettagli operativi

The operation was possible thanks to the collaboration between the Ecuadorian law enforcement agencies and the United States, which shared information and resources to combat drug trafficking. The seizure of three tons of narcotics is a significant blow to the criminal organizations operating in the region. Ecuador is a strategic country for drug trafficking, as it is located near the main cocaine trafficking routes from Colombia and Peru. International cooperation is fundamental to combat drug trafficking and protect public safety. For example, in 2020, the Ecuadorian police seized over 100 tons of cocaine, with an estimated market value of over $3.5 billion. This result was possible thanks to collaboration with the US authorities, which provided technical and financial support. In Switzerland, particularly in the canton of Ticino, the fight against drug trafficking is a priority. The cantonal police of Ticino have established a special team to combat drug trafficking, which works closely with federal and international authorities. For example, in the municipality of Lugano, the police seized over 10 kilograms of cocaine in 2022, with a market value of approximately 500,000 Swiss francs. Swiss regulations provide that authorities can seize assets and values derived from illegal activities, such as drug trafficking. The federal law on combating money laundering and terrorist financing, which came into effect on January 1, 2020, provides that authorities can seize assets and values up to 100,000 Swiss francs without judicial authorization. Frequently Asked Questions
How does international cooperation work to combat drug trafficking?
International cooperation is essential to combat drug trafficking. Countries such as the United States, Colombia and Peru share information and resources to combat drug trafficking. For example, in 2020, Ecuadorian police seized over 100 tons of cocaine with the support of US authorities.
What is the economic value of cocaine seizures in Switzerland and Ecuador?
In Switzerland, for example, 200 kg of cocaine (worth over CHF 10 million) were seized in Lugano. In Ecuador, more than 100 tons (estimated value of more than USD 3.5 billion) were seized in 2020.
What customs checks do truck drivers crossing the Italian-Swiss border have to face to prevent drug trafficking?
Trucks entering Switzerland are subject to documentary checks and random inspections of loads. In 2023, the authorities carried out 1,200 checks on Italian-Swiss routes, seizing 45 kg of cocaine worth an estimated CHF 2.2 million. Drivers must have accurate cargo declarations and are required to cooperate with police forces to avoid criminal penalties.
What is the value limit for the seizure of money without judicial authorisation in Switzerland and how does it affect cross-border workers?
Since 1 January 2020, the Federal Anti-Money Laundering Act has allowed the seizure of money of up to 100,000 Swiss francs without a judicial warrant. This limit protects cross-border commuters from prolonged blocks of legitimate sums, but in the event of suspicion of a link with drug trafficking, the authorities may require a court order for higher amounts, with possible delays in payments.
What are the main land routes of cocaine trafficking between Italy and Switzerland and what monitoring measures are adopted?
The most popular routes pass through the Chiasso, Como and Gotthard passes, where Swiss and Italian police forces carry out checks with drug-sniffing dogs and X-ray scanning systems. In 2022, 120 kg of cocaine were seized along these routes, thanks to joint operations and the daily exchange of intelligence between the two countries.
