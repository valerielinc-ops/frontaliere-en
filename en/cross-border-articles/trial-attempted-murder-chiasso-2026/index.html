Trial for attempted murder in Chiasso: attack with dumbbell

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 29 April 2026

The trial begins for an Eritrean citizen accused of attacking his partner with a gym dumbbell in Chiasso.

Contesto

In brief - Trial for attempted murder in Chiasso - Accused: Eritrean citizen - Date of attack: Night between 20 and 21 January 2024 ## Key facts - What: Attempted murder - When: Night between 20 and 21 January 2024 - Where: Chiasso, in an apartment on via Bossi - Who: Eritrean citizen, defended by lawyer Carolina Lamorgese - Amount: Sentence requested over five years The trial begins today at the Criminal Assizes against an Eritrean citizen, accused of attacking his partner during the night between 20 and 21 January 2024 in Chiasso. The man, defended by lawyer Carolina Lamorgese, will appear today in court before the Criminal Assizes Court presided over by Judge Amos Pagnamenta. Public prosecutor Roberto Ruggeri will request attempted murder against the defendant, who allegedly attacked his partner, driven by jealousy, hitting her on the head with a gym dumbbell. ### Circumstances of the attack The attack took place in an apartment on via Bossi in Chiasso. After the attack, the perpetrator fled with the two children in the car before being stopped the same night, shortly after 4:30 am, near Brienz, by the Bernese police. The psychiatric evaluation carried out on the man did not find any diminished culpability. In his favor goes only the exclusion of the danger of recidivism. ### Modalities of the attack According to what was anticipated by Rsi last November, the modalities of the attack would have been particularly cruel. The prosecution claims that the defendant would have lashed out against the victim with extreme violence. The defense, however, contests the intention to kill, a circumstance that the Criminal Assizes Court will be called to establish. ### Legal consequences For the defendant, a sentence of over five years will be requested. What protective measures are available for victims of domestic violence in Switzerland?

Children who witness episodes of domestic violence may develop anxiety, depression, behavioral problems, and academic difficulties. It is crucial that these children receive psychological support and are protected from further episodes of violence.
