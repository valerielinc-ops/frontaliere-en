Bellinzona Fortress Referendum: Vote on Sept 27 (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 30 May 2026

Bellinzona Municipality validated 3,738 signatures for the Fortress referendum. Here's what changes and why the vote is happening.

Contesto

In brief - Referendum on the Bellinzona Fortress deemed admissible - 3,738 signatures validated out of 4,650 submitted - Vote scheduled for 27 September - Project costs and management contested ## Key facts - What: Referendum against the tourist revival project of the Bellinzona Fortress - When: Vote on 27 September 2025 - Where: Bellinzona, Canton Ticino - Who: Interparty group coordinated by former mayor Brenno Martignoni Polti - Amount: 19 million Swiss francs allocated by the Municipal Council The Municipality of Bellinzona has validated 3,738 of the 4,650 signatures submitted on 11 May for the referendum 'Let's Save Our Castles'. This referendum was launched against the allocation of 19 million francs approved in March by the Municipal Council for the implementation of the first phase of the tourist, historical and cultural revival of the Fortress and for the planning of the second phase. ### Referendum context The referendum was promoted by an interparty group coordinated by former mayor Brenno Martignoni Polti and consisting of members of the UDC, Communist Party, Avanti con Ticino&Lavoro, supported by other forces such as the Movement for Socialism and HelvEthica. The 3,738 validated signatures far exceed the minimum of three thousand required, but significant are the over 900 excluded signatures, attributable to non-residents and foreigners without voting rights in Bellinzona. ### Reasons for the contestation There are three main elements of the contestation: the designation of Fortress instead of Castles, the paid entry to some sectors currently free and the assignment of management to a Foundation formed by various institutions rather than the Municipality. Dettagli operativi

Practical analysis: implications for cross-border workers The project to revitalise Bellinzona’s Fortezza could have significant implications for cross-border workers employed in Ticino. If approved, the initiative may lead to a surge in tourism across the region, bringing benefits to the local economic fabric. This could translate into new job opportunities in the tourism and service sectors, which often employ cross-border commuters. ### Economic impact The investment of 19 million Swiss francs, shared between the City, Canton, Confederation and private entities, could stimulate the local economy by creating new jobs and increasing demand for services. This could be particularly advantageous for cross-border workers in sectors such as catering, hospitality and retail. ### Tax considerations For cross-border commuters, it is important to consider the tax implications of any changes in the local economic landscape. A rise in tourism could lead to higher prices for services and goods, affecting the cost of living in Ticino. However, it could also result in higher wages in the tourism and service sectors, improving the economic conditions for cross-border workers. ### Comparison with the current situation Currently, access to Bellinzona’s castles is free for certain groups. The introduction of an entrance fee could influence the habits of cross-border workers who visit these sites during their free time. However, the project could also lead to improved infrastructure and services, making visits more enjoyable and engaging. ## Recommended tools For an updated estimate, use the net salary calculator and the CHF-EUR exchange comparator.

What to do: procedure and deadlines For cross-border workers who wish to participate in the vote on 27 September, it is important to be informed about the procedures and deadlines. Here is a step-by-step guide on what to do: ### Step 1: Verify the right to vote Cross-border workers residing in Switzerland who are Swiss citizens have the right to vote. It is important to check your registration in the electoral register of your municipality of residence. ### Step 2: Learn about the project Before voting, it is advisable to gather detailed information about the project to relaunch the Bellinzona Fortress. You can consult the website of the Municipality of Bellinzona or attend public meetings organised by the local authorities. ### Step 3: Participate in the vote The vote will take place on 27 September. It is important to go to the polling station in your municipality of residence with a valid identity document. ### Useful tools For further information on the tax and employment implications of the project, you can use the salary calculator to assess the economic impact of any changes in salaries and costs. ### Final considerations The referendum on the Bellinzona Fortress represents an opportunity for cross-border workers to express their opinion on a project that could have a significant impact on the economic and social life of the region. Frequently Asked Questions
What is the objective of the referendum on the Bellinzona Fortress?
The referendum aims to challenge the allocation of 19 million Swiss francs approved by the Municipal Council for the implementation of the first phase of the tourist, historical, and cultural revitalization of the Bellinzona Fortress.
Who can participate in the vote on September 27?
Swiss citizens residing in Bellinzona and registered in the municipal electoral roll are eligible to vote.
What are the main objections to the project?
The main objections concern the use of the term Fortress instead of Castles, the introduction of paid entry for some areas that are currently free, and the management being assigned to a Foundation rather than the Municipality.
