Italy delays health credit reimbursements: Quadri asks for clarity

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 30 April 2026

National Councillor Lorenzo Quadri questions the Federal Council about Italy's delays in health reimbursements

Contesto

In brief - Italy accumulates delays in health refunds to Switzerland - Quadri requests updated data for 2024-2025 - Critical situation also for Campione d'Italia ## Key facts - What: Interpellation on health credit reimbursements - When: April 30, 2026 - Where: Bern - Who: National Councilor Lorenzo Quadri - Amount: Unspecified arrears Credits related to international healthcare and their possible effects on health insurance premiums are back at the center of political debate. With an interpellation, National Councilor Lorenzo Quadri (Lega dei Ticinesi) asks the Federal Council to clarify the situation of reimbursements between Switzerland and Italy, in light of the critical issues that have emerged in recent years. The topic fits into a broader context, marked by the constant increase in healthcare costs that weighs on citizens and insured individuals. In particular, Quadri highlights the difficulties reported in 2023 by federal authorities regarding the reimbursement of credits derived from international healthcare, regulated by European coordination law. According to the findings, Italy would not have systematically respected payment deadlines, accumulating significant arrears, including those related to Campione d'Italia. Despite some partial reimbursements, a substantial part of the credits would still be outstanding. ### Implications for cross-border workers The critical situation of health reimbursements between Italy and Switzerland could have direct repercussions on cross-border workers who use healthcare services in both countries. In particular, payment delays could lead to an increase in insurance premiums, directly affecting the pockets of transborder workers. Dettagli operativi

With his interpellation, Quadri asks the Federal Council for updated data for 2024-2025 on the total amount of credits held by Switzerland against Italy, as well as the portion still outstanding and unpaid. Among the points raised are any late payment interest applied for accumulated delays and the current status of payments by Italy. Special attention is given to the case of Campione d'Italia, for which the national councillor demands precise figures and updates on the status of payments. Quadri also wants to know what concrete measures have been taken by the Italian authorities to ensure compliance with deadlines and what guarantees Switzerland has today to avoid a repetition of similar situations. Finally, the interpellation focuses on the economic consequences of these delays, asking to what extent the uncollected amounts affect the overall costs of the Swiss healthcare system and, indirectly, the premiums of insured individuals. ### Analysis of Economic Consequences Delays in the payment of healthcare credits can have a significant impact on the Swiss healthcare system. In particular, uncollected amounts could lead to an increase in insurance premiums, directly affecting citizens and cross-border workers who use healthcare services in both countries. Furthermore, the lack of clarity on future guarantees could generate uncertainty among citizens who rely on healthcare facilities in Italian territory. ### Possible Corrective Measures To prevent the situation from worsening, the Federal Council could adopt more stringent corrective measures, such as mandatory recovery plans or stricter guarantees for future payments.
