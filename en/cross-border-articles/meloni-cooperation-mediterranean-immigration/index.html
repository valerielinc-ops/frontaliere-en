Meloni highlighted how uncontrolled migratory flows put citizens' security to the test and can compromise state stability, also affecting the economy and the labor market. 'We know that uncontrolled migratory flows put citizens' security to the test and can even compromise state stability. But they also hit the economy, putting public resources to the test and influencing the labor market. They weaken competitiveness, increasing uncertainty and social tensions,' Meloni stated. ### Implications for cross-border workers Meloni's statements have direct implications for cross-border workers who commute to Switzerland and reside in Italy, especially in Canton Ticino. Economic and social stability is crucial for those who cross the border daily to work. 'First of all - explained the Prime Minister - we cannot address only one aspect of this polycrisis. Secondly, migration is an integral part of the polycrisis. Thirdly, no one can tackle it alone. Therefore, cooperation in this area is a prerequisite,' Meloni added. ### International cooperation In recent years, the European Union has made significant progress with a new pact on asylum, a new definition of safe third countries, and new regulations on repatriations.
