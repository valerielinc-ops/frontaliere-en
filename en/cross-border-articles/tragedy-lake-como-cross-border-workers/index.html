Tragedy at Lake Como: 15-year-old boy dies (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 3 May 2026

A 15-year-old boy loses his life in Lake Como. His friends raise the alarm, but rescuers fail to save him.

Contesto

In brief - A 15-year-old dies in Lake Como - Dive from a pier in Mandello del Lario - Coast Guard and Firefighters intervene ## Key facts - What: Fatal dive in Lake Como - When: Early afternoon - Where: Mandello del Lario, Lecco province - Who: A 15-year-old and his friends - Amount: Not specified A 15-year-old lost his life in Lake Como. He had dived in the early afternoon from a pier in Mandello del Lario, in the province of Lecco. His friends, not seeing him resurface, raised the alarm. The Coast Guard and the Firefighters' helicopter, with divers on board, immediately intervened and recovered the body from the bottom. The carabinieri are present to reconstruct the dynamics of the event. The mayor of Mandello del Lario, Riccardo Fasoli, commented on the tragedy on Facebook: 'We need to do something. We put up signs, staff, local police. But here it must be explained that the rules exist because they are for the protection of everyone. Everyone's life. Social media, television, and everything else teach to transgress, to be against. Today a boy lost his life, in search of fun, in a dangerous place, with access prohibited and in a lake that, if you don't know how to swim well in fresh water, is deadly. Something needs to be done in the homes and communities from where these boys leave in the morning, for an afternoon of fun that often is already in the idea of becoming transgressive. And it takes very little. Very little.' ### Implications for cross-border workers This tragedy raises important questions about the safety of lakes and preventive measures. Local authorities are evaluating the introduction of additional warning signs and an increased presence of security personnel in the bathing areas. Frequently Asked Questions
What are the local regulations for beach safety?
Local regulations for beach safety may vary depending on the location. It's important to inform yourself through local authorities or consult official websites to get updated information. In general, it's crucial to respect access or diving bans from certain points and pay attention to danger signs.
What should I do in case of an emergency in the water?
In case of an emergency in the water, it's essential to immediately call for help using the emergency number 112. Do not attempt to intervene alone without adequate equipment. Wait for the arrival of professional rescuers and report the danger to local authorities and nearby swimmers.
Where can I find updated information on local regulations?
You can consult the official websites of local authorities or contact the competent offices directly to obtain updated information on local regulations. Additionally, it's useful to use dedicated beach safety apps or attend swimming and water safety courses organized by local authorities or sports associations.
