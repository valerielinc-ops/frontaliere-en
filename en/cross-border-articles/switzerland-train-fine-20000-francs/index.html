Switzerland: 20,000 francs fine for traveling without a ticket (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 6 May 2026

A 40-year-old man from the Canton of Schwyz was fined 20,000 francs for traveling without a ticket and forging documents.

Contesto

In brief - Record fine of 20,000 francs for traveling without a ticket - False identity and fraud uncovered - Criminal record aggravates the penalty ## Key facts - What: Traveling without a ticket and document fraud - When: Last year - Where: Canton of Schwyz, Switzerland - Who: Swiss Federal Railways (SBB) - Amount: 20,000 francs In Switzerland, traveling without a ticket on public transport is an offense monitored with extreme rigor. However, the case of a 40-year-old resident in the Canton of Schwyz is incredible. The Swiss Federal Railways (SBB) provide an administrative sanction of around 90 francs for the first offense, but for this passenger, the final bill has soared to the astronomical figure of 20,000 francs. It all started when the man, last year, was intercepted on board an SBB train without a valid ticket. In an attempt to avoid the fine, the forty-year-old showed the controllers a stolen ID card, subsequently signing the report with a false signature. The fraud was uncovered by a technological mishap: the rightful owner of the document, unaware of the incident, received an official notification via email from the SBB regarding the fine issued on the train. Suspicious of the charge for a journey he had never taken, the user immediately filed a complaint, triggering the investigations that revealed the crime of impersonation. ### The verdict of the Schwyz Prosecution The Schwyz Prosecution issued a formal indictment against the forty-year-old, accusing him of forging identity documents and making false accusations. However, the amount of the fine was not only increased by the serious offenses committed on the train, but above all by the subject's complex judicial past. Travelling without a ticket in Switzerland can result in heavy fines, and the consequences can be even more severe if one attempts to evade responsibilities through fraudulent means. ### Comparison with the previous situation Before this case, fines for travelling without a ticket in Switzerland were known to be strict but predictable. The standard fine for a first offence was around 90 francs, with progressive increases in case of repeat offences. However, this case has shown that the consequences can be much more severe if further crimes are committed, such as the forgery of documents. ### Concrete scenarios Imagine a cross-border worker who regularly travels to Switzerland for work. If they were to forget to buy a ticket, the fine would be around 90 francs. However, if they attempted to avoid the fine through fraudulent means, such as the use of false documents, the consequences could be much more serious, as demonstrated by this case. It is therefore fundamental to always be in order with documents and transport tickets to avoid unpleasant legal consequences. ### Concrete procedures To avoid incurring fines, cross-border workers should follow some concrete procedures: 1.
