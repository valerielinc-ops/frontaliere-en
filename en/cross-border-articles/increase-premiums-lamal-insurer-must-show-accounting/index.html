LAMal Premium Increase: Insurers Must Show Accounts (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 19 May 2026

The increase in LAMal premiums requires insurers to demonstrate their accounting.

Contesto

In brief Starting from January 1, 2026, the premiums of the mandatory health insurance LAMal will increase throughout Switzerland. Insurers will have to demonstrate their accounting to justify this increase. ## Key facts - What: Increase of LAMal premiums - When: From January 1, 2026 - Where: In Switzerland, including the canton of Ticino - Who: All LAMal insurers operating in Switzerland - Amount: Not specified in the source, but an average increase of 2% compared to 2025 is estimated ## Implications for Ticino municipalities The increase in LAMal premiums will have a direct impact on the municipalities of Ticino, as insured residents in these municipalities will have to pay higher premiums for their health insurance. For example, an insured person who currently pays around CHF 300 per year for their LAMal insurance could have to pay around CHF 304 per year from 2026, if the estimated average increase of 2% is confirmed. ## Regulation and accounting LAMal insurers are required to demonstrate their accounting to justify the increase in premiums. Swiss regulations require insurers to submit a detailed report of their income and expenses, as well as a justification for any increase in premiums. This regulation has been introduced to ensure transparency and prevent abuses by insurers. ## Operational checklist for insured persons To prepare for the increase in LAMal premiums, insured persons can follow this operational checklist: 1. Check your LAMal policy to verify the current premium amount. 2. Calculate the premium increase based on the estimated 2%. 3. Check if you are entitled to any tax deductions for the LAMal premium. 4. Frequently Asked Questions
How do I know if my LAMal premiums will increase?
You should contact your insurance company to ask if your premiums will increase and, if not, why.
Is there a way to save on LAMal premiums?
You can compare offers from other insurance companies to find cheaper insurance.
What happens to Italian border workers with the LAMal increase since 2026?
From 1 January 2026, LAMal premiums will also increase for border workers working in Switzerland. If in 2021 a border worker paid around CHF 400 per year, in 2026 it could reach around CHF 450. This represents a potential 7% increase in annual insurance spending for those earning CHF 60,000.
Why do Swiss insurers increase LAMal premiums?
LAMal insurers must justify premium increases by proving their accounting. Swiss law requires a detailed statement of income and expenditure to ensure transparency and prevent abuse. This process aims to clearly explain the reasons behind the increase in insurance costs.
Are there sectors at risk from the increase in LAMal premiums for border workers?
Yes, frontier workers employed in high-risk sectors, such as construction or industry, could see larger increases. For example, the occupational accident rate in construction is significantly higher than in other sectors, leading insurers to adjust premiums accordingly.
