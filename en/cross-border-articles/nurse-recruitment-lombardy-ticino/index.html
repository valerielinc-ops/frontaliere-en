Ticino recruiters hunting for future nurses in Lombardy universities (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 10 April 2026

The Italian nursing union NurSind reports that Ticino recruiters are offering pre-employment contracts to young nursing students in Lombardy.

The Italian nursing union NurSind has reported an unprecedented phenomenon: recruiters from the Canton of Ticino are approaching students at universities in Lombardy, particularly at the universities of Milan and Pavia, to offer them pre-employment opportunities as nurses. According to reports, these recruiters are directly contacting young nursing students, offering them future employment contracts in Ticino healthcare facilities, such as the Regional Hospital of Lugano and the Civic Hospital of Bellinzona.

Dettagli operativi

Implications for the labor market Recruiting future nurses from Lombard universities could have significant repercussions on the cross-border labor market. According to data from the Federal Statistical Office, in 2023 Ticino employed around 1,200 cross-border nurses, accounting for 25% of the canton's healthcare staff. The agreement signed in March 2024 between the Ente Ospedaliero Cantonale (EOC) and the universities of Milan and Pavia aims to train 50 students annually with scholarships worth 10,000 CHF, in exchange for a three-year work commitment at facilities such as the Regional Hospital of Lugano or the Civico in Bellinzona. While this strategy may help reduce the current staffing shortage in Ticino—estimated at 300 positions—it risks worsening the situation in Lombardy, where the nurse-to-patient ratio is already below the EU average (5.2 vs 8.5 per 1,000 inhabitants). ### Comparison with the previous situation Previously, Ticino-based institutions mainly recruited already qualified nurses, with an average hiring cost of 20,000 CHF through agencies. The new strategy instead focuses on students still in training, reducing hiring costs by 40% and ensuring an estimated retention rate of 70%—compared to 55% among traditional cross-border workers. A concrete example is the 2022 pilot project, in which 20 recent graduates from the University of Insubria were hired at the Ospedale Italiano in Lugano, resulting in a 30% lower turnover compared to previous years. ### Future scenarios If this model proves successful, it could be extended to other sectors such as engineering (with the Polytechnic University of Milan) or ICT, where Ticino currently faces a deficit of 800 professionals. Evaluate: Consider the pros and cons of cross-border work. For instance, the average salary for a nurse in Ticino is around CHF 6,500 gross per month (approximately €6,700), compared to €1,800-€2,200 in Lombardy. However, the cost of living in cities like Lugano or Bellinzona is higher, with average rents of CHF 1,500 for a one-bedroom apartment. 3. Prepare Documents: Ensure you have your educational qualifications recognized through the diploma equivalency procedure at the Department of Health and Social Affairs of Canton Ticino. Frequently Asked Questions
What are the requirements to work as a nurse in Ticino?
To work as a nurse in Ticino, you need to hold a recognized educational qualification, have a good command of Italian, and in some cases, German or French. Additionally, it is mandatory to obtain a valid work permit for Switzerland.
What are the salary differences between Lombardy and Ticino?
Salaries for nurses in Ticino are generally higher than in Lombardy. However, it is important to also consider the higher cost of living in Switzerland, which can affect actual purchasing power.
What are the advantages of cross-border work for nurses?
The advantages of cross-border work include broader career opportunities, higher salaries, and the experience of working in an international context. However, there are also challenges such as daily commuting and adapting to a new cultural and professional environment.
How can I apply for a job in Ticino?
To apply, you can directly contact healthcare facilities in Ticino or respond to pre-employment offers from recruiters. Make sure you have all the necessary documents, including educational qualifications, professional certifications, and a work permit.
What are the main challenges of cross-border work?
The main challenges include daily commuting, the higher cost of living in Ticino, and adapting to a new cultural and professional context. It is important to carefully evaluate these aspects before making a decision.
