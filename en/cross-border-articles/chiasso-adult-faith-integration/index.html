Chiasso: the faith rediscovered by adults and the changing Ticino (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 1 March 2026

In Chiasso, confirmation is celebrated at 40 years old, a sign of an evolving Ticino where faith becomes a conscious choice. What are the implications for the cross-border community?

Contesto

TL;DR - Adults in Chiasso seek confirmation by choice, not tradition. - Chiasso's cross-border workers experience evolving faith dynamics. - Don Feliciani highlights a shift from 'faith by inertia' to 'faith by choice'. ## Key facts - Location: Chiasso, Mendrisiotto, Ticino - Trend: Adults in their 20s, 30s, and 40s seeking confirmation - Priest: Don Gianfranco Feliciani, retiring next summer - Confirmation Candidates: 22 candidates, nearly half are adults - Date: Reported by Tio.ch on February 28, 2026 - Impact: Rediscovery of spirituality with mature awareness - Future Plans: Don Feliciani will move to Rancate but remain available to the community A significant social phenomenon is emerging in the heart of Mendrisiotto, particularly in the city of Chiasso, a crucial crossroads for thousands of cross-border workers who cross the border daily to work in Ticino. Don Gianfranco Feliciani, the long-serving parish priest of Chiasso for 25 years and set to retire next summer, has revealed a significant change in local religious practices: confirmation, a sacrament traditionally associated with adolescence, is now being requested and celebrated by adults in their 20s, 30s, and even 40s. This trend, described by the priest as a shift from a faith 'by inertia' to a 'by choice', reflects a broader transformation in Ticinese society and, by extension, in the cultural context in which cross-border workers find themselves living. Don Feliciani's observation, having served the Chiasso community with a booming and often provocative voice, highlights that "Christianity does not work as it once did. Today, sacraments are no longer received because 'one must'. But because one wants to. And indeed, I have people being confirmed at 40 years old. By choice." This is not an isolated case...

Dettagli operativi

This evolution in religious practices in Chiasso is not an isolated phenomenon, but fits into a broader context of social change affecting the entire Canton of Ticino and, more generally, Europe. Don Feliciani indeed emphasized that "our weary Europe at a certain point thought it could set aside even Christian faith. It believed it could do without it. But then what happens? Faith is rediscovered in another way. With even more conviction." This 'change of approach' has significant implications for social cohesion and the sense of community, fundamental aspects also for cross-border workers who experience the Ticinese reality daily. ## Impact on Cross-Border Workers and Ticinese Society - Cultural Integration: For cross-border workers, understanding these cultural and social nuances can facilitate deeper integration. It is not just about fulfilling work or tax obligations, but about understanding the roots and evolutions of the society that hosts them. The rediscovery of faith as a personal choice, for example, may indicate a greater openness and tolerance towards different spiritual and cultural sensitivities within the community. - Social Dynamics in Chiasso: As a border city with a high percentage of residents and commuters from various nations, Chiasso is particularly exposed to these transformations. The presence of "populations from various ethnicities and different migrants" mentioned by Don Feliciani makes the city a microcosm of the challenges and opportunities related to diversity.
