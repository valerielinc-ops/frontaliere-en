Rancate: monthly neighborhood market opens (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 5 May 2026

In Rancate, Mendrisio, the neighborhood market has opened, an initiative promoted by the City of Mendrisio and Pro Senectute Ticino and Moesano

Contesto

In brief - New neighborhood market in Rancate, Mendrisio - Initiative promoted by the City of Mendrisio and Pro Senectute - First event on May 22, 2026 ## Key facts - What: Neighborhood market - When: First event on May 22, 2026, from 8:30 AM to 11:30 AM - Where: Rancate, Mendrisio - Who: City of Mendrisio and Pro Senectute Ticino and Moesano - Amount: Not specified In Rancate, a neighborhood of Mendrisio, the Neighborhood Market has been launched, an initiative that promises to become a regular event for the local community. The market was developed through collaboration between the City of Mendrisio and the Pro Senectute Ticino and Moesano Foundation. This project is part of the activities of "La Dispensa," an initiative launched in January 2025 by the City of Mendrisio and the Rancate District Commission, with methodological support from Pro Senectute Ticino and Moesano. "La Dispensa," located at the foot of the town hall, has established itself as a socio-aggregative space capable of fostering social cohesion and solidarity among the neighborhood's residents and the entire municipality. Throughout 2025 and the first months of 2026, numerous free activities open to all ages have been organized, including neighborhood dinners and aperitifs, playful moments, and cultural initiatives carried out in collaboration with local associations. Since May of this year, there has also been a collaboration with the L'Orto di Muzzano association, which every Friday morning offers a stall with their own produce. The initiative has been a great success, becoming a regular appointment for many people in Rancate and surrounding areas, who take advantage not only to shop but also to share convivial moments. Concrete actions for cross-border workers For cross-border workers who wish to participate in the Rancate Neighborhood Market, there are some concrete steps to follow. First of all, it is important to register as sellers by contacting the number 058 688 36 28 or by writing to the address ladispensa@mendrisio.ch. It is necessary to provide all the information required for setting up the stall and to respect the indicated deadlines. ### Registration procedure 1. Contact the number 058 688 36 28 or write to the address ladispensa@mendrisio.ch 2. Provide all the information necessary for setting up the stall 3. Respect the indicated deadlines 4. Participate in the market on the day of the event ### Participation as visitors For cross-border workers who wish to participate in the Neighborhood Market as visitors, no registration is required. Just show up at the market on the day of the event to discover the products and initiatives on offer. It is advisable to arrive early to have the opportunity to buy the most appealing products and participate in the proposed activities. ### Useful tools For further information and to stay updated on the initiatives of the Neighborhood Market, it is possible to visit the website of the City of Mendrisio or follow the social pages of Pro Senectute Ticino and Moesano. Frequently Asked Questions
How can I participate in the neighborhood market as a seller?
To participate in the neighborhood market as a seller, you need to register by contacting the number 058 688 36 28 or by writing to the address ladispensa@mendrisio.ch. It is important to provide all the necessary information for setting up the stall and to respect the indicated deadlines.
What are the advantages for cross-border workers who participate in the neighborhood market?
Cross-border workers who participate in the neighborhood market can benefit from numerous advantages, including the opportunity to further integrate into the local community, support the local economy, and reduce the environmental impact related to transportation.
What can I find at the neighborhood market of Rancate?
At the neighborhood market of Rancate, you can find local and artisan products, as well as participate in cultural and social initiatives. Additionally, you can buy homegrown vegetables thanks to the collaboration with the association L'Orto di Muzzano.
