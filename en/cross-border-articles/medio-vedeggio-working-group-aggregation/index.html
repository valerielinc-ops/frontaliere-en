On March 2, 2026, the Municipal Councils of Bedano, Cadempino, Gravesano, and Lamone formally established a working group with the goal of conducting an in-depth study aimed at merging these four municipalities, which together have around 6,000 inhabitants. This decision stems from a favorable political climate and a shared drive to strengthen the presence and territorial efficiency of these communities within the cantonal context. The working group will consist of two representatives from each municipality, tasked with developing a joint project by spring 2028 that can be submitted for public consultation and subsequently approved by the municipal assemblies. This initiative fits into a broader framework of reforms and administrative optimizations promoted by the Department of Finance and Economy (DFE) of Canton Ticino, which in recent years has encouraged the creation of mergers to improve services offered to citizens and optimize management costs. Operational details

The regulatory process for municipal mergers in Ticino involves a series of well-defined stages that the Middle Vedeggio working group must follow. First, the merger study must analyze the economic and social impact of the potential fusion, evaluating transition costs, economies of scale, and the potential rationalization of public services. According to current cantonal law, every merger project must be accompanied by a detailed plan that also includes human resource management, urban planning, and the financial framework, with particular attention to maintaining the quality of services for citizens and businesses. The deadline set for completing the study is spring 2028, coinciding with the end of the current legislative term and allowing for the planning of a possible public vote before the term expires. After the publication of the results, each municipality is expected to organize informational meetings and public consultations to gather citizens’ opinions. Only with broad consensus will it be possible to proceed with the actual merger, which requires direct voter approval. From a financial perspective, Canton Ticino guarantees economic incentives for municipalities that choose to merge, reducing the tax burden and improving investment capacity. These contributions are particularly relevant in an area like Middle Vedeggio, where the presence of numerous cross-border workers and proximity to important communication hubs such as the Lamone-Cadempino station can foster sustainable economic development. Moreover, the merger could facilitate the management of integration policies for cross-border workers, a crucial topic given the strong presence of Italians who daily cross the Brogeda and Gaggiolo checkpoints to reach Ticino. Key points

For residents and workers of Middle Vedeggio, the municipal merger represents more than just an administrative reform: it is a concrete opportunity to improve the quality of public services and the livability of the area. Those living in Bedano, Cadempino, Gravesano, or Lamone will benefit from a more efficient administration, capable of responding more quickly to daily needs, from traffic management to social policies. Economically, a larger municipal structure can attract investments and develop joint projects in vocational training, in collaboration with Ticino institutions such as SUPSI and USI. This is particularly relevant for Italian cross-border workers employed in Ticino, as a cohesive municipality will be able to improve the management of cross-border relations and facilitate access to dedicated services, such as employment offices and administrative support. Furthermore, the synergies created by the merger can lead to more effective management of territory and infrastructure, with a positive impact on costs for taxpayers. For example, the rationalization of school, healthcare (in collaboration with the EOC), and security services could result in improved quality without excessive cost increases.
