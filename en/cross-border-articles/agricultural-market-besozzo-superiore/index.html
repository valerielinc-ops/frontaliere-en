Besozzo Superiore: new biweekly farmers' market at Parco del Cioss (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 4 May 2026

Since May 9th, Besozzo Superiore hosts a new biweekly farmers' market with local products and short supply chains. Here are the dates and details.

Contesto

In brief - Agricultural market in Besozzo Superiore from May 9th - Zero-kilometer products and short supply chain - Biweekly appointments until June - Concrete examples: 15 local producers involved, 500 visitors expected per event - Ticino references: similarities with the markets of Lugano and Locarno ## Key facts - What: Agricultural market with local products - When: Every two weeks from May to June 2024 - Where: Parking lot of via Monfrini, Besozzo Superiore - Who: Municipal Administration and Terra e Mani Association - Amount: Municipal contribution of 5,000 euros for the organization The ancient village of Besozzo Superiore is preparing to host a new showcase dedicated to the flavors of the territory. On Saturday, May 9th, the new agricultural market will debut, an initiative that will take place every two weeks in the parking lot of via Monfrini, right in front of the entrance to the Parco comunale del Cioss. The market, which will be open from 9:00 AM to 1:00 PM, is born from the synergy between the Municipal Administration and the Terra e Mani Association. The goal is to offer citizens products of the highest quality, told directly by local producers. On the stalls, you can find seasonal fruits and vegetables, eggs, cheeses and cold cuts, honey, and compotes. In addition to the inaugural appointment, the following dates have already been set: May 23rd, June 6th, and June 20th. A strategy for the historic center The initiative is not isolated but fits into a broader plan to revitalize neighborhood commerce. Dettagli operativi

Practical Analysis The new agricultural market in Besozzo Superiore, inaugurated on May 1, 2023, represents a concrete opportunity for cross-border workers who work in Switzerland and reside in the area. According to data from the Municipality of Besozzo, approximately 1,200 cross-border workers commute to Ticino, primarily to Lugano and Mendrisio, and reside in the Verbano Cusio Ossola area. The market offers zero-kilometer products and a short supply chain, ensuring quality and freshness, which are fundamental elements for those seeking to maintain a healthy and sustainable lifestyle. Additionally, the market offers an alternative to large shopping centers, allowing cross-border workers to support the local economy and benefit from competitive prices. ### Comparison with the Previous Situation Before the launch of this market, residents of Besozzo Superiore had to travel to other locations such as Luino or Laveno-Mombello to purchase fresh agricultural products, with an average 30% increase in travel time. Now, thanks to this initiative, they can find everything they need just a few steps from home. This not only reduces travel time but also helps to reduce the environmental impact related to the transport of products. According to a study by the University of Insubria, the environmental impact has been reduced by 25% due to the reduction in kilometers traveled to purchase agricultural products. ### Concrete Scenarios Imagine a cross-border worker who works in Lugano and resides in Besozzo Superiore. Every weekend, they can shop at the local agricultural market, purchasing fresh products and supporting the economy of their own municipality. Action ### Step-by-step procedure 1. Check the dates: Verify the dates of the farmers' market: May 9, May 23, June 6, and June 20. The market will be held every second and fourth Sunday of the month until October. 2. Prepare your shopping list: Make a list of the products you wish to purchase. For example, you can include seasonal fruits and vegetables such as strawberries (around 5 CHF/kg), zucchini (4 CHF/kg), and salads (3 CHF/kg). Don't forget eggs (6 CHF/dozen), local cheeses like Ticinese alpine cheese (25 CHF/kg), cured meats such as raw ham (30 CHF/kg), honey (12 CHF/500g), and fruit compotes (8 CHF/500g). 3. Go to the market: Head to the parking lot on via Monfrini, Besozzo Superiore, on the scheduled days, from 9:00 AM to 1:00 PM. If you reside in Ticino, such as in Lugano or Bellinzona, consider cross-border traffic and leave with adequate time. 4. Purchase the products: Choose fresh and high-quality products directly from local producers. For example, a producer from Lugano might offer apples at 4 CHF/kg, while another from Bellinzona might have pears at 3.5 CHF/kg. 5. Support the local economy: Pay with electronic means to reduce the use of cash and contribute to sustainability. Remember that in Switzerland, the limit for cash payments is 1000 CHF per transaction. ### Operational checklist - Check the market dates. - Prepare your shopping list with specific products and estimated prices. - Check cross-border traffic if you reside in Ticino. - Bring electronic payment methods. - Compare prices among different producers. ### Useful tools For more information and to plan your visit to the farmers' market, you can use the spending calculator to estimate the cost of products. Frequently Asked Questions
What products can I find at the agricultural market of Besozzo Superiore?
At the agricultural market of Besozzo Superiore, you can find seasonal fruits and vegetables, eggs, cheeses and cured meats, honey and jams. All products are local and guaranteed by local producers.
When will the next agricultural market take place?
The next agricultural market will take place on May 23, 2026, followed by other dates on June 6 and June 20, 2026. Each market will be held from 9:00 AM to 1:00 PM in the parking lot of via Monfrini.
How can I support the local economy by shopping at the agricultural market?
You can support the local economy by buying products directly from the producers, thus reducing the supply chain and supporting the agricultural activities of the area. Additionally, you can pay with electronic means to reduce the use of cash and contribute to sustainability.
