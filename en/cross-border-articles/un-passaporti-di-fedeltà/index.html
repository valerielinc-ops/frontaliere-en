Frequently Asked Questions
How does the loyalty passport contest organized by Gastronomic Review Mendrisio and Lower Ceresio work?
The competition requires the collection of 8 stamps on a loyalty passport, obtainable with purchases of dishes or drinks in participating restaurants. The winners, selected by lot, receive awards such as subscriptions to gastronomic clubs or local product kits.
How many restaurants and bars have participated in the loyalty passport contest of the Gastronomic Review Mendrisio and Basso Ceresio?
The exact number of participating restaurants and bars is not specified in the article, but are mentioned some examples such as 'La Tavola' in Mendrisio, 'Il Giardino del Vino' in Basso Ceresio, 'Ristorante La Terrazza' in Mendrisio and 'Ristorante Il Giardino' in Lugano, indicating a plural participation.
What are the requirements to participate in the loyalty passport competition if I am an Italian frontier worker working in Ticino?
Italian border guards simply need to buy at least one dish or drink in one of the participating restaurants/bars and request a digital or paper stamp for each purchase. No further formalities are required, but a valid ID must be presented at the time of the collection of the 8 stamps.
Is the digital stamp collection system also accessible by Italian smartphones? And what data is required?
Yes, the digital system is accessible via mobile browser. Restaurants only ask for an email address and a telephone number to send confirmation of stamps, in accordance with the GDPR. No sensitive data such as the Italian tax code is collected.
If I win the competition, how are the awards given if I live in Italy and work in Ticino?
Prizes (e.g. subscriptions or local product kits) are sent directly to the address provided at the time of registration, both in Italy and in Ticino. Old Masseria SA covers shipping costs for residents in Italy, as specified in the terms of the competition.
