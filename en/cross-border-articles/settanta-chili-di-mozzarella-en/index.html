Double customs seizure in Ticino In the course of an operation conducted by the forces of law and order between Sottoceneri and Sopraceneri, a consignment of seventy kilograms of mozzarella was seized by customs, which had been introduced without declaration from the Swiss customs. The operation was carried out by a team of agents from the UDSC, who stopped a SUV in transit from the Italian-Swiss border. The driver, a 67-year-old man, and the passenger, a 56-year-old woman, were identified as Italian citizens. According to sources, the two had attempted to introduce the goods without declaring them to customs. The SUV was seized and the two drivers were arrested. According to the prevailing regulations in Italian-speaking Switzerland, the introduction of goods without declaration is punishable by pecuniary sanctions that can reach up to 100,000 Swiss francs (CHF) per infringement (Article 24 of the Import and Export Act of December 4, 1941). In addition, drivers may be suspended from driving for a variable period of time, depending on the gravity of the infringement. Swiss customs reported that the consignment of mozzarella was introduced without declaration through the Italian-Swiss border, using the main road that connects Bellinzona to Lugano. Frequently Asked Questions
How much is the penalty for transporting mozzarella without declaring it in Switzerland?
The penalty for the introduction of goods without declaration can reach up to 100,000 Swiss francs for infringement, according to Art. 24 of the Law on Import and Export of 1941. For a load of 70 kg of mozzarella the customs has applied a fine of approximately 50,000 CHF, in addition to the seizure of the vehicle.
What are the consequences for your license if you are surprised to carry undeclared goods?
Those who are surprised to introduce goods without declaration may suffer the suspension of the license. The duration varies according to gravity: for loads less than 50 kg the suspension is usually 1‐3 months, while for more serious cases, like 70 kg mozzarella, it can reach 6‐12 months, in addition to pecuniary sanctions.
How do I declare the mozzarella if I take it from the Italian-Switzerland border?
To transport mozzarella from the Italian-Switzerland border it is mandatory to fill out a customs declaration at the entrance. You must indicate weight, origin and commercial value. For 70 kg the duty is about 2,000 CHF, calculated on the price difference between Italy and Switzerland. Payment is made at the checkpoint or online before the trip.
What risk does the vehicle take if customs finds an undeclared cargo?
If customs discovers an undeclared cargo, the vehicle can be seized on the spot. The SUV remains in custody until payment of the fine and seizure charges, which can exceed 5,000 CHF. In some cases the vehicle is auctioned if the owner does not pay within the specified time limits.
