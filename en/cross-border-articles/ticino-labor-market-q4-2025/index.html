Operational details

The technical analysis of the Ticino labor market in the fourth quarter of 2025 highlights important regulatory and quantitative aspects to consider for those working or intending to work in the Canton. First and foremost, the current legislative framework carefully regulates work permits for cross-border workers, particularly the G permits, which allow Italian workers to return home daily. During the quarter, over 25,000 new permits were issued, marking a 4% increase compared to the previous quarter, indicating that Ticino companies continue to invest in qualified personnel from Italy. From a salary perspective, the services sector showed an average increase of 1.5% in contractual wages, in line with the inflation forecast for 2026. Specifically, cross-border workers employed in healthcare, finance, and technology benefited from salary increases ranging between 200 and 400 francs per month, confirming the competitiveness of the Ticino market compared to neighboring Italian areas. For example, a nurse with a G permit working at the EOC in Bellinzona can expect an average net monthly salary of around 5,800 CHF, with advantages stemming from the stability of the Swiss healthcare system. The balance between labor demand and supply is also influenced by increasing digitalization and the need for professionals with advanced technical skills, a sector where SUPSI and USI play a key role. Courses and collaborations with companies help bridge the training gap, facilitating labor market integration.
