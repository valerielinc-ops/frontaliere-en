Bankitalia: Limited margins due to debt, not just EU rules

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 28 April 2026

Limited margins stem from the need to reduce debt, not just EU rules. Downward path from 2027.

Contesto

In brief - Limited margins to contain debt, not just due to EU rules - Debt descending path from 2027 - Reduction in the yield differential between Italian and German securities ## Key facts - What: Limited margins to contain debt - When: Debt descending path from 2027 - Where: Italy - Who: Department of Economics and Statistics of the Bank of Italy - Amount: Reduction in the yield differential between Italian and German securities The head of the Department of Economics and Statistics of the Bank of Italy, Andrea Brandolini, stated in a hearing before the joint Budget Committees of the Chamber and Senate on the Public Finance Document that the limited margins available derive, even before the rules of European governance, from the need to put the debt in relation to the product on a descending path. According to the trend scenario of the DFP, this would occur from 2027. It would be a significant positive signal, also for the confidence of savers and for the assessment given to our country in the financial markets. The prudence in the management of public accounts and the willingness to respect the new European rules framework have been appreciated both by investors - with a marked reduction in the yield differential between Italian and German ten-year government bonds - and by rating agencies - which in 2025 revised upwards the assessment of the creditworthiness of the Italian state. Banks, businesses, and families can all benefit from these developments, as they enjoy a lower cost of borrowing. ### Implications for cross-border workers For cross-border workers who work in Ticino and reside in Italy, the reduction in the yield differential between Italian and German government bonds can have a positive impact on the cost of credit. Practical Analysis The reduction in the yield differential between Italian and German government bonds represents a positive signal for the economic stability of the country. This improvement can have a direct impact on credit conditions for families and businesses, including cross-border workers who work in Ticino and reside in Italy. ### Impact on Credit Costs A smaller yield differential between Italian and German bonds can translate into more favorable conditions for mortgages and other loans. This could improve the investment and spending capacity of cross-border worker families, allowing them to benefit from lower interest rates and more advantageous credit conditions. ### Benefits for Businesses Companies operating in Ticino and Italy can benefit from the reduction in borrowing costs. This can stimulate investments and economic growth, improving business prospects for cross-border worker companies. ### Comparison with Other Regions Compared to other Italian regions, Ticino benefits from a strategic position that facilitates commercial exchanges and investments between Italy and Switzerland.
