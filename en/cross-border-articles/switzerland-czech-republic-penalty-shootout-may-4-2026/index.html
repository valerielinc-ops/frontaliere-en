The good news is that, unlike what happened on Thursday against Sweden and then again on Saturday against Finland in the match lost 3-5 by Thürkauf and company, this time the score at the first break is still 0-0. And, indeed, at times it is the Swiss game that is preferred. It is therefore no coincidence that the busiest of the two goalkeepers in the first twenty minutes is Korenar, who is particularly good at 11'57" to thwart a solo shot by Herzog. However, it is in the middle period that, before the match against the Czech Republic, in this penultimate trio of pre-World Cup friendlies, Switzerland had shown a worrying double drop, allowing the opponent of the turn to take the lead. This time, however, things go differently at the Budvar Arena, with a Switzerland that clearly shows a different face and above all a different attitude compared to that of the two previous matches.
