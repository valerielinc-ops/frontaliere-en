Black day for commuters: accidents and heavy traffic on the A2, queues from Rivera to Lugano Sud and border crossings congested

In brief - Traffic jam on the A2 from Rivera to Lugano Sud - Accidents in Grancia and San Salvatore tunnel - Border crossings congested ## Key facts - What: Heavy traffic and accidents on the A2 - When: April 30, 2026 - Where: A2, Grancia area, San Salvatore tunnel, border crossings - Who: Commuters and drivers - Impact: Traffic jams from Rivera to Lugano Sud A nightmarish day of car commuting this Thursday. The exodus for the long weekend and two accidents in the Grancia area played a role. A painful (and quite predictable) morning rush hour was followed by a return journey on the A2 with severe traffic congestion. The long weekend (with the exodus southward, also from our canton), rush hour traffic, and an accident - which occurred just before 5 PM - near the San Salvatore tunnel all contributed. Following the incident, the overtaking lane was closed. According to reports, a second accident also occurred, a rear-end collision involving a truck and a car near the Lugano Sud exit. At the moment, traffic jams heading south start already near Rivera. There are also traffic jams near the border crossings with the customs offices of Ponte Tresa, Chiasso, and Stabio, which are particularly congested. Slowdowns are also occurring at Brusata. ### Implications for cross-border workers Cross-border workers who commute to Switzerland and reside in Italy are among the most affected by this situation. Long traffic jams and slowdowns can result in significant delays in returning home, with consequent impacts on quality of life and leisure time. This may be due not only to heavy traffic but also to more stringent customs checks or technical issues. Delays at these crossings can cause queues that extend for several kilometers, further increasing travel times for commuters. ### Comparison with other periods of heavy traffic Compared to other periods of heavy traffic, such as summer vacations or festive weekends, the current situation seems particularly critical.
