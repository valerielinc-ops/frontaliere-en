Swiss pig compensation for reduced production (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 7 May 2026

Switzerland offers compensation to reduce pig meat overproduction. Here's what changes for cross-border workers and farmers.

Contesto

In brief - Pig meat overproduction in Switzerland - Compensation for farmers to reduce production - Unprecedented measure to stabilize prices ## Key facts - What: Compensation for pig farmers - When: Suisseporcs delegates' assembly - Where: Switzerland - Who: Suisseporcs, pig farmers - Amount: Not yet specified The grilling season is about to begin and it is during this period that pork is particularly in demand in Switzerland. However, despite the high demand, current production is excessive. To stop overproduction and stabilize prices, the pig farmers' association intends to resort to an unusual measure. Kind regards from Berna. Every week in Switzerland, 47,000 to 48,000 pigs are slaughtered. Pork is the most loved in Switzerland, with an average per capita consumption of 19 kilograms in 2024 and a share of domestic production that reaches 95%. Despite being so appreciated, there is currently an excess of pork in Switzerland. 'Currently, 47,000 to 48,000 pigs are slaughtered every week, but the market, according to Suisseporcs, can only absorb about 44,000 heads per week,' writes the Tages-Anzeiger today. The excess chops end up for sale, causing a drop in prices, or are exported abroad at lower rates. For this year's grilling season, stocks in cold storage have already been accumulated in 2023. Suisseporcs, the Swiss association of pork producers and farmers, now wants to motivate with a prize those who breed to voluntarily give up the activity. This is an unprecedented strategy, on which the association will express itself during its delegates' assembly. Practical Analysis The overproduction of pork in Switzerland is a complex problem that requires a balanced solution. On one hand, it is important to ensure the sustainability of the agricultural sector and the stability of prices. On the other hand, it is necessary to consider the impact on job availability and the quality of the products. ### Comparison with Other Countries Switzerland is not the only country facing the problem of pork overproduction. Other European countries, such as Germany and France, have also implemented similar measures to stabilize prices and ensure the sustainability of the sector. However, Switzerland's strategy of offering compensation to farmers is relatively unprecedented and could serve as a model for other countries. ### Environmental Impact Reducing pork production could have a positive impact on the environment. Intensive pig farming is one of the main sources of greenhouse gas emissions and water pollution. More sustainable production could help reduce the environmental impact of the agricultural sector. ### Product Quality Stabilizing prices and reducing production could lead to higher quality products. Farmers could have more time and resources to focus on the quality of their products, ensuring a final product of greater value. This could also lead to an increase in demand for Swiss pork, both nationally and internationally. ### Job Opportunities Despite the reduction in production, there could be new job opportunities in the agricultural sector. The transition to more sustainable production could require new skills and technologies, creating new opportunities for qualified workers. Additionally, market stabilization could lead to greater job stability and security in the sector. ### Useful Tools For cross-border workers...
