Contesto

In brief - The mixed 4x100 m relay qualifies for Beijing 2027 with a time of 41''02 - The women's 4x100 m relay misses qualification by 2 hundredths, finishing at 42''92 - No results for the 4x400 m relays, with times of 3'01''59 and 3'01''18 respectively ## Key facts - What: Qualifications for the Beijing 2027 World Championships - When: 3 May 2026 - Where: Gaborone, Botswana - Who: Swiss relays - Amount: 41''02 for the mixed 4x100 m, 42''92 for the women's 4x100 m, 3'01''59 and 3'01''18 for the 4x400 m The Swiss mixed 4x100 m relay achieved their goal of qualifying for the 2027 World Championships in Beijing. The quartet composed of Enrico Güntert from Lugano, Ajla Del Ponte from Bellinzona, Felix Svensson, and Salomé Kora secured second place in the second repechage heat, running in 41''02. This result allowed the team to secure one of the four available spots on the second day of competition. For context, the time of 41''02 was sufficiently competitive to guarantee qualification, considering the required limit was 41''05. The women's 4x100 m relay, composed of Chloé Rabac from Locarno, Fabienne Hoenke, Léonie Pointet, and Céline Bürgi, narrowly missed qualification. The Swiss athletes finished third with a time of 42''92, just 2 hundredths behind the qualifying second place achieved by Great Britain. On Saturday, the same athletes had run in 43''11, a time that would have placed them among the top two in the first repechage heat. For comparison, the time of 42''92 was sufficient to qualify in other international competitions, but it did not meet the required limit for Beijing 2027. ### The disappointments of the 4x400 m relays No notable results, however, for the 4x400 m relay teams.
