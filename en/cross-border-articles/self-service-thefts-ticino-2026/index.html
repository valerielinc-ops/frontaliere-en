Di Valerie Linc · Published on 7 May 2026

Self-service stores in Ticino are being targeted by thieves. Here's what's happening and how owners are reacting.

Contesto

In brief - Self-service shops targeted by thieves in Ticino - Owners report frequent thefts - Cameras and reports not always effective ## Key facts - What: Thefts in self-service shops - When: In recent months - Where: Sant’Antonino and Ambrì - Who: Owners of agricultural shops - Amount: Up to 1,400 francs worth of stolen goods Self-service shops in Ticino are facing a worrying increase in thefts. Christian Bassi, owner of OrtiCola Bassi in Sant’Antonino, stated: ‘The list of thefts is long. We continue for honest people.’ The problem is not new, but it seems to have intensified in recent months. Bassi reported numerous thefts, with the last incident occurring two or three weeks ago. The culprit was caught in the act and reported, but the thefts continue. ### Owners’ experiences Roberto Ponzio, owner of Fattoria Ponzio in Sant’Antonino, had similar experiences. ‘Unfortunately, thefts do happen. Last year, I caught a guy who had stolen 1,400 francs worth of goods. But since he was under assistance, with a guardian, it’s practically impossible to get the money back.’ Ponzio has adopted security measures, such as cameras and a cash register bolted in the wardrobe, but the thefts persist. ### Security measures Emilio Bossi, owner of Azienda Agricola Bossi in Ambrì, has encountered minor but significant problems. ‘We had two or three thefts of money from the cash register last spring. Once 100 francs were missing, another time 50, and another time 20.’ Bossi is considering installing cameras to improve security. ### Current situation Despite the reports and cameras, the thefts continue. Frequently Asked Questions
What should you do if you witness a theft in a self-service store?
If you witness a theft, it's important to immediately report the suspicious behavior to the store owner or local police. Providing as much information as possible can help with the investigation.
How can cross-border workers help prevent theft?
Cross-border workers can help prevent theft by being vigilant and reporting any suspicious behavior. Additionally, following the store's rules and always paying for purchased products can help create a safer environment.
What security measures are store owners adopting?
Store owners are adopting various security measures, such as installing cameras and securing cash registers in cabinets. Some are considering the use of vending machines, but the required investment is significant.
