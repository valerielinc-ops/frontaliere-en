In brief - Thunderstorm alert level 3 in Lugano area - Risk of landslides, flooding, and hail damage - Traffic slowed down in Grancia area ## Key facts - What: Thunderstorm alert level 3 - When: 18 hours ago - Where: Lugano area, Grancia zone - Who: MeteoSwiss, ViaSuisse - Amount: Expected accumulations between 50 and 80 mm MeteoSwiss has issued an alert level 3 – significant danger – for thunderstorms in the Lugano area. The main risks include landslides on steep slopes, sudden flash floods in streams, and flooding. Additionally, there is a possibility of hail damage, broken branches, uprooted trees, and lightning strikes. The situation is critical, with specific recommendations for those outdoors. ### Risks and recommendations In the presence of lightning, it is advised to assume a crouched position with feet together, minimizing contact with the ground. Avoid watercourses, exposed locations such as peaks and ridges, trees, poles or towers, as well as open areas like clearings and soccer fields. These precautions are fundamental to reducing the risk of accidents during thunderstorms. ### Impact on traffic According to ViaSuisse, highway traffic in the Grancia area is currently slowed down due to a hailstorm. This slowdown can significantly impact commuters and cross-border workers traveling daily between Italy and Switzerland. It is important to monitor traffic updates and plan for potential delays. ### Alert level 2 for rain In addition to the thunderstorm alert, MeteoSwiss has issued an alert level 2 – moderate danger – for rain. Although the danger is moderate, the rain could cause an increase in the level of streams if the soil is already saturated.
