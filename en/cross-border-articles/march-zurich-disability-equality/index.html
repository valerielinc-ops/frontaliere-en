March from Ticino to Zurich: Disability Equality Demands

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 2 May 2026

About twenty people from Ticino participate in the national demonstration for equality of people with disabilities in Zurich

Contesto

In brief - Around twenty Ticinese people participate in the national demonstration in Zurich - The demonstration calls for equality and inclusion for people with disabilities - The event includes speeches, music, and dance performances ## Key facts - What: National demonstration for the equality of people with disabilities - When: May 2, 2026 - Where: Zurich - Who: Around twenty people from Ticino, along with other participants - Amount: Not specified - Program: Speeches, musical moments, and dance performances - Demands: Equal rights and greater participation in all areas of life Around twenty people left Ticino this morning heading to Zurich to participate in the National Demonstration for the Equality of People with Disabilities. Together with other participants, they made their voices heard 'to not be treated as second-class citizens or be excluded.' Now in its fifth edition, the initiative has been organized continuously since 2021 and represents an important moment of awareness and mobilization in favor of the rights and full inclusion of people with disabilities in society. The demonstration advocates for equal rights and full and effective participation in society, politics, culture, education, sports, and all areas of life. The program includes around twenty speeches, also translated into Italian, as well as musical moments and dance performances proposed by people with disabilities. During the event, a series of concrete demands will also be presented and handed over to political representatives aimed at promoting equal opportunities and greater participation. ### The voice of disability The demonstration is a crucial moment to give voice to people with disabilities and to demand concrete changes. Dettagli operativi

Concrete demands During the demonstration, a series of concrete demands will be presented and handed over to political representatives. These include universal accessibility, workplace inclusion, and the elimination of architectural and cultural barriers. The demands aim to promote equal opportunities and greater participation in all areas of life, from politics to culture, from education to sports. ### Dance and music performances Dance performances and musical moments are a way to showcase the talent and creativity of people with disabilities. These moments help dispel prejudices and stereotypes, showing that people with disabilities can be active and creative in many areas. The performances are also a way to engage the public and raise awareness about the importance of inclusion. ### Impact on society The demonstration has a significant impact on society, promoting a culture of inclusion and equality. The demands presented can have a direct impact on the daily lives of people with disabilities, improving accessibility and participation opportunities. The demonstration is a call to action for everyone, regardless of place of residence or work. ### Comparisons with the previous situation Comparing the current situation with the previous one, significant progress can be noted in awareness and mobilization in favor of the rights of people with disabilities. The demonstration in Zurich is an example of how people with disabilities can come together and make a difference. The concrete demands presented to political representatives are an important step towards change. ### Future scenarios In the future, it is hoped that the demands presented during the demonstration will be accepted and implemented.
