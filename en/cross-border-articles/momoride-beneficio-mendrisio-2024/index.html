MomòRide: first charity challenge for Ticino commuters

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 29 April 2026

In May, the first collective challenge by MomòRide starts to promote carpooling and raise funds for Mendrisiotto charities.

Contesto

In brief - First collective challenge of MomòRide in Ticino - Goal: 40,000 points in a month - Donation of one thousand francs to the Provvida Madre Foundation ## Key facts - What: Collective carpooling challenge - When: From May 1st for a month - Where: Balerna, Chiasso, and Novazzano - Who: Commuters and companies from the three municipalities - Amount: Donation of one thousand francs The challenge, which involves car sharing, will start on May 1st and represents a first in Ticino. The goal is to create a virtuous circle with benefits for mobility and the environment. In May, the first collective challenge within the MomòRide project will begin, a biennial carpooling initiative for companies and commuters in Balerna, Chiasso, and Novazzano, in collaboration with ERS-MB, PSE Pian Faloppia, and with the support of lastminute foundation and Mobitrends. The challenge, which will last throughout the month of May, is sponsored by Banca dello Stato del Canton Ticino and includes a donation of one thousand francs. All carpoolers working in the area of Chiasso, Balerna, and Novazzano are called upon to join forces to reach the common goal of 40,000 points collected in a month, which will result in a donation of one thousand francs to the initiatives of the Provvida Madre Foundation in Balerna. In concrete terms, participants will have to commit to tracking all their commuting trips shared by car with the Mobalt app, thus accumulating points for each trip that will be added to those of all other carpoolers in the area. Dettagli operativi

The MomòRide challenge represents a concrete opportunity for commuters in Mendrisiotto to join forces for a common goal. Participating in the challenge not only promotes carpooling, reducing traffic and environmental impact, but also makes a significant contribution to the local community. The Provvida Madre Foundation of Balerna, the beneficiary of the donation, is an organization that supports families and people in need, offering assistance and support services. ### How to Participate To participate in the challenge, commuters must download the Mobalt app and track all shared car journeys. Each trip contributes to the accumulation of points, which add up to those of other participants. The goal of 40,000 points in a month is ambitious, but with the collaboration of all commuters in the area, it is achievable. The Bank of the Canton of Ticino has confirmed a donation of one thousand francs upon reaching the target, demonstrating its commitment to socially useful and sustainable initiatives. ### Benefits for Cross-Border Workers Cross-border workers who work in the municipalities of Balerna, Chiasso, and Novazzano can derive numerous benefits from participating in the challenge. In addition to the charitable contribution, carpooling offers an economical and sustainable way to reach the workplace. Sharing the car reduces transportation costs and environmental impact, contributing to a more sustainable work environment. Furthermore, the challenge promotes collaboration among colleagues, creating a sense of community and solidarity. ### Future Prospects The MomòRide challenge is just the beginning of a broader project aimed at promoting carpooling and sustainable mobility in Ticino. Frequently Asked Questions
How can I participate in the MomòRide challenge?
To participate in the challenge, you need to download the Mobalt app, register for the collective challenge, and track all your carpooling trips. Each journey contributes to the accumulation of points, which are added to those of other participants.
What is the goal of the challenge?
The goal of the challenge is to accumulate 40,000 points in a month. Once the target is reached, the Bank of the Canton of Ticino will donate one thousand francs to the Fondazione Provvida Madre di Balerna.
Who can participate in the challenge?
The challenge is open to all commuters who work in the municipalities of Balerna, Chiasso, and Novazzano. In addition to companies, all employees of the three municipalities are invited to participate.
