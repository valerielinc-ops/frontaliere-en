Cannabis liberalization: protect minors from the start (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 6 May 2026

The Swiss National Council's health commission examines the cannabis legalization bill, with measures to protect minors.

Contesto

In brief - The Health Commission of the Swiss National Council is examining the bill on the liberalization of cannabis. - The Swiss Blue Cross calls for greater protection for minors and age-differentiated rules. - The proposed model includes three distinct levels: minors, young adults, and adults over 25. ## Key facts - What: Bill on the liberalization of cannabis - When: Session from May 6 to 8, 2026 - Where: Bern - Who: Health Commission of the Swiss National Council - Amount: Not yet specified The Health Commission of the Swiss National Council is preparing to examine, during the session from May 6 to 8, the bill on the liberalization of the cultivation, trade, and consumption of cannabis among adults. The preliminary text includes some prevention measures but still has significant gaps, especially regarding the protection of young people. According to the Swiss Blue Cross, an organization that historically fights against the negative effects of substance consumption such as alcohol and drugs, it is necessary to significantly strengthen the protection of minors and define differentiated rules based on age groups. The organization also calls for the decriminalization of consumption for adolescents, following the model of what happens with alcohol and tobacco, while maintaining the absolute ban on sales to minors. In case of violations, the revocation of the license for retailers should be provided. ### Three-tier model The proposed model is based on three distinct levels: minors, young adults, and adults over 25. For the latter, it is essential to have full awareness of the risks, so that they can make informed decisions. Practical Analysis: Implications for Cross-Border Workers The liberalization of cannabis in Switzerland could have several implications for cross-border workers in the Canton of Ticino. It is important to understand how the new regulations may affect daily life and what measures are planned to protect minors. The Swiss Blue Cross has emphasized the need for differentiated rules based on age groups, with particular attention to young adults and minors. ### Comparison with the Previous Situation Before liberalization, cannabis consumption was subject to severe restrictions and penalties. With the new bill, greater regulation and control are expected, with the aim of reducing abuse and protecting the youngest. However, it is essential that the authorities ensure a national network of information and counseling to support citizens and prevent consumption among minors. ### Concrete Scenarios 1. Access to low-THC products: Young adults will have exclusive access to products with low THC content to reduce risks to brain development. 2. Ban on online sales and vending machines: This measure aims to prevent impulsive behavior and increase control over sales. 3. Protection from passive smoking: Extension of bans to all public spaces to ensure greater safety for children and adolescents. ### Concrete Procedures For cross-border workers who wish to be informed about the new regulations, it is possible to consult the official websites of the Swiss authorities and participate in information campaigns promoted by the Swiss Blue Cross. Frequently Asked Questions
What measures are provided to protect minors?
The bill provides for differentiated rules based on age groups, with an absolute ban on sales to minors and exclusive access to low-THC products for young adults. Additionally, a national network of information and counseling is planned to prevent consumption among minors.
What happens in case of violation of the regulations?
In case of violation of the regulations, the revocation of the license for retailers is provided. This is to ensure compliance with the rules and protect minors from the negative effects of cannabis consumption.
What are the positive elements of the bill?
Among the positive elements of the bill are the state monopoly, the cantonal licensing system, the total ban on advertising, neutral packaging, the ban on additives, controls through test purchases, and the traceability of products throughout the supply chain.
