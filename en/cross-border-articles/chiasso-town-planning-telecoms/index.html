Chiasso: the Federal Court orders the rewriting of the Zoning Plan for telecommunications (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 12 March 2026

The municipality of Chiasso will have to review the Zoning Plan variant for mobile phone antennas, following an appeal by industry operators. The Federal Court's ruling has accepted the operators' requests, which challenged the lack of an environmental impact assessment.

Dettagli operativi

The Federal Court's ruling orders the municipality of Chiasso to review the Zoning Plan variant for mobile phone antennas, particularly regarding the environmental impact assessment. The ruling, issued under Article 7 of the Federal Law on Environmental Protection (LPA), emphasizes the importance of a thorough assessment of the effects of the antennas on public health and the environment. The LPA, which came into force in 1985, establishes the principles and regulations for environmental protection in Switzerland. Article 7 of the LPA, in particular, focuses on environmental impact assessment, requiring that such an assessment be carried out for projects that may have a significant impact on the environment. In the case of mobile phone antennas, the Federal Court found that the lack of an environmental impact assessment could have negative consequences and that the municipality of Chiasso must therefore review its planning. The Federal Court's ruling has also emphasized the importance of involving stakeholders in the planning process. The municipality of Chiasso, therefore, must ensure the active participation of telecommunications operators, environmental associations, and the local community in the rewriting of the Zoning Plan variant. This participation process is crucial to ensure that the planning is transparent, fair, and sustainable. Rewriting the Zoning Plan variant will be a long and complex process, requiring time and resources. The municipality of Chiasso has already announced that it will collaborate with industry experts and cantonal and federal authorities to ensure that the new planning is in line with current regulations and best practices in mobile telecommunications.
