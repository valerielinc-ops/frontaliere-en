Como, a journey through time: Quinto, Caffè&Caffè, Ul Pan de Comm

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 1 May 2026

Discover how some areas of Como retain their timeless charm, with spots frequented by locals rather than tourists.

In brief Como offers still authentic corners Places like Quinto and Ul Pan de Comm resist tourism Regular customers preferred to tourists ## Key facts - What: Historic venues in Como - When: For years, with an increase in tourists in the last 5 years - Where: Via Milano, Como - Who: Local residents and workers - Amount: Not specified Como, just outside the historic center, offers a unique experience. In Via Milano, a few meters from the walled city, time seems to have stood still. Here, places like Bar Caffè&Caffè, Ul Pan de Comm, and Pizzeria da Quinto maintain the charm of old times, with regular customers preferring these places to the more touristy ones in the center. Carlo Brenna, manager of Bar Caffè&Caffè since 2013, tells: 'We are in the boundary area because beyond the Tower the city has become very touristy, but this area is still one of the last pieces of Como as we remember it a few years ago.' Despite the increase in holiday homes and tourists in the last five years, Comaschi remain the majority of customers. Ul Pan de Comm, a historic bakery, is an institution in the city. Mara, the owner, tells: 'The shop has been here since 1927 and next year it will be one hundred years old, of which more than thirty under our management.' The artisan oven in the back room is a rarity today. Pizzeria da Quinto, open since 1940, is another place where time seems to have stood still. Luigia de Matteis, known as 'la Gigia,' serves generations of Comaschi. 'At our tables, tourists also sit down, of course, but for the most part they are regular customers.' ### Resistance to mass tourism These venues represent a beacon of hope for those seeking an authentic experience in Como.
