In brief - New 3 km gallery in Canton Schwyz - Completion expected by 2033 - Total cost of 1.2 billion francs - Replaces the current road exposed to landslides ## Key facts - What: New Axenstrasse gallery - When: Completion expected in 2033 - Where: Canton Schwyz, along Lake of the Four Cantons - Who: Canton Schwyz and Confederation - Amount: 1.2 billion francs Canton Schwyz has started work on a new gallery along the Axenstrasse, a strategic road that runs along Lake of the Four Cantons. The new gallery, nearly 3 kilometers long, will replace the current road, which is increasingly exposed to landslides and bad weather. The completion of the work is expected by 2033, with a total estimated cost of 1.2 billion francs, most of which will be covered by the Confederation. To symbolically kick off the work was Nina Christen, Olympic shooting champion from Schwyz, who activated the first blast of the excavation. André Rüegsegger, State Councilor of Canton Schwyz, explained that the current road is no longer safe due to bad weather and falling material, making the construction of the new gallery necessary. The new Axenstrasse is part of a larger project that also includes the 4.4-kilometer-long Sisikon tunnel. This intervention is crucial to improve the safety and usability of the road, which is one of the main communication routes in the region. ### Impact on mobility The new gallery will not only improve safety but also contribute to reducing travel times and ensuring greater traffic stability, especially during adverse weather conditions. Practical Analysis: Implications for Cross-Border Workers The new Axenstrasse represents an important opportunity for cross-border workers who commute to the Canton of Schwyz from the Canton of Ticino. Safety and practicality of the road are fundamental aspects for those who need to travel daily between the two cantons. The new tunnel will not only reduce travel times but also ensure greater safety during the journey. ### Comparison with the Current Situation Currently, the Axenstrasse is subject to frequent closures due to landslides and rockfalls, especially during the rainy season. The new tunnel, being underground, will be less exposed to these natural risks, ensuring greater continuity of traffic. Furthermore, the new infrastructure will be equipped with modern monitoring and safety technologies, further reducing closure times for maintenance. ### Concrete Scenarios For cross-border workers who work in the Canton of Schwyz and reside in the Canton of Ticino, the new Axenstrasse will offer a safer and more reliable alternative compared to the current road. This infrastructural improvement is particularly important for those who use this route daily to commute to work, especially during periods of bad weather. ### Concrete Procedures Work on the new Axenstrasse has begun and is expected to last until 2033. During this period, some partial closures of the current road will be necessary to allow excavation and construction operations to be carried out.
