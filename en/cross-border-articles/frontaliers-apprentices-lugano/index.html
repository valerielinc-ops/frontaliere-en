Cross-border apprentices? 'They don't work for Lugano' (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 30 May 2026

The City of Lugano responds to a question from councilor Omar Wicht: no cross-border apprentices currently employed.

Contesto

In brief - No cross-border apprentice currently employed by the City of Lugano - 27 Swiss apprentices and 5 with G permit - Participating companies manage hiring autonomously ## Key facts - What: No cross-border apprentice employed by the City of Lugano - When: Currently - Where: Lugano - Who: Municipality of Lugano - Amount: 27 Swiss apprentices, 5 with G permit The increasing number of young cross-border workers, especially apprentices, concerns Lugano city councilor Omar Wicht. The Lega politician submitted a question to the Municipal Executive asking if the City and the municipality's participating companies such as AIL, LAC, and Casinò Lugano employ cross-border workers as apprentices. Today, the municipal executive gave a clear answer. "As far as the municipal administration is concerned, the City of Lugano does not currently have cross-border apprentices in its employ." Of the 32 apprentices active at the City, 27 are of Swiss nationality and 5 hold a G permit, it is specified. Regarding the participating companies such as AIL, LAC, Casinò, and others, the City disclaims responsibility, not having consolidated data on the possible hiring of cross-border apprentices. "These entities manage their respective selection and hiring processes for training personnel autonomously."
