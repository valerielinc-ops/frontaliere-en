Fuel prices rise: petrol nears 1.9 euros per litre (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 3 May 2026

Petrol and diesel prices increasing, self-service close to 1.9 euros per litre. Analysis on impact for Ticino cross-border workers.

Contesto

In brief - Petrol at 1.890 euros/l self service - Diesel at 2.047 euros/l - Increase of 9.8 cents on motorways ## Key facts - What: Fuel prices - When: 3 May 2026 - Where: Italy - Who: Ministry of Enterprises and Made in Italy - Amount: 1.890 euros/l petrol, 2.047 euros/l diesel The high cost of fuel continues to hit the wallets of Italians, with a significant increase in prices at the pump. According to the latest data collected by the Fuel Prices Observatory of the Mimit, today, Sunday 3 May 2026, the cost in 'self service' mode along the national road network is 1.890 euros/litre for petrol and 2.047 euros/litre for diesel. On the motorway network, however, the average self price is 1.956 euros/litre for petrol and 2.117 euros/litre for diesel. This was communicated by the Ministry of Enterprises and Made in Italy. "A blow for those returning from the 1 May bank holiday. Thanks to the government's bad idea of reducing the cut in excise duty on petrol by as much as 15 cents, i.e. 18.3 cents including VAT, today, the first day of actual application of the decree law, self-service petrol has increased by 9.8 cents per litre on motorways compared to yesterday, equivalent to 4 euros and 90 cents for a 50-litre tank. And we're just getting started, as it's Sunday, many self-service service stations, if they don't also sell full service, have not yet changed the price. In short, it will be even worse tomorrow." This is denounced in a note by Massimiliano Dona, president of the National Consumer Union. ### Impact on cross-border workers The rise in fuel prices also has a significant impact on cross-border workers who daily cross the border between Italy and the Canton Ticino. Dettagli operativi

Analysis of the implications The rise in fuel prices has a direct impact on the daily expenses of cross-border workers who commute to Switzerland and live in Italy. With gasoline prices approaching 1.9 euros per liter, fuel costs represent a significant expense for those who travel daily for work. For a cross-border worker who covers approximately 100 kilometers a day, the increase in fuel prices can mean a monthly expense increase of about 50-60 euros. ### Comparison with prices in Switzerland In Switzerland, fuel prices are generally higher than in Italy. However, the increase in prices in Italy could reduce the gap and make refueling in Switzerland less convenient. It is important to monitor fuel prices in both Italy and Switzerland to identify savings opportunities. ### Impact on the local economy The increase in fuel prices can have a negative impact on the local economy, especially for small businesses that depend on the transport of goods and people. For companies operating in the transport sector, the increase in fuel costs can translate into higher service prices, with repercussions on the final consumers. ### Savings strategies For cross-border workers who want to reduce the impact of the increase in fuel prices, various savings strategies can be adopted. For example, planning trips to optimize consumption and minimize unnecessary travel. Additionally, consider using alternative means of transport such as trains or carpooling.
