Tram-train, another appeal. Zali: 'Frivolous challenge' (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 28 May 2026

The Federal Court receives a new appeal against the Lugano tram-train project, but without suspensive effect. Zali: 'Frivolous challenge'.

Contesto

In brief New appeal against the Lugano tram-train Appeal without suspensive effect Works scheduled for 2027 Zali: ‘Reckless challenge’ ## Key facts - What: Appeal against the tram-train project - When: Filed this morning - Where: Federal Court - Who: Appellants and cantonal government - Amount: Not yet specified The tram-train project for the Lugano area, valued at approximately 700 million francs, appears to be moving towards the start of construction, scheduled for the first half of 2027. However, a new appeal has been filed with the Federal Court, but without a request for suspensive effect. This suggests that a halt by the court is unlikely. State Councillor Claudio Zali remains confident and described the challenge as ‘reckless,’ especially since it is brought by individuals not directly affected by the route. The appeal raises criticisms related to the construction works and the future operation of the tram, estimated to take around ten years. The Federal Administrative Court had already dismissed previous objections, ruling them inadmissible. The new challenge repeats the same criticisms but without suspensive effect, making a legal halt difficult. In case of potential delays, the canton reserves the right to seek compensation. The project, already approved, benefits from the support of the Federal Court, which has deemed the public interest to be paramount. Requests for route modifications or variants have been rejected as disproportionate to the goals of sustainable mobility. Meanwhile, the Lugano tram-train network confirms its schedule. Ten design mandates have been awarded, and the first tender has been published. The aim is to begin preliminary works in autumn, adhering to the deadlines set in 2023.

Dettagli operativi

Practical implications for cross-border workers The tram-train project in the Lugano region represents a great opportunity to improve public transportation in the area, with an investment of around 700 million francs. Its realization would lead to an enhancement of sustainable mobility, with concrete benefits for cross-border workers who daily cross the border between Italy and Switzerland. The confirmation of the timeline, despite the appeal, allows for more secure planning of one's travels. For cross-border workers, the most important thing is to follow updates on the works and any changes to the schedule. The presence of an appeal to the Federal Court, however, should not block the works, since no suspensive effect was requested. This means that, barring unforeseen circumstances, the projects should proceed without obstacles. Moreover, the tram-train project could positively influence travel times and the quality of public transportation services in the region. For those who work or reside in Italy but commute daily to Switzerland, the new transportation lines represent an improvement in quality of life and a potential time saving. It is essential to periodically check the status of the works and consult official tools for any updates on the construction sites. The support of the Federal Court for the project, deemed to be in the public interest, fits into the context of growing attention towards sustainable mobility in Ticino. The possibility of alternative routes, rejected as disproportionate, also testifies to the willingness to respect development objectives without compromising the public interest. ## Recommended tools For an updated estimate, use the net salary calculator and the CHF-EUR exchange comparator.
