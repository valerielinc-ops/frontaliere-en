Swiss initiative: 1.5 billion for trains and mobility vouchers

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 28 April 2026

A Swiss popular initiative aims to tax flight tickets to fund public transport and mobility vouchers. Here's what changes.

Contesto

In brief - Popular initiative to tax airline tickets - Revenue of 1.5 billion francs - Mobility vouchers worth 110 francs ## Key facts - What: Popular initiative to tax airline tickets - When: Launched on April 28, 2026 - Where: Switzerland - Who: Straffico (Umverkehr) organization - Amount: 1.5 billion francs per year Initiative launched to tax air transport and promote rail The environmental organization Straffico (Umverkehr) has launched a popular initiative to introduce a tax on airline tickets in Switzerland. The initiative, named "For strong public transport and fair flight prices (Mobility Voucher Initiative)", aims to fund public transport and redistribute the proceeds to the population through mobility vouchers. According to Franziska Ryser, co-president of the association and national councillor (Greens/SG), air traffic is reaching unprecedented levels with "disastrous consequences for the climate". To achieve carbon neutrality by 2050, "effective measures that can garner the support of a broad majority" are necessary. The initiative proposes introducing a contribution of at least 30 francs on airline tickets, based on the principle of "the polluter pays", which would vary depending on the route and the class booked. For each takeoff of a private jet, a tax of at least 500 francs would be levied. The exact amounts will be set by the Federal Council and Parliament. ### Revenue and mobility vouchers The expected revenue would amount to approximately 1.5 billion francs per year. At least two-thirds of this sum would be distributed to the population in the form of vouchers for public transport worth around 110 francs per year, usable at regional, national, and international levels.
