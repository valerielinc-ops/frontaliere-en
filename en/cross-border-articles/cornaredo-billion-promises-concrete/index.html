In brief - Discussions on the revival of the Cornaredo area - Criticism of extreme densification and unfulfilled promises - 1 billion francs invested, but public works blocked ## Key facts - What: Discussions on the revival of the Cornaredo area - When: Over 20 years after the plan's inception - Where: Lugano, Canton Ticino - Who: Municipal councilors from various parties - Amount: 1 billion francs invested The Cornaredo area in Lugano is at the center of heated discussions. In an interpellation to the Municipality, municipal councilors Sara Beretta Piccoli (PVL), Carlo Zoppi (PS), Tamara Merlo (Più Donne), Luisella Petrini Fumasoli (Avanti con Ticino e Lavoro), and Angelo Petralli (Il Centro) expressed concerns about the development of the new neighborhood. Over 20 years after the plan's inception, the current scenario is radically different from the idyllic visions of the original Masterplan. The interlocutors deplore extreme densification, with private building complexes perceived as exaggerated and out of context. A glaring example is the 15-story tower, approximately 45 meters high, under construction near the Vedeggio-Cassarate gallery, a symbol of forced verticalization that seems to respond more to speculative profit logic than to real quality housing needs. The rejection of the Cassarate roundabout by the Federal Court has worsened the situation. This crucial junction was fundamental for the sustainable mobility of the project, but without it, the promises of protected bike lanes and safe pedestrian connections have remained on paper. Vehicle traffic continues to congest the area without effective relief valves. Dettagli operativi

Analysis of Unfulfilled Promises The promises made at the time of the construction application for the 'Parco Cornaredo' project have not been kept. Fundamental sports facilities, such as the underground private ice rink and the underground shooting range for the Civici Carabinieri Lugano, have not been realized. This has raised suspicions that these promises were used only to obtain facilitated building permits and to soften the impact of massive construction. ### Impact on Sustainable Mobility The rejection of the large roundabout on the Cassarate by the Federal Court has had a significant impact on the sustainable mobility of the Cornaredo area. Without this crucial junction, the promises of protected bike lanes and safe pedestrian connections have remained on paper. Vehicle traffic continues to congest the area without effective relief valves, making it more difficult for cross-border commuters to move around the zone. ### Urban Planning Logic and Extreme Densification The urban planning logic that justifies the authorization of towers up to 15 floors in an area lacking a definitive road layout and public services proportional to such density has been called into question. The questioners ask the Municipality to clarify this logic and explain how it intends to recover the vision of sustainable mobility now that the large roundabout has been rejected. ### Investments and Priorities The questioners ask the Municipality to clarify the investments planned by the Luganese Transport Plan, amounting to 1 billion francs. They want to know how much of these investments have actually been spent in the New Cornaredo Quarter (NQC) and for which specific works. It is important to monitor the Municipality's decisions regarding sustainable mobility and the promised infrastructures.
