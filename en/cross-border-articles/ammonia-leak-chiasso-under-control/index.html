Ammonia leak in Chiasso, situation under control

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 28 April 2026

Incident at the ice rink, three people intoxicated but none seriously. The population is not in danger.

In brief - Ammonia leak at Chiasso ice rink - Three people intoxicated, rescued by Mendrisiotto ambulance service - Situation under control, no danger to the population ## Key facts - What: Ammonia leak - When: Tuesday - Where: Chiasso ice rink, machine room - Who: Mendrisiotto firefighters commander Corrado Tettamanti - Number of affected: Three people intoxicated An ammonia leak occurred on Tuesday at the Chiasso ice rink, specifically inside the machine room. The commander of the Mendrisiotto firefighters, Corrado Tettamanti, confirmed to RSI that the situation is under control and that there is no danger to the population. The point where a small leak occurred has been identified, and operations are underway to close it. Three people who were working at the site became intoxicated and were rescued by the Mendrisiotto ambulance service. ### Emergency procedure The emergency procedure was activated immediately. Firefighters intervened to isolate the area and contain the ammonia leak. Operations to close the leak are ongoing, and it is expected that the situation will return to normal in the coming hours. Local authorities have assured that the population is not at risk. ### Implications for the population The population of Chiasso and surrounding areas can rest assured, as the situation is under control. However, it is always advisable to follow the instructions of local authorities in case of similar emergencies. People near the ice rink are advised to stay calm and follow the instructions provided by law enforcement and emergency services. ### Comparisons with past incidents This incident reminds of a similar episode that occurred in Stabio, where an ammonia leak caused the evacuation of a hundred people and the hospitalization of five people for checks. Frequently Asked Questions
What are the symptoms of ammonia poisoning?
Symptoms of ammonia poisoning may include difficulty breathing, coughing, eye and skin irritation, dizziness, and nausea. If symptoms occur, it is important to seek medical assistance immediately.
What to do in case of ammonia leakage?
In case of ammonia leakage, it is important to immediately leave the area, call for help, and follow the instructions provided by local authorities. Avoid approaching the accident site and provide fresh air to those who are poisoned.
What are the emergency procedures for the population?
