Swiss to cut 300 jobs with voluntary departures (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 6 May 2026

The airline offers incentives to reduce cabin crew without layoffs

Contesto

In brief - Swiss eliminates 300 cabin crew positions with voluntary departures - Incentives up to 15,000 CHF for those who leave by August 2026 - Engine and pilot shortages reduce flight capacity ## Key facts - What: Voluntary departures to eliminate excess staff - When: Program concluded at the end of April 2026 - Where: Kloten (ZH), Switzerland - Who: Swiss International Air Lines - Amount: 15,000 CHF lump sum indemnity Swiss has announced the elimination of 300 cabin crew positions through a voluntary departure program. The airline offered financial incentives to encourage departures without resorting to layoffs. Finance chief Dennis Weber told news agency Awp that the surplus was absorbed without any layoffs. Workers who agreed to consensually terminate their employment by the end of April received a lump sum indemnity of 15,000 francs, provided they left the company by August 2026. The program is now concluded and no further financial incentives will be offered. ### Causes of the surplus The cause of the surplus lay in initially higher capacity plans, which were then scaled back due to a shortage of engines and pilots. CEO Jens Fehlinger explained that the engines require much more maintenance than expected, forcing Swiss to ground several aircraft and resort to so-called 'wet lease', i.e., leasing aircraft with crew from other companies. The shortage of aviators, although improving, is not yet resolved: the training of new hires absorbs considerable operational resources, limiting flight capacity. ### Impact on cross-border workers For cross-border workers employed in the aviation sector, this news could have significant implications.
