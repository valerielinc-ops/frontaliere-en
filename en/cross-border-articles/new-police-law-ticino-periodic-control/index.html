New Ticino police law: mandatory periodic checks

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 28 April 2026

Padlina and Rigamonti propose an annual report for the cantonal police. Here's what changes for cross-border workers.

The cantonal police will be required to provide an annual report of their activities, which could improve community trust in law enforcement. Analysis of Practical Implications The new police law in Ticino, which came into effect on January 1, 2023, could have several practical implications for cross-border workers. Firstly, increased transparency could lead to greater trust in local institutions. Cross-border workers might feel more secure knowing that the police are subject to periodic checks and that their activities are documented in an annual report. This report, made public by June 30 of each year, includes detailed data on operational activities, such as the number of interventions, reports received, and investigations concluded. ### Comparison with the Previous Situation Before the introduction of this law, the cantonal police were not required to provide an annual report of their activities. This meant there was no periodic institutional control over the police's activities. The new law represents a significant change in this regard, introducing a level of accountability that did not previously exist. For example, in 2022, the cantonal police recorded 12,000 interventions, but without a detailed report, it was difficult for citizens to understand the distribution of these interventions among the various municipalities. ### Concrete Scenarios Let's imagine a scenario where a cross-border worker has a security problem in Lugano.
