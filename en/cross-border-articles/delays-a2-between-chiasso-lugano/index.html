Di Valerie Linc · Published on 30 April 2026

Red alert this morning on the A2 motorway between Chiasso and Lugano, with delays of up to 104 minutes due to a broken down vehicle.

According to ViaSuisse, around 6:00 AM, a broken-down vehicle between Melide and the Grancia tunnel caused the right lane to close, leading to considerable slowdowns. Analysis of Causes The congestion on the A2 motorway is a recurring problem, especially during peak hours. The presence of a breakdown vehicle has worsened an already critical situation, but it is not the only cause of the slowdowns. The increase in cross-border traffic, the lack of adequate infrastructure, and traffic management are all factors that contribute to these situations. - Cross-border traffic: According to data from the Swiss Federal Statistical Office, in 2022 the number of cross-border workers crossing the border between Italy and Switzerland reached 71,000, an increase of 5% compared to the previous year. This increase has led to greater congestion on the roads, particularly on the A2 motorway. - Infrastructure: The A2 motorway, particularly the stretch between Chiasso and Lugano, was designed in the 1960s and has not been adequately expanded to cope with the increase in traffic. The lack of additional lanes and emergency rest areas makes the situation critical. - Traffic management: The current traffic management system is not sufficiently efficient to handle emergency situations. The lack of adequate traffic lights and effective communication systems between the various entities responsible for traffic management further aggravates the situation. ### Comparison with the Previous Situation In recent years, the number of cross-border workers crossing the border between Italy and Switzerland has increased significantly. Frequently Asked Questions
What were the maximum delays on the A2 between Chiasso and Lugano on 5 May 2024?
The morning of 5 May 2024 saw significant delays on the northbound A2 motorway, between Chiasso and Lugano. Due to a vehicle breaking down between Melide and the Grancia tunnel, the slowdowns reached up to 104 minutes (one hour and forty), significantly impacting cross-border commuters heading to work in Switzerland.
How much productivity time do cross-border commuters lose due to delays such as the one on the A2?
An average delay of 60 minutes for each of the approximately 65,000 cross-border commuters in Ticino translates into an estimated loss of 6,500 working hours in total. For a single cross-border commuter going from Como to Lugano, a usual 30-minute journey can extend to over an hour and a half, causing a delay of one hour on the start time of work.
What safety measures were taken during the congestion on the A2 and what alternatives can I consider?
The closure of the right lane was necessary for safety. To avoid future inconvenience, it is recommended to check traffic with apps such as ViaSuisse or Google Maps, consider alternative routes such as the SS340 between Como and Chiasso, leave 30-45 minutes early and listen to local radio stations such as RSI for updates.
What economic impact do cross-border commuters' delays have on Swiss companies?
Extended delays can cause significant productivity losses for businesses. For example, a company with 100 cross-border employees who are delayed by an average of 60 minutes could lose up to 100 working hours per day, with an estimated cost of CHF 5,000 per day (based on an average hourly cost of CHF 50).
Why is the A2 motorway between Chiasso and Lugano often congested?
Congestion on the A2 is a recurring problem due to several factors. The number of cross-border commuters increased to 71,000 in 2022 (+5% compared to the previous year, according to the Federal Statistical Office). The infrastructure, designed in the 1960s, has not been adequately expanded to handle this increase, and traffic management is not always efficient in emergency situations.
