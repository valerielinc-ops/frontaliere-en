Italy and Switzerland join forces in research

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 29 April 2026

The ninth Italian Research Day in the world celebrated in Plan-les-Ouates with a focus on health sciences

Contesto

In brief - Italy and Switzerland celebrate research with the Grim - Bracco Suisse hosts the event in Plan-les-Ouates - Focus on innovation in health sciences ## Key facts - What: Ninth Italian Research Day in the World (Grim) - When: April 29, 2026 - Where: Bracco Suisse, Plan-les-Ouates - Who: Italian Consul General in Geneva Nicoletta Piccirillo - Amount: 80 million euros invested in expanding the plant On the invitation of the Italian Consul General in Geneva Nicoletta Piccirillo, the community of Italian researchers gathered at the Bracco Suisse headquarters in Plan-les-Ouates to celebrate the ninth Italian Research Day in the World (Grim). The Grim takes place every year at this time to commemorate the birth on April 22 of Professor Rita Levi-Montalcini, Nobel Prize winner in medicine in 1986 and the first Italian woman to receive such recognition in the scientific field. The event was attended by about sixty specialists who shared their experiences and explored new opportunities for cooperation. The program focused on innovation in health sciences. ### Focus on innovation In her speech, the Italian Consul General in Geneva Nicoletta Piccirillo emphasized the value of the Italian Research Day as a moment of celebration and well-deserved visibility for Italian researchers abroad: 'This year we are doing it in a symbolic place for the value of Italian research. Bracco Imaging is a world excellence in medical-scientific research, and the plant hosting us is the jewel in the crown of Italian research in Switzerland. The training received in Italy continues to represent a solid starting point for successful careers also at an international level, as demonstrated by the solid and extensive community of Italian researchers in French-speaking Switzerland.' ### Investme...
