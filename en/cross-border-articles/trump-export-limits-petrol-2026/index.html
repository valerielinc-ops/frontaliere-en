In brief - USA fuel prices +50% since start of war in Iran - Exports reduce domestic stocks - Trump may ban exports for national emergency ## Key facts - What: Possible limit on USA oil exports - When: From May 2026 - Where: United States - Who: Trump Administration - Amount: +50% USA fuel prices NEW YORK - The surge in fuel prices in the United States, up 50% since the start of the war in Iran, is fueling political pressures and opens the possibility of limiting or suspending oil exports to contain domestic price increases. According to an in-depth analysis by Il Sole 24 Ore, the idea of finding a lifeline in USA oil to counter the energy crisis gripping the world is fading. The growth of exports, fueled by the crisis in the Persian Gulf, is reducing domestic stocks and pushing prices up, with effects on inflation and political consensus. In this context, markets are beginning to price in the risk of possible trade barriers and a change in energy strategy by Washington. In fact, Donald Trump can ban the export of crude oil for up to a year, even bypassing Congress in case of 'national emergency', a move allowed by the Energy Policy and Conservation Act (Epca). It should be noted that Americans pay at the pump figures still much lower than ours: almost half compared to European prices. But what citizens feel is a rapid and intense rise in costs, closely linked to the drop in production in the Persian Gulf and the consequent increase in USA crude oil exports.
