Labour Decree, Coldiretti Varese: measures for job stability (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 6 May 2026

Coldiretti Varese supports the Labour Decree, considering the adopted measures an important step to strengthen job stability and quality in the agricultural sector.

Contesto

In brief - Coldiretti Varese promotes the Labour Decree for occupational stability - Incentives for hiring to better plan agricultural activities - Strengthening of leader bargaining to counter contractual dumping ## Key facts - What: Labour Decree with measures for stability and quality of employment in the agricultural sector - When: Not yet specified - Where: Province of Varese, Italy - Who: Coldiretti Varese - Amount: Not yet specified Coldiretti Varese has expressed appreciation for the path initiated by the Government with the Labour Decree, considering the measures adopted an important step to strengthen stability and quality of employment in the agricultural sector. According to the association, the provision responds to the needs of a territory like that of Varese, where agriculture acts as an economic and social bulwark between the plain, hill, and mountain areas. The attention of Coldiretti is particularly focused on the stability of employment relationships and the quality of employment in the primary sector. The measures introduced aim to promote hiring and the transformation of open-ended contracts, thus allowing agricultural companies to plan their activities with greater peace of mind. This approach is considered fundamental to ensure continuity of work and consolidate the bond of trust between the company and its collaborators. ### Stability and quality of employment Another central aspect of the decree concerns the strengthening of 'leader bargaining', i.e., that carried out by the comparatively most representative organizations. This measure is seen as an essential tool to counter the phenomenon of contractual dumping and ensure workers the recognition of a fair wage. Practical Analysis The Labour Decree represents a significant opportunity for cross-border workers employed in the agricultural sector in the province of Varese. The measures introduced by the government, such as hiring incentives and the stabilization of permanent contracts, can have a direct impact on the working lives of many cross-border workers. ### Impact on Cross-Border Workers For cross-border workers employed in agricultural companies in the province of Varese, the new measures can mean greater job stability. The possibility of converting fixed-term contracts into permanent contracts offers greater economic security and the ability to better plan for the future. This is particularly important for cross-border workers who often face challenges related to dual residency and managing taxes in two different countries. ### Comparison Before and After the Labour Decree Before the introduction of the Labour Decree, cross-border workers in the agricultural sector could face situations of job insecurity, with fixed-term contracts that offered no guarantee of continuity. With the new measures, agricultural companies are encouraged to offer permanent contracts, thus improving job stability and the quality of work. ### Concrete Scenarios Imagine a cross-border worker employed in an agricultural company in the province of Varese. Before the Labour Decree, their contract was fixed-term, renewed every six months without any certainty for the future. Frequently Asked Questions
What are the main benefits of the Labour Decree for cross-border workers in the agricultural sector?
The main benefits of the Labour Decree for cross-border workers in the agricultural sector include job stability through the conversion of fixed-term contracts into permanent contracts, hiring incentives, and the strengthening of collective bargaining to ensure fair wages. These benefits offer greater economic security and the possibility of better planning your work future.
How can I check if my agricultural company intends to take advantage of the hiring incentives?
To check if your agricultural company intends to take advantage of the hiring incentives, it is important to contact your employer directly and ask for specific information about the measures provided by the Labour Decree. You can also consult the official government website or contact Coldiretti Varese for further details.
What documents are required for the conversion to a permanent contract?
The documents required for the conversion to a permanent contract may vary, but generally include the cross-border worker permit, the current work contract, and any other documentation required by the company or the competent authorities. It is advisable to contact your employer or a labour consultant to obtain a complete list of the required documents.
