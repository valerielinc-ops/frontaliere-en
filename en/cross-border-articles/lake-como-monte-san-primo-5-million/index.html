The 'Save Monte San Primo' committee opposes new low-altitude ski lifts. Regional question for environmental requalification.

Contesto

In brief - Monte San Primo, jewel of Lake Como, at risk - Interrogation in the Region to block low-altitude ski facilities - 5 million euros of public funding ## Key facts - What: Interrogation to block low-altitude ski facilities - When: Presented in the Region in recent weeks - Where: Monte San Primo, Lake Como - Who: Councilors Onorio Rosati and Angelo Orsenigo - Amount: 5 million euros of public funding The battle to save Monte San Primo, a jewel of Lake Como, continues to make headlines. At the forefront is the coordination committee 'Salviamo il Monte San Primo' (Save Monte San Primo), which opposes the construction of new low-altitude ski facilities. The Circolo Ambiente Ilaria Alpi has revealed that an interrogation regarding the Monte San Primo issue has been presented in the Region. Councilors Onorio Rosati and Angelo Orsenigo have asked whether the Regional Executive intends to promote a review of the project for Monte San Primo, excluding the construction of new low-altitude ski facilities and directing resources towards environmental rehabilitation, sustainable mobility, trail networks, and slow tourism. The interrogation follows the hearing of October 15, 2025, when the 'Salviamo il Monte San Primo' coordination committee was received by the Environment and Territory Commissions. On that occasion, the committee, consisting of 39 associations, highlighted environmental, climatic, and economic concerns related to the construction of new ski facilities between 1,100 and 1,200 meters, involving artificial snowmaking and the creation of a dedicated water basin. Dettagli operativi

• ### Implications for Tourism and the Environment The possible revision of the project for Monte San Primo could have a significant impact on tourism and the environment. If low-altitude ski facilities were excluded, resources could be directed towards environmental rehabilitation and sustainable tourism. This could attract more environmentally conscious and respectful tourists, contributing to the economic sustainability of mountain areas. - ### Comparison with Other Mountainous Areas The case of Monte San Primo is not isolated. In other mountainous areas of Lombardy and the Canton of Ticino, similar projects are being discussed that involve the construction of low-altitude ski facilities. However, local communities and environmental associations increasingly oppose these projects, highlighting the environmental and climatic issues related to artificial snowmaking and soil movement. - ### Future Scenarios If the Regional Council decided to promote a revision of the project, excluding the construction of new low-altitude ski facilities, resources could be used for the creation of hiking trails, the promotion of slow tourism, and the rehabilitation of degraded areas. This could contribute to creating a more diversified and sustainable tourist offer, attracting a wider and more environmentally respectful public. - ### Impact on the Local Community The construction of new low-altitude ski facilities could have a significant impact on the local community. Soil movement and the construction of new infrastructures could alter the landscape and natural environment, negatively influencing the quality of life of residents. Frequently Asked Questions
What are the environmental challenges related to the construction of new low-altitude ski resorts?
The environmental challenges related to the construction of new low-altitude ski resorts include soil movement works, artificial snowmaking facilities, water and energy resource consumption, and the creation of new service infrastructures. These interventions can alter the landscape and natural environment, negatively impacting the quality of life for residents and the local ecosystem.
What does the Coordinamento Salviamo il Monte San Primo ask for?
The Coordinamento Salviamo il Monte San Primo asks for a revision of the project for Monte San Primo that excludes the construction of new low-altitude ski resorts. Resources should be directed towards environmental rehabilitation, sustainable mobility, trail networks, and slow tourism, ensuring consistency between regional investments, climate adaptation policies, and the economic sustainability of mountain areas.
How can I support the cause to save Monte San Primo?
You can support the cause to save Monte San Primo by joining the Coordinamento Salviamo il Monte San Primo. You can participate in hearings and discussions with the Regional Council, expressing your opinion and suggesting alternative solutions for the area's rehabilitation. Additionally, you can share your experiences and proposals with friends and family, raising awareness about the cause.
