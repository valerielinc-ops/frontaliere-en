Free museum entry in swimwear

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 1 May 2026

The Beyeler Foundation in Riehen offers free entry to visitors in swimwear, inspired by the exhibition dedicated to Paule Cézanne.

Contesto

In brief - Free entry to the museum in swimwear - Initiative by the Beyeler Foundation in Riehen - Inspired by the Paule Cézanne exhibition ## Key facts - What: Free entry to the museum in swimwear - When: Friday - Where: Beyeler Foundation, Riehen (BS) - Who: Beyeler Foundation - Cost: Free The Beyeler Foundation in Riehen has launched a unique initiative: free entry to the museum for visitors in swimwear. This proposal, inspired by the exhibition dedicated to the French painter Paule Cézanne and his paintings of bathers, aims to reduce the distance between the public and the artworks. The cultural operator explains on its website that the initiative changes perception, breaks down barriers, and creates a playful mix of humor and freedom, very much in the spirit of Maurizio Cattelan, the Italian artist known for the banana taped to the wall. The event has garnered great participation, according to the organizers. However, for the real outdoor bath, you will have to wait: in the region, the bathing establishments will only reopen in the coming weekends. The exhibition dedicated to Paule Cézanne is a unique opportunity to appreciate the masterpieces of the French painter in an unusual and fun context. ### Implications for cross-border workers This initiative could attract many cross-border workers who work in Switzerland and reside in Italy, offering them a unique opportunity to visit the museum without having to pay the ticket. Practical Analysis The initiative by the Beyeler Foundation to offer free entry to visitors in swimwear is an excellent opportunity for cross-border workers who work in Switzerland and reside in Italy. This initiative not only provides a unique chance to visit the museum without paying an entrance fee but could also encourage cross-border workers to spend more time in Switzerland, exploring the numerous cultural attractions the country has to offer. ### Comparison with Other Cultural Initiatives This initiative stands out for its originality and playful approach. While many cultural institutions offer discounts or free entry on certain days or for specific groups of people, the Beyeler Foundation has launched an initiative that directly involves visitors, asking them to come dressed in swimwear. This approach not only attracts public attention but also creates a unique and memorable experience. ### Implications for Tourism The initiative could have a positive impact on tourism in the region, attracting visitors from all over Switzerland and abroad. Additionally, it could encourage cross-border workers to spend more time in Switzerland, exploring not only cultural attractions but also the numerous parks, lakes, and mountains the country has to offer. This could have a positive impact on the local economy, increasing demand for tourist services and creating new job opportunities. ### Comparison with the Previous Situation Before this initiative, entry to the museum was paid for all visitors. The Beyeler Foundation decided to launch this promotion to attract a wider audience and create a more accessible and fun experience. Frequently Asked Questions
Who can participate in the Beyeler Foundation's initiative?
All visitors who present themselves at the museum in swimwear can participate in the initiative and obtain free entry. It is not necessary to book in advance or show a ticket.
What is the goal of the initiative?
The initiative aims to reduce the distance between the public and works of art, creating a unique and fun experience. Additionally, the initiative wants to attract a wider audience and create a relaxed and playful atmosphere within the museum.
Are there other similar initiatives in Switzerland?
At the moment, there are no other similar initiatives in Switzerland. However, if the Beyeler Foundation's initiative is successful, other cultural institutions might be inspired and launch similar initiatives in the future.
