Separations in Ticino: support network for parents

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 29 April 2026

FaMo+ celebrates 40 years with a new guide for families going through separation. Network approach and round table in Bellinzona.

Contesto

In brief - FaMo+ celebrates 40 years with a new guide for separations - Round table in Bellinzona to coordinate professionals - "Affectionate" guide available for free online ## Key facts - What: Presentation of separation guide and round table - When: Recent event, date not specified - Where: Bellinzona, Canton Ticino - Who: FaMo+ (formerly Ticino Association of Single-Parent and Reconstituted Families) - Amount: 40 years of association activity The Famiglie Monoparentali+ (FaMo+) association celebrated its 40 years of activity with a significant event in Bellinzona. During the celebration, a new "Affectionate Guide" for parents going through separation was presented, a booklet designed to support Ticino families during a delicate moment. The guide, available for free on FaMo+'s website, addresses various aspects of separation, including legal, economic, legal, emotional, and emotional aspects. The event saw the participation of professionals from different sectors, including social workers, psychologists, lawyers, and judges. The president of FaMo+, Sandra Killer, emphasized the importance of a network approach to address the challenges of separation. "The network is made up of very different professionals: from social workers, to psychologists, to lawyers and judges. For this reason, it is important that the actors have the opportunity to communicate and also understand each other better," explained Killer. The guide, presented by Valentina Testoni, head of training at FaMo+, uses the metaphor of the sea to describe the journey of a separation. "Embarking on a separation can represent a stormy sea, or in any case, waters to navigate without certainty about where you end up," explained Testoni. Frequently Asked Questions
What does the "affectionate" guide of FaMo+ contain?
The "affectionate" guide of FaMo+ contains information on how to address the legal, economic, legal, emotional, and affectionate challenges of separations. It uses the metaphor of the sea to help parents visualize the separation process as a journey, with all its challenges and uncertainties.
How can I get support for a separation in Ticino?
You can get support for a separation in Ticino by downloading the "affectionate" guide of FaMo+, contacting the association to speak with a professional, attending events and round tables, using the online resources offered by FaMo+, and speaking with a tax consultant for questions about tax aspects.
What is the importance of the network approach for separations?
The network approach is fundamental to addressing the complexities of separations. It allows professionals from different sectors to coordinate and collaborate more effectively, providing comprehensive support to families. This approach is particularly important in a context like Ticino, where families may face unique challenges related to double taxation and other tax issues.
