USA-Switzerland tensions over organic products and tariffs

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 29 April 2026

Washington criticizes Bern for agricultural rules and the dominance of Coop and Migros. New 10% tariffs until 2027

Contesto

In brief - USA criticizes Swiss agricultural regulations - 10% tariffs until 2027 - Trade talks ongoing ## Key facts - What: USA report on trade barriers - When: April 29, 2026 - Where: Switzerland-USA - Who: US Department of Commerce - Amount: 10% tariffs for 150 days Trade relations between Switzerland and the United States are further complicated. A recent report by the US Department of Commerce, titled 'Foreign Trade Barriers,' criticizes the 'rigorous and detailed' Swiss regulations on organic agriculture, as well as the dominance of two large distribution chains that would favor local brands. Although not directly mentioned, the chains seem to be Coop and Migros. The report acknowledges, however, that the trade balance between the two countries is favorable to the United States by over $32 billion in the services sector. The situation is different for goods, with a deficit in favor of Switzerland. Meanwhile, negotiations for a trade agreement between Washington and Bern, which were supposed to conclude by March 31 last year, have been complicated by the late February ruling of the US Supreme Court. The ruling established that Donald Trump had exceeded his powers in imposing high customs tariffs on trading partners. Switzerland had initially managed to obtain a reduction in tariffs from 39% to 15%.
