A Sunday exploring Celtic secrets at Pineta Park, Tradate

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 29 April 2026

Discover the secrets of the Celts at Pineta Park in Tradate with the Kaitorikes association. A free event to immerse yourself in the history and customs of the 5th century.

Contesto

In brief - Discover the secrets of the Celts at Pineta Park in Tradate - Free event with the Kaitorikes association - Historical reenactment with clothing and customs of the 5th century ## Key facts - What: Historical reenactment of the Celts - When: Sunday, May 17, 2026, from 2 PM to 6 PM - Where: Centro Didattico Scientifico, Via Ronchi 75, Tradate - Who: Kaitorikes association - Cost: Free The Kaitorikes association offers a free meeting to delve into the figure of the 'supreme guest' and the customs of the populations that inhabited the area in the 5th century. The appointment is set for Sunday, May 17, from 2 PM to 6 PM, in the spaces of the Centro Didattico Scientifico located on Via Ronchi in Tradate. The initiative is inspired by a discovery made by chance in 1966 in Prestino, during the works for the Grandate highway interchange, where a sandstone slab of fundamental archaeological importance emerged. The artifact, datable between the 6th and the beginning of the 4th century BC, presents North-Etruscan characters that transcribe a Celtic language. It is the main testimony of the Lepontic language spoken in the 5th century in these territories and reports the dedication of a monument by a man named Unamokozis. The meaning of his name, referring to the expression 'the supreme guest,' gives the sense of the welcome that the reenactors intend to offer to visitors. Inside the park, you can meet enthusiasts in historical costumes faithful to the era of Unamokozis, ready to illustrate various aspects of ancient daily life. The narrative path delves into places of prayer, combat techniques, armor, clothing, and food habits of the time. Practical Implications for Visitors The event at Parco Pineta in Tradate offers visitors a unique opportunity to immerse themselves in the history and customs of the Celts. The free participation makes the initiative accessible to everyone, allowing them to discover fascinating details about the daily life of this ancient population. Enthusiasts in historically accurate costumes from the time of Unamokozis will illustrate various aspects of ancient daily life, from places of worship to combat techniques, from weaponry to clothing and dietary habits. This practical and engaging approach allows visitors to better understand Celtic culture and its impact on the region. ### Involvement of Milanese Realities The event is not limited to Tradate alone but also involves Milanese realities. This interdisciplinary approach allows for the exploration of the role of women in Celtic society and the deepening of lesser-known aspects of daily life. The participation of experts and enthusiasts from Milan further enriches the experience, offering a more complete and detailed picture of Celtic culture. ### Educational Opportunities The initiative represents a unique educational opportunity for students and history enthusiasts. The possibility of interacting with historical reenactors and exploring archaeological artifacts offers a practical and engaging learning experience. The organizers invite the public to come with curiosity, emphasizing the importance of an active and participatory approach to discovering history. ### Accessibility and Logistics The Scientific Didactic Center of Tradate is easily accessible and offers all the necessary comforts for a comfortable experience. Frequently Asked Questions
where is the scientific educational centre in Tradate located?
The Scientific Educational Centre is located in Via Ronchi 75, Tradate. The event on Celtic culture will be held right here, offering a unique opportunity to explore the history and customs of this ancient population in an accessible and well-organized context.
how much does the Celts event cost in Tradate?
The event 'A Sunday among the secrets of the Celts' at the Pineta di Tradate Park has free admission. Reservations are not required, making the initiative completely accessible to all those who wish to deepen their knowledge of the Lepontian language and the customs of the fifth century.
what do you learn at the Celts event?
The event explores customs, daily life, places of prayer, combat techniques, armament, clothing, nutrition, medicine and the role of women in 5th century Celtic society. It is based on the discovery of a lepontia slab and the figure of the 'supreme guest'.
when will the historical re-enactment of the Celts take place?
The historical re-enactment dedicated to the Celts will be held on Sunday, May 17, 2026, from 2:00 p.m. to 6:00 p.m. This afternoon appointment offers an opportunity to immerse yourself in ancient history and learn about the customs of a fascinating population.
who organizes the event on the Celts in Tradate?
The event is organised by the Kaitorikes association, a group of enthusiasts dedicated to the historical re-enactment and dissemination of Celtic culture. For more information you can contact them at the email address kaitorikes@gmail.it.
