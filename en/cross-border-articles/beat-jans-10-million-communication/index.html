Di Valerie Linc · Published on 2 May 2026

Analysis of Beat Jans' Statements The statements made by Beat Jans during an intervention in Basel have sparked criticism from UDC deputy Eric Hess. Jans allegedly claimed that without foreign personnel, the university hospital would have to close from one day to the next. This statement was considered imprecise and has led to an investigation by the National Council's Management Commission. The verification of the Jans case is scheduled for the autumn, after the vote on the 'No to a Switzerland of 10 million' initiative. ### Impact on the Electoral Campaign The campaign against the UDC initiative 'No to a Switzerland of 10 million' is in full swing. Beat Jans, as head of the justice and police department, is at the forefront of convincing voters to vote 'no'. However, his statements have raised doubts about the correctness of his institutional communication. This could influence the electorate's perception and the credibility of the campaign. ### Reactions of the Federal Council Guy Parmelin, President of the Confederation, has invited Jans to be more balanced and objective. The reaction of the Federal Council highlights the importance of correct institutional communication and respect for the rules. Verification Procedure The Management Commission of the National Council has decided to examine the behavior of Beat Jans. This verification process is scheduled for the autumn, after the vote on the initiative 'No to a Switzerland of 10 million.' The Commission will assess whether Jans has exceeded the limits of institutional communication and whether his statements have been accurate and objective. This process is important to ensure that institutional communication is conducted transparently and in accordance with the rules. ### Comparisons with the Past This is not the first time a federal councilor has been accused of violating the rules of institutional communication. In 2020, during the campaign on the initiative for responsible businesses, the left criticized Karin Keller-Sutter for her statements. This case led to an update of the rules on communication before the votes, in order to ensure greater transparency and objectivity. The case of Beat Jans follows a similar trend, highlighting the importance of correct and rule-respecting institutional communication. ### Concrete Actions for Cross-Border Workers Cross-border workers employed in Switzerland should closely monitor the developments of the campaign against the SVP initiative 'No to a Switzerland of 10 million.'
