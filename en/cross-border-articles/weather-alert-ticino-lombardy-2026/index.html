Weather alert in Ticino and Lombardy: rain and storms

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 28 April 2026

The Lombardy Civil Protection has issued an alert for storms and heavy rain, with a risk of strong wind in Valtellina.

Contesto

According to forecasts, local downpours or thunderstorms are expected on the mountains and in the evening scattered in the northern sectors, even of moderate intensity. Hail of small to medium size is not excluded. Isolated showers are also possible in the plains. The snow level is expected between 2300 and 2800 meters. In Valtellina, strong wind gusts are expected throughout the day. ### Forecasts for tomorrow For tomorrow, April 28, 2026, residual widespread rains are expected between the night and the morning, especially in the northern sectors, locally even of moderate intensity and possible downpours or thunderstorms. The snow level is expected between 2000 and 2500 meters. In the afternoon, precipitation is expected to weaken starting from the southwest, with some isolated showers even in the evening. ### Implications for cross-border workers Cross-border workers who work in Lombardy and reside in Ticino should pay attention to adverse weather conditions. Analysis of the implications The weather alert issued by the Lombardy Civil Protection has several implications for cross-border workers who work in Lombardy and reside in Ticino. Adverse weather conditions can affect traffic and cause disruptions to daily commutes. It is essential to monitor weather forecasts and plan any trips in advance, considering the possibility of delays or road closures. ### Traffic and transport Heavy rain and storms can cause flooding and landslides, especially along mountain roads and border crossings. Cross-border workers who use their car to cross the border should pay attention to road conditions and check for any closures or detours. It is advisable to use alternative means of transport, such as trains, if the weather conditions are particularly critical. ### Work and schedules Adverse weather conditions can also affect work schedules and productivity. Employers must be prepared for possible delays or absences of cross-border workers and plan their work activities accordingly. It is important to communicate clearly with employees and provide updated information on weather conditions and possible traffic disruptions. ### Health and safety Heavy rain and storms can increase the risk of road accidents and falls. Cross-border workers should pay attention to driving and follow the instructions of local authorities to ensure their safety. Additionally, it is important to have an emergency kit with water, food, a blanket, and a torch, especially if long car journeys are expected. ### Comparisons with other regions Compared to other Italian regions, Lombardy and Ticino are particularly exposed to extreme weather phenomena, such as storms and heavy rain.
