Concert in Luino: Vivaldi and Bach at Santuario del Carmine

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 2 May 2026

On Saturday, May 2, 2026, the Milano Festival Chamber Orchestra performs in Luino with a classical music program.

Contesto

In brief - Concert in Luino on May 2, 2026 - Milano Festival Chamber Orchestra performs Vivaldi and Bach - Program includes opera arias by Mozart, Rossini, Verdi - Tickets starting at 25 CHF for Ticino residents ## Key facts - What: Classical music concert - When: Saturday, May 2, 2026 at 9 PM - Where: Santuario della Madonna del Carmine, Luino - Who: Milano Festival Chamber Orchestra conducted by Giorgio Rodolfo Marini - Price: Tickets starting at 25 CHF for Ticino residents, 30 CHF for others On Saturday, May 2, 2026, at 9 PM, the Santuario della Madonna del Carmine in Luino, located just a few kilometers from the Swiss border, will host a concert by the Milano Festival Chamber Orchestra, conducted by Giorgio Rodolfo Marini. The event is part of the "Concerts of Summer Around the Lakes 2026" series and is dedicated to Paul Baumgartner. The program, titled "Teach me to trust my heart...", intertwines music and poetic suggestions. On stage, high-level soloists will perform: Andrea Bordonali on violin, Gabriele Zolli on violin and mandolin, Francesca Patanè (soprano), Edoardo Manzardo (tenor), and Marco Chingari (baritone). The repertoire will range from Vivaldi, with the Concerto for two violins and strings in A minor and the Concerto for mandolin, to Bach with the Concerto for two violins in D minor. Additionally, opera arias by Mozart, Rossini, Bizet, Verdi, Puccini, and Mascagni will be performed. ### A tribute to poetry The evening is part of the tribute to the "Tramontana di versi" Poetry Festival, highlighting the connection between music and poetry. Implications for cross-border workers The concert in Luino represents a cultural opportunity for cross-border workers who commute to Ticino and reside in nearby Lombardy. Luino, located on the Lombard shore of Lake Maggiore, is easily reachable by car or public transport, offering an excellent occasion for an evening of musical entertainment. According to data from the Federal Statistical Office, approximately 65,000 cross-border workers commute to Ticino, with a significant concentration of residents in the Lombard communes of Luino, Ponte Tresa, and Varese. The concert, which will take place on July 15, 2023, is a must-attend event for those who wish to combine work and culture. ### Comparison with similar events Compared to other summer concerts in the region, this event stands out for its program that combines classical music and poetry. The choice of the Santuario della Madonna del Carmine as the venue adds historical and architectural value to the experience, making it unique. Unlike the Locarno Festival, which focuses primarily on cinema, or the Blues to Bop in Lugano, which is centered around jazz, this concert offers a fusion of genres that makes it accessible to a wider audience. ### Practical scenarios Cross-border workers who wish to attend the concert can plan their evening considering travel times and parking options in Luino. The journey from Lugano to Luino, for example, takes approximately 40 minutes by car, while the train from Lugano to Luino takes about 1 hour and 10 minutes. It is advisable to arrive early to find parking and fully enjoy the atmosphere of the place. Frequently Asked Questions
When and where will the classical music concert be held in Luino?
The concert will be held on Saturday, May 2, 2026 at 9 p.m. at the Sanctuary of Our Lady of Mount Carmel in Luino, a few kilometers from the Swiss border. It will be performed by the Milano Festival Chamber Orchestra, conducted by Giorgio Rodolfo Marini, with a program that includes Vivaldi and Bach.
How much does the ticket for the Luino concert cost for Ticino residents?
For Ticino residents, tickets for the concert start at CHF 25. For all other participants, the price is CHF 30. Tickets can be purchased online or at the ticket offices of the municipalities of Lugano, Mendrisio and Bellinzona, taking advantage of the discounted rates provided for by Swiss legislation.
Which composers and works will be performed at the Luino concert?
The concert program includes works by Vivaldi, such as the Concerto for two violins and strings in A minor and the Mandolin Concerto, and by Bach, with the Concerto for two violins in D minor. Opera arias by Mozart, Rossini, Bizet, Verdi, Puccini and Mascagni will also be performed, with high-level soloists.
How can I get to Luino from Lugano for the concert and how long does it take?
From Lugano, you can reach Luino in about 40-45 minutes by car. Alternatively, the train takes about 1 hour and 10 minutes. Shuttle bus services are also available from Lugano and Mendrisio. It is advisable to arrive early to find parking, with a rate of about 1.50 euros per hour.
Why is the Luino concert interesting for cross-border commuters?
The concert in Luino offers an accessible cultural opportunity for the approximately 65,000 cross-border commuters who work in Ticino and reside in neighboring Lombardy. Luino is easily accessible and the event allows you to combine work and culture, enjoying high-level classical music at a discounted price for Ticino residents.
