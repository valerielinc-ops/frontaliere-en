Locarno disposes waste in Landquart: 1,000 tonnes over 156 km

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 28 April 2026

Locarno disposes of plant waste in Landquart, generating 6.5 tonnes of CO2 annually. Interrogation to the Municipality for lack of environmental coherence.

Contesto

In brief - Locarno disposes of 1000 tonnes of green waste in Landquart - 156 km distance generates 6.5 tonnes of CO2 annually - Interpellation to the Municipality for environmental inconsistency ## Key facts - What: Green waste disposal - When: 2026 - Where: Locarno, Landquart - Who: Locarno Municipality, Axpo - Amount: 1000 tonnes, 6.5 tonnes CO2 Locarno has chosen to dispose of its green waste in Landquart, in the Grisons, despite the presence of closer facilities. The decision, which concerns 1000 tonnes per year, has raised questions about the environmental coherence of the municipal policy. According to an interpellation to the Municipality signed by Marko Antunovic and other local politicians, the 156-kilometer transport generates over 6.5 tonnes of CO2 emitted into the territory every year. A figure that, as highlighted by the Swiss Academy of Sciences, weighs on the effectiveness of Swiss environmental policy, defined as 'little coherent and far-sighted'. The issue has returned to the forefront after an article by tio.ch which highlighted how the Landquart option is a trend for several Ticino municipalities. Subsequently, an interpellation to the State Council was signed by Sara Beretta Piccoli and co-signed by 14 deputies from different political areas. The parliamentary act denounced a presumed inconsistency between the environmental objectives declared and the practices adopted by several municipalities. Beretta Piccoli, who is also a municipal councilor in Lugano, had already raised the issue at the municipal level, posing a series of questions to those managing the green waste issue on the shores of Lake Ceresio. ### Interpellations and questions to the Municipality Now there are also questions in Piazza Grande. Implications for cross-border workers and the environment The decision to dispose of green waste in Landquart, despite the presence of closer facilities, raises questions not only about environmental coherence but also about the practical implications for cross-border workers and the local community. Long-distance transportation results in increased CO2 emissions, which weighs on the environment and the sustainability of local policies. For cross-border workers living and working in Ticino, this situation could represent an additional reason to reflect on waste management and environmental sustainability. The choice to dispose of waste in Landquart, in fact, could be seen as a sign of lack of commitment from local authorities towards more ambitious sustainability goals. ### Comparison with other solutions There are closer and more sustainable alternatives for the disposal of green waste in Locarno. The presence of facilities less than 20 kilometers from the city offers the opportunity to significantly reduce CO2 emissions and promote more sustainable waste management. However, the choice to rely on Landquart raises doubts about the transparency and effectiveness of the tendering procedures and the coherence of the municipal environmental policies. ### Future scenarios In a context of growing attention to environmental sustainability, Locarno's decision could have negative repercussions on the city's image and the citizens' trust in local authorities. Frequently Asked Questions
What are the nearest waste disposal facilities to Locarno?
According to the municipal inquiry, there are waste disposal facilities within less than 20 kilometers of Locarno. However, the current choice is to rely on Landquart, in the Grisons, for the disposal of green waste.
What are the award criteria for the disposal of green waste in Locarno?
The award criteria for the disposal of green waste in Locarno are as follows: 1. Price 50%, 2. Product suitability 20%, 3. References 12%, 4. Delivery location distance 10%, 5. Apprentice training 5%, 6. Professional development 3%.
