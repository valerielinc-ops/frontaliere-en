No asylum seeker experiments in Pasture (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 6 May 2026

The Marchesi-Fonio-Gianini motion requests Cantonal and Municipal consent for SEM pilot projects in Balerna.

Contesto

In brief - The Marchesi-Fonio-Gianini motion requires the consent of the Canton and municipalities for SEM pilot projects. - The Mendrisiotto region has already faced issues related to asylum, security, and degradation. - The SEM only evaluates projects within the centers, not the external effects. ## Key facts - What: Motion for SEM pilot projects - When: May 6, 2026 - Where: Federal center of Pasture in Balerna - Who: Ticino deputies Piero Marchesi, Giorgio Fonio, Simone Gianini - Amount: Not specified The motion promoted in the Commission of Political Institutions by the Ticino deputies Piero Marchesi, Giorgio Fonio, and Simone Gianini emphasizes a fundamental principle: pilot projects of this type by the SEM should not be imposed from above but should only be implemented with the consent of the Canton and the municipalities involved. This principle was reiterated by Moreno Colombo, former mayor of Chiasso and vice-mayor of Morbio Inferiore. The motion does not obstruct every proposal on principle but recognizes that when a federal decision has concrete effects on a territory, that territory must be heard and respected. The Mendrisiotto region has already contributed significantly to the asylum front. Chiasso, Balerna, Novazzano, and other municipalities in the region are well aware of the difficulties related to security, degradation, crime, and petty crime. These are not abstract fears but situations experienced by the population and repeatedly reported by local authorities. The SEM can evaluate a project by looking mainly at what happens inside the center. In fact, it does not even concern itself with what happens outside. However, the municipalities must deal with what happens outside: in the streets, at the station, in the neighborhoods, in the shops, and in...

Dettagli operativi

The Marchesi-Fonio-Gianini motion is a concrete and necessary response to ensure that federal decisions are not imposed from above without the consent of local authorities. This principle is fundamental to ensure that local communities are involved in decisions that directly affect their daily lives. The motion does not close the door to any experimentation, but it establishes that without the consent of the Canton and the municipalities, no action is taken. ### Implications for Cross-Border Workers Cross-border workers who live and work in Mendrisiotto are directly affected by this situation. The motion ensures that federal decisions are not made without the involvement of local authorities, guaranteeing that their concerns are heard and considered. This is particularly important for cross-border workers who must face the direct consequences of federal decisions on security and quality of life in their communities. ### Possible Scenarios If the SEM pilot project were approved without the consent of local authorities, Mendrisiotto could face further challenges related to security and degradation. The Marchesi-Fonio-Gianini motion seeks to prevent this situation, ensuring that decisions are made in collaboration with local authorities. This approach is essential to guarantee that local communities are protected and that their concerns are heard. ### Conclusion The Marchesi-Fonio-Gianini motion is a balanced and necessary response to ensure that federal decisions are not imposed from above without the consent of local authorities. This principle is fundamental to ensure that local communities are involved in decisions that directly affect their daily lives.
