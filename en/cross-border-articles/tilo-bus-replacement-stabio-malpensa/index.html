TILO S40 and S50 Replaced by Buses for a Month (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 29 May 2026

From June 7 to July 5, 2024, TILO S40 and S50 services will be replaced by buses between Stabio, Varese, Gallarate, and Malpensa Airport.

Contesto

In brief - TILO S40 and S50 train connections replaced by buses for one month. - Bus replacement service between Stabio and Malpensa Airport. - Longer travel times expected. - Dedicated assistance for passengers with disabilities. ## Key facts - What: Temporary replacement of TILO trains with buses. - When: From June 7 to July 5, 2024. - Where: Between Stabio, Varese, Gallarate, and Malpensa Airport. - Who: Rete Ferroviaria Italiana (RFI) and Trenord. - Amount: Not specified. From June 7 to July 5, 2024, the TILO S40 and S50 train connections will be replaced by buses due to infrastructure enhancement works carried out by Rete Ferroviaria Italiana (RFI). This intervention affects the routes between Stabio, Varese, Gallarate, and Malpensa Airport, significantly impacting commuters who use these lines to travel between Italy and Switzerland. ### Replacement bus service The replacement bus service will be managed by Trenord and includes two types of connections: - A bus every 30 minutes between Stabio and Gallarate, stopping at all intermediate stations of the S40 and S50 lines. - A direct bus every hour between Stabio and Malpensa Airport, with no intermediate stops. Passengers traveling between Terminal 1 and Terminal 2 of Malpensa Airport can use the internal airport services. ### Limitations and travel times Bicycles cannot be transported on the replacement buses. Additionally, seating will be limited, with longer travel times compared to trains. This may cause inconvenience to commuters, especially during peak hours. ### Assistance for passengers with disabilities TILO informs that passengers with disabilities can request assistance for boarding and alighting buses by calling the toll-free number 0800 007 102 from Switzerland or +41 800 007 102 from abroad, avai...
