ABT: Ticino's financial hub stronger than ever (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 4 May 2026

The Association Bancaire Ticinese has renewed its leadership for the 2026-2029 term. Petruzzella re-elected as president.

Contesto

In brief - ABT offices renewed for the 2026-2029 triennium - Petruzzella confirmed as president - Ticino financial hub among the most solid in Switzerland ## Key facts - What: Renewal of ABT offices - When: May 4, 2026 - Where: Villa Negroni, Vezia - Who: Ticino Banking Association - Amount: 40 credit institutions, 1000 asset management companies, 10,000 employees The Ticino Banking Association (ABT) renewed its offices for the 2026-2029 triennium during the 106th General Assembly held on Monday, May 4, at Villa Negroni in Vezia. Alberto Petruzzella was confirmed as president, supported by a new Executive Committee composed of representatives from various Ticino banks. The members include Alina Augello from the Federazione Banche Raiffeisen Ticino and Moesano, Claude-Alain Cariola from EFG Bank, and Fabrizio Cieslakiewicz from the Banca dello Stato del Canton Ticino. The Ticino financial hub, with around forty credit institutions, a thousand asset management companies, and ten thousand employees, remains one of the most important in Switzerland after Zurich and Geneva. Petruzzella emphasized the solidity of the Ticino banking sector, despite recent global crises. Private wealth management for international clients remains the main activity, but there is also a strengthening of retail banking and commercial and mortgage credit. ### Generational change and innovation A central theme addressed during the assembly was generational change. Petruzzella highlighted the need to recruit and train around two thousand young people to meet the new needs of the sector. Frequently Asked Questions
Which are the main banks represented in the new ABT Executive Committee?
The main banks represented in the new ABT Executive Committee include Federazione Banche Raiffeisen Ticino e Moesano, EFG Bank, Banca dello Stato del Canton Ticino, Cornèr Banca, Banca Julius Baer & Co., BPS Suisse, Banca Rothschild, UBS, Banca del Sempione, BNP Paribas and PKB Private Bank.
What are the main challenges faced by the Ticino banking sector?
The main challenges faced by the Ticino banking sector include generational turnover, the need to attract young talents, maintaining competitive framework conditions and managing the risk of a real estate bubble.
What job opportunities are there for cross-border workers in the Ticino banking sector?
Job opportunities for cross-border workers in the Ticino banking sector include positions in retail banking, commercial and mortgage credit, and wealth management. Ticino banks are looking for young and qualified profiles, offering career opportunities and continuous training.
