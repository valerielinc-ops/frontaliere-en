International operation against cocaine trafficking with raids also conducted in Ticino. Seven suspects arrested in Italy and France.

TL;DR - International operation targets cocaine trafficking in Ticino - Luxury vehicles used to transport drugs with hidden compartments - Seven arrests in France and Italy, no arrests in Switzerland ## Key facts - Cosa: Operazione internazionale contro traffico di cocaina e riciclaggio di denaro - Quando: Inizio indagine: gennaio 2023 - Dove: Cantone Ticino, Svizzera, Francia, Italia - Chi: Camorra e 'Ndrangheta coinvolte - Importo: Beni sequestrati: veicoli di lusso, immobili per oltre 5 milioni di euro, criptovalute per 60.000 euro, 26.000 euro in contanti - Scadenza: Possibili controlli doganali più intensi nei prossimi mesi - Porto: Antwerp identificato come punto di ingresso principale per la cocaina dall'America Latina An international operation targeting cocaine trafficking and money laundering has also involved the Canton of Ticino, leading to several raids in the area. The investigation, which began in January 2023 following a report by Swiss authorities, uncovered a complex criminal network linked to the Camorra and the 'Ndrangheta. According to the Office of the Attorney General of Switzerland (OAG), the drugs were transported in luxury vehicles equipped with special hidden compartments. Last Monday, authorities in Switzerland, France, and Italy conducted a coordinated operation that resulted in the arrest of seven individuals: four in France and three in Italy. While no arrests were made in Switzerland, numerous luxury vehicles, real estate worth over €5 million, cryptocurrencies valued at €60,000, and €26,000 in cash were seized. Additionally, Italian authorities confirmed that the criminal group utilized shell companies and false invoices to launder money.
