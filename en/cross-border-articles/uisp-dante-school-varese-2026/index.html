UISP promotes inclusive sports at Dante School in Varese

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 1 May 2026

The meeting between UISP and the Parents' Association at Dante School in Varese promoted sports for all, with inclusive projects and activities for people with disabilities.

Contesto

In brief - UISP meeting at Dante School in Varese on April 24, 2026 - Presentation of inclusive projects and sports activities for people with disabilities - Participation of Linda Casalini from the Italian Paralympic Committee ## Key facts - What: UISP meeting at Dante School in Varese - When: April 24, 2026 - Where: Dante Primary School, Varese - Who: UISP, Parents' Association, Italian Paralympic Committee - Membership: Over 1 million and 300 thousand UISP members in Italy On Friday, April 24, 2026, Dante School in Varese hosted a meeting organized by UISP, thanks to the availability of the headteacher Patrizia Iotti. The event, organized by Simona Miglierina, president of the Associazione Amici Dante Alighieri Varese, had significant cultural value, focusing on inclusive sports and education on the value of diversity through sports. The UISP president Rita Di Toro opened the proceedings by recalling that UISP, since 1948, sees sports as a fundamental right included in the constitutional principles. 'We are over 1 million and 300 thousand members throughout Italy,' she explained, 'and our mission is to make physical activity accessible to everyone, without exception.' UISP's commitment is a mosaic of concrete activities, including excellence in basketball, free psychomotor activities for the youngest, and cutting-edge projects such as baseball for the visually impaired or gymnastics in prisons. A date to mark in red is May 17 in Cerro, on Lake Maggiore, where young people with disabilities will be the protagonists of an inclusive sailing day, demonstrating that sports know no barriers.
