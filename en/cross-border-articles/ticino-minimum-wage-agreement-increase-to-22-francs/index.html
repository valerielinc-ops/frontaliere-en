Ticino minimum wage: aiming for 22 CHF per hour (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 23 February 2026

A draft agreement has been presented that would raise the minimum wage to 22 CHF per hour by 2029. A gradual increase in three stages starting from 2027 is planned.

From the offices of the Management Commission in Bellinzona, a draft compromise has emerged that could reshape the pay slips of thousands of workers, including many cross-border commuters. The text, presented as a counter-proposal to the Socialist Party's popular initiative "For a social minimum wage," outlines a path for a gradual increase of the wage threshold. The goal is ambitious: to move from the current 20.50 francs per hour to a range between 21.75 and 22.25 francs, with an average target of 22 francs per hour. This milestone, equivalent to about 4,000 gross francs per month, would not be reached immediately. Dettagli operativi

The agreement does not just define a figure; it also addresses two technical aspects of great impact for cross-border workers: the calculation of the wage and its relationship with collective labor agreements (CLAs). ## What is included in the minimum wage calculation? One of the most significant changes concerns the composition of the minimum wage. In addition to base pay, all benefits recognized as salary according to AVS criteria will also be considered. These are accessory advantages that represent a real economic benefit for the employee, such as the private use of a company car or meal vouchers of a certain value. Purely optional elements would be excluded. This measure aims to simplify the application of the rules, aligning it with practices already established in many collective agreements and clarifying what actually contributes to reaching the minimum threshold. ## Collective agreements and the safeguard clause From 2030, the law will take precedence. The legal minimum wage will prevail over what is stipulated in CLAs, unless they provide more favorable conditions. A three-year transition period is planned for existing agreements to adapt. However, it will not be possible to conclude new collective agreements with downward derogations. To protect employment, a safeguard clause has been included: in the event of significant and documented job losses, the Tripartite Commission can grant a temporary extension to mitigate the repercussions on the Ticino labor market.

This potential increase, although spread over several years, is significant news for all cross-border workers in Ticino, especially those employed in low-wage sectors. The increase from 20.50 to 22 francs per hour translates, on a basis of 42 hours per week, to a gross monthly increase of about 270 francs once fully implemented. This is a non-negligible amount that will positively impact purchasing power. 💡 Practical advice for cross-border workers: - Check your payslip: See if you receive benefits considered as AVS salary. Under the new rules, these items could be included in the calculation to reach the legal minimum. - Inquire about your CLA: If your sector is regulated by a collective agreement, check the current salary conditions and how they might change with the new primacy of the legal minimum from 2030. - Plan your budget: A gradual increase allows for better financial planning. Consider how this extra income could be used, for example, to contribute to a third pillar or for other forms of savings. Understanding how these changes will affect your net salary is crucial. What is the minimum wage expected in Canton Ticino after the new agreement?

How are benefits considered in the new calculation of minimum wage in Canton Ticino?
In addition to the basic pay, all benefits recognised as wages will be considered according to the criteria of AVS, such as the private use of a company car or meal vouchers of a certain value, excluding purely optional items.
How will the minimum wage increase affect the social security contributions of frontier workers?
The increase in the minimum wage considered in the calculation of the AVS wage could lead to higher social security contributions, as these are calculated on the total wage. However, the precise impact depends on the included benefit level and the current contribution threshold.
