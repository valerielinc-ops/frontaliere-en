SBB: Direct trains to Rimini and France from 30 May 2026 (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 16 April 2026

From 30 May to 4 October, direct connections will run between Zurich and Rimini, with stops in Bellinzona, Lugano and Chiasso. From Lausanne and Geneva, TGV trains will serve Avignon, Aix-en-Provence and Marseille until 2 November.

Frequently Asked Questions
What are the exact dates for the new direct Zurich-Rimini train connections?
The new direct Zurich-Rimini train connections will run from **30 May to 4 October 2026**, with stops in Bellinzona, Lugano and Chiasso. There will be **14 weekly departures**, every day of the week.
Do I need to book a ticket for the Zurich-Rimini train?
Booking is not mandatory for the Zurich-Rimini train, but it is **strongly recommended** to secure a seat, especially in July and August. Tickets can be purchased on the SBB website or via the SBB Mobile app.
What happens if I travel to Rimini after 7 June 2026?
After 7 June 2026, direct Zurich-Rimini connections may be **suspended or operate irregularly** due to construction work on the Simplon line. In this case, you will need to travel via **Milan** or use **replacement buses** for the Iselle-Domodossola section.
How does booking work for TGV trains to France?
For TGV trains from Lausanne and Geneva to Avignon, Aix-en-Provence and Marseille, **booking is mandatory** and must be done through the SBB website or SNCF Connect. Tickets can be purchased up to **three months in advance** to secure the best fares.
What alternatives are available for travellers from Lugano to Rimini during the construction work?
During the construction period (7 June - 26 July 2026), the simplest option is to take a train from Lugano to **Chiasso**, then a regional train to **Milano Centrale**, and finally a train to Rimini. Alternatively, you can use the **replacement buses** between Iselle and Domodossola, but you will need to complete the journey with an Italian regional train.
Are replacement buses free for passengers with a train ticket?
Yes, your train ticket is valid for the **replacement bus** as well. No additional ticket is required, but you must show your travel document to the bus staff.
