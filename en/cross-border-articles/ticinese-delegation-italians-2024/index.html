The Ticino delegation met with the human resources managers of a federal department to discuss the representation of Italian speakers in the Federal Administration. The meeting was held after the December meeting with the Federal Department of Foreign Affairs led by Ignazio Cassis. The Ticino delegation emphasized the importance of creating more opportunities for Italian-speaking staff, intervening both on the political and administrative levels.

Dettagli operativi

The Ticino delegation requested more information on the recruitment and justification procedures for Italian-speaking employees in the Federal Administration, highlighting the need to create more opportunities for Italian-speaking staff. Punti chiave

The Ticino delegation asked to be informed about the next actions of the federal government to improve the representation of Italian speakers in the Federal Administration. The delegation also called for the creation of an action plan to improve the representation of Italian speakers in the Federal Administration. The action plan should provide for the establishment of an office dedicated to the management of Italian-speaking human resources, as well as the creation of a monitoring committee to follow the development of financial equalization. Frequently Asked Questions
How high is the quota of italophons used in Bern in 2022?
According to data from the Federal Office of Roads (USTRA), in 2022, 12.5% of the employees of this branch were of Italian origin, compared to 4.2% in the headquarters in Bern.
What is the representation roof proposed by the Ticino Congress for Italian staff in the Federal Administration?
The proposed roof is 20% within the next 10 years.
What are the proposals from Ticino to improve language requirements for roles of responsibility?
The Ticino Congress has proposed introducing an Italian language knowledge requirement for candidates wishing to access responsibility roles in the Federal Department of Foreign Affairs. This aims to ensure that Italian language officials can have equal opportunities to those of their German and French speaking colleagues.
How does the Ticino Congress intend to use the funds of financial perequation to support Italian language officials?
The Ticino Congress has proposed to create a fund for Italian language officials wishing to move to Bern, funded with financial compensation funds. This fund aims to facilitate the transfer and integration of Italian language officials into the Federal Administration.
What measures have been discussed to ensure that Italian language officials receive official instructions and documents in their native language?
Ticino has proposed introducing a rule establishing the right of Italian language officials to receive official instructions and documents in their native language. In addition, the creation of a support unit was discussed to help Italian language officials solve any language problems.
