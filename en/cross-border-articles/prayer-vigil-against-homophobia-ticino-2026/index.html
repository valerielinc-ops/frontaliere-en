Prayer vigil against homophobia, biphobia and transphobia in Lugano (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 7 May 2026

On the International Day against Homophobia, a prayer vigil in Lugano to counter discrimination and violence.

Contesto

In brief - Prayer vigil against homophobia, biphobia, and transphobia in Lugano - Appointment on May 22 at the Evangelical Reformed Church - Initiative promoted by 'La Porta Aperta – Spazi di inclusione' ## Key facts - What: Prayer vigil against homophobia, biphobia, and transphobia - When: May 22, 2026, at 8:30 PM - Where: Evangelical Reformed Church of Lugano, Viale Cattaneo 2 - Who: Group 'La Porta Aperta – Spazi di inclusione' - Cost: Free event open to all On the occasion of the International Day against Homophobia, Biphobia, and Transphobia, the group 'La Porta Aperta – Spazi di inclusione' has organized a prayer vigil open to the entire citizenship. The appointment is for Friday, May 22, at 8:30 PM, at the Evangelical Reformed Church of Lugano, in Viale Cattaneo 2. The vigil is born as a moment of reflection, listening, and intercession for all those who, in different parts of the world, suffer discrimination, marginalization, or violence due to their sexual orientation or gender identity. This initiative is part of the numerous similar initiatives that will take place in different cities around the world. The initiative is promoted by the group 'La Porta Aperta' with Azione Cattolica Ticinese and is in collaboration with various Christian realities present in the territory, such as the Reformed Church of Ticino and the Christian Catholic Church of Switzerland. Ministers of the Roman Catholic Church will also be present, as a sign of a path that, despite different sensitivities, wishes to continue in dialogue and mutual listening. ### The guiding verse The verse that accompanies this year's vigil, 'Do not fear, for I have redeemed you, I have called you by name' (Isaiah 43:1), recalls the unique and irreplaceable value of every person and their being recogniz...
