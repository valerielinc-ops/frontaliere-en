Cardano al Campo: 2026 Eco Week focuses on WEEE recycling

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 1 May 2026

From May 5th to 9th, Cardano al Campo promotes the recycling of electrical waste with events and collections. Goal: to improve WEEE collection, currently below 1%.

Contesto

In brief - Cardano al Campo celebrates the second Ecological Week from May 5 to 9, 2026 - Focus on WEEE: collection of small electronic devices - Goal: improve WEEE collection, currently below 1% ## Key facts - What: Ecological Week with a focus on WEEE - When: May 5-9, 2026 - Where: Cardano al Campo, Varese - Who: Municipality of Cardano al Campo, Curiamo Cardano, ProGitec, Erion WEEE, Laura Prati Association - Amount: Municipal recycling rate of 84.7%; WEEE below 1% The town of Cardano al Campo, near Malpensa, is preparing to celebrate its second ecological week, which this year will focus on WEEE, or Waste Electrical and Electronic Equipment. The initiative, which will take place from May 5 to 9, 2026, is an opportunity to raise awareness in the community about the correct management of technological waste and to promote sustainable recycling. The week, with the patronage of the Municipality and the collaboration of Curiamo Cardano, ProGitec, Erion WEEE, and the Laura Prati Association, aims to collect small WEEE, electronic devices smaller than 25 cm, such as phones, headphones, small appliances, chargers, mice, and much more. Citizens are invited to participate in the scheduled events and to bring their old devices for proper recycling. Claudio Trevisan, the Environment Councillor, emphasized the continuous commitment of Cardano al Campo in promoting environmental sustainability. 'After the great success of last year, the second ecological week marks another step towards the implementation of the recycling culture. This year's focus on WEEE is fundamental, as we are a Recycling Municipality with an average recycling rate of 84.7%. Dettagli operativi

Implications for the Community The Ecological Week of Cardano al Campo represents a concrete opportunity for the community to reflect and act sustainably. The collection of WEEE, in particular, is a crucial issue to reduce the environmental impact of electronic waste, which often contains hazardous materials and is difficult to dispose of. The focus on small devices such as phones and chargers is strategic, as these items are common in many citizens' homes and often end up in drawers or garbage bins instead of being properly recycled. ### Comparison with the Previous Situation Last year, the first edition of the Ecological Week recorded good success, with active participation from the community. However, the Municipality acknowledged that there is still room for improvement, especially in the collection of WEEE. The recycling average of the Municipality is already high, at 84.7%, but WEEE represents less than 1% of the total, compared to a national average of 1.4%. This indicates that there is space to improve awareness and recycling practices among citizens. ### Future Scenarios If the Ecological Week manages to involve an increasing number of citizens, a significant increase in the collection of WEEE could be seen. This would not only reduce the environmental impact but could also generate economic value, as recycled materials can be reused in new products. Furthermore, the community's commitment could inspire similar initiatives in other municipalities, contributing to a more widespread and aware recycling culture. ### Involvement of Families The initiative of 'Curiamo Cardano' is an example of how family involvement can make a difference. What to Do During the Ecological Week To participate in the Ecological Week, citizens of Cardano al Campo can follow a few simple steps: 1. Info Point in Piazza Mercato: On May 5th, from 9:30 AM to 12:00 PM, citizens can bring their small WEEE items for recycling. 2. Public meeting: On May 6th, at 8:45 PM, at Sala Pertini, Via Verdi, there will be a meeting dedicated to ecology and the care of the territory. 3. Waste collection in the woods: On May 9th, meeting in Via Carreggia at 2:30 PM, participants can join the collection of waste abandoned in the woods. A refreshment is planned in the mid-afternoon for all participants. ### Procedure for WEEE Recycling To properly recycle WEEE, citizens can follow these guidelines: 1. Collect small electronic devices: Phones, headphones, small appliances, chargers, mice, and other devices smaller than 25 cm. 2. Bring them to the Info Point: During the Info Point in Piazza Mercato on May 5th, citizens can drop off their WEEE for recycling. 3. Participate in events: Attend the scheduled events to learn more about recycling and the environmental impact of electronic waste. ### Useful Tools For more information and to stay updated on the events and initiatives of the Ecological Week, citizens can visit the website of the Municipality of Cardano al Campo or contact the organizers. Frequently Asked Questions
What are WEEE and why is it important to recycle them?
WEEE, or Waste Electrical and Electronic Equipment, includes electronic devices such as phones, headphones, small appliances, and chargers. Properly recycling them is crucial to reducing environmental impact, as they contain hazardous materials that are difficult to dispose of. Additionally, recycling WEEE can generate economic value, as the materials can be reused in new products.
How can I participate in Cardano al Campo's Eco Week?
You can participate by bringing your small WEEE to the Info Point in Piazza Mercato on May 5th, from 9:30 AM to 12:00 PM. Additionally, you can attend the public meeting on May 6th at Sala Pertini and join the collection of abandoned waste in the woods on May 9th, meeting at Via Carreggia at 2:30 PM.
What are the benefits of WEEE recycling for the community?
Recycling WEEE reduces the environmental impact of electronic waste, contributes to sustainability, and can generate economic value. Furthermore, it promotes a more aware recycling culture among citizens, involving families and communities in concrete actions for the care of the territory.
