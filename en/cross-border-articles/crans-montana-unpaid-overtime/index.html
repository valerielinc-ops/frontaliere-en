In brief - Unpaid overtime for Crans Montana rescuers - Tensions between healthcare personnel and Valais institutions - Payment pending for the Le Constellation bar fire ## Key facts - What: Failure to pay overtime to rescuers - When: Four months after the Le Constellation bar fire - Where: Crans Montana, Valais Canton - Who: Healthcare personnel and Valais Hospital - Amount: Not specified Four months after the tragic New Year's Eve night, tensions arise between healthcare personnel and Valais institutions over unpaid overtime. During the fire at the Le Constellation bar, which resulted in 41 deaths and 115 injuries, operators from the Valais Hospital's ambulance company worked tirelessly, often intervening on their days off. Despite their extraordinary efforts, many rescuers still haven't received payment for the extra hours worked. "We worked long hours on site, many came in even on their days off, but four months later, the overtime pay still hasn't been received," explained an anonymous healthcare worker to a Romandy newspaper. The issue has been raised internally, but according to employees, the response indicates a stalemate between the involved institutions. "We were told that our employer, the hospital, and the Canton are passing the buck on who should cover these costs," the rescuer added. ### Valais Hospital's Position Valais Hospital confirms that the payment of overtime is still pending. Through its communication manager, Joakim Faiss, the institution explains that, given the exceptional nature of the event, the matter requires special management. "It's about defining a coverage between the Canton and us, as is already the case for other professionals involved in this tragedy." What should I do if my employer does not pay for overtime hours?
If the employer does not pay for overtime hours, it is advisable to contact the Cantonal Public Health Service or a union for assistance. In complex cases, it may be necessary to consult a lawyer specializing in labor law.
How long does it take to receive payment for overtime hours?
The time required to receive payment for overtime hours can vary depending on the complexity of the case. In normal situations, payment should occur within a reasonable period. In exceptional cases, such as that of Crans Montana, negotiations can be prolonged.
How to document the overtime hours worked?
