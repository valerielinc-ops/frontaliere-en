Swiss stops in-flight duty-free sales

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 29 April 2026

From 30 September 2026, duty-free products will only be available online. Up to 50% off from June.

Contesto

In brief - Duty-free sales on board ending on September 30, 2026 - Products available only online on Worldshop - Up to 50% discounts from June ## Key facts - What: End of duty-free sales on board - When: September 30, 2026 - Where: Swiss flights - Who: Swiss International Air Lines - Amount: Up to 50% discounts Shortly before the end of the flight, the cabin crew used to ask passengers if they wanted to buy cigarettes, watches, jewelry, or perfumes. Well, soon Swiss will put an end to this traditional on-board ritual. The airline announced in a press release that duty-free sales on flights will cease at the end of September. From that moment on, duty-free products will be available exclusively in the 'Worldshop' online store of the Miles&More program. Swiss is adapting to the changing purchasing habits of passengers. An analysis of sales on board its aircraft over the past few years has shown a clear result: fewer and fewer passengers make purchases on the plane. Impulse buying from the duty-free cart has become an exception. On the contrary, an increasing number of passengers inform themselves about the products once at home, compare prices online, and make purchases even before arriving at the airport. ### What changes for cross-border workers Cross-border workers who use Swiss flights for their trips between Italy and Switzerland will have to adapt to this new way of shopping. Those who were used to making duty-free purchases during the flight will now have to plan their purchases online before departure. This could influence the choice of some cross-border workers who prefer to shop during the flight to avoid carrying bulky items with them. ### Discounts and sales Before the end of duty-free product sales on board, there will be a sale. Dettagli operativi

Analysis of Shopping Habits The analysis of onboard sales has revealed that fewer and fewer passengers make purchases during the flight. This change is attributed to the growing trend of researching products online and comparing prices before making a purchase. Passengers now prefer to shop online, where they can find a wider range of products and often at more competitive prices. ### Comparison with the Previous Situation Before this decision, passengers could purchase a variety of duty-free products onboard, including cigarettes, watches, jewelry, and perfumes. This offered the opportunity for last-minute shopping and to take advantage of special offers available only onboard. With the end of duty-free sales onboard, passengers will now have to plan their purchases in advance and rely on the online store Worldshop. ### Future Scenarios With the elimination of duty-free sales onboard, Swiss aims to optimize onboard operations and meet the needs of passengers who prefer to shop online. This change could also influence the marketing strategies of airlines, which might focus more on online sales and loyalty programs such as Miles&More. ### Impact on Cross-Border Commuters For cross-border commuters who frequently travel between Italy and Switzerland, this decision could have a significant impact. Those who were used to making duty-free purchases during the flight will now have to plan their online purchases before departure. This could influence the choice of some cross-border commuters who prefer to shop during the flight to avoid carrying bulky items with them. ### Comparison with Other Airlines Other airlines might follow Swiss's example and reduce or eliminate duty-free sales onboard. Frequently Asked Questions
When will duty-free sales on board Swiss flights end?
Duty-free sales on board Swiss flights will end on 30 September 2026. From that moment on, duty-free products will only be available online at the Worldshop of the Miles&More program.
Will there be discounts on duty-free products before on-board sales end?
Yes, starting in June, Swiss will offer a selection of remaining duty-free items at reduced prices, with discounts starting at 25% and up to stock depletion.
What will remain available on board after duty-free sales end?
After duty-free sales end, beverages, snacks, and other items will remain available on board. Duty-free products will only be available online at the Worldshop.
