The NoG7 coalition has announced a protest on Geneva soil for June 14, with or without authorization. The Geneva State Council, concerned about the recurrence of clashes that occurred during the G8 in 2003, has asked the Confederation to close the borders with France for security reasons. The G7 summit, which will take place from June 15 to 17, 2026, will host senior officials from the G7 member countries and the European Union, as well as other invited heads of state and government. The Federal Council has already approved the deployment of the army to support civil authorities regarding security measures during the G7, as well as a contribution from the Confederation to cover the security costs incurred by the cantons of Geneva, Vaud, and Valais. ### Security measures and border closures Monica Bonfanti, commander of the Geneva cantonal police, has also expressed her support for closing the borders and other aspects related to security. Bonfanti explained that, in case of a threat to public order, the Federal Council can decide to increase the security measures at the borders. Implications for cross-border workers Possible partial closures of the borders between Geneva and France could have a significant impact on cross-border workers who work in Switzerland and reside in Italy. In particular, those who cross the border daily to go to work may face stricter checks and longer waiting times. ### Possible scenarios 1. Partial closure of border crossings: If some of the 34 borders between Geneva and France were to close, cross-border workers might have to use alternative crossings, resulting in longer travel times. It is important to monitor updates from the authorities to know in real-time which crossings will be closed and which will remain open. 2. Systematic checks: Even at the crossings that remain open, stricter checks may be introduced. This could result in longer waiting times and the need to have additional documents with you, such as a residence permit or a work contract. 3. Demonstrations and disruptions: The demonstration announced for June 14 could cause disruptions and roadblocks. It is advisable to avoid crossing the border on those days, unless absolutely necessary. ### What to do To minimize disruptions, cross-border workers can take some precautions: - Inform yourself in advance: Monitor updates from Swiss and French authorities to know in real-time any closures or restrictions. - Plan alternative routes: Identify alternative border crossings and plan alternative routes to avoid delays. - Documents in order: Make sure you have all the necessary documents with you, such as the residence permit, work contract, and passport. - Avoid critical days: If possible, avoid crossing the border on days when demonstrations or particularly strict checks are expected. ### Useful tools To help cross-border workers plan their movements, t...

Procedure to cross the border during the G7 1. Check open border crossings: Before crossing the border, check which crossings are open and which are closed. You can find this information on the website of the Swiss or French authorities. 2. Check your documents: Make sure you have all the necessary documents with you, such as your passport, residence permit, work contract, and G permit. 3. Plan your route: Plan your route in advance, taking into account possible disruptions and longer waiting times. 4. Monitor updates: During the G7 period, monitor updates from the authorities for any changes in border crossing conditions. 5. Prepare a plan B: In case of unexpected closures or particularly strict checks, prepare a plan B with alternative routes and different border crossings. ### Useful tools To help you plan your trips, you can use the traffic calculator and the permit comparator. These tools can provide you with up-to-date information on waiting times at border crossings and procedures for obtaining or renewing residence permits. ### Conclusion The possible partial closures of the borders and stricter checks during the G7 in Evian could cause inconvenience to cross-border workers who work in Switzerland and reside in Italy. However, with good planning and the use of the right tools, it is possible to minimize inconveniences and cross the border safely and promptly.
