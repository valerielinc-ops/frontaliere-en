Dettagli operativi

Ticino: Cashless Tickets on Public Transport The decision to eliminate ticket machines on public transport in Ticino has raised some concerns. According to the Association of Traffic and Environment (ATA), the risk is that some users may encounter difficulties. 'It is essential to maintain the possibility of purchasing a ticket without a card, especially for younger people and some elderly individuals,' notes National Councilor and ATA Ticino President Bruno Storni. For example, in Lugano, where 30% of the population is over 65, the elimination of ticket machines could create problems for seniors who are not familiar with digital technologies. Similarly, in Chiasso, where 20% of the population is under 18, the absence of ticket machines could make it difficult for students to purchase tickets. To address this issue, transport companies are studying the introduction of a rechargeable value card that can be purchased in cash at served sales points and then used on new digital distributors. This card, called the 'Ticino Card,' would have an initial value of 10 francs and could be recharged up to a maximum of 100 francs. The Ticino Card would be a practical solution for users who do not have access to credit cards or prefer to pay in cash. Additionally, the card could be used to purchase tickets for multiple people, such as a family or group of friends. According to federal regulations effective January 1, 2022, transport companies must offer passengers the option to purchase tickets using electronic payment methods. However, the regulations do not exclude the possibility of offering cash payment options. Ticino: Cashless Tickets on Public Transport The reorganization will also change the presence of ticket machines at stops: from around 500 current machines to about 30, concentrated mainly at major interchange nodes such as Lugano, Bellinzona, and Locarno. Meanwhile, assisted sales points will continue to offer the possibility of purchasing tickets and subscriptions in cash. According to data provided by Ticino News, in 2022, over 1.5 million tickets were sold on Ticino public transport, with a turnover of around 30 million francs. Most tickets were purchased using electronic payment methods, but a significant proportion was still paid in cash. The decision to reduce the number of ticket machines at stops was made to optimize costs and improve service efficiency. However, this could create difficulties for some users, such as seniors or those without access to electronic payment methods. To mitigate this impact, the Ticino public transport company has announced that it will be possible to purchase tickets and subscriptions at assisted sales points, such as the Lugano and Bellinzona stations, where it will be possible to pay in cash. Additionally, it will be possible to use the mobile payment service to purchase tickets and subscriptions directly from a smartphone. Frequently Asked Questions
When will the new equipment be installed to buy public transport tickets in Ticino?
By 30 June 2024, with the aim of reducing electronic waste and increasing efficiency.
How many tickets can be purchased with the Ticino Card at most?
The Ticino Card can be recharged up to a maximum of 100 francs, without specific limits on the number of tickets available within this limit.
How can I recharge the Ticino Card if I live in Italy?
The Ticino Cards can be recharged in Italy at the agreed tobacco shops, stores and mobile app available on iOS and Android. You can also charge online with SEPA transfer or credit card, with instant credit and a maximum limit of 100 francs.
What are the fees for using credit cards on new vending machines on buses?
Credit or debit card transactions on the new vending machines on board the buses do not involve additional fees for the passenger; The cost is equal to the value of the ticket. Only international prepaid cards can incur a small fee of CHF 1.00, while Swiss and Italian cards are exempt.
