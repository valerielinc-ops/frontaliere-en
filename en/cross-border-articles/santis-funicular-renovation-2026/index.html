Säntis cableway closed for months: 30 million upgrade

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 1 May 2026

The Säntis cableway will close for several months for a 30 million francs upgrade. Reopening expected in autumn.

Contesto

In brief - Säntis Cable Car closed from April 30, 2026 - 30 million francs modernization - Reopening expected in autumn ## Key facts - What: Complete modernization of the Säntis Cable Car - When: Closed from April 30, 2026, reopening in autumn - Where: Mount Säntis, Eastern Switzerland - Who: Säntis Cable Car authorities - Amount: 30 million francs The Säntis Cable Car, one of the most frequented ski lifts in Switzerland, will make its last passenger transport on April 30, 2026. For several months it will be out of service for a complete overhaul, with a total cost of 30 million francs. The first trips with the new cable car are expected in late autumn, but the actual opening date depends on the weather conditions of the coming months. Bookings for events at the summit will not be accepted until the end of the year. During the works, the Säntis will only be reachable on foot and the restaurant at the summit will remain closed. The new cable car cabins will be able to transport, as before, 85 passengers, and the duration of the journey, of about eight minutes, will remain unchanged. However, the technical innovation will improve the comfort of guests and stability in case of strong gusts of wind. Entering service in the summer of 1935, the Säntis Cable Car transports about half a million passengers a year. The facilities were completely modernized for the last time in 1974, while new cabins arrived in 2000. ### Impact on cross-border workers For cross-border workers who use the Säntis Cable Car to reach the summit, the closure represents a significant inconvenience.
