Val Calanca, four municipalities are exploring a structural merger The process of municipal mergers in Ticino is increasingly seen as a key strategy to address sustainability and efficiency challenges in public services, especially in rural and low-density areas. The Calanca merger exemplifies this trend, involving the municipalities of Arvigo, Braggio, Roveredo, and Santa Maria in Calanca. These entities aim to pool resources and expertise to improve the quality of public offerings, reduce costs, and enhance their ability to attract investments. According to cantonal regulations, this operation requires a detailed process: first, public consultations with residents, businesses, and local associations; then impact assessments evaluating social, economic, and environmental effects. The cantonal law, approved in 2018, stipulates that the proposal be submitted to the Cantonal Review Commission, with an estimated timeline of about three years. The current phase, involving community meetings and workshops, will conclude in 2027, with the goal of adopting a shared plan. > “The aim is to create a stronger entity capable of supporting quality services and attracting resources,” states the director of the Department of Finance of Canton Ticino. To make this strategy concrete, the municipalities need to develop resource management plans, forecast budgets, and integrated development strategies. Preliminary studies indicate that, currently, the four separate municipalities spend about 12 million Swiss francs annually on administration; merging could reduce these costs by up to 20%, optimizing resources and improving service quality, such as social assistance, waste management, and urban planning. Frequently Asked Questions
What does the merger of four municipalities in the Canton of Ticino mean?
The merger of four municipalities in the Canton of Ticino means the creation of a new, more effective administrative body, capable of managing the critical issues related to depopulation, the aging of the population and the shortage of qualified personnel.
What could be the annual savings for the municipalities of Val Calanca after the merger?
According to preliminary studies, the merger could lead to a reduction of up to 20% in administrative costs, which currently amount to around CHF 12 million per year, resulting in potential savings of up to CHF 2.4 million per year.
What are the main tax advantages for citizens after a municipal merger in Ticino?
The merger could lead to a reduction in administrative costs of up to 20%, with an estimated saving of around 2.4 million francs per year compared to the current 12 million francs. In addition, cantonal law provides tax breaks for merged municipalities, such as relief on local taxes and access to federal and cantonal funds for infrastructure projects, up to 50 million francs in 2023.
How is the population involved in the decision-making process on the merger?
Ticino legislation provides for public consultations, meetings with citizens, workshops and a formal consultation with the City Council. The popular vote is scheduled for 2026. The procedure also includes social and economic impact analyses, with the aim of ensuring transparency and active consent.
What public services could improve after the merger of the Municipalities of Val Calanca?
The merger would make it possible to optimize services such as social assistance, waste management and urban planning, thanks to greater management capacity and shared resources. In addition, strategic infrastructure projects, such as the maintenance of mountain roads, could be activated with cantonal and federal funding.
