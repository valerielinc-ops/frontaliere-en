Nurses: no to reducing weekly hours

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 28 April 2026

The National Council has decided to maintain the maximum weekly working hours for nurses at 50, despite the popular initiative approved in 2021.

Contesto

In brief - The National Council has decided to maintain the maximum weekly working hours for nurses at 50 hours. - The normal duration of the working week has been set at 42 hours. - Discussions continue on improving the working conditions of nursing staff. ## Key facts - What: Decision on the maximum weekly working hours for nurses - When: 28 April 2026 - Where: Bern - Who: Swiss National Council - Amount: 50 hours (maximum weekly duration), 42 hours (normal weekly duration) The Swiss National Council has decided to maintain the maximum weekly working hours for nurses at 50 hours, despite the popular initiative approved on 28 November 2021. The decision was made during the discussion of the law implementing the initiative, which aims to improve the working conditions of nursing staff. The normal duration of the working week has been set at 42 hours, with 117 votes in favor and 72 against. Diverging views emerged early in the debates, with the SVP and FDP presenting two proposals to refer the dossier back to the government. For the Democrats, the higher costs caused by the project should be entirely compensated by restrictions in the catalog of services of compulsory health insurance. According to the FDP, the project should be coordinated with the introduction of uniform financing of care (EFAS Reform) and with tariff negotiations. The proposals on the table today will cause an increase in premiums of 2-4%, Diana Gutjahr (SVP/TG) has argued. For the Thurgovian, it is therefore necessary to have a discussion about the catalog of services. 'Yes, it is very annoying, but it is necessary,' she argued. Dettagli operativi

Analysis of the decisions taken The National Council decided to maintain the maximum weekly working hours for nurses at 50 hours, despite the Federal Council's proposals to reduce it to 45 hours. The decision was made with 110 votes against 84, with the majority considering that the Federal Council's proposals constituted excessive interference in labor relations and involved disproportionate costs. The commission rapporteur Benjamin Roduit (Centre/VS) emphasized that reducing the hours could paradoxically exacerbate the shortage of qualified labor and exert greater pressure on the sector. This would go against the main objective of the initiative accepted by the people, which is to improve the working conditions of nursing staff. ### Implications for cross-border workers The National Council's decision has important implications for cross-border workers employed in the Swiss healthcare sector. Maintaining the maximum weekly working hours at 50 could influence the availability of qualified nursing staff, especially in a context of labor shortages. Furthermore, the decision could impact the costs of the healthcare sector, with possible repercussions on health insurance premiums. For cross-border workers in Ticino, it is important to monitor the evolution of discussions on improving the working conditions of nursing staff. Decisions made by the National Council could influence local policies and working conditions in the Canton of Ticino. ### Comparison with the previous situation Before the National Council's decision, the Federal Council had proposed reducing the maximum weekly working hours to 45 and the normal hours to 40-42. Frequently Asked Questions
What is the maximum weekly working time for nurses in Switzerland after recent decisions?
The Swiss National Council has decided to maintain the maximum weekly duration of work for nurses at 50 hours. This decision, taken on 28 April 2026 by 110 votes to 84, goes against the proposals of the Federal Council that aimed to reduce it to 45 hours. The aim is to avoid accentuating the shortage of skilled labour.
How many normal weekly hours have been set for nursing staff in Switzerland?
The normal working week length for nursing staff in Switzerland has been set at 42 hours. This decision was approved by the National Council with 117 votes in favour and 72 against, as part of the implementation of the initiative to improve working conditions in the health sector.
How does the decision on nursing hours affect border workers operating in Switzerland?
The decision to maintain a maximum of 50 hours per week for nurses has important implications for border workers. It could affect the availability of qualified personnel and the costs of the health sector, with possible repercussions on health insurance premiums. It is essential to monitor the evolution of local policies, especially in Ticino.
Will the new regulation of working hours for Swiss nurses lead to an increase in insurance premiums?
Yes, proposals on the table to improve the conditions of nurses could cause an increase in compulsory insurance premiums. During the debates, it was argued that the increase could be 2-4%. This has generated discussions about the need to compensate for higher costs, including through restrictions in the performance catalogue.
Why did the National Council decide to maintain a maximum of 50 hours of work for nurses instead of reducing them?
The National Council maintained the maximum duration at 50 hours, against the Federal Council's proposal of 45 hours, by 110 votes to 84. The majority considered that a reduction would constitute excessive interference in employment relationships and entail disproportionate costs. It was also feared that it could accentuate the shortage of skilled labour.
