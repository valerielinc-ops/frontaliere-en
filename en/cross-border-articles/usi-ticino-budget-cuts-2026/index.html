USI: 'We must cut to the bone, side measures are no longer enough'

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 27 April 2026

Monica Duca Widmer, president of the University of Italian Switzerland, announces structural cuts to address reduced funding.

Contesto

In brief - USI reviews strategic plan for budget cuts - Structural cuts expected in addition to ancillary measures - Possible increase in university tuition fees ## Key facts - What: Reviewing the strategic plan and structural cuts - When: Not yet specified - Where: Università della Svizzera Italiana, Lugano - Who: Monica Duca Widmer, president of the University Council - Amount: Not yet specified Monica Duca Widmer, president of the Università della Svizzera Italiana, announced during the 30th Dies Academicus that the USI will have to face structural cuts due to reduced funding. 'We are reviewing the strategic plan,' explained Duca Widmer, adding that 'we will have to decide on priorities: in addition to the Canton, there are federal cuts and probably also those of the City of Lugano. Therefore, we will have to plan other savings measures: this time we are going to touch, to cut to the quick. It will no longer be just ancillary measures, but at a strategic level we have to decide in which direction to go.' ### Implications for students The structural cuts could have a significant impact on students, with possible increases in tuition fees and a review of the programs offered. 'In part, we have already had to set student limits, given the logistics and the fact that the spaces we have are now occupied,' explained Duca Widmer. This could lead to greater selectivity in student admissions, especially for the most popular programs. ### New rector Another topic concerns the appointment of the new rector. 'We are well underway, I hope that by the end of May we can also have the name,' explained Duca Widmer. 'We are very pleased with the candidacies received.' The appointment of the new rector will be crucial to guide the USI through this period of financial transition....

Analysis of Practical Implications The structural cuts announced by USI will have a significant impact on various aspects of the university, from students to academic programs. Let's take a closer look at what will change. ### Impact on Students With the structural cuts, it is likely that USI will increase tuition fees for students. This could make higher education less accessible, especially for cross-border commuters who already face high costs for living and studying in Switzerland. Additionally, limiting the number of students could make it harder to gain admission to the most popular programs, increasing competition among prospective students. ### Review of Academic Programs USI is already reviewing some programs to optimize the academic offer. This could mean the reduction of less popular courses or the merger of similar programs to save resources. Current students may have to face changes in their study plans, with possible delays in graduation if the required courses are no longer available. ### Comparison with Other Swiss Universities Compared to other Swiss universities, USI is in a particularly difficult position due to the cuts announced by the Government for the implementation of initiatives on health insurance. While other universities may have more flexibility in managing their budgets, USI will have to make difficult choices to maintain the quality of education despite reduced funding. ### Future Scenarios If the structural cuts are not sufficient to balance the budget, USI may have to consider further measures, such as reducing staff or closing certain departments. What are the main savings measures announced by USI?
USI has announced that it will have to make significant cuts, going beyond collateral measures. This includes reviewing the strategic plan, limiting the number of students, and revising academic programs. Additionally, there may be an increase in university tuition fees to boost revenue.
