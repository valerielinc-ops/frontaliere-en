In brief - Josi, Niederreiter, Hischier and Meier confirm their participation - Suter already added, Kurashev in doubt due to injury - World Championships start on 15 May 2026 ## Key facts - What: Confirmation of participation in the hockey World Championships - When: 4 May 2026 - Where: Zurich and Fribourg - Who: Roman Josi, Nino Niederreiter, Nico Hischier, Timo Meier - Amount: 5 NHL players confirmed Roman Josi, Nino Niederreiter, Nico Hischier and Timo Meier have officially confirmed their participation in the 2026 Hockey World Championships, which will be held in Zurich and Fribourg. The confirmation came through two videos published by Swiss Ice Hockey on their social networks. Josi, a defender for Nashville, is very likely to become the captain of the team coached by Jan Cadieux. The other three players, Niederreiter, Hischier and Meier, are respectively a winger for Winnipeg and attackers for the New Jersey Devils. These four players join Pius Suter, who was already added to the group last week. The Swiss roster now includes 29 players, four of whom are from Fribourg and five from Davos. However, thirteen players have been excluded from the final roster. Among these, Jonas Siegenthaler is already out due to injury, while the arrival of Philipp Kurashev is uncertain due to an injury. Janis Moser and Kevin Fiala are not mentioned, with the latter still in recovery after a serious leg injury. ### Implications for cross-border workers The Hockey World Championships represent a major event for the canton of Ticino and for cross-border workers living and working in the region.
