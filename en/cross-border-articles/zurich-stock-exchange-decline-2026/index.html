Frequently Asked Questions
How do the recent declines in European stock markets affect frontier investors?
Border crossers investing in the stock market could suffer losses due to the general downturn in European markets. For example, the Euro Stoxx 50 lost 2.3% in the week of October 15-19, 2023. It is advisable to closely monitor the markets and consider diversification.
What is the current situation of the Swiss stock market for frontier workers?
The Swiss market, represented by the Swiss Market Index (SMI), showed greater resistance than other European indices, dropping 0.9% in the week of 15-19 October 2023. Despite this, we cannot speak of a real 'take-off', but it offers relative stability.
What are the tax implications for border workers who invest in the stock exchange and live in Italy?
Capital gains from financial investments are generally tax-free in Switzerland. However, frontier workers must declare such income in Italy according to Italian tax regulations. It is essential to check the specific regulations in force to avoid tax problems.
Which Swiss sectors have been most affected by recent stock market declines?
In the week of 15-19 October 2023, the Swiss market also saw declines in some key sectors. The banking sector, with losses for UBS (1.2%) and Credit Suisse (1.5%), and the pharmaceutical sector, with Novartis (0.9%) and Roche (0.7%), recorded declines.
What investment strategies are recommended for frontier workers in this period of market uncertainty?
Portfolio diversification is crucial. Investing in both European and Swiss markets can help limit losses. Also consider more resilient sectors such as technology or renewable energy and consult a financial advisor to optimize the strategy.
