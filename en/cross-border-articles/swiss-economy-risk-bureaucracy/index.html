Swiss economy at risk: bureaucracy and internal divisions

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 24 April 2026

Economist Rudolf Walser raises the alarm: Switzerland risks losing competitiveness due to federal bureaucracy and internal divisions.

Contesto

TL;DR - Swiss economy threatened by excessive federal bureaucracy - Federal administration now includes multiple offices with varying interests - Internal divisions and power shifts disadvantage private economy - Cross-border workers face more complex administrative procedures ## Key facts - Main Concern: Excessive interference of federal bureaucracy in Swiss economy - Key Example: Federal Council introducing stricter rules for companies despite economic resistance - Change in Administration: Multiple federal offices now involved in economic issues, not just Seco - Historical Context: State previously had to justify regulation, now must justify lack of it - External Influences: Federal bureaucracy affected by directives from OECD, G20, EU, and UN - Impact on Economy: Continuous regulation shifts power balance against private economy - Future Threat: Swiss economic hub seriously threatened by bureaucratic interference - Cross-border Workers: Face more complex administrative procedures and potential job market impacts ## Rudolf Walser's Warning Economist Rudolf Walser has raised a concerning alarm about the Swiss economy, criticizing the excessive interference of federal bureaucracy and the Confederation. In an interview with the Neue Zürcher Zeitung (NZZ), Walser painted a worrying picture for the country's future, dominated by increasingly influential bureaucracy and less impactful economic associations. Walser, a former member of the Vorort (predecessor of Economiesuisse), cited a concrete example: the Federal Council wants to introduce stricter rules for companies to counter the popular initiative “For responsible large companies - to protect human beings and the environment”, despite the resistance of economic organizations.
