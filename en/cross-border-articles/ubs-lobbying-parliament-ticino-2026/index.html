UBS accused of aggressive lobbying on Swiss Parliament

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 28 April 2026

UBS is exerting unprecedented pressure on MPs to block a law that would force it to strengthen its capital.

Contesto

In brief - UBS aggressively lobbies the Swiss Parliament - The bank opposes a law that would force it to strengthen its capital - Finance Minister Karin Keller-Sutter criticizes UBS's actions ## Key facts - What: UBS lobbying against a law that would force the bank to strengthen its capital - When: April 28, 2026 - Where: Switzerland - Who: UBS, Karin Keller-Sutter, Swiss Parliament - Amount: 20 billion CHF in additional equity required from UBS Federal Councillor Karin Keller-Sutter has denounced unprecedented lobbying by UBS to obstruct the bill that would force the bank to strengthen its capital. According to Keller-Sutter, UBS is pitting the bank's interests against those of taxpayers. The finance minister said in an interview with the Zurich daily Blick that many parliamentarians are concerned about possible reductions in contributions to their party from UBS. 'One can have different opinions. But it is not usual to oppose our institutions so firmly,' said Keller-Sutter. The Parliament has thoroughly examined the management of the collapse of Credit Suisse, particularly within the framework of a parliamentary commission of inquiry. Many parliamentarians agree with the objectives of the Federal Council, but the pressure exerted by UBS is not easy to manage. The proposed measures are the result of an in-depth analysis of the failure of Credit Suisse and aim to fill existing gaps. The Federal Council has taken its responsibilities; now it is up to Parliament to act. 'Ultimately, the question is which interests will prevail: those of taxpayers or those of UBS,' Keller-Sutter emphasized. ### The Federal Council's requests To fully cover participations in foreign branches, UBS must provide an additional 20 billion CHF in equity, according to the Federal Council.
