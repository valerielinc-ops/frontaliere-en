Pentecost 2026: traffic returning to Ticino (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 25 May 2026

After the Pentecost long weekend, the return to Ticino promises fewer traffic jams than in previous years. Here's what to expect.

In brief - Pentecost 2026 return: fewer queues expected - Monday, May 25th peak traffic day - Maximum 2 km of queue at Airolo ## Key facts - What: Pentecost return - When: May 25th, 2026 - Where: A2 motorway, San Gottardo south portal - Who: Federal Office for Roads (FEDRO) - Amount: 21 km of queue in 2025 After the long Pentecost weekend, the return to Ticino is expected to be less problematic compared to previous years. According to FEDRO forecasts, Monday, May 25th will be the last day with high traffic. The south portal of the San Gottardo, particularly at Airolo, will be the critical point, but no critical situations like those recorded between Friday and Saturday are expected, with a peak of 21 kilometers of queue and waiting times that exceeded four hours. ### Pentecost 2025: the comparison Last year, during the Pentecost holiday, traffic at Airolo reached a maximum wait time of one hour and 20 minutes. Traffic remained intense from 10 AM to 8 PM, then gradually decreased during the night. In previous years, both in 2023 and 2024, delays never exceeded 40 minutes. This Sunday, some returns were also recorded, with a queue that fluctuated between one and two kilometers throughout the day. Traffic heading north was sustained but within the norm, with delays that never exceeded 30 minutes. ### Implications for cross-border workers For cross-border workers who commute to Ticino and reside in Italy, the Pentecost return is a crucial moment. This year's forecasts indicate a less critical situation compared to the past, but it is always advisable to plan the trip in advance. However, it's important to consider various factors that can influence the journey. For example, weather conditions can play a crucial role. In 2025, traffic peaked at 21 kilometers of queue, with waiting times exceeding four hours. In 2024, however, delays never exceeded 40 minutes. This comparison shows how traffic conditions can vary significantly from one year to the next. ### Possible scenarios There are several possible scenarios for the Pentecost 2026 return. If the forecasts prove accurate, traffic should be manageable, with waiting times lower than in previous years. However, if adverse weather conditions or other unforeseen events occur, queues could lengthen. In this case, it's important to have a plan B, such as alternative routes or flexible departure times. ### Practical advice For cross-border workers who need to cross the Alps heading south, there are some practical tips to follow.
