Targeted interventions to improve safety and traffic flow along the SS 340 Regina, with ANAS funding and provincial coordination

Contesto

In brief - Targeted interventions to improve traffic flow on the SS 340 Regina - ANAS funding and coordination with municipalities - Goal: reduce congestion and improve safety ## Key facts - What: Safety and functional improvement interventions - When: Project presentation on May 4, 2026 - Where: State Road 340 Regina, Lake Como - Who: Province of Como, municipalities of Colonno, Sala Comacina, Tremezzina, Menaggio and Carlazzo, ANAS - Amount: Funding fully covered by ANAS Today, at the headquarters of the Province of Como, an operational meeting was held between the President of the Province, the Mayors of Colonno, Sala Comacina, Tremezzina, Menaggio and Carlazzo, and ANAS technicians Pietro Gualandi, Lorenzo Giusta, and Enzo Nicotera, to present the projects planned by the recently signed convention for the improvement of the State Road 340 "Regina". The convention coordinates roles, competencies, and operational methods for the implementation of a series of localized safety and functional improvement interventions along one of the most delicate and busy stretches of Larian traffic. The interventions illustrated today concern: - In Colonno, adaptation and safety works, including the setback of elements that narrow the carriageway; - In Sala Comacina, the setback of a stone fence along the state road; - In Tremezzina, interventions to widen the road near the town hall of Lenno; - In Menaggio, the widening of the "Vischi" hairpin bend and of via Don Moltrasio. The request of the municipality of Carlazzo for technical support to improve safety, including the installation of pedestrian call traffic lights, was also discussed. Dettagli operativi

Implications for cross-border workers The planned interventions along the Strada Statale 340 Regina will have a significant impact on cross-border workers who daily travel this stretch of road to reach their workplaces in Switzerland. Improving traffic flow and reducing congestion will make the daily commute easier. In particular, the widening of the carriageways and the removal of obstacles that narrow the road will improve road safety, reducing the risk of accidents. This is particularly important for cross-border workers who use this route to reach border crossings such as Brogeda and Gaggiolo. ### Procedures and Timelines The projects presented today will enter a consultation phase with the private entities affected by the interventions. This phase is crucial to ensure that all technical and regulatory aspects are adequately considered. Subsequently, the projects will be submitted to the Superintendence for the necessary opinions. Once all the necessary authorizations have been obtained, the executive design and the start of the works will proceed. The exact timing of the interventions is not yet specified, but it is expected that the works could begin by the end of 2026, with a total duration that will depend on the complexity of each intervention. ### Comparison with the Previous Situation Before these interventions, the Strada Statale 340 Regina was characterized by numerous critical points, including bottlenecks in residential areas and intense traffic flows, exacerbated by the ongoing works of the Tremezzina Variant. The planned interventions aim to resolve these issues, improving safety and traffic flow. Punti chiave

What to do for cross-border workers For cross-border workers using the Strada Statale 340 Regina, it's important to monitor the progress of the works and any changes in traffic conditions. It is advisable to consult the websites of the municipalities involved and the Province of Como for updates on timelines and any detours. Furthermore, it is essential to follow road signs and respect any speed limits or other restrictions imposed during the works. This will help ensure the safety of all road users and facilitate the execution of the works. ### Useful tools To best plan their movements, cross-border workers can use tools such as the route calculator and the traffic comparator available on the Frontaliere Ticino website. These tools allow you to monitor real-time traffic conditions and plan alternative routes if necessary. Additionally, it is useful to consult the practical guides available on the site, such as the traffic guide and the work permit guide, to have updated and detailed information on all issues related to cross-border work. ### Conclusion The interventions planned along the Strada Statale 340 Regina represent an important step to improve the safety and fluidity of traffic, with a positive impact on cross-border workers who use this road to reach their workplaces in Switzerland. Frequently Asked Questions
What are the main interventions planned along the State Road 340 Regina?
The main interventions planned include the setback of elements that narrow the carriageway in Colonno, the setback of a stone fence in Sala Comacina, the widening of the roadway in Tremezzina, and the widening of the 'Vischi' bend and Don Moltrasio Street in Menaggio. Additionally, pedestrian call buttons will be installed in Carlazzo.
Who will finance the interventions along the State Road 340 Regina?
The interventions will be fully financed by ANAS, which will also act as the implementing body, overseeing the executive design, tendering, and execution of the works, as well as the final acceptance.
What are the expected timelines for the start of the works?
The exact timelines have not been specified yet, but it is expected that the works could begin by the end of 2026, with a total duration that will depend on the complexity of each intervention.
