The 2026 summer holidays are expected to be more expensive due to the war in Iran and the consequent blockade of the Strait of Hormuz, one of the most critical transit routes for oil. According to an analysis by the travel portal HolidayCheck, the prices of summer holidays from Switzerland, including Ticinese towns such as Lugano, Bellinzona, and Locarno, are 10-20% higher than last year. This increase is mainly due to the surge in energy prices, with aircraft fuel (kerosene) almost doubling in a few weeks, rising from around 500 to 900 dollars per barrel. ### The Most Expensive Destinations The Azores and Sicily are among the destinations that have seen the highest price increases, with respective increases of 22% and 18%. Reaching the Seychelles (+22%), Thailand (+14%), Sri Lanka (+12%), and the Maldives (+12%) has also become more expensive.
