Unitas celebrates 80 years with an audio magazine

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 28 April 2026

The association for the visually impaired marks the milestone with an audio magazine and future projects

Contesto

In brief - Unitas celebrates 80 years with an audio magazine - Economic difficulties but optimism for the future - Technology for inclusion in the workplace ## Key facts - What: Audio magazine to celebrate 80 years of Unitas - When: April 28, 2026 - Where: Lugano - Who: Unitas, association for the blind and visually impaired - Duration: Two hours and fifteen minutes of recordings Founded on April 28, 1946 in Lugano, Unitas celebrates eighty years of activity with a magazine that is not read but listened to. The association for the blind and visually impaired in Italian-speaking Switzerland has prepared two hours and fifteen minutes of recordings that retrace the birth and development of the services offered. Franca Taddei, head of the Unitas library, explains that the recordings allow you to listen to the stories of those who have contributed to the development of the association over the years. The association, which was born as a self-help entity, has evolved its mission towards inclusion. Fabio Casgnola, president of Unitas, states that inclusion means being able to fully live one's life in society. However, the change in associationalism also has an impact on finances. Casgnola admits that at the moment there are financial difficulties, as all associations experience, and that the continuous effort will be that of seeking funds. Director Gian Luca Cantarelli looks to the future with optimism, emphasizing that thanks to technology it is possible to access the world of work even with a visual disability. Technology offers concrete tools, such as applications to take public transport or move around inside a shop. Cantarelli recalls the importance of having someone who explains and makes these technologies practical throughout the day. ### Celebrations and future p...

Dettagli operativi

• ## The impact of technology on inclusion Technology plays a crucial role in the inclusion of people with visual impairments. Gian Luca Cantarelli, director of Unitas, emphasized how technological applications can facilitate access to the job market. For example, applications for taking public transport or navigating inside a store are concrete tools that improve the autonomy and independence of blind people. ### Economic challenges Despite optimism for the future, Unitas faces economic difficulties. Fabio Casgnola, president of the association, admitted that all associations are going through a financial crisis. Finding funds has become a priority to ensure the continuity of the services offered. The association is exploring new forms of association and collaborations to overcome these challenges. ### Future projects and collaborations The celebrations for Unitas' 80th anniversary include a series of initiatives that will continue until autumn. Among these, a concert, a day dedicated to sports, and other cultural activities. One of the most interesting collaborations is with SUPSI and Radio Gwen for the broadcast 'Ci vediamo dopo'. Lalitha Del Parente and Tommaso Mainardi will talk about the daily lives of people with and without visual impairments, aiming to make the content accessible and fun for everyone. ### The importance of inclusion Inclusion is a central theme for Unitas. Fabio Casgnola emphasized that self-help will always be a fundamental part of the association, but it is necessary to adapt to new forms of association.
