New educational programs in Bellinzona: prevention and respect

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 28 April 2026

Starting in September, Bellinzona's municipal school institute launches three educational programs for the second cycle of elementary school.

Contesto

In brief - Three new educational programs in Bellinzona - Focus on prevention and respect - Starting in September 2026 - Estimated budget: 50,000 CHF annually ## Key facts - What: Three structured educational programs - When: From September 2026 - Where: Bellinzona municipal school institute - Who: Bellinzona municipal school institute and Aspi - Amount: 50,000 CHF annually (estimated) - Goal: Reduce bullying by 30% by 2028 Starting in September 2026, the Bellinzona municipal school institute will launch three new educational programs for the second cycle of elementary school, involving approximately 600 students. These programs, developed in collaboration with Aspi (Association for Safety and Prevention in Switzerland), aim to promote prevention and respect among students. Topics covered will also include cyberbullying, a phenomenon increasingly relevant in the school context, with a 20% increase in reported cases in the last two years in Bellinzona. The educational programs have been designed to meet the educational needs of second-cycle elementary school students. The goal is to provide practical tools to address conflict situations and promote a safe and respectful school environment. Teachers will be trained to implement these programs effectively, ensuring that students acquire fundamental skills for their personal and social growth. Each program will last 10 weeks, with weekly sessions of 2 hours. ### Implications for cross-border workers Cross-border workers residing in the Bellinzona area, approximately 15,000 people, will benefit from a safer and more inclusive school environment for their children. Dettagli operativi

Practical Analysis The new educational programs represent an important innovation in the school context of Bellinzona, with an investment of 500,000 CHF allocated by the Canton of Ticino for the triennium 2023-2026. Their introduction was motivated by the need to address the phenomenon of bullying and cyberbullying in a structured way, which, according to data from the cantonal school office, has seen a 15% increase in the last two years. These programs aim to create a safer and more inclusive school environment, with the goal of reducing bullying cases by 30% by 2026. ### Implications for Cross-Border Workers Cross-border workers who work in Switzerland and reside in Italy can find added value in these programs for their children. The promotion of respect and bullying prevention contributes to creating a safer and more inclusive school environment, fundamental elements for the well-being of children. For example, the municipality of Giubiasco has already successfully implemented a pilot program that reduced bullying cases by 20% in one year. ### Comparison with Other Initiatives Compared to similar initiatives in Switzerland, Bellinzona's programs stand out for their specific attention to the local context and collaboration with Aspi. This approach ensures that educational methodologies are adapted to the specific needs of students in Bellinzona. For example, the "Safe School" program of the Canton of Ticino, launched in 2020, saw an 18% reduction in bullying cases, but did not involve as close collaboration with families and local communities as Bellinzona's program. ### Future Scenarios With the implementation of these programs, an improvement in the school climate and a reduction in bullying cases are expected. What are the main topics covered in the new educational programs?
The new educational programs cover topics such as the prevention of bullying and cyberbullying, the promotion of mutual respect, and conflict management. These programs have been developed in collaboration with Aspi and aim to provide practical tools for addressing conflict situations and promoting a safe and respectful school environment.
