Housing emergency in Como: PD analysis and views

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 28 April 2026

The Democratic Party of Como organizes a meeting to discuss the housing emergency in the city, with experts and speakers.

Contesto

In brief - Housing emergency in Como: PD meeting today at 9 PM - Expert speakers and moderator Patrizia Lissi - Analysis and visions for the future of housing in the city ## Key facts - What: Meeting on the topic of the housing emergency in Como - When: Tuesday, April 28, 2026, at 9 PM - Where: Headquarters of the Asylum Association in via Colonna 7, Como - Who: Democratic Party of Como - Cost: Not yet specified The Democratic Party of Como has organized a meeting titled 'Housing emergency in Como – Current situation: analysis and visions.' The event will take place today, Tuesday, April 28, 2026, at 9 PM at the headquarters of the Asylum Association in via Colonna 7, Como. The meeting was organized to discuss the current housing situation and possible future solutions. ### Analysis and visions The issue of the housing emergency is central to Como today and tomorrow, and it requires a strategic vision. The PD has decided to dedicate a path to this topic, which will also be central to the 2027 Elections. The meeting on Tuesday will see the introduction by the provincial secretary Carla Gaiani and the city secretary Daniele Valsecchi. Among the speakers will be Raffaele Monteleone, professor of Social Policies at the University of Milan Bicocca and social researcher for Metodi, Antonio Lamarucciola of the Cooperativa di Abitazione Comense, Francesca Paini of the Fondazione GB Scalabrini Ets, and Renato Brenna of the Cooperativa Edificatrice of housing for workers. The moderator will be Patrizia Lissi, PD group leader in the municipal council. The meeting will be an opportunity to analyze the current situation and future possibilities, both qualitative and scientific, but also based on the experiences of the realities that have been dealing with the issue in the city...

Dettagli operativi

Analysis of Housing Challenges The housing emergency in Como is a multifaceted problem that requires an integrated approach. The main challenges include the scarcity of affordable housing, the rise in rental costs, and the need to redevelop abandoned areas for new housing projects. ### Comparison with Other Cities Como is not the only city facing this emergency. Many other Italian cities are struggling with similar problems, but Como has unique characteristics that make the situation particularly complex. The proximity to Switzerland and the presence of a significant number of cross-border workers add further challenges to housing planning. ### International Experiences The experiences of other European cities can offer interesting insights into addressing the housing emergency. For example, some German cities have successfully implemented policies for the redevelopment of abandoned areas and the construction of social housing. These models could be adapted to Como's situation to provide practical and sustainable solutions. ### Impact on the Labor Market The housing emergency has a significant impact on the labor market. The lack of affordable housing can deter workers, particularly cross-border commuters, from moving to Como. This can have negative consequences for the local economy and the availability of skilled labor. ### Safety and Quality of Life The lack of adequate housing can also affect safety and quality of life in the city. The presence of abandoned areas and the lack of adequate infrastructure can create situations of decay and insecurity. Punti chiave

Concrete actions to address the housing emergency To tackle the housing emergency in Como, it is necessary to adopt a series of concrete actions. Here are some steps that can be taken: ### 1. Redevelopment of disused areas Identify and redevelop disused areas for new housing projects. This can include the construction of social housing and the redevelopment of existing buildings. ### 2. Incentives for the construction of affordable housing Offer tax and financial incentives to encourage the construction of affordable housing. This can include tax breaks for builders and favorable financing for buyers. ### 3. Collaboration with housing cooperatives Work with housing cooperatives to develop sustainable housing projects. Cooperatives can offer innovative and affordable solutions to address the housing emergency. ### 4. Improvement of infrastructure and transport Invest in improving infrastructure and transport to make the city more livable and attractive for new residents. This can include the construction of new roads, the redevelopment of green areas, and the expansion of public services. ### 5. Promotion of social inclusion policies Adopt social inclusion policies to ensure that all residents have access to adequate housing. This can include social assistance programs, support services, and initiatives to combat poverty. ### 6. Community involvement Involve the local community in the planning and implementation of new housing projects. This can include public consultations, workshops, and citizen participation initiatives to ensure that the solutions adopted meet the needs of the community. ### Useful tools For further information and useful tools, visit the salary calculator to compare job and housing opportunities in Como and the surrounding area.
