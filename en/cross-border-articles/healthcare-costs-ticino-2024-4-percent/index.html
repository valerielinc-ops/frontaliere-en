TL;DR - Healthcare spending in Switzerland reached 97 billion francs in 2024, a 4% increase. - Ticino families paid 21.3% of healthcare costs out of pocket. - Inpatient curative treatments increased by 6.6% in 2024. - Prevention expenses decreased by 15.8% compared to 2023. ## Key facts - Total healthcare spending: 97 billion francs in 2024 - Increase in spending: Over 4% compared to 2023 - Household funding: Nearly two-thirds of total funding - Ticino out-of-pocket: 21.3% of funding - Inpatient treatments increase: 6.6% annual increase - Prevention expenses decrease: 15.8% compared to 2023 - Medications increase: 6.3% increase in costs - Ticino per capita costs: 10,792 francs in 2024 ## Record Increase in Healthcare Costs In 2024, healthcare spending in Switzerland reached 97 billion francs, with an increase of over 4% compared to the previous year. This significant rise, communicated by the Federal Statistical Office (UST), particularly affected household economies, which covered nearly two-thirds of the total funding. Ticino families paid 21.3% of the funding directly out of pocket, while 40.7% came in the form of indirect contributions, mainly through the payment of health insurance premiums. The Cantons, on the other hand, covered 31.3% of the costs. ### The Heaviest Expenditure Items Between 2023 and 2024, household expenses increased by 5.8%, while those of the State grew by 5.7%. Treatments and aid services accounted for two-thirds of healthcare sector costs, with a 4.4% annual increase. In particular, inpatient curative treatments increased by 6.6%, while long-term treatments increased by 5.9%. Implications for cross-border workers The increase in healthcare costs in 2024 has a significant impact on cross-border workers employed in Ticino. With the rise in health insurance premiums and direct costs, cross-border families face greater financial pressure. For example, a cross-border worker residing in Chiasso and working in Lugano might see an increase in their insurance premium from CHF 300 to CHF 324 per month, a difference that, multiplied by 12, results in an additional annual expenditure of CHF 360. This can affect the family budget and the choice to reside in Ticino, especially for those who have to pay withholding tax. ### Comparison with the previous situation Before 2024, healthcare costs were more contained, and families could better plan their expenses. With a 4% increase, many cross-border workers may need to review their budget, especially if they have to pay for stationary or long-term curative treatments. For example, a one-week stationary treatment in a Ticino clinic might cost CHF 5,000 in 2024, compared to CHF 4,800 in 2023. Furthermore, the reduction in prevention expenses could lead to an increase in illnesses, with a further impact on healthcare costs.
