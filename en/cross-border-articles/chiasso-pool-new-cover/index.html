Chiasso's Swimming Pool Gets a Facelift: New Cover Coming Soon (cross-border guide)

Chiasso allocates 850,000 CHF to replace the municipal pool's cover, enhancing safety and sustainability.

TL;DR - Chiasso's pool cover needs replacement due to wear and safety risks. - New cover will be double-skin, more durable, and safer. - Project costs 850,000 CHF, with 60,000 CHF net burden for the municipality. - Work to begin in October 2026, with four-month delivery time. ## Key facts - Cosa: Replacement of the PVC pressostatic cover of the municipal swimming pool. - Quando: Work expected to begin in October 2026. - Dove: Chiasso, Switzerland. - Chi: City Council of Chiasso. - Importo: 850,000 CHF total cost, 60,000 CHF net burden for the municipality. - Scadenza: Delivery time for the new structure is estimated at four months. - Materiale: New cover will feature a four-layer structure, ensuring greater durability and reliability. - Regolamenti: New cantonal and federal regulations for heated pressostatic covers. In Chiasso, the municipal swimming pool is gearing up for a major transformation. After 15 years of use and over 30 assembly and disassembly operations, the PVC pressostatic cover is showing clear signs of wear, posing risks to the structure's safety. The City Council has therefore requested a 850,000 CHF credit to replace the old triple-membrane cover with an upgraded double-skin version. According to the official statement, the current cover has undergone significant deterioration: the reduced elasticity of the material not only complicates its assembly but also increases the risk of sudden breakage. To meet new recommendations for heated pressostatic covers, the new solution will feature a four-layer structure, ensuring greater durability and reliability. The updates don't stop there: to support the new cover, the concrete foundations will also need to be adjusted, doubling their linear anchorage. Dettagli operativi

The financial aspect of this project represents a virtuous example of collaboration between public and private entities. Although the total cost amounts to 850,000 CHF, the net burden for the Municipality of Chiasso will be only 60,000 CHF, thanks to regional contributions and private foundations. This mixed financing model alleviates the economic strain on municipal budgets, making the project sustainable. This intervention is not just about aesthetics or safety. New cantonal and federal regulations for heated pressostatic covers aim to improve the energy efficiency and resilience of these structures. The choice of a double-layer skin not only extends the material's lifespan but also reduces long-term maintenance costs. Additionally, the foundation adjustments will ensure greater stability, especially during adverse weather conditions—a critical factor for a structure exposed to the elements.
