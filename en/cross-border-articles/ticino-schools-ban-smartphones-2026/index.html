Di Valerie Linc · Published on 21 March 2026

Viganello's Liceo Lugano 3 has decided to adopt a restrictive measure on the use of smartphones at school, extending the ban already in place at Lugano 1 and Locarno high schools. Starting from April 13th, students will have to deposit their mobile phones in a container at the beginning of classes and will not be able to retrieve them during short breaks between lessons. This decision was made after careful observation by the teachers, who noticed that many students tended to use their devices during breaks, getting distracted and interrupting the social interactions that take place during these moments. Lorenzo Ravano, a history teacher and president of the teachers' college at the Lugano institute, explained to SEIDISERA that the decision was made to promote a more focused learning environment and to improve social relations among students. The measure was approved by the high school's administration and will come into force on April 13th, upon return from Easter break. Students will have to deposit their smartphones in a container at the beginning of classes and will not be able to use them during short breaks between lessons. This restriction is already in place at Lugano 1 and Locarno high schools, and LiLu3 director Gianmarco Zenoni consulted with colleagues to better understand the challenges and opportunities of this measure. Communication to parents and students was sent out last week, and some parents have already expressed their support for this initiative. Liceo Lugano 3 has decided to adopt this measure to introduce a positive habit among students, creating a continuity that helps turn phone deposit into an automatic gesture. This restriction is not limited to compulsory schools but is also being extended to post-compulsory schools. Liceo Lugano 3 is the first post-compulsory institute to introduce this measure, and it is hoped that it will have a positive impact on students' learning and social relations.
