The Canton of Ticino has announced a crackdown on cars registered in other cantons, particularly in the Grisons, but which in fact predominantly stay and circulate in Ticinese territory. This was announced by State Councilor Claudio Zali, responsible for the cantonal police, in an interview with the RSI's SEIDISERA program. The phenomenon, described as 'known and monitored for some time', will now be addressed in a more systematic way. Over the past two years, the number of reports has halved, from 30-40 to around 20 per year, but the authorities believe the problem persists. 📊 The traffic section explained that the conformity of the vehicle's parking with federal regulations will be checked, going beyond mere reporting. Until now, it was mainly the precise denunciations that brought to light the suspicious cases. Now, the Canton intends to move in a more active way, directly involving the cantonal police. Dettagli operativi

Tighter Controls for Vehicles Registered in the Grisons The federal regulation stipulates that a vehicle must be registered in the canton where it is predominantly stationed. This principle, often not well-known by owners, is the basis for the new control campaign. The Traffic Section of the Department of Public Safety in the Grisons canton clarified that many may have acted in good faith, believing that obtaining a Grisons license plate would allow for the use of the vehicle without any particular restrictions. In reality, the federal order is clear: the license plate issued by a canton implies that the vehicle is predominantly stationed in that territory. In previous years, the Traffic Section recorded between 30 and 40 summonses annually to regularize these situations. The recent decline may be due to: - New calculation basis for the circulation tax - Reduction of taxes in Ticino - Greater awareness of the risks However, the authorities believe that the phenomenon is still significant and deserves particular attention. The cantonal police will be sensitized on this issue and will be given higher priority in their operational tasks. Concrete Examples For example, if a vehicle owner registered in Bellinzona, Switzerland, resides in Lugano, Switzerland, and regularly works in Como, Italy, they may be considered a vehicle registered in Switzerland that is predominantly stationed in Italy. In this case, the owner may be subject to controls and may need to regularize the situation by paying any outstanding taxes or fines. Frequently Asked Questions
Why is Canton Ticino increasing controls on cars registered in other cantons?
The Canton Ticino is increasing the controls on cars registered in other cantons because the problem of the presence of external cars in the canton does not seem to have been resolved, despite the decrease of the reports in the last two years.
What is the main criterion for determining where a vehicle must be registered under federal law?
The main criterion is the place where the vehicle is mainly stationary.
How does the presence of external cars on the Ticino territory affect control policies?
The Ticino authorities are stepping up controls on cars registered in other cantons, in particular Grigioni, to verify compliance with federal regulations on parking and reduce the presence of vehicles that station and circulate illegally in the territory.
