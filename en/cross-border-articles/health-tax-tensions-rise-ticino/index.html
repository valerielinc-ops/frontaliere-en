Health Tax: Tensions Rise Between Italy and Ticino (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 16 February 2026

The health tax continues to generate debate and tensions between Italy and Ticino. Statements by Lorenzo Quadri and reactions from border mayors.

TL;DR - Tensioni tra Italia e Ticino per la tassa salute - Quadri propone di bloccare i rimborsi all'Italia - Mastromarino invita a usare canali diplomatici ## Key facts - Cosa: Tassa salute per compensare costi sanitari per lavoratori frontalieri - Quando: Commissione congiunta si è riunita a Bellinzona a ottobre 2023 - Dove: Canton Ticino, Svizzera e Italia - Chi: Lorenzo Quadri (Lega) e Massimo Mastromarino (ACIF) - Importo: Rimborso Svizzera-Italia nel 2024: oltre 120 milioni di franchi - Scadenza: Nessuna scadenza specificata per la risoluzione della controversia The issue of the health tax is straining relations between Italy and the Canton of Ticino. Recent statements by Lorenzo Quadri, a national councilor of the Lega, suggesting to "turn off the taps to Italy" as retaliation for the health tax, have provoked strong reactions. Massimo Mastromarino, mayor of Lavena Ponte Tresa and president of ACIF (Association of Italian Border Municipalities), has sharply criticized these statements, stressing that this is not the way to resolve differences. According to Mastromarino, it is essential to follow the institutional channels provided for in the new fiscal agreement, in particular Article 5, which provides for the convening of a joint commission in the event of disputes. This commission met in Bellinzona last October, but without specifically addressing the issue of the health tax.

Dettagli operativi

The health tax, introduced to compensate for the health costs incurred by the Canton of Ticino for cross-border workers, has become a significant point of friction. The Lega dei Ticinesi seems intent on using the issue of blocking refunds as leverage to exert pressure on Italy. However, Mastromarino warns that this strategy could be counterproductive and calls for the use of diplomatic channels to find a shared solution. It is important to remember that refunds represent a fundamental resource for Italian border municipalities. In 2024, the check paid by Switzerland to Italy exceeded 120 million francs, a record that testifies to the importance of these funds for local administrations.

Frequently Asked Questions
What is the impact of the health tax for Swiss-Italian border workers?
The health tax, introduced by the Canton of Ticino, offsets health costs for border workers. Use our calculation tools to estimate the impact on your net salary.
How many Italian border municipalities could be directly affected by the blockade of restaurants?
According to ACIF, around 30 Italian border municipalities receive refreshments from Switzerland, with a record cheque of over 120 million Swiss francs in 2024.
How to calculate the health tax amount for a border worker earning CHF 3,500 per month?
A percentage of monthly gross income is applied to calculate the health tax, currently set at 3% with a ceiling of CHF 150 per month. A frontier worker who receives CHF 3 500 pays CHF 3 500 × 0.03 = CHF 105; if the result exceeds CHF 150, the maximum of CHF 150 is paid.
What are the tax consequences if the joint committee does not resolve the health tax dispute?
If the mixed commission fails to find an agreement, the matter may be sent to the Swiss federal court or to the Italian court, depending on the nature of the dispute. In this case, penalties for non-payment are likely, which may reach 10 % of the amount due, more retroactive interests up to 5 % annually.
Where can I find the form to request reimbursement of an excess health tax paid in 2023?
The form to request the reimbursement of an excessive health tax is available on the ACIF website in the section 'Modules per frontalieri'. It must be completed by 30 June 2025 and sent by PEC or registered mail to the address of the cantonal tax department. You must attach paid envelopes and the certification of the amount paid.
