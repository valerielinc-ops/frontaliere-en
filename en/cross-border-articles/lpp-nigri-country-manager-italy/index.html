Frequently Asked Questions
What new job opportunities does the expansion of Sinsay in Italy offer for border workers?
The expansion of Sinsay in Italy, led by Marco Nigri, foresees new openings in Milan, Rome and Turin by 2024. This will create new job and career opportunities in the retail and food sector for border workers, especially those residing in Ticino and neighbouring regions such as Varese and Como, thanks to an investment of 50 million euros.
How much can Ticino border workers earn working for Sinsay in Italy?
Ticino border workers working for Sinsay in Italy could benefit from salaries up to 15% higher than the Italian average. For example, an employee in Milan could earn around €1,700 net, against an Italian average of around €1,500 net. LPP aims to offer competitive conditions and greater economic stability.
Who is Marco Nigri and what role does he play in the expansion of LPP in Italy?
Marco Nigri has been appointed Country Manager for Italy by LPP, a Swiss group based in St. Gallen, starting from October 2023. With over 15 years of retail experience, Nigri will lead Sinsay's expansion strategy in Italy, focusing on value-for-money and brand development with new openings.
Where and when are the new Sinsay openings planned in Italy?
Sinsay's new openings in Italy are expected by 2024, with a focus on the cities of Milan, Rome and Turin. This expansion is part of the LPP group's strategy to strengthen its presence in the Italian market, offering new job opportunities in the retail and food sector.
What are the key steps for a border worker interested in working for Sinsay in Italy?
Interested border workers should monitor job vacancies on the LPP site, check visa and work permit requirements, and compare the salaries offered with living costs. It is advisable to consult an accountant to optimise your tax return and find out about Italian labour and tax regulations.
