From generators to reconstruction: Switzerland's 1.5 billion CHF plan for Kyiv

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 2 May 2026

Jacques Gerber, the Swiss federal councilor for Ukraine, outlines the aid program worth 1.5 billion francs.

Contesto

In brief - Aid program worth 1.5 billion francs until 2028 - 85 generators purchased for 32 million francs - Humanitarian aid, development, economic cooperation, and peace promotion ## Key facts - What: Swiss aid program for Ukraine - When: Until 2028 - Where: Ukraine, with a focus on the Odessa region - Who: Federal Council delegate for Ukraine, Jacques Gerber - Amount: 1.5 billion francs - Priority: Immediate humanitarian aid and long-term reconstruction The Federal Council delegate for Ukraine, Jacques Gerber, manages an aid program worth 1.5 billion francs until 2028. During a recent visit to the Odessa region, he emphasized the importance of targeted aid projects as well as long-term support for the country. 'In humanitarian aid, the priority remains urgency,' the Jura resident underscores in an interview published today by the Romandy newspapers La Tribune de Genève and 24 heures. Gerber cites as an example the purchase of 85 generators, at the request of Ukrainian authorities, for a total amount of 32 million francs. The specific needs, characteristics, and installation locations are defined in advance with the Ukrainian government. The Confederation then handles the purchase, transport, and installation of the generators, without any intermediate storage. ### Focus on specific projects The Swiss program covers the entire range of international cooperation tools: humanitarian aid, development, economic cooperation, and peace promotion. An allocation of 500 million francs is also dedicated to the private sector, in order to involve Swiss companies in Ukraine, recalls the government delegate.
