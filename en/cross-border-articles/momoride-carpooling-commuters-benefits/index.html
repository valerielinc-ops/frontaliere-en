Company carpooling: cross-border workers earn up to 500 francs

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 29 April 2026

The MomòRide project offers cash incentives and charitable opportunities for cross-border workers in Balerna, Chiasso, and Novazzano

Contesto

In brief - Carpooling for cross-border workers with rewards up to 500 francs - Collective goal of 40,000 points to donate 1,000 francs to the Fondazione Provvida Madre - Participation open to workers and companies in Balerna, Chiasso, and Novazzano ## Key facts - What: MomòRide corporate carpooling project - When: Starting May 1, 2026 - Where: Balerna, Chiasso, and Novazzano - Who: Cross-border workers and local companies - Amount: Up to 500 francs in monthly incentives - Goal: 40,000 points to donate 1,000 francs - Sponsor: Banca dello Stato del Canton Ticino On May 1, 2026, the first collective challenge MomòRide will begin, an initiative of corporate carpooling aimed at cross-border workers in Balerna, Chiasso, and Novazzano. This two-year project, promoted by the local municipalities, offers economic incentives and the opportunity to contribute to charitable initiatives. Participants can earn up to 500 francs per month by tracking their commutes with the Mobalt app. Additionally, by joining forces to reach the goal of 40,000 points in a month, the teams can donate 1,000 francs to the Fondazione Provvida Madre in Balerna. ### How it works To participate, cross-border workers must track their carpooling trips with the Mobalt app. Each trip accumulates points that add up to those of other carpoolers in the area. Upon reaching the target of 40,000 points, Banca dello Stato del Canton Ticino, the sponsor of the challenge, will make the donation. This collective challenge model aims to create a virtuous circle, promoting carpooling and generating positive impacts for the environment and the community. ### Incentives and participation With April, the fourth month of distributing the prize pool of 500 francs for teams from companies based in the three municipalities com...

Dettagli operativi

Practical Implications for Cross-Border Workers The MomòRide project offers numerous practical advantages for cross-border workers employed in the Balerna, Chiasso, and Novazzano area. Firstly, corporate carpooling helps reduce transportation costs by sharing fuel and toll expenses. Moreover, the opportunity to earn up to 500 francs per month serves as a significant economic incentive, which can be used to cover other daily expenses. ### Before/After Comparisons Before the introduction of the MomòRide project, cross-border workers had to bear the costs of individual transportation, often high due to traffic and tolls. With corporate carpooling, not only are these costs reduced, but there is also the opportunity to earn monthly bonuses. Furthermore, the possibility of contributing to charitable initiatives adds a social value to the project, making it even more appealing. ### Concrete Scenarios Imagine a cross-border worker who travels 50 kilometers daily to reach their workplace. With carpooling, they can share the journey with three other colleagues, thus reducing transportation costs by 75%. Additionally, by tracking their movements with the Mobalt app, they can accumulate points and earn up to 500 francs per month. If all participants join forces to reach the goal of 40,000 points, they can donate 1,000 francs to the Provvida Madre Foundation, actively contributing to the common good. ### Environmental Benefits Carpooling not only offers economic advantages but also has a positive impact on the environment. By reducing the number of vehicles on the road, CO2 emissions are decreased, and the sustainability of the area is enhanced. This is particularly important in an area like Balerna, Chiasso, and Novazzano, where traffic is one of the main sources of pollution....

How to Participate To participate in the MomòRide project, cross-border workers must follow a few simple steps: 1. Download the Mobalt app on your smartphone. 2. Register as a user and select the carpooling option. 3. Track each commute shared by car with the app. 4. Earn points for every trip made. 5. Join other carpoolers in your area to reach the goal of 40,000 points. 6. Benefit from monthly prizes and contribute to charitable initiatives. ### Deadlines and Prizes Monthly prizes of 500 francs are distributed to participants who track their trips with the Mobalt app. Additionally, each month a new collective challenge is launched with the goal of accumulating 40,000 points. Upon reaching this goal, the Bank of the Canton of Ticino donates 1,000 francs to a public utility entity in the territory. ### Useful Tools To learn more about the opportunities offered by the MomòRide project and calculate potential savings and earnings, cross-border workers can use the carpooling savings calculator. This tool allows you to estimate individual transportation costs and potential savings from corporate carpooling. ### Conclusion The MomòRide project represents an important opportunity for cross-border workers from Balerna, Chiasso, and Novazzano to benefit from economic incentives, contribute to the common good, and improve their quality of life. By participating in corporate carpooling, cross-border workers can reduce transportation costs, earn monthly prizes, and contribute to charitable initiatives. Frequently Asked Questions
How can I track my movements with the Mobalt app?
To track your movements with the Mobalt app, you first need to download the app on your smartphone and register as a user. Then, select the corporate carpooling option and track each shared car journey. Every tracked movement will accumulate points that will add up to those of other carpoolers in the area.
What prizes does the MomòRide project offer?
The MomòRide project offers monthly prizes up to 500 francs for crews that track their movements with the Mobalt app. Additionally, by reaching the collective goal of 40,000 points, participants can donate 1,000 francs to a public utility of the territory.
How can I contribute to charitable initiatives with the MomòRide project?
To contribute to charitable initiatives with the MomòRide project, you need to join other carpoolers in the area to reach the goal of 40,000 points. Upon reaching this goal, the Bank of the Canton of Ticino will donate 1,000 francs to a public utility of the territory.
