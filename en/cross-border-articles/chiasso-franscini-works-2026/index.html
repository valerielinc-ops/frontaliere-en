Roadworks in Chiasso: new closures on Via Franscini (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 6 May 2026

Partial closure on Via Franscini starting Monday, May 11th. Detours for cars and public transport. Estimated duration: 4 weeks.

Contesto

In brief - Work starts on Via Franscini in Chiasso from Monday, May 11th - Traffic closure between Via Lavizzari and Casa Anziani exit - Diversions for private and public transport, expected duration 4 weeks - Casa Anziani bus stop suspended for the entire duration of the works ## Key facts - What: Overhaul of pipelines and paving in the Franscini area - When: Work on Via Franscini from Monday, May 11th, 2026, expected duration 4 weeks - Where: Chiasso, section of Via Franscini between Via Lavizzari and the Casa Anziani parking exit - Who: Municipality of Chiasso, AGE SA - Intervention: Replacement of municipal pipelines and upgrading of AGE infrastructure - Traffic impact: Road closure, mandatory diversions, limited access - Public transport: New route via Simen, Fontana da Sagno, Alighieri, Verdi - Suspended stop: Casa Anziani, for the entire duration of the works Monday, May 11th, 2026 marks the start of a new phase of works for the redevelopment of the Franscini area in Chiasso, with direct impacts on city traffic and the daily routines of residents and cross-border workers. The Municipality of Chiasso has announced that work will begin on Via Franscini to overhaul the pipelines, a strategic intervention for the upgrading of water and sewerage infrastructure, complementing the ongoing road redevelopment works in the urban complex. The intervention concerns the section in front of the Casa Anziani 'Giardino', where traffic will be closed between Via Lavizzari and the parking exit of the structure. This measure, necessary to allow excavation and laying of new pipes, will result in a significant diversion of vehicular and public transport traffic. Dettagli operativi

Implications for cross-border workers and commuters The ongoing works in Chiasso, a crucial hub for thousands of cross-border workers who daily cross the border from Lombardy to the Canton Ticino, could generate significant delays in the usual routes of those heading to the industrial area or the city center. The detour via Valdani, although functional, could become congested during peak hours, especially considering that traffic from Como and the Chiasso Sud highway toll booth will converge on a reduced route. For cross-border workers residing in Lombardy municipalities such as Camerlata, Grandate, or Lomazzo, the impact will be direct if their daily route includes via Franscini or Corso San Gottardo. In particular, those who use public transport and usually get off at the Casa Anziani stop will need to disembark earlier or extend their walk from stops like via Alighieri or Corso San Gottardo. This can be a burden for elderly or disabled individuals, as well as a stress factor for those with rigid work schedules. ### Comparison with the previous situation Before the start of the works, via Franscini was passable in both directions and constituted a moderate transit route between the center of Chiasso and the residential quarter of San Pietro. With the intervention, the central section becomes inaccessible, fragmenting the vehicular connection and increasing the pressure on secondary roads. The absence of a definite stop for public transport in the Casa Anziani area for about a month requires prior planning by users. Frequently Asked Questions
When do the works on Via Franscini in Chiasso start?
The works on Via Franscini in Chiasso start on Monday, May 11, 2026. The intervention involves the renovation of the pipelines and is estimated to last approximately 4 weeks.
How does the public transport route change during the works?
During the works, public transport lines will be diverted from Via Franscini to Via Simen, Via Fontana da Sagno, Via Alighieri, and Via Verdi, before rejoining Corso San Gottardo. The Casa Anziani stop will be temporarily suspended.
Is it possible to access the AGE SA parking lot during the works?
Yes, access to the AGE SA parking lot, located behind Piazza Bernasconi 2, will be ensured through extraordinary measures arranged by the company. Specific operational details have not been disclosed, but it is advisable to verify directly with AGE SA or with your employer.
