May Day in Varese: ACLI calls for dignified work

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 30 April 2026

The Varese provincial ACLI put the topic of dignified work at the heart of the debate, indicating it as the urgency of the present and a fundamental lever for the future.

Contesto

In brief - The ACLI of Varese demand decent work as a present urgency - Proposal for a Decent Work Index to monitor job quality - Need for concrete policies to retain skills in the territory ## Key facts - What: Promotion of decent work - When: May Day 2026 - Where: Varese - Who: Varese provincial ACLI - Amount: Not yet specified On the occasion of May Day 2026, the provincial ACLI of Varese once again bring the issue of work to the forefront of the debate, indicating it as one of the main urgencies of the present and a fundamental lever for the future. In an international context marked by growing inequalities, environmental crises, and geopolitical tensions, decent work is indicated as a key element for social and democratic stability. Work as a social pillar For the ACLI, work represents 'the pillar of social justice, democracy, citizenship, and peace'. The association underlines the need to promote an employment model capable of guaranteeing rights, adequate wages, and stability, countering phenomena such as precariousness, poor work, and black labor. According to what was highlighted in the communiqué, the quality of work directly impacts the freedom and dignity of people, as well as the possibility of building sustainable economic growth. The urgency of new policies At the heart of the May Day message is a key word: urgency. The ACLI demand concrete policies capable of ensuring decent work for all, with particular attention to young people. The aim is to create the conditions to retain and bring back to the territory skills and professionalism often forced to seek opportunities abroad. Dettagli operativi

Analysis of ACLI's Proposals The ACLI's proposals to promote dignified work are manifold and aim to improve working conditions for everyone, including cross-border workers. Among the main proposals is the introduction of a Dignified Work Index, which would allow measuring the quality of employment and monitoring wages. This tool could be particularly useful for cross-border workers who work in Ticino and reside in Italy, offering them a clearer picture of the available job opportunities. ### Practical Implications for Cross-Border Workers For cross-border workers, the ACLI's proposals could mean greater job stability and more adequate wages. This could help reduce precarity and improve the quality of life. Furthermore, the proposal for a Dignified Work Index could facilitate the choice of more stable and remunerative job opportunities, offering a more transparent picture of working conditions. ### Comparison with Other Regions Compared to other Italian regions, Varese represents a particular case due to the significant number of cross-border workers who commute to Ticino. The ACLI's proposals could therefore have a significant impact on the lives of cross-border workers, offering them greater opportunities and better working conditions. This could contribute to retaining skills in the area and strengthening the local economic and social fabric. ### Future Scenarios If the ACLI's proposals were implemented, cross-border workers could benefit from better working conditions, with adequate wages and greater stability.
