The deficit in water reserves is 37% lower than the average for the reference period. The lack of snow, with an accumulation 40% lower than the seasonal average, has worsened the situation, making it urgent to adopt measures to address the emergency. During the regional water resources meeting held in Palazzo Lombardia, the regional councillor with responsibility for water resources, Massimo Sertori, underlined the importance of postponing the irrigation season and continuing to store water in the reservoirs. ### Urgent measures In today's meeting, it was requested to postpone the irrigation season to avoid excessive reductions in lake levels. Furthermore, hydroelectric reservoir managers were invited to accumulate as much water as possible. The reduction in water resources could affect agriculture and industry, sectors in which many cross-border workers are employed. For example, in Lombardy, the agricultural sector employs approximately 150,000 people, with an annual turnover of over 6 billion euros. In Ticino, municipalities like Mendrisio and Chiasso host numerous cross-border workers who work in sectors sensitive to water availability. ### Comparison with the previous situation Before the emergency, Lombardy had sufficient water reserves to face the summer season. Now, with a 37% deficit, the situation is critical. Postponing the irrigation season and accumulating water in reservoirs are necessary measures to avoid a greater crisis. However, these interventions may not be sufficient if the situation does not improve. ### Future scenarios If the situation does not improve, further measures may be necessary, such as rationing and stricter restrictions. Frequently Asked Questions
What are the main causes of water shortages in Lombardy?
The main causes of water shortages in Lombardy are the lack of snow and low precipitation during the winter. These factors have reduced water reserves, leading to a 37% deficit compared to the average of the reference period.
What measures have been taken to address the emergency?
The measures taken include postponing the irrigation season and accumulating water in hydroelectric reservoirs. Additionally, it has been decided to raise the maximum regulation level of Lake Maggiore to accumulate additional water resources.
How can cross-border workers prepare for the water emergency?
Cross-border workers can prepare by staying informed about the latest news, reducing water consumption, and participating in collective efforts to address the emergency. It is important to monitor official communications and follow the instructions of local authorities.
