The Serie A and B designator Gianluca Rocchi is being investigated by the Milan Prosecution for complicity in sports fraud (for some episodes of the 2024/25 season) and has been notified of a warning notice. What happened? The accusation is of having put pressure on some referees and chosen referees 'liked by Inter'. Here's the situation on the investigation conducted by the prosecutor Maurizio Ascione and the news up to today, Friday 1 May, after yesterday's interrogation of the Var supervisor Andrea Gervasoni. The facts at the center of the investigation refer to the past season: there are some Serie A matches (Udinese-Parma and Bologna-Inter), the return leg of the Coppa Italia semi-final Inter-Milan and Salernitana-Modena of Serie B. Investigators also want to clarify Inter-Verona of 2023-24: in particular for the episode of Bastoni's elbow on Duda. The spotlight has also been turned on one of the most contested episodes of last season: the penalty not given to Inter in the match against Roma, despite an obvious pull by Ndicka on Bisseck. Frequently Asked Questions
Who are the suspects in the referees' investigation?
The confirmed suspects are Gianluca Rocchi, Andrea Gervasoni, Daniele Paterna, Rodolfo Di Vuolo, and Luigi Nasca. Other VAR assistants might be under investigation, but their identities have not been made public yet.
Which matches are at the center of the investigation?
The matches at the center of the investigation include Udinese-Parma, Bologna-Inter, the second leg of the Coppa Italia semi-final Inter-Milan, and Salernitana-Modena from Serie B. Additionally, investigators are looking into Inter-Verona from the 2023-24 season.
What has Gianluca Rocchi said in his defense?
Gianluca Rocchi has stated that he works transparently and does not have major issues. He also claimed to have always worked for greater clarity in the VAR system.
