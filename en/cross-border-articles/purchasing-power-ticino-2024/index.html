Purchasing power drops: ACSI warns of growing poverty in Ticino (cross-border guide)

In brief - Purchasing power declining in Ticino - Consumer confidence at -40 points - Health insurance premiums too high for many families ## Key facts - What: Declining purchasing power and rising poverty - When: 2024 data - Where: Canton Ticino - Who: ACSI (Associazione Consumatori Svizzeri Italiani) - Amount: Consumer confidence at -40 points The economic situation in Ticino is increasingly concerning. ACSI, the Swiss Italian Consumers' Association, has raised the alarm about the decline in purchasing power and the growing financial difficulties for families. Antonella Crüzer, ACSI's general secretary, stated: 'In Ticino, we've seen for some time now that broader segments of the population are being pushed downwards, towards the poverty line.' ### Consumer confidence declining Consumer confidence in Ticino stands at -40 points, a figure that, while slightly improved compared to last year (by two percentage points), remains very low. 'Consumer confidence is low in a world truly going through very deep crises,' explained Crüzer. ### Health insurance premiums and unexpected expenses One of the main issues is health insurance premiums, which many families struggle to afford. 'Many can't have a CHF 2,500 deductible, because if something happened, they wouldn't have the money set aside to pay for it,' added Crüzer. This is a worrying sign, as purchasing power is the engine of the economy, and if it's lacking, problems can arise for everyone. ### Tips for managing expenses ACSI has provided some tips for managing expenses best during a crisis. Economic situation analysis The economic situation in Ticino is complex and requires an in-depth analysis. The decline in purchasing power and the increase in expenses represent a challenge for many families, particularly those with average incomes. ACSI has reported that many families struggle to afford health insurance premiums, which are a significant expense. ### Comparison with the previous situation Compared to last year, consumer confidence has slightly improved but remains very low. This indicates that the economic situation has not significantly improved and that families continue to struggle to manage their expenses. Furthermore, the increase in scams represents an additional problem for consumers. ### Future scenarios If the situation does not improve, further difficulties may arise for Ticino families. The decline in purchasing power could lead to an increase in poverty and greater difficulty in covering daily expenses. Additionally, the rise in scams could affect more consumers, making it even harder to manage the family budget. ### Impact on cross-border workers Cross-border workers who work in Ticino and reside in Italy could be particularly affected by this situation. With the decline in purchasing power and the increase in expenses, managing the family budget becomes increasingly difficult. Moreover, health insurance premiums and unexpected expenses can add further strain on families. ### What to do To address this situation, it is important to follow ACSI's advice and try to avoid unnecessary expenses. Additionally, it is crucial to be vigilant against scams and always request quotes for significant purchases.
