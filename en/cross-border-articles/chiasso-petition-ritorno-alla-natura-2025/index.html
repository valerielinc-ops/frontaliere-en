Statue ‘Return to Nature’ in Chiasso: petition stalled for a year (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 16 April 2026

The petition to remove the statue ‘Return to Nature’ in Chiasso, signed by 433 people in May 2025, is still awaiting an official response from the Municipal Council. Municipal councillor Francesco Romano (HelvEthica Ticino) is calling for clarity.

Nearly a year later, the petition has still not received an official response from the municipal administration. This is the complaint raised by municipal councillor Francesco Romano, representative of the HelvEthica Ticino association, who in an official question to the Chiasso town hall highlighted several critical points regarding the months-long silence. In the text of the question, Romano explicitly asks the Chiasso town hall to provide a formal response to the petition’s promoters, noting how the delay in responding 'raises questions about administrative timeliness, transparency, and respect for civic participation.' Here’s what you need to know. ### The value of civic participation in Chiasso Chiasso, a border city with over 7,000 cross-border workers crossing the Brogeda checkpoint daily to commute to work in Ticino, is a socially and politically dynamic hub. The petition about the statue is just one of many instances where citizens demand to be heard on issues affecting the city’s livability. However, when responses are delayed, the risk is generating frustration and disillusionment toward institutions. Councillor Francesco Romano highlighted in his statement how the administration’s silence on the statue case could undermine the credibility of the Municipal Council. ‘Administrative promptness is not an option but a necessity to maintain citizens’ trust,’ he emphasized in a press release. For a cross-border worker commuting to Chiasso daily for work or services, this situation can be frustrating, especially considering that the city serves as a reference point for thousands of people living between Italy and Switzerland. ### What changes for cross-border workers? For cross-border workers transiting through Chiasso daily, the statue and petition case carries particular significance.
