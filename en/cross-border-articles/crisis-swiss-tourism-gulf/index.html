Operational details

The crisis in the Persian Gulf has had a significant impact on the Swiss tourism sector, with flights suspended and fuel prices skyrocketing. The current situation has led many travelers to reconsider their holiday plans, with thousands of Swiss tourists still trapped in the region, awaiting a repatriation flight. Swiss airline Swiss has seen its connections to Dubai suspended until March 10 and those to Tel Aviv until March 22. Heike Birlenbach, Swiss's commercial director, explained that customers can freely modify their bookings, choosing another destination or postponing the trip. However, this option is not available for flights scheduled for autumn. The increase in fuel prices, which has reached 50% since the start of the tensions, represents an additional problem for Swiss. Dennis Weber, the company's financial manager, estimated that if the costs remain at these levels, Swiss would face an additional cost of three figures, expressed in millions of Swiss francs. This could have drastic consequences for the airline's finances. Edelweiss, which connects Zurich to Muscat and Salalah in Oman, declared that it is waiting for further developments before drawing conclusions. A spokesperson for the company indicated that passengers have canceled or postponed flights, while demand for new bookings is lower than in the past. The Swiss Travel Federation (FSV) has expressed concern about a possible reduction in the flow of tourism between Switzerland and the Persian Gulf. This tourist crisis represents a moment of uncertainty for the sector, with potential long-term repercussions.
