In brief - Easyjet strike on May 11, 2026, from 10 AM to 6 PM - Protected time slots: 7 AM-10 AM and 6 PM-9 PM - Ground and airport services also involved ## Key facts - What: Easyjet national strike - When: May 11, 2026, 10:00 AM-6:00 PM - Where: Malpensa and other Italian airports - Who: Easyjet pilots and flight attendants - Amount: Not specified Air transport in Italy is at risk on May 11, 2026, due to a series of strikes that will also involve Malpensa Airport. The Easyjet national strike, which will take place from 10 AM to 6 PM, is the focus of the day of protests. The protest was proclaimed by Filt Cgil, Fit Cisl, Uiltrasporti, Ugl Ta, Anpac, who denounce the 'stalemate in the contract renewal negotiations' and 'the deterioration of industrial relations and operational criticalities'. During the strikes, there are protected time slots, from 7 AM to 10 AM and from 6 PM to 9 PM, during which flights must still be operated, in addition to state, military, emergency, health, humanitarian, and rescue flights. The list of guaranteed flights is available on the Enac website. In addition to the Easyjet strike, other air transport workers across the country will also stop working. From 12 PM to 4 PM, the handling services workers in Palermo, particularly Asc Handling, Aviapartner, Gh Palermo, are on strike. In Naples and Rome, Enav employees will stop from 10 AM to 6 PM. In Cagliari, the main companies operating at the airport will stop from 1 PM to 5 PM. Rome Fiumicino Airport will also be involved, with Adr Security employees on strike from 12 PM to 4 PM. At Malpensa, the air cargo operators Alha and Mle-Bcube will stop from 1 PM to 5 PM.
