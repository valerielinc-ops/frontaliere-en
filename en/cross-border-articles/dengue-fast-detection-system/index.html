Dengue: towards a rapid system to identify it in Ticino

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 27 April 2026

New measures for the early diagnosis of dengue in Canton Ticino. Here's what changes for cross-border workers.

The health authorities of the Canton of Ticino are working on a new system to quickly identify cases of dengue. This system aims to improve early diagnosis and reduce the risk of disease spread. The new dengue identification system will be implemented in collaboration with local health facilities and research centers. The goal is to ensure a rapid and effective response in case of suspected infections. This is particularly important for cross-border workers who work and live in Ticino, as dengue can have a significant impact on their health and well-being. ### Impact on cross-border workers Cross-border workers in Ticino should be aware of the symptoms of dengue and the preventive measures to be taken. Dengue can cause high fever, headache, muscle and joint pain, and in some cases can lead to serious complications. It is essential that cross-border workers immediately consult a doctor in case of suspicious symptoms. Dettagli operativi

Practical Analysis The new dengue identification system will have a significant impact on the lives of cross-border workers in Ticino. Here are some practical implications and concrete scenarios: ### Early Diagnosis Early diagnosis of dengue is crucial to prevent serious complications. The new system will allow for rapid identification of dengue cases and prompt initiation of treatment. This is particularly important for cross-border workers who work in sectors with a high risk of exposure to mosquitoes. ### Awareness Campaigns Awareness campaigns promoted by Ticino health authorities will be crucial to inform cross-border workers about the risks of dengue and preventive measures. These campaigns will include information on how to protect oneself from mosquitoes and how to recognize the symptoms of the disease. Cross-border workers must be aware of the importance of these measures for their health and that of the community. ### Preventive Measures Adopting preventive measures is fundamental to prevent the spread of dengue. Cross-border workers can protect themselves from mosquitoes by using repellents, wearing long-sleeved clothing, and using mosquito nets. Additionally, it is important to eliminate any collections of stagnant water that could become breeding grounds for mosquitoes. ### Mosquito Surveillance Ticino health authorities are improving mosquito surveillance to identify any outbreaks of dengue. This is an important step to prevent the spread of the disease and protect the health of cross-border workers and the local population. Concrete action For cross-border workers living and working in Ticino, it is crucial to adopt preventive measures to protect themselves from dengue. Here is a step-by-step procedure to ensure safety and health: 1. Learn about symptoms: Cross-border workers should be aware of the symptoms of dengue, which include high fever, headache, muscle and joint pain. If suspicious symptoms occur, it is important to consult a doctor immediately. 2. Adopt preventive measures: Use mosquito repellents, wear long-sleeved clothing, and use mosquito nets. Eliminate any collections of stagnant water that could become breeding grounds for mosquitoes. 3. Collaborate with health authorities: Report any suspected cases of dengue to local health authorities. Collaborate with awareness campaigns promoted by the authorities to learn about the risks of dengue and preventive measures. 4. Use prevention tools: The Ticino health authorities are working to improve mosquito surveillance and identify any outbreaks of dengue. Cross-border workers must collaborate with these initiatives to ensure the safety and health of the community. 5. Consult a doctor: In case of suspicious symptoms, it is essential to consult a doctor immediately to obtain a quick diagnosis and start treatment promptly. Frequently Asked Questions
What are the symptoms of dengue?
Symptoms of dengue include high fever, headache, muscle and joint pain. In some cases, dengue can cause serious complications, so it's important to see a doctor immediately if you experience suspicious symptoms.
How can I protect myself from mosquitoes?
To protect yourself from mosquitoes, you can use repellents, wear long-sleeved clothing, and use mosquito nets. Additionally, it's important to eliminate any standing water that could become breeding grounds for mosquitoes.
What should I do if I suspect I have dengue?
If you suspect you have dengue, it's crucial to see a doctor immediately to get a quick diagnosis and start treatment promptly. This is especially important to prevent serious complications.
