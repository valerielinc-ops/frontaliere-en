Alain Berset backs Mathias Reynard after Crans-Montana tragedy

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 28 April 2026

Former Federal Councillor Alain Berset has offered psychological and professional support to Mathias Reynard after the New Year's Eve fire in Crans-Montana.

Contesto

In brief - Alain Berset supported Mathias Reynard after the Crans-Montana fire - The two are bound by a close friendship - Reynard was president of the Valais government for a difficult year ## Key facts - What: Psychological and professional support - When: After the January 2, 2026 fire - Where: Crans-Montana, Valais - Who: Alain Berset and Mathias Reynard - Amount: Not specified After the New Year's fire in Crans-Montana (VS), former Federal Councillor Alain Berset gave his "greatest support" to Valais State Councillor Mathias Reynard. The two are bound by a "close friendship," explained Reynard himself today to the newspaper "Walliser Bote." After a year at the helm of the Valais government, Reynard is "exhausted." It was a "very difficult" period, marked first by the Blatten landslide and then by the Crans-Montana disaster. Psychological support was therefore "absolutely necessary." ### Berset's support And it was Berset who gave him the most support after the fire at the bar "Le Constellation." Until today he had never spoken about it with anyone, because Berset himself preferred it that way, but now he can say it. "Alain Berset contacted me immediately and visited me on January 2 to give me his support," said Reynard. The former Socialist Federal Councillor has been by his side all this time, he added. "Berset has great experience in crisis management and his advice, as well as his support, have been extremely important to me," continued the State Councillor, defining the Secretary General of the Council of Europe "a true friend" who helped him on a professional and human level.
