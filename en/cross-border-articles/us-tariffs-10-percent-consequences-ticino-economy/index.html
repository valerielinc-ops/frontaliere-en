10% US tariffs: alarm for Ticino's exports? (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 19 February 2026 · Updated on 20 February 2026

Bern analyzes the impact of Trump's proposed global tariffs. Ticino's pharmaceutical and mechanical industries, crucial for cross-border employment, watch with concern.

Contesto

TL;DR - USA proposes 10% global tariff on imports, impacting Swiss exports. - Ticino's economy, reliant on high-value sectors, faces competitiveness threat. - Swiss government remains cautious, continues trade talks with the USA. - Uncertainty may affect jobs and investments in Ticino's export-oriented economy. ## Key facts - Proposta tariffa: 10% globale su importazioni USA. - Impatto su Ticino: Minaccia competitività settori farmaceutico, med-tech e meccanica di precisione. - Posizione Svizzera: Continua negoziati per accordo legalmente vincolante. - Delegazione EFTA/EU: Visita USA per rafforzare relazioni economiche bilaterali. - Settori chiave USA: Farmaceutica e medical technology in Indiana. - Impatto lavoratori: Rischio medio termine su investimenti e assunzioni. - Consiglio pratico: Monitorare situazioni, dialogare con datori di lavoro, pianificare finanziariamente. - Fonte: Tio.ch, 21 febbraio 2026. The echo of Donald Trump's statements on introducing a 10% global tariff on imports into the United States has reached Bern loud and clear, and in turn, is causing considerable concern for Ticino's economic fabric. The Federal Council immediately tasked the competent offices, primarily the Federal Department of Economic Affairs (DEFR), with urgently analyzing the potential consequences of such a protectionist measure. For now, no predictions are leaking from the Federal Palace about the actual rate that could affect Swiss goods. The official line, expressed by the President of the Confederation Guy Parmelin, is one of caution: the statements are acknowledged, but not commented on. The DEFR confirmed to Keystone-ATS that the repercussions are being «analyzed continuously and as quickly as possible». This move by Washington, if confirmed, would strike at the heart...

Dettagli operativi

Despite the political storm brewing overseas, Bern is staying the course. The DEFR has assured that the Swiss position in the ongoing trade talks with Washington will not change. The primary objective remains the signing of a legally binding agreement that offers Swiss companies the greatest possible legal certainty. A clear statement of intent that almost sounds like a warning. > «Switzerland takes into account the ongoing developments within the negotiations and aims for a result that achieves this objective, regardless of legal and political developments in the United States,» the Department specified. Internal pressures are not lacking. Left-wing politicians have called for a halt to the negotiations, but the federal government recalled that this eventuality had already been considered during consultations with parliamentary committees and the Cantons, with no one requesting a stop. The State Secretariat for Economic Affairs (SECO) is therefore continuing with its mandate. ## Diplomatic mission to the USA In almost perfect timing, an EFTA/EU parliamentary delegation will travel to the United States this week for a working visit. The agenda includes meetings with members of Congress in Washington D.C. and a stop in Indianapolis. The choice of Indiana is not random: it is a strategic hub for the pharmaceutical and medical technology industries, key sectors for Ticino's economy and for Switzerland as a whole. A trip that assumes strategic importance to strengthen bilateral economic relations in a time of great uncertainty.

Punti chiave

What does all this mean for the thousands of cross-border workers who cross the Brogeda, Gaggiolo, or Ponte Tresa borders every day? Although a direct and immediate impact on salary is unlikely, the uncertainty generated by this potential trade war should not be underestimated. A slowdown in exports for Ticino companies could translate, in the medium term, into a contraction of investments, a hiring freeze, or, in the worst cases, a review of staffing levels. The health of the Swiss economy, and particularly that of Ticino, is directly proportional to job stability for those living across the border. 💡 Practical advice in uncertain times: - Monitor the situation: Stay informed about the evolution of negotiations between Switzerland and the USA. - Dialogue at your company: Understand the weight of the American market for your employer. - Financial planning: Having a clear picture of your income and expenses becomes even more important. In such a volatile global economic context, having full control of your finances is the first step to face any eventuality. Understanding exactly what your net salary in euros amounts to, after taxes and contributions, is crucial. Frequently Asked Questions
What effect would a 10% duty on imports into the United States on the Ticino economy?
A 10% duty would pose a serious threat to the competitiveness of Ticino's products on the American market, being able to scramble the tables and have cascading effects on the entire production chain and employment.
What impact could have a 10% duty on Ticino pharmaceutical products exported to the United States?
A 10% duty could increase costs for Ticino pharmaceutical companies of approximately CHF 3-5 million per year, reducing their competitiveness in the US market, with potential employment repercussions (source: sector data 2022).
What sectors in Ticino could be more affected by a 10% duty on imports in the United States?
The most affected sectors would be pharmaceutical, med-tech and precision mechanics, as they are strongly export oriented towards the US market. A 10% duty would increase costs for Ticino companies, compromising the competitiveness of their products and potentially leading to a reduction in exports.
How could a 10% duty influence employment in Ticino?
A 10% duty could reduce the competitiveness of Ticino exports, leading to a decrease in orders and investments. This could result in a contraction of recruitment or, in worst cases, in a revision of the staff, negatively affecting employment, especially for frontier workers who depend on companies with a strong trade link with the United States.
What is the goal of the AELS/EU diplomatic mission in the United States?
The aim of the AELS/EU diplomatic mission in the United States is to strengthen bilateral economic relations, particularly in the pharmaceutical and med-tech sectors, at a time of great trade uncertainty. The delegation will hold meetings with members of Congress in Washington D.C. and Indianapolis, a strategic pole for these industries, to try to mitigate the impact of any duties and promote economic stability.
