In brief - Accident on A8 between Castellanza and Busto Arsizio - Up to 4 km of traffic towards Varese - Two injured rescued by 118 ## Key facts - What: Road accident - When: May 4, 2026, 09:30 - Where: A8 motorway, between Castellanza and Busto Arsizio - Who: Two men aged 25 and 35 - Impact: Up to 4 km of traffic A difficult morning for traffic along the A8 Milan-Varese motorway due to an accident that occurred between Castellanza and Busto Arsizio. The alarm was raised around 09:30 on Monday, May 4, involving two people and disrupting traffic heading north. The incident occurred on the carriageway leading towards Varese. According to initial information, two men were involved, aged 25 and 35. The rescuers from the Legnano Red Cross and the Ats Insubria medical team quickly intervened to provide first aid. To ensure the safety of the area and the removal of the vehicles, the Varese Fire Department was also alerted. The coordination of the rescue operations was managed by Soreu Laghi, which monitored the mission from the first moments after the emergency call. The accident caused significant repercussions on motorway traffic. By around 09:50, a queue of about 4 kilometers had formed starting from kilometer 20, with estimated travel times of over half an hour to pass the critical section. The situation remained complex for much of the morning. Around 10:10, reports still indicated heavy slowdowns and queues exiting Castellanza for those coming from Milan, due both to the lane narrowing for the surveys and the heavy traffic that usually characterizes the artery at these times. ### Impact on Cross-Border Workers The accident had a significant impact on cross-border workers who use the A8 motorway to reach their workplace in Switzerland.
