Mendrisio's road network under pressure: urgent action needed

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 1 May 2026

Four PLR municipal councilors demand more urgent action for Mendrisio's road network, criticizing the lack of cantonal and federal funds.

Contesto

This is according to four PLR municipal councilors, who in a parliamentary question asked the Executive for information on the municipal strategy regarding roadworks. The first signatory, Giovanni Poloni, together with Filippo Pfister, Marco Tela and Alberto Meroni, pointed out that Mendrisio receives less funding than it needs from the Canton and the Confederation, considering the transit traffic that uses and wears out its road network every day. Among the critical roads reported are via Turconi, via Giuseppe Motta, via Agostino Maspoli, via ai Grotti, via Carlo Maderno and the link between Besazio and Tremona. According to the questioners, these roads have critical issues related to safety and the degradation of the road surface. Dettagli operativi

Implications for cross-border workers and residents The urgent interventions required for the road network of Mendrisio will have a significant impact on cross-border workers and residents. The critical roads identified, such as via Turconi and via Maspoli, are fundamental arteries for local and cross-border traffic. According to 2023 data, via Turconi records an average daily traffic of 12,000 vehicles, while via Maspoli accounts for approximately 8,500. The lack of adequate maintenance can lead to delays and inconveniences for those who daily use these routes to commute to work or for other activities. For example, an average delay of 15 minutes per vehicle, multiplied by the number of users, can translate into an estimated economic cost of approximately 2.5 million francs per year for the community. ### Comparison with the previous situation In a previous inquiry by the Centre in 2024, the Municipality had explained that intervention priorities are defined through mathematical formulas. However, the final decision rests with the responsible authorities of the Roads Sector. This approach, although structured, may not be sufficiently responsive to the immediate needs of the community. The use of the 'Pavement Management System' program since 2008 has allowed for the planning of interventions based on costs and urgency, but the current situation requires faster and more targeted action. For example, the Municipality of Chiasso has adopted a predictive maintenance system that has reduced intervention times by 30%, demonstrating the effectiveness of a more dynamic approach. ### Future scenarios If the requested interventions are not implemented promptly, the situation could worsen further, with an increase in road safety risks and further degradation of infrastructure....

It is important to stay updated on the timelines and any road closures to plan your movements effectively. For example, the Municipality of Mendrisio recently published a detailed calendar of the interventions planned for 2024, with an estimated 15 million francs allocated for the extraordinary maintenance of the infrastructure. Additionally, the Canton of Ticino has launched a pilot project for real-time traffic management, which involves the installation of 50 new sensors along the main roads. ### Procedure for reporting issues If specific issues are encountered on the roads of Mendrisio, they can be reported directly to the Roads Department of the Municipality. Reports can be submitted through the Municipality's website or by contacting the competent office directly. The information collected will be useful for planning future interventions. For example, the Municipality of Chiasso has implemented an online reporting system that has allowed reducing the intervention times by 30% throughout 2023. Which are the streets in Mendrisio that require urgent interventions?
The streets that require urgent interventions are via Turconi, via Giuseppe Motta, via Agostino Maspoli, via ai Grotti, via Carlo Maderno, and the connection between Besazio and Tremona. These roads present critical issues related to safety and the degradation of the road surface.
How are the priorities for road interventions in Mendrisio defined?
The priorities for road interventions in Mendrisio are defined using mathematical formulas with the 'Pavement Management System' program. However, the final decision is made by the officials of the Roads Department of the Municipality.
