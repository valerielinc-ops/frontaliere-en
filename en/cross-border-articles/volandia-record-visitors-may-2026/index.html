Volandia: record visitors during May long weekend (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 6 May 2026

Over 2,200 visitors in three days, 400 helicopter flight baptisms. The flight museum in Somma Lombardo confirms its success

Contesto

In brief - Volandia surpasses 2,200 visitors during the May bank holiday - 400 helicopter flight baptisms with Eliossola - The museum attracts families and curious young people ## Key facts - What: Record number of visitors at Volandia - When: May bank holiday 2026 - Where: Somma Lombardo, near Malpensa - Who: Volandia, flight museum - Amount: Over 2,200 visitors in three days - Activity: Helicopter flight baptisms - Collaboration: With Eliossola - Passengers: 400 people - Route: Panoramic view of Malpensa and the Ticino area The early May bank holiday brought a record influx to Volandia, the Park and Flight Museum located in the historic Caproni workshops near Malpensa Airport. In just three days, over 2,200 visitors passed through the gates of the facility, a number that testifies to the growing appeal of the museum. The success was driven by the initiative of helicopter flight baptisms, organized in collaboration with Eliossola. More than 400 people had the opportunity to experience the thrill of vertical flight, admiring from a privileged perspective the runways of Malpensa and the natural area of Ticino. This type of experience, as emphasized by the museum, is particularly appreciated not only by aviation enthusiasts but also by those who want to overcome their fear of flying. ### A museum for all ages The attendance figures confirm Volandia's ability to attract a diverse audience, increasingly composed of families with children and curious young people. The formula that combines the preservation of aeronautical culture with experiential entertainment proves to be successful. The management of Volandia expressed great satisfaction with these numbers, emphasizing how the museum has become a reference point for quality leisure time. The success of the May weekend...

The museum is located in Somma Lombardo, just a few kilometers from the Italian-Swiss border. To reach the museum, you can use your car or public transportation. By car, you can take the A8 highway towards Malpensa and exit at Somma Lombardo. Alternatively, you can take the train to Somma Lombardo station and then continue on foot or by local bus. ### Opening hours and tickets Volandia is open every day, with hours varying depending on the season.
