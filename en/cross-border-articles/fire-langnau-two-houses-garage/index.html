Fire in Langnau: two houses and a garage destroyed (cross-border guide)

A fire destroyed two houses and a garage in Bärau, in the municipality of Langnau (BE). No injuries, but a large rescue operation.

In brief - Fire in Langnau destroys two houses and a garage - No injuries, but extensive rescue operation - Column of smoke visible from afar ## Key facts - What: Fire that destroyed two houses and a garage - When: 3 May 2026, shortly after midnight - Where: Bärau, municipality of Langnau (BE), Switzerland - Who: Bernese cantonal police, several ambulances, Care Team, representatives of the municipality and the prefecture - Amount: Not specified A fire that broke out this morning in Bärau, in the municipality of Langnau (BE), completely destroyed two houses and a garage. According to initial reports, there were no injuries, but the flames developed an intense column of smoke visible from afar. The blaze triggered a extensive rescue operation, as confirmed by the Bernese cantonal police to the Keystone-ATS agency. During the night, the Alertswiss application had warned the population about the presence of thick smoke and unpleasant odors. According to the police, the alarm was triggered shortly after midnight. Upon their arrival, the officers found the two houses and the garage engulfed in flames. Based on current data, all residents have been rescued. Specialists from the Cantonal Police, several ambulances, a Care Team, representatives of the municipality and the competent prefecture intervened at the scene, as added by the police spokesperson. Road closures were arranged and rail traffic in the region also experienced disruptions. The population was invited to close doors and windows and to turn off ventilation and air conditioning systems. Frequently Asked Questions
What safety measures should be adopted in case of a fire?
In case of a fire, it is essential to follow the instructions of local and national authorities. Having an emergency plan, an emergency kit, and participating in regular drills can make a difference. Make sure you have a meeting point, a list of important contacts, and be ready to evacuate quickly if necessary.
How can I prepare for potential emergencies?
To prepare for potential emergencies, it is important to have a family emergency plan, an emergency kit, and participate in regular drills. Follow the instructions of local and national authorities and keep your emergency contacts updated. Make sure you have water, non-perishable food, a flashlight, a battery-powered radio, and a first aid kit.
What steps should be followed in case of a fire alarm?
In case of a fire alarm, follow the instructions of local and national authorities. Close doors and windows, turn off ventilation and air conditioning systems, and follow the instructions of emergency services. Avoid the affected area and stay updated through alert systems like Alertswiss.
