Two people were seriously injured and transported to the hospital with the emergency helicopter. The Soreu dei Laghi central dispatch sent numerous emergency vehicles, including three ambulances, a medical car, and two Areu helicopters, one from Como Villa Guardia and one from Sondrio. Frequently Asked Questions
What are the most common causes of road accidents on the Via Regina?
The Via Regina is one of the busiest roads in Ticino, with over 10,000 vehicles crossing it every day. The average speed of cars along this road is around 60 km/h, but in case of an emergency, law enforcement can reduce the speed to 30 km/h to ensure the safety of travelers.
What is the average response time of Areu helicopters in the event of an emergency on the Via Regina?
According to the article, the response time is about 10 minutes.
What are the emergency procedures for Italian cross-border commuters involved in an accident on the Via Regina with serious injuries?
In the event of an accident with serious injuries on the Via Regina, cross-border commuters must immediately call 118 (Italy) or 144 (Switzerland). The Soreu dei Laghi center coordinates rescue operations between Ticino and Lombardy, sending ambulances, medical cars and Areu helicopters (response time: 10-15 minutes). Patients are transported to the nearest hospitals, such as Bellinzona or Lugano.
How is health insurance coverage managed for an Italian cross-border commuter involved in a road accident in Switzerland?
Italian cross-border commuters with a European Health Insurance Card (TSE) are entitled to emergency treatment in Switzerland at no direct cost, thanks to the EU-Switzerland agreement. For subsequent treatment, it is advisable to take out supplementary health insurance that covers costs not reimbursed by the Swiss system (e.g. reimbursement of hospital co-payments).
What are the speed limits and specific rules for heavy vehicles on the Via Regina between Italy and Switzerland?
On the Via Regina, heavy vehicles over 3.5 tonnes must comply with a speed limit of 50 km/h in Ticino and 60 km/h in Lombardy. In an emergency, law enforcement can reduce the speed to 30 km/h to ensure safety. The use of seat belts and respect for safety distances is mandatory.
