In brief - Bper grows and attracts competitors' attention - Market capitalization is the real defense - Goal: 3-4 large banking groups in Italy ## Key facts - What: Wolf metaphor used by Unipol - When: Speech at the Merger & Acquisition Summit 2026 - Where: Organized by Il Sole 24 Ore - Who: Gianni Franco Papa, CEO of Bper - Amount: 134 banking intermediaries in Italy by the end of 2024 Amidst the Italian banking risk game, the solidity of accounts and growth become the first line of defense against potential external assaults. This is the message from Bper Banca, through its CEO Gianni Franco Papa, who spoke at the 'Merger & Acquisition Summit 2026' organized by Il Sole 24 Ore. The metaphor of the 'wolves,' used by the main shareholder Unipol, was recalled by Papa to emphasize that when one becomes appealing to competitors, the real protection is not traditional defense but comes from market capitalization. The more a bank performs, the more costly and complex any attempt at a takeover becomes. ### Consolidation context Italy, historically fragmented, is evolving towards a European model based on a few large groups. According to Papa, the goal is clear: the country needs to reach three or four large national players capable of supporting the economy and competing internationally. Currently, there are two in Italy: Intesa Sanpaolo and UniCredit. Over the last 30 years, the number of banks has decreased in many countries, while the average size of institutions has increased and large diversified groups have emerged.
