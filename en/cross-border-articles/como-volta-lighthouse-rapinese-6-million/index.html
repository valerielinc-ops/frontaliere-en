Como mayor accuses Rome over Volta funds

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 1 May 2026

The mayor of Como accuses Rome of allocating 6 million for a sitcom on Volta, nothing for monuments. Rebuttal from the National Committee.

Contesto

In brief - Rapinese accuses Rome of allocating 6 million for a sitcom on Volta - Zero funds for Como monuments - National Committee's reply: 'Statements are false' ## Key facts - What: Mayor Rapinese's accusations about funds for Volta - When: April 27, 2026 - Where: Como City Council - Who: Mayor Alessandro Rapinese, National Committee for Voltian Celebrations - Amount: 6 million euros for a sitcom, zero for Como monuments During the city council meeting on Monday, April 27, the leader of Fratelli d'Italia Lorenzo Cantalubppi and his colleague Tony Tufano presented a motion regarding the Voltian Lighthouse and the enhancement of the city's cultural heritage. Cantalubppi explained that the lighthouse is an important monument for the area, dedicated to Alessandro Volta, whose bicentennial of death will be in 2027. The motion asked the mayor and the council to make the lighthouse accessible again, as its light has been recently turned on, but the structure is no longer open to visitors. Furthermore, it requested to start necessary interventions for safety and reopening, finding the required resources in the budget and presenting a precise timeline for the reopening. Mayor Alessandro Rapinese replied by asking for clarifications on the investments for the Voltian monuments. He stated that the National Committee had allocated 6 million euros for a sitcom on Alessandro Volta's life, but zero for the monuments of Como. Rapinese asked if any news had come from the Undersecretary to the Presidency of the Council, Alessio Butti, regarding the investments and what they had in mind to honor the Voltian monuments.
