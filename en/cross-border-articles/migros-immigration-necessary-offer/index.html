Migros: Immigration vital for supply guarantee

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 2 May 2026

The CEO of Migros emphasizes the importance of immigration for the food and retail sectors in Switzerland.

Contesto

In brief - Migros relies on immigration to ensure supply - 60% of Micarna are foreign citizens - 1,000 vacant positions in Ticino ## Key facts - What: Need for immigration in the food sector - When: Interview published on May 2, 2026 - Where: Switzerland, with a focus on Ticino - Who: Mario Irminger, CEO Migros - Amount: 60% of Micarna are foreigners, 90% in processing departments The president of the board of directors of the Migros Federation of Cooperatives, Mario Irminger, stated in an interview with 'Schweiz am Wochenende' that the food industry and retail trade particularly depend on immigration. Irminger opposed the SVP initiative 'No to a Switzerland of 10 million!', stating that 'we need a certain level of immigration, even just to guarantee the current level of performance in retail trade'. ### Impact on the labor market In the coming years, many employees will retire, further increasing the need for personnel. Currently, around 60% of Micarna, the Migros Industrie company specializing in meat production, are foreign citizens, and in the processing departments, the percentage even reaches 90%. 'We already depend on them today, and this will also be the case to fill the gaps that will arise in the future,' Irminger added. ### Consequences for cross-border workers Without immigration, there is a high probability that service and supply will decrease, for example with shorter opening hours, fewer branches, or a more limited selection. Currently, the orange giant has around 1,000 vacant positions that are difficult to fill, with a particular need for collaborators in bakeries and in the meat and dairy processing sectors. ### Future scenario A maximum limit on the population, as requested by the SVP initiative, would create new problems according to Irminge...

Which sectors are experiencing the greatest shortage of personnel?
According to Mario Irminger's interview, the sectors with the greatest shortage of personnel are bakeries and meat and dairy processing departments.
What documents are required to work in Ticino?
To work in Ticino, cross-border workers need a G permit and must be registered with the AHV. Additionally, it's important to prepare the curriculum vitae and the necessary references for the application.
What assistance services are provided by the Canton of Ticino?
The Canton of Ticino offers assistance services for cross-border workers to facilitate the application and integration process. These services may include support in job searching, legal assistance, and tax-related advice.
