The La Rondine Literary Award is gearing up for its seventh edition, establishing itself as a significant event for youth creativity and cultural collaboration between Italy and Canton Ticino. After the submission deadline closed on February 15, the organizers collected an impressive 231 letters from students across 18 different schools, highlighting the growing interest in this initiative that unites territories and communities. Dettagli operativi

The organizational structure of the La Rondine Award 2026 involves meticulous work spanning over three months: since the call closed on February 15, the technical and artistic juries have been evaluating the 231 submitted works, divided into the traditional categories Rondinotti (children), Rondinelle (youngsters), and Rondini (teenagers). Each category corresponds to specific age groups, allowing a fair and targeted selection based on originality, style, and content criteria. The evaluation process is thorough and benefits from experts in the literary and cultural fields from both Italy and Canton Ticino, particularly from institutions such as SUPSI and USI, which often support cross-border cultural initiatives. Alongside the evaluation, the organization is preparing the official anthology, a volume that will collect all the selected letters and serve as an important reference document for the 2026 edition. This anthology will be distributed in schools and libraries across both territories, including Ticino municipalities directly involved like Mendrisio and Bellinzona, helping to spread literary culture and sensitivity towards youth perspectives. No specific cash prizes or financial contributions have been announced, but the symbolic and cultural value of the initiative is significant, especially in a border area where dialogue and cooperation are essential for the social and economic fabric. The final awards event, scheduled for May 2026, promises to be a grand celebration and recognition moment, with participation from local authorities, school representatives, cultural community members, and of course the protagonists—the winning students.
