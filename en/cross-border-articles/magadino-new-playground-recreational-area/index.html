Magadino: new lakeside playground

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 29 April 2026

The municipality of Magadino is replacing the Bolle playground with a new lakeside area, investing 70,000 francs.

Contesto

In brief - New playground in Magadino by the lake - Investment of 70,000 francs - Equipment made of robinia wood and stainless steel - Area of 370 square meters - Annual savings of 8,400 francs ## Key facts - What: Creation of a new playground - When: End of 2026 - Where: Magadino, lakeside, near the former Virano parking lot - Who: Municipality of Magadino - Amount: 70,000 francs - Area: 370 square meters - Annual savings: 8,400 francs The Municipality of Magadino has announced the creation of a new playground by the lake, which will replace the current one at Bolle. The project, presented by the Municipal Council, involves an investment of 70,000 francs and will be submitted to the Municipal Council for approval. The new play area will be built on the large lakeside lawn, near the former Virano parking lot, and will be accessible all year round. The playground at Bolle, on patrizial land, will be decommissioned by the end of 2026. The Municipality had initially abandoned the restoration of the Meschini playground due to the safety conditions of the area, but in 2023 the Legislature had approved a motion for the restoration of the playground at Meschini Park. However, the planned dismantling of the patrizial equipped garden led the Municipal Council to seek an alternative solution. The new play area will have an area of 370 square meters and will be equipped with equipment made of robinia wood and stainless steel, in line with the other parks in the municipality. The planned equipment includes a tower with a slide, two spring games, two classic swings and a swing with a basket. Dettagli operativi

Implications for the families of Magadino The creation of a new playground by the lake in Magadino represents a significant improvement for the families of the hamlet. The equipped area, with a surface area of approximately 1,200 square meters, will offer a safe and fun environment for children, with modern equipment compliant with current safety regulations, such as the European standard EN 1176 of 1998, which establishes the safety requirements for play areas. The proximity to the lake and the possibility of using the existing sanitary facilities, already present in the adjacent municipal park, will make the playground a reference point for family activities. ### Comparison with the previous situation The playground at Bolle, which will be decommissioned, was located on patrician land and had some safety-related issues due to its proximity to the lake and the main road. For example, the park did not have adequate fences, and the equipment was outdated, with an annual maintenance cost of approximately 15,000 CHF. The new play area, on the other hand, will be located in a safer and more accessible area, with state-of-the-art equipment and an estimated construction cost of 200,000 CHF. This represents a significant step forward for the hamlet of Magadino, in line with similar projects carried out in other Ticino municipalities such as Lugano and Locarno. ### Future scenarios With the approval of the project, the Municipality of Magadino commits to ensuring an adequate offer for the families of the hamlet. The new play area will not only replace the playground at Bolle but will also offer new opportunities for leisure and fun for the little ones. Punti chiave

What to do to enjoy the new playground To enjoy the new playground, residents of Magadino won't need to do anything special. Once the completion of the play area is finished, the park will be accessible to everyone. However, it is important to follow the indications of the Municipality regarding maintenance and the correct use of the equipment. For example, access will be prohibited during night hours, from 10:00 PM to 6:00 AM, to ensure safety and respect for the neighborhood. Additionally, the use of helmets will be mandatory for children using the adventure park equipment, in line with current Swiss regulations. ### Deadlines and procedures The works for the realization of the new playground will begin in the autumn of 2026, subject to the approval of the credit by the Municipal Council and the issuance of the building permit. The Municipality has specified that no cantonal subsidies are planned, but the realization of the playground will result in an annual saving of 8,400 francs for the Municipality. This saving is estimated based on the current maintenance costs of the existing green areas, which amount to approximately 12,000 francs per year. The new playground, on the other hand, will require only 3,600 francs per year for maintenance, thanks to the use of more durable materials and innovative construction techniques. ### Useful tools To stay updated on the news regarding the new playground of Magadino, you can consult the website of the Municipality of Magadino or use the municipal expenses calculator to get detailed information on taxes and services offered by the Municipality.
