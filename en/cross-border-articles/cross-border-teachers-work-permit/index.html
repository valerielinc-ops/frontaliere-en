Dettagli operativi

The procedure for obtaining a work permit for cross-border workers in the education sector in Ticino is well-defined and subject to strict regulations. According to the Federal Act on Foreign Nationals and Integration (FNAI), permit applications must be submitted no later than three months before the start of employment. Failure to meet this deadline triggers a contravention procedure, which may result in financial penalties. In the specific case of the Canton of Ticino, the Migration Office is the authority responsible for monitoring and enforcing these rules. A concrete example of irregularity was recorded during the 2025/26 school year when a cross-border teacher hired at a middle school in the Mendrisiotto submitted their permit application more than a month late. Following this infraction, the teacher was fined 500 Swiss francs. According to the Migration Office, this was the only case of non-compliance with the procedure during that school year. In Lugano, Locarno, and Bellinzona, no similar irregularities were reported during the same period. School administrators, both at the cantonal and municipal levels, are responsible for ensuring that cross-border teachers have a valid work permit before signing their contracts. To facilitate compliance with procedures, the Ticino Government has issued an operational checklist, which includes the following key points: - Submit the permit application within 90 days of starting work. - Attach a copy of the signed employment contract. - Provide proof of stable residence in a cross-border municipality (e.g., Como or Varese). The current regulations, updated in January 2023, stipulate that without a valid permit, the contract cannot take effect. The Canton of Ticino, which hosts over 70,000 cross-border workers, requires careful management of bureaucratic procedures to ensure compliance with current regulations. For example, teachers wishing to work in municipalities such as Lugano, Mendrisio, or Bellinzona must submit their work permit application (G permit for cross-border workers) no later than 8 days after starting their activity. Frequently Asked Questions
How does the procedure for hiring frontier teachers in Ticino work?
Frontier teachers can start working immediately after submitting the application for work permit, without having to wait for formal release. This procedure is in accordance with Article 7 of Annex I to the Agreement on the free movement of persons, in force since June 2002.
What is the rate of compliance with the rules for frontier teachers in Ticino?
The compliance rate is higher than 99% as indicated by the data of the year analysed.
What are the health insurance requirements for frontier teachers in Ticino?
Frontier teachers in Ticino must ensure health insurance in accordance with Swiss requirements. This means having a cover that respects Swiss regulations, thus ensuring proper medical care during your working stay in Ticino. Insurance is a fundamental requirement for obtaining a work permit.
How many Ticino schools have confirmed compliance with recruitment procedures for frontier teachers?
In Canton Ticino, 53 municipalities confirmed compliance with recruitment procedures for frontier teachers. This includes schools in municipalities such as Mendrisio, Chiasso and Lugano, where controls have evidenced the absence of irregularities in recruitment practices.
What is the role of school leaders in the process of recruiting frontier teachers in Ticino?
School managers, both of the cantonal and communal schools, have the task of checking in advance that frontier teachers have a valid work permit before signing the employment contract. This control is essential to ensure that all regulations are respected, and to prevent any irregularities in the recruitment process.
