Hantavirus can cause various syndromes, including hemorrhagic fever with renal syndrome (HFRS), epidemic nephropathy (NE), and hantavirus pulmonary syndrome (HCPS). ### Syndromes Related to Hantavirus - Hemorrhagic fever with renal syndrome (HFRS): Common in Europe and Asia, this syndrome can damage blood vessels, increasing vascular permeability and leading to hypotension, hemorrhagic manifestations, and shock. - Epidemic nephropathy (NE): A mild form of HFRS observed in Europe. Symptoms may include fever, headache, muscle pain, and kidney problems. - Hantavirus pulmonary syndrome (HCPS): Common in the Americas, this syndrome can cause severe respiratory problems and requires immediate treatment. ### Prevention Measures for Cross-Border Workers Cross-border workers in high-risk sectors, such as tourism and navigation, must be particularly cautious. It is important to follow the guidelines of local and international health authorities to prevent infection.
