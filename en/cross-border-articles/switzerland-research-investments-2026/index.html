Contesto

By 2025, Swiss groups had one of the highest research and development investment rates in the world, ranking third in research intensity, behind only the Netherlands and the United States. For example, Switzerland has been able to maintain its position as the world leader in the watch industry thanks to continuous innovation and research in the sector. Furthermore, investments in research and development contribute to the creation of new jobs and the growth of the Swiss economy. According to the Federal Department of Economic Affairs, Education and Research (DEFR), investments in research and development created around 100,000 jobs in Switzerland in 2019. Ticino, in particular, is a commune that has benefited from investments in research and development. In 2019, the canton received CHF 120 million in federal funding for research and development projects, with a particular focus on digital innovation and sustainability. This has led to the creation of new jobs and the growth of the local economy. However, Switzerland's competitiveness in terms of business investments in research could be threatened by recent regulations. For example, the Federal Act on the Promotion of Research and Development (R&D) of 2018 introduced new restrictions on federal funding for research and development, limiting financial support for large companies.
