Ticino Police: Zali confirms, project not shelved

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 30 April 2026

State Councillor Claudio Zali clarifies that the Ticino Police project has not been discarded, but remains one of the possible solutions.

Contesto

In brief - Ticino Police project not discarded - Zali: remains a possible solution - Municipalities critical of the project ## Key facts - What: Ticino Police project - When: Consultation concluded this morning - Where: Canton Ticino - Who: State Council and Municipalities - Amount: Not specified Councilor of State Claudio Zali clarified that the Ticino Police project has not been discarded or shelved. In an interview with Ticinonews, Zali explained that the work has been done and now we are entering the decision-making phase. The project, which represents a softer alternative to the Single Police dossier, remains one of the possible solutions for the future of the Police in Ticino. Together with maintaining the current situation or further centralization efforts towards a Single Police, the Ticino Police project remains on the table. The final decision will be up to the Grand Council, which will eventually have to adopt the legal amendments proposed by the State Council. The project was advanced when the political responsibility of the Police was held by Norman Gobbi. Gobbi criticized the Municipalities for not wanting to be actively involved in a necessary reform process to manage an essential service like public security, but also to contain costs. ### Municipalities' Reactions The reactions of the Municipalities to the project have not been enthusiastic. Karin Valenzano Rossi, head of the Security and Urban Spaces Department of Lugano, welcomed the decision of the State Council to stop the project, defining it as 'born badly, continued worse and ended in a document that only received many criticisms'. Analysis of Implications The Ticino Police project, although not discarded, has sparked various reactions among the municipalities. The State Council's decision to conclude the project phase without proceeding with the immediate development of the project has raised questions about the future directions of police reform in Ticino. ### Current Scenario Currently, the police in Ticino consists of a cantonal police force and municipal police departments. The Ticino Police project aims to optimize tasks and resources between these two structures. However, the municipalities have shown resistance to this project, preferring to maintain the current situation or explore other solutions. ### Possible Future Developments Zali indicated that there are three possible solutions for the future of the police in Ticino: maintaining the current situation, proceeding with further centralization towards a single police force, or adopting the Ticino Police project. The final decision will be up to the Grand Council, which will have to evaluate the proposals of the State Council. ### Implications for Cross-Border Workers Cross-border workers who work in Ticino may be affected by any changes in the police structure. Greater centralization could lead to greater efficiency and coordination, but also to possible changes in procedures and services offered. It is important that cross-border workers stay informed about any developments and prepare for any changes in regulations and security services. ### Comparisons with Other Regions In other Swiss regions, such as the Canton of Zurich, single police models have been successfully implemented.
