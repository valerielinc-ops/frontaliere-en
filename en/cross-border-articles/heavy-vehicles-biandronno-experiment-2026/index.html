Heavy traffic, Biandronno starts trial

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 1 May 2026

Mayor Massimo Porotti announces the start of the trial to limit heavy traffic in Medio Verbano

Contesto

In brief - Trial to limit heavy traffic in Biandronno - Mayor Porotti: 'Important step after years of work' - Six municipalities in the Medio Verbano involved ## Key facts - What: Trial to limit heavy traffic - When: Start of trial not yet specified - Where: Biandronno and five other municipalities in the Medio Verbano - Who: Municipal administration of Biandronno and supra-municipal entities - Amount: Not yet specified The mayor of Biandronno, Massimo Porotti, announced the start of a trial to limit heavy traffic in the Medio Verbano area. The decision follows years of work and institutional dialogue to address an issue that has caused significant inconvenience to the citizens of Biandronno and nearby Bardello. 'Since my appointment in 2019,' declares Mayor Porotti, 'I have considered it a priority to concretely address the problem of heavy traffic that weighs on our territory. This is a complex issue, felt for years by citizens, and which has required constant work of dialogue and institutional collaboration.' The path taken has even deeper roots: as early as 2014, the then mayor of Bardello, Egidio Calvi, had started work on the subject, subsequently continued by Luciano Puggioni. Over the years, numerous technical tables have been held in the Province, involving the competent authorities and the administrations concerned, to identify a shared and effective solution, capable of reconciling the needs of traffic with the protection of the quality of life of residents. ### Implications for cross-border workers The trial launched in Biandronno could also have repercussions for cross-border workers who pass through the Medio Verbano area daily. Practical Analysis: Implications for Cross-Border Workers The experiment launched in Biandronno to limit heavy traffic could have several implications for cross-border workers who daily cross the Medio Verbano territory. In particular, traffic restrictions could influence travel times and preferred routes for those traveling between Italy and Switzerland. It is important to monitor the developments of the experiment to better understand how this initiative could impact cross-border mobility. ### Comparison with the Previous Situation Before the start of this experiment, heavy traffic was one of the main critical issues for the residents of Biandronno and Bardello. The new initiative aims to improve the quality of life for citizens, reducing the discomfort related to the passage of trucks. The experiment represents a concrete step towards more efficient traffic management, in line with the needs of the local communities. ### Possible Scenarios Among the possible scenarios, it is hypothesized that the limitation of heavy traffic could lead to a reduction in travel times for light vehicles, improving traffic flow. However, it is also possible that some alternative routes become more congested, requiring greater attention in planning movements. It is essential that cross-border workers are informed promptly of any changes in traffic to best adapt their movements. ### Implications for Cross-Border Traffic The experiment could also have repercussions on cross-border traffic, particularly for cross-border workers who use the roads of Medio Verbano to reach their workplaces in Switzerland.
