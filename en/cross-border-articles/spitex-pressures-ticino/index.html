The new tariff, of 50 cents per five minutes of assistance, applies to recipients of home care services, excluding those receiving supplementary AVS-AI benefits. Approved by the Ticino Parliament in the 2026 budget (deliberated in November 2024) and ratified by the Council of State in January 2025, this measure aims to curb cost increases and make citizens more responsible for the use of public and private resources. > Concrete example: a recipient receiving 2 hours of assistance daily (120 minutes) will pay about 2.00 CHF per day, or roughly 60 CHF per month, based on an active daily routine. This amount could pose a challenge for some families, especially in municipalities like Lugano or Bellinzona, where home care is highly requested. The process involves, once a care request is activated, the patient receiving a detailed monthly invoice, with the total amount calculated based on hours of assistance provided. Payment responsibility also extends to involved family members, reinforcing the idea of shared accountability. > The regulation specifies that beneficiaries of urgent services or assistance modes included in the individual care plan will be exempt from this tariff, to avoid penalizing emergency or critical situations. This exception aims to protect the most vulnerable groups and ensure prompt intervention without additional costs. > ⚠️ The main challenge for municipalities and Spitex organizations will be balancing service quality with financial sustainability, preventing user contributions from becoming overly burdensome. For example, in Lugano and Bellinzona, the tariffs for one hour of Spitex assistance increased from about 35 to 45 CHF, with even larger increases for specialized services.
