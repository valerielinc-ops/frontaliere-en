Construction: New Ticino collective labor agreement signed

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 27 April 2026

The new collective labor agreement for the construction and civil engineering sectors in Ticino has been signed, valid until 2031.

Contesto

In brief - New Ticino construction CCL signed - Valid from May 1, 2026 to 2031 - Less flexibility to protect workers ## Key facts - What: New collective labor agreement for construction and civil engineering - When: Valid from May 1, 2026 to 2031 - Where: Canton of Ticino - Who: Swiss Society of Construction Entrepreneurs, OCST, UNIA - Amount: Not specified The new collective labor agreement (CCL) for construction and civil engineering in Ticino was signed on Monday, completing the national agreement approved earlier this year. The contract, valid from May 1, 2026 until 2031, was signed by the Swiss Society of Construction Entrepreneurs and the trade unions OCST and UNIA. After a long and demanding negotiation, as stated in a press release, the respective bodies approved the agreement, thus allowing the signing of the new contract. Companies and workers will be informed in detail shortly. Compared to the rest of the country, the Ticino contract provides less flexibility in annual planning to protect workers. However, employers will have the possibility to maintain a bank of hours to compensate for days of extreme heat or bad weather. ### Implications for cross-border workers Cross-border workers employed in the construction sector in Ticino will have to adapt to the new provisions of the CCL. The reduced flexibility in annual planning could affect working hours and holidays. However, the possibility to compensate for days of extreme heat or bad weather could offer greater job stability. ### Comparison with the national contract The new Ticino CCL adds to the national contract signed at the end of last year.
