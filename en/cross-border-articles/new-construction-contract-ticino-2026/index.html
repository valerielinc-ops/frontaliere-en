Dettagli operativi

The new cantonal collective agreement for the construction sector in Ticino introduces important innovations that will have a significant impact on workers and companies in the industry. The main changes concern the management of working hours and protection from adverse weather conditions, crucial aspects to ensure safety and sustainability in the construction sector. ### Implications for workers The new rules introduce concrete measures to improve the working conditions of employees. Counting work transfers from the first minute ensures that travel time is considered an integral part of working hours, preventing workers from incurring unpaid additional costs and time. This is particularly important for cross-border workers who commute daily between Italy and Switzerland. The ban on scheduling overtime and the reduction in flexibility compared to the national contract aim to prevent excessive workload, reducing the risk of accidents and improving workers' well-being. These measures are particularly relevant in a context like Ticino, where weather conditions can change rapidly and make working days longer and more dangerous. ### Implications for companies For companies, the new contract offers greater flexibility in managing non-productive days. The introduced system allows compensating for days when work cannot be carried out due to adverse weather conditions or other adverse conditions. This is fundamental to ensuring the economic stability of companies, especially in a sector like construction, where weather conditions can significantly impact productivity. ### Generational turnover and attractiveness of the sector The new contract is also an important step in addressing the problem of generational turnover in the construction sector. Here is a practical guide to navigating the novelties introduced. ### Procedures for workers 1. Counting transfers: Workers must ensure that transfer time is recorded from the first minute. This includes travel time between the place of residence and the construction site. 2. Overtime: It is forbidden to plan overtime. Workers must respect the established working hours and report any violations to the union. 3. Reduced flexibility: Flexibility compared to the national contract is reduced. Workers must be aware of the new rules and the limitations imposed. ### Procedures for companies 1. Frequently Asked Questions
What are the main innovations introduced by the new cantonal collective agreement for construction in Ticino?
The main innovations include counting business trips from the first minute, the ban on planning overtime, and reduced flexibility compared to the national contract. These measures aim to improve the safety and well-being of workers, ensuring more sustainable working conditions.
How can companies compensate for non-productive days due to bad weather?
Companies can use the system introduced by the new contract to compensate for days when work cannot be carried out due to bad weather or other adverse conditions. This system offers greater flexibility and reduces the risk of having to pay workers on non-productive days.
What are the implications of the new contract for generational turnover in the construction sector?
The new contract aims to improve the attractiveness of construction professions by offering safer and more sustainable working conditions. This is crucial to address the growing shortage of skilled workers and ensure a stable future for the Ticino construction sector.
