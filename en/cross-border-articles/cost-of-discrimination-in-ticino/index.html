Dettagli operativi

Discrimination in the labour market has a significant impact on the economy of Ticino and Switzerland. According to estimates, discrimination can cost up to CHF 35 billion to the Swiss economy, with 52% of these cases occurring in the labour market. This data highlights the importance of addressing this issue and promoting integration in the labour market. The Canton of Ticino, with its strategic position on the border with Italy, is a place where cultural diversity is an everyday reality. Ticino businesses must be aware of this diversity and adopt measures to ensure that all employees, regardless of their nationality or origin, are treated fairly and with respect. Discrimination in the labour market can manifest in various ways. For example, foreigners may be subject to wage disparities, with salaries on average 24% lower than those of Swiss nationals. This wage gap is unacceptable and must be addressed to ensure a fair and just workplace. Businesses must adopt hiring and promotion policies based on candidates' skills and qualifications rather than their nationality or origin. This will ensure that the best talents are selected and that businesses can benefit from the diversity of their workforce. Additionally, it is important for businesses to provide an inclusive and respectful workplace environment where all employees feel valued and appreciated. This will not only improve employee well-being but also contribute to a more productive and collaborative work environment. To promote integration in the labour market, businesses can adopt various measures. For example, they can organize training courses on diversity and inclusion to raise awareness among employees on these topics and promote a respectful workplace environment. If you are a foreign worker in Ticino and have experienced discrimination or have doubts about your rights, do not hesitate to contact the Office for the Integration of Foreigners. Frequently Asked Questions
How to deal with discrimination in the world of work in Switzerland?
The cantonal delegate for integration, Michela Trisconi, stresses that discrimination in the world of work is a loss of potential. Companies must ensure that their employees are treated fairly, regardless of their nationality or origin.
How can I check if my salary has been affected by the 24% salary gap for foreigners in Switzerland?
To check, compare your salary with the average salary data for your position and industry in Switzerland (e.g. use statistics from the SFO - Federal Statistical Office). Also consider discussing with your HR department or legal counsel for a personalized analysis.
What are the main strategies to promote the integration of foreign workers in Ticino?
Companies can organize diversity trainings, partner with integration organizations, and adopt skills-based hiring policies, thereby creating more inclusive and respectful workplaces.
