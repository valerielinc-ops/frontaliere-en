Campione is reborn: green light for hiring (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 23 February 2026

The Ministry of the Interior confirms the end of financial distress from January 1, 2026. What this means for the local economy and job opportunities.

Contesto

TL;DR - Campione d'Italia ends financial distress on Jan 1, 2026 - Municipality regains full managerial autonomy - New hires allowed without COSFEL prior authorization - Recovery boosts local economy and cross-border stability ## Key facts - Location: Campione d'Italia - End of distress: January 1, 2026 - Authority: Ministry of the Interior via Prefecture of Como - Main cause: Bankruptcy of the Casino - New power: Hiring staff without COSFEL authorization - Source date: February 24, 2026 This news marks a historic turning point for the Italian exclave in Ticino territory. In an official communication, the Municipality of Campione d'Italia announced the end of its state of financial distress starting from January 1, 2026. The confirmation came directly from the Ministry of the Interior, via the Prefecture of Como, putting a definitive end to one of the darkest periods in the town's recent history, triggered by the bankruptcy of the Casino. The most immediate and tangible consequence of this change is the municipality's regained managerial autonomy. Specifically, the Municipality can now "proceed with new hires without the prior authorization" of the Commission for the financial stability of local authorities (COSFEL). This is a crucial step that not only restores full operational capacity to the administration but also sends a strong signal of stability and confidence to the entire economic fabric of the region. The Municipality's note also emphasizes the importance of this confirmation to silence critical voices: "This officially confirms what has been repeatedly stated and illustrated by the Administration [...] in response to erroneous and misleading statements made by some minority councillors."

Dettagli operativi

For cross-border workers and the Lugano area's economy, the recovery of Campione d'Italia is not a marginal event. The end of financial distress means the public administration can once again invest in and strengthen services, a process that requires personnel. Although public sector jobs in Italy are generally reserved for Italian citizens, the reactivation of the administrative machine acts as a driving force for the entire local economy. A functioning and investing municipality generates a ripple effect: contracts for suppliers, maintenance, new projects. This can translate into opportunities for Ticino-based companies and, consequently, for their cross-border employees. ## A signal of stability for the border area The newfound ability to hire staff is a symptom of recovered financial health. For years, Campione was seen as an economically depressed area, an unknown variable in the heart of Canton Ticino. The end of COSFEL's oversight represents the closing of a chapter of forced austerity and the beginning of a reconstruction phase. This not only improves the exclave's image but also strengthens the entire cross-border ecosystem. A more solid Campione is a more reliable partner for Swiss businesses and authorities, fostering greater integration and collaboration on future projects that could involve mobility, tourism, and services along the Lugano-Como axis.

Punti chiave

The road to complete normalization is still long, but this milestone is fundamental. The ability to hire new staff will allow the Municipality of Campione to fill the staffing gaps created during the crisis years, improving the efficiency of public services for the benefit of residents and businesses.
