Contesto

In brief - 550 runners at the Life Run 2026 in Gallarate - Mattia Grifa and Federica Cerruti win in the Fidal race - Martina Menegotto sets the best women's overall time ## Key facts - What: Life Run 2026, running event - When: Sunday, May 3, 2026 - Where: Gallarate, Italy - Who: Organized by Life Village and Nuoto me Fitness Varese - Participants: 550 A wave of energy swept through Gallarate on the first Sunday in May. Over 550 runners took part in the 2026 edition of the Life Run, an event organized by Life Village and Nuoto me Fitness Varese. The race, included in the national Fidal calendar, offered high-level technical insights. In the men's race, Mattia Grifa crossed the finish line in 32'20”, beating Mengistu Rossi by just one second. Among the women, Federica Cerruti won in 42'52” ahead of Marta Piterà and Cecilia D'Andrea. The 10 km challenge dedicated to non-affiliated runners saw Alessandro Santaromita win with a time of 32'49”. The real surprise of the day came from the women's sector, where Martina Menegotto set the best overall time among all women in the race, stopping the clock at 41'39”. The event saw the participation of numerous local running groups, with prizes for the largest societies going to Aermacchi, Podistica Mezzanese, and Podistica Arsaghese. The closing of the event was spectacular, with a walk accompanied by the rhythm of Silent Disco Headphones, turning the city streets into a silent but vibrant celebration of colors. ### Implications for cross-border workers The Life Run 2026 not only promoted physical and mental well-being but also strengthened the sense of community among participants, many of whom are cross-border workers who work in Switzerland and live in Italy.
