In brief - Alex Zanardi suffered a serious accident in 2001 during a Champ Car race. - He lost both legs in the accident and underwent a long rehabilitation process. - He returned to racing and won Paralympic medals. ## Key facts - What: Car accident - When: September 15, 2001 - Where: Lausitzring, Germany - Who: Alex Zanardi - Impact speed: 320 km/h On September 15, 2001, at the Lausitzring in Germany, Alex Zanardi's life changed forever. During a Champ Car race, after a pit stop, Zanardi's car lost control on a dirty section of the track. His Reynard Honda, almost stationary, was violently hit by the Forsythe driven by Alex Tagliani. In the impact, which occurred at approximately 320 km/h, Zanardi's car was cut in half, causing immediate amputation of both legs. The medical director of Cart, Steve Olvey, arrived at the scene and stopped the blood flow by closing the femoral arteries. Zanardi was airlifted to a hospital in Berlin, where he underwent fifteen operations and remained in a coma for four days. On October 31, 2001, after six weeks of hospitalization, Zanardi was discharged from the German clinic, ready to begin the second part of his life. ### Sporting rebirth Zanardi did not give up and began a rehabilitation process that allowed him to walk with prostheses and return to racing. His new passion became the handbike, with which he participated in numerous races, winning Paralympic medals and setting records. In 2007, Zanardi finished fourth in his first race, the New York Marathon, and in 2011 he set the record for 42 km in the same city. In London, he won his first Paralympic gold medals, and in Rio de Janeiro, four years later, he won two more golds and a silver in the road race.
