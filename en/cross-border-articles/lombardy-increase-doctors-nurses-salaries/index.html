Lombardy Region: Increase Salaries for Doctors and Nurses in Border Areas with Switzerland

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 24 April 2026

The Lombardy Region introduces a regulation to increase the salaries of doctors and nurses in border areas with Switzerland. The measure aims to retain healthcare professionals on the Lombardy territory.

Contesto

TL;DR - Lombardy increases salaries for doctors and nurses near Swiss border. - 20% raise for doctors, CHF 5,400/year for nurses. - CHF 45 million investment to retain healthcare workers. - Salary hike starts September 2023. ## Key facts - Cosa: Aumento stipendiale per medici e infermieri - Quando: Da settembre 2023 - Dove: Aree di confine con la Svizzera - Chi: Regione Lombardia - Importo: 20% per medici, CHF 5,400/anno per infermieri - Investimento: CHF 45 milioni - Contributo: 3% dello stipendio netto - Operatori coinvolti: Circa 7,000 The Lombardy Region has announced that the norm to increase the salary of doctors and nurses in border areas with Switzerland will be operational from September. Assessor of the Lombardy Region for Local Entities and Montagna Massimo Sertori explained that the goal is to retain healthcare operators on the Lombardy territory, preventing them from crossing the border. The norm is foreseen by the state law 213 of 2023, which provides a 20% increase in salary for doctors and approximately CHF 5,400 net per year for nurses. Lombardy has chosen a contribution of 3% of the net salary, which is approximately CHF 120 per month for a net salary of CHF 4,000. Mario Melazzini, General Director of Welfare of the Lombardy Region, estimated that approximately 7,000 healthcare operators will be affected, with an investment of approximately CHF 45 million. 📊 To better understand the impact of this measure, it is useful to consider some concrete examples. Dettagli operativi

The increase in salaries for doctors and nurses in areas bordering Switzerland has significant implications for cross-border workers operating in these areas. The measure aims to improve working conditions and make working in these areas more attractive. This could influence the choice of doctors and nurses working in Lombardy, as the possibility of earning more could incentivize them to stay in the Lombardy region. Additionally, the increase in salaries could influence the demand for healthcare personnel in these areas, making it easier to find qualified staff. The Lombardy Region has also emphasized that the funds for this increase will be obtained with a 3% contribution from the net salary of 'old' cross-border workers, referring to those who have already worked in Lombardy and have an established work contract. This contribution will be used to strengthen healthcare in border areas, to the benefit of the cross-border workers themselves and their families. 📊 Concrete examples: For example, in Bellinzona, a Ticinese town located just a few kilometers from the border with Lombardy, the increase in salaries could lead to a reduction in the number of doctors and nurses moving to Lombardy for work. This could reduce the pressure on the Lombardy healthcare system and improve the quality of services offered in border areas. 💡 Regulations and dates: The increase in salaries was officially announced on January 1, 2023, with the aim of being implemented by June 30 of the same year.
