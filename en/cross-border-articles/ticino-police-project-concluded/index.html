Ticino police project ends without progress

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 30 April 2026

The State Council decided not to continue with the 'Ticino Police' project after the planning phase.

Contesto

In brief - 'Ticino Police' project concluded - Government Council decides not to proceed - In-depth analysis of tasks between cantonal and municipal police ## Key facts - What: 'Ticino Police' project - When: Project phase concluded in 2026 - Where: Canton of Ticino - Who: Government Council - Amount: Not specified The Government Council of the Canton of Ticino has decided not to proceed with the 'Ticino Police' project after the project phase. Launched in 2016, the project aimed to improve the organization and collaboration between the Cantonal Police and the municipal police forces. In light of the observations that emerged during the consultation, the Government Council deemed it appropriate to conclude the project phase without further developments. The working group conducted an in-depth analysis of the division of tasks, responsibilities, and governance models between the two institutional levels. The proposal was based on the principles of subsidiarity and financial neutrality, distinguishing between political and operational responsibilities. Municipal police forces were valued for their proximity role. During 2025, the report was put out for consultation with the municipalities and the main institutional stakeholders. However, the feedback received showed opposition to the proposed changes. The Government Council took note of this opposition and decided not to proceed with further investigations. ### Implications for cross-border workers The decision not to proceed with the 'Ticino Police' project could have implications for cross-border workers residing and working in the Canton of Ticino. Dettagli operativi

Analysis of the implications The decision not to proceed with the 'Polizia ticinese' project raises some questions about future security strategies in the Canton of Ticino. The lack of closer coordination among police forces could impact the ability to respond to emergencies and prevent crime. However, the work done in recent years could be used to gradually improve police services. ### Comparison with other regions In other regions of Switzerland, similar projects have been successfully implemented, improving collaboration among police forces. The decision of the Canton of Ticino not to proceed could be seen as a missed opportunity to improve efficiency and security. However, the specific situation of Ticino, with its proximity to Italy and the flow of cross-border workers, might require a different approach. ### Implications for cross-border workers Cross-border workers who live and work in the Canton of Ticino might notice few immediate changes. However, the lack of closer coordination among police forces could impact security and the efficiency of services. It is important that cross-border workers are aware of current procedures and available resources to ensure their safety. ### Current procedures Currently, cross-border workers can turn to local police for local security issues and to the Cantonal Police for broader matters. It is important to know the contacts and procedures for reporting any problems or emergencies. Local police are well-positioned to quickly respond to local needs, while the Cantonal Police can provide support for more complex issues. ### Available resources The Canton of Ticino offers various resources for cross-border workers, including information on security procedures and police contacts.
