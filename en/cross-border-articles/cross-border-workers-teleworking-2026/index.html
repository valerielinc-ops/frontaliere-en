In brief - New rules for cross-border workers telecommuting from 2026 - Up to 50 days of working from home in Italy - Impact on AHV, BVG, and LAMal ## Key facts - What: New rules for cross-border workers telecommuting - When: From January 1, 2026 - Where: Canton Ticino and Italy - Who: Cross-border workers with a G permit - Amount: Up to 50 days of working from home per year The Canton of Ticino has announced new rules for cross-border workers who wish to work from home in Italy. From January 1, 2026, cross-border workers with a G permit will be able to work up to 50 days a year from their home in Italy. This measure aims to improve the quality of life for cross-border workers, allowing them to save time and commuting costs. ### Impact on social insurances The new rules will have a significant impact on social insurances such as AHV, BVG, and LAMal. Cross-border workers will continue to pay contributions in Switzerland, but the rules for calculating contributions may change. It is important that cross-border workers inform themselves through their employer and the competent authorities to understand how these changes will affect their specific situation. ### Procedures for cross-border workers Cross-border workers who wish to take advantage of telecommuting will have to follow a specific procedure. First, they will need to obtain approval from their employer. Subsequently, they will need to inform the competent Swiss authorities, such as the Cantonal Employment Office, and the Italian authorities, such as the INPS.
