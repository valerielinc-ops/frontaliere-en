Seized warehouse in Como: investigation into abandoned cars

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 28 April 2026

Como's local police have seized a body shop warehouse for environmental violations, discovering abandoned waste and irregularities in the temporary storage.

In brief - Body shop warehouse seized in Como for environmental violations - Investigations started from car parts abandoned in public areas - Over 80 fines issued for incorrect waste disposal ## Key facts - What: Seizure of a body shop warehouse for environmental violations - When: April 28, 2026 - Where: Como - Who: Local police of Como - Amount: Over 80 fines issued for incorrect waste disposal - Who: Four cases of driving license suspension (four to six months) and fines between 1,000 and 3,000 euros - Who: Seizure of a truck used for illegal waste transport The action against environmental crimes in the Larian territory has recorded a new decisive intervention. Through integrated monitoring between surveillance cameras, checks by environmental inspectors and voluntary ecological guards, the local police of Como identified and fined the responsible parties for an uncontrolled waste deposit. The origin of the investigation stemmed from the discovery of mechanical components and spare parts of vehicles abandoned in public areas. The investigations conducted by the agents allowed to initially trace the owner of the vehicle and subsequently the professional in charge of the repair. The investigative results imposed an immediate verification at the activity's headquarters to ascertain the conformity of the waste storage and disposal procedures. The inspection confirmed the non-compliance with the current regulations regarding temporary storage. The operators noted the presence of a disorderly accumulation of various items within the company perimeter.
