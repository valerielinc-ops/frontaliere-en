Lega Ticino proposes one-week Italy border closure (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 3 May 2026

The Ticino League proposes closing borders with Italy for a week to see the effect, quoting Jannacci.

Contesto

In brief - The Lega dei Ticinesi proposes closing the borders with Italy for a week - Criticism over unpaid healthcare bills from Italy - Interpellation in Bern to clarify healthcare debts ## Key facts - What: Proposal to close borders with Italy - When: Not yet specified - Where: Canton Ticino, Switzerland - Who: Lega dei Ticinesi - Amount: Tens of millions of Swiss francs The limit of tolerance seems to have been exceeded. Switzerland is questioning the silence of the Federal Council in the face of what is described as a persistent media and institutional hate campaign from Italy, sparked by the dramatic fire in Crans. At the center of the latest controversy is the management of hospital bills. Switzerland has been harshly criticized for applying international regulations governing healthcare abroad, rules that have been in place long before the Constellation fire. The controversy does not spare Swiss domestic politics. In Il Mattino della Domenica – a weekly publication of the Lega dei Ticinesi – the federal 'governicchio' is accused of excessive weakness. And it is from the columns of the newspaper that a series of accusations are leveled: Italy would not have applied the Dublin Treaty since 2022, forcing Switzerland to bear the cost of migrants, with an extra expense of tens of millions of francs for Swiss taxpayers. According to statements by the Lega State Councillor Norman Gobbi in the Corriere del Ticino, Italy would be in arrears in paying several healthcare bills. In addition to the costs of Crans-Montana, there are uncollected amounts linked to the case of Campione d'Italia and other arrears that affect the health insurance premiums in Switzerland.
