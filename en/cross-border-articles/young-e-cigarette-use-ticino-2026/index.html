E-cigarettes: young people use them like traditional ones

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 1 May 2026

25.2% of young people consume both traditional and electronic cigarettes, a 3% increase from 2023

Contesto

In brief - 25.2% of young people consume both traditional and electronic cigarettes - 3% increase compared to 2023 - Representative study on 5,818 people ## Key facts - What: Consumption of electronic and traditional cigarettes - When: 2025 - Where: Switzerland - Who: Addiction Switzerland - Amount: 25.2% of monthly consumers In 2025, 22.5% of the population aged 15 and over consumed a tobacco or nicotine product at least once a month, according to Addiction Switzerland. This percentage has been stable since 2023, with 15.8% consuming daily. Traditional cigarettes remain the most consumed product, but among young people aged 15 to 17, monthly consumption of electronic cigarettes (8.4%) is almost equal to that of traditional cigarettes (6.8%). The representative study was carried out on behalf of the Tobacco Prevention Fund and in collaboration with the Federal Office of Public Health. 5,818 people over the age of 15 were interviewed between April and June 2025. ### Implications for young people The multiple use of nicotine products is increasing, with more than a quarter (25.2%) of monthly consumers using different products. This represents an increase from 22% in 2023. The increase in the consumption of electronic cigarettes among young people raises concerns for public health and the need for targeted prevention campaigns. ### Comparison with previous data The number of cigarettes smoked per day increases with age, but the consumption of electronic cigarettes among young people has become significant. Dettagli operativi

Trend Analysis The increase in the consumption of electronic cigarettes among young people raises several issues. E-cigarettes are often perceived as less harmful than traditional ones, but their use can still lead to addiction and health problems. Moreover, the multiple consumption of nicotine-based products increases the risk of addiction and negative health effects. ### Comparison with other regions In other regions of Switzerland, the consumption of electronic cigarettes among young people is similar to that in the Canton of Ticino. However, the proximity of Ticino to Italy could influence consumption behaviors, especially among cross-border workers who may be exposed to different regulations and product availability. ### Prevention strategies Swiss health authorities are evaluating various strategies to address the consumption of electronic cigarettes among young people. These include awareness campaigns, restrictions on advertising and access to products, and educational programs in schools. It is important that these strategies are adapted to local specificities and the consumption habits of young people. ### Role of schools Schools play a crucial role in preventing nicotine consumption among young people. Targeted educational programs can help raise awareness among young people about the risks associated with the consumption of electronic and traditional cigarettes. Furthermore, schools can collaborate with health authorities to develop effective prevention strategies. ### Involvement of families Families play an important role in preventing nicotine consumption among young people. Concrete actions for young people and families To address the consumption of electronic cigarettes among young people, it is important to adopt concrete measures. Here are some actions that young people and families can take: 1. Inform yourself about the risks: Knowing the risks associated with the consumption of electronic and traditional cigarettes is the first step to prevent consumption. Health authorities and schools can provide accurate and up-to-date information. 2. Talk to young people: Parents and educators should openly discuss with young people the risks of nicotine consumption. This can help prevent consumption and promote healthy lifestyles. 3. Promote alternative activities: Encouraging young people to participate in alternative activities such as sports, art, and music can help reduce nicotine consumption. Schools and communities can organize events and programs to promote these activities. 4. Collaborate with health authorities: Families and schools can collaborate with health authorities to develop effective prevention strategies. This can include educational programs, awareness campaigns, and restrictions on access to products. 5. Monitor behavior: Parents and educators should monitor the behavior of young people and intervene promptly in case of signs of nicotine consumption. This can include open conversations, emotional support, and, if necessary, professional interventions. ### Useful tools For further information and support, young people and families can use the following tools: - Risk calculator - Prevention guide - Educational programs ### Conclusion Addressing the consumption of electronic cigarettes among young people requires an integrated approach involving health authorities, schools, families, and the community. Frequently Asked Questions
What are the risks associated with the consumption of electronic cigarettes?
The consumption of electronic cigarettes can lead to addiction and health problems, such as respiratory and heart diseases. Moreover, the multiple use of nicotine-based products increases the risk of negative health effects.
What can schools do to prevent nicotine consumption among young people?
Schools can develop targeted educational programs, collaborate with health authorities, and organize events and alternative activities to promote healthy lifestyles.
How can families prevent nicotine consumption among young people?
Families can educate their children about the risks of nicotine consumption, encourage healthy lifestyles, and collaborate with schools and health authorities to develop effective prevention strategies.
