Dettagli operativi

An analysis of this situation reveals how the skills and infrastructure present in Chiasso constitute a valuable asset that can be leveraged to boost local employment and the economy, even during times of crisis. The reduction of activities in the rail freight sector, in fact, risks having repercussions not only on jobs but also on logistics between Italy and Switzerland. For cross-border workers, this period of uncertainty could lead to increased competition for available positions or a redistribution of activities. It is important to understand how cantonal and federal authorities can intervene to support the sector—perhaps through new investments, training incentives, and innovation projects. A comparison between the past and present shows how Chiasso has always been a key hub for freight traffic, thanks to its infrastructure and the expertise of specialized technicians. Punti chiave

For cross-border workers and companies in the freight sector in Chiasso, it's essential to take immediate action. The first phase involves engaging with the cantonal authorities and Swiss Railways, seeking clarification on the cuts and proposing alternative solutions. Here are some concrete steps: - Inform yourself about the development plans for the freight sector at the DFE and the Canton of Ticino. - Participate in public consultations and comparison tables open to social partners. - Evaluate opportunities for professional requalification, for example through training courses organized by institutions such as SUPSI or the Chambers of Commerce. - Explore possibilities of collaboration between Italian and Swiss companies to create logistical synergies. - Monitor the tenders and incentives available for investments in the sector. The active involvement of all stakeholders will be crucial to relaunch the freight sector and protect jobs. Transparent communication and continuous dialogue with the authorities will allow for shared solutions to be identified and concrete projects to be implemented. For cross-border workers, it's advisable to stay updated on developments through the official channels of the Canton of Ticino and Swiss Railways. Participation in public meetings and membership in category associations will help defend one's rights and seize any new opportunities. Finally, we invite you to use tools like the salary calculator to evaluate possible scenarios of redistribution or professional requalification, thus contributing to a conscious planning of one's future work.
