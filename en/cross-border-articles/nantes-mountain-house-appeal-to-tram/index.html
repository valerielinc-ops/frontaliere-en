Cost and Implications Analysis The decision by the State Council to reject the appeal by three citizens against the resolution adopted by the Legislature on June 11, 2025, has raised several questions regarding the transparency and accuracy of the information provided by the Municipality of Monteceneri. According to the referendum committee, the Municipality had to provide missing clarifications, and significant discrepancies have emerged compared to the initial promises. In particular, the ruling indicates that the cost to the municipal finances will not be just 2 million francs, as initially promised, but at least 3 million. This is because 2 million will be financed through a loan and 1 million will come from a 'budget reserve,' i.e., money already belonging to the Municipality. This revelation raises doubts about the financial management of the project and the transparency of the information provided to citizens. ### Third-Party Contributions and Cantonal Subsidies Another critical point concerns third-party contributions, particularly cantonal subsidies. According to the referendum committee, there is finally written confirmation that the approximately 1.7 million francs in third-party contributions are not at all guaranteed. This means that the cantonal subsidies, which were presented as a certainty, remain today a mere hypothesis or hope. This lack of guarantee could have a significant impact on the municipal budget and the sustainability of the project. ### Implications for the Community The case of the mountain house Nante also raises important questions for the local community.
