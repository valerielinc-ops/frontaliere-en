Minimum wage in Ticino: new rules from 2027

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 21 April 2026

From 2027, the minimum wage in Ticino will be between 20.50 and 21 francs per hour, with gradual increases over the next two years.

Contesto

TL;DR - Ticino approva nuovo salario minimo 2027-2029 - Aumento graduale da 20.50 a 21.50 CHF/ora - 23.000 lavoratori beneficiari, 8.000 residenti - Nuovo salario minimo supera accordi collettivi ## Key facts - Salario minimo: 20.50-21.50 CHF/ora dal 2027 al 2029 - Aumento mensile: Da ~3,300 a 4,200 CHF lordi/mese - Beneficiari: 23,000 lavoratori, 8,000 residenti - Data inizio: 1 gennaio 2027 (previsto) - Impatto settori: Turismo, ristorazione, costruzione - Prevalenza: Salario cantonale > accordi collettivi - Incertezza: Camere federali potrebbero modificare regole - Obiettivo: Proteggere lavoratori e competitività aziende The Grand Council of the Canton of Ticino has approved with a large majority the compromise reached between the parties regarding the new minimum wage. Starting from 2027, the minimum wage will range between 20.50 and 21 francs per hour. This value will then be gradually increased over the next two years, reaching 21.50 francs per hour in 2029. The compromise was reached despite the amendment proposals put forward by the Movement for Socialism (MPS), which were rejected. The counter-proposal accepted in the chamber provides that, in three years, the minimum wage will be approximately 4,000 francs gross per month for twelve months for full-time employment, equivalent to 33,600 francs gross annually. The Director of the Department of Finance and Economy, Christian Vitta, praised the work of the Commission that found a shared path on a delicate issue for the Canton of Ticino. Vitta emphasized that the minimum wage touches concrete aspects such as wage conditions, purchasing power, business competitiveness, and the balance of the labor market.
