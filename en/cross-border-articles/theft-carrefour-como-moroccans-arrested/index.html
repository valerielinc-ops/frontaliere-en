Two Moroccans arrested for Carrefour theft in Como (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 5 May 2026

Two homeless Moroccan citizens were arrested for attempted robbery and aggravated theft after an incident at the Carrefour on via Recchi.

Contesto

In brief - Two Moroccans arrested for theft and assault on Carrefour security guard in Como - A 31-year-old for attempted robbery, a 23-year-old for attempted aggravated theft - Stolen bottle of wine returned ## Key facts - What: Attempted robbery and aggravated theft - When: 05/05/2026, midnight - Where: Carrefour in via Recchi, Como - Who: State Police of Como - Amount: Stolen bottle of wine The State Police of Como arrested two Moroccan citizens without fixed abode in Italy for an incident that occurred last night at the Carrefour in via Recchi. A 31-year-old was arrested for attempted robbery, while a 23-year-old was arrested for attempted aggravated theft. The incident began when the 23-year-old tried to steal a bottle of wine by hiding it in his pants. The security officer noticed and asked him to leave the merchandise, but the young man threatened and punched him in the chest. The accomplice, the 31-year-old, tried to flee but was stopped by the officers. The stolen merchandise was returned to the security officer. After consulting the Judicial Authority and evaluating the specific police prejudices, the two were arrested. ### Security implications This episode once again raises the issue of security in large shopping centers and supermarkets.
