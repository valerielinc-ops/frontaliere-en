Record haul in Colverde: over 100 kg of marijuana seized (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 17 April 2026

The provincial police of Como have seized 105 kg of marijuana found in abandoned sacks near the Ticino border. The operation was launched following an environmental check.

Dettagli operativi

The seizure of over 105 kg of marijuana in Colverde, near the Ticino border, marks a significant episode in the landscape of illegal trafficking affecting the Italy-Switzerland border area. But what are the concrete implications for cross-border workers and the security of Ticino territory? ### The role of territorial policing According to the declaration of the President of the Province of Como, Fiorenzo Bongiasca, the operation confirms how constant territorial policing is essential to counter criminal phenomena. For cross-border workers who cross the border daily, this means a higher likelihood of encountering targeted checks, especially in high-traffic areas such as border crossings and nearby residential centers. Local police have emphasized how environmental monitoring can yield unexpected results, as in this case, where a waste inspection led to the discovery of a drug shipment. ### Comparison with previous operations at the border In recent months, the Brogeda border crossing has been the site of several drug seizures, with arrests and anti-drug operations also involving cross-border workers. The Colverde case, however, stands out due to the quantity seized and the method of concealing the drugs. While at Brogeda, trafficking often involves small quantities hidden in vehicles or luggage, here the drugs were abandoned in sacks, likely to avoid checks during transport. ### Possible scenarios for the destination of the drugs A quantity of this size could be destined for several markets: - The Italian domestic market, particularly in regions where demand for marijuana is high; - The cross-border network, with possible distribution into Switzerland through secondary channels; - The local Lombard market, given the proximity to Como and the ease of distribution....

Punti chiave

What should cross-border workers and Ticino residents do following the discovery of this drug seizure near the border? Authorities are urging collaboration by reporting suspicious situations, but what are the concrete steps to take to act effectively? Here's a step-by-step guide to help contribute to territorial security without exposing yourself to unnecessary risks. ### Step 1: Recognize a suspicious situation Not all piles of abandoned bags in green areas are necessarily dangerous, but certain elements can raise suspicions: - Excessive packaging: bags wrapped in multiple layers of plastic film, adhesive tape, or other materials; - Unusual location: piles in isolated areas, far from busy roads or public spaces; - Distinctive odor: presence of a strong, persistent smell typical of narcotic substances; - Other clues: traces of recent passage, abandoned objects near the bags, vehicles parked nearby. ### Step 2: Do not intervene directly It is crucial not to touch the bags or suspicious objects. Even if they appear to contain only waste, they could conceal dangerous substances or other illegal materials. Additionally, tampering with the scene could compromise any investigations by the authorities. ### Step 3: Report to the competent authorities If you are in Ticino, you can contact: - Ticino cantonal police: dial 117; - Border Guard: for reports related to border crossings or frontier zones; - Municipal police of your place of residence; - Carabinieri or State Police (if in Italian territory): dial 112. ### Step 4: Provide precise information When making a report, it is helpful to provide as much detail as possible: - Exact location of the pile of bags (address, landmarks, GPS coordinates if available); - Detailed description of the bags and their apparent content...

Frequently Asked Questions
What are the risks for a cross-border worker if they unknowingly transport drugs across the border?
In Switzerland, drug trafficking is a criminal offense, even if unintentional. Penalties can reach up to 20 years in prison, depending on the quantity and circumstances. In Italy, the offense is serious and carries criminal sanctions. It is crucial to avoid any contact with suspicious substances or objects and to report the situation immediately to the authorities.
How can I tell the difference between a normal pile of waste and a suspicious one?
Suspicious bags often have excessive packaging (multiple layers of film, adhesive tape), are located in isolated areas, and may emit strong odors. If you notice these signs, do not touch the bags and report them immediately to the authorities. Never open or move suspicious objects.
Which Ticino-Como border crossings are under increased scrutiny following this operation?
Following the seizure in Colverde, the crossings at **Brogeda** (Chiasso) and **Gaggiolo** (Stabio) are at higher risk of extraordinary checks. The **Ponte Chiasso** bridge may also receive greater attention from authorities.
Could I be called as a witness after making a report?
Yes, if your report leads to an investigation, you may be contacted by authorities to provide a statement. Authorities guarantee confidentiality and protection of personal data. It is important to respond accurately, without adding unverified details.
Are there official channels for reporting suspicious situations in Ticino?
Yes. In addition to emergency numbers **117** (cantonal police) and **112** (Carabinieri/State Police), Ticino has online reporting services via official police apps or municipal websites. For cross-border workers, it is advisable to use communication channels dedicated to border crossings.
