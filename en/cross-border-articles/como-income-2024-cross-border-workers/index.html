Como Province is wealthier: income rises but the town isn't in the top 10

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 27 April 2026

The per capita income of Como Province has increased, but the town isn't in the top 10. Discover the details and implications for cross-border workers.

Contesto

TL;DR - Como's per capita income is 23,931 euros in 2024 - Milan leads with 36,187 euros per capita - Maccastorna tops municipalities with 72,684 euros ## Key facts - Cosa: Reddito pro capite della provincia di Como - Quando: Anno fiscale 2024 - Dove: Provincia di Como - Chi: Ministero dell'Economia e delle Finanze - Importo: 23.931 euro - Posizione: 37° posto in Italia - Media italiana: 23.155 euro ## In brief - Per capita income Como: 23,931 euros - Milan tops the list with 36,187 euros - Maccastorna at the top with 72,684 euros ## Key facts - What: Per capita income of the province of Como - When: Tax year 2024 - Where: Province of Como - Who: Ministry of Economy and Finance - Amount: 23,931 euros The province of Como has recorded a slight improvement of two positions compared to the previous survey. The per capita income, based on the absolute value, is 23,931 euros, placing Como in 37th place. Milan confirms its first place with a per capita income of 36,187 euros, followed by Monza and Brianza with 30,182 euros, Lecco with 25,159 euros, and Bergamo with 24,778 euros. Behind Como are Brescia with 23,672 euros and Sondrio with 23,609 euros. The Italian average is 23,155 euros. Regarding individual municipalities, Maccastorna, in the province of Lodi, is at the top with a per capita income of 72,684 euros. Followed by Lajatico (Pisa) with 69,475 euros and Portofino with 61,611 euros. In the top ten, there is no Como, but three other Lombard municipalities: Basiglio with 53,687 euros, Cusago Milanino with 43,404 euros, and Milan with 40,237 euros. These data could have significant implications for cross-border workers working in the province of Como.
