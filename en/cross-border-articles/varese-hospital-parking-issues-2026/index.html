Varese: technical meeting to solve the parking problem at Sette Laghi Hospital

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 27 April 2026

The Municipality of Varese and ASST Sette Laghi meet to address the critical parking issues for healthcare workers. Meeting scheduled for April 28, 2026.

Contesto

In brief - Meeting between the Municipality of Varese and ASST Sette Laghi on April 28, 2026 - Parking issues at three healthcare hubs: Circolo, Del Ponte, Casa di Comunità - Fials praises the long-awaited initiative ## Key facts - What: Technical table to solve parking problems - When: April 28, 2026 - Where: Varese - Who: Municipality of Varese, ASST Sette Laghi, Fials - Amount: 100 additional parking spaces in via Merini The Municipality of Varese and ASST Sette Laghi have scheduled a meeting for Tuesday, April 28, 2026, to address the critical parking issues for the hospital workers. The problem is concentrated in three main areas: Circolo Hospital, Del Ponte, and the Casa di Comunità in Viale Monte Rosa. The Fials union has welcomed the initiative, which has been awaited for years, hoping that concrete and immediately implementable proposals will emerge from the table. The parking problem repeatedly arises in the different healthcare hubs. Two years ago, the battle of the confederate unions with a petition led the hospital and the Municipality of Varese to review the parking plan of the area. They decided to allocate the multi-level car park in via Guicciardini (excluding one floor for external users) to the workers, while the Municipality opened an additional 100 parking spaces around the new RSA and in via Merini. A multi-level car park is also available near Del Ponte, and the issue lies with the spaces reserved for employees and the cost of the subsidized subscription. Since 2016, Fials has been drawing attention to the problem, especially at the Ponte Hospital. Dettagli operativi

Implications for workers and the healthcare system The issue of parking for ASST Sette Laghi workers has significant implications for both healthcare staff and the healthcare system as a whole. The lack of adequate parking spaces can negatively impact the quality of life for workers, increasing stress and reducing job satisfaction. This, in turn, can affect the quality of services offered to patients. The exodus of staff to Switzerland, where economic and organizational conditions are more favorable, represents an additional critical element. The loss of experienced healthcare professionals can compromise the system's ability to meet the needs of the population, especially in a context of already structural staff shortages. ### Comparison with the previous situation Two years ago, the battle of the confederate unions led to a revision of the parking plan for the area, with the allocation of the multi-storey car park in via Guicciardini to workers and the opening of a further 100 parking spaces in via Merini. However, the problem persists, especially regarding spaces reserved for employees and the cost of the subsidized subscription. The current situation requires a more structural and lasting solution, which takes into account the needs of healthcare staff and the dynamics of the labor market. ### Future scenarios If the technical table between the Municipality of Varese and ASST Sette Laghi can find concrete and immediately implementable solutions, it could represent an important step towards resolving the parking problem. However, the complexity of the issue requires an integrated approach that considers not only the immediate needs of the staff but also the long-term dynamics of the healthcare labor market. Frequently Asked Questions
What are the main points of the parking problem at Sette Laghi Hospital?
The main points of the parking problem at Sette Laghi Hospital concern the three healthcare hubs: Circolo Hospital, Del Ponte, and the Community House on Viale Monte Rosa. The issue is focused on the availability of parking spaces for workers and the cost of subsidized parking permits.
What has the Municipality of Varese done to solve the parking problem?
The Municipality of Varese has allocated the multi-storey car park on via Guicciardini to workers, with the exception of one floor for external users, and has opened an additional 100 parking spaces on via Merini. However, the problem persists, especially regarding the spaces reserved for employees and the cost of subsidized parking permits.
What are the economic incentives for healthcare personnel in border areas?
Regione Lombardia has introduced economic incentives for healthcare personnel in border areas, with estimated increases between 5,000 and 10,000 euros per year. These incentives are provided for by law 213 of 2023 and are considered useful, but not decisive, in stemming the flight of professionals to Switzerland.
