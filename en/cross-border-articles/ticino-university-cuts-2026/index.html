Cuts to contributions and limited spots: what changes at USI

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 27 April 2026

The University of Italian Switzerland reduces spots for international students. Tuition doubled for those coming from abroad. Federal cuts starting in 2027.

Contesto

In brief - USI loses 6.5 million in annual contributions - Federal cuts to universities from 2027 - Tuition for international students doubled ## Key facts - What: Cuts to public contributions - When: From 2026 (canton) and 2027 (federal) - Where: Università della Svizzera italiana (USI), Lugano - Who: Ticino cantonal government and Confederation - Amount: -6.5 million/year for USI; -60 million/year for cantonal universities The Università della Svizzera italiana (USI) in Lugano is facing an unprecedented financial crisis. In December 2025, the Canton cut one million francs in annual contributions, and now the State Council has announced an additional reduction of 5.5 million francs every year. Interim Rector Gabriele Balbi told the Corriere del Ticino that this cut of 6.5 million from a budget of 130 million 'is definitely felt.' The situation is also critical for students. USI has reached 4,749 enrolled students across bachelor's, master's, and PhD programs, and the infrastructure may not hold up. For this reason, starting in September 2026, some courses will have limited enrollment: 190 spots for Economics in Italian, 150 for Communication, and 70 each for the master's programs in Finance and Management. Swiss students will have guaranteed access, while international students will be disadvantaged. ### Tuition doubled for international students The cantonal government has clearly stated that USI 'will have to implement measures to contain spending and/or increase revenue, for example by raising tuition fees for foreign students.' USI is already the most expensive university in Switzerland, with differentiated fees: students from other countries pay double. Implications for International Students The new measures will have a significant impact on students arriving from abroad to study in Switzerland. With the numerus clausus, many may not find a place in the most sought-after degree courses. Furthermore, the increase in tuition fees will make USI even more expensive, putting international students' pockets to the test. ### Comparison with Other Swiss Universities USI is not the only Swiss university to introduce numerus clausus and differentiated tuition fees. The University of St. Gallen, for example, limits the percentage of international students to 25% of the total, with tuition fees reaching CHF 3,300 per semester. The École Polytechnique Fédérale de Lausanne (EPFL) has also introduced an indirect limit on the number of foreign students, setting a maximum ceiling of 3,000 first-year bachelor's degree enrollments. ### Possible Scenarios If the situation were to worsen, many international students might decide not to enroll at USI or transfer to other European universities. This could have a negative impact on the cultural and academic diversity of the institution, which has always attracted students from around the world. ### Comparison with the Previous Situation Before the cuts, USI was an accessible institution for international students, with differentiated but not prohibitive tuition fees. Now, with the increase in tuition fees and numerus clausus, the situation has completely changed. Swiss students will have guaranteed access, while those coming from abroad will face greater difficulties. ### Comparison with Other European Universities Compared to other European universities, USI is already one of the most expensive.
