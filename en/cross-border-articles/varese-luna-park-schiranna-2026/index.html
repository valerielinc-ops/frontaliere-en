Dettagli operativi

The Life of Showmen and Showwomen The life of showmen and showwomen in Ticino is a blend of tradition and modernity, with deep roots in the local culture. Families working in the traveling entertainment sector, such as those operating at the Schiranna Amusement Park, often pass down the profession from generation to generation. This creates strong cohesion within the community, where everyone knows and supports each other. The caravans, which serve as mobile homes, are equipped with all the necessary comforts for life on the road. For example, an average showman family may own up to three caravans, each with an area of about 25-30 square meters, equipped with a kitchen, bathroom, and autonomous heating systems. The families move from one city to another following the calendar of fairs and amusement parks, adapting to different weather conditions and public needs. In Ticino, the main events include the Lugano Fair, which attracts about 300,000 visitors per year, and the Mendrisio Grape Festival, with over 150,000 attendees. The rides must be set up and dismantled in record time, often in less than 48 hours, to meet the deadlines imposed by event organizers. ### The Work in Detail The work of showmen is very varied and requires great technical expertise. Each ride has its own specific characteristics and requires constant maintenance. For example, a ride like the 'Top Spin' requires daily checks of safety systems, such as safety belts and locking mechanisms, which must be certified according to Swiss regulations in force since 2018, which provide for fines of up to 20,000 CHF in case of non-compliance. Frequently Asked Questions
Who are the showmen and showwomen?
Showmen and showwomen are operators in the traveling entertainment industry who set up, manage, and dismantle the rides at the amusement park. They work in close-knit communities and often pass down the trade from generation to generation.
How do the children of showmen attend school?
The children of showmen attend different schools in each city, adapting to new classmates and teachers. Some associations and institutions offer specific programs to support their education.
What are the main challenges of the traveling entertainment industry?
The main challenges include competition with other forms of entertainment, the need to innovate to attract audiences, and compliance with safety and environmental regulations.
