As of May 2, 2026, excise duties on gasoline and LPG have increased, while diesel has slightly decreased. Here's how fuel costs change for cross-border workers

In brief - Petrol and LPG more expensive from May 2, 2026 - Diesel slightly down - New excise duties: +15 cents for petrol ## Key facts - What: Increase in excise duties on fuels - When: From May 2, 2026 - Where: Italy - Who: Ministry of Enterprises and Made in Italy - Amount: +15 cents per litre for petrol, +4.1 cents for LPG From May 2, 2026, the new excise duties on fuels will have different impacts on pump prices. According to the latest data from Staffetta Quotidiana, petrol and LPG are more expensive, while diesel is slightly down. This change is due to the increase in excise duties on green petrol, which has risen by 15 cents per litre, and on LPG, which has increased by 4.1 cents. Including VAT, the increases are respectively 18.3 cents for petrol and 5 cents for LPG. Today, Monday, May 4, the national average prices of fuels at the pump show a strong increase for petrol and LPG, while diesel registers a slight decrease. Self-service petrol on the road network is at 1.899 euros/litre, with an increase of 14.5 cents compared to Thursday. Diesel is at 2.047 euros/litre, with a decrease of 5 thousandths. LPG is at 0.814 euros/litre, with an increase of 1.9 cents, while methane is at 1.573 euros/kg, with a decrease of 0.3 cents. On the motorway, self-service petrol is at 1.967 euros, with an increase of 16.6 cents, diesel at 2.116 euros, with an increase of 2 cents, LPG at 0.924 euros, with an increase of 2.4 cents, and methane at 1.594 euros, with a decrease of 0.3 cents. ### Comparison between brands Different oil companies have applied different increases.
