Lugano Airport: 4 million in unauthorized expenses (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 4 May 2026

Municipality questioned: 2.7 million for fuel, VAT not included. Strategy and timelines still unclear

Contesto

In brief - 25 questions to the Municipality about the future of the airport - 4 million spent without the approval of the Municipal Council - VAT not included, costs could exceed 4.5 million ## Key facts - What: Interpellation to the Municipality of Lugano - When: 4 May 2026 - Where: Lugano, Canton Ticino - Who: Municipal Councilors Sara Beretta Piccoli and Carola Barchi (PLR-PVL) - Amount: 4 million francs spent The Lugano Airport is at the center of a heated political debate. An interpellation presented to the Municipality by Municipal Councilor Sara Beretta Piccoli and her colleague Carola Barchi, both from the PLR-PVL group, has raised questions about the financial management of the airport. The interpellants highlighted that, in addition to the credit of 1.2 million approved at the end of 2024 for urgent maintenance and safety interventions, other funds have been committed using the salami technique. ### Unauthorized expenses According to the interpellants, based on the available information regarding public contracts in 2025, amounts totaling over 4 million francs have been committed. Of these, approximately 2.7 million are related to the purchase of fuel, intended for resale. However, the total amount does not include VAT, which could bring the actual total to exceed 4.5 million francs. ### Bypassing the will of the Municipal Council The fragmentation of spending commitments into individual contracts of relatively small size could constitute a bypass of the will expressed by the Municipal Council and its competencies in financial decision-making. Dettagli operativi

Analysis of the implications The situation at Lugano Airport raises several issues that go beyond mere financial management. The implications for cross-border workers and the local economy are significant and deserve an in-depth analysis. ### Impact on costs for cross-border workers The increase in the airport's operating costs could lead to higher air ticket prices and ancillary services. This could directly impact cross-border workers who regularly use the airport for their travels. Moreover, the lack of clarity on the development strategy could lead to a reduction in the quality of services offered, causing inconvenience for users. ### Comparison with the previous situation Before the approval of the 1.2 million CHF credit at the end of 2024, Lugano Airport had already faced several challenges related to maintenance and security. The current situation, with the addition of further unauthorized expenses, represents a further step backward in the management of the airport. The fragmentation of expenses and the lack of transparency could lead to a situation of financial instability, with possible repercussions on the sustainability of the project. ### Future scenarios In an optimistic scenario, the Municipality of Lugano could respond to the criticisms and provide detailed clarifications on the expenses and future development strategies. This could lead to greater transparency and better planning, with benefits for cross-border workers and the local economy. However, in a pessimistic scenario, the situation could worsen, with an increase in costs and a reduction in the quality of services offered. ### Comparisons with other airports A comparison with other regional airports could provide further food for thought.
