Dettagli operativi

The office will be responsible for ensuring the safety and protection of cross-border workers, and for managing requests for permits and declarations. The new law will come into force on 1 January 2026 and will apply to all cross-border workers working in Canton Ticino. This legislation represents a significant step towards more efficient and secure management of the flow of cross-border workers, a crucial sector for the Ticino economy. The law introduces new procedures for managing work permits, with the aim of reducing irregularities and improving control over activities. For example, workers will have to submit monthly declarations of their working hours, a significant change compared to previous regulations. This requirement was introduced in response to growing concerns regarding the lack of transparency and possible tax evasion by some workers. > "The new law is an important step towards greater legality and transparency in the cross-border work sector," stated the head of the office, underlining the importance of these changes for the economic stability of Canton Ticino. The new regulations also provide for the introduction of an operational checklist for workers, which includes the need to record working hours, keep relevant documents, and submit quarterly declarations. This checklist was designed to facilitate compliance with the new rules, but also to ensure that workers can maintain strict control over their activities. 📊 Key data: - Number of cross-border workers in Ticino: Approximately 50,000, with a significant concentration in the municipalities of Lugano, Bellinzona and Locarno. - Working hours declared monthly: From January 2026, workers will have to declare the hours worked each month, a 50% increase compared to the previous quarterly frequency....
