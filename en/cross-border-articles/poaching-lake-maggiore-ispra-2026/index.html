Illegal fishing on Lake Maggiore: spear fisherman reported

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 2 May 2026

Carabinieri Forestali operation in Varesotto: seizures and criminal complaint for illegal pikeperch fishing during biological closure

In brief - Fisherman with spear reported on Lake Maggiore - Seizures and criminal complaint for illegal fishing - Stopped during the biological rest period of the zander - Fine up to 12,000 euros and penalty up to 2 years in prison ## Key facts - What: Fish poaching with spear - When: May 1, 2026 - Where: Lake Maggiore, Ispra (VA) and borders with Locarno (TI) - Who: Forestry Carabinieri of the Environmental, Agri-Food and Forestry Investigative Police Unit (NIPAAF) of Varese - Amount: Penalty up to 2 years in prison or fine up to 12,000 euros The Forestry Carabinieri conducted an operation on Lake Maggiore to combat fish poaching and protect the biodiversity of the basin. Thanks to the collaboration of the association “Tritone APS”, the military of the Environmental, Agri-Food and Forestry Investigative Police Unit (NIPAAF) of Varese caught a man fishing illegally using a spear, a tool banned for the capture of fish. The illegal activity was discovered in the municipality of Ispra, in the province of Varese, during the biological rest period of the zander, which runs from April 1 to May 31. The biological rest is a crucial measure for the safeguarding of the species, as during this period the male builds and defends the nest on the bottom. The capture of the zander during this phase exposes the eggs to predators, with the risk of destroying an entire generation and compromising the balance of the lake ecosystem. What to Do in Case of Poaching Sightings If illegal fishing activities are spotted, it is crucial to immediately report the incident to the competent authorities. Reports can be made through the official channels of the Forestry Carabinieri or local associations that operate in environmental protection. Providing detailed information, such as the exact location (GPS coordinates if possible) and a description of the illegal activity, can help authorities intervene promptly. For example, in 2022, thanks to a precise report, the Forestry Carabinieri of Locarno seized 15 kg of illegally caught fish in Lake Maggiore. ### Importance of the Biological Moratorium The biological moratorium is a conservation measure that protects fish species during critical reproduction periods. For the zander, this period runs from April 1st to May 31st. During this time, it is forbidden to catch adult specimens to ensure they can reproduce and contribute to the survival of the species. Violating this regulation can result in criminal and administrative penalties, as demonstrated by the operation of the Forestry Carabinieri on Lake Maggiore, where a fisherman was fined 5,000 CHF for violating the biological moratorium. ### How to Respect Fishing Regulations To avoid incurring penalties, it is essential to respect the current fishing regulations. This includes using permitted equipment, respecting biological moratorium periods, and having a valid fishing permit. Fishermen must be aware of the specific rules for each species and fishing area, which can vary based on local and national regulations. Frequently Asked Questions
What are the penalties for illegal fishing?
Penalties for illegal fishing can include a criminal complaint with a penalty of up to two years in prison or a fine of up to 12,000 euros. Additionally, administrative sanctions may apply, such as the seizure of the catch and the equipment used.
What should I do if I spot illegal fishing activities?
If you spot illegal fishing activities, it is crucial to report the incident immediately to the competent authorities, such as the Forestry Carabinieri or local associations. Providing detailed information can help authorities intervene promptly.
What is the importance of the biological closure?
The biological closure is a conservation measure that protects fish species during critical reproduction periods. For the zander, this period runs from April 1st to May 31st. During this time, the capture of adult specimens is prohibited to ensure they can reproduce and contribute to the survival of the species.
