Ticino Police Audit: Government Requests Six-Month Extension

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 1 May 2026

The State Council asks for an extension for the message on the Canton Police audit. Dadò and Ferrara insist on urgency.

Contesto

In brief - Government of Ticino requests six additional months for the message on the audit of the Police - Dadò and Ferrara insist on urgent verification - Motion submitted in October 2025 ## Key facts - What: Extension for the message on the audit of the Cantonal Police - When: April 30, 2026 - Where: Canton Ticino - Who: State Council, Dadò (Centre), Ferrara (PLR) - Amount: Not yet specified The State Council of Canton Ticino has requested a six-month extension for the presentation of the message regarding the audit of the Cantonal Police. The request was made to the presidential office of the parliament, sparking protests from the motion presenters Fiorenzo Dadò (Centre) and Natalia Ferrara (PLR). The motion, submitted in October 2025, called for an urgent verification of the situation of the cantonal police. Dadò and Ferrara expressed strong dissent to the request for an extension, arguing that the verification requested is urgent and cannot be postponed. The original motion, submitted through Ti-Press, highlighted the need for immediate intervention to ensure the efficiency and transparency of the law enforcement authorities. The situation has sparked heated debate within the Ticino parliament, with the government justifying the request for an extension with the need for further analysis and preparation of the message. However, the motion presenters insist on the importance of acting promptly to address any critical issues that may have emerged from the audit. ### Implications for cross-border workers The audit of the Cantonal Police could have significant implications for cross-border workers who work and reside in Canton Ticino.
