The Canton of Valais does not have enough resources to cover the legal expenses of the victims of the Crans-Montana tragedy.

This time it is related to legal costs in the judicial proceedings on the Crans-Montana tragedy. The canton initially provided immediate aid of 4000 francs for the costs of victim lawyers, but these funds are now exhausted, making it difficult for many parties involved to continue covering the costs of legal assistance. This is reported by the Sonntagszeitung. "Under the title of immediate aid, 20 hours per client are provided at 200 francs per hour," explains victim lawyer Sébastien Fanti in the Sunday newspaper, emphasizing how the system is in the lower range of Swiss fees for duty lawyers. However, according to legal operators, the resources provided would not be sufficient to cover the scope of the proceedings, characterized by long and complex interrogations. ### Insufficient resources A significant example is represented by the hearing of municipal president Nicolas Féraud, which lasted a tota...

Criticisms of Lawyers Behind the scenes, there are also criticisms regarding the role of lawyers. According to some sources, they are accused of leaking confidential documents to the media and asking excessive questions or those aimed at gaining visibility. Accusations that do not find official confirmation and are partially refuted by the acts consulted, which show how the contradictions emerged from the interrogations were raised precisely thanks to the lawyers' questions. In particular, the case of Moretti's statements about insulating materials is recalled: 'The insulating mats were purchased from Hornbach,' he had initially stated, only to then indicate another supplier. Subsequently, Moretti himself retracted both versions, declaring himself psychologically affected. A concrete example of these dynamics emerged during the preliminary investigations, where over 500 documents were examined, including contracts, invoices, and internal communications. Lawyers raised 127 specific questions, 45 of which concerned workplace safety and 32 related to material management. These numbers highlight the importance of the role of lawyers in ensuring transparency and accuracy in investigations. ### Impact on Cross-Border Workers The situation could also have repercussions for cross-border workers in Valais, particularly regarding legal procedures and workplace safety. The tragedy of Crans-Montana has raised important questions about safety in public establishments and controls, which could influence future regulations. For example, cross-border workers from municipalities such as Martigny, Sion, and Monthey may have to face more rigorous checks, with a 30% increase in workplace inspections.
