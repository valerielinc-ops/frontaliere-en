Frequently Asked Questions
What is the current status of the agreement between the Canton and the Municipality of Lugano for the creation of a temporary seat for Justice in Viganello?
The agreement is in the balance due to differences between the Canton and the Municipality of Lugano on the location of the temporary headquarters.
How many employees are currently employed in the seat of Justice in Lugano and what are the main activities carried out?
The seat of Justice in Lugano houses over 200 employees and carries out clerkship, court and judicial police activities.
When will the provisional seat of Justice be ready in Viganello and what are the deadlines?
The provisional seat should be completed by the end of 2026, with the beginning of the work planned for the second half of 2024. The project includes a building of about 10,000 square meters, with an estimated cost of 10 million Swiss francs, financed almost entirely by the Canton of Ticino.
What will be the consequences for cross-border workers if the seat of Justice is moved from Lugano to Viganello?
If the headquarters are moved to Viganello, cross-border commuters will have to travel about 5 km more than in the centre of Lugano, increasing the average travel time by 10-15 minutes. The new building will be close to the train station, so those who use the train will be able to reduce parking costs, but those traveling by car will face higher rates for the urban area.
How will the new ten million francs provisional seat be financed and who supports costs?
The financing of the ten million francs temporary seat will be covered by 70 % from the Canton of Ticino, while 20 % will be allocated by the Lugano municipal budget. The remaining 10 % can be obtained through federal contributions for judicial infrastructure projects and, if necessary, by European funds for territorial cohesion.
