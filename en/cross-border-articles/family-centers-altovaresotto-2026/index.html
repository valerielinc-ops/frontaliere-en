Coordinators Chiara Ribaga and Maria Sartorio explained the work done alongside families, caregivers, youth, and seniors, with the goal of building a social network capable of listening, guiding, and supporting people through different life stages. ### The sandwich generation One of the central issues addressed is that of the so-called 'sandwich generation': adults who simultaneously take care of their children and elderly parents. An increasingly common situation that often leads to stress, isolation, and a sense of disorientation. The family centers seek to respond to these vulnerabilities by offering listening, pedagogical consultations, and guidance towards the services available in the area. In Cuveglio, for example, a pedagogist assists families in managing their children's educational dynamics and also in dealing with degenerative diseases such as Alzheimer's and Parkinson's that affect elderly parents. ### Workshops and support networks In addition to individual consultations, the Mete project offers group activities and workshops designed to create relationships and lighten the burden of daily care. Practical implications for cross-border workers The Family Centers in Alto Varesotto offer valuable support for cross-border workers living in Ticino and working in Lombardy. These centers can help families manage daily challenges, such as childcare and elderly care, by providing pedagogical consultations and guidance towards available local services. For cross-border workers, who often navigate between two different healthcare and educational systems, these centers can be a crucial point of reference. ### Comparison with the previous situation Before the establishment of the Family Centers, many families in Alto Varesotto had to face the difficulties related to caring for family members on their own. The lack of an adequate support network often led to increased stress and isolation. With the introduction of these centers, families now have access to free listening and guidance services, which can make a significant difference in managing complex situations. ### Concrete scenarios Imagine a cross-border worker family with an adolescent child and an elderly parent suffering from Alzheimer's. Thanks to the Family Centers, this family can receive support in managing the educational dynamics of the child and dealing with the parent's illness. The center's pedagogist can offer personalized consultations and guide the family towards the most suitable healthcare and social services for their needs. Additionally, art therapy workshops and caregiver exchange moments can help break the isolation and create a network of mutual support. ### Concrete procedures To access the services offered by the Family Centers, families can directly contact the centers present in the area. Frequently Asked Questions
Who can access the services of the Family Centers in Alto Varesotto?
All residents and cross-border workers who live or work in the Alto Varesotto area can access the services offered by the Family Centers. The services are free and open to families, caregivers, young people, and the elderly.
What services are offered by the Family Centers?
The Family Centers offer listening services, pedagogical consultations, guidance towards available local services, group workshops, and art therapy activities. Additionally, they organize moments of exchange among caregivers to break isolation and create a network of mutual support.
How can I contact the Family Centers in Alto Varesotto?
To contact the Family Centers, you can visit the website of the Mete project or directly reach out to the involved centers. Coordinators Chiara Ribaga and Maria Sartorio are available to provide information and support.
