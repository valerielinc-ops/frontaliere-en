Splitting health insurance initiatives proposed

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 29 April 2026

Fiorenzo Dadò proposes to handle the two health insurance initiatives approved in Ticino separately to avoid blockages and provide quick answers to citizens.

Contesto

In brief - Two initiatives on health insurance in Ticino - Proposal to split to avoid blockages - Risk of one initiative being shelved ## Key facts - What: Splitting the initiatives on health insurance - When: Proposal after two weeks since the State Council's plan presentation - Where: Canton Ticino - Who: Fiorenzo Dadò, day president - Amount: 60 million Swiss francs Two weeks after the State Council presented its plan to implement - partially from next year and definitively from 2029 - the health insurance initiatives approved in Ticino, political discontent remains widespread. The 60 million package does not convince the main parties and, reports Rsi, in Parliament an alternative is gaining traction: separating the two projects and treating them distinctly. At the end of the meeting and the Management session, day president Fiorenzo Dadò proposed the split, considering the initiatives different in nature and objectives. Proceeding together, he warned, risks blocking everything and not providing answers to citizens in the short term. The division would lead the Grand Council to express itself on two tracks: on one hand the extension of subsidies proposed by the Socialist Party, on the other the total relief of premiums supported by the Lega. The financing nodes remain open, which could also be delegated to the Government in the budget. However, concerns are not lacking: by separating the dossiers, one of the two could be shelved. From the PS, the guarantee is requested that both initiatives come into force, together or not. Analysis of the Implications The proposal to split the initiatives on health insurance could have a significant impact on cross-border workers in Ticino. The extension of subsidies proposed by the Socialist Party could offer broader financial support, while the total exemption of premiums supported by the Lega could reduce the tax burden for cross-border workers. However, the division of the dossiers could also lead to delays in the implementation of the measures, leaving cross-border workers in a situation of uncertainty for a longer period. ### Comparison between the two initiatives The extension of subsidies proposed by the Socialist Party aims to provide broader financial support to citizens, including cross-border workers. This could translate into an increase in subsidies for health insurance, thus reducing the final cost for workers. On the other hand, the total exemption of premiums supported by the Lega aims to completely eliminate health insurance premiums for certain categories of workers, offering an immediate and significant saving. ### Risks and Opportunities The separation of the dossiers presents both risks and opportunities. On the one hand, it could accelerate the approval of one of the initiatives, offering an immediate benefit to citizens. On the other hand, it could lead to the shelving of one of the measures, leaving part of the population without the expected support. Furthermore, the division could complicate the financing of the initiatives, making it more difficult to guarantee the necessary resources for both. ### Implementation Scenarios If the Grand Council decides to proceed with the splitting, there would be two possible scenarios. Frequently Asked Questions
What are the two initiatives on health insurance proposed in Ticino?
The two initiatives are the extension of subsidies proposed by the Socialist Party and the full exemption of premiums supported by the Lega. Both aim to provide financial support to citizens, but with different approaches.
Why was the separation of the initiatives proposed?
The separation was proposed to avoid blockages in the legislative process and to offer quicker responses to citizens. Fiorenzo Dadò warned that dealing with the initiatives together could delay the implementation of the measures.
What are the risks of separating the initiatives?
The risks include the possibility of one of the initiatives being shelved and the complication of financing. Separating the dossiers could make it more difficult to guarantee the necessary resources for both measures.
