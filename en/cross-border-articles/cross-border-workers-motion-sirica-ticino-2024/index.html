Cross-border workers: motion against workplace discrimination in Ticino

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 27 April 2026

Fabrizio Sirica presents motion to combat wage dumping and promote employment of residents

Contesto

In brief - Fabrizio Sirica presents motion against workplace discrimination - Massagno company seeks only cross-border workers for 2,900 CHF gross - Motion calls for cantonal strategy for resident employment ## Key facts - What: Motion against workplace discrimination - When: Recently presented - Where: Canton Ticino - Who: Fabrizio Sirica and Maurizio Canetta - Amount: 2,900 CHF gross per month for an 80% contract The socialist grand councillor Fabrizio Sirica has presented a motion to the State Council of Canton Ticino to combat workplace discrimination against residents. The motion, co-signed by Maurizio Canetta, follows the report of a discriminatory job advertisement by a company in Massagno. The advertisement, which seeks a temporary customer service specialist, specifies that the position is open exclusively to cross-border workers. The contract offered is for 80% with a gross salary of 34,800 francs annually, equivalent to 2,900 francs per month for 12 months. Sirica has already reported to the Labor Inspectorate Office the ambiguity of writing 34,800 francs for an 80% contract but indicating in the working hours 80/100%. The motion raises a generalized problem: wage dumping, which leads to preferring cross-border workers over residents because the former accept lower wages. This phenomenon creates social tension, higher unemployment compared to the Swiss average, and precarity. ### Analysis of the phenomenon Sirica has conducted a targeted analysis that confirms the thesis that the presence of cross-border workers is more marked in sectors with company collective agreements below the minimum wage threshold. This demonstrates that wage dumping is a widespread practice and that many companies speculate on this logic of exploitation to maximize profits. ###...

Dettagli operativi

Analysis of Implications The Sirica motion raises crucial questions regarding the labor market in the Canton of Ticino. Wage dumping not only harms residents but also creates an unstable and precarious work environment. The preference for cross-border workers, often justified by their willingness to accept lower wages, leads to higher unemployment rates compared to the Swiss average and greater social tension. ### Comparison with the Previous Situation Before the introduction of the minimum wage, the situation was even more critical. Low-value-added sectors were dominated by cross-border workers who accepted less favorable working conditions. With the introduction of the minimum wage, there is hope to improve working conditions for everyone, but strategic support is necessary to ensure that residents can benefit from these new opportunities. ### Future Scenarios If the motion is approved, there could be an increase in the hiring of residents in low-wage sectors. This could reduce unemployment and improve the economic stability of the Canton of Ticino. However, it is essential that companies collaborate with the authorities to implement the proposed measures. ### Coordination Among Entities Coordination between the Regional Employment Office, the Office of Social Support and Integration, economic associations, and companies is essential to ensure that integration policies are effective. This coordination should include the definition of clear and verifiable quantitative objectives, as well as an annual monitoring system to measure the evolution of the relationship between resident workers and cross-border workers. ### Training and Retraining Targeted support measures, such as training and retraining, are fundamental to facilitate the integration of residents int...

Punti chiave

Concrete actions For residents of the Canton of Ticino looking for work, it's important to be aware of the opportunities offered by the new policies. People seeking employment or receiving social assistance should contact the regional employment office for information on training and requalification programs. Companies, on the other hand, should collaborate with the authorities to define recruitment plans that favor the integration of residents. ### Procedure for residents 1. Contact the regional employment office for information on training and requalification programs. 2. Participate in the training programs offered to improve the skills required by the job market. 3. Apply for the available job positions, with the help of the regional employment office. 4. Monitor progress and job opportunities through the annual monitoring system. ### Procedure for companies 1. Collaborate with the regional employment office and economic associations to define recruitment plans. 2. Implement targeted support measures to facilitate the integration of residents. 3. Monitor progress and job opportunities through the annual monitoring system. 4. Participate in the training and requalification programs offered to improve the skills of employees. ### Useful tools For further information and to use useful tools, visit the salary calculator and the permit comparator. Frequently Asked Questions
What does the motion presented by Fabrizio Sirica provide?
The motion asks the State Council to present a Message that defines a binding cantonal strategy to accompany the introduction of the minimum wage, with clear and verifiable quantitative objectives for the professional integration of residents, an annual monitoring system, and an operational coordination device between various entities.
What are the practical implications of the motion?
The motion aims to reduce wage dumping and promote the employment of residents, improving the economic stability of the Canton of Ticino and reducing unemployment.
What can residents looking for work do?
Residents looking for work should contact the regional employment office for information on training and retraining programs, participate in these programs, apply for available job positions, and monitor progress and job opportunities through the annual monitoring system.
