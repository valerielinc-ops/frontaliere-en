Crans-Montana: 700 cases handled by victim support centers

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 1 May 2026

Almost 700 cases handled and 400,000 francs paid to victims of the New Year's Eve fire in Crans-Montana.

Contesto

In brief - 700 files managed for the New Year's Eve fire in Crans-Montana - 400'000 francs already paid - 400 files in Valais alone ## Key facts - What: Files managed by consultancies for victim assistance - When: After the New Year's Eve 2026 fire - Where: Crans-Montana, Valais - Who: Victim support consultancies - Amount: 400'000 francs paid Four months after the New Year's Eve fire at the bar Le Constellation in Crans-Montana, the victim support consultancies, established under the relevant regulation, are managing nearly 700 files, 400 of which are in Valais alone. So far, 400'000 francs have been paid in the canton. 'Throughout 2026, for the services provided by the Valais consultancies, we should reach one million francs,' said Roland Favre, head of the cantonal office for coordinating services, to Keystone-ATS, who wanted to take stock of the application of the Federal Law on Victim Assistance (LAV). 'As early as January 2nd, victims (including from abroad) contacted us to receive support and be informed about their rights regarding legal, financial and psychological assistance,' recalls Sonia Golay, cantonal head of the consultancies. Already operating at the limit of their capacity, the consultancies were able to benefit, as of January, from additional resources granted by the Valais State Council: the staff has increased from 10 to 15.4 full-time equivalent positions. Overall, the files relating to the Crans-Montana tragedy represent about a third of those currently open since the beginning of the year by the Valais consultancies (1'112). Frequently Asked Questions
What are the rights of crime victims in Switzerland?
Victims of crime in Switzerland are entitled to legal, financial, and psychological assistance through victim support centers. The Federal Law on Assistance to Victims of Crime (LAV) provides that immediate assistance is granted regardless of the financial means of the beneficiaries.
How can I contact victim support centers?
You can contact victim support centers through the official channels of the canton where you are located. Make sure to provide all necessary information to open a file and receive the required support.
Which documents are required to open a file?
To open a file, you need to provide documents such as your residence permit and documents related to the incident or crime you suffered. Make sure to have all the necessary documents to facilitate the process.
