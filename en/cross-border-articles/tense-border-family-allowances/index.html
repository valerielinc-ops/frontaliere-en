Dettagli operativi

The issue of family allowances for cross-border workers is regulated by precise rules aimed at avoiding double payments and ensuring fair treatment for workers and their families. Current regulations establish the principle of priority of the state of residence of the children, which in the case of Italian cross-border workers working in Switzerland means that the family allowance must be paid first by Italy. However, to ensure that this principle is correctly applied, it is essential that INPS provides accurate and complete information on the amount of family allowances received in Italy. This information is essential for the Swiss IAS (Institute of Social Insurance), which is responsible for calculating and paying family allowances for cross-border workers. The problem lies in the fact that INPS is not providing this information in a timely and accurate manner, thus creating the risk of double payments or excessive payments by Switzerland. Quadri's motion aims to resolve this situation by asking UFAS to intervene with INPS to ensure that the necessary information is loaded onto the dedicated platform correctly and promptly. The RINA platform, used for managing family allowances, has been at the center of some critical issues. INPS has encountered difficulties in loading the information relating to family allowances received in Italy, thus creating delays and inaccuracies in the process of paying allowances by Switzerland. Quadri's motion emphasizes the need for swift action by the Swiss authorities to resolve these critical issues and ensure that cross-border workers and their families receive the family allowances they are entitled to, without delays or errors. Frequently Asked Questions
How does the health tax for frontier workers in Switzerland and Italy work?
Health tax is a complex issue involving both countries. In Switzerland, border workers must pay health tax, while in Italy, workers are covered by a public health insurance system. In order to avoid duplication, Switzerland only intervenes to bridge the difference between the Italian and the Swiss, if the latter is higher.
What to do if there are irregularities in family allowances for frontier workers?
Report the problem to Swiss IAS or UFAS for immediate intervention.
What are the main risks of delays in family allowances for frontier workers?
Delays in practices can cause suspensions or delays in payments of family allowances, compromising economic support for families and increasing tensions between Switzerland and Italy. The lack of timely communication of accurate information from INPS to AIS can aggravate this situation.
