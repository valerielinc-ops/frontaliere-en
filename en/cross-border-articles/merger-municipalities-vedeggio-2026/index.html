Vedeggio municipalities take first step towards merger (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 8 May 2026

Bedano, Cadempino, Gravesano and Lamone submit formal request to State Council for aggregation study. Goal: shared project by spring 2028

Contesto

In brief - Four municipalities in the Vedeggio area submit formal request for aggregation study - Goal: shared project by spring 2028 - Municipalities linked by geographical and historical context ## Key facts - What: Aggregation study for possible municipal merger - When: Request submitted in May 2026 - Where: Municipalities of Bedano, Cadempino, Gravesano, and Lamone - Who: Municipal councils of the four municipalities - Population: Combined population of approximately 6,800 inhabitants The Municipal Councils of Bedano, Cadempino, Gravesano, and Lamone have decided to submit a formal request to the State Council to proceed with an aggregation study. This first step towards a possible municipal merger was confirmed by the mayor of Bedano, Dario Fraschina, in an interview with 'laRegione'. 'We are at the beginning of the operation, what we have done is only the first step,' Fraschina declared, emphasizing that discussions began last year and that another meeting is scheduled for next week. ### Context and motivations The four municipalities, which together reach approximately 6,800 inhabitants, are linked 'by the geographical and historical context, as well as by the numerous services they share,' as highlighted in a note released last March. The initiative is seen as an opportunity in the current political context, characterized by apparent cross-party support for the aggregation hypothesis. The goal is to reach 'a shared solution, in the interest of the entire community, aimed at strengthening the position of our four municipalities within the Vedeggio Valley area and in the cantonal context.' ### Next steps By spring 2028, a shared project is expected to be available for public consultation and subsequent approval by the municipal assemblies. Analysis of Opportunities and Challenges The possible merger of the four municipalities of Vedeggio represents an opportunity to strengthen the position of the Vedeggio Valley within the Canton of Ticino. However, the aggregation process also presents some challenges. One of the main concerns is ensuring that public services remain accessible and of high quality for all residents, including cross-border workers. ### Comparison with the Current Situation Currently, the four municipalities manage their services independently, which can lead to inefficiencies and fragmentation of services. A merger could lead to greater coordination and better allocation of resources. For example, joint management of schools and public transport could result in significant savings and an improvement in the quality of services. ### Possible Scenarios One possible scenario is that the merger will lead to greater integration of municipal services, making it easier for cross-border workers to access services such as healthcare and education. Furthermore, greater integration could facilitate communication between the different local authorities, improving the management of infrastructure and public services. ### Comparison with Other Municipal Mergers In Ticino, there have been other examples of municipal mergers that have led to significant improvements in the management of public services. For example, the merger of the municipalities of Lugano and Paradiso led to greater efficiency in the management of public transport and infrastructure. Similarly, the merger of the municipalities of Vedeggio could lead to similar improvements, making life easier for cross-border workers living and working in this area.
