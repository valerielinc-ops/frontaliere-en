Nurses in short supply: surge in arrivals from Tunisia, India and Paraguay

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 27 April 2026

The shortage of nurses in Italy is driving companies to recruit abroad, with over 1,000 professionals selected in the last three years.

Contesto

In brief - Shortage of 1 million nurses in Europe by 2030 - Over 1,000 professionals selected in the last three years - Lombardy and Piedmont are the most active regions ## Key facts - What: Shortage of nurses and healthcare staff in Italy - When: Current data and forecasts up to 2030 - Where: Italy, with a focus on Lombardy, Piedmont, Sardinia, Veneto, Calabria - Who: Openjobmetis Spa, International Recruitment - Amount: 1,000 professionals selected, 5,000 interviews abroad The shortage of nurses and healthcare staff in Italy has become a real emergency. According to the World Health Organization, by 2030 Europe will be short of 1 million nurses. This critical situation has pushed recruitment companies to seek solutions abroad. Openjobmetis Spa, through its International Recruitment division, has selected and trained over 1,000 healthcare professionals in the last three years, coming from countries such as Tunisia, India, and Paraguay. Lombardy is the region that has welcomed the largest number of these professionals, with 50% of activations, followed by Piedmont with 19%. Sardinia, Veneto, and Calabria complete the ranking of the regions most active in recruiting foreign healthcare staff. The motivations that push these professionals to move to Italy are mainly economic, with the possibility of improving professionally in our hospitals. ### Countries of origin and profile of professionals The countries of origin of the selected professionals are mainly Tunisia (30%), India (25%), and Paraguay (15%), followed by Brazil, Peru, El Salvador, and others. The profile of the nurses hired is 85% represented by women, of whom 70% have children. The average age is between 25 and 38 years, with an average previous professional experience of 5 years. Implications for cross-border workers and the healthcare system The shortage of healthcare personnel in Italy has a significant impact on cross-border workers in the sector. The presence of foreign professionals can influence the labor market dynamics, offering new opportunities but also creating competition. For cross-border workers in Ticino and neighboring regions, it is important to be aware of the new dynamics in the healthcare labor market. ### Comparison with the previous situation Before this wave of recruitment abroad, Italian healthcare facilities mainly relied on local personnel and some professionals from European Union countries. The arrival of nurses and healthcare professionals from non-European countries represents a significant novelty. This change can lead to greater cultural diversity within healthcare facilities, with benefits in terms of knowledge and skill exchange. ### Future scenarios With the increasing demand for healthcare personnel, it is likely that recruitment abroad will continue to grow. This could lead to greater integration of foreign professionals in the Italian healthcare system, with possible impacts on immigration policies and training requirements. What are the requirements to work as a nurse in Italy?
To work as a nurse in Italy, you need a recognized degree, a good command of the Italian language, and prior professional experience. Additionally, a period of on-the-job training is required to perfect Italian practices.
What are the job opportunities for cross-border workers in the healthcare sector?
Job opportunities for cross-border workers in the healthcare sector include working in Italian healthcare facilities, participating in training and professional development programs, and benefiting from cultural integration policies.
