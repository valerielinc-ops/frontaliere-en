Ticino's 2025 cantonal finances: criticism and challenges (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 9 June 2026

The Ticino parliament discusses the 2025 financial statement: 32.5 million deficit despite 80 million BNS profits. Criticism of tax relief and personnel management.

Contesto

In brief - The 2025 financial statement closes with a deficit of 32.5 million. - Without BNS profits, it would have been 112.6 million. - Criticism of tax relief and personnel management. ## Key facts - What: 2025 Cantonal financial statement Ticino. - When: Year 2025. - Where: Canton of Ticino. - Who: Council of State and Ticino Parliament. - Amount: Deficit of 32.5 million. The Ticino Parliament has entered into the debate on the 2025 financial statement of the cantonal finances. The budget closed with a deficit of 32.5 million francs, a better result than the 96 million forecasted. However, without the distribution of profits from the Swiss National Bank (BNS) amounting to 80 million, the deficit would have been 112.6 million. Fiorenzo Dadò, rapporteur of the majority report, criticized the Executive for inertia and lack of responsible financial policy. He emphasized the need for more incisive interventions at the level of direction and management, with clear priorities. The debate saw critical interventions from various political groups. The PS and the Greens presented a minority report rejecting the Council of State's proposal. Ivo Durisch of the PS highlighted how tax relief reduced revenues by about 300 million without generating the promised growth. The UDC also criticized the accounts, recalling the Morisoli decree of 2022, which called for a balanced budget by 2025, a goal not achieved. Alain Bühler of the UDC warned of a 'perfect financial storm' trajectory if the financial drift is not corrected. ### Critical issues and prospects The 2025 financial statement highlighted several critical issues, including personnel management and the consequences of tax relief. Dettagli operativi

Implications for Cross-Border Workers The decisions made on the Ticino Canton's finances have direct repercussions on cross-border workers who work in Ticino. The financial stability of the Canton is essential to maintain public services and the competitiveness of the local economy. Tax breaks, although criticized for not generating the expected growth, have had a direct impact on tax revenues and therefore on the services and infrastructure that cross-border workers also benefit from. ### Impact on Taxation The reduction in tax revenues due to tax breaks could translate into a greater tax burden in the future or cuts to services. Cross-border workers, who contribute to the Ticino tax system through withholding tax, could be affected by potential changes in fiscal policy. ### Services and Infrastructure Prudent management of the Canton's finances is crucial to maintaining the quality of public services, such as healthcare and education, which are essential for cross-border workers and their families. An increase in public debt or a reduction in investments in these sectors could have negative consequences on the quality of life of cross-border workers. ### Future Prospects The debate on the 2025 Consuntivo and future discussions on the 2027 Preventivo will be crucial in defining the Canton's fiscal and budgetary policies.
