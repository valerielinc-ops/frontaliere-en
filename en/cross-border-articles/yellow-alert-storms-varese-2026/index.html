Yellow alert for thunderstorms across the province of Varese

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 27 April 2026

The Civil Protection of the Lombardy Region has issued a yellow weather alert for thunderstorms across the entire province of Varese, with risks of damage and hydraulic criticalities.

Contesto

In brief - Yellow alert for thunderstorms in Varese - Risks of damage to roofs and structures - Possible localized hydrogeological instability ## Key facts - What: Yellow weather alert for thunderstorms - When: April 27, 2026, from 12:00 - Where: Province of Varese - Who: Civil Protection of the Lombardy Region - Amount: Not specified The Civil Protection of the Lombardy Region issued a weather alert today, Monday, April 27, 2026, signaling a yellow-level thunderstorm risk in part of the province of Varese. The Varesotto territory is divided into two homogeneous alert zones with different scenarios. The Lakes and Varesine Prealps area is subject to the yellow alert for thunderstorms already from 12 noon today, expiring at 14:00 tomorrow, Tuesday, April 28. The area of the lower province, which also includes part of the province of Varese together with Milan, Monza Brianza, Como, and Lecco, is instead subject to a yellow alert for thunderstorms starting from midnight tonight until 14:00 tomorrow. For both areas, the hydrogeological, hydraulic, and strong wind risks remain at the green level for the moment. On the meteorological level, in the afternoon and evening of today, scattered showers or thunderstorms are possible in the northern sectors, even of moderate intensity, with possible hail of small and medium size. During the day tomorrow, the widespread precipitation, expected during the night and in the morning, is forecast to gradually decrease in the afternoon. ### Risks and precautions Civil Protection asks local systems to maintain an operational phase of Attention, preparing for the prompt activation of monitoring actions. Analysis of Critical Issues The yellow alert issued by the Civil Protection of the Lombardy Region for the province of Varese represents an important signal for the population and, in particular, for cross-border workers who daily move between Ticino and Lombardy. The forecasted thunderstorms can have a significant impact on traffic and the safety of infrastructure, making specific precautions necessary. ### Impact on Traffic One of the main risks reported is represented by traffic issues. Intense thunderstorms can cause flooding, landslides, and falling trees, making roads and highways dangerous. For cross-border workers who daily use the connecting roads between Ticino and the province of Varese, it is fundamental to plan movements in advance and consider possible alternatives in case of road closures or slowdowns. ### Hydraulic and Hydrogeological Criticalities Intense precipitation can also cause hydraulic and hydrogeological criticalities, such as floods and landslides. Urban drainage networks might not be sufficient to manage the volume of water, increasing the risk of flooding in urban areas. It is therefore important that residents and cross-border workers verify the conditions of their homes and be ready to intervene in case of emergency. ### Preparation of Homes To minimize the damage caused by thunderstorms, it is advisable to adopt some preventive measures. For example, ensure that gutters are clean and functional, that windows and doors are well sealed, and that there are no objects outside that could be carried away by the wind. Additionally, it is useful to have an emergency kit with water, food, flashlight, and essential medications. ### Constant Monitoring Another crucial aspect is the constant monitoring of weather forecasts.
