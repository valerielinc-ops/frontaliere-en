Lombardy: 30 million for the neighborhood that revolutionizes bills

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 28 April 2026

A 30 million investment for the Livorno Parco neighborhood in Brescia, with savings of up to 60% on energy bills.

Contesto

In brief - 30 million investment for the Livorno Parco district in Brescia - Up to 60% savings on energy bills - Efficiency and safety improvements ## Key facts - What: Renovation of the Livorno Parco residential complex - When: Inaugurated on April 28, 2026 - Where: Brescia, Lombardy - Who: Lombardy Region and Aler Brescia-Cremona-Mantova - Amount: 30 million euros The Lombardy Region has allocated 30 million euros for the renovation of the Livorno Parco district in Brescia. The initiative, inaugurated on April 28, 2026, saw the participation of Paolo Franco, the regional assessor for Housing and Social Housing, and Giorgio Maione, the assessor for Environment and Climate. The project involved 94 apartments, 89 of which are owned by Aler, with interventions for seismic safety and energy efficiency. ### Interventions and Results The interventions involved buildings B, C, D, and H of the Aler houses at via Livorno 68. The buildings have been made safer and more efficient, with an expected savings of up to 60% on energy bills. The apartments now offer greater living comfort and healthier common areas. ### Investments and Tax Deductions The Lombardy Region contributed 12 million euros, covering the non-deductible portion of Aler Brescia-Cremona-Mantova's expenses. Thanks to tax deductions, the total investment amounted to 30 million euros, covering extraordinary structural interventions. ### Implications for Cross-Border Workers This project could also have implications for cross-border workers residing in Ticino and working in Lombardy.
