Parents and citizens' committee protest against the closure of Socco primary school in Fino Mornasco

Contesto

In brief - Socco primary school in Fino Mornasco closed by order - 13 families enrolled without consent for transfer - Administration justifies with lack of funds ## Key facts - What: Closure of the "G. Rodari" school complex in Socco - When: 2026/2027 school year - Where: Fino Mornasco, Como province - Who: "Rodari" Socco Citizens' Committee - Amount: 300,000 euros invested in the "C. Colombo" complex in Valle Mulini The anger of parents and the "Rodari" Socco Citizens' Committee has erupted in Fino Mornasco, where the decision to close the "G. Rodari" primary school in Socco has been made. The decision, which will come into force from the 2026/2027 school year, has sparked protests and petition drives, with 830 signatures collected to oppose the closure. The "Rodari" Citizens' Committee has denounced that 13 families had chosen to enroll their children in Socco after an Open Day held on December 13, 2025. However, on February 25, 2026, the Ministry accepted the enrollments, but subsequently, without the parents' consent, the students were transferred to the "C. Colombo" complex in Valle Mulini. The families stated that they had never signed any transfer request. The Administration justified the closure by stating that the building in Valle Mulini is more modern and up to code. However, it does not appear that funds were requested to bring the Socco complex up to code, despite the availability of substantial post-Covid funds, including those from the PNRR. Moreover, the Socco complex already has a disabled bathroom, a ramp and handrail access, and a ground-floor gym without barriers. Dettagli operativi

Analysis of Motivations The Administration justified the closure of the Socco building by stating that the Valle Mulini building is more modern and up to code. However, this claim was contested by the 'Rodari' Citizens' Committee, which highlighted the availability of funds for bringing school buildings up to code, including those from the PNRR. The Committee emphasized that the Socco building already has accessible facilities, such as a disabled bathroom and a ground-floor gym without barriers. ### Impact on Families The closure of the Socco building will create economic and organizational difficulties for families. Many of them may be forced to enroll their children in schools in neighboring municipalities, such as Vertemate con Minoprio or Cadorago, which are logistically closer and easier to reach. This could increase transportation costs and generate additional stress for families already engaged in cross-border work. ### Future Scenarios If the closure of the Socco building proceeds, it is likely that many families will choose to enroll their children in schools in other municipalities. This could lead to a reduction in the number of students in the schools of Fino Mornasco, with consequent repercussions on the quality of the educational offer and the vitality of the local schools.
