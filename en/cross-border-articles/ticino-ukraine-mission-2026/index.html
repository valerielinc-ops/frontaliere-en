Ticinese volunteers on humanitarian mission in Ukraine: the seventh mission of HUNPA

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 27 April 2026

Ticinese volunteers are preparing to bring medical assistance and comfort to the Ukrainian population affected by the war.

Contesto

In brief - Ticinese volunteers on humanitarian mission in Ukraine - Specific training to operate in war zones - Seventh mission for the HUNPA association ## Key facts - What: Humanitarian mission in Ukraine - When: Weekend, unspecified date - Where: Ronco Bedretto, Ticino - Who: HUNPA association - Number: Between 4 and 6 volunteers The Ticinese volunteer association Humanitarian Nursing and Paramedical Aid (HUNPA) has trained its new volunteers in preparation for their seventh mission in Ukraine. This weekend, in Ronco Bedretto, the volunteers prepared to provide medical assistance and comfort to the Ukrainian population affected by the conflict. The mere presence of the volunteers brings relief, in addition to the distribution of essential goods, food, and medicines. Yves Toutounghi, vice-president of HUNPA, explains that "when they see us arrive, the first thing they tell us is that they don't feel abandoned." The association's goal, since 2003, has been to provide healthcare in disadvantaged areas suffering the effects of war. The volunteers visit people, help with health needs, and distribute medicines, also bringing a gesture of solidarity. ### Motivations and training Cristiano Fusetti, one of the volunteers, recounts having always been interested in humanitarian volunteering since his youth, when he approached the world of the Red Cross. Specific training for future missions is fundamental. "If I know how to protect myself and if I reflect on what I need to do, I've done 50% of the work," emphasizes Toutounghi. The preparation includes learning field dynamics; after theoretical training, the volunteers will "go into the field" to understand how to move strategically, what to observe, and how to behave in a group. Practical Analysis: Challenges and Opportunities The humanitarian mission in Ukraine presents a significant challenge for Ticinese volunteers. Operating in a war zone requires specific preparation and strong situational awareness. Volunteers must be ready to face difficult conditions and provide medical assistance and comfort to the local population. Theoretical and practical training is fundamental to ensure the safety and effectiveness of field operations. ### Challenges in the Field War zones present numerous risks and challenges for volunteers. Situational awareness is crucial for operating safely. Volunteers must be able to constantly assess the surrounding environment and quickly adapt to emergency situations. Specific training includes learning field dynamics, such as moving strategically, observing the environment, and behaving in groups. These elements are essential to ensure the safety of volunteers and the effectiveness of operations. ### Opportunities for Solidarity The humanitarian mission also offers opportunities for solidarity and international cooperation. Ticinese volunteers, along with those from other regions, contribute to strengthening ties between communities and promoting values of mutual support. The presence of volunteers brings comfort and hope to affected populations, demonstrating that they are not alone in facing the difficulties of war. ### Conclusion The HUNPA mission in Ukraine is an example of humanitarian commitment and international solidarity. Ticinese volunteers, with their training and preparation, are ready to provide assistance and comfort to populations affected by war.
