One in three Ticinese refuels in Italy (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 6 May 2026

The rise in fuel prices in Italy is pushing cross-border workers to refuel in Ticino. Here's what changes

Contesto

In brief - Gasoline price in Italy soared to 1.90 euros - One in three Ticinese refuels across the border - Expiry of Italian measure: May 22nd ## Key facts - What: Increase in gasoline price in Italy - When: May 6, 2026 - Where: Italy and Ticino - Who: Ticinese cross-border workers - Amount: From 1.70 to 1.90 euros per liter The sudden 20-cent increase per liter in the price of gasoline in Italy has had immediate repercussions in Ticino. The cost at the pump went from around 1.70 euros to 1.90 euros in just a few hours, following decisions by the Italian Council of Ministers. The extension of the tax cut on fuels was confirmed until May 22, but with a clear distinction between diesel and gasoline. While the tax discount for diesel remained unchanged at 20 cents per liter, for gasoline the reduction was cut to just 5 cents. ### Effects on the border The move has had immediate repercussions in Ticino, partially reducing the heavy competitive disadvantage that suffocates Ticinese gas stations. Boris Martinoni, CEO of ECSA Energy and spokesperson for the Ticinese Association of Service Stations (ATSS), confirmed that the Italian decision has rebalanced, at least partially, the price gap across the border, at least as far as gasoline is concerned. Currently, the price of gasoline has stabilized both in Ticino and in the nearby Peninsula around 1.90 euros. Martinoni, however, points out that, despite the progress, the obstacle of currency exchange remains: with the strong franc, refueling in Italy is still slightly more convenient at the same nominal price. ### Significant losses for the sector Despite the partial realignment, the situation for Ticinese distributors remains critical. Martinoni emphasizes how the current minimal gap is not enough to generate new queues...

With the price of gasoline stabilized at 1.90 euros both in Italy and Ticino, many cross-border workers are considering whether to refuel in Italy or Switzerland. However, the currency exchange remains an obstacle, making refueling in Italy still slightly more convenient. ### Comparison between Italy and Ticino | Parameter | Italy | Ticino | |-----------------------|------------------------|------------------------| | Gasoline price | 1.90 euros per liter | 1.90 euros per liter | | Tax discount | 5 cents per liter | Not specified | | Convenience | Slightly more convenient due to currency exchange | Slightly less convenient due to currency exchange | ### Future Scenarios With the expiration of the Italian measure on May 22, many cross-border workers are waiting to see if there will be further changes in gasoline prices. The lack of a long-term vision makes it difficult for Ticino distributors to plan effective containment measures. This could lead to further loss of customers and a negative impact on employment. ### Employment Impact The employment impact is already evident with less work for truck drivers and gas station staff. With a drop in volumes between 30% and 40% in the last two months, many workers in the sector could find themselves in difficulty.
