In brief - Lidl Switzerland will have a Swiss CEO for the first time - Michael Kunz will replace Nicholas Pennanen at the end of summer 2026 - Kunz is currently CEO of Lidl Austria ## Key facts - What: Change of CEO at Lidl Switzerland - When: End of summer 2026 - Where: Frauenfeld (TG) - Who: Michael Kunz, current CEO of Lidl Austria - Amount: Not specified Frauenfeld (TG) - Change of guard at Lidl Switzerland: the current CEO Nicholas Pennanen will leave his position at the end of summer to take on new roles within the group at an international level. He will be replaced by Michael Kunz, a Swiss national who is currently the number one at Lidl in Austria. 'Over the past three years, Nicholas Pennanen has left an indelible mark at Lidl Switzerland,' reads a statement today. 'Thanks to the launch of the private label 'Qualité Suisse', attention to customer needs, and a consistent expansion strategy, he and his team have significantly increased market shares in Switzerland.' For Kunz, the change means a return to the country where he was born. The executive has been active at Lidl Switzerland since 2011, before becoming the managing director of the Austrian branch in 2024. 'His career path underscores the importance that Lidl places on the internal promotion of young talents,' reads the note. 'Nicholas is handing me a well-oiled team and a company in excellent health, which offers me the best premises for the future,' states Kunz, quoted in the press document. 'I am really happy to return to my home country and to continue, together with my colleagues, the successful path of Lidl Switzerland,' adds the 41-year-old.
