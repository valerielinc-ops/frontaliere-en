Farewell to Salandin, historic Cgil leader

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 30 April 2026

The Varese trade union world is in mourning for the passing of Giovanni Salandin, a historic Cgil leader who dedicated his life to defending workers' rights.

In brief - Giovanni Salandin, a historic Cgil leader, has passed away. - Final farewell in Brissago Valtravaglia on April 30, 2026. - He curated the 'Rido!' exhibition for the Cgil centennial. ## Key facts - What: Passing of Giovanni Salandin, historic Cgil leader. - When: April 30, 2026. - Where: Brissago Valtravaglia, Ticino. - Who: Cgil of Varese and cross-border workers. - Amount: Not specified. The Varese trade union world is in mourning for the passing of Giovanni Salandin, a historic leader of the Cgil who marked decades of struggles and cultural initiatives in the territory. A respected figure for his balance and dedication, Salandin managed to combine the defense of workers' rights with a deep passion for information and training, leaving an indelible mark on various categories, from graphic workers to cross-border commuters. Giovanni Salandin's career began directly in the field, as a union delegate at the Plv cartotecnica in Daverio. In that productive environment, he became known and appreciated by colleagues and coworkers for his constant commitment, which led him, shortly after the mid-1980s, to become the provincial secretary of Filis, the federation that brought together workers in information and entertainment. A crucial step in his career occurred in 1996 when he was elected general secretary of Slc, the new name born from the unification between Filis and the Postelegrafonici. It was a complex task that Salandin faced with great dedication, managing to bring together very different working cultures. At the same time, he never forgot the importance of transmitting values to new generations, regularly going to Enaip in Varese to teach students the basics of union rights. ### Protection of cross-border workers and the 'Rido!' exhibition Between 2001...

Dettagli operativi

Giovanni Salandin's commitment to cross-border workers has been fundamental for the economy of the province of Varese. His dedication has improved contractual and working conditions for many cross-border workers, who represent a significant part of the workforce in the region. The 'Rido!' exhibition was a major cultural initiative that involved important figures from the world of culture and information. This project not only celebrated the centenary of the CGIL but also contributed to raising public awareness about workers' rights and the importance of trade unions. ### Practical implications for cross-border workers Cross-border workers who have benefited from Salandin's work can continue to refer to the contractual achievements obtained thanks to his commitment. It is important that workers are aware of their rights and the trade union protections available, especially in a complex cross-border context like that between Italy and Switzerland. For young people who wish to pursue a trade union path, Salandin's example represents a model of dedication and passion. His experience in teaching young students at ENAIP Varese demonstrates the importance of transmitting trade union values to new generations, thus ensuring the continuity of the commitment to workers' rights. ### Comparisons and scenarios Before Salandin's arrival, the situation of cross-border workers was more precarious, with fewer contractual protections and fewer opportunities for training. Thanks to his work, significant improvements have been achieved, making cross-border work safer and more dignified. In a context where workers' rights are often at risk, Salandin's legacy represents an important point of reference. What were the main contributions of Giovanni Salandin for cross-border workers?
