Ticino ranks 8th in Switzerland for attracting companies (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 30 May 2026

New Crif data reveals Ticino's net growth in attracting businesses from other cantons in 2024.

Contesto

In brief - Ticino ranks eighth among cantons for attracting businesses. - In 2024, 53 new companies registered a net increase from other regions. - Zug, Solothurn, and Grisons lead the national ranking. ## Key facts - What: Transfer of companies between Swiss cantons - When: Data for 2024 - Where: Canton Ticino and Swiss territory - Who: Crif, based on the Commercial Register - Amount: 53 net transfers in Ticino Ticino confirms itself as an attractive destination for the national economic fabric, ranking eighth among Swiss cantons that, in 2024, have attracted the highest number of companies from other Swiss regions. The analysis was conducted by Crif, a company specializing in solvency checks, anti-fraud actions, and information for individuals and businesses, using official evidence from the Commercial Register. With a net balance of 53 new companies established, Ticino aligns with the results obtained by the Canton of St. Gallen, demonstrating a consistent ability to attract businesses in the Swiss economic landscape. ### National dynamics The national ranking sees cantons that have recorded significant net growth at the top: Zug leads with 68 transfers, followed by Solothurn with 67 and Grisons with 66. In contrast to these regions, large urban centers such as Zurich and Bern experienced an outflow of companies, recording a net loss of 252 and 117 units, respectively. Overall, during the year, the movement of companies across the Swiss territory was intense: out of 38,081 companies that relocated their domicile, 80.8% did so within their canton of origin, while 7,517 made an intercantonal transfer. The arrival of new businesses in the Canton of Ticino is a key indicator of the evolution of the local labor market. For those living in Italy and working in Switzerland, the dynamism of these businesses directly impacts job stability and career opportunities. The statistic of 53 more companies, although an aggregated figure, reflects a vitality that translates into a constant demand for labor, including specialized labor from bordering territories. In case of transfer or new opportunities, it is always necessary to verify the correct management of the G permit and the implications deriving from the withholding tax, which remains the cornerstone of taxation for those who work in Switzerland but reside in Italy. The current regulations, including the details of the new tax agreement, precisely define how incomes should be treated. Frequently Asked Questions
What does the figure of 53 more companies mean for Ticino?
The figure of 53 companies represents the net growth, i.e., the balance between the companies that have transferred their headquarters to Ticino and those that have left the canton during 2024. This number places Ticino in eighth position in the Swiss national ranking, confirming its ability to attract new productive or administrative activities from other regions of the Confederation.
Which cantons attracted the most companies in 2024?
According to data published by Crif, the cantons that recorded the highest number of net incoming transfers are Zug (68), Solothurn (67), and Grisons (66). Ticino, with 53 units, is in eighth place, tied with the Canton of St. Gallen, demonstrating a prominent position in the Swiss economic context despite strong competition from other centers.
Can companies moving to Ticino influence the work of cross-border commuters?
Yes, the arrival of new companies directly affects the employment dynamics of the Canton of Ticino. Since companies often require qualified professional figures, their establishment can translate into new job opportunities. For cross-border commuters, this means a more dynamic market, which however requires constant attention to tax compliance, social security, and the correct management of work permits according to current regulations.
