In brief - Building permit has become final - Asbestos removal underway - Three years of work planned ## Key facts - What: Eiger Palace project in Besso, Lugano - When: Permit became final in January 2026 - Where: Corner of via Basilea and via Sorengo, Besso district - Who: Sofis Sa, architects Tibiletti Associati - Amount: Underground parking for 140 cars The building permit for the Eiger Palace project in Besso, Lugano, has become final without any major issues. The project, located at the corner of via Basilea and via Sorengo, in the Besso district above Lugano station, is finally entering the operational phase. Currently, asbestos removal is underway, being carried out with all necessary safety measures. Completion of this phase is expected by the end of the month. Subsequently, tenders and final agreements with banks will be defined, after which the construction site will be launched. The Eiger Palace project is set to transform the entire district. The L-shaped building will extend for 92 and 84 meters and will house offices and commercial activities. Between the two branches, a park and an urban portico will arise, connecting the main access roads, as well as an underground parking lot for 140 cars. Despite some opposition, these have been resolved through agreements with neighbors. ### Impact on the territory The project promoter emphasized attention to the impact on the territory.
