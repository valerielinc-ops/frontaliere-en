Drought in Ticino: summer 2026 at risk

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 1 May 2026

April 2026 was the driest in recent years, with precipitation at 10% of the average. Fire risk and critical water basins.

Contesto

In brief - April 2026 was extremely dry - Risk of forest fires in Ticino - Water reservoirs below seasonal levels ## Key facts - What: Drought situation - When: April 2026 - Where: Switzerland, particularly Ticino and Grisons - Who: MeteoSwiss - Amount: 10% of average precipitation Despite the recent rains, the precipitation situation in the first months of 2026 remains well below average. April was historically dry, with very low soil moisture levels and an increasing risk of forest fires, particularly in eastern Switzerland and Ticino. In several regions near the Alps, including the Valposchiavo, there is a strict ban on lighting fires outdoors. According to MeteoSwiss data, the ground is dry to a depth of 40-50 centimeters, and water reservoirs are also below seasonal levels: in Grisons, the fill level is around 10%, compared to an average of 20% for this period. ### Immediate consequences The drought situation has already led to immediate consequences, such as the ban on lighting fires outdoors in various regions. This ban has been imposed to prevent forest fires, which have already been reported in several areas of Ticino and eastern Switzerland. Furthermore, the lack of rain has led to a significant drop in water reservoir levels, with potential consequences for agriculture and water supply. ### Future prospects According to climatologist Thomas Schlegel, this April will likely rank among the driest since records began. The month was also about 2.5 degrees warmer than average and particularly sunny. The main cause is a persistent high-pressure situation over central Europe, which blocks Atlantic disturbances. Analysis of the implications The drought in Ticino and Eastern Switzerland has significant implications for various sectors, including agriculture, water supply, and land management. The lack of rain has already led to a drop in water basin levels, with potential consequences for agriculture and water supply. Additionally, the risk of forest fires is high, requiring rigorous preventive measures. ### Impact on agriculture Agriculture is one of the sectors most affected by the drought. The lack of rain can lead to a reduction in crops and a decline in agricultural production. This could have a significant impact on the local economy and the availability of agricultural products. It is crucial that farmers adopt measures to mitigate the effects of drought, such as using efficient irrigation systems and choosing crops more resistant to drought. ### Impact on water supply Drought can also have a significant impact on water supply. The lack of rain can lead to a drop in water basin levels, with potential consequences for the supply of drinking water. It is essential that local authorities closely monitor water conditions and take measures to ensure adequate water supply. This could include water rationing and awareness campaigns to reduce water consumption. ### Risk of forest fires The risk of forest fires is high in drought conditions.
