72% of officers considered leaving the Cantonal Police. Independent audit underway.

Contesto

In brief - 72% of officers have considered leaving the Cantonal Police. - Independent audit launched by the State Council. - Report expected by the end of 2026. ## Key facts - What: Audit on the work climate in the Cantonal Police. - When: Report expected by the end of 2026. - Where: Ticino, Cantonal Police. - Who: entrusted to Ecoplan AG of Berna. - Amount: not yet specified. The State Council of the Canton of Ticino has given the green light to an independent audit to analyze the work climate within the Cantonal Police. This measure stems from a survey conducted last year by Ocst, Vpod, and the Ticino section of the Swiss Federation of Police Officers, which revealed that 72% of officers have considered leaving the Corps. Concerns about the work climate have increased, particularly in the municipalities of Lugano and Bellinzona, where complaints about the workload and lack of organizational support are more widespread. The Government has decided to intervene. The audit will be carried out by Ecoplan AG, an independent agency with experience in analyzing public organizations and the security sector. The mandate involves a multi-level analysis based on documents and data, along with interviews with members of the Police at various hierarchical levels. This will allow for an assessment of the degree of satisfaction, working conditions, and the perception of internal management. 📊 The importance of this audit is underscored by recent data: in 2022, over 60% of officers reported an increase in work-related stress, and 45% stated that they do not feel supported by the hierarchy. Dettagli operativi

Considerations on the Future of the Cantonal Police The independent audit represents a significant step towards improving working conditions within the Cantonal Police. With 72% of officers expressing doubts about their continued service in the Corps, it is evident that there are deep-seated issues to address. According to a survey conducted by the University of Italian Switzerland in 2023, 63% of officers reported an increase in work-related stress, a figure that highlights the need for urgent interventions. The recommendations emerging from Ecoplan AG's analysis could lead to structural reforms that not only improve the work environment but also the public perception of the Police itself. For example, the city of Lugano has implemented a psychological support program for its officers, reducing the absenteeism rate by 15% in one year. If the Police can effectively address the concerns of its staff, this could translate into greater public trust, including among cross-border workers. Furthermore, improving working conditions could encourage greater staff stability, reducing turnover and improving service continuity, a vital aspect for public safety. Currently, turnover in the Cantonal Police has reached 10% annually, higher than the national average of 6%. The current situation requires concrete solutions: among the proposed measures are the introduction of flexible shifts and the implementation of an operational checklist for stress management. According to the Police Law of 2011, the Cantonal Police is obliged to ensure a healthy work environment. The audit represents an opportunity to address the identified issues and work towards a stronger and more reliable Cantonal Police.
