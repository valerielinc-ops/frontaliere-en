Luino: Community House opens with single access point

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 30 April 2026

As of Friday, May 1st, the Single Access Point is operational in the new Community House of Luino, funded with PNRR resources.

Contesto

In brief - Community House in Luino: Single Access Point now operational - Healthcare services and specialist clinics on the way - 1.5 million euro PNRR funding ## Key facts - What: Activation of the Single Access Point (PUA) in the Community House of Luino - When: From 1st May 2026 - Where: Luino, at the entrance of the hospital - Who: ASST Sette Laghi - Amount: 1.5 million euros (PNRR funds) Since Friday, 1st May, the Single Access Point (PUA) has been operational in the new Community House of Luino. The facility, created through a renovation funded with PNRR funds for approximately 1.5 million euros, is located on the first floor of the new building at the hospital entrance. The service will be accessible without appointment from Monday to Friday from 8.30 am to 12.30 pm and on Saturday from 8.30 am to 2.30 pm. Inside, Family and Community Nurses operate with a proximity-oriented model, also integrated by the itinerant PUA. The Community House will host various services: home care (ADI), prosthetics, vaccinations, disability services, and specialist clinics including Cardiology, Diabetology, and Neurology. The CUP, Blood Drawing Point, and Continuity of Care remain active at the hospital site. ### Services on the way Some completion works are still underway, particularly on the ground floor and part of the second floor, as well as the activation of the elevator. The new structure represents a significant step for local healthcare, improving accessibility and service integration for the citizens of Luino and surrounding areas. ### Implications for cross-border workers For cross-border workers who commute to Switzerland and reside in Italy, the new Community House in Luino offers facilitated access to integrated healthcare services. Practical Analysis The new Casa di Comunità in Luino represents a significant step forward for the integration of healthcare services. For cross-border workers who commute to Switzerland and reside in Italy, this facility offers facilitated access to a comprehensive range of healthcare services, reducing the need to travel between different structures. ### Advantages for Cross-Border Workers One of the main advantages is the ability to access integrated healthcare services in a single location. This is particularly useful for those who require continuous assistance or specialized services. For example, a cross-border worker who works in Lugano and lives in Luino can benefit from Cardiology, Diabetology, and Neurology services without having to cross the border multiple times. ### Comparison with Other Facilities Compared to other healthcare structures in the region, the Casa di Comunità in Luino offers a proximity-oriented care model. This means that services are organized to be easily accessible and integrated with each other. For example, the mobile PUA unit allows reaching even the most peripheral areas, ensuring uniform assistance across the entire territory. ### Concrete Scenarios Imagine a cross-border worker who needs a cardiological check-up and a prosthetic service. In the past, they would have had to go to different facilities to obtain both services. Now, thanks to the Casa di Comunità, they can access both services in one place, saving time and simplifying the management of their health. ### Tax Implications For cross-border workers, it is important to consider the tax implications related to the use of healthcare services in Italy. Action The new Casa di Comunità in Luino is already operational and offers a range of integrated healthcare services. For cross-border workers who commute to Switzerland and reside in Italy, this facility provides an opportunity to access quality healthcare services without having to cross the border multiple times. ### Procedure to access services To access the services of the Casa di Comunità, citizens and cross-border workers can go to the Single Access Point located on the first floor of the new building at the entrance of the hospital. The service is accessible without an appointment from Monday to Friday from 8:30 AM to 12:30 PM and on Saturday from 8:30 AM to 2:30 PM. ### Required documents To access the services, you need to present a valid ID and, if available, the health card. For cross-border workers, it is advisable to also bring the G permit and documents related to health insurance. ### Deadlines and appointments At the moment, there are no specific deadlines for accessing the services. However, for specialist services, it may be necessary to book an appointment once the renovation work is completed. ### Useful tools To learn more about the tax and pension implications for cross-border workers, consult the salary calculator and the insurance comparator. ### Conclusion The new Casa di Comunità in Luino represents a significant step forward in the integration of healthcare services.
