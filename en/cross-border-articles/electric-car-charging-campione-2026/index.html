For cross-border workers who daily cross the border between Italy and the Canton of Ticino, this infrastructure provides a concrete opportunity to reduce waiting times and improve the travel experience. Additionally, its strategic location along Lake Lugano makes it ideal for tourists visiting the area year-round. ## Comparison with the Previous Situation Before the activation of this station, cross-border workers and tourists often had to search for charging stations in other locations, with longer waiting times and less convenience. For example, a cross-border worker from Lugano working in Campione d’Italia often had to go to Lugano or Mendrisio to charge their electric vehicle, with waiting times that could reach up to 45 minutes. The new station in Campione d’Italia offers a more efficient service, with higher power charging columns (up to 350 kW) and a simplified payment system. Frequently Asked Questions
Where is the new Ewiva charging station for electric vehicles in Campione d 'Italia and how many points does it have?
The new Ewiva station is located in Campione d 'Italia, in Piazzale Maestri Campionesi, opposite the Casino. It is equipped with 3 High Power Charging infrastructures: two 150 kW and one 300 kW columns, offering a total of 6 charging points. The location is strategic for border crossers and tourist travel.
What payment methods are accepted to recharge the electric car at the Ewiva stations in Campione d 'Italia?
The Ewiva stations in Campione d 'Italia accept contactless payments via credit, debit or prepaid cards (Mastercard, Visa, VPay, Maestro), as well as Apple Pay and Google Pay. You can also use the app or an operator RFID card interoperable with Ewiva, and Plug&Charge technology for compatible vehicles.
How long does it take to charge an electric car at Campione d 'Italia station compared to Lugano or Chiasso?
Campione d 'Italia station offers columns up to 300 kW. For comparison, the Ewiva station in Lugano (4x150 kW) allows a full charge in about 20-30 minutes, while that of Chiasso (2x150 kW) takes about 25-35 minutes. The new station in Campione offers a more efficient and faster service.
Are there incentives for the purchase of electric vehicles for border crossers travelling between Italy and Switzerland?
Yes, in Italy the 2020 Relaunch Decree provides for contributions of up to 10,000 euros for low-emission vehicles and tax reductions for private stations. In Switzerland, the Canton of Ticino offers incentives of up to 6,000 Swiss francs for the purchase of electric vehicles and up to 500 francs for private stations, with mandatory infrastructure for new construction.
How does the new charging station in Campione d 'Italia improve border crossers' movements?
The new Campione d 'Italia station, with ultra-fast columns up to 300 kW, significantly reduces the waiting time for charging to about 20-30 minutes. Before, border workers had to look for stations elsewhere with waits of up to 45 minutes. Its strategic location improves the efficiency and convenience of daily flows between Italy and Canton Ticino.
