Olgiate Olona: smart home for disabled via Pnrr funds (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 5 May 2026

A smart home for people with disabilities has been inaugurated in Olgiate Olona, funded by the Pnrr. Goal: housing and work autonomy.

Contesto

In brief - New smart home for disabled people in Olgiate Olona - PNRR funding of 715,000 euros - Project for housing and work autonomy ## Key facts - What: Smart home for disabled people - When: Inaugurated in May 2026 - Where: Olgiate Olona, historic center - Who: Municipality of Olgiate Olona, Medio Olona Special Consortium Company - Amount: 715,000 euros (PNRR funding) This morning, a new apartment for people with disabilities was inaugurated in the historic center of Olgiate Olona as part of the 'Paths to Autonomy' project, funded by the PNRR. The initiative aims to promote housing independence and work integration, strengthening the network of services in the area. The inauguration was attended by representatives of the municipalities in the Castellanza area, the Medio Olona Servizi alla Persona Special Consortium Company, the cooperatives involved in the project, and the social workers who follow the individualized paths of the beneficiaries. Leonardo Richiusa, deputy mayor and councilor for social services of the Municipality of Olgiate Olona, welcomed the guests. Paola Chiara Bottazzi, coordinator of the Planning Office of the Castellanza Area (lead agency Medio Olona Servizi alla Persona Special Consortium Company), and Fausto Panizza of the Lavoro e Solidarietà Social Cooperative spoke about how the project was born and developed. Maria Raffaela Rinaldi, an educator from the Lavoro e Solidarietà Social Cooperative, accompanied the young people involved in the project and read a reflection on the path they have undertaken. Dettagli operativi

Implications for cross-border workers The "Percorsi di autonomia" project represents a significant opportunity for cross-border workers who work in the Canton Ticino and reside in Italy. The housing and professional autonomy promoted by the project can have a positive impact on the quality of life of cross-border workers with disabilities, offering them greater opportunities for integration and independence. ### Comparison with the previous situation Before the start of this project, people with disabilities in the Olgiate Olona area and surroundings often relied on temporary housing solutions or family support. The creation of dedicated smart apartments represents a significant step forward, offering permanent and safe housing solutions. Furthermore, support for professional integration through internships and occupational paths supported by dedicated tutors adds value to the economic autonomy of the beneficiaries. ### Concrete scenarios Imagine the case of a cross-border worker who works in Ticino and resides in one of the locations involved in the project. Thanks to the smart apartment, this person could benefit from a safe and autonomous environment, with advanced technologies that facilitate daily life. Moreover, professional integration supported by the project could offer opportunities for professional and personal growth, improving overall quality of life. ### Concrete procedures To access the benefits of the project, interested cross-border workers with disabilities will need to follow a specific procedure. First, it will be necessary to contact the Azienda Speciale Consortile Medio Olona Servizi alla Persona for a preliminary assessment. What to do to access the project For cross-border workers interested in participating in the "Paths to Autonomy" project, it is important to follow some concrete steps. First of all, it is necessary to contact the Azienda Speciale Consortile Medio Olona Servizi alla Persona for a preliminary assessment. This assessment will serve to determine the candidate's suitability and to define a personalized path. Subsequently, a multidisciplinary team composed of social workers, educators, psychologists and employment operators will assess the specific needs and propose an intervention plan. ### Deadlines and required documents No specific deadlines have been set yet for the submission of applications, but it is advisable to contact the Azienda Speciale Consortile Medio Olona Servizi alla Persona promptly to obtain updated information. Among the documents required for the preliminary assessment, it is likely that the following will be requested: identity documents, medical certificates attesting to the disability, and any work documents demonstrating employment in Ticino. ### Useful tools For cross-border workers who wish to explore the opportunities offered by the project, it is useful to consult the website of the Azienda Speciale Consortile Medio Olona Servizi alla Persona. Furthermore, the salary calculator can be a useful tool to assess your financial resources and plan the best way to access the domotic accommodation. ### Conclusion The "Paths to Autonomy" project represents a concrete opportunity for cross-border workers with disabilities who work in Ticino and reside in Italy. Frequently Asked Questions
Who can access the 'Paths to Autonomy' project?
The project is aimed at people with disabilities residing in the involved municipalities, including Olgiate Olona, Castellanza, Fagnano Olona, Gorla Maggiore, Gorla Minore, Marnate, and Solbiate Olona. Cross-border workers who work in Ticino and reside in one of these locations can apply.
What are the requirements to access the domotic housing?
Specific requirements have not yet been defined, but it is likely that identity documents, medical certificates attesting to the disability, and any work documents demonstrating employment in Ticino will be required. It is advisable to contact the Medio Olona Special Consortium Company Services to Persons for updated information.
How can I apply for the project?
To apply, you need to contact the Medio Olona Special Consortium Company Services to Persons for a preliminary assessment. Subsequently, a multidisciplinary team will define a personalized path. No specific deadlines have been specified yet, so it is advisable to act promptly.
