Blue parking in Como: residents fooled, empty streets despite promises (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 4 May 2026

Blue parking spots in central Como, excluded from the reform for residents, remain empty. Councilor Paola Ceriello denounces the situation.

Contesto

In brief - Blue parking spots in Como center remain empty - Residents discouraged by fees and fines - Councilor Ceriello denounces the situation ## Key facts - What: Blue parking spots not assigned to residents - When: Since early 2026 - Where: Via dei Partigiani and surrounding streets, Como center - Who: Councilor Paola Ceriello (Forza Italia) - Amount: Fees to be paid from early morning The center of Como is at the heart of a new controversy regarding blue parking spots. Despite promises of greater availability of parking spaces, the streets exclusive to residents remain empty. Councilor Paola Ceriello, former Rapinese Mayor and now in Forza Italia, has reported the situation criticizing the management of the parking spots. "Good morning, this report to report that every morning and every evening Via dei Partigiani and the surrounding streets that have been excluded from resident parking present themselves empty," wrote Ceriello in an email to the ComoZero editorial staff. The attached photos clearly show the situation, with blue parking spots used only during the day by those coming from outside. The councilor emphasized that residents are discouraged from leaving their cars in these parking spots due to the fees to be paid from early morning and the risk of fines. "They are used only during the day by those coming from outside and it is an embarrassing situation also because more parking spots were promised," added Ceriello. ### The current situation The parking reform, which came into force at the beginning of 2026, assigned many blue spots to residents, but some streets have been excluded. Dettagli operativi

Analysis of the situation The situation of blue parking spaces in Como city center is an example of how political promises may not translate into reality. Despite assurances from the authorities, residents find themselves facing daily difficulties in finding a parking spot. ### Unkept promises At the beginning of 2026, the reform of blue parking spaces promised to increase the availability of parking spots for residents. However, some streets were excluded from the reform, leaving residents without adequate solutions. This has led to a situation of discontent and frustration among the population. ### Consequences for residents Residents of the streets excluded from the reform find themselves in a difficult situation. On one hand, they cannot benefit from the blue parking spaces reserved for residents, on the other hand, paid parking spaces remain empty during the night, making the inefficiency of the management evident. 'It's a mockery for the residents,' commented a resident of via dei Partigiani. 'We pay taxes and still can't even park in front of our own homes.' ### Possible solutions To solve the situation, immediate intervention by the authorities is necessary. Councilor Ceriello has requested a review of the decision to exclude these streets from the reform and has proposed to guarantee residents the right to park in front of their own homes. ### Comparisons with other cities The situation in Como is not an isolated case. In other Italian cities, residents also face similar difficulties in finding a parking spot. However, some cities have found innovative solutions to solve the problem.
