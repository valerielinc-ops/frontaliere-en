The increase in car registrations in the Canton of Ticino is a positive signal for the local economy, especially in a still uncertain global economic context. This data reflects a recovery in both the new and used car markets, facilitated by easier access to incentives and growing demand from cross-border workers. Car registration regulations in Switzerland and Italy require in-depth knowledge and careful management of the necessary documentation. For cross-border workers, for instance, it is essential to comply with provisions related to insurance and emissions. In Switzerland, every vehicle must meet EURO 6 standards, and the Traffic Office in Bellinzona provides detailed information on costs and requirements. For cars imported from Italy, one must consider the import tax, which amounts to 4% of the vehicle's value, in addition to the Swiss VAT of 7.7%.
