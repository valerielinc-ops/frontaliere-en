Practical Implications for Cross-Border Workers The new Centromedico Castello facility offers various opportunities for cross-border workers employed in Ticino. Among these, the possibility of accessing high-quality medical services with reduced waiting times and geographical proximity for those residing in Italy stands out. ### Comparison with the Previous Situation Before the opening of Centromedico Castello, cross-border workers needing outpatient surgical procedures often had to turn to facilities located in other parts of the Canton Ticino or even in Italy. This resulted in longer waiting times and more complex travel arrangements. With the new facility, however, it is possible to access advanced services more quickly and conveniently. ### Concrete Scenarios Consider the case of a cross-border worker needing ophthalmological surgery. Before the opening of Centromedico Castello, they would have had to turn to a specialized center in Italy or wait longer in Switzerland. Now, thanks to the presence of Swiss Visio within the facility, they can access high-quality services more quickly and closer to their workplace. Another scenario involves a cross-border worker needing orthopedic surgery. In this case as well, the new facility offers the possibility of accessing advanced services with reduced waiting times, ensuring a quick return to daily life. ### Comparison with Other Facilities To better understand the advantages offered by Centromedico Castello, it is useful to compare the facility with other clinics in Ticino. For example, Clinica Luganese Moncucco offers similar services but is located in Lugano, farther from the main border crossings. Documentation: Before the visit, it is necessary to prepare all the required documentation. This includes the identity card, the G permit, the health insurance card, and any previous medical reports. It is important to verify with the facility which documents are necessary for your specific case. 3. Frequently Asked Questions
What medical services does the new Centromedico Castello di Bellinzona offer?
Centromedico Castello in Bellinzona, part of the Swiss Medical Network group, offers advanced outpatient surgery services. Among the specialties, ophthalmology (managed by Swiss Visio), orthopaedics, gynaecology and general surgery stand out. The facility, operational from 7 May 2026, is dedicated to interventions that do not require hospitalisation, guaranteeing cutting-edge technologies and qualified personnel.
What are the main advantages of Centromedico Castello for Italian border workers?
For border crossers, the Centromedico Castello offers access to high quality medical services with reduced waiting times. Its location in Bellinzona makes it easily accessible from the main Italian border crossings, minimising travel. The facility guarantees cutting-edge technologies and qualified personnel, facilitating a quick return to daily life after outpatient interventions.
How can border workers access the services of Centromedico Castello and how does insurance coverage work?
Border workers can book a visit through the Centromedico Castello website or by contacting the structure directly. It is essential to check your Swiss health insurance coverage, as most cover outpatient interventions. It is advisable to check the details of your policy, as in some cases you may need to anticipate expenses and request a later refund.
Does the Castello di Bellinzona Medical Centre include specialised eye care services?
Yes, Centromedico Castello houses a specialist ophthalmology centre managed by Swiss Visio. This integration ensures patients, including border workers, have access to advanced eye care services with state-of-the-art technologies and highly qualified staff. It represents an important opportunity for those who need outpatient eye surgery.
How does the Centromedico Castello differ from other medical facilities in Ticino for border workers?
Centromedico Castello stands out for its strategic position in Bellinzona, easily accessible by border crossers compared to clinics such as the Clinica Luganese Moncucco in Lugano. It offers reduced wait times and a full range of outpatient surgery, including specialist ophthalmology with Swiss Visio, representing a significant improvement over previous longer commutes or extended waits.
