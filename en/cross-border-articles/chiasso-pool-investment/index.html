Chiasso: 850,000 francs for the municipal swimming pool (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 24 February 2026

Chiasso is investing 850,000 francs to replace the cover of the municipal swimming pool, involving neighboring municipalities as well.

Contesto

TL;DR - Chiasso invests 850,000 francs in new pool cover - Old cover installed in 2010 needs replacement - Project aims to improve safety and energy efficiency - Cross-border workers benefit from the facility ## Key facts - Investimento: 850.000 franchi - Data installazione copertura: novembre 2010 - Scopo intervento: migliorare sicurezza ed efficienza energetica - Utenti principali: residenti e lavoratori transfrontalieri - Confini vicini: Brogeda e Ponte Chiasso - Vantaggi attesi: struttura moderna e sostenibile per uso annuale - Cooperazione: coinvolgimento di altri comuni per condividere i costi - Impatto: miglioramento esperienza utente e potenziale aumento ricavi Chiasso is preparing to breathe new life into its municipal swimming pool with a significant investment of 850,000 francs. The Municipality has recently requested funding to replace the aging pressostatic cover, which was installed in November 2010. This intervention has become necessary to ensure safety and maintain the attractiveness of the facility, which serves as a key hub for the local community and cross-border workers living nearby. The current cover, commonly referred to as the 'bubble', has exceeded 15 years of use, during which time weather conditions and natural wear and tear have compromised its performance. According to the Municipality, involving other municipalities in the region will be essential to share the costs of this project and strengthen regional cooperation. The Chiasso swimming pool is frequented not only by residents but also by cross-border workers and families who cross the nearby Brogeda and Ponte Chiasso border points. > 'An ambitious project, but essential for the community's quality of life,' declared Prisca Colombini, reflecting the intentions of local authorities. Dettagli operativi

Implications for cross-border workers and the region The intervention at the municipal swimming pool is not just a local matter: it is an example of how infrastructure can also impact the quality of life for cross-border workers. Many of them use Ticino's sports facilities, both for physical activity and leisure time with their families. By replacing the cover, a significant improvement in the user experience is expected, thanks to a more modern and sustainable structure. From a fiscal perspective, contributions from neighboring municipalities reflect a model of cooperation that could serve as an example for other public projects in the border region. Shared cost management is particularly relevant in a context where public resources must be used prudently. Additionally, a renovated facility could attract more users, increasing revenue for the municipal coffers and creating a virtuous cycle for the local economy. ⚠️ For cross-border workers wishing to access these facilities, it is worth considering the existing benefits for residents of neighboring municipalities. We encourage you to check for any discounts or reduced rates that may apply for using the pool once the renovations are completed.
