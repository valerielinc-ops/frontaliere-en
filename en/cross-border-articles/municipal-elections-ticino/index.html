Municipal Elections in Ticino: What Changes (cross-border guide)

Analysis of the municipal elections in Ticino and their impact on cross-border workers: new opportunities and economic challenges.

Contesto

TL;DR - Municipal elections in Ticino concluded with 62% voter turnout. - Bellinzona, Lugano, and Mendrisio are key centers for new priorities. - Cross-border work and resource management are major focus areas. ## Key facts - Voter turnout: 62%, an increase from 58% in previous elections. - Key centers: Bellinzona, Lugano, and Mendrisio. - Cross-border workers: Impacted by policies on transportation, taxation, and infrastructure. - Transportation: Enhancing rail and road connections between Lombardy and Ticino. - Taxation: Revision of local tax rates proposed by some municipalities. - Infrastructure: CHF 10 million initiative in Gandria for new job opportunities. - Policy changes: Potential changes to taxation and transportation systems by 2026. The municipal elections in Ticino, which have just concluded, represent a crucial moment for the economic and social future of the region. With significant voter turnout, despite challenges related to the pandemic and recent political tensions, the results are beginning to outline new priorities for Ticino's municipalities. Bellinzona, Lugano, and Mendrisio emerge as key centers, with particular attention to issues related to cross-border work and resource management. 📊 Key data: Voter turnout reached 62%, an increase from 58% in the previous elections. Dettagli operativi

Implications for Cross-Border Workers Municipal elections do not only affect Ticino residents but also have a direct impact on cross-border workers. In particular, policies related to transportation, urban planning, and municipal taxation will be crucial for those who work in Switzerland but live in Italy. For example: - Transportation: Discussions have begun to enhance rail and road connections between Lombardy and Ticino, aiming to reduce travel times and improve the quality of life for commuters. - Taxation: Some municipalities are proposing a revision of local tax rates, which could have repercussions on costs for cross-border workers. According to local sources, the new administration in Mendrisio is considering closer collaboration with Italian municipalities to address issues related to tax refunds. - Infrastructure: Development projects, such as the CHF 10 million initiative in Gandria, could create new job opportunities, including for cross-border workers, but clarity is needed regarding residency requirements and work permits. ⚠️ Keep an eye on: Potential changes to taxation policies or transportation systems could come into effect as early as 2026. Frequently Asked Questions
How have the municipal elections changed in Ticino compared to the previous ones?
The participation rate increased from 58% to the previous elections to 62% of the recent elections, reflecting an increasing interest of the population towards local choices.
What is the expected impact of new municipal tax policies for Italian frontier workers?
The new municipal administrations could review the local rates, affecting the costs for frontier workers. For example, Mendrisio is considering a collaboration with Italian municipalities to solve the problems of tax refreshments, with possible changes starting from 2026.
What are the new priorities for the Ticino municipalities after the municipal elections?
New priorities for Ticino municipalities include the integration of frontiers, infrastructure development and investment in the cross-border public transport network, with the aim of improving the quality of commuters' lives and reducing travel times.
How could railway and road connections change between Lombardy and Ticino following the elections?
The elections started a discussion to strengthen railway and road links between Lombardy and Ticino, with the aim of reducing travel times for frontier workers, thus improving their quality of life.
