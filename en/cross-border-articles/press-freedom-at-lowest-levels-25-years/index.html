Di Valerie Linc · Published on 1 May 2026

Switzerland ranks 8th in the global press freedom index, but faces legal and economic challenges.

Contesto

In brief - Press freedom at historic lows in 25 years - Switzerland ranks eighth in RSF classification - Legal and economic problems for journalists ## Key facts - What: Press freedom around the world - When: April 30, 2026 - Where: World - Who: Reporters Without Borders (RSF) - Amount: Average score of countries examined at historic low Press freedom around the world has reached historic lows in the last 25 years, according to a report by Reporters Without Borders (RSF). Switzerland ranks eighth in the annual classification of the non-governmental organization, thanks to the decline of Portugal. Despite the overall good performance, the Confederation faces problems related to laws and finances that undermine press freedom. ### Legal and economic challenges RSF highlights that journalism is 'suffocated by hostile political discourse' and that the 'fragile' economic situation weakens the profession. Journalists are subjected to increased pressure from laws used as weapons against the press. Of the five indicators that measure the state of press freedom, the one related to the legal framework shows the most significant decline this year, a sign of increasing criminalization of journalism. ### The situation in Switzerland In Switzerland, legal problems, particularly the application of banking secrecy to journalists and measures to block journalistic content in civil proceedings, weigh heavily. Denis Masmejan, secretary general of RSF Switzerland, states that Switzerland has all the cards in hand to become a model of excellence in terms of press freedom. Frequently Asked Questions
What are the main legal issues affecting press freedom in Switzerland?
The main legal issues include the application of banking secrecy to journalists and measures to block journalistic content in civil proceedings. These measures limit journalists' ability to investigate and report on matters of public interest.
How can I support press freedom in Switzerland?
You can support press freedom in Switzerland by supporting independent media, participating in public debates, always verifying the information received, and using reliable information tools such as the [tax calculator](nav:calculator) and the [permit comparator](nav:permit-compare).

The implications for cross-border workers include a potential negative impact on the quality and quantity of information available, which can affect their ability to make informed decisions regarding work, residence, and financial planning.
