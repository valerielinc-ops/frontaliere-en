Precision farming cuts pesticide use in Switzerland (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 5 May 2026

A pilot project reduces pesticide use by 25% thanks to technology. See how agriculture is changing in Ticino and Grisons

Contesto

In brief - Pilot project reduces pesticides by 25% - Technology saves up to 80% in some situations - Economic and practical obstacles for large-scale application ## Key facts - What: Pilot project on precision farming - When: 2026 - Where: Three cantons in German-speaking Switzerland - Who: Farmers and phytosanitary services - Amount: Average reduction of 25% in pesticide use A pilot project conducted in three cantons of German-speaking Switzerland demonstrates how digitalization can become a valuable ally for the environment. Thanks to precision farming, it has been possible to reduce the use of pesticides by an average of 25%. In some situations, the results have been even more significant. Thomas Käser, a vegetable producer who participated in the project, explains: 'I remove weeds from a lettuce field and apply phytosanitary products to each head, in a targeted manner.' Thanks to a series of cameras and intelligent systems, he is able to distinguish crops from weeds and intervene only where necessary. In this phase of production, Käser has managed to save up to 80% of pesticides. ### Benefits and challenges Alongside the benefits, the study has also highlighted numerous difficulties. Andreas Distel, head of the phytosanitary service of the canton of Aargau, states: 'These are expensive technologies and not all companies have the necessary means.' This is added to the characteristics of Swiss agriculture, made up of small fields and very diversified crops, which often make the purchase of such complex machinery unprofitable. New practical limits also emerge. Käser recounts: 'In our company we cultivate 30 different types of vegetables. Impact on Ticino Agriculture Precision agriculture represents a challenge for the Canton of Ticino as well, where the small size of agricultural businesses and the diversity of crops make it difficult to adopt advanced technologies. However, the environmental benefits and long-term cost reduction could encourage a change. Businesses operating in Ticino could benefit from subsidies or funding for the purchase of machinery, making the adoption of these technologies more accessible. ### Comparison with Traditional Agriculture Traditional agriculture in Ticino is based on established methods that require more intensive use of pesticides and labor. Precision agriculture, on the other hand, allows for the optimization of resources and the reduction of waste. For example, the targeted use of pesticides not only reduces the environmental impact but also production costs. However, the transition requires a significant initial investment and adequate training for workers. ### Future Scenarios If precision agriculture were to spread in Ticino, a significant reduction in the use of pesticides and an improvement in the quality of crops could be seen. Agricultural businesses could become more competitive in the market, thanks to more efficient and sustainable production. However, the transition requires time and resources, and not all businesses will be able to immediately adopt these new technologies. ### Comparison with the Previous Situation Before the introduction of precision agriculture, agricultural businesses in Ticino relied on traditional methods that required more intensive use of pesticides. Concrete actions for agricultural producers For agricultural producers in Ticino who wish to adopt precision agriculture, it is essential to follow a series of concrete steps. First, it is necessary to assess the economic feasibility of the investment. This includes estimating the initial costs and potential long-term savings. Subsequently, companies must purchase the necessary machinery, such as cameras and intelligent systems for monitoring crops. Finally, it is important to train staff in the correct use of new technologies. ### Monitoring and optimization Once the new technologies have been adopted, it is fundamental to monitor the results and make any changes to optimize the use of the technologies. This includes collecting data on crops and analyzing the results to identify any areas for improvement. Agricultural companies can also benefit from consultations with industry experts to optimize the use of technologies. ### Useful tools For agricultural producers in Ticino, a series of useful tools are available to adopt precision agriculture. Frequently Asked Questions
What are the main benefits of precision agriculture?
The main benefits of precision agriculture include a significant reduction in the use of pesticides, greater efficiency in crop management, and a reduction in production costs. Additionally, this technology allows for the optimization of resource use and the improvement of the sustainability of agricultural operations.
What are the main challenges in adopting precision agriculture?
The main challenges in adopting precision agriculture include the high costs of advanced machinery, the need for adequate training for staff, and the diversity of crops, which makes large-scale adoption difficult. Furthermore, the transition requires time and resources, and not all companies will be able to immediately adopt these new technologies.
How can I start adopting precision agriculture in my company?
To start adopting precision agriculture in your company, it is essential to assess the economic feasibility of the investment, purchase the necessary machinery, and train the staff. Additionally, it is important to monitor the results and make any necessary adjustments to optimize the use of technologies. You can also benefit from expert consultations and useful tools to optimize the use of technologies.
