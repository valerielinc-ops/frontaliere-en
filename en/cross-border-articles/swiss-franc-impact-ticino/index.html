The Swiss National Bank (SNB) has repeatedly reaffirmed its willingness to counter rapid franc appreciation, but so far has not announced concrete or structural measures. Current tools include foreign currency purchases, negative interest rates, and communication strategies aimed at shaping market expectations. However, increasing pressure from the strong franc is prompting the Swiss Union of Swiss (USS) to demand more decisive and frequent interventions, such as direct market operations. The regulatory horizon extends until 2026, when the SNB can conduct unlimited currency buy and sell operations. The immediate deadline concerns updating intervention strategies, which must be finalized by the end of the first half of this year, in anticipation of potential increased currency volatility. For Ticino businesses, especially those in Chiasso, Mendrisio, and Lugano, monitoring SNB decisions has become crucial. An unfavorable exchange rate can significantly impact profit margins, especially for export-oriented consumer goods companies like watchmakers, jewelers, and electronic component producers. For instance, a 5% franc appreciation relative to the euro, with the current exchange rate around 1.02, could reduce euro revenues by 4-5%, directly affecting the competitiveness of companies like Richemont or Swatch Group based locally. For cross-border workers, the impact translates into pressure on wages and living costs, particularly in border areas like Chiasso, Mendrisio, and Stabio. Frequently Asked Questions
What is the current real value of the Swiss franc?
According to the USS, the real value of the franc has surpassed the threshold of 120, a peak seen since 2010.
How can Ticino companies diversify their outlet markets to counter the strong pressure of the Swiss franc?
Companies in Ticino can diversify their outlet markets by focusing on countries in Asia and the Middle East, where demand for high-quality products is growing. In addition, they can explore non-traditional markets in Latin America and Africa, adapting their products to local needs.
What do frontier workers risk with the strong Swiss franc?
Border workers risk a pressure on wages and an increase in living costs, especially in border areas. A stronger franc makes daily expenses more expensive in Switzerland and limits the possibility of salary adjustments, penalizing those who receive payments in euros.
What are the USS's proposals to counter the strength of the franc?
The USS calls for stronger interventions by the Swiss National Bank (SNB), such as direct intervention in the foreign exchange market. Foreign currency purchase transactions of at least 2 billion francs per month are suggested, to prevent the Swiss franc from becoming an obstacle to the export and survival of local businesses.
How can Ticino companies manage the reduction in profit margins due to the strong franc?
Companies can review export prices, negotiate contracts in different currencies, and invest in innovation to reduce production costs. It is also useful to strengthen the digital sector and online sales, as well as collaborate with trade associations to obtain financial and tax support.
