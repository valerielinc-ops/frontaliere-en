Analysis of Implications The increase in fuel expenses represents a significant challenge for Air France, which has already seen its operating result remain in the red by 27 million euros, although it improves by 301 million compared to the first quarter of last year, while the net loss remains substantially unchanged at 252 million. The increase in fuel prices resulting from the outbreak of the war in Ukraine was approximately 30% in 2022, with peaks up to 50% in some periods, directly impacting the company's operating cost dynamics. This increase was not visible in the first quarter due to the delay in its impact on price dynamics, but it was a key factor in revising the 2026 forecasts. Air France has revised its 2026 forecasts "due to current geopolitical uncertainty", with capacity increasing from +2% to +4% compared to 2025 (previously the range was +3-5%). This increase was estimated based on an average fuel price of 2022, which was approximately 1.20 USD/gallon, compared to 0.90 USD/gallon in 2021.
