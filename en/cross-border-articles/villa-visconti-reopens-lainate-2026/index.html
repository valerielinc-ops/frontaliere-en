Villa Visconti Borromeo Litta reopens in Lainate

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 1 May 2026

The historic villa in Lainate reopens on May 1st with its famous Nymphaeum and monumental gardens, offering a unique experience between art and nature.

The 'pleasure villa,' famous throughout Europe for its architecture and breathtaking scenery, is ready to welcome visitors again with an experience that combines art, history, and a touch of fun. The complex, designed in 1585 by Count Pirro I Visconti Borromeo, showcases its splendor once more, offering a journey through time among frescoed rooms and monumental gardens where hydrangeas burst into bloom in spring. ### The Ninfeo and the engineering of the fountain masters The true star of the visit remains the Ninfeo, also known as the Palace of Water. Considered the most important and significant example of its kind, the complex is a true rarity: thanks to sophisticated hydraulic mechanisms preserved over time, it functions today exactly as it did in the Cinquecento. Under the guidance of skilled fountain masters, visitors can relive the emotion of the famous 'water jokes,' a system of sudden sprays and automata that centuries ago served to amaze and entertain the Count's guests during lavish receptions. Implications for cross-border workers and visitors The reopening of Villa Visconti Borromeo Litta, located in Lainate, represents a unique opportunity for cross-border workers and visitors who wish to explore the wonders of Lombardy's historical heritage. The villa, easily accessible from many locations in the Canton of Ticino, is an ideal destination for a day trip. For example, residents of Mendrisio and Chiasso can reach Lainate in about 30-40 minutes by car, while those coming from Lugano will take approximately 50 minutes. Visitors can enjoy a unique cultural experience, immersed in the history and art of the Renaissance. ### Comparison with other tourist attractions Villa Visconti Borromeo Litta stands out for its monumental gardens and the Ninfeo, offering a different experience compared to other tourist attractions in the area. Unlike many historical sites that focus exclusively on architecture, this villa combines art, nature, and hydraulic engineering, creating a unique and fascinating environment. Visitors can enjoy a walk among centuries-old trees and botanical architectures, as well as discover the wonders of the frescoed rooms and decorated grottos. For example, the Ninfeo, a hydraulic structure from the 16th century, is a distinctive element that has no equal in the nearby villas of Delizia di Palazzo and Villa Melzi. ### Practical scenarios for visitors For cross-border workers who wish to visit the villa, it is important to plan the visit in advance. The villa is open to the public from May 1st, but it is advisable to check the opening hours and book tickets in advance, especially on weekends when the villa is more crowded.
