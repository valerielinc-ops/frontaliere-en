Operational details

The expansion of the TILO service is based on a series of infrastructure interventions and regulations that have made cross-border mobility more efficient and attractive. The Mendrisio–Varese Railway (FMV), inaugurated in 2018, marked a turning point by directly linking the Ticino rail network with Lombardy’s, integrating the two railway systems. Added to this is the Ceneri Base Tunnel (CBT), which reduced travel times between the main Ticino cities, enabling greater frequency and punctuality of trains. The enhancement of stops, with the introduction of new stations, also facilitated access to the service for new user bases, including the municipalities of Mendrisio, Chiasso, and Locarno. From a regulatory perspective, the agreement between Switzerland and Italy has facilitated the transit of cross-border workers, simplifying permits and customs controls, a crucial element in maintaining the smooth flow of daily travel. In 2025, overall punctuality of the TILO service remained at high levels, although a slight decline was recorded in Ticino compared to the previous year. Conversely, punctuality improved in Lombardy, particularly on the RE80 line connecting Milan to Lugano and Locarno, a positive sign for the integrated management of cross-border rail traffic. Operationally, TILO trains carried an average of 76,400 passengers per weekday, with peaks of 87,700 daily travelers recorded in October 2025, a landmark month for the network’s historic record. These figures show the growing dependence of workers, students, and tourists on this infrastructure, emphasizing the service’s importance also for Ticino’s economy. Key points

For Ticino’s cross-border workers and commuters, TILO’s success represents a tangible opportunity to improve quality of life and the efficiency of daily travel. With the growing number of passengers, it is important to know the tools available to optimize journeys and better manage work and tax situations. For example, those living in Lombardy and working in Ticino can use the salary calculator to estimate their net pay and plan their household budget taking into account the specific tax rates of Canton Ticino and their municipalities of residence. Moreover, the increasing integration between the Ticino and Lombardy rail networks makes it worthwhile to consider subscribing to integrated or combined passes, which offer savings and greater flexibility for daily trips between Chiasso, Mendrisio, Lugano, and Milan. On the administrative side, it is useful to regularly check updates on work permits and customs rules through the permits service, to avoid surprises at the border and simplify procedures. The improvement in punctuality in Lombardy, especially on the RE80 line, is a positive sign, but cross-border workers should still allow time buffers for possible delays, particularly during high traffic months such as October. For those who also travel by car, the site offers tools like car-transfer and border-map to plan routes and choose less congested border crossings. The growth in TILO passengers confirms the central role of public transport for the sustainable development of Ticino and the quality of life of cross-border workers.
