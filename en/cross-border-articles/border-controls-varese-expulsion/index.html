Frequently Asked Questions
I'm a Swiss citizen living in Varese, what happens if I'm stopped by law enforcement during border control?
If false documents or evidence of unlawful activity are found, you may be reported and expelled immediately. The Italian legislation provides for reclusion up to 6 years and pecuniary sanctions of variable amount, while in Switzerland there are up to 3 years of imprisonment and fines up to 30,000 Swiss francs.
Can I be expelled from Switzerland if I am an Italian citizen with legal residence in Switzerland but am I involved in a control for illegal activities in Varese?
According to the Swiss LASI, foreign citizens with legal residence may be expelled if they represent a risk for public security or violate living standards. However, for EU citizens like Italians, procedures are more stringent and require serious reasons (Art. 50 of LASI).
What are the legal consequences for an Italian citizen found in possession of false documents during cross-border control?
If an Italian citizen is found in possession of false documents during a control between Italy and Switzerland, he can be accused of falsification and attempt to evade border controls, risking criminal penalties up to 6 years of imprisonment in Italy and up to 3 years in Switzerland, with possible expulsion.
