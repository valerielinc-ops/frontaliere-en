André Wyss is the new SBB president

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 29 April 2026

André Wyss succeeds Monika Ribar at the helm of Swiss Federal Railways. Goal: innovation and stability.

Contesto

In brief - André Wyss is the new president of the SBB - He succeeds Monika Ribar after reaching the term limit - Goal: sustainable and innovative evolution of the SBB ## Key facts - What: Election of the new SBB president - When: Wednesday, March 11, 2026 - Where: Bern, during the ordinary general assembly - Who: André Wyss, new president; Monika Ribar, former president - Amount: Not specified André Wyss is the new president of the Board of Directors of Swiss Federal Railways (SBB). The election took place today, Wednesday, March 11, 2026, during the ordinary general assembly in Bern, as stated in a press release. Wyss succeeds Monika Ribar, who is stepping down from the presidency after reaching the term limit. With forty years of managerial experience in the construction, real estate, and pharmaceutical sectors, he has been a member of the SBB Board of Directors for one year. As the new president, his goal is to 'further evolve the SBB as a modern and sustainable backbone of Switzerland, with great attention to both stability and innovation.' New members Anna Barbara Remund, Dino Cauzza, Dominique Pierre Locher, and Maria-Antonella Bino were also elected to the Board of Directors. ### Future challenges for the SBB The Swiss Federal Railways face numerous challenges, including increased passenger traffic and the need to modernize infrastructure. André Wyss, with his managerial experience, aims to address these challenges with an innovative and sustainable approach. The SBB's punctuality, which reached 94.1% in 2025 despite 20,000 construction sites, is a positive indicator that Wyss intends to maintain and improve. ### Innovation and sustainability Wyss emphasized the importance of combining stability and innovation to ensure a prosperous future for the SBB.
