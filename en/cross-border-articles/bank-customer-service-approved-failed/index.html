Bank customer service: UBS leads, digital banks lag

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 28 April 2026

UBS and Basel-Landschaft Cantonal Bank offer 24/7 service, while online banks like Revolut and Neon rank last.

Contesto

In brief - UBS and BLKB offer 24/7 customer service - Revolut, Neon and Zak are at the bottom of the ranking - Yuh reduces service hours ## Key facts - What: Analysis of the availability of banking helpdesks - When: 2026 - Where: Switzerland - Who: HSLU - Amount: 56 banks analyzed In Switzerland, traditional banking customer service continues to excel, while digital banks struggle to provide adequate assistance. The Lucerne University of Applied Sciences and Arts (HSLU) has analyzed the availability of the helpdesks of the main Swiss retail banks, including the digital platforms Revolut, Neon, Yuh, Zak and Swissquote. The results show a clear difference between traditional institutions and online ones. UBS and the Basel-Landschaft Cantonal Bank (BLKB) confirm their top position, offering a customer service available 24 hours a day, seven days a week. On the contrary, Revolut, Neon and Zak are placed in the lowest range of the ranking, with limited availability and only one star. Yuh and Swissquote also receive only two stars, with an availability defined as average. In particular, Yuh has reduced its customer service, going from 70 to 55 hours per week, with reduced hours from Monday to Friday. ### Implications for cross-border workers For cross-border workers residing in Ticino and working in Switzerland, the choice of bank can have a significant impact on the quality of service received. Traditional banks like UBS and BLKB offer continuous availability, which can be crucial for those who need assistance outside working hours. On the other hand, digital banks, while offering reduced commissions and lean structures, may not guarantee the same level of support. ### Comparison with the previous situation Compared to 2024, the situation has not improved. Analysis of the performance of digital banks Digital banks continue to gain ground in the Swiss financial sector thanks to lean structures and reduced commissions. However, the HSLU analysis highlights that these banks lag behind traditional institutions when it comes to customer service. Revolut, Neon, and Zak, in particular, offer limited availability, with only one star in the HSLU ranking. ### Why digital banks are struggling Digital banks often rely on advanced technologies, such as artificial intelligence, to manage customer requests. However, this approach can be ineffective for those who need personalized assistance. Furthermore, the reduction in service hours, as in the case of Yuh, can further limit customer satisfaction. ### What customers are looking for Customers primarily seek availability and expertise. Traditional banks, such as UBS and BLKB, offer both aspects, ensuring high-quality customer service.
