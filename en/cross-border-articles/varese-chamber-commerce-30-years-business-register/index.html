Contesto

In brief - Business Register turns 30 - 5.8 million businesses and 10 million people registered - Transformation towards advanced tertiary sector ## Key facts - What: Business Register - When: February 1996 - Where: Italy - Who: Varese Chamber of Commerce - Amount: 5.8 million businesses and 10 million people The Varese Chamber of Commerce is celebrating the 30th anniversary of the Business Register, a digital infrastructure that has revolutionized the relationship between institutions, the market, and businesses. Officially launched in February 1996, although provided for in the Civil Code since 1942, the Business Register today represents the fulfillment of a vision born in the 1970s. The goal was to connect through a computer network the registers of all the Chambers of Commerce, allowing real-time access to information on all Italian businesses. Today, the Business Register is a cutting-edge infrastructure that manages at the national level the data of 5.8 million businesses and 10 million people, including entrepreneurs, partners, and administrators. This unique system of legal advertising in Europe is enriched with information from INPS, the Revenue Agency, and other public entities, ensuring the highest quality and accuracy of the data. At the local level, the Business Register of the Varese Chamber of Commerce captures a constantly evolving entrepreneurial system. If in 1996 the provincial economic fabric was characterized by a strong prevalence of the manufacturing sector, today Movimprese data show a transformation towards the advanced tertiary/services sector. Practical Analysis The Business Register is not just a bureaucratic archive, but a strategic tool for economic development. For companies, the quality and certainty of data mean trust and competitiveness. In an era where information is the new 'gold', the Register ensures that this asset is public, certified, and accessible to everyone through tools like the 'impresa italia' digital app or the registroimprese.it portal. ### Comparison with the Previous Situation Before the introduction of the Business Register, accessing information about companies was more complex and less transparent. Digitalization has streamlined bureaucratic processes and made information more accessible. This has had a positive impact on both businesses and citizens, simplifying procedures and increasing transparency. ### Concrete Scenarios Imagine a cross-border worker who works in Italy and lives in Switzerland. Thanks to the Business Register, they can easily access information about Italian companies they collaborate with. This allows them to make informed decisions and manage their affairs more effectively. Additionally, the ability to consult real-time updated data is crucial for those operating in a cross-border context. ### Concrete Procedures To access the information in the Business Register, cross-border workers can use the 'impresa italia' digital app or the registroimprese.it portal. Frequently Asked Questions
What is the Business Register?
The Business Register is a digital infrastructure that manages data for 5.8 million companies and 10 million individuals in Italy. It is a legal advertising tool that ensures transparency and access to company information.
How can I access information from the Business Register?
You can access information from the Business Register through the 'impresa italia' digital app or the registroimprese.it portal. These tools provide quick and secure access to data on Italian companies.
What are the advantages of the Business Register for cross-border workers?
The Business Register offers cross-border workers the ability to access real-time information on Italian companies, ensuring transparency and data certainty. This is crucial for those operating in a cross-border context.
