Dettagli operativi

Practical Implications for Residents The planning of the new Gera district in Iragna, approved by the Municipality in 2023, has significant implications for residents and potential buyers of the new homes. Current regulations, such as the Federal Law on Protection against X-Rays and Ultraviolet Rays (LPR) of 2006, ensure that homes are safe and comply with the electromagnetic field limits of 100 µT (microtesla) for 50 Hz magnetic fields. This is particularly important for families with young children and for people sensitive to electromagnetic fields. ### Comparison with the Previous Situation Before the project's approval, there were concerns about the proximity of high-voltage power lines, located approximately 300 meters from the district. However, in-depth analyses conducted by the Federal Office of Energy (UFE) and technical checks have shown that the electromagnetic values are within the permitted limits, with measurements showing average values of 1.2 µT, well below the legal limit. This comparison shows that the current planning is safer and more compliant with regulations than the initial concerns. ### Future Scenarios With the project's approval, residents can expect a well-planned new residential area that complies with regulations. The homes will be equipped with all necessary infrastructure, such as roads, parking, underground structures, a 5,000 m² park, and a waste collection area with a differentiated sorting system. Frequently Asked Questions
What are the electromagnetic field limit values respected in the Gera district?
The electromagnetic field limit values respected in the Gera district are 1 microtesla for sensitive use areas. These checks are based on data provided by Swissgrid in 2015.
What infrastructure is planned in the Gera district?
The Gera district plans the construction of eight single-family homes, including streets, parking, substructures, a park, and a waste collection area.
