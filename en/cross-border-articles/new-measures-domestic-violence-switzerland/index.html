Di Valerie Linc · Published on 27 April 2026

The Federal Department of Justice and Police proposes new measures to combat domestic violence, with a focus on early prevention.

Analysis of the new measures The new measures proposed by the Federal Department of Justice and Police aim to improve the protection of victims of domestic violence. These measures include the introduction of early prevention tools and the enhancement of electronic monitoring. The Swiss cantons, including the Canton of Ticino, are involved in defining uniform quality standards for managing threats. These standards help to identify risks of violence promptly and contain dangerous situations. ### Comparison with the previous situation Before the introduction of the new measures, Switzerland had already launched a roadmap against domestic and sexual violence five years ago. This roadmap proved effective, but the increase in cases of domestic violence and femicides made further measures necessary. The new measures proposed aim to fill existing gaps and improve the protection of victims. ### Concrete scenarios Imagine a scenario in which a cross-border worker residing in Ticino and working in Italy is a victim of domestic violence. Thanks to the new measures, the victim could benefit from greater protection and support. Legal procedures could be more efficient, and available resources could be more accessible. Furthermore, electronic monitoring could be used to monitor the behavior of the aggressor and prevent further episodes of violence. ### Practical implications The new measures could have significant practical implications for cross-border workers. For example, new support services for victims of domestic violence could be introduced. Additionally, legal procedures could be simplified and made more efficient. Frequently Asked Questions
What are the new measures against domestic violence in Switzerland?
The new measures include strengthening early prevention, electronic monitoring, and defining uniform quality standards for threat management. These measures aim to improve the protection of victims and make legal procedures more efficient.
How can I report a case of domestic violence?
You can report a case of domestic violence to the competent authorities, such as the local police or social services. It is important to provide all necessary information and cooperate with the authorities to ensure the safety of the victims.
What resources are available for victims of domestic violence?
Victims of domestic violence can benefit from various support services, such as reception centers, legal advice, and electronic monitoring services. It is important to inform yourself about the available resources and use the services offered by the local authorities.
