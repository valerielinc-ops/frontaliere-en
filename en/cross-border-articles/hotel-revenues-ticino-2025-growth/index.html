Swiss hospitality sector saw a 3.9% rise in 2025, with 5-star hotels peaking at 6.3%.

In brief - Hotel revenues +3.9% in 2025 - 5-star hotels +6.3% per night - Average price 139.80 CHF ## Key facts - What: Hotel sector revenues - When: 2025 - Where: Switzerland - Who: Federal Statistical Office - Amount: 6.2 billion CHF The Swiss hotel sector, including Ticino, closed 2025 with a turnover of 6.2 billion francs, marking a 3.9% increase compared to the previous year. The data, provided by the Federal Statistical Office, shows growth mainly driven by demand, with earnings per single night increasing by only 0.6%, averaging 139.80 francs. Growth was particularly evident in the summer months, with July and August recording 680 million and 670 million francs respectively, with increases of 5.8% and 6.8%. The highest rates were observed in winter, with an average of 162 francs in January. ### Seasonal and category differences Seasonal differences were marked, with prices peaking in winter. In January, the average was just over 162 francs, while in August it was nearly 133 francs. Hotel categories also showed significant differences: in 5-star hotels, the average revenue per night was around 410 francs, with a 6.3% annual increase; in 3-star establishments, however, the nightly price remained almost unchanged at 108 francs. ### Geographical differences Mountain resorts showed more pronounced seasonal fluctuations compared to cities. In winter, the average price per night was 166 francs, compared to 125 francs in the summer season. The highest prices were recorded in city centers, with almost 152 francs per night, while in mountain resorts the average was just under 145 francs. ### Concrete examples and real numbers In Ticino, Lugano saw a 4.5% increase in hotel revenues, with a peak of 180 francs per night during Carnival.
