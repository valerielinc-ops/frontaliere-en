Grisons tighten permits after mafia infiltration

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 30 April 2026

New rules for residence permits in Grisons after arrests linked to the Camorra and 'Ndrangheta in Roveredo

Contesto

In brief - Grisons changes residence permit rules - Criminal record declaration mandatory - Stricter controls on the way ## Key facts - What: New rules for residence permits - When: To be defined - Where: Canton of Grisons - Who: State Councillor Peter Peyer - Amount: Not specified Following the arrest of four people linked to the Camorra and ’Ndrangheta residing in Roveredo, the Canton of Grisons announced a change in the rules for residence permits. Applicants will have to declare any criminal records or ongoing proceedings. This measure could lead to stricter controls. Grisons State Councillor Peter Peyer announced that applicants will have to explicitly declare any criminal records or ongoing proceedings. This information could lead to further checks, such as requesting a criminal record extract. In the presence of well-founded doubts, these elements could be considered in the material judgment and motivate a negative decision. ### Implications for cross-border workers The new procedure could have a significant impact on cross-border workers wishing to obtain a residence permit in the Canton of Grisons. Those who provide false information in the self-declaration will risk the immediate withdrawal of the permit. The exact date for the entry into force of the new procedure has not yet been established. Grisons parliamentarian Samuele Censi raised the issue in the Grand Council, intervening at the cantonal level in collaboration with the Municipality of Roveredo from the outset. Analysis of the new rules The new rules for residence permits in the Canton of Grisons represent a significant step towards greater transparency and security. The mandatory declaration of any criminal records or ongoing proceedings is a measure aimed at preventing the infiltration of criminal organizations into the cantonal territory. ### Implications for cross-border workers For cross-border workers who wish to obtain a residence permit in the Canton of Grisons, the new rules involve greater attention in completing the application. It is essential to provide accurate and complete information to avoid the withdrawal of the permit. Requesting a criminal record extract could become a systematic practice, as is already the case in the Canton of Ticino. ### Comparison with the Canton of Ticino The Canton of Ticino has already adopted similar measures in 2009, then moving in 2015 to the systematic request of the criminal record. This comparison highlights the importance of closer collaboration between the cantons to effectively address the problem of mafia infiltration. ### Verification of companies The verification of the actual operation of companies is a crucial issue to prevent the use of fictitious companies to obtain residence permits. Frequently Asked Questions
What are the new rules for residence permits in the Canton of Grisons?
The new rules require applicants to declare any criminal records or ongoing proceedings. This information may be used for further checks, such as requesting a certificate of good conduct.
What happens if false information is provided in the self-declaration?
Anyone who provides false information in the self-declaration risks immediate withdrawal of the residence permit. It is essential to provide accurate and complete information to avoid negative consequences.
What is the difference between the new rules in the Canton of Grisons and those in the Canton of Ticino?
The Canton of Ticino has already adopted similar measures in 2009, then moving in 2015 to the systematic request of the certificate of good conduct. The new rules in the Canton of Grisons represent an intermediate step towards greater transparency and security.
