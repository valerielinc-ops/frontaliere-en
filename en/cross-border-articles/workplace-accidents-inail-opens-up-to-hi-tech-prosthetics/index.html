Dettagli operativi

INAIL's new operational instructions, introduced in 2026, represent an important step forward in protecting the rights of workers who have suffered serious workplace accidents. These changes, which concern both cross-border and Italian workers, are the result of a long process of discussion and collaboration between INAIL and ANMIL. Among the main innovations introduced, the expansion of access to hi-tech prosthetics stands out, in particular to electronically controlled knee joints. Previously, access to this technology was limited to a few specific cases, but now INAIL has decided to extend this possibility to a greater number of workers who need it. This change was made possible thanks to the work of ANMIL's Severe Disabilities Group, chaired by Maurizio Borelli, which brought the concrete needs of those living with severe amputations to the technical tables. Hi-tech prosthetics, in addition to improving workers' mobility and autonomy, also offer greater functionality and a more natural appearance. These advanced prosthetics are equipped with sensors and motors that allow the natural movements of the knee to be reproduced, thus ensuring greater freedom of movement and a better quality of life. In addition, the new operational instructions introduce shorter replacement times for prosthetics, thus guaranteeing a faster and less demanding rehabilitation process for workers. This change was introduced to simplify workers' rehabilitation pathway, reducing waiting times and ensuring more effective and personalized support. INAIL has also simplified rehabilitation pathways, ensuring more effective and personalized support for workers who must face the challenges of severe disability. Frequently Asked Questions
What are hi-tech prostheses and how can they help front workers?
Hi-tech prostheses are advanced electronic devices that can significantly improve the quality of life of those who need it. In particular, the electronic control knee is a technology that offers greater mobility and autonomy in everyday life.
What is the path to access specialized rehabilitation services for frontier workers in Italy?
Frontier workers can access specialized rehabilitation services through INAIL, which has close collaborations with state-of-the-art rehabilitation centers. You need to contact the INIL to start the path, which will be customized according to the individual needs of the worker.
What are the waiting times for the replacement of a hi‐tech prosthesis for the fronts?
The new instructions provide for a replacement of the hi‐tech prosthesis within three months of wear reporting, against the six-nine months envisaged in the past. The process includes a quick clinical evaluation and direct booking at the partner centers, reducing waiting times for frontier workers.
How can I request the support of a specialized rehabilitation center near the Italo-Switzerland border?
To access a rehabilitation center near the border, the border should open the practice on the Inail portal, indicate its residence and the workplace in Switzerland, and choose between the structures agreed in the area of Lugano or Como. The service is managed by a local representative who coordinates visits and interventions.
What costs are covered by Inail for electronic prostheses and who pays any additional costs?
Inail covers 100 % of the cost of electronic prosthesis, including sensors, engines and the first programming. Any expenses for aesthetic personalization or subsequent maintenance are borne by the worker or his private insurance, but only after receiving the written authorization from Inail.
