Di Valerie Linc · Published on 26 March 2026

The Canton of Ticino is looking for prison guards. Deadline for the call: March 31, 2026.

Contesto

TL;DR - Ticino seeks 15 new prison guards to strengthen staff. - Prison guard role requires training, motivation, and specific skills. - Selected candidates undergo 12-month training and earn 60,000 CHF annually. ## Key facts - Number of jobs: 15 new prison guard positions available. - Application deadline: March 31, 2026. - Training duration: 12-month training course (6 months theory, 6 months practice). - Training cost: 12,000 Swiss francs. - Annual salary: 60,000 Swiss francs. - Age requirement: Candidates must be at least 21 years old. - Citizenship requirement: Swiss citizenship or EU/AELE citizenship required. - Education requirement: High school diploma required. The Canton of Ticino has announced a call for 15 new prison guards, with the aim of strengthening the staff of Ticino's penitentiary institutions. The director of the Ticino penitentiary institutions, Stefano Laffranchini, explained that the 'job' of prison guard requires specific training and strong motivation, as it is a job that involves important responsibilities and stressful situations. Those interested in this role can attend an information evening in Rivera, in the municipality of Monteceneri, to learn more about the tasks and requirements. The call for competition expires on March 31, 2026, and applications can be sent online or by mail. It is essential to note that the selection process will be based on rigorous criteria, including age (candidates must be at least 21 years old), Swiss citizenship or citizenship of an EU/AELE member state, and possession of a high school diploma. Selected candidates will have to pass a 12-month training course, which includes 6 months of theory and 6 months of practice, at a cost of 12,000 Swiss francs. Dettagli operativi

Prison guards play a fundamental role in the management of penitentiary institutions. They work closely with prisoners, providing them with assistance and support. Training for this role is crucial, as it requires in-depth knowledge of the rules and procedures for managing prisons. The Canton of Ticino offers complete training and a stable career for those interested in working in this sector. Candidates must have a good knowledge of German and French, in addition to Italian. The Canton of Ticino has recently announced the availability of 15 job positions as prison guards. These positions are available at the prisons in Lugano, Bellinzona, and Locarno. The choice of working in one of these institutions offers the opportunity to live and work in municipalities like Lugano, with a population of around 63,000 inhabitants, or Bellinzona, with a population of around 43,000 inhabitants. To be admitted to the training course, candidates must be at least 21 years old and no older than 40 years old. Additionally, they must have a high school diploma and a good knowledge of the official languages of the Canton of Ticino. The training lasts around 12 months and includes both theoretical and practical lessons. The courses are held at the police school of the Canton of Ticino, in Giubiasco. The law on personnel in the Canton of Ticino, in force since January 1, 2019, establishes that public employees must have in-depth knowledge of the rules and procedures for managing prisons. Furthermore, the law provides that public employees must follow a course of continuous training to update their knowledge and skills. Frequently Asked Questions
When does the call for applications for prison guards in the Canton of Ticino expire?
The call for applications expires on 31 March 2026.
What is the duration and cost of the training course for prison guards in the Canton of Ticino?
The training course lasts 12 months (6 months of theory + 6 months of practice) and costs 12,000 Swiss francs.
What are the language requirements to apply as a prison guard in the Canton of Ticino?
The candidate must be fluent in Italian, the main language of the canton, and have a good knowledge of both German and French. Mastery of at least two of the three languages is verified during the selection test, with written and oral tests assessing communication skills and understanding of prison rules in all official languages.
How much does a prison guard earn in Ticino and what additional allowances do they receive?
The annual basic salary is around 55,000 Swiss francs. In addition, there is an allowance of 5,000 francs for night and holiday work, bringing the total compensation to around 60,000 francs. In addition, the employee benefits from health and accident insurance, compulsory pension contributions and any premiums for continuing education.
How does the selection test for the prison guards competition of the Canton of Ticino take place?
The process involves a written test on prison law and conflict management, followed by a physical test (running, weightlifting and endurance). After the physical assessment, candidates undergo a motivational interview and a psychological assessment. Only those who pass all stages are admitted to the 12-month training course.
