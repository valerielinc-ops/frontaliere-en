Coop Recalls Cheeses: May Contain Salmonella (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 5 April 2026

Coop has recalled some cheeses due to the presence of salmonella, which can be particularly hazardous for immunocompromised individuals, pregnant women, young girls and boys, and the elderly.

Contesto

TL;DR - Coop recalls cheeses due to salmonella risk - Affected products include soft, aged, fresh, and creamy cheeses - Refunds available for purchased items - High-risk groups advised not to consume ## Key facts - Affected products: Soft cheese 'Ticinese', aged cheese 'Battù', fresh cheese 'Gorgonzola', creamy cheese 'Raclette' - Recall reason: Potential salmonella contamination in production process - High-risk groups: Immunocompromised individuals, pregnant women, children, and the elderly - Refund process: Customers can return products to Coop stores with receipt and ID for refund - Regulation: Public Health Regulation of July 15, 2015, Article 12 - Investigation: Food Supervision Service of the canton of Ticino has opened an investigation - Start date of sale: January 1, 2024 - End date of sale: March 15, 2024 Coop recalls cheese: may contain salmonella Coop has announced the recall of some cheese products produced by the Landbrügg dairy, which may contain salmonella. The recall affects the following products: Soft cheese "Ticinese" (250g and 500g) Aged cheese "Battù" (200g and 400g) Fresh cheese "Gorgonzola" (120g and 250g) Creamy cheese "Raclette" (150g and 300g) The sale of these articles has already been blocked and the purchase price will be refunded. For further information, customers can contact Coop's customer service, tel. 0848 888 444. The recommendation not to consume these products is particularly important for immunocompromised individuals, pregnant women, children, and the elderly. "The elderly and individuals with a weakened immune system are particularly vulnerable to salmonella infections," says Dr. Giovanni Bianchi, head of public health in the canton of Ticino. Start and end dates of sale The sale of the affected items has already been halted, and the purchase price will be refunded. What to do Affected customers can return already purchased products to Coop stores and receive a refund of the purchase price. Frequently Asked Questions
Which cheeses were recalled by Coop due to the presence of salmonella?
The cheeses recalled are 'Ticinese' soft cheese, 'Battù' aged cheese, 'Gorgonzola' fresh cheese and 'Raclette' cream cheese.
Where can I find information about cheeses recalled by Coop for salmonella?
For information on cheeses recalled by Coop due to potential salmonella contamination, you can contact Coop Customer Service directly on 0848 888 444. The article also mentions that the sale has been blocked and the price will be refunded.
What types of Landbrügg cheese are affected by the recall?
The recall concerns different types of Landbrügg cheese, including 'Ticinese' (sizes of 250g and 500g), 'Battù' (sizes of 200g and 400g), 'Gorgonzola' (sizes of 120g and 250g) and 'Raclette' (sizes of 150g and 300g). 'Ticinese' type 'Bella Cura' and 'Sbrinz' type 'Vecchio' are also mentioned.
Who are the people most at risk if they consume the cheeses recalled by Coop?
The people most vulnerable to the consumption of salmonella-contaminated cheeses are immunocompromised people, pregnant women, children and the elderly. In case of symptoms such as diarrhoea, vomiting or fever, it is advisable to consult a doctor.
How do I get reimbursed for recalled Coop cheeses?
To get the refund, customers must bring back products purchased in Coop stores. It is necessary to present the original product, the purchase receipt and an identity document. The sales price paid will be refunded.
