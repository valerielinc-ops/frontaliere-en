This data emerges from a survey that involved 1,387 hikers in over 20 locations in the Confederation. Frequently Asked Questions
What should I do in case of vertigo during a hike?
If you experience vertigo during a hike, it's important to stay calm and find a stable place to sit down. Avoid looking down and focus on a fixed point on the horizon. If possible, ask for help from a hiking companion or a mountain guide. In case of need, do not hesitate to call for rescue.
How can I prevent vertigo in the mountains?
To prevent vertigo in the mountains, it's important to choose routes suitable for your abilities and physical condition. Make sure you have adequate equipment, such as hiking shoes with anti-slip soles and trekking poles. Additionally, inform yourself about the weather conditions and plan your route in detail.
What are the symptoms of vertigo?
Symptoms of vertigo can include a sensation of dizziness, instability, nausea, sweating, and difficulty maintaining balance. If you experience these symptoms, it's important to stop and find a stable place to sit down. Avoid looking down and focus on a fixed point on the horizon.
