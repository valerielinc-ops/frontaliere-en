Car thefts in Ticino: AXA data shows surge (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 5 May 2026

Car thefts in Ticino are on the rise, with the canton above the Swiss average. AXA reports cases nearly doubled compared to pre-pandemic.

According to AXA insurance data, 398 theft cases were recorded in 2025, with a growing trend for luxury cars. The likelihood of a Land Rover SUV being stolen is over four times higher than the average. Alfa Romeo and Porsche are also particularly targeted. 'Swiss roads are seeing more modern, better-equipped vehicles. Thieves seem to be increasingly targeting these models and, apparently, are increasingly successful in stealing high-value vehicles,' observes Michael Villiger, head of motor vehicle claims at AXA. ### Proximity to the border The proximity to the Italian border appears to be a determining factor. 'In these regions, organized gangs seem to operate more frequently, transporting vehicles abroad,' explains Villiger. The most affected cantons are Geneva (120 thefts), Vaud (95 thefts), and Basel-City (80 thefts), but Ticino also ranks above the Swiss average with 398 thefts. Thefts are rarer in Obwalden (15 thefts) and Glarus (20 thefts). ### Most stolen cars AXA's analysis highlights that the most stolen models are Land Rover SUVs (80 thefts), followed by Alfa Romeo (60 thefts) and Porsche (50 thefts). Also targeted are Audi (40 thefts), Jeep (35 thefts), Fiat (30 thefts), BMW (25 thefts), and Mercedes-Benz (20 thefts). Security Measures To reduce the risk of theft, it is advisable to take some precautions. First of all, park in well-lit and monitored areas, preferably in closed garages or guarded parking lots. Using security devices such as alarms, steering wheel locks, and GPS trackers can significantly increase vehicle protection. Additionally, avoiding leaving valuable items in plain sight inside the car can deter thieves. ### Operational Checklist - Park in well-lit and monitored areas - Install alarms and GPS devices - Hide valuable items - Use steering wheel locks - Check insurance and coverage ### Comparison with Pre-Pandemic Before the pandemic, the number of car thefts in Ticino was significantly lower. According to AXA, during the pre-pandemic period, figures ranged between 2.5 and 3 million francs. In 2025, however, the amount nearly doubled, reaching 5 million francs. This increase is linked not only to the greater presence of luxury vehicles but also to the better organization of theft rings. 📊 AXA Data: - Pre-pandemic: 2.5-3 million francs - 2025: 5 million francs - Increase: nearly doubled ### Concrete Scenarios Let's imagine two scenarios: a cross-border worker who works in Lugano and lives in Varese, and another who works in Bellinzona and lives in Como. Frequently Asked Questions
Which are the most stolen cars in Ticino?
According to AXA, the most stolen models in Ticino are Land Rover SUVs, followed by Alfa Romeo and Porsche. Audi, Jeep, Fiat, BMW, and Mercedes-Benz are also particularly targeted.
What should I do if my vehicle has been stolen?
Contact the Swiss and Italian authorities immediately, providing all necessary details. Make sure you have a copy of your insurance contract and the vehicle's registration certificate. Report the theft to your insurance company to claim compensation.
How can I protect my vehicle from theft?
Park in well-lit and monitored areas, preferably in closed garages or attended parking lots. Use security devices such as alarms, steering wheel locks, and GPS trackers. Avoid leaving valuable items visible inside the car.
