Luigi Bignami at Insubria: the art of science communication

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 2 May 2026

Geologist and science journalist Luigi Bignami met with students at the University of Insubria to discuss science communication.

Contesto

In brief - Luigi Bignami at Insubria to talk about scientific communication - Scientific dissemination requires rigor and accessibility - Bignami: 'Simplifying is not trivializing' ## Key facts - What: Lecture by Luigi Bignami on scientific communication - When: Wednesday, April 29, 2026 - Where: Università degli Studi dell'Insubria, Varese - Who: Luigi Bignami, geologist and science journalist - Fee: Not specified The renowned science communicator Luigi Bignami met with students at Università degli Studi dell'Insubria to explain how to translate complexity to the general public without trivializing it. The lecture, organized as part of the Science Communication course directed by professor Federico Pasquarè Mariotto, offered students a clear picture of the science communicator's profession. Bignami emphasized that science communication is a job that requires rigor, constant study, and the ability to be understood without reducing complexity to something banal. ### The professional turning point Bignami's professional turning point came after the Valtellina flood in 1987. During that event, Bignami realized the gap that existed between the technical knowledge of scientists and the public's perception. 'I knew what was happening, but the people didn't. Until the last moment, they hoped that nothing serious would ever happen,' he recounted. That episode was the spark that ignited in Bignami the desire to learn how to translate geological data into stories accessible to everyone. ### Simplifying without trivializing During the lecture, Bignami insisted on the need to build a relationship of trust with the reader and with the editorial staff. Dettagli operativi

Implications for Scientific Communication Luigi Bignami's lecture at the University of Insubria highlighted the importance of effective and accessible scientific communication. For journalists and communicators, this means adopting an approach that balances rigor and simplicity. Bignami emphasized that the ability to translate complex concepts into understandable language is fundamental to gaining the public's trust. ### The Importance of Training Another crucial aspect that emerged during the meeting is the importance of continuous training. Bignami explained that his method is based on daily study and cross-analysis of sources. This approach not only improves the quality of communication but also helps maintain the reader's attention. For young communicators, this means investing time and resources in their own training to always be up-to-date and prepared. ### The Role of the Media Bignami also spoke about the role of the media in scientific dissemination. He emphasized that the media have the responsibility to transmit accurate and understandable information. This is particularly important in an era where misinformation is widespread. The media must be a bridge between the scientific world and the public, ensuring that information is transmitted clearly and precisely. ### Challenges and Opportunities The lecture also addressed the challenges and opportunities in the field of scientific communication. Bignami spoke about the difficulties in proposing scientific topics to editors and news editors, emphasizing the importance of determination. He also highlighted the importance of insisting and not giving up, even in the face of obstacles. How to Become a Good Science Communicator To become a good science communicator, it's essential to follow some concrete steps. First, it's important to invest in your education. This means constantly studying, staying updated on the latest scientific discoveries, and developing the ability to translate complex concepts into understandable language. Bignami emphasized the importance of a methodical approach based on daily study and cross-referencing sources. ### How to Face the Challenges of the Sector Facing the challenges of scientific communication requires determination and resilience. Bignami advised being persistent in proposing scientific topics to editors and head editors. It's important to clearly explain why a topic is relevant and insist until its value is recognized. Additionally, it's crucial to build a trusting relationship with the public, providing accurate and understandable information. ### Useful Tools for Scientific Communication There are several tools that can help scientific communicators improve their effectiveness. For example, the use of images and videos can enrich the scientific message, making it more engaging and immediate. Additionally, cross-referencing sources can help ensure the accuracy of information. Bignami emphasized the importance of using clear and precise language, avoiding oversimplifications. ### Conclusion and CTA In conclusion, Luigi Bignami's lesson at the University of Insubria offered valuable advice on becoming a good science communicator. Frequently Asked Questions
What is the importance of scientific communication?
Scientific communication is crucial for conveying accurate and understandable information to the public. It helps bridge the gap between the scientific world and the population, ensuring that information is transmitted clearly and precisely. This is particularly important in an era where misinformation is widespread.
How can I become a good science communicator?
To become a good science communicator, it's important to invest in your education, study constantly, and develop the ability to translate complex concepts into understandable language. Additionally, it's essential to use a methodical approach based on daily study and cross-referencing sources.
What are the challenges in the field of scientific communication?
The challenges in scientific communication include the difficulty of presenting scientific topics to editors and section heads, the need to be determined and persistent, and the responsibility of ensuring the accuracy of information. Moreover, it's important to build a trust relationship with the public by providing accurate and understandable information.
