Bikes: billions in six cycling routes in Varese (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 5 May 2026

The Lombardy Region is investing tens of millions of euros in cycling paths, with six routes crossing the province of Varese, a strategic hub for cycle tourism.

Contesto

In brief - Six cycling routes cross the province of Varese - Investments amounting to tens of millions of euros - The bicycle is recognized as a sustainable and inclusive mode of transport ## Key facts - What: Regional cycling mobility plan - When: Current framework document - Where: Province of Varese, Lombardy - Who: Lombardy Region - Amount: Approximately 40 million euros of PNRR funds The Lombardy Region is heavily investing in cycling routes, with a plan that includes six main routes crossing the province of Varese. These routes are not just a geographical coincidence but reflect the strategic position of the Varese area as a crossroads between the major north-south routes coming down from Switzerland and the east-west axes of the foothill belt. The lakes of Varese, Maggiore, Comabbio, and Monate constitute a network of premier cyclotourist attractions. Cycling mobility is no longer a niche choice. In the Lombardy Regional Cycling Mobility Plan, the bicycle is recognized as one of the most sustainable, accessible, and inclusive modes of transport, low-cost and healthy. This aligns with the European Declaration on Cycling Mobility approved by the EU Parliament in April 2024. The bicycle is seen as a structural component of the future transport system, with environmental, economic, and social benefits. ### Investments and benefits From an environmental perspective, the decarbonization of the transport sector is one of the cornerstones of the European Green New Deal and the Fit for 55 package. Reducing dependence on private cars means reducing climate-altering emissions, and the bike is the most immediate and cost-effective tool to do so. Dettagli operativi

The six routes crossing the province of Varese The six cycling routes form an interconnected system. The Ticino (PCIR 1) is the major vertical route: it enters the province at Lavena Ponte Tresa, descends along the lakes and valleys to Sesto Calende and then towards Pavia, drawing one of the most complete cycling routes in northern Italy. The Pedemontana Alpina (PCIR 2) is instead the horizontal backbone: it starts at Sesto Calende, skirts Lake Varese, touches Azzate and continues east through Castiglione Olona and Malnate, before continuing into the province of Como. Lake Maggiore (PCIR 20) is the most thoroughly Varesino route: 62 kilometers entirely within the province, from the Swiss border at Maccagno to Sesto Calende, along the Lombard shore of the lake. These routes not only improve local mobility but also attract tourists and cyclists from all over Europe, contributing to the local economy. ### Impact on cross-border workers For cross-border workers who live in Italy and work in Switzerland, these cycling routes offer new opportunities for sustainable mobility. The possibility of using the bicycle for daily commutes or leisure time can reduce transportation costs and improve quality of life. Additionally, safe and well-connected cycling routes can make rural areas and villages more attractive, offering new housing options for cross-border workers seeking a quieter and greener environment. ### Practical scenarios Imagine a cross-border worker living in Varese and working in Lugano. With the new cycling routes, they could choose to cycle along Lake Maggiore, enjoying breathtaking scenery and arriving at work in a sustainable way. Frequently Asked Questions
What are the main cycling routes in the province of Varese?
The main cycling routes in the province of Varese are the Ticino (PCIR 1), the Alpine Foothills (PCIR 2), and Lake Maggiore (PCIR 20). These routes offer strategic connections and top cycling tourism attractions.
How much has been invested in the creation of cycling routes in Lombardy?
Lombardy has invested around 40 million euros of PNRR funds and a total investment in regional cycle paths exceeding 146 million euros of the Lombardy Plan.
What are the benefits of using the bicycle for cross-border workers?
Using the bicycle for cross-border workers offers numerous benefits, including reduced transportation costs, improved quality of life, and the opportunity to discover new places of interest along the cycling routes.
