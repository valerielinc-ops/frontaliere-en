The aggregation of Basso Mendrisiotto is an ambitious project aiming to create a larger and stronger municipality, but it is not without challenges. Among the main issues raised are financial tensions between the involved municipalities, with Chiasso emerging as the main point of discussion. Vacallo Mayor Marco Rizza pointed the finger at Chiasso, accusing it of a lack of self-criticism on the financial situation. 'Some self-criticism would be necessary,' Rizza declared. 'Financial humility is needed. This attitude could slow down the process.' This criticism is particularly significant given that Chiasso is the largest municipality in the area and has a current tax multiplier of 88%, higher than that of other municipalities in the region, such as Mendrisio (85%) and Riva San Vitale (80%). This means Chiasso contributes more significantly to the balance of the future aggregated municipality. At the center of the discussion remains the tax multiplier of the future municipality. The aggregation study assumes an average of around 95%, a percentage higher than that of several municipalities in the region. This could have significant repercussions on the budget of the new municipality, which will need to find a balance between the different financial needs of the involved municipalities. For example, if the tax multiplier of the new municipality were 95%, the municipality would have to manage a higher share of local taxes, which could influence budgets for public services such as schools, hospitals, and infrastructure.
