In brief - Delays in U.S. Patriot systems - Evaluation of alternative systems - Germany, France, Israel, and South Korea involved ## Key facts - What: Evaluation of alternative air defense systems - When: From March 2026 - Where: Switzerland - Who: Swiss Confederation - Amount: Not specified The Swiss Confederation is evaluating the acquisition of an additional long-range air defense system to complement the U.S. Patriot missiles. Delays in the delivery of the Patriot systems, which are expected to be four or five years behind schedule, have prompted the Federal Council to seek alternative solutions. The Federal Office for Defence Procurement (Armasuisse) has sent requests for information to Germany, France, Israel, and South Korea, aiming to gather data on delivery times, costs, performance, and the share of production in Europe, ideally in Switzerland. ### Procedure and Timelines The information collected should be available by the end of May 2026. However, this does not yet constitute a formal offer, so the Federal Council will not make a final decision at this stage. Armasuisse has confirmed that it has not released specific statements about the manufacturers and systems involved. ### Implications for National Security Defense Minister Martin Pfister explained that the decision to evaluate alternative systems is a response to the escalation of threats in Europe, particularly in light of the wars in Ukraine and the Middle East. The purchase of a second system aims not only to compensate for the delays in the Patriot systems but also to reduce dependence on the United States, increasing flexibility in the event of conflict. ### Involvement of International Manufacturers In addition to the four countries contacted, Armasuisse has also reached out to five manufactu...
