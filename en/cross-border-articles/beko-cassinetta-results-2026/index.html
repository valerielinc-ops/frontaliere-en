Beko Cassinetta: overcoming the crisis, results matter now

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 30 April 2026

One year after the agreement that saved the Beko plant in Cassinetta, the first results and future challenges emerge

Contesto

In brief - Beko Cassinetta has overcome the critical phase - Investments activated: 110 million euros - Short-time work still in use ## Key facts - What: Monitoring of Beko's industrial plan - When: April 29, 2026 - Where: Ministry of Enterprises and Made in Italy - Who: Honourable Antonio Ferrara (M5S) - Amount: 110 million euros of activated investments A year after the agreement that prevented the closure of Beko's plants, the deputy from Varese, Antonio Ferrara, attended the monitoring of the industrial plan at the Ministry of Enterprises and Made in Italy. Ferrara, a member of the Productive Activities Commission, commented: 'Being present at this table means verifying in detail what is happening. After a year, it is fair to recognize that the most critical phase has been overcome, but today we are in the decisive phase: that of concrete results.' Regarding Cassinetta di Biandronno, a clear picture emerges: the reorganization has been carried out, but the full phase of industrial consolidation has not yet been reached. Ferrara continued: 'In Cassinetta, we have intervened on the organization and personnel, but the subsequent phase, that of investments which must stabilize production and employment, is not yet fully visible. Volumes are not growing significantly and the use of short-time work continues, which should instead progressively decrease.' To date, approximately 110 million euros of investments have been activated, but the overall plan envisages a much broader commitment. Ferrara emphasized: 'The point is not only how much is invested, but when these investments arrive. Timing is crucial: without acceleration, the risk is that the plan will lose effectiveness.' • ## Analysis of the current situation The current situation of the Beko plant in Cassinetta di Biandronno is complex and requires an in-depth analysis. Although the critical phase has been overcome, concrete results are still far off. The investments activated amount to approximately 110 million euros, but the overall plan envisages a much broader commitment. This means that, although significant steps forward have been made, there is still a lot of work to be done to achieve the set objectives. ### Investments and timelines One of the key points highlighted by Ferrara concerns the timelines of the investments. It is crucial that these arrive promptly to avoid the plan losing effectiveness. Without acceleration, the risk is that the objectives will not be achieved within the expected times. This could have negative repercussions not only on production but also on employment and the stability of the plant. ### Impact on production and employment Production and employment are two closely related aspects. The reduction of personnel has been implemented, but the industrial relaunch is still partial. Volumes and turnover are substantially in line with those of the previous year, a sign that the expected quality leap has not yet occurred. This means that, despite the efforts, there is still a lot to do to improve the situation. ### Situation of the workers The situation of the workers employed and technicians is another fundamental aspect. These workers are involved in the reorganization processes and must be guaranteed transparency and correctness in the employment paths.
