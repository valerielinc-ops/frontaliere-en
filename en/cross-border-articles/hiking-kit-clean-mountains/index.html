Contesto

In brief - Hiker kit promotes a clean mountain - Contains eco-friendly soap, waste bags, and illustrated guide - Teaches how to relieve oneself in nature with zero impact - Available in 15 Ticino municipalities, with over 1,000 kits distributed in 2023 ## Key facts - What: 'Leave No Trace' kit for hikers - When: Current initiative, launched in 2022 - Where: Ticino mountains, with a focus on Alpe Veglia, Verzasca Valley, and Alpe di Bosco Gurin - Who: 'Clean Mountains' project promoted by the Ticino Territory Department - Amount: Kit purchasable for CHF 15, free for those participating in quizzes or awareness activities The 'Leave No Trace' kit is an initiative promoted as part of the 'Clean Mountains' project to teach hikers how to respect the mountain environment. The mini-equipment, available in 15 Ticino municipalities, contains an eco-friendly soap with low environmental impact, two biodegradable bags, and an illustrated guide explaining how to relieve oneself with the least possible impact. In 2023, over 1,000 kits were distributed, a 30% increase compared to the previous year. Samuel Notari, project manager, explains that the kit is designed for those who frequent the mountains and want to make a difference. 'Surely,' explains Notari, 'you should distance yourself from trails and watercourses. We then suggest digging a hole at least 15-20 cm deep, which should then be covered with a stone or foliage. Dettagli operativi

Practical Implications for Hikers The use of the 'Leave No Trace' kit has several practical implications for those who frequent the mountains. Firstly, it promotes greater awareness of the importance of leaving the mountain environment clean and unpolluted. This not only improves the experience for hikers themselves but also contributes to preserving the biodiversity and health of mountain ecosystems. For example, in the municipality of Acquarossa, where the kit was introduced in 2022, there was a 40% reduction in abandoned waste in natural areas. The kit includes an ecological biodegradable soap, bags for collecting waste, a trowel for burying organic waste, and an informative comic with detailed instructions. ### Comparison with the Previous Situation Before the introduction of this kit, many hikers were unaware of the correct practices for managing their physiological needs in the mountains. This led to an increase in pollution and a negative impact on the environment. With the introduction of the kit, it is hoped to reverse this trend and promote more responsible behavior. In the municipality of Blenio, for example, before the introduction of the kit, there were about 200 annual interventions for cleaning natural areas, with an average cost of 500 francs per intervention. After the introduction of the kit, interventions decreased by 30%, saving about 30,000 francs per year. ### Concrete Scenarios Imagine you are in a remote area of the Ticino mountains, far from any hut or public toilet. With the 'Leave No Trace' kit, you have everything you need to manage your physiological needs safely and respectfully to the environment. What to do concretely To obtain the 'Leave No Trace' kit, you can participate in quizzes or awareness activities organized by the 'Clean Mountains' project. For example, the municipality of Locarno hosted an event in 2023 with over 200 participants, where 500 free kits were distributed. Alternatively, the kit can be purchased online or at some specialized stores for hiking equipment, such as the 'Alpino' store in Lugano, for 15 CHF. ### Deadlines and procedures There are no specific deadlines for using the kit, but it is important to remember that the promoted practices are valid at any time during mountain excursions. Following the instructions provided in the kit will help you minimize environmental impact and fully enjoy nature. For example, the 2022 cantonal regulation of Ticino imposes a fine of 200 CHF for those who do not respect the rules of waste disposal in protected natural areas. ### Useful tools For further information and to participate in quizzes and awareness activities, visit the official website of the 'Clean Mountains' project. You can also use the environmental impact calculator to assess your personal impact on the environment and discover how to reduce it. For example, a hiker using the 'Leave No Trace' kit can reduce up to 30% of the waste produced during a day's excursion. ### Operational checklist - Before the excursion: - Check the contents of the kit and make sure you have everything you need. - Plan the route and verify local regulations. - During the excursion: - Use the provided bags to collect waste. - Follow the marked trails to avoid damaging the local flora. - After the excursion: - Dispose of the collected waste in the designated bins. - Share your experience on social media to raise awareness among other hikers....
