SBB and Siemens sign contract for 116 new trains

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 30 April 2026

SBB and Siemens Mobility Switzerland have signed the contract for 116 new double-decker trains for the S-Bahn, with an investment of up to 2 billion francs.

Contesto

In brief - SBB and Siemens sign for 116 new double-decker trains - Investment up to 2 billion francs - Service entry scheduled from 2031 ## Key facts - What: Contract for 116 new double-decker trains - When: Recently signed, service entry from 2031 - Where: Switzerland, mainly Zurich and Western Switzerland - Who: SBB and Siemens Mobility Switzerland - Amount: Up to 2 billion francs Swiss Federal Railways (SBB) and Siemens Mobility Switzerland have signed the contract for the supply of 116 new double-decker trains destined for the S-Bahn. This significant agreement, valued at up to 2 billion francs, marks a major milestone for Swiss rail transport. The new trains, which will enter service from 2031, will replace the first-generation models currently in use. The new trains will offer a significantly higher capacity compared to current models, with around 540 seats. According to the railway companies, travelers will have 10% more seats and 30% more standing room. This improvement is particularly relevant for urban areas like Zurich, where demand for public transport is constantly increasing. ### Context and Challenges The awarding of the contract was not without controversy. Initially, competitor Stadler Rail had contested the awarding of the contract to Siemens, filing an appeal. However, Stadler later withdrew the appeal, making the award to Siemens final. This withdrawal was communicated by SBB already at the beginning of April, thus allowing the signing of the contract. The new trains will be distributed between the Zurich S-Bahn, which will receive 95 vehicles, and Western Switzerland, which will receive 21. This distribution aims to improve the quality of railway service in both regions, offering passengers a more comfortable and efficient travel experience....

This increased capacity will be particularly useful during peak hours when demand for public transport is highest. For cross-border workers who travel daily between the Canton of Ticino and other Swiss regions, this greater capacity means less waiting time and more comfort during the journey. The new trains will be equipped with advanced technologies and improved comfort, making the trip more pleasant and practical. ### Environmental Impact The increased capacity of the trains will also contribute to environmental sustainability. With more passengers choosing public transport over private vehicles, road traffic and CO2 emissions will be reduced. This is particularly important in a context where Switzerland is making significant efforts to reduce greenhouse gas emissions and promote sustainable mobility. ### Comparison with the Previous Situation Before the introduction of the new trains, first-generation models were often overcrowded, especially during peak hours.
