Ticino-Gaza twinning exhibition

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 30 April 2026

An exhibition in Viganello tells the story of the twinning between Ticinese and Gaza students, a Future in Peace project for human rights education.

Contesto

In brief - Exhibition on the twinning between Ticino and Gaza students in Viganello - Future in Peace project for human rights education - Exhibition until May 7, 2026 ## Key facts - What: Exhibition on the twinning between schools in Viganello and Gaza - When: From April 27 to May 7, 2026 - Where: Viganello Middle School, via Boscioro 7 - Who: Future in Peace association and Viganello Middle School - Amount: Not yet specified The project, exhibited at Viganello Middle School until May 7, 2026, 'has directly connected young people living in deeply different realities.' This is how the note from the Future in Peace association, titled 'Two schools, two worlds, one bond: an exhibition on the twinning between Ticino and Gaza students,' begins. The exhibition presents drawings, letters, and testimonies from the students, offering the public a direct, authentic, and, at times, unsettling glimpse into this encounter between two worlds. Opening hours: Monday to Friday, during school hours. ### The twinning project The project, born from the collaboration between the Future in Peace association and Italian teacher Alessandro Fanciotti, has directly connected young people living in deeply different realities. Through the exchange of letters, drawings, and personal stories, the students did not just communicate: they recognized each other, sharing dreams, fears, and hopes. For the boys and girls of Gaza, the twinning represented much more than a school exchange. It was a moment of respite, a fragment of normality in a life marked by deprivation and uncertainty. Speaking with peers from the other side of the world gave them a sensation often denied: that of being seen, heard, and considered.
