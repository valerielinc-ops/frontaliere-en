Bodio-Giornico: slow and sustainable mobility

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 28 April 2026

The new cycle and pedestrian path between Bodio and Giornico has been completed, a project for sustainable mobility in the region

Contesto

In brief - New cycling and pedestrian path between Bodio and Giornico - Length of 2.1 kilometers - Total cost of 3.7 million francs ## Key facts - What: New cycling and pedestrian path - When: Completed in 2026 - Where: Between Bodio and Giornico, Canton Ticino - Who: Department of Territory (DT), Federal Roads Office (FEDRO), Municipality of Giornico - Amount: 3.7 million francs The new cycling and pedestrian path connecting the neighborhoods of Bodio and Giornico along the Ticino River is finally completed. This project, announced by the Department of Territory (DT), the Federal Roads Office (FEDRO), and the Municipality of Giornico, represents an important step towards sustainable mobility in the region. The 2.1-kilometer path is part of the national route N3 North-South of the Swissmobile network and offers a dedicated and safer connection for cyclists and pedestrians, separated from motorized traffic. With the realization of this infrastructure, conflicts between cycling mobility and heavy traffic have been eliminated, particularly in the industrial area of Bodio. The new route represents the completion of the interventions carried out in recent years by FEDRO as part of the bypass of the industrial area between Bodio and Giornico. The construction took place in coordination with the Heavy Vehicle Control Center of Giornico (HVCC). The opening of the path also has symbolic value, coinciding with the first anniversary of the birth of the new Municipality of Giornico, born from the merger of Giornico and Bodio. This physical connection between the two neighborhoods promotes slow mobility and territorial cohesion. ### Costs and funding The cost of the first section, fully funded by FEDRO, is approximately 2.2 million francs. Analysis of Implications The new cycle-pedestrian path between Bodio and Giornico not only represents an infrastructural improvement but also has important implications for the local community and for cross-border workers who live and work in the region. The path offers a safe and sustainable alternative to motorized traffic, reducing environmental impact and improving air quality. This is particularly relevant for cross-border workers who daily commute between Switzerland and Italy, often using private transportation. ### Comparison with the Previous Situation Before the construction of the cycle-pedestrian path, conflicts between cycling mobility and heavy traffic were a constant concern in the industrial area of Bodio. The presence of heavy vehicles made movements dangerous for cyclists and pedestrians. With the new infrastructure, these conflicts have been eliminated, making movements safer and easier. Moreover, the path offers a direct connection between the two neighborhoods, promoting territorial cohesion and improving the quality of life for residents. ### Future Scenarios With the opening of the cycle-pedestrian path, an increase in the use of bicycles and non-motorized transport in the region is expected. This could lead to a reduction in vehicular traffic and a decrease in atmospheric pollution. Furthermore, the path could become a tourist attraction, drawing visitors interested in exploring the natural beauty of the Ticino River and its surroundings. ### Implications for Cross-Border Workers For cross-border workers, the new cycle-pedestrian path offers an opportunity to reduce transportation costs and improve quality of life.
