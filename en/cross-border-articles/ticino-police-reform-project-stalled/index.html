Ticino police reform: project stalled

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 30 April 2026

The State Council is not implementing the reorganization between cantonal and municipal police. Zali: 'Political decision not yet made'

Contesto

In brief - Ticino police project completed but not implemented - Municipal police opposed to the project - Political decision not yet taken ## Key facts - What: Reorganization of cantonal and municipal police - When: Proposal born in 2016 - Where: Canton Ticino - Who: State Council, Claudio Zali - Amount: Not specified The project to reorganize the Ticino police, started in 2016, has been completed but will not be implemented for the moment. This was decided by the State Council. The reorganization proposal aimed to modify the distribution of tasks between the cantonal police and the municipal police. The latter, opposed to the project, say they are satisfied with the current model. For State Councilor Claudio Zali, the political responsible for the cantonal police, the project has not been shelved, but is available. Speaking to RSI, he explains that the work is finished and the results 'are one of the possible future decisions of the State Council'. This means that it has not been decided whether 'this project will be shelved or not, or whether it will really constitute the future basis for the functioning of the police in our Canton', explains Zali. 'This is a political decision that has not yet been taken'. ### Implications for cross-border workers The decision not to implement the reorganization project of the Ticino police could have implications for cross-border workers living and working in the Canton Ticino. The reorganization could have led to greater efficiency and coordination among police forces, with possible benefits for security and the quality of services offered to the population, including cross-border workers.
