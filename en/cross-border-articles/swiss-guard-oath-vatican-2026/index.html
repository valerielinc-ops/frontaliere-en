28 new Swiss Guards to swear oath at the Vatican

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 29 April 2026

The ceremony will take place on May 6 in the Courtyard of St. Damasus of the Apostolic Palace in the presence of the Pope.

Contesto

In brief - 28 new recruits will swear in on May 6 - Ceremony in the Courtyard of St. Damasus - Presence of the Pope and the Swiss Confederation president ## Key facts - What: Oath of 28 new recruits of the Pontifical Swiss Guard - When: May 6, 2026 - Where: Courtyard of St. Damasus of the Apostolic Palace - Who: Pontifical Swiss Guard, Pope, President of the Confederation Guy Parmelin - Amount: Not specified The oath ceremony for the new recruits of the Pontifical Swiss Guard will take place on May 6 in the Courtyard of St. Damasus of the Apostolic Palace. The date coincides with the anniversary of the sack of Rome in 1527, during which 189 Swiss Guards defended Pope Clement VII against the army of Charles V. The new recruits, 28 in total, will wear the so-called 'Gran Gala,' the uniform inclusive of armor that is worn for the papal blessing 'Urbi et Orbi' at Christmas and Easter. The ceremony will take place at 5 PM and will be presided over by the Pope. According to the Holy See, President of the Confederation Guy Parmelin will attend and be received by the pontiff. ### History and tradition The Pontifical Swiss Guard is one of the oldest military institutions still in operation. Founded in 1506 by Pope Julius II, it has the task of protecting the Pope and the Vatican City. The oath ceremony is an event of great symbolic and historical importance, recalling the tradition and sacrifice of the Swiss guards who lost their lives in 1527 to defend the Pope. ### The 'Gran Gala' The 'Gran Gala' uniform is one of the most iconic and recognizable in the world. It includes a helmet with plumes, a breastplate, a blue and red skirt, and a halberd. This uniform is worn only on special occasions, such as the oath of new recruits and papal blessings.
