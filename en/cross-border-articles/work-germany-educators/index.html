Frequently Asked Questions
How can I work as a child educator in Germany with a good salary?
You can access employment contracts as a childcare educator in Germany with salaries of up to €3,000 per month thanks to the Sunflower Exchange project. To be eligible, you must be a young person between 18 and 35 years old with a degree in Education or Pedagogy and know the German language at level A2 or B1.
What is the level of German language proficiency required to work as a child educator in Germany through Sunflower Exchange?
A German language level of A2 or B1 is required. Sunflower Exchange offers specific language courses to bridge the language gap, often funded by the European Union.
How much does it cost to live in Bavaria for a child educator with a salary of 3,000 euros per month?
On average, the rent of a studio apartment in Munich or Nuremberg is between 800 and 1,200 euros; utility costs add 150 euros. The monthly costs for food, transport and insurance are around 600-700 euros. After taxes (about 20%) and costs, the net available is between 2,200 and 2,400 euros.
What documents do I need to request a refund for the move offered by Sunflower Exchange?
Required: copy of the German employment contract, detailed transport and packaging invoices, proof of payment (bank transfer or receipt), signed declaration of minimum stay of six months and, if available, the refund form provided by Sunflower Exchange. All documents must be sent within 30 days of the transfer.
How does the continuing education course for educators work in Germany and what salary increases can it guarantee?
German facilities offer specialization courses (e.g. intercultural pedagogy, management of 0-3 year old groups) financed by the employer. After 12 months of training and certification, educators can access coordinator roles with salary increases of 10% to 20%, bringing the salary to about 3,600 euros per month.
