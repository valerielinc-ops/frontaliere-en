In Ticino, the wage gap between residents and cross-border workers is 19%

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 23 April 2026

The study by the Canton Ticino's Statistical Office reveals a 19.4% wage gap between cross-border workers and residents, with a slight reduction compared to 2022.

Contesto

TL;DR - Cross-border workers in Ticino earn 19.4% less than residents in 2024. - Wage gap slightly reduced from 20.2% in 2020–2022. - Gap widens with higher responsibilities and wage levels. - Minimum wage in Ticino is 20 CHF/hour in 2024, rising in 2027. ## Key facts - Wage gap 2024: 19.4% lower for cross-border workers than residents. - Wage gap 2020-2022: 20.2% lower for cross-border workers than residents. - Median salary 2024: 4,600 CHF/month for cross-border workers, 5,708 CHF/month for residents. - Minimum wage 2024: 20 CHF/hour, increasing in 2027. - Salary calculation: Includes shift allowances, 1/12 of thirteenth salary and bonuses. - Unexplained gap: Attributable to factors like negotiation skills and willingness to accept lower pay. - High responsibility roles: Gap remains significant even in higher wage brackets. - Recommendations: Acquire skills, negotiate salaries, monitor high-value sectors. ## Wage gap decreases, but remains significant According to the latest study by the Canton Ticino Statistics Office (USTAT), in 2024 the median salary of cross-border workers in the private sector in Ticino was 19.4% lower than that of residents. This figure confirms a structural disparity, but also marks a slight reduction compared to the 2020–2022 period, when the gap was 20.2%. A very modest reduction, but one that USTAT defines as statistically significant. Practical Implications and Concrete Scenarios The slight reduction in the salary differential observed between 2020 and 2024 is not coincidental, but fits into a deeply changed regulatory and fiscal context. On one hand, the introduction and gradual adjustment of the cantonal minimum wage have particularly affected the lowest wages. In 2024, the minimum threshold is around 20 gross francs per hour, with an increase expected for 2027. ### Concrete Scenarios for Cross-Border Workers For cross-border workers employed in Ticino, the reduction in the salary gap represents a step forward, but the gap remains significant. For example, a cross-border worker with a median salary of approximately 4,600 gross francs per month could see an increase of about 100 francs per month thanks to the reduction in the gap. However, this increase is still far from bridging the difference with residents, who receive a median salary of approximately 5,708 francs. ### Comparisons with the Previous Situation Before the introduction of the minimum wage and the new tax agreement with Italy, the salary gap was even wider. In 2020, the gap was 20.2%, with cross-border workers earning on average 20% less than residents.
