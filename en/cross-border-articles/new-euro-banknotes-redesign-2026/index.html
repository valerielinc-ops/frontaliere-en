New euro banknotes: two themes and historical faces in the running

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 27 April 2026

The European Central Bank is speeding up the redesign of euro banknotes with two proposals: European culture and nature. Final decision by the end of 2026.

After years of announcements, the project has entered the decisive phase. There is no official date yet for the circulation, but two possible graphic lines have been defined that could characterize the future series. The first proposed theme is 'European culture,' which aims to strengthen the common identity through symbolic figures of the continent. Among the names are Maria Callas for the 5 euro, Ludwig van Beethoven on the 10, Marie Curie on the 20, Miguel de Cervantes on the 50, Leonardo da Vinci on the 100, and Bertha von Suttner on the 200 euro. The alternative is the theme 'Rivers and birds,' with naturalistic images designed to represent freedom of movement and absence of borders.
