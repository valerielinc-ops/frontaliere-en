Wage gap in Ticino: residents earn 1,157 francs more

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 24 April 2026

The cantonal statistical office reveals that residents in Ticino earn an average of 5,957 francs, while cross-border workers earn 4,800 francs. The gap is 19.4%.

Dettagli operativi

Why do residents earn more? But how much does the "status" of a cross-border worker really weigh on the paycheck? The Statistical Office of the Canton of Ticino (USTAT) tried to isolate this factor by comparing workers with the same characteristics (same age, experience, sector, and role). Result? The "pure" gap drops to 10.4%. This means that about half of the wage difference is due to objective factors (the type of work or experience), while the other half is linked to less tangible elements: negotiation skills, different economic needs, or, in some cases, disparities in treatment. ### Practical Implications The wage gap has significant implications for cross-border workers. For example, a cross-border worker who earns 4,800 francs gross per month, after taxes, might find themselves with a significantly lower net amount compared to a resident with the same gross salary. This can influence savings capacity and quality of life. For residents, the gap represents an economic advantage, but also a challenge. The Canton of Ticino is trying to make sectors so far dominated by cross-border workers more appealing to residents (unemployed or low-skilled), hoping that the new salary ranges approved in Parliament can, over time, further balance the market. ### Concrete Examples - Lugano: A resident employee in a service company earns an average of 6,500 francs gross per month, while a cross-border worker with the same duties receives approximately 5,343 francs. - Bellinzona: A resident construction worker has an average salary of 5,200 francs gross, compared to 4,148 francs for a cross-border colleague. - Locarno: A resident teacher earns approximately 7,000 francs gross, while a cross-border worker receives 5,950 francs. ### Regulations and Dates - Canton Law of 2023: Th...
