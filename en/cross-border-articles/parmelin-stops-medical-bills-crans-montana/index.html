Parmelin confirms: no more medical bills for Crans-Montana victims' families (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 6 May 2026

Swiss president Guy Parmelin has reiterated that no more medical bills will be sent to the families of the victims of the New Year's Eve fire in Crans-Montana.

Contesto

In brief - Parmelin confirms stop to medical bills for Crans-Montana victims - Meeting with Mattarella in Rome - Bills sent by law ## Key facts - What: Stop sending medical bills - When: May 5, 2026 - Where: Rome, Italy - Who: Swiss President Guy Parmelin - Amount: Not specified Swiss President Guy Parmelin reiterated the stop to sending medical bills to the families of Italian victims of Crans-Montana, meeting Italian President Sergio Mattarella in Rome on Tuesday morning. This was reported by news agencies ANSA, Adnkronos, and Askanews. On Monday, Parmelin had already expressed himself in this regard, meeting Italian Prime Minister Giorgia Meloni on the sidelines of the European Political Community (EPC) in Yerevan, Armenia. 'To avoid any misunderstanding, Switzerland will no longer send copies of the bills to the families of the victims' of the tragic New Year's Eve fire at the Le Constellation bar in Crans-Montana, the Confederation President had declared. Bills, we recall, for which payment was never requested. In the Confederation, entities and subjects providing medical services, such as in this case the Sion hospital, are obliged to send a copy of the bills to the people they have treated. This is provided for in the Federal Law on Health Insurance (LAMal) in Article 42, paragraph 3. The logic behind this legal obligation is to allow patients to verify the information contained in the bill and, if necessary, communicate any corrections to their respective insurances. ### Implications for cross-border workers This decision has a significant impact on cross-border workers who work in Switzerland and reside in Italy. Dettagli operativi

Practical Implications for Cross-Border Workers The decision by Parmelin has a direct impact on cross-border workers who work in Switzerland and reside in Italy. In particular, for those who have family members who have received medical treatment in Switzerland, this decision avoids further bureaucratic complications and emotional stress. It is important to note that, although bills will no longer be sent, the costs of medical care will still be covered by Swiss health insurance, as provided for by the LAMal. ### Procedure for Cross-Border Workers For cross-border workers who have already received medical bills, it is advisable to contact the Swiss health insurance to clarify any doubts or requests for reimbursement. Additionally, it is important to keep all documentation related to the medical care received, even if the bills will no longer be sent. This is to ensure transparency and accuracy of the information provided to the insurers. ### Comparison with the Previous Situation Before this decision, family members of the victims of Crans-Montana received medical bills, despite not being required to pay. This caused controversy and discontent, especially in Italy. Parmelin's decision represents a step forward in managing Italian-Swiss relations, avoiding further tensions and ensuring more humane and respectful treatment for the victims and their families. ### Analysis of the International Situation The meeting between Parmelin and Mattarella also addressed other topics of common interest, including the promotion of peace and mediation policies.
