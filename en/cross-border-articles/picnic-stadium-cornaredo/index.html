Huge Community Celebration at Cornaredo: Final Picnic on June 4 (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 8 May 2026

On June 4, Cornaredo Stadium will host a major event to bid farewell to the venue, celebrating 75 years since its opening with activities and live music.

Contesto

In brief Special event at Cornaredo on June 4th from 5:00 PM to 10:00 PM First activity for children between 5 and 14 years old with well-known players Picnic, music, and entertainment on the field ## Key facts - What: Community celebration and picnic at Cornaredo Stadium - When: Thursday, June 4th, from 5:00 PM to 10:00 PM - Where: Cornaredo Stadium, Lugano - Who: Organized by the City of Lugano - Admission: Free entry; the first 300 registrants will receive a free picnic basket This event marks a farewell to the historic stadium, inaugurated on November 25, 1951. The celebration, called “Grass Picnic,” will temporarily transform the football pitch into a gathering and leisure space. From 5:00 PM to 6:00 PM, children aged 5 to 14 will have the chance to participate in training sessions with Mattia Bottani, Mijat Maric, and, if the playoffs allow, Jonathan Sabbatini. This activity aims to engage young football enthusiasts, giving them the opportunity to meet and play with their idols. Starting at 6:30 PM, the field will become a large social space for a picnic featuring live music and entertainment. There will be bar points and a free buffet until supplies last. Participation is open to everyone, with registration available via the MyLugano app. The first 300 registrants will receive a free picnic basket. Entertainment will be provided by Circo Fortuna artists, while at 8:30 PM, the United Soloists Orchestra will take the stage. Cornaredo Stadium, opened in 1951, has played a central role in Lugano’s sporting and cultural life. Before its construction, in 1908, a group of young people created a football field at Campo Marzio, which was used until the stadium’s opening. Dettagli operativi

Practical implications for cross-border workers For cross-border workers residing in Italy, the event represents a unique opportunity to engage and remember the sporting roots of Lugano. Free and open to all, participation allows for a moment of socializing and exchange between border communities, strengthening the sense of belonging. The presence of bar points and a free buffet facilitates staying on site, even with the aim of organizing one's afternoon without additional costs. From a practical standpoint, cross-border workers in Italy should consider the possibility of reaching Lugano by public transport or car, keeping in mind that the event takes place in the afternoon. Current tax agreements and reimbursement methods allow for the use of any facilities or refunds on travel expenses, especially if participating in activities with children. For those working in Switzerland and residing in Northern Italy, this festival can also represent an opportunity for leisure and strengthening relationships between the two shores, contributing to strengthening the sense of community. For those not yet registered, it is advisable to download the MyLugano app and register for the dedicated services, so as to receive updates and participate in the assignment of free baskets. Participation in the afternoon training, reserved for children between 5 and 14 years old, instead requires a reservation via email. It is also important to know the access and safety rules, respecting the indications provided by the organizers, to make the most of this special day. Furthermore, this initiative can be an example of how Lugano's sports and recreational facilities are being renewed and enhanced, with new areas dedicated to young people, families, and athletes, in view of the new Sports and Eve...

Punti chiave

How to Participate and What to Do To take part in the event, start by downloading the MyLugano app. Registration allows you to sign up for various program activities, receive a free picnic basket (for the first 300 registrants), and get real-time updates. Here are the steps to follow: - Download the MyLugano app from your app store. - Create an account and log in. - Register for the “Picnic sull’erba” through the events section. - If you're interested in the children's activities, send an email to sport@lugano.ch specifying age and name. - On June 4th, come with an ID and, if registered, bring your registration confirmation. - Arrive early to secure your free picnic basket and participate in the activities. In case of bad weather, the celebration will be moved to Sunday, June 7th, with the same schedule. Remember to follow the current safety regulations, maintain social distancing in the refreshment areas, and heed the organizers' instructions. For those wishing to learn more about participation options or download informational materials, it is recommended to visit the official page www.lugano.ch. You can also use tools like permits if you need to organize your trip or check parking access conditions. Finally, this day offers a great opportunity to discover the upcoming green and sports areas near the stadium, helping to improve quality of life and community gathering spaces in the heart of Lugano. Don't miss this chance to enjoy a special moment with your family, taking advantage of the resources offered by the website and digital services dedicated to cross-border workers and local communities. Frequently Asked Questions
How can I participate in the picnic at Cornaredo Stadium?
To take part, download the MyLugano app, register for the event, and if you're interested in activities for children, book via email at sport@lugano.ch. Remember to arrive early on June 4th, bringing an ID document. The first 300 registrants will receive a free picnic basket.
What activities are planned for children?
From 5:00 PM to 6:00 PM, children aged 5 to 14 can train with Mattia Bottani, Mijat Maric, and, if playoffs allow, Jonathan Sabbatini, at the Cornaredo field. Booking must be made via email, specifying the child's name and age.
What happens in case of bad weather?
The event will be postponed to Sunday, June 7th, with the same schedule. It is important to check for updates on the official website www.lugano.ch and the MyLugano app.
How can cross-border workers obtain travel reimbursements or discounts to participate?
Cross-border workers can benefit from tax agreements and reimbursement schemes that facilitate travel and transportation costs. For more details, consult comparison tools like [cost-of-living](nav:cost-of-living).
What safety rules must be followed?
During the event, respect the instructions of the organizers, maintain social distancing at refreshment areas, and follow the safety guidelines in place. It is advisable to check the official website for updates.
