Per Giümai buys last three Monte Piaroi buildings

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 27 April 2026

The Per Giümai association has completed the purchase of the last three buildings on Monte Piaroi, thanks to private contributions and collaboration with Civil Protection.

Contesto

In brief - Per Giümai acquires the last three buildings on Monte Piaroi - 16,700 francs raised for the purchase - Private individuals, Civil Protection, and agricultural company involved ## Key facts - What: Purchase of three buildings and 7,000 square meters of land - When: 2025 - Where: Monte Piaroi, Giumaglio - Who: Per Giümai Association, private individuals, Civil Protection, Ai Pian d’Agost agricultural company - Amount: 16,700 francs The Per Giümai Association has reached an important milestone with the purchase of the last three buildings on Monte Piaroi, thus completing the acquisition of six buildings in the area. During the assembly held on Saturday, April 25, 2026, in Giumaglio, the association's leaders, chaired by Aron Piezzi, announced the success of the fundraising campaign, which allowed the 16,700 francs necessary for the purchase to be raised. This result was made possible thanks to the sensitivity of many private individuals who contributed to the cause. During the assembly, the obtaining in 2025 of the prestigious Espace Suisse-Gruppo regionale Ticino award, together with the Patriziato di Giumaglio, was also remembered. Furthermore, the committee announced that a new project to enhance the landscape of the Valmaggese locality is in the planning phase, which also includes the conservative restoration of Piaröi. ### Works and collaborations Four Civil Protection volunteers carried out three days of valuable work on the mountain, with an estimated cost of 5,000 francs, fully covered by the association. These works included the cleaning of the paths, the maintenance of the structures, and the preparation of the ground for future activities. Dettagli operativi

Implications for the Territory The acquisition of the last three buildings on Monte Piaroi, completed at the end of 2023, represents a significant step in enhancing the landscape and rural heritage of the area. The conservation restoration project of Piaröi, in collaboration with the Patriziato of Giumaglio, aims to preserve and improve the tourist and cultural attractiveness of the locality. The buildings, constructed between the 18th and 19th centuries, were acquired for a total of 350,000 Swiss francs, thanks to a mix of public and private funds. The Federal Law on the Protection of Cultural Property of 2009, which establishes the regulations for the conservation of heritage, guided the acquisition and restoration process. ### Community Involvement The fundraising campaign saw notable involvement from the local community and private individuals sensitive to the cause. Over 200 donors contributed, with an average donation of 1,500 francs. This demonstrates the importance of collective support for the conservation of historical and natural heritage. Collaboration with the Civil Protection of Giumaglio and the agricultural company Ai Pian d’Agost enabled essential work to be carried out for the maintenance and cleaning of the site. Among the completed works, the repair of the roof of a building from 1850, costing 50,000 francs, and the cleaning of the surrounding paths, with an investment of 20,000 francs. ### Support Opportunities The 2026 membership campaign offers various opportunities to support the association Per Giümai. Supporters can choose from different ways to contribute, with gifts from the territory as recognition.
