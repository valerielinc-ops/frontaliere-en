Italian ambassador Cornado responds to Cassis' criticism over hospital bills for Italian victims of the Crans-Montana fire.

Contesto

In brief - Italian ambassador Cornado responds to Cassis' criticism - Meloni asks for attention on hospital bills - Cassis accuses Cornado of fueling tensions ## Key facts - What: Cassis' criticism of ambassador Cornado - When: Interview published on Thursday - Where: Bern, Switzerland - Who: Ignazio Cassis (FDFA), Gian Lorenzo Cornado (Italian ambassador) - Amount: Not yet specified Italian ambassador Gian Lorenzo Cornado has responded to criticism from the head of the Federal Department of Foreign Affairs (FDFA), Ignazio Cassis. In an interview with the Neue Zürcher Zeitung, Cornado stated: 'I speak without mincing words. It's my style.' The Italian Prime Minister, Giorgia Meloni, would have asked to pay particular attention to the hospital bills related to the care provided to injured Italians. 'And that's exactly what I did,' Cornado declared. Cassis' criticism Cassis had stated to Ticinonews that the 'endless discussion' about the Crans-Montana fire (in which, we recall, 41 people lost their lives and over a hundred were injured) was also fueled by Cornado. According to the FDFA head, it would be the ambassador's duty to ease tensions in bilateral relations. The controversy over the bills At the end of April, Meloni had expressed indignation at the fact that hospital bills and related copies had been sent from Switzerland to Italy. Such bills concerned the care provided to the victims of the fire that broke out on New Year's Eve at the Le Constellation bar in Crans-Montana. Towards a solution The President of the Confederation, Guy Parmelin, assured that this will not happen again. Analysis of bilateral tensions The tensions between Italy and Switzerland regarding hospital bills for the victims of the Crans-Montana fire raise important questions about bilateral relations. The controversy concerns not only medical expenses but also the management of emergencies and cooperation between the two countries. Ambassador Cornado stated that the issue should be resolved soon, but it is important to understand the implications of this situation for cross-border workers. ### Comparison with the previous situation Before this controversy, the procedures for the reimbursement of cross-border medical expenses were relatively clear. However, the sending of hospital bills from Italy to Switzerland has created confusion and tensions. It is fundamental that cross-border workers understand how this situation could influence their health insurance and reimbursement procedures. ### Possible scenarios 1. Maintaining the status quo: If the issue is resolved without regulatory changes, current procedures will remain unchanged. 2. Regulatory changes: If new regulations are introduced, cross-border workers will have to adapt to the new reimbursement procedures. 3. Strengthened cooperation: If Italy and Switzerland decide to strengthen cooperation, a more efficient system for managing cross-border medical expenses could be introduced. ### Comparison between the two countries Italy and Switzerland have different approaches to managing cross-border medical expenses. In Italy, INPS is responsible for reimbursing medical expenses for cross-border workers, while in Switzerland private insurances manage these expenses.
