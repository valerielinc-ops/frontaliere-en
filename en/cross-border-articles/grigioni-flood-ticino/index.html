Frequently Asked Questions
What is the cantonal contribution paid for the restoration of agricultural structures damaged by the 2024 storms in the Graubünden region?
The cantonal contribution is 511,000 francs.
What is the procedure for claiming the cantonal contribution for agricultural damage in Graubünden after a storm?
Farmers must apply for the contribution through the Municipality of Lostallo, providing documentation of the damage and invoices. The Municipality verifies the documents and manages the centralized payment.
How long does it take to receive the cantonal contribution after submitting the application?
Payment is made within 30 to 45 days from the date of acceptance of the application, provided that all the required documents – damage report, verified invoices and certificate from the Municipality of Lostallo – are complete. In the event of incomplete documentation, the deadline can be extended up to 60 days.
What is the maximum contribution limit per individual farmer and how is it calculated?
The maximum ceiling for each farmer is set at 30% of the total eligible costs, with an absolute ceiling of 500,000 francs. To calculate it, the value of the documented expenses (structural, infrastructural, agricultural and real estate restoration) is added and the percentage provided is applied, without exceeding the limit of CHF 500,000.
How are the funds distributed among the municipalities of the Graubünden region?
The municipality of Lostallo collects the entire amount of 511,000 francs and divides it among the municipalities involved according to the share of expenditure approved for each project. For 2024, the breakdown was: Roveredo 40% (204,400 CHF), Grono 20% (102,200 CHF), Cama 15% (76,650 CHF), Soazza 15% (76,650 CHF) and Mesocco 10% (51,100 CHF).
