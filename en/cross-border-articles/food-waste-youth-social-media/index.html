Deputy Maria Chiara Gadda highlights the importance of social networks to combat food waste and engage young people.

Contesto

Deputy Maria Chiara Gadda has placed social networks and new communication languages at the heart of the strategy to combat food waste. During the conference 'Don't throw it away! The role of green influencers in combating food waste and promoting good practices,' held at Montecitorio on May 5, 2026, Gadda outlined a balance of the first decade of Law 166. The law, known as the anti-waste law, was signed by Gadda and outlined a balance of the first decade of the legislation during the conference dedicated to the role of green influencers. Gadda emphasized the importance of further spreading anti-waste culture and correct information about the value of food. 'We must do this using new words and new communication tools. In this, social networks can be a very powerful vehicle, especially to involve young people who are interested in sustainability and want to give their active contribution through volunteering or by disseminating credible content,' Gadda said. Dettagli operativi

Practical Analysis The use of social networks to combat food waste offers numerous practical advantages. Firstly, social media allows reaching a wider and more diverse audience, including young people who are often more active on these platforms. This approach can increase awareness and education about the value of food and the importance of reducing waste. ### Engaging the Youth Young people are a crucial target for this campaign. They are often more sensitive to sustainability issues and can be motivated to actively participate through volunteering or sharing content on social media. Initiatives like Equoevento at the Rome Tennis Internationals demonstrate how young people can be involved in concrete projects that have a positive impact on society. ### Role of Influencers Green influencers, such as Alessio Cicchini, play a fundamental role in spreading credible information and sustainable practices. These influencers can reach a wider audience and positively influence consumption habits. Furthermore, they can collaborate with local organizations to promote events and initiatives that reduce food waste. ### Opportunities for Cross-Border Workers Cross-border workers who work in Ticino and reside in Italy can benefit from these initiatives by actively participating. They can find volunteering opportunities and involvement in local projects aimed at reducing food waste. Additionally, they can use social networks to share their experiences and promote sustainable practices among their friends and family. ### Examples of Local Initiatives In Ticino, there are numerous local initiatives aimed at reducing food waste.
