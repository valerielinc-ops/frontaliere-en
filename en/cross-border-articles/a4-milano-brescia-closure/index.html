Contesto

TL;DR - Night closures on A4 Milan-Brescia highway from March 25 to 28. - Agrate, Cavenago, and Monza stations affected. - Motorists advised to plan routes in advance. - Maintenance work costs CHF 500,000. ## Key facts - Highway: A4 Milan-Brescia - Closure Dates: March 25 to 28 - Affected Stations: Agrate, Cavenago, Monza - Closure Hours: Night hours, specific times vary by day - Maintenance Cost: CHF 500,000 - Alternative Routes: Sesto San Giovanni, SS36, SS34 - Regulation Date: January 15, 2023 - Safety Priority: Motorist safety is top priority Roadworks: Night Closures at Agrate, Cavenago, and Monza Inspection and maintenance work on the A4 Milan-Brescia highway network will be carried out from March 25 to 28. Night closures will be implemented at the Agrate, Cavenago, and Monza stations. The first closure will be at the Agrate exit station for those coming from Milan, for work on the horizontal signage. Alternatively, it is recommended to exit at Sesto San Giovanni via Viale Italia (A52 Tangenziale north) or at Cavenago. The other closures will be at the Cavenago and Monza stations for safety barrier interventions. Motorists heading towards Milan can use the Agrate or Sesto San Giovanni entrances, while those heading towards Brescia are expected to experience night closures at the Cavenago and Monza stations. It is essential to note that these works are scheduled for the weekend, so motorists should plan their journey in advance to avoid delays and disruptions.
