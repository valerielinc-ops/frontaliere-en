In brief - New bike strategy for Bellinzona and the Valleys - 13 official mountain bike trails - International events and collaborations ## Key facts - What: Bike strategy for Bellinzona and the Valleys - When: Presented in May 2026 - Where: Bellinzona and the Valleys, Ticino - Who: Bellinzona and Valleys Tourism - Amount: 13 official mountain bike trails Bellinzona and Valleys Tourism has presented to the press its strategy dedicated to the world of two wheels, a path that has been underway for years and is now more structured. The goal is clear: to position the destination as a reference point for enthusiasts and the general public, thanks to a diversified and continuously evolving offer. Central to this is the work with local partners, including the Regional Development Authority for Bellinzona and the Valleys, Ticino Tourism, other Regional Tourist Organizations, and local associations such as Blenio Bike and the newly formed Leventina Bike. ### Growing offer between mountain bike and road The destination boasts 13 official mountain bike trails and a flow trail at Nara, appreciated by experts and beginners alike. These are complemented by the Bike Parks of Airolo and Nara, expanding realities that are investing in the development of the summer offer with packages and dedicated activities, also contributing to destagionalization. Among the ongoing projects is a new mountain bike trail between Campra, Gorda, and Nara, which will be integrated into the Quattro and Cinque Passi Bike itineraries around the Gottardo massif, strengthening the interregional network with destinations such as Andermatt, Disentis-Sedrun, and Goms. Dettagli operativi

Targeted Promotion and International Vision The strategy is also based on structured promotion, with collaborations with influencers and testimonials that have contributed to strengthening the visibility of the destination and the growth of digital communities. These are complemented by partnerships with specialized media such as ALVENTO for the Italian and international market and Ride.ch for the national public. The activities are part of a coordinated strategy with Ticino Turismo and Switzerland Tourism, with a focus on foreign markets, particularly Italy. Among the initiatives, participation in events such as the Cycling Tourism Fair and the Cycle Week in Zurich. ### Bike Events, Drivers of Visibility Events represent a pillar of the strategy and in recent years have registered significant growth. Among the main appointments, the passage of the Giro d'Italia on May 26 stands out, with a stage that will cross Bellinzona, Biasca, the Riviera, the Leventina, and the Valle di Blenio, offering an international showcase. From the Giro will also be born a new event, the Biasca-Faido-Carì Bike Day on May 23, destined to be repeated in the coming years. The strategy of Bellinzona and Valli Turismo not only promotes cycling tourism but also contributes to the destagionalization and diversification of the region's tourist offer. This integrated approach, which combines infrastructure, events, and promotion, aims to create a complete cycling ecosystem, attractive to all types of cyclists, from professionals to occasional enthusiasts. ### Implications for Cross-Border Workers For cross-border workers who live in Italy and work in Ticino, this strategy represents an opportunity to explore new outdoor activities during their free time. Frequently Asked Questions
What are the main mountain bike trails available in Bellinzona and the surrounding valleys?
The region offers 13 official mountain bike trails, including a flow trail at Nara and the Bike Parks of Airolo and Nara. Additionally, a new trail is being developed between Campra, Gorda, and Nara, which will be integrated into the Quattro and Cinque Passi Bike itineraries around the Gottardo massif.
What cycling events are planned in the region?
Among the main events are the passage of the Giro d'Italia on May 26 and the Biasca-Faido-Carì Bike Day on May 23, which will be repeated in the coming years. Additionally, the cantonal project Ticino Traverso is scheduled for October 2-4.
How can I participate in the cycling events organized by Bellinzona and Valli Turismo?
To participate in the events, you can visit the official website of Bellinzona and Valli Turismo for detailed information and registration procedures. Some events may require advance booking, so it's important to check the details in advance.
