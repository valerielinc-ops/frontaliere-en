Operational details

The increase in rail traffic in Ticino has been constant in recent years, confirming the positive trend already observed earlier. In 2019, TILO transported 16.7 million passengers, while in 2025 the number rose to 25 million, representing a 50% increase in six years. The daily average of passengers transported by TILO is around 76,400 people, with peaks recorded during morning and evening rush hours. This increase is also due to the introduction of new stops and the improvement of services. For example, the opening of the new Lugano Paradiso stop in 2022 increased the frequency of trains and reduced travel times between nearby locations. Furthermore, the introduction of the cadenced timetable has made the service more predictable and reliable, encouraging more people to use public transport. The new Lugano Paradiso stop recorded an average of 1,200 passengers per day in the first half of 2023, significantly contributing to the overall increase in passengers. Another factor that contributed to the increase in passengers was the adjustment of tariffs and commercial offers. TILO introduced a new system of monthly and annual subscriptions in 2023, which is more convenient for commuters, with an average saving of 15% compared to previous tariffs. The data show that the municipalities most served by TILO, such as Lugano, Bellinzona, and Chiasso, have recorded the largest increases in the number of passengers. In particular, the Lugano-Bellinzona line saw a 60% increase in the number of daily passengers compared to 2019.
