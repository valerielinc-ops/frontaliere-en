Open dairies in Ticino: 18 farms to visit (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 5 May 2026

On Saturday 9 and Sunday 10 May 2026, 18 dairies in Ticino open their doors to the public for the 13th edition of Open Dairies

Contesto

In brief - 18 dairies open in Ticino - Saturday 9 and Sunday 10 May 2026 - Tastings and guided tours ## Key facts - What: 13th edition of Open Dairies - When: 9-10 May 2026, 10:00-17:00 - Where: 18 dairies in Ticino - Who: Agriculture Section of the Economy Division - Amount: 200 dairy realities in Ticino The 13th edition of the agro-food event 'Open Dairies' is scheduled for Saturday 9 and Sunday 10 May 2026. During these two days, from 10:00 to 17:00, 18 dairies spread across the entire canton will open their facilities to the public. The initiative is promoted by the Agriculture Section of the Economy Division, with the involvement of the Agro-Food Conference. The Canton of Ticino boasts a diversified and high-quality dairy production. In the territory, almost 200 realities of the sector operate: about a hundred on the alpine pastures, eighty in-farm processors and a dozen dairies that purchase milk from third parties. The event is organized in collaboration with alpinavera, an inter-cantonal association co-financed by the Confederation for the promotion of regional products in the Cantons of Ticino, Grisons, Uri and Glarus. ### Program and activities Each company will offer a personalized program, with tastings, informative moments, activities for children, animations and catering offers. Visitors will be able to meet the producers in person, visit stables and processing facilities, witness the various stages of processing and taste fresh and aged cheeses, yogurt and other milk derivatives. Particular attention is paid to products certified with the Ticino regio.garantie label, which guarantees the origin and quality of regional productions. Implications for cross-border workers The "Caseifici Aperti" event represents a unique opportunity for cross-border workers living in Italy and working in Ticino. This initiative not only allows them to discover the richness of local dairy production but also offers an occasion to deepen their knowledge of the territory and its producers. For cross-border workers residing in Lombardy and working in Ticino, participating in this event can be a way to better integrate into the local community and discover the agro-food traditions of the Canton Ticino. ### Comparison with the previous situation Compared to previous editions, this 13th edition of "Caseifici Aperti" stands out for the record number of participating dairies, which have increased from 15 to 18. This increase reflects the growing attention towards the valorization of local products and the promotion of the Ticino agro-food sector. Furthermore, the collaboration with alpinavera has allowed the offer of activities and tastings to be expanded, making the event even richer and more varied. ### Practical scenarios Cross-border workers who decide to participate in "Caseifici Aperti" can choose to visit one or more dairies based on their time availability and interests. For example, those passionate about aged cheeses can opt for a visit to a dairy specializing in this type of product, while those who prefer fresh cheeses can choose a dairy that offers tastings of soft cheeses.
