In brief - Uzbekistan opens up to new investors with Mirziyoyev - Gold and gas at the heart of economic growth - Karimova trial in Switzerland with financial implications ## Key facts - What: Economic and political opening in Uzbekistan - When: Since 2016 with Mirziyoyev - Where: Uzbekistan, with impacts in Ticino - Who: President Shavkat Mirziyoyev - Amount: 2025 GDP at $137 billion, growth 6.8% Uzbekistan, located in the heart of Central Asia, represents one of the most strategic yet least known countries on the international scene. A former Soviet republic, independent since 1991, it lived for decades under the presidency of Islam Karimov, until 2016, a highly centralized and authoritarian system. However, in recent years, with the transition to Shavkat Mirziyoyev, there has been a gradual process of political and economic opening, accompanied by growing interest from the international community, especially due to its strategic position and underground wealth. Between modernization, attempts at reform, and persistent challenges, Uzbekistan finds itself at the center of the new Great Game, in Kipling's memory, among neighboring powers, Russia and China, with new and interested external actors, from the United States to the European Union. Gulnara Karimova, daughter of the former Uzbek president, has been on trial since Monday in Bellinzona. She is accused of money laundering, corruption, and heading a criminal organization. Also indicted is a former manager of Lombard Odier and the bank itself. The disputed funds amount to hundreds of millions. The verdict is expected by the end of May. ### Complex Transition After Karimov's death ten years ago, power passed to Mirziyoyev, a man of the system, who had held the role of prime minister since 2003. Implications for Ticino and Cross-Border Workers The economic opening of Uzbekistan and the ongoing trial in Bellinzona against Gulnara Karimova and other defendants, including the Lombard Odier bank, have significant implications for Ticino and cross-border workers. The presence of disputed funds amounting to hundreds of millions of dollars, along with allegations of money laundering and corruption, raise important questions about transparency and financial security in the region. ### Impact on Financial Stability The ongoing legal proceedings could influence investor confidence and the stability of the Ticino financial system. Lombard Odier, involved in the trial, is one of Switzerland's most prestigious financial institutions and a reference point for many cross-border workers operating in the canton. The transparency and security of the Swiss banking system are fundamental to attracting and maintaining foreign investments, and any disruption could have repercussions on investor confidence. ### Investment Opportunities On the other hand, the economic opening of Uzbekistan represents an opportunity for Ticino companies and cross-border workers operating in the financial and natural resources sectors. Uzbekistan, with its mineral and energy wealth, could offer new investment and collaboration opportunities, especially for Swiss companies looking to diversify their portfolios. ### Challenges and Considerations However, it is important to consider the challenges related to this opening. Uzbekistan, despite its progress, remains a country with a still highly centralized political system and limitations on freedom of expression and political participation. What are the main natural resources of Uzbekistan?
Uzbekistan is rich in natural resources, including gold, natural gas, uranium, copper, tungsten, zinc, and molybdenum. It is the seventh-largest gold producer globally and has one of the largest gold reserves in the world. Additionally, it is one of the major natural gas producers in the region.
