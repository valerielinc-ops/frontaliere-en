Solidarity steps: walk in Porto Ceresio for blood donation

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 28 April 2026

On Sunday, May 3rd, Avis Valceresio organizes a walk along the lake to raise awareness about blood donation. Meet at 9 am in Piazza Bossi.

Contesto

In brief - Solidarity walk in Porto Ceresio on May 3rd - Organized by Avis Valceresio for blood donation - Meeting at 9 am in Piazza Bossi, departure at 10 am ## Key facts - What: Solidarity walk - When: Sunday, May 3rd, 2026 - Where: Porto Ceresio, lakeside - Who: Avis Valceresio - Cost: Free and open to all The appointment is for Sunday, May 3rd in Porto Ceresio for "Steps of Solidarity," a walk organized by Avis Valceresio along the lake shores. The event, open to everyone, combines outdoor activity and awareness on blood donation. The meeting point is set at 9 am in Piazza Bossi, with departure at 10 am. Participation is free and open to people of all ages, with the goal of involving the community in a moment of sharing and promoting the values of solidarity. ### An event beyond sports Avis Valceresio, through this event, aims to spread the importance of blood donation and strengthen the bond with the territory. At the end of the walk, a refreshment will be offered to the participants, an opportunity to socialize and conclude the morning together. In case the weather conditions do not allow the event to take place, the walk will be postponed to Saturday, May 10th. The walk will take place along the streets and paths of the town overlooking the lake, offering participants the opportunity to enjoy a suggestive landscape while contributing to a noble cause. The initiative is a concrete way to promote solidarity and sharing, fundamental values for the local community. ### Implications for cross-border workers For cross-border workers residing in the Porto Ceresio area, this initiative represents an opportunity to further integrate into the local community. Practical Analysis: The Importance of Blood Donation Blood donation is an act of solidarity that saves lives. Every year, thousands of people need blood transfusions to undergo surgical procedures, treatments for chronic diseases, or medical emergencies. Blood donation is a safe and relatively simple process that can make a huge difference in the lives of those in need. ### Implications for Cross-Border Workers For cross-border workers who work in Switzerland and reside in Italy, participating in initiatives like 'Passi di solidarietà' can be a way to further integrate into the local community. Participating in awareness events can help better understand the needs of the community and actively contribute to its well-being. ### Comparisons with Other Initiatives Initiatives like 'Passi di solidarietà' are becoming increasingly common in Switzerland and Italy. Action: how to participate and what to do To participate in the "Steps of Solidarity" walk in Porto Ceresio, follow these simple steps: 1. Meeting point: Gather in Piazza Bossi in Porto Ceresio starting from 9 am. 2. Registration: No prior registration is required, but it is advisable to arrive a bit early to secure a spot. 3. Departure: The walk will start at 10 am and will take place along the lake shores. 4. Refreshments: At the end of the walk, enjoy the refreshments offered to participants. 5. Monitoring: In case of bad weather, the event will be postponed to Saturday, May 10th. Monitor any updates on the Avis Valceresio website or the association's social media channels. ### Procedures for blood donation If you are interested in donating blood, follow these steps: 1. Check requirements: Make sure you meet the age, weight, and health requirements. 2. Booking: Schedule an appointment at a donation center or participate in a blood collection day organized by Avis. 3. Donation: During the donation, blood is drawn safely and the process takes about 10-15 minutes. 4. Recovery: After the donation, rest and hydrate properly. ### Useful tools For more information on blood donation and how to participate, visit the Avis Valceresio website or contact the association directly.
