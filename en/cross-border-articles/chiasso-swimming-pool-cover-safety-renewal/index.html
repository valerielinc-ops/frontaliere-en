Chiasso: 850,000 francs for swimming pool safety (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 24 February 2026

The Municipality of Chiasso has requested a credit of 850,000 francs to replace the pressure-supported cover of the municipal swimming pool, which is now dilapidated after 15 years of operation.

The Municipality recently approved a proposal requesting a credit of 850,000 francs for the replacement of the pressure-supported cover of the municipal swimming pool. This structure, a landmark for residents and many cross-border commuters who live or work in the region, now shows the unmistakable signs of fifteen years of intense use and thirty assembly and disassembly operations. The synthetic (PVC) sheets, constantly exposed to weather and operational stress, show a state of disrepair that can no longer be ignored. Dettagli operativi

The intervention is not limited to the simple replacement of the sheets. New recommendations for heated pressure-supported covers require the adoption of a double-sheath membrane, composed of four sheets, instead of the current single-sheath membrane with three sheets. This technological upgrade, although more costly, will ensure a substantial increase in the safety and durability of the structure, reducing maintenance frequency and operational risks. The adoption of the double sheath also entails an adaptation of the existing concrete foundation, which will need to be widened to allow for the anchoring of the second set of sheets. This technical detail highlights the complexity of the operation and its nature as a long-term investment. From a financial perspective, the net burden on the Municipality of Chiasso is significantly mitigated. Despite the credit request being 850,000 francs, the actual exposure for the municipal coffers is estimated at only 60,000 francs. This is possible thanks to significant regional participation and expected contributions from various bodies and foundations, a virtuous example of Ticinese inter-institutional collaboration for the public good. The delivery time for the new dome is estimated at four months, with the resolution needing to be finalized by the end of April 2026 to allow for the installation of the new structure and the adaptation of the foundation by October of the same year.
