EOC Midwives: Conference on Physiological Birth in Mendrisio

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 27 April 2026

On May 6, 2026, EOC is organizing a public conference on midwifery care at Mendrisio Hospital. Focus on physiological birth and continuity of care.

Contesto

In brief - Midwifery conference EOC on May 6, 2026 in Mendrisio - Focus on physiological birth and continuity of care - Entertainment area for children aged 4-10 ## Key facts - What: Public conference on midwifery care - When: May 6, 2026, 16:30-19:00 - Where: Ospedale Beata Vergine, Mendrisio - Who: Ente Ospedaliero Cantonale (EOC) - Cost: Not specified On May 6, 2026, the Ente Ospedaliero Cantonale (EOC) will organize a public conference on midwifery care at the Ospedale Beata Vergine in Mendrisio. The event, on the occasion of International Midwife Day, will take place from 16:30 to 19:00 in the auditorium located on the -1 floor. The initiative aims to promote and enhance midwifery care throughout the birth process, with a particular focus on physiological birth. During the conference, the team of specialists from the Women and Children's Department will present models of care that promote physiological birth. The event, moderated by PD Dr. med. Andrea Braga, is aimed at the public and all professional figures involved in the birth process. The goal is to foster a constructive exchange on best practices in midwifery care. To allow parents with children to participate, from 17:00 to 19:00 there will be a supervised entertainment area for children aged 4 to 10. The young guests will also be offered a healthy snack. ### Detailed program The conference program includes several thematic sessions: - 16:30: Welcome - 17:00: Welcome by PD Dr. med. Dettagli operativi

The conference organized by EOC represents a unique opportunity to delve into the crucial role of midwives in the birth process. Physiological birth, which refers to natural childbirth without medical interventions, is at the center of attention. This approach aims to minimize unnecessary medical interventions, promoting a more natural and respectful birth experience for both maternal and fetal physiology. The exchange between professionals and the public is fundamental to continuously improve obstetric practices. The discussions planned during the conference will allow for sharing experiences and knowledge, contributing to a constant improvement in the quality of obstetric care. ### The importance of continuity of care Another central theme of the conference is postpartum continuity of care. The first 1000 days of a child's life are crucial for their development and well-being. Postpartum continuity of care ensures that the mother and child receive the necessary support during this delicate period. This includes emotional support, health education, and monitoring of both the mother's and child's health. ### Practical implications for cross-border workers For cross-border workers residing in Ticino and working in Italy, the conference represents an opportunity to better understand the Swiss healthcare system and local obstetric practices. Participation in such events can help reduce linguistic and cultural barriers, facilitating access to quality healthcare services. Furthermore, the conference offers the opportunity to learn about the resources available for families, such as children's play areas during public events.
