Como company cuts CO2 with solar panels: 25 tons less

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 29 April 2026

A photovoltaic system on an industrial roof in Cucciago reduces CO2 emissions by 25.71 tons per year, benefiting the local community.

Contesto

In brief - 99.55 kWp photovoltaic plant on 500 m² of industrial roof - 119,460 kWh of clean energy per year - 25.71 tons of CO2 less - Initiative by Greenvolt Next Italia and Benati Enrico Torneria Metalli ## Key facts - What: Installation of a photovoltaic plant on an industrial roof - When: Project completed and operational - Where: Cucciago plant, Como province - Who: Greenvolt Next Italia and Benati Enrico Torneria Metalli - Amount: 25.71 tons of CO2 saved annually - Surface: 500 m² out of 1,300 m² total - Power: 99.55 kWp - Energy produced: 119,460 kWh/year A photovoltaic plant that reduces emissions, improves the energy efficiency of the plant, and brings value to the local community. This is the heart of the new project carried out by Greenvolt Next Italia, part of the Greenvolt group, an international leader in the field of renewable energies, which supports Benati Enrico Torneria Metalli, a company founded in 1976, in its decarbonization path through the production of renewable energy in the Cucciago plant, in the province of Como. The intervention involved the refurbishment of the industrial roof over an area of approximately 500 square meters out of the 1,300 owned and the installation of a 99.55 kWp photovoltaic plant, already completed and fully operational. The system is capable of generating 119,460 kWh of clean energy per year, avoiding the emission of 25.71 tons of CO₂ every year, with a concrete contribution to reducing the environmental impact of the production site. ### Benefits for the community The refurbishment of the industrial roof will improve the infrastructure efficiency and safety of the building. • ## Implications for the Community and the Environment The project carried out by Greenvolt Next Italia and Benati Enrico Torneria Metalli not only reduces CO2 emissions but also contributes to the well-being of the local community. The energy produced by the photovoltaic plant will be shared with CER, CERS of Lario and Brianza, allowing the reinvestment of incentives in social initiatives. This virtuous model strengthens the bond between companies, local authorities, and citizens, making renewable energy a concrete tool for territorial cohesion. ### Energy Transition and Competitiveness For Benati Enrico Torneria Metalli, the intervention represents a further step towards a more efficient and sustainable production model. For Greenvolt Next Italia, it is confirmation of its ability to support companies in customized decarbonization projects, integrating infrastructure upgrading, renewable self-production, and innovative energy-sharing models. With over 800 projects completed in Italy and an installed capacity exceeding 200 MW, Greenvolt Next Italia is today one of the leading operators in the field of distributed renewable generation. ### Economic and Social Benefits The photovoltaic plant not only reduces energy costs for the company but also contributes to creating shared value for the local community. The incentives generated by energy sharing will be reinvested in social initiatives, creating economic and social benefits for the community.
