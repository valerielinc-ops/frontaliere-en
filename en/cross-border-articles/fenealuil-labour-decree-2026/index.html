2026 Labour Decree: FenealUil moderately satisfied

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 28 April 2026

Mauro Franzolini, FenealUil general secretary, comments on the draft labour decree being examined by the Council of Ministers.

Contesto

In brief - FenealUil moderately satisfied with the draft of the 2026 labor decree - Focus on the most representative collective contracts - Tax exemption of contractual increases among hot topics ## Key facts - What: Comment by Mauro Franzolini on draft labor decree - When: 28 April 2026 - Where: Italy - Who: Mauro Franzolini, general secretary of FenealUil - Amount: Not yet specified Mauro Franzolini, general secretary of FenealUil, expressed moderate satisfaction regarding the draft of the labor decree that should be discussed this afternoon by the Council of Ministers. In an interview with Adnkronos/Labitalia, Franzolini highlighted that the draft collects some of the requests advanced by his confederation, the Uil. In particular, he appreciated the fact that the decree focuses on the evaluation of the most representative national collective contracts, signed by Cgil, Cisl, and Uil. 'We are moderately satisfied,' declared Franzolini, 'in the sense that there are some steps that certainly collect what are our requests, in particular from our confederation, the Uil. The fact that the focus is on the evaluation of the most representative national collective contracts is a first step forward.' ### A step forward for workers Franzolini explained that this focus on the most representative contracts is a way to prevent the creation of conditions that could lead to a structural reduction of workers' advantages and rights. He also emphasized the importance of the tax exemption of contractual increases, a topic on which the Uil has worked intensively. 'A first result we have achieved with the 2026 budget law, and now we hope to be able to extend the range of beneficiaries further,' he added. ### Future challenges Despite the progress, Franzolini recognized that there is...

What does the tax exemption of contractual increases mean?
The tax exemption of contractual increases means that part of the salary increases provided for in collective agreements will not be subject to taxation. This results in an increase in the net salary received by workers, improving their purchasing power and economic situation.
