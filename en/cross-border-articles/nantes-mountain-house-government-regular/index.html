Nante mountain house: Government confirms all is regular

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 28 April 2026

The Ticino State Council has dismissed the appeal against the Madonna delle Nevi mountain house renovation project in Nante, confirming a credit of 4.71 million francs.

Contesto

In brief - The State Council has rejected the appeal against the restructuring of the mountain house in Nante - Credit of 4.71 million francs confirmed - The project was deemed compliant with regulations ## Key facts - What: Restoration of the mountain house Madonna delle Nevi in Nante - When: State Council decision on April 28, 2026 - Where: Monteceneri, Canton Ticino - Who: Ticino State Council - Amount: 4.71 million francs The Ticino State Council has rejected the appeal filed by three citizens of Monteceneri against the restoration project of the mountain house Madonna delle Nevi in Nante. The decision confirms the credit of 4.71 million francs allocated to the project, approved by the Municipal Council last June 11. The appellants had contested several aspects of the decision, including the alleged lack of information on costs, external financing, and the future management model. According to them, the project presented financial uncertainties and an excessive risk for the municipality, as well as possible violations of financial management principles and the prohibition of speculation. The State Council found these criticisms unfounded, highlighting how the project and the budget were elaborated in a complete and compliant manner with regulations. The increase in costs compared to the initial estimates was justified by the evolution of the project and the adaptation to legal requirements, particularly energy-related ones. Regarding financing, the cantonal government noted that the municipal message clearly indicated the expected sources, including cantonal contributions, energy incentives, and own funds. ### Implications for the community The State Council's decision has important implications for the community of Monteceneri and for cross-border workers who...

Analysis of Financial Implications The decision by the State Council has significant financial implications for the municipality of Monteceneri and its taxpayers. The CHF 4.71 million credit represents a substantial investment, but the cantonal government has assured that the financing has been planned responsibly. The sources of funding include cantonal contributions, energy incentives, and own funds, which reduces the financial risk for the municipality. However, the committee that promoted the referendum has raised doubts about the solidity of these financings. In particular, it highlighted that the cantonal subsidies are not yet guaranteed and that the Municipality has not started the formal procedure to request the subsidies. This could lead to delays in obtaining the necessary funds and increase the financial risk for the municipality. ### Impact on the Community and Cross-Border Workers The renovation of the Madonna delle Nevi mountain house will have a significant impact on the community of Monteceneri and the cross-border workers who commute to Ticino.
