Hupac ends 2025 with lower profit but positive balance

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 29 April 2026

The Hupac group closed 2025 with a net profit of 3.5 million Swiss francs, down from 9.4 million in 2024, but with volumes up 4.3%.

Contesto

In brief - Hupac closes 2025 with a net profit of 3.5 million CHF - Volumes up by 4.3%, revenue at 646 million CHF - Gross margin at 21.6%, EBIT at 1.9% ## Key facts - What: Hupac 2025 annual report - When: Fiscal year 2025 - Where: Switzerland and transalpine traffic - Who: Hupac Group - Amount: Net profit 3.5 million CHF, revenue 646 million CHF The year 2025 ends on a positive note for the Hupac Group, which confirms its solidity despite a complex context. The balance sheet highlights a net profit of 3.5 million Swiss francs, down from 9.4 million the previous year, but still indicative of the company's ability to stay on course in a challenging scenario. The results were mainly supported by traffic volumes, which grew by 4.3% to approximately 975,000 road shipments transported, equivalent to 1.85 million TEU (Twenty-foot Equivalent Unit). Particularly relevant was the performance of the transalpine core business through Switzerland, which recorded a 4.5% increase, also thanks to the development of new flows on the Belgium-Italy axis. For example, the Chiasso terminal saw a 5.2% increase in shipments, while the Lugano terminal recorded a 3.8% increase. ### Impact of railway construction sites Revenue reached 646 million francs (+3.1%), but growth was curbed by the negative impact of exchange rates and the increase in operating costs. The latter grew faster than revenue, leading to a contraction of the gross margin to 21.6% and an EBIT limited to 1.9%. The European infrastructure situation particularly affected the balance sheet. Numerous railway construction sites, with interruptions and capacity reductions, penalized the efficiency of combined traffic, forcing Hupac to adopt stabilization measures to ensure continuity and reliability to customers.
