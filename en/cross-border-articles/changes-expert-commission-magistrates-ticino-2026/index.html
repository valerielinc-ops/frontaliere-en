The news was revealed this evening by LaRegione. This opens the procedure for her replacement, which will be the responsibility of Parliament. The nomination should take place in the next session of the Grand Council, scheduled to start on Monday, 18 May. A name has already been proposed: the Presidential Office has indeed indicated to the plenary Luca Marazzi, former judge of the Federal Court, as the new member of the Commission. The relevant report was published this afternoon. The Commission of Experts, renewed by four fifths by the Grand Council in May of last year, is currently composed, in addition to the outgoing president, of Franco Lardelli, Stefano Manetti, Luca Marcellini and Francesca Piffaretti-Lanz. ### Nomination procedure The procedure for nominating the new president of the Commission of Experts for the election of magistrates is a delicate process involving several stages. First, candidates must submit their documentation and pass an initial interview. Subsequently, the Commission evaluates the candidates' skills and experience through specific tests and practical assessments. Finally, the Commission issues a preliminary notice to the Grand Council, which makes the final decision. ### Impact on Judicial Procedures Changes in the leadership of the Commission can influence judicial procedures in the Canton of Ticino.
