Equans Rivera cuts 19 jobs, rejects social plan

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 27 April 2026

The multinational Equans in Rivera is laying off 19 employees without a social agreement. Lorenzo Jelmini, Ocst: 'It's a shame there was no consideration for the territory'

Contesto

In brief - Equans Rivera lays off 19 employees - Social plan rejected - Jelmini: 'Disappointed by the lack of consideration' ## Key facts - What: Collective dismissal - When: Procedure started in March 2026 - Where: Rivera, Canton Ticino - Who: Equans, multinational - Amount: 19 jobs The Rivera branch of the multinational Equans has announced the cutting of 19 jobs, without agreeing on a social plan. Lorenzo Jelmini, cantonal secretary of the Ocst union, expressed disappointment with the decision. The consultation procedure began last late March, under Article 335 of the Swiss Code of Obligations. The workers involved operate in the sectors of fiber optics, administration, and heating system installation. The reduction in staff is due to a contraction in orders and turnover in the telecommunications sector. ### Reasons for the layoffs Jelmini explained that the reduction in the fiber optics sector is understandable, as it depends on Swisscom and the municipalities, but for the rest, it is a decline not due to the market. 'It is disappointing to note that the management of this branch of a multinational, which has recorded profits, has not had regard for the territory, leaving all the consequences of the difficulties to the employees,' Jelmini declared. The union had proposed alternative measures to layoffs, such as a reduction in the percentage of work in the administrative and sanitary systems sectors, but the management refused. ### Implications for cross-border workers The layoffs also affect cross-border workers who work in Ticino. The reduction in staff could have repercussions on the local job market, especially at a time when Canton Ticino is seeking to attract and retain talent.
