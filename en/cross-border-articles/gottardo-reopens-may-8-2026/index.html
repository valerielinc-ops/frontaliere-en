Gottardo reopens May 8: Easier traffic for cross-border workers (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 5 May 2026

The Gotthard Pass reopens on May 8, 2026, two weeks earlier than planned. Special lanes for Airolo and Gotthard and Novena passes.

Contesto

In brief - Gottard Pass reopens on May 8, 2026 - Early reopening by one/two weeks - Cupra special lane available from Ascension Bridge ## Key facts - What: Reopening of the Gottard Pass - When: May 8, 2026 at 11:00 - Where: Gottard Pass, Switzerland - Who: Federal Office of Roads - Amount: Not specified The Gottard Pass will reopen to traffic on Friday, May 8, 2026, at 11:00 AM. This early reopening, compared to previous years, is due to favorable weather conditions and less snowfall than last year. The Federal Office of Roads explained that the snow clearing and road restoration work have progressed faster than expected. The reopening thus occurs one or two weeks earlier than usual. The reopening of the Gottard Pass is of particular importance for cross-border commuters using the north-south axis on the A2 highway. The reopening will help alleviate traffic during the Ascension and Pentecost holidays. The extended exit lane in Göschenen, on the A2, will also be put back into service. This lane, approximately 3 kilometers long, starts immediately after the entrance of Wassen and aims to protect the area from parasitic traffic. Starting from the Ascension Bridge, the special Cupra lane will also be available at the south portal of the San Gottard tunnel, for easier exit towards Airolo and the Gottard and Novena passes. However, the opening date of the Novena Pass has not yet been set. ### Impact on cross-border commuters The early reopening of the Gottard Pass represents a significant advantage for cross-border commuters who use this road to travel between Switzerland and Italy.
