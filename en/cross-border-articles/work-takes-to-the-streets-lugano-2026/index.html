May Day in Lugano: workers take to the streets

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 1 May 2026

Hundreds of people marched through Lugano under the slogan 'Neither exploited, nor divided' to demand fair wages and workplace safety

Contesto

In brief - Hundreds of people in Lugano's square for May 1st - Slogan of the demonstration: 'Neither exploited, nor divided' - Main demands: fair wages and no wage dumping ## Key facts - What: Unitary demonstration of the trade unions - When: May 1st, 2026 - Where: Lugano, from Campo Marzio to Piazza Manzoni - Who: Swiss Trade Union Federation (USS), Unia Ticino, Vpod, Syndicom, Sev - Amount: Not specified Under the slogan 'Neither exploited, nor divided,' hundreds of people marched through the streets of Lugano this afternoon on the occasion of Labor Day. The unitary demonstration organized by the Swiss Trade Union Federation (USS) turned into an event that managed to combine the firmness of trade union demands regarding job security, wages, and dignity with the warmth of popular participation. The procession, which started from Campo Marzio and was colored by flags and banners, then reached Piazza Manzoni for the speeches of the trade union leaders. 'Taking to the streets all together on May 1st means demonstrating in a tangible way the unity of the workers' movement, at a time when this unity is more necessary than ever,' said Giangiorgio Gargantini, regional secretary of Unia Ticino. Fair wages and 'no' to wage dumping and precarity are the themes highlighted also by the other representatives of the trade union organizations present in the square today (Vpod, Syndicom, Sev). ### The day continues with music and popular dinner The day of the Labor Day will continue at 19:00 with a popular dinner and a long musical kermesse that will start at 18:00 with the Ctrl+Riot to light up the atmosphere. Main Demands The demonstration in Lugano today highlighted several key demands, including the fight against wage dumping and job insecurity. These issues are of particular interest to cross-border workers, who often work in sectors where the risk of exploitation is higher. The active participation of Ticino trade unions and popular mobilization can have a significant impact on working conditions and the rights of cross-border workers, improving their economic and job security. ### Comparison with Other Demonstrations Compared to other May Day demonstrations in Switzerland and Italy, the one in Lugano stands out for its trade union unity and popular participation. While in other cities protests may be fragmented or limited to specific sectors, in Lugano the demonstration saw the participation of various trade union groups, uniting forces to demand common rights. This unitary approach can be more effective in advancing workers' demands and achieving concrete results. ### Future Scenarios Today's demonstration could be just the beginning of a series of trade union actions and protests that will develop in the coming months. With the increase in job insecurity and pressure on wages, trade unions may intensify their activities to protect workers' rights, including those of cross-border workers.
