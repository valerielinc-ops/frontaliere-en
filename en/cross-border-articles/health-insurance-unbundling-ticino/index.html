Practical Implications for Cross-Border Workers The separation of the popular initiatives on health insurance premiums in Ticino will have a significant impact on cross-border workers. By splitting the two proposals, each initiative can be evaluated on its own merits, preventing a less financially sustainable proposal from blocking the other. The Lega's initiative, which is financially sustainable and simple to implement, could be rolled out more quickly, offering benefits to cross-border workers in terms of fairer insurance premiums. The PS's initiative, which involves greater income redistribution at the expense of the middle class, may take longer to evaluate and implement. ### Concrete Procedures Cross-border workers residing in Italy and working in Ticino should closely monitor the development of the two initiatives. It is important to understand how each proposal could influence insurance premiums and AVS/LPP contributions. Cross-border workers should also consider participating in public consultations and expressing their opinions to the cantonal authorities. Additionally, cross-border workers should be prepared to provide additional documentation, such as their employment contracts and details of their current insurance premiums, to demonstrate how the new rules could affect their financial situation. ### Practical Scenarios 1.
