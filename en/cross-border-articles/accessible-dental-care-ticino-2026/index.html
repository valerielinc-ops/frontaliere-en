BELLINZONA - The promoting committee today presented the 'Yes to dental care' campaign at Casa Marta, Bellinzona, for the cantonal vote on June 14, 2026, on the generic popular legislative initiative 'For the reimbursement of dental care'. A broad coalition of parties, unions, associations, and healthcare professionals defends five fundamental arguments: equity in access, public health, support for families, a proven insurance model, and a necessary constitutional principle. The generic popular legislative initiative 'For the reimbursement of dental care' was submitted in 2015 with 8,283 valid signatures by the Left and Unions. After more than ten years of parliamentary process, the Grand Council rejected it on January 27, 2026, with 49 no and 22 yes votes. Since the promoters did not withdraw the initiative, the Ticino people are called to vote on June 14, 2026. Dettagli operativi

Implications for cross-border workers The initiative for reimbursing basic dental care in Ticino could have a significant impact on cross-border workers employed in the canton. Currently, many cross-border workers forgo essential dental care due to high costs, which can represent a significant burden for families. If the initiative is approved, cross-border workers will be able to benefit from a system of reimbursement for basic dental care, funded through contributions proportional to income. This could mean significant savings for cross-border worker families, who will finally be able to access dental care without fear of costs. ### Comparison with the current situation Currently, over 90% of dental costs are borne by patients, either directly or through private complementary insurance. This means that many families, especially those in the middle class, have to face high expenses for basic dental care. According to data from the IHP Survey 2023 by the Commonwealth Fund, 28.8% of the Swiss population has forgone dental care for financial reasons, a significant increase from 20.7% in 2016. The initiative proposes a public, transparent, and sustainable insurance model, inspired by the AVS system. Contributions would be proportional to income and funded equally by workers and employers, with state support for those who do not engage in paid work. This model could ensure more equitable access to dental care for all residents of the Canton of Ticino, including cross-border workers. ### Future scenarios If the initiative is approved, cross-border workers will be able to benefit from a system of reimbursement for basic dental care. Frequently Asked Questions
What are the five fundamental arguments defended by the Promoting Committee?
The five fundamental arguments defended by the Promoting Committee are: equity in access, public health, support for families, a tried-and-tested insurance model, and a necessary constitutional principle.
How will the basic dental care reimbursement system be financed?
The basic dental care reimbursement system will be financed with contributions proportional to the income of workers, employers, and the state. The implementation details, such as rates and procedures, will be defined subsequently by Parliament.
What percentage of the Swiss population gave up dental care for financial reasons in 2023?
According to the 2023 IHP Survey by the Commonwealth Fund, 28.8% of the Swiss population gave up dental care for financial reasons in 2023.
