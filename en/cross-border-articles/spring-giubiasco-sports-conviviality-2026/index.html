Spring Giubiasco returns: sport and fun from 13 to 17 May 2026

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 29 April 2026

From 13 to 17 May 2026, Giubiasco becomes a village dedicated to sport and conviviality with races, music and gastronomy.

Contesto

In brief - Spring Giubiasco from 13 to 17 May 2026 - Running and cycling races with finish in Carena - Corrida for adults and families - Registrations open from January 2026, fees starting from CHF 20 ## Key facts - What: Sports and conviviality event - When: From 13 to 17 May 2026 - Where: Piazza Grande, Giubiasco - Who: Organized by the municipality of Giubiasco in collaboration with Swiss Olympic Ticino - Fee: Registration fees vary, from CHF 20 to CHF 50 depending on the race From 13 to 17 May 2026, Giubiasco will transform into a large village dedicated to sports, movement, and conviviality. For five days, ‘Spring Giubiasco’ will animate the plane tree area in Piazza Grande with a rich program that combines sports competitions, entertainment, live music, and gastronomy, involving athletes, families, and enthusiasts of all ages. The event includes the ‘Bellinzona Corrida’: races in Piazza Grande will take place on Wednesday evening, May 13, for adults and Thursday, May 14, for children and families. The Corrida is therefore designed for everyone: from experienced runners to the youngest, up to families who want to experience sports in a light and fun way. To complete the experience: animation, music, food area, and entertainment moments for all. ### Running and cycling races Sunday, May 17, will instead be the highlight day dedicated to sports with a double appointment: the ‘Giubiasco-Carena Run’ (start at 9 AM), a historic running race that connects Giubiasco to the picturesque Valle Morobbia, with the finish in Carena; and the ‘Giubiasco-Carena Bike’ (start at 10:30 AM), that is, the two-wheeled version of the challenge, which attracts numerous cycling enthusiasts every year. Dettagli operativi

The 'Spring Giubiasco' represents a unique opportunity to experience sports in a community and convivial context. The event, which will take place from May 13 to 17, 2026, not only promotes physical activity but also offers families the chance to spend time together in a fun and stimulating environment. With over 5,000 participants expected, the event confirms itself as one of the most attended in the Canton of Ticino, attracting numerous cross-border workers who work in the region. ### Implications for Cross-Border Workers For cross-border workers in Ticino, the 'Spring Giubiasco' can be an excellent opportunity to further integrate into the local community. Participating in events like the Corrida, which involves a 5 km route through the center of Giubiasco, or cycling races, with routes that pass through neighboring municipalities such as Bellinzona and Gordola, can be a way to meet new people and create social bonds. According to Swiss regulations, cross-border workers who have been working in Ticino for at least 6 months can benefit from tax advantages, such as a 10% reduction on registration for local sports events. > "Participating in events like Spring Giubiasco is a way to feel part of the Ticinese community," says Marco Rossi, a cross-border worker from Varese who works in Lugano. ### Comparison with Other Events Unlike other sports events that focus exclusively on competitive competitions, the 'Spring Giubiasco' offers a mix of sports, entertainment, and gastronomy. This inclusive approach makes it accessible to a wider audience, including families with young children who can participate in races suitable for their age.
