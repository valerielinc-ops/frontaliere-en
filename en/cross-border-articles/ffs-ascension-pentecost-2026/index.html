SBB: 58 extra trains for Ascension and Pentecost (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 4 May 2026

SBB enhances its offer with 58 special trains and 130,000 additional seats between German-speaking Switzerland and Ticino

Contesto

In brief - 58 extra trains for Ascension and Pentecost - 130,000 additional seats - Booking recommended ## Key facts - What: 58 special trains and 130,000 additional seats - When: Ascension and Pentecost 2026 - Where: Between German-speaking Switzerland and Ticino - Who: SBB - Amount: 130,000 extra seats This year, for Ascension and Pentecost, the Swiss Federal Railways (SBB) have decided to strengthen the offer between German-speaking Switzerland and Ticino. 58 special trains and around 130,000 additional seats will be made available. Despite these measures, certain trains may still experience high passenger numbers. SBB therefore recommends planning your journey in advance and booking your seats. ### Extraordinary measures SBB has announced that, in view of the expected increase in the number of travelers for the Ascension and Pentecost weekends, 58 additional trains will run. These trains will allow smooth travel between German-speaking Switzerland and Ticino. To increase the number of seats, SBB will also add carriages to regular trains, including EuroCity trains traveling to Italy. In addition, on Ascension Thursday and the Saturday before Pentecost, an additional TILO RE80 connection will be offered from Locarno to Milano Porta Garibaldi. These connections will not pass through Chiasso as usual, but through Varese, with intermediate stops at Gallarate and Rho Fiera Milano. ### Booking and bike transport SBB recommends planning your journey in advance by checking the special train offer in the online timetable. You can book your seats and take into account the obligation to book for bikes. Dettagli operativi

Implications for travelers The extraordinary measures adopted by the SBB for Ascension and Pentecost 2026 have important implications for travelers, particularly for cross-border workers commuting between German-speaking Switzerland and Ticino. With the increase in the number of seats and the addition of special trains, travelers will have more opportunities to plan their trips more flexibly. However, despite the increase in supply, some trains may still be very crowded. Therefore, it is essential to book seats in advance to avoid inconvenience. The possibility of booking bike spaces and selecting desired seats via the SBB Mobile app and the SBB website makes the trip planning process simpler and more convenient. ### Comparison with the previous year Compared to 2025, the SBB has increased the number of seats by 25%. This represents a significant improvement for travelers, who will benefit from greater availability of seats and more flexibility in schedules. However, it is important to note that the increase in supply does not completely eliminate the risk of overcrowding, especially on weekends with higher attendance. ### Practical scenarios For cross-border workers who work in Switzerland and live in Italy, the possibility of booking seats in advance is particularly important. For example, a cross-border worker who works in Zurich and lives in Varese could plan his trip back home during the Ascension weekend by booking a seat on one of the special trains. This would allow him to avoid overcrowding and travel more comfortably. Similarly, a traveler who wishes to visit Ticino during the Pentecost weekend could book a seat on one of the special trains to ensure a stress-free journey. Frequently Asked Questions
How can I book a seat on a special train?
You can book a seat through the FFS Mobile App or the FFS website. It is advisable to do so in advance to ensure availability.
What special trains are available for Ascension and Pentecost?
The FFS have made available 58 special trains between German-speaking Switzerland and Ticino. Additionally, there are extra TILO RE80 connections from Locarno to Milano Porta Garibaldi.
How can I transport my bicycle on a train?
You can book a spot for your bicycle through the FFS Mobile App or the FFS website. However, the availability of bicycle spots is subject to limitations, especially for trains operating in the Gotthard base tunnel.
