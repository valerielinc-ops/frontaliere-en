Rega, with its fleet of 14 helicopters equipped for high-altitude rescue, can quickly intervene in case of emergency. In the specific case of Monte Boglia, located between the municipalities of Blenio and Acquarossa, Rega took approximately 30 minutes to reach the scene of the accident and rescue an injured hiker. This intervention highlighted the efficiency of the Swiss air rescue system, which in 2022 carried out over 16,000 interventions, approximately 1,200 of which were in Ticino. ### Comparisons with similar situations In the past, there have been numerous Rega interventions in Ticino and other Swiss regions. For example, in 2021, Rega carried out an intervention on Pizzo di Claro, near Faido, to rescue a fallen climber. Frequently Asked Questions
What are the emergency numbers in Switzerland?
In Switzerland, the general emergency number is 112. For mountain rescue, you can contact Rega at 1414. It's important to memorize these numbers and always have them at hand during a hike.
How can I prepare for a mountain hike?
To prepare adequately for a mountain hike, it's important to check the weather conditions and the route. It's advisable to bring a first aid kit, water, food, and appropriate clothing. Additionally, it's useful to take training courses to increase awareness of risks and rescue procedures.
What should I do in case of an injury in the mountains?
In case of an injury in the mountains, it's crucial to stay calm and assess the situation. If possible, it's advisable to call the rescue services immediately and provide accurate information about your location and the condition of the person in distress. It's important to stay sheltered and wait for the arrival of the rescue services, avoiding moving unless absolutely necessary.
