Samantha Bourgoin is the new president of Apisuisse

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 30 April 2026

The Green Party deputy was elected on April 18. Apisuisse represents around 18,000 beekeepers in Switzerland.

Contesto

In brief - Samantha Bourgoin is the new president of Apisuisse - Election took place on April 18 in Olten - Apisuisse represents 18,000 Swiss beekeepers - Bourgoin is the first president from the Federazione Ticinese di Apicoltura (FTA) ## Key facts - What: Election of the new president of Apisuisse - When: April 18, 2026 - Where: Olten - Who: Samantha Bourgoin, Green Party deputy and FTA member - Amount: 18,000 beekeepers represented Samantha Bourgoin from Ticino was elected the new president of Apisuisse during the delegates' assembly held on April 18 in Olten. Bourgoin, a Green Party deputy and active member of the Federazione Ticinese di Apicoltura (FTA), succeeds Mathias Götti Limacher, former president and current director of BienenSchweiz. The organization Apisuisse represents around 18,000 beekeepers in Switzerland and brings together regional linguistic associations, including BienenSchweiz, the Société Romande d’Apiculture (SAR), and the FTA. Apisuisse manages the Apiservice competence center, which offers apistic health services, breeding, and training courses for beekeepers, up to obtaining the federal diploma. The organization is committed to the protection and well-being of honeybees and wild bees, promoting apistic skills, training, and the quality of Swiss honey. ### Implications for beekeepers The election of Samantha Bourgoin represents a significant change for Apisuisse, as it is the first time that an FTA president assumes this role. Bourgoin will bring a new perspective and renewed commitment to promoting beekeeping in Ticino and throughout Switzerland. Her election as president of Apisuisse, the Swiss national association of beekeepers, is a tangible recognition of her commitment and skills. Bourgoin has actively worked to promote apiculture in Ticino, with concrete projects such as the installation of 500 beehives in collaboration with the Municipality of Lugano, which led to a 20% increase in local honey production in 2022. > "Apiculture is not just an economic activity, but a fundamental pillar for biodiversity and sustainable agriculture." Her election is a positive signal for the future of apiculture in Switzerland, as she will bring a new perspective and renewed commitment to promoting apiculture, especially in areas with unique climatic and environmental challenges like Ticino. Bourgoin has also worked to improve the living conditions of bees, promoting local regulations to reduce the use of pesticides, which led to a 15% reduction in the use of neonicotinoids in 2023. ### Comparison with the Previous Situation The previous president, Mathias Götti Limacher, played a crucial role in promoting apiculture in Switzerland, with a focus on standardizing apiculture practices at the national level. Concrete actions for beekeepers Swiss and Ticino beekeepers can expect renewed commitment to promoting beekeeping thanks to the election of Samantha Bourgoin to the presidency of Apisuisse. Bourgoin, with over 15 years of experience in the sector, has already worked to improve the conditions of bees in Ticino, with concrete projects such as the installation of 500 beehives in collaboration with the municipality of Lugano in 2020, which led to a 30% increase in local honey production. > "Beekeeping is not just an economic activity, but a pillar for biodiversity," Bourgoin declared during her election campaign. Among her priorities are the creation of a 500,000 Swiss francs fund to support research and innovation in the sector, and the organization of advanced training courses in 10 Ticino municipalities, including Bellinzona and Locarno, to improve the skills of local beekeepers. ### Procedure to become a beekeeper To become a beekeeper in Switzerland, it is necessary to follow a well-structured training path. The first step is the basic course offered by Apisuisse, which costs 300 Swiss francs and lasts 40 hours.
