Implications for the Future The defeat against Sweden has highlighted some critical issues within the Swiss national hockey team. The lack of young talents and the difficulty of giving them space in the league are problems that need to be addressed urgently. Sweden, on the other hand, has demonstrated how a system that favors the growth of young players can lead to immediate results and a promising future. > "In Sweden, 60% of the players in the national team are under 25 years old, while in Switzerland this percentage drops to 35%." ### Comparison with the Current Situation In Switzerland, generational change is a hot topic. Many experienced players are aging, and there are not enough young players ready to take their place. This could lead to a decline in competitiveness in the near future. What to do now The Swiss national team has a crucial opportunity this weekend to bounce back after their defeat against Sweden. The matches against Finland (tomorrow) and Czechia (Sunday) will be decisive in demonstrating tangible improvements. In particular, it will be fundamental to strengthen the defensive order, which has shown some vulnerabilities, and increase incisiveness in the offensive zone. The team has shown a large amount of play, but often struggled to capitalize on the opportunities created. In view of the World Championships, scheduled from May 8 to 24, 2025, it will be essential to find that balanced game that in recent years has allowed Switzerland to reach the final twice. The defeat against Sweden, with a score of 5-2, highlighted the need for a tactical and mental change.
