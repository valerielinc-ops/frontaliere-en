SBB Cargo confirms Chiasso operations, staff to be reorganized (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 15 May 2026

SBB will remain in Chiasso despite announced cuts, with staff changes by 2027.

Contesto

In brief - FFS Cargo confirms the presence of the depot in Chiasso - Reorganization announced with changes in personnel - Possible closure postponed to 2027 ## Key facts - What: Confirmation of the FFS Cargo site in Chiasso with personnel changes - When: Decisions expected on May 19, 2026, possible closures from 2027 - Where: Chiasso, Canton Ticino - Who: FFS Cargo, director Roberta Cattaneo - Amount: Not specified In the context of the Swiss Federal Railways (FFS) freight transport reorganization announced last year, the Chiasso freight depot remains operational, but with some uncertainties regarding the employed personnel. The reorganization had already led to the closure of eight terminals in Switzerland, including those in Cadenazzo and Lugano, with a loss of about forty full-time jobs in Canton Ticino. The committee "No to the dismantling of FFS Cargo in Ticino" has reported anticipation of new cuts and a possible closure of the Chiasso facility, resulting in further job losses. However, the director of FFS Southern Region, Roberta Cattaneo, reassured that "Cargo will remain in Chiasso" and that details will be presented at a press conference in Bellinzona on May 19. During the meeting, the new production model planned from December 13, 2026, will be illustrated. This model aims to make single wagon traffic more efficient by increasing train occupancy, reducing costs, and maintaining service at the national level. It remains confirmed that there will be changes in the personnel structure, which "will remain present in the border town but with a different workforce than the current one." Dettagli operativi

The recent news about the confirmation of FFS Cargo's headquarters in Chiasso and the changes planned in the staff reflect directly on the lives of workers, many of whom are cross-border residents in Italy. The reorganization of personnel, even if it does not involve an immediate closure, represents a source of occupational uncertainty and could influence the labor market in the transfrontier region. ### Implications for cross-border workers Many cross-border workers with a G or B permit employed by FFS Cargo in Chiasso may have to face contract changes or reassignments. The reduction or change in staff could affect job stability, with potential effects on AVS, LPP, and LAMal contributions. A personnel reorganization could also influence source tax procedures, considering contract and hourly changes. From a tax perspective, maintaining the headquarters in Chiasso is a positive signal for the economic stability of the Canton Ticino, which is also important for cross-border workers who benefit from the new 2026 double taxation agreement between Switzerland and Italy. However, changes in staff could require an update in the management of Ticino benefits and INPS contributions in Italy. ### Comparison with the previous situation Compared to the closure of terminals in Cadenazzo and Lugano, which has already resulted in a loss of about forty jobs, the confirmation of the permanence of the Chiasso depot is a sign of continuity. However, the fact that the staff will undergo changes makes this continuity less reassuring. The new production model, scheduled for December 2026, aims to increase the efficiency of freight transport, but could translate into a different occupational structure.
