The ErreDiPi committee disputes the 2025 ISMAT survey data on cantonal employees' satisfaction

Contesto

In brief - 59% of employees feel overburdened - 300 people report episodes of discrimination - Only 44% see professional growth opportunities ## Key facts - What: ISMAT 2025 survey on job satisfaction - When: Data collected in 2025, published in 2026 - Where: Canton Ticino - Who: Employees of the cantonal administration and educational institutions - Amount: 59% feel overburdened, 300 episodes of discrimination The State Council of Canton Ticino has published the statistical report by Ismat Group on the job satisfaction of the cantonal administration personnel. However, the ErreDiPi Committee (Network for the Defense of Pensions) has expressed doubts about the presented results. According to the Government, the data confirm high satisfaction among employees, with the majority proud to work for the State. However, the ErreDiPi Committee has highlighted some signs of discontent. ### Critical data The 59% of the staff feels overburdened in terms of workload. Moreover, 300 people have reported episodes of discrimination, isolation, or personal attacks, often with at least monthly frequency. Only 44% believe there are real opportunities for professional growth, while 43% are not satisfied with their remuneration. Furthermore, 55% of employees believe that their efforts are not adequately recognized. The ErreDiPi Committee also pointed out that half of the employees, probably the most discontent, did not respond to the survey despite repeated invitations. This suggests that reality might be even more concerning than what emerged from the data. ### Implications for cross-border workers The data from the ISMAT 2025 survey could also have implications for cross-border workers employed in Canton Ticino.
