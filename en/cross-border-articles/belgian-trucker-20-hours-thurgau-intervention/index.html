Frequently Asked Questions
What are the regulations for professional drivers in Switzerland?
In Switzerland, professional drivers cannot drive for more than 9 consecutive hours without a mandatory break of at least 45 minutes. Additionally, within a 24-hour period, the maximum driving time is 10 hours, extendable to 11 hours no more than twice a week.
What happens if a driver violates the driving regulations?
A driver who violates the driving regulations may be subject to penalties, ranging from heavy fines to the suspension of the license. In serious cases, immediate intervention may be required to prevent further risks.
How can cross-border workers avoid violating driving regulations?
Cross-border workers can avoid violating driving regulations by planning their route, respecting driving times, taking mandatory breaks, keeping an accurate record of driving hours, and consulting a specialized lawyer in case of doubt.
