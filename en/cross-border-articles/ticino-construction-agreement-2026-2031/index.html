Record agreement for Ticino construction: contract until 2031

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 27 April 2026

The new collective cantonal contract for Ticino construction has been signed, valid until 2031. Bagnovini: 'A base for planning'.

Contesto

In brief - New Ticino construction collective agreement until 2031 - Six-year agreement never reached before - Bagnovini (Ssic): 'Base for planning' - Locatelli (Ocst): 'Training at the center' - Average annual wage increase of 2% - Mandatory 40 hours of annual training for workers ## Key facts - What: Cantonal construction collective agreement - When: Signed on April 27, 2026 - Where: Canton of Ticino - Who: Ssic and Ocst - Amount: Duration of 6 years (record) - Details: First agreement of this duration - Coverage: 12,000 construction workers - Sectors involved: Builders, installers, masons, carpenters The 'thorny' negotiation for the renewal of the cantonal collective agreement of Ticino's construction sector has concluded with a record agreement. The new agreement, signed on April 27, 2026, will last until 2031, a six-year agreement never reached before in the sector. The signing of the agreement represents a turning point for Ticino's construction sector, which can now plan with greater certainty for the future. ### Statements from the parties The president of Ssic, Paolo Bagnovini, positively commented on the agreement: 'This contract represents a solid base for planning the future of Ticino's construction sector. The exceptional duration of six years allows us to work with greater stability and security.' The president of Ocst, Locatelli, also expressed satisfaction, emphasizing the importance of training: 'Training is at the center of our agreement, a crucial element to ensure adequate skills in the sector.' ### Agreement details The new agreement provides for an average annual wage increase of 2%, with a 2.5% increase for workers under 30 to encourage young people to enter the sector. Practical Implications for Workers The signed agreement will have significant implications for workers in the Ticino construction sector. First, the exceptional duration of six years guarantees contractual stability, a fundamental factor for the long-term planning of companies and workers. For example, a construction worker from Lugano can now plan personal investments such as buying a house with greater security, knowing that their collective agreement will not undergo sudden changes. ### Comparison with the Previous Situation Before this agreement, collective contracts in the Ticino construction sector had an average duration of three years. The new agreement, however, allows avoiding frequent renewals and establishing more stable and predictable working conditions. This is particularly important in an economic context where job security and family planning are priorities for many workers. > "With this agreement, we can finally look to the future with greater serenity," declared a trade union representative from Bellinzona. ### Future Scenarios With a contract valid until 2031, Ticino construction companies can plan long-term investments and projects with greater certainty. For workers, this means greater job stability and the opportunity to access continuous training and professional updates guaranteed by the agreement.
