Analysis of Implications The appeal by the faithful to the Diocese of Lugano raises important questions about the role of the Catholic Church in the context of discrimination based on sexual orientation and gender identity. The vigil planned for May 22, 2026, represents a crucial moment for the Ticino Catholic community, which finds itself having to balance welcoming and inclusivity with fidelity to the doctrine and Magisterium of the Church. ### Comparison with the Previous Situation Last year's petition has already had a significant impact, preventing the vigil from taking place at the Basilica del Sacro Cuore in Lugano. However, the promoting group believes that further intervention is necessary to ensure clarity and coherence with the Church's teachings. The 2026 vigil could represent a turning point in the debate within the Ticino Catholic Church, with potential implications for Christian formation and pastoral accompaniment. ### Possible Scenarios If the Apostolic Administrator were to intervene with the Azione Cattolica Ticinese, a change in the communication and organization of the vigil might be requested. This could include greater attention to coherence with the Church's teachings and a clear distinction between pastoral accompaniment and adherence to ideological categories. On the other hand, if no action were taken, the vigil could proceed as planned, raising further questions among the faithful. ### Implications for the Faithful For those who identify with the petition, the call for clarity represents an opportunity to reiterate the importance of fidelity to the doctrine and Magisterium of the Church. Frequently Asked Questions
What is the goal of the appeal by the faithful to the Diocese of Lugano?
The goal of the appeal is to obtain clarity regarding the vigil planned for May 22, 2026, which aims to overcome discrimination based on sexual orientation and gender identity. The faithful ask that the Ticino Catholic Action be called back to its ecclesiastical mission and that pastoral accompaniment be distinguished from adherence to ideological categories.
What are the concerns raised by the promoting group?
The concerns raised by the promoting group involve the ideological language and theological approach adopted in the vigil. Additionally, the group is concerned about the involvement of the Ticino Catholic Action and the presence of ministers of the Catholic Church, which could promote changes in the doctrine and the perennial Magisterium.
What concrete actions can the faithful take?

Additionally, they can contact the Apostolic Administrator directly to express their concerns and request a clarifying intervention. They can write a formal letter, send it to the Diocesan Curia of Lugano, and request a formal meeting.
