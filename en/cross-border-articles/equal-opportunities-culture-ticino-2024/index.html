In brief - Conference 'What kind of culture?' in Lugano - Imbalances in the artistic and cultural sector - Focus on funding, visibility, and decision-making roles ## Key facts - What: Conference on equal opportunities in the artistic and cultural sector - When: Tuesday - Where: Asilo Ciani, Lugano - Who: Councilor of State Marina Carobbio Guscetti, municipal Raoul Ghisletta - Cost: Not yet specified The first conference 'What kind of culture?' took place on Tuesday at Asilo Ciani in Lugano. The event saw a numerous and attentive participation, confirming the strong interest and the need to address the issue of equal opportunities in the artistic and cultural sector in a structured way. The afternoon opened with the speeches of the Councilor of State and director of DECS Marina Carobbio Guscetti and the municipal of the City of Lugano Raoul Ghisletta, who reiterated the need for an effort by the institutions to promote a more equitable, inclusive, and representative cultural system. The introductory speech by Rachele Santoro, Delegate for equal opportunities, and Roland Hochstrasser, Head of the Office of Analysis and Digital Cultural Heritage, outlined the situation. It emerged that significant imbalances persist in the distribution of opportunities, particularly regarding access to funding, visibility of female artists, and their presence in decision-making roles. The need to strengthen the systematic collection of data was also emphasized. ### Implications for cross-border workers The conference highlighted how disparities in the cultural sector can also affect cross-border workers in the artistic field. Dettagli operativi

Practical Analysis: Implications for Cross-Border Workers The conference 'What Kind of Culture?' highlighted several areas of imbalance in the artistic and cultural sector of the Canton of Ticino. For cross-border workers in this sector, the implications are significant and require in-depth analysis. ### Access to Funding One of the main imbalances highlighted concerns access to funding. Artists, particularly cross-border workers, may find it difficult to obtain funding for their projects. This can limit their ability to develop and promote their work. To address this challenge, it is essential that cross-border artist women inform themselves about the available funding programs and actively participate in professional networks that offer support and resources. ### Visibility and Representation The visibility of artists and their presence in decision-making roles are other critical areas. Cross-border artist women can benefit from visibility platforms and mentoring initiatives offered by organizations such as SWAN and Helvetiarockt. Participating in these networks can increase their visibility and offer opportunities for collaboration and professional growth. ### Comparison with the Previous Situation Before the conference, cross-border artist women could face greater difficulties in finding funding and visibility. The initiatives that emerged during the conference represent a significant step forward towards a more equitable and inclusive environment. Frequently Asked Questions
What are the main challenges faced by cross-border worker artists in the cultural sector?
The main challenges include access to funding, visibility of their works, and representation in decision-making roles. These challenges can limit career opportunities and make it harder to compete in an already competitive market.
How can cross-border worker artists benefit from the initiatives that emerged from the conference?
Cross-border worker artists can benefit by participating in professional networks such as SWAN, Helvetiarockt, and the Swiss Society of Visual Arts (SSAA). These networks offer support, visibility, and opportunities for collaboration and professional growth.
What are the concrete steps that cross-border worker artists can take to improve their situation?
Cross-border worker artists can join professional networks, monitor the cultural policies of the Canton of Ticino, participate in conferences and seminars, and use visibility platforms and mentoring initiatives offered by these networks.
