In brief - 50th edition of the Prix Walo in Zurich - Hecht best Pop/Rock group - Stephan Eicher wins in the Singing category ## Key facts - What: 50th edition of the Prix Walo - When: May 2, 2026 - Where: Zurich - Who: Hecht, Stephan Eicher, Zoë Më - Amount: Not yet specified The 50th edition of the Prix Walo took place last night in Zurich, an event that saw Hecht triumph as the best group in the Pop/Rock category. The Lucerne band confirmed their current golden moment, having recently won several awards at the Swiss Music Awards. Hecht outshone competitors Krokus and Megawatt, once again demonstrating their talent and popularity. In the 'Pop/Rock Singing' category, the Prix Walo went to Stephan Eicher, who swept the competition. In his acceptance speech, Eicher simply stated: 'I just like to sing.' This victory represents a significant recognition for the singer, who continues to be a prominent figure in the Swiss music scene. In addition to the awards in the main categories, Zoë Më was also honored in the 'Newcomer' category. Zoë Më represented Switzerland at the Eurovision Song Contest (ESC) in Basel last year, and this victory represents further recognition of her emerging talent. Zoë Më surpassed the dialect indie-pop musician Edb and the folk music group Heimatliebi. The Prix Walo Lifetime Achievement Award went to the eighty-year-old music manager and organizer Freddy Burger. According to the organizers, the Prix Walo is the oldest award in Europe and the most important distinction in the Swiss show business.
