Contesto

In brief - The dispute between RSI and Paola Nurnberg is closed - RSI has paid all the claims made - Unia confirms the validity of the demands - What: The dispute between RSI and Paola Nurnberg is closed - When: May 29, 2026 - Where: Lugano - Who: RSI, Paola Nurnberg, Unia - Amount: 4 months' salary The dispute between RSI and journalist Paola Nurnberg, who was fired from her position in 2023 following a Twitter post, has been resolved. This was confirmed by the union UNIA, which supported Nurnberg and stated that 'RSI has paid all the claims made.' The final decision was reached 'at a rapid pace until the desired goal was achieved: the dismissal.' 'The arguments put forward by the Unia union, which clearly demonstrated the total disproportion between the violation committed and the punishment, were to no avail,' the union continues, 'and the RSI management was unable (or unwilling?) to even offer Paola Nurnberg alternative employment within a company that employs 1,200 staff.' In the UNIA statement, the issue of harassment against her is also mentioned, for which an internal procedure had been initiated: 'the thesis we have put forward is that a female employee who had previously found the courage to report a superior was targeted.' Regarding the satisfied claims: 'the request for payment of 4 months' salary as compensation for wrongful dismissal does not even begin to cover the damage suffered by the worker with the loss of her job,' but it does lead to the closure of the legal dispute: 'This unilateral payment renders the case moot: in other words, the procedure must be dismissed and Paola cannot proceed further,' Unia confirms, emphasizing that this is 'an important result.'
