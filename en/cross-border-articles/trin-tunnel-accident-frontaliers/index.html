Frequently Asked Questions
What is the emergency procedure for a front panel in case of an accident in the Trin tunnel?
In case of an accident, call 112 or 113 to alert emergency services. The police will shut down the road for investigations, as seen in the 30-year-old accident.
What are the safety regulations for the Trin Tunnel?
The Trin Tunnel is subject to the Federal Act of 20 June 1960, which provides for the provision of safety devices such as lighting and signalling systems, as well as periodic safety checks.
How does reporting a vehicle with technical problems in the Trin Tunnel work to avoid accidents?
In the event of a breakdown, the driver must immediately activate the four hazard arrows, place the warning triangle 100 metres away and, if possible, move the vehicle to one of the designated parking areas. It is mandatory to call 117 (Swiss police) or 112 (European emergency) to report the situation.
What are the penalties for those who do not respect the speed limits in the Trin tunnel?
The limits are 80 km/h for light vehicles and 60 km/h for heavy vehicles. Exceeding this will result in fines of CHF 250 to CHF 1,000 and, in the event of a repeat offence, the suspension of the licence. The detection cameras are active 24/7 and the data is transmitted directly to the cantonal authorities.
Is it possible to walk through the Trin Tunnel in case of an emergency?
No, it is forbidden to walk inside the tunnel for safety reasons. In case of emergency, it is necessary to stay in the car with the seat belts fastened, turn off the engine and wait for help by calling 117. Emergency exits are signposted every 250 meters.
