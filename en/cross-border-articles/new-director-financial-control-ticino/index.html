Ticino seeks new finance director

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 1 May 2026

Giovanni Cavallero will retire in 2027. The Canton has opened the competition for his replacement.

Contesto

In brief - Giovanni Cavallero will retire in 2027 - The Canton is looking for a new director for the Cantonal Financial Control - The tender was published today ## Key facts - What: Competition for the new director of the Cantonal Financial Control - When: Published on April 30, 2026 - Where: Canton of Ticino - Who: State Council of the Canton of Ticino - Amount: Not specified BELLINZONA - Today, the competition for the replacement of the Director of the Cantonal Financial Control, Giovanni Cavallero, who will retire starting April 1, 2027, was published. This was announced today by the State Council. The Cantonal Financial Control (CCF) is the highest body of the Canton in terms of financial control, endowed with autonomy and independence. Recognized as a federal auditor, it annually verifies the accounts and the budget of the State and carries out audit activities in the Cantonal Administration and external entities designated by the State Council. ### Role and responsibilities of the director The Director of the Cantonal Financial Control is responsible for the operational and technical management of the Service, ensuring its administrative, financial, and human resources management. He/she coordinates internal and external audit activities in compliance with regulations and professional standards, ensuring the achievement of strategic objectives. He/she collaborates with the Grand Council in the context of high supervision and provides qualified advice to the State Council, also participating in working groups internal and external to the Cantonal Administration. ### Requirements for candidacy To occupy this strategic position, several years of experience in accounting auditing, or alternatively in financial management, are required. Implications for cross-border workers and the Ticino economy The replacement of the Director of the Cantonal Financial Control (CCF) represents a crucial moment for the cantonal administration and the Ticino economy. The CCF plays a fundamental role in verifying the accounts and budget of the State, ensuring transparency and correctness in financial management. For cross-border workers employed in Ticino, the stability and efficiency of the cantonal financial system are essential elements for the security and planning of their economic activities. For example, in 2022, cross-border workers represented approximately 20% of the Ticino workforce, with a significant impact on the regional GDP. Fiscal security and clarity in administrative procedures are crucial for attracting and retaining this workforce. ### Comparison with the previous situation With the change in leadership at the CCF, it is important that the new director maintains the high standards of professionalism and independence that characterized Giovanni Cavallero's term. Continuity in financial management is crucial to avoid discontinuities and ensure the trust of stakeholders, including cross-border workers operating in the Canton of Ticino. During Cavallero's term, the CCF implemented stricter controls on municipalities, reducing irregularities by 15% in the biennium 2020-2022. For example, in the municipality of Lugano, the controls led to a saving of approximately 5 million francs in 2021. ### Future scenarios The new director will have to face several challenges, including optimizing review processes and adapting to new financial regulations.
