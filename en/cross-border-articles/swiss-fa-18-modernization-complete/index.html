F/A-18 upgrade: Swiss fleet ready until 2030 (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 1 June 2026

The fleet of 30 F/A-18C/D aircraft has been modernized to operate until the early 2030s, with an investment of 450 million francs.

Contesto

In brief - The fleet of 30 F/A-18 has been modernized - Investment of 450 million francs - Operational until the early 2030s - Work began in 2017 ## Key facts - What: Modernization of the F/A-18C/D fleet - When: Work began in 2017, completed in 2026 - Where: Switzerland - Who: Federal Office of Armaments (armasuisse) - Amount: 450 million francs The modernization work on the fleet of 30 F/A-18C/D fighter jets has been completed, allowing the fleet to operate until the early 2030s. This intervention, costing 450 million francs, was necessary to ensure the operational capability of the aircraft until the new F-35As enter service. ### Modernization details The work, which began in 2017, included the installation of new communication, navigation, and identification systems. The aircraft software, mission planning systems, and flight simulator were also updated. At the end of April 2026, armasuisse completed the partial project for the verification and restoration of the aircraft structures. ### Current situation Currently, all 30 aircraft are operational and can be used for up to 6,000 flight hours per aircraft. However, some logistical supplies, such as spare parts and support services, are still pending. The remaining supplies and the conclusion of the project are expected by the end of 2027.

Dettagli operativi

The modernization of the F/A-18 has significant implications for Switzerland's air safety and, consequently, for cross‑border workers living and working in Ticino. The operational capability of the aircraft is crucial for protecting Swiss airspace, which also includes the sky over Ticino, a region with a high number of cross‑border commuters. ## Impact on regional safety The presence of modernized, operational combat aircraft ensures greater safety for all residents and workers in Ticino. This is especially important for cross‑border commuters who cross the Italy‑Switzerland border every day, whether for work or personal reasons. ## Comparison with the previous situation Before the upgrade, the F/A-18 fleet had limited operational capability and could not guarantee air safety until the new F‑35A arrived. Now, with the operational capability extended into the early 2030s, Switzerland can rely on a more reliable and secure fleet. ## Future scenarios With a fully operational F/A-18 fleet, Switzerland can better meet future air‑security challenges.
