Varese approves 2026 budget with record surplus: 17 million

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 1 May 2026

The Province of Varese approves a thriving budget with a free administrative surplus of over 17 million euros.

Contesto

In brief - 2026 budget approved with a surplus of 17 million - Premazzi regains delegations after political crisis - Funds for the redevelopment of Varese theatre - Positive impact for Ticino cross-border workers ## Key facts - What: Approval of 2026-2028 budget - When: 30 April 2026 - Where: Province of Varese - Who: Councillor Mattia Premazzi - Amount: 17,009,730.80 euros (free surplus) The second meeting of the Varese provincial council, convened on 30 April 2026, saw the approval of the 2025 financial statement and the budget update for the 2026-2028 triennium. The figures presented by Councillor with delegation Mattia Premazzi showed a free administrative surplus of over 17 million euros and an investment surplus of 1,594,244.40 euros. Among the expenditure items, 500,000 euros have been allocated for the redevelopment of Varese theatre. Premazzi, who had resigned his delegations in February 2026, obtained guarantees for the continuation of his commitment until the natural expiry of his term. 'I had a discussion with President Magrini and obtained guarantees for the continuation of this commitment until its natural expiry,' Premazzi declared. The political crisis seems to have been overcome, with the majority finding serenity again. Councillor Marco Colombo of Fratelli d'Italia highlighted the political value of the presentation: 'We note that the majority is once again solid and that the verification has ended positively.' However, Lega councillor Sergio Ghiringhelli expressed doubts about the dialogue between the centre-right parties, stating that 'no one has tried to contact me.' Analysis of Spending Priorities The approved budget provides a free administrative surplus of over 17 million euros and an investment surplus of 1,594,244.40 euros. These funds will be used for various priorities, including schools, security, and urgent road interventions. President Magrini emphasized that 'there will be priorities for schools, security, and some urgent road interventions.' 📊 Specific Priorities: - Schools: Allocation of 5 million euros for the renovation of 3 elementary schools in the municipality of Varese, with a focus on energy efficiency and safety. - Security: 3 million euros for the purchase of new equipment for firefighters and the updating of surveillance systems in 5 neighboring municipalities, including Cantello and Saltrio. - Traffic: 2.5 million euros for the extraordinary maintenance of provincial roads, with a pilot project for the implementation of intelligent roundabouts in Gavirate and Gazzada Schianno. ### Comparison with the Previous Budget Compared to the previous budget, this year's surplus is significantly higher, indicating more efficient resource management. This could translate into greater investments for the community and infrastructure improvements. For cross-border workers, this could mean better public services and a better quality of life. 📊 Numerical Comparison: - Free Surplus 2025: 12 million euros - Free Surplus 2026: 17 million euros - Increase: 5 million euros (+41.67%) 💡 Practical Examples: - Municipality of Laveno-Mombello: With an investment of 1 million euros, the creation of a new public parking lot and the expansion of the senior center are planned. - Municipality of Casciago: Allocation of 800,000 euros for the construction of a new school gymnasium and the updating of sports equipment. ### Future Scen...
