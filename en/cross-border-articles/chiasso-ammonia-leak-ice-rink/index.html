Ammonia leak at Chiasso ice stadium: three poisoned

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 28 April 2026

Three people poisoned due to an ammonia leak at the ice stadium in Chiasso. Firefighters and medical teams intervened.

Contesto

In brief - Three people affected by ammonia leak in Chiasso - Firefighters and medical services intervene - No hospitalizations required ## Key facts - What: Ammonia leak - When: April 28, 2026, 8:05 AM - Where: Chiasso Ice Stadium, via Primo agosto - Who: Mendrisiotto Firefighters, Mendrisiotto Ambulance Service, Croce verde Lugano - Affected: Three people This morning, April 28, 2026, three adults underwent prolonged medical checks at the Chiasso Ice Stadium, via Primo agosto, due to mild intoxication caused by an ammonia leak. The incident occurred around 8:05 AM when a maintenance professional reported the leak. The Mendrisio firefighters promptly intervened, identifying a broken sleeve as the cause of the ammonia dispersion in a technical room of the facility. Specialists from the Chemical Defense Unit proceeded with the reabsorption of the liquids and the initial repair of the leak. The intoxicated individuals were treated on-site by the medical staff of the Mendrisiotto Ambulance Service and the Croce verde di Lugano, with no need for hospital admission. ### Implications for cross-border workers Cross-border workers who work at or frequent the Chiasso Ice Stadium might be concerned about the safety of the facilities.
