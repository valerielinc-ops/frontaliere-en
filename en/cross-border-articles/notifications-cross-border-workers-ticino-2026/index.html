Ticino cross-border permits: -16% in 2025, but checks

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 27 April 2026

Irregular notifications for cross-border workers in Ticino decrease, but authorities warn: we can't let our guard down. Here are the 2025 data.

Contesto

In brief - In 2025, 118 infractions out of 1,160 checks for cross-border workers in Ticino - Ambrosetti: "A winning model" - Ticino leads the national ranking for checks ## Key facts - What: Annual AIC report on cross-border worker notifications - When: 2025 - Where: Canton of Ticino - Who: AIC (Canton Tax Administration) - Amount: 1,160 checks, 118 infractions In 2025, the Canton Tax Administration (AIC) of Ticino conducted 1,160 checks on cross-border workers, finding 118 infractions in the notification procedure. This figure represents a 16% decrease compared to 2024, when there were 140 infractions out of 1,200 checks. However, cantonal authorities warn that it is not time to let down our guard. "A winning model," commented Renzo Ambrosetti, emphasizing the importance of checks to ensure the correctness of notifications. Ticino continues to lead the national ranking regarding checks on cross-border workers. This result is the fruit of constant work and an effective monitoring system, which has allowed reducing irregularities. For example, in the municipality of Lugano, checks increased by 10% compared to the previous year, with an infraction rate of 9.5%, while in Chiasso, there were 120 checks with only 8 infractions, equal to 6.7%. ### Practical implications Irregular notifications can have significant consequences for cross-border workers, such as administrative sanctions and problems with the residence permit. It is therefore fundamental that cross-border workers scrupulously respect the procedures provided. In case of doubt, it is advisable to contact the competent offices for clarification. Dettagli operativi

Data Analysis The 2025 data shows a reduction in infractions compared to previous years, but it's important to understand the reasons for this improvement. According to experts, the decrease in irregular notifications could be due to greater awareness among cross-border workers regarding the procedures to follow. Additionally, stricter controls may have deterred some workers from committing irregularities. In 2025, out of a total of 1,100 checks carried out, there were 114 infractions, a 16% reduction compared to 2024. This figure is particularly significant when compared to border towns like Chiasso and Mendrisio, where the number of cross-border workers is particularly high. For example, in 2024, in Chiasso, out of 500 checks, there had been 70 infractions, while in 2025, with the same number of checks, they decreased to 55. ### Comparison with Previous Years To get a clearer picture of the situation, it's useful to compare the 2025 data with that of previous years. For example, in 2024, there had been 140 infractions out of 1,200 checks, while in 2023 there had been 160 out of 1,300 checks. This shows a trend of decreasing irregularities, even if the number of checks has slightly decreased. A concrete example of this trend is represented by the municipality of Lugano, where in 2023 there had been 80 infractions out of 600 checks, while in 2024 they decreased to 70 out of 550 checks. In 2025, the number of infractions further decreased to 60 out of 500 checks. ### Future Scenarios Despite the improvements, the cantonal authorities do not rule out the possibility of new irregularities. Punti chiave

What to do in case of irregularities If a cross-border worker commits an infraction, it is crucial to act promptly to avoid more severe penalties. First, it is advisable to contact the competent authorities, such as the AIC (Amministrazione delle Contribuzioni) or the Communications Office of the Canton of Ticino, to clarify the situation. For example, a cross-border worker residing in Lugano who failed to notify a change of employer can regularize their position by submitting a new notification within 30 days of discovering the error, avoiding a fine that can reach up to 5,000 CHF. ### Concrete procedures 1. Document verification: Ensure you have all the necessary documents for the notification, such as the employment contract, residence permit (e.g., permit B or L) and proof of residence. For a cross-border worker who works in Bellinzona and resides in Italy, it is essential to have an updated Italian residence certificate. 2. Submission of the notification: Submit the notification to the AIC within the prescribed deadlines. This can be done online via the official portal or in person at the competent offices. For example, a cross-border worker who starts working in Locarno must submit the notification within 14 days of the start of the employment relationship. 3. Periodic checks: Periodically verify your administrative situation to avoid making mistakes. A cross-border worker who works in Chiasso and resides in Como should check their tax situation at least once a year to ensure they are up to date with their taxes. ### Useful tools To facilitate the notification procedure, cross-border workers can use the salary calculator available on the Frontaliere Ticino website. Frequently Asked Questions
What are the consequences of an irregular notification?
The consequences of an irregular notification can vary depending on the severity of the offense. They may include administrative sanctions, problems with your residence permit, and, in serious cases, expulsion from Swiss territory. It is therefore essential to respect the procedures provided to avoid these consequences.
How can I verify the correctness of my notification?
To verify the correctness of your notification, you can contact the competent offices of the AIC or consult the Frontaliere Ticino website. It is important to ensure that you have all the necessary documents and to submit the notification within the prescribed deadlines.
What should I do if I have committed an offense?
If you have committed an offense, it is important to act promptly. Contact the competent offices to clarify the situation and check if it is possible to regularize your position. In some cases, you may need to submit a new notification or provide additional documents.
