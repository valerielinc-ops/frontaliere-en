Dettagli operativi

Practical Implications for Citizens The switch to the electronic identity card (CIE) represents a significant change for the citizens of Varese, especially for those who work or live in Ticino. The CIE offers numerous advantages, including greater security and the possibility of using it for travel abroad. However, it is important to be aware of the deadlines and procedures necessary to obtain the new document, especially for those who frequently travel between Italy and Switzerland. ### What Changes with the CIE The electronic identity card contains a microchip that stores the biographical data and image of the holder. This makes the document more secure and difficult to forge. Additionally, the CIE can be used for travel to all countries of the European Union and many other foreign countries, without the need to request a passport. For example, for a citizen of Varese who frequently travels to Lugano for work, the CIE greatly simplifies the identification and border control process. ### Deadlines and Procedures It is essential to respect the deadline of August 3, 2026 to avoid problems with the validity of the document. To request the CIE, you must appear at the appointment at the Anagrafe Office in via Sacco with the required documents, which include a recent photograph and the old identity document. The process of issuing the CIE takes approximately 6-8 working days, so it is advisable to book in advance. For residents of Varese who work in Ticino, it is possible to request the CIE also at the border municipal offices such as Mendrisio or Chiasso, where dedicated services are available. ### Operational Checklist - Required Documents: - Recent photograph (ID format, white background) - Old identity document - Tax code - Possible residence permit for re...

What to do to obtain the CIE To obtain the electronic identity card (CIE), it is necessary to follow some simple but important steps. Here is a step-by-step guide to request the CIE, with concrete examples and specific references for residents in Ticino. 1. Online booking: Access the website of the Municipality of residence and book an appointment through the dedicated agenda. For example, in the Municipality of Lugano, it is possible to book online through the official portal, with an average availability of 15 working days. In Bellinzona, on the other hand, the booking can be made through the municipal computer system, with waiting times that vary between 7 and 10 days. 2. Required documents: Prepare a recent photograph (format 35x40 mm, white background) and the old paper identity document. Some municipalities, such as Locarno, also require a copy of the residence permit for foreign citizens. It is important to verify the specific requirements of your municipality of residence. 3. Appointment at the Registry Office: Attend the appointment at the Registry Office with the required documents. In Mendrisio, for example, the Registry Office is located in Via G. Cattaneo 2, and the opening hours to the public are from Monday to Friday, from 8:30 am to 12:00 pm and from 2:00 pm to 5:00 pm. In Chiasso, the Registry Office is located in Via San Francesco 3, with similar opening hours. 4. Collection of the document: After a few days, collect the CIE at the Registry Office or receive it by post, depending on the methods provided by the Municipality.
