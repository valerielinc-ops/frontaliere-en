Bike sharing in Como: 80 bikes from April 30th. Where to find them and how it works (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 14 April 2026

The bike sharing service in Como will be operational again on April 30th, 2026, with 80 customised bikes and 17 pickup points. Discounted rates are coming for the summer season.

The presentation event, scheduled at the Town Hall’s docking stations, will showcase how the service will integrate into the urban fabric. The declared goal is to promote modern and sustainable mobility, with operational management designed to ensure efficiency and widespread coverage. As the company highlights, work on infrastructure upgrades is now in its final stages and is expected to be completed by the launch date. Dettagli operativi

For cross-border workers living in Como or frequently commuting between Lombardy and Ticino, the return of bike sharing opens up new possibilities for sustainable mobility. How will this service integrate with the existing public transport offerings? The goal is to create an efficient intermodal system where bike sharing serves as the missing link between the various transport options. ### A sustainable choice for daily cross-border commuters Cross-border workers who work in Ticino but live in Lombardy may find bike sharing an appealing option for short-distance travel within the city of Como. For example, someone arriving by train at Como Lago station could rent a bike to reach their workplace or an appointment in the city centre, avoiding the use of a private car and helping to reduce traffic and pollution. Another advantage is flexibility: unlike public transport, which follows fixed schedules, bike sharing allows users to travel according to their own needs. This can be particularly useful for those commuting outside peak hours or who need to make multiple stops during the day. ### Comparing with other Ticino cities: Como takes a step forward While Ticino already boasts well-established bike sharing services in cities like Lugano and Bellinzona, Como is positioning itself as a new player in the sustainable mobility landscape between Lombardy and Ticino. Como’s service could also attract tourists and commuters travelling between the two regions, offering an economical and eco-friendly alternative to using a car. However, it remains to be seen how the new system will integrate with other transport options. Frequently Asked Questions
Which pickup points are confirmed for the Como bike-sharing service?
Currently, 17 pickup points are confirmed, including the one behind the Town Hall (via Vittorio Emanuele II and via Tommaso Perti), Como Lago railway station, the lakeside area, and the Spina Verde park. A detailed map will be published before the official launch on 30 April 2026.
How do I register as a new user for the Como bike-sharing service?
Registration is done via the dedicated app, which can be downloaded for free from Google Play and the App Store. You will need to provide a valid ID, a credit/debit card, and a phone number. After completing the registration, you can choose your preferred pricing plan.
What are the pricing options for the Como bike-sharing service?
The final pricing details have not yet been announced, but Spazio Visibile is developing a system that includes time-based fares, weekly/monthly subscriptions, and tourist packages. The options will be presented at the launch event on 30 April 2026.
What happens if I return the bike late?
If you fail to return the bike within the maximum allowed time, you may incur a penalty. The terms of use will be specified during registration and in the app. It is essential to respect the time limits to avoid additional charges.
Is the Como bike-sharing service compatible with Ticino’s public transport services?
No specific agreements with Ticino’s public transport companies have been announced yet. However, the service is designed to integrate with other mobility options, including public transport. Check official updates for any potential benefits.
