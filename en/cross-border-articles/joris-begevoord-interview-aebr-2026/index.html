The new tax agreement in 2026 could mitigate this problem, offering greater clarity and reducing tax burdens. ### Impact on Withholding Taxes Withholding taxes are a crucial aspect for cross-border workers in Switzerland. With the new tax agreement, it is possible that the tax rates will be revised to better reflect current economic conditions. This could lead to significant savings for cross-border workers, improving their purchasing power and quality of life. ### Health Insurance and Pensions Another area of concern is the management of health insurance and pensions. The LAMal, the Swiss healthcare system, and the AVS, the pension system, must be coordinated with the corresponding Italian systems. This is particularly important for cross-border workers who must navigate between two different systems, often with complex rules and procedures. ### Cross-Border Mobility Cross-border mobility is another key issue. Border crossings such as Brogeda and Gaggiolo are critical points for cross-border workers who move daily between Italy and Switzerland. Begevoord suggested that regional cooperation projects could help solve these issues. ### Regional Cooperation Projects Regional cooperation projects are fundamental to promoting collaboration between border regions.
