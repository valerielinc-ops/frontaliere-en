Chièscia Bòsc Carnival 2026: a celebration in Blenio Valley (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 18 February 2026

From February 19-21, 2026, in Ludiano, three days of Ticino tradition with food, music, and a masked ball. An opportunity for cross-border workers.

Contesto

TL;DR - Chièscia Bòsc Carnival in Ludiano, Blenio Valley, Feb 19-21, 2026. - Gastronomy, music, and fun for all ages in a village festival atmosphere. - Community events, masked ball, and local specialties to enjoy. ## Key facts - Evento: Chièscia Bòsc Carnival - Quando: February 19-21, 2026 - Dove: Ludiano, Blenio Valley, Canton Ticino - Aperitivo Reale: Friday, February 20 at 6:30 PM - Ball Mascherato: Saturday, February 21 at 9:00 PM, free entry - Cena Venerdì: Pollo al Cestello, prenotazione obbligatoria - Premiazione Maschere: Saturday, February 21 at 11:30 PM - Prenotazioni Cena: Contact 076 376 61 65 The Canton of Ticino is not just about work and finance. For those who cross the border every day, getting to know local traditions is a way to integrate and fully experience Swiss life. An unmissable opportunity is on the horizon: the Chièscia Bòsc Carnival in Ludiano, in the heart of the Blenio Valley. From February 19 to 21, 2026, the recreation area in the municipality of Serravalle will transform into a "wonderland," offering a rich program that combines gastronomy, music, and fun for all ages. This event represents an escape from the daily routine and an authentic immersion into the culture of Ticino's valleys, which is often very different from that of urban centers like Lugano or Chiasso. For cross-border workers and their families, it's the perfect opportunity to discover the most festive and communal side of the Canton, tasting local specialties and participating in a collective ritual rooted in the region's history. The atmosphere is genuine, typical of village festivals where the community gathers and warmly welcomes visitors. It's an invitation to leave behind worries about the franc-euro exchange rate for a weekend and enjoy a piece of real Ticino.

Dettagli operativi

The Program: Three Days of Celebration The organizers have put together a packed schedule of events, designed to involve everyone, from the youngest to the oldest. Here are the highlights not to be missed: - Thursday, February 19: The event kicks off at 11:30 AM with the opening and music by the duo Tirabüscion. At 12:00 PM, a community gesture: gnocchi with ragù offered to pensioners residing in Serravalle (registration required). The afternoon continues with the handing over of the keys to King and Queen Ronc Zora at 2:30 PM, followed by a tombola with local cured meat products. The evening comes alive at 6:00 PM with an aperitif accompanied by the Bau Band from Giornico and concludes with a "Karaoke Nostran". - Friday, February 20: The afternoon is entirely dedicated to children. From 1:30 PM, there will be face painting, and at 2:00 PM, a tombola for the little ones. This will be followed by "Alice in Wonderland" themed games and a complimentary snack for everyone. For the adults, the royal aperitif at 6:30 PM precedes dinner: you can choose between "Vasel Sbudei con Pollo al Cestello" (by reservation only, also available for takeout) or a classic maccheronata. The night lights up with music from Vasel Sbudei and a 360° DJ set by Markolino DJ. - Saturday, February 21: The grand finale. After the bar opens at 5:00 PM, dinner features traditional flavors: Büseca or Gnocchi with luganiga sausage and salad. At 8:30 PM, another round of tombola, but the main event arrives at 9:00 PM with the free-entry Masked Ball, animated by the band "I Nuovi Eroi". At 11:30 PM, the best masks will be awarded prizes; costumes must be announced by 10:30 PM. The party will continue late into the night with Markolino DJ.

Punti chiave

Experiencing the Carnival as a Cross-Border Worker: A Practical Tip Participating in the Chièscia Bòsc Carnival is not just a way to spend a different kind of weekend, but a real investment in cultural integration. For those living the life of a cross-border worker, often confined to the home-work commute, events like this offer a precious window into the social life of the most authentic Ticino. 💡 Tips for your visit: - Reservations: For Friday's dinner ("Pollo al Cestello"), booking is essential. Contact the number 076 376 61 65. - Location: The event takes place at the Area di Svago in Ludiano (municipality of Serravalle). For those coming from Lombardy, the Blenio Valley is easily accessible from the Biasca motorway exit. - Masked Ball: Entry is free, which is great news. Remember to register your costume by 10:30 PM on Saturday if you want to compete for the rich prizes. Experiencing local traditions is one of the many advantages of working in Ticino, but to enjoy them to the fullest, it's essential to have a clear view of your financial situation. Knowing exactly how much of your salary in francs is left after withholding taxes, social contributions, and the conversion to euros is crucial for planning the family budget, including leisure expenses. Frequently Asked Questions
What is the program of the event Carnival Chièscia Bòsc of Ludiano?
The event will take place from 19 to 21 February 2026 and includes a series of events, including music, gastronomy and fun for all ages.
What are the booking options for the special dinner at the Carnival Chièscia Bòsc?
