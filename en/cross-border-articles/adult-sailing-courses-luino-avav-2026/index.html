Luino: AVAV's adult sailing courses return (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 3 May 2026

The Alto Verbano Sailing Association is relaunching cabin courses for 2026, open to beginners on Lake Maggiore

Contesto

In brief - Adult sailing courses in Luino in 2026 - Open to beginners - Progressive courses on cabin cruisers - Certifications and memberships required - Personalized lessons and small groups ## Key facts - What: Adult sailing courses - When: 2026 - Where: Luino, Lake Maggiore - Who: Associazione Velica Alto Verbano (AVAV) - Cost: €250-€500 per person (depending on the course) - Certifications: AVAV permit, FIV membership - Requirements: Non-competitive medical certificate The adult sailing courses organized by the Associazione Velica Alto Verbano (AVAV) in Luino will return in 2026. This historic institution on Lake Maggiore expands its educational offerings to include those who want to start from scratch. The activity takes place on cabin cruisers and is structured in progressive courses, designed for both beginners and those who want to improve their skills. ### Training paths The journey begins with a basic module dedicated to the fundamentals of navigation, leading up to more advanced levels that delve into boat handling even in more challenging conditions. In addition to the traditional path, there is also a specific course on regatta techniques, aimed at those who want to improve precision and tactical skills. The lessons are held in small groups supervised by qualified instructors from the Italian Sailing Federation, ensuring a gradual and safe learning process. One of the goals of the course is to obtain the AVAV permit, which allows members to use the club's boats independently, within certain wind limits, representing an important milestone for those who want to continue practicing sailing. ### Personalized training The association also offers customized formulas, with individual or small group lessons, to adapt to the different needs of the participants...

Dettagli operativi

Implications for Cross-Border Workers The adult sailing courses organized by AVAV represent a unique opportunity for cross-border workers living and working in the Luino area. The chance to participate in sailing courses not only offers a recreational activity but can also improve nautical skills, useful for those who spend a lot of time on Lake Maggiore. According to data from the Canton of Ticino's Labor Office, approximately 7,000 cross-border workers work in the Luino region, and many of them reside in Ticinese municipalities such as Mendrisio, Chiasso, and Lugano. These courses offer a way to better integrate into the local community and make the most of their free time. ### Comparison with Other Training Offers Unlike other sailing courses available in the region, AVAV offers a structured path from beginners to advanced levels, with a specific focus on racing techniques. This makes the offer particularly interesting for those who want to not only learn to navigate but also compete safely and effectively. For example, the basic course lasts 10 lessons of 2 hours each, costing 300 CHF, while the advanced course, which includes racing techniques, costs 500 CHF for 15 lessons. In comparison, courses offered by other sailing schools in the Luino area can reach up to 700 CHF for a similar path, without including access to the club's boats. ### Practical Scenarios Imagine being a cross-border worker working in Luino and wanting to spend your free time actively and stimulatingly. Registration Procedure To participate in the AVAV adult sailing courses, follow these steps: 1. Medical Certificate: Obtain a non-competitive medical certificate valid for the current year. The average cost for this certificate is around 50-70 CHF in Ticino, such as in Lugano or Mendrisio. 2. Membership: Register with the club and the Italian Sailing Federation. The cost of membership with the FIV is 30€ for the base fee, while registration with the AVAV club is 50€. 3. Contact the Secretariat: Get information on course dates and costs. Courses start in May and end in September, with a cost of 300€ for the complete package of 10 lessons. 4. Registration: Complete the registration procedure through the official AVAV website. Frequently Asked Questions
Who can participate in the AVAV adult sailing courses?
The courses are open to all adults, both beginners and advanced, who wish to learn or improve their cabin cruiser navigation techniques.
What are the requirements to participate?
To participate, you need a non-competitive medical certificate and membership to the club and the Italian Sailing Federation.
How can I enroll in the courses?
You can enroll by contacting the AVAV secretariat or consulting the official website for more information.
