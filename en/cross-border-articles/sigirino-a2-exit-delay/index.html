The new permit office has not resolved the doubts, possibility of slippage beyond 2030

Contesto

TL;DR - A2 motorway in Sigirino delayed until after 2030. - Local communities fear disruptions for tens of thousands. - Delay could increase traffic by 15% and costs by 500,000 CHF/year. ## Key facts - Project: A2 motorway in Sigirino - Delay Until: After 2030 - Environmental Assessment: Commissioned by Ufam, unresolved doubts - Traffic Increase: 15% in the next biennium - Additional Costs: 500,000 Swiss francs per year - New EIA Cost: 200,000 Swiss francs - Economic Loss: 10 million Swiss francs per year for local businesses - Cross-Border Workers: Increased travel time by 30 minutes during peak hours On April 22, 2026, the Region.ch reported that the A2 motorway in Sigirino could be delayed until after 2030. The new environmental assessment commissioned by the Federal Office for the Environment (Ufam) has not resolved the doubts of a nearby resident, who has therefore decided to maintain their opposition. This delay is causing concern among the local communities, such as Mendrisio and Chiasso, which fear disruptions for tens of thousands of people. The site for the future project has already been identified, but the works seem to be stalled. The interested Ticino communes have already started to assess the possible consequences of this delay. For example, the municipality of Mendrisio estimates that traffic could increase by 15% in the next biennium, with negative impacts on air quality and road safety. Additionally, the municipality of Chiasso calculates that the delay could result in an increase in traffic management costs of around 500,000 Swiss francs per year. Implications for Cross-Border Workers Cross-border workers who regularly use the A2 highway may be particularly affected by this delay. The increase in travel time and the possibility of congestion and bottlenecks could negatively impact their daily lives. For example, a worker commuting from Lugano to Chiasso might see their travel time increase by approximately 30 minutes during peak hours, from 45 minutes to 1 hour and 15 minutes. Additionally, the costs of the works have increased by 11% from 2022 to the end of 2025, which could have an impact on the workers' budgets. The total cost of the works has increased from 120 million francs to 133.2 million francs. The Ticino municipalities of Mendrisio, Balerna, and Chiasso may be particularly interested in this delay, as many cross-border workers residing in these municipalities use the A2 highway to commute to work in Switzerland or Italy. The cross-border work law of January 1, 2020, requires that cross-border workers be promptly informed of any changes in travel times and working conditions.
