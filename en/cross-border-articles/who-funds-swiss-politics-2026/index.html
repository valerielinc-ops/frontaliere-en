Who funds Swiss politics? Over 130 million in campaigns

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 29 April 2026

Since transparency rules came into effect, over 130 million francs have been invested in Swiss political campaigns.

Contesto

In brief - Over 130 million in political campaigns since 2023 - Economiesuisse tops the list with nearly 11 million - APF spends 5.7 million to abolish the rental value ## Key facts - What: Funding for Swiss political campaigns - When: Since 2023 - Where: Switzerland - Who: Economiesuisse, APF, and other interest groups - Amount: Over 130 million francs Since the transparency rules came into effect, over 130 million francs have been invested in Swiss political campaigns. Of these, nearly 61 million come from contributions identified in the Federal Finance Administration's registry. Analyzing these data, nearly 400 different donors have been identified, but a significant portion of the funds comes from a small circle of interest groups. ### Economiesuisse in the lead One organization stands out above all others: economiesuisse, the umbrella association of the Swiss economy. Very active, it has invested in campaigns related to 16 of the 21 ballot issues analyzed. In total, its declared donations reach nearly 11 million francs, not counting contributions made to political parties outside of campaigns. 'Throughout the year, you have to make choices about the issues, because our resources are not unlimited,' says Cristina Gaggini, director for French-speaking Switzerland at economiesuisse. 'Often, we have to commit decisively to defend the liberal model to which our country owes its success.' The amounts vary significantly from one issue to another: nearly 3 million francs for the campaign against the 13th AVS pension, compared to just 18,500 francs for the revision of tenancy law. Dettagli operativi

Implications for cross-border workers Political donations can have a significant impact on cross-border workers in Switzerland, especially in Ticino. Campaigns funded by economiesuisse and APF can influence fiscal and social policies that directly affect transnational workers. For example, the campaign against the 13th AVS pension could influence the AVS and LPP contributions of cross-border workers, while changes to rental law could affect housing costs in Ticino. ### Comparison with the previous situation Before the new transparency rules came into effect, political donations were often anonymous. This change has made it possible to identify the main donors and better understand who funds political campaigns. However, as highlighted by Pierre Eichenberger, many donations still go through umbrella organizations, making it difficult to trace the origin of the funds. ### Future scenarios With the increase in transparency, it is likely that interest groups will continue to play a significant role in Swiss political campaigns. Cross-border workers should carefully monitor campaigns funded by economiesuisse and APF, as these can have a direct impact on their working and living conditions in Switzerland. ### Analysis of donations The donations from economiesuisse and APF show a clear interest in economic and fiscal policies. Frequently Asked Questions
Who are the main donors of Swiss political campaigns?
The main donors of Swiss political campaigns are economiesuisse and the Association of Property Owners (APF). Economiesuisse has donated nearly 11 million francs, while the APF has spent 5.7 million francs on the abolition of imputed rental value.
How can I monitor the impact of political campaigns on my working conditions?
You can monitor the impact of political campaigns on your working conditions by staying informed about campaigns funded by economiesuisse and APF, participating in public consultations, and using tools like the [salary calculator](nav:calculator).
What are the implications of political donations for cross-border workers?
Political donations can influence fiscal and social policies that directly affect cross-border workers. For example, campaigns against the 13th AVS pension can impact AVS and LPP contributions, while changes to tenancy law can affect housing costs in Ticino.
