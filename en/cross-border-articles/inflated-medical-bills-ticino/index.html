Inflated Medical Bills: An Increasingly Common Problem in Ticino (cross-border guide)

In Ticino, 60% of patients have received incorrect medical bills, impacting care costs and insurance premiums.

For some time now, patients in the Canton of Ticino have been facing an increasingly concerning issue: inflated medical bills. An investigation conducted by 'Patti chiari' revealed that healthcare providers collect around 4 billion francs annually in inappropriate billing. The issue has become so significant that since 2022, it is mandatory for every patient to receive a detailed copy of the invoices for visits and healthcare services. However, many do not check these documents, and thus find themselves paying amounts that do not correspond to the services received. Dettagli operativi

Examining the issue of inflated medical bills more closely, it is essential to consider the regulatory requirements in place. Since 2022, as mentioned, it is mandatory for doctors to provide patients with a detailed copy of the services rendered. This measure was introduced to ensure greater transparency in the healthcare sector and to combat irregularities. However, despite this regulation, many patients do not feel comfortable disputing bills, fearing it might compromise their trust relationship with the doctor. The situation is unsustainable: with 60% of people experiencing errors in their bills, it is clear that there is an urgent need for greater vigilance. The most common errors include incorrect recording of visit times, where, for example, a group physiotherapy session was billed as individual therapy, resulting in significantly higher costs. The primary responsibility falls on patients, who must take a proactive approach to verify bills and contest any discrepancies. At the same time, greater training and awareness among healthcare providers are necessary to prevent similar situations from occurring. Healthcare facilities and doctors must recognize their role in ensuring the accuracy of billing and should be willing to correct any errors without creating friction. ACSI has launched a campaign to educate patients on how to check their bills and know how to act in case of disputes. By prohibiting unfair practices, the entire healthcare system can be improved, making it fairer and more sustainable for everyone. In Ticino, where the healthcare system is already under economic pressure, it is essential that citizens trust the services they receive and can rely on a system that works for them. It is not just a matter of saving money, but of rights and justice in the healthcare system. Institutions like ACSI and the Ethical Commission of the Medical Association are available to support patients in this process. Frequently Asked Questions
What happens if you receive an unjustified medical bill?
If you receive an unjustified medical bill, it is important to carefully check the invoice and compare the costs with the really received performance. It is also essential to collect all the necessary evidence and submit the complaint to the doctor or health care provider. If the situation is not resolved, you can contact the Order of Doctors to get help.
How can I contest an incorrect medical bill in the canton of Ticino without compromising the relationship with my doctor?
Contact your doctor or health care professional in a respectful way, providing evidence of inconsistencies. If it is not resolved, addressed to the Deontological Commission of the Order of Doctors of the Canton Ticino.
What are the concrete steps that a patient can follow if a health facility does not respond to the challenge of an incorrect invoice?
If a health facility does not respond to the challenge of an incorrect invoice, the patient should first collect all the necessary evidence, such as documents and visits logs. Afterwards, you can contact the Deontological Commission of the Order of Doctors of the Canton Ticino, which has the task of investigating ethical and professional violations. In addition, it is possible to request consumer protection for legal assistance.
How do inflated medical bills affect patient cash premiums in Ticino?
Inflated medical bills contribute to the increase in sick cash premiums in the Canton of Ticino, as the high costs of undue billing are poured into the insurance system. This phenomenon has led to the highest percentage increase in insurance premiums in Switzerland in 2026, putting further pressure on citizens' finances, already proven by the increase in the cost of living.
What are the responsibilities of healthcare facilities in ensuring the correctness of billing?
Healthcare facilities have the responsibility to ensure the correctness of billings by providing patients with detailed copies of the performance provided, as required by law since 2022. They must correct any errors without creating friction and adopt transparent practices to avoid irregularities. It is essential that health professionals be trained adequately to prevent errors in billing and comply with ethical and professional standards.
