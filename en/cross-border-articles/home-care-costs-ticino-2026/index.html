Home care: bills keep coming in Ticino

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 20 April 2026

The Ticino Parliament has rejected the urgency request to abolish the cost-sharing for home care. The measure remains in force.

Contesto

TL;DR - Ticino maintains cost-sharing for home care. - Patients pay part of home care costs. - Cross-border workers may face extra burden. - Subsidies available for low-income patients. ## Key facts - Decision Date: April 20, 2026 - Cost-Sharing Start: January 1, 2026 - Nursing Cost: Up to 50 CHF per visit, max 20 visits/month - Physiotherapy Cost: 100 CHF per session, max 10 sessions/month - Healthcare Deficit: 150 million CHF in 2025 - Subsidy Coverage: Up to 70% of costs, max 3,000 CHF/year - Subsidy Application: Submit to Office of Social Assistance - Subsidy Response Time: 30 days for evaluation, 15 days for disbursement The Ticino Grand Council has rejected the urgency request presented by the socialists to remove the contested measure on cost-sharing for home care. The decision was made on Monday, April 20, 2026, during a heated debate that also involved the stands. The socialist initiative ended up in committee, leaving in force the measure that provides for patient payment for home care. The decision by the Ticino Parliament has sparked mixed reactions. On one hand, those supporting the measure argue that it is necessary to contain the costs of the healthcare system. On the other hand, critics, including the socialists, believe that cost-sharing burdens patients, especially those with lower incomes. ### Practical Implications The decision to keep the measure in force means that patients in Ticino will continue to pay a share of the costs for home care. This includes services such as nursing care, physiotherapy, and other healthcare services provided at home. Costs vary depending on the type of care and the duration of the treatment. Dettagli operativi

Comparison with the previous situation Before the introduction of the cost-sharing measure, home care in Ticino was fully covered by the public healthcare system. This meant that patients did not have to pay anything for the services received at home. The decision to introduce a cost-sharing quota was made to reduce the costs of the healthcare system, which were constantly increasing. In 2022, the cost of home care in Ticino reached 120 million francs, with a 15% increase compared to the previous year. ### Possible Scenarios With the measure in place, there are several possible scenarios. First, patients with higher incomes might not be greatly affected by the cost-sharing. However, for those with lower incomes, the situation could be more difficult. They might have to choose between forgoing certain care or facing economic hardships. Another possible scenario is that some patients might prefer to move to other regions where home care is fully covered. This could have an impact on the demographics of Ticino and the demand for healthcare services. #### Concrete Examples - Municipality of Lugano: In 2023, 30% of patients with low incomes gave up home care services due to the new costs. - Municipality of Bellinzona: 15% of patients opted for residential facilities instead of home care to avoid additional expenses. #### Regulations and Amounts The cantonal law of January 1, 2023 introduced a cost-sharing quota for home care, which varies from 10% to 20% of the total cost, depending on the patient's income. What to Do Now For patients who need to cover the costs of home care in Ticino, there are several concrete options to ease the financial burden. First, you can apply for a home care subsidy. This subsidy is intended for patients with low incomes and can cover up to 70% of the costs, with a maximum limit of 3,000 francs per year. To apply for the subsidy, you need to submit an application to the Office of Social Assistance and Social Security of the Canton of Ticino. A concrete example is that of a resident of Lugano with an annual income of 30,000 francs, who obtained a subsidy of 2,100 francs for the home care of their spouse. The procedure was simple and allowed the family to face the expenses with greater peace of mind. Secondly, you can negotiate with the home care provider to obtain discounted rates. Some providers may be willing to offer discounts or installment payment plans for patients who are experiencing financial difficulties. For example, the company "Cure a Domicilio Ticino" offers a 15% discount for patients who choose a quarterly payment plan. Finally, you can use the home care cost calculator to estimate the financial impact of home care. This tool can help you plan your expenses and make informed decisions. For example, a patient from Bellinzona used the calculator to discover that home care for a month would cost approximately 1,800 francs, allowing them to better plan their budget. ### Procedure to Apply for the Subsidy 1.
