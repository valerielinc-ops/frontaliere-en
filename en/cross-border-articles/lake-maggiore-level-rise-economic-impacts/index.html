Lake Maggiore rise: economic and environmental impacts

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 30 April 2026

The Po River Basin Authority sets the maximum level at 140 cm, but the area fears irreversible damage to the economy and biodiversity.

Contesto

In brief - Lake Maggiore level set at 140 cm above zero hydrometric. - 20-30 million cubic meters more for irrigation. - Local administrators fear economic and environmental damage. ## Key facts - What: Rise in Lake Maggiore level. - When: April 29, 2026. - Where: Lake Maggiore, Varesotto and Piedmont shores. - Who: Po River Basin Authority. - Amount: 140 cm above zero hydrometric of Sesto Calende. - Additional volume: 20-30 million cubic meters for irrigation. The Po River Basin Authority has set the maximum level of Lake Maggiore at 140 centimeters above the zero hydrometric of Sesto Calende. This decision, made on April 29, 2026, has raised concerns among local administrators of the Varesotto and Piedmont shores of Verbano. Despite the agreement between Italy and Switzerland guaranteeing an additional volume of 20-30 million cubic meters for the irrigation of the plain, the mayors of Verbania, Baveno, and Cannobio have defined the choice as 'erroneous and not shared with the territory'. ### Environmental concerns The environment assessor of Angera, Milo Manica, emphasized the fragility of the wet areas and the need to review the current agricultural model. 'Water storage for crops is often considered, but no one ever thinks about converting these crops,' Manica stated. The rise in the lake level could cause the disappearance of reeds and the loss of historical habitats such as those of Isolino Partegora, already documented in the 19th century and now almost extinct. ### Impacts on tourism The mayors of Verbania, Baveno, and Cannobio have also expressed concerns for the tourism sector. Dettagli operativi

Impact Analysis The rise in the water level of Lake Maggiore will have significant consequences for both the environment and the local economy. Historical natural habitats, such as reed beds and Isolino Partegora, could disappear, with serious repercussions for biodiversity. The disappearance of reed beds, for example, would put species like the Lopinga butterfly and Lataste's frog at risk. ### Economic Impacts The tourism sector is one of the most concerned. The reduction of beaches, caused by the rise in the lake's water level, could reduce the tourist attractiveness of Lake Maggiore. Beaches are a determining factor for the local economy, and their reduction could have a negative impact on the revenues of the lakeside municipalities. ### Management of the Shores Managing the shores during periods of high water will be more difficult. The impossibility of retreating vegetation due to the presence of roads and campsites could cause damage to local infrastructure and ecosystems. This could lead to additional costs for the maintenance and protection of the shores. ### Reactions of Local Administrations Local administrations have expressed their opposition to the decision through all available legal and political means. The mayors of Verbania, Baveno, and Cannobio have declared that they will continue to oppose the decision, criticizing the Piedmont Region for not firmly representing the interests of the lakeside municipalities. ### Proposed Solutions Milo Manica has proposed looking at the experiences of the Ticino Park, where the resource is accumulated underground through marcite and aquifer recharge.
