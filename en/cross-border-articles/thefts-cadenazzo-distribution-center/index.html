Two employees fired for thefts at Cadenazzo parcel center

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 28 April 2026

Two Swiss Post employees were fired for thefts at the Cadenazzo parcel center. Part of the stolen goods has been recovered.

Contesto

In brief - Two employees fired for thefts at the Cadenazzo Parcels Centre - Part of the stolen goods recovered - Complaint filed with the public prosecutor's office ## Key facts - What: Theft of goods in transit - When: Not specified - Where: Cadenazzo Parcels Centre, Ticino - Who: Two employees of the Swiss Post - Amount: Not specified Two employees of the Swiss Post have been fired and reported for thefts at the Cadenazzo Parcels Centre. According to sources consulted by colleagues at TicinoNews.ch, part of the stolen goods has been recovered. The two employees, a 61-year-old and a 64-year-old, were suspended from duty and then fired concurrently with the complaint to the public prosecutor's office by the employer. The thefts involve the unauthorized appropriation of goods in transit at the distribution base. It is not yet clear whether this is a single incident or repeated thefts over time. The situation is particularly serious considering that the employees had privileged access and worked in a highly controlled environment, with cameras and scanners both outside and inside the facility. ### Implications for cross-border workers Cross-border workers employed in similar facilities must be aware of the rigorous controls and legal consequences in case of theft.
