Switzerland-France border checks: what changes for cross-border workers (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 25 May 2026

From June 10 to 19, 2024, Switzerland will reintroduce border checks with France for the G7 in Evian-les-Bains. Here's what changes for cross-border workers.

Contesto

In brief - Switzerland-France border controls from June 10 to 19, 2024 - Extraordinary measure for the G7 in Evian-les-Bains - Security risks and NoG7 protests - What: Temporary reintroduction of border controls - When: From June 10 to 19, 2024 - Where: Switzerland-France border, Lake Geneva region - Who: Swiss Federal Council and Canton of Geneva - Amount: Not specified - Reason: G7 summit in Evian-les-Bains (France) - Risks: NoG7 protests and political tensions - Authorities: Cantonal police and Federal Customs and Border Security Office (UDSC) Switzerland will temporarily reintroduce border controls along the French border from June 10 to 19, 2024. This extraordinary measure, decided by the Federal Council, was adopted in view of the G7 summit to be held in Evian-les-Bains (on the French shore of Lake Geneva) from June 15 to 17. The measure responds to a specific request from the Canton of Geneva, responsible for coordinating operations on Swiss territory, to ensure collective security and prevent disorder. The Lake Geneva region, and in particular the cities of Geneva and Lausanne, are considered high-risk areas during the summit days. International summits of this magnitude historically attract strong political and social tensions. The Swiss government justified the decision by recalling historical precedents: - The 2004 precedents: During the 2004 G8 in Evian, the alter-globalist demonstration (which recorded between 50,000 and 100,000 participants) degenerated into violent clashes, looting, and acts of vandalism caused by hundreds of 'black bloc' members. - Current threats: The NoG7 coalition has already announced a large demonstration in Geneva on June 14, specifying that the event will take place regardless of whether or not official authorization is obtain...
