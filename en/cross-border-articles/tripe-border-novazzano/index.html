Frequently Asked Questions
How many kg of fresh meat can I import to Switzerland as private?
According to current regulations, the limit for the importation of fresh meat by private individuals is 1 kg.
What is the limit for the import of processed food from private individuals in Switzerland?
The limit for the import of processed foodstuffs by private individuals in Switzerland is 2 kg.
What happens if I buy meat in Italy but do not have the invoice or the receipt at the border with Switzerland?
In the absence of any documentation that you claim legal purchase, Swiss authorities may seize the product and apply a fine of up to 5,000 francs. It is mandatory to declare all foodstuffs of animal origin on the border.
Can I bring Italian cheeses to Switzerland without limits of quantity?
No, for processed dairy products the limit is 2 kg per person. Overcoming it involves the seizure of the product and an administrative sanction of up to 500 francs. Always check labelling for origin and composition.
What are the limits for fresh fruit and vegetables when entering Switzerland from Italy?
Swiss legislation allows the import of up to 5 kg of fresh fruit and vegetables per person, provided they are not subject to plant protection restrictions. Some species (e.g. citrus) have specific limits: always check the latest provisions of the UFSP.
