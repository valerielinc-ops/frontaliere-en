Varese among Europe's greenest cities

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 30 April 2026

Varese is one of only two European cities where over 50% of the population lives by the 3-30-300 rule.

Contesto

In brief - Varese is among the greenest cities in Europe - Over 50% of the population respects the 3-30-300 rule - Only 13.5% of European citizens live in similar conditions ## Key facts - What: Study on 862 European cities - When: Published in Nature Communications - Where: Varese, Italy - Who: Nature and JRC - Amount: 50% of Varese's population respects the 3-30-300 rule Varese has been recognized as one of the greenest cities in Europe according to a study published in Nature Communications. The research, which analyzed 862 cities on the continent, used the 3-30-300 rule developed by educator Cecil Konijnendijk. This rule states that every resident should have three trees visible from their window, live in a neighborhood with at least 30% tree cover, and be no more than 300 meters from an accessible park or green area. Varese, along with Espoo in Finland, is one of only two European cities where more than 50% of the population lives in full compliance with these criteria. This achievement places the Lombard city at the top of the continent, ahead of capitals like Stockholm, Helsinki, and Berlin. ### Comparison with other Italian cities In Italy, Varese tops the national ranking alongside Reggio Emilia, Pavia, and Perugia. In these cities, over 24% of residents live in neighborhoods that meet the 3-30-300 rule parameters. Conversely, in southern regions, most municipalities have less than 2% of residents with uniform access to green spaces. The study highlights that overall compliance with the rule does not depend solely on the score achieved in individual criteria but also on the spatial distribution of green areas. Varese offers a rare insight: being cited in a continental-scale scientific analysis not as a problem to be solved, but as a model to be studied. #...

Practical Analysis The 3-30-300 rule not only improves the quality of life but also has a significant impact on the environment and public health. Varese, with its high percentage of tree cover and access to parks and green areas, offers a model of urban sustainability that can be adopted in other cities as well. ### Benefits for Cross-Border Workers For cross-border workers living and working in urban areas, the presence of accessible green spaces can significantly improve the quality of life. The ability to easily access parks and green areas can reduce stress and improve mental health, as well as promote a more active and healthy lifestyle. ### Comparison with Other European Cities Varese and Espoo are the only two cities in Europe where more than 50% of the population adheres to the 3-30-300 rule. This comparison highlights the importance of urban planning that takes into account the equitable distribution of public green spaces. Cities that fail to ensure equitable access to nature risk creating concrete inequalities, not only environmental but also social and economic. ### Future Challenges The challenge for the coming decades is to integrate green spaces into urban planning in a systematic and widespread manner, reaching even the most marginalized neighborhoods. This requires concrete commitment from local authorities and close collaboration with urban planning and environmental experts. ### Events and Initiatives Events such as the one with architect Elena Granata represent an opportunity to discuss and promote sustainable practices in urban contexts.
