Fire in Chiasso: Injured Workers Evacuated (cross-border guide)

A fire on the roof of a building in Chiasso caused injuries and the evacuation of about thirty people.

Contesto

In brief - Fire on the roof of a building in Chiasso - Four workers injured, two hospitalized - Thirty residents evacuated ## Key facts - What: Fire on the roof of a building - When: Just before 13:30 - Where: Piazza Indipendenza, Chiasso - Who: Four workers, Cantonal Police, Municipal Police, SAM, CSCPM - Impact: Two workers hospitalized A fire broke out on the roof of a building in Piazza Indipendenza, Chiasso this afternoon. According to the Cantonal Police, the blaze started just before 13:30 while four workers were carrying out repairs with a gas torch. The flames were contained to the exterior of the building, but as a precaution, around thirty people present in the building were evacuated. On the scene, Cantonal Police officers were supported by Chiasso Municipal Police officers, Mendrisiotto Autoambulance Service (SAM) rescuers, and firefighters from the Mendrisiotto Cantonal Fire and Rescue Center (CSCPM). ### Consequences for residents The fire caused the temporary closure of Via Livio and Corso San Gottardo to allow extinguishing operations. Evacuated residents were temporarily accommodated in nearby facilities until they could return to their homes. ### Injuries and hospitalizations According to an initial medical assessment, one worker suffered minor burns to the hands, while another experienced mild symptoms of intoxication. Both were taken to the hospital by ambulance for further examination. Their conditions have not yet been disclosed. ### Implications for cross-border workers For cross-border workers living or working in the Chiasso area, the fire represents another cause for concern regarding the safety of the buildings where they live or work.
