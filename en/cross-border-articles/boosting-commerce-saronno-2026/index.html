Saronno: boosting trade with structural measures

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 28 April 2026

The municipal council of Saronno is examining a motion to boost local trade with structural and permanent interventions

Contesto

In brief - Motion to boost commerce in Saronno - Accessibility and security issues - Proposed structural interventions ## Key facts - What: Motion to boost commerce in Saronno - When: 28 April 2026 - Where: Saronno, Italy - Who: Forza Italia councillors and Luca Amadio - Amount: Not yet specified Tonight, Tuesday 28 April, the Municipal Council of Saronno will examine a motion presented by the Forza Italia – PPE councillors, Rienzo Azzi, Agostino De Marco, Lorenzo Azzi, and independent councillor Luca Amadio. The aim is to outline guidelines for revitalizing commerce in the city, a crucial topic for local economic vitality. Proximity commerce is considered fundamental for the economic development of Saronno. However, in recent years, the city center has seen a progressive loss of attractiveness, with the closure of numerous historic businesses and increasing difficulty in finding new commercial operators willing to invest in the area. Despite the administration's efforts, such as organizing events and exhibitions, the response from shops has often been disappointing. Events that attract many visitors do not automatically translate into increased sales. ### Main issues The motion emphasizes that the revitalization of commerce cannot happen through temporary solutions but requires a structural strategy. Among the main problems highlighted are poor traffic organization, lack of parking near the center, and the need to enhance urban security, both in terms of perception and reality. Additionally, there is no coordinated plan that clearly defines the interventions, priorities, and quality standards for improving public spaces. Practical Analysis The revival of commerce in Saronno requires a structural approach and not just temporary events. The motion presented by the councilors of Forza Italia and the independent councilor Luca Amadio aims to improve accessibility, security, and urban decor. These interventions are essential to attract new commercial operators and maintain existing ones. ### Accessibility and Traffic Flow One of the critical points is the review of traffic flow and the enhancement of nearby parking. The presence of the Ztl (limited traffic zone) has made access to the center difficult. An organic parking plan could make the area more functional and attractive to visitors and traders. Furthermore, better organization of traffic could facilitate the movement of people and vehicles, improving the shopping experience. ### Urban Security Urban security is another fundamental aspect. The motion emphasizes the need to intervene both on the perception and the reality of security in commercial areas. This could include increasing the presence of law enforcement, installing surveillance cameras, and organizing regular patrols. A safe environment attracts more customers and contributes to a climate of trust among traders. ### Urban Decor Urban decor is a key element to attract visitors and traders. A coordinated plan for cleaning and maintenance of public spaces could significantly improve the appearance of the city center. This includes the maintenance of roads, the cleaning of green areas, and the management of waste. A clean and well-kept environment is more inviting and contributes to a better shopping experience. ### Administrative Structure The administrative structure of the city, particularly the SUAP, needs to be enhanced. Concrete Actions To revitalize commerce in Saronno, it is necessary to adopt a series of concrete and targeted actions. Here is a step-by-step guide to better understand the proposals and necessary actions. ### Step 1: Review of Traffic Flow The first proposed action is the review of traffic flow. This includes creating an organic parking plan that makes access to the city center more functional. A well-structured plan could include the creation of new parking spaces, the reorganization of existing ones, and the promotion of alternative solutions such as carpooling or bike sharing. ### Step 2: Enhancement of Urban Security The second step is the enhancement of urban security. This could include increasing the presence of law enforcement, installing surveillance cameras, and organizing regular patrols. A safe environment attracts more customers and contributes to a climate of trust among traders. ### Step 3: Improvement of Urban Decor The third step is the improvement of urban decor. A coordinated plan of cleaning and maintenance interventions for public spaces could significantly improve the appearance of the city center. This includes the maintenance of roads, the cleaning of green areas, and the management of waste. A clean and well-maintained environment is more inviting and contributes to a better shopping experience. ### Step 4: Enhancement of SUAP The fourth step is the enhancement of SUAP (Single Office for Productive Activities).
