LATI wins Intesa Sanpaolo's Best Business Award

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 2 May 2026

The Varese-based company LATI Industria Termoplastici joins the network of outstanding SMEs for its solidity, innovation, and international vision

Contesto

In brief - LATI awarded among Intesa Sanpaolo's Winning Enterprises - Recognition for economic solidity, innovation, and ESG impact - Part of the network of excellence SMEs in Lombardy, with potential expansion into Ticino ## Key facts - What: "Winning Enterprises" recognition - When: May 1, 2026 - Where: Bergamo, during the national roadshow - Who: Intesa Sanpaolo - Amount: Among the ten companies awarded for Northern Lombardy - LATI 2025 turnover: €50 million, a 15% increase compared to 2024 - ESG: 20% reduction in CO2 emissions in 2025 compared to 2023 LATI Industria Termoplastici S.p.A., a Varese-based company specializing in high-performance thermoplastic materials, has been selected among Intesa Sanpaolo's "Winning Enterprises." The recognition, awarded during the Bergamo leg of the national roadshow, celebrates the company's economic solidity, strategic vision, and ability to generate long-term value. LATI was included among the ten companies awarded for the Northern Lombardy area, alongside representatives of strategic Made in Italy supply chains, including nautical, aerospace, construction, tourism, and textile sectors. The "Winning Enterprises" program, now in its sixth edition, highlights the excellence of Italian small and medium-sized enterprises. Particular attention is given to ESG criteria, human capital enhancement, and international development. LATI was recognized for its industrial growth path and constant commitment to developing high-performance thermoplastic materials, contributing to the competitiveness of the supply chains in which it operates. ### Michela Conterno's comment > "LATI is a company with a strong technical DNA and a family soul, capable of combining an international vision with local roots. Dettagli operativi

Implications for the territory and cross-border workers The recognition of LATI as a Winning Enterprise not only enhances the company, but also has a positive impact on the surrounding area, including cross-border workers who work in Lombardy and reside in Ticino. LATI's innovation and industrial growth can contribute to creating new job opportunities and strengthening the competitiveness of local supply chains, indirectly benefiting cross-border workers as well. For example, LATI has recently invested 5 million euros in a new production plant in Drezzo, in the province of Varese, which has created 30 new jobs. This project has a direct impact on cross-border workers from Ticinese municipalities such as Mendrisio, Chiasso, and Stabio, who represent approximately 40% of LATI's workforce. The geographical proximity and tax benefits for cross-border workers, such as exemption from income tax in Switzerland for workers residing in Ticino and working in Italy, make these opportunities particularly attractive. ### Comparison with other excellences Being included among the Winning Enterprises allows LATI to compare itself with other Italian excellences, sharing values and growth paths. This comparison can lead to strategic collaborations and greater visibility at national and international levels, opening new prospects for the company and its employees, including cross-border workers operating in the technopolymers sector. LATI can now access a network of over 500 selected Italian companies, with an average turnover of 150 million euros. This comparison can lead to concrete collaborations, such as the recent agreement signed with a Lombard company specializing in 3D printing technologies, which has allowed LATI to expand its product range and attract new talents. ### Fut...
