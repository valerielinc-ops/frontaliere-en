Procedure and Tools For cross-border workers who wish to verify whether the perceived price increases are justified or not, the first step is to consult the cost calculator available on the official Canton Ticino website. This tool allows you to compare the average prices of goods and services in Ticino with those in other regions, including Italian regions, and to monitor recent trends over the past months. In case of suspicion of unfair practices or unjustified hikes, you can submit a report to the price surveillance office by following the procedure outlined on the site and attaching proof of increased costs. The price surveillance law stipulates that companies are subject to inspections and sanctions if they practice non-transparent or unjustified pricing. Additionally, canton authorities are enhancing the digital control system by facilitating the submission of reports through online platforms and investing in data analysis tools. For cross-border workers, it is important to know that the monitoring tools are continuously updated and that reports can contribute to a fairer market. Remember to keep all purchase receipts and price comparison evidence, such as screenshots and receipts, for potential checks. Finally, the system of reimbursements between Italy and Switzerland, concerning tax and social security refunds, can be affected by price trends and rate variations. For further information, it is advisable to consult the cost calculator and keep an eye on official communications from canton authorities.
