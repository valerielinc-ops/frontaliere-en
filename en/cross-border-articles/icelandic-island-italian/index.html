Leonardo Piccione, a native of Corato, Puglia, born in 1987, spent several months each year on the island of Grímsey, in Iceland. With a PhD in Statistical Sciences, Piccione has worked in various jobs, from hotel reception to a sandwich kiosk. His experience is recounted in the book 'Things to Do at Night in the Arctic Circle,' published in 2026. Grímsey is an Arctic island with an area smaller than Vienna's Prater and not much larger than New York's Central Park. It is home to just over fifty humans and countless puffins. The island is crossed by the Arctic Circle and has a long list of unique attributes, such as the absence of trees and the irregularity of its shape. Piccione won over the inhabitants of Grímsey with his cooking, particularly his Barese focaccia and ice cream brioche. His book is a memoir of a winter stay in a place on the edge of habitability, a biography of a forgotten character, an essay on the irresistible attraction that islands exert on humans, and a meteorological, geological, and ornithological diary of an extreme land. ### The Forgotten Character Piccione's book also focuses on Daniel Willard Fiske, a bibliographer and American collector obsessed with Grímsey. Leonardo Piccione is not your typical adventurer seeking total isolation. In fact, he carries a router to avoid complete isolation. Solitude attracts and scares him in equal measure, and to avoid succumbing to addiction, he goes to the village emporium every day, which is only open for one hour a day. ### Daily Life in Grímsey Daily life in Grímsey is characterized by a series of activities that Piccione describes with precision. For example, you can organize a dinner with company even in bad weather. Piccione has discovered that the key to gaining the trust of the islanders is to show up at the pool and the emporium counter. ### The Allure of the Islands Piccione's book is also an essay on the irresistible allure that islands exert on human beings. Piccione reviews a series of island narrators, from D.H. Lawrence to Jonathan Franzen, from Fredrik Sjöberg to Paolo Rumiz, showing how each of them has found a unique way to describe island life. ### The Geology and Ornithology of Grímsey Piccione dedicates a part of his book to the description of the geology and ornithology of Grímsey. His descriptions are detailed and precise, showing a great attention to sensory perception. For example, he describes the wind in the window joints as powerful and roaring, a noise that amplifies the perception of Arctic silence. ### The Importance of Silence Silence is a central theme in Piccione's book. He explains that Arctic silence is not absolute silence, but relative silence.
