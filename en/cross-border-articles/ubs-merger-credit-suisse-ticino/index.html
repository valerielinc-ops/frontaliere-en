Frequently Asked Questions
How many jobs are expected to be lost in Switzerland after the integration of Credit Suisse in UBS?
It is expected to lose approximately 3,000 jobs, of which 1,500 already cut, mainly in back-office and IT.
What are the tax benefits for frontier workers in Ticino according to the law of 19 June 2023?
According to the law, border workers in Ticino may benefit from tax relief and access to social services, provided they comply with specific criteria of residence and imponiability, although specific details have not been expressly mentioned in the article.
How does UBS-Credit Suisse merger affect the employment contracts of Ticino frontier workers with Italian companies?
Contracts of frontier workers working for Italian companies but are hired by Swiss banks as UBS must now comply with the law of 19 June 2023. This legislation provides that frontier workers retain the rights provided for by the Italian contract, but with some exceptions related to the bank's registered office.
What are the consequences for frontier workers if UBS reduces jobs in Ticino?
The Ticino frontier workers hired by UBS, if involved in cuts, could access voluntary retirement or social transfers, as provided for in the restructuring plan. The benefits depend on the residence and duration of the contract, but the rights are protected by Swiss law.
What does it change for frontierers working in private banking after the merger UBS-Credit Suisse?
Frontiers employed in private banking in Lugano, Bellinzona or Mendrisio may see a reorganization of services or teams, but UBS is trying to maintain key skills. Training and updating of contracts are a priority to ensure operational continuity.
