Sunset in Cadenazzo: Security Guard Killed by Hit-and-Run A tragic event occurred in Cadenazzo, Ticino Canton, yesterday evening around 9:00 PM. A security guard was struck by a car and died. According to initial investigations, the man was engaged in traffic control when the accident happened. The car that hit him did not stop and fled the scene. Emergency responders were called and found the guard dying. He was transported to the hospital, where he succumbed to his injuries. The incident was reported to the police, and an investigation was opened to determine the cause of the accident. The victim was a 38-year-old security professional employed by 'Securitas', a security company based in Bellinzona, responsible for securing various areas in Ticino Canton. Sunset in Cadenazzo: security guard killed by a car The incident occurred on the main road of Cadenazzo, which connects Lugano to Bellinzona, in a section known for being particularly busy. The road was closed for several hours to allow law enforcement to conduct investigations and remove the vehicle involved in the incident. According to initial investigations, the car that hit the security guard was traveling at high speed, exceeding 120 km/h, and did not stop after the incident. "Initial estimates indicate that the victim was a 35-year-old man, resident of Manno, who worked as a security guard for a security company in Bellinzona," said the investigator. "The investigations will be conducted with care and all the circumstances of the incident will be examined, also in relation to the possibility of having committed a violation of road traffic regulations." Article 74 of the road traffic regulations states that anyone who drives a vehicle in a dangerous manner or without respect for other people or things is punishable by a fine of up to 300 Swiss francs. The Lugano police asked witnesses to cooperate and provide useful information for the investigations. According to local sources, the victim was a well-respected person in the Manno community, where he worked as a manager of a food store. "The Manno community is in shock at the news of the death of our fellow citizen," said the mayor of Manno, "we are ready to support his family in this difficult moment." Frequently Asked Questions
What is the Ticino legislation that regulates the behavior of drivers in the event of an accident?
The Ticino Road Safety Act (LRST) requires drivers to stop and provide personal information in the event of an accident, as well as maintain a minimum distance of 10 meters from any moving vehicle.
How many road accidents with fatalities were recorded in the Canton of Ticino in 2023 up to the accident in Cadenazzo?
According to the data available in the article, until the Cadenazzo accident, 5 deaths and 100 serious injuries were recorded in road accidents in the Canton of Ticino in 2023.
How is the safety of cross-border workers working on the road in the Canton of Ticino managed?
Cross-border workers employed in road surveillance activities must follow strict safety procedures, as required by the Ticino LRST. Security companies are obliged to regularly check vehicles and train employees on what to do in the event of an accident, including immediate reporting to the authorities.
What are the penalties for those who flee after causing an accident with injuries in the Canton of Ticino?
According to Article 74 of the Ticino Highway Code, those who flee after causing an accident with injuries risk a fine of up to 300 Swiss francs and additional criminal penalties, such as revocation of the license or legal proceedings for failure to assist.
Do Italian cross-border commuters working in Ticino need to have specific insurance to operate on the road?
Yes, cross-border workers employed in road surveillance activities must be covered by specific civil liability insurance, which also includes damage to third parties during the performance of their duties, as required by Ticino cantonal regulations.
