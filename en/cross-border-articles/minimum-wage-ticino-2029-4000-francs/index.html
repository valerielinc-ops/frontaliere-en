Minimum wage in Ticino: 4,000 CHF gross per month from 2029

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 22 April 2026

The Ticino cantonal parliament has approved the increase of the minimum wage to 4,000 CHF gross per month from 2029, benefiting 23,000 workers, including 15,000 cross-border commuters.

This increase will benefit approximately 23,000 workers, of which 15,000 are cross-border commuters. The agreement, resulting as a counter-proposal to the initiative of the Socialist Party (PS) named 'For a social minimum wage,' provides for a progressive increase in the minimum wage. From 2027 it will rise to 20.50-21 francs per hour, from 2028 to 21-21.50 francs, and from 2029 to 21.75-22.25 francs. From 2030 the salary will also be indexed to inflation. Compared to the current situation, the minimum wage will thus be increased by over 300 francs per month and will reach approximately 4,000 francs gross (4,360 euros). ### Who will benefit from the increase The co-president of the PS, Fabrizio Sirica, illustrated in the chamber that over 23,000 people will benefit from this measure, of which 8,000 are residents. Dettagli operativi

The increase in the minimum wage in Ticino will have a significant impact on thousands of workers, particularly cross-border commuters who make up a substantial part of the workforce in the canton. The progressive increase in the minimum wage, which will reach 4,000 francs gross per month by 2029, represents an important step towards greater wage equity and a reduction in disparities between residents and cross-border workers. ### Implications for Cross-Border Workers For cross-border workers, the increase in the minimum wage represents a significant improvement in working and living conditions. Many cross-border workers are employed in low-wage sectors where current wages are often insufficient to cover living expenses. With the increase in the minimum wage, cross-border workers will benefit from fairer remuneration that meets their needs. This will not only improve their quality of life but also reduce their dependence on state assistance. ### Comparison with the Current Situation Currently, the minimum wage in Ticino is between 20 and 20.50 francs per hour, which corresponds to approximately 3,400 francs gross per month. With the planned increase, the minimum wage will reach 4,000 francs gross per month, representing an increase of over 17%. This increase is particularly significant given that the cost of living in Ticino is high and many workers, particularly cross-border commuters, struggle to cover basic expenses. ### Future Scenarios With the increase in the minimum wage, many low-wage sectors, such as clothing and catering, will need to adjust their wage policies to comply with the new regulations.
