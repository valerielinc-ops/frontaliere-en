Dettagli operativi

From a regulatory and technical perspective, the situation of Società Navigazione Lago fits into a complex framework involving several cantonal and federal institutions. SNL operates under a cantonal concession, with clear obligations regarding collective labor agreements (CLA) and transparency in managing human and financial resources, including the Pension Fund. Cantonal legislation in Ticino requires companies operating in public and tourist transport to comply with minimum wage standards and working conditions defined by the CLA, which must be regularly updated. In SNL’s case, unions argue that the wages paid are below the established parameters, creating a gap that particularly penalizes cross-border workers, who face high commuting costs, for example at the Brogeda and Gaggiolo border crossings. Another critical point concerns the Pension Fund, essential for ensuring a secure retirement future for employees. Accusations of a lack of transparency and unclear management of the Fund raise alarms not only among workers but also cantonal oversight bodies such as the DFE and the Labor Office. The absence of detailed and verifiable information on reserves and contributions threatens to undermine confidence in the company’s social security system. Regarding labor violations, SEV reported that in February 2026 cases of non-compliance with safety rules and working hours were detected, resulting in sanctions and warnings from supervisory authorities. These incidents add to an already long list of issues reported in recent years, prompting unions to demand more decisive interventions from the Canton and SECO to ensure compliance with laws and protection of workers’ rights. Moreover, monitoring the Pension Fund is essential: every employee should periodically request official information about their pension status to avoid surprises in the future. ⚠️ If doubts or irregularities arise, it is recommended to contact unions such as SEV or cantonal labor offices, which offer free advice and can intervene to protect workers’ rights. Frequently Asked Questions
What is the state of the working and wage conditions for cross-border commuters at Società Navigazione Lago (SNL) in Lugano?
The working and wage conditions for cross-border workers at SNL are criticized for irregularities in compliance with regulations, wages judged to be unfair compared to the cost of living in the Canton of Ticino, and lack of transparency in the management of the Pension Fund, despite promises of improvement.
What is SECO's role in monitoring the working conditions of cross-border workers at SNL?
The SECO (Federal Labour Service) monitors the situation of the Ticino labour market, ensuring compliance with labour laws and workers' rights, including for cross-border workers at the SNL, in collaboration with the cantonal authorities.
How can I check whether my SNL Pension Fund complies with Ticino pension standards?
You can request a copy of your current pension position from the SNL Pension Fund or from your employer. Compare the contributions paid with the cantonal legal minimums (e.g. at least 7% of the salary for the working party) and check that the returns are in line with market benchmarks.
What are the rights of SNL cross-border commuters in the event of unreported accidents at work?
Cross-border commuters are also entitled to compulsory insurance coverage (UVG) for accidents that are not reported in good time. Within 48 hours of the accident, report it to your employer and to the UVG insurance (e.g. Suva) to activate the health and economic protections provided for by Swiss law.
What happens if the SNL does not comply with the wage payment times established by the collective agreement?
You can contact SECO or the SEV Trade Union to report the delay. Ticino law provides for penalties for the company and, in the event of repetition, the revocation of the concession. Keep documents (pay stubs, communications) to support your request.
