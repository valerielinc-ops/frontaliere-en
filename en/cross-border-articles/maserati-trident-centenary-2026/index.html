From 1926 to 2026: Maserati celebrates the centenary of its iconic logo and the first sports victory at the Targa Florio

Contesto

In brief - Maserati celebrates 100 years of the Trident and the first victory at the Targa Florio - Issuance of a dedicated stamp by the Ministry of Enterprises and Made in Italy - Global campaign with 3D video and artificial intelligence ## Key facts - What: Centenary of the Trident and first sports victory - When: 1926-2026 - Where: Modena, Italy - Who: Maserati - Amount: 100 years Maserati celebrates in 2026 a double anniversary: the hundred years of its iconic logo, the Trident, and the first sports victory achieved in 1926 at the Targa Florio. On April 25 of that year, Alfieri Maserati, at the wheel of the Tipo 26, won first place in class and eighth overall, starting a racing tradition that would deeply mark the identity of the brand. Born to compete on the road and on the track, the Trident has become over time a global symbol of Italian excellence. Throughout 2026, all Maserati activities around the world - from sports events to elegance competitions, to cultural initiatives - will celebrate this milestone. To underline its value, the issuance of a dedicated stamp by the Ministry of Enterprises and Made in Italy, recognizing the cultural relevance of the brand in over 70 markets. "The Trident embodies the most authentic soul of Maserati. Born on the track in 1926, it carries with it the heritage of a company that has learned to build cars chasing tenths of a second. An invaluable heritage - made of sports victories, iconic models and technical records - that the women and men of Maserati guard and renew every day in Modena and around the world. In 2026 we also celebrate 112 years since the foundation, confirming ourselves as the longest-lasting brand of the Motor Valley and one of the ambassadors of Made in Italy at the international level. The Global Campaign for the Centenary To celebrate its centenary, Maserati has launched a new global campaign that bridges the past and present. The main video features historic cars like the Tipo 26, Ghibli, and MC12 alongside contemporary models such as the GranTurismo Trofeo, Grecale Trofeo, and MCXtrema. The images depict a symbolic journey where the cars, traversing evocative landscapes, form a giant Trident seen from above.
