Chiasso: two men arrested for bicycle theft

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 27 April 2026

Two Romanian citizens arrested in Chiasso after fleeing a gas station without paying for fuel. Stolen bicycles and burglary tools found.

Contesto

In brief - Two arrested in Chiasso for bicycle theft - Fleeing a gas station without paying for fuel - Three stolen bicycles and burglary tools found - Ongoing investigations for other thefts in Ticino, focusing on Lugano and Mendrisio - Estimated value of the thefts: over 5,000 CHF ## Key facts - What: Arrests for bicycle theft - When: April 25, 2026 - Where: Chiasso, Mendrisiotto, with possible links to Lugano and Mendrisio - Who: Cantonal police, Chiasso municipal police, Federal Customs Office - Amount: Three stolen bicycles found, estimated value 3,000 CHF Two Romanian citizens, a 24-year-old and a 21-year-old, were arrested in Chiasso after fleeing a gas station in Coldrerio where they had filled up without paying. The incident occurred on April 25, 2026, and led to the discovery of three stolen bicycles and burglary tools inside their vehicle. The report came from the gas station, leading to the intervention of the authorities. ### Police operation The search operation, coordinated by the Cantonal Police with the support of the Chiasso Municipal Police and the Federal Customs and Border Security Office, allowed the interception of the car near the Chiasso train station. During the searches, three disassembled bicycles were found inside the vehicle, with the wheels separated from the frame. Some of the bicycles were already reported as stolen, with an estimated value of 3,000 CHF. Burglary tools, presumably used to force the bicycle locks, were also found in the vehicle. ### Ongoing investigations Investigations are now underway to verify any possible involvement of the two arrested individuals in other e-bike and bicycle thefts that occurred in recent weeks in Ticino. Frequently Asked Questions
What should I do if my bicycle has been stolen?
Report the theft to the local police or cantonal police immediately. Provide as much information as possible about the bicycle, such as the frame number, brand, and characteristics of the vehicle. Note all details of the theft, including the time, place, and circumstances. Consider installing a GPS tracking system on your bicycle.
What are the legal consequences for bicycle theft?
The legal consequences for bicycle theft can include charges of theft, damage, receiving stolen property, and driving without authorization. These charges can lead to criminal penalties and significant legal consequences.
How can I protect my bicycle from theft?
Always use high-quality locks and secure the bicycle to a stable structure. Prefer to park the bicycle in monitored areas or locked spaces. Avoid leaving the bicycle in isolated or poorly lit places. Consider installing a GPS tracking system on your bicycle.
