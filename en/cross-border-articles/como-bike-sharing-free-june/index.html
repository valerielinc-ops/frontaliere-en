Como: free bike sharing for 3 hours until June

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 30 April 2026

The bike sharing service in Como is back with 80 bicycles and 17 stations. Free for the first 3 hours until June 30th.

Contesto

In brief - Bike sharing returns to Como - Free for first 3 hours until June - 80 bicycles and 17 stations - Comparison with Lugano and Bellinzona ## Key facts - What: Return of bike sharing to Como - When: From April 2024 - Where: Como, Italy - Who: Spazio Visibile - Amount: 80 bicycles, 17 stations Finally, bike sharing has returned to Como. The service, managed by Spazio Visibile, was officially presented this morning. The main station is located on Via Vittorio Emanuele, and the yellow bicycles are already ready for use. This service has been reactivated after a period of pause and represents an important novelty for the citizens and tourists of the city. Spazio Visibile, which recently acquired the business branch from Bicincittà Italia, announced the details of the service's return. The custom bicycles for bike sharing are complete and will be 80. In parallel, the work to adapt the pickup points in the city is almost complete, which should be 17. The service offers a special promotion to encourage citizens and tourists to try bike sharing. Until June 30, the vehicles can be used for free for the first 3 hours. To ensure the availability of bicycles to everyone and avoid prolonged use, a consumption rate applies after 3 hours: 1 euro for the first half hour and 2 euros for every subsequent half hour (or fraction). Frequently Asked Questions
How much does bike sharing cost in Como after the first 3 hours free?
After the first 3 hours free, the cost is 1 euro for the first half hour thereafter. Each subsequent half-hour or fraction thereof costs 2 euro. For example, a total use of 4 hours would cost 3 euros (1 euro for the first extra half hour and 2 euros for the second extra half hour).
How can I register for Como's bike share service?
To register, it is necessary to download the service's official app, called ComoNext, and complete the registration by providing a valid ID. Bicycle reservations are then made through the same app.
How many bikes and stations are there in Como's bike sharing service?
The bike sharing service in Como has 80 bicycles, distributed in 17 stations strategically located around the city. The main station is located on Via Vittorio Emanuele.
What are the bike sharing rates in Lugano and Bellinzona for comparison?
In Lugano, the service costs 3 Swiss francs for the first half hour and 2 francs for each subsequent half hour. Bellinzona offers similar rates, with 50 bikes and 10 stations operated by Mobility.
What are the advantages of bike sharing for cross-border commuters working in Como?
Bike sharing offers cross-border commuters an economical and sustainable alternative for short trips to Como, avoiding parking costs or waiting for public transport. For example, it can save up to €10 per week compared to using a car.
