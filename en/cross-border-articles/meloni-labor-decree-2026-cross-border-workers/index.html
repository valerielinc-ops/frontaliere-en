Meloni's Labour Decree: incentives and fair wages for cross-border workers

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 28 April 2026

The Council of Ministers has given the green light to the labour decree with incentives for hiring and fair wages for workers

Contesto

In brief - Green light for the labor decree with incentives for hiring - Incentives linked to fair wages - Nearly one billion euros to renew employment incentives - New opportunities for Ticino cross-border workers ## Key facts - What: Labor decree with incentives for hiring - When: April 28, 2026 - Where: Italy - Who: Council of Ministers - Amount: Nearly one billion euros - Cross-border workers: 65,000 workers from Ticino in Italy The Council of Ministers has approved the new labor decree, issued in view of May 1st. The decree allocates nearly one billion euros for the renewal of some important and effective employment incentives, particularly those linked to the hiring of young people under 35, the hiring of women, and workers in the Zes area. The President of the Council, Giorgia Meloni, explained that the decree is a piece of a broader strategy aimed at supporting the creation of more employment, but also stable and quality employment. ### Implications for cross-border workers The decree introduces an important novelty: access to these incentives is only possible if the 'fair wage' is recognized and applied to one's workers. This overall economic treatment is not composed solely of the hourly wage, but of all the economic elements that contribute to forming the contract in favor of the worker. Practical Analysis: What Changes for Cross-Border Workers The labor decree introduces a series of incentives for hiring, but only for those who apply the 'fair wage'. This means that employers who want to access the incentives must guarantee their workers an overall economic treatment that respects the national collective labor agreements signed by the most representative trade union and employers' organizations at the national level. 📊 Concrete Examples: - A company in Lugano hiring a cross-border worker from Campione d'Italia must guarantee a minimum salary of at least 2,000 euros gross per month for a full-time contract, in line with the national collective labor agreement (CCNL) of the sector. - A company in Mendrisio hiring a cross-border worker from Como must respect the CCNL of commerce, which provides for a minimum salary of 1,800 euros gross per month for a full-time contract. ### Procedure to Access Incentives To access the incentives, employers must: 1. Concrete actions for cross-border workers Cross-border workers seeking employment in Italy should inform themselves about job opportunities offered by companies that respect the 'fair wage'. In particular, it is crucial to know the Italian regulations on labor, such as the Meloni Labor Decree of 2023, which introduces incentives for companies that respect national collective labor agreements (CCNL). For example, a cross-border worker from Chiasso working in Como should verify that their salary is in line with the CCNL of their sector. For a construction worker, the minimum wage set by the CCNL Edile Industria in Lombardy is approximately €1,800 gross per month for 14 months. Frequently Asked Questions
What is meant by 'fair salary'?
The 'fair salary' is the overall economic treatment received by the worker, composed of all the economic elements that contribute to forming the contract in favor of the worker. This includes not only the hourly wage, but also other benefits and contributions.
How can I verify if a company respects the 'fair salary'?
You can verify if a company respects the 'fair salary' by checking if it applies the national collective labor agreements stipulated by the most representative trade union and employers' organizations at the national level. You can also use the [salary calculator](nav:calculator) to compare job offers.
What are the incentives provided by the labor decree?
The labor decree provides incentives for the hiring of young people under 35, women, and workers in the Zes area. However, these incentives are only accessible if the employer recognizes and applies the 'fair salary' to their workers.
