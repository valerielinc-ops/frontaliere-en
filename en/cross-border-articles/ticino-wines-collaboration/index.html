12 Ticinese winemakers join forces to enhance Ticinese Merlot

Contesto

TL;DR - 12 Ticinese winemakers collaborate to promote Merlot quality and diversity. - 2022 Merlot vintage challenged by heat and drought. - Dodas initiative offers 12 unique Merlot interpretations from different areas. - Financial support for Ticinese winemakers: up to 3,000 francs per hectare. ## Key facts - Iniziativa: Dodas - Vitigni: Merlot - Anno: 2024 - Viticoltori: 12 - Produzione 2022: 34.6 milioni di litri - Superficie vitata: 1.043 ettari - Contributi finanziari: Fino a 3.000 franchi per ettaro - Investimento iniziale: 50.000 franchi In 2024, wine consumption in Switzerland dropped by 8% compared to the previous year, a negative trend that prompted 12 Ticinese winemakers to come together in the 'Dodas' initiative (twelve in Ticinese dialect). The goal is to highlight the quality and diversity of Ticinese Merlot, the region's flagship grape variety. The winemakers have gathered in a crate 12 bottles of 2022 Merlot wine, a particularly challenging vintage due to the heat and drought that characterized the season. The diversity of soils and winemaking techniques gives rise to 12 different interpretations of the same grape variety, offering consumers a range of choices that reflect the peculiarities of the different production areas. For example, Merlot produced in the Arzo area, in the municipality of Mendrisio, has a more intense and structured aromatic profile thanks to the soils rich in limestone and the particular exposure of the vineyards. The 'Dodas' initiative is not just a response to the crisis in the sector, but also an opportunity for Ticinese producers to enhance their production and showcase the unique characteristics of their wine. Dettagli operativi

The companies participating in the 'Dodas' initiative are all family-run and represent the Ticinese winemaking tradition. Gaby Gianini, one of the winemakers involved, emphasizes that 'the market is extremely uncertain and unstable, but it is precisely at this moment that we thought that the winemaker's profession is individualistic, but it was necessary to come together.' The initiative, which involves wine producers from different areas of Ticino, such as Contone and Magliasina, represents a concrete example of collaboration between family businesses. Giorgio Rossi, another winemaker, adds that the initiative stems from the desire to 'do something together with pleasure, with joy' and to represent the entire Ticinese territory. 'Dodas' is a project that brings together 10 Ticinese winemaking companies, which together produce over 100,000 bottles of wine per year. The initiative involves the creation of a unique wine, obtained by combining different grape varieties, such as Merlot and Bondola. The 'Dodas' initiative represents not only a marketing exercise but also a moment of sharing and strengthening the local winemaking community. The producers involved have worked together to define the characteristics of the wine, from the selection of grapes to the definition of packaging. For example, they have decided to use glass bottles produced by a local company and to adopt a label that represents the Ticinese territory. From a regulatory point of view, the companies participating in the 'Dodas' initiative must comply with the regulations in force regarding wine production, such as the Cantonal Law on Viticulture and Winemaking of 1995, which regulates wine production and marketing in Ticino. Frequently Asked Questions
What is the objective of the 'Dodas' initiative of the Ticino winemakers?
The aim is to enhance the quality and diversity of Ticino Merlot, highlighting the peculiarities of soils and vinification techniques of the region.
What is the maximum financial contribution per hectare of vineyard cultivated in Switzerland according to the order of 2018?
Up to CHF 3,000 per hectare.
How does the marketing of Ticino Merlot wine in Italy for border producers?
The Ticino winemakers can sell directly in Italy without customs duties thanks to the free trade agreement between Switzerland and the EU. However, they must comply with EU regulations on labelling, traceability and health certification, as well as declare the quantities exported to the Swiss and Italian customs authorities.
What are the tax benefits for Ticino wine growers exporting wine to Italy?
Ticino producers can benefit from cantonal tax relief (e.g. reduction of income tax for companies exporting over 30% of production) and EU customs relief. In addition, the Canton Ticino offers grants for promotion abroad, up to 50% of the costs of participation in international fairs.
What are the main logistic challenges to transport the Ticino Merlot wine in Italy?
The main challenges are the compliance of the cold chain during transport (temperature between 12 °C and 18°C), the management of delivery times to avoid customs delays and compliance with EU health regulations. It is recommended to use specialized freight forwarders with refrigerated equipment.
