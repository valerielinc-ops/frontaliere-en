Ermotti: UBS deserves debate

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 2 May 2026

UBS CEO Sergio Ermotti defends the bank from accusations of aggressive pressure on the debate about own funds.

Contesto

In brief - UBS rejects accusations of aggressive lobbying - Ermotti: 'We have the right to participate in the discussion' - Federal Council's proposals deemed 'excessive' ## Key facts - What: Accusations of aggressive lobbying against UBS - When: April 29, 2026 - Where: Switzerland - Who: Sergio Ermotti, UBS CEO - Amount: Not specified UBS CEO Sergio Ermotti has dismissed accusations of aggressive pressure in the debate on banks' own funds. In a conference call with the media, Ermotti stated that the bank has 'very valid arguments' to put forward and that 'we have too much respect for Parliament to issue threats.' The Ticino manager also appealed to the Federal Council, asking for the discussion to return to an 'appropriate level.' Finance Minister Karin Keller-Sutter had stated in an interview with Blick that she had heard from some parliamentarians who fear that UBS could reduce contributions to their party. According to the Federal Councillor, such strong lobbying from a private actor is rather unusual in Switzerland. ### Criticism of the Federal Council's proposals Ermotti explained that UBS does not intend to speculate in public about countermeasures, but reiterated that the Federal Council's proposals are 'excessive' and 'disproportionate.' In particular, the bank considers the planned full coverage with own funds of foreign branches a 'thorn in the side.' The CEO emphasized that for Credit Suisse, the problem was not the structure of regulation, but its implementation and communication. ### Statements by the Chairman of the Board of Directors Ermotti defended the statements made in mid-April by Chairman of the Board of Directors Colm Kelleher at the general assembly. Analysis of the Federal Council's Proposals The Federal Council's proposals, which UBS considers excessive, include full capital coverage for foreign branches. This measure could have a significant impact on Swiss banks, particularly those with a strong international presence like UBS. The bank has emphasized that while adjustments are necessary, the current proposals are disproportionate and could compromise the competitiveness of Swiss banks. ### Reactions from the Financial Sector The tensions between UBS and the Federal Council have sparked reactions within the Swiss financial sector. Some experts have expressed concern over the potential consequences of overly stringent regulation, while others have advocated for the need to ensure the stability of the financial system. Statements by Ermotti and Kelleher have further polarized the debate. ### Implications for Cross-Border Workers Any changes to the regulation of own funds could have repercussions for cross-border workers employed in the Swiss financial sector.
