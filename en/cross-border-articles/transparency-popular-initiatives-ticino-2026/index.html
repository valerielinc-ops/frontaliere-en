Transparency in popular initiatives: the proposal to the Grand Council

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 28 April 2026

A new parliamentary initiative aims to strengthen financial transparency in popular initiatives in Ticino, with clearly defined thresholds for sustainability.

Contesto

In brief - PLR initiative for financial transparency in the Grand Council - Proposed threshold: 10 million francs annually - Mandatory assessment of sustainability ## Key facts - What: Generic parliamentary initiative PLR - When: Submitted on April 28, 2026 - Where: Grand Council of the Canton of Ticino - Who: Promoted by Simona Genini - Amount: Threshold of 10 million francs annually Direct democracy is one of Switzerland's most valuable tools, but in recent years, initiatives with significant impacts on public finances have been presented without clearly indicating the funding methods. This is the problem that the generic parliamentary initiative presented by Simona Genini on behalf of the PLR group in the Grand Council aims to address. The goal is to strengthen financial transparency and accountability in the use of popular rights. According to the promoters, current regulations allow for the proposal of new tasks or services at the expense of the State without explicitly outlining the financial consequences or related coverages. This dynamic risks creating legitimate expectations in the population, leaving the question of sustainability unresolved. Direct democracy, the initiative emphasizes, should not become a mechanism that separates the spending decision from the responsibility of funding. The proposal aims to amend the Law on the Exercise of Political Rights (LEPD), introducing a requirement for financial transparency for popular initiatives that entail significant new burdens for the Canton. In particular, it is requested that initiatives with financial consequences exceeding a defined threshold - proposed at 10 million francs annually, including amortizations on investments - must contain a detailed and verifiable description of the funding methods. ###...

Dettagli operativi

Practical Analysis The proposal to amend the LEPD introduces a financial transparency requirement for popular initiatives that entail significant new burdens for the Canton. This means that initiatives with financial consequences exceeding 10 million francs annually will need to include a detailed and verifiable description of the funding methods. ### What changes for cross-border workers? For cross-border workers, this initiative could mean greater transparency in decisions that affect their daily lives. For example, if a popular initiative proposes the construction of new schools or hospitals, cross-border workers might have access to clearer information on how such projects will be funded and whether they will be sustainable in the long term. ### Concrete Scenarios 1. Initiative for new infrastructure: If a popular initiative proposes the construction of a new road or bridge, the promoters will need to provide a detailed description of how they intend to fund the project. This could include tax increases, new taxes, or cuts to other public services. 2. Initiative for social services: If an initiative proposes the expansion of social services for cross-border workers, the promoters will need to demonstrate how they intend to fund such services without compromising the financial sustainability of the Canton. 3. Initiative for tax reductions: If an initiative proposes tax reductions for cross-border workers, the promoters will need to show how they intend to compensate for the loss of tax revenue, for example through cuts to other services or increases in other taxes. ### Before/After Comparison Before this initiative, popular initiatives could be presented without having to provide details on the funding methods. Frequently Asked Questions
What happens if a popular initiative does not meet the financial transparency requirements?
If a popular initiative does not meet the financial transparency requirements, it may be rejected or amended to include the required information. This ensures that all popular initiatives are based on complete and transparent information.
How can I participate in the debate on popular initiatives?
You can participate in the debate on popular initiatives by informing yourself about the proposals, attending public debates and consultations, and voting when an initiative comes to a vote. Use the site's tools to understand how political decisions might affect your income and taxes.
