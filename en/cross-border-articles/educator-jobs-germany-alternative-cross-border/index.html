Educators in Germany: salaries up to 3,000 euros, an alternative for cross-border workers? (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 1 March 2026

While Ticino attracts, a new opportunity opens up in Germany for educators, with salaries up to 3,000 euros and relocation support. An option to consider for young people in the border area.

This opportunity, aimed at young people between 18 and 35 years old, promises salaries that can reach 3,000 euros per month, with prospects for increases after the first six months. Positions are open in key German regions such as Bavaria, Baden-Württemberg, and North Rhine-Westphalia, in daycare centers for children. These are not just temporary experiences, but real employment contracts with a minimum required stay of six months, an aspect that lends stability and seriousness to the offer. Dettagli operativi

The Ticino job market, while offering multiple opportunities for cross-border workers, also presents specific challenges, especially in sectors like education. On one hand, the geographical and cultural proximity makes Ticino a preferred destination for many, but on the other hand, competition can be high, and the recognition of Italian qualifications in Switzerland is not always straightforward, sometimes requiring lengthy procedures with the DECS (Department of Education, Culture, and Sport) or other cantonal bodies, with times and costs that can impact career planning. For a cross-border educator, the salary in Ticino, while generally higher than in Italy, must be compared to the cost of living and tax and social deductions, which can vary significantly depending on the municipality of residence in Italy and the applicable tax regime. Let’s consider the German proposal: a salary of up to 3,000 euros net per month represents a significant figure, especially when compared to average Italian salaries for similar roles. In Ticino, an educator with little experience might start with a gross salary of about 3,500-4,000 Swiss francs, which net and converted to euros, might not differ greatly from the German figure, but with the burden of a considerably higher cost of living in Switzerland. The accommodation offered by the employer in Germany, although at the employee's expense, greatly simplifies the relocation phase, a critical aspect for those seeking work across the border. Frequently Asked Questions
How much does an educator earn in Germany?
Salaries can reach 3,000 euros per month, with the possibility of an increase after the first six months.
What is the level of knowledge of German required to access the educator positions in Germany offered by Sunflower Exchange?
The level of knowledge of German required is A2/B1. The agency offers targeted language courses to help candidates reach this level.
What are the requirements to apply as an educator in Germany through Sunflower Exchange?
To apply you must be between 18 and 35 years old, have a degree in Educational Sciences or Pedagogy, and a level of knowledge of the German language equal to at least A2/B1. The agency offers courses to reach the required level.
How long is the employment contract in Germany for educators?
The offer provides for employment contracts with a minimum required stay of six months. These are not just temporary experiences, but a structured professional commitment with growth prospects.
What help does Sunflower Exchange offer for those moving to Germany?
Sunflower Exchange supports candidates in arranging online interviews, offers German language courses, assists in locating accommodation, and provides reimbursements for moving and language courses required for certifications.
