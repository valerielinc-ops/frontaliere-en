Drug trafficking: six arrests in Switzerland and abroad

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 28 April 2026

Joint operation by the Federal Department of Justice and Police against a criminal organization.

Contesto

In brief - Six arrests across Switzerland, France, and Germany - A fedpol employee involved - 12 house searches ## Key facts - What: Operation against drug trafficking - When: Tuesday - Where: Cantons of Basel City and Basel Country, France, and Germany - Who: Federal Public Prosecutor's Office (MPC) and Federal Criminal Police (PGF) - Amount: 12 house searches The Federal Public Prosecutor's Office (MPC), together with the Federal Criminal Police (PGF), conducted an operation against a criminal organization active in drug trafficking. The operation led to the arrest of six people, including an employee of the Federal Security Service of the Federal Police Office (fedpol). The searches were carried out in the Cantons of Basel City and Basel Country, as well as in France and Germany as part of international judicial assistance. Among those arrested is also an employee of fedpol, accused of selling confidential information. The man is suspected of passive corruption, breach of official secrecy, and complicity. The alleged recipient of the information has also been arrested and is suspected of active corruption of a Swiss public official. For all the people detained, the MPC will request pre-trial detention from the competent court of the Canton of Berna. ### Implications for cross-border workers The operation highlighted international collaboration between Swiss law enforcement and those of other countries. This type of operation can have a significant impact on security and the perception of security in the region, indirectly affecting cross-border workers who daily cross the borders between Switzerland and Italy.
