St. Gotthard Pass reopens Friday (cross-border guide)

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 5 May 2026

The St. Gotthard Pass reopens to traffic on Friday at 11 AM, in time for the Ascension and Pentecost bank holidays, easing traffic on the A2 motorway.

Contesto

In brief - San Gottardo pass reopens Friday at 11 - Available for Ascension and Pentecost holidays - Extended exit lane at Göschenen ## Key facts - What: Reopening of the San Gottardo pass - When: Friday at 11 - Where: San Gottardo pass - Who: Federal Roads Office (FEDRO) - Amount: Not specified The San Gottardo pass will reopen to traffic on Friday at 11. This reopening is particularly important for cross-border commuters using the north-south axis on the A2 motorway, especially in view of the Ascension and Pentecost holidays. The Federal Roads Office (FEDRO) announced that due to favorable weather conditions and less snowfall compared to last year, the clearing and restoration work on the road has progressed faster than expected. With the opening of the pass, the extended exit lane at Göschenen, on the A2, will also be put back into service. This lane, about 3 kilometers long, starts immediately after the entrance of Wassen and aims to protect the area from parasitic traffic. Furthermore, starting from the Ascension bridge, the special CUPRA lane will also be available at the south portal of the San Gottardo tunnel, for an easier exit towards Airolo and the pass. ### Impact on cross-border commuters For cross-border commuters who regularly use the San Gottardo pass, this reopening represents a significant advantage. The availability of the road during the period of heavy traffic for the holidays will help avoid congestion and delays, facilitating travel between the Canton of Ticino and the rest of Switzerland.
