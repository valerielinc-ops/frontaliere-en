PalaRaiffeisen in Lugano: open day to discover the mega-project (cross-border guide)

On Saturday, 18 April 2026, the City of Lugano will host an open day at the construction site of the future PalaRaiffeisen as part of the Sports and Events Complex.

The open day, organized in 30-minute slots, allowed hundreds of people to get up close and personal with the progress of the works and learn about the project’s details. The PalaRaiffeisen is a key component of the area’s future layout, which will also include the newly completed stadium. Dettagli operativi

The open day at the PalaRaiffeisen was not just an opportunity to admire an evolving construction site, but also a chance to reflect on the opportunities this major project will bring to Lugano and Ticino. For cross-border workers in particular, the project holds strategic value, both in terms of employment and logistics. ### Impact on cross-border workers: what’s changing? For transborder commuters who cross the border daily to reach Lugano, the PalaRaiffeisen and the new stadium represent an opportunity in terms of mobility and accessibility. The proximity of the sports hub to the railway station and major public transport hubs will streamline travel, reducing commute times and improving connectivity. Furthermore, the increased appeal of the city could lead to a higher demand for services, potentially benefiting the local job market as well. However, it’s important to consider any potential disruptions during the construction phase. Work, which will continue until the end of 2026, may result in traffic restrictions and limited parking in the surrounding areas. Punti chiave

The open day at the PalaRaiffeisen gave citizens and cross-border workers a preview of what the future sports and events hub in Lugano will look like. But what are the next steps for those who want to follow the project's progress or get actively involved? Here’s a practical guide to staying updated and making the most of the opportunities offered by the construction site. --- ### Tracking construction progress For those who couldn’t attend the open day, it’s possible to follow the construction site’s progress through the official channels of the City of Lugano. The municipal website regularly publishes photographic and video updates showing the work’s advancement. Additionally, further public engagement initiatives—such as guided tours and informational meetings—will be announced promptly. It’s also advisable to subscribe to the City of Lugano’s newsletter to receive direct updates in your email inbox. This way, you’ll always be informed about upcoming public openings and any changes to access routes to the construction site. --- ### Planning your commute: what to know For cross-border workers who travel to Lugano daily, staying updated on any traffic restrictions or parking limitations during the construction phase is essential. The City of Lugano has already created an interactive map highlighting the areas affected by the works and recommending alternative routes. This tool, available on the official website, allows you to view real-time congestion risks and plan your commute with greater ease. For those using public transport, it’s useful to check the updated schedules of SBB and Ticino’s regional bus services, as temporary route modifications may be introduced due to the construction work. Frequently Asked Questions
How can I register to participate in the next guided tours of the PalaRaiffeisen construction site?
Registration for guided tours is done through the official website of the City of Lugano. It has not yet been confirmed whether online booking will be available or if a telephone contact will be required. We recommend checking the dedicated section on the City’s events page for updates on upcoming public openings.
What are the main disruptions expected for cross-border workers during the construction phase?
During the works, traffic and parking restrictions will be in place in the areas surrounding the PalaRaiffeisen. The City of Lugano has created an interactive map with recommended alternative routes, available on its official website. We advise consulting it regularly to plan your journeys.
Will the PalaRaiffeisen be accessible during sporting or cultural events once completed?
Yes, once construction is finished, the PalaRaiffeisen will host sporting events, concerts, and major initiatives. The venue will therefore be open to the public during events, with entry and security procedures to be defined based on current regulations.
Are there job opportunities for cross-border workers on the PalaRaiffeisen construction site?
Yes, the construction site requires skilled labour in various sectors, including construction, engineering, and safety. Job offers are published by the companies involved, and in some cases, specific training courses may be organised. We recommend monitoring the websites of the companies and the official pages of the City of Lugano for updates.
How can I follow the progress of the construction work if I did not attend the open day?
You can track the construction site’s progress through regular photo and video updates published on the City of Lugano’s official website. Additionally, by subscribing to the City’s newsletter, you will receive direct communications about project developments and upcoming citizen engagement initiatives.
