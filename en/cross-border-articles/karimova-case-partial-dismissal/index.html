Karimova case: partial dismissal for Gulnara

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 28 April 2026

The Swiss Federal Criminal Court dismisses the case against Gulnara Karimova, daughter of the former Uzbek president, but proceedings continue against the bank and the asset manager

Contesto

In brief - Gulnara Karimova cannot leave Uzbekistan until 2028 - Case dismissed due to procedural impediment - Proceedings continue for bank and asset manager ## Key facts - What: Partial dismissal of the case against Gulnara Karimova - When: April 28, 2026 - Where: Federal Criminal Court, Bellinzona - Who: Gulnara Karimova, Geneva-based bank Lombard Odier, former asset manager - Amount: Not specified The Federal Criminal Court has decided to dismiss the case against Gulnara Karimova, daughter of the former president of Uzbekistan, accused of money laundering, breach of trust, corruption, and heading a criminal organization. The decision, made on Tuesday, is due to a lasting procedural impediment: the defendant cannot leave her country until December 2028, as she is serving a prison sentence. The Court cannot issue a verdict before the statute of limitations for the case. The dismissal only concerns Gulnara Karimova, while the trial continues for a former asset manager of Lombard Odier and the Geneva-based private bank itself, both pursued for money laundering. Gulnara Karimova was accused of having opened bank accounts at Swiss financial institutions through nominees. These accounts were then used to launder millions of dollars of criminal origin. For its part, the Geneva-based bank did not take all the organizational measures to prevent money laundering acts and did not sufficiently monitor the activities of its asset manager. ### Implications for the Swiss financial system The partial dismissal of the Karimova case raises questions about the control and prevention system for money laundering in Switzerland.
