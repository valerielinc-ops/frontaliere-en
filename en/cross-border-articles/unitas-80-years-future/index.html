Unitas turns 80: The association's outlook and future

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 29 April 2026

The Association for the Blind and Visually Impaired of Italian-speaking Switzerland celebrates 80 years, facing financial challenges and new inclusive projects in the region.

Contesto

In brief - Unitas celebrates 80 years of activity in the Ticino region. - President Fabio Casgnola will step down on May 23. - An inclusive concert with the OSI has been announced for late August. - Financial resources remain a priority challenge. ## Key facts - What: Eightieth anniversary of Unitas. - When: Celebrations underway, change of presidency on May 23. - Where: Historic headquarters on Via Nassa, Lugano. - Who: Association for the blind and visually impaired of Italian-speaking Switzerland. - Founder: Tarcisio Bisi (1946). The Association for the blind and visually impaired of Italian-speaking Switzerland, known as Unitas, has officially inaugurated the celebrations for its eightieth anniversary. The event, presented during a press conference held on Via Nassa in Lugano, retraced the history of the organization, founded in 1946 on that very same street by Tarcisio Bisi. The anniversary represents not only a chronological milestone but also a moment of reflection on the current needs of the community it serves. During the meeting, a clear desire to look toward the future emerged, while maintaining a firm commitment to supporting the people the association assists daily. The outgoing president, Fabio Casgnola, took the opportunity to outline future prospects, confirming that the appointment of the new presidency will take place on May 23, marking the end of his three-year term. The association emphasized that the keyword for this special year is 'future', understood as the continuous evolution of services and activities needed to meet the members' needs in a constantly changing social and economic context. Dettagli operativi

Prospects and Challenges of the Inclusive System The eightieth anniversary of Unitas raises important questions about the dynamics of assistance in Ticino. For citizens and families who interact with social services and support, the stability of entities like Unitas is crucial. The criticality related to financial contributions, explicitly mentioned by President Casgnola, reflects a tension common to many public utility entities in the canton. When funding does not cover expenses, there is a risk of contracting the services provided, with a direct impact on the quality of life of those who benefit from the services. The analysis of the current situation suggests that the future of inclusion in Ticino will depend on the ability to diversify revenues and maintain constant dialogue with public entities. Compared to the past, needs have become more complex: it is no longer just about basic assistance, but about cultural and social integration, as demonstrated by the initiative with the Orchestra della Svizzera italiana (OSI). This type of activity is not only recreational but serves as a model for active inclusion, allowing members to experience music from within the orchestra. For anyone operating or residing in the territory, supporting these realities passes through knowledge of their activities and the structural difficulties they face. The comparison with previous years highlights greater operational pressure, aggravated by rising costs and a need for technological innovation that requires constant investment. The association, while looking ahead, does not forget its history but must necessarily adapt the funding model. Participate in Events and Support the Association For those who wish to support Unitas' activities or take part in the initiatives planned for its 80th anniversary, it is essential to follow the official updates. The program includes high-profile events designed to raise awareness about the association beyond its usual circle. The inclusive concert at the end of August, organized in collaboration with OSI, offers a unique opportunity for 120 members to experience an orchestral performance first-hand. Additionally, the open house day scheduled for mid-October, coinciding with White Cane Day, will be a key moment to raise public awareness about issues related to visual disabilities. Frequently Asked Questions
What is the main event scheduled for the 80th anniversary of Unitas?
The association has announced several initiatives. Among these, a highlight is an inclusive concert with the OSI scheduled for the end of August, which will allow 120 members to sit among the musicians. Additionally, an open house day is planned for mid-October to mark White Cane Day.
Are there any changes in the association's leadership?
Yes. President Fabio Casgnola has announced that he will be stepping down after three years in the role. The appointment of the new association presidency is set for Saturday, May 23.
What is the association's main current challenge?
The main challenge highlighted during the press conference concerns financial resources. The association has noted that the financial contributions currently available are not sufficient to cover all the needs and planned activities.
