PLR criticizes professional training management tender requirements

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 27 April 2026

The PLR criticizes the requirements of the tender for the management of professional training in Ticino, raising concerns about the procedural handling.

Contesto

In brief - The PLR criticizes the requirements of the tender for the direction of Vocational Training. - Speziali and Piezzi ask for explanations on the procedural management. - The competition was extended without communications. ## Key facts - What: Criticism of the tender for the direction of Vocational Training. - When: April 25, 2026. - Where: Canton Ticino. - Who: PLR, Alessandro Speziali, Aron Piezzi. - Amount: Not specified. The PLR is back with criticism of the tender for the direction of Vocational Training in Canton Ticino. Alessandro Speziali and Aron Piezzi have expressed doubts about the competition requirements and procedural management. 'Questionable requirements and doubts about procedural management. Why an extra week of competition without saying anything or explaining?', the two PLR representatives declared. The discussion is still ongoing and the liberal radical party does not seem inclined to let the matter drop. The tender, which saw an extension without explanations, has raised doubts about the transparency and efficiency of the selection process. ### Implications for cross-border workers Cross-border workers working in the vocational training sector may be interested in changes in the direction of training. Dettagli operativi

Analysis of PLR Criticisms The PLR's criticisms focus on two main aspects: the tender requirements and procedural management. According to Speziali and Piezzi, the requirements for the leadership of Vocational Training are questionable and not clearly defined. This could lead to a less meritocratic selection and more subject to arbitrary interpretations. ### Procedural Management Procedural management is another critical point. The extension of the competition without explanations has raised doubts about the transparency of the process. According to the PLR, an additional week of competition without clear communications is unacceptable and could compromise the trust of candidates and the public. ### Implications for Cross-Border Workers Cross-border workers employed in the vocational training sector could be affected by the PLR's criticisms. If the tender requirements were to be modified, new conditions could be introduced that might influence their employment status. Furthermore, greater transparency in procedural management could improve the trust of cross-border workers in the cantonal system. ### Comparison with Other Regions In other Swiss regions, competitions for managerial positions are often managed with greater transparency. The Canton of Ticino could benefit from adopting best practices from other regions, ensuring a fairer and more transparent selection process. ### Possible Scenarios If the PLR's criticisms were to lead to changes in the tender, the requirements for the leadership of Vocational Training could be reviewed. This could affect both local candidates and those from Italy, especially if the new requirements were more stringent or less transparent. ### Comparative Table | Aspect | Current Situation | Desired Situation by PLR | |---------|----...
