Swiss banks prepare for Gulf clients

Di Valerie Linc · Published on 2 May 2026

Fund transfers underway to Switzerland from Gulf countries due to the war in Iran. Here's what changes for cross-border workers.

Contesto

In brief - Funds transfers from the Gulf to Switzerland - Doubts about security in the Emirates - Renewed interest in the Swiss financial hub ## Key facts - What: Funds transfers and possible residency changes - When: From March 2026 - Where: Switzerland, particularly Canton Ticino - Who: Wealthy clients from the Gulf and Swiss banks - Amount: Almost a quarter of the assets managed in Switzerland come from the Gulf The war in Iran is having unexpected consequences, such as renewed interest in the Swiss financial hub. Some clients are already transferring funds, or even considering moving, as the conflict affects a region that has focused on promoting economic and financial stability. It's not yet an exodus, but the signs are becoming clearer. ### Ongoing transfers "Fund transfers are underway" confirms Patrick Akiki, head of the financial services market for the consulting firm PwC Switzerland. But it takes time, he continues, because "regulatory constraints have changed. We are much stricter, and Swiss banks are doing what is necessary to ensure compliance of cases before proceeding with transfers." ### Banks' discretion Officially, most banks do not comment, preferring to remain discreet, perhaps to avoid giving the impression of taking advantage of a delicate situation. They also do not comment on another emerging trend: that of large fortunes that in recent years have moved to Dubai or other Emirates and now doubt their choice. ### Doubts about security in the Emirates As for the Emirates, their model is faltering with current tensions. "The region has developed essentially on the principle of security, on the level of education, on medical support and also on the quality of life," adds Patrick Akiki. Firstly, it could increase the demand for financial and real estate services, which could lead to higher prices and greater competition for access to these services. ### Comparison with the previous situation Before the conflict in Iran, Switzerland was already a popular destination for large fortunes from the Gulf, but the interest was less urgent. Now, with the ongoing tensions, Switzerland is seen as a safe haven, which could lead to an increase in fund transfers and a possible rise in demand for real estate and financial services. ### 'What if' scenarios If the conflict in the Gulf were to persist, it is likely that interest in Switzerland as a safe haven would further increase. This could lead to an increase in fund transfers and a possible rise in demand for real estate and financial services in Switzerland, particularly in the canton of Ticino, which is already a popular destination for cross-border workers. ### Concrete procedures For cross-border workers considering a move, it is important to know that the procedures for opening bank accounts and purchasing real estate in Switzerland are rigorous. First, it is important to consult a financial advisor to understand Swiss regulations and the requirements for opening bank accounts and purchasing real estate. ### Required documentation Swiss banks require detailed documentation, which may include a passport, proof of residence, tax returns, and bank references.
