In brief - European funds in Ticino and Lombardy - Difference between direct and indirect funds - Examples of funded projects ## Key facts - What: European funds FSE, FESR, PNRR, Horizon - When: Currently ongoing - Where: Lombardy and Ticino - Who: European Commission, Regions, local authorities - Amount: Approximately 80% of the funds are indirectly managed European funds are a crucial resource for the economic and social development of Ticino and Lombardy. Among the most well-known programs are the European Social Fund (FSE), the European Regional Development Fund (FESR), the National Recovery and Resilience Plan (PNRR), and Horizon, dedicated to research and innovation. These funds are managed both directly by the European Commission and through the Regions, which publish specific tenders for businesses, associations, and citizens. A crucial aspect is the distinction between directly and indirectly managed funds. The former are administered directly by Brussels and often require co-financing from applicants. Among these, the Horizon programs stand out, supporting research and innovation projects. Indirectly managed funds, on the other hand, represent approximately 80% of European resources and are allocated to the Regions, which distribute them through dedicated tenders. In Lombardy, for example, tenders are published through regional channels dedicated to economic development, training, the environment, and innovation. Dettagli operativi

European funds not only finance large-scale projects but also support initiatives that improve the daily lives of citizens. For example, the European Regional Development Fund (ERDF) has funded the creation of bike lanes, the purchase of electric buses, and the establishment of museums and local infrastructure. These interventions contribute to making cities more livable and sustainable, offering citizens green spaces, eco-friendly transportation, and cultural opportunities. ### Impact on cross-border workers For cross-border workers employed in Ticino, European funds offer numerous opportunities for training and professional development. The European Social Fund (ESF) supports professional retraining courses, particularly useful for those affected by industrial crises or production changes. These courses enable workers to acquire new skills and improve their career prospects. Furthermore, the National Recovery and Resilience Plan (NRRP) funds research and innovation projects, providing job opportunities and collaboration with universities and research centers. This is particularly relevant for cross-border workers interested in high-tech and innovative sectors such as artificial intelligence, digitalization, and energy transition. ### Concrete examples Among the projects funded by European funds in Lombardy and Ticino are the redevelopment of the Reggia di Venaria Reale, interventions at the Villa Reale di Monza, and support for the Naples metro. Locally, funds have supported projects such as the redevelopment of Busto Arsizio's city center, Villa Toeplitz, and the Carducci and Isis Newton schools in Varese. To access European funds, it's important to follow some key steps. First, you need to identify the relevant calls for proposals published by the Regions. These calls are available on regional websites dedicated to economic development, training, the environment, and innovation. Once you have identified the calls of interest, it's crucial to carefully read the requirements and deadlines, ensuring that you submit your application within the established terms. ### Step-by-step to access funds 1. Identify calls: Visit regional websites to find relevant calls. 2. Read requirements: Check eligibility criteria and required documents. 3. Prepare the application: Fill out the application carefully, providing all necessary information. 4. Submit the application: Send the application by the established deadline. 5. Monitor the application status: Periodically check the status of your application and promptly respond to any requests for clarification. ### Useful tools To facilitate access to European funds, you can use tools such as the funding calculator and the call comparator available on the Frontaliere Ticino website. These tools allow you to quickly identify relevant calls and calculate the maximum available funding. ### Conclusion European funds represent a valuable resource for Ticino and Lombardy, offering concrete opportunities for economic and social development.
