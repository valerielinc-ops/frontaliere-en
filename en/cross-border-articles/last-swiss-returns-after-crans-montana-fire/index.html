In brief - Last Swiss patient hospitalized returns to Switzerland - UFPC no longer publishes weekly data - Three new hearings scheduled ## Key facts - What: Return of the last Swiss citizen hospitalized abroad - When: Next week - Where: Crans-Montana - Who: Federal Office for Civil Protection (UFPC) - Amount: 38 injured in total, 6 still under care in Lausanne, 4 in Zurich The last Swiss citizen hospitalized abroad following the New Year's tragedy in Crans-Montana is ready to return to Switzerland next week. This situation has led the Federal Office for Civil Protection (UFPC) to no longer publish weekly data relating to the place of hospitalization of the injured in the fire. 'The main reason why the UFPC recorded national data was to plan repatriations based on hospital capacities,' the office specified today to Keystone-ATS. 'This data was therefore an essential element for the decision-making process of the competent authorities. As this need is now satisfied, such data is no longer collected,' it was emphasized. ### Last injured person returns home In the last count published on April 29, there were 38 injured, six of whom are still under care at CHUV in Lausanne and four in Zurich. With the return of the last injured person, a painful chapter for the Swiss community comes to an end. The fire caused 41 deaths and 115 injuries, leaving a deep mark on the Crans-Montana region. ### The investigation continues Meanwhile, the investigation into the fire continues. Three new suspects in relation to the fire at the bar Le Constellation will be questioned next week by the group of prosecutors in charge of the case. Two of them come from the political environment.
