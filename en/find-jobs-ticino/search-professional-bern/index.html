More about this search for cross-border applicants

Bern, the de facto capital of Switzerland, hosts a diverse professional job market spanning federal administration, healthcare, research institutions, and precision engineering. While the city operates primarily in German, international organizations and multinational firms often use English as a working language. For cross-border workers from northern Italy, Bern presents distinct considerations: the city lies outside the Italian-speaking cantons, requiring German proficiency for most roles, and commuting from the Ticino border would exceed practical daily travel limits. The local labor market favors candidates with Swiss or EU permits, with salaries typically aligned to the higher Swiss German cost of living.
