Original description

The Office for Forests and Natural Hazards (AWN) is the cantonal agency for forests and protection against natural hazards. The AWN employees are committed to the needs of society in the forest and ensure that the forest is dealt with sustainably. ## Tasks - Support of the specialist in natural hazards in the southern Grisons region from planning/conception regarding hazard assessment, risk analyses to statements on building applications and test procedures to the implementation of expert opinions and projects for the attention of the danger zones or emergency planning in the sense of prevention and precaution - Independent processing of selected, object/project-specific orders and hazard assessments in the natural hazard processes avalanche, fall, landslide or water - Preparation and participation in inspections of the hazard commission, recording of the results for the attention of the hazard commission and development of the plans of the hazard commission - Support of the specialists in natural hazards and the regional forestry engineers in the management of natural hazard events - Support of the regional forestry engineers in the recording and control of events in the register of incidents - Support of the specialist protective structures in the company of the cantonal protective structures cadastre ## • Requirements - A higher education (ETH, university, university of applied sciences or HF) or equivalent training corresponding to the job profile - In-depth knowledge of natural hazards, namely process knowledge and ArcGIS Pro - Committed, team-oriented and reliable personality - General working language is German, good knowledge of several cantonal languages is an advantage
