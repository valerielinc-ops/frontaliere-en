The "Studio Foundations" was first taught in the autumn semester 2025 as a first year course in the revised Bachelor curriculum in Architecture at ETH Zurich. The course lays the foundation of the architectural design for about 400 D-ARCH students and is jointly developed by a collective of eight professors: ins, who teaches the areas of design (Roger Boltshauser), Computational Design (Fabio Gramazio), construction (Daniel Mettler), building materials (Jacqueline Pauli), structure (Philippe Block), energy and climate systems (Arno Schlüter Delke) The didactic concept of the course is based on the integration of various disciplines within the design teaching. This competence-oriented approach promotes a complex and systemic understanding of architecture and enables students to address architectural challenges from different perspectives and to address current issues. The focus is on the simple, sustainable construction in a contemporary architectural language. The "Studio Foundations" focuses on students and orchestrates the application of disciplinary knowledge and skills in the design over two semesters. Through direct and sensual access, students acquire basic concepts such as context and landscape, climate and energy, light and space, meaning and form as well as material and structure and understand their influence on architectural design. The course emphasizes interdisciplinary and thus critical thinking by combining abstract concepts with real experiences in different standards – from living spaces to urban landscapes.
