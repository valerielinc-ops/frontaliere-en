Main responsibilities

  • and activities
  • The candidate will have the opportunity to take part in an engaging and stimulating interdisciplinary research project in the field of Alpine geology by integrating terrain and analytical data with three-dimensional geological models. In particular:
  • Through geological cartography, it collects, processes and interprets terrain data in specific areas on the eastern edge of the Lepontine cathedral.
  • Describes thin sections of rocks and reconstructs metamorphic processes.
  • Performs geochemical analyzes with XRF, Electron Probe Micro-Analyser, LA-ICPMS and possibly with SIMS methods.
  • Use software to calculate phase diagrams and thermodynamic data (Perplex), to simulate the diffusion of elements in metamorphic minerals (geospeedometry) and, as needed, to model tectonic structures.
  • Collaborates closely with researchers responsible for the thermomechanical modeling of the Alpine mountain range in 3D.
  • Collaborates with IST in applied research and geological mapping projects. Requirements
  • We are looking for a person with a solid academic background, motivated to work in a research and development context. The following are required:
  • University Master's degree in geology, possibly with specialization in structural geology or metamorphic petrology.
