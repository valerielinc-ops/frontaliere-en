Original description

Mission With more than 100 apprentices in training in a dozen professions, EPFL is a fully-fledged training company. Regardless of the apprenticeship chosen at EPFL, apprentices carry out their vocational training in an extraordinary environment composed of research laboratories in fields as varied as engineering, the exact sciences and architecture. They benefit from all the dynamism and diversity of a university campus that offers them many opportunities, advantages and opportunities for personal development. At the École polytechnique fédérale de Lausanne (EPFL), you have the opportunity to embark on an exciting learning in the field of automation, as automatic or automatic. Main tasks and responsibilities At EPFL, the automatic apprentice or the automatic apprentice will discover a complete trade combining mechanical, electronic and industrial computing. He or she will participate in the manufacture of mechanical parts, both by manual machining and on machine tools. The training will also include wiring and assembly of electrical plates, installation, commissioning and troubleshooting of automated machines, including robots and robotic systems. The apprentice will be required to design mechanical components and prototypes using 3D modeling tools (CAO) and 3D printing. He or she will develop and program Industrial Automation (PLC) and help integrate specialized automated systems, including smart sensors, electrical drives and robots. During his training, the apprentice will learn how to analyse the overall operation of an installation, diagnose and correct faults, optimize performance and apply safety and quality standards. The working environment is innovative and constantly evolving, at the heart of cutting-edge research. As automation progresses rapidly, the apprentice will have the opportunity to become familiar with emerging technologies while developing concrete, interdisciplinary and highly sought after skills in an exciting and forward-looking sector.
