Main responsibilities

  • and activities
  • The candidate will have the opportunity to take part in the research, mandate and service activities of the Climate Change and Territory Competence Center (CCCT), a reference point at the southern Alpine level in the field of applied geomorphology, where he will have the opportunity to develop and perfect the following skills:
  • Cartography, monitoring and modeling of slope movements in an Alpine environment
  • Evaluation of natural hazards and risks of geomorphological origin
  • Remote sensing monitoring, in particular through the autonomous acquisition of images from a drone and the processing and analysis of aerial and satellite images (especially satellite radar interferometry) Requirements
  • We are looking for a person with a solid academic background, motivated to work in an applied research and development context. The following are required:
  • Master's degree in geosciences or related fields
  • Knowledge in applied geomorphology/geology, with particular emphasis on the Alpine environment
  • Experience in field monitoring and remote sensing data acquisition
  • Knowledge and experience with Territorial Information Systems (GIS)
